BOQONNAA 101. Dekanter.

Ere dooniin Ingilizii ija irraa haftee, asitti kaa'amee haa ta'u, akka isheen Landan irraa dhufte, fi maqaa boqonnaa kan hin qabne Saamu'eel Enderby, daldalaa magaalaa sanaa, kan jalqabaa mana qurxummii galaanaa beekamaa Enderby & Sons; mana akka yaada nama qurxummii galaanaa hiyyeessaa kootti, manneen mootii tokkummaa warra Tuudor fi Bourbons irraa fagoo hin taane, qabxii fedhii seenaa dhugaa ta’een. Bara Gooftaa keenyaa 1775 dura, manni qurxummii guddaan kun hanga yoomiitti akka ture, sanadoonni qurxummii koo baay’een ifa hin ta’an; garuu bara sanatti (1775) dooniiwwan Ingilizii jalqabaa yeroo hunda Sperm Whale adamsan qopheesse; haa ta’u malee waggoota kurnan tokko tokko dura (bara 1726 irraa eegalee) Coffins fi Maceys keenya jajjaboon kan Nantucket fi Vineyard doonii gurguddaa ta’een Leviathan sana hordofaa turan, garuu Kaabaa fi Kibba Atlaantiik qofa keessatti: bakka biraatti miti. Asitti addatti kan galmaa’e haa ta’u, Nantucketers dhala namaa keessaa warra jalqabaa sibiila qaroomaatiin Harpoon guddaa Sperm Whale akka ta’an; akkasumas walakkaa jaarraa tokkoof namoota addunyaa guutuu keessatti akkasitti isa harpoon godhan isaan qofa akka ta’an.

Bara 1778tti dooniin gaariin Ameeliyaa jedhamtu kaayyoo ariifachiisaa kanaaf kan qophaaʼe siʼa taʼu, Enderbys warra ciccimoo qofaan kan qophaaʼe siʼa taʼu, ija jabinaan Keep Hoorn kan naannessite siʼa taʼu, saboota keessaa bidiruu qurxummii galaanaa gosa kamiiyyuu kan guddaa Galaana Kibbaa. Imalli kun ogummaa fi carraa kan qabu ture; akkasumas qabannaa ishee isparmii gatii guddaa qabuun guutamee gara buufata ishee deebitee, fakkeenyi Ameeliyaa yeroo muraasa booda dooniiwwan biroo, Ingilizii fi Ameerikaa hordofan, akkasitti lafti bal’aan Ispeermii Whale kan Paasifikii banaa ta’e. Garuu hojii gaarii kanaan osoo hin quufne, manni hin dadhabne sun ammas of kakaase: Saamu’eel fi Ilmaan isaa hundi-meeqa, haati isaanii qofti beekti-akkasumas to’annoo battalaa isaanii jalatti, fi gartokkoon, baasii isaaniitiin, mootummaan Ingilizii kakaafame jedheen yaada doonii sloop-of-war Rattler imala argannoo qurxummii galaanaatiin gara Galaana Kibbaatti erguuf. Raatler kan ajajamu Poostaa Kaapteen humna galaanaatiin, imala rattling ishee godhe, tajaajila tokko tokkos hojjete; meeqa hin mul’atu. Garuu kana qofa miti. Bara 1819tti, manni sun doonii qurxummii qurxummii mataa isaanii taʼe qopheesse, gara bishaan fagoo Jaappaanitti imala dhandhamaa deemuuf. Dooniin sun—“Syren” jedhamtee akka gaariitti kan waamamu—yeroo yaalii kabajamaa taʼe goote; akkasumas akkasitti ture kan Jaappaan guddaan Whaling Ground jalqaba akka waliigalaatti beekame. Sireen imala beekamaa kana keessatti Kaapteen Coffin, Nantucketer jedhamuun kan ajajamu ture.

Kabajni hundi Enderbies, kanaaf, kan manni isaanii, hanga har’aatti jira jedheen yaada; shakkii tokko malee Saamu’eel inni jalqabaa yeroo dheeraa dura Galaana Kibbaa guddaa addunyaa isa kaaniif keebilii isaa siqsee ta’uu qaba.

Dooniin maqaa isaatiin moggaafamte kun kabaja kanaaf kan malu yoo ta’u, baay’ee saffisaa kan deemtuu fi karaa hundumaan ogummaa ogummaa olaanaa kan qabdu turte. Yeroo tokko halkan walakkaa qarqara galaanaa Paatagoniyaan bakka tokkotti ishee yaabbadhee, forecastle keessatti flip gaarii gadi dhuge. Gam gaarii nuti qabnu ture, hundi isaaniis trumps turan-lubbuu doonii sana keessa jiru hunda. Jireenya gabaabaa isaaniif, fi du'a gammachiisaa. Gam gaariin ani qabu sun immoo—Ahaab dulloomaan kofoo isaa isa gaanfa gaanfaatiin pilaanii ishee erga tuqee yeroo dheeraa, baayʼee dheeraa booda—keessummummaa kabajamaa, jabaa, Saaksonii doonii sanaa na yaadachiisa; akkasumas paarsiin koo na haa dagatu, yoom iyyuu yoon jalaa bade seexannis na haa yaadatu. Garagalchuu? Flip qabna jedheen turee? Eeyyee, saʼaatii tokkotti saffisa gaalonii kudhaniin garagalchina turre; akkasumas yeroo obomboleettiin dhufu (for it’s squally off there by Patagonia), fi harki hundi—daawwattootaa fi hundi—gara reef topsails waamaman, nuti baay’ee gubbaa-ulfaataa waan turreef, sarara bowwaa keessatti ol wal jigsuu qabna; akkasumas wallaalummaadhaan siree jaakkeetii keenyaa gara doonii sanaatti waan galchineef, achitti fannifne, obomboleettii iyyaa jiru keessatti saffisaan reefed, fakkeenya akeekkachiisaa daakiiwwan machaa’an hundaaf. Haataʼu malee, mastoonni sun garmalee hin deemne; yeroo yeroon gadi buune, akka malee of eeggannoodhaan, ammas garagalcha sana darbuu qabna turre, haa ta’u malee, biifaan soogidda gara jabeessa forecastle scuttle gadi dhoo’u, baay’ee diluted fi pickled it to my taste.

Foon loonii sun gaarii ture—jabaa, garuu qaama of keessaa qaba. Foon re’ee ture jedhan; kaan immoo, foon loonii dromedary akka ta’e; garuu sun akkamitti akka ture mirkanaa’ee hin beeku. Isaanis dumplings qaban turan; xixiqqoo, garuu guddaa, simeetarikii geengoo ta’anii fi kan hin balleessine dumplings. Isaan akka sitti dhaga’amu, fi erga liqimfamanii booda si keessatti akka isaan marsanii naanna’u jedheen yaada ture. Yoo gara fuulduraatti garmalee jilbeenfatte, akka kubbaa biliyaardii si keessaa ba'uu isaanii balaadhaaf saaxila turte. Daabboon sun—garuu sun gargaaruu hin dandeenye; kana malees, farra iskorbuutikii ture; gabaabumatti daabboon sun gatii haaraa isaan qaban qofa of keessaa qaba ture. Garuu forecastle baayyee salphaa hin turre, yeroo nyaattu gara golee dukkanaa'aa tokkotti tarkaanfachuun baay'ee salphaa ture. Garuu walumaa galatti, konkolaataa fe’umsaa irraa gara konkolaachisaa ishee geessuun, boba’aa gogaa lubbuu qabu dabalatee, safara boba’aa nyaata bilcheessuu sanaa ilaalcha keessa galchuun; fuulduraa fi duubaan, ani jedha, Saamu’eel Enderby doonii gammachiisaa ture; kan gatii gaarii fi baay’ee; fine flip fi cimaa; crack fellows hunda, fi kaappitaala boot heels irraa kaasee hanga hat-band.

Garuu maaliif akkas ta’e, yaadi, Saamu’eel Enderby, fi warri qurxummii Ingilizii kanneen biroo ani beeku tokko tokko—hundi isaanii osoo hin taane—doonii beekamoo, keessummeessitoota akkasii turan; kan foon loonii, fi buddeena, fi qaruuraa, fi qoosaa naannoo darbe; yeroo muraasa boodas nyaachuu, dhuguu fi kolfuu hin dadhabne? Ani isinitti hima. Gammachuun gaariin warra qurxummii galaanaa Ingilizii kanaa baay’inaan mul’atu qorannoo seenaatiif dhimma ta’a. Akkasumas qorannoo seenaa qurxummii galaanaa irraa tasumaa hin qusanne, yeroo barbaachisaa ta’ee mul’atutti.

Ingilizoonnis qurxummii qurxummii qurxummii galaanaa irratti kan dursan lammiilee Hoolaandii, Ziilaandii fi Deenmaark turan; jechoota hedduu ammallee qurxummii qabachuu keessatti jiran kan irraa argatan; kana caalaas, faashinii isaanii isa durii furdaa, nyaachuu fi dhugaatii baay’ee tuquu. Sababni isaas, akka waan waliigalaa tokkootti, dooniin daldalaa Ingilizii hojjettoota ishee ni scrimps; garuu akkas miti kan Ingilizii qurxummii galaanaa. Kanarraa ka’uun, afaan Ingiliffaa keessatti wanti gammachuu gaarii qurxummii galaanaa (whaling good cheer) jedhu kun waanuma jiruu fi uumamaa osoo hin taane, akka tasaa fi adda ta’eedha; akkasumas, kanaaf, ka’umsa addaa tokko tokko qabaachuu qaba, kunis asitti kan ibsame yoo ta’u, ammas caalaatti ifa ta’a.

Seenaa Leviathanic irratti qorannoowwan koo yeroon godhu, jildii durii Dutch tokko irratti gufadhe, kunis, urgaa isaa isa xuraa’aa qurxummii qabuun, waa’ee warra qurxummii qurxummii qaban ta’uu akka qabu nan beeka ture. Mata dureen isaa, “Dan Coopman,” kanaaf, dooniin qurxummii qurxummii qabu hundinuu cooper isaa baattee deemuu waan qabuuf, kun yaadannoo gatii guddaa hin qabne kan qurxummii Amsterdam cooper tokko tokkoo ta’uu qaba jedheen xumure. Yaada kanaan kan na cime oomisha “Fitz Swackhammer” tokkoo ta’uu isaa arguun ture. Garuu hiriyaan koo Dr. Snodhead, nama baay’ee barate, piroofeesara barnoota Low Dutch fi High German kolleejjii Santa Claus fi St. Pott’s keessatti, kan ani hojii sana hiikkaaf itti kenne, rakkina isaaf saanduqa shugguxii isparmii kenne— Dr. Snodhead kun, akkuma kitaabicha basaaseen, “Dan Coopman” jechuun “The Cooper” jechuu akka hin taane, “The Merchant” jechuu akka ta’e naaf mirkaneesse. Gabaabumatti, kitaabni durii fi baratame Low Dutch kun waa’ee daldala Holland kan ilaallatu ture; akkasumas, dhimmoota biroo keessaa, waa’ee qurxummii qurxummii qurxummii galaanaa ishee baay’ee hawwataa ta’e qabateera. Boqonnaa kana keessattis, mata duree, “Smeer,” ykn “Fat” jedhuun tarree bal’aa dheeraa uffata mana kuusaa fi mana kuusaa namoota doonii 180 kanneen qurxummii galaanaa Nezerlaand ta’aniif oolu argadhe; tarree kana keessaa, akka Dr. Snodhead hiikeetti, kanneen armaan gadii barreessa:

400,000 lbs. kan foon loonii. 60,000 lbs. Foon allaattii Friesland. 150,000 lbs. kan qurxummii istookii. 550,000 lbs. kan biskutii. 72,000 lbs. kan daabboo lallaafaa. dhadhaa firkii 2,800 ta'a. 20,000 lbs. Daabboo Texel & Leyden jedhamuun beekama. 144,000 lbs ta'a. cheese (barruu gadi aanaa ta’uu hin oolu). 550 ankers kan Jeeneevaa. biiraa barmeela 10,800 ta'eera.

Gabateewwan istaatiksii irra caalaan isaanii dubbisa keessatti goggogaa dha; haala ammaa keessatti garuu akkas miti, dubbisaan tuuboo guutuu, barmeela, kuart, fi gills of good gin and good cheer lolaafame.

Yeroo sanatti, guyyoota sadii biiraa, foon loonii fi daabboo kana hunda qorannoodhaan daakuuf nan dabarseera, yeroo sanatti yaadonni gadi fagoo hedduun akka tasaatti natti yaadaman, kanneen hojii irra oolmaa ol’aanaa fi Pilaatoonikii ta’uu danda’an; akkasumas, kana malees, gabatee dabalataa mataa kootii qindeesseen, baay’ina qurxummii istookii fi kkf ta’uu danda’u tuqee, kan harpooneer Low Dutch hundaan qurxummii qurxummii galaanaa Giriinlaandii fi Spitzbergen durii sana keessatti nyaatu. Jalqaba irratti, hammi dhadhaa, fi Texel fi Leyden cheese nyaatame, waan ajaa'ibaa fakkaata. Ani garuu, uumama isaanii uumamaan kan hin xuroofne ta’uu isaaniitiin, uumama waamicha isaaniitiin ammas caalaatti kan hin xuroofne ta’uu isaaniitiin, fi keessumaa immoo Galaana Poolaar qorraa sana keessatti tapha isaanii hordofuu isaaniitiin, qarqara galaanaa biyya Esquimaux sanaa bakka namoonni dhalattoonni michuu ta’an waadaa galan sanaatti nan lakkaa’a walii isaanii baampaara zayita baaburaa keessatti.

Baay’inni biiraas, baay’ee guddaadha, barmeela 10,800. Amma, akkuma qurxummiin poolaarii sun ganna gabaabaa haala qilleensaa sanaa keessatti qofa himatamuu danda’u, kanaaf, imala gabaabaa gara galaana Spitzbergen fi deebi’uu dabalatee, guutummaan doonii namoota qurxummii galaanaa Nezerlaand kana keessaa tokkoo, ji’a sadii baay’ee hin caalu, jedhaa, akkasumas tokkoon tokkoon doonii isaanii kan doonii 180 ta’eef dhiirota 30 yoo lakkoofne, walumaa galatti namoota galaanarraa 5,400 Low Dutch qabna; kanaaf, ani akkan jedhutti, nama tokkoof sirriitti barmeela biiraa lama qabna, torban kudha lamaaf, qooda isaa isa madaalawaa ankers 550 sana irraa osoo hin dabalatin. Amma, harpooneers gin fi biiraa kun, hamma namni tokko akkas jedhee yaadutti fuddled ta'anii, namoota sirrii mataa bidiruu keessa ka'anii, fi balali'aa whales irratti kaayyoo gaarii qabaachuu danda'an ta'uu fi dhiisuu isaanii; kun hamma tokko waan hin yaadamne fakkaata. Ta'us isaan irratti kaayyeffatan, isaanis rukutaniiru. Garuu kun Kaaba baayyee fagoo ture, haa yaadatamu, bakka biirri heera mootummaa waliin akka gaariitti walii galu; Ikuwaator irratti, qurxummii keenya isa kibbaa keessatti, biirri harpooneer mataa mast-mast irratti hirriba fi bidiruu isaa keessatti boozy gochuuf mijataa ta’a; akkasumas kasaaraa cimaan Naantucket fi New Bedford irratti mudachuu danda’a.

Garuu kana booda hin jiru; warri durii Nezerlaand jaarraa lama ykn sadii dura qurxummii galaanaa qaban kalee olka’aa akka turan agarsiisuuf gahaa ta’eera; akkasumas warri qurxummii galaanaa Ingilizii fakkeenya gaarii akkasii akka hin tuffanne. Sababni isaas, akka jedhanitti, yeroo doonii duwwaa keessa deemtan, addunyaa kanarraa waan kana caalu argachuu yoo hin dandeenye, yoo xiqqaate irbaata gaarii keessaa argadhaa. Kunis decanter duwwaa godha.





BOQONNAA 102. Bower Arsacides keessatti.

Hanga ammaatti, waa’ee Ispeermii Whale ibsaan ilaaluu keessatti, irra caalaa dinqii gama alaa isaa irratti xiyyeeffadheera; yookiin addaan baafamee fi bal’inaan amala caasaa keessaa muraasa tokko tokko irratti. Garuu hubannoo guddaa fi gadi fageenyaan isa bal’aa ta’eef, amma ammas caalaatti isa hiikuu, fi qabxiiwwan hoosii isaa hiikuu, garters isaa hiikuu, fi hookkoo fi ija walqabsiisa lafee isaa isa keessaa hiikuu, kaa’ame hiikuun naaf mala isa si duratti ultimatum isaa keessatti; jechuunis lafee isaa haalduree hin qabne keessatti.

Amma garuu akkamitti Ismaa'eel? Akkamitti, ati, nama qurxummii qabuu keessatti doonii oofu qofa, waa’ee kutaalee qurxummii galaanaa lafa jalaa waan tokko akka beektu fakkeessita? Erudite Stubb, capstan kee irratti fe'amee, waa'ee anatoomii Cetacea irratti barumsa kenniteeraa; akkasumas gargaarsa windlass, agarsiisaaf lafee cinaachaa saayimenii ol qabachuu? Ismaa’eel of ibsi. Akkuma namni nyaata bilcheessuun allaattii bilcheefame nyaachisu, qurxummii galaanaa guutuu taʼe tokko deekii kee irratti buufachuu dandeessaa? Dhugumatti akkas miti. Ismaa’el, hanga ammaatti dhugaa ba’aa taatee jirta; garuu akkamitti mirga Yoonaas qofa akka fayyadamtu of eeggannoo qabaadhu; mirga joists fi beams irratti discoursing gochuu; rafters, ridge-pole, sleepers, fi under-pinnings, frame-work leviathan kan uumu; akkasumas akka tallow-vats, dairy-rooms, butteries fi cheeseries garaacha isaa keessa jiru.

Ani nan himadha, erga Yoonaas, namoonni qurxummii galaanaa muraasni gogaa qurxummii ga’eessotaa jalatti baay’ee fagoo akka seenan; kanas ta’e sana, carraa xiqqaadhaan isa qoqqooduun eebbifameera. Doonii ani kan ani ta’e tokko keessatti, yeroo tokko ilmoon xiqqaan Sperm Whale jedhamu tokko, poke ykn boorsaa isaaf, barb harpoons, fi mataa eeboodhaaf sheath hojjechuuf qaama isaatiin gara deck ol kaafamee ture. Carraa sana akkan gadhiise sitti fakkaata, osoo bidiruu-hatchet fi jack-knife koo hin fayyadamin, fi chaappaa cabsee qabiyyee ilmoo xiqqaa sanaa hunda osoo hin dubbisin?

Akkasumas beekumsa sirrii lafee leviathan guddina isaanii guddaa, guutuu guddate keessatti qabu ilaalchisee, beekumsa hin argamne sanaaf hiriyyaa koo mootii du’aan boqote Tranquo, mootii Tranque, warra Arsacides keessaa tokko irraa liqii qaba. Tranque keessa waanan tureef, waggoota dura, yeroon doonii daldalaa Dey of Algiers jedhamtutti maxxane, ayyaana Arsacidean keessaa gar tokko gooftaa Tranque wajjin, viila isaa soorama ba’e kan meexxii Pupella jirutti akkan dabarsu affeeramee ture; gaara qarqara galaanaa jiru kan dooniiwwan keenya magaalaa guddoo isaa Baambuu-Magaalaa jedhanii waamu irraa baayʼee fagoo hin taane.

Amaloota gaarii biroo hedduu keessaa, hiriyaan koo mootii Traanquo, dhimmoota barbaric vertu hundaaf jaalala cimaa kennaa waan ta’eef, wantoota baay’ee hin argamne kanneen namoonni isaa keessaa caalaatti ogummaa qaban kalaquu danda’an kamiyyuu Pupella keessatti walitti fidee ture; irra caalaa muka meeshaalee dinqisiisoo bocaman, qola ciccitaa, eeboo bocame, doonii gatii guddaa baasu, doonii urgooftuu; kun hundinuu immoo dinqii uumamaa kamiyyuu gidduutti raabsamanii, dalga ajaa’ibaa fe’amee fi gibira kaffalu qarqara galaanaa isaa irratti darbate.

Kanneen boodaa keessaa inni guddaan Sperm Whale guddaa ture, innis, obomboleettii yeroo dheeraa hin baratamneen booda, du’ee fi qabamee argame, mataan isaa muka kookaa-nut irratti, kan rifeensa fakkaatu, tufted droopings jeettii isaa isa magariisa fakkaatu ture. Qaamni bal’aan sun dhuma irratti golgaa isaa isa hamma fathom gad fagoo ta’e irraa mulqamee, lafeen isaas aduu keessatti dafqaan yeroo gogu, sana booda lafeen sun of eeggannoodhaan gara boolla Pupella glen, bakka amma mana qulqullummaa guddaan muka gooftaa itti gaaddisa ta’etti ol geejjibame.

Lafee cinaachaa waancaadhaan fannifamee ture; lafee dugdaatti kan bocaman seenaa Arsacidean annals, hieroglyphics ajaa'ibaatiin; mataa keessaa luboonni abidda urgooftuu hin dhaamne waan qabaniif, mataan iccitii sun ammas danfaa isaa erge; yeroo ta’u, damee tokko irraa fannifamee, jilbi gadii suukaneessaan sun akka billaa rifeensa irratti fannifamee fi Daamookilis baay’ee sodaachisee waaqeffattoota hunda irratti raafama ture.

Mul'ata ajaa'ibaa ture. Mukti sun akka moosii Icy Glen magariisa ture; mukkeen ol ka’anii fi of tuulanii dhaabbatanii, dhangala’aa lubbuu qabu itti dhaga’ama; lafti cimaan jala jiru akka meeshaa huccuu huccuu, kaarpeeti bareedaa kan qabu siʼa taʼu, innis citaa wayinii lafa irraa marfamee fi huccuu, daraaraan lubbuu qabu immoo fakkiiwwan uumu ture. Mukkeen hundinuu, damee isaanii isa fe’ame hundumaa wajjin; biqiltoota, ferns, fi marga hundumaa; qilleensa ergaa baatu; kun hundinuu osoo addaan hin kutin sochii taasisaa turan. Karaa hidhaa baala sanaa, aduun guddaan sun, shaatal balaliʼu kan marga hin dadhabne sana hodhuu fakkaata ture. Yaa nama hojiin itti baay'atu! nama hin mul’anne!—dhaabbadhu!—jecha tokko!—huccuun eessatti yaa’a? masaraa akkamii deck gochuu danda'a? maaliif dadhabbiin hin dhaabbanne kun hundi? Dubbadhu, huccuu!—harka kee turi!—garuu jecha tokko qofa si waliin! Lakki—shaatal balaliʼa—fakkiin sun loom irraa gara fuulduraatti ni yaaʼa; kaarpettiin freshet-rushing bara baraan slides away. Waaqni huccuu, ni hodha; hodhuu sanaan immoo sagalee nama duʼu akka hin dhageenyeef gurri isaa ni dubbata; humming sanaan nutis, warri loom ilaallu gurri keenya ni cufa; sagalee kumaan lakkaa'aman karaa isaa dubbatan immoo yeroo itti miliqnu qofa dhageenya. Sababni isaas, warshaalee meeshaa hunda keessatti akkas ta’a. Jechoota dubbataman kanneen ispiindii balali’an gidduutti hin dhaga’amne; jechoonni sun dallaa malee, keessummoota banaman irraa dho’anii ifatti dhaga’amu. Kanaanis jalloonni adda baafamaniiru. Ah, nama du'aa! achiis, of eeggannoo godhaa; kanaaf, sagalee addunyaa guddichaa kana hundumaa keessatti, yaadni kee inni dhokataan fagootti dhaga’amuu danda’a.

Amma, lafee magariisaa fi jireenya boqonnaa hin qabne kan muka Arsacidean sanaa gidduutti, lafeen guddaan adiin waaqeffamu sun, siʼa taʼu, jechuunis nama hojii malee hojjetu guddaadha! Taʼus, yeroo hunda hodhamaa kan taʼe warp fi woof magariisa taʼe sun wal makaa fi naannoo isaatti gungumaa yeroo jirutti, namni hojii malee hojjetu inni jabaan sun nama haxxummaadhaan hodhamu fakkaata ture; ofii isaatii hunduu muka wayiniitiin kan hodhame; ji’a ji’aan magariisa, lalisa haaraa ta’e fudhachuudhaan; garuu ofii isaatii lafeedha. Jireenyi dachaafame Du'a; Duuti trellised Jireenya; waaqa gaddisiisaa Jireenya dargaggummaatiin haadha manaa, ulfina mataa qaxxaamuraa isa dhalche.

CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. He had lighted with such energy upon a thwart of his boat that his ivory leg had received a half-splintering shock. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
