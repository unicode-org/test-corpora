BOQONNAA 124. Cirracha.

Borumtaa isaa ganama galaanni ammallee hin hir’anne sun, dambalii dheeraa suuta jedhu kan baay’ee guddaa ta’een, fi daandii Pequod’s gurgling track keessatti carraaquun, akka harka gurguddaa diriirfameetti ishee dhiibe. Qilleensi cimaan hin raafamne baayʼee waan tureef, samii fi qilleensi doonii balʼaa garaa alaa fakkaatu; addunyaan guutuun qilleensa dura boo’e. Ifa ganama guutuu keessatti kan cufame, aduun ijaan hin mul’anne, cimina babal’ina bakka isaa qofaan beekama ture; bakka balaqqeessaan baayooneetii isaa tuullaadhaan itti fufe. Faayaan, akka moototaa fi mootota Baabiloon gonfoo godhatanitti, waan hundumaa irratti bulchaa ture. Galaanni akka warqee baqfame, kan ifaafi hoʼaan akka bubbuʼutti utaalu ture.

Ahaab yeroo dheeraaf callisa faarfataa taʼe kan eege siʼa taʼu, addaan baʼee dhaabbate; dooniin rifachaa jirtus yeroo hundumaa gadi bu’ee bowsprit ishee gadi dhiiftu, balaqqeessa aduu ifaa fuuldura isaatti uumamu ijaaf garagalee ture; akkasumas yeroo isheen gadi fageenyaan dugda doonii bira qubattu, inni duubatti garagalee, bakka aduun duubatti jirtu, akkasumas akkamitti balaqqeessa keelloo sun dammaqina isaa isa hin maqne waliin akka makamu arge.

“Ha, ha, doonii koo! ati amma gaarii galaana aduu jettee fudhatamuu dandeessa. Ho, ho! isin saboonni hundinuu koora koo duratti, ani aduu isiniif fida! Harqoota billows itti aanu irratti; hallo! a tandem, galaana nan oofaa!”

Garuu akka tasaa yaada faallaa tokko tokkoon duubatti deebi'ee, gara konkolaachisaa sanaatti ariifate, huskily dooniin sun akkamitti akka deemtu gaafate.

“Baha-kibba-bahaa, obbo” jedhe konkolaachisaan sodaate.

“Ati sobda!” harka isaa isa walitti hidhameen isa rukutee. “Ganama sa’aatii kanatti gara Bahaatti qajeelaa, aduun immoo duubatti deebi’aa?”

Kana irratti lubbuun hundi ni burjaaja’e; sababiin isaas, taatee yeroo sanatti Ahaab ilaale, namoota kaan hundumaa malee itti gaafatamummaa malee miliqee waan tureef; garuu baay’ee ija namaa jaamsuun isaa kan tuqamu ta’uun isaa sababa ta’uu qaba.

Mataa isaa walakkaa gara binnacle sanaatti dhidhiitee, Ahaab koompaasii sana tokko arge; harki isaa ol kaafame suuta jedhee kufe; yeroo muraasaaf waan raafamu fakkaata ture. Duuba isaa dhaabatee Starbuck ilaale, kunoo! koompaasonni lamaan gara Bahaatti kan akeekan siʼa taʼu, Pequod akkasuma dogoggora malee gara Dhihaatti deemaa ture.

Garuu ere alarm bosona jalqabaa hojjettoota doonii gidduutti biyya alaa bahuu isaa dura, jaarsi kolfa jabaadhaan, “Ani qaba! Kanaan duras ta'ee jira. Obbo Istaarbaak, qilleensi kaleessa galgalaa koompaasii keenya garagalche-kana qofa. Ati kanaan dura waan akkasii dhageesseetta, nan fudhadha.”

“Aye; garuu kanaan dura na mudatee hin beeku gooftaa” jedhe hiriyaan isaa inni diimaan sun gadda guddaadhaan.

Asitti, balaan akkanaa haala tokkoo ol dooniiwwan obomboleettii hamaa keessatti mudateera jechuun dirqama. Anniisaa maagneetii, akkuma cirracha nama galaanaa keessatti guddate, akkuma namoonni hundi beekan, bu’uuraan humna elektirikii samii irratti mul’atu wajjin tokko dha; kana irraa ka’uun baay’ee kan nama ajaa’ibu miti, wantootni akkasii ta’uun isaanii. Fakkeenyota balaqqeessaan dhuguma doonii sana rukutee, akka spars fi rigging tokko tokko rukutee, dhiibbaan needle irratti yeroo tokko tokko ammas caalaatti lubbuu namaa galaafateera; safuu dhagaa fe’umsaa isaa hundi waan badeef, sibiilli maagneetii duraan ture, cirracha haadha manaa dulloomtuu hodhuu caalaa faayidaa hin qabu ture. Garuu haala kamiinuu, cirrachi lammata, ofuma isaatiin, safuu jalqabaa akkasitti manca’e ykn bade deebisee hin argatu; akkasumas koompaasonni binnacle yoo miidhaman, hiree wal fakkaataan kanneen biroo doonii sana keessa ta’uu danda’an hunda bira ga’a; kan gadii illee kelson keessa galfamaniiru.

Itti yaadee binnacle dura dhaabatee, fi koompaasii transpointed ijaan ilaalaa, jaarsichi, qara harka isaa diriirfameen, amma bearing sirrii aduu fudhate, fi cirrachoonni sirritti garagalfamuu isaaniitti quufe, ajaja isaa daandii doonii sanaaf iyye haaluma kanaan jijjiiramuu qaba. Mooraawwan sun ol jabaa turan; ammas Pequod bowwaa ishee hin sodaanne qilleensa faallaa sanatti darbatte, sababiin isaas inni bareedduu jedhame sun ishee juggling qofa waan tureef.

Gidduu kana yaadni dhoksaa mataa isaa maal iyyuu yoo ta’e, Starbuck homaa hin dubbanne, garuu callisee ajaja barbaachisu hunda kenne; yeroo taʼu, Stubb fi Flask—yeroo sanatti hamma tokko miira isaa kan qooddatan fakkaatu—akkasumas osoo hin gungumiin tole jedhanii fudhatan. Dhiirota yoo ilaalle, gariin isaanii gadi aanaadhaan kan gunguman ta’us, sodaan isaan Ahaab irraa qaban sodaa Hiree caalaa ture. Garuu akkuma yeroo kamiyyuu, harpooneers warra ormaa guutummaatti jechuun ni danda’ama osoo hin dinqisiifamne; yookaan yoo dinqisiifate, maagneetii murtaa’e kan Ahaab isa hin jijjiiramne irraa gara garaa isaanii isa mijatutti dhukaafame qofaan ture.

Bakka tokkoof jaarsi sun abjuu gulufaadhaan deekii sana irra deeme. Garuu carraa kofoo isaa isa gaanfa gaanfaatiin lilmoodhaan siquuf, tuuboowwan ijaa sibiila diimaa caccaban kan kudhaama guyyaa duraa qabu sanaa gara deekii sanaatti fiigan arge.

“Ati hiyyeessa, boonsaa samii ilaalu fi balaliisaa aduu! kaleessa si caccabseera, har'as koompaasonni na caccabsu turan. Kanaaf, akkas. Garuu Ahaab ammallee dhagaa fe'umsaa sadarkaa irratti gooftaa dha. Obbo Istaarbaak—eeboo ulee hin qabne; top-maul, fi cirracha nama doonii hojjetu keessaa isa xiqqaadha. ariitii!"

Accessory, tarii, kaka'umsa wanta amma hojjechuuf jiru murteessuuf, kaka'umsa of eeggannoo murtaa'an turan, kaayyoon isaaniis hafuura hojjettoota isaa rukuttaa ogummaa isaa isa dhokataa ta'een deebisee jiraachisuu ta'uu danda'a, dhimma akkas ajaa'ibaa ta'e keessatti koompaasii garagalfame. Kana malees, jaarsichi, cirracha qaxxaamuraatiin oofuun, haala salphaa taʼeen kan dandaʼamu taʼus, dooniiwwan amantaa sobaa qabaniin, kan raafamuu fi mallattoo hamaa tokko tokko malee, wanta irra darbu akka hin taane sirriitti beeka ture.

“Dhiirota,” jedhe, dhaabbataadhaan hojjettoota doonii irratti garagalee, yeroo hiriyaan isaa wantoota inni gaafate itti kennu, “namoota koo, qilleensi sun cirracha Ahaab dulloomaa garagalche; sibiilli xinnoo kana irraa garuu Ahaab kan ofii isaatii tokko tolchuu dandaʼa, kunis akkuma kamiiyyuu dhugaa taʼuu isaa ni akeeka.”

Ilaalchi qaanii ajaa’ibsiisaa garbummaa dooniiwwaniin wal jijjiirame, akkuma kun jedhame; ija hawwataniin immoo falfala hordofuu danda’u hunda eegaa turan. Garuu Starbuck ija irraa garagalee ilaale.

Ahaab rukuttaa isa gubbaa-maul irraa dhufeen mataa sibiilaa eeboo sanaa irraa rukutee, achiis ulee sibiilaa dheeraa hafe hiriyyaa isaatiif kenne, osoo inni deekii hin tuqin, qajeelee akka qabatu ajaje. Achiis, maul sanaan, irra deddeebi’ee fiixee gubbaa ulee sibiilaa kanaa erga rukutee booda, cirracha blunted sana dhuma irratti gubbaa isaa kaa’e, akkasumas cimina xiqqaadhaan kan rukute, yeroo hedduu, hiriyaan gaa’elaa ammallee ulee sana akkuma duraanii qabate. Achiis sochiiwwan ajaaʼibsiisoo xixiqqoo tokko tokko keessa darbuun, sibiilli sun maagneetii gochuuf kan hin hafne taʼuu fi dhiisuu isaa, ykn sodaa hojjettoota doonii sanaa guddisuuf qofa kan yaadame taʼuu isaa mirkanaaʼaa miti—innis funyoo quncee talbaa akka waamu waame; fi gara binnacle tti socho’ee, cirrachoonni lamaan duubatti deebi’an achitti siqsee baasee, cirracha doonii sana walakkaa isaatiin, kaardii koompaasii tokko irratti, garagalchee fannise. Jalqaba irratti sibiilli sun naannoo fi naannoo kan deemu siʼa taʼu, fiixee lamaanuu ni raafama, ni raafama; dhumarratti garuu bakka isaa qubate, yeroo Ahaab inni bu’aa kana xiyyeeffannaadhaan ilaalaa ture, ifatti binnacle irraa duubatti deebi’ee, harka isaa diriirfame gara isaatti akeekee, iyye,—“Ahaab yoo hin taane, ofuma keessanii ilaalaa gooftaa dhagaa fe'umsaa sadarkaa! Aduun Bahaa dha, koompaasiin sun immoo ni kakata!”

Ija isaanii malee wanti wallaalummaa akka isaanii amansiisuu waan hin dandeenyeef, wal duraa duubaan keessa ilaalan, wal duraa duubaan immoo siqanii deeman.

Ija isaa isa abiddaa tuffii fi injifannootiin, yeroo sanatti Ahaab of tuulummaa isaa isa du’aa hundumaa keessatti argite.

Amma Pequod hiree ta'e imala kana yeroo dheeraaf kan ture yoo ta'u, mukaa fi sararri kun yeroo muraasaaf itti fayyadamaa ture. Karaalee bakka dooniin sun itti jirtu murteessuuf gargaaran kan biraa irratti ofitti amanamummaadhaan hirkatanii waan jiraniif, daldaltoonni tokko tokkoo fi namoonni qurxummii galaanaa hedduun, keessumaa yeroo doonii irra deeman, guutummaatti muka sana ol kaasuu tuffatu; yeroo wal fakkaatutti, fi yeroo baay’ee waan hundumaa caalaa bifaaf jecha ta’us, yeroo hunda daandii dooniin geggeessu, akkasumas sa’aatii hundumaatti saffisa guddinaa giddu galeessaa jedhamee tilmaamamu, slate aadaa irratti lafa kaa’a. Pequod waliin akkasitti ture. Reeliin mukaa fi muka kofa qabu kan itti maxxane, yeroo dheeraaf osoo hin tuqamne, reeliingii dallaawwan boodaa jalatti qofa fannifamee ture. Roobaa fi biifaan isa jiisisee ture; aduu fi qilleensi isa jal’isee ture; elementoonni hundi walitti makamanii waan akkasitti hojii malee fannifame tokko manca’anii turan. Garuu kana hundumaa osoo hin hubatin, miirrii isaa Ahaab qabate, akka tasaatti reelii sana ilaalee, sa’aatii baay’ee osoo hin taane, mul’ata maagneetii booda, akkamitti kuadraan isaa kana booda akka hin jirre yaadate, akkasumas waa’ee loogii fi sarara sadarkaa sanaa kakaa isaa isa ariifachiisaa yaadate. Dooniin sun bishaan keessaa liqimfamtee deemaa turte; astern billows jeequmsa keessatti rolled.

“Fuulduratti, achi! Muka ol kaasaa!”

Namoonni galaanaa lama dhufan. Tahitian halluu warqee qabuu fi Manxman grizzly. “Reel fudhadhu, isin keessaa tokko, nan ol kaasa.”

“Ani ispoolii qabadheera obbo. Garuu akkuma kaappiteeniin koo jedhu. Rifeensa koo halluu diimaa qabu kanaan 'tis yeroo falmuu gatii hin qabu, 'keessattuu nama ol'aanaa, who'll ne'er confess.'

BOQONNAA 126. Jireenya-Buoy.

Amma gara kibba bahaatti sibiilli Ahaab wal qixa taʼeen qajeelchuu, guddinni ishee immoo muka sadarkaa Ahaab fi sarara qofaan kan murtaaʼu dha; Pequod daandii ishee gara Ikuwaatoritti geessu qabatte. Bishaan akkasii yeroo baay’ee hin deemne keessa darbuu akkas dheeraa gochuu, doonii tokkollee ibsuu, fi ere dheeraa, qilleensa daldalaa hin jijjiiramneen cinaachaan kan socho’u, dalga monotonoously salphaa ta’e irratti; kun hundinuu wantoota tasgabbaa’oo ajaa’ibaa mul’ata jeequmsaa fi abdii kutannaa tokko tokko dursan fakkaata ture.

Dhumarratti, dooniin sun akkas jechuudhaan qarqara qurxummii Ikuwaatooriyaalitti yeroo dhihaattu, fi dukkana gadi fagoo barii dura deemu keessatti, tuuta odola dhagaa taʼaniin deemaa turte; sa’aatiin sun—yeroo sanatti Flask’n kan hoogganamu—iyya akka malee bosona fi lafa irraa hin taaneetti—akka boo’icha walakkaa ibsame kan hafuurota Heroodis Qulqulloota ajjeefaman hundumaa—tokko fi hundi isaanii, abjuu isaanii irraa, akkasumas bakka tokko tokkoof jalqaban yeroon dhaabbate, ykn taa’e, ykn hunduu akka garba Roomaa bocame sanaatti dhaggeeffachaa ture, iyyi bosona sun dhaga’uu keessa hafee, dhaggeeffachaa ture. Kutaan hojjettoota doonii Kiristaana ykn qaroome sun mermaids akka ta'e dubbatee, ni raafame; garuu warri harpooneers warra ormaa hin rifatin hafan. Ta’us Manxman halluu diimaa qabu—kan hundumaa caalaa kan dulloomaan galaanaa—sagaleewwan bosona nama gammachiisan kan dhaga’amu, sagalee namoota haaraa galaana keessatti liqimfaman akka ta’e labse.

Hammaak isaa jalatti, Ahaab hamma bari'utti, yeroo gara doonii sanaa dhufutti kana hin dhageenye; sana booda hiika dukkanaa’aa akeekkachiifameen kan hin deemne miti, Flask’n irra deebi’amee itti himame. Innis hollowly kolfe, akkasitti ajaa'iba sana ibse.

Odolawwan dhagaa dooniin sun bira darbite sun bakka bashannana siiloonni baayʼee taʼan siʼa taʼu, siiloonni ijoolleen tokko tokko hidha isaanii dhaban, ykn hidoonni ilmoolee isaanii dhaban tokko tokko doonii sanatti dhihoo kaʼanii booʼaa fi booʼaa ishee wajjin taʼuu qabu boo’icha gosa namaa isaanii wajjin. Garuu kun isaan keessaa tokko tokko caalaa kan dhiibbaa uume, sababiin isaas irra caalaan dooniiwwan irra caalaan isaanii waa’ee chaappaawwan miira baay’ee amantaa sobaa ta’e ni kunuunsu, kunis yeroo dhiphina keessa jiran sagalee addaa isaanii irraa qofa osoo hin taane, ilaalcha namaa mataa isaanii geengoo fi fuula walakkaa sammuu isaanii, ijaan ilaalamu irraa kan ka’e dha fincila bishaan irraa cinaatti. Mr. Starbuck, look at the binnacle watch, and in three minutes from this present instant warn off all strangers: then brace forward again, and let the ship sail as before.”

Hurriedly turning, with averted face, he descended into his cabin, leaving the strange captain transfixed at this unconditional and utter rejection of his so earnest suit. But starting from his enchantment, Gardiner silently hurried to the side; more fell than stepped into his boat, and returned to his ship.

Soon the two ships diverged their wakes; and long as the strange vessel was in view, she was seen to yaw hither and thither at every dark spot, however small, on the sea. This way and that her yards were swung round; starboard and larboard, she continued to tack; now she beat against a head sea; and again it pushed her before it; while all the while, her masts and yards were thickly clustered with men, as three tall cherry trees, when the boys are cherrying among the boughs.

But by her still halting course and winding, woeful way, you plainly saw that this ship that so wept with spray, still remained without comfort. She was Rachel, weeping for her children, because they were not.





CHAPTER 129. The Cabin.

( Ahab moving to go on deck; Pip catches him by the hand to follow. )

“Lad, lad, I tell thee thou must not follow Ahab now. The hour is coming when Ahab would not scare thee from him, yet would not have thee by him. There is that in thee, poor lad, which I feel too curing to my malady. Like cures like; and for this hunt, my malady becomes my most desired health. Do thou abide below here, where they shall serve thee, as if thou wert the captain. Aye, lad, thou shalt sit here in my own screwed chair; another screw to it, thou must be.”

“No, no, no! ye have not a whole body, sir; do ye but use poor me for your one lost leg; only tread upon me, sir; I ask no more, so I remain a part of ye.”

“Oh! spite of million villains, this makes me a bigot in the fadeless fidelity of man!—and a black! and crazy!—but methinks like-cures-like applies to him too; he grows so sane again.”

“Weep so, and I will murder thee! have a care, for Ahab too is mad. Listen, and thou wilt often hear my ivory foot upon the deck, and still know that I am there. And now I quit thee. Thy hand!—Met! ( Ahab goes; Pip steps one step forward. )

“Here he this instant stood; I stand in his air,—but I'm alone. Now were even poor Pip here I could endure it, but he's missing. Pip! Pip! Ding, dong, ding! Who's seen Pip? He must be up here; let's try the door. What? neither lock, nor bolt, nor bar; and yet there's no opening it. Here, then, I'll seat me, against the transom, in the ship's full middle, all her keel and her three masts before me. Here, our old sailors say, in their black seventy-fours great admirals sometimes sit at table, and lord it over rows of captains and lieutenants. Ha! what's this? epaulets! epaulets! the epaulets all come crowding! Pass round the decanters; glad to see ye; fill up, monsieurs! CHAPTER 130. The Hat.

And now that at the proper time and place, after so long and wide a preliminary cruise, Ahab,—all other whaling waters swept—seemed to have chased his foe into an ocean-fold, to slay him the more securely there; now, that he found himself hard by the very latitude and longitude where his tormenting wound had been inflicted; now that a vessel had been spoken which on the very day preceding had actually encountered Moby Dick;—and now that all his successive meetings with various ships contrastingly concurred to show the demoniac indifference with which the white whale tore his hunters, whether sinning or sinned against; now it was that there lurked a something in the old man's eyes, which it was hardly sufferable for feeble souls to see. As the unsetting polar star, which through the livelong, arctic, six months' night sustains its piercing, steady, central gaze; so Ahab's purpose now fixedly gleamed down upon the constant midnight of the gloomy crew. It domineered above them so, that all their bodings, doubts, misgivings, fears, were fain to hide beneath their souls, and not sprout forth a single spear or leaf.

In this foreshadowing interval too, all humor, forced or natural, vanished. Stubb no more strove to raise a smile; Starbuck no more strove to check one. Alike, joy and sorrow, hope and fear, seemed ground to finest dust, and powdered, for the time, in the clamped mortar of Ahab's iron soul. Like machines, they dumbly moved about the deck, ever conscious that the old man's despot eye was on them.

But did you deeply scan him in his more secret confidential hours; when he thought no glance but one was on him; then you would have seen that even as Ahab's eyes so awed the crew's, the inscrutable Parsee's glance awed his; or somehow, at least, in some wild way, at times affected it. Such an added, gliding strangeness began to invest the thin Fedallah now; such ceaseless shudderings shook him; that the men looked dubious at him; half uncertain, as it seemed, whether indeed he were a mortal substance, or else a tremulous shadow cast upon the deck by some unseen being's body. And that shadow was always hovering there. For not by night, even, had Fedallah ever certainly been known to slumber, or go below. He would stand still for hours: but never sat or leaned; his wan but wondrous eyes did plainly say—We two watchmen never rest.

Nor, at any time, by night or day could the mariners now step upon the deck, unless Ahab was before them; either standing in his pivot-hole, or exactly pacing the planks between two undeviating limits,—the main-mast and the mizen; or else they saw him standing in the cabin-scuttle,—his living foot advanced upon the deck, as if to step; his hat slouched heavily over his eyes; so that however motionless he stood, however the days and nights were added on, that he had not swung in his hammock; yet hidden beneath that slouching hat, they could never tell unerringly whether, for all this, his eyes were really closed at times; or whether he was still intently scanning them; no matter, though he stood so in the scuttle for a whole hour on the stretch, and the unheeded night-damp gathered in beads of dew upon that stone-carved coat and hat. The clothes that the night had wet, the next day's sunshine dried upon him; and so, day after day, and night after night; he went no more beneath the planks; whatever he wanted from the cabin that thing he sent for.

He ate in the same open air; that is, his two only meals,—breakfast and dinner: supper he never touched; nor reaped his beard; which darkly grew all gnarled, as unearthed roots of trees blown over, which still grow idly on at naked base, though perished in the upper verdure. But though his whole life was now become one watch on deck; and though the Parsee's mystic watch was without intermission as his own; yet these two never seemed to speak—one man to the other—unless at long intervals some passing unmomentous matter made it necessary. Though such a potent spell seemed secretly to join the twain; openly, and to the awe-struck crew, they seemed pole-like asunder. If by day they chanced to speak one word; by night, dumb men were both, so far as concerned the slightest verbal interchange. At times, for longest hours, without a single hail, they stood far parted in the starlight; Ahab in his scuttle, the Parsee by the mainmast; but still fixedly gazing upon each other; as if in the Parsee Ahab saw his forethrown shadow, in Ahab the Parsee his abandoned substance.

And yet, somehow, did Ahab—in his own proper self, as daily, hourly, and every instant, commandingly revealed to his subordinates,—Ahab seemed an independent lord; the Parsee but his slave. Still again both seemed yoked together, and an unseen tyrant driving them; the lean shade siding the solid rib. For be this Parsee what he may, all rib and keel was solid Ahab.

At the first faintest glimmering of the dawn, his iron voice was heard from aft,—“Man the mast-heads!”—and all through the day, till after sunset and after twilight, the same voice every hour, at the striking of the helmsman's bell, was heard—“What d'ye see?—sharp! sharp!”

But when three or four days had slided by, after meeting the children-seeking Rachel; and no spout had yet been seen; the monomaniac old man seemed distrustful of his crew's fidelity; at least, of nearly all except the Pagan harpooneers; he seemed to doubt, even, whether Stubb and Flask might not willingly overlook the sight he sought. But if these suspicions were really his, he sagaciously refrained from verbally expressing them, however his actions might seem to hint them.

“I will have the first sight of the whale myself,”—he said. “Aye! Ahab must have the doubloon!” and with his own hands he rigged a nest of basketed bowlines; and sending a hand aloft, with a single sheaved block, to secure to the main-mast head, he received the two ends of the downward-reeved rope; and attaching one to his basket prepared a pin for the other end, in order to fasten it at the rail. This done, with that end yet in his hand and standing beside the pin, he looked round upon his crew, sweeping from one to the other; pausing his glance long upon Daggoo, Queequeg, Tashtego; but shunning Fedallah; and then settling his firm relying eye upon the chief mate, said,—“Take the rope, sir—I give it into thy hands, Starbuck.” Then arranging his person in the basket, he gave the word for them to hoist him to his perch, Starbuck being the one who secured the rope at last; and afterwards stood near it. And thus, with one hand clinging round the royal mast, Ahab gazed abroad upon the sea for miles and miles,—ahead, astern, this side, and that,—within the wide expanded circle commanded at so great a height.

When in working with his hands at some lofty almost isolated place in the rigging, which chances to afford no foothold, the sailor at sea is hoisted up to that spot, and sustained there by the rope; under these circumstances, its fastened end on deck is always given in strict charge to some one man who has the special watch of it. Because in such a wilderness of running rigging, whose various different relations aloft cannot always be infallibly discerned by what is seen of them at the deck; and when the deck-ends of these ropes are being every few minutes cast down from the fastenings, it would be but a natural fatality, if, unprovided with a constant watchman, the hoisted sailor should by some carelessness of the crew be cast adrift and fall all swooping to the sea. So Ahab's proceedings in this matter were not unusual; the only strange thing about them seemed to be, that Starbuck, almost the one only man who had ever ventured to oppose him with anything in the slightest degree approaching to decision—one of those too, whose faithfulness on the look-out he had seemed to doubt somewhat;—it was strange, that this was the very man he should select for his watchman; freely giving his whole life into such an otherwise distrusted person's hands.

Now, the first time Ahab was perched aloft; ere he had been there ten minutes; one of those red-billed savage sea-hawks which so often fly incommodiously close round the manned mast-heads of whalemen in these latitudes; one of these birds came wheeling and screaming round his head in a maze of untrackably swift circlings. Then it darted a thousand feet straight up into the air; then spiralized downwards, and went eddying again round his head.

But with his gaze fixed upon the dim and distant horizon, Ahab seemed not to mark this wild bird; nor, indeed, would any one else have marked it much, it being no uncommon circumstance; only now almost the least heedful eye seemed to see some sort of cunning meaning in almost every sight.

“Your hat, your hat, sir!” suddenly cried the Sicilian seaman, who being posted at the mizen-mast-head, stood directly behind Ahab, though somewhat lower than his level, and with a deep gulf of air dividing them.

But already the sable wing was before the old man's eyes; the long hooked bill at his head: with a scream, the black hawk darted away with his prize.

An eagle flew thrice round Tarquin's head, removing his cap to replace it, and thereupon Tanaquil, his wife, declared that Tarquin would be king of Rome. But only by the replacing of the cap was that omen accounted good. Ahab's hat was never restored; the wild hawk flew on and on with it; far in advance of the prow: and at last disappeared; while from the point of that disappearance, a minute black spot was dimly discerned, falling from that vast height into the sea.
