O LE MATAUPU 55. O Ata Mata'utia o Tafola.

O le a le umi ona ou valiina oe e pei o se tasi e leai se tapoleni, o se mea e pei o le foliga moni o le tafola e pei ona foliga moni mai i le mata o le tagata fagota pe a oo i lona tino atoa o loo taula ai le tafola i autafa o le vaa tafola ina ia e mafai ona laa i ai o ia. Atonu e aoga se taimi muamua e fa'asalalau atu ai i na ata fa'amanino o ia e o'o mai lava i le taimi nei ma le mautinoa e lu'iina ai le fa'atuatuaga o le tagata fai fanua. Ua oo i le taimi e faasaʻo ai le lalolagi i lenei mataupu, e ala i le faamaonia o ia ata o le tafola e sese.

Atonu o le puna autū o na faaseseega uma o ata o le a maua mai i faatagata matutua o Hindoo, Aikupito, ma Eleni. Aua talu mai lava na taimi faafoliga ae le faamaoni a o i luga o ie maamora o malumalu, o faavae o faatagata, ma talipupuni, pine, ipu, ma tupe, na tosoina ai le luko i una o le ofutau filifili e pei o Saladin, ma le ulu pulou e pei o St. George's; talu mai lena taimi sa i ai se mea o le ituaiga laisene tutusa, e le gata i le tele o ata lauiloa o le tafola, ae i le tele o faʻamatalaga faasaienisi o ia.

I le taimi nei, e le gata i lea, o le ata sili ona leva o loʻo i ai nei e foliga mai o le tafola, o loʻo maua i totonu o le ana-pagoda lauiloa o Elephanta, i Initia. E talitonu le au Brahmins e faapea, i le toetoe lava a le gata o faatagata o lena pagoda anamua, o fefaʻatauaiga uma ma sailiga, soʻo se mea e mafai ona mafaufauina e le tagata, na faʻataʻitaʻiina i tausaga aʻo leʻi oʻo mai se tasi o na mea. Leitioa la, i se tulaga sa tatau ai ona faamalumalu mai iina la tatou galuega faatamalii o le fagota. O le tafola Hindoo o loʻo taʻua, e tupu i se isi matagaluega o le puipui, o loʻo faʻaalia ai le faʻaalia o Vishnu i foliga o leviathan, ua lauiloa o le Matse Avatar. Ae e ui lava o lenei faatagata o le afa tagata ma le afa tafola, ina ia na o le tuuina atu o le siʻusiʻu o le mulimuli, ae o lena vaega itiiti o ia e sese uma. E foliga mai e pei o le si'usi'u fa'ai'u o se anaconda, nai lo alofilima lautele o fu'a mata'utia o le tafola.

Ae alu i le Faleata tuai, ma vaai nei i le ata o se tusiata Kerisiano maoae o lenei i’a; aua e le sili atu lona manuia nai lo le tagata Hindoo anamua. O le ata o Guido o Perseus o loʻo laveaʻiina Andromeda mai le sami-monster poʻo le tafola. O fea na maua ai e Guido le faʻataʻitaʻiga o se meaola uiga ese e pei o lena? E le o Hogarth, i le valiina o le vaaiga lava lea e tasi i lana lava "Perseus Descending," e sili atu le lelei. O le tino tele o lena sau'ai Hogarthian e sosolo i luga, e tau le maua ai se inisi se vai. E iai se ituaiga o howdah i lona tua, ma o lona gutu fa'agogo o lo'o ta'avale i ai le galu, e mafai ona ave mo le Faitoto'a o Fa'a'ole'ole e alu atu mai le Thames i le vai i totonu o le Olo. O lo'o iai fo'i tafola o Prodromus a le Scotch Sibbald tuai, ma le tafola a Iona, e pei ona fa'aalia i lomitusi o Tusi Paia tuai ma tipi o ulua'i tusi. O le a se tala e uiga i nei mea? A o le tafola a le tagata fai tusi e taamilomilo e pei o se laau vine e faataamilo i le tumu o se taula e alu ifo—e pei ona faailogaina ma faapepa i tua ma itulau autu o le tele o tusi tuai ma tusi fou—o se mea e sili ona matagofie ae e matua ofoofogia lava; fa'aa'oa'o, 'ou te 'avea, mai fa'atusa fa'atusa i ipu tuai. E ui ina ta'ua lautele o se lupe, ae ou te ta'ua le i'a lea o le fusitusi o se taumafaiga i se tafola; auā sa fa'apea na fa'amoemoeina i le taimi na fa'ailoa muamua ai le masini. Na faalauiloa mai e se tagata talaʻi tuai Italia i se mea pe tusa o le 15 senituri, i le taimi o le Toefuataiga o le Aʻoaʻoina; ma i na aso, ma e oo lava i se vaitaimi e faatusatusa i se vaitaimi tuai, o lumefe sa masani ona manatu o se ituaiga o le Leviatana.

I ata ma isi teuteuga o nisi o tusi anamua o le a e feiloai ai i nisi taimi ma le fia iloa o le tafola, lea e oso aʻe ai ituaiga uma o puna, jet d'eau, vaipuna vevela ma le malulu, Saratoga ma Baden-Baden, e oso aʻe i luga mai lona fale. fai'ai e le'i vaivai. I le ulutala-itulau o le ulua'i lomiga o le “Aluiga i luma o le A'oa'oina” o le ae maua ai ni tafola fiailoa.

Ae o le tuuese o nei taumafaiga le lelei uma, seʻi o tatou tilotilo i na ata o leviatana o loʻo faʻapea o ni faʻamatalaga faʻasaienisi, e i latou e iloa. I le aoina o folauga a Harris tuai o loo i ai ni papatusi o tafola na maua mai se tusi Dutch o folauga, TA 1671, ua faaulutalaina “A Whaling Voyage to Spitzbergen in the ship Jonas in the Whale, Peter Peterson of Friesland, master.” I se tasi o na papatusi, o tafola, e pei o ni ogalaau tetele, o loo ta'atia i totonu o motu aisa, faatasi ai ma urosa pa'epa'e o lo o taufetuli io latou tua. I le isi ipu, o le fa'alavelave mata'utia o lo'o fa'atusalia ai le tafola e fa'atutu sa'o.

E le gata i lea, o loʻo i ai se vaega mataʻina, na tusia e se tasi Kapeteni Colnett, o se Kapeteni Faʻasalalau i le neivi Peretania, ua faʻaulutalaina "A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, mo le faʻamoemoe e faʻalautele le Spermaceti Whale Fisheries." I totonu o lenei tusi o loo i ai se auivi e foliga mai o se “Ata o le Physeter po o le Spermaceti whale, na tusia i le fua mai le tasi na fasiotia i le talafatai o Mekisiko, Aokuso, 1793, ma sii i luga o le fogavaa.” Ou te le masalosalo na pueina e le kapeteni lenei ata moni mo le manuia o lana marini. E tasi lava le mea e uiga i ai, sei ou fai atu e i ai lona mata e faatatau, e tusa ai ma le fua o loo aumai faatasi, i se tafola matua, e avea ai le mata o lena tafola o se faamalama aufana pe a ma le lima futu le umi. Oi, lo'u kapeteni toa, se a le mea na e le tuuina mai ai ia te i matou Iona e vaai ese mai lena mata?

E leai foi ni tuufaatasiga sili ona magafagafa o Talafaasolopito Faanatura mo le manuia o talavou ma agamalu, e saoloto mai le leaga lava e tasi o mea sese. Vaai i lena galuega lauiloa "Goldsmith's Animated Nature." I le lomiga faapuupuu a Lonetona i le 1807, o loo i ai papatusi o se “tafola” ma se “narwhale.” Ou te le manaʻo e foliga faʻatauvaʻa, ae o lenei tafola mataga e foliga mai o se puaa ua tipi ese; ma, e tusa ai ma le narwhale, o le tasi tepa i ai ua lava lea e ofo ai se tasi, i lenei seneturi sefuluiva e mafai ona faʻaogaina se alofilima mo le moni i luga o soʻo se tagata atamamai o tamaiti aoga.

I le 1825 foi, na lomia ai e Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, o se tagata poto faapitoa i mea tau natura, se tusi tafola ua faatulagaina faasaienisi, lea o loo iai ni ata o ituaiga eseese o le Leviathan. O nei mea uma e le gata e le saʻo, ae o le ata o le Mysticetus poʻo Greenland whale (o lona uiga, le Right whale), e oʻo lava ia Scoresby, o se tagata ua leva ona iai le poto masani i le paʻi atu i lea ituaiga, na ia taʻutino mai e leai sona tutusa i le natura.

Ae o le tuʻuina atu o le fusi saito i nei pisinisi faʻasese uma na faʻaagaga mo le saienitisi Frederick Cuvier, uso o le lauiloa Baron. I le 1836, na ia lomia ai se Talafaasolopito Faanatura o Tafola, lea na ia tuuina mai ai le mea na ia taʻua o se ata o le Tafola Sperm. A'o le'i fa'aali atu lena ata i so'o se Nantucketer, e sili ona lelei lou tu'uina atu mo lau fa'apotopotoga solomuli mai Nantucket. I se upu, o le tafola a Frederick Cuvier's Sperm Whale e le o se Sperm Whale, ae o se maukeni. O le mea moni, e leʻi maua lava e ia se aogā o se folauga fagota (e seāseā maua e tagata faapena), ae o fea na ia maua mai ai lenā ata, o ai na te iloa? Masalo na ia mauaina e pei o lona muamua faasaienisi i le matata lava lea e tasi, Desmarest, na maua se tasi o ana faapau pepe moni; o lona uiga, mai se ata Saina. Ma o a ituaiga tama olaola ma penitala ia Saina, o le tele o ipu ma sosi ese e faailoa mai ia i matou.

Ae o tafola a le au fai fa’ailoga ua vaaia i le alatele o tautau i luga o faleoloa a le au fai suau’u, o le a se tala e uiga ia i latou? E masani lava o Richard III. o tafola, e i ai i'a ma'a, ma e matua'i fe'ai; o le taeao i luga o seila tats se tolu pe fa, o vaa tafola e tumu i tautai: o latou faaletonu o loʻo faʻafefe i sami o le toto ma le vali lanumoana.

Ae o nei mea sese tele i le faʻaalia o le tafola e le o se mea e ofo tele ai. Mafaufau! O le tele o ata fa'asaienisi na aumai mai i'a ua pa'ulia; ma o nei mea e tutusa lelei lava e pei o se ata o se vaa ua malepelepe, ua gau le tua, o le a faatusa sa'o ai le manu tauleleia lava ia i lona faamaualuga e lei motusia uma o le tino o le vaa ma sima. E ui lava ina tutu elefane mo o latou umi, ae o le Leviatana ola e leʻi faʻafefeteina lava o ia lava mo lana ata. O le tafola ola, i lona mamalu atoatoa ma lona taua, ua na o le vasa i le sami e le mafaatusalia; ma o loo opeopea le vaega tele o ia ua le iloa atu, e pei o se laina-o-taua vaa; ma mai lena elemene o se mea e faavavau e le mafaia e le tagata faaletino ona sii a’e o ia i le tino i le ea, ina ia mafai ona faasaoina ai ona afu tetele uma ma galu. Ma, ae le'i ta'ua ai le ese'esega tele o le tafola i le va o se tama'i tafolā susu ma le leviatana a Platonian matua; ae, e oo lava i le tulaga o se tasi o na tama'i tafolā susu na sisi i luga o le fogāva'a, o lona uiga uiga ese, pei o le tuna, fa'alatalafu, eseese ona foliga, e le mafai e le tiapolo lava ia ona pu'eina.

Ae atonu e fa'apea, mai le auivi le lavalava o le tafola pa'ia, e mafai ona maua mai ai ni fa'amatalaga sa'o e pa'i atu i lona tino moni. E leo mea uma. Aua o se tasi o mea e sili ona fia iloa e uiga i lenei Leviatana, o lona auivi e maua ai se manatu itiiti i lona foliga lautele. E ui lava o le auivi a Jeremy Bentham, lea e tautau mo moligao i le faletusi a se tasi o ana faʻatonu, o loʻo faʻaalia saʻo ai le manatu o se tama matua faʻaoga faʻapitoa, ma isi uiga taʻutaʻua uma o Jeremy; ae e leai se mea faapena e mafai ona iloa mai soo se ivi o leviatana. O le mea moni, e pei ona taʻua e le Hunter sili, o le auivi o le tafola e tutusa lava le sootaga ma le manu faʻapipiʻi atoa ma faʻaofuofu e pei ona faia e le iniseti i le chrysalis lea e faʻaofuofu lapotopoto. O lenei tulaga uiga ese o loʻo faʻaalia i le ulu, e pei o nisi vaega o lenei tusi o le a faʻaalia faʻafuaseʻi. O lo'o fa'aalia fo'i ma le ma'ale'ale i le fa'apa'u pito, o ponaivi e toetoe lava a tali tutusa i ponaivi o le lima o le tagata, ae to'esea na'o le limamatua. O lenei apa e fa ona ponaivi-tamailima masani, o le faasino igoa, ogatotonu, mama, ma le tamailima. Ae o nei mea uma e mau i totonu o latou ufiufi aano, e pei o tamatamaʻilima o le tagata i se ufiufi faʻaofuofu. “Ae ui i lea, e mafai e le tafola ona auauna mai ia i tatou i nisi taimi,” o le tala lea a Stubb malie i se tasi aso, “e le mafai lava ona faapea mai na te taulimaina i tatou e aunoa ma ni miteni.”

Ona o nei mafuaaga uma, o soo se auala lava e te tilotilo ai i ai, e tatau ona e manatu o le Leviatana sili o le tasi lea o meaola i le lalolagi e tatau ona tumau e le valiina seia oo i le iuga. E moni, o se ata e tasi atonu e sili atu ona latalata le faailoga nai lo le isi, ae e leai se tasi e mafai ona lavea i se tulaga sili ona saʻo. E leai la se auala faalelalolagi e iloa tonu ai foliga o le tafola. Ma e na o le pau lava le auala e mafai ai ona e maua mai se manatu fa'amalieina o lona si'osi'omaga o lo'o ola ai, o lou alu lava oe e fagota; ae o le faia faapea, e te ono lamatia ai le avea ma ogaumu e faavavau ma goto ai e ia. O le mea lea, e foliga mai ia te a’u e sili ona aua ne’i outou soona fiailoa i lenei Leviatana.





O LE MATA'UPU 56. O Ata E Le'i Sese o Tafola, ma Ata Moni o Vaaiga Tau Tafola.

I le fesoʻotaʻi ma ata mataʻutia o tafola, ua matua faʻaosoosoina aʻu iinei e ulufale atu i na tala sili atu ona mataʻutia o latou o loʻo maua i nisi tusi, i aso anamua ma aso nei, aemaise lava i Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, ma isi. Ae ou te pasia lena mataupu.

Ou te iloa na o le fa ni auivi lolomi o le Sperm Whale tele; Colnett's, Huggins's, Frederick Cuvier's, ma Beale's. I le mataupu talu ai na taʻua ai Colnett ma Cuvier. O Huggins e sili mamao atu nai lo latou; ae, i le tele o faigata, o Beale e sili. O ata uma a Beale o lenei tafola e lelei, sei vagana ai le ata ogatotonu i le ata o tafola e tolu i uiga eseese, faʻapipiʻi lana mataupu lona lua. O lana pito i luma, o va'a e osofa'ia Sperm Whales, e ui lava e mautinoa na fuafua e fa'aosofia ai le masalosalo fa'aletagata a nisi o tamaloloa, e matua'i maoa'e le sa'o ma fa'aola i lona aafiaga lautele. O nisi o ata o le Sperm Whale i le J. Ross Browne e sa'o lelei le fa'asologa; a ua togitogiina leaga lava. E le o sona sese lena.

O le Tafola Sa'o, o ata pito sili ona lelei o lo'o i Scoresby; ae e tosoina i latou i se fua la'ititi tele e fa'ailoa mai ai se lagona mana'omia. E tasi lava lana ata o vaaiga fagota, ma o se tulaga faanoanoa lea, aua e na o ata faapena, pe a lelei uma, e mafai ai ona e maua se mea e pei o se manatu moni o le tafola ola e pei ona vaaia e ana tagata tulimanu ola.

Ae, e fa'atatau i mea uma, i le mamao e sili ona lelei, e ui lava i nisi fa'amatalaga e le sili ona sa'o, fa'aaliga o tafola ma vaaiga fa'afaila e maua i so'o se mea e maua, o ni togitogiga tele Farani e lua, na lelei ona fa'atinoina, ma na faia mai atavali a le tasi Garnery. E tusa ai, e fai ma sui o osofaiga i le Sperm ma Right Whale. I le togitogiga muamua o se tafola mamalu o le Sperm Whale o loʻo faʻaalia i le mamalu atoatoa o le malosi, na o le tulaʻi mai i lalo o le vaʻa mai le loloto o le vasa, ma faʻaeʻe maualuga i luga o le ea i luga o lona tua le mataʻutia na malepe o laupapa ogaumu. O le taumua o le vaa e le motusia se vaega, ma e toso na o le faapaleniina i luga o le ivi o le sau'ai; ma o lo o tu i lena taumua, mo lena emo e tasi e le mafaitaulia le taimi, ua e iloa atu ai se tagata foe, ua ufitia le afa i le puna vela o le tafola, ma o loo osooso, e peiseai mai se tofe. O le faatinoga o le mea atoa e ofoofogia lelei ma moni. Ua opeopea i luga o le sami pa'epa'e le fa'a'afa gaogao; o pou laupapa o nafa masa'a o lo'o fa'afefete i totonu; o ulu o le au 'au'au e ta'ape'ape i le tafola i fa'aaliga eseese o le fefe; a'o i ai i le va o le afa uliuli o lo'o tu'u i lalo le va'a. E mafai ona maua se ma'i tuga i fa'amatalaga fa'anatura o lenei tafola, ae tu'u pea lena; talu ai, mo lo'u olaga, sa le mafai ona ou tusia se ata lelei tele.

I le togitogiga lona lua, o le va'a o lo'o fa'agasolo ata tusi fa'atasi ma le augutu o se Tafola Taumatau tele o lo'o ta'avale ai lana vao uliuli i le sami e pei o ni papa ma'a limu mai papa o Patagonian. O ona afu e tutu, tumu, ma uliuli e pei o le sooga; ona o le tele o le asu i totonu o le alaasu, e te manatu ai e tatau ona i ai se talisuaga lototoa e kuka i totonu o le manava tele i lalo. O moa o le sami o lo'o feanu i tama'i paa, figota-i'a, ma isi lole ma makaroni, lea e amoina e le Tafola Taumatau i lona tua fa'ama'i. Ma o le taimi uma lava o loʻo faanatinati atu le leviatana laugutu mafiafia i totonu o le loloto, ma tuʻu ai le tele o tone paʻepaʻe paʻepaʻe i luga o ia, ma mafua ai ona luluina le vaʻa itiiti i le galu e pei o se vaa e latalata i uili alo o se vasa. O le mea lea, o le pito i luma o le vevesi tele; ae o tua atu, i se eseesega matagofie o faatufugaga, o loo i ai le tulaga tioata o se sami ua mapu, o le mago o le vaa le masima o le vaa ua leai se malosi, ma le tele o se tafola mate, o se olo ua faatoilaloina, faatasi ai ma le fu'a o le pueina faapagota o tautau ma le paie i le tafola- fa'aulu i totonu o lona pu.

O ai Garnery le tusiata, pe sa i ai, ou te le iloa. Ae o lo'u olaga mo ia, a le o le masani lava o ia i lana mataupu, po o le ofoofogia o le aoaoina e se tagata fagota poto masani. O le Farani o tama mo le valivali. Alu ma matamata i ata vali uma o Europa, ma o fea e te maua ai se ala o le ola ma le manava o le vavao i luga o tapoleni, e pei o lena maota manumalo i Versailles; lea e tau ai le tagata vaai i lona ala, pell-mell, i taua tetele sosoo a Farani; o fea o pelu uma e foliga mai o se emo o Malamalama i Matu, ma o tupu faaauupegaina sosoo ma Emeperoa o le a feosofi mai, e pei o se taupulepulega o centaurs? E le'o matua le agavaa mo se nofoaga i totonu o lena faletusi, o nei mea taua o le sami o Garnery.

O le fa'anatura fa'anatura o Falani mo le pu'eina o le matagofie o mea e foliga mai e fa'apitoa ona fa'aalia i ata ma togitogiga o lo'o i ai o latou vaaiga fagota. E le o se tasi vaesefulu o le poto masani a Egelani i faigafaiva, ae le o le tasi le afe o vaega o Amerika, e ui i lea ua latou tuuina atu i atunuu uma e lua ni ata uma ua maeʻa e mafai ona faʻaalia ai le agaga moni o le tuliga tafola. Mo le tele o vaega, o le au faufau tafola Peretania ma Amerika e foliga mai ua faamalieina atoatoa i le tuʻuina atu o faʻamatalaga faʻainisinia o mea, e pei o le avanoa avanoa o le tafola; lea, e tusa ai ma le matagofie o aafiaga, e faatatau i le tusia o le talaaga o se pyramid. E oo lava ia Scoresby, le tafolā Matau lauiloa, ina ua uma ona tuuina mai ia i tatou le umi malō o le tafola Greenland, ma le tolu pe fa tama'i tama'i narwhales ma popoise, ua tatou faia i se faasologa o togitogiga masani o matau vaa, naifi tipi, ma grapnels; ma fa'atasi ai ma le filiga fa'apitoa a le Leuwenhoeck e tu'uina atu i le asiasiga o se lalolagi tete'e e ivasefulu ono fac-similes o tioata kiona Arctic tetele. O le uiga o laʻu tala e leai se faʻaleagaina i le tagata folau sili (Ou te faʻaaloalo ia te ia mo se tagata tuai), ae i se mea taua tele e mautinoa lava o se faʻalavelave e leʻi mauaina mo tioata uma se faʻamatalaga tauto na faia i luma o le Greenland Justice of the Peace.

I le faaopoopo atu i na togitogiga lelei mai Garnery, o loʻo i ai isi togitogiga Farani e lua e tatau ona matauina, e se tasi na te lesitalaina o ia lava "H. Durand.” O se tasi o ia mea, e ui lava e leʻi faʻafetaui lelei i lo tatou faʻamoemoega i le taimi nei, ae e tatau ona taʻua i isi tala. O se vaaiga filemu i le aoauli i motu o le Pasefika; o se fagota Falani na taula, i uta, i se toafilemu, ma le paie e ave vai i luga o le vaa; o la matala o le va'a, ma lau uumi o alofilima i tua, o lo'o fa'au'u fa'atasi uma i le ea savili. E lelei tele le aafiaga, pe a iloiloina e tusa ai ma le tuʻuina atu o le aufaifaiva malosi i lalo o se tasi o latou vaega o le malolo i sasaʻe. O le isi togitogiga o se mea e ese lava: o le va'a e fa'ae'e i luga o le sami, ma i totonu lava o le fatu o le olaga Leviatanic, ma le Tafola Sa'o i tafatafa; o le va'a (i le gaioiga o le tipiina) e fa'ae'e i luga o le sau'ai e pei o se uafu; ma o se vaʻa, faʻanatinati ese mai lenei vaaiga o gaioiga, e uiga i le tulituliloaina o tafola i se mea mamao. O LE MATAUPU 59. Squid.

Na savalia lemu le Pequod i le vao o brit, sa tumau pea le Pequod i lona ala i matu-sasa'e agai i le motu o Java; o se ea malu o lo'o fa'aosoina lona kiuli, ma i totonu o le to'afilemu si'osi'omaga na talotalo malie atu ai ana pou fa'ai'u uumi uumi e tolu i lena savili vaivai, e pei o ni alofilima malu e tolu i luga o le fanua laugatasi. Ae i se tasi taeao lanumoana lanumoana, ina ua sosolo atu le filemu i luga o le sami, e ui i lea e leʻi tausia ma soʻo se toʻafilemu; ina ua foliga mai o se tamatamailima auro na tuu i luga o latou luga o le vai i le susulu umi o le la, e ta'u mai ai se mea lilo; ina ua musumusu faatasi peau maseesee a o tafe lemu; i lenei filemu loloto o le lalolagi vaaia na vaaia ai e Daggoo se vaaiga uiga ese mai le ulu-autu-ulu.

I se mea mamao, o se paʻepaʻe tele na oso aʻe ma le paie, ma aʻe i luga ma maualuga atu, ma vavae ese o ia lava mai le azure, mulimuli ane iila i luma o lo matou taumua e pei o se kiona, seʻe fou mai mauga. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
