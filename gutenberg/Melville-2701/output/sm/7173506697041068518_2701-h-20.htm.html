O LE MATA'UPU 101. O le Tu'u.

A'o le'i mou atu le va'a a Peretania, pe tu'u i lalo, na sau o ia mai Lonetona, ma sa fa'aigoaina ia Samuel Enderby, le fa'atau'oa o lena 'a'ai, o le ulua'i fale fagota ta'uta'ua o Enderby & Sons; o se fale e tusa ai ma lo'u manatu o se tafola mativa, e le mamao i tua atu o fale tautupu tu'ufa'atasi o Tudors ma Bourbons, i se tulaga o le fiafia moni i tala faasolopito. O le a le umi, a'o le'i oo i le tausaga o lo tatou Alii 1775, na i ai lenei fale fagota tele, e le o manino mai a'u pepa i'a e tele; ae o le tausaga lena (1775) na fa'apipi'iina ai ulua'i va'a fa'a-Peretania e masani ona tuli le Sperm Whale; e ui ina mo ni nai tausaga talu ai (talu mai lava i le 1726) o a tatou Puti ma Matai totoa o Nantucket ma le Tovine sa tuliloa e vaa tetele lena Leviatana, ae na o le Atelani i Matu ma Saute: e le o se isi mea. Ia fa'amauina manino iinei, o le au Nantucketers o tagata muamua ia na latou fa'atauina ma le silamanino le tafolā tele; ma mo le afa seneturi e na oi latou lava o tagata o le kelope atoa na fa'apea ona latou fa'apea.

I le 1778, na saunia ai se vaa matagofie, o le Amelia, mo le faamoemoega tonu, ma i le pule e tasi a Enderbys malolosi, na si'o ai ma le lototele Cape Horn, ma o ia o le tagata muamua i atunuu na tuutuu ifo i lalo se vaa tafola o soo se ituaiga i le Sami tele i Saute. O le folauga o se malaga poto ma laki; ma toe foi atu i lona moega ma lona taofi ua tumu i le sua taua, na mulimuli ane ai le faataitaiga a Amelia e isi vaa, Peretania ma Amerika, ma faapea ona matala ai le fanua tele o Tafola o le Sperm Whale o le Pasefika. Ae e lei faamalieina i lenei galuega lelei, na toe faagaee ai le fale le vaivai: Samuelu ma ona atalii uma—e toafia, e na o lo latou tina na te iloa—ma i lalo o la latou vaavaaiga vave, ma o se vaega, ou te manatu, i lo latou totogi, na faatosina ai le malo o Peretania. e auina atu le sloop-of-war Rattler i se malaga fagota e maua i le Sami i Saute. Na ta'ita'ia e le Kapeteni Fa'atonu o le Fua, na faia ai e le Rattler se folauga fa'ate'a i ai, ma faia ai se tautua; o le a le tele e le o aliali mai. Ae e le ona pau lea. I le 1819, o le fale lava lea e tasi na faʻapipiʻiina se latou lava vaʻa tafola, e alu ai i se folauga tofo i ogasami mamao o Iapani. O lena vaa—ua ta’ua o le “Syren”—na faia se folauga faata’ita’i mamalu; ma o le mea lea na muai lauiloa ai le Eleele Fagota a Iapani. O le Syren i lenei malaga lauiloa sa taitaia e le Kapeteni Coffin, o se Nantucketer.

O le mea lea, o le faʻaaloalo uma i Enderbies, o lo latou fale, ou te manatu, o loʻo i ai i le taimi nei; e ui lava e le taumate o le uluai Samuelu atonu ua leva ona se'e lana uaea mo le Sami Tele i Saute o le isi lalolagi.

O le vaa na faaigoa ia te ia sa agavaa mo le mamalu, o se tautai saosaoa ma o se vaa tamalii i itu uma. Na ou tietie atu ia te ia i le vaeluaga o le po i se mea mai le talafatai o Patagonian, ma ou inu lelei i lalo i le vavalo. O se taaloga manaia na matou faia, ma o ni pu uma lava—tagata uma i luga o le vaa. O se olaga puupuu ia i latou, ma se oti fiafia. Ma o lena taaloga lelei sa ou faia—ua leva, ua leva ona pa'i atu le toeaina o Aapo i ana laupapa i lona mulivae nifo elefane—e te manatua ai le mamalu, mausali, talimalo Saxon o lena vaa; ma ia galo aʼu i laʼu faifeau, ma manatua aʼu e le tiapolo, pe afai e galo ia te aʼu. Su'e? Na ou fai atu sa matou fetogi? Ioe, ma sa matou feliua'i i le saoasaoa o le sefulu kalone i le itula; ma ina ua oo mai le matagi (aua e tau ese mai ai iina e latalata ane i Patagonia), ma o lima uma—tagata asiasi ma tagata uma—sa valaauina i luga o le aau, sa matua mamafa tele i matou ma sa tatau ai ona luelue le tasi i le isi i luga o ipu; ma sa matou tuuina atu ma le valea le sakete o o matou peleue i luga o la, ina ia matou tautau ai iina, ma aau vave i le agi malosi, o se faataitaiga lapatai i ta onā uma. Peitaʻi, e leʻi ova atu pou; ma i lea taimi ma lea taimi sa matou taufetuli ifo i lalo, ma le maaleale, ma sa tatau ai ona matou toe pasia le masi, e ui o le masima masima na pa'u ifo i lalo o le vavao, ae ua tele naua le vai ma piki i lo'u tofo.

Sa lelei le povi—malosi, ae sa i ai le tino i totonu. Sa latou fai mai o le povi povi; o isi, e faapea o le aano o povi; ae ou te le iloa, ma le mautinoa, pe na faapefea lena mea. Sa i ai foi a latou dumplings; tama'i, ae tele, simmetrically globular, ma le mafai ona faaleagaina dumplings. Na ou manaʻo e te lagona i latou, ma taʻavale solo i totonu ia te oe pe a uma ona foloina. Afai e te punou mamao i luma, e te lamatia lo latou togiina mai ia te oe e pei o polo piliati. O le areto—ae sa le mafai ona fesoasoani; e le gata i lea, o se anti-scorbutic; i se faapuupuuga, o le falaoa sa i ai na o le pau lava le pasese fou sa latou maua. Ae e leʻi malamalama tele le vavalo, ma e faigofie tele ona laa atu i se tulimanu pogisa pe a e ʻai. Ae o mea uma lava, o le aveina o ia mai le loli i le foeuli, pe a fua i le fua o le kuka a le kuka, e aofia ai ana lava fagu paʻu ola; i luma ma tua, ou te fai atu, o le Samuel Enderby o se vaa fiafia; lelei ma tele; susu lelei ma malosi; ta'e tagata uma, ma tupe faavae mai mulivae seevae i le pulou pulou.

Ae aisea la, ia outou manatu, o le Samuel Enderby, ma isi tagata fagota Peretania ou te iloa—e le o mea uma—o ni vaa ta'uta'ua ma talimālō; na fealuai solo i le povi, ma le areto, ma le apa, ma le ula; e lei vaivai foi i latou i le ai, ma le inu, ma le talie? O le a ou ta'u atu ia te oe. O le tele o le agaga fiafia o nei tagata fagota tafola Peretania e taua mo suʻesuʻega faʻasolopito. Ou te le'i fa'asaoina fo'i su'esu'ega tafola fa'asolopito, pe a foliga mai e mana'omia.

Na muamua atu Peretania i faigafaiva tafola e le au Holani, tagata Sila, ma Tenimaka; lea na latou maua mai ai le tele o faaupuga o loo i ai pea i faiga faiva; a o le mea e sili, o latou tu‘inanau pepeti, o lo‘o papa‘i i mea e ‘ai ma mea e inu ai. Aua, i se tulaga lautele, o le va'a fa'atauloa Peretania e fa'amama lana auvaa; ae le faapena le tafola Egelani. O le mea lea, i le gagana Peretania, o lenei mea o le fagotaina o le loto fiafia e le masani ma masani, ae faʻafuaseʻi ma faʻapitoa; ma, o le mea lea, e tatau ona i ai se tupuaga fa'apitoa, lea o lo'o ta'u mai i inei, ma o le a fa'amalamalamaina atili.

I le taimi o aʻu suʻesuʻega i tala faasolopito o le Leviatanic, sa ou tautevateva ai i se tusi tuai a Holani, lea, ona o le manogi leaga o le fagota, na ou iloa ai e uiga i tagata fagota. O le ulutala, “Dan Coopman,” o le mea lea na ou manatu ai e tatau ona avea lenei ma manatuaga taua a nisi o Amsterdam cooper i faigafaiva, aua e tatau i vaa tafola uma ona ave lana cooper. Na faʻamalosia aʻu i lenei manatu i le vaʻaia o le gaosiga o le tasi "Fitz Swackhammer." Ae o la’u uo o Dr. Snodhead, o se alii sili ona aoaoina, polofesa o Low Dutch ma High German i le kolisi o Santa Claus ma St. Pott’s, lea na ou tuuina atu i ai le galuega mo le faaliliuga, ma tuuina atu ia te ia se pusa moligao sperm mo lona faafitauli— O lenei lava Dr. Snodhead, i le taimi lava na ia matamata ai i le tusi, na ia faamautinoa mai ia te au o le "Dan Coopman" e le o lona uiga "O le Cooper," ae o le "The Merchant." I se faapuupuuga, o lenei tusi Low Dutch anamua ma aʻoaʻoina e uiga i pisinisi a Holani; ma, faatasi ai ma isi mataupu, sa i ai se tala manaia tele o lana faigafaiva fagota. Ma i totonu o lenei mataupu, o le faaulutala, “Smeer,” po o le “Fat,” na ou maua ai se lisi auiliili umi o ofu mo le ato ma potu o le 180 folau a tagata tafola Dutch; mai le fea lisi, e pei ona faaliliuina e Dr. Snodhead, ou te tusia mea nei:

400,000 pauna. o povi. 60,000 lbs. puaa Friesland. 150,000 pauna. o fa'asoa i'a. 550,000 pauna. o masi. 72,000 lbs. o falaoa mālū. 2,800 fikini o pata. 20,000 lbs. Texel & Leyden sisi. 144,000 lbs. sisi (atonu o se mea maualalo). 550 ankers o Sineva. 10,800 paelo pia.

O le tele o laulau fa'afuainumera e matua matutu ile faitauga; e le faapena foi i le tulaga o iai nei, ae peitai, ua lofia ai le tagata faitau i paipa atoa, paelo, quarts, ma gills of gini lelei ma le fiafia.

I lena taimi, sa ou tuuto atu i aso e tolu i le suʻesuʻeina ma le suʻesuʻeina o nei pia uma, povi, ma falaoa, lea na faʻafuaseʻi ona fautua mai ai ia te aʻu le tele o mafaufauga loloto, e mafai ona faʻaaogaina e sili atu ma Platonic; ma, e le gata i lea, sa ou tuufaatasia a'u lava laulau faaopoopo, e pa'i atu i le tele o i'a, ma isi mea, o lo'o 'aina e tagata tafola Low Dutch uma i lena fagotaga tafola anamua Greenland ma Spitzbergen. I le mea muamua, o le aofaʻi o pata, ma Texel ma Leyden sisi faʻaumatia, e foliga mai e ofoofogia. E ui i lea, ou te manatu i ai, i lo latou natura le mautonu, ona ua sili atu ona le mautonu i le natura o la latou galuega, aemaise lava i lo latou tulituliloaina o latou taaloga i na Polar Seas malulu, i luga o le gataifale o lena atunuu o Esquimaux lea na tautino ai e tagata fiafia. le tasi i le isi i bumpers o le suauu nofoaafi.

O le aofaiga foi o pia, e matua tele lava, 10,800 paelo. O lea la, ona o na faigafaiva i polar e faatoa mafai ona molia i le taumafanafana puupuu o lena tau, o lea o le malaga atoa a se tasi o nei tafola Holani, e aofia ai ma le malaga puupuu i ma toe foi mai i le sami Spitzbergen, e le sili atu i le tolu masina, fai mai, ma le faitauina o tagata e 30 i a latou fuāvaa taitasi e 180 folau, e 5,400 a matou tautai Low Dutch i latou uma; o le mea lea, ou te fai atu ai, e lua tonu lava a tatou paelo pia i le tagata, mo le alauni e sefululua vaiaso, ae vagana ai lona fua tutusa o lena 550 ankers o gin. O lea la, pe o nei tagata harpooneers sini ma pia, e matua faufau e pei ona manatu se tasi ia i latou, o se ituaiga tagata sa'o e tutu i luga o le ulu o se vaa, ma taulai lelei i tafola falele; e foliga mai e le mafai. Ae sa latou tulimata'i ia i latou, ma ta foi i latou. Ae o le mea lea e mamao tele i Matu, ia manatua, lea e fetaui lelei le pia ma le faavae; i luga o le Ekueta, i la tatou faiga faiva i saute, o le pia o le a mafai ona fa'amoe ai le tagata tautumu i le ulu o le pou ma inupia i lona va'a; ma o le to'ilalo matuia e ono o'o mai ia Nantucket ma New Bedford.

Ae le toe; ua lava le tala e fa'aalia ai o le au tafola tuai a Holani i le lua pe tolu seneturi talu ai o ni ate maualuga; ma e le'i tuulafoa'ia e le au fagota tafola Egelani se faataitaiga lelei tele. Aua, fai mai latou, pe a folau i se vaa gaogao, afai e le mafai ona e maua se mea e sili atu mai le lalolagi, ia maua se taumafataga manaia mai ai, a itiiti mai. Ma o lenei mea e sasaa ai le decanter.





O LE MATAUPU 102. O se Bower i Arsacides.

E o'o mai i le taimi nei, i le fa'amatala fa'amatala o le Tafola Fa'alano, sa sili ona ou nofo i luga o le ofoofogia o ona foliga i fafo; po'o le tu'uese'ese ma fa'amatalaga i luga o ni nai vaega fa'afale totonu. Ae i se malamalama tele ma le atoatoa e uiga ia te ia, ua tatau nei ia te aʻu ona tatala atili o ia, ma tatala pito o lona faʻauʻu, tatala ona fusi, ma tatala faʻamau ma mata o sooga o ona ivi pito i totonu, faʻapipiʻi. o ia i o outou luma i lana pule; o lona uiga, i lona auivi le faatuaoia.

Ae faapefea nei, Isamaeli? E fa'apefea, ua na'o oe o se tagata foe i le faigafaiva, e fa'afoliga e te iloa se mea e uiga i vaega i lalo ole tafola? O Stubb, o lo'o fa'ae'e i luga o lou kapeteni, na ia faia ni lauga i le tino o le Cetacea; ma e ala i le fesoasoani a le savili, uu i luga se ivi asoaso mo le fa'aaliga? Faamatala oe lava, Isamaeli. E mafai ona e tu'u se tafola matua i luga o lou fogavaa mo le su'esu'ega, e pei ona kukaina e se kuka se puaa tao? E mautinoa lava e leai. O se molimau moni lava e oo mai i le taimi nei, Isamaeli; ae ia e popole pe faapefea ona e mauaina le avanoa na o Iona; le avanoa e lauga ai i luga o sooga ma pou; o amo, o le tuasivi, o mea e moe ai, ma fa'amau i lalo, ua fai ai le auivi o leviatana; ma e pei o sau, potu susu, pata, ma sisi i lona manava.

Ou te ta’utino atu, talu mai Iona, e to’aitiiti lava tafola ua oo i lalo ifo o le pa’u o le tafola matua; e ui i lea, ua faamanuiaina a'u i se avanoa e tosoina ai o ia i ni mea laiti. I totonu o se vaa sa ou i ai, o se tama'i tama'i tafola o le Sperm Whale sa sii a'e lona tino i luga o le fogavaa mo lana tui po o lana ato, e fai ai faaofuofu mo u'amea, ma ulu o tao. E te manatu ua ou tuu atu lena avanoa, e aunoa ma le faaaogaina o la'u to'o ma la'u naifi, ma le talepeina o le faamaufaailoga ma faitau uma mea o loo i totonu o lena tamai tamai?

Ae mo lo'u iloa tonu o ponaivi o le leviatana i lo latou tuputupu a'e tele ma le matua, ona o lena malamalama seasea ou te nofo aitalafu ai i la'u uo tautupu ua maliu o Tranquo, le tupu o Tranque, o se tasi o Arsacides. Ona o lo'u i ai i Tranque, i tausaga ua mavae, ina ua pipii atu i le va'a fefa'ataua'iga Dey o Algiers, sa vala'aulia a'u e fa'aalu se vaega o aso malolo Arsacidean ma le ali'i o Tranque, i lona fale pama ua litaea i Pupella; ose faga i le sami e le mamao tele mai le mea na ta'ua e le auvaa o le Bamboo-Town, o lona laumua.

Faatasi ai ma le tele o isi uiga lelei, o la’u uo tautupu o Tranquo, ona sa faameaalofaina i se alofa faamaoni mo mea uma o le vertu o nuu ese, na ia aumaia faatasi ai i Pupella soo se mea seasea e mafai ona faufau e ona tagata sili atu ona popoto; o la'au ua vaneina sili o mea matagofie, atigi togitogi, tao tao, foe taugata, paopao manogi; ma o i latou uma nei na tufatufa atu i soo se mea ofoofogia faalenatura, o galu tauofo, ma taulaga o lafoga na lafo i luga o ona matafaga.

O le pito sili i nei tafola o se Tafola Tele, lea, ina ua mavae se matagi malosi e le masani ai, na maua atu ua mate ma pa'ulia, ma lona ulu i luga o le niu, o lona fulufulu e pei o lona fuifui, e foliga mai o lana vaalele lanumeamata. Ina ua i'u ina aveese le tino tele o ona fusiga e oo i le gatama, ma matutu ai ivi i le la, ona ave lea ma le faaeteete o le auivi i luga o le Pupella glen, lea ua malu ai nei se malumalu tele o alofialii.

O ivi sa tautau i ipu; o le vertebræ sa vaneina i tala fa'asolopito o Arsacidean, i tala fa'atusa uiga ese; i totonu o le ulupoo, na tuu ai e le au ositaulaga se afi manogi e le matineia, o lea na toe sasaa mai ai e le ulu o le mea lilo lona asu ausa; a'o tautau mai se lala, na tetete le auvae pito i lalo i luga o tagata tapua'i uma, e pei o le pelu tautau-ulu na matua fefefe ai Damocles.

O se vaaiga ofoofogia. O le fafie sa lanu meamata e pei o mosses o le Icy Glen; sa tutu maualuluga ma faafefeteina laau, ma lagona lo latou sua ola; o le eleele galue i lalo e pei o se lalaga lalaga, ua i ai se kapeta matagofie i ona luga, ua fai a'e ai fuga o le vine o le filo ma le filo, ma o fuga ola o faatusa. O la‘au uma lava, ma o latou lālā uma ‘ua mamafa; o la‘au la‘au uma, ma ‘apa, ma mutia; o le ea feaveai; o nei mea uma sa galulue e le aunoa. E ala atu i laiga o laulaau, o le la tele na foliga mai o se vaa lele o loo lalagaina le lauusiusi le vaivai. Oi, pisi lalaga! lalaga lē vaaia!—tumau!—tasi le upu!—o fea e tafe ai le ie? O le a le maota e mafai ona fa'ae'e ai? se a le mea o nei galuega mamafa uma? Fai mai lau lalaga!—tumau lou lima!—ae tasi le upu ia te oe! E leai—ua lele le vaa—o opeopea mai fafo o le lalaga; e se'e ese le kapeta fa'aola fou. O le atua lalaga, na te lalaga; ma o lena lalaga ua tutuli ai o ia, ua le faalogoina ai e ia le leo faaletino; ma o lena hamu, o i tatou foi, o e vaavaai i le lalaga ua tutuli; ma seiloga tatou te sosola ese ona tatou faalogoina lea o leo e afe o loo tautala mai ai. Aua e faapena foi i falefaigaluega uma. O ‘upu tautala e lē fa‘alogoina i va‘a fa‘aola; o na lava upu o lo'o fa'alogoina manino e aunoa ma ni puipui, o lo'o pa mai i fa'amalama matala. O lea ua iloa ai tagata leaga. Oi, tino! ona, ia outou mataala; aua o lea, i lenei leo uma o le lalaga tele o le lalolagi, e mafai ona lagona mamao atu ou mafaufauga poto.

O lea la, i le lotolotoi o le mea lalaga lanumeamata ma le le mautonu o lena laau o le Arsacidean, o le auivi sili, paepae, tapuaʻi o loʻo taoto - o se tagata paie tele! Peita'i, a'o fefiloi ma fa'amumu fa'ata'ali'oli'o mai ia te ia le filo lanumeamata ua lalagaina, o le tagata paie malosi na foliga mai o le lalaga lalaga poto; ua lalaga uma lava o ia i vine; i masina ta'itasi e fa'apea e sili atu ona lanumeamata, fou; a o ia o se auivi. Ola gaugau Oti; Oti trellised Ola; o le atua leaga na faaipoipo i le Ola talavou, ma na fanaua ai o ia o mamalu faaumiumi ulu.

O lenei, ina ua ou asiasi atu i le tupu Tranquo lenei tafola ofoofogia, ma vaaia le ulupoo o se fata faitaulaga, ma le asu faafoliga alu aʻe mai le mea na alu atu ai le vaalele moni, sa ou maofa i le tupu e tatau ona manatu i se falesa o se mea o vertu. Sa ata o ia. Ae na sili atu ona ou maofa i le tautō atu o ositaulaga o lana asu asu e moni. Na ou savali solo i luma o lenei auivi—tuu ese vine—ua malepe i ivi-aso-aso—ma se polo o le filo Arsacidean, ua feseseai, ma ua umi i le lotolotoi o le tele o ona pou fe'avea'i, paolo ma uafu. Ae e le'i umi ae uma la'u laina; ma mulimuli i tua, na ou sau mai le avanoa na ou ulu atu ai. Ou te lei vaai i se mea ola i totonu; e leai se mea na i ai na o ivi.

Na ou tipiina se laau fua lanu meamata, na ou toe maulu ai i totonu o le auivi. Na iloa mai e le au ositaulaga aʻu o loʻo alu aʻe le maualuga o le ivi asoaso mulimuli, “E faapefea nei!” sa latou alalaga; “E te fuaina ea lo matou atua lea! E mo tatou lena.” “Ioe, ositaulaga—ia, o le a le umi tou te faia ai o ia?” Peitai o lea na tulai mai ai se tauvaga malosi i totonu ia te i latou, e uiga i vae ma inisi; sa latou ta'e le tasi ma le isi ia la'au o le fanua—na ta'u le ulupoo tele-ma faoa lena avanoa laki, sa vave ona ou faaiuina a'u lava fua.

O nei fa'atonuga ua ou fa'ailoa atu e tu'u atu i ou luma. Ae muamua, ia faamauina, e faapea, i lenei mataupu, ou te le saoloto e ta'u atu soo se fua faanaunau ou te fiafia i ai. Ona o loʻo i ai pulega auivi e mafai ona e vaʻai i ai, e suʻe ai loʻu saʻo. O loo i ai se Falemataaga o le Leviatanic, latou te fai mai ia te au, i Hull, Egelani, o se tasi o taulaga fagota o lena atunuu, o loo i ai ni a latou faʻataʻitaʻiga lelei o apaapa ma isi tafola. E faapena foi, ua ou faalogo i le falemataaga o Manchester, i New Hampshire, o loo ia i latou le mea ua taʻua e le au pule “na o le pau lea o le ata faataitai atoatoa o le Greenland po o le River Whale i le Iunaite Setete.” E le gata i lea, i se nofoaga i Yorkshire, Egelani, o Burton Constable lona igoa, o loo ia te ia se auivi o se Tafola Sperm, o se alii o Sir Clifford Constable, ae e feololo lona lapopoa, e le o se tulaga matua tele o laʻu uo o King Tranquo. .

I tulaga uma e lua, o tafola na pa'ulia e iai nei auivi e lua, na muai ta'ua e i latou e ana i luga o mafuaaga tutusa. O LE MATAUPU 104. O le Tafola Fosi.

Mai lona tele tele e maua ai e le tafola se autu e sili ona manaia e faʻalautele ai, faʻalautele, ma faʻasalalau lautele. Mata e te le mafai ona oomiina o ia. Talu ai ua ou taumafai e pulea lenei Leviatana, ua tatau ai ia te a'u ona faamaonia a'u lava e matua'i atoatoa i le galuega; e le o le amana'iaina o siama semina itiiti o lona toto, ma viliina o ia i fafo e oo atu i le pito o lona manava. Ua uma ona fa'amatalaina o ia i le tele o mea o lo'o nofo ai nei ma fa'apitoa fa'apitoa, o lo'o tumau pea le fa'alauteleina o ia i se vaaiga fa'asolopito, fossiliferous, ma antediluvian. Pe a faatatau atu i so o se isi lava meafaiola nai lo le Leviatana—i se loi po o se fulu—o na faaupuga mataʻutia e ono manatu tonu lava e matuā tāua tele. Ae ina ua avea le Leviatana ma tusitusiga, ua suia le mataupu. Ou te fia tautevateva i lenei emprise i lalo o upu sili ona mamafa o le lomifefiloi. Ma o le mea lea e faapea atu, soo se taimi lava e faigofie ai ona feutagai ma se tasi i le faagasologa o nei tusitusiga, sa masani ona ou faaaogaina se lomiga tai kuata tele o Johnson, na faatau manino mai mo lena faamoemoe; aua o le tele o le tele o tagata e le masani ai le tusitala ta'uta'ua na sili atu ona fetaui ma ia e tuufaatasia ai se lomifefiloi e faaaoga e se tusitala tafola e pei o a'u.

E masani ona faalogo se tasi i tusitala e tulaʻi mai ma faʻateleina a latou mataupu, e ui atonu e foliga mai o se mea masani. E faapefea la ona ou tusia lenei Leviatana? Ma le le iloa ua fa'alauteleina la'u chirography i mataitusi fa'ailoga. Tu'u mai ia te a'u se ie tui! Aumai ia te au le lua o Vesuvius mo se vaitusi! Uo, uu o'u lima! Auā o le na'o le tusia o o'u manatu i lenei Leviatana, latou te fa'avaivaia ai a'u, ma fa'avaivaia ai a'u i lo latou fa'ao'o mai o le salu, e peiseai e aofia ai le li'o atoa o le faasaienisi, ma tupulaga uma o tafola, ma tagata, ma mastodons, taimi ua tuanai, taimi nei, ma o le a oʻo mai, faatasi ai ma ata faataamilosaga uma o le malo i luga o le fogaeleele, ma i le atulaulau atoa, e le aofia ai ona tuaoi. E faapena, ma le matua faateleina, o le uiga lelei o se autu tele ma saoloto! Matou te faʻalauteleina i lona tele. Ina ia maua se tusi malosi, e tatau ona e filifilia se autu malosi. E leai se voluma tele ma tumau e mafai ona tusia i luga o le fulu, e ui lava o le toatele o i latou ua uma ona faataitai.

A'o le'i o'o atu i le mataupu e uiga i Tafola Fossil, ou te tuuina atu lo'u agavaa o se tagata su'esu'e i le eleele, e ala i lo'u ta'u atu i lo'u taimi eseese sa avea ai a'u ma ma'a ma'a, ma o se eli tele foi o alavai, alavai ma vaieli, pusa uaina, fale puipui, ma vaieli o ituaiga uma. E faapena foi, e ala i le amataga, ou te manao e faamanatu atu i le au faitau, e ui i le vaega muamua o le eleele o loo maua ai toega o sau'ai ua toetoe lava a mou atu; o mea na maua mulimuli ane na maua i mea ua taʻua o le Tertiary formations e foliga mai o se sootaga, pe i so o se tulaga ua motusia soʻotaga, i le va o meaola e le masani ai, ma i latou o a latou fanau mamao ua fai mai na ulu atu i le Atolaau; o Tafola Fossil uma na maua e o'o mai i le taimi nei, o le vaitaimi Tertiary, o le taimi mulimuli lea na muamua atu i le fa'avae papa'u. Ma e ui lava e leai se tasi oi latou e tali saʻo i soʻo se ituaiga o loʻo iloa i le taimi nei, ae o loʻo tutusa lava i latou i tulaga lautele, e faʻamaonia ai lo latou tulaga o Cetacean fossils.

O togavao malepelepe o tafola muai-adamite, o vaega o o latou ivi ma auivi, na maua i totonu o le tolusefulu tausaga talu ai, i taimi eseese, na maua i le pito i lalo o le Alps, i Lombardy, i Farani, i Egelani, i Sikotilani, ma i le Setete o Louisiana, Mississippi, ma Alabama. Faatasi ai ma le sili atu o le fia iloa o ia toega o se vaega o se ulupulu, lea i le tausaga 1779 na malepelepe i le Rue Dauphine i Pale, o se auala puupuu e matala toetoe lava i luga o le maota o Tuileries; ma ua faaletonu ivi i le eliina o uafu tetele o Antwerp, i taimi o Napoleone. Na taʻua e Cuvier o nei fasi vaega e iai i nisi o ituaiga Leviatanic e matua le iloa.

Ae o le mea aupito sili ona matagofie o mea uma a Cetacean o le auivi tele na toetoe a atoatoa o se sau'ai ua mate, na maua i le tausaga 1842, i luga o le faatoaga a Judge Creagh, i Alabama. Na ave e pologa maofa ma le talitonu i tafatafa ane o ivi o se tasi o agelu pa'ū. Na taʻua e fomaʻi Alabama o se meaola tele, ma faʻauʻu i ai le igoa o Basilosaurus. Ae o nisi o ponaivi faʻataʻitaʻiga na ave i luga o le sami ia Owen, le English Anatomist, na faʻaalia ai o lenei mea o loʻo taʻua o se tafola, e ui lava o se ituaiga o meaola. O se fa'ata'ita'iga taua o le mea moni, o lo'o ta'ua pea lava pea i totonu o lenei tusi, o le auivi o le tafola e na'o sina fa'amatalaga itiiti lava i foliga o lona tino atoa. O lea na toe faaigoa ai e Owen le sau'ai o Zeuglodon; ma i lana pepa na faitauina i luma o le London Geological Society, na ia ta'ua ai, i lona tino, o se tasi o meaola sili ona tulaga ese ua soloiesea e suiga o le kelope.

Pe a ou tu i totonu o nei auivi malolosi o le Leviatana, ulupoo, gutu, auvae, ivi, ma vertebræ, e iloga uma ona foliga tutusa ma ituaiga o meaola o le sami; ae i le taimi lava e tasi o loo i ai i le isi itu o sootaga tutusa ma le Leviatana o mea anamua ua faaumatia, o latou matutua e le mafaitaulia; O a'u, e ala i se lolo, ua toe aumaia i tua i lena vaitau ofoofogia, ae le'i mafai ona faapea ua amata le taimi; auā o le taimi na amata mai i le tagata. O lea ua taamilo mai ai le vevesi faaefuefu o Saturn i o'u luga, ma ua ou maua ai ni vaaiga faanenefu ma tetete i na Polar e faavavau; ina ua fetaomi malosi atu aisa ua taia i le mea ua ta'ua nei o le Teropika; ma i le 25,000 maila uma o le si'osi'omaga o lenei lalolagi, e le'i mafai ona iloa atu se lautele o le laueleele. Ona o le tafola lea o le lalolagi atoa; ma, le tupu o le foafoaga, na ia tuua lona ala i luga o laina nei o Andes ma Himalehs. O ai e mafai ona fa'aalia se gafa e pei o Leviatana? Na faamasaaina e le taulima a Aapo le toto matua atu nai lo le toto o Farao. E foliga mai o Metusela o se tama aoga. Ou te tilotilo solo e faatalofa ma Semu. Ou te matua ofo i lenei mea e le mafaamatalaina, o le mataʻutia le mafaamatalaina o le tafola, lea, sa i ai muamua i taimi uma, e tatau ona i ai pe a uma tausaga faaletagata soifua.

Ae e le na o le Leviatana na tuua ai ona uiga a o lei oo i le amita i papatusi faafoliga o le natura, ma i maa lime ma marl na tuufaasolo mai ai lona tino anamua; but upon Egyptian tablets, whose antiquity seems to claim for them an almost fossiliferous character, we find the unmistakable print of his fin. In an apartment of the great temple of Denderah, some fifty years ago, there was discovered upon the granite ceiling a sculptured and painted planisphere, abounding in centaurs, griffins, and dolphins, similar to the grotesque figures on the celestial globe of the moderns. Gliding among them, old Leviathan swam as of yore; was there swimming in that planisphere, centuries before Solomon was cradled.

Nor must there be omitted another strange attestation of the antiquity of the whale, in his own osseous post-diluvian reality, as set down by the venerable John Leo, the old Barbary traveller.

“Not far from the Sea-side, they have a Temple, the Rafters and Beams of which are made of Whale-Bones; for Whales of a monstrous size are oftentimes cast up dead upon that shore. The Common People imagine, that by a secret Power bestowed by God upon the Temple, no Whale can pass it without immediate death. But the truth of the Matter is, that on either side of the Temple, there are Rocks that shoot two Miles into the Sea, and wound the Whales when they light upon 'em. They keep a Whale's Rib of an incredible length for a Miracle, which lying upon the Ground with its convex part uppermost, makes an Arch, the Head of which cannot be reached by a Man upon a Camel's Back. This Rib (says John Leo) is said to have layn there a hundred Years before I saw it. Their Historians affirm, that a Prophet who prophesy'd of Mahomet, came from this Temple, and some do not stand to assert, that the Prophet Jonas was cast forth by the Whale at the Base of the Temple.”

In this Afric Temple of the Whale I leave you, reader, and if you be a Nantucketer, and a whaleman, you will silently worship there.





CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. He had lighted with such energy upon a thwart of his boat that his ivory leg had received a half-splintering shock. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
