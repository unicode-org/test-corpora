NDZIMA YA 89. Fast-Fish na Loose-Fish.

Ku kombeteriwa eka waif na waif-poles eka ndzima yohetelela kambe yin’we, swilava ku hlamusela kun’wana ka milawu na swiletelo swa vumaki bya ti whale, laha waif yinga tekiwa tani hi xikombiso lexikulu na beji.

Hakanyingi swi humelela leswaku loko swikepe swo hlayanyana swi ri karhi swi famba-famba swin’we, whale yi nga ha biwa hi xikepe xin’we, ivi yi baleka, ivi eku heteleleni yi dlayiwa ivi yi khomiwa hi xikepe xin’wana; naswona laha hi ndlela leyi nga kongomangiki ku vumbiwa swiendlakalo swo tala leswitsongo leswi nga languteriwangiki, hinkwaswo swi hlanganyela eka xivumbeko lexi xin’we lexikulu. Hi xikombiso,—endzhaku ka ku hlongorisa ni ku khomiwa ka whale hi ku karhala ni loku nga ni khombo, ntsumbu wu nga ha ntshunxeka exikepeni hikwalaho ka xidzedze xa matimba; naswona yi khulukela ekule yi ya eleeward, yi tlhela yi tekiwa hi muphasi wa vumbirhi wa whale, loyi, hi ku rhula, hi ku rhula a yi koka etlhelo, handle ka khombo ra vutomi kumbe layini. Xisweswo minjhekanjhekisano leyi karhataka swinene ni ya madzolonga hakanyingi a yi ta va kona exikarhi ka vaphasi va tinhlampfi, loko a ku nga ri na nawu wo karhi lowu tsariweke kumbe lowu nga tsariwangiki, wa misava hinkwayo, lowu nga kanetiwiki lowu tirhaka eka milandzu hinkwayo.

Kumbexana nawu wun’we ntsena wa ximfumo wa ku hlota ti whale lowu pfumeleriweke hi nawu wa nawu, a ku ri wa le Holland. Yi lerisiwe hi States-General hi AD 1695. Kambe hambi leswi ku nga riki na tiko rin’wana leri nga tshama ri va na nawu lowu tsariweke wo phasa ti whale, hambiswiritano vaphasi va tihlampfi va le Amerika va vile vaendli va milawu na magqweta ya vona vini emhakeni leyi. Va nyikile sisiteme leyi hi ku angarhela yi tlulaka Justinian’s Pandects na Milawu ya Xichayina ya Sosayiti ya China yo Tshikelela ku Nghenelela eka Mabindzu ya Vanhu van’wana. Ina; milawu leyi yi nga ha va yi vatliwile eka farthing ya Queen Anne, kumbe eka barb ya harpoon, naswona yi ambariwa enhan’wini, so i yitsongo swinene.

I. Fast-Fish yi wela eka party fast eka yona.

II. Loose-Fish i ntlangu lowu lulameke eka un’wana na un’wana loyi anga hatlisaka ku yi khoma.

Kambe lexi tlangaka vubihi eka nawu lowu wa vutshila i ku koma loku hlamarisaka ka wona, loku lavaka vholumo leyikulu ya tinhlamuselo ku yi hlamusela.

Xo sungula: Xana Fast-Fish i yini? Nhlampfi leyi hanyaka kumbe leyi feke hi tlhelo ra vutshila yi hatlisa, loko yi hlanganisiwa ni xikepe kumbe xikepe lexi tshamaka vanhu, hi xitirhisiwa xihi na xihi lexi lawuriwaka nikatsongo hi loyi a tshamaka eka xona kumbe lava tshamaka eka xona,—xigolonyana xa le henhla, xihaha-mpfhuka, ntambhu ya tisentimitara ta nhungu, ntambhu ya thelegiramu, kumbe ntambhu ya xivungu, hinkwaswo swa fana. Hilaha ku fanaka nhlampfi yi hatlisa hi tlhelo ra xithekiniki loko yi rhwala waif, kumbe xikombiso xin’wana na xin’wana lexi tiviwaka xa ku va na xona; ntsena loko vandla leri yi tshikaka ri kombisa kahle vuswikoti bya vona nkarhi wihi na wihi byo yi teka etlhelo, kun’we ni xikongomelo xa vona xo endla tano.

Leti i tinhlamuselo ta sayense; kambe tinhlamuselo ta vahlaseri va whale hi voxe minkarhi yin’wana ti vumbiwa hi marito yo tika ni ku gongondza lokukulu—Coke-upon-Littleton ya xibakele. I ntiyiso, exikarhi ka vahlayisi va whale lava lulameke swinene ni lava xiximekaka minkarhi hinkwayo ku nyikiwa mali yo pfuneta eka milandzu yo hlawuleka, laha ku nga ta va ku pfumaleka ka vululami loku chavisaka ka mahanyelo leswaku ntlawa wun’wana wu vula leswaku wu ni whale leyi tshameke yi hlongoriwa kumbe ku dlayiwa hi ntlawa wun’wana. Kambe van’wana a va na vukheta swonghasi nikatsongo.

Kwalomu ka makume ntlhanu wa malembe lama hundzeke ku vile na mhaka yo hlamarisa ya whale-trover leyi tengiweke eNghilandhi, laha vamangaleri va vekeke leswaku endzhaku ko hlongorisa whale hi matimba emalwandle ya N’walungu; naswona loko hakunene vona (vamangaleri) va humelerile ku khoma tinhlampfi hi harpoon; eku heteleleni, hi khombo ra vutomi bya vona, va boheke ku nga tshiki milayeni ya vona ntsena, kambe xikepe xa vona hi xoxe. Eku heteleleni vamangaleriwa (vatirhi va xikepe xin’wana) va tile na whale, va ba, va dlaya, va khoma, naswona eku heteleleni va yi teka emahlweni ka mahlo ya vamangaleri. Naswona loko vamangaleriwa volavo va kanetiwa na, mukaputeni wa vona u tshove tintiho ta yena eka meno ya vamangaleri, kutani a va tiyisekisa leswaku hi ndlela ya doxology eka papila leri a ri endleke, sweswi u ta hlayisa ntambhu ya vona, tiharpoon, na xikepe, leswi a swi tshame swi khomanile eka whale hi nkarhi wa ku khomiwa. Hikwalaho sweswi vamangaleri va mangalele leswaku va vuyiseriwa nkoka wa whale ya vona, layini, tiharpoon ni xikepe xa vona.

Nkulukumba Erskine a a ri gqweta ra vamangaleriwa; Lord Ellenborough a a ri muavanyisi. Loko a ri karhi a tiyimelela, Erskine wa vutlhari u ye emahlweni a kombisa xiyimo xa yena, hi ku kombetela eka vugevenga bya sweswinyana. con. mhaka, laha wanuna wa xiyimo xa le henhla, endzhaku ko ringeta mahala ku lawula tihanyi ta nsati wa yena, eku heteleleni a n’wi tshikile emalwandle ya vutomi; kambe hi ku famba ka malembe, hi ku hundzuka eka goza rero, u sungule goza ro tlhela a n’wi teka. Erskine a a ri eka tlhelo lerin’wana; naswona u tlhele a swi seketela hi ku vula leswaku, hambi leswi eku sunguleni wanuna loyi a a bohe wansati loyi hi harpoon, naswona a tshame a titsona swakudya, naswona ntsena hikwalaho ka ntshikilelo lowukulu wa vubihi bya yena lebyi a byi ri karhi byi nwela, eku heteleleni u n’wi tshikile; kambe u n’wi tshikile u endle tano, lerova a va nhlampfi leyi ntshunxekeke; naswona hikwalaho loko wanuna loyi a landzeleke a n’wi boha hi harpoon nakambe, wansati loyi u tlhele a va nhundzu ya wanuna yoloye loyi a landzeleke, kun’we ni harpoon yihi na yihi leyi nga ha vaka yi kumiwile yi namarhela eka yena.

Sweswi eka mhaka ya sweswi Erskine u kanetile leswaku swikombiso swa whale na wansati a swi kombisana hi ku tlherisela.

Swikombelo leswi, ni swivilelo leswi tlheriseriweke, loko swi twiwile hi ndlela leyi faneleke, muavanyisi la dyondzekeke swinene hi swipimelo leswi vekiweke u endle xiboho, ku nga,—Leswaku loko ku ri xikepe, u xi nyike vamangaleri, hikuva va xi tshikile ntsena leswaku va ponisa vutomi bya vona; kambe leswaku malunghana ni whale leyi kanetiwaka, ti- harpoon ni layini, a ti ri ta vamangaleriwa; whale, hikuva a ku ri Loose-Fish hi nkarhi wa ku khomiwa ko hetelela; na tiharpoon na layini hikuva loko nhlampfi yi famba na swona, yona (nhlampfi) yi kume nhundzu eka swilo sweswo; naswona hikokwalaho un’wana na un’wana loyi endzhaku a tekeke tinhlampfi a a ri na mfanelo eka tona. Sweswi vamangaleriwa endzhaku ka sweswo va teke tinhlampfi; ergo, swihloko leswi boxiweke laha henhla a swi ri swa vona.

Munhu la tolovelekeke loko a languta xiboho lexi xa Muavanyisi la dyondzekeke swinene, swi nga ha endleka a xi kaneta. Kambe loko yi rimiwile kuya fika eka ribye rosungula ra mhaka, misinya yimbirhi leyikulu leyi vekiweke ehansi eka milawu ya mawele ya whale leyi tshahiweke khale, naswona yitirhisiwile naku hlamuseriwa hi Lord Ellenborough eka nandzu lowu tshahiweke laha henhla; milawu leyi yimbirhi leyi khumbaka Fast-Fish na Loose-Fish, ndzi ri, loko yi anakanyisisa, yi ta kumiwa yi ri masungulo ya vutivi hinkwabyo bya nawu bya vanhu; hikuva ku nga khathariseki vutshila bya yona lebyi rharhanganeke bya swifaniso leswi vatliweke, Tempele ya Nawu, ku fana ni Tempele ya Vafilista, yi ni swilo swimbirhi ntsena leswi yi nga yimaka eka swona.

Xana a hi rito leri nga enon’wini wa un’wana ni un’wana, leri nge, Ku va ni nhundzu i hafu ya nawu: hi leswaku, ku nga khathariseki leswaku nchumu wolowo wu ve njhani? Kambe hakanyingi ku va ni nhundzu i nawu hinkwawo. Hi yihi misiha ni mimoya-xiviri ya malandza ya le Rhaxiya ni mahlonga ya Riphabliki handle ka Fast-Fish, leyi ku va ni yona ku nga nawu hinkwawo? Xana eka n’wini wa ndhawu loyi a nga ni vudlakuta i yini lexi nga ni xivungu xo hetelela xa noni kambe i Fast-Fish? I yini kwalahaya yindlu ya marble ya villain leyi nga voniwiki leyi nga na door-plate ya waif; i yini sweswo handle ka Fast-Fish? Hi yihi nhlawulo lowu onhaka lowu Mordekayi, muxavisi wa swilo, a wu kumaka eka Woebegone wa xisiwana, loyi a nga bankrupt, hi xikweleti xo hlayisa ndyangu wa ka Woebegone leswaku wu nga dlayiwi hi ndlala; i yini discount yoleyo yo onha handle ka Fast-Fish? Xana i yini muholo wa Bixopo-nkulu wa Savesoul wa £100,000 lowu tekiweke eka xinkwa lexitsongo ni chizi ya madzana ya magidi ya vatirhi lava tshovekeke (hinkwavo va tiyiseka hi tilo handle ka mpfuno wihi na wihi wa Savesoul) i yini £100,000 yoleyo ya xirhendzevutana kambe i Fast-Fish? Hi wahi madoroba na swimitana swa ndzhaka swa Duke of Dunder handle ka Fast-Fish? Xana eka muchayeri yoloye wa harpoone la kanakanisiwaka, John Bull, ku vuriwa yini eka Ireland leyi pfumalaka, kambe i Fast-Fish? Ku vuriwa yini eka muendli yoloye wa muapostola, Makwerhu Jonathan, Texas kambe i Fast-Fish? Naswona malunghana ni leswi hinkwaswo, xana Ku va ni Ndhuma a hi nawu hinkwawo?

Kambe loko dyondzo ya Fast-Fish yi tirha hi ku angarhela, dyondzo ya muxaka wa Loose-Fish ya ha ri tano ngopfu. Sweswo swi tirha emisaveni hinkwayo ni emisaveni hinkwayo.

Xana Amerika a ku ri yini hi 1492 handle ka Loose-Fish, laha Columbus a hlaseleke mpimanyeto wa le Spain hi ndlela yo wu tshika eka n’wini wakwe wa vuhosi ni n’wini wakwe wa xisati? Xana Poland a ri ri yini eka Czar? Hi rihi Greece eka Turk? I yini India ku ya eNghilandhi? Xana eku heteleleni Mexico ri ta va yini eka United States? Hinkwaswo Loose-Fish.

Hi tihi Timfanelo ta Munhu na Ntshunxeko wa Misava handle ka Loose-Fish? I yini miehleketo na mavonelo ya vavanuna hinkwavo handle ka Loose-Fish? Hi wihi nsinya wa nawu wa ripfumelo ra vukhongeri eka vona handle ka Loose-Fish? Xana eka vativi va marito lava tinyungubyisaka hi ku ngungumerisa i miehleketo ya vaehleketi kambe ya Loose-Fish? Xana i yini globe leyikulu hi yoxe handle ka Loose-Fish? Nakona u yini, muhlayi, handle ka Loose-Fish na Fast-Fish, na wena?





NDZIMA YA 90. Tinhloko kumbe Misisi.

“De balena vero swi enerile, si rex habeat caput, na regina caudam.” Bracton, xihlamusela-marito xa l. 3, c. 3. 3. .

Xilatini kusuka eka tibuku ta Milawu ya England, leswi tekiweke xikan’we na mongo, swivula, leswaku eka ti whale hinkwato leti khomiweke hi mani na mani eribuweni ra tiko rero, Hosi, tani hi Honorary Grand Harpooneer, yifanele kuva na nhloko, naswona Hosi ya xisati yifanele kuva yona hi xichavo yi nyikeriwa hi ncila. Ku avana loku, eka whale, kufana swinene na ku tsema apula hi hafu; a ku na masalela ya le xikarhi. Sweswi tanihi leswi nawu lowu, ehansi ka xivumbeko lexi cinciweke, wu tirhaka ku fikela namuntlha eNghilandhi; naswona tanihi leswi yi nyikelaka hi tindlela to hambana ku nga tolovelekanga loku nga tolovelekangiki loku khumbaka nawu wo angarhela wa Fast and Loose-Fish, laha yi langutisiwa eka ndzima leyi hambaneke, eka nsinya wa nawu lowu fanaka wa xichavo lowu susumetelaka swiporo swa Manghezi ku va hi ku tirhisa movha lowu hambaneke , leyi vekeriweke hi ku hlawuleka ndhawu ya vuhosi. Eka xiyimo xo sungula, hi vumbhoni byo lava ku tiva bya leswaku nawu lowu boxiweke laha henhla wa ha tirha, ndzi ya emahlweni ndzi veka emahlweni ka n’wina xiyimo lexi humeleleke eka malembe mambirhi lama hundzeke.

Switikomba onge vatluti van’wana vo tshembeka va Dover, kumbe Sandwich, kumbe un’wana wa Cinque Ports, endzhaku ko hlongorisa swinene va humelerile ku dlaya naku yi veka eribuweni ra lwandle ra whale ya kahle leyi ekusunguleni ava yi hlamusele ekule na ribuwa. Sweswi Cinque Ports hi xiphemu kumbe hi ndlela yo karhi yi le hansi ka vulawuri bya muxaka wa phorisa kumbe beadle, leyi vuriwaka Lord Warden. Ku khoma hofisi hi ku kongoma ku suka eka harhi, ndza pfumela, swiendlakalo hinkwaswo swa miholo ya vuhosi ku ya eka tindhawu ta Ribuwa ra Cinque swi hundzuka hi ku averiwa ka yena. Hi vatsari van’wana hofisi leyi yi vuriwa sinecure. Kambe a swi tano. Hikuva Lord Warden u thoriwile hi ku khomeka minkarhi yin’wana eku fobbini ka ti perquisites ta yena; leti nga ta yena ngopfu-ngopfu hikwalaho ka ku fobbing koloko loku fanaka ka tona.

Sweswi loko vatluti lava va swisiwana lava hisiweke hi dyambu, va nga ambalanga nchumu, naswona va khome maburuku ya vona va rhendzelekile ehenhla hi milenge ya vona ya eely, hi ku karhala va koke tinhlampfi ta vona to nona ehenhla ti tlhela ti oma, va titshembisa £150 leyinene eka mafurha ya risima na marhambu; naswona hi ku ehleketa va nwa tiya leyi nga tolovelekangiki ni vasati va vona, ni ale leyinene ni vanghana va vona, hi matimba ya swiavelo swa vona hi ku hambana ka vona; up steps a very learned and most Christian and charitable gentleman, na kopi ya Blackstone ehansi ka voko ra yena; naswona loko a yi veka enhlokweni ya whale, u ri—“Mavoko! nhlampfi leyi, vini va mina, i Fast-Fish. Ndzi yi khoma tanihi ya Hosi Mulanguteri.” Ehenhla ka leswi vatluti lava pfumalaka hi ku chava ka vona loku xiximekaka—leswi hakunene va nga Manghezi—hi ku nga tivi leswaku va nga vula yini, va wela eku khwaxani ka tinhloko ta vona hi matimba matlhelo hinkwawo; hi nkarhi wolowo hi ku vilela a languta ku suka eka whale ku ya eka munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki. Kambe sweswo a swi lulamisa mhaka leyi hi ndlela yo karhi, kutani swi olovisa mbilu yo nonon’hwa ya wanuna la dyondzekeke hi kopi ya Blackstone. Eku heteleleni un’wana wa vona, endzhaku ko khwaxa nkarhi wo leha a lava mianakanyo yakwe, u endle xivindzi xo vulavula, .

“Ndzi kombela, nkulukumba, i mani Lord Warden?”

“Xifundzhankulu.”

“Kambe ndhuna a yi nga ri na mhaka na ku teka nhlampfi leyi?”

“I wa yena.”

“Hi vile eka xiphiqo lexikulu, na khombo, na ku tirhisiwa ko karhi, naswona i hinkwaswo sweswo ku ya eka ku vuyerisa Duke; hi nga kumi nchumu nikatsongo hikwalaho ka switlhavi swa hina handle ka swirhumbana swa hina?”

“I wa yena.”

“Xana Duke u xisiwana swinene lerova a sindzisiwa ku endla ndlela leyi yo heleriwa hi ntshembo yo kuma mali yo tihanyisa?”

“I wa yena.”

“Ndzi ehlekete ku ntshunxa manana wa mina wa khale la etleleke emubedweni hi xiphemu xa xiphemu xa mina xa whale leyi.”

“I wa yena.”

“Xana Duke a nge eneriseki hi kotara kumbe hafu?”

“I wa yena.”

Hi rito rin’we, whale leyi yi khomiwile yi xavisiwa, naswona Grace wa yena Duke wa Wellington u kume mali yoleyo. Hi ku ehleketa leswaku loko yi langutiwa hi mavoni man’wana yo karhi, nandzu lowu wu nga ha tekiwa hi ku koteka loku nga riki na nchumu hi mpimo wo karhi lowutsongo, ehansi ka swiyimo, tanihi lowu tikaka swinene, mufundhisi wo tshembeka wa doroba hi xichavo u rhumele papila eka Grace wa yena, a n’wi kombela leswaku a teka mhaka ya vatluti volavo lava nga riki na nkateko yi tekeriwa enhlokweni hi ku helela. Leswi Lord Duke wa mina hi xiviri a hlamuleke (mapapila hamambirhi ya kandziyisiwile) leswaku se u endlile tano, naswona u amukerile mali, naswona u ta boheka eka reverend gentleman loko eka nkarhi lowu taka yena (the reverend gentleman) a ta ala ku nghenelela eka ya vanhu van’wana bindzu. Xana loyi i mukhalabye la ha lwaka, la yimeke emakhoneni ya mimfumo yinharhu, hi mavoko hinkwawo a sindzisa tinyiko ta tintswalo ta vakomberi?

Swi ta voniwa hi ku olova leswaku emhakeni leyi mfanelo leyi ku vuriwaka leswaku Duke a yi ri na yona eka whale a ku ri leyi averiweke eka Hosi. Hi fanele ku lava ku vutisa kutani eka nsinya wihi wa nawu lowu Hosi yi vekiweke eku sunguleni hi mfanelo yoleyo. Nawu hi woxe se wu vekiwile. Kambe Plowdon u hi nyika xivangelo xa swona. Plowdon u ri, whale leyi khomiweke hi ndlela yoleyo i ya Hosi na Hosi ya Xisati, “hikwalaho ka vutshila bya yona lebyi tlakukeke.” Naswona hi vaxopaxopi lava twalaka swinene leswi swi tshame swi khomiwa tanihi njhekanjhekisano lowu khorwisaka etimhakeni to tano.

Kambe ha yini Hosi yi fanele yi va ni nhloko, kasi Hosi ya xisati yi fanele yi va ni ncila? Xivangelo xa sweswo, n’wina magqweta!

Eka tsalwa rakwe leri vulavulaka hi “Queen-Gold,” kumbe Queen-pinmoney, mutsari wa khale wa King’s Bench, William Prynne un’wana, u vulavula hi ndlela leyi: “N’wina ncila i wa Hosi ya Xisati, leswaku n’wina wodropo ya Hosi ya xisati mi ta phakeriwa n’wina marhambu ya whale.” Sweswi leswi swi tsariwile hi nkarhi lowu marhambu ya ntima ya limber ya Greenland kumbe Right whale a ya tirhisiwa ngopfu eka ti bodices ta vavasati. Kambe rhambu leri rin’we a ri le ncileni; swi le nhlokweni, leswi nga xihoxo lexi khomisaka gome eka gqweta ra vutlhari ro fana na Prynne. Kambe xana Hosi ya Xisati i mermaid, ku nyikiwa ncila? Nhlamuselo ya xifaniso yi nga ha tumbela laha.

Ku ni tinhlampfi timbirhi ta vuhosi leti endliweke hi ndlela yoleyo hi vatsari va nawu va Manghezi—whale ni sturgeon; havumbirhi bya yona i nhundzu ya vuhosi ehansi ka swipimelo swo karhi, naswona hi vito yi phakela rhavi ra vukhume ra mali leyi tolovelekeke ya harhi. A ndzi swi tivi leswaku ku na mutsari un’wana loyi a nga ringanyeta mhaka leyi; kambe hi ku ehleketa swi vonaka eka mina leswaku sturgeon yi fanele yi avanyisiwa hi ndlela leyi fanaka ni ya whale, Hosi yi amukela nhloko leyi tsindziyeleke swinene ni leyi olovaka leyi hlawulekeke eka nhlampfi yoleyo, leyi, loko yi tekiwa hi ndlela yo fanekisela, yi nga ha vaka yi sekeriwe hi ndlela yo hlekisa eka ku va yi ri leyi ehleketiwaka leswaku yi amukeleka. Nakona xisweswo ku vonaka ku ri na xivangelo eka swilo hinkwaswo, hambi ku ri eka nawu.





NDZIMA YA 91. Pequod Yi Hlangana Na Rose-Bud.

“A swi nga pfuni nchumu a ku ri ku rake for Ambergriese eka paunch ya Leviathan leyi, insufferable fetor a kaneta ku nga vutisi.” Nkulukumba T. Browne, V. E

A ku ri vhiki kumbe mambirhi endzhaku ka loko xivono xo hetelela xa ku hlotiwa ka ti- whale xi hlamuseriwe, naswona loko hi ri karhi hi famba hi xikepe hakatsongo-tsongo ehenhla ka lwandle leri nga ni vurhongo, leri nga ni mpfula, ra le xikarhi ka siku, leswaku tintiho to tala leti a ti ri exitulwini xa Pequod ti tikombe ti ri leti xalamukeke swinene ku tlula mimpatswa yinharhu ya mahlo lama nga ehenhla. Ku nun’hwela loku hlawulekeke ni loku nga tsakisiki ngopfu a ku nun’hweriwa elwandle.

Stubb u te: “Ndzi ta becha swo karhi sweswi leswaku kun’wana laha ku nga ni tin’wana ta ti- whale toleto leti nga ni swidzidzirisi leti hi ti rhurhumeleke siku rin’wana. A ndzi ehleketa leswaku va ta keel up ku nga ri khale.”

Sweswi, mimpfumawulo leyi a yi ri kona ka ha ri emahlweni yi rhelerile yi ya etlhelo; naswona kwalaho ekule a ku ri ni xikepe, lexi maseyila ya xona lama govekeke a ma kombisa leswaku muxaka wo karhi wa whale wu fanele wu ri etlhelo. Loko hi ri karhi hi rhelela hi tshinela, munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki u kombise mihlovo ya Xifurwa ku suka enhlohlorhini yakwe; naswona hi papa leri rhendzelekaka ra swinyenyana swa le lwandle swa vulture leswi a swi rhendzeleka, ni ku rhendzeleka, ni ku n’wi rhendzela, a swi ri erivaleni leswaku whale leyi nga etlhelo ka yona yi fanele yi ri leyi vaphasi va tinhlampfi va yi vitanaka blasted whale, hi leswaku, whale leyi feke yi nga karhatiwanga elwandle , naswona xisweswo ku papamala ntsumbu lowu nga fanelangiki. Ndzi rivele ku vula leswaku a ku kumiwile eka ambergris leyi, tipuleti to karhi to tiya, ta xirhendzevutani, ta marhambu, leti eku sunguleni Stubb a a ehleketa leswaku ti nga va ti button ta maburuku ya vatluti; kambe endzhaku ka sweswo swi kumekile leswaku a ku nga ri nchumu handle ka swiphemu swa marhambu lamatsongo ya squid lama omisiweke hi ndlela yoleyo.

Sweswi ku nga onhaki ka ambergris leyi yo nun’hwela swinene ku faneleke ku kumiwa embilwini ya ku bola ko tano; xana leswi a hi nchumu? Ehleketa hi rito rero ra Mukwetsimi Pawulo eka Vakorinto, mayelana na ku onhaka ni ku nga onhaki; kunene hi byariwile hi tingana, kambe hi pfuxiwile hi ku vangama. Naswona hi ku fanana tsundzuka xivuriso xexo xa Paracelsus mayelana na leswi hi swona leswi endlaka musk lowunene ngopfu. Nakambe u nga rivali mhaka leyi hlamarisaka ya leswaku eka swilo hinkwaswo leswi nga tsakisiki, mati ya Cologne, eka switeji swa wona swa masungulo swa vumaki, hi wona yo biha swinene.

I should like to conclude the chapter with the above appeal, but cannot, owing to my anxiety to repel a charge often made against whalemen, and which, in the estimation of some already biased minds, might be considered as indirectly substantiated by what has been said of the Frenchman's two whales. Elsewhere in this volume the slanderous aspersion has been disproved, that the vocation of whaling is throughout a slatternly, untidy business. But there is another thing to rebut. They hint that all whales always smell bad. Now how did this odious stigma originate?

I opine, that it is plainly traceable to the first arrival of the Greenland whaling ships in London, more than two centuries ago. Because those whalemen did not then, and do not now, try out their oil at sea as the Southern ships have always done; but cutting up the fresh blubber in small bits, thrust it through the bung holes of large casks, and carry it home in that manner; the shortness of the season in those Icy Seas, and the sudden and violent storms to which they are exposed, forbidding any other course. The consequence is, that upon breaking into the hold, and unloading one of these whale cemeteries, in the Greenland dock, a savor is given forth somewhat similar to that arising from excavating an old city grave-yard, for the foundations of a Lying-in Hospital.

I partly surmise also, that this wicked charge against whalers may be likewise imputed to the existence on the coast of Greenland, in former times, of a Dutch village called Schmerenburgh or Smeerenberg, which latter name is the one used by the learned Fogo Von Slack, in his great work on Smells, a text-book on that subject. As its name imports (smeer, fat; berg, to put up), this village was founded in order to afford a place for the blubber of the Dutch whale fleet to be tried out, without being taken home to Holland for that purpose. It was a collection of furnaces, fat-kettles, and oil sheds; and when the works were in full operation certainly gave forth no very pleasant savor. But all this is quite different with a South Sea Sperm Whaler; which in a voyage of four years perhaps, after completely filling her hold with oil, does not, perhaps, consume fifty days in the business of boiling out; and in the state that it is casked, the oil is nearly scentless. The truth is, that living or dead, if but decently treated, whales as a species are by no means creatures of ill odor; nor can whalemen be recognised, as the people of the middle ages affected to detect a Jew in the company, by the nose. Nor indeed can the whale possibly be otherwise than fragrant, when, as a general thing, he enjoys such high health; taking abundance of exercise; always out of doors; though, it is true, seldom in the open air. I say, that the motion of a Sperm Whale's flukes above water dispenses a perfume, as when a musk-scented lady rustles her dress in a warm parlor. What then shall I liken the Sperm Whale to for fragrance, considering his magnitude? Must it not be to that famous elephant, with jewelled tusks, and redolent with myrrh, which was led out of an Indian town to do honor to Alexander the Great?





CHAPTER 93. The Castaway.

It was but some few days after encountering the Frenchman, that a most significant event befell the most insignificant of the Pequod's crew; an event most lamentable; and which ended in providing the sometimes madly merry and predestinated craft with a living and ever accompanying prophecy of whatever shattered sequel might prove her own.

Now, in the whale ship, it is not every one that goes in the boats. Some few hands are reserved called ship-keepers, whose province it is to work the vessel while the boats are pursuing the whale. As a general thing, these ship-keepers are as hardy fellows as the men comprising the boats' crews. But if there happen to be an unduly slender, clumsy, or timorous wight in the ship, that wight is certain to be made a ship-keeper. It was so in the Pequod with the little negro Pippin by nick-name, Pip by abbreviation. Poor Pip! ye have heard of him before; ye must remember his tambourine on that dramatic midnight, so gloomy-jolly.

In outer aspect, Pip and Dough-Boy made a match, like a black pony and a white one, of equal developments, though of dissimilar colour, driven in one eccentric span. But while hapless Dough-Boy was by nature dull and torpid in his intellects, Pip, though over tender-hearted, was at bottom very bright, with that pleasant, genial, jolly brightness peculiar to his tribe; a tribe, which ever enjoy all holidays and festivities with finer, freer relish than any other race. For blacks, the year's calendar should show naught but three hundred and sixty-five Fourth of Julys and New Year's Days. Nor smile so, while I write that this little black was brilliant, for even blackness has its brilliancy; behold yon lustrous ebony, panelled in king's cabinets. But Pip loved life, and all life's peaceable securities; so that the panic-striking business in which he had somehow unaccountably become entrapped, had most sadly blurred his brightness; though, as ere long will be seen, what was thus temporarily subdued in him, in the end was destined to be luridly illumined by strange wild fires, that fictitiously showed him off to ten times the natural lustre with which in his native Tolland County in Connecticut, he had once enlivened many a fiddler's frolic on the green; and at melodious even-tide, with his gay ha-ha! had turned the round horizon into one star-belled tambourine. So, though in the clear air of day, suspended against a blue-veined neck, the pure-watered diamond drop will healthful glow; yet, when the cunning jeweller would show you the diamond in its most impressive lustre, he lays it against a gloomy ground, and then lights it up, not by the sun, but by some unnatural gases. Then come out those fiery effulgences, infernally superb; then the evil-blazing diamond, once the divinest symbol of the crystal skies, looks like some crown-jewel stolen from the King of Hell. But let us to the story.

It came to pass, that in the ambergris affair Stubb's after-oarsman chanced so to sprain his hand, as for a time to become quite maimed; and, temporarily, Pip was put into his place.

The first time Stubb lowered with him, Pip evinced much nervousness; but happily, for that time, escaped close contact with the whale; and therefore came off not altogether discreditably; though Stubb observing him, took care, afterwards, to exhort him to cherish his courageousness to the utmost, for he might often find it needful.

Now upon the second lowering, the boat paddled upon the whale; and as the fish received the darted iron, it gave its customary rap, which happened, in this instance, to be right under poor Pip's seat. The involuntary consternation of the moment caused him to leap, paddle in hand, out of the boat; and in such a way, that part of the slack whale line coming against his chest, he breasted it overboard with him, so as to become entangled in it, when at last plumping into the water. That instant the stricken whale started on a fierce run, the line swiftly straightened; and presto! poor Pip came all foaming up to the chocks of the boat, remorselessly dragged there by the line, which had taken several turns around his chest and neck.

Tashtego stood in the bows. He was full of the fire of the hunt. He hated Pip for a poltroon. Snatching the boat-knife from its sheath, he suspended its sharp edge over the line, and turning towards Stubb, exclaimed interrogatively, “Cut?” Meantime Pip's blue, choked face plainly looked, Do, for God's sake! All passed in a flash. In less than half a minute, this entire thing happened.

“Damn him, cut!” roared Stubb; and so the whale was lost and Pip was saved.

So soon as he recovered himself, the poor little negro was assailed by yells and execrations from the crew. Tranquilly permitting these irregular cursings to evaporate, Stubb then in a plain, business-like, but still half humorous manner, cursed Pip officially; and that done, unofficially gave him much wholesome advice. The substance was, Never jump from a boat, Pip, except—but all the rest was indefinite, as the soundest advice ever is. Now, in general, Stick to the boat , is your true motto in whaling; but cases will sometimes happen when Leap from the boat , is still better. Moreover, as if perceiving at last that if he should give undiluted conscientious advice to Pip, he would be leaving him too wide a margin to jump in for the future; Stubb suddenly dropped all advice, and concluded with a peremptory command, “Stick to the boat, Pip, or by the Lord, I won't pick you up if you jump; mind that. We can't afford to lose whales by the likes of you; a whale would sell for thirty times what you would, Pip, in Alabama. Bear that in mind, and don't jump any more.” Hereby perhaps Stubb indirectly hinted, that though man loved his fellow, yet man is a money-making animal, which propensity too often interferes with his benevolence.

But we are all in the hands of the Gods; and Pip jumped again. It was under very similar circumstances to the first performance; but this time he did not breast out the line; and hence, when the whale started to run, Pip was left behind on the sea, like a hurried traveller's trunk. Alas! Stubb was but too true to his word. It was a beautiful, bounteous, blue day; the spangled sea calm and cool, and flatly stretching away, all round, to the horizon, like gold-beater's skin hammered out to the extremest. Bobbing up and down in that sea, Pip's ebon head showed like a head of cloves. No boat-knife was lifted when he fell so rapidly astern. Stubb's inexorable back was turned upon him; and the whale was winged. In three minutes, a whole mile of shoreless ocean was between Pip and Stubb. Out from the centre of the sea, poor Pip turned his crisp, curling, black head to the sun, another lonely castaway, though the loftiest and the brightest.

Now, in calm weather, to swim in the open ocean is as easy to the practised swimmer as to ride in a spring-carriage ashore. But the awful lonesomeness is intolerable. The intense concentration of self in the middle of such a heartless immensity, my God! who can tell it? Mark, how when sailors in a dead calm bathe in the open sea—mark how closely they hug their ship and only coast along her sides.

But had Stubb really abandoned the poor little negro to his fate? No; he did not mean to, at least. Because there were two boats in his wake, and he supposed, no doubt, that they would of course come up to Pip very quickly, and pick him up; though, indeed, such considerations towards oarsmen jeopardized through their own timidity, is not always manifested by the hunters in all similar instances; and such instances not unfrequently occur; almost invariably in the fishery, a coward, so called, is marked with the same ruthless detestation peculiar to military navies and armies.

But it so happened, that those boats, without seeing Pip, suddenly spying whales close to them on one side, turned, and gave chase; and Stubb's boat was now so far away, and he and all his crew so intent upon his fish, that Pip's ringed horizon began to expand around him miserably. By the merest chance the ship itself at last rescued him; but from that hour the little negro went about the deck an idiot; such, at least, they said he was. The sea had jeeringly kept his finite body up, but drowned the infinite of his soul. Not drowned entirely, though. Rather carried down alive to wondrous depths, where strange shapes of the unwarped primal world glided to and fro before his passive eyes; and the miser-merman, Wisdom, revealed his hoarded heaps; and among the joyous, heartless, ever-juvenile eternities, Pip saw the multitudinous, God-omnipresent, coral insects, that out of the firmament of waters heaved the colossal orbs. He saw God's foot upon the treadle of the loom, and spoke it; and therefore his shipmates called him mad. So man's insanity is heaven's sense; and wandering from all mortal reason, man comes at last to that celestial thought, which, to reason, is absurd and frantic; and weal or woe, feels then uncompromised, indifferent as his God.

For the rest, blame not Stubb too hardly. The thing is common in that fishery; and in the sequel of the narrative, it will then be seen what like abandonment befell myself.
