NDZIMA YA 32. Cetology.

Se hi rhumeriwe hi xivindzi ehenhla ka vuenti; kambe ku nga ri khale hi ta lahleka eka vukulu bya yona lebyi nga riki na ribuwa, lebyi nga riki na ribuwa. Ere sweswo swi humelela; ere xikepe xa Pequod lexi nga ni nhova xi rhendzeleka xi ri swin’we ni swikepe leswi nga ni barnacled swa leviathan; eku sunguleni i swinene ku tekela enhlokweni mhaka leyi lavaka ku va leyi nga lavekiki eka ku twisisa loku tlangelaka loku heleleke ka tinhlavutelo to hlawuleka swinene ta leviathanic ni swikombo swa mixaka hinkwayo leswi nga ta landzela.

I nkombiso wun’wana lowu hlelekeke wa whale eka tinxaka ta yona to anama, lowu sweswi ndzi nga ta fain ku wu veka emahlweni ka n’wina. Hambi swi ri tano xana a hi ntirho wo olova. Ku hlawuriwa ka swiaki swa mpfilumpfilu, a ku na nchumu lowu nga ehansi ka wona laha wu tsariweke. Yingisela leswi valawuri lavanene ni va sweswinyana va swi vekeke.

“Ku hava rhavi ra Zoology leri katsekaka ngopfu ku fana na leri nga na nhlokomhaka leyi nge Cetology,” ku vula Captain Scoresby, AD 1820.

“A hi xikongomelo xa mina, loko a swi ri ematimbeni ya mina, ku nghena eka vulavisisi mayelana ni ndlela ya ntiyiso yo avanyisa ti- cetacea hi mintlawa ni mindyangu. * * * Ku pfilunganyeka loku heleleke ku kona exikarhi ka van’wamatimu va xiharhi lexi” (sperm whale), ku vula Dokodela wa Vuhandzuri Beale, AD 1839.

“Ku nga faneleki ku endla ndzavisiso wa hina ematini lama nga pimekiki.” “Xifunengeto lexi nga nghenekiki lexi funengetaka vutivi bya hina bya ti- cetacea.” “Nsimu leyi hangalakeke hi mitwa.” “Swikombiso leswi hinkwaswo leswi nga helelangiki kambe swi tirha ku xanisa hina vativi va ntumbuluko.”

Xisweswo vulavula hi whale, Cuvier lonkulu, na John Hunter, na Lesson, mavoni wolawo ya ntivo-swiharhi ni anatomy. Hambi swi ri tano, hambileswi vutivi bya xiviri byi nga byintsongo, kambe tibuku ti tele; naswona hi ndlela yoleyo hi mpimo wo karhi lowutsongo, hi cetology, kumbe sayense ya ti whale. Vo tala i vavanuna, lavatsongo ni lavakulu, va khale ni lavantshwa, vatirhi va misava ni vatluti, lava hi ku angarhela kumbe hi switsongo, va tsaleke hi whale. Tsutsuma eka swi nga ri swingani:—Vatsari Va Bibele; Aristotle; Pliny; Aldrovandi na yena; Nkulukumba Thomas Browne; Gesner; Ray; Linnæus, loyi a a ri; Rondeletius wa yena; Willoughby, loyi a nga; Rihlaza; Artedi; Sibbald; Brisson; Marten; Lacépède ya le xihundleni; Bonneterre, loyi a nga ni vuxiyaxiya; Desmarest; Baron Cuvier; Frederick Cuvier, loyi a a ri na yena; John Hunter, loyi a nga ni vuxiyaxiya; Owen; Swikoweto hi; Beale; Bennett, loyi a a ri; J. Ross Browne loyi a a ri; Mutsari wa buku leyi nge Miriam Coffin; Olmstead, naswona; na Mufundhisi T. Cheever. Kambe leswaku i xikongomelo xihi xo hetelela xo andlala lexi hinkwavo va xi tsaleke, swiphemu leswi tshahiweke laha henhla swi ta kombisa.

Eka mavito lama nga eka nxaxamelo lowu wa vatsari va ti- whale, i lava landzeleke Owen ntsena lava tshameke va vona ti- whale leti hanyaka; naswona kambe un’wana wa vona a a ri muchayeri wa xiviri wa xiphurofexinali wa harpooneer na whaleman. Ndzi vula Kaputeni Scoresby. Eka mhaka leyi hambaneke ya Greenland kumbe right-whale, hi yena mulawuri lonene ngopfu loyi a nga kona. Kambe Scoresby a a nga tivi nchumu naswona a nga vuli nchumu hi sperm whale leyikulu, loko yi pimanisiwa na yona Greenland whale yi lava ku va yi nga fanelanga ku boxiwa. Naswona laha ku vuriweke, leswaku Greenland whale i mudlayi exiluvelweni xa malwandle. Hambi ku ri hi ndlela yihi na yihi a hi lonkulu eka ti whale. Hambi swiritano, hikokwalaho ka ku rhangisa nkarhi woleha ka swihehlo swa yena, na ku pfumala vutivi lokukulu loku, kufikela kwalomu ka makume nkombo wa malembe endzhaku, ku vekisaka sperm-whale leyi a yi ri ya xitori hi nkarhi wolowo kumbe leyi a yi nga tiviwi hi ku helela, naswona ku pfumala vutivi loku ku fikela namuntlha ka ha fumaka eka hinkwaswo handle ka swin’wana swi nga ri swingani swa ku tlhelela endzhaku ka sayense na swikepe swa tinhlampfi ta whale; ku tekiwa loku ku hetisekile hi tindlela hinkwato. Ku kombetela eka kwalomu ka hinkwaswo leswi kombetelaka leviathanic eka vatlhokovetseri lavakulu va masiku lama hundzeke, swi ta ku enerisa leswaku Greenland whale, leyi nga riki na muphikizani un’we, eka vona a yi ri hosi ya malwandle. Kambe eku heteleleni nkarhi wu fikile wa ku huwelela lokuntshwa. Lexi i Xihambano xa Charing; yingisani! vanhu lavanene hinkwavo,—whale ya le Greenland yi susiwile exiluvelweni,—sperm whale leyikulu sweswi ya fuma!

Ku ni tibuku timbirhi ntsena leti nga kona leti nikatsongo ti tiendlaka onge ti veka sperm whale leyi hanyaka emahlweni ka wena, naswona hi nkarhi lowu fanaka, hi mpimo wa le kule swinene ti humelela eku ringeteni. Tibuku teto i ta Beale na Bennett; havumbirhi bya vona hi nkarhi wa vona a va ri madokodela ya vuhandzuri eka swikepe swa tinhlampfi swa le Lwandle ra Dzonga swa le Nghilandhi, naswona havumbirhi bya vona a va ri vavanuna lava kongomeke ni lava tshembekaka. Mhaka yo sungula leyi khumbaka sperm whale leyi kumekaka eka tivholumo ta yona hi ku kongoma i yitsongo; kambe ku fikela laha yi yaka kona, i ya khwalithi leyinene ngopfu, hambileswi ngopfu-ngopfu yi pfaleriweke eka nhlamuselo ya sayense. Kambe ku fikela sweswi, sperm whale, ya sayense kumbe ya vutlhokovetseri, a yi hanyi yi nga helelanga eka tibuku tihi na tihi. Ehenhla swinene ka ti- whale tin’wana hinkwato leti hlotiweke, ya yena i vutomi lebyi nga tsariwangiki.

Sweswi tinxaka to hambana ta ti whale ti lava muxaka wo karhi wa ku hlawuriwa loku heleleke loku dumeke, loko ku ri ntsena loku olovaka ka xivumbeko xa sweswi, endzhaku ka sweswo ku ta tatiwa eka tindzawulo ta tona hinkwato hi vatirhi lava landzelaka. Loko ku nga ri na wanuna wo antswa loyi a yaka emahlweni ku teka mhaka leyi hi voko, ndzi nyikela matshalatshala ya mina yo biha. A ndzi tshembisi nchumu lowu heleleke; hikuva nchumu wun’wana ni wun’wana wa munhu lowu ku vuriwaka leswaku wu helerile, wu fanele hikwalaho ka xivangelo xexo wu va ni xihoxo hi ndlela leyi nga hoxeke. A ndzi nge endli onge ndzi hlamusela hi ndlela leyitsongo ya anatomical ya tinxaka to hambana-hambana, kumbe—endhawini leyi hi xiringaniso—hi nhlamuselo yihi na yihi yo tala. Xikongomelo xa mina laha i ku projekta ntsena tsalwa ra ku hleriwa ka cetology. Hi mina mutivi wa swa masungulo, ku nga ri muaki.

Kambe i ntirho wo tika; ku hava muhlawuri wa mapapila wo tolovelekile eka Hofisi ya Poso loyi a ringanaka na yona. Ku khoma-khoma ehansi ehansi ka lwandle endzhaku ka vona; ku va na mavoko ya munhu exikarhi ka masungulo lama nga vulavuriwiki, timbanga, na xirhendzevutani swinene xa misava; leswi i nchumu lowu chavisaka. Ndzi yini leswaku ndzi fanele ndzi essay ku khoma nomo wa leviathan leyi! Ku hlekula loku chavisaka ka Yobo ku nga ha ndzi chavisa swinene. Xana u ta (levhiyathani) a endla ntwanano na wena? Waswivo, ntshembo wa yena i wa hava! Kambe ndzi hlambele etilayiburari ni ku famba hi xikepe emalwandle; Ndzi ve ni ku endla hi ti- whale leti nga ni mavoko lawa lama vonakaka; Ndzi tiyimisele; naswona ndzi ta ringeta. Kuna swin’wana swosungula leswi faneleke ku lulamisiwa.

Xo sungula: Xiyimo lexi nga tiyisekiki, lexi nga tshamisekangiki xa sayense leyi ya Cetology xi le ka vestibule hi yoxe leyi tiyisisiweke hi mhaka, leswaku eka tindhawu tin’wana swa ha tshama swi ri mhaka leyi nga kanetiwiki loko whale yi ri nhlampfi. Eka System of Nature, AD 1776, Linnæus u tivisa, “Hi ndlela leyi ndzi hambanisa ti whale eka tinhlampfi.” Kambe hi ku tiva ka mina, ndza swi tiva leswaku ku fikela hi lembe ra 1850, tishaka ni ti- shad, ti- alewive ni ti- herring, leti lwisanaka ni xileriso lexi kongomeke xa Linnæus, a ta ha kumiwa ti ri karhi ti avanyisa vulawuri bya malwandle lama fanaka ni ya Leviathan.

Swisekelo leswi Linnæus a a ta titivala ha swona a swi hlongola ti- whale ematini, u vula hi ndlela leyi landzelaka: “Hikwalaho ka mbilu ya tona leyi kufumelaka ya bilocular, mahahu ya tona, swirhendzevutana swa tona leswi famba-fambaka, tindleve ta tona leti nga ni swirhendzevutana, penem intrantem feminam mammis lactantem,” naswona eku heteleleni, . “ex lege naturæ hi nawu.” Leswi hinkwaswo ndzi swi rhumele eka vanghana va mina Simeon Macey na Charley Coffin, va le Nantucket, havambirhi va ri messmates ya mina eka riendzo ro karhi, naswona va hlanganile hi vonelo ra leswaku swivangelo leswi vekiweke a swi nga enelanga nikatsongo. Charley hi ndlela yo nyenyetsa u ringanyete leswaku va humbug.

Swi tiviwe leswaku, ndzi tshika ku kanetana hinkwako, ndzi teka misava leyinene ya khale ya leswaku whale i nhlampfi, kutani ndzi vitana Yonasi wo kwetsima leswaku a ndzi seketela. Mhaka leyi ya xisekelo yi tshamisekile, yinhla leyi landzelaka hileswaku, hi ndlela yihi ya le ndzeni leyi whale yi hambanaka na tinhlampfi tin’wana. Laha henhla, Linnæus u ku nyike swilo sweswo. Kambe hi ku komisa, hi leswi: mahahu ni ngati yo kufumela; kasi, tinhlampfi tin’wana hinkwato a ti na mahahu naswona ti ni ngati yo titimela.

Lexi landzelaka: hi ta yi hlamusela njhani whale, hi swilo swa yona swa le handle leswi nga erivaleni, leswaku hi ndlela leyi vonakaka hi yi lebula eka nkarhi hinkwawo lowu taka? Kutani, ku komisa, whale i nhlampfi leyi rhurhumelaka leyi nga ni ncila lowu nga etlhelo . Kwalaho u na yena. Hambi swi ri tano, nhlamuselo yoleyo i vuyelo bya ku anakanyisisa loku andlariweke. Walrus yi rhurhumela ku fana ni whale, kambe walrus a hi nhlampfi, hikuva yi hanya ematini ni le misaveni. Kambe rito ro hetelela ra nhlamuselo ra ha khorwisa swinene, tanihi leswi ri hlanganisiweke ni ro sungula. Kwalomu ka un’wana na un’wana u fanele a swi xiyile leswaku tihlampfi hinkwato leti tolovelekeke eka vanhu va misava a ti na xiphepherhele, kambe ti na ncila lowu yimisiweke, kumbe lowu tlakukeke na le hansi. Kasi, exikarhi ka tinhlampfi leti khulukaka ncila, hambi leswi wu nga ha vaka ni xivumbeko lexi fanaka, hi ku nga cinci wu teka xiyimo lexi nga etlhelo.

Hi nhlamuselo leyi nga laha henhla ya leswi whale yi nga swona, a ndzi swi hlongoli nikatsongo eka vumakwerhu bya leviathanic xivumbiwa xihi na xihi xa le lwandle lexi ku fikela sweswi xi tiviweke na whale hi va-Nantucket lava nga ni vutivi lebyinene; naswona, hi hala tlhelo, ku hlanganisa na yona nhlampfi yihi na yihi leyi ku fikela sweswi yi tekiwaka hi vulawuri tanihi leyi humaka ematikweni mambe.* Hikwalaho, tinhlampfi hinkwato letitsongo, leti khulukaka ni leti nga ni ncila lowu nga etlhelo ti fanele ti katsiwa eka pulani leyi ya le hansi ya Cetology. Kutani sweswi, ku ta ku avana lokukulu ka muhaxi hinkwawo wa tinhlampfi ta whale.

*Ndza switiva leswaku kufikela sweswi, tihlampfi letinga style Lamatins na Dugongs (Pig-fish na Sow-fish ta Coffins ya Nantucket) ti katsiwa hi vativi votala va ntumbuluko exikarhi ka ti whale. Kambe tani hileswi tinhlampfi leti ta tinguluve tinga sete leyi nga na huwa, leyi nyenyetsaka, ngopfu ngopfu yi tumbele emilon’wini ya milambu, naswona yidya byanyi lebyi tsakamaka, naswona ngopfu ngopfu tani hileswi tinga khuluki, ndzi kaneta switifiketi swa tona tani hi ti whale; naswona va va nyikile mapasi ya vona leswaku va tshika Mfumo wa Cetology.

Xo sungula: Hi ku ya hi vukulu ndzi avanyisa ti- whale hi TIBUKU tinharhu ta nkoka (ti avanyisiwa hi TINDLELA), naswona leti ti ta ti twisisa hinkwato, letitsongo na letikulu.

TIFOLIOS. Exikarhi ka leswi ndzi katsa tindzima leti landzelaka:—I. Xiharhi xa Sperm Whale ; II. xiharhi xa Right Whale ; III. xiharhi xa Fin-Back Whale ; IV. xiharhi lexi vuriwaka Hump-backed Whale ; V. xiharhi xa Razor Back Whale ; VI. xiharhi lexi vuriwaka Sulphur Bottom Whale .

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ), NDZIMA YA I. ( Sperm Whale ).—Whale leyi, exikarhi ka Manghezi ya khale leyi tiviwaka hi ndlela leyi nga twisisekiki tanihi Trumpa whale, na Physeter whale, na Anvil Headed whale, i Cachalot ya sweswi ya Mafurwa, na Pottsfich ya Majarimani, na Macrocephalus ya Marito yo Leha. Handle ko kanakana, hi yena muaki lonkulu ku tlula hinkwavo emisaveni; leyi chavisaka swinene eka ti whale hinkwato leti u nga hlanganaka na tona; loyi a nga ni vukulukumba swinene hi tlhelo; naswona xo hetelela, ku fikela sweswi i ya nkoka swinene eka mabindzu; yena tanihi xivumbiwa xin’we ntsena lexi nchumu wolowo wa nkoka, ku nga spermaceti, wu kumiwaka eka xona. Swihlawulekisi swa yena hinkwaswo, etindhawini tin’wana to tala, swi ta kurisiwa. Ngopfu-ngopfu i vito ra yena leri sweswi ndzi faneleke ndzi ri endla. Loko swi langutiwa hi tlhelo ra ntivo-ririmi, a swi twali. Malembe nyana lawa yanga hundza, loko Sperm whale ayi lava kuva yinga tiviwi hiku hetiseka hi vumunhu bya yona lebyi faneleke, na loko mafurha ya yona ya kumiwile ntsena hi xihoxo eka nhlampfi leyi pfaleriweke; emasikwini wolawo, swi nga vonaka onge, hi vanhu vo tala a ku vuriwa leswaku spermaceti yi huma eka xivumbiwa lexi fanaka ni lexi hi nkarhi wolowo a xi tiviwa eNghilandhi tanihi Greenland kumbe Right Whale. A ku ri mianakanyo nakambe, leswaku spermaceti leyi fanaka a ku ri ku hlekisa koloko loku hatlisisaka ka Greenland Whale loku xitwari xo sungula xa rito leri xi swi kombisaka hi ku kongoma. Hi minkarhi yoleyo, nakambe, spermaceti a yi kayivela swinene, a yi nga tirhisiwi ku vonakala, kambe ntsena tanihi mafurha ni murhi. A yi fanele ku va kona ntsena eka vaxavisi va mirhi tani hi leswi masiku lawa u xavaka ounce ya rhubarb. Loko, hilaha ndzi ehleketaka hakona, hi ku famba ka nkarhi, muxaka wa ntiyiso wa spermaceti wu tiveka, vito ra yona ro sungula a ra ha hlayisiwile hi vaxavisi; handle ko kanakana ku ndlandlamuxa nkoka wa yona hi mianakanyo leyi nga ya nkoka hi ndlela leyi hlamarisaka ya ku kayivela ka yona. Naswona hikwalaho vito leri eku heteleleni ri fanele ri nyikiwile whale leyi spermaceti leyi yi humaka eka yona hakunene.

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ), NDZIMA YA II. ( Right Whale ).—Hi ndlela yin’wana leyi hi yona leyi xiximekaka swinene eka ti- leviathan, hi yona yo sungula ku hlotiwa nkarhi na nkarhi hi munhu. Yi humesa xihloko lexi hi ntolovelo xi tiviwaka tanihi whalebone kumbe baleen; ni oyili leyi tiviwaka hi ku hlawuleka tanihi “mafurha ya whale,” ku nga nchumu wa xiyimo xa le hansi eka mabindzu. Exikarhi ka vaphasi va tinhlampfi, u hlawuriwa handle ko hlawula hi swithopo hinkwaswo leswi landzelaka: The Whale; xiharhi lexi vuriwaka Greenland Whale; xiharhi lexi vuriwaka Black Whale; xiharhi lexi vuriwaka Great Whale; xiharhi xa Ntiyiso xa Whale; xiharhi xa Right Whale. Ku ni ku nga twisiseki lokukulu malunghana ni vutivi bya muxaka lowu xisweswo wu khuvuriweke hi vunyingi. Kutani i yini whale, leyi ndzi yi katsaka eka muxaka wa vumbirhi wa Folios ya mina? I Mysticetus Lonkulu wa vativi va ntumbuluko va Manghezi; Greenland Whale ya vahlaseri va whale va Manghezi; Baleine Ordinaire wa vahlaseri va ti- whale va le Furwa; nhlampfi ya Growlands Walfish ya le Sweden. I whale leyi ku tlula malembe xidzana mambirhi lama hundzeke yi hlotiweke hi Madachi na Manghezi emalwandle ya Arctic; i whale leyi vaphasi va tihlampfi va le Amerika va nga khale va yi hlongorisa elwandle ra Indiya, eBrazil Banks, eNor’ West Coast, ni le tindhawini tin’wana to hambana-hambana ta misava, leti hlawuriweke hi vona Right Whale Cruising Grounds.

Van’wana va endla onge va vona ku hambana exikarhi ka Greenland whale ya Manghezi ni right whale ya Maamerika. Kambe ti pfumelelana kahle eka swivumbeko swa tona hinkwaswo leswikulu; naswona a ku si nyikeriwa mhaka yin’we leyi tiyeke leyi ku nga sekeriwaka ku hambana lokukulu eka yona. Hi ku avanyisa loku nga heriki loku sekeriweke eka ku hambana loku nga tiyisekiki swinene, laha tindzawulo tin’wana ta matimu ya ntumbuluko ti vaka leti rharhanganeke hi ndlela leyi hlongorisaka. Whale ya xinene yi ta tshunguriwa kun’wana hi ku leha ko karhi, hi ku kombetela eka ku paluxa sperm whale.

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ), NDZIMA YA III. ( Fin-Back ).—Ehansi ka nhloko leyi ndzi hlayela xiharhi xa nhova lexi, hi mavito yo hambana-hambana ya Fin-Back, Tall-Spout na Long-John, xi voniweke kwalomu ka elwandle rin’wana ni rin’wana naswona hi ntolovelo i whale leyi jet ya yona ya le kule yi nga hakanyingi swi hlamuseriwa hi vakhandziyi lava tsemakanyaka lwandle ra Atlantic, eka swiporo swa tiphakiti swa le New York. Eka ku leha loku a ku fikelelaka, naswona eka baleen ya yena, Fin-back yifana na right whale, kambe yina portly girth leyintsongo, na muhlovo wo vevuka, yi tshinelela olive. Milomu ya yena leyikulu yi humesa xivumbeko xo fana na khebulu, lexi vumbiwaka hi swiphemu leswi soholotiweke, leswi rhetaka swa swirhumbana leswikulu. Xihlawulekisi xa yena lexikulu, ku nga fin, leyi a tekeke vito ra yena eka yona, hakanyingi i nchumu lowu vonakaka. Xiphepherhele lexi xi lehe kwalomu ka timitara tinharhu kumbe mune, xi kula xi yimile ku suka eka xiphemu xa le ndzhaku xa le ndzhaku, xi ri ni xivumbeko xa angular, naswona xi ni makumu lama tlhavaka swinene lama nga ni tinhla. Hambiloko ku nga vonaki xiphemu xin’wana lexitsongo xa xivumbiwa lexi, xiphepherhele lexi lexi nga xoxe, minkarhi yin’wana, xi ta voniwa xi huma erivaleni xi huma ehenhla. Loko lwandle ri rhulile hi xiringaniso, naswona ri funghiwile nyana hi swirhendzevutani, naswona fin leyi yofana na gnomon yi yima yi hoxa ndzhuti eka ndzhawu leyi rhurhumeleke, swinga endleka kahle leswaku xirhendzevutani xa mati lexi xi rhendzeleke xifana nyana na dial, na xitayili xa xona na milayeni ya tiawara leyi nga ni magandlati leyi vatliweke eka yona. Eka Ahaz-dial yoleyo ndzhuti hakanyingi wu tlhelela endzhaku. Fin-Back a yi na vuxaka bya vanhu. U vonaka a ri muvengi wa ti whale, tanihi leswi vavanuna van’wana va vengaka vanhu. Tingana swinene; minkarhi hinkwayo ku famba u ri wexe; ku tlakuka swi nga languteriwanga ku ya ehenhla ematini ya le kule swinene ni lama nga ni gome swinene; xihaha-mpfhuka xa yena xo kongoma ni xin’we lexi tlakukeke lexi tlakukeke ku fana ni fumu ro leha ra misanthropic ehenhla ka rivala leri nga riki na nchumu; nyiko ya matimba yo hlamarisa ni rivilo ro hlambela, lerova a kaneta ku hlongorisa hinkwako ka sweswi loku humaka eka munhu; leviathan leyi yi vonaka yi ri Kayini la hlongoriweke ni la nga hluriwiki wa rixaka rakwe, a rhwale mfungho wa yena xitayili xexo enhlaneni wakwe. Kusuka eka kuva na baleen enon’wini wa yena, Fin-Back minkarhi yin’wana yi katsiwa na whale ya xinene, exikarhi ka muxaka wa theoretic lowu vitaniwaka Whalebone whale , hi leswaku, whale leyingana baleen. Eka leti ti vuriwaka Whalebone whale, a ku ta vonaka ku ri ni tinxaka to hlayanyana, leti vunyingi bya tona, hambiswiritano, ti nga tiviwiki ngopfu. Ti- whale leti nga ni tintiho to anama ni tinhlampfi leti nga ni nomu; ti- whale leti nga ni tinhloko ta pike; ti- whale leti hlengeletiweke; under-jawed whale na rostrated whale, i mavito ya vaphasi va tinhlampfi eka tinxaka ti nga ri tingani.

Malunghana ni xithopo lexi xa “Whalebone whale,” i swa nkoka swinene ku boxa, leswaku hambiswiritano mavito yo tano ma nga olova eku oloviseni ka ku kombetela eka muxaka wo karhi wa tinhlampfi, hambiswiritano i swa hava ku ringeta ku hlawula loku nga erivaleni ka Leviathan, . yi simekiwile ehenhla ka baleen ya yena, kumbe hump, kumbe fin, kumbe meno; ku nga khathariseki leswaku swiphemu sweswo leswi funghiweke kumbe swivumbeko swi le rivaleni swinene swi vonaka swi lulamisiwe ku antswa leswaku swi kota ku fikelela xisekelo xa fambiselo ra nkarhi na nkarhi ra Cetology ku tlula ku hambana kun’wana ni kun’wana ka miri loku hambanisiweke, loku whale, hi tinxaka ta yona, yi ku nyikelaka. Kutani ke, njhani? Baleen, hump, back-fin na meno; leswi i swilo leswi swihlawulekisi swa swona swi hangalakeke hi ndlela leyi nga hlawuriki exikarhi ka mixaka hinkwayo ya ti- whale, handle ko tekela enhlokweni leswaku i yini lexi nga vaka muxaka wa xivumbeko xa tona eka swin’wana ni swa nkoka swinene. Xisweswo, sperm whale na humpbacked whale, yin’wana na yin’wana yi na hump; kambe kwalaho ku fana ka kona ku hela. Kutani, yona humpbacked whale leyi na Greenland whale, yin’wana na yin’wana ya tona yina baleen; kambe kwalaho nakambe ku fana ka kona ku hela. Naswona swi tano ni hi swiphemu leswin’wana leswi boxiweke laha henhla. Eka mixaka yo hambana-hambana ya ti- whale, ti vumba ku hlangana ko tano loku nga tshamisekangiki; kumbe, eka mhaka ya un’wana na un’wana wa vona loyi a hambanisiweke, ku tihambanisa koloko loku nga riki ka nkarhi na nkarhi; tanihi ku kaneta hi ku helela maendlelo hinkwawo yo angarhela lama vumbiweke eka xisekelo xo tano. Eka ribye leri un’wana ni un’wana wa vativi va ntumbuluko wa ti- whale u hambanile.

Kambe swi nga ha endleka ku ehleketiwa leswaku, eka swiphemu swa le ndzeni swa whale, eka anatomy ya yona—laha, hi xiringaniso, hi ta kota ku ba ku hlawuriwa loku faneleke. E-e; hi xikombiso, hi xihi nchumu lowu nga kona eka anatomy ya Greenland whale lowu hlamarisaka ku tlula baleen ya yona? Hambi swiritano hi vonile leswaku hi baleen ya yena aswi koteki ku hlawula kahle Greenland whale. Naswona loko u rhelela emarhumbyini ya ti- leviathan to hambana-hambana, hikokwalaho ka yini kwalaho u nga kumi ku hambana ka xiphemu xa makume ntlhanu tanihi lexi kumekaka eka muendli wa maendlelo ku fana na sweswo swa le handle leswi se swi hlayiweke. Kutani ku sala yini? a ku na nchumu handle ko khoma ti- whale hi miri, hi vholumo ya tona hinkwayo ya ntshunxeko, ivi hi xivindzi ti ti hlela hi ndlela yoleyo. Naswona leyi i endlelo ra Bibliographical leri amukeriweke laha; naswona hi yona ntsena leyi nga humelelaka, hikuva yona ntsena ya koteka. Ku ya emahlweni.

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ) NDZIMA YA IV. ( Hump Back ).—Hakanyingi whale leyi yi voniwa eribuweni ra le n’walungwini wa Amerika. U tshame a khomiwa kwalaho, naswona u kokeriwe eribuweni. U na phasela lerikulu eka yena ku fana na muxavisi; kumbe u nga ha n’wi vitana Elephant and Castle whale. Ku nga khathariseki leswaku swi njhani, vito leri dumeke ra yena a ri n’wi hambanisi hi laha ku eneleke, tanihi leswi sperm whale na yona yi nga ni xirhendzevutana hambileswi xi nga xitsongo. Mafurha ya yena a hi ya nkoka ngopfu. U na baleen. I gamesome swinene naswona ari na mbilu yo olova eka ti whale hinkwato, a endla gay foam yotala na mati yo basa hiku angarhela kutlula tin’wana ta tona.

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ), NDZIMA YA V. ( Razor Back ).—Hi whale leyi a ku tiviwi swo tala handle ka vito ra yona. Ndzi n’wi vonile ekule swinene na Cape Horn. Hi muxaka wa ku wisa, u balekela vahloti ni vativi va filosofi. Hambileswi a nga riki na xigangu, a nga si tshama a kombisa xiphemu xa yena handle ka nkolo wakwe, lowu tlakukeke hi xirhendzevutana xo leha xo kariha. N’wi tshikeni a famba. A ndzi tivi switsongo swo tala hi yena, naswona ku nga ri na munhu un’wana.

BUKU YA I. ( Folio ), NDZIMA YA VI. ( Sulphur Bottom ).—Munhu un’wana la nga le ku wiseni, la nga ni khwiri ra xibamu, handle ko kanakana u kume hi ku khwaxa tithayile ta Tartarian eka tin’wana ta ku nwela ka yena loku dzikeke. A nga tali ku voniwa; hi xiringaniso a ndzi si tshama ndzi n’wi vona handle ka malwandle ya le kule ya le dzongeni, ivi minkarhi hinkwayo a va ekule swinene lerova ndzi nga dyondza hi xikandza xa yena. A nga si tshama a hlongoriwa; a a ta baleka a ri ni tintambhu leti fambaka hi tintambhu. Prodigies are told of him. Adieu, Sulphur Bottom! I can say nothing more that is true of ye, nor can the oldest Nantucketer.

Thus ends BOOK I. ( Folio ), and now begins BOOK II. ( Octavo ).

OCTAVOES.*—These embrace the whales of middling magnitude, among which present may be numbered:—I., the Grampus ; II., the Black Fish ; III., the Narwhale ; IV., the Thrasher ; V., the Killer .

*Why this book of whales is not denominated the Quarto is very plain. Because, while the whales of this order, though smaller than those of the former order, nevertheless retain a proportionate likeness to them in figure, yet the bookbinder's Quarto volume in its dimensioned form does not preserve the shape of the Folio volume, but the Octavo volume does.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER I. ( Grampus ).—Though this fish, whose loud sonorous breathing, or rather blowing, has furnished a proverb to landsmen, is so well known a denizen of the deep, yet is he not popularly classed among whales. But possessing all the grand distinctive features of the leviathan, most naturalists have recognised him for one. He is of moderate octavo size, varying from fifteen to twenty-five feet in length, and of corresponding dimensions round the waist. He swims in herds; he is never regularly hunted, though his oil is considerable in quantity, and pretty good for light. By some fishermen his approach is regarded as premonitory of the advance of the great sperm whale.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER II. ( Black Fish ).—I give the popular fishermen's names for all these fish, for generally they are the best. Where any name happens to be vague or inexpressive, I shall say so, and suggest another. I do so now, touching the Black Fish, so-called, because blackness is the rule among almost all whales. So, call him the Hyena Whale, if you please. His voracity is well known, and from the circumstance that the inner angles of his lips are curved upwards, he carries an everlasting Mephistophelean grin on his face. This whale averages some sixteen or eighteen feet in length. He is found in almost all latitudes. He has a peculiar way of showing his dorsal hooked fin in swimming, which looks something like a Roman nose. When not more profitably employed, the sperm whale hunters sometimes capture the Hyena whale, to keep up the supply of cheap oil for domestic employment—as some frugal housekeepers, in the absence of company, and quite alone by themselves, burn unsavory tallow instead of odorous wax. Though their blubber is very thin, some of these whales will yield you upwards of thirty gallons of oil.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER III. ( Narwhale ), that is, Nostril whale .—Another instance of a curiously named whale, so named I suppose from his peculiar horn being originally mistaken for a peaked nose. The creature is some sixteen feet in length, while its horn averages five feet, though some exceed ten, and even attain to fifteen feet. Strictly speaking, this horn is but a lengthened tusk, growing out from the jaw in a line a little depressed from the horizontal. But it is only found on the sinister side, which has an ill effect, giving its owner something analogous to the aspect of a clumsy left-handed man. What precise purpose this ivory horn or lance answers, it would be hard to say. It does not seem to be used like the blade of the sword-fish and bill-fish; though some sailors tell me that the Narwhale employs it for a rake in turning over the bottom of the sea for food. Charley Coffin said it was used for an ice-piercer; for the Narwhale, rising to the surface of the Polar Sea, and finding it sheeted with ice, thrusts his horn up, and so breaks through. But you cannot prove either of these surmises to be correct. My own opinion is, that however this one-sided horn may really be used by the Narwhale—however that may be—it would certainly be very convenient to him for a folder in reading pamphlets. The Narwhale I have heard called the Tusked whale, the Horned whale, and the Unicorn whale. He is certainly a curious example of the Unicornism to be found in almost every kingdom of animated nature. From certain cloistered old authors I have gathered that this same sea-unicorn's horn was in ancient days regarded as the great antidote against poison, and as such, preparations of it brought immense prices. It was also distilled to a volatile salts for fainting ladies, the same way that the horns of the male deer are manufactured into hartshorn. Originally it was in itself accounted an object of great curiosity. Black Letter tells me that Sir Martin Frobisher on his return from that voyage, when Queen Bess did gallantly wave her jewelled hand to him from a window of Greenwich Palace, as his bold ship sailed down the Thames; “when Sir Martin returned from that voyage,” saith Black Letter, “on bended knees he presented to her highness a prodigious long horn of the Narwhale, which for a long period after hung in the castle at Windsor.” An Irish author avers that the Earl of Leicester, on bended knees, did likewise present to her highness another horn, pertaining to a land beast of the unicorn nature.

The Narwhale has a very picturesque, leopard-like look, being of a milk-white ground colour, dotted with round and oblong spots of black. His oil is very superior, clear and fine; but there is little of it, and he is seldom hunted. He is mostly found in the circumpolar seas.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER IV. ( Killer ).—Of this whale little is precisely known to the Nantucketer, and nothing at all to the professed naturalist. From what I have seen of him at a distance, I should say that he was about the bigness of a grampus. He is very savage—a sort of Feegee fish. He sometimes takes the great Folio whales by the lip, and hangs there like a leech, till the mighty brute is worried to death. The Killer is never hunted. I never heard what sort of oil he has. Exception might be taken to the name bestowed upon this whale, on the ground of its indistinctness. For we are all killers, on land and on sea; Bonapartes and Sharks included.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER V. ( Thrasher ).—This gentleman is famous for his tail, which he uses for a ferule in thrashing his foes. He mounts the Folio whale's back, and as he swims, he works his passage by flogging him; as some schoolmasters get along in the world by a similar process. Still less is known of the Thrasher than of the Killer. Both are outlaws, even in the lawless seas.

Thus ends BOOK II. ( Octavo ), and begins BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ).

DUODECIMOES.—These include the smaller whales. I. The Huzza Porpoise. II. The Algerine Porpoise. III. The Mealy-mouthed Porpoise.

To those who have not chanced specially to study the subject, it may possibly seem strange, that fishes not commonly exceeding four or five feet should be marshalled among WHALES—a word, which, in the popular sense, always conveys an idea of hugeness. But the creatures set down above as Duodecimoes are infallibly whales, by the terms of my definition of what a whale is— ie a spouting fish, with a horizontal tail.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER 1. ( Huzza Porpoise ).—This is the common porpoise found almost all over the globe. The name is of my own bestowal; for there are more than one sort of porpoises, and something must be done to distinguish them. I call him thus, because he always swims in hilarious shoals, which upon the broad sea keep tossing themselves to heaven like caps in a Fourth-of-July crowd. Their appearance is generally hailed with delight by the mariner. Full of fine spirits, they invariably come from the breezy billows to windward. They are the lads that always live before the wind. They are accounted a lucky omen. If you yourself can withstand three cheers at beholding these vivacious fish, then heaven help ye; the spirit of godly gamesomeness is not in ye. A well-fed, plump Huzza Porpoise will yield you one good gallon of good oil. But the fine and delicate fluid extracted from his jaws is exceedingly valuable. It is in request among jewellers and watchmakers. Sailors put it on their hones. Porpoise meat is good eating, you know. It may never have occurred to you that a porpoise spouts. Indeed, his spout is so small that it is not very readily discernible. But the next time you have a chance, watch him; and you will then see the great Sperm whale himself in miniature.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER II. ( Algerine Porpoise ).—A pirate. Very savage. He is only found, I think, in the Pacific. He is somewhat larger than the Huzza Porpoise, but much of the same general make. Provoke him, and he will buckle to a shark. I have lowered for him many times, but never yet saw him captured.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER III. ( Mealy-mouthed Porpoise ).—The largest kind of Porpoise; and only found in the Pacific, so far as it is known. The only English name, by which he has hitherto been designated, is that of the fishers—Right-Whale Porpoise, from the circumstance that he is chiefly found in the vicinity of that Folio. In shape, he differs in some degree from the Huzza Porpoise, being of a less rotund and jolly girth; indeed, he is of quite a neat and gentleman-like figure. He has no fins on his back (most other porpoises have), he has a lovely tail, and sentimental Indian eyes of a hazel hue. But his mealy-mouth spoils all. Though his entire back down to his side fins is of a deep sable, yet a boundary line, distinct as the mark in a ship's hull, called the “bright waist,” that line streaks him from stem to stern, with two separate colours, black above and white below. The white comprises part of his head, and the whole of his mouth, which makes him look as if he had just escaped from a felonious visit to a meal-bag. A most mean and mealy aspect! His oil is much like that of the common porpoise.

* * * * * *

Beyond the DUODECIMO, this system does not proceed, inasmuch as the Porpoise is the smallest of the whales. Above, you have all the Leviathans of note. But there are a rabble of uncertain, fugitive, half-fabulous whales, which, as an American whaleman, I know by reputation, but not personally. I shall enumerate them by their fore-castle appellations; for possibly such a list may be valuable to future investigators, who may complete what I have here but begun. If any of the following whales, shall hereafter be caught and marked, then he can readily be incorporated into this System, according to his Folio, Octavo, or Duodecimo magnitude:—The Bottle-Nose Whale; the Junk Whale; the Pudding-Headed Whale; the Cape Whale; the Leading Whale; the Cannon Whale; the Scragg Whale; the Coppered Whale; the Elephant Whale; the Iceberg Whale; the Quog Whale; the Blue Whale; etc. From Icelandic, Dutch, and old English authorities, there might be quoted other lists of uncertain whales, blessed with all manner of uncouth names. But I omit them as altogether obsolete; and can hardly help suspecting them for mere sounds, full of Leviathanism, but signifying nothing.

Finally: It was stated at the outset, that this system would not be here, and at once, perfected. You cannot but plainly see that I have kept my word. But I now leave my cetological System standing thus unfinished, even as the great Cathedral of Cologne was left, with the crane still standing upon the top of the uncompleted tower. For small erections may be finished by their first architects; grand ones, true ones, ever leave the copestone to posterity. God keep me from ever completing anything. This whole book is but a draught—nay, but the draught of a draught. Oh, Time, Strength, Cash, and Patience!
