NDZIMA YA 4. Xiphemu xa Counterpane.

Loko ndzi pfuka mixo lowu landzelaka loko ku ri ni ku vonakala ka nhlikanhi, ndzi kume voko ra Queequeg ri lahleriwe ehenhla ka mina hi ndlela ya rirhandzu ni ya rirhandzu swinene. A wu lava ku ehleketa leswaku ndzi ve nsati wa yena. Counterpane a yi ri ya swiphemuphemu, yi tele hi swikwere leswitsongo leswi nga tolovelekangiki swa mihlovo ya parti na ti triangles; naswona voko leri ra yena ri tsariwe hinkwako-nkwako hi labyrinth ya Kreta leyi nga heriki ya xifaniso, ku nga ri na swiphemu swimbirhi swa yona leswi a swi ri swa ndzhuti wun’we lowu kongomeke—hikwalaho ka mina ndzi ehleketa leswaku hikwalaho ka ku hlayisa voko ra yena elwandle hi ndlela leyi nga riki ya maendlelo edyambyini ni le ndzhutini, mavoko ya hembe yakwe ma phutseriwile hi ndlela leyi nga tshamisekangiki hi minkarhi yo hambana-hambana—voko leri rin’we ra yena, ndzi ri, ri lave misava hinkwayo ku fana ni xiphepherhele xa xiambalo xexo lexi fanaka xa swiphepherhele. Hakunene, xiphemu xin’wana xi etlele ehenhla ka yona tanihi leswi voko ri endleke hakona loko ndzi pfuka ro sungula, a ndzi nga swi koti ku swi vona hi ku olova eka xihuku, va hlanganise swinene mihlovo ya vona swin’we; naswona a ku ri hi ku tika ni ntshikilelo ntsena leswi a ndzi swi vona leswaku Queequeg a a ndzi angarha.

Mintlhaveko ya mina a yi hlamarisa. Ndzi ta ringeta ku ma hlamusela. Loko ndza ha ri n’wana, ndzi xi tsundzuka kahle xiyimo lexi fanaka hi ndlela yo karhi lexi ndzi weleke; ku nga khathariseki leswaku a ku ri ntiyiso kumbe norho, a ndzi nga si tshama ndzi swi kota ku tshamiseka hi ku helela. Xiyimo a xi ri lexi. A ndzi ri karhi ndzi tsema caper yin’wana kumbe yin’wana—ndzi ehleketa leswaku a yi ringeta ku gayela ehenhla ka xichimney, tanihi leswi ndzi voneke ku sweka nyana ku endla masiku ma nga ri mangani lama hundzeke; na manana wa mina wa vumbirhi loyi, hi ndlela yo karhi kumbe yin’wana, a a tshamela ku ndzi ba, kutani a ndzi rhumela emubedweni ndzi nga dyi swakudya swa nimadyambu,—mana wa mina u ndzi koke hi milenge a huma exichimney kutani a ndzi paka a ya etlela, hambileswi a ku ri awara ya mbirhi ntsena hi madyambu ya ti 21st June, siku ro leha swinene elembeni eka hemisphere ya hina. Ndzi titwe hi ndlela yo chavisa. Kambe a ku nga ri na mpfuno eka swona, kutani ndzi tlhandlukela hi switepisi ndzi ye ekamareni ra mina leritsongo eka xithezi xa vunharhu, ndzi ti hluvula hi ku nonoka hilaha swi nga kotekaka hakona leswaku ndzi ta dlaya nkarhi, kutani hi ku hefemula ko vava ndzi nghene exikarhi ka maphepha.

Ndzi etlele kwalaho hi ndlela yo vava ndzi hlayela leswaku khume-nhungu wa tiawara leti heleleke ti fanele ku hundza ndzi nga si tshemba ku pfuxiwa ka vafi. Khume-nhungu wa tiawara emubedweni! leswintsongo swa nkolo wa mina a swi vava loko ndzi swi ehleketa. Nakona na swona a swi vevuka swinene; dyambu leri a ri voninga hi fasitere, na ku rhurhumela lokukulu ka swigolonyana eswitarateni, na mpfumawulo wa marito ya tinghwavava endlwini hinkwayo. Ndzi titwe ndzi bihe ngopfu—eku heteleleni ndzi pfukile, ndzi ambala, naswona hi ku olova ndzi rhelela hi milenge ya mina leyi nga ni switoki, ndzi lava manana wa mina wa vumbirhi, kutani hi xitshuketa ndzi tiphofula emilengeni ya yena, ndzi n’wi kombela tanihi tintswalo to karhi leswaku a ndzi nyika ku rhetela lokunene ka mina ku tikhoma loko biha; xin’wana ni xin’wana hakunene handle ko ndzi sola ku etlela abed nkarhi wo leha swonghasi lowu nga tiyiselekiki. Kambe a a ri lonene naswona a ri ni ripfalo lerinene eka vamanana va vumbirhi, naswona endzhaku a ndzi fanele ndzi ya ekamareni ra mina. Ku ringana tiawara to hlayanyana ndzi etlele kwalaho ndzi pfukile, ndzi titwa ndzi bihe ngopfu ku tlula leswi ndzi tshameke ndzi swi endla ku sukela kwalaho, hambi ku ri hikwalaho ka makhombo lamakulu lama landzeleke. Eku heteleleni ndzi fanele ndzi wele eka norho wo biha lowu karhataka wa ku etlela; naswona hi ku nonoka ndzi pfuka eka yona—hafu ya yona yi nghene emilorhweni—ndzi pfule mahlo ya mina, naswona kamara ra le mahlweni leri voningeriweke hi dyambu sweswi a ri phutseriwe hi munyama wa le handle. Hi ku hatlisa ndzi twe ku chava ku tsutsuma eka furemu ya mina hinkwayo; a ku nga ri na nchumu lowu a wu ta voniwa, naswona a ku nga ri na nchumu lowu a wu ta twiwa; kambe voko leri tlulaka ra ntumbuluko a ri vonaka ri vekiwile eka ra mina. Voko ra mina a ri lengalenga ehenhla ka counterpane, naswona xivumbeko lexi nga riki na vito, lexi nga ehleketiwiki, lexi nga vulavuriki kumbe phantom, lexi voko a ri ri ra xona, a xi vonaka xi tshamile ekusuhi etlhelo ka mubedo wa mina. Hikuva leswi a swi vonaka onge malembe lama hlengeletiweke hi malembe, ndzi etlele kwalaho, ndzi titimela hi ku chava loku chavisaka swinene, ndzi nga ri na xivindzi xo koka voko ra mina; kambe ndzi tshame ndzi ehleketa leswaku loko ndzi nga swi kota ku yi hlanganisa hi intshi yin’we, ku loya loku chavisaka a ku ta tshoveka. A ndzi nga swi tivi leswaku ripfalo leri eku heteleleni ri rhelela njhani eka mina; kambe loko ndzi pfuka nimixo, ndzi swi tsundzuke hinkwaswo hi ku rhurhumela, naswona ku ringana masiku ni mavhiki ni tin’hweti endzhaku ka sweswo ndzi lahlekeriwe hi matshalatshala lama pfilunganyaka yo hlamusela xihundla. Doo, ku fikela eka awara leyi, hakanyingi ndzi tiphina hi swona.

Sweswi, susa ku chava loku chavisaka, naswona ku twa ka mina eka ku twa voko leri tlulaka ra ntumbuluko eka ra mina a swi fana swinene, eka ku hlamarisa ka swona, na leswi ndzi swi tokoteke loko ndzi pfuka ndzi vona voko ra Queequeg ra vuhedeni ri lahleriwa eka mina. Kambe eku heteleleni swiendlakalo hinkwaswo swa vusiku lebyi hundzeke hi ku hluteka swi tlhele swi humelela, ha xin’we-xin’we, hi ntiyiso lowu nga cinciki, ivi ndzi etlela ntsena ndzi hanya eka xiphiqo lexi hlekisaka. Hikuva hambileswi ndzi ringeteke ku fambisa voko rakwe—ku pfula xikhomela-ndhawu xa yena xa muteki—kambe, a etlele tanihi leswi a a ri xiswona, a ha ndzi angarha hi matimba, onge hiloko a ku na nchumu handle ka rifu lowu faneleke wu hi hambanisa vambirhi. Sweswi ndzi tikarhatele ku n’wi pfuxa—“Queequeg!”—kambe nhlamulo ya yena ntsena a ku ri ku rhurhumela. Ndzi tlhele ndzi rhendzeleka, nhamu ya mina yi twa onge yi le ka khola ya tihanci; naswona hi xitshuketa u twe ku rhurhumela nyana. Hi ku lahlela etlhelo counterpane, a ku etlele tomahawk yi etlele etlhelo ka savage, onge hi loko yi ri n’wana wa xikandza xa hatchet. Pickle yo saseka, hakunene, ndzi ehlekete; abed laha endlwini leyi nga tolovelekangiki hi siku ro anama, na mudlayi wa vanhu na tomahawk! “Queequeg!—hi vito ra vunene, Queequeg, pfuka!” Eku heteleleni, hi ku rhurhumela lokukulu, ni ku hlamusela lokukulu ni loku nga heriki ehenhla ka ku nga fanelanga ka ku angarha ka yena wanuna-kulobye hi muxaka wolowo wa xitayili xa vukati, ndzi humelerile ku humesa ku rhurhumela; naswona sweswi, u tlherisele voko ra yena endzhaku, a ti rhurhumela hinkwako ku fana na mbyana ya Newfoundland ntsena ku suka ematini, kutani a tshama ehenhla emubedweni, a tsanile ku fana na nhonga ya pike, a ndzi langutile, a ndzi tswontswa mahlo onge a nga ha tsundzuki hi ku helela ndlela leyi ndzi fikeke ndzi va kona ha yona, hambileswi ku twisisa loku nga vonakiki ka ku tiva swo karhi hi mina a ku vonaka ku ri karhi ku n’wi pfurha hakatsongo-tsongo. Hi nkarhi wolowo, ndzi etlele ndzi n’wi langutile hi ku miyela, ndzi nga ri na ku kanakana lokukulu sweswi, naswona ndzi korhamile ku languta hi ku nonoka xivumbiwa lexi a xi lava ku tiva swonghasi. Loko, eku heteleleni, mianakanyo ya yena yi vonaka yi endliwile yi khumba vumunhu bya mutirhi-kulobye wa le mubedweni, naswona u ve, hi ndlela yo fanekisela, ku vuyelelana ni ntiyiso; u tlulerile a huma ehansi, naswona hi swikombiso swo karhi ni mimpfumawulo swi ndzi nyike ku twisisa leswaku, loko swi ndzi tsakisa, a a ta rhanga a ambala ivi a ndzi siya ndzi ambala endzhaku ka sweswo, a siya fulete hinkwayo ndzi ri ndzexe. Miehleketa mina, Queequeg, ehansi ka swiyimo, leyi i overture leyi hluvukeke swinene; kambe, ntiyiso hi leswaku, swiharhi leswi swa tihanyi swi ni ku titwa loku velekiweke na kona ka ku nandziha, vula leswi u swi lavaka; swa hlamarisa ndlela leyi kahle-kahle va nga ni xichavo ha yona. Ndzi hakela ku bumabumela loku ko hlawuleka eka Queequeg, hikuva u ndzi khome hi vumunhu lebyikulu ni ku anakanyela, kasi a ndzi ri ni nandzu wa vusopfa lebyikulu; ku n’wi languta hi tihlo ro tshwuka a ri emubedweni, ni ku languta swiendlo swa yena hinkwaswo swa xihambukelo; hi nkarhi wolowo ku navela ka mina ku tiva ku antswa eka ku fuwa ka mina. Hambi swi ri tano, wanuna wo fana na Queequeg a wu n’wi voni siku na siku, yena na tindlela ta yena a swi fanerile swinene ku swi tekela enhlokweni hi ndlela leyi nga tolovelekangiki.

U sungule ku ambala ehenhla hi ku ambala xihuku xakwe xa beaver, lexi leheke swinene, hi le tlhelo, ivi—a ha susa buruku rakwe—a hlota maphaxani yakwe. Leswi ehansi ka matilo a swi endleleke swona, a ndzi swi tivi, kambe ku famba ka yena loku landzelaka a ku ri ku ti pfotlosa—a khome maphaxani, ni xihuku a ambale—ehansi ka mubedo; loko, hi ku hefemula ka madzolonga yo hambana-hambana ni ku tikarhata, ndzi gimete hi leswaku a a tirha hi matimba a ti-booter; hambileswi hi ku nga ri na nawu wa ku faneleka lowu ndzi tshameke ndzi wu twa, wanuna wihi na wihi a boheka ku va exihundleni loko a ambala maphaxani yakwe. Kambe Queequeg, wa swi vona, a a ri xivumbiwa lexi a xi ri eka xiyimo xa ku cinca—ku nga ri xivungu kumbe phaphatani. A a ri ni nhluvuko lowu ringaneke ntsena leswaku a kombisa vumunhu bya yena lebyi nga tolovelekangiki hi tindlela leti nga tolovelekangiki. Dyondzo ya yena a yi nga si hela. A a ri mudyondzi wa undergraduate. Loko a a nga ri na nhluvuko wa xiyimo lexitsongo, kumbexana ngopfu-ngopfu a a nga ta tikarhata hi maphaxani nikatsongo; kambe endzhaku, loko a nga ha ri xiharhi xa tihanyi, a a nga ta lorha hi ku nghena ehansi ka mubedo leswaku a ti ambala. Eku heteleleni, u hume a ri ni xihuku xakwe lexi rhurhumeleke swinene naswona xi pfotlosekile ehenhla ka mahlo yakwe, kutani a sungula ku rhurhumela ni ku rhurhumela ekamareni, onge hi loko, a nga tolovelanga ngopfu maphaxani, mpatswa wakwe wa maphaxani lama tsakamaka, lama rhurhumeleke—kumbexana a nga endliwanga tano order either—ematshan’weni ya sweswo yi n’wi pfotlose ni ku n’wi xanisa eku sunguleni ka mixo lowu titimelaka swinene.

Hi ku vona, sweswi, leswaku a ku nga ri na tikheteni efasitereni, ni leswaku xitarata a xi ri xitsongo swinene, yindlu leyi a yi langutane na yona a yi lerisa ku vona kahle ekamareni, ni ku languta ngopfu xifaniso lexi nga fanelangiki lexi Queequeg a xi endleke, a ri karhi a rhendzeleka-rhendzeleka hi swin’wana switsongo handle xihuku ni maphaxani yakwe a ambale; Ndzi n’wi kombele kahle hilaha ndzi nga kotaka hakona, leswaku a hatlisisa xihambukelo xa yena hi ndlela yo karhi, naswona ngopfu-ngopfu leswaku a nghena eka ti-pantaloon ta yena hi ku hatlisa hilaha swi nga kotekaka hakona. U pfumerile, ivi a ya emahlweni a hlamba. Hi nkarhi wolowo nimixo Mukreste un’wana ni un’wana a a ta va a hlambe xikandza xakwe; kambe Queequeg, lexi ndzi hlamariseke, u enerisekile hi ku siveriwa ka ku hlamba ka yena exifuveni, mavoko ni mavoko. U tlhele a ambala xihuku xa yena, kutani a teka xiphemu xa xisibi xo tiya etafuleni ra le xikarhi ra xitshamo xo hlamba, a xi nghenisa ematini kutani a sungula ku rhurhumela emahlweni ka yena. A ni langutile ku vona laha a hlayiseke xirhendzevutani xa yena, loko lo and behold, a teka harpoon ekhonweni ya mubedo, a rhelela a humesa xitoko xo leha xa mapulanga, a pfula nhloko, a yi whet nyana eka xihuku xa yena, a striding up to the bit ya xivoni leyi a yi langutane ni rirhangu, u sungula ku khwaxa hi matimba, kumbe ku vula ntiyiso ku ba marhama yakwe hi harpoon. Thinks I, Queequeg, leswi swi tirhisa ti cutlery ta kahle swinene ta Rogers hi ku rihisela. Endzhaku ka sweswo ndzi tivutisile ngopfu eka vuhandzuri lebyi loko ndzi tiva leswaku i nsimbi yihi ya kahle leyi nhloko ya harpoon yi endliwaka, na leswaku matlhelo yo leha yo kongoma ya tshama ya ri karhi ya kariha ku fikela kwihi.

Xihambukelo xa yena lexi seleke xi hatle xi fikeleriwa, naswona hi ku tinyungubyisa u humile ekamareni, a phutseriwe hi xiketi xa yena lexikulu xa tindlopfu ta muchayeri wa xihahampfhuka, naswona a tlanga harpoon ya yena ku fana na nhonga ya marshal.





NDZIMA YA 5. Swakudya swa nimixo.

Ndzi hatle ndzi landzela, kutani ndzi rhelela ekamareni ra le bareni ndzi hlasele n’wini wa muti loyi a a n’wayitela hi ndlela yo tsakisa swinene. A ndzi nga ri na vubihi eka yena, hambileswi a a ri karhi a skylarking na mina ku nga ri katsongo emhakeni ya mutirhi-kulorhi wa le mubedweni.

Hambiswiritano, ku hleka lokunene i nchumu lowunene wa matimba, naswona ematshan’wini ya sweswo i nchumu lowunene lowu kayivelaka ngopfu; ku ya ku va ni ntwela-vusiwana. Kutani, loko wanuna un’we, hi munhu wa yena loyi a faneleke, a kota ku xava swilo swa xihlekiso lexinene eka mani na mani, a nga tlheleli endzhaku, kambe hi ntsako a pfumelele ku tirhisa mali no tirhisiwa hi ndlela yoleyo. Naswona wanuna loyi a nga na nchumu wo hlekisa hi ndlela yo tala hi yena, tiyisisa leswaku ku na swo tala eka wanuna yoloye ku tlula leswi kumbexana u swi ehleketaka.

Kamara ra le bareni sweswi a ri tele hi va-boarder lava a va ri karhi va wisa vusiku lebyi hundzeke, naswona lava a ndzi nga si va languta kahle. Kwalomu ka hinkwavo a va ri vahlaseri va ti- whale; vanghana lavakulu, ni vanghana va vumbirhi, ni vanghana va vunharhu, ni vavatli va le lwandle, ni vafuri va le lwandle, ni vafuri va tinsimbi va le lwandle, ni vachayeri va tiharpone, ni varindzi va swikepe; khampani ya buraweni ni leyi nga ni matimba, leyi nga ni malebvu ya bosky; sete leyi nga tsemiwangiki, leyi nga ni swirhumbana, hinkwavo va ambale tijakete ta tinyenyana ta swiambalo swa nimixo.

A wu swi vona kahle leswaku yin’wana ni yin’wana yi ni nkarhi wo tanihi kwihi yi ri eribuweni. Marhama ra jaha leri leri hanyeke kahle ri fana na phere leri tostiweke hi dyambu hi muhlovo, naswona ri nga vonaka ri nun’hwela kwalomu ka musky; a nga ha va a ve masiku manharhu a fika emisaveni ku suka eka riendzo rakwe ra le Indiya. Wanuna yoloye loyi a nga ekusuhi na yena u languteka a olovile hi tinxaka ti nga ri tingani; u nga ha vula leswaku ku khumba ka mapulanga ya satin ku le ka yena. Eka nhlonge ya n’we-xa-nharhu xa ha tshama ku ri ni tawn ya le tindhawini to hisa, kambe yi basile nyana withal; handle ko kanakana u tshame mavhiki hinkwawo eribuweni. Kambe i mani loyi a nga kombisaka marhama yo fana na Queequeg? leyi, leyi pfaleriweke hi mihlovo yo hambana-hambana, a yi vonaka yi fana ni xirhendzevutani xa le vupela-dyambu bya Andes, leswaku yi kombisiwa hi nxaxamelo wun’we, maxelo lama hambaneke, hi ndhawu yin’wana ni yin’wana.

“Grub, ho!” sweswi ku huwelela n’wini wa muti, a hoxa rivanti, kutani hi nghena hi ya dya swakudya swa nimixo.

Va vula leswaku vavanuna lava voneke misava, xisweswo va va lava ntshunxekeke swinene hi ndlela, va tikhoma swinene hi vanghana. Kambe a hi minkarhi hinkwayo: Ledyard, mufambi lonkulu wa le New England, na Mungo Park, wa le Scotland; eka vavanuna hinkwavo, a va ri ni xitiyisekiso lexitsongo ephalari. Kambe kumbexana ku tsemakanya ntsena ka Siberia hi xigolonyana lexi kokiwaka hi timbyana tanihi leswi Ledyard a endleke hakona, kumbe ku famba nkarhi wo leha u ri wexe u nga ri na nchumu, embilwini ya vantima ya Afrika, leswi a ku ri nhlayo ya mintlangu ya Mungo ya xisiwana—ku famba ka muxaka lowu, . Ndzi ri, swi nga ha endleka ku nga ri ndlela leyinene ngopfu yo fikelela ku pholisha ka le henhla ka ntshamisano. Hambi swi ri tano, hi xiphemu lexikulu, muxaka wolowo wa nchumu wu fanele ku va kona kun’wana ni kun’wana.

Ku anakanyisisa loku laha ntsena ku vangiwa hi xiyimo xa leswaku endzhaku ka loko hinkwerhu hi tshamile etafuleni, naswona a ndzi tilulamisela ku twa switori swin’wana leswinene mayelana na ku hlota ti whale; lexi ndzi hlamariseke ngopfu, kwalomu ka wanuna un’wana ni un’wana u hlayise ku miyela lokukulu. Nakona a hi sweswo ntsena, kambe a va languteka va khomiwe hi tingana. Ina, laha a ku ri ni ntlawa wa timbyana ta le lwandle, leti to tala ta tona handle ko khomiwa hi tingana nikatsongo a ti khandziye ti- whale letikulu elwandle lerikulu—a ti nga va tivi nikatsongo eka tona—naswona ti ti duele ti file handle ko pfotlosa mahlo; naswona hambi swi ri tano, laha va tshame etafuleni ra swakudya swa nimixo ra ntshamisano—hinkwavo va vitaniwa hi ndlela leyi fanaka, hinkwavo va ri va ku navela loku fanaka—va langutana hi tingana onge hiloko va nga si tshama va nga voniwi eka xivala xin’wana xa tinyimpfu exikarhi ka Tintshava ta Rihlaza. Xivono xo lava ku tiva; tibere leti ta tingana, vahlaseri lava va ti- whale va nyimpi lava nga ni tingana!

Kambe loko ku ri Queequeg—ha yini, Queequeg u tshame kwalaho exikarhi ka vona—emahlweni ka tafula, na yena, swi lo tiendlekela swinene; ku kufumela ku fana ni xirhendzevutana xa ayisi. Ku tiyiseka a ndzi nge vuli swo tala hi ku fuwa ka yena. Mutsakeri wa yena lonkulu a nga ta va a lulamile hi rirhandzu ku tisa ka yena harpoon ya yena eka swakudya swa nimixo na yena, na ku yi tirhisa kwalaho handle ka nkhuvo; ku fikelela ehenhla ka tafula hi yona, ku ya ekhombyeni leri tshinelaka ra tinhloko to tala, ni ku khoma-khoma nyama ya homu a kongoma eka yena. Kambe sweswo hakunene swi endliwe hi ndlela yo kufumela swinene hi yena, naswona un’wana na un’wana wa swi tiva leswaku hi ku ringanyeta ka vanhu vo tala, ku endla xin’wana na xin’wana hi ku kufumela i ku swi endla hi vukheta.

A hi nge vulavuli hi swihlawulekisi hinkwaswo swa Queequeg laha; ndlela leyi a papalataka kofi na ti rolls to hisa, naswona a tirhisa nyingiso wa yena lowu nga avanyisiwangiki eka beefsteaks, leyi endliwaka hi xiwelo. Swi ringene, leswaku loko swakudya swa nimixo swi herile u tlhelele endzhaku ku fana na lavan’wana hinkwavo a nghena ekamareni ra mani na mani, a lumeka phayiphi ya yena ya tomahawk, naswona a tshamile kwalaho a miyerile a ri karhi a gayela no tswonga musi a ambale xihuku xa yena lexi nga hambanisiwiki, loko ndzi sallied out for a stroll.





NDZIMA YA 6. Xitarata.

Loko a ndzi hlamarile eku sunguleni loko ndzi vona munhu wo hlamarisa swonghasi ku fana na Queequeg a ri karhi a rhendzeleka exikarhi ka vanhu lava nga ni xichavo va doroba leri hluvukeke, ku hlamala koloko ku hatle ku suka loko ndzi teka riendzo ra mina ro sungula ra siku eswitarateni swa New Bedford.

Eka magondzo lama nga ekusuhi ni swikepe, ribuwa rihi na rihi lerikulu ra lwandle hakanyingi ri ta nyikela ku languta swilo leswi nga hlamuselekiki leswi langutekaka swi ri queerest ku suka eswiphen’wini swa matiko mambe. Hambi ku ri eswitarateni swa Broadway na Chestnut, vatluti va le Mediteraniya minkarhi yin’wana va ta rhurhumela vamanana lava chavaka. Regent Street a hi leyi nga tiviwiki hi va-Lascar ni va-Malay; naswona eBombay, eApolo Green, ma-Yankees lama hanyaka hakanyingi ma chavise vaaki va kwalaho. Kambe New Bedford yi hlula hinkwaswo Water Street na Wapping. Eka tindhawu leti to hetelela leti boxiweke u vona vatluti ntsena; kambe eNew Bedford, vanhu lava dyaka vanhu va xiviri va yime va ri karhi va bula emakhoneni ya switarata; swiharhi swa tihanyi hi ku kongoma; vo tala va vona va ha rhwala marhambu ya vona nyama leyi nga kwetsimangiki. Swi endla leswaku munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki a languta hi tihlo ro tshwuka.

Tatana Mapple u pfukile, naswona hi rito ro olova ra vulawuri lebyi nga tikukumuxiki u lerise vanhu lava hangalakeke leswaku va hlangana. “Starboard gangway, kwalaho! tlhelo ekule ku ya eka larboard—larboard gangway ku ya eximatsini! Swikepe swa le xikarhi! exikarhi ka swikepe!”

A ku ri na ku rhurhumela ka le hansi ka maphaxani yo tika ya le lwandle exikarhi ka mabenci, na ku rhurhumela nyana ka tintangu ta vavasati, naswona hinkwaswo a swi rhulile nakambe, naswona tihlo rin’wana na rin’wana a ri ri eka muchumayeri.

U yimilenyana; kutani a khinsama eka swirhendzevutana swa pulpiti, a peta mavoko ya yena lamakulu ya buraweni exifuveni xa yena, a tlakusa mahlo ya yena lama pfalekeke, kutani a endla xikhongelo lexi tinyiketeleke swinene lerova a vonaka a khinsamile a khongela ehansi ka lwandle.

Leswi swi hele, hi tithoni to leha to xiximeka, ku fana ni ku rila loku hambetaka ka bele exikepeni lexi nga eku wiseni elwandle hi nkungu—hi tithoni to tano u sungule ku hlaya risimu leri landzelaka; kambe a cinca ndlela ya yena a kongoma eka swivulwa swo hetelela, a pfurha hi ntsako lowu rhurhumelaka ni ntsako— .

Kwalomu ka hinkwavo va hlanganyerile eku yimbeleleni ka risimu leri, leri a ri pfimba ehenhla ka ku rila ka xidzedze. Ku landzerile ku yimanyana; muchumayeri u hundzuluxile matluka ya Bibele hakatsongo-tsongo, naswona eku heteleleni, a peta voko rakwe ehansi eka tluka leri faneleke, u te: “Vanhu lava rhandzekaka lava famba-fambaka na xikepe, khomani ndzimana yo hetelela ya ndzima yo sungula ya Yonasi—‘Naswona Xikwembu a xi lunghisele nhlampfi leyikulu ku mita Yonasi.’”

“Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

“With this sin of disobedience in him, Jonah still further flouts at God, by seeking to flee from Him. He thinks that a ship made by men will carry him into countries where God does not reign, but only the Captains of this earth. He skulks about the wharves of Joppa, and seeks a ship that's bound for Tarshish. There lurks, perhaps, a hitherto unheeded meaning here. By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
