Kambe a ku nga ri vusiku lebyi ngopfu-ngopfu laha, loko a ri yexe eka xiyindlwana xa yena, Akabu xisweswo a anakanyisisa hi tichati ta yena. Kambe a swi vonaki tano eka Akabu, loyi a a tiva ku hlangana ka magandlati hinkwawo ni maboboma hinkwawo; naswona hi ndlela yoleyo ku hlayela ku khuluka ka swakudya swa sperm whale; naswona, nakambe, ku tsundzuxa tinguva ta nkarhi na nkarhi, leti tiyisisiweke to n’wi hlota eka ti latitude to karhi; a nga fikelela swiringanyeto leswi twalaka, leswi lavaka ku tshinela eka swilo leswi tiyisekisiweke, malunghana ni siku leri nga enkarhini swinene ro va endhawini leyi kumbe yoleyo a ri karhi a lava xiharhi lexi a xi dlayeke.

Handle ka sweswo, loko ti endla ndlela yo suka endhawini yin’wana yo dyela eka yona ku ya eka yin’wana, ti- sperm whale, leti kongomisiwaka hi ntumbuluko wo karhi lowu nga hoxeke—ku nga ri, vutlhari bya le xihundleni lebyi humaka eka Xikwembu—ngopfu-ngopfu ti hlambela hi misiha , hilaha ti vitaniwaka hakona; va ya emahlweni ni ndlela ya vona hi layini ya lwandle leyi nyikiweke hi ku kongoma loku nga hambuki, lerova ku hava xikepe lexi tshameke xi famba hi ndlela ya xona, hi chati yihi na yihi, hi vukhume byin’we bya ku kongoma loku hlamarisaka swonghasi. Hambi leswi, eka swiyimo leswi, nkongomiso lowu tekiwaka hi whale yin’wana na yin’wana wu kongomeke tani hi parallel ya mupimi, naswona hambi leswi layini ya ku ya emahlweni yi pfaleriweke hi ku helela eka ku pfuka ka yona loku nga papalatekiki, loku kongomeke, kambe i xirho xa ku tihlawulela lexi hi minkarhi leyi ku vuriwaka leswaku u le ka xona ku hlambela, hi ntolovelo ku katsa tikhilomitara ti nga ri tingani hi ku anama (ku tlula kumbe ku tlula, tanihi leswi misiha yi ehleketiwaka leswaku yi andlala kumbe yi hunguteka); kambe a yi si tshama yi tlula ku swekiwa loku vonakaka loku humaka eka tinhloko ta mast-ship ta xikepe xa whale, loko hi vukheta xi rhelela endhawini leyi ya masalamusi. Naswona hikwalaho a hi minkarhi leyi tiyisiweke ntsena, ehenhla ka tindhawu leti tivekaka swinene to dyela eka tona leti hambaneke, Akabu a a ta va ni ntshembo wo hlangana ni xiharhi lexi a xi dlayeke; kambe eku tsemakanyeni ka mati yo anama swinene exikarhi ka tindhawu teto a nga swi kota, hi vutshila bya yena, hi yexe a veka na nkarhi hi yexe endleleni ya yena, tani hi leswi hambi ku ri hi nkarhi wolowo a nga ta va a nga ri na ntshembo hi ku helela wa nhlengeletano.

A ku ri na xiyimo lexi loko u xi vona ro sungula a xi vonaka xi pfilunganya xikimi xa yena xo pfilunganyeka kambe xa ha ri xa maendlelo. Kambe a swi tano entiyisweni, kumbexana. Hambi leswi ti gregarious sperm whale tingana tinguva ta tona ta ntolovelo eka tindzhawu tokarhi, hambiswiritano hiku angarhela awu nge gimeti hi leswaku mintlhambi leyi ayi hlongorisa latitude kumbe longitude yo tano na yo tano lembe leri, hiku vula, yita hundzuka leyi fanaka na leyi ayi kumeka kona nguva leyi hundzeke; hambileswi ku nga ni swiendlakalo leswi hlawulekeke ni leswi nga kanakanisiki laha leswi hambaneke ni leswi swi tikombiseke swi ri ntiyiso. Hi ntolovelo, marito lama fanaka, ntsena endzeni ka mpimo lowu nga anamangiki ngopfu, ma tirha eka vanhu lava nga voxe ni vahlapfa exikarhi ka ti- sperm whale leti vupfeke, leti dyuhaleke. Leswaku hambileswi Moby Dick a yi voniwe eka lembe ra khale, hi xikombiso, eka leswi vuriwaka misava ya Seychelle elwandle ra Indiya, kumbe Volcano Bay eRibuweni ra Japani; kambe a swi landzelanga, leswaku loko Pequod a yi endzela yin’wana ya tindhawu teto eka nguva yihi na yihi leyi landzelaka leyi fambelanaka, a yi ta hlangana na yena kwalaho handle ko hoxa. Swi tano ni hi tindhawu tin’wana to dyela eka tona, laha minkarhi yin’wana a a tivulavule kona. Kambe leswi hinkwaswo a swi vonaka swi ri tindhawu ta yena to yima hi ku olova ni tindhawu to wisela eka tona ta le lwandle, hi ndlela yo fanekisela, ku nga ri tindhawu ta yena to tshama nkarhi wo leha. Naswona laha minkarhi ya Akabu yo hetisisa xikongomelo xa yena ku fikela sweswi yi vulavuriweke ha yona, ku kombeteriwa ntsena eka ndlela yihi na yihi ya tlhelo rin’wana, ya le mahlweni, mintshembo leyi engetelekeke leyi a yi ri ya yena, ere nkarhi wo karhi lowu vekiweke kumbe ndhawu yi nga si fikeleriwa, loko hinkwaswo leswi nga kotekaka swi ta hundzuka swilo leswi nga kotekaka, naswona , hilaha Akabu a a ehleketa hakona hi ntsako, ku koteka kun’wana ni kun’wana i nchumu lowu landzelaka ku ya eka ku tiyiseka. Nkarhi wolowo wo hlawuleka lowu vekiweke ni ndhawu swi hlanganisiwe eka xiga xin’we xa vutshila—Nkarhi-eka-Nkarhi. Hikuva kwalaho na le ndzhaku, ku ringana malembe yo hlayanyana hi ku landzelelana, Moby Dick a a hlamuseriwa nkarhi na nkarhi, a tshama ematini wolawo swa xinkarhana, tanihi leswi dyambu, eka xirhendzevutana xa rona xa lembe na lembe, ri ri karhi ri rhendzeleka hi nkarhi lowu vhumbhiweke eka xikombiso xihi na xihi xin’we xa Zodiac. Kwalaho, nakambe, a ku ri leswaku ku hlangana ko tala loku dlayaka ni white whale ku humelerile; kwalaho magandlati a ma hlamuseriwa hi swiendlo swa yena; nakambe a ku ri na ndhawu yoleyo yo vava laha mukhalabye wa monomaniac a kumeke nsusumeto wo chavisa wa ku rihisela ka yena. Kambe eka ku angarhela hi vukheta ni ku xalamuka loku nga tsekatsekiki loku Akabu a hoxeke moya-xiviri wakwe lowu a wu ri eku hloteni loku nga tsekatsekiki, a a nga ta pfumela ku veka ntshembo wakwe hinkwawo eka mhaka yin’we leyikulu leyi boxiweke laha henhla, hambiloko yi nga ha va yi rhuketela eka mintshembo yoleyo; naswona hi ku pfumala vurhongo ka xihlambanyo xa yena a nga swi kotanga ku rhurisa mbilu ya yena leyi nga rhuliki lerova a tlherisela endzhaku ku lavisisa hinkwako loku nghenelelaka.

Sweswi, xikepe xa Pequod a xi sukile eNantucket eku sunguleni swinene ka Season-on-the-Line. Ku hava matshalatshala lawa yanga kotekaka hi nkarhi walowo lawa yanga endla leswaku ndhuna ya yena yikota ku endla ndlela leyikulu kuya e dzongeni, kambirhi Cape Horn, kutani yi tsutsuma ehansi ka makume tsevu wa ti degrees ta latitude yifika e equatorial Pacific hi nkarhi leswaku yita famba hi xikepe kona. Hikwalaho, u fanele a rindza nguva leyi landzelaka leyi landzelaka. Hambi swi ri tano awara leyi nga si fika nkarhi ya ku famba ka xikepe xa Pequod, kumbexana, a yi hlawuriwile hi ndlela leyinene hi Akabu, hi xikongomelo xa xivumbeko lexi xa swilo. Hikuva, nkarhi wa madzana manharhu na makume-nhungu-ntlhanu wa masiku na vusiku a wu ri emahlweni ka yena; nkarhi lowu, ematshan’weni yo tiyisela eribuweni hi ku pfumala ku lehisa mbilu, a a ta wu heta a ri eku hloteni ka swilo swo hambana-hambana; loko hi xihoxo White Whale, leyi hetaka nkarhi wa yona wo wisa emalwandle lama nga ekule swinene ni tindhawu ta yona to dyela eka tona nkarhi na nkarhi, yi fanele ku hundzuluxa xikandza xa yona lexi rhurhumeleke ehandle ePersian Gulf, kumbe eBengal Bay, kumbe China Seas, kumbe ematini man’wana wahi na wahi lama hlaseriwaka hi ya yona rixaka. Leswaku Monsoons, Pampas, Nor'-Westers, Harmattans, Mabindzu; moya wun’wana na wun’wana handle ka Levanter na Simoon, wu nga ha hunga Moby Dick eka xirhendzevutana xa misava lexi hambukeke xa zig-zag xa Pequod’s circumnavigating wake.

Kambe ku pfumelela hinkwaswo leswi; kambe, loko ku langutiwa hi vutlhari ni hi ku kufumela, swi vonaka swi nga ri kambe mianakanyo yo penga, leyi; leswaku elwandle leri anameke leri nga riki na ndzilakano, whale yin’we leyi nga yoxe, hambiloko yi hlangana na yona, yi fanele ku ehleketiwa leswaku yi kota ku tiviwa hi munhu hi xiyexe eka muhloti wa yona, hambi ku ri tanihi Mufti wa malebvu yo basa emagondzweni lama nga ni vanhu vo tala ya Constantinople? Ina. Hikuva xikandza xo hlawuleka xa Moby Dick xo basa ku fana na gamboko, na hump ya yena yo basa ku fana na gamboko, a swi nga ta swi kota kambe swi nga kanakanisi. Naswona xana a ndzi hlayanga whale, Akabu a a ta ti ngundzuvanya, tanihi leswi endzhaku ko kambisisa tichati ta yena ku kondza nkarhi wo leha endzhaku ka awara ya nhungu nivusiku a a ta ti lahlela endzhaku hi ku lorha—a n’wi hlayela, naswona xana u ta baleka? Tinsiva ta yena to anama ti borile, naswona ti scalloped out ku fana na ndleve ya nyimpfu leyi lahlekeke! Nakona laha, miehleketo ya yena yo penga a yi ta tsutsuma emahlweni eka mphikizano wo hefemula; ku kondza ku karhala ni ku titivala ka ku anakanyisisa swi ta ehenhla ka yena; naswona emoyeni lowu pfulekeke wa xitepisi a a ta lava ku vuyisa matimba yakwe. Ah, Xikwembu! i ku tiyisela kwihi ka ku xanisiwa loku munhu yoloye a ku tiyiselaka loyi a dyiweke hi ku navela kun’we loku nga fikeleriki ka ku rihisela. U etlela a khome mavoko; naswona u pfuka a ri ni swipikiri swa yena leswi nga ni ngati emavokweni yakwe.

Hakanyingi, loko a sindzisiwa ku suka eka hammock ya yena hi milorho leyi karharisaka ni leyi vonakaka hi ndlela leyi nga tiyiselekiki ya vusiku, leyi, yi tlhela yi sungula miehleketo ya yena n’wini leyikulu siku hinkwaro, a yi yi yisa emahlweni exikarhi ka ku lwisana ka ti- phrensi, ivi yi yi rhendzeleka ni ku rhendzeleka ni ku rhendzeleka ebyongweni bya yena lebyi pfurhaka, . ku kondza ku ba ka ndhawu ya yena ya vutomi ku hundzuka ku vaviseka loku nga tiyiselekiki; naswona loko, tani hi leswi a swi ri xiswona minkarhi yin’wana, ku xaniseka loku ka moya eka yena ku tlakusela vukona bya yena ehenhla ku suka ehansi ka byona, naswona xivandla xi vonaka xi pfuleka eka yena, lexi eka xona malangavi lama forokeke ni rihati swi duvuleke ehenhla, naswona swidimona leswi rhukaniweke swi n’wi kombetela ku tlula ehansi exikarhi ka swona; loko tihele leti hi toxe ti rhurhumela ehansi ka yena, xirilo xa nhova a xi ta twiwa hi xikepe; naswona hi mahlo lama hatimaka Akabu a a ta pfurha ekamareni rakwe ra mfumo, onge hiloko a baleka emubedweni lowu pfurhaka ndzilo. Hambi swi ri tano leswi, kumbexana, ematshan’wini yo va swikombiso leswi nga tshikilekiki swa ku tsana ko karhi loku fihliweke, kumbe ku chava hi ku tiyimisela ka yena n’wini, a ku ri swikombiso leswi nga erivaleni ntsena swa ku nonoka ka kona. Hikuva, hi minkarhi yo tano, Akabu la pengaka, muhloti la nga ni makungu, la nga tsakisiki la tiyeke wa white whale; Akabu loyi loyi a a ye exigwitsirisini xa yena, a a nga ri muyimeri loyi hi ndlela yoleyo a n’wi endleke leswaku a pfurha eka yona hi ku chava nakambe. Leyo hetelela a ku ri nsinya wa nawu lowu nga heriki, lowu hanyaka kumbe moya-xiviri eka yena; naswona evurhongweni, hi nkarhi wo karhi yi hambanisiwile ni mianakanyo leyi hlawulaka, leyi minkarhi yin’wana a yi yi tirhisa tanihi movha wa yona wa le handle kumbe muyimeri, hi ku tisungulela yi lave ku baleka eka ku hlangana loku hisaka ka nchumu lowu nga ni vuxiyaxiya, lowu, hi nkarhi wolowo, a wu nga ri wona ku leha i integral. Kambe tanihi leswi mianakanyo yi nga riki kona handle ka loko yi fambisana ni moya-xiviri, hikwalaho swi fanele swi ve tano, emhakeni ya Akabu, ku nyiketa mianakanyo ya yena hinkwayo ni ku navela ka yena eka xikongomelo xa yena xin’we lexi tlakukeke; xikongomelo xexo, hi ku tikhoma ka xona loku heleleke ka ku navela, xi ti sindzisile ku lwisana ni swikwembu ni mademona xi va muxaka wa xivumbiwa lexi titekaka, lexi tiyimeleke xa xona. E-e, a yi nga hanya hi ndlela yo vava yi tlhela yi hisa, kasi matimba lama tolovelekeke lawa a yi hlanganisiwe na wona, ma balekile hi ku chava ku suka eka ku velekiwa loku nga rhambiwangiki ni loku nga riki na tatana. Hikwalaho, moya lowu xanisiweke lowu a wu hatima wu huma ematihlweni ya miri, loko leswi a swi vonaka onge Akabu a tsutsumela ekamareni ra yena, a ku ri swa nkarhi wolowo kambe nchumu lowu nga riki na nchumu, xivumbiwa lexi nga riki na xivumbeko xa ku etlela, miseve ya ku vonakala loku hanyaka, ku tiyiseka, kambe ku nga ri na nchumu wo wu endla muvala, naswona hikwalaho i ku pfumala nchumu hi koxe. Xikwembu xi ku pfune mukhalabye, miehleketo ya wena yi tumbuluxe xivumbiwa eka wena; naswona loyi ku ehleketa ka yena lokukulu xisweswo ku n’wi endlaka Prometheus; ximanga xi dya mbilu yoleyo hilaha ku nga heriki; ximanga xexo xivumbiwa lexi xi xi tumbuluxaka.





NDZIMA YA 45. Xihlambanyo.

Ku fikela laha ku nga vaka kona eka xitlhokovetselo ebukwini leyi; naswona, hakunene, tanihi leswi hi ndlela leyi nga kongomangiki yi khumbaka mhaka yin’we kumbe yimbirhi leyi tsakisaka swinene ni leyi tsakisaka eka mikhuva ya ti- sperm whale, ndzima leyi nga laha henhla, eka xiphemu xa yona xo sungula, i ya nkoka ku fana ni leyi nga ta kumeka eka vholumo leyi; kambe mhaka leyi rhangelaka ya yona yi lava ku kurisiwa ku ya emahlweni ni hi ndlela leyi tolovelekeke swinene, leswaku yi twisisiwa hi laha ku ringaneke, naswona ku tlula kwalaho ku susa ku nga tshembi kwihi na kwihi loku ku pfumala vutivi lokukulu ka mhaka hinkwayo ku nga ku hlohlotelaka emianakanyweni yin’wana, malunghana ni ntiyiso wa ntumbuluko ya tinhla-nkulu ta mhaka leyi.

Ndzi khathala hi ku nga endli xiphemu lexi xa ntirho wa mina hi ndlela ya maendlelo; kambe u ta eneriseka ku humesa nhlamuselo leyi lavekaka hi ku tshahiwa loku hambaneke ka swilo, leswi ndzi swi tiviwaka hi ndlela leyi tirhaka kumbe hi ndlela leyi tshembekaka tanihi mudlayi wa ti- whale; naswona eka swikombo leswi, ndza swi teka—makumu lama kongomisiweke eka wona hi ntumbuluko ma ta landzela hi woxe.

Xo sungula: Mina hi ndzexe ndzi swi tivile swiendlakalo swinharhu laha whale, endzhaku ko amukela harpoon, yi endleke leswaku yi baleka hi ku helela; naswona, endzhaku ka nkarhi wo karhi (eka xiendlakalo xin’we xa malembe manharhu), u tlhele a biwa hi voko leri fanaka, ivi a dlayiwa; loko tinsimbi letimbirhi, leti havumbirhi bya tona ti funghiweke hi cypher yin’we ya le xihundleni, ti tekiwile emirini. Eka xiyimo lexi ku ngheneleke malembe manharhu exikarhi ka ku hoxiwa ka tiharpoon letimbirhi; naswona ndzi ehleketa leswaku swi nga ha va swi ri swin’wana swo tlula sweswo; wanuna loyi a va rhurhumeleke va humelela, exikarhi ka nkarhi, ku ya hi xikepe xa mabindzu eka riendzo ro ya eAfrika, a ya eribuweni kwalaho, a joyina ntlawa wo tshubula, kutani a nghena ekule endzeni ka tiko, laha a fambeke nkarhi wa kwalomu ka malembe mambirhi, . hakanyingi yi le khombyeni hi tinyoka, swiharhi swa nhova, tinghala, ti- miasma leti nga ni chefu, ni makhombo man’wana hinkwawo lama tolovelekeke lama humelelaka eka ku famba-famba exikarhi ka tindhawu leti nga tiviwiki. Hi nkarhi wolowo, whale leyi a yi tlhaveke na yona yi fanele yi ri eka maendzo ya yona; handle ko kanakana a yi rhendzele misava kanharhu, yi hlantswa hi matlhelo ya yona matlhelo hinkwawo ya Afrika; kambe ku nga ri na xikongomelo. Wanuna loyi na whale leyi va tlhele va hlangana, naswona yin’wana yi hlule yin’wana. Ndzi ri mina, hi ndzexe, ndzi tivile swiendlakalo swinharhu leswi fanaka na leswi; hi leswaku eka timbirhi ta tona ndzi vone ti- whale ti biwa; naswona, eka nhlaselo wa vumbirhi, u vone tinsimbi timbirhi leti nga ni swikombiso leswi faneleke ti tsemiwile eka tona, endzhaku ka sweswo ti tekiwile eka tinhlampfi leti feke. Eka xiendlakalo xa malembe manharhu, yi wile swinene lerova ndzi ve ebyatsweni minkarhi leyimbirhi, ro sungula ni ro hetelela, naswona ro hetelela ndzi xiye kahle muxaka wo hlawuleka wa xitsotswana lexikulu ehansi ka tihlo ra whale, lexi ndzi xi voneke kwalaho malembe manharhu lama hundzeke. Ndzi vula malembe manharhu, kambe ndzi tiyiseka swinene leswaku a swi tlula sweswo. Kutani hi leswi swiendlakalo swinharhu leswi mina hi ndzexe ndzi tivaka ntiyiso wa swona; kambe ndzi twe hi ta swiendlakalo swin’wana swo tala ku suka eka vanhu lava ntiyiso wa vona emhakeni leyi ku nga riki na xivangelo lexinene xo susa exikhundlheni.

Xavumbirhi: Switiveka swinene eka Sperm Whale Fishery, hambi misava yale ribuweni yinga pfumala vutivi hi swona, leswaku kuve na swiendlakalo swotala swa matimu leswinga rivalekiki laha whale yokarhi elwandle yingava kona eka minkarhi yale kule na tindzhawu leti tivekaka hi vanhu votala. Leswaku ha yini whale yo tano yi funghiwe hi ndlela yoleyo a ku nga ri hi ku helela naswona eku sunguleni a ku vangiwa hi ku hlawuleka ka miri wa yona tanihi leswi a hambanisiweke ni ti- whale tin’wana; hikuva hambileswi whale yihi na yihi ya xitshuketa yi nga ha va yi hlawulekile emhakeni yoleyo, hi ku hatlisa va herise ku hlawuleka ka yona hi ku yi dlaya, ni ku yi virisa yi va mafurha ya nkoka hi ndlela yo hlawuleka. Doo: xivangelo akuri lexi: leswaku eka mintokoto leyi dlayaka ya vumaki bya tihlampfi akuri na ndhuma yo chavisa ya khombo mayelana na whale yotani tani hi leswi swinga endla swona mayelana na Rinaldo Rinaldini, lerova vaphasi votala ava eneriseka ku n’wi lemuka hiku khumba ntsena ti tarpaulins ta vona loko ata endla tano ku tshuburiwa u lounging hi vona elwandle, handle ko lava ku hlakulela ku tolovelana lokukulu. Ku fana ni mademona man’wana ya swisiwana lama nga eribuweni lama humelelaka ma tiva wanuna lonkulu la hlundzukeke, ma n’wi losa hi ndlela ya le kule hi ndlela leyi nga kavanyetiki exitarateni, leswaku loko va hlongorisa loyi va n’wi toloveleke ku ya emahlweni, va nga kuma ku biwa hi ku komisa hikwalaho ka ku tikukumuxa ka vona.

Xana a swi nga ri tano, Wena Don Miguel! wena Chilian whale, leyi funghiweke ku fana ni ximanga xa khale lexi nga ni swifaniso swa xihundla leswi nga ni swifaniso swa xihundla endzhaku! Hi vutlhokovetseri lebyi nga erivaleni, hi leswi ti- whale ta mune leti tiviwaka kahle hi swichudeni swa Matimu ya Cetacean tanihi Marius kumbe Sylla eka xidyondzi xa xikhale.

Kambe leswi a hi swona ntsena. New Zealand Tom na Don Miguel, endzhaku ka loko himinkarhi yohambana hambana va endle ku onhaka lokukulu exikarhi ka swikepe swa swikepe swohambana hambana, ekuheteleleni va fambile kuya lava, va hlotiwa hindlela leyi hlelekeke, va hlongoriwa naku dlayiwa hi vakaputeni va xivindzi va whale, lava a va tlakusa ti anchors ta vona hi nchumu wolowo wo hatlisa tani hi hi ku languta ngopfu, tanihi loko a ri karhi a famba hi le Narragansett Woods, Kaputeni Butler wa khale a a ri emianakanyweni yakwe ku khoma Annawon yoloye wa tihanyi la dumeke wo dlaya, nhenha leyikulu ya Hosi Philip wa le Indiya.

A ndzi tivi leswaku ndzi nga yi kuma kwihi ndhawu yo antswa ku tlula laha ntsena, ku endla ku boxiwa ka nchumu wun’we kumbe swimbirhi swin’wana, leswi eka mina swi vonakaka swi ri swa nkoka, tanihi hi xivumbeko lexi kandziyisiweke lexi simekaka hi tindlela hinkwato ku twala ka xitori hinkwaxo xa White Whale, . ngopfu-ngopfu khombo lerikulu. Hikuva lexi i xin’wana xa swiendlakalo sweswo leswi hetaka matimba laha ntiyiso wu lavaka ku tiyisiwa hi ku helela ku fana ni xihoxo. Vanhu votala va misava ava pfumala vutivi bya swin’wana swa swihlamariso leswinga erivaleni na leswi vonakaka swinene swa misava, lero handle ka switsundzuxo swin’wana leswi khumbaka mintiyiso leyi nga erivaleni, ya matimu na swin’wana, ya vumaki bya tihlampfi, vanga scout eka Moby Dick tani hi ntsheketo wa ximanga, kutani va ha bihe ngopfu na ku nyenyetsa swinene, xifaniso lexi nyenyetsaka ni lexi nga tiyiselekiki.

Xo sungula: Hambileswi vavanuna vo tala va nga ni mianakanyo yin’wana leyi nga twisisekiki ya makhombo lama tolovelekeke ya vumaki bya tinhlampfi lebyikulu, hambiswiritano a va na nchumu lowu fanaka ni mianakanyo leyi nga cinciki, leyi vonakaka ya makhombo wolawo, ni nkarhi lowu ma tlhelaka ma humelela ha wona. Xivangelo xin’wana kumbexana hi leswaku, a ku na un’we eka makume ntlhanu wa makhombo ya xiviri na mafu hi ku vaviseka eka vumaki bya tihlampfi, loyi a tshamaka a kuma rhekhodo ya mani na mani ekaya, hambi yi ri ya xinkarhana naswona yi rivele rhekhodo yoleyo hi ku hatlisa. Xana u ehleketa leswaku xisiwana xexo xa kwalaho, lexi hi nkarhi lowu kumbexana xi khomiweke hi layini ya tinhlampfi ta whale eribuweni ra New Guinea, xi rhwariwe ehansi ku ya ehansi ka lwandle hi leviathan leyi twalaka—xana u ehleketa leswaku vito ra xisiwana xexo ri ta humelela eka phephahungu obituary u ta yi hlaya mundzuku eka swakudya swa wena swa nimixo? Doo: hikuva ti poso a ti tshamisekangi ngopfu exikarhi ka laha na New Guinea. Entiyisweni, xana u tshame u twa leswi nga ha vuriwaka mahungu ya nkarhi na nkarhi hi ku kongoma kumbe hi ndlela leyi nga kongomangiki ku suka eNew Guinea? Hambi swi ri tano ndza mi byela leswaku eka riendzo rin’we ro karhi leri ndzi ri tekeke ePacific, exikarhi ka swin’wana swo tala hi vulavule swikepe swa makume nharhu swo hambana, xin’wana na xin’wana xa swona xi dlayiwile hi whale, swin’wana swa swona swi tlula xin’we, na swinharhu leswi xin’wana na xin’wana xi lahlekeke vatirhi va le xikepeni. Hikwalaho ka Xikwembu, va ni mali yo tala hi mavoni ni makhandlela ya n’wina! ku nga ri galoni leyi u yi hisaka, kambe thonsi rin’we ra ngati ya munhu ri halatiwile hikwalaho ka yona.

Xa vumbirhi: Vanhu lava nga eribuweni hakunene va ni mianakanyo yo karhi leyi nga pimiwangiki ya leswaku whale i xivumbiwa lexikulu lexi nga ni matimba lamakulu swinene; kambe ndzi tshame ndzi kuma leswaku loko ndzi va hlamusela xikombiso xo karhi lexi kongomeke xa vukulu lebyi bya matlhelo mambirhi, va ndzi bumabumerile swinene hikwalaho ka ku va ni xikandza xa mina; loko, ndzi tivisa ehenhla ka moya-xiviri wa mina, a ndzi nga ri na mianakanyo ya ku va ni xikandza ku tlula Muxe, loko a tsala matimu ya makhombo ya Egipta.

Kambe nkateko wa kona yinhla yo hlawuleka leyi ndzi yi lavaka laha yi nga simekiwa eka vumbhoni lebyi tiyimeleke hi ku helela eka bya mina. Mhaka yoleyo hi leyi: Sperm Whale eka swiyimo swin’wana yi ni matimba lama eneleke, ya tiva naswona yi ni vubihi hi vutlhari, ku fana ni ku anakanyisisa ka ha ri emahlweni hi ku kongoma leswaku yi papalata, yi lovisa hi ku helela ni ku nwela xikepe lexikulu; naswona lexi tlulaka kwalaho, Sperm Whale yi swi endlile.

Leswi a tlheriseriweke ekaya eku heteleleni, Kaputeni Pollard u tlhele a khandziya xikepe a kongoma ePacific a ri karhi a rhangela xikepe xin’wana, kambe swikwembu swi n’wi tshove xikepe nakambe ehenhla ka maribye lama nga tiviwiki ni swikepe leswi tshovekeke; ra vumbirhi xikepe xa yena xi lahleka hi ku helela, naswona hi ku hatlisa hi ku hlambanya lwandle, a nga si tshama a ri ringa ku sukela kwalaho. But I must be content with only one more and a concluding illustration; a remarkable and most significant one, by which you will not fail to see, that not only is the most marvellous event in this book corroborated by plain facts of the present day, but that these marvels (like all marvels) are mere repetitions of the ages; so that for the millionth time we say amen with Solomon—Verily there is nothing new under the sun.

In the sixth Christian century lived Procopius, a Christian magistrate of Constantinople, in the days when Justinian was Emperor and Belisarius general. As many know, he wrote the history of his own times, a work every way of uncommon value. By the best authorities, he has always been considered a most trustworthy and unexaggerating historian, except in some one or two particulars, not at all affecting the matter presently to be mentioned.

Now, in this history of his, Procopius mentions that, during the term of his prefecture at Constantinople, a great sea-monster was captured in the neighboring Propontis, or Sea of Marmora, after having destroyed vessels at intervals in those waters for a period of more than fifty years. A fact thus set down in substantial history cannot easily be gainsaid. Nor is there any reason it should be. Of what precise species this sea-monster was, is not mentioned. But as he destroyed ships, as well as for other reasons, he must have been a whale; and I am strongly inclined to think a sperm whale. And I will tell you why. For a long time I fancied that the sperm whale had been always unknown in the Mediterranean and the deep waters connecting with it. Even now I am certain that those seas are not, and perhaps never can be, in the present constitution of things, a place for his habitual gregarious resort. But further investigations have recently proved to me, that in modern times there have been isolated instances of the presence of the sperm whale in the Mediterranean. I am told, on good authority, that on the Barbary coast, a Commodore Davis of the British navy found the skeleton of a sperm whale. Now, as a vessel of war readily passes through the Dardanelles, hence a sperm whale could, by the same route, pass out of the Mediterranean into the Propontis.

In the Propontis, as far as I can learn, none of that peculiar substance called brit is to be found, the aliment of the right whale. But I have every reason to believe that the food of the sperm whale—squid or cuttle-fish—lurks at the bottom of that sea, because large creatures, but by no means the largest of that sort, have been found at its surface. If, then, you properly put these statements together, and reason upon them a bit, you will clearly perceive that, according to all human reasoning, Procopius's sea-monster, that for half a century stove the ships of a Roman Emperor, must in all probability have been a sperm whale.





CHAPTER 46. Surmises.

Though, consumed with the hot fire of his purpose, Ahab in all his thoughts and actions ever had in view the ultimate capture of Moby Dick; though he seemed ready to sacrifice all mortal interests to that one passion; nevertheless it may have been that he was by nature and long habituation far too wedded to a fiery whaleman's ways, altogether to abandon the collateral prosecution of the voyage. Or at least if this were otherwise, there were not wanting other motives much more influential with him. It would be refining too much, perhaps, even considering his monomania, to hint that his vindictiveness towards the White Whale might have possibly extended itself in some degree to all sperm whales, and that the more monsters he slew by so much the more he multiplied the chances that each subsequently encountered whale would prove to be the hated one he hunted. But if such an hypothesis be indeed exceptionable, there were still additional considerations which, though not so strictly according with the wildness of his ruling passion, yet were by no means incapable of swaying him.

To accomplish his object Ahab must use tools; and of all tools used in the shadow of the moon, men are most apt to get out of order. He knew, for example, that however magnetic his ascendency in some respects was over Starbuck, yet that ascendency did not cover the complete spiritual man any more than mere corporeal superiority involves intellectual mastership; for to the purely spiritual, the intellectual but stand in a sort of corporeal relation. Starbuck's body and Starbuck's coerced will were Ahab's, so long as Ahab kept his magnet at Starbuck's brain; still he knew that for all this the chief mate, in his soul, abhorred his captain's quest, and could he, would joyfully disintegrate himself from it, or even frustrate it. It might be that a long interval would elapse ere the White Whale was seen. During that long interval Starbuck would ever be apt to fall into open relapses of rebellion against his captain's leadership, unless some ordinary, prudential, circumstantial influences were brought to bear upon him. Not only that, but the subtle insanity of Ahab respecting Moby Dick was noways more significantly manifested than in his superlative sense and shrewdness in foreseeing that, for the present, the hunt should in some way be stripped of that strange imaginative impiousness which naturally invested it; that the full terror of the voyage must be kept withdrawn into the obscure background (for few men's courage is proof against protracted meditation unrelieved by action); that when they stood their long night watches, his officers and men must have some nearer things to think of than Moby Dick. For however eagerly and impetuously the savage crew had hailed the announcement of his quest; yet all sailors of all sorts are more or less capricious and unreliable—they live in the varying outer weather, and they inhale its fickleness—and when retained for any object remote and blank in the pursuit, however promissory of life and passion in the end, it is above all things requisite that temporary interests and employments should intervene and hold them healthily suspended for the final dash.

Nor was Ahab unmindful of another thing. In times of strong emotion mankind disdain all base considerations; but such times are evanescent. The permanent constitutional condition of the manufactured man, thought Ahab, is sordidness. Granting that the White Whale fully incites the hearts of this my savage crew, and playing round their savageness even breeds a certain generous knight-errantism in them, still, while for the love of it they give chase to Moby Dick, they must also have food for their more common, daily appetites. For even the high lifted and chivalric Crusaders of old times were not content to traverse two thousand miles of land to fight for their holy sepulchre, without committing burglaries, picking pockets, and gaining other pious perquisites by the way. Had they been strictly held to their one final and romantic object—that final and romantic object, too many would have turned from in disgust. I will not strip these men, thought Ahab, of all hopes of cash—aye, cash. They may scorn cash now; but let some months go by, and no perspective promise of it to them, and then this same quiescent cash all at once mutinying in them, this same cash would soon cashier Ahab.

Nor was there wanting still another precautionary motive more related to Ahab personally. Having impulsively, it is probable, and perhaps somewhat prematurely revealed the prime but private purpose of the Pequod's voyage, Ahab was now entirely conscious that, in so doing, he had indirectly laid himself open to the unanswerable charge of usurpation; and with perfect impunity, both moral and legal, his crew if so disposed, and to that end competent, could refuse all further obedience to him, and even violently wrest from him the command. From even the barely hinted imputation of usurpation, and the possible consequences of such a suppressed impression gaining ground, Ahab must of course have been most anxious to protect himself. That protection could only consist in his own predominating brain and heart and hand, backed by a heedful, closely calculating attention to every minute atmospheric influence which it was possible for his crew to be subjected to.

For all these reasons then, and others perhaps too analytic to be verbally developed here, Ahab plainly saw that he must still in a good degree continue true to the natural, nominal purpose of the Pequod's voyage; observe all customary usages; and not only that, but force himself to evince all his well known passionate interest in the general pursuit of his profession.

Be all this as it may, his voice was now often heard hailing the three mast-heads and admonishing them to keep a bright look-out, and not omit reporting even a porpoise. This vigilance was not long without reward.





CHAPTER 47. The Mat-Maker.

It was a cloudy, sultry afternoon; the seamen were lazily lounging about the decks, or vacantly gazing over into the lead-coloured waters. Queequeg and I were mildly employed weaving what is called a sword-mat, for an additional lashing to our boat. So still and subdued and yet somehow preluding was all the scene, and such an incantation of reverie lurked in the air, that each silent sailor seemed resolved into his own invisible self.

I was the attendant or page of Queequeg, while busy at the mat. As I kept passing and repassing the filling or woof of marline between the long yarns of the warp, using my own hand for the shuttle, and as Queequeg, standing sideways, ever and anon slid his heavy oaken sword between the threads, and idly looking off upon the water, carelessly and unthinkingly drove home every yarn: I say so strange a dreaminess did there then reign all over the ship and all over the sea, only broken by the intermitting dull sound of the sword, that it seemed as if this were the Loom of Time, and I myself were a shuttle mechanically weaving and weaving away at the Fates. There lay the fixed threads of the warp subject to but one single, ever returning, unchanging vibration, and that vibration merely enough to admit of the crosswise interblending of other threads with its own. This warp seemed necessity; and here, thought I, with my own hand I ply my own shuttle and weave my own destiny into these unalterable threads. Meantime, Queequeg's impulsive, indifferent sword, sometimes hitting the woof slantingly, or crookedly, or strongly, or weakly, as the case might be; and by this difference in the concluding blow producing a corresponding contrast in the final aspect of the completed fabric; this savage's sword, thought I, which thus finally shapes and fashions both warp and woof; this easy, indifferent sword must be chance—aye, chance, free will, and necessity—nowise incompatible—all interweavingly working together. The straight warp of necessity, not to be swerved from its ultimate course—its every alternating vibration, indeed, only tending to that; free will still free to ply her shuttle between given threads; and chance, though restrained in its play within the right lines of necessity, and sideways in its motions directed by free will, though thus prescribed to by both, chance by turns rules either, and has the last featuring blow at events.

Thus we were weaving and weaving away when I started at a sound so strange, long drawn, and musically wild and unearthly, that the ball of free will dropped from my hand, and I stood gazing up at the clouds whence that voice dropped like a wing. High aloft in the cross-trees was that mad Gay-Header, Tashtego. His body was reaching eagerly forward, his hand stretched out like a wand, and at brief sudden intervals he continued his cries. To be sure the same sound was that very moment perhaps being heard all over the seas, from hundreds of whalemen's look-outs perched as high in the air; but from few of those lungs could that accustomed old cry have derived such a marvellous cadence as from Tashtego the Indian's.

As he stood hovering over you half suspended in air, so wildly and eagerly peering towards the horizon, you would have thought him some prophet or seer beholding the shadows of Fate, and by those wild cries announcing their coming.

“There she blows! there! there! there! she blows! she blows!”

“Where-away?”

“On the lee-beam, about two miles off! a school of them!”

Instantly all was commotion.

The Sperm Whale blows as a clock ticks, with the same undeviating and reliable uniformity. And thereby whalemen distinguish this fish from other tribes of his genus.

“There go flukes!” was now the cry from Tashtego; and the whales disappeared.

“Quick, steward!” cried Ahab. “Time! time!”

Dough-Boy hurried below, glanced at the watch, and reported the exact minute to Ahab.

The ship was now kept away from the wind, and she went gently rolling before it. Tashtego reporting that the whales had gone down heading to leeward, we confidently looked to see them again directly in advance of our bows. For that singular craft at times evinced by the Sperm Whale when, sounding with his head in one direction, he nevertheless, while concealed beneath the surface, mills round, and swiftly swims off in the opposite quarter—this deceitfulness of his could not now be in action; for there was no reason to suppose that the fish seen by Tashtego had been in any way alarmed, or indeed knew at all of our vicinity. One of the men selected for shipkeepers—that is, those not appointed to the boats, by this time relieved the Indian at the main-mast head. The sailors at the fore and mizzen had come down; the line tubs were fixed in their places; the cranes were thrust out; the mainyard was backed, and the three boats swung over the sea like three samphire baskets over high cliffs. Outside of the bulwarks their eager crews with one hand clung to the rail, while one foot was expectantly poised on the gunwale. So look the long line of man-of-war's men about to throw themselves on board an enemy's ship.
