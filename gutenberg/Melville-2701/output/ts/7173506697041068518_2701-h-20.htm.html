NDZIMA YA 101. Mufambisi wa Xikolo.

Ere xikepe xa Manghezi xi nyamalala xi nga ha voniwi, ku nga va xi vekiwile ehansi laha, leswaku xi huma eLondon, naswona xi thyiwile vito ra mufi Samuel Enderby, muxavisi wa doroba rero, wo sungula wa yindlu ya ndhuma ya ti whale ya Enderby & Sons; yindlu leyi hi ku vona ka mina wa xisiwana wa whaleman, yi fikaka kule endzhaku ka tindlu ta vuhosi leti hlanganeke ta ma-Tudor na ma-Bourbon, eka yinhla ya ku tsakela ka matimu ya xiviri. Leswaku nkarhi wo tanihi kwihi, emahlweni ka lembe ra Hosi ya hina 1775, yindlu leyi leyikulu yo phasa tinhlampfi a yi ri kona, matsalwa ya mina yo tala ya tinhlampfi a ma swi veki erivaleni; kambe hi lembe rero (1775) yi lunghiselele swikepe swo sungula swa Manghezi leswi tshameke swi hlota Sperm Whale; hambi leswi eka malembe yo karhi ya makume lama hundzeke (ku sukela hi 1726) Mabokisi ya hina ya xivindzi na Maceys ya Nantucket na Vineyard a va hlongorisile Leviathan yoleyo hi swikepe leswikulu, kambe ntsena eN’walungwini na le Dzongeni wa Atlantic: ku nga ri kun’wana. Ku nga va swi tsariwile hi ku kongoma laha, leswaku va ka Nantucketer hi vona vo sungula exikarhi ka vanhu ku harpoon hi nsimbi leyi hluvukeke Sperm Whale leyikulu; ni leswaku ku ringana hafu ya lembe-xidzana a ku ri vona ntsena vanhu va misava hinkwayo lava n’wi boheke hi harpoon hi ndlela yoleyo.

Hi 1778, xikepe lexinene, Amelia, xi lunghiselele xikongomelo xo hatlisa, naswona hi ku lawuriwa ntsena hi va-Enderby lava nga ni matimba, hi xivindzi xi rhendzele Cape Horn, naswona a xi ri xo sungula exikarhi ka matiko ku ehlisa xikepe xa whale xa muxaka wihi na wihi e Lwandle lerikulu ra le Dzongeni. Riendzo leri a ri ri ra vutshila ni nkateko; naswona loko a tlhelela laha a tshamaka kona a khome swikepe swa yena leswi teleke hi mbewu ya vununa ya risima, xikombiso xa Amelia xi hatle xi landzeriwa hi swikepe swin’wana, swa Manghezi ni swa le Amerika, naswona xisweswo ndhawu leyikulu ya Sperm Whale ya le Pacific yi lahleriwe yi pfulekile. Kambe hi ku nga eneriseki hi xiendlo lexi lexinene, yindlu leyi nga karhaliki yi tlhele yi tikarhata: Samuel na Vana va yena hinkwavo—i vangani, mana wa vona u va tiva ntsena—naswona ehansi ka vulawuri bya vona bya xihatla, naswona xiphemu xin’wana, ndzi ehleketa leswaku, hi ku lahlekeriwa hi vona, hulumendhe ya Britain yi hlohloteriwile ku rhumela xikepe lexi vuriwaka sloop-of-war Rattler eka riendzo ro hlota tinhlampfi ta tinhlampfi to tshubula eLwandle ra le Dzongeni. Hi ku lawuriwa hi Post-Captain ya masocha ya le matini, Rattler yi endle riendzo ro rhurhumela ra yona, naswona yi endle ntirho wo karhi; i mali muni leyi nga vonakiki. Kambe leswi a hi swona ntsena. Hi 1819, yindlu leyi fanaka yi lunghiselele xikepe xa yona xa ti- whale lexi tshuburiweke, leswaku yi ya eka riendzo ro nantswa yi ya ematini ya le kule ya Japani. Xikepe xexo—lexi vitaniwaka kahle “Syren”—xi endle riendzo ra xiyimo xa le henhla ro ringeta; naswona hi xisweswo ndhawu leyikulu ya Japanese Whaling Ground yi sungule ku tiviwa hi vanhu hinkwavo. Xikepe xa Syren eka riendzo leri leri dumeke a xi lawuriwa hi Captain Coffin, mufambisi wa Nantucketer.

Ku xiximiwa hinkwako eka va ka Enderbies, hikokwalaho, lava yindlu ya vona, ndzi ehleketa leswaku, yinga kona kufikela namuntlha; hambileswi handle ko kanakana Samuwele wo sungula u fanele a rhelerile khale swinene hi ntambhu yakwe leswaku a ya eLwandle lerikulu ra le Dzongeni ra misava yin’wana.

Xikepe lexi thyiweke vito ra yena a xi fanele ku xiximiwa, a xi ri xikepe lexi fambaka hi rivilo lerikulu swinene naswona a xi ri xikepe xa vutshila ematlhelo hinkwawo. Ndzi n’wi khandziyile kan’we exikarhi ka vusiku kun’wana ehandle ka ribuwa ra Patagonia, kutani ndzi nwa flip leyinene ehansi eka forecastle. A ku ri gam leyinene leyi a hi ri na yona, naswona hinkwavo a va ri ti trumps—moya-xiviri wun’wana ni wun’wana lowu a wu ri ebyatsweni. Vutomi byo koma eka vona, ni rifu ro tsakisa. Naswona gam yoleyo leyinene leyi a ndzi ri na yona—endzhaku ko leha, nkarhi wo leha swinene endzhaku ka loko Akabu wa khale a khumbe mapulanga yakwe hi xirhendze xakwe xa timhondzo ta tindlopfu—yi ndzi ehleketa hi malwandla lama tlakukeke, lama tiyeke, ya Masaxon ya xikepe xexo; naswona onge mufundhisi wa mina a nga ndzi rivala, naswona diyavulosi a ndzi tsundzuka, loko ndzi tshuka ndzi lahlekeriwa hi ku swi vona. Hundzuluxa? Xana ndzi vule leswaku hi na flip? Ina, naswona a hi yi tlherisela endzhaku hi rivilo ra khume ra tilitara hi awara; naswona loko xidzedze xi fika (hikuva xi squally off kwalahaya hi Patagonia), naswona mavoko hinkwawo—vaendzi na hinkwavo—ya vitaniwile eka ti reef topsails, a hi ri na top-heavy swinene lerova hi boheke ku swing un’wana na un’wana ehenhla eka bowlines; naswona hi ku pfumala vutivi hi hoxe swiketi swa tijakete ta hina emaseyila, lerova hi lenga-lenga kwalaho, hi ri karhi hi rhurhumela hi matimba eka xidzedze lexi rilaka, xikombiso xa xitsundzuxo eka titar hinkwato leti dakweke. Hambiswiritano, swigolonyana a swi tlulanga mpimo; naswona hi ku hatlisa hi rhurhumerile ehansi, hi ri na vuxiyaxiya swinene, lerova hi boheke ku hundza flip nakambe, hambi leswi xifafazeri xa munyu xa tihanyi lexi a xi pfurha ehansi ka forecastle scuttle, ematshan’wini yo chela ngopfu no yi pickled hi ku ya hi ku navela ka mina.

Nyama ya homu a yi ri kahle—yi tiyile, kambe yi ri ni miri endzeni ka yona. Va vule leswaku a ku ri nyama ya homu; van’wana, leswaku a ku ri nyama ya homu ya dromedary; kambe a ndzi swi tivi, hi ku tiyiseka, leswaku sweswo a swi ri njhani. Na vona a va ri ni ti- dumplings; ti dumplings letintsongo, kambe letikulu, letingana xivumbeko xa symmetrically, naswona tinga onhiwiki. A ndzi ehleketa leswaku u nga swi twa, naswona u swi rhendzeleka eka wena endzhaku ka loko swi mitiwile. Loko u korhama u ya emahlweni ngopfu, a wu nghenisa ekhombyeni ku hoxa ka vona ehandle ka wena ku fana ni tibolo ta billiard. Xinkwa—kambe sweswo a swi nga pfuni nchumu; handle ka sweswo, a ku ri murhi lowu lwisanaka ni scorbutic; hi ku komisa, xinkwa lexi a xi ri ni mali yo tala ntsena leyi a va ri na yona. Kambe forecastle a yi nga olovi ngopfu, naswona a swi olova swinene ku kandziya ekhonweni ya munyama loko u yi dya. Kambe hinkwaswo hi ku angarhela, ku n’wi teka ku suka eka lori ku ya eka xihaha-mpfhuka, a anakanya hi mpimo wa swigwitsirisi swa mupheki, ku katsa ni swigwitsirisi swa yena swa dzovo leswi hanyaka; emahlweni na le ndzhaku, ndzi ri, Samuel Enderby a ku ri xikepe xo tsakisa; ya mali leyinene ni yo tala; flip leyinene naswona yi tiyile; crack fellows hinkwaswo, na capital kusuka eka boot heels kuya eka hat-band.

Kambe hikokwalaho ka yini, anakanya, leswaku Samuel Enderby, na vaphasi van’wana va ti whala va Manghezi lava ndzi va tivaka—ku nga ri hinkwavo hambiswiritano—a va ri swikepe leswi dumeke swonghasi, leswi nga ni malwandla; leswi a swi hundza swi rhendzela nyama ya homu, na xinkwa, na xigwitsirisi, na xihlekiso; naswona a va nga karhali hi ku hatlisa hi ku dya, ni ku nwa, ni ku hleka? Ndzi ta ku byela. Ku tsaka lokukulu ka vaphasi lava va tinhlampfi ta Manghezi i mhaka ya ndzavisiso wa matimu. Naswona a ndzi nga ri na mhaka nikatsongo eka ndzavisiso wa matimu wa ti- whale, loko wu vonaka wu laveka.

Manghezi ma rhangele hi vanhu va le Holland, va le Zealand ni va le Denmark eku phaseni ka tinhlampfi ta whale; loyi va kumeke eka yena marito yo tala lama ha riki kona eka vumaki bya tihlampfi; naswona leswi tlulaka sweswo, tifexeni ta vona ta khale leti noneke, leti khumbaka swo tala swo dya ni ku nwa. Hikuva, tanihi nchumu lowu tolovelekeke, xikepe xa vaxavisi xa Manghezi xi hlongorisa vatirhi va xona; kambe a swi tano hi mudlayi wa tinhlampfi wa Manghezi. Hikwalaho, eka Xinghezi, nchumu lowu wa ku tsaka lokunene ka whaling a hi lowu tolovelekeke ni wa ntumbuluko, kambe i wa xitshuketa ni wo hlawuleka; naswona, hikwalaho, yi fanele ku va na masungulo yo karhi yo hlawuleka, lawa laha ya kombisiweke, naswona ya ha ta hlamuseriwa ku ya emahlweni.

Hi nkarhi wa ndzavisiso wa mina eka matimu ya Leviathanic, ndzi khunguvanyekile eka vholumo ya khale ya Madachi, leyi, hi ku nun’hwela ka yona ka musty whale, a ndzi swi tiva leswaku yi fanele yi vulavula hi vaphasi va whale. Nhlokomhaka akuri, “Dan Coopman,” hikokwalaho ndzi gimete hi leswaku leswi swifanele kuva switsundzuxo swa nkoka swinene swa swin’wana swa Amsterdam cooper eka vumaki bya tihlampfi, tani hileswi xikepe xin’wana na xin’wana xa whale xifaneleke ku rhwala cooper ya xona. Ndzi tiyisiwile eka vonelo leri hi ku vona leswaku a ku ri vuhumelerisi bya “Fitz Swackhammer” yin’we. Kambe munghana wa mina Dokodela Snodhead, wanuna la dyondzekeke swinene, profesa wa Xidachi xa le Hansi ni Xijarimani xa le Henhla ekholichi ya Santa Claus na St. loyi a nga yena Dokodela Snodhead, hi ku hatlisa loko a ri karhi a hlota buku leyi, u ndzi tiyisekise leswaku “Dan Coopman” a yi nga vuli “The Cooper,” kambe “Muxavisi.” Hi ku komisa, buku leyi ya khale ni leyi dyondzekeke ya Xidachi xa le Hansi a yi vulavula hi mabindzu ya le Holland; naswona, exikarhi ka timhaka tin’wana, a yi ri ni rungula leri tsakisaka swinene ra vuphasi bya yona bya tinhlampfi ta whale. Naswona eka ndzima leyi a ku ri, leyi nga ni nhloko-mhaka leyi nge, “Smeer,” kumbe “Fat,” laha ndzi kumeke nxaxamelo wo leha lowu nga ni vuxokoxoko bya swiambalo swa swigwitsirisi ni swigwitsirisi swa 180 wa maseyila ya vahlaseri va ti- whale va le Netherlands; eka nxaxamelo wolowo, hilaha wu hundzuluxeriweke hakona hi Dr. Snodhead, ndzi tsala leswi landzelaka:

400,000 wa ti lbs. ya nyama ya homu. 60,000 wa ti lbs. Nyama ya nguluve ya Friesland. 150,000 wa ti lbs. ya nhlampfi ya xitoko. 550,000 wa ti lbs. ya xigwitsirisi. 72,000 wa ti lbs. ya xinkwa xo olova. 2 800 wa ti- firkin ta botere. 20,000 wa ti lbs. Xisibi xa Texel & Leyden. 144,000 wa ti lbs. chizi (kumbexana i xihloko xa le hansi). 550 wa ti- ankers ta le Geneva. 10,800 wa migerho ya biya.

Matafula yo tala ya tinhlayo ya omile hi ndlela yo oma eka ku hlaya; hambiswiritano, a swi tano eka mhaka ya sweswi, laha muhlayi a talaka hi tiphayiphi leti heleleke, migerho, ti- quart ni ti- gill ta gin leyinene ni ku tsaka lokunene.

Hi nkarhi wolowo, ndzi nyikele masiku manharhu ndzi ri karhi ndzi gayela hi vukheta byalwa lebyi hinkwabyo, nyama ya homu ni xinkwa, laha hi nkarhi wolowo mianakanyo yo tala leyi dzikeke yi ringanyetiweke hi xihoxo eka mina, leyi kotaka ku tirhisiwa hi ndlela leyi tlulaka ni ya Plato; naswona, ku tlula kwalaho, ndzi hlengelete matafula yo engetela ya mina, ndzi khumba nhlayo leyi nga ha vaka kona ya tihlampfi ta xitoko, na swin’wana, leti dyiwaka hi muchayeri un’wana na un’wana wa harpooneer wa Low Dutch eka vumaki byebyo bya khale bya Greenland na Spitzbergen whale. Eka xiyimo xosungula, nhlayo ya botere, na Texel na Leyden cheese leswi dyiweke, swi vonaka swi hlamarisa. Kambe, ndzi swi veka hikwalaho ka ntumbuluko wa vona wa ntumbuluko lowu nga riki na vuxiyaxiya, ku endliwa wu nga ha ri na vuxiyaxiya swinene hi muxaka wa ntirho wa vona, naswona ngopfu-ngopfu hi ku landzelela ka vona ntlangu wa vona eka Malwandle wolawo yo titimela ya Polar, ematlhelo ka wona ya tiko rero ra Esquimaux laha vaaki va ndhawu yoleyo lava nga ni vuxaka bya rimbewu va tshembisaka kona un’wana na un’wana eka ti bumpers ta oyili ya xitimela.

Nhlayo ya biya, na yona, yikulu swinene, 10,800 wa ti barrels. Sweswi, tanihi leswi vumaki byebyo bya le polar a byi ta hehliwa ntsena hi ximumu xo koma xa maxelo wolawo, lerova riendzo hinkwaro ra xikepe ra un’wana wa vahlaseri lava va ti- whale va le Netherlands, ku katsa ni riendzo ro koma ro ya ni ku vuya elwandle ra Spitzbergen, a ri nga hundzi ngopfu tin’hweti tinharhu, hi ku vula, . naswona loko hi hlayela vavanuna va 30 eka xin’wana ni xin’wana xa swikepe swa vona swa maseyila ya 180, hi ni vatluti va 5 400 va le Low Dutch hinkwavo; hikokwalaho, ndzi ri, hi na kahlekahle migerho yimbirhi ya biya hi munhu, eka mali ya khume mbirhi wa mavhiki, ku nga katsiwi xiphemu xa yena lexi ringaneleke xa 550 wa ti ankers teto ta gin. Sweswi, hambi kuri va-harpooneer lava va gin na biya, va fuddled swinene tani hileswi munhu anga ehleketaka leswaku ava ri xiswona, akuri muxaka lowu faneleke wa vavanuna ku yima enhlokweni ya xikepe, naswona va teka xikongomelo lexinene eka ti whale leti hahaka; leswi a swi ta vonaka swi nga koteki hi ndlela yo karhi. Hambi swi ri tano va va kongomise, naswona va va ba na vona. Kambe leswi a swi ri ekule swinene eN’walungwini, swi nga tsundzukiwa, laha biya yi pfumelelanaka kahle na vumbiwa; ehenhla ka Equator, eka vumaki bya hina bya tihlampfi bya le dzongeni, biya a yi ta va yi faneleka ku endla leswaku muchayeri wa harpooneer a etlela enhlokweni ya mast-head naswona a va ni byala ebyatsweni byakwe; naswona ku lahlekeriwa lokukulu ku nga ha va kona eka Nantucket na New Bedford.

Kambe a ku nge he vi kona; ku vuriwile leswi ringaneke ku kombisa leswaku vaphasi va khale va Madachi va whalasi va malembe-xidzana mambirhi kumbe manharhu lama hundzeke a va ri ni swivindzi leswi tlakukeke; ni leswaku vaphasi va tinhlampfi va Manghezi a va xi honisa xikombiso lexinene swonghasi. Hikuva, va vula leswaku, loko u ri karhi u famba hi xikepe lexi nga riki na nchumu, loko u nga swi koti ku kuma nchumu wo antswa emisaveni, kuma swakudya swa nimadyambu leswinene eka swona, hi xiringaniso. Naswona leswi swi humesa nchumu eka decanter.





NDZIMA YA 102. A Bower eka Arsacides.

Ku fikela sweswi, eku hlamuseleni ka Sperm Whale hi ndlela leyi hlamuselaka, ngopfu-ngopfu ndzi tshame ndzi vulavula hi swihlamariso swa xivumbeko xa yona xa le handle; kumbe hi ku hambana na hi vuxokoxoko eka swivumbeko swin’wana swi nga ri swingani swa xivumbeko xa le ndzeni. Kambe eka ku twisisa lokukulu no sweka loku heleleke ka yena, swi ndzi fanela sweswi ku n’wi pfula swikomba-nkarhi ku ya emahlweni, ni ku pfula tinhla ta hose ya yena, ku pfula ti-garter ta yena, ni ku ntshunxa swikhomo ni mahlo ya mahlangano ya marhambu ya yena ya le ndzeni, lama vekiweke yena emahlweni ka wena eka ultimatum ya yena; hi leswaku, eka marhambu ya yena lama nga riki na swipimelo.

Kambe swi njhani sweswi, Iximayele? Swi tise ku yini, leswaku wena, mufambisi wa swikepe ntsena eka vumaki bya tinhlampfi, u endla onge u tiva nchumu hi swiphemu swa le hansi ka misava swa whale? Xana Stubb la dyondzekeke, la khandziyeke ehenhla ka capstan ya wena, u nyikele tinkulumo ta anatomy ya Cetacea; naswona hi ku pfuniwa hi windlass, khoma rib ya xikombiso leswaku yi kombisiwa? Tihlamusele, wena Iximayele. Xana u nga veka whale leyi kuleke hi ku helela ehenhla ka xitepisi xa wena leswaku yi kamberiwa, tanihi leswi mupheki a dyaka nguluve leyi oxiweke? Hakunene a swi tano. Ku fikela sweswi u ve mbhoni ya ntiyiso, Iximayele; kambe va ni nkhathalelo wa ndlela leyi u ri tirhisaka ha yona lunghelo ra Yonasi a ri yexe; lunghelo ro vulavula hi swirhendzevutana ni swirhendzevutana; swirhendzevutana, ridge-pole, sleepers, na under-pinnings, leswi endlaka furemu ya leviathan; naswona a fana ni swibya swa mirhi, makamara ya masi, swilo swo endla botere ni swilo swo endla tichizi emarhumbyini yakwe.

Ndza pfumela, leswaku ku sukela eka Yonasi, a hi vangani vahlaseri va whale lava ngheneke ekule swinene ehansi ka nhlonge ya whale leyikulu; hambi swi ri tano, ndzi katekisiwile hi lunghelo ro n’wi tsemelela hi xifaniso lexitsongo. Eka xikepe lexi a ndzi ri xa xona, xinyenyana lexitsongo xa Sperm Whale xi tshame xi tlakusiwa emirini xi ya ehenhla ka xitepisi leswaku xi ta poke kumbe nkwama wa xona, ku endla swifunengeto swa ti barbs ta ti harpoons, na tinhloko ta mabanga. U ehleketa leswaku ndzi tshike nkarhi wolowo wu famba, handle ko tirhisa xikepe xa mina xa xikepe na jack-knife, ni ku tshovela xilemo ni ku hlaya hinkwaswo leswi nga endzeni ka xinyenyana xexo lexitsongo?

Naswona loko ku ri vutivi bya mina lebyi kongomeke bya marhambu ya leviathan eka nhluvukiso wa wona lowukulu, lowu kuleke hi ku helela, hikwalaho ka vutivi byebyo lebyi nga tolovelekangiki ndzi kolota munghana wa mina wa vuhosi loyi a feke Tranquo, hosi ya Tranque, un’wana wa Vaarsacides. Hikuva ndziri e Tranque, eka malembe lawa yanga hundza, loko ndzi khomanisiwile na xikepe xa mabindzu xa Dey xa Algiers, ndzi rhambiwile kuva ndzi heta xiphemu xa tiholideyi ta Arsacidean na hosi ya Tranque, eka villa ya yona ya ncindzu leyi nga huma penceni e Pupella; nkova lowu nga etlhelo ka lwandle lowu nga riki kule ngopfu ni leswi vatluti va hina va swi vitaneke Bamboo-Town, ntsindza wa yena.

Exikarhi ka timfanelo tin’wana to tala letinene, munghana wa mina wa vuhosi Tranquo, hi ku va a nyikiwe rirhandzu leri tinyiketeleke eka timhaka hinkwato ta vuharhi bya vuharhi, a a hlanganise ePupella swilo swihi na swihi leswi nga tolovelekangiki leswi vanhu va yena lava nga ni vutlhari swinene va nga swi tumbuluxaka; ngopfu-ngopfu tihunyi leti vatliweke ta switirhisiwa swo hlamarisa, swiphepherhele leswi tsemiweke, mafumu lama pfanganisiweke, swikepe leswi durhaka, swikepe leswi nun’hwelaka; naswona hinkwaswo leswi swi hangalasiwa exikarhi ka swihlamariso swihi na swihi swa ntumbuluko, magandlati lama rhwaleriweke hi swihlamariso, lama hakelaka xibalo a ma hoxe emaribyeni yakwe.

Lexikulu exikarhi ka leswi swo hetelela a ku ri Sperm Whale leyikulu, leyi, endzhaku ka xidzedze xo leha hi ndlela leyi nga tolovelekangiki, a yi kumiwile yi file naswona yi pfaleriwile, nhloko ya yona yi langutane ni murhi wa cocoa-nut, lowu swirhendzevutana swa wona leswi fanaka ni mapapa, leswi nga ni swirhabyani a swi vonaka onge i jet ya yona ya rihlaza. Loko miri lowukulu eku heteleleni wu hluvuriwile swifunengeto swa wona leswi dzikeke ku fika eka fathom, naswona marhambu ya hundzuka ritshuri hi dyambu, kutani marhambu lawa ma yisiwe hi vukheta ku ya ehenhla enkoveni wa Pupella, laha tempele leyikulu ya mincindzu ya vuhosi sweswi a yi ri sirhelela kona.

Timbanga a ti hayekiwe hi swidlodlo; ti- vertebræ a ti vatliwe hi tibuku ta matimu ta le Arsacidean, hi matsalelo lama nga tolovelekangiki; eka khwiri, vaprista a va hlayisa langavi leri nun’hwelaka leri nga timekiki, lerova nhloko ya xihundla yi tlhela yi rhumela xipfalo xa yona xa mpfula; kasi, yi hayekiwile eka rhavi, nhlonge ya le hansi leyi chavisaka a yi rhurhumela ehenhla ka vagandzeri hinkwavo, ku fana ni banga leri hayekiweke hi misisi leri chaviseke Damocles swinene.

A ku ri xivono lexi hlamarisaka. Tihunyi a ti ri ta rihlaza ku fana ni switsotswana swa Icy Glen; mirhi a yi yime ehenhla naswona yi tikukumuxa, yi twa mati ya yona lama hanyaka; misava leyi tirhaka hi matimba ehansi a yi fana ni xiambalo xo luka, lexi nga ni khapete yo saseka eka yona, leyi swirhabyani swa vhinya leswi nga ehansi a swi vumba xirhendzevutana ni swiambalo, naswona swiluva leswi hanyaka a swi vumba swifaniso. Mirhi hinkwayo, ni marhavi ya yona hinkwawo lama rhwaleke; swihlahla hinkwaswo, ni swihlahla swa fern, ni byanyi; moya lowu rhwalaka marungula; hinkwaswo leswi a swi nga tshiki a swi ri karhi swi tirha. Hi ku tirhisa tintambhu ta matluka, dyambu lerikulu a ri vonaka ri ri xihaha-mpfhuka lexi hahaka lexi lukaka rihlaza leri nga karhaliki. Oho, muluki la khomekeke! unseen weaver!—yimanyana!—rito rin’we!—lapi ri khuluka kwihi? i xigodlho xihi lexi xi nga xi deck? hikwalaho ka yini ku tikarhata loku hinkwako loku nga heriki? Vulavula, muluka!—tshama voko ra wena!—kambe rito rin’we na wena! E-e—xihaha-mpfhuka xa xihahampfhuka xa haha—swifaniso swi papamala swi huma emahlweni ka xiambalo xo luka; khapete leyi tsutsumaka hi freshet hi laha ku nga heriki yi rhelela yi famba. Xikwembu xa muluka, xa luka; naswona hi ku luka koloko u pfariwe tindleve, leswaku a nga twi rito leri faka; naswona hi ku rhurhumela koloko, na hina, lava langutaka eka xitirhisiwa xo luka hi pfariwa tindleve; naswona ntsena loko hi baleka hi ta twa marito ya gidi lama vulavulaka ha wona. Hikuva hambi swi ri tano swi tano eka tifektri hinkwato ta swilo leswi vonakaka. Marito lama vulavuriwaka lama nga twiwiki exikarhi ka swipikiri leswi hahaka; marito wolawo lama fanaka ma twiwa kahle handle ka marhangu, ma pfurha ma huma eka swiphepherhele leswi pfulekeke. Hi ndlela yoleyo ku kumiwile swigevenga. Ah, munhu wo fa! kutani, va ni vuxiyaxiya; hikuva hi ndlela yoleyo, eka mpfilumpfilu lowu hinkwawo wa ku lomba ka misava leyikulu, miehleketo ya wena leyi tumbeleke swinene yi nga twiwa ekule.

Sweswi, exikarhi ka xiambalo xa rihlaza, lexi nga tshamisekangiki xa vutomi xa murhi wolowo wa Arsacidean, marhambu lamakulu, yo basa, lama gandzeriwaka a ma etlele ma ri karhi ma wisa—mutivi lonkulu lonkulu! Hambi swi ri tano, loko swirhendzevutana swa rihlaza leswi tshamaka swi lukiwile swi ri karhi swi hlangana ni ku rhurhumela swi n’wi rhendzela, muendli wa matimba a a vonaka a ri muluka wa mano; yena hi yexe a lukiwile hi mirhi ya vhinya; n’hweti yin’wana ni yin’wana hi ehleketa leswaku i rihlaza, ri ri ni rihlaza leri tengeke; kambe yena hi yexe a ri xirho xa marhambu. Vutomi byi hlanganisiwe Rifu; Rifu ri trellised Vutomi; xikwembu lexi nga ni gome xi tekile Vutomi bya vuntshwa, naswona xi n’wi veleke ku vangama ka tinhloko leti govekeke.

Sweswi, loko na Tranquo wa vuhosi ndzi endzele whale leyi yo hlamarisa, kutani ndzi vona khwiri alitari, na musi wo endliwa wu tlhandluka kusuka laha jet ya xiviri yi humeke kona, ndzi hlamarile leswaku hosi yifanele ku teka chapel tani hi nchumu wa vertu. U hlekile. Kambe ngopfu ndzi hlamarile leswaku vaprista va fanele ku hlambanya leswaku jet ya yena ya musi a yi ri ya xiviri. Ndzi famba-famba emahlweni ka marhambu lawa—ndzi brashile mirhi ya vhinya etlhelo—ndzi tshove timbanga—naswona hi bolo ya ntambhu ya Arsacidean, ndzi famba-famba, ndzi rhendzeleka nkarhi wo leha exikarhi ka tikholonade ta rona to tala leti rhendzelekaka, leti nga ni ndzhuti ni ti- arbour. Kambe hi ku hatlisa layini ya mina a yi huma; naswona ndzi yi landzerile endzhaku, ndzi humile eka xivulwa laha ndzi ngheneke kona. A ndzi vonanga nchumu lowu hanyaka endzeni; a ku nga ri na nchumu handle ka marhambu.

Hi ku ndzi tsema nhonga ya rihlaza yo pima, ndzi tlhele ndzi nwela endzeni ka marhambu. Hi ku ya hi miseve ya vona leyi rhurhumeleke khwiri, vaprista va ndzi vonile ndzi ri karhi ndzi teka ku tlakuka ka rimba ro hetelela, “Swi njhani sweswi!” va huwelela; “Dar’st u pima xikwembu xa hina lexi! Sweswo i swa hina.” “Ina, vaprista—kahle-kahle, xana mi n’wi endla nkarhi wo tanihi kwihi?” Kambe endzhaku ka sweswo ku pfuke mphikizano lowukulu exikarhi ka vona, malunghana ni milenge ni tisentimitara; va pandzele swikotela swa un’wana ni un’wana hi tinhonga ta vona ta le jarateni—xikandza lexikulu xi twala—naswona hi ku teka nkarhi wolowo wa nkateko, ndzi hatle ndzi gimeta ku pima ka mina.

Swipimelo leswi sweswi ndzi ringanyeta ku swi veka emahlweni ka n’wina. Kambe xo sungula, a swi rhekhodiwe, leswaku, emhakeni leyi, a ndzi ntshunxekanga ku vula mpimo wihi na wihi lowu ehleketiwaka lowu ndzi wu tsakelaka. Hikuva ku na valawuri va marhambu lava u nga kongomisaka eka vona, ku kambela ku pakanisa ka mina. Va ndzi byela leswaku ku ni Muziyamu ya Leviathanic, eHull, le Nghilandhi, yin’wana ya tindhawu to phasa tinhlampfi ta le tikweni rero, laha ti nga ni swikombiso swin’wana leswinene swa ti- fin-back ni ti- whale tin’wana. Hilaha ku fanaka, ndzi twe leswaku emuziyamu ya le Manchester, eNew Hampshire, va ni leswi vini va yona va swi vitanaka “xikombiso xin’we ntsena lexi hetisekeke xa Greenland kumbe River Whale eUnited States.” Ku tlula kwalaho, endhawini yin’wana eYorkshire, eNghilandhi, Burton Constable hi vito, Sir Clifford Constable wo karhi u ni marhambu ya Sperm Whale, kambe ya mpimo lowu ringaniseriweke, nikatsongo ku nga ri ya vukulu lebyi heleleke bya munghana wa mina King Tranquo .

Eka swiyimo leswimbirhi, ti whale leti pfaleriweke leti marhambu lawa mambirhi a ya ri ya tona, eku sunguleni a ti vuriwa hi vini va tona hikwalaho ka swivangelo leswi fanaka. Hosi Tranquo a khoma ya yena hikuva a yi lava; na Sir Clifford, hikuva a a ri hosi ya ti-seignories ta swiphemu sweswo. Whale ya Sir Clifford yi hlamuseriwile hinkwako; leswaku, ku fana ni bokisi lerikulu ra tidrowara, u ta n’wi pfula ni ku n’wi pfala, eka swiphemu swa yena hinkwaswo swa marhambu—u andlala timbanga ta yena ku fana ni fan leyikulu—u tlhela u rhendzeleka siku hinkwaro ehenhla ka nhlonge yakwe ya le hansi. Swilotlelo swi fanele ku vekiwa eka tin’wana ta tinyangwa ta yena ta mintlhamu ni swipfalo swa yena; naswona mufambisi wa milenge u ta kombisa round vayeni va nkarhi lowu taka na ntlhambi wa swilotlelo etlhelo ka yena. Sir Clifford u ehleketa ku hakela ti pence timbirhi hi ku peep eka gallery leyi hlevetela eka spinal column; threepence ku twa echo leyi nga ekheleni ra cerebellum ya yena; na sixpence eka ku vona loku nga ringanisiwiki na nchumu ku suka enhan’wini ya yena.

Tidimenxini ta marhambu leti sweswi ndzi nga ta ya emahlweni ndzi ti veka ehansi ti kopiwile hi rito hi rito ku suka evokweni ra mina ra xinene, laha ndzi endleke leswaku ti tattoo; tanihi le ka ku famba-famba ka mina ka nhova hi nkarhi wolowo, a ku nga ri na ndlela yin’wana leyi sirhelelekeke yo hlayisa tinhlayo to tano ta nkoka. Kambe loko ndzi tele hi vanhu leswaku ndzi lava ndhawu, naswona ndzi navela leswaku swirho leswin’wana swa miri wa mina swi tshama swi ri tluka leri nga riki na nchumu ra xitlhokovetselo lexi a ndzi xi vumba hi nkarhi wolowo—hi xiringaniso, hi swihi swiphemu leswi nga riki na ti-tattoo leswi nga ha sala—a ndzi tikarhatanga hi tisentimitara leti nga tolovelekangiki; naswona, hakunene, tisentimitara a ti fanelanga ti nghena nikatsongo eka ku pima loku tsakisaka ka whale.





NDZIMA YA 103. Ku pimiwa ka Marhambu ya Whale.

Eka xiyimo xo sungula, ndzi navela ku veka emahlweni ka n’wina xitatimende xo karhi, lexi nga erivaleni, lexi khumbaka xiphemu lexikulu lexi hanyaka xa leviathan leyi, leyi marhambu ya yona hi nga ta ma kombisa hi ku komisa. Xitatimende xo tano xi nga ha tikomba xi pfuna laha.

Hikuya hi xibalo xa vukheta lexi ndzi xi endleke, naswona lexi xiphemu xin’wana ndzi xi seketelaka eka xiringanyeto xa Captain Scoresby, xa makume nkombo wa tithani eka Greenland whale ya vukulu swinene ya makume tsevu wa timitara hiku leha; hi ku ya hi xibalo xa mina xa vukheta, ndzi ri, Sperm Whale ya vukulu lebyikulu, exikarhi ka makume-nhungu-ntlhanu na makume-nhungu wa timitara hi ku leha, na nchumu lowu nga ehansi ka makume mune wa timitara hi ku rhendzeleka ka yona loku heleleke, whale yo tano yi ta tika kwalomu ka makume-nhungu wa tithani; leswaku, loko a hlayela vavanuna va khume-nharhu eka thani, a a ta tlula swinene vaaki lava hlanganisiweke va ximutana hinkwaxo xa vaaki va gidi na dzana.

Xana a wu ehleketi ke leswaku byongo, ku fana ni tihomu leti bohiweke joko, byi fanele ku vekiwa eka leviathan leyi, leswaku yi endla leswaku yi tshukatshuka nikatsongo eka mianakanyo ya munhu wihi na wihi wa misava?

Leswi se hi tindlela to hambana-hambana ndzi veke emahlweni ka wena khwiri rakwe, xihloka xa yena, nhlonge yakwe, meno, ncila, nhloko, tinsiva ni swiphemu swin’wana leswi nweleke mati, sweswi ndzi ta kombisa ntsena leswi tsakisaka ngopfu eka xiphemu lexikulu xa marhambu yakwe lama nga siveleriwangiki. Kambe tanihi leswi khwiri lerikulu ri amukelaka xiphemu lexikulu swinene xa vukulu hinkwabyo bya marhambu; tanihi leswi ku nga xiphemu lexi rharhanganeke swinene ku fikela sweswi; naswona tanihileswi ku nga riki na nchumu lowu faneleke ku phindha-phindhiwa mayelana na yona eka ndzima leyi, a wu fanelanga ku tsandzeka ku yi rhwala emiehleketweni ya wena, kumbe ehansi ka voko ra wena, loko hi ya emahlweni, handle ka sweswo a wu nge kumi mianakanyo leyi heleleke ya xivumbeko xo angarhela lexi hi nga kusuhi no xi languta .

Hiku leha, marhambu ya Sperm Whale e Tranque aya pima makume nkombo mbirhi wa timitara; lerova loko a vekiwile hi ku helela ni ku engeteriwa evuton’wini, u fanele a lehe timitara ta makume-nhungu; hikuva eka whale, marhambu ma lahlekeriwa hi kwalomu ka n’we-xa-ntlhanu hi ku leha loko ma pimanisiwa ni miri lowu hanyaka. Eka timitara leti ta makume nkombo mbirhi, khwiri na nhlonge ya yena a swi vumba kwalomu ka makume mbirhi wa timitara, swi siya kwalomu ka makume ntlhanu wa timitara ta marhambu ya nkolo yo olova. Ku namarhetiwe eka rhambu leri ra nkolo, ku ringana nchumu lowu nga ehansi ka n’we xa nharhu xa ku leha ka wona, a ku ri na xirhundzu xa matimba xa xirhendzevutana xa timbanga lexi khale a xi pfalela swirho swa yena swa nkoka.

Eka mina xifuva lexi lexikulu lexi nga ni timbanga ta timhondzo ta tindlopfu, lexi nga ni nkolo wo leha, lowu nga ntshunxiwangiki, lowu hangalakeke ekule na wona hi layini yo kongoma, a wu fana nikatsongo ni xivumbeko xa xikepe lexikulu lexi ha ku vekiwaka ehenhla ka switoko, loko ku ri kwalomu ka makume-mbirhi ntsena wa xona a va nga ambalanga nchumu ku nghenisiwa ti bow-ribs, naswona keel hi ndlela yin’wana, hi nkarhi wolowo, kambe i mapulanga yo leha, lama nga hlanganisiwangiki.

Timbanga a ti ri khume hi tlhelo rin’we. Xo sungula, ku sungula enhan’wini, a xi lehe kwalomu ka timitara ta tsevu; ya vumbirhi, ya vunharhu ni ya vumune yin’wana ni yin’wana a yi lehe hi ku landzelelana, ku kondza u fika emakumu ka ya vuntlhanu, kumbe yin’wana ya timbanga ta le xikarhi, leti a ti lehe timitara ta tsevu ni tisentimitara tin’wana. Ku sukela eka xiphemu xexo, timbanga leti saleke ti hungutekile, ku fikela loko ya vukhume ni yo hetelela yi anama ntsena timitara ta ntlhanu ni tisentimitara tin’wana. Hi ku angarhela hi ku enta, hinkwato a ti ri ni ku fambisana loku vonakaka ni ku leha ka tona. Timbanga ta le xikarhi a ti ri leti nga ni swirhendzevutana swinene. Eka tin’wana ta ti Arsacides titirhisiwa eka ti beams leti ku vekiwaka mabuloho ya tindlela ta milenge eka swinambyana leswintsongo.

Loko ndzi kambisisa timbanga leti, a ndzi nga ta swi kota ku nga hlaseriwi hi vuntshwa hi xiyimo lexi, lexi phindhiweke hi ndlela yo hambana-hambana ebukwini leyi, lerova marhambu ya whale nikatsongo a hi xivumbeko xa xivumbeko xa yona lexi vekiweke. Leyikulu swinene eka ti ribs ta Tranque, yin’wana yale xikarhi, ayi teka xiphemu xexo xa nhlampfi leyi, evuton’wini, yinga yikulu swinene hiku enta. Sweswi, vuenti lebyikulu bya miri lowu vekiweke wa whale leyi yokarhi byifanele kuva byiri kwalomu ka khume-nhungu wa timitara; kasi, rib leri fambisanaka na rona a ri pima kambe ku tlulanyana timitara ta tsevu. Leswaku ribha leri ri hundzisele ntsena hafu ya mianakanyo ya ntiyiso ya vukulu lebyi hanyaka bya xiphemu xexo. Handle ka sweswo, hi ndlela yo karhi, laha sweswi ndzi voneke ntsena nkolo lowu nga ambalangiki nchumu, hinkwaswo leswi khale a swi phutseriwe hi tithani ta xitalo lexi engeteriweke xa nyama, misiha, ngati ni marhumbu. Ku tlula kwalaho, eka tinsiva leti eneleke, laha ndzi vone mahlangano ma nga ri mangani lama nga hlelekangiki ntsena; naswona ematshan’weni ya swiphepherhele leswi tikaka ni leswikulu, kambe leswi nga riki na marhambu, ku nga ri na nchumu lowu heleleke!

Kutani, ndzi ehlekete leswaku i swa hava ni vuphukuphuku swonghasi, leswaku wanuna la nga fambiki la nga ni tingana a ringeta ku twisisa kahle whale leyi yo hlamarisa, hi ku pfotlosa ntsena ehenhla ka marhambu ya yona lama feke lama tsaneke, lama andlariweke ekhwatini leri ro rhula. Doo, ntsena embilwini ya makhombo lama hatlisaka swinene; ntsena loko yi ri endzeni ka swirhendzevutana swa swiphepherhele swa yena leswi hlundzukeke; ntsena elwandle leri dzikeke leri nga riki na swipimelo, laha whale leyi vekiweke hi ku helela yi nga kumiwaka hakunene ni hi ndlela leyi hanyaka.

Kambe nkolo. Eka sweswo, ndlela leyinene leyi hi nga yi tekelaka enhlokweni i, hi crane, ku hlengeleta marhambu ya yona ehenhla emakumu. Ku hava bindzu leri hatlisaka. Kambe sweswi swi endliwile, swi languteka swinene ku fana na Nsika ya Pompey.

Kuna makume mune na odd vertebræ hinkwato, leti eka marhambu tinga pfaleriwangiki swin’we. Ngopfu-ngopfu ti etlela ku fana ni switina leswikulu leswi nga ni swikhomo leswi nga ehenhla ka xihondzo xa Gothic, ti vumba tikhoso leti tiyeke ta maribye yo tika. Leyikulu swinene, ya le xikarhi, hi ku anama yi ni nchumu lowu nga ehansi ka timitara tinharhu, naswona yi enta ku tlula mune. Leyintsongo swinene, laha nkolo wu rhetaka kuya eka ncila, wu anama ti inches timbirhi ntsena, naswona wu languteka kufana na bolo yo basa ya billiard-ball. Ndzi byeriwe leswaku ka ha ri ni leswitsongo, kambe a swi lahlekile hi swinyenyana swin’wana leswi dyaka vanhu, vana va muprista, lava swi yiveke leswaku va tlanga hi marble. Xisweswo hi vona ndlela leyi nkolo wa hambi ku ri swivumbiwa leswikulu leswi hanyaka wu hungutekaka ha yona eku heteleleni wu va ntlangu wo olova wa vana.





NDZIMA YA 104. Xiharhi xa Fossil Whale.

Ku suka eka vunyingi bya yona bya matimba whale yi nyika nhlokomhaka leyi tsakisaka swinene laha yi nga kurisa, ku kurisa, naswona hi ntolovelo yi nga andlala. Xana a wu ta swi kota, a wu nga ta n’wi tshikilela. Hi timfanelo letinene u fanele ku khomiwa ntsena eka imperial folio. Ku nga ha vuli nakambe tifurlong ta yena ku suka eka xirhendzevutani ku ya eka ncila, na tijarateni leti a ti pimaka hi le xikarhi ka khwiri; ehleketa ntsena hi ku rhendzeleka lokukulu ka marhumbu yakwe, laha ma etleleke eka yena ku fana ni tintambhu letikulu ni swihaha-mpfhuka leswi rhendzelekaka eka orlop-deck ya le hansi ka misava ya line-of-battle-ship.

Tanihi leswi ndzi tiyimiseleke ku khoma Levhiyathani leyi hi ndlela yo biha, swi ndzi fanela ku ti amukela hi ku tiva hinkwaswo ndzi ri loyi a heleleke eka bindzu leri; ku nga languti switsongwatsongwana leswitsongo swa mbewu ya ngati ya yena, ni ku n’wi rhendzeleka ku ya fika eka khoyili leyi heleleke ya marhumbu yakwe. Leswi se yi n’wi hlamuseleke eka vunyingi bya swihlawulekisi swa yena swa sweswi swa vutshamo na anatomical, sweswi swa ha ri ku n’wi kurisa hi langutelo ra vuyimburi, fossiliferous na antediluvian. Loko swi tirhisiwa eka xivumbiwa xin’wana ni xin’wana handle ka Levhiyathani—eka ximanga kumbe ximanga—marito yo tano ya portly ma nga ha tekiwa hi ndlela leyi faneleke tanihi lamakulu hi ndlela leyi nga fanelangiki. Kambe loko Levhiyathani ku ri tsalwa, mhaka ya cinciwa. Fain am I to stagger to this emprise ehansi ka marito yo tika swinene ya xihlamusela-marito. Naswona laha a ku vuriwa, leswaku nkarhi wun’wana na wun’wana loko swi olovile ku vulavurisana na yin’we eka ku famba ka tidissertation leti, ndzi tirhise hi ku nga cinci nkandziyiso lowukulu wa quarto wa Johnson, lowu xaviweke hi ku kongoma hi xikongomelo xexo; hikuva vunyingi bya munhu hi xiyexe lebyi nga tolovelekangiki bya mutsari yoloye wa swihlamusela-marito la dumeke a byi n’wi fanela swinene ku hlengeleta xihlamusela-marito lexi nga ta tirhisiwa hi mutsari wa ti- whale wo fana na mina.

Munhu u tala ku twa hi vatsari lava pfukaka va tlhela va pfimba hi mhaka ya vona, hambi leswi yi nga ha vonakaka yi ri leyi tolovelekeke. Kutani, xana ndzi tsala njhani hi Levhiyathani leyi? Handle ko swi xiya chirography ya mina yi ndlandlamuka yi va ti- placard capitals. Ndzi nyike xiphepherhele xa condor! Ndzi nyike xihlovo xa Vesuvius xa xitshamo xa inki! Vanghana, khomani hi mavoko ya mina! Hikuva eka xiendlo ntsena xo tsala miehleketo ya mina ya Levhiyathani leyi, va ndzi karhata, va ndzi endla ndzi titivala hi ku fikelela ka vona loku heleleke ka ku sweka, onge hi loko va katsa xirhendzevutana hinkwaxo xa sayense, na tinxaka hinkwato ta ti whale, na vanhu, na mastodons, ya khale, ya sweswi, na leyi taka, na ti panorama hinkwato leti rhendzelekaka ta mfumo emisaveni, na le ka vuako hinkwabyo, ku nga katsiwi tindhawu ta wona ta le makaya. Xo tano, naswona lexi kurisaka swinene, i vunene bya nhloko-mhaka leyikulu ni leyi ntshunxekeke! Hi ndlandlamuxa ku ya eka vunyingi bya yona. Leswaku u humesa buku ya matimba, u fanele u hlawula nhloko-mhaka ya matimba. Ku hava vholumo leyikulu ni leyi nga heriki leyi nga tsariwaka eka ximanga, hambileswi ku nga ni vo tala lava yi ringeteke.

Ere ku nghena eka mhaka ya Fossil Whales, ndzi humesa switifiketi swa mina tani hi mutivi wa misava, hi ku vula leswaku eka nkarhi wa mina wo hambana hambana ndzi vile muendli wa maribye, naswona ndzi tlhele ndzi va muceri lonkulu wa migerho, migerho na swihlovo, ti wine-vaults, ti cellars, . ni swihlovo swa mixaka hinkwayo. Hilaha ku fanaka, hi ndlela yo sungula, ndzi navela ku tsundzuxa muhlayi, leswaku hambi leswi eka ti geological strata ta khale ku kumekaka ti fossils ta swiharhi swa nhova leswi sweswi swi lavaka ku nyamalala hi ku helela; masalela lama landzeleke lama kumiweke eka leswi vuriwaka swivumbeko swa Tertiary swi vonaka swi ri swihlanganisi leswi hlanganisaka, kumbe hi mpimo wihi na wihi leswi kavanyetiweke, exikarhi ka swivumbiwa leswi lwisanaka ni nkarhi wo leha, ni leswi ku vuriwaka leswaku vatukulu va swona va le kule va nghene eAreka; ti Fossil Whales hinkwato letinga tshuburiwa kufikela sweswi ti wela eka nkarhi wa Tertiary, leyi kunga yohetelela emahlweni ka swivumbeko swale henhla. Naswona hambi leswi ku nga riki na yin’we ya tona leyi hlamulaka hi ku kongoma eka muxaka wihi na wihi lowu tivekaka wa nkarhi wa sweswi, ya ha fana hi laha ku ringaneke na tona hi ku angarhela, ku lulamisa ku teka xiyimo xa tona tanihi ti fossils ta Cetacean.

Ti fossils leti tshovekeke leti hambanisiweke ta ti whale ta pre-adamite, swiphemu swa marhambu na marhambu ya tona, eka malembe ya makume nharhu lawa ya hundzeke, hi nkarhi wo hambana hambana, ti kumiwile ehansi ka tintshava ta Alps, e Lombardy, e France, e England, e Scotland, na le ka Swifundzha swa Louisiana, Mississippi na Alabama. Exikarhi ka masalela yo tano lama tsakisaka swinene ku ni xiphemu xa khwiri, lexi hi lembe ra 1779 xi nga lahliwaka eRue Dauphine eParis, ku nga xitarata xo koma lexi pfulekeke kwalomu ka hi ku kongoma exigodlhweni xa Tuileries; ni marhambu lama lahliweke loko ku ceriwa swikepe leswikulu swa swikepe swa Antwerp, hi nkarhi wa Napoleon. Cuvier u vule leswaku swiphemu leswi a swi ri swa tinxaka tin’wana ta Leviathanic leti nga tiviwiki nikatsongo.

Kambe ku fikela sweswi lexi hlamarisaka ngopfu eka masalela hinkwawo ya Cetacean a ku ri marhambu lamakulu lama lavaka ku helela ya xivandzana lexi nga nyamalala, lexi kumiweke hi lembe ra 1842, ensin’wini ya Judge Creagh, eAlabama. Mahlonga lama nga pfumeriki lama chavisiweke lama nga ekusuhi ma yi teke tanihi marhambu ya yin’wana ya tintsumi leti weke. Madokodela ya le Alabama ma xi vule leswaku i xihadyana lexikulu lexi hahaka, naswona ma xi nyike vito ra Basilosaurus. Kambe marhambu man’wana ya xikombiso xa yona loko ya yisiwa etlhelo ka lwandle eka Owen, Mutivi wa Mina wa Mina wa Munghezi, swi kume leswaku xihadyana lexi lexi ku vuriwaka leswaku xi hahaka a ku ri xiharhi xa whale, hambileswi a xi ri xa muxaka lowu sukeke. Xifaniso xa nkoka xa mhaka, leyi phindhiweke hi ku phindha-phindha ebukwini leyi, leswaku marhambu ya whale ma nyikela kambe xikombiso xitsongo xa xivumbeko xa miri wa xona lowu vekiweke hi ku helela. Kutani Owen u tlhele a khuvula xiharhi xa nhova lexi vuriwaka Zeuglodon; naswona eka phepha ra yena leri hlayiweke emahlweni ka London Geological Society, u ri vitanile, hi xiviri, xin’wana xa swivumbiwa leswi nga tolovelekangiki leswi ku cinca ka swiaki swa misava ku swi sula leswaku swi nga ha vi kona.

Loko ndzi yima exikarhi ka marhambu lawa ya matimba ya Leviathan, swikandza, tinsiva, tinhlaya, timbanga ni ti- vertebræ, hinkwaswo swi tiveka hi ku fana hi xiphemu ni tinxaka leti nga kona ta swiharhi swa le lwandle; kambe hi nkarhi lowu fanaka hi hala tlhelo ku ri ni ku yelana loku fanaka ni Valevhiyani lava herisiweke lava lwisanaka ni nkarhi wo leha, vadyuhari va vona lava nga hlayekiki; Ndzi, hi ndhambi, ndzi tlheriseriwe endzhaku eka nkarhi wolowo wo hlamarisa, ere nkarhi hi woxe wu nga vuriwa leswaku wu sungurile; hikuva nkarhi wu sungule hi munhu. Laha mpfilumpfilu wa grey wa Saturn wu rhendzeleka ehenhla ka mina, naswona ndzi kuma swivono leswi nga vonakiki, leswi rhurhumelaka eka vutomi byebyo lebyi nga heriki bya Polar; loko swisirhelelo leswi nga ni swisirhelelo swa ayisi swi tshikilele swinene laha sweswi ku nga Tropics; naswona eka tikhilomitara hinkwato ta 25 000 ta ku rhendzeleka ka misava leyi, a ku vonaka ku nga ri na voko leri anameke ra voko leri a ri vonaka. Kutani misava hinkwayo a yi ri ya whale; naswona, hosi ya ntumbuluko, u siye ku pfuka ka yena eka milayeni ya sweswi ya Andes na Himmalehs. I mani loyi a nga kombisaka rixaka ro fana na Levhiyathani? Harpoon ya Akabu a yi halate ngati ya khale ku tlula ya Faro. Methusela u vonaka a ri mufana wa xikolo. Ndzi languta etlhelo leswaku ndzi khomana hi mavoko na Xeme. Ndzi chavisiwa hi vukona lebyi bya antemosaic, lebyi nga riki na xihlovo bya ku chavisa loku nga vulavuriwiki ka whale, loku, tanihileswi a ku ri kona emahlweni ka nkarhi hinkwawo, ku faneleke ku lava ku va kona endzhaku ka loko malembe hinkwawo ya vumunhu ma herile.

Kambe a hi yena ntsena Levhiyathani loyi a siyeke swikombiso swa yena swa le mahlweni ka Adamite eka tipuleti ta ntumbuluko ta stereotype, naswona eka ribye ra kalk na marl u siye ndzhaka ya xivumbeko xa yena xa khale; but upon Egyptian tablets, whose antiquity seems to claim for them an almost fossiliferous character, we find the unmistakable print of his fin. In an apartment of the great temple of Denderah, some fifty years ago, there was discovered upon the granite ceiling a sculptured and painted planisphere, abounding in centaurs, griffins, and dolphins, similar to the grotesque figures on the celestial globe of the moderns. Gliding among them, old Leviathan swam as of yore; was there swimming in that planisphere, centuries before Solomon was cradled.

Nor must there be omitted another strange attestation of the antiquity of the whale, in his own osseous post-diluvian reality, as set down by the venerable John Leo, the old Barbary traveller.

“Not far from the Sea-side, they have a Temple, the Rafters and Beams of which are made of Whale-Bones; for Whales of a monstrous size are oftentimes cast up dead upon that shore. The Common People imagine, that by a secret Power bestowed by God upon the Temple, no Whale can pass it without immediate death. But the truth of the Matter is, that on either side of the Temple, there are Rocks that shoot two Miles into the Sea, and wound the Whales when they light upon 'em. They keep a Whale's Rib of an incredible length for a Miracle, which lying upon the Ground with its convex part uppermost, makes an Arch, the Head of which cannot be reached by a Man upon a Camel's Back. This Rib (says John Leo) is said to have layn there a hundred Years before I saw it. Their Historians affirm, that a Prophet who prophesy'd of Mahomet, came from this Temple, and some do not stand to assert, that the Prophet Jonas was cast forth by the Whale at the Base of the Temple.”

In this Afric Temple of the Whale I leave you, reader, and if you be a Nantucketer, and a whaleman, you will silently worship there.





CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. He had lighted with such energy upon a thwart of his boat that his ivory leg had received a half-splintering shock. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
