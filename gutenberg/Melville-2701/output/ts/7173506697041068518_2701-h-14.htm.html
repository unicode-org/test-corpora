NDZIMA YA 67. Ku tsema In.

A ku ri vusiku bya Mugqivela, naswona Savata yo tano yi landzele! Vaprofesa va ex officio va ku tshova Savata hinkwavo i vahlaseri va whale. Pequod ya timhondzo ta tindlopfu yi hundzuriwile leswi a swi vonaka swi ri xihoxo; mutluti un’wana ni un’wana i muxavisi wa nyama. A wu ta ehleketa leswaku hi nyikela hi khume ra magidi ya tihomu to tshwuka eka swikwembu swa lwandle.

Eku sunguleni, switirhisiwa leswikulu swo tsema, exikarhi ka swilo swin’wana swo tika leswi katsaka ntlawa wa swibokisana leswi hi ntolovelo swi pendiweke hi rihlaza, naswona leswi ku nga riki na wanuna un’we loyi a nga swi tlakusaka—ntlhambi lowu lowukulu wa madiriva a wu rhurhumerisiwa ku ya fika ehenhla-nkulu ivi wu boheleriwe hi matimba eka lower mast-head, ndhawu leyi tiyeke swinene kun’wana ni kun’wana ehenhla ka xitepisi xa xikepe. Makumu ya ntambhu yo fana na hawser leyi rhendzelekaka hi swilo leswi swo rharhangana, kutani yi yisiwa eka windlass, naswona block leyikulu yale hansi ya ti tackles ayi rhendzeleka ehenhla ka whale; eka xibokisana lexi a ku boheleriwe xihuku lexikulu xa blubber, lexi tikaka kwalomu ka dzana ra tiphawundi. Nakona sweswi va hayekiwile hi switeji ehenhla ka tlhelo, Starbuck na Stubb, vanghana va vukati, va hlomile hi swipachi swa vona swo leha, va sungule ku tsema khumbi emirini leswaku ku nghenisiwa xihuku ehenhla nyana ka le kusuhi swinene eka ti side-fins timbirhi. Leswi swi endliwile, layini yo anama, ya xirhendzevutani yi tsemiwa ku rhendzela khumbi, xihuku xi nghenisiwa, naswona miri lowukulu wa vatirhi va le xikepeni wu ba khwayere ya nhova, sweswi wu sungula ku tlakuka eka ntshungu wun’we wo tsindziyela eka windlass. Loko hi ku hatlisa, xikepe hinkwaxo xi rhendzeleka hi tlhelo ra xona; xibolwana xin’wana ni xin’wana lexi nga eka yena xi sungula ku fana ni tinhloko ta swipikiri ta yindlu ya khale eka maxelo lama nga ni xirhami; u rhurhumela, a rhurhumela, naswona u pfumela hi nhloko hi tinhloko ta yena ta mast leti chavaka ti kongoma exibakabakeni. U ya a titshega hi whale, kasi ku tlakuka kun’wana ni kun’wana loku hefemulaka ka windlass ku hlamuriwa hi ku tlakuka loku pfunaka loku humaka eka swidzedze; ku kondza eku heteleleni, ku twakala mpfumawulo wa xihatla, lowu hlamarisaka; hi swash leyikulu xikepe xi rhendzeleka xi ya ehenhla na le ndzhaku ku suka eka whale, naswona xitirhisiwa xo hlula xi tlakuka xi vonaka xi koka endzhaku ka xona makumu ya semicircular lama nga hambanisiwiki ya strip yo sungula ya blubber. Sweswi tani hi leswi blubber yi funengetaka whale kahle kahle tani hileswi rind yi endlaka orange, switlhela yi hluvuriwa emirini kahle kahle tani hileswi orange minkarhi yin’wana yi hluvuriwaka hiku yi spiralizing. Hikuva ntshikelelo lowu tshamaka wu ri karhi wu hlayisiwa hi windlass wu tshama wu ri karhi wu endla leswaku whale yi tshama yi ri karhi yi rhendzeleka ematini, naswona loko blubber eka strip yin’we yi ri karhi yi rhurhumela hi ndlela leyi fanaka eka layini leyi vuriwaka “xikhafu,” hi nkarhi wun’we yi tsemiwa hi swipachi swa Starbuck na Stubb, . vanghana va vukati; naswona hiku hatlisa tani hileswi yi tshoveriwaka xisweswo, naswona hakunene hi xiendlo xexo hi xoxe, nkarhi hinkwawo yi tlakusiwa kuya e henhla kuya ehenhla kufikela loko makumu ya yona yale henhla ya risa main-top; vavanuna lava nga eka xisirhelelo xa moya va tshika ku tlakuka, naswona hi nkarhinyana kumbe timbirhi ntshungu lowu hlamarisaka lowu thonaka ngati wu rhendzeleka wu ya hala ni hala onge hiloko wu hungutiwa wu huma exibakabakeni, naswona un’wana ni un’wana loyi a nga kona u fanele a yingisela swinene ku wu papalata loko wu rhendzeleka, handle ka sweswo a nga ha n’wi boxa tindleve ta yena ivi a n’wi hoxa hi nhloko ehenhla ka lwandle.

Un’wana wa vachayeri va harpoone lava a va ri kona sweswi u ya emahlweni hi tlhari ro leha, leri nga ni vuxiyaxiya leri vuriwaka banga ro khandziya, naswona loko a languta nkarhi wakwe u tsemelela hi vutshila khumbi lerikulu exiphen’wini xa le hansi xa xirhendzevutana lexi tsekatsekaka. Eka khumbi leri, makumu ya xisirhelelo lexikulu xa vumbirhi lexi cincanaka ma tlhela ma khomiwa leswaku ku hlayisiwa ku khoma eka xirhundzu, leswaku ku lunghiseleriwa leswi landzelaka. Hikwalaho, mudlayi loyi wa mabanga la nga ni vutshila, a tsundzuxa mavoko hinkwawo leswaku ma yima, u tlhela a endla ku tsutsuma ka sayense eka misa, naswona hi swilayi swi nga ri swingani swa le tlhelo, leswi heleriweke hi ntshembo, leswi rhurhumelaka, u yi tsema hi ku helela hi swiphemu swimbirhi; lerova loko xiphemu xo koma xa le hansi xa ha hatlisa, xirhendzevutana xo leha xa le henhla, lexi vuriwaka blanket-piece, xi rhendzeleka kahle, naswona hinkwako xi lunghekele ku ehlisa. Va heavers forward sweswi va tlhela va sungula risimu ra vona, naswona loko tackle yin’we yi ri karhi yi rhurhumela no tlakusa strip ya vumbirhi kusuka eka whale, yin’wana yi olovisiwa hakatsongotsongo, naswona ehansi yi ya strip yosungula hi hatchway leyikulu yale hansi swinene, kuya eka parlor leyinga na fanichara leyi vuriwaka the blubber-kamara ra swilo. Eka fulete leyi ya vusiku bya madyambu mavoko yo hambana-hambana lama nga ni vutshila ma tshama ma ri karhi ma rhendzeleka ni xiphemu xo leha xa xibalesa onge hiloko ku ri ntshungu lowukulu lowu hanyaka wa tinyoka leti lukiweke. Nakona hi ndlela leyi ntirho wu ya emahlweni; ti tackles timbirhi ti tlakusa na ku ehlisa hi nkarhi wun’we; havumbirhi bya ti- whale ni ti- windlass ti ri karhi ti tlakusa, va- heaver va yimbelela, vakulukumba va le kamareni ra blubber va ri karhi va rhendzeleka, vanghana va vukati va ri karhi va rhurhumela, xikepe xi ri karhi xi tikarhata naswona mavoko hinkwawo ma rhukana nkarhi na nkarhi, hi ndlela yo hunguta ku holova loku tolovelekeke.





NDZIMA YA 68. Xifunengeto.

Ndzi nyikele nyingiso lowutsongo eka mhaka yoleyo leyi nga karhataka, ku nga nhlonge ya whale. Ndzi vile na minjhekanjhekisano hi swona na vahlayisi va ti whale lava nga na ntokoto lava papamalaka, na vativi va ntumbuluko lava dyondzekeke eribuweni. Mavonelo ya mina yo sungula a ma cincanga; kambe i vonelo ntsena.

Xivutiso hi leswaku, xana nhlonge ya whale yi kwihi naswona yi kwihi? Se wa swi tiva leswaku blubber ya yena i yini. Blubber yaleyo i nchumu wa kufana ka nyama ya homu leyi tiyeke, leyingana mavele lawa yanga kusuhi, kambe ya tiya, ya olova naswona yi hlangana, naswona yisuka eka tsevu kumbe khume kuya eka khume mbirhi na khume ntlhanu wa ti inches hiku enta.

Sweswi, hambi swi nga ri swa vuhunguki eku sunguleni swi nga ha vonaka swi vulavula hi nhlonge ya xivumbiwa xin’wana ni xin’wana tanihi leyi nga ya muxaka wolowo wa ku ringanana ni ku tiya, hambiswiritano entiyisweni leswi a hi minjhekanjhekisano leyi lwisanaka ni ku ehleketiwa ko tano; hikuva a wu nge tlakusi leyara yin’wana yo enta leyi funengetaka ku suka emirini wa whale handle ka blubber yoleyo leyi fanaka; naswona leyara ya le handle swinene leyi funengetaka ya xiharhi xihi na xihi, loko yi tsindziyerile hi ndlela leyi ringaneleke, sweswo swi nga va yini handle ka nhlonge? I ntiyiso, eka ntsumbu lowu nga onhakiki wa whale, u nga ha khwaxa hi voko ra wena nchumu wo olova swinene, lowu nga erivaleni, lowu fanaka hi ndlela yo karhi ni swiphemu swo olova swa isinglass, ntsena wu lava ku olova ni ku olova ku fana ni satin; hi leswaku, loko yinga se omisiwa, loko yinga pfangani naku tiya ntsena, kambe yi tiya swinene naswona yi tshoveka. Ndzi ni swiphemu swo hlayanyana swo tano leswi omisiweke, leswi ndzi swi tirhisaka ku va timaraka eka tibuku ta mina ta ti- whale. Swi le rivaleni, hilaha ndzi vuleke hakona eku sunguleni; naswona hi ku vekiwa ehenhla ka tluka leri kandziyisiweke, minkarhi yin’wana ndzi titsakisile hi ku ehleketa leswaku ri ni nkucetelo lowu kurisaka. Ku nga khathariseki leswaku swi njhani, swa tsakisa ku hlaya hi ti- whale hi ku tirhisa swivoni swa tona, hilaha u nga ha vulaka hakona. Kambe leswi ndzi chayelaka eka swona laha hi leswi. Nchumu wolowo lowu fanaka wo olova swinene, wa isinglass, lowu, ndza pfumela, wu vekisaka miri hinkwawo wa whale, a wu fanelanga ngopfu ku tekiwa tanihi nhlonge ya xivumbiwa, tanihi nhlonge ya nhlonge, hi ndlela yo vulavula; hikuva a swi hlekisa ntsena ku vula, leswaku nhlonge leyi faneleke ya whale leyikulu yi tsindziyerile naswona yi olova ku tlula nhlonge ya n’wana la ha ku velekiwaka. Kambe ku hava swin’wana swa leswi.

Ku ehleketa leswaku blubber i nhlonge ya whale; kutani, loko nhlonge leyi, tanihi le mhakeni ya Sperm Whale leyikulu swinene, yi ta humesa xiphemu lexikulu xa dzana ra migerho ya oyili; naswona, loko ku tekiwa leswaku, hi nhlayo, kumbe ku vula ntiyiso hi ntiko, mafurha wolawo, eka xiyimo xa wona lexi kombisiweke, i ntsena swiphemu swinharhu swa mune, naswona a hi nchumu hinkwawo wa jazi; hikwalaho ku nga ha va ni mianakanyo yin’wana ya vukulu bya xirhendzevutana xexo lexi hanyaka, lexi xiphemu xa xona ntsena xi humesaka tiva ra mati yo tanihi rero. Loko u hlayela khume ra migerho eka thani, u na khume ra tithani eka ntiko wa nete wa tikotara tinharhu ntsena ta swilo swa nhlonge ya whale.

Evuton’wini, vuandlalo lebyi vonakaka bya Sperm Whale a hi byintsongo eka swihlamariso swo tala leswi a swi nyikelaka. Kwalomu ka minkarhi hinkwayo yi tsemakanye hi ndlela yo oblique naswona yi tlhela yi tsemakanya hi swikombiso swo kongoma leswi nga hlayekiki hi nxaxamelo wo tiya, nchumu lowu fanaka ni lowu nga eka swifaniso leswinene ngopfu swa milayeni ya le Italy. Kambe swikombiso leswi a swi vonaki swi tsariwe eka nchumu wa isinglass lowu boxiweke laha henhla, kambe swi vonaka swi voniwa hi wona, onge hiloko swi vatliwe emirini hi woxe. Naswona leswi a hi swona ntsena. Eka swiyimo swin’wana, eka tihlo leri hatlisaka, leri xiyaka, swikombiso sweswo swa milayeni, tanihi le ka xifaniso xa xiviri lexi vatliweke, kambe swi nyika misava ya ku kombisa swifaniso swin’wana swinene. Leswi i swa hieroglyphical; hi leswaku, loko u vitana ti- cypher toleto leti nga twisisekiki emakhumbini ya tiphiramidi hi ti- hieroglyphic, kutani rero i rito leri faneleke ku ri tirhisa eka vuxaka bya sweswi. Hi ku tsundzuka ka mina loku khomekeke ka swifaniso swa hieroglyphic eka Sperm Whale yin’we ngopfu-ngopfu, ndzi hlamarisiwe ngopfu hi pulati leyi yimelaka swimunhuhatwa swa khale swa Maindiya leswi vatliweke eka ti palisades leti dumeke ta hieroglyphic eribuweni ra Upper Mississippi. Ku fana ni maribye wolawo ya xihundla, na wona, whale leyi nga ni mfungho wa xihundla yi tshama yi nga twisiseki. Ku kombeteriwa loku ka maribye ya Maindiya ku ndzi tsundzuxa nchumu wun’wana. Handle ka swiendlakalo swin’wana hinkwaswo leswi ehandle ka Sperm Whale ku swi humesaka, a nga tali ku kombisa nkolo, naswona ngopfu-ngopfu matlhelo ya yena, lama susiweke eka xiphemu lexikulu xa xivumbeko xa ntolovelo xa linear, hikwalaho ka ku khwaxa ko tala ka vusopfa, hinkwaswo ka leswi nga tshamisekangiki, leswi nga hlelekangiki xiphemu. Ndzi fanele ku vula leswaku maribye wolawo ya le New England lama nga eribuweni ra lwandle, lawa Agassiz a anakanyaka ma ri ni swikombiso swa ku hlangana ka tihanyi hi ku khwaxa ni tintshava letikulu ta ayisi leti papamalaka—ndzi fanele ku vula, leswaku maribye wolawo a ma fanelanga ma fananyana ni Sperm Whale eka leswi swo hlawuleka. Nakambe swi vonaka eka mina leswaku ku khwaxa ko tano eka whale kumbexana ku endliwa hi ku hlangana hi vulala ni ti whale tin’wana; hikuva ndzi ti xiye ngopfu eka tinkunzi letikulu, leti kuleke hi ku helela ta muxaka wolowo.

Rito rin’wana kumbe mambirhi malunghana ni mhaka leyi ya nhlonge kumbe xirhumbana xa whale. Se ku vuriwile, leswaku yi hluvuriwa eka yena hi swiphemu swo leha, leswi vuriwaka swiphemu swa swiambalo. Kufana na ti sea-terms totala, leyi ya tsakisa swinene naswona ya nkoka. Hikuva hakunene whale yi phutseriwe hi blubber ya yona ku fana ni le ka xifunengeto xa xiviri kumbe counterpane; kumbe, ku antswa swinene, poncho ya Muindiya yi rhelerile ehenhla ka nhloko ya yena, naswona yi rhendzeleka na makumu ya yena. Hikwalaho ka ku funengetiwa loku ko rhula ka miri wa yena, laha whale yi endlaka leswaku yi kota ku tshama yi ntshunxekile eka maxelo hinkwawo, malwandle hinkwawo, minkarhi hinkwayo ni magandlati hinkwawo. Xana a ku ta humelela yini hi whale ya le Greenland, hi ku vula, emalwandle wolawo lama rhurhumelaka, lama nga ni ayisi ya le N’walungwini, loko a yi nga phakeriwi surtout ya yona yo rhula? I ntiyiso leswaku tinhlampfi tin’wana ti kumeka ti ri ni rivilo lerikulu ematini wolawo ya le Hyperborean; kambe leti, hambi swi xiyiwa, i tinhlampfi ta wena ta ngati yo titimela, leti nga riki na mahahu, leti tikhwiri ta tona hi toxe ku nga tifiriji; swivumbiwa, leswi kufumelaka ehansi ka xidzedze xa ntshava ya ayisi, tanihi leswi mufambi hi xixika a a ta titsakisa emahlweni ka ndzilo wa ndhawu ya vaendzi; kasi, ku fana ni munhu, whale yi ni mahahu ni ngati yo kufumela. Freeze ngati ya yena, kutani a fa. Swi hlamarisa swonghasi hi nkarhi wolowo—handle ka endzhaku ka nhlamuselo—leswaku xivandzana lexi lexikulu, lexi eka xona ku kufumela ka miri ku nga lavekiki ku fana ni le ka munhu; swa tsakisa swonghasi leswaku a kumeka ekaya, a nghenisiwa emilon’wini yakwe vutomi hinkwabyo ematini wolawo ya le Arctic! laha, loko vatluti va wela ehenhla ka lwandle, minkarhi yin’wana va kumekaka, endzhaku ka tin’hweti, va titimela hi ku kongoma etimbilwini ta masimu ya ayisi, tanihi leswi xinyenyana xi kumekaka xi namarhetiwe hi amber. Kambe lexi hlamarisaka ngopfu i ku tiva, hilaha swi kombisiweke hakona hi ku ringeta, leswaku ngati ya Polar whale ya kufumela ku tlula ya Borneo negro hi ximumu.

Swi vonaka eka mina, leswaku laha hi vona vunene lebyi nga tolovelekangiki bya matimba ya munhu hi xiyexe yo tiya, na vunene lebyi nga tolovelekangiki bya marhangu yo tiya, na vunene lebyi nga tolovelekangiki bya ku anama ka le ndzeni. Oho, wanuna! titsakele ni ku tiendlela xikombiso xa whale! Na wena, tshama u kufumela exikarhi ka ayisi. Xana na wena, hanya emisaveni leyi u nga ri wa yona. Vana lava kufumelaka eka equator; hlayisa mati ya wena ya ngati eka Pole. Ku fana ni dome leyikulu ya St. Peter’s, ni ku fana ni whale leyikulu, hlayisa, Wena munhu! hi tinguva hinkwato ku hisa ka wena.

Kambe swa olova ni ku pfumala ntshembo swonghasi ku dyondzisa swilo leswi leswinene! Eka ku pfuka ka miri, i vatsongo swinene lava nga ni domed ku fana ni ya St. ya swivumbiwa, swi nga ri swingani swonghasi leswikulu ku fana ni whale!





NDZIMA YA 69. Xilahlo.

“Koka hi tinketana! Ntsumbu wu tshike wu ya endzhaku!”

Ti tackles letikulu sweswi ti endle ntirho wa tona. Miri wo basa lowu tshoveriweke wa whale leyi tsemiweke nhloko wu voninga ku fana ni sirha ra marble; hambileswi yi cinceke hi muhlovo, a yi lahlekeriwanga hi nchumu hi xitalo hi ndlela leyi vonakaka. Ya ha ri leyikulu swinene. Hakatsongo-tsongo yi papamala yi ya yi famba, mati lama yi rhendzeleke ma handzuriwa ni ku khuluka hi tishaka leti nga enerisiki, naswona moya lowu nga ehenhla wu karhatiwa hi ku haha ​​loku nga ni vuxiyaxiya ka swinyenyana leswi huwelelaka, leswi milomu ya swona yi fanaka ni ti- poniard to tala swinene leti rhuketelaka eka whale. Phantom leyikulu yo basa leyi nga riki na nhloko yi papamala yi ya emahlweni yi suka exikepeni, naswona nhonga yin’wana ni yin’wana leyi yi papamalaka hi ndlela yoleyo, leswi vonakaka swi ri swikwere swa tishaka ni swikhubiki swa swinyenyana, swi engetela mpfumawulo lowu dlayaka. Ku ringana tiawara to tala ku suka eka xikepe lexi lavaka ku yima xivono xexo xo nyenyetsa xa voniwa. Ehansi ka xibakabaka xa azure lexi nga riki na mapapa ni lexi olovaka, ehenhla ka xikandza xo saseka xa lwandle leri tsakisaka, leri tshukatshukaka hi mimoya yo tsakisa, ntshungu wolowo lowukulu wa rifu wu papamala wu ya emahlweni wu ya emahlweni, ku kondza wu lahleka eka mavonelo lama nga heriki.

Kuna xilahlo lexi nga ni gome swinene ni lexi hlekulaka swinene! Ti- sea-vulture hinkwato ti ri eku rileni ka vukhongeri, ti- air-shark hinkwato hi nkarhi hi nkarhi ti ambale ntima kumbe hi swivati. Evuton’wini kambe a hi vangani va vona lava a va ta va va pfunile whale, I ween, loko kumbexana a a yi lava; kambe eka nkhuvo wa xilahlo xa yena va pounce hi ku chava Xikwembu swinene. Oho, vu-vultureism byo chavisa bya misava! leyi ku nga riki whale ya matimba swinene leyi ntshunxekeke eka yona.

Naswona lawa a hi makumu. Hambileswi miri wu nyamisiweke, xipoko lexi rihiselaka xa pona ivi xi rhendzeleka ehenhla ka wona leswaku xi chavisa. Ku hlotiwa hi munhu un’wana wa nyimpi la nga ni tingana kumbe xikepe xo tshubula lexi hoxeke lexi humaka ekule, loko mpfhuka lowu fihlaka swinyenyana leswi rhurhumelaka, hambi swi ri tano wa ha kombisa nhlayo yo basa leyi papamalaka edyambyini, ni xifafazeri xo basa lexi tlakukeke ehenhla eka xona; hi ku kongoma ntsumbu wa whale lowu nga vavisiki, lowu nga ni tintiho leti rhurhumelaka wu vekiwile ehansi eka timhandzi— swirhendzevutana, maribye ni swiphepherhele laha: tivoneleni! Naswona ku ringana malembe endzhaku ka sweswo, kumbexana, swikepe swi papalata ndhawu leyi; ku tlula ehenhla ka yona tanihi leswi tinyimpfu ta vuphukuphuku ti tlulaka ehenhla ka xigwitsirisi, hikuva murhangeri wa tona eku sunguleni a a tlula kwalaho loko ku khomiwe nhonga. Kuna nawu wa wena wa swikombiso; ku na ku pfuna ka wena ka mindhavuko; ku na xitori xa ku pona ka wena hi ku nonoka ka ripfumelo ra khale leri nga si tshamaka ri fika ehansi emisaveni, naswona sweswi a ri nga ri na hambi ku ri ku rhendzeleka emoyeni! Ku ni vukhongeri bya orthodoxy!

Xisweswo, hambileswi evuton’wini miri wa whale leyikulu wu nga ha vaka wu chavisa hakunene eka valala va yona, eku feni ka yona xipoko xa xona xi hundzuka ku chava loku nga riki na matimba eka misava.

Xana u mupfumeri eka swipoko, munghana wa mina? Kuna swipoko swin’wana handle ka xa Cock-Lane, na vavanuna lava dzikeke swinene kutlula Dokodela Johnson lava pfumelaka eka swona.





NDZIMA YA 70. Xifunengeto xa Sphynx.

A swi nga fanelanga swi tshikiwa leswaku emahlweni ko hluvula leviathan hi ku helela emirini, u tsemiwile nhloko. Sweswi, ku tsemiwa ka nhloko ya Sperm Whale i ntirho wa sayense wa anatomical, lowu madokodela ya vuhandzuri bya whale lama nga ni ntokoto ma tinyungubyisaka swinene ha wona: naswona a hi handle ka xivangelo.

Xiya leswaku whale a yi na nchumu lowu nga vuriwaka nhamu hi laha ku faneleke; ku hambana na sweswo, laha nhloko na miri wa yena swi vonakaka swi hlangana, kwalaho, eka yona ndhawu yoleyo, ku na xiphemu xo tiya swinene xa yena. Nakambe tsundzuka leswaku dokodela wa vuhandzuri u fanele a endla vuhandzuri a ri ehenhla, kwalomu ka timitara ta tsevu kumbe khume a nghenelela exikarhi ka yena ni munhu loyi a n’wi langutaka, naswona munhu yoloye a lava ku tumbetiwa elwandle leri hundzukeke muhlovo, leri rhendzelekaka naswona hakanyingi ri nga ni mpfilumpfilu ni ku pfurha. CHAPTER 72. The Monkey-Rope.

In the tumultuous business of cutting-in and attending to a whale, there is much running backwards and forwards among the crew. Now hands are wanted here, and then again hands are wanted there. There is no staying in any one place; for at one and the same time everything has to be done everywhere. It is much the same with him who endeavors the description of the scene. We must now retrace our way a little. It was mentioned that upon first breaking ground in the whale's back, the blubber-hook was inserted into the original hole there cut by the spades of the mates. But how did so clumsy and weighty a mass as that same hook get fixed in that hole? It was inserted there by my particular friend Queequeg, whose duty it was, as harpooneer, to descend upon the monster's back for the special purpose referred to. But in very many cases, circumstances require that the harpooneer shall remain on the whale till the whole flensing or stripping operation is concluded. The whale, be it observed, lies almost entirely submerged, excepting the immediate parts operated upon. So down there, some ten feet below the level of the deck, the poor harpooneer flounders about, half on the whale and half in the water, as the vast mass revolves like a tread-mill beneath him. On the occasion in question, Queequeg figured in the Highland costume—a shirt and socks—in which to my eyes, at least, he appeared to uncommon advantage; and no one had a better chance to observe him, as will presently be seen.

Being the savage's bowsman, that is, the person who pulled the bow-oar in his boat (the second one from forward), it was my cheerful duty to attend upon him while taking that hard-scrabble scramble upon the dead whale's back. You have seen Italian organ-boys holding a dancing-ape by a long cord. Just so, from the ship's steep side, did I hold Queequeg down there in the sea, by what is technically called in the fishery a monkey-rope, attached to a strong strip of canvas belted round his waist.

It was a humorously perilous business for both of us. For, before we proceed further, it must be said that the monkey-rope was fast at both ends; fast to Queequeg's broad canvas belt, and fast to my narrow leather one. So that for better or for worse, we two, for the time, were wedded; and should poor Queequeg sink to rise no more, then both usage and honor demanded, that instead of cutting the cord, it should drag me down in his wake. So, then, an elongated Siamese ligature united us. Queequeg was my own inseparable twin brother; nor could I any way get rid of the dangerous liabilities which the hempen bond entailed.

So strongly and metaphysically did I conceive of my situation then, that while earnestly watching his motions, I seemed distinctly to perceive that my own individuality was now merged in a joint stock company of two; that my free will had received a mortal wound; and that another's mistake or misfortune might plunge innocent me into unmerited disaster and death. Therefore, I saw that here was a sort of interregnum in Providence; for its even-handed equity never could have so gross an injustice. And yet still further pondering—while I jerked him now and then from between the whale and ship, which would threaten to jam him—still further pondering, I say, I saw that this situation of mine was the precise situation of every mortal that breathes; only, in most cases, he, one way or other, has this Siamese connexion with a plurality of other mortals. If your banker breaks, you snap; if your apothecary by mistake sends you poison in your pills, you die. True, you may say that, by exceeding caution, you may possibly escape these and the multitudinous other evil chances of life. But handle Queequeg's monkey-rope heedfully as I would, sometimes he jerked it so, that I came very near sliding overboard. Nor could I possibly forget that, do what I would, I only had the management of one end of it.*

*The monkey-rope is found in all whalers; but it was only in the Pequod that the monkey and his holder were ever tied together. This improvement upon the original usage was introduced by no less a man than Stubb, in order to afford the imperilled harpooneer the strongest possible guarantee for the faithfulness and vigilance of his monkey-rope holder.

I have hinted that I would often jerk poor Queequeg from between the whale and the ship—where he would occasionally fall, from the incessant rolling and swaying of both. But this was not the only jamming jeopardy he was exposed to. Unappalled by the massacre made upon them during the night, the sharks now freshly and more keenly allured by the before pent blood which began to flow from the carcass—the rabid creatures swarmed round it like bees in a beehive.

And right in among those sharks was Queequeg; who often pushed them aside with his floundering feet. A thing altogether incredible were it not that attracted by such prey as a dead whale, the otherwise miscellaneously carnivorous shark will seldom touch a man.

Nevertheless, it may well be believed that since they have such a ravenous finger in the pie, it is deemed but wise to look sharp to them. Accordingly, besides the monkey-rope, with which I now and then jerked the poor fellow from too close a vicinity to the maw of what seemed a peculiarly ferocious shark—he was provided with still another protection. Suspended over the side in one of the stages, Tashtego and Daggoo continually flourished over his head a couple of keen whale-spades, wherewith they slaughtered as many sharks as they could reach. This procedure of theirs, to be sure, was very disinterested and benevolent of them. They meant Queequeg's best happiness, I admit; but in their hasty zeal to befriend him, and from the circumstance that both he and the sharks were at times half hidden by the blood-muddled water, those indiscreet spades of theirs would come nearer amputating a leg than a tail. But poor Queequeg, I suppose, straining and gasping there with that great iron hook—poor Queequeg, I suppose, only prayed to his Yojo, and gave up his life into the hands of his gods.

Well, well, my dear comrade and twin-brother, thought I, as I drew in and then slacked off the rope to every swell of the sea—what matters it, after all? Are you not the precious image of each and all of us men in this whaling world? That unsounded ocean you gasp in, is Life; those sharks, your foes; those spades, your friends; and what between sharks and spades you are in a sad pickle and peril, poor lad.

But courage! there is good cheer in store for you, Queequeg. For now, as with blue lips and blood-shot eyes the exhausted savage at last climbs up the chains and stands all dripping and involuntarily trembling over the side; the steward advances, and with a benevolent, consolatory glance hands him—what? Some hot Cognac? No! hands him, ye gods! hands him a cup of tepid ginger and water!

“Ginger? Do I smell ginger?” suspiciously asked Stubb, coming near. “Yes, this must be ginger,” peering into the as yet untasted cup. Then standing as if incredulous for a while, he calmly walked towards the astonished steward slowly saying, “Ginger? ginger? and will you have the goodness to tell me, Mr. Dough-Boy, where lies the virtue of ginger? Ginger! is ginger the sort of fuel you use, Dough-boy, to kindle a fire in this shivering cannibal? Ginger!—what the devil is ginger? Sea-coal? firewood?—lucifer matches?—tinder?—gunpowder?—what the devil is ginger, I say, that you offer this cup to our poor Queequeg here.”

“There is some sneaking Temperance Society movement about this business,” he suddenly added, now approaching Starbuck, who had just come from forward. “Will you look at that kannakin, sir: smell of it, if you please.” Then watching the mate's countenance, he added, “The steward, Mr. Starbuck, had the face to offer that calomel and jalap to Queequeg, there, this instant off the whale. Is the steward an apothecary, sir? and may I ask whether this is the sort of bitters by which he blows back the life into a half-drowned man?”

“I trust not,” said Starbuck, “it is poor stuff enough.”

“Aye, aye, steward,” cried Stubb, “we'll teach you to drug a harpooneer; none of your apothecary's medicine here; you want to poison us, do ye? You have got out insurances on our lives and want to murder us all, and pocket the proceeds, do ye?”

“It was not me,” cried Dough-Boy, “it was Aunt Charity that brought the ginger on board; and bade me never give the harpooneers any spirits, but only this ginger-jub—so she called it.”

“Ginger-jub! you gingerly rascal! take that! and run along with ye to the lockers, and get something better. I hope I do no wrong, Mr. Starbuck. It is the captain's orders—grog for the harpooneer on a whale.”

“Enough,” replied Starbuck, “only don't hit him again, but—”

“Oh, I never hurt when I hit, except when I hit a whale or something of that sort; and this fellow's a weazel. What were you about saying, sir?”

“Only this: go down with him, and get what thou wantest thyself.”

When Stubb reappeared, he came with a dark flask in one hand, and a sort of tea-caddy in the other. The first contained strong spirits, and was handed to Queequeg; the second was Aunt Charity's gift, and that was freely given to the waves.





CHAPTER 73. Stubb and Flask kill a Right Whale; and Then Have a Talk over Him.

It must be borne in mind that all this time we have a Sperm Whale's prodigious head hanging to the Pequod's side. But we must let it continue hanging there a while till we can get a chance to attend to it. For the present other matters press, and the best we can do now for the head, is to pray heaven the tackles may hold.

Now, during the past night and forenoon, the Pequod had gradually drifted into a sea, which, by its occasional patches of yellow brit, gave unusual tokens of the vicinity of Right Whales, a species of the Leviathan that but few supposed to be at this particular time lurking anywhere near. And though all hands commonly disdained the capture of those inferior creatures; and though the Pequod was not commissioned to cruise for them at all, and though she had passed numbers of them near the Crozetts without lowering a boat; yet now that a Sperm Whale had been brought alongside and beheaded, to the surprise of all, the announcement was made that a Right Whale should be captured that day, if opportunity offered.

Nor was this long wanting. Tall spouts were seen to leeward; and two boats, Stubb's and Flask's, were detached in pursuit. Pulling further and further away, they at last became almost invisible to the men at the mast-head. But suddenly in the distance, they saw a great heap of tumultuous white water, and soon after news came from aloft that one or both the boats must be fast. An interval passed and the boats were in plain sight, in the act of being dragged right towards the ship by the towing whale. So close did the monster come to the hull, that at first it seemed as if he meant it malice; but suddenly going down in a maelstrom, within three rods of the planks, he wholly disappeared from view, as if diving under the keel. “Cut, cut!” was the cry from the ship to the boats, which, for one instant, seemed on the point of being brought with a deadly dash against the vessel's side. But having plenty of line yet in the tubs, and the whale not sounding very rapidly, they paid out abundance of rope, and at the same time pulled with all their might so as to get ahead of the ship. For a few minutes the struggle was intensely critical; for while they still slacked out the tightened line in one direction, and still plied their oars in another, the contending strain threatened to take them under. But it was only a few feet advance they sought to gain. And they stuck to it till they did gain it; when instantly, a swift tremor was felt running like lightning along the keel, as the strained line, scraping beneath the ship, suddenly rose to view under her bows, snapping and quivering; and so flinging off its drippings, that the drops fell like bits of broken glass on the water, while the whale beyond also rose to sight, and once more the boats were free to fly. But the fagged whale abated his speed, and blindly altering his course, went round the stern of the ship towing the two boats after him, so that they performed a complete circuit.

Meantime, they hauled more and more upon their lines, till close flanking him on both sides, Stubb answered Flask with lance for lance; and thus round and round the Pequod the battle went, while the multitudes of sharks that had before swum round the Sperm Whale's body, rushed to the fresh blood that was spilled, thirstily drinking at every new gash, as the eager Israelites did at the new bursting fountains that poured from the smitten rock.
