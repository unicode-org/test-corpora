NDZIMA YA 33. Va-Specksnyder.

Malunghana na vaofisiri va whale-craft, leyi yivonaka yiri ndzhawu ya kahle kufana na yin’wana na yin’wana yo veka ehansi peculiarity nyana yale kaya eka ship-board, leswi humaka eka vukona bya ntlawa wa harpooneer wa maphorisa, ntlawa lowu awu tiveki kahle kahle eka marine yin’wana na yin’wana kutlula swikepe leswi vuriwaka whale-fleet.

Nkoka lowukulu lowu vekiweke eka ku vitaniwa ka harpooneer wu kombisiwa hi mhaka ya leswaku ekusunguleni eka Dutch Fishery ya khale, eka malembe xidzana mambirhi na kutlula lawa yanga hundza, vulawuri bya xikepe xa whale abyi nga ngheni hiku hetiseka eka munhu loyi sweswi a vuriwaka captain, kambe abyi avanyisiwile exikarhi yena ni phorisa leri a ri vitaniwa va ka Specksnyder. Hi ku kongoma rito leri ri vula Fat-Cutter; ku tirhisiwa, hambiswiritano, hi ku famba ka nkarhi swi endle leswaku yi ringana na Chief Harpooneer. Emasikwini wolawo, vulawuri bya mufambisi wa xikepe a byi pimiwile eka ku fambisa xikepe ni ku lawula hi ku angarhela; loko a ri ehenhla ka ndzawulo yo hlota ti whale ni swilo hinkwaswo leswi yi swi karhataka, Specksnyder kumbe Chief Harpooneer a yi fuma. Eka British Greenland Fishery, ehansi ka vito leri onhakeke ra Specksioneer, mutirhela-mfumo loyi wa khale wa Dutch wa ha hlayisiwile, kambe xindzhuti xa yena xa khale xa hungutiwile hi ndlela yo vava. Sweswi u le ka xiyimo xa le henhla ntsena tanihi Harpooneer lonkulu; naswona tani hi leswi, i yin’wana ntsena ya ti subalterns ta le hansi swinene ta captain. Hambi swiritano, tani hi mahanyelo ya kahle ya va harpooneers ku humelela ka riendzo ro hlota ti whale swiya ngopfu, naswona tani hileswi eka American Fishery angari ntsena mutirhela mfumo wa nkoka eka xikepe, kambe ehansi ka swiyimo swokarhi (ku rindza vusiku eka ndzhawu yo phasa ti whale) xileriso ya xikepe xa le henhla na yona i ya yena; hikwalaho xivuriso lexikulu xa tipolitiki xa lwandle xi lava, leswaku hi vito u fanele ku hanya a hambana na vavanuna lava nga emahlweni ka mast, naswona hi ndlela yo karhi a hambanisiwa tanihi mufambisi wa vona wa xiphurofexinali; hambi leswi minkarhi hinkwayo, hi vona, hi ku toloveleka va tekiwaka tanihi lava ringanaka na vona eka ntshamisano.

Sweswi, ku hambana lokukulu loku dirowiweke exikarhi ka phorisa ni munhu elwandle, hi loku—wo sungula u hanya endzhaku, wo hetelela wu hanya emahlweni. Hikwalaho, eka swikepe swa whale ni vaxavisi hi ku fanana, vanghana va vukati va ni tindhawu to tshama eka tona ni mufambisi wa xikepe; naswona hi ndlela yaleyo, na swona, eka vunyingi bya vaphasi va ti whale va le Amerika va-harpooneer va tshama eka xiphemu xa le ndzhaku xa xikepe. Hi leswaku, va teka swakudya swa vona eka khabini ya captain, va etlela endhawini leyi nga kongomangiki va vulavurisana na yona.

Hambi leswi nkarhi wo leha wa riendzo ro hlota ti whale ta le Dzongeni (ku nga ri khale swinene eka maendzo hinkwawo ya sweswi kumbe lama tshameke ma endliwa hi munhu), makhombo yo hlawuleka ya rona, na vaaki va ntsakelo lava nga kona exikarhi ka khamphani, lava hinkwavo, va le henhla kumbe va le hansi, va titshegeke hikwalaho ka vuyelo bya vona, ku nga ri hi muholo lowu vekiweke, kambe hikwalaho ka nkateko wa vona lowu tolovelekeke, swin’we ni ku xalamuka ka vona loku tolovelekeke, ku pfumala xivindzi ni ku tirha hi matimba; hambileswi swilo leswi hinkwaswo swi endlaka eka swiyimo swin’wana swi talaka ku tswala ndzayo leyi nga nyawuriki ku tlula eka vaxavisi hi ku angarhela; kambe, ku nga khathariseki ndlela leyi ku fana ni ndyangu wa khale wa le Mesopotamiya ha yona vahlaseri lava va ti- whale, eka swiyimo swin’wana swa khale, va nga ha tshama swin’we; eka hinkwaswo sweswo, swilo swa le handle leswi nga ni nkarhi, hi xiringaniso, swa kotara-deki a swi tali ku ntshunxeka hi tlhelo ra swilo leswi vonakaka, naswona eka xiyimo xihi na xihi a swi herisiwi. Hakunene, swo tala i swikepe swa Nantucket leswi eka swona u nga ta vona mufambisi wa xikepe a ri karhi a parade quarter-deck ya yena hi vukulu lebyi tsakisaka lebyi nga tluriwiki eka vuthu rihi na rihi ra le matini ra masocha; e-e, ku koxa kwalomu ka ku xiximiwa lokukulu ka le handle ku fana ni loko a ambale xivunguvungu xa vuhosi, naswona ku nga ri lapi ro khwaxa swinene ra lapi ra muchayeri wa xihaha-mpfhuka.

Naswona hambi leswi eka vavanuna hinkwavo mukaputeni wa xikepe xa Pequod loyi a nga ni miehleketo a a nga nyikiwanga ngopfu eka muxaka wolowo wa ku ehleketa loku nga ehansi swinene; naswona hambileswi ku xiximeka ntsena loku a tshameke a ku lava, a ku ri ku yingisa loku nga kongomangiki, ka xihatla; hambileswi a a nga lavi munhu leswaku a susa tintangu emilengeni yakwe a nga si kandziya ehenhla ka kotara ya xitepisi; naswona hambileswi a ku ri ni minkarhi leyi, hikwalaho ka swiyimo swo hlawuleka leswi fambisanaka ni swiendlakalo leswi nga ta hlamuseriwa hi vuxokoxoko, a swi vulavuleke hi marito lama nga tolovelekangiki, hambi ku ri ya ku titsongahata kumbe hi ku chavisa , kumbe hi ndlela yin’wana; kambe hambi ku ri Kaputeni Akabu a a nga swi xiyi nikatsongo swivumbeko ni matirhiselo ya lwandle lamakulu.

Naswona, kumbexana, a swi nge tsandzeki ku hetelela swi voniwe, leswaku endzhaku ka swivumbeko sweswo ni matirhiselo, hi ndlela yo fanekisela, minkarhi yin’wana a a tifihla; hi xihoxo ku ti tirhisa eka swikongomelo swin’wana na swa le xihundleni ku tlula leswi a swi kunguhatiwile ku swi tirhela hi nawu. Vusultan byebyo byo karhi bya byongo bya yena, lebyi hi ndlela yin’wana a byi ri hi mpimo lowunene a byi nga vonaki; hi ku tirhisa swivumbeko sweswo vusultan lebyi fanaka byi ve bya nyama eka vulawuri bya tihanyi lebyi nga siveriwiki. Hikuva ku va ku tlakuka ka vutlhari ka munhu hi ndlela leyi swi nga ta va xiswona, a yi nge pfuki yi teke vukulukumba lebyi tirhaka, lebyi kumekaka eka vavanuna van’wana, handle ko pfuniwa hi muxaka wo karhi wa vutshila bya le handle ni ku dzima timitsu, minkarhi hinkwayo, hi yoxe, ku tlula kumbe ku tlula loku nga nyawuriki ni ku va ehansi. Leswi hi swona, leswi hi laha ku nga heriki swi hlayisaka tihosana ta ntiyiso ta Xikwembu ta Mfumo eka ku hlongoriwa ka misava; naswona yi siya ku xiximiwa loku tlakukeke loku moya lowu wu nga ku nyikaka, eka vavanuna volavo lava dumeke ngopfu hi ku va va ri ehansi loku nga heriki eka voko leri fihliweke leri hlawuriweke ra Divine Inert, ku tlula hi ku tlakuka ka vona loku nga kanakanisiki eka mpimo lowu feke wa vunyingi. Vunene lebyikulu swonghasi byi tumbele eka swilo leswi leswitsongo loko vukholwa-hava lebyi hundzeletiweke bya tipolitiki byi byi vekisa, lerova eka swiyimo swin’wana swa vuhosi hambi ku ri eka vuphukuphuku bya swiphukuphuku byi nyike matimba. Kambe loko, ku fana ni le mhakeni ya Nicholas the Czar, harhi leyi nga ni swirhendzevutana ya mfumo wa ntivo-misava yi rhendzele byongo bya vuhosi; kutani, mintlhambi ya plebeian yi korhama yi titshege emahlweni ka ku vekiwa exikarhi lokukulu. Naswona, mutsari wa mintlangu wa khombo loyi a a ta kombisa ku nga lawuleki loku faka hi ku sweka ka kona loku heleleke ni ku rhendzeleka loku kongomeke, a nge pfuki a rivale xitsundzuxo, lexi hi xihoxo xi nga xa nkoka swinene eka vutshila bya yena, tanihi lexi sweswi ku vulavuriwaka ha xona.

Kambe Akabu, Kaputeni wa mina, wa ha famba emahlweni ka mina hi ku vilela ka yena hinkwako ka Nantucket na ku rhurhumela; naswona eka xiphemu lexi lexi khumbaka Vafumi na Tihosi, a ndzi fanelanga ku tumbeta leswaku ndzi na mhaka ntsena na xisiwana xa khale xa muhloti wa ti whale ku fana na yena; naswona, hikwalaho, swirhangiso hinkwaswo swa le handle swa vukulukumba ni tindlu swi ariwile mina. Oho, Akabu! leswi nga ta va leswikulu eka wena, swi fanele swi lava ku tshoveriwa empfhukeni, ivi swi nwela evuentweni, ni ku kombisiwa emoyeni lowu nga riki na miri!

NDZIMA YA 35. Nhloko ya Mast.

A ku ri hi nkarhi wa maxelo lama tsakisaka swinene, laha hi ku rhendzeleka loku faneleke ni vatluti van’wana nhloko ya mina yo sungula ya mast-head yi fike yi rhendzeleka.

Eka vahlayisi votala va ti whale va le Amerika ti mast-heads ti tirhisiwa kwalomu ka nkarhi wun’we na xikepe lexi sukaka eribuweni ra xona; hambi leswi a nga ha vaka na khume ntlhanu wa magidi ya tikhilomitara, na ku tlula, ku famba hi xikepe ere a fika eka ndhawu ya yena leyi faneleke yo famba hi xikepe. Naswona loko, endzhaku ka riendzo ra malembe manharhu, mune kumbe ntlhanu a ri karhi a tshinela ekaya a ri ni nchumu wun’wana ni wun’wana lowu nga riki na nchumu eka yena—hi ku vula, xibya lexi nga riki na nchumu hambi ku ri—kutani, tinhloko ta yena ta mast ti hlayisiwa ti ri ni vanhu ku fikela emakumu; naswona a hi ku kondza tinsika ta yena ta skysail ti nghena exikarhi ka swihondzo swa ribuwa, u tshika hi ku helela ntshembo wo khoma whale yin’we yin’wana.

Sweswi, tani hileswi bindzu ro yima ti mast-heads, eribuweni kumbe ehansi ka mati, ri nga ra khale swinene naswona ri tsakisaka, a hi andleni hi mpimo wo karhi laha. Ndzi swi teka, leswaku vayimeri vo sungula va tinhloko ta mast a ku ri Vaegipta va khale; hikuva, eka ndzavisiso wa mina hinkwawo, a ndzi kumi na wun’we emahlweni ka vona. Hikuva hambi leswi vakokwa wa vona, vaaki va Babele, handle ko kanakana, hi xihondzo xa vona, va faneleke va kunguhata ku kurisa nhloko ya mast-head leyi tlakukeke swinene eAsia hinkwaro, kumbe Afrika na rona; kambe (emahlweni ka loko lori yo hetelela yi vekiwile eka yona) tanihi leswi xisirhelelo xexo lexikulu xa ribye xa vona xi nga vuriwa leswaku xi fambe hi bodo, eka xidzedze lexi chavisaka xa vukarhi bya Xikwembu; hikwalaho, a hi nge nyiki vaaki lava va le Babele ku rhangisa Vaegipta. Naswona leswaku Vaegipta a va ri tiko ra vayimeri va tinhloko ta mast, i ku tiyisisa loku sekeriweke eka ripfumelo leri tolovelekeke exikarhi ka vayimburi, leswaku tiphiramidi to sungula ti simekiwile hi swikongomelo swa tinyeleti: dyondzo leyi seketeriweke hi vun’we hi xivumbeko xo hlawuleka lexi fanaka ni switepisi xa matlhelo hinkwawo ya mune ya miako yoleyo; laha, hi ku tlakuka ko leha loku hlamarisaka ka milenge ya vona, vativi volavo va tinyeleti va khale a va tolovele ku khandziya ku ya fika enhlohlorhini, ni ku yimbelela tinyeleti letintshwa; hambi ku ri tanihi leswi swivoni swa xikepe xa manguva lawa swi yimbelelaka leswaku ku ta famba hi seyila, kumbe whale leyi vonakaka ntsena. Eka Saint Stylites, muhlapfa wa Mukreste la dumeke wa minkarhi ya khale, loyi a n’wi akeleke phuphu ya maribye leyi tlakukeke emananga naswona a heteke xiphemu hinkwaxo xo hetelela xa vutomi bya yena ehenhla ka nhlohlorhi ya yona, a tlakusa swakudya swa yena ehansi hi xitirho; eka yena hi ni xikombiso lexi hlamarisaka xa munhu la nga chaviki loyi a yimaka-ti-mast-heads; loyi a a nga fanelanga a hlongoriwa endhawini ya yena hi nkungu kumbe xirhami, mpfula, xihangu kumbe xidzedze; kambe hi xivindzi a langutane ni hinkwaswo ku ya fika emakumu, hi ku kongoma u file exitulwini xakwe. Eka ti standers-of-mast-heads ta manguva lawa hi na kambe sete leyi nga hanyiki; vavanuna va ribye, nsimbi ni koporo ntsena; lava, hambileswi va kotaka kahle ku langutana ni xidzedze lexikulu, va ha pfumala vuswikoti nikatsongo eka bindzu ro yimbelela loko va tshubula xivono xihi na xihi lexi nga tolovelekangiki. Ku ni Napoleon; loyi, ehenhla ka kholomo ya Vendome, a yimeke a khome mavoko, kwalomu ka dzana na makume ntlhanu wa timitara emoyeni; ku pfumala vukheta, sweswi, i mani la fumaka tidekhi leti nga ehansi; hambi ku ri Louis Philippe, Louis Blanc kumbe Louis Diyavulosi. Great Washington, na yena, u yime ehenhla ehenhla eka xihondzo xa yena lexikulu lexi tlakukeke eBaltimore, naswona ku fana na yin’wana ya tiphuphu ta Hercules, kholomo ya yena yi fungha yinhla yoleyo ya vukulu bya vanhu leyi ku tlula yona vanhu va nga ri vangani lava faka. Admiral Nelson, na yena, eka capstan ya gun-metal, u yime nhloko ya yena ya mast eTrafalgar Square; naswona minkarhi hinkwayo loko yi fihliwa ngopfu hi musi wolowo wa le London, xikombiso xa ha nyikiwa xa leswaku nhenha leyi fihliweke yi kona; hikuva laha ku nga ni musi, ku fanele ku ri ni ndzilo. Kambe hambi ku ri Washington lonkulu, kumbe Napoleon, kumbe Nelson, a va nge hlamuli xihangu xin’we lexi humaka ehansi, hambiloko va vitaniwa hi ndlela yo penga leswaku va endla xinghana hi switsundzuxo swa vona eka tidekhi leti kavanyetiweke leti va ti langutaka; hambiswiritano swi nga ha ehleketiwa, leswaku mimoya ya tona yi nghena hi le xikarhi ka xivungu xo tiya xa nkarhi lowu taka, naswona yi hlamusela leswaku i swirhendzevutana swihi ni maribye wahi lama faneleke ku papalatiwa.

Swi nga ha vonaka swi nga fanelanga ku hlanganisa hi ndlela yihi na yihi swilo leswi yimeke swa tinhloko ta swigolonyana swa misava ni swa le lwandle; kambe leswaku entiyisweni a swi tano, swi kombisiwa kahle hi nchumu lowu Obed Macy, n’wamatimu a ri yexe wa Nantucket, a yimaka a tihlamulela eka wona. Obed loyi a fanelekaka u hi byela, leswaku eminkarhini yo sungula ya ku phasa tinhlampfi ta whale, ere swikepe swi tshamela ku rhumeriwa leswaku swi hlongorisa ntlangu lowu, vanhu va xihlala xexo a va yimisa swirhendzevutana leswi tlakukeke eribuweni ra lwandle, laha vahlaleri a va tlhandlukela kona hi tindlela ya ti- cleat leti beleriweke, nchumu wo fana ni swinyenyana leswi yaka ehenhla endlwini ya tihuku. Emalembeni ma nga ri mangani lama hundzeke pulani leyi fanaka yi amukeriwile hi vahlaseri va Bay whale va le New Zealand, lava, loko va hlamusela ntlangu lowu, va nyikeke xitiviso eka swikepe leswi lunghekeleke vanhu ekusuhi ni ribuwa ra lwandle. Kambe mukhuva lowu sweswi wu hundzuke lowu nga ha tirhisiwiki; hi hundzuluka kutani hi ya eka nhloko yin’we leyi faneleke ya mast-head, leyi nga ya xikepe xa whale elwandle. Ti mast-head tinharhu ti hlayisiwa ti ri na vanhu kusukela loko dyambu rihuma kuya fika loko dyambu ripela; vatluti va ri karhi va jika-jika ka vona nkarhi na nkarhi (tanihi loko va ri eka xifambo), naswona va ntshunxana endzhaku ka tiawara tin’wana ni tin’wana timbirhi. Eka maxelo yo rhula ya tindzhawu to hisa switsakisa ngopfu nhloko ya mast; e-e, eka wanuna la anakanyisisaka la lorhaka swa tsakisa. Kwalaho u yime, dzana ra timitara ehenhla ka swithezi leswi nga vulavuriki, u kandziya hi milenge evuentweni, onge hi loko swisirhelelo swa kona ku ri swikhandziyo leswikulu, kasi ehansi ka wena ni le xikarhi ka milenge ya wena, hi ndlela yo fanekisela, u hlambela swivandzana leswikulu swinene swa lwandle, hambi leswi swikepe swi tshameke swi famba exikarhi ka maphaxani ya Colossus la dumeke eRhodes ya khale. Kwalaho u yime, u lahlekile eka nxaxamelo lowu nga heriki wa lwandle, u nga ri na nchumu lowu rhurhumelaka handle ka magandlati. Xikepe lexi nga ni vuxiyaxiya xi rhendzeleka hi ku tikhoma; mimoya ya mabindzu leyi nga ni vurhongo ya hunga; hinkwaswo swi ku lulamisa eka languor. Hi xiphemu lexikulu, eka vutomi lebyi bya ku hlota tinhlampfi ta le tindhawini to hisa, ku nga humeleli loku tlakukeke ku ku vekisa; a wu twi mahungu; ku nga hlayi tigazete; swin’wana leswi engetelekeke leswi nga ni marungula lama hlamarisaka ya swilo leswi tolovelekeke a swi si tshama swi ku kanganyisa eka ku nyanyuka loku nga lavekiki; a wu twi hi ta ku xaniseka ka le kaya; tisekhurithi leti nga bankrupt; ku wa ka switoko; a wu si tshama u karhatiwa hi ku ehleketa hi leswi u nga ta swi dya swakudya swa nimadyambu—hikuva swakudya swa wena hinkwaswo ku ringana malembe manharhu ni ku tlula swi vekiwile kahle eka swibya, naswona mali ya wena ya mali a yi cinci.

Eka un’wana wa vahlaseri volavo va le dzongeni, eka riendzo ro leha ra malembe manharhu kumbe mune, hilaha swi talaka ku humelela hakona, nhlayo ya tiawara to hambana-hambana leti u ti hetaka u ri enhlongeni ya mast-head a yi ta fika eka tin’hweti to hlayanyana leti heleleke. Naswona swi vilerisiwa ngopfu leswaku ndhawu leyi u nyikelaka xiphemu lexikulu swinene xa nkarhi hinkwawo wa vutomi bya wena bya ntumbuluko, yi fanele yi pfumala hi ndlela yo vava swinene eka nchumu wun’wana ni wun’wana lowu tshinelaka eka vutshamo byo rhula, kumbe ku tolovela ku tswala ndhawu leyi ntshunxekeke ya mintlhaveko, . swo tanihi leswi fambisanaka ni mubedo, xigolonyana, movha wa vafi, bokisi ra varindzi, pulpiti, xigolonyana kumbe xin’wana ni xin’wana xa swilo sweswo leswitsongo ni leswi rhuleke leswi vavanuna va tihambanisaka na swona swa xinkarhana. Ndzhawu ya wena leyi tolovelekeke swinene yo tshama eka yona i nhloko ya t’ gallant-mast, laha u yimeke ehenhla ka tinhonga timbirhi to olova leti fambelanaka (leti lavaka ku hlawuleka eka vahlayisi va ti whale) leti vuriwaka t’ gallant cross-trees. Laha, loko a ri karhi a rhurhumela hi lwandle, loyi a sungulaka u titwa a ntshunxekile ku fana ni loko a yime hi timhondzo ta nkunzi. Ku tiyiseka, hi maxelo yo titimela u nga ha rhwala yindlu ya wena yi ya ehenhla, yi ri ni xivumbeko xa jazi ra wachi; kambe hi ndlela leyi faneleke jazi ro tiya swinene ra wachi a hi yindlu ku tlula miri lowu nga ambariwangiki; hikuva tanihi leswi moya-xiviri wu namarheriweke endzeni ka tabernakele ya wona ya nyama, naswona wu nga swi kotiki ku famba-famba hi ku ntshunxeka eka wona, hambi ku ri ku suka eka wona, handle ko va ekhombyeni lerikulu ro lova (ku fana ni mupfhumba la nga riki na vutivi la tsemakanyaka tintshava ta Alps leti nga ni gamboko hi xixika); hikwalaho jazi ra wachi a hi yindlu ngopfu kambe i mvhilopho ntsena, kumbe nhlonge leyi engetelekeke leyi ku funengetaka. A wu nge swi koti ku veka xelufu kumbe xigwitsirisi emirini wa wena, naswona a wu nge he endli xigwitsirisi lexi olovaka xa jazi ra wena ra wachi.

Malunghana ni leswi hinkwaswo, swa khomisa gome swinene leswi tinhloko ta mast- ta xikepe xa le dzongeni wa ti- whale ti nga nyikiwangiki matende wolawo lamatsongo lama navetisaka mavondzo kumbe tipulpiti, leti vuriwaka crow’s-nests , laha tindhawu leti langutisiweke ta muphasi wa tinhlampfi ta le Greenland ti sirheleriwaka eka swilo swo biha maxelo ya malwandle lama nga ni ayisi. Eka xitlhokovetselo xa le tlhelo ka ndzilo xa Kaputeni Sleet, lexi nga ni nhloko-mhaka leyi nge “Riendzo exikarhi ka Tintshava ta Ayisi, hi ku lava Greenland Whale, naswona hi xihoxo ku tlhela ku tshuburiwa Tikoloni ta le Iceland leti Lahlekeke ta le Greenland ya Khale;” eka vholumo leyi yo hlamarisa, hinkwavo lava yimeke va tinhloko ta mast va nyikiwe rungula leri tsakisaka ra xiyimo xa xisaka xa crow’s-nest lexi a xi ha ku tumbuluxiwa hi nkarhi wolowo xa Glacier, lexi a xi ri vito ra vutshila lebyinene bya Captain Sleet. U xi vitane xisaka xa Sleet’s crow’s-nest , ku tixixima; yena tanihi musunguri wo sungula na muthori wa patent, naswona a ntshunxekile eka swilo hinkwaswo swo nandziha swa mavunwa leswi hlekisaka, naswona a khome leswaku loko hi vitana vana va hina hi mavito ya hina (hina vatatana hi ri vasunguri vo sungula na lava nga na patent), hi ndlela leyi fanaka hi fanele ku ti thya vito ra hina xitirhisiwa xin’wana na xin’wana hi nga ha veleka. Hi xivumbeko, xisaka xa Sleet’s crow’s-nest i nchumu wofana na tierce leyikulu kumbe phayiphi; hambiswiritano, yi pfulekile ehenhla laha yi nga ni xisirhelelo xa le tlhelo lexi famba-fambaka leswaku yi tshama yi ri eku wiseni hi moya loko u ri ni xidzedze xo tika. Leswi u tshameke u tshama enhlohlorhini ya mast, u tlhandlukela eka yona hi ku tirhisa xipfalo lexitsongo xa trap-hatch lexi nga ehansi. Eka tlhelo ra le ndzhaku, kumbe tlhelo leri nga ekusuhi ni le ndzhaku ka xikepe, ku ni xitulu lexinene, lexi nga ni loyara ehansi ka tiambulense, swichavelelo ni majazi. Emahlweni ku ni xigwitsirisi xa dzovo, lexi eka xona u nga hlayisaka mhalamhala ya wena leyi vulavulaka, phayiphi, theleskopu ni swilo swin’wana leswi olovaka swa le lwandle. Loko Captain Sleet hi xiviri a yime mast-head ya yena eka crow’s-nest leyi ya yena, u hi byela leswaku minkarhi hinkwayo ari na xibamu na yena (na xona xi vekiwile eka rack), xikan’we na powder flask naku duvula, hi xikongomelo xo popping ku suka eka ti- narwhale leti hambukeke, kumbe ti- sea unicorn leti famba-fambaka leti hlaselaka ematini wolawo; hikuva a wu nge swi koti ku ti duvula hi laha ku humelelaka u ri ehenhla ka xitepisi hikwalaho ka ku lwisana ka mati, kambe ku ti duvula ehansi i nchumu lowu hambaneke swinene. Sweswi, swi le rivaleni leswaku a ku ri ntirho wa rirhandzu eka Kaputeni Sleet ku hlamusela, tanihilaha a endlaka hakona, swilo hinkwaswo leswintsongo leswi nga na vuxokoxoko byo olova bya xisaka xa yena xa crow’s-nest; kambe hambileswi a kurisaka swonghasi eka swo tala swa leswi, naswona hambileswi a hi khomaka hi rungula ra sayense swinene ra ku ringeta ka yena exihlobyeni lexi xa ximanga, hi khampasi leyitsongo leyi a yi hlayiseke kwalaho hi xikongomelo xo lwisana ni swihoxo leswi vangiwaka hi leswi vuriwaka “ . ku koka rinoko ka ndhawu” ka maginete hinkwawo ya binnacle; xihoxo lexi nga ha vaka xi vangiwa hi ndhawu leyi nga etlhelo leyi nga etlhelo ka nsimbi leyi nga eka mapulanga ya xikepe, naswona emhakeni ya Glacier, kumbexana, hikwalaho ka leswi a ku ri ni vafuri vo tala va nsimbi lava tshovekeke exikarhi ka vatirhi va yena; Ndzi vula, leswaku hambi leswi Kaputeni a nga na vutlhari swinene na sayense laha, hambiswiritano, eka hinkwaswo leswi a swi dyondzeke “ku hambuka ka binnacle,” “ku langutisisa ka khampasi ya azimuth,” na “swihoxo leswi ringanyetiweke,” u swi tiva kahle swinene, Kaputeni Sleet, leswaku a nga ri tano u nghene ngopfu eka ku anakanyisisa koloko lokukulu ka maginete, ku fikela loko a tsandzeka ku kokiwa nkarhi na nkarhi eka bodlhela rero leritsongo leri taleke kahle ra xigwitsirisi, leri tumbeleke kahle swinene eka tlhelo rin’we ra xisaka xa yena xa ximanga, laha voko ra yena ri nga fika hi ku olova. Nakona ndzi pfumeleleni endhawini leyi ndzi mi tsundzuxa hi ndlela leyi khumbaka, n’wina vini va swikepe va Nantucket! Tivonele ku tsarisa eka vumaki bya wena bya tihlampfi lebyi xalamukeke jaha rihi na rihi leri nga ni xikandza xo olova ni tihlo leri nga ni swirhendzevutana; ku nyikiwa ku anakanyisisa loku nga riki na tinguva; naswona loyi a tinyiketelaka ku rhumela xikepe na Phædon ematshan’wini ya Bowditch enhlokweni ya yena. Tivonele eka munhu wo tano, ndzi ri; ti whale ta wena ti fanele ti voniwa ti nga si dlayiwa; naswona muntshwa loyi wa Muplaton wa mahlo lama dzimeke u ta ku koka khume ra ku pfuka emisaveni hinkwayo, naswona a nga ka a nga ku endle pinti yin’we ya mbewu ya vununa u fuwa ngopfu. Naswona swikombiso leswi a swi laveki nikatsongo. Eka masiku lawa, vuphasi bya tinhlampfi ta whale byi nyika vutumbelo eka majaha yo tala ya rirhandzu, ya gome, na ku nga ri na miehleketo, lama nyenyetsaka hi ku khathalela ka misava loku nga ni vuxiyaxiya, naswona ma lava mintlhaveko hi xikontiri ni le ka xigwitsirisi. Childe Harold not unfrequently perches himself upon the mast-head of some luckless disappointed whale-ship, and in moody phrase ejaculates:—

“Roll on, thou deep and dark blue ocean, roll!
Ten thousand blubber-hunters sweep over thee in vain.”

Very often do the captains of such ships take those absent-minded young philosophers to task, upbraiding them with not feeling sufficient “interest” in the voyage; half-hinting that they are so hopelessly lost to all honorable ambition, as that in their secret souls they would rather not see whales than otherwise. But all in vain; those young Platonists have a notion that their vision is imperfect; they are short-sighted; what use, then, to strain the visual nerve? They have left their opera-glasses at home.

“Why, thou monkey,” said a harpooneer to one of these lads, “we've been cruising now hard upon three years, and thou hast not raised a whale yet. Whales are scarce as hen's teeth whenever thou art up here.” Perhaps they were; or perhaps there might have been shoals of them in the far horizon; but lulled into such an opium-like listlessness of vacant, unconscious reverie is this absent-minded youth by the blending cadence of waves with thoughts, that at last he loses his identity; takes the mystic ocean at his feet for the visible image of that deep, blue, bottomless soul, pervading mankind and nature; and every strange, half-seen, gliding, beautiful thing that eludes him; every dimly-discovered, uprising fin of some undiscernible form, seems to him the embodiment of those elusive thoughts that only people the soul by continually flitting through it. In this enchanted mood, thy spirit ebbs away to whence it came; becomes diffused through time and space; like Cranmer's sprinkled Pantheistic ashes, forming at last a part of every shore the round globe over.

There is no life in thee, now, except that rocking life imparted by a gently rolling ship; by her, borrowed from the sea; by the sea, from the inscrutable tides of God. But while this sleep, this dream is on ye, move your foot or hand an inch; slip your hold at all; and your identity comes back in horror. Over Descartian vortices you hover. And perhaps, at mid-day, in the fairest weather, with one half-throttled shriek you drop through that transparent air into the summer sea, no more to rise for ever. Heed it well, ye Pantheists!





CHAPTER 36. The Quarter-Deck.

( Enter Ahab: Then, all. )

It was not a great while after the affair of the pipe, that one morning shortly after breakfast, Ahab, as was his wont, ascended the cabin-gangway to the deck. There most sea-captains usually walk at that hour, as country gentlemen, after the same meal, take a few turns in the garden.

Soon his steady, ivory stride was heard, as to and fro he paced his old rounds, upon planks so familiar to his tread, that they were all over dented, like geological stones, with the peculiar mark of his walk. Did you fixedly gaze, too, upon that ribbed and dented brow; there also, you would see still stranger foot-prints—the foot-prints of his one unsleeping, ever-pacing thought.

But on the occasion in question, those dents looked deeper, even as his nervous step that morning left a deeper mark. And, so full of his thought was Ahab, that at every uniform turn that he made, now at the main-mast and now at the binnacle, you could almost see that thought turn in him as he turned, and pace in him as he paced; so completely possessing him, indeed, that it all but seemed the inward mould of every outer movement.

“D'ye mark him, Flask?” whispered Stubb; “the chick that's in him pecks the shell. 'Twill soon be out.”

The hours wore on;—Ahab now shut up within his cabin; anon, pacing the deck, with the same intense bigotry of purpose in his aspect.

It drew near the close of day. Suddenly he came to a halt by the bulwarks, and inserting his bone leg into the auger-hole there, and with one hand grasping a shroud, he ordered Starbuck to send everybody aft.

“Sir!” said the mate, astonished at an order seldom or never given on ship-board except in some extraordinary case.

“Send everybody aft,” repeated Ahab. “Mast-heads, there! come down!”

When the entire ship's company were assembled, and with curious and not wholly unapprehensive faces, were eyeing him, for he looked not unlike the weather horizon when a storm is coming up, Ahab, after rapidly glancing over the bulwarks, and then darting his eyes among the crew, started from his standpoint; and as though not a soul were nigh him resumed his heavy turns upon the deck. With bent head and half-slouched hat he continued to pace, unmindful of the wondering whispering among the men; till Stubb cautiously whispered to Flask, that Ahab must have summoned them there for the purpose of witnessing a pedestrian feat. But this did not last long. Vehemently pausing, he cried:—

“What do ye do when ye see a whale, men?”

“Sing out for him!” was the impulsive rejoinder from a score of clubbed voices.

“Good!” cried Ahab, with a wild approval in his tones; observing the hearty animation into which his unexpected question had so magnetically thrown them.

“And what do ye next, men?”

“Lower away, and after him!”

“And what tune is it ye pull to, men?”

“A dead whale or a stove boat!”

More and more strangely and fiercely glad and approving, grew the countenance of the old man at every shout; while the mariners began to gaze curiously at each other, as if marvelling how it was that they themselves became so excited at such seemingly purposeless questions.

But, they were all eagerness again, as Ahab, now half-revolving in his pivot-hole, with one hand reaching high up a shroud, and tightly, almost convulsively grasping it, addressed them thus:—

“All ye mast-headers have before now heard me give orders about a white whale. Look ye! d'ye see this Spanish ounce of gold?”—holding up a broad bright coin to the sun—“it is a sixteen dollar piece, men. D'ye see it? Mr. Starbuck, hand me yon top-maul.”

While the mate was getting the hammer, Ahab, without speaking, was slowly rubbing the gold piece against the skirts of his jacket, as if to heighten its lustre, and without using any words was meanwhile lowly humming to himself, producing a sound so strangely muffled and inarticulate that it seemed the mechanical humming of the wheels of his vitality in him.

Receiving the top-maul from Starbuck, he advanced towards the main-mast with the hammer uplifted in one hand, exhibiting the gold with the other, and with a high raised voice exclaiming: “Whosoever of ye raises me a white-headed whale with a wrinkled brow and a crooked jaw; whosoever of ye raises me that white-headed whale, with three holes punctured in his starboard fluke—look ye, whosoever of ye raises me that same white whale, he shall have this gold ounce, my boys!”

“Huzza! huzza!” cried the seamen, as with swinging tarpaulins they hailed the act of nailing the gold to the mast.

“It's a white whale, I say,” resumed Ahab, as he threw down the topmaul: “a white whale. Skin your eyes for him, men; look sharp for white water; if ye see but a bubble, sing out.”

All this while Tashtego, Daggoo, and Queequeg had looked on with even more intense interest and surprise than the rest, and at the mention of the wrinkled brow and crooked jaw they had started as if each was separately touched by some specific recollection.

“Captain Ahab,” said Tashtego, “that white whale must be the same that some call Moby Dick.”

“Moby Dick?” shouted Ahab. “Do ye know the white whale then, Tash?”

“Does he fan-tail a little curious, sir, before he goes down?” said the Gay-Header deliberately.

“And has he a curious spout, too,” said Daggoo, “very bushy, even for a parmacetty, and mighty quick, Captain Ahab?”

“And he have one, two, three—oh! good many iron in him hide, too, Captain,” cried Queequeg disjointedly, “all twiske-tee be-twisk, like him—him—” faltering hard for a word, and screwing his hand round and round as though uncorking a bottle—“like him—him—”

“Corkscrew!” cried Ahab, “aye, Queequeg, the harpoons lie all twisted and wrenched in him; aye, Daggoo, his spout is a big one, like a whole shock of wheat, and white as a pile of our Nantucket wool after the great annual sheep-shearing; aye, Tashtego, and he fan-tails like a split jib in a squall. Death and devils! men, it is Moby Dick ye have seen—Moby Dick—Moby Dick!”

“Captain Ahab,” said Starbuck, who, with Stubb and Flask, had thus far been eyeing his superior with increasing surprise, but at last seemed struck with a thought which somewhat explained all the wonder. “Captain Ahab, I have heard of Moby Dick—but it was not Moby Dick that took off thy leg?”

“Who told thee that?” cried Ahab; then pausing, “Aye, Starbuck; aye, my hearties all round; it was Moby Dick that dismasted me; Moby Dick that brought me to this dead stump I stand on now. Aye, aye,” he shouted with a terrific, loud, animal sob, like that of a heart-stricken moose; “Aye, aye! it was that accursed white whale that razed me; made a poor pegging lubber of me for ever and a day!” Then tossing both arms, with measureless imprecations he shouted out: “Aye, aye! and I'll chase him round Good Hope, and round the Horn, and round the Norway Maelstrom, and round perdition's flames before I give him up. And this is what ye have shipped for, men! to chase that white whale on both sides of land, and over all sides of earth, till he spouts black blood and rolls fin out. What say ye, men, will ye splice hands on it, now? I think ye do look brave.”

“Aye, aye!” shouted the harpooneers and seamen, running closer to the excited old man: “A sharp eye for the white whale; a sharp lance for Moby Dick!”

“God bless ye,” he seemed to half sob and half shout. “God bless ye, men. Steward! go draw the great measure of grog. But what's this long face about, Mr. Starbuck; wilt thou not chase the white whale? art not game for Moby Dick?”

“I am game for his crooked jaw, and for the jaws of Death too, Captain Ahab, if it fairly comes in the way of the business we follow; but I came here to hunt whales, not my commander's vengeance. How many barrels will thy vengeance yield thee even if thou gettest it, Captain Ahab? it will not fetch thee much in our Nantucket market.”

“Nantucket market! Hoot! But come closer, Starbuck; thou requirest a little lower layer. If money's to be the measurer, man, and the accountants have computed their great counting-house the globe, by girdling it with guineas, one to every three parts of an inch; then, let me tell thee, that my vengeance will fetch a great premium here! ”

“He smites his chest,” whispered Stubb, “what's that for? methinks it rings most vast, but hollow.”

“Vengeance on a dumb brute!” cried Starbuck, “that simply smote thee from blindest instinct! Madness! To be enraged with a dumb thing, Captain Ahab, seems blasphemous.”

“Hark ye yet again—the little lower layer. All visible objects, man, are but as pasteboard masks. But in each event—in the living act, the undoubted deed—there, some unknown but still reasoning thing puts forth the mouldings of its features from behind the unreasoning mask. If man will strike, strike through the mask! How can the prisoner reach outside except by thrusting through the wall? To me, the white whale is that wall, shoved near to me. Sometimes I think there's naught beyond. But 'tis enough. He tasks me; he heaps me; I see in him outrageous strength, with an inscrutable malice sinewing it. That inscrutable thing is chiefly what I hate; and be the white whale agent, or be the white whale principal, I will wreak that hate upon him. Talk not to me of blasphemy, man; I'd strike the sun if it insulted me. For could the sun do that, then could I do the other; since there is ever a sort of fair play herein, jealousy presiding over all creations. But not my master, man, is even that fair play. Who's over me? Truth hath no confines. Take off thine eye! more intolerable than fiends' glarings is a doltish stare! So, so; thou reddenest and palest; my heat has melted thee to anger-glow. But look ye, Starbuck, what is said in heat, that thing unsays itself. There are men from whom warm words are small indignity. I meant not to incense thee. Let it go. Look! see yonder Turkish cheeks of spotted tawn—living, breathing pictures painted by the sun. The Pagan leopards—the unrecking and unworshipping things, that live; and seek, and give no reasons for the torrid life they feel! The crew, man, the crew! Are they not one and all with Ahab, in this matter of the whale? See Stubb! he laughs! See yonder Chilian! he snorts to think of it. Stand up amid the general hurricane, thy one tost sapling cannot, Starbuck! And what is it? Reckon it. 'Tis but to help strike a fin; no wondrous feat for Starbuck. What is it more? From this one poor hunt, then, the best lance out of all Nantucket, surely he will not hang back, when every foremast-hand has clutched a whetstone? Ah! constrainings seize thee; I see! the billow lifts thee! Speak, but speak!—Aye, aye! thy silence, then, that voices thee. ( Aside ) Something shot from my dilated nostrils, he has inhaled it in his lungs. Starbuck now is mine; cannot oppose me now, without rebellion.”

“God keep me!—keep us all!” murmured Starbuck, lowly.

But in his joy at the enchanted, tacit acquiescence of the mate, Ahab did not hear his foreboding invocation; nor yet the low laugh from the hold; nor yet the presaging vibrations of the winds in the cordage; nor yet the hollow flap of the sails against the masts, as for a moment their hearts sank in. For again Starbuck's downcast eyes lighted up with the stubbornness of life; the subterranean laugh died away; the winds blew on; the sails filled out; the ship heaved and rolled as before. Ah, ye admonitions and warnings! why stay ye not when ye come? But rather are ye predictions than warnings, ye shadows! Yet not so much predictions from without, as verifications of the foregoing things within. For with little external to constrain us, the innermost necessities in our being, these still drive us on.

“The measure! the measure!” cried Ahab.

Receiving the brimming pewter, and turning to the harpooneers, he ordered them to produce their weapons. Then ranging them before him near the capstan, with their harpoons in their hands, while his three mates stood at his side with their lances, and the rest of the ship's company formed a circle round the group; he stood for an instant searchingly eyeing every man of his crew. But those wild eyes met his, as the bloodshot eyes of the prairie wolves meet the eye of their leader, ere he rushes on at their head in the trail of the bison; but, alas! only to fall into the hidden snare of the Indian.

“Drink and pass!” he cried, handing the heavy charged flagon to the nearest seaman. “The crew alone now drink. Round with it, round! Short draughts—long swallows, men; 'tis hot as Satan's hoof. So, so; it goes round excellently. It spiralizes in ye; forks out at the serpent-snapping eye. Well done; almost drained. That way it went, this way it comes. Hand it me—here's a hollow! Men, ye seem the years; so brimming life is gulped and gone. Steward, refill!

“Attend now, my braves. I have mustered ye all round this capstan; and ye mates, flank me with your lances; and ye harpooneers, stand there with your irons; and ye, stout mariners, ring me in, that I may in some sort revive a noble custom of my fisherman fathers before me. O men, you will yet see that—Ha! boy, come back? bad pennies come not sooner. Hand it me. Why, now, this pewter had run brimming again, wer't not thou St. Vitus' imp—away, thou ague!

“Advance, ye mates! Cross your lances full before me. Well done! Let me touch the axis.” So saying, with extended arm, he grasped the three level, radiating lances at their crossed centre; while so doing, suddenly and nervously twitched them; meanwhile, glancing intently from Starbuck to Stubb; from Stubb to Flask. It seemed as though, by some nameless, interior volition, he would fain have shocked into them the same fiery emotion accumulated within the Leyden jar of his own magnetic life. The three mates quailed before his strong, sustained, and mystic aspect. Stubb and Flask looked sideways from him; the honest eye of Starbuck fell downright.

“In vain!” cried Ahab; “but, maybe, 'tis well. For did ye three but once take the full-forced shock, then mine own electric thing, that had perhaps expired from out me. Perchance, too, it would have dropped ye dead. Perchance ye need it not. Down lances! And now, ye mates, I do appoint ye three cupbearers to my three pagan kinsmen there—yon three most honorable gentlemen and noblemen, my valiant harpooneers. Disdain the task? What, when the great Pope washes the feet of beggars, using his tiara for ewer? Oh, my sweet cardinals! your own condescension, that shall bend ye to it. I do not order ye; ye will it. Cut your seizings and draw the poles, ye harpooneers!”

Silently obeying the order, the three harpooneers now stood with the detached iron part of their harpoons, some three feet long, held, barbs up, before him.

“Stab me not with that keen steel! Cant them; cant them over! know ye not the goblet end? Turn up the socket! So, so; now, ye cup-bearers, advance. The irons! take them; hold them while I fill!” Forthwith, slowly going from one officer to the other, he brimmed the harpoon sockets with the fiery waters from the pewter.

“Now, three to three, ye stand. Commend the murderous chalices! Bestow them, ye who are now made parties to this indissoluble league. Ha! Starbuck! but the deed is done! Yon ratifying sun now waits to sit upon it. Drink, ye harpooneers! drink and swear, ye men that man the deathful whaleboat's bow—Death to Moby Dick! God hunt us all, if we do not hunt Moby Dick to his death!” The long, barbed steel goblets were lifted; and to cries and maledictions against the white whale, the spirits were simultaneously quaffed down with a hiss. Starbuck paled, and turned, and shivered. Once more, and finally, the replenished pewter went the rounds among the frantic crew; when, waving his free hand to them, they all dispersed; and Ahab retired within his cabin.
