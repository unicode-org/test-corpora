NDZIMA YA 86. Ncila.

Vatlhokovetseri van’wana va rhurhumerile hi ku dzunisa ka tihlo ro olova ra ximanga, ni mapapa lama rhandzekaka ya xinyenyana lexi nga si tshamaka xi xika; less celestial, ndzi tlangela ncila.

Ku hlayela ncila wa Sperm Whale wa vukulu lebyikulu swinene kuva wusungula eka ndzhawu yaleyo ya nsinya laha wu hungutaka kuya kwalomu ka ku rhendzeleka ka wanuna, wuvumbiwa ehenhla ka vuandlalo bya yona byale henhla ntsena, ndzhawu ya kwalomu ka makume ntlhanu wa swikwere-fiti. Miri wa xirhendzevutani lowu hlanganisiweke wa rimitsu ra yona wu anama wu va swikunwana swimbirhi swo anama, leswi tiyeke, leswi nga ni swiphepherhele kumbe swiphepherhele, hakatsongo-tsongo swi rhurhumela ku ya fika ehansi ka intshi hi ku enta. Eka crotch kumbe junction, ti flukes leti ti hlangana nyana, kutani etlhelo ti tlhelela endzhaku eka yin’wana kufana na timpapa, ti siya ndzhawu yo anama exikarhi. Eka xivumbiwa xihi na xihi lexi hanyaka laha milayeni ya ku saseka yi hlamuseriweke hi ndlela leyinene ku tlula eka mindzilakano ya crescentic ya swiphepherhele leswi. Eka ku anama ka yona lokukulu eka whale leyi kuleke hiku hetiseka, ncila wuta tlula swinene makume mbirhi wa timitara hiku anama.

Xirho hinkwaxo xivonaka xiri mubedo wo enta wa webbed wa ti sinews leti weldiweke; kambe tsema eka yona, kutani u kuma leswaku swiphemu swinharhu leswi hambaneke swi yi vumba:—ehenhla, ya le xikarhi ni ya le hansi. Ti fibre eka layers yale henhla nale hansi, ti lehe naswona ti horizontal; leti ta le xikarhi, ti komile swinene, naswona ti tsutsumaka hi ku tsemakanya exikarhi ka swiphemu swa le handle. Xivumbeko lexi xa vunharhu-un’we, ku fana ni xin’wana ni xin’wana, xi nyika matimba eka ncila. Eka xichudeni xa marhangu ya khale ya Rhoma, leyara yale xikarhi yita nyika kufana loku hlamarisaka eka khoso yo olova ya tithayile leti minkarhi hinkwayo ti cincana na ribye eka masalela wolawo yo hlamarisa ya khale, naswona lawa handle ko kanakana ya hoxaka xandla swinene eka matimba lamakulu ya masonry.

Kambe onge hi loko matimba lawa lamakulu ya ndhawu eka ncila wa tendinous a ma enelanga, xiphemu lexikulu xa leviathan xi lukiwile hi warp na woof ya ti fibre ta misiha na ti filaments, leti hundzaka eka matlhelo hamambirhi ya marhaku naswona ti tsutsumela ehansi eka ti flukes, hi ndlela leyi nga twisisekiki ku hlangana na vona, naswona ngopfu-ngopfu va hoxa xandla eka matimba ya vona; lerova eka ncila matimba lama nga pimekiki lama hlanganeke ya whale hinkwayo ma vonaka ma hlengeletane ku fikela eka xiyimo xo karhi. Xana ku herisiwa ku nga humelela ku va mhaka, leswi a ku ri nchumu wo wu endla.

Naswona leswi—matimba ya yona lama hlamarisaka, nikatsongo a ma talanga ku lamata ku gombonyoka lokunene ka ku tshukatshuka ka yona; laha vuhlangi bya ku olova byi rhendzelekaka hi ku tirhisa Titanism ya matimba. Ku hambana ni sweswo, minsusumeto yoleyo yi kuma ku saseka ka yona loku chavisaka swinene eka yona. Matimba ya xiviri a ma si tshama ma onha ku saseka kumbe ku twanana, kambe hakanyingi ma ku nyika; naswona eka xin’wana ni xin’wana lexi sasekeke hi ndlela leyi hlamarisaka, matimba ma fambisana ngopfu ni masalamusi. Susa misiha leyi bohiweke leyi hinkwako yi vonakaka yi pfurha eka marble eka Hercules leyi vatliweke, naswona ku saseka ka yona a ku ta va ku nga ha ri kona. Loko Eckerman la tinyiketeleke a ri karhi a tlakusa xiphepherhele xa lineni eka ntsumbu lowu nga ambalangiki nchumu wa Goethe, u karhatiwe hi xifuva lexikulu xa wanuna loyi, lexi a xi vonaka xi ri xirhendzevutana xa ku hlula xa Rhoma. Loko Angelo a penda hambi ku ri Xikwembu Tatana hi xivumbeko xa munhu, fungha leswaku i ku tiya kwihi loku nga kona. Naswona xin’wana ni xin’wana lexi va nga xi paluxaka hi rirhandzu ra le henhla eka N’wana, swifaniso swo olova, leswi govekeke, swa le Italy leswi nga ni rimbewu leri hambaneke, leswi eka swona mianakanyo yakwe yi katsiweke hi ndlela leyi humelelaka swinene; swifaniso leswi, hambileswi swi pfumalaka matimba hinkwawo, a swi kombisi nchumu hi matimba wahi na wahi, kambe lexi nga riki xinene ntsena, xa xisati xa ku titsongahata ni ku tiyisela, leswi emavokweni hinkwawo swi pfumeleriwaka, swi vumbaka timfanelo letinene leti tirhaka to hlawuleka ta tidyondzo ta yena.

Ku olova loku tumbeleke ka xirho lexi ndzi xi tshungulaka ku tano, lerova hambi xi tirhisiwa eka mintlangu, kumbe hi ku tiyimisela, kumbe hi ku hlundzuka, ku nga khathariseki leswaku i xiyimo xihi lexi xi nga eka xona, ku gombonyoka ka xona a ku cinci ku funghiwa hi tintswalo leti tlulaka mpimo. Eka kona ku hava voko ra fairy leri nga ri tlulaka.

Ku tshukatshuka lokukulu ka ntlhanu i loku hlawulekeke eka yona. Xo sungula, loko yi tirhisiwa tanihi fin leswaku yi ya emahlweni; Xa vumbirhi, loko yi tirhisiwa tanihi mace enyimpini; Xa vunharhu, eka ku sweka; Xa vumune, eka lobtailing; Xa vuntlhanu, eka ti peaking flukes.

Xo sungula: Leswi ncila wa Levhiyathani wu nga etlhelo ka xiyimo xa wona, wu tirha hi ndlela leyi hambaneke ni misisi ya swivumbiwa leswin’wana hinkwaswo swa le lwandle. A yi si tshama yi rhurhumela. Eka munhu kumbe nhlampfi, ku rhurhumela i xikombiso xa ku va ehansi. Eka whale, ncila wa yona hi wona ntsena ndlela yo susumeta. Scroll-wise coiled forwards ehansi ka miri, kutani hiku hatlisa yi tshuka yi tlhelela endzhaku, hi swona leswi nyikaka leswaku singular darting, leaping motion eka xiharhi xa nhova loko xi hlambela hiku hlundzuka. Ti side-fins ta yena ti tirha ntsena ku kongomisa hi.

Xa vumbirhi: I swa nkoka nyana, leswaku hambi leswi sperm whale yin’wana yi lwisanaka ntsena na sperm whale yin’wana hi nhloko na nhlonge ya yona, hambiswiritano, eka ku lwisana ka yona na munhu, ngopfu ngopfu na hi ku delela yi tirhisa ncila wa yona. Loko a ba xikepe, u goveka hi ku hatlisa swibalesa swa yena eka xona, naswona ku biwa ku endliwa ntsena hi ku tlhelela endzhaku. Loko yi endliwa emoyeni lowu nga siveriwiki, ngopfu-ngopfu loko yi rhelela yi ya fika eka mfungho wa yona, xitlhavi xa kona xi va xi nga siveriwi ntsena. Ku hava timbanga ta munhu kumbe ta xikepe leti nga tiyiselaka. Ku ponisiwa ka wena ntsena ku le ku ku balekeleni; kambe loko yi ta hi le tlhelo hi mati lama kanetanaka, kutani xiphemu xin’wana hikwalaho ka ku khuluka ko olova ka xikepe xa whale-boat, ni ku olova ka swilo swa xona, rib leri pandzekeke kumbe pulani leyi tshovekeke kumbe mambirhi, muxaka wa ku rhungela etlhelo, hi ntolovelo swi tano vuyelo lebyikulu swinene. Switlhavi leswi swa le matlhelo leswi nga ehansi ka mati swi tala ku amukeriwa eka vumaki bya tihlampfi, lerova swi tekiwa tanihi ntlangu wa vana ntsena. Un’wana u hluvula frock, kutani khumbi ri yimisiwa.

Xa vunharhu: A ndzi nge swi koti ku swi kombisa, kambe swi vonaka eka mina, leswaku eka whale ku twa ku khumba ku dzike eka ncila; hikuva emhakeni leyi ku ni swakudya swo nandziha eka yona leswi ringanaka ntsena ni ku nandziha ka xikundzu xa ndlopfu. Ku nandziha loku ku vonaka ngopfu hi xiendlo xo sweka, loko hi ku olova ka vunhwana whale hi ku nonoka ko karhi ko olova yi fambisa swiphepherhele swa yona leswikulu ku suka eka tlhelo rin’wana ku ya eka rin’wana ehenhla ka lwandle; naswona loko a titwa ntsena a ri marhambu ya mutluti, khombo eka mutluti yoloye, madzovo na hinkwaswo. Mawaku ku olova loku nga kona eka ku khumba koloko ko sungula! Loko ncila lowu a wu ri na matimba yo khoma, ndzi fanele ndzi ndzi ehleketa hi ku kongoma hi ndlopfu ya Darmonodes leyi a yi tala ku ya eka makete wa swiluva, naswona hi ku losa ka le hansi a yi nyikela ti nosegay eka vanhwanyana, ivi yi tswontswa tizoni ta tona. Eka swiviko swo tala ku tlula xin’we, lexi khomisaka gome hileswi whale a yi na vunene lebyi byo khoma enhan’wini ya yona; hikuva ndzi twe hi ta ndlopfu yin’wana, leyi loko yi vavisekile enyimpini, yi govekeke yi rhendzela xikundzu xa yona ivi yi humesa miseve.

Xa vumune: Loko a yiva a nga swi xiyi ehenhla ka whale eka vuhlayiseki lebyi ehleketiwaka bya le xikarhi ka malwandle lama nga yoxe, u n’wi kuma a nga goveki hikwalaho ka miri lowukulu wa xindzhuti xa yena, naswona a fana ni ximanga, u tlanga elwandle onge hiloko ku ri ndhawu yo hisela eka yona. Kambe hambi swi ri tano u vona matimba ya yena eka ntlangu wa yena. Swikunwana swo anama swa ncila wa yena swi tlangiwa ehenhla emoyeni; kutani loko yi ba ehenhla, ku pfimba loku nga ni ku dzindza ka tilo ku twala tikhilomitara to tala. U nga ha ehleketa leswaku ku duvuriwile xibamu lexikulu; naswona loko wo xiya xirhangiso xo vevuka xa mpfula lexi humaka eka xirhendzevutana lexi nga emakumu yakwe man’wana, a wu ta ehleketa leswaku wolowo a ku ri musi lowu humaka eka khumbi ro khumba.

Xa vuntlhanu: Ku fana ni le ka xiyimo lexi tolovelekeke xo papamala xa leviathan ti- fluke ti etlela swinene ehansi ka mpimo wa nkolo wa yena, kutani ti nga ha voniwi hi ku helela ehansi ka vuandlalo; kambe loko a ri kusuhi no nghena evuentweni, ti flukes ta yena hinkwato leti nga na kwalomu ka makume nharhu wa timitara ta miri wa yena ti hoxiwa ti yimile emoyeni, naswona hi ndlela yaleyo ti tshama ti ri karhi ti rhurhumela nkarhinyana, ku kondza ti duvula ehansi ti nga voniwi. Handle ka ku tshoveka loku tlakukeke —kun’wana loku nga ta hlamuseriwa—ku tlakuka loku ka swiphepherhele swa whale kumbexana i xivono lexikulu swinene lexi voniwaka eka ntumbuluko hinkwawo lowu hanyaka. Eka vuenti lebyi nga riki na makumu ncila lowukulu wu vonaka wu ri karhi wu hlomula hi ndlela yo tshukatshuka etilweni leri tlakukeke swinene. Kutani emilorhweni, xana ndzi vone Sathana lonkulu a ri karhi a humesa ntambhu yakwe leyikulu leyi xanisiweke yi huma eka malangavi ya Baltic ya Tihele. Kambe eku languteni ka swivono swo tano, hinkwaswo swi le ka xiyimo xihi lexi u nga eka xona; loko u ri eDantean, madimona ma ta ta emiehleketweni ya wena; loko eka ya Esaya, tintsumi letikulu. Loko ndzi yime enhlokweni ya xikepe xa mina hi nkarhi wa ku huma ka dyambu loku a ku ri ni xibakabaka ni lwandle ro tshwuka, ndzi tshame ndzi vona ntlhambi lowukulu wa tinhlampfi leti vuriwaka whale evuxeni, hinkwato ti kongoma edyambyini, naswona hi nkarhinyana ti ri karhi ti rhurhumela hi ku twanana hi swibalesa leswi nga ni tinhlohlorhi. Hilaha a swi vonaka hakona eka mina hi nkarhi wolowo, xifaniso lexikulu swonghasi xa ku gandzela swikwembu a xi si tshama xi voniwa, hambi ku ri ePeresiya, kaya ra vagandzeri va ndzilo. Tanihi leswi Ptolemy Philopater a nyikeleke vumbhoni hi ndlopfu ya le Afrika, ndzi tlhele ndzi nyikela vumbhoni hi whale, ndzi yi vula loyi a tinyiketeleke ku tlula swivumbiwa hinkwaswo. Hikuva hi ku ya hi Hosi Juba, tindlopfu ta masocha ta khale hakanyingi a ti bumabumela nimixo ti tlakusiwe misisi ya tona hi ku miyela lokukulu swinene.

Ku pimanisiwa ka xitshuketa eka ndzima leyi, exikarhi ka whale na ndlopfu, ku fikela laha swiyenge swin’wana swa ncila wa yin’wana na nsinya wa yin’wana swi khumbaka, a swi fanelanga ku va na mboyamelo wo veka swirho sweswo swimbirhi leswi hambaneke eka ku ringana, ngopfu ngopfu swivumbiwa leswi hi ku landzelelana swi welaka eka swona. Hikuva tanihi leswi ndlopfu ya matimba swinene yi nga ximanga ntsena eka Levhiyathani, hi ndlela yoleyo, loko yi pimanisiwa ni ncila wa Levhiyathani, xikundzu xa yona i xirhabyani xa lily ntsena. Ku biwa loku chavisaka swinene loku humaka eka xikundzu xa ndlopfu a ku ri ku fana ni ku pompa loku tlangaka ka fan, loko ku pimanisiwa ni ku pfotlosiwa loku nga pimekiki ni ku tshoveka ka swiphepherhele leswi tikaka swa sperm whale, leswi eka swiendlakalo leswi phindha-phindhiweke swi hoxeke swikepe hinkwaswo leswi nga ni swikepe hinkwaswo ni vatirhi va swona eka swona moya, swinene tanihi leswi muendli wa swibalesa wa le Indiya a hoxaka mabilomu yakwe.*

*Hambileswi ku pimanisiwa hinkwako hi ndlela ya vukulu byo angarhela exikarhi ka whale na ndlopfu swi nga riki swa vuhunguki, tanihileswi eka sweswo swo karhi ndlopfu yi yime hi xichavo lexi fanaka swinene na whale lexi mbyana yi yi endlaka eka ndlopfu; hambi swi ri tano, a ku lavi tinhla tin’wana ta ku fana loku hlamarisaka; exikarhi ka leswi ku ni xihloka. Swi tiveka kahle leswaku hakanyingi ndlopfu yi ta koka mati kumbe ntshuri eka xikundzu xa yona, ivi yi yi tlakusa, yi yi rhurhumerisa enambyeni.

Loko ndzi ya ndzi anakanya hi ncila lowu wa matimba, ndzi ya ndzi vilerisiwa hi ku tsandzeka ka mina ku wu phofula. Minkarhi yin’wana ku ni swikombiso swa mavoko eka yona, leswi, hambileswi swi nga ta khavisa voko ra munhu kahle, swi tshamaka swi nga hlamuseleki nikatsongo. Eka ntlhambi lowukulu, lowu hlamarisaka swinene, nkarhi wun’wana, ku ni swikombiso leswi swa xihundla, lerova ndzi twe vahloti lava swi vuleke leswaku swi fana ni swikombiso ni swikombiso swa Free-Mason; leswaku whale, hakunene, hi tindlela leti hi vutlhari yi vulavule ni misava. Naswona a ku na ku lava ku tshukatshuka kun’wana ka whale emirini wa yona hi ku angarhela, leyi teleke hi ku hlamarisa, naswona yi nga tihlamuli eka muhlaseri wa yona la nga ni ntokoto swinene. Dissect him how I may, then, I but go skin deep; A ndzi n’wi tivi, naswona a ndzi nge pfuki ndzi n’wi tivi. Kambe loko ndzi nga tivi hambi ku ri ncila wa whale leyi, xana nhloko ya yona yi yi twisisa njhani? ngopfu ngopfu, xana u twisisa njhani xikandza xa yena, kasi xikandza a nga na xona? U ta vona swirho swa mina swa le ndzhaku, ncila wa mina, u vonaka a vula, kambe xikandza xa mina a xi nge voniwi. Kambe a ndzi swi koti ku swi vona hi ku helela swirho swa yena swa le ndzhaku; and hint what he will about his face, ndzi tlhela ndzi vula leswaku a nga na xikandza.





NDZIMA YA 87. Grand Armada.

Nhlonhle yo leha ni yo koma ya Malacca, leyi hangalakeke edzonga-vuxa ku suka etindhawini ta Birmah, yi vumba ndhawu leyi nga edzongeni swinene eka Asia hinkwaro. Eka layini leyi yaka emahlweni ku suka eka nhlonhle yoleyo ku anama swihlala swo leha swa Sumatra, Java, Bally na Timor; leyi, ni yin’wana yo tala, yi vumbeke xirhendzevutana lexikulu, kumbe rirhangu, hi ku leha ri hlanganisa Asia na Australia, naswona ri avanyisa lwandle ra Indiya leri nga tshoveka nkarhi wo leha ni swihlala swa le vuxeni leswi nga ni swirhendzevutana swo tika. Rirhangu leri ri tlhaviwe hi swikepe swo hlayanyana leswi vuriwaka sally-port leswaku swi olovela swikepe ni ti- whale; leswi vonakaka exikarhi ka swona ku ni swinambyana swa Sunda na Malacca. Hi swinambyana swa Sunda, ngopfu-ngopfu, swikepe leswi yaka eChayina ku suka evupela-dyambu, swi huma emalwandle ya China.

Swinambyana sweswo swo koma swa Sunda swi avanyisa Sumatra na Java; naswona yi yime exikarhi ka rirhangu rero lerikulu ra swihlala, yi seketeriwe hi xirhendzevutana xexo xa rihlaza lexi nga ni xivindzi, lexi tiviwaka hi vatluti tanihi Java Head; a swi fambisani nikatsongo ni nyangwa wa le xikarhi lowu pfulekeke eka mfumo wun’wana lowukulu lowu nga ni marhangu: naswona loko hi tekela enhlokweni rifuwo leri nga heriki ra swinun’hweriso, ni silika, ni swibye swa risima, ni nsuku, ni timhondzo ta tindlopfu, leswi swihlala swa gidi swa lwandle rero ra le vuxeni swi fuweke ha swona, swi vonaka onge lunghiselelo ra nkoka ra ntumbuluko, leswaku rifuwo ro tano, hi ku vumbiwa ka tiko hi roxe, hi xiringaniso ri fanele ku rhwala xivumbeko, hambi swi nga tirhi, xa ku rindziwa eka misava ya le vupela-dyambu leyi khomaka hinkwaswo. Ribuwa ra Straits of Sunda a ri phakeriwi hi makhokholo wolawo lama fumaka lama rindzaka tinyangwa to nghena eMediteraniya, Baltic na Propontis. Ku hambana ni vanhu va le Denmark, vanhu lava va le Vuxeni a va lavi ku xiximiwa loku nga nyawuriki ka maseyila ya le henhla lama ehliweke ku suka eka riendzo leri nga heriki ra swikepe emahlweni ka moya, leswi eka malembe-xidzana lama hundzeke, nivusiku ni ninhlikanhi, swi hundzeke exikarhi ka swihlala swa Sumatra na Java, leswi rhwariweke nhundzu ndzhwalo lowu durhaka swinene wa le vuxeni. Kambe hambileswi va tshikaka nkhuvo wo fana ni lowu hi ku ntshunxeka, a va tshiki nikatsongo ku tivula ka vona ka xibalo lexi tiyeke swinene.

Nkarhi wu nga ha ri emiehleketweni ti- proas ta vugevenga bya le Malay, leti tumbeleke exikarhi ka swikepe leswi nga ni ndzhuti wa le hansi ni swihlala leswitsongo swa Sumatra, ti rhendzele swikepe leswi fambaka hi le matini lama nga ni swinambyana, ti lava hi matimba ku hakeriwa xibalo emakumu ka mafumu ya tona. Hambileswi hi ku xupuriwa loku phindha-phindhiweke ka ngati loku va ku kumeke emavokweni ya swikepe swa le Yuropa leswi fambaka hi swikepe, xivindzi xa vachayeri lava va ti- corsair sweswinyana xi tshikileriwile hi ndlela yo karhi; kambe, hambi ku ri esikwini ra namuntlha, minkarhi yin’wana hi twa hi swikepe swa Manghezi ni swa le Amerika, leswi, ematini wolawo, swi khandziyiweke ni ku phangiwa handle ko tisola.

Hi moya wo saseka, wo tenga, Pequod sweswi a yi tshinela ekusuhi na swinambyana leswi; Akabu hi xikongomelo xo hundza eka tona a nghena elwandle ra Javan, naswona ku suka kwalaho, a ri karhi a famba hi xikepe a kongoma en’walungwini, ehenhla ka mati lama tiviwaka leswaku ma tala ku endzeriwa laha na hala hi Sperm Whale, a sweka eribuweni hi Swihlala swa Philippine, ivi a kuma ribuwa ra le kule ra Japani, hi nkarhi wa nguva leyikulu ya ku hlota ti whale kwalaho. Hi tindlela leti, Pequod leyi rhendzelekaka a yi ta sweka kwalomu ka tindhawu hinkwato leti tiviwaka ta Sperm Whale to famba hi swikepe emisaveni, emahlweni ka ku rhelela ehenhla ka Line ePacific; laha Akabu, hambi leswi kun’wana hinkwako a tsandzekeke eku hlongoriseni ka yena, a hlayele swinene ku nyika nyimpi eka Moby Dick, elwandle leri a a tiveka ngopfu hi ku ya eka rona; naswona hi nguva leyi hi laha ku twisisekaka swinene a nga ha tekiwaka a ri karhi a yi karhata.

Kambe xana sweswi? eka ku lavisisa loku ka zoned, xana Akabu a nga khumbi tiko? xana vatirhi va yena va nwa moya? Hakunene, u ta yima a lava mati. E-e. Ku ringana nkarhi wo leha, sweswi, dyambu leri tsutsumaka hi tiserekisi ri tsutsumile endzeni ka ring ya rona ya ndzilo, naswona a ri lavi swakudya handle ka leswi nga eka rona n’wini. Kutani Akabu. Fungha leswi, na swona, eka mudlayi wa whale. Loko swikepe swin’wana swi layichiwile ehansi hi swilo swa le handle, leswaku swi hundziseriwa eka swikepe swa le matikweni mambe; xikepe xa whale lexi famba-fambaka emisaveni a xi rhwali ndzhwalo handle ka xona ni vatirhi va xona, matlhari ya vona ni leswi va swi lavaka. U ni leswi nga endzeni ka tiva hinkwaro leri nga emabodlheleni eka xikhomela xa yena xo tala. U ballast hi switirhisiwa swa vukorhokeri; kungari hiku hetiseka na pig-lead leyinga tirhisiwiki na kentledge. U rhwala mati ya malembe eka yena. Mati ya khale ya prime Nantucket yo basa; leyi, loko malembe manharhu yi ri karhi yi papamala, Nantucketer, ePacific, yi tsakela ku nwa emahlweni ka mati lama nga ni munyu, kambe tolo yi fambe hi swikepe hi swibya, yi huma eswinambyini swa le Peru kumbe swa le Indiya. Ndzi boxa xiyimo lexi, hikuva, onge hi loko tihomu na marhole swi pfaleriwile hi xikongomelo eka xivala lexi xa le ndzeni swinene; naswona onge hi loko ku anama ka ntlhambi ku fikela sweswi ku va sivele ku dyondza xivangelo lexi kongomeke xa ku yima ka wona; kumbe, swi nga ha endleka, ku va ntsongo swinene, a nga ri na vutshila, naswona hi tindlela hinkwato a nga ri na nandzu naswona a nga ri na ntokoto; hambi swi ri tano, ti- whale leti letitsongo—sweswi na sweswi leti a ti endzela xikepe xa hina lexi rhuleke ti ri etlhelo ka tiva—ti kombise ku pfumala ku chava loku hlamarisaka ni ku titshemba, kutani handle ka sweswo ku chava loku ha rhuleke loku a swi nga koteki ku nga hlamarisiwi. Ku fana ni timbyana ta le kaya a ti ta ti ri karhi ti hi rhendzela, ti ya fika eka swibamu swa hina, ti ti khumba; ku kondza swi lava ku vonaka onge ku loya kun’wana ku va fuyile hi xitshuketa. Queequeg u va tswontswe emimombyeni; Starbuck u va rhurhumerile hi tlhari ra yena; kambe hi ku chava vuyelo bya kona, hikuva nkarhi wolowo u papalate ku yi rhurhumerisa.

Kambe ehansi swinene ka misava leyi yo hlamarisa ehenhla ka misava, misava yin’wana naswona ya ha nga yi tiviki yi hlangane ni mahlo ya hina loko hi languta etlhelo. Hikuva, leswi hayekiweke eka swibye sweswo swa mati, a ku papamala swivumbeko swa vamanana lava an’wisaka va ti- whale, naswona leswi hi ku rhendzela ka swona lokukulu a swi vonaka swi ta va vamanana ku nga ri khale. Tiva leri, hilaha ndzi ringanyeteke hakona, a ri ri erivaleni swinene ku ya fika evuentweni lebyikulu; naswona tanihi tincece ta vanhu loko ti ri karhi ti an’wisa ti ta languta ekule ni xifuva hi ku rhula ni hi ku tiyimisela, onge hiloko ti hanya vutomi byimbirhi byo hambana hi nkarhi wolowo; naswona loko wa ha koka swakudya leswi faka, va ha ri eku dyeni hi tlhelo ra moya hi ku tsundzuka swin’wana leswi nga riki swa laha misaveni;—hambi ku ri tano vana va ti- whale leti a va vonaka va langute ehenhla eka hina, kambe ku nga ri eka hina, onge hi loko hi ri xitsongo ntsena xa Gulfweed eka vana va tona lava ha ku velekiwaka vona. Hi ku papamala ematlhelo ka vona, vamanana na vona a va vonaka va hi languta hi mahlo hi ku miyela. Yin’wana ya tincece leti letintsongo, leyi kusuka eka swikombiso swokarhi swa queer ayi vonaka yinga ri na siku, yingava yi lehe kwalomu ka khume mune wa timitara, naswona kwalomu ka tsevu wa timitara hiku leha. A a ri ni xivundza nyana; hambi leswi miri wa yena a wu vonaka wu kayivela kambe wu horile eka xiyimo xexo xo karhata lexi a wu ri na xona sweswinyana eka reticule ya manana; where, tail to head, and all ready for the final spring, the unborn whale lies bent like a Tartar's bow. The delicate side-fins, and the palms of his flukes, still freshly retained the plaited crumpled appearance of a baby's ears newly arrived from foreign parts.

“Line! line!” cried Queequeg, looking over the gunwale; “him fast! him fast!—Who line him! Who struck?—Two whale; one big, one little!”

“What ails ye, man?” cried Starbuck.

“Look-e here,” said Queequeg, pointing down.

As when the stricken whale, that from the tub has reeled out hundreds of fathoms of rope; as, after deep sounding, he floats up again, and shows the slackened curling line buoyantly rising and spiralling towards the air; so now, Starbuck saw long coils of the umbilical cord of Madame Leviathan, by which the young cub seemed still tethered to its dam. Not seldom in the rapid vicissitudes of the chase, this natural line, with the maternal end loose, becomes entangled with the hempen one, so that the cub is thereby trapped. Some of the subtlest secrets of the seas seemed divulged to us in this enchanted pond. We saw young Leviathan amours in the deep.*

*The sperm whale, as with all other species of the Leviathan, but unlike most other fish, breeds indifferently at all seasons; after a gestation which may probably be set down at nine months, producing but one at a time; though in some few known instances giving birth to an Esau and Jacob:—a contingency provided for in suckling by two teats, curiously situated, one on each side of the anus; but the breasts themselves extend upwards from that. When by chance these precious parts in a nursing whale are cut by the hunter's lance, the mother's pouring milk and blood rivallingly discolour the sea for rods. The milk is very sweet and rich; it has been tasted by man; it might do well with strawberries. When overflowing with mutual esteem, the whales salute more hominum .

And thus, though surrounded by circle upon circle of consternations and affrights, did these inscrutable creatures at the centre freely and fearlessly indulge in all peaceful concernments; yea, serenely revelled in dalliance and delight. But even so, amid the tornadoed Atlantic of my being, do I myself still for ever centrally disport in mute calm; and while ponderous planets of unwaning woe revolve round me, deep down and deep inland there I still bathe me in eternal mildness of joy.

Meanwhile, as we thus lay entranced, the occasional sudden frantic spectacles in the distance evinced the activity of the other boats, still engaged in drugging the whales on the frontier of the host; or possibly carrying on the war within the first circle, where abundance of room and some convenient retreats were afforded them. But the sight of the enraged drugged whales now and then blindly darting to and fro across the circles, was nothing to what at last met our eyes. It is sometimes the custom when fast to a whale more than commonly powerful and alert, to seek to hamstring him, as it were, by sundering or maiming his gigantic tail-tendon. It is done by darting a short-handled cutting-spade, to which is attached a rope for hauling it back again. A whale wounded (as we afterwards learned) in this part, but not effectually, as it seemed, had broken away from the boat, carrying along with him half of the harpoon line; and in the extraordinary agony of the wound, he was now dashing among the revolving circles like the lone mounted desperado Arnold, at the battle of Saratoga, carrying dismay wherever he went.

But agonizing as was the wound of this whale, and an appalling spectacle enough, any way; yet the peculiar horror with which he seemed to inspire the rest of the herd, was owing to a cause which at first the intervening distance obscured from us. But at length we perceived that by one of the unimaginable accidents of the fishery, this whale had become entangled in the harpoon-line that he towed; he had also run away with the cutting-spade in him; and while the free end of the rope attached to that weapon, had permanently caught in the coils of the harpoon-line round his tail, the cutting-spade itself had worked loose from his flesh. So that tormented to madness, he was now churning through the water, violently flailing with his flexible tail, and tossing the keen spade about him, wounding and murdering his own comrades.

This terrific object seemed to recall the whole herd from their stationary fright. First, the whales forming the margin of our lake began to crowd a little, and tumble against each other, as if lifted by half spent billows from afar; then the lake itself began faintly to heave and swell; the submarine bridal-chambers and nurseries vanished; in more and more contracting orbits the whales in the more central circles began to swim in thickening clusters. Yes, the long calm was departing. A low advancing hum was soon heard; and then like to the tumultuous masses of block-ice when the great river Hudson breaks up in Spring, the entire host of whales came tumbling upon their inner centre, as if to pile themselves up in one common mountain. Instantly Starbuck and Queequeg changed places; Starbuck taking the stern.

“Oars! Oars!” he intensely whispered, seizing the helm—“gripe your oars, and clutch your souls, now! My God, men, stand by! Shove him off, you Queequeg—the whale there!—prick him!—hit him! Stand up—stand up, and stay so! Spring, men—pull, men; never mind their backs—scrape them!—scrape away!”

The boat was now all but jammed between two vast black bulks, leaving a narrow Dardanelles between their long lengths. But by desperate endeavor we at last shot into a temporary opening; then giving way rapidly, and at the same time earnestly watching for another outlet. After many similar hair-breadth escapes, we at last swiftly glided into what had just been one of the outer circles, but now crossed by random whales, all violently making for one centre. This lucky salvation was cheaply purchased by the loss of Queequeg's hat, who, while standing in the bows to prick the fugitive whales, had his hat taken clean from his head by the air-eddy made by the sudden tossing of a pair of broad flukes close by.

Riotous and disordered as the universal commotion now was, it soon resolved itself into what seemed a systematic movement; for having clumped together at last in one dense body, they then renewed their onward flight with augmented fleetness. Further pursuit was useless; but the boats still lingered in their wake to pick up what drugged whales might be dropped astern, and likewise to secure one which Flask had killed and waifed. The waif is a pennoned pole, two or three of which are carried by every boat; and which, when additional game is at hand, are inserted upright into the floating body of a dead whale, both to mark its place on the sea, and also as token of prior possession, should the boats of any other ship draw near.

The result of this lowering was somewhat illustrative of that sagacious saying in the Fishery,—the more whales the less fish. Of all the drugged whales only one was captured. The rest contrived to escape for the time, but only to be taken, as will hereafter be seen, by some other craft than the Pequod.





CHAPTER 88. Schools and Schoolmasters.

The previous chapter gave account of an immense body or herd of Sperm Whales, and there was also then given the probable cause inducing those vast aggregations.

Now, though such great bodies are at times encountered, yet, as must have been seen, even at the present day, small detached bands are occasionally observed, embracing from twenty to fifty individuals each. Such bands are known as schools. They generally are of two sorts; those composed almost entirely of females, and those mustering none but young vigorous males, or bulls, as they are familiarly designated.

In cavalier attendance upon the school of females, you invariably see a male of full grown magnitude, but not old; who, upon any alarm, evinces his gallantry by falling in the rear and covering the flight of his ladies. In truth, this gentleman is a luxurious Ottoman, swimming about over the watery world, surroundingly accompanied by all the solaces and endearments of the harem. The contrast between this Ottoman and his concubines is striking; because, while he is always of the largest leviathanic proportions, the ladies, even at full growth, are not more than one-third of the bulk of an average-sized male. They are comparatively delicate, indeed; I dare say, not to exceed half a dozen yards round the waist. Nevertheless, it cannot be denied, that upon the whole they are hereditarily entitled to en bon point .

It is very curious to watch this harem and its lord in their indolent ramblings. Like fashionables, they are for ever on the move in leisurely search of variety. You meet them on the Line in time for the full flower of the Equatorial feeding season, having just returned, perhaps, from spending the summer in the Northern seas, and so cheating summer of all unpleasant weariness and warmth. By the time they have lounged up and down the promenade of the Equator awhile, they start for the Oriental waters in anticipation of the cool season there, and so evade the other excessive temperature of the year.

When serenely advancing on one of these journeys, if any strange suspicious sights are seen, my lord whale keeps a wary eye on his interesting family. Should any unwarrantably pert young Leviathan coming that way, presume to draw confidentially close to one of the ladies, with what prodigious fury the Bashaw assails him, and chases him away! High times, indeed, if unprincipled young rakes like him are to be permitted to invade the sanctity of domestic bliss; though do what the Bashaw will, he cannot keep the most notorious Lothario out of his bed; for, alas! all fish bed in common. As ashore, the ladies often cause the most terrible duels among their rival admirers; just so with the whales, who sometimes come to deadly battle, and all for love. They fence with their long lower jaws, sometimes locking them together, and so striving for the supremacy like elks that warringly interweave their antlers. Not a few are captured having the deep scars of these encounters,—furrowed heads, broken teeth, scolloped fins; and in some instances, wrenched and dislocated mouths.

But supposing the invader of domestic bliss to betake himself away at the first rush of the harem's lord, then is it very diverting to watch that lord. Gently he insinuates his vast bulk among them again and revels there awhile, still in tantalizing vicinity to young Lothario, like pious Solomon devoutly worshipping among his thousand concubines. Granting other whales to be in sight, the fishermen will seldom give chase to one of these Grand Turks; for these Grand Turks are too lavish of their strength, and hence their unctuousness is small. As for the sons and the daughters they beget, why, those sons and daughters must take care of themselves; at least, with only the maternal help. For like certain other omnivorous roving lovers that might be named, my Lord Whale has no taste for the nursery, however much for the bower; and so, being a great traveller, he leaves his anonymous babies all over the world; every baby an exotic. In good time, nevertheless, as the ardour of youth declines; as years and dumps increase; as reflection lends her solemn pauses; in short, as a general lassitude overtakes the sated Turk; then a love of ease and virtue supplants the love for maidens; our Ottoman enters upon the impotent, repentant, admonitory stage of life, forswears, disbands the harem, and grown to an exemplary, sulky old soul, goes about all alone among the meridians and parallels saying his prayers, and warning each young Leviathan from his amorous errors.

Now, as the harem of whales is called by the fishermen a school, so is the lord and master of that school technically known as the schoolmaster. It is therefore not in strict character, however admirably satirical, that after going to school himself, he should then go abroad inculcating not what he learned there, but the folly of it. His title, schoolmaster, would very naturally seem derived from the name bestowed upon the harem itself, but some have surmised that the man who first thus entitled this sort of Ottoman whale, must have read the memoirs of Vidocq, and informed himself what sort of a country-schoolmaster that famous Frenchman was in his younger days, and what was the nature of those occult lessons he inculcated into some of his pupils.

The same secludedness and isolation to which the schoolmaster whale betakes himself in his advancing years, is true of all aged Sperm Whales. Almost universally, a lone whale—as a solitary Leviathan is called—proves an ancient one. Like venerable moss-bearded Daniel Boone, he will have no one near him but Nature herself; and her he takes to wife in the wilderness of waters, and the best of wives she is, though she keeps so many moody secrets.

The schools composing none but young and vigorous males, previously mentioned, offer a strong contrast to the harem schools. For while those female whales are characteristically timid, the young males, or forty-barrel-bulls, as they call them, are by far the most pugnacious of all Leviathans, and proverbially the most dangerous to encounter; excepting those wondrous grey-headed, grizzled whales, sometimes met, and these will fight you like grim fiends exasperated by a penal gout.

The Forty-barrel-bull schools are larger than the harem schools. Like a mob of young collegians, they are full of fight, fun, and wickedness, tumbling round the world at such a reckless, rollicking rate, that no prudent underwriter would insure them any more than he would a riotous lad at Yale or Harvard. They soon relinquish this turbulence though, and when about three-fourths grown, break up, and separately go about in quest of settlements, that is, harems.

Another point of difference between the male and female schools is still more characteristic of the sexes. Say you strike a Forty-barrel-bull—poor devil! all his comrades quit him. But strike a member of the harem school, and her companions swim around her with every token of concern, sometimes lingering so near her and so long, as themselves to fall a prey.
