Loko ndzi ri karhi ndzi famba, a ndzi tele hi ku ehleketa; leswi a swi paluxiwile hi xihoxo eka mina hi Kaputeni Akabu, swi ndzi tatile hi ku nga twisiseki ko karhi ka nhova ka ku vava malunghana na yena. NDZIMA YA 17. Ramadan.

Tanihi leswi Ramadan ya Queequeg, kumbe Ku Titsona swakudya ni ku Titsongahata, a yi fanele ku ya emahlweni siku hinkwaro, a ndzi hlawulanga ku n’wi kavanyeta ku kondza ku va vusiku byi ya; hikuva ndzi tlangela xichavo lexikulu eka swiboha swa vukhongeri bya un’wana na un’wana, ku nga khathariseki leswaku swi hlekisa ku fikela kwihi, naswona a ndzi swi kumanga embilwini ya mina ku tekela ehansi hambi ku ri vandlha ra tinjiya leti gandzelaka xitulu xa tinjiya; kumbe swivumbiwa sweswo swin’wana eswiphen’wini swo karhi swa misava ya hina, leswi hi mpimo wo karhi wa vutshila bya milenge lowu nga si tshamaka wu va kona eka tipulanete tin’wana, swi nkhinsamaka emahlweni ka miri wa n’wini wa misava loyi a feke ntsena hikwalaho ka nhundzu leyi nga ringaniki leyi nga ha riki ya n’wini ni ku hirhiwa hi vito ra yena.

Ku holova ka hina hinkwako na yena a ku nga ta pfuna; a a ve, ndzi ri: naswona Tilo ri hi twele vusiwana hinkwerhu—Mapresbyterian na Vahedeni hi ku fanana—hikuva hinkwerhu hi ndlela yo karhi hi pandzekile hi ndlela yo chavisa hi nhloko, naswona hi ndlela yo vava hi lava ku lunghisiwa.

Hi mina—Iximayele.” Kambe hinkwaswo swi tshame swi miyerile ku fana ni le ku sunguleni. Ndzi sungule ku chava swinene. A ndzi n’wi pfumelele nkarhi wo tala swonghasi; A ndzi ehleketa leswaku swi nga ha endleka a khomiwe hi vuvabyi bya apoplectic fit. Ndzi langute hi le xikarhi ka xilotlelo; kambe nyangwa wu pfuleka ekhonweni leyi nga tolovelekangiki ya kamara, ntshembo wa xilotlelo a wu ri lowu gombonyokeke ni wo biha ntsena. A ndzi vona ntsena xiphemu xa foot-board ya mubedo ni layini ya rirhangu, kambe a ku nga ri na swin’wana swo tlula sweswo. Ndzi hlamarile ku vona ku wisa erirhangwini xisirhelelo xa mapulanga xa harpoon ya Queequeg, lexi n’wini wa muti madyambu lama hundzeke a xi tekeke eka yena, hi nga si khandziya hi ya ekamareni. Sweswo swa hlamarisa, ndzi ehlekete; kambe hi ndlela yihi na yihi, tani hi leswi harpoon yi yimeke kwalahaya, naswona yi nga talaka kumbe yi nga si tshama yi ya ehandle ka yona, hikwalaho yi fanele yi va endzeni ka laha, naswona ku nga ri na xihoxo lexi nga kotekaka.

“Queequeg!—Queequeg!”—hinkwaswo swi ha ri kona. Ku fanele ku humelele swo karhi. Apoplexy! Ndzi ringete ku pfula rivanti hi ku pfurha; kambe hi ku nonoka yi lwisane na swona. Loko ndzi tsutsumela ehansi hi switepisi, ndzi hatlise ndzi vula ku kanakana ka mina eka munhu wo sungula loyi ndzi hlanganeke na yena—ku nga mutirhi wa le kamareni. “La! la!” a rila, “Ndzi ehlekete leswaku ku fanele ku ri mhaka leyi nga kona. Ndzi ye ndzi ya endla mubedo endzhaku ka swakudya swa nimixo, naswona nyangwa a wu lotleriwile; naswona ku nga ri mbeva leyi nga ta twiwa; naswona a ku ri ntsena ku miyela swinene ku sukela kwalaho. Kambe ndzi ehlekete, swi nga ha endleka, havumbirhi bya n’wina a mi fambile mi ya khiya mindzhwalo ya n’wina leswaku yi hlayisiwa hi ndlela leyi hlayisekeke. Manana Hussey u hatle a humelela, a khome xibya xa mustard hi voko rin’we na vinegar-cruet hi lerin’wana, a ha ku hambana na ntirho wo khathalela ti castor, naswona a holovela mufana wa yena lontsongo wa muntima hi nkarhi wolowo.

Xana ku khomiwe hi yini wena mutirhi kulorhi wa xikepe?”

A ndzi si yi vona ku sukela loko ndzi yi vekile kwalaho.” Hi ku tsutsumela eka xigwitsirisi lexitsongo ehansi ka ndhawu yo fika eka yona ya switepisi, u langute endzeni, kutani a vuya, a ndzi byela leswaku harpoon ya Queequeg a yi nga ri kona. U rila a ku: “U tidlayile.” “I khombo ra kona Stiggs u endliwe nakambe—ku famba counterpane yin’wana—Xikwembu xi twela mana wa xona wa xisiwana vusiwana!—swi ta va ku ri marhumbi ya yindlu ya mina. Xana jaha ra xisiwana ri na makwavo wa xisati? U kwihi nhwanyana yoloye?—kwalaho, Betty, yana eka Snarles the Painter, kutani u n’wi byela leswaku a ndzi penda mfungho, lowu nga na—“ku nga pfumeleriwi ku tidlaya laha, naswona ku nga ri na ku dzaha fole ephalari;”—swi nga ha dlaya swinyenyana leswi haswimbirhi hi nkarhi wun’we. Dlaya? HOSI Xikwembu a xi twele vusiwana eka xipoko xa xona! I yini mpfumawulo wolowo kwalahaya? Wena jaha, avast kwalahaya!”

Futhi a tsutsumela ehenhla ka mina, u ni khomile loko ni ri karhi ni tlhela ni ringeta ku pfula rivanti hi nkani.

“A ndzi swi pfumeleli; A ndzi nge vi na ndhawu ya mina leyi onhakeke. Famba u ya lava muendli wa swilotlelo, ku na un’we kwalomu ka khilomitara ku suka laha. Kambe avast!” a veka voko ra yena exikhwameni xa yena xa le tlhelo, “hi lexi xilotlelo lexi nga ta nghena, ndza ehleketa; a hi voneni.” Nakona hi sweswo, u yi hundzuluxile eka xilotlelo; kambe, khombo ra kona! Boluti yo engetela ya Queequeg yi tshame yi nga hoxiwanga endzeni.

“Have to burst it open,” ku vula mina, naswona a ndzi ri karhi ndzi tsutsuma ndzi ya ehansi hi ndlela yo nghena nyana, ku sungula kahle, loko n’wini wa muti a ndzi khoma, nakambe a hlambanya leswaku a ndzi fanelanga ku mbundzumuxa ndhawu ya yena; kambe ndzi n’wi handzule, naswona hi ku tsutsuma ka miri ka xitshuketa ndzi ti tsutsumele hi ku helela eka mfungho.

Hi mpfumawulo wo hlamarisa nyangwa wu haha ​​wu pfuleka, naswona xikomba-nkarhi xi rhurhumela erirhangwini, xi rhumela pulasita ehenhla ka silingi; naswona kwalaho, matilo lamanene! a ku tshame Queequeg, loyi a a ri ni xivundza naswona a a tihlengeletile; kunene exikarhi ka kamara; a rhurhumela ehenhla ka ti ham ta yena, a khoma Yojo ehenhla ka nhloko ya yena. A nga languteki hi ndlela yin’we kumbe hi tlhelo rin’wana, kambe a a tshame ku fana ni xifaniso lexi vatliweke lexi nga ni xikombiso lexi kayivelaka xa vutomi lebyi gingiritekaka.

“Queequeg,” ku vula mina, ndzi tlhandlukela eka yena, “Queequeg, xana ku khomiwe hi yini?”

“A nga si tshama a tshama siku hinkwaro, a swi tano ke?” ku vula n’wini wa muti.

Kambe hinkwaswo leswi hi swi vuleke, a ku na rito ni rin’we leri hi nga ri koka ri huma eka yena; Ndzi hetelele ndzi titwe ndzi lava ku n’wi susumeta ehenhla, leswaku ndzi ta cinca xiyimo xa yena, hikuva a swi lava ku nga tiyiseleleki, a swi vonaka swi vava swinene naswona swi nga siveriwa hi ndlela leyi nga riki ya ntumbuluko; ngopfu-ngopfu, tanihi leswi hi ku koteka hinkwako a a tshame tano ku tlula tiawara ta tsevu kumbe khume, a famba na yena handle ka swakudya swa yena swa nkarhi na nkarhi.

"Manana. Hussey,” ku vula mina, “wa hanya eka swiendlakalo hinkwaswo; kutani hi tshike, loko u swi rhandza, kutani ndzi ta tiyisela mhaka leyi leyi nga tolovelekangiki hi ndzexe.”

Hi ku pfala nyangwa eka n’wini wa muti, ndzi ringete ku hlula Queequeg leswaku a teka xitulu; kambe a swi pfuni nchumu. Kwalaho u tshame kona; naswona hinkwaswo leswi a a ta swi endla—eka vutshila bya mina hinkwabyo bya xichavo ni ku rhurhumela ka mina—a a nga ta tshukatshukisa xipikiri, kumbe ku vula rito ni rin’we, hambi ku ri ku ndzi languta, kumbe ku xiya vukona bya mina hi ndlela yitsongo.

Ndza tivutisa, ndzi ehlekete, loko leswi swi nga ha va xiphemu xa Ramadan ya yena; xana va titsona swakudya eka ti- ham ta vona hi ndlela yoleyo exihlaleni xa rikwavo. Swi fanele swi ri tano; ina, i xiphemu xa ripfumelo ra yena, ndza ehleketa; kahle-kahle, a a wisa; u ta pfuka hi ku hatlisa kumbe endzhakunyana, handle ko kanakana. A swi nge tshami hilaha ku nga heriki, ndza khensa Xikwembu, naswona Ramadan ya yena yi ta kan’we ntsena hi lembe; naswona a ndzi tshembi leswaku swi hlayisa nkarhi swinene hi nkarhi wolowo.

Ndzi rhelerile ndzi ya dya swakudya swa nimadyambu. Endzhaku ko tshama nkarhi wo leha va yingisela switori swo leha swa vatluti van’wana lava a va ha ku huma eka riendzo ra plum-pudding, hilaha a va ri vitana hakona (hileswaku, riendzo ro koma ro hlota ti whale hi xikepe xa schooner kumbe brig, leri pfaleriweke en’walungwini wa layini , eLwandle ra Atlantic ntsena); endzhaku ko yingisela ti plum-puddingers leti ku fikela kwalomu ka awara ya khume-n’we, ndzi tlhandlukele switepisi leswaku ndzi ya etlela, ndzi titwa ndzi tiyisile swinene hi nkarhi lowu Queequeg hakunene u fanele a tisile Ramadan ya yena eku heleni. Kambe e-e; kwalaho a a ri ntsena laha ndzi n’wi siyeke kona; a a nga si rhurhumela ni yin’we. Ndzi sungule ku n’wi karhata; a swi vonaka swi nga ri na xikongomelo ni ku penga swinene ku va a tshame kwalaho siku hinkwaro ni hafu ya vusiku eka ti- ham ta yena ekamareni ro titimela, a khome xiphemu xa mapulanga enhlokweni yakwe.

“Hikwalaho ka tilo, Queequeg, pfuka u ti rhurhumela; pfuka u ya dya swakudya swa ni nhlikanhi. U ta dlayiwa hi ndlala; u ta tidlaya, Queequeg.” Kambe a nga hlamulanga ni rito ni rin’we.

Hikwalaho, hi ku n’wi heleriwa hi ntshembo, ndzi tiyimisele ku ya etlela ni ku etlela; naswona handle ko kanakana, ku nga si hela nkarhi wo leha, a a ta ndzi landzela. Kambe emahlweni ko hundzuka, ndzi teke xiketi xa mina xo tika xa dzovo ra bere, ndzi xi lahlela ehenhla ka yena, tani hi leswi a xi tshembisa ku va vusiku byo titimela swinene; naswona a a nga ri na nchumu handle ka xiketi xa yena xa xirhendzevutana lexi tolovelekeke. Ku ringana nkarhi wo karhi, endla hinkwaswo leswi a ndzi ta swi endla, a ndzi nga swi koti ku nghena eka vurhongo lebyi nga nyawuriki. A ndzi timile khandlela; ni ku ehleketa ntsena hi Queequeg—ku nga ri ku suka eka timitara ta mune—a tshame kwalaho eka xiyimo xexo lexi nga oloviki, a ri yexe hi xirhami ni emunyameni; leswi swi ndzi endle ndzi va ni gome hakunene. Anakanya hi swona; ku etlela vusiku hinkwabyo ekamareni rin’we na muhedeni loyi a pfukeke swinene eka ti ham ta yena eka Ramadan leyi yo vava, leyi nga riki na vutihlamuleri!

Kambe hi ndlela yo karhi ndzi wile eku heteleleni, naswona a ndzi nga tivi nchumu wun’wana ku fikela loko ri pela; loko, a languta etlhelo ka mubedo, ku ri na Queequeg loyi a a rhurhumerile, onge hi loko a rhurhumerisiwile ehansi. Kambe hi ku hatlisa loko ku vona dyambu ro sungula ku nghena hi fasitere, a tlakuka, a ri na mahlangano yo tiya no grating, kambe hi ku languta loku tsakeke; a rhelela a kongoma eka mina laha a ndzi etlele kona; a tshikilela nhloko ya yena nakambe eka ya mina; naswona u vule leswaku Ramadan ya yena yi herile.

Sweswi, tani hi leswi ndzi nga tshama ndzi ringanyeta, a ndzi na ku kanetana na vukhongeri bya munhu wihi na wihi, ku nga ha va byihi, ntsena loko munhu yoloye a nga dlayi kumbe ku rhuketela munhu un’wana, hikuva munhu yoloye un’wana na yena a nga swi pfumeli. Kambe loko vukhongeri bya wanuna byi va lebyi nga ni vuxiyaxiya hakunene; loko ku ri ku xanisiwa lokunene eka yena; naswona, eku heteleleni, yi endla misava leyi ya hina yi va ndhawu yo wisela eka yona leyi nga nyawuriki ku tshama eka yona; kutani ndzi ehleketa leswaku i nkarhi wa le henhla ku teka munhu yoloye etlhelo ndzi kanetana na yena yinhla.

Nakona ntsena hi swona sweswi ndzi swi endleke na Queequeg. “Queequeg,” ku vula mina, “nghena emubedweni sweswi, u etlela u ndzi yingisela.” Kutani ndzi ye emahlweni, ndzi sungula hi ku tlakuka ni nhluvuko wa vukhongeri bya khale, ni ku fika ehansi eka vukhongeri byo hambana-hambana bya nkarhi wa sweswi, hi nkarhi wolowo ndzi tirhe hi matimba ku komba Queequeg leswaku hinkwaswo leswi swa Tilente, Tiramadan ni ku tshama nkarhi wo leha ka ham-squattings hi xirhami , makamara lama nga tsakisiki a ma ri vuhunguki lebyikulu; swo biha eka rihanyo; leswi nga pfuniki nchumu eka moya-xiviri; ku kanetana, hi ku komisa, na milawu leyi nga erivaleni ya Vuhlayiseki na vutlhari lebyi tolovelekeke. Ndzi n’wi byerile, na mina, leswaku yena hi ku va eka swilo swin’wana a ri xiharhi lexi nga na vutlhari byo tlula mpimo na vutlhari, swi ndzi vavisile, swi ndzi vavisile swinene, ku n’wi vona sweswi a ri xiphukuphuku hi ndlela yo vava swinene hi Ramadan leyi yo hlekisa ya yena. Handle ka sweswo, ndzi kanetile, ku titsona swakudya swi endla leswaku miri wu nghena; hikwalaho moya wu nghena; naswona mianakanyo hinkwayo leyi velekiweke hi ku titsona swakudya yi fanele yi dlawa hi ndlala hi hafu. Lexi hi xona xivangelo lexi endlaka leswaku vunyingi bya vukhongeri lebyi nga ni vuvabyi bya dyspeptic byi tlangela mianakanyo yo tano ya gome malunghana ni vutomi bya vona bya le ndzhaku ka rifu. Hi rito rin’we, Queequeg, ku vula mina, hi ndlela yo hambukisa; tihele i mianakanyo leyi tswariweke ro sungula eka apula-dumpling leyi nga gayiwangiki; naswona ku sukela kwalaho yi yisiwe emahlweni hi ku tirhisa dyspepsias ya ndzhaka leyi kurisiweke hi Ramadan.

Kutani ndzi vutise Queequeg loko yena hi byakwe a tshame a karhatiwa hi vuvabyi bya ku tsandzeka ku dya; ku phofula mianakanyo leyi erivaleni swinene, leswaku a ta yi amukela U te e-e; ntsena eka xiendlakalo xin’we lexi nga rivalekiki. A ku ri endzhaku ka nkhuvo lowukulu lowu nyikeriweke hi tata wa yena hosi, eku kumeni ka nyimpi leyikulu laha makume ntlhanu wa valala va dlayiweke kwalomu ka awara ya mbirhi nimadyambu, naswona hinkwavo va swekiwile va dyiwa hi wona madyambu wolawo.

“A ku nge he vi kona, Queequeg,” ku vula mina, ndzi ri karhi ndzi rhurhumela; “leswo swi ta endla;” hikuva a ndzi tiva swibumabumelo handle ka loko a swi ringanyeta ku ya emahlweni. A ndzi vone mutluti loyi a endzeleke xihlala xexo, kutani a ndzi byela leswaku a ku ri mukhuva, loko nyimpi leyikulu yi kumiwile kwalaho, ku barbecue hinkwavo lava dlayiweke exivaveni kumbe entangeni wa muhluri; kutani, ha un’we-un’we, a ti vekiwa eka swigwitsirisi leswikulu swa mapulanga, naswona ti khavisiwe hi ku rhendzeleka ku fana ni pilau, hi mihandzu ya xinkwa ni tikhokhonati; naswona va ri na parsley yin’wana emilon’wini ya vona, va rhumeriwe ku rhendzeleka na ku bumabumela ka muhluri eka vanghana va yena hinkwavo, onge hi loko tinyiko leti a ti ri ti turkey to tala swinene ta Khisimusi.

Phela, a ndzi ehleketa leswaku marito ya mina malunghana ni vukhongeri ma endle leswaku Queequeg yi khumbe ngopfu. Hikuva, eku sunguleni, hi ndlela yo karhi a a vonaka a nga ri na vuxiyaxiya ku twa emhakeni yoleyo ya nkoka, handle ka loko a kambisisiwa hi ku ya hi langutelo ra yena n’wini; naswona, eka xiyimo xa vumbirhi, a nga ndzi twisisanga ku tlula n’we-xa-nharhu, a sofa miehleketo ya mina ntsena hilaha ndzi nga ta endla hakona; naswona, eku heteleleni, handle ko kanakana a a ehleketa leswaku u tiva swo tala hi vukhongeri bya ntiyiso ku tlula mina. U ndzi langutile hi muxaka wo karhi wa ku vilela loku titsongahataka ni ntwela-vusiwana, onge hiloko a ehleketa leswaku swi khomisa gome swinene leswaku jaha ro tano leri twisisekaka ri fanele ri lahleka hi ndlela leyi nga riki na ntshembo eka vukhongeri bya vuhedeni bya evhangeli.

Eku heteleleni hi pfukile hi ambala; na Queequeg, hi dya swakudya swa nimixo leswi hlamarisaka swa ti- chowder ta mixaka hinkwayo, leswaku n’wini wa muti a nga endli vuyelo byo tala hikwalaho ka Ramadan ya yena, hi humile hi ku hatlisa hi ya khandziya xikepe xa Pequod, hi ri karhi hi rhendzeleka, hi tlhela hi tshovela meno ya hina hi marhambu ya halibut.





NDZIMA YA 18. Marka ya yena.

Loko hi ri karhi hi famba ehansi emakumu ka ribuwa ra lwandle hi kongoma exikepeni, Queequeg a rhwale harpoon ya yena, Captain Peleg hi rito ra yena ro khwaxa u hi bumabumerile hi rito lerikulu ku suka eka wigwam ya yena, a vula leswaku a nga ehleketi leswaku munghana wa mina i mudlayi wa vanhu, naswona ku ya emahlweni a tivisa leswaku u pfumelele e-e vanhu lava dyaka vanhu lava a va ri eka xikepe xexo xa vutshila, handle ka loko va tshame va humesa maphepha ya vona.

“U vula yini hi sweswo, Kaputeni Peleg?” ku vula mina, sweswi ndzi tlula-tlula ehenhla ka swisirhelelo, ndzi siya comrade ya mina yi yime eribuweni.

U hlamule a ku: “Ndzi vula leswaku u fanele a kombisa maphepha yakwe.”

“Ina,” ku vula Kaputeni Bildad hi rito ra yena ro khwaxa, a ri karhi a nghenisa nhloko ya yena ku suka endzhaku ka ya Peleg, a huma eka wigwam. “U fanele ku kombisa leswaku u hundzukile. N’wana wa munyama,” u engeterile, a hundzulukela eka Queequeg, “xana sweswi u le ku hlanganyeleni ni kereke yihi na yihi ya Vukreste?”

Ndzi te: “Ha yini, i xirho xa Kereke yo sungula ya Vandlha.” Hi leswi, leswaku swiharhi swo tala swa tihanyi leswi nga ni ti- tattoo leswi fambaka hi xikepe eswikepeni swa Nantucket eku heteleleni swi fika swi hundzuriwa tikereke.

Bildad u huwelerile a ku: “Kereke yo Sungula ya Vandlha, ku yini! leyi gandzelaka endlwini ya nhlengeletano ya Mudiyakoni Deteronoma Coleman?” naswona hi ku vula sweswo, a humesa tinghilazi ta yena ta mahlo, a ti tswontswa hi xihuku xa yena lexikulu xo tshwuka xa bandana, kutani a ti ambala hi vukheta swinene, a huma eka wigwam, naswona a titshege hi ku nonoka ehenhla ka swisirhelelo, a languta kahle nkarhi wo leha eka Queequeg.

“Xana a ri xirho ku fikela rini?” u tlhele a ku, a hundzuluka a languta eka mina; “a swi lehe ngopfu, ndzi tsakela ku ringanyeta, jaha.”

“E-e,” ku vula Peleg, “naswona na yena a nga khuvuriwanga kahle, kutani a swi ta va swi n’wi hlambele swin’wana swa vulungu bya diyavulosi yoloye emahlweni ka yena.”

Bildad u huwelerile a ku: “Tivisa, sweswi, xana Mufilisita loyi i xirho xa nkarhi na nkarhi xa nhlengeletano ya Mudiyakoni Deteronoma? A ndzi si tshama ndzi n’wi vona a ya kona, naswona ndzi ri hundzisela siku rin’wana ni rin’wana ra Hosi.”

“A ndzi tivi nchumu hi Mudiyakoni Deteronoma kumbe nhlengeletano ya yena,” ku vula mina; “leswi ndzi swi tivaka hileswaku, leswaku Queequeg laha i xirho lexi velekiweke xa Kereke yo Sungula ya Vandlha. Hi yexe i mudiyakoni, Queequeg i mudiyakoni.”

Bildada u te hi ku nonoka a ku: “Jaha, u skylarking na mina—tihlamusele, wena jaha ra Muhiti. Xana u vula kereke yihi? ndzi hlamule.”

Hi ku tikuma ndzi susumetiwile hi matimba hi ndlela yoleyo, ndzi hlamurile. “Ndzi vula, nkulukumba, Kereke yin’we ya khale ya Khatoliki leyi mina na wena, na Kaputeni Peleg kwalahaya, na Queequeg lahawani, na hina hinkwerhu, na n’wana un’wana na un’wana wa manana na moya wa hina hi welaka eka yona; Vandlha ro Sungula lerikulu ni leri nga heriki ra misava leyi hinkwayo ya vugandzeri; hinkwerhu hi wela eka sweswo; ntsena van’wana va hina hi tlangela ti- crotchet tin’wana ta queer no ways touching the grand ripfumelo; hi leswaku hinkwerhu hi hlanganisa mavoko.”

“Splice, u vula ku hlanganisa mavoko,” ku huwelela Peleg, a tshinela. “Jaha, swa antswa u rhumela xikepe xa murhumiwa, ematshan’wini ya voko ra le mahlweni; A ndzi si tshama ndzi twa dyondzo yo antswa. Diyakoni Deteronoma—ha yini Tatana Mapple hi yexe a nga swi kotanga ku yi hlula, naswona u hlayele swo karhi. Tanani khandziya, tana u khandziya; never mind hi maphepha. Ndzi ri, byela Quohog kwalaho—xana u n’wi vitana yini? byela Quohog leswaku a famba hi milenge. Hi anchor leyikulu, i harpoon yonghasi leyi a nga na yona kwalaho! swi languteka ku fana na swilo swa kahle leswi; naswona u swi khoma hi ndlela leyinene. Ndzi ri, Quohog, kumbe vito ra wena rihi, xana u tshame u yima enhlokweni ya xikepe xa whale-boat? xana u tshame u ba nhlampfi?”

Handle ko vula rito, Queequeg, hi ndlela ya yena ya muxaka wa nhova, u tlulele ehenhla ka swisirhelelo, ku suka kwalaho a nghena eka marha ya xin’wana xa swikepe swa whale leswi lengalengaka etlhelo; kutani a tiyisa matsolo yakwe ya ximatsi, ni ku yimisa harpoon yakwe, a huwelela hi ndlela yo karhi yo tanihi leyi:—

“Cap’ain, wa n’wi vona xitsongo drop tar ehenhla ka mati dere? Wa n’wi vona? kahle-kahle, n’wi nyike tihlo rin’we ra whale, kahle-kahle, den!” naswona hi ku yi kongomisa hi ndlela yo kariha, u tsutsumele nsimbi kunene ehenhla ka rimhondzo ro anama ra Bildad wa khale, a basile a tsemakanya swithezi swa xikepe, kutani a ba ndhawu leyi hatimaka ya xikontiri leswaku yi nga voniwi.

“Sweswi,” ku vula Queequeg, a ri karhi a koka layini hi ku miyela, “spos-ee him whale-e eye; hikokwalaho ka yini, tatana whale u file.”

“Hatlisa, Bildad,” ku vula Peleg, munghana wa yena, loyi, hi ku hlamarisiwa hi ndhawu ya le kusuhi ya harpoon leyi hahaka, a tlheleleke endzhaku a kongoma egongweni ya le khabini. “Hatlisa, ndzi ri, wena Bildad, u teka maphepha ya xikepe. Hi fanele hi va na Hedgehog kwalahaya, ndzi vula Quohog, eka xin’wana xa swikepe swa hina. Languta wena, Quohog, hi ta ku nyika lay ya vu makume nhungu, naswona sweswo swi tlula rini na rini swi nyikiwile harpooneer yet out of Nantucket.”

Kutani ehansi hi nghene endzeni ka khabini, naswona lexi ndzi tsakiseke swinene Queequeg u hatle a tsarisiwa exikarhi ka khampani leyi fanaka ya xikepe leyi mina hi ndzexe a ndzi ri eka yona.

Loko ti preliminaries hinkwato ti herile naswona Peleg a lunghekele hinkwaswo ku sayina, u hundzuluke eka mina kutani a ku, “Ndzi ehleketa leswaku, Quohog kwalaho a va tivi ku tsala, a swi tano ke? Ndzi ri, Quohog, blast ye! xana u sayina vito ra wena kumbe u endla mfungho wa wena?”

Kambe eka xivutiso lexi, Queequeg, loyi a nga hlanganyela kambirhi kumbe kanharhu emahlweni ka minkhuvo leyi fanaka, a a vonaka a nga khomiwi hi tingana hi tindlela; kambe ku teka peni leyi nyikeriweke, leyi kopiweke ehenhla ka phepha, endhawini leyi faneleke, leyi fanaka hi ku kongoma ya xifaniso xa xirhendzevutana xa queer lexi a xi tsariwile evokweni ra yena; lerova hi xihoxo xa Kaputeni Peleg xo nonon’hwa lexi khumbaka xivilelo xa yena, xi yime nchumu wo fana ni lowu:—

Quohog. mfungho wa yena wa X.

Hi nkarhi wolowo Kaputeni Bildad u tshame hi ku hiseka ni hi ku tiyimisela a langute Queequeg, naswona eku heteleleni a pfuka hi ku xiximeka ni ku rhurhumela eswikhwameni leswikulu swa jazi rakwe leri nga ni swikete swo anama, a humesa ntlhambi wa swiphephana, ivi a hlawula xin’wana lexi nga ni nhloko-mhaka leyi nge “The Latter Day Coming; kumbe No Time to Lose,” a yi veka emavokweni ya Queequeg, ivi a va khoma na buku hi ya yena havumbirhi bya yona, a languta hi ku hiseka ematihlweni ya yena, kutani a ku, “N’wana wa munyama, ndzi fanele ku endla ntirho wa mina hi wena; Ndzi xiphemu xa n’wini wa xikepe lexi, naswona ndzi titwa ndzi khathala hi mimoya-xiviri ya vatirhi va xona hinkwavo; loko wa ha namarhela tindlela ta wena ta Vuhedeni, leti ndzi ti chavaka hi ku vava, ndza ku kombela, u nga tshami u ri hlonga ra Belial. Surn xifaniso xa Bell, na dragona yo nyenyetsa; tshika vukarhi lebyi taka; anakanya tihlo ra wena, ndzi ri; Oho! vunene bya tintswalo! fambela ekule ni mugodi lowu pfurhaka ndzilo!”

Xin’wana xa lwandle ra munyu kambe xi tshame xi ri kona eka ririmi ra khale ra Bildad, hi ku hambana-hambana xi pfanganisiwe ni swiga swa Matsalwa ni swa le kaya.

“Avast kwalahaya, avast kwalahaya, Bildad, avast sweswi yi onha harpooneer ya hina,” ku huwelela Peleg. “Varpoone lava chavaka Xikwembu a va si tshama va endla vafambi lavanene—yi humesa shaka eka vona; ku hava harpooneer leyi ringanaka na straw loyi aint pretty sharkish. A ku ri na Nat Swaine lontsongo, loyi a tshameke a va xivindzi xa nhloko ya xikepe eka hinkwaswo swa Nantucket na Vineyard; u joyine nhlengeletano, naswona a nga kalanga a fika eka leswinene. U chavile swinene hi moya-xiviri wa yena wa plaguy, lerova a hunguteka a tlhela a rhurhumela eka ti whale, hi ku chava ku ba mavoko endzhaku, loko ko tshuka ku va na xitofu ivi a ya eka Davy Jones.”

“Peleg! Peleg!” ku vula Bildada, a tlakusa mahlo ni mavoko, “wena hi wexe, ku fana na mina, u vone minkarhi yo tala ya khombo; wa swi tiva, Pelegi, leswi ku nga ku chava rifu; kutani, u nga swi kotisa ku yini ku prate hi xivumbeko lexi xo ka xi nga chavi Xikwembu. U kaneta mbilu ya wena n'wini, Pelegi. Ndzi byele, loko yona Pequod leyi lahawani a yi ri na swigolonyana swa yona swinharhu ehenhla ka lwandle eka xidzedze xexo eJapani, riendzo rero leri fanaka loko u famba munghana na Kaputeni Akabu, xana a wu nga ehleketi hi Rifu ni Ku Avanyisa hi nkarhi wolowo?”

“N’wi yingiseni, n’wi tweni sweswi,” ku huwelela Peleg, a macha a tsemakanya khabini, a nghenisa mavoko ya yena ehansi swinene eswikhwameni swa yena,—“n’wi yingiseni, hinkwenu. Anakanya hi sweswo! Loko nkarhi wun’wana ni wun’wana hi ehleketa leswaku xikepe xi ta nwela! Rifu na Vuavanyisi ke? Yini? Hi timhandzi hinkwato tinharhu ti endla ku duma ko tano loku nga heriki etlhelo; ni lwandle rin’wana ni rin’wana leri tshovekaka ehenhla ka hina, emahlweni ni le ndzhaku. Xana u ehleketa hi Rifu Ni Ku Avanyisa ke? E-e! a ku na nkarhi wo ehleketa hi Rifu hi nkarhi wolowo. Vutomi a ku ri lebyi mina na Kaputeni Akabu a hi ehleketa ha byona; ni ndlela yo ponisa mavoko hinkwawo—ndlela yo rig jury-masts—ndlela yo nghena eribuweni ra le kusuhi; sweswo hi swona leswi a ndzi swi ehleketa.”

Bildad a nga ha vulanga, kambe a ri karhi a khoma-khoma jazi rakwe, a hlongorisa ehenhla ka deki, laha hi n’wi landzeleke kona. Kwalaho u yime, a rhulile swinene a langute vaendli van’wana va maseyila lava a va lunghisa seyila ya le henhla emasengeni. Sweswi na sweswi a a korhama leswaku a ya teka xiphemu, kutani a hlayisa makumu ya ntambhu leyi nga ni xikontiri, leyi handle ka sweswo a yi nga ha va yi tlangisiwile.





NDZIMA YA 19. Muprofeta.

“Van’wakulobye, xana mi rhumele xikepe xexo xana?”

Mina na Queequeg a hi ha ku huma ePequod, naswona a hi ri karhi hi rhendzeleka-rhendzeleka ekule na mati, hi nkarhi wolowo un’wana na un’wana a khomekile hi miehleketo ya yena, loko marito lama nga laha henhla ya hi vekiwa hi munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki, loyi, a yimanyana emahlweni ka hina, a ringaniseke rintiho ra yena lerikulu ra le mahlweni xibya lexi ku vulavuriwaka ha xona. A a ambale hi ndlela yo biha ntsena hi xiketi lexi nyamalaleleke ni maburuku lama nga ni swiphepherhele; xihuku xa xihuku xa ntima xi vekisa nhamu ya yena. Xitsongwatsongwana lexi hlanganaka a xi khulukile hi matlhelo hinkwawo ehenhla ka xikandza xa yena, xi xi siya ku fana na mubedo lowu rharhanganeke wa timbanga wa nambu, loko mati lama tsutsumaka ma omile.

“Xana mi rhumele xikepe eka yena?” a phindha-phindha.

“U vula xikepe xa Pequod, ndzi ehleketa,” ku vula mina, ndzi ringeta ku kuma nkarhinyana wo n’wi languta handle ko kavanyetiwa.

“Aye, Pequod—xikepe xexo kwalaho,” a vula, a tlherisela voko ra yena hinkwaro endzhaku, ivi hi ku hatlisa a ri susumeta hi ku kongoma ehandle eka yena, hi bayonet leyi nga cinciki ya rintiho ra yena leri kongomeke yi tsutsuma hi ku helela eka nchumu wolowo.

Ndzi te: “Ina, ha ha ku sayina swihloko.”

“Xin’wana ni xin’wana laha hansi hi mimoya-xiviri ya n’wina?”

“Malunghana ni yini?”

“Oho, kumbexana a wu si kuma yin’we,” a vula hi ku hatlisa. “Hambi swi ri tano, ndzi tiva ti-chap to tala leti nga si kumaka,—good luck to 'em; naswona hinkwavo va antswa swinene hikwalaho ka swona. Moya-xiviri i muxaka wa vhilwa ra vuntlhanu eka golonyi.”

“U jaba hi yini, mutirhi kulobye wa xikepe?” ku vula mina.

“ He’s got enough, though, to make up for all deficiencies of that sort in other chaps,” hi xitshuketa u vule munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki, a kandziyisa hi ku chava rito leri nge he .

“Queequeg,” ku vula mina, “a hi fambeni; mufana loyi u tshovekile a huma kun’wana; u vulavula hi nchumu wo karhi ni munhu loyi hi nga n’wi tiviki.”

"Yima!" ku huwelela munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki. “Mi vule ntiyiso—a mi si vona Old Thunder, a swi tano ke?”

“I Mani Mukhalabye wa ku Dzunisa?” ku vula mina, nakambe ndzi rivet hi ku tiyimisela ka vuhlangi ka mukhuva wa yena.

“Kaputeni Akabu.”

"Yini! mufambisi wa xikepe xa hina, ku nga Pequod?”

“Aye, exikarhi ka van’wana va hina va khale va sailor chaps, u famba hi vito rero. A mi si n'wi vona, a swi tano ke?”

“E-e, a hi si endla tano. Wa vabya va vula, kambe wa antswa, naswona u ta tlhela a va kahle ku nga ri khale.”

“Swi lulamile nakambe ku nga ri khale!” ku hleka munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki, hi muxaka wa ku hleka loku xiximekaka. “Vonani n'wina; loko Kaputeni Akabu a ri kahle, kutani voko leri ra ximatsi ra mina ri ta va ri ri kahle; ku nga ri khale.”

“Xana u tiva yini hi yena?”

“Xana va ku byele yini hi yena? Vula sweswo!”

“A va vulanga swo tala hi nchumu hi yena; ntsena ndzi twe leswaku i muhloti lonene wa ti whale, naswona i mukaputeni lonene eka vatirhi va yena.”

“Sweswo i ntiyiso, sweswo i ntiyiso—ina, havumbirhi bya swona i ntiyiso hi laha ku ringaneke. Kambe u fanele u tlula loko a nyikela xileriso. Kandziya u tlhela u rhurhumela; gungulela u famba—hi rona rito leri nga na Kaputeni Akabu. Kambe ku hava nchumu hi nchumu wolowo lowu n’wi humeleleke ehandle ka Cape Horn, khale swinene, loko a etlele onge a file masiku manharhu na vusiku; a ku na nchumu hi skrimmage yoleyo yo dlaya na Mupaniya afore alitari eSanta?—a wu twanga nchumu hi sweswo, eh? A ku na nchumu hi calabash ya silivhere leyi a nga tshwutela eka yona? Naswona a ku na nchumu mayelana ni ku lahlekeriwa ka yena hi nenge eka riendzo ro hetelela, hi ku ya hi vuprofeta. Xana a mi twanga na rito hi vona timhaka na swin'wana, eh? Doo, a ndzi ehleketa leswaku mi swi endlile; xana a mi swi kotisa ku yini? I mani la swi tivaka? A hi hinkwavo va Nantucket, ndza ehleketa. Kambe hows'ever, mayhap, mi twile ku vula hi nenge, na ndlela leyi a wu lahlekeriweke ha yona; ina, mi swi twile sweswo, ndzi na xivindzi xo vula. Oh ina, leswaku un’wana na un’wana wa swi tiva a’most—ndzi vula leswaku va swi tiva leswaku i nenge wun’we ntsena; ni leswaku parmacetti yi hluvule leyin’wana.”

Ndzi te: “Munghana wa mina, leswaku vuhunguki lebyi hinkwabyo bya wena byi vulavula hi yini, a ndzi swi tivi, naswona a ndzi na mhaka ngopfu; hikuva swi vonaka eka mina leswaku u fanele u onhakilenyana enhlokweni. Kambe loko u vulavula hi Kaputeni Akabu, wa xikepe xexo kwalahaya, Pequod, kutani ndzi ta ku byela, leswaku ndzi tiva hinkwaswo hi ku lahlekeriwa hi nenge wa yena.”

“ Hinkwaswo hi swona, eh—hakunene wa swi endla?—hinkwaswo?”

“Ndzi tiyisile swinene.”

Hi rintiho leri kongomeke naswona tihlo ri langute Pequod, muendzi loyi a fanaka ni mukomberi u yime nkarhinyana, onge hiloko a lo lorha hi ku karhateka; kutani a sungulanyana, a hundzuluka a ku:—“Mi rhumele xikepe, a swi tano ke? Mavito ehansi eka maphepha? Kahle kahle, kahle, leswi sayiniweke, swi sayiniwile; naswona leswi nga ta va, swi ta va; nakona nakambe, kumbexana a swi nge vi tano, phela. Anyhow, hinkwaswo swi lulamisiwile no lulamisiwile a'ready; naswona vatluti van’wana kumbe van’wana va fanele ku famba na yena, ndza ehleketa; xikan'we na lava ku fana na vavanuna van'wana, Xikwembu xi va twela vusiwana! Mixo eka n'wina, vatirhikulobye, mixo; matilo lama nga hlamuselekiki ma katekisa; Ndzi rivaleli ku mi yimisa.”

“Languta laha, munghana,” ku vula mina, “loko u ri na nchumu wa nkoka wo hi byela, ehandle na wona; kambe loko u ringeta ku hi bamboozle ntsena, u hoxile eka ntlangu wa wena; sweswo hi swona ntsena leswi ndzi faneleke ku swi vula.”

“Nakona swi vuriwile kahle swinene, nakona ndzi tsakela ku twa chap a vulavula ehenhla hi ndlela yaleyo; u munhu ntsena eka yena—lava fanaka na n’wina. Mixo eka n’wina, vanghana va xikepe, mixo! Oho! loko mi fika kwalaho, mi byele leswaku ndzi gimete hi ku ka ndzi nga endli yin’we ya tona.”

“Ah, murhandziwa wa mina, a wu nge hi kanganyisa hi ndlela yoleyo—a wu nge hi kanganyisa. I nchumu wo olova swinene emisaveni leswaku munhu a languteka onge u ni xihundla lexikulu eka yena.”

“Mixo eka n’wina, vafambisi va xikepe, mixo.”

“Mixo i,” ku vula mina, “Tana, Queequeg, a hi tshikeni wanuna loyi wo penga. Kambe yima, ndzi byele vito ra wena, a swi tano ke?”

“Eliya.”

Eliya! ndzi ehlekete, kutani hi famba, havumbirhi bya hina hi ri karhi hi hlamusela, endzhaku ka fexeni ya un’wana na un’wana, ehenhla ka mutluti loyi wa khale wo khwaxa; naswona u pfumerile leswaku a nga ri nchumu handle ka humbug, a ringeta ku va bugbear. Kambe a hi nga si famba kumbexana ehenhla ka dzana ra timitara, loko hi tshuka hi hundzuluka ekhonweni, naswona hi languta endzhaku loko ndzi ri karhi ndzi endla tano, i mani loyi a faneleke ku voniwa handle ka Eliya a hi landzela, hambi leswi a ri ekule. Hi ndlela yo karhi, ku n’wi vona swi ndzi hlamarisile swinene, lerova a ndzi vulanga nchumu eka Queequeg hi ku va a ri endzhaku, kambe ndzi hundzele na comrade ya mina, ndzi vilerisiwa hi ku vona loko munhu loyi a nga n’wi tiviki a ta jika ekhonweni leyi fanaka na leyi hi yi endleke. U endlile tano; kutani swi vonaka eka mina onge a a hi rhuketela, kambe hi xikongomelo xihi lexi a ndzi nga ta swi kota hikwalaho ka vutomi bya mina xi ehleketa. Xiyimo lexi, xikan’we na muxaka wa yena wa mbulavulo lowu nga twisisekiki, lowu nga hafu ya ku ringanyeta, lowu paluxaka hafu, lowu funengetiweke, sweswi wu tswale eka mina tinxaka hinkwato ta swihlamariso leswi nga twisisekiki na ku chava ka hafu, naswona hinkwaswo swi fambelanisiwile na Pequod; na Kaputeni Akabu; ni nenge lowu a a wu lahlekile; na Cape Horn fit; ni xihlahla xa silivhere; ni leswi Kaputeni Peleg a swi vuleke hi yena, loko ndzi suka exikepeni siku leri hundzeke; na ku vhumbha ka squaw Tistig; ni riendzo leri a hi tibohile ku ri famba hi xikepe; ni swilo swin’wana swa ndzhuti swa dzana.

Ni tiyimisele ku ti enerisa loko Elijah loyi wo rhurhumela a hi rhuketela hakunene kumbe e-e, naswona hi xikongomelo xexo ndzi tsemakanye ndlela na Queequeg, naswona eka tlhelo rero ra yona ndzi tlhelele endzhaku hi magoza ya hina. Kambe Eliya u hundzile, a nga vonaki a hi xiya. Leswi swi ndzi ntshunxekile; naswona kan’we nakambe, naswona eku heteleleni hilaha a swi vonaka hakona eka mina, ndzi n’wi vitanile embilwini ya mina, a ri humbug.





NDZIMA YA 20. Hinkwavo Astir.

Ku hundze siku rin’we kumbe mambirhi, naswona ku ve ni ntirho lowukulu eka xikepe xa Pequod. A ku nga lunghisiwanga ntsena maseyila ya khale, kambe maseyila lamantshwa a ma ri karhi ma nghena ebyatsweni, ni tiboloti ta canvas, ni tikhoyile ta rigging; hi ku komisa, hinkwaswo swi kombise leswaku malunghiselelo ya xikepe a ma hatlisa ma ya eku heleni. Kaputeni Peleg a nga tali ku ya eribuweni kumbe a nga si tshama a ya eribuweni, kambe u tshame eka wigwam ya yena a ri karhi a languta ehandle hi ndlela yo kariha emavokweni: Bildad a a endla hinkwaswo swo xava na ku phamela eswitolo; naswona vavanuna lava thoriweke eka hold na le ka rigging a va tirha ku fikela nkarhi wo leha endzhaku ka loko vusiku byi pela.

Hi siku leri landzeleke loko Queequeg a sayinile swilo leswi, ku nyikiwe rito eka tindhawu hinkwato to wisela eka tona laha khampani ya xikepe a yi yima kona, leswaku swifuva swa vona swi fanele swi ri endzeni ka xikepe ku nga si va vusiku, hikuva a ku nga ri na ku vula leswaku xikepe xi nga ha va xi famba hi ku hatlisa ku fikela kwihi. Kutani mina na Queequeg hi wile emintlhan’wini ya hina, hi kunguhate, hambiswiritano, ku etlela eribuweni ku fikela ro hetelela. Kambe swi vonaka onge minkarhi hinkwayo va nyikela xitiviso xo leha swinene eka timhaka leti, naswona xikepe a xi fambanga masiku yo hlayanyana. Kambe a swi hlamarisi; a ku ri na swo tala leswi a swi fanele ku endliwa, naswona a ku na ku vula leswaku i swilo swingani leswi a swi fanele ku ehleketiwa, Pequod yi nga si hlomisiwa hi ku helela.

Un’wana ni un’wana wa swi tiva leswaku i swilo swo tala swonghasi—mibedo, tipani to sweka, swibakele ni tiforoko, mafoxolo ni swingwavila, ti- napkin, swiphepherhele swa tinyawa ni leswi nga riki swa nkoka eka bindzu ra ku hlayisa yindlu. Swi tano ni hi ku hlota tinhlampfi leti vuriwaka whale, leswi lavaka ku hlayisiwa ka yindlu ku ringana malembe manharhu elwandle leri anameke, ekule ni vaxavisi hinkwavo va swakudya, vaxavisi va ti- coster, madokodela, vabaki ni vabaki. Naswona hambi leswi leswi swi nga ntiyiso ni le ka swikepe swa vaxavisi, kambe a swi tano hi ndlela yihi na yihi hi mpimo lowu fanaka ni wa vahlaseri va ti- whale. Hikuva handle ka ku leha lokukulu ka riendzo ro hlota tinhlampfi, swilo swo tala leswi hlawulekeke eka ku xupuriwa ka vuphasi bya tinhlampfi, ni ku nga koteki ku swi siva etibuweni ta le kule leti hi ntolovelo ti talaka ku endliwa, swi fanele ku tsundzukiwa, leswaku eka swikepe hinkwaswo, swikepe swo phasa tinhlampfi hi swona swi paluxiweke ngopfu eka timhangu ta mixaka hinkwayo, naswona ngopfu-ngopfu eka ku lovisiwa ni ku lahlekeriwa hi swona swilo leswi ku humelela ka riendzo ku titshegeke ngopfu ha swona. Hikwalaho, swikepe swa xiphemu, swipaya swa xiphemu, na milayeni ya xiphemu na tiharpoon, na swilo hinkwaswo swa xiphemu, kwalomu ka, kambe Captain ya xiphemu na xikepe lexi phindhiweke.

Hi nkarhi lowu hi fikeke ha wona eXihlaleni, vuhlayiselo byo tika swinene bya Pequod a byi ri kusuhi ni ku hela; leyi katsaka nyama ya yena ya homu, xinkwa, mati, mafurha ni swihuku ni tinhonga ta nsimbi. Kambe, tani hileswi swinga ringanyetiwa ekusunguleni, kuringana nkarhi woleha akuri na ku hambeta ku tekiwa naku rhwala eka swikepe swa divers odds and ends of things, leswikulu na leswintsongo.

Lonkulu exikarhi ka lava va endleke ku teka na ku rhwala loku a ku ri sesi wa Kaputeni Bildad, wansati wa khale wo olova wa moya wo tiyimisela swinene na lowu nga karhaliki, kambe withal wa mbilu ya musa swinene, loyi a vonaka a tiyimisele leswaku, loko a nga swi kota ku swi pfuna, a ku na nchumu lowu faneleke ku kumiwa wu pfumala eka Pequod , endzhaku ka loko kan’we yi fike elwandle hi ndlela leyi faneleke. Hi nkarhi wun’wana a a nghena ebyatsweni a khome xibya xa ti- pickles ta xigwitsirisi xa mulanguteri; nkarhi wun’wana a ri ni ntlhambi wa swiphepherhele swa desika ra munghana lonkulu, laha a a hlayisa xihlahla xakwe; a third time with a roll of flannel for the small of some one's rheumatic back. Never did any woman better deserve her name, which was Charity—Aunt Charity, as everybody called her. And like a sister of charity did this charitable Aunt Charity bustle about hither and thither, ready to turn her hand and heart to anything that promised to yield safety, comfort, and consolation to all on board a ship in which her beloved brother Bildad was concerned, and in which she herself owned a score or two of well-saved dollars.

But it was startling to see this excellent hearted Quakeress coming on board, as she did the last day, with a long oil-ladle in one hand, and a still longer whaling lance in the other. Nor was Bildad himself nor Captain Peleg at all backward. As for Bildad, he carried about with him a long list of the articles needed, and at every fresh arrival, down went his mark opposite that article upon the paper. Every once in a while Peleg came hobbling out of his whalebone den, roaring at the men down the hatchways, roaring up to the riggers at the mast-head, and then concluded by roaring back into his wigwam.

During these days of preparation, Queequeg and I often visited the craft, and as often I asked about Captain Ahab, and how he was, and when he was going to come on board his ship. To these questions they would answer, that he was getting better and better, and was expected aboard every day; meantime, the two captains, Peleg and Bildad, could attend to everything necessary to fit the vessel for the voyage. If I had been downright honest with myself, I would have seen very plainly in my heart that I did but half fancy being committed this way to so long a voyage, without once laying my eyes on the man who was to be the absolute dictator of it, so soon as the ship sailed out upon the open sea. But when a man suspects any wrong, it sometimes happens that if he be already involved in the matter, he insensibly strives to cover up his suspicions even from himself. And much this way it was with me. I said nothing, and tried to think nothing.

At last it was given out that some time next day the ship would certainly sail. So next morning, Queequeg and I took a very early start.





CHAPTER 21. Going Aboard.

It was nearly six o'clock, but only grey imperfect misty dawn, when we drew nigh the wharf.

“There are some sailors running ahead there, if I see right,” said I to Queequeg, “it can't be shadows; she's off by sunrise, I guess; come on!”

“Avast!” cried a voice, whose owner at the same time coming close behind us, laid a hand upon both our shoulders, and then insinuating himself between us, stood stooping forward a little, in the uncertain twilight, strangely peering from Queequeg to me. It was Elijah.

“Going aboard?”

“Hands off, will you,” said I.

“Lookee here,” said Queequeg, shaking himself, “go 'way!”

“Ain't going aboard, then?”

“Yes, we are,” said I, “but what business is that of yours? Do you know, Mr. Elijah, that I consider you a little impertinent?”

“No, no, no; I wasn't aware of that,” said Elijah, slowly and wonderingly looking from me to Queequeg, with the most unaccountable glances.

“Elijah,” said I, “you will oblige my friend and me by withdrawing. We are going to the Indian and Pacific Oceans, and would prefer not to be detained.”

“Ye be, be ye? Coming back afore breakfast?”

“He's cracked, Queequeg,” said I, “come on.”

“Holloa!” cried stationary Elijah, hailing us when we had removed a few paces.

“Never mind him,” said I, “Queequeg, come on.”

But he stole up to us again, and suddenly clapping his hand on my shoulder, said—“Did ye see anything looking like men going towards that ship a while ago?”

Struck by this plain matter-of-fact question, I answered, saying, “Yes, I thought I did see four or five men; but it was too dim to be sure.”

“Very dim, very dim,” said Elijah. “Morning to ye.”

Once more we quitted him; but once more he came softly after us; and touching my shoulder again, said, “See if you can find 'em now, will ye?

“Find who?”

“Morning to ye! morning to ye!” he rejoined, again moving off. “Oh! I was going to warn ye against—but never mind, never mind—it's all one, all in the family too;—sharp frost this morning, ain't it? Good-bye to ye. Shan't see ye again very soon, I guess; unless it's before the Grand Jury.” And with these cracked words he finally departed, leaving me, for the moment, in no small wonderment at his frantic impudence.

At last, stepping on board the Pequod, we found everything in profound quiet, not a soul moving. The cabin entrance was locked within; the hatches were all on, and lumbered with coils of rigging. Going forward to the forecastle, we found the slide of the scuttle open. Seeing a light, we went down, and found only an old rigger there, wrapped in a tattered pea-jacket. He was thrown at whole length upon two chests, his face downwards and inclosed in his folded arms. The profoundest slumber slept upon him.

“Those sailors we saw, Queequeg, where can they have gone to?” said I, looking dubiously at the sleeper. But it seemed that, when on the wharf, Queequeg had not at all noticed what I now alluded to; hence I would have thought myself to have been optically deceived in that matter, were it not for Elijah's otherwise inexplicable question. But I beat the thing down; and again marking the sleeper, jocularly hinted to Queequeg that perhaps we had best sit up with the body; telling him to establish himself accordingly. He put his hand upon the sleeper's rear, as though feeling if it was soft enough; and then, without more ado, sat quietly down there.

“Gracious! Queequeg, don't sit there,” said I.

“Oh! perry dood seat,” said Queequeg, “my country way; won't hurt him face.”

“Face!” said I, “call that his face? very benevolent countenance then; but how hard he breathes, he's heaving himself; get off, Queequeg, you are heavy, it's grinding the face of the poor. Get off, Queequeg! Look, he'll twitch you off soon. I wonder he don't wake.”

Queequeg removed himself to just beyond the head of the sleeper, and lighted his tomahawk pipe. I sat at the feet. We kept the pipe passing over the sleeper, from one to the other. Meanwhile, upon questioning him in his broken fashion, Queequeg gave me to understand that, in his land, owing to the absence of settees and sofas of all sorts, the king, chiefs, and great people generally, were in the custom of fattening some of the lower orders for ottomans; and to furnish a house comfortably in that respect, you had only to buy up eight or ten lazy fellows, and lay them round in the piers and alcoves. Besides, it was very convenient on an excursion; much better than those garden-chairs which are convertible into walking-sticks; upon occasion, a chief calling his attendant, and desiring him to make a settee of himself under a spreading tree, perhaps in some damp marshy place.

While narrating these things, every time Queequeg received the tomahawk from me, he flourished the hatchet-side of it over the sleeper's head.

“What's that for, Queequeg?”

“Perry easy, kill-e; oh! perry easy!”

He was going on with some wild reminiscences about his tomahawk-pipe, which, it seemed, had in its two uses both brained his foes and soothed his soul, when we were directly attracted to the sleeping rigger. The strong vapor now completely filling the contracted hole, it began to tell upon him. He breathed with a sort of muffledness; then seemed troubled in the nose; then revolved over once or twice; then sat up and rubbed his eyes.

“Holloa!” he breathed at last, “who be ye smokers?”

“Shipped men,” answered I, “when does she sail?”

“Aye, aye, ye are going in her, be ye? She sails to-day. The Captain came aboard last night.”

“What Captain?—Ahab?”

“Who but him indeed?”

I was going to ask him some further questions concerning Ahab, when we heard a noise on deck.

“Holloa! Starbuck's astir,” said the rigger. “He's a lively chief mate, that; good man, and a pious; but all alive now, I must turn to.” And so saying he went on deck, and we followed.

It was now clear sunrise. Soon the crew came on board in twos and threes; the riggers bestirred themselves; the mates were actively engaged; and several of the shore people were busy in bringing various last things on board. Meanwhile Captain Ahab remained invisibly enshrined within his cabin.
