NDZIMA YA 74. Nhloko Ya Sperm Whale—Langutelo Leri Hambanisiweke.

Laha, sweswi, ku ni ti- whale timbirhi letikulu, ti veka tinhloko ta tona swin’we; a hi va hlanganiseni, hi veka swin'we swa hina.

Eka ntlawa lowukulu wa ti- folio leviathan, Sperm Whale na Right Whale hi tona leti xiyekaka swinene. Hi tona ntsena ti whale leti tshamelaka ku hlotiwa hi munhu. Eka Nantucketer, ti humesa swilo swimbirhi leswi hundzeletiweke swa tinxaka hinkwato leti tivekaka ta whale. Tanihi leswi ku hambana ka le handle exikarhi ka vona ku vonakaka ngopfu etinhlokweni ta vona; naswona tani hi nhloko ya yin’wana na yin’wana nkarhi lowu wu lenga-lenga etlhelo ka Pequod; naswona tanihi leswi hi nga ha fambaka hi ku ntshunxeka ku suka eka yin’wana ku ya eka yin’wana, hi ku kandziya ntsena ku tsemakanya xitepisi:—xana, ndzi fanele ku tsakela ku tiva, xana u ta kuma kwihi nkarhi wo antswa wo dyondza cetology leyi tirhaka ku tlula laha?

Eku sunguleni, u hlamarisiwa hi ku hambana loku tolovelekeke exikarhi ka tinhloko leti. Havumbirhi bya swona i swikulu hi laha ku ringaneke eka ripfalo hinkwaro; kambe kuna symmetry yokarhi ya tinhlayo eka Sperm Whale leyi ya Right Whale yi pfumalaka hi ndlela yo vava. Kuna vumunhu byotala enhlokweni ya Sperm Whale. Loko u swi vona, hi ku nga swi lavi u nyikela ku tlakuka lokukulu eka yena, hi ku ya hi xindzhuti lexi hangalakeke. Eka xiyimo xa sweswi, na xona, xindzhuti lexi xi tlakusiwa hi muvala wa phepha na munyu wa nhloko ya yena enhlohlorhini, leswi nyikaka xikombiso xa malembe yo dyuhala na ntokoto lowukulu. Hi ku komisa, hi yena loyi vaphasi va tinhlampfi hi tlhelo ra vutshila va n’wi vitanaka “grey-headed whale.”

Sweswi a hi xiyeni leswi nga faniki ngopfu eka tinhloko leti—ku nga swirho swimbirhi swa nkoka swinene, tihlo ni ndleve. Ekule endzhaku etlhelo ka nhloko, naswona ehansi ehansi, ekusuhi na angle ya nhlonge ya whale yin’wana na yin’wana, loko u lavisisa hi ku olova, eku heteleleni u ta vona tihlo leri nga riki na tinsiva, leri u nga ri ehleketaka ku ri tihlo ra ximbutana lexitsongo; hikwalaho eka mpimo hinkwawo wu ni vukulu bya nhloko.

Sweswi, ku suka eka xiyimo lexi xo hlawuleka xa le tlhelo xa mahlo ya whale, swi le rivaleni leswaku a nge pfuki a vone nchumu lowu nga emahlweni kahle, ku nga ri ku tlula leswi a nga swi kotaka ku vona nchumu lowu nga endzhaku hi ku kongoma. Hi rito rin’we, xiyimo xa mahlo ya whale xi fambisana ni xa tindleve ta munhu; naswona u nga ha ehleketa, hi wexe, ndlela leyi swi nga ta famba ha yona eka wena, xana u kambisise swilo etlhelo hi tindleve ta wena. U ta kuma leswaku u nga lerisa ntsena tidigri ta kwalomu ka makume nharhu ta ku vona ka ha ri emahlweni ka layini yo kongoma ya le tlhelo ya ku vona; na kwalomu ka makume nharhu man’wana endzhaku ka yona. Loko nala wa wena wo vava swinene a a famba a kongoma eka wena, a tlakukile banga hi siku ro anama, a wu nga ta swi kota ku n’wi vona, ku fana ni loko a ku yiva hi le ndzhaku. Hi rito rin’we, a wu ta va ni minkolo yimbirhi, hi ndlela yo fanekisela; kambe, hi nkarhi lowu fanaka, nakambe, matlhelo mambirhi ya le mahlweni (matlhelo ya le tlhelo): hikuva i yini lexi endlaka emahlweni ka munhu—i yini, hakunene, handle ka mahlo ya yena?

Ku tlula kwalaho, hambileswi eka swiharhi swin’wana swo tala leswi sweswi ndzi nga swi ehleketaka, mahlo ma byariwile hi ndlela leyi nga vonakiki leswaku ma hlanganisa matimba ya wona yo vona, leswaku ma humesa xifaniso xin’we ku nga ri swimbirhi eka byongo; xiyimo xo hlawuleka xa mahlo ya whale, hi ndlela leyi humelelaka lama avaneke tanihi leswi ma nga xiswona hi tikhubiki timitara to tala ta nhloko leyi tiyeke, leyi tlakukeke exikarhi ka wona ku fana ni ntshava leyikulu leyi hambanisaka mativa mambirhi emikoveni; leswi, hi ntiyiso, swi fanele ku hambanisa hi ku helela mavonelo lawa xirho xin’wana na xin’wana lexi tiyimeleke xi ma nyikaka. Hikwalaho, whale yi fanele yi vona xifaniso xin’we lexi hambaneke eka tlhelo leri, ni xifaniso xin’wana lexi hambaneke eka tlhelo rero; kasi hinkwaswo leswi nga exikarhi ka swona swi fanele swi va munyama lowukulu ni ku pfumala nchumu eka yena. Kahle-kahle, munhu a nga ha vuriwa leswaku u languta misava a ri ebokisini ra varindzi leri nga ni swirhendzevutana swimbirhi leswi hlanganisiweke swa fasitere rakwe. Kambe eka whale, ti sashes leti timbirhi ti nghenisiwa hiku hambana, leswi endlaka mafasitere mambirhi yohambana, kambe lexi khomisaka gome swi onha ku vona. Ku hlawuleka loku ka mahlo ya whale i nchumu lowu faneleke wu tekeriwa enhlokweni minkarhi hinkwayo eka vumaki bya tihlampfi; na ku tsundzukiwa hi muhlayi eka swivono swin’wana leswi landzelaka.

Xivutiso lexi tsakisaka ni lexi pfilunganyaka swinene xi nga ha sunguriwa malunghana ni mhaka leyi leyi voniwaka tanihi leyi khumbaka Levhiyathani. Kambe ndzi fanele ndzi eneriseka hi xitsundzuxo. Loko ntsena mahlo ya munhu ma pfulekile eku vonakaleni, xiendlo xo vona a hi ku tihlawulela; hi leswaku, endzhaku ka sweswo a nge swi koti ku pfuna hi muchini ku vona swilo swihi na swihi leswi nga emahlweni ka yena. Hambi swi ri tano, ntokoto wa munhu wihi na wihi wu ta n’wi dyondzisa, leswaku hambileswi a nga amukelaka ku sweka swilo hi ndlela leyi nga riki na xihlawuhlawu hi ku languta kan’we, a swi koteki nikatsongo eka yena, hi vukheta, ni hi ku helela, ku kambisisa swilo swihi na swihi swimbirhi—hambi swi ri swikulu kumbe switsongo ku fikela kwihi—hi ku languta kun’we ni nkarhi lowu fanaka wa nkarhi; never mind loko va etlela va tlhela va khumbana. Kambe loko sweswi u ta ku ta hambanyisa swilo leswi swimbirhi, naswona u rhendzela xin’wana ni xin’wana hi xirhendzevutana xa munyama lowukulu; kutani, leswaku u vona yin’wana ya tona, hi ndlela leyi nga ta tisa mianakanyo ya wena leswaku yi tiyisela, leyin’wana yi ta hlongoriwa hi ku helela eka mianakanyo ya wena ya nkarhi wolowo. Kutani, swi njhani hi whale? I ntiyiso leswaku mahlo yakwe hamambirhi, hi woxe, ma fanele ma teka goza hi nkarhi wun’we; kambe xana byongo bya yena byi anama swinene, byi hlanganisa naswona byi tumbete ku tlula bya munhu, lerova hi nkarhi wun’we a nga kambisisa hi vukheta mintshembo yimbirhi leyi hambaneke, yin’wana yi ri eka tlhelo rin’wana ra yena, kasi yin’wana yi ri eka tlhelo leri hambaneke hi ku kongoma? Loko a swi kota, kutani xana i nchumu wo hlamarisa ku fana na yena, onge hi loko wanuna a swi kota hi nkarhi wun’we ku hundza eka minkombiso ya swiphiqo swimbirhi leswi hambaneke eka Euclid. Naswona, loko ku lavisisiwa hi ku hetiseka, a ku na ku nga twanani eka ku pimanisa loku.

Swi nga ha endleka ku ri ku navela loku nga pfuniki nchumu, kambe minkarhi hinkwayo swi vonake eka mina, leswaku ku tsekatseka loku nga tolovelekangiki ka ku famba-famba loku kombisiwaka hi ti- whale tin’wana loko ti hlaseriwe hi swikepe swinharhu kumbe mune; ku va ni tingana ni ku va ni vutihlamuleri eka ku chava loku nga tolovelekangiki, loku tolovelekeke swinene eka ti- whale to tano; Ndzi ehleketa leswaku leswi hinkwaswo hi ndlela leyi nga kongomangiki swi huma eka ku pfilunganyeka loku nga pfuniki nchumu ka ku tihlawulela, laha matimba ya vona lama avaneke ni lama hambaneke swinene ya ku vona ma faneleke ma va katsa.

Kambe ndleve ya whale yi tele ku lava ku tiva ku fana ni tihlo. Loko u ri munhu loyi u nga n’wi tiviki hi ku helela eka rixaka ra vona, u nga ha hlota ehenhla ka tinhloko leti timbirhi ku ringana tiawara to tala, naswona u nga tshuki u tshubula xirho xexo. Ndleve a yi na tluka ra le handle hambi ku ri rihi; naswona eka khumbi hi roxe a wu nge swi koti ku nghenisa quill, so wondrously minute is it. Yi tshama endzhakunyana ka tihlo. Malunghana ni tindleve ta tona, ku hambana loku ka nkoka ku fanele ku voniwa exikarhi ka sperm whale ni ya xinene. Hambileswi ndleve ya leyo sungula yi nga ni xipfalo xa le handle, ya leyo hetelela yi funengetiwe hi ku helela naswona hi ku ringana hi xirhendzevutana, lerova yi nga vonaki nikatsongo loko u ri ehandle.

Xana a swi tsakisi, leswaku xivumbiwa lexikulu swonghasi ku fana na whale xi fanele ku vona misava hi tihlo leritsongo swonghasi, naswona xi twa ku dzindza ka tilo hi ndleve leyitsongo ku tlula ya hare? Kambe loko mahlo yakwe a ma anamile ku fana ni lensi ya theleskopu leyikulu ya Herschel; ni tindleve ta yena ti anama ku fana ni makhumbi ya tikereke; xana sweswo a swi ta n’wi endla a kota ku vona nkarhi wo leha, kumbe a va ni vuxiyaxiya bya ku twa? A swi tano nikatsongo.—Kutani ha yini u ringeta ku “kurisa” mianakanyo ya wena? Yi subtilize hi ndlela yo biha.

Sweswi a hi hi ti-lever tihi na tihi na ti-steam-engine leti hi nga na tona, hi nga cant ehenhla ka nhloko ya sperm whale, leswaku yi ta etlela ehansi ku ya ehenhla; kutani, u tlhandlukela hi xitepisi u ya enhlohlorhini, u hlometela ehansi enon’wini; naswona loko a ku nga ri leswaku miri sweswi wu hambanisiwile hi ku helela na wona, hi rivoni hi nga ha rhelela eka Bako lerikulu ra Kentucky Mammoth ra khwiri ra yena. Kambe a hi khomeleni lahawani hi meno lawa, hi languta etlhelo ka hina laha hi nga kona. Hakunene i nomu wo saseka ni lowu langutekaka wu basile! ku suka ehansi ku ya fika esirheni, yi fuleriwile, kumbe ku vula ntiyiso yi endliwe maphepha hi xirhendzevutana xo basa lexi hatimaka, lexi hatimaka ku fana ni ti- satin ta vukati.

Kambe huma sweswi, u languta eka nhlonge leyi ya le hansi ya portentous, leyi vonakaka ku fana na xifunengeto xo leha xo koma xa bokisi lerikulu ra snuff-box, na hinge emakumu man’wana, ematshan’wini ya tlhelo rin’we. Loko u yi pry up, leswaku u ta yi kuma ehenhla ka nhloko, u paluxa tilayini ta yona ta meno, yi vonaka yi ri portcullis yo chavisa; ni swo tano, khombo ra kona! yi tikombisa eka vo tala yi ri wight ya xisiwana eka vumaki bya tihlampfi, loyi swipikiri leswi swi welaka eka yena hi matimba yo belela. Kambe swa chavisa swinene ku vona, loko u fathoms ehansi elwandle, u vona sulky whale yin’wana, yi papamala kwalaho yi hayekiwile, na nhamu ya yona yo hlamarisa, kwalomu ka khume ntlhanu wa timitara hi ku leha, yi lenga-lenga hi ku kongoma ehansi hi ti right-angle na miri wa yona, eka hinkwaswo misava ku fana ni jib-boom ya xikepe. Whale leyi a yi fanga; u hele matimba ntsena; ehandle ka mixaka, kumbexana; ku va na vuvabyi bya hypochondria; naswona a tshamile swinene, lerova swirhendzevutana swa nhlonge ya yena swi ntshunxekile, swi n’wi siya kwalaho eka muxaka wolowo wo biha wa xiyimo xo biha, ku rhuketeriwa eka rixaka ra yena hinkwaro, leri, handle ko kanakana, ri faneleke ku n’wi rhuketela hi tinhlaya to pfala.

Hakanyingi nhlonge leyi ya le hansi—leswi yi pfuriwaka hi ku olova hi mutshila la titoloveteleke—yi hambanisiwa ivi yi tlakusiwa ehenhla ka xitepisi hi xikongomelo xo humesa meno ya timhondzo ta tindlopfu, ni ku nyika mphakelo wa rhambu rero ro tiya ro basa ra whale leri vaphasi va tinhlampfi va endlaka mixaka hinkwayo ya swilo leswi lavaka ku tiva ha rona, . ku katsa ni tinhonga, switolwa swa ambulense ni swikhomo ku ya eka swibakele swo khandziya.

Hi hoist yo leha, leyi karhaleke nhlonge yi kokiwa ehenhla ka xikepe, onge hiloko ku ri anchor; naswona loko nkarhi lowu faneleke wu fika—masiku ma nga ri mangani endzhaku ka ntirho wun’wana—Queequeg, Daggoo na Tashtego, tanihi leswi hinkwavo va nga madokodela ya meno lama nga ni vutshila, va lunghekele ku koka meno. Hi xipachi xo tsema lexi nga ni vuxiyaxiya, Queequeg u lance marhimila; kutani nhlonge yi bohiwa ehansi eka swirhendzevutana, naswona xitirhisiwa lexi rhungiweke xi ri ehenhla, va koka meno lawa, tanihi leswi tihomu ta le Michigan ti kokaka swikundzu swa mirhi ya khale ya oak yi huma ematikweni ya tihunyi ta nhova. Hi ntolovelo ku ni meno ya makume mune-mbirhi hinkwawo; eka ti whale ta khale, leti onhakeke swinene, kambe ti nga boli; kumbe ku tatiwa endzhaku ka fexeni ya hina yo endliwa. Endzhaku ka sweswo nhlonge yi tsemiwa yiva swiphepherhele, naswona yi hlengeletiwa kufana na ti joists to aka tindlu.





NDZIMA YA 75. Nhloko Ya Right Whale—Mavonelo Lama Hambana.

Loko hi tsemakanya xitepisi, sweswi a hi languteni kahle hi nkarhi wo leha enhlokweni ya Right Whale.

Tani hileswi hi xivumbeko xo angarhela nhloko ya Sperm Whale ya xiyimo xale henhla yinga fanisiwa na kalichi ya nyimpi ya Rhoma (ngopfu ngopfu emahlweni, laha yinga xirhendzevutani hiku anama swinene); hikwalaho, loko u languta hi ku anama, nhloko ya Right Whale yi ni ku fana loku nga nyawuriki ni xihlangi lexikulu lexi nga ni swikunwana swa galliot. Malembe ya madzana mambirhi lama hundzeke mufambi wa khale wa Mudachi u fanise xivumbeko xa yona ni xa xo hetelela xa muendli wa swiambalo. Nakona eka yona yo hetelela kumbe xihlangi lexi, mukhegula yoloye wa ntsheketo wa vana, na vana lava rhurhumelaka, va nga ha tshama hi ku ntshunxeka swinene, yena na vatukulu va yena hinkwavo.

Kambe loko u tshinela eka nhloko leyi leyikulu yi sungula ku teka swiyenge swo hambana, hi ku ya hi vonelo ra wena. Loko wo yima ehenhla ka nhlohlorhi ya yona ivi u languta swipfalo leswi swimbirhi swa xivumbeko xa F, u ta teka nhloko hinkwayo yi ya eka bass-viol leyikulu swinene, na swirhendzevutana leswi, swipfalo leswi nga eka sounding-board ya yona. Kutani, nakambe, loko wo veka tihlo ra wena eka xirhendzevutani lexi xo hlamarisa, lexi nga ni swirhendzevutana, lexi fanaka ni xikamarana ehenhla ka xirhendzevutana—nchumu lowu wa rihlaza, lowu nga ni swirhendzevutana, lowu vanhu va le Greenland va wu vitanaka “harhi,” naswona vaphasi va tinhlampfi va le Dzongeni i “bonete” ya wona xiharhi xa Right Whale; hi ku languta mahlo ya wena eka leswi ntsena, a wu ta teka nhloko yi ya eka xikundzu xa murhi wun’wana lowukulu wa oak, lowu nga ni xisaka xa xinyenyana exifuveni xa wona. Ku nga khathariseki leswaku swi njhani, loko u hlalela ti- crab teto leti hanyaka leti tshamaka laha eka bonnet leyi, mianakanyo yo tano yi ta va yi ri ekusuhi ni ku tiyiseka leswaku yi ta eka wena; handle ka loko, hakunene, ku navela ka wena ku lulamisiwile hi rito ra xithekiniki leri nge “harhi” leri na rona ri nyikiweke rona; eka xiyimo xexo u ta tsakela swinene ku ehleketa ndlela leyi xivandzana lexi xa matimba entiyisweni xi nga hosi ya lwandle leyi nga ni xidlodlo, leyi harhi ya yona ya rihlaza yi hlanganisiweke ha yona hi ndlela leyi yo hlamarisa. Kambe loko whale leyi yi ri hosi, i munhu wo languteka a ri sulky swinene ku khavisa diadem. Languta nomu wolowo wa le hansi lowu lengalengaka! i sulk ni pout leyikulu swonghasi leyi nga kona! sulk na pout, hi ku pima ka muvatli, kwalomu ka makume mbirhi wa timitara hi ku leha na ntlhanu wa timitara hi ku enta; sulk na pout leyi nga ta ku humesa kwalomu ka 500 wa tilitara ta oyili na ku tlula.

Khombo lerikulu, sweswi, leswaku whale leyi ya khombo yi fanele ku va hare-lipped. Fissure yi kwalomu ka foot hi ku anama. Kumbexana manana loyi hi nkarhi wa nkoka a a ri karhi a famba hi xikepe a rhelela eribuweni ra Peru, loko ku tsekatseka ka misava ku endle leswaku ribuwa ra lwandle ri pfuleka. Ehenhla ka nomu lowu, tanihi le henhla ka xirhendzevutana lexi rhetaka, sweswi hi rhelela enon’wini. Ehenhla ka rito ra mina a ndzi ri eMackinaw, ndzi fanele ku teka leswi ku va endzeni ka wigwam ya Maindiya. Hosi leyinene! xana hi yona ndlela leyi Yonasi a fambeke ha yona? Fulelo ra kona ri lehe kwalomu ka khume mbirhi wa timitara, naswona ri tsutsuma kuya eka angle yo kariha swinene, onge hiloko kuri na ridge-pole ya ntolovelo kwalahaya; kasi matlhelo lawa ya ribbed, arched, hairy, ya hi nyika ti slats toleto to hlamarisa, hafu yo yima, ya xivumbeko xa scimetar ta whalebone, hi ku vula madzana manharhu eka tlhelo rin’we, leswi titshegeke kusuka eka xiphemu xale henhla xa nhloko kumbe rhambu ra harhi, swi vumbaka mabofu wolawo ya Venetian lawa ti boxiwile kun’wana hi ku kongoma. Makumu ya marhambu lawa ya rhendzeriwe hi ti fibre ta voya, leti Right Whale yi sefa mati ha tona, naswona eka ku rharhangana ka tona yi hlayisa nhlampfi leyintsongo, loko yi pfule nomu yi hundza emalwandle ya brit hi nkarhi wo dya. Eka mabofu ya le xikarhi ya marhambu, loko ya yime hi ku landzelelana ka wona ka ntumbuluko, ku ni swikombiso swo karhi leswi hlamarisaka, ku gombonyoka, swirhendzevutana ni swirhendzevutana, laha vahlaseri van’wana va ti- whale va hlayelaka malembe ya xivumbiwa lexi, tanihi malembe ya oak hi swirhendzevutana swa yona swa xirhendzevutana. Hambileswi ku tiyiseka ka xipimelo lexi ku nga ekule swinene ni ku kombisiwa, hambiswiritano ku ni nantswo wa ku koteka loku fanaka. Hi ndlela yihi na yihi, loko hi pfumela eka yona, hi fanele hi nyika malembe lamakulu swinene eka Right Whale ku tlula loko hi ku languta ro sungula swi nga ta vonaka swi twala.

Eminkarhini ya khale, swi vonaka onge a ku ri ni ku navela loku tsakisaka swinene malunghana ni mabofu lawa. Mufambi un’wana wa swikepe ePurchas u ma vitana “tinsiva” leti hlamarisaka endzeni ka nomu wa whale;* un’wana, “swirhumbana swa tinguluve”; mukhalabye wa vunharhu eHackluyt u tirhisa ririmi leri landzelaka ro saseka: “Ku ni tinsiva ta kwalomu ka madzana mambirhi na makume ntlhanu leti milaka eka tlhelo rin’wana ni rin’wana ra chop ya yena ya le henhla , leti rhendzelekaka ehenhla ka ririmi ra yena eka tlhelo rin’wana ni rin’wana ra nomu wakwe.”

*Leswi swi hi tsundzuxa leswaku Right Whale hakunene yina muxaka wa tinsiva, kumbe ku vula ntiyiso, malebvu, lawa ya vumbiwaka hi voya byintsongo byo basa lebyi hangalakeke eka xiphemu xale henhla xa makumu yale handle ya nhlonge yale hansi. Minkarhi yin’wana swirhabyani leswi swi nyika xivumbeko xa vuxisi swinene eka xikandza xa yena lexi a xi ri xa nkoka hi ndlela yin’wana.

Hilaha un’wana ni un’wana a swi tivaka hakona, “swirhumbana leswi fanaka swa tinguluve,” “tifin,” “tinsiva,” “mabofu,” kumbe xin’wana ni xin’wana lexi u xi tsakelaka, swi nyika vavasati lava mabaku ya vona ni swilo swin’wana swo nonon’hwa. Kambe eka leswi swo hlawuleka, i khale xilaveko xi ri karhi xi hunguteka. Hi nkarhi wa Queen Anne laha marhambu lawa aya ri eku vangameni ka wona, farthingale akuri fexeni hinkwayo hi nkarhi walowo. Naswona tanihi leswi ti- dame toleto ta khale a ti famba-famba hi ntsako, hambileswi a ti ri eka tinhlaya ta whale, hilaha u nga ha vulaka hakona; hambi swi ri tano, eka shawara, hi ku pfumala ku ehleketa loku fanaka, xana masiku lawa hi haha ​​ehansi ka tinhlaya leti fanaka leswaku hi kuma nsirhelelo; ambulense ku ri tende leri hangalakeke ehenhla ka marhambu lama fanaka.

Kambe sweswi rivala hinkwaswo hi mabofu na tinsiva swa xinkarhana, naswona, u yime enon’wini wa Right Whale, languta etlhelo ka wena hi vuntshwa. Loko u vona tikholonade leti hinkwato ta marhambu ti ri karhi ti rhendzeleka hi ndlela ya maendlelo, xana a wu nga ta ehleketa leswaku u le ndzeni ka xirho lexikulu xa Haarlem, naswona u languta eka tiphayiphi ta xona ta gidi? Eka khapete eka organ hi na rug ya Turkey yo olova swinene—ririmi, leri namarhetiwa, hi ndlela yo fanekisela, ehansi ka nomu. Yi nonile swinene naswona yi olova, naswona yifanele ku handzula hi swiphemu eka ku yi tlakusa ehenhla ka deck. “Yimani ekule na xibalesa!” ku huwelela rito ro fana ni ku buluka ka rocket.

Kwalomu ka hi nkarhi lowu fanaka, hi ku dzindza ka tilo, nhlayo leyikulu swinene yi wele elwandle, ku fana ni Table-Rock ya Niagara yi nghena eka xidzedze; xikepe lexi ntshunxekeke hi xitshuketa xi rhendzelekile xi suka eka xona, xi ya ehansi swinene eka koporo ya xona leyi hatimaka; naswona hinkwavo va khome moya, tanihi leswi hafu ya swona yi rhendzelekaka—sweswi yi ri ehenhla ka tinhloko ta vatluti, naswona sweswi ehenhla ka mati—Daggoo, hi le xikarhi ka nkungu wo tiya wa ku fafazela, a a voniwa hi ndlela yo ka yi nga ri kahle a namarhela switirho leswi rhetaka, loko Tashtego wa xisiwana, la lahliweke la hanyaka a ri karhi a nwela ku ya fika ehansi hi ku helela ehansi ka lwandle! Kambe a ku nga si basiwa mpfula leyi dlayaka mahlo, loko xifaniso lexi nga ambalangiki nchumu lexi nga ni banga ro khandziya evokweni ra xona, xi voniwe hi nkarhi wun’we hi ku hatlisa xi ri karhi xi rhendzeleka ehenhla ka swisirhelelo. Hi siku leri landzelaka, ku rhurhumela lokukulu ku tivise leswaku Queequeg wa mina wa xivindzi u nghene hi nhloko leswaku a ta pfuna. Ku tsutsumela kun’we loku pakiweke ku endliwile etlhelo, naswona tihlo rin’wana na rin’wana ri hlaye xidzedze xin’wana na xin’wana, loko nkarhi wu landzela nkarhi, naswona a ku nga voniwi xikombiso xa hambi ku ri musindzi kumbe mudyondzisi. Mavoko man’wana sweswi ya tlulele ebyatsweni etlhelo, naswona ya susumeta nyana kusuka eka xikepe.

“Ha! ha!” ku huwelela Daggoo, hinkwaswo hi nkarhi wun’we, ku suka eka xitulu xa yena lexi sweswi xi rhuleke, lexi rhendzelekaka ehenhla ka nhloko; naswona loko hi languta ekule swinene hi le tlhelo, hi vone voko leri susumetiweke ri yimile ri huma eka magandlati ya wasi; xivono lexi nga tolovelekangiki ku xi vona, tanihi voko leri humesiweke ku suka ebyanyini ehenhla ka sirha.

"Swimbirhi! havumbirhi bya swona!—i havumbirhi bya swona!”—ku huwelela Daggoo nakambe hi ku huwelela ka ntsako; and soon after, Queequeg was seen boldly striking out with one hand, and with the other clutching the long hair of the Indian. Drawn into the waiting boat, they were quickly brought to the deck; but Tashtego was long in coming to, and Queequeg did not look very brisk.

Now, how had this noble rescue been accomplished? Why, diving after the slowly descending head, Queequeg with his keen sword had made side lunges near its bottom, so as to scuttle a large hole there; then dropping his sword, had thrust his long arm far inwards and upwards, and so hauled out poor Tash by the head. He averred, that upon first thrusting in for him, a leg was presented; but well knowing that that was not as it ought to be, and might occasion great trouble;—he had thrust back the leg, and by a dexterous heave and toss, had wrought a somerset upon the Indian; so that with the next trial, he came forth in the good old way—head foremost. As for the great head itself, that was doing as well as could be expected.

And thus, through the courage and great skill in obstetrics of Queequeg, the deliverance, or rather, delivery of Tashtego, was successfully accomplished, in the teeth, too, of the most untoward and apparently hopeless impediments; which is a lesson by no means to be forgotten. Midwifery should be taught in the same course with fencing and boxing, riding and rowing.

I know that this queer adventure of the Gay-Header's will be sure to seem incredible to some landsmen, though they themselves may have either seen or heard of some one's falling into a cistern ashore; an accident which not seldom happens, and with much less reason too than the Indian's, considering the exceeding slipperiness of the curb of the Sperm Whale's well.

But, peradventure, it may be sagaciously urged, how is this? We thought the tissued, infiltrated head of the Sperm Whale, was the lightest and most corky part about him; and yet thou makest it sink in an element of a far greater specific gravity than itself. We have thee there. Not at all, but I have ye; for at the time poor Tash fell in, the case had been nearly emptied of its lighter contents, leaving little but the dense tendinous wall of the well—a double welded, hammered substance, as I have before said, much heavier than the sea water, and a lump of which sinks in it like lead almost. But the tendency to rapid sinking in this substance was in the present instance materially counteracted by the other parts of the head remaining undetached from it, so that it sank very slowly and deliberately indeed, affording Queequeg a fair chance for performing his agile obstetrics on the run, as you may say. Yes, it was a running delivery, so it was.

Now, had Tashtego perished in that head, it had been a very precious perishing; smothered in the very whitest and daintiest of fragrant spermaceti; coffined, hearsed, and tombed in the secret inner chamber and sanctum sanctorum of the whale. Only one sweeter end can readily be recalled—the delicious death of an Ohio honey-hunter, who seeking honey in the crotch of a hollow tree, found such exceeding store of it, that leaning too far over, it sucked him in, so that he died embalmed. How many, think ye, have likewise fallen into Plato's honey head, and sweetly perished there?





CHAPTER 79. The Prairie.

To scan the lines of his face, or feel the bumps on the head of this Leviathan; this is a thing which no Physiognomist or Phrenologist has as yet undertaken. Such an enterprise would seem almost as hopeful as for Lavater to have scrutinized the wrinkles on the Rock of Gibraltar, or for Gall to have mounted a ladder and manipulated the Dome of the Pantheon. Still, in that famous work of his, Lavater not only treats of the various faces of men, but also attentively studies the faces of horses, birds, serpents, and fish; and dwells in detail upon the modifications of expression discernible therein. Nor have Gall and his disciple Spurzheim failed to throw out some hints touching the phrenological characteristics of other beings than man. Therefore, though I am but ill qualified for a pioneer, in the application of these two semi-sciences to the whale, I will do my endeavor. I try all things; I achieve what I can.

Physiognomically regarded, the Sperm Whale is an anomalous creature. He has no proper nose. And since the nose is the central and most conspicuous of the features; and since it perhaps most modifies and finally controls their combined expression; hence it would seem that its entire absence, as an external appendage, must very largely affect the countenance of the whale. For as in landscape gardening, a spire, cupola, monument, or tower of some sort, is deemed almost indispensable to the completion of the scene; so no face can be physiognomically in keeping without the elevated open-work belfry of the nose. Dash the nose from Phidias's marble Jove, and what a sorry remainder! Nevertheless, Leviathan is of so mighty a magnitude, all his proportions are so stately, that the same deficiency which in the sculptured Jove were hideous, in him is no blemish at all. Nay, it is an added grandeur. A nose to the whale would have been impertinent. As on your physiognomical voyage you sail round his vast head in your jolly-boat, your noble conceptions of him are never insulted by the reflection that he has a nose to be pulled. A pestilent conceit, which so often will insist upon obtruding even when beholding the mightiest royal beadle on his throne.

In some particulars, perhaps the most imposing physiognomical view to be had of the Sperm Whale, is that of the full front of his head. This aspect is sublime.

In thought, a fine human brow is like the East when troubled with the morning. In the repose of the pasture, the curled brow of the bull has a touch of the grand in it. Pushing heavy cannon up mountain defiles, the elephant's brow is majestic. Human or animal, the mystical brow is as that great golden seal affixed by the German emperors to their decrees. It signifies—“God: done this day by my hand.” But in most creatures, nay in man himself, very often the brow is but a mere strip of alpine land lying along the snow line. Few are the foreheads which like Shakespeare's or Melancthon's rise so high, and descend so low, that the eyes themselves seem clear, eternal, tideless mountain lakes; and all above them in the forehead's wrinkles, you seem to track the antlered thoughts descending there to drink, as the Highland hunters track the snow prints of the deer. But in the great Sperm Whale, this high and mighty god-like dignity inherent in the brow is so immensely amplified, that gazing on it, in that full front view, you feel the Deity and the dread powers more forcibly than in beholding any other object in living nature. For you see no one point precisely; not one distinct feature is revealed; no nose, eyes, ears, or mouth; no face; he has none, proper; nothing but that one broad firmament of a forehead, pleated with riddles; dumbly lowering with the doom of boats, and ships, and men. Nor, in profile, does this wondrous brow diminish; though that way viewed its grandeur does not domineer upon you so. In profile, you plainly perceive that horizontal, semi-crescentic depression in the forehead's middle, which, in man, is Lavater's mark of genius.

But how? Genius in the Sperm Whale? Has the Sperm Whale ever written a book, spoken a speech? No, his great genius is declared in his doing nothing particular to prove it. It is moreover declared in his pyramidical silence. And this reminds me that had the great Sperm Whale been known to the young Orient World, he would have been deified by their child-magian thoughts. They deified the crocodile of the Nile, because the crocodile is tongueless; and the Sperm Whale has no tongue, or at least it is so exceedingly small, as to be incapable of protrusion. If hereafter any highly cultured, poetical nation shall lure back to their birth-right, the merry May-day gods of old; and livingly enthrone them again in the now egotistical sky; in the now unhaunted hill; then be sure, exalted to Jove's high seat, the great Sperm Whale shall lord it.

Champollion deciphered the wrinkled granite hieroglyphics. But there is no Champollion to decipher the Egypt of every man's and every being's face. Physiognomy, like every other human science, is but a passing fable. If then, Sir William Jones, who read in thirty languages, could not read the simplest peasant's face in its profounder and more subtle meanings, how may unlettered Ishmael hope to read the awful Chaldee of the Sperm Whale's brow? I but put that brow before you. Read it if you can.





CHAPTER 80. The Nut.

If the Sperm Whale be physiognomically a Sphinx, to the phrenologist his brain seems that geometrical circle which it is impossible to square.

In the full-grown creature the skull will measure at least twenty feet in length. Unhinge the lower jaw, and the side view of this skull is as the side of a moderately inclined plane resting throughout on a level base. But in life—as we have elsewhere seen—this inclined plane is angularly filled up, and almost squared by the enormous superincumbent mass of the junk and sperm. At the high end the skull forms a crater to bed that part of the mass; while under the long floor of this crater—in another cavity seldom exceeding ten inches in length and as many in depth—reposes the mere handful of this monster's brain. The brain is at least twenty feet from his apparent forehead in life; it is hidden away behind its vast outworks, like the innermost citadel within the amplified fortifications of Quebec. So like a choice casket is it secreted in him, that I have known some whalemen who peremptorily deny that the Sperm Whale has any other brain than that palpable semblance of one formed by the cubic-yards of his sperm magazine. Lying in strange folds, courses, and convolutions, to their apprehensions, it seems more in keeping with the idea of his general might to regard that mystic part of him as the seat of his intelligence.

It is plain, then, that phrenologically the head of this Leviathan, in the creature's living intact state, is an entire delusion. As for his true brain, you can then see no indications of it, nor feel any. The whale, like all things that are mighty, wears a false brow to the common world.

If you unload his skull of its spermy heaps and then take a rear view of its rear end, which is the high end, you will be struck by its resemblance to the human skull, beheld in the same situation, and from the same point of view. Indeed, place this reversed skull (scaled down to the human magnitude) among a plate of men's skulls, and you would involuntarily confound it with them; and remarking the depressions on one part of its summit, in phrenological phrase you would say—This man had no self-esteem, and no veneration. And by those negations, considered along with the affirmative fact of his prodigious bulk and power, you can best form to yourself the truest, though not the most exhilarating conception of what the most exalted potency is.

But if from the comparative dimensions of the whale's proper brain, you deem it incapable of being adequately charted, then I have another idea for you. If you attentively regard almost any quadruped's spine, you will be struck with the resemblance of its vertebræ to a strung necklace of dwarfed skulls, all bearing rudimental resemblance to the skull proper. It is a German conceit, that the vertebræ are absolutely undeveloped skulls. But the curious external resemblance, I take it the Germans were not the first men to perceive. A foreign friend once pointed it out to me, in the skeleton of a foe he had slain, and with the vertebræ of which he was inlaying, in a sort of basso-relievo, the beaked prow of his canoe. Now, I consider that the phrenologists have omitted an important thing in not pushing their investigations from the cerebellum through the spinal canal. For I believe that much of a man's character will be found betokened in his backbone. I would rather feel your spine than your skull, whoever you are. A thin joist of a spine never yet upheld a full and noble soul. I rejoice in my spine, as in the firm audacious staff of that flag which I fling half out to the world.

Apply this spinal branch of phrenology to the Sperm Whale. His cranial cavity is continuous with the first neck-vertebra; and in that vertebra the bottom of the spinal canal will measure ten inches across, being eight in height, and of a triangular figure with the base downwards. As it passes through the remaining vertebræ the canal tapers in size, but for a considerable distance remains of large capacity. Now, of course, this canal is filled with much the same strangely fibrous substance—the spinal cord—as the brain; and directly communicates with the brain. And what is still more, for many feet after emerging from the brain's cavity, the spinal cord remains of an undecreasing girth, almost equal to that of the brain. Under all these circumstances, would it be unreasonable to survey and map out the whale's spine phrenologically? For, viewed in this light, the wonderful comparative smallness of his brain proper is more than compensated by the wonderful comparative magnitude of his spinal cord.

But leaving this hint to operate as it may with the phrenologists, I would merely assume the spinal theory for a moment, in reference to the Sperm Whale's hump. This august hump, if I mistake not, rises over one of the larger vertebræ, and is, therefore, in some sort, the outer convex mould of it. From its relative situation then, I should call this high hump the organ of firmness or indomitableness in the Sperm Whale. And that the great monster is indomitable, you will yet have reason to know.
