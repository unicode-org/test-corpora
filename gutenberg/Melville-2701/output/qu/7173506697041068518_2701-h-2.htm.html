CAPÍTULO 4. Contrapanel nisqa.

Paqaristinmi p’unchay k’anchay hina rikch’arispa tarirqani Queequegpa makinta aswan munakuywan munakuywan hawayman wikch’uykusqata. Yaqapaschá piensarqanki warmin kasqayta. Contrapanelqa karqan patchwork nisqamanta, hunt’a impar huch’uy parti colorniyoq cuadradokunawan, kinsa kuchukunawan ima; kay makintaq tukuyninpi tatuasqa karqan mana tukukuq laberinto cretanowan huk figuramanta, manan iskay partenpas huk llantuyllachu karqan —suyasaqmi makinta lamar qochapi mana metodicamente intipi llanthunpi waqaychasqanrayku, camisanpa mangasnin mana regularta k’uyusqa kasqanrayku imaymana pachakunapi —kay kikin makinpa, nini, llapa pachata maskharqan chay kikin parcheado edredón hina. Chiqapmi, huk chikanpi chaypi siriykuspan imaynam brazopas ruwarqa puntata rikcharisqaypi hina, yaqapaschá edredónmantaqa musyakurqani, chaynatam llimpisqankuta chaqrurqaku; hinaspapas llasa kasqaywan, ñit’isqa kasqaywanllan repararqani Queequeg abrazawashasqanmanta.

Sensacionniykunaqa admirakuypaqmi karqa. Chaykunata sut’inchanaypaq kallpachakusaq. Warma kaspayqa allintam yuyarini chayman rikchakuq imapas pasawasqanmanta; cheqaqña otaq mosqoyña karqan chaypas, manan hayk’aqpas llapantachu tiyayta atirqani. Chay circunstanciaqa kaymi karqa. Wakin alcaparratapas huktapas kuchuchkarqanim —yuyaykuni chimeneaman qasquykuyta munachkaqta, imaynam huk iskay kimsa punchaw ñawpaqta huk uchuy barreyta rikurqani hina; hinaspa madrastraypas, payqa imaynatapas otaq hukmantapas sapa kutillanmi laq’awashaq, otaq mana cenayoq puñunayman kachawaq, —mamaymi chakiymanta aysaspa chimeneamanta horqowaspa puñunayman apawarqan, iskay horasllaña kashaqtinpas 21 punchaw junio killapi tardepi, chaymi watapi aswan unay punchaw hemisferionchikpi. Manchakuypaqmi sientekurqani. Ichaqa manan ima yanapaypas karqanchu chaypaq, chaymi escalera pataman rirqani kinsa kaq pisopi kaq cuartochaman, pisi pisimantan p’achayta horqokurqani tiempota wañuchinaypaq, hinaspan k’arak suspirowan sábanas chawpiman haykurqani.

Chaypin llakisqa siriykurqani, chunka soqtayoq hora hunt’ata pasananta yupaspa, manaraq kawsarimpuyta suyakushaqtiy. ¡Chunka suqtayuq hora puñunapi! chayta yuyaykuspaymi wasanpa huch’uyllan nanawarqan. Hinaspapas ancha llamp’ullataqmi karqan; intipa ventananta yaykumuptin, callekunapi carrokunapa hatun qapariynin, hinaspa tukuy wasipi gay kunkakunapa qapariynin. Aswan mana allinmanmi sientekurqani —qhepamanmi hatarirqani, p’achasqa, medianayoq chakiypi llamp’ullata uraykamurqani, madrastrayta maskharqani, hinaspan qonqaylla chakiman wikch’ukurqani, paytan valekurqani huk favor particular hina allin pantalonta qowananpaq ñoqapaq mana allin ruway; imapas cheqaqtapuni ichaqa huchachawaspa llullakunaypaq abed chhayna mana aguantay atina unay tiempo. Ichaqa payqa aswan allin, aswan concienciayoqmi karqan madrastrakunamanta, kutimuspataq cuartoyman rinay karqan. Achka horakunam chaypi puñurqani ancha rikchasqa, chaymantapacha mana haykapipas ruwasqaymanta aswan mana allin sientekuspay, chaymanta aswan hatun desgraciakunamantapas. Qhepamanmi urmaykurqani puñuyllamanta sasachakuypi kaq millay mosqoyman; chaymanta pisi pisimanta rikch’arispa —kuskan musquykunawan hunt’asqa— ñawiyta kicharirqani, manaraq inti k’anchariq cuartotaq kunanqa hawa tutayaqwan k’uyusqaña kashasqa. Chay ratopin huk ch’aqway llapa marcoypi phawaykachashaqta sienterqani; mana imapas rikukunanchu karqan, manataqmi imapas uyarikunanchu karqan; ichaqa huk mana rikuy atina makiymi ñoqaq makiypi churasqa hina karqan. Makiymi warkusqa kachkasqa contrapaldo hawanpi, mana sutiyuq, mana yuyaymanay atina, upallalla forma utaq fantasma, mayqinmanchus maki perteneceq, camaypa waqtanpi hichpallapi tiyasqa hina karqa. Imaraykuchus edadkuna watakunaman huñusqa hina, chaypi puñurqani, aswan manchay manchakuykunawan chirisqa, mana makiyta aysayta atrevikuspa; chaywanpas hayk’aqllapas yuyaykuspaymi, sichus huk centímetrollata kuyurichiyta atiyman chayqa, chay manchay hechizoqa p’akikunmanmi nispa. Manam yacharqanichu imaynatam kay conciencia tukupaypiqa ñuqamanta lluqsirurqa; ichaqa tutamanta rikch’arispaymi khatatataspa llapanta yuyarirqani, chaymantataq p’unchaykuna semanakuna killakuna chay pakasqa kaqta sut’inchanaypaq pantasqa kallpachakuykunapi chinkarqani. Manan, kay horakamaqa sapa kutillanmi chaywanqa musphani.

Kunanqa, manchay manchakuyta qechuy, hinallataqmi ñoqaq makiypi mana rikukuq makiyta sientenaypaq sensacionniykunaqa ancha rikch’akuqmi karqan, mana reqsisqa kayninpi, chaykunatan experimentarqani rikch’arispa Queequegpa pagano makinta muyuriqniypi wikch’usqa kashaqta rikuspay. Ichaqa unaymantan llapa qayna tuta sucesokuna allin yuyaywan kutirimurqan, hukmanta hukmanta, fijo realidadpi, chaymantataq kawsashaqlla puñurqani chay asichikuq sasachakuyman. Imaraykuchus makinta kuyuchiyta munaspaypas —noviopa hap’inta kichariyta munarqani—, chaywanpas, puñushaspapas, sinchitan abrazawarqan, wañuylla iskayman t’aqawananchisman hina. Kunanqa kallpachakurqanin rikch’arichinaypaq —“¡Queequeg!”, ichaqa kutichisqanqa huk roncollanmi karqan. Hinaptinmi kuyurirqani, kunkayqa caballopa collarninpipas hinam karqa; hinaspa qunqayllamanta huk pisilla raqrakuyta sienterqa. Contrapanelta huk ladoman wikch’uspa, chaypin tomahawkqa sallqa runaq ladonpi puñushasqa, hacha uyayoq wawapas kanman hina. Huk sumaq encurtido, chiqap, yuyaykurqani; abed kaypi huk mana riqsisqa wasipi ancho p’unchawpi, huk runa mikhuqwan huk tomahawkwan! “¡Queequeg!—allin kaypa sutinpi, Queequeg, rikchariy!” Tukuyninpiqa, achka kuyuriykunawan, hinaspa kallpawan mana samaspa expostulacionkunawan, chay matrimonial clase estilopi huk qari masinta abrazasqanmanta mana allin kasqanmanta, huk gruñidota hurquyta atirqani; hinaspa kunanpacha, makinta kutichispa, unumantallaraq Terranova allqu hina tukuyninta kuyuchispa, camapi tiyaykurqa, lucio k’aspi hina k’ullu, qhawariwaspa, ñawintataq maqchikuspa mana llapanta yuyarinmanchu hina imaynatas chaypi hamurqani, ichaqa pisi pisimantan ñoqamanta imatapas yachanaypaq pisilla conciencia payman achikyamusharqan. Chaykamataq, ch’inlla qhawaspa puñurqani, kunanqa manaña hatun iskayrayayniyuqchu kani, k’umuykuspataq chhayna yachay munaq unanchata k’iskillapi qhawarqani. Qhepaman yuyaynin puñunamasinpa caracterninta llamispa ruwasqa hina kaqtin, hinaspan, nisunman hina, cheqaq kaqwan allipunasqaña kapurqan; pampaman saltaykusqa, wakin señalkunawan, t’uqyaykunawan ima entiendechiwarqan, kusichiwaqtinqa ñawpaqta p’achakunanta, chaymantataq qhepaman p’achakunaypaq saqewanman, llapan apartamentotataq ñoqallaman saqespa. Yuyaykuni, Queequeg, circunstanciakunapi, kayqa ancha civilizada ubertura; ichaqa, cheqaq kaqmi, kay sallqakunaqa nacesqankumantapachan sumaq mikhuyniyoq kanku, munasqaykita niy; admirakuypaqmi imayna esencially cortés kasqankuqa. Kay particular elogiota pagani Queequegman, imaraykuchus payqa tratawarqan ancha civilidadwan hinallataq consideracionwan, ñoqataq huchayoq karqani hatun grosero kaymanta; puñunamanta qhawaspa, hinaspa llapa baño kuyuyninta qhawaspa; chay pachapaq curiosidadniy aswan allinta uywasqayta. Chaywanpas, Queequeg hina runaqa manan sapa p’unchaychu rikunki, paypas ñanninkunapas allinmi karqan mana costumbre hina qhawarinapaq.

Payqa patamanta p’achakuyta qallarirqan castor sombreronta churakuspa, ancha hatun sombrerota, huk lado qayllapi, chaymantataq —pantalonninta pisiyachisparaq— botankunata cazarqan. Imapaqtaq hanaq pachaq uranpi ruwasqanqa, manan yachayta atiymanchu, ichaqa qatiqnin kuyuyninqa karqan ñut’ukuymi —botas makinpi, sombreroyoqtaq— camapa uranpi; maypachachus, imaymana maqanakuy jadeokunamanta, tensionkunamanta ima, inferir kani payqa sinchita llamk’achkan botawan; ichaqa manan mayqen leypas uyarisqayman hinachu, manan mayqen runapas botanta churakuspaqa sapallan kananchu. Ichaqa Queequeg, rikunkichu, huk unancha karqan transición etapapi —ni oruga nitaq pillpintu. Payqa civilizasqallan karqan, mana reqsisqa kayninta rikuchinanpaq. Educacionninqa manaraqmi tukusqachu. Payqa pregrado nisqapi estudiaqmi karqan. Sichus mana huk huch’uy grado civilizadochu kanman karqan chayqa, yaqapaschá mana botaswanqa sasachakunmanchu karqan ni imapipas; ichaqa chaymanta, mana sallqaraqchu kanman karqan chayqa, manan hayk’aqpas musqukunmanchu karqan cama ukhuman haykuspa chaykunata churananpaq. Qhepamanmi lloqsimurqan sombreronta ancha k’uyusqa, ñawinpa hawanpi ñut’usqa, hinaspan cuarto muyuriqpi qapariyta, llañuyayta qallarirqan, manapas anchata yachasqa hinachu botasman, iskay humedad, arrugasqa waka qara qarankunaman —manapaschá chayta ruwasqachu ordenpas —aswantaqa k’irisqa, ñak’arichisqa ima, k’arak chiri paqarinmanta ñawpaq kutipi.

Rikuspa, kunan, mana cortinakuna kasqanmanta, hinallataq calle ancha k’iti kasqanmanta, chimpanpi kaq wasiqa kamachirqan huk sut’i qhawayta cuartoman, hinaspa aswan aswanta qhawaspa Queequegpa ruwasqan mana allin figurata, pisilla hukwan llallinakuspa sombreronwan botasninwan churasqa; Atisqayman hinan allinta valekurqani, bañonta huk chhikanta usqhaylla ruwananpaq, astawanqa pantalonkunaman usqhaylla haykunanpaq. Payqa kasukurqa, chaymantataq mayllakunanpaq puririrqa. Chay pacha tutamantan mayqen cristianopas uyanta mayllikunman karqan; ichaqa Queequegqa, admirakuyniymi, sinqanpi, makinpi, makinllapi ablucionninta hark’aspa contentakurqan. Chaymantataq chaleconta churakurqan, hinaspan mayllanapaq sayana chawpi mesapi sinchi jabonta hap’ispa unuman challpuykurqan, uyantataq espumayta qallarirqan. Qawachkarqanim maypi navajasninta waqaychasqanmanta, chaypacham lo y behold, camapa esquinamanta arpónta hurquspan, chay suni maderamanta stockta lluqsichispa, umantapas vainamanta hurquspan, botanpi asllatapas llañuyachispa, hinaspam chay pedazokama saruykuspan de espejo perqaman, qallarikun kallpawan raspayta, otaq aswan allinta nisunman arpónwan uyanta. Yuyaykuni ñuqa, Queequeg, kayqa Rogerspa aswan allin cubiertosninta huk venganzawan llamk’achkan. Chay qhepamanmi aswan pisita tapukurqani kay operacionpi, chaymi yacharqani ima sumaq aceromanta ruwasqa kasqanmanta arpón uma, hinallataq imayna sinchi filoyoq suni derecho kantunkuna sapa kutilla waqaychasqa kasqanmanta.

Pisi tiempollamantan bañonpa puchuqninqa hunt’akurqan, hinaspan hatunchakukuspa chay cuartomanta lloqsirqan, hatun piloto mono chaleconwan p’istuykukuspa, arpónnintataq mariscalpa baston hina pukllachispa.





CAPÍTULO 5. Desayuno.

Usqhaylla qatirqani, bar-cuartoman uraykuspaymi ancha kusisqa asirikuq wasiyuqta asuykurqani. Manam ima millay kaytaqa paywanqa anchatachu chanincharqani, ichaqa manam pisillatapas puñunamasiypa asuntonpiqa ñuqawan skylarking karqachu.

Ichaqa, allin asikuyqa atiyniyoq allinmi, aswanpas ancha pisilla allin kaqmi; aswanmi llakipayay. Chaymi, sichus mayqen huk runapas, kikin runanpi, pimanpas allin asichiypaq imakunatapas pagan chayqa, ama qhepaman kutichunchu, aswanpas kusisqa saqechun chay hinata gastananpaq, gastananpaqpas. Hinaspapas chay runaqa imapas askha asikunapaq hina paymanta, seguro kay chay runapi aswan askha kasqanmanta, ichapas yuyaykusqaykimantaqa.

Bar-roomqa kunanqa hunt’a kasharqan ñawpaq tutapi urmamuq internadokunawan, manaraq allintachu qhawarirqani. Yaqa llapankum ballena hapiq karqaku; umalliq compañerokuna, iskay kaq compañerokuna, kinsa kaq compañerokuna, lamar qochapi carpinterokuna, lamar qochapi herrerokuna, lamar qochapi herrerokuna, arponerokuna, barcokuna qhawaqkuna ima; huk marrón hinaspa kallpasapa compañía, bosky barbayuq; mana rutusqa, llañullaña churasqa, llapankum paqarin pachapaq mono chalecoyuq.

Yaqa sut’itan reparawaq sapankanku hayk’a tiempo qocha patapi kasqankuta. Kay waynaq qhali uyanqa intiwan tostado pera hinan llimp’iyuq, yaqapas almizcle hina asnaq hina kanman; manataqmi kimsa punchawchu indio purisqanmanta allpaman chayarunman karqa. Chay ladonpi kaq runaqa iskay kinsa llimp’ikuna aswan k’anchariqmi rikch’akun; niwaqmi huk llamiy satin madera paypi kasqanmanta. Kimsa kaqpa tezninpiqa hinallam kachkan huk tawnal tropico, ichaqa aslla blanqueado withal; mana iskayrayaspaqa semanantinmi lamar-qocha patapi qhepakurqan. Ichaqa, ¿pitaq Queequeg hina uyanta qawachinman? chaytaq, imaymana tintekunawan hark’asqa, Andes llaqtaq inti haykuy ladonman rikch’akurqan, huk matrizllapi rikuchikunanpaq, climas contrastante, zona por zona.

“¡Grub, ho!”, nispa. kunanqa wasiyuqqa waqaykusqa, huk punkuta wischuykuspan, hinaspa yaykumurqaniku desayunaq.

Ninkum, kay pachata qawaq qarikunaqa, chaynapim allinta kawsanku, compañiapiqa allintam kikinkumanta hapipakunku. Ichaqa manan sapa kutichu: Ledyard, hatun Musuq Inglaterra puriq, Mungo Park, Escociamanta; llapa qharikunamantaqa aswan pisi seguridadniyoqmi karqanku salón nisqapi. Ichaqa ichapas Siberia llaqtata chimpaspalla huk trineopi allqukuna aysasqa Ledyard hina, utaq ch’usaq wiksallapi sapallan unay puriyta ruway, África suyupa yana sunqunpi, chaymi karqa wakcha Mungopa ruwasqanpa huñusqan —kayhina puriy, Nini, manapaschá aswan allinchu kanman huk alto pulido social nisqaman chayanapaq. Chaywanpas yaqa llapanpiqa chayna kaqqa maypipas kananmi.

Kay yuyaymanaykunaqa kayllapim rikurimun chay circunstanciawan, chaymi llapallanchik mesapi tiyasqanchik qipata, hinaspa ballena hapiymanta allin willakuykunata uyarinaypaq allichakuchkaptiy; mana pisichu admirakurqani, yaqa llapa qharikuna sinchita ch’inlla karqanku. Hinaspapas manan chayllachu, aswanpas p’enqasqa hinan qhawarikurqanku. Arí, kaypin kasharqanku huk t’aqa lamar qochapi allqokuna, askhan mana pisi p’enqakuspa hatun lamar-qochapi hatun ballenakunaman seqarqanku —paykunapaqqa mana reqsisqan—, hinaspan mana ñawi ch’ipispa wañusqata duelopi ruwarqanku; chaywanpas, kaypi tiyarqanku huk mesa de desayuno social nisqapi —llapanku huk waqyasqa, llapanku huk ayllu gustoyuq— muyuriqninta qhawanakurqanku uwiha hina p’inqay hina qhawarinakuspa mana hayk’aqpas rikusqankuman hina wakin uwihakunaq t’aqanmanta Verde Orqokuna ukhupi. Curioso rikuy; kay p’enqakuq osokuna, kay manchali maqanakuq ballenakuna!

Ichaqa Queequegmanta rimaspaqa —imarayku, Queequegqa paykuna ukhupi tiyaykusqa— mesa umapi, chaypas, chay hina casualmente; hielo hina q’uñi. Seguro kanaypaqqa manam anchatachu niyta atiyman mirasqanmantaqa. Aswan hatun admiraqninqa manan cordialmente justificanmanchu karqan arpónninta desayunoman apamusqanmanta, hinaspa chaypi mana ceremoniayoq utilizasqanmanta; chaywan mesa hawaman haykuspa, askha umakunaq peligroman chayananpaq, hinaspa waka aycha mikhunata payman hap’ipakuspa. Ichaqa chaytaqa cheqaqtapunin ancha q’oñillata ruwarqan, llapa runapas yachanmi yaqa llapan runakunaq qhawarisqankuman hina, imatapas q’oñillata ruwayqa llamp’u sonqowan ruwaymi.

Kaypiqa manan rimasunchu Queequegpa llapa peculiaridadninkunamanta; imaynatas kukata, q’oñi rollokunatapas karunchakurqan, mana rakisqa yuyaynintataq waka aycha mikhuykunaman churarqan, chaytaqa pisillatan ruwarqanku. Basta, chaymi desayuno tukukuptinqa wakinkuna hina cuarto público nisqaman lluqsirqa, tomahawk-tubonta rawrachirqa, hinaspa chaypi tiyachkasqa upallalla digerir hinaspa fumachkasqa mana rakina atina sombreronwan, chayllamanmi lluqsirqani huk paseoman.





CAPÍTULO 6. Calle.

Sichus qallariypi muspharqani huk qhawariyta hap’ispa Queequeg hina ancha extraño runata huk civilizado llaqtaq allin qhawarisqa sociedadnin ukhupi purishaqta chayqa, chay admirakuyqa pisi tiempollamantan ripurqan New Bedford llaqtaq callenkunapi ñawpaq p’unchay k’anchaypi purisqaypi.

Muellekunaq qayllanpi kaq carreterakunapiqa, mayqen hatun puertopas sapa kutillanmi qonqa hawa llaqtakunamanta aswan queeer rikch’ayniyoq mana sut’inchasqa kaqkunata qhawanapaq. Broadway, Castaño callekunapipas Mediterráneo lamar qochapi barcopi puriqkunan mayninpiqa mancharisqa señoraskunata maqapanqaku. Regent calleqa manan mana reqsisqachu Lascarkunaq, malayokunaqpas; hinaspa Bombay llaqtapi, Apollo Green nisqapi, kawsaq Yankees nisqakunaqa sapa kutim chay llaqtayuq runakunata manchachirqaku. Ichaqa New Bedfordqa llapa Water Streetta, Wappingtapas atipan. Kay qhipa rimasqanchik wasikunapiqa marinerokunallatam rikunki; ichaqa New Bedford llaqtapiqa cheqaq runa mikhuqkunan calle esquinakunapi rimaspa sayashanku; sallqakuna sut’imanta; Paykunamanta askharaqmi tullunkupi mana ch’uya aychata apashanku. Mana riqsisqa runata qhawapayachin.

Ichaqa, Feegeeans, Tongatobooarrs, Erromanggoans, Pannangians, Brighggians nisqakunamanta hawa, hinallataq, sallqa especímenes de la ballena-artesanía nisqamanta hawa, chaykuna mana uyarisqa carrete callekunamanta, rikunki huk rikuykunata aswan curiosoraq, seguro aswan asichikuq. Sapa semanan kay llaqtaman chayanku askha verde Vermont runakuna, New Hampshire qharikuna, llapankun ch’akisqa kanku gananciata hinallataq hatunchakuypaq challwakuypi. Yaqa llapankum wayna-sipaslla kanku, sinchi marcoyuq; yunkakunata kuchuspa runamasikuna, kunantaq hachata urmachispa ballena lanzata qechuyta maskhanku. Achkataqmi maymanta hamusqanku Verde Urqukuna hina verde. Wakin imakunapiqa piensawaqmi ichaqa iskay kinsa horakunayoq. ¡Qhawariy chaypi! chay chap strutting muyuriqpi esquinapi. Payqa castor sombreroyuq, golondrina chupayuq punchuyuq, marinero cinturonwan, vaina cuchillowan ima watasqa. Kaypi hamun huk sou'-westerwan hinaspa bombazine mantowan.

Manan mayqen llaqtapi uywasqa dandypas tupanqachu huk llaqtapi uywasqawan —niyta munani huk downright bumpkin dandy — huk runa, allqu-p’unchawkunapi, iskay acresninta rutunqa buckskin guanteswan manchakuspa makinta bronceayta. Kunanqa kay hina llaqta dandy umallanman hap’ispa allin reqsisqa kayta ruwananpaq, hinaspa hatun ballena challwayman hukllawakuqtin, rikunayki tiyan asichikuq ruwaykunata lamar qocha puertoman chayaspa ruwasqankunata. Lamar qochapi p’achanmanta rimaspanmi, chaleconkunaman campanakunata kamachin; lonamanta pantalonkunaman watasqa. ¡Ah, wakcha Hay-Seed! imayna k’araktataq chay watasqakunata p’akinqa ñawpaq kaq qapariq wayrapi, wayra-paraq kunkan urayman tanqasqa kashaqtiyki, watasqakuna, botones, llapanpas.

Ichaqa ama piensaychu kay reqsisqa llaqtapiqa arponerokunalla, runa mikuq runakuna, toqllakunallan kan, visitaqninkunaman rikuchinankupaq. Manam, manapunim. Hinallataqmi New Bedford llaqtaqa huk queer lugar. Manachus ñoqayku ballena hap’iq runakuna kaykuman karqan chayqa, kunan p’unchaypas yaqapaschá chay allpaqa Labrador llaqtaq lamar-qochan hina qapariq hina kashanman karqan. Imayna kasqanman hinaqa, qhepa llaqtanpa wakin partenkunan hukta manchachinanpaq hina, ancha tullu hinan rikch’akunku. Kikin llaqtaqa ichapas aswan munasqa tiyanapaq, llapan Musuq Inglaterra suyupi. Aceite allpan, cheqaqmi: ichaqa manan Canaán hinachu; huk allpa, hinallataq, saramanta, vinomantawan. “Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

“With this sin of disobedience in him, Jonah still further flouts at God, by seeking to flee from Him. He thinks that a ship made by men will carry him into countries where God does not reign, but only the Captains of this earth. He skulks about the wharves of Joppa, and seeks a ship that's bound for Tarshish. There lurks, perhaps, a hitherto unheeded meaning here. By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
