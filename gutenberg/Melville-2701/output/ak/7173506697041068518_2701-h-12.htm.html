TI 55. Ɛfa Akraman Mfonini a Ɛyɛ Hu Ho.

I shall ere long paint to you as well as one can without canvas, biribi te sɛ whale no su ankasa sɛnea ɛda adi ankasa wɔ whaleman no ani so bere a wɔ n’ankasa nipadua koraa mu no whale no akyekye whale-ship no nkyɛn sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a wobetumi atiatia no so wɔ hɔ wɔ ɔkwan a ɛfata so. Enti ebia mfaso wɔ so sɛ kan no yɛbɛbɔ saa ne mfonini ahorow a wɔde wɔn adwene ayɛ a ɛyɛ nwonwa a ɛde besi nnɛ mpo de ahotoso kasa tia asase wura no gyidi no ho dawuru. Bere aso sɛ yɛsiesie wiase no wɔ asɛm yi mu, denam adanse a yɛbɛkyerɛ sɛ akraman ho mfonini a ɛte saa no nyinaa yɛ mfomso no so.

Ebia wobehu saa mfonini mu nsusuwii hunu no nyinaa fibea titiriw wɔ Hindufo, Misrifo, ne Helafo ahoni a akyɛ sen biara no mu. Efi saa bere a na wɔyɛ nneɛma foforo nanso na wonni adwempa bere a na ɛwɔ asɔredan ahorow a wɔde abohene a wɔfrɛ no marble ayɛ so, ahoni a wɔde gyina so, ne kyɛm, nkonimbo, nkuruwa, ne sika so no, na wɔde nkɔnsɔnkɔnsɔn akode nsenia te sɛ Saladin de no twetwe ɔkraman no, na na ɔte sɛ ne ti a wɔde dade kyɛw Ɔhotefo George’s; efi saa bere no, biribi a ɛte sɛ tumi krataa a ɛte saa ara adi nkonim, ɛnyɛ whale mfonini dodow no ara a agye din mu nko, na mmom wɔ nyansahu mu nsɛm pii a wɔka faa ne ho mu.

Mprempren, sɛnea ɛte biara no, wobetumi ahu mfonini a ɛwɔ hɔ tete sen biara a ɛda so ara wɔ hɔ ɔkwan biara so a wɔkyerɛ sɛ ɛyɛ akraman de no wɔ ɔbodan-pagoda a agye din a ɛwɔ Elephanta, wɔ India no mu. Brahminfo kyerɛ sɛ wɔ saa pagoda a enni awiei no mu no, na wɔayɛ nnwuma ne nneɛma a wodi nyinaa, onipa adwuma biara a wobetumi asusuw ho, mfe pii ansa na emu biara reba ankasa. Enti ɛnyɛ nwonwa sɛ wɔ ɔkwan bi so no, anka ɛsɛ sɛ yɛn adwuma a ɛkorɔn a ɛne sɛ wɔkyere mpataa no yɛ sunsuma wɔ hɔ. Hindoo whale a wɔka ne ho asɛm no, pue wɔ ɔfasu no fã bi a ɛyɛ soronko, na ɛkyerɛ Vishnu honam a ɔdaa ne ho wɔ leviathan kwan so, a wosua sɛ wɔfrɛ no Matse Avatar. Nanso ɛwom sɛ saa ohoni yi yɛ onipa fã ne akraman fã, sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a ɛbɛma nea otwa to no dua nkutoo de, nanso saa ne fã ketewaa no nyinaa di mfomso. Ɛte sɛ anaconda dua a ɛyɛ teateaa, sen nokware whale flukes akɛse no nsateaa a ɛtrɛw no.

Nanso kɔ Galleries dedaw no mu, na hwɛ afei Kristoni mfoniniyɛfo kɛse bi mfonini a ɛfa apataa yi ho; efisɛ odi nkonim yiye nsen Hindoofo a na wɔwɔ hɔ ansa na nsuyiri reba no. Ɛyɛ Guido mfonini a ɛkyerɛ Perseus a ɔregye Andromeda afi po mu aboa anaa akraman nsam. Ɛhe na Guido nyaa abɔde a ɔyɛ nwonwa saa no nhwɛso? Saa ara nso na Hogarth, wɔ mfonini koro no ara a ɔyɛe wɔ n’ankasa “Perseus Descending” mu no, nkyerɛ sɛ ɛyɛ papa kakra. Saa Hogarthian aboa kɛse no duru kɛse no yɛ asorɔkye wɔ soro, na ɛkame ayɛ sɛ ɛtwetwe nsu nsateakwaa biako. Ɛwɔ howdah bi wɔ n’akyi, na n’ano a ɛtrɛw a ɛyɛ ntini a asorɔkye no rehuruhuruw akɔ mu no, wobetumi de akɔfa Atorofo Pon a ɛnam nsu so fi Thames kɔ Abantenten no mu no. Afei, Prodromus whale a ɛwɔ Scotch Sibbald dedaw mu, ne Jonah whale, sɛnea wɔayɛ ho mfonini wɔ Bible dedaw a wɔatintim ne primer dedaw a wɔatwitwa mu no nso wɔ hɔ. Dɛn na wɔbɛka afa eyinom ho? Ɛdefa nhoma-kyekyerefo no whale a ɛkyinkyini te sɛ bobe dua twa sɛkyɛ a ɛresiane no dua ho hyia—sɛnea wɔde stamp ne sika kɔkɔɔ akyerɛw wɔ nhoma dedaw ne foforo pii akyi ne nkratafa—no, ɛno yɛ abɔde a ne ho yɛ fɛ yiye nanso ɔyɛ nwonwa ara kwa no, . asuasua no, misusuw sɛ efi mfonini ahorow a ɛte saa ara a ɛwɔ tete nkuku so no mu. Ɛwom sɛ wɔfrɛ no amansan nyinaa sɛ dolphin de, nanso mefrɛ nhoma-kyekyerefo yi apataa sɛ mmɔden a wɔbɔ sɛ wɔbɛbɔ whale; efisɛ na wɔahyɛ da ayɛ saa bere a wodii kan de mfiri no bae no. Italiani ɔdawurubɔfo dedaw bi na ɔde bae wɔ baabi bɛyɛ afeha a ɛto so 15, wɔ Adesua Nkannyan bere mu; na saa nna no mu, ne bere a etwa to mpo no, na nnipa pii susuw sɛ dolphins yɛ Leviathan bi.

Wɔ tete nhoma ahorow bi mu vignettes ne nneɛma afoforo a wɔde asiesie mu no, ɛtɔ mmere bi a wubehyia nneɛma a ɛyɛ nwonwa kɛse a wɔde ka whale no ho, baabi a nsu a ɛtwetwe, jets d’eau, nsu a ɛyɛ hyew ne awɔw ahorow nyinaa, Saratoga ne Baden-Baden, ba a ɛrehuruhuruw fi ne de no mu amemene a ɛnbrɛ. Wɔ “Adesua Nkɔso” a edi kan no asɛmti kratafa no mu no wubehu whale bi a wɔpɛ sɛ wohu nneɛma pii.

Nanso bere a yegyae mmɔdenbɔ ahorow a ɛnyɛ adwumayɛfo de yi nyinaa no, momma yɛnhwɛ saa leviathan mfonini ahorow a wɔkyerɛ sɛ ɛyɛ anifere, nyansahu mu mfonini ahorow, a wɔn a wonim no yɛe no. Wɔ Harris dedaw no akwantu ahorow a waboaboa ano no mu no, whale mprɛte bi wɔ hɔ a woyii fii Dutchfo akwantu ho nhoma bi mu, A.D. 1671, a wɔato din “A Whaling Voyage to Spitzbergen in the ship Jonas in the Whale, Peter Peterson of Friesland, master.” Wɔ saa mprɛte no biako mu no, wɔayɛ whale te sɛ nnua akɛse a wɔde ahyɛ nsu mu a wɔda nsukyenee nsupɔw mu, a asono fitaa tu mmirika fa wɔn akyi a wɔte ase no ho mfonini. Wɔ mprɛte foforo mu no, wɔyɛ mfomso a ɛyɛ nwonwa no sɛ wɔde fluke ahorow a ɛteɛteɛm gyina hɔ ma whale no.

Afei bio, quarto bi a ɛyɛ nwonwa wɔ hɔ, a Captain Colnett biako, Post Captain wɔ Engiresi po so asraafo mu kyerɛwee, a wɔato din “A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, a atirimpɔw ne sɛ wɔbɛtrɛw Spermaceti Whale Fisheries no mu.” Wɔ nhoma yi mu no, wɔde nsusuwso bi a wɔkyerɛ sɛ ɛyɛ “Physeter anaa Spermaceti whale mfonini, a wɔde nsenia atwe afi nea wokum no wɔ Mexico mpoano, August, 1793, na wɔmaa so wɔ hyɛn no so no mu.” Migye kyim sɛ ɔhyɛnkafo no maa wɔtwee mfonini a ɛyɛ nokware yi sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a obenya n’ahyɛn so asraafo no so mfaso. Sɛ mɛka ho asɛm biako pɛ a, ma menka sɛ ɛwɔ aniwa bi a sɛ wɔde di dwuma, sɛnea nsenia a ɛka ho no kyerɛ no, wɔ sperm whale a wanyin koraa so a, ɛbɛma saa whale no aniwa ayɛ bow-window a ne tenten bɛyɛ anammɔn anum. Ah, me sahene kokodurufo, adɛn nti na woamma yɛn Yona a ɔrehwɛ saa aniwa no!

Saa ara nso na Abɔde Ho Abakɔsɛm nhoma ahorow a wɔaboaboa ano a wɔde ahonim ayɛ sen biara no nyɛ nea ɛbɛboa mmerante ne mmabaa ne wɔn a wɔn ho yɛ hyew, a mfomso a ɛyɛ abofono koro no ara nni mu. Hwɛ saa nhoma a agye din “Goldsmith’s Animated Nature.” Wɔ London de a wɔatwa no tiawa a wɔyɛe wɔ 1807 mu no mu no, mprɛte bi a wɔkyerɛ sɛ ɔyɛ “akraman” ne “narwhale” bi wɔ hɔ. Mempɛ sɛ mɛyɛ te sɛ nea me ho nyɛ fɛ, nanso saa whale a ne ho nyɛ fɛ yi te sɛ ɔkraman a wɔatwitwa no kɛse; na, ɛdefa narwhale ho no, nea ɛyɛ ketewaa bi a wobɛhwɛ no dɔɔso sɛ ɛbɛma obi ho adwiriw no, sɛ wɔ afeha a ɛto so dunkron yi mu no, na wobetumi de nsateaa abɔ hippogriff a ɔte saa akyerɛ sɛ ɔyɛ nokware wɔ sukuu mmarimaa ɔmanfo biara a wonim nyansa so.

Afei, bio, wɔ 1825 mu no, Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, abɔde ho nimdefo kɛse, tintim nyansahu mu nhyehyɛe ho nhoma bi a ɛfa whale ho, a Leviathan ahorow ahorow no ho mfonini pii wom. Ɛnyɛ sɛ eyinom nyinaa nyɛ nokware nko, na mmom Mysticetus anaa Greenland whale (kyerɛ sɛ, Right whale) mfonini no, Scoresby mpo, ɔbarima a ne ho akokwaw bere tenten wɔ saa mmoa no ho no, pae mu ka sɛ onni ne yɔnko wɔ abɔde mu.

Nanso na wɔde kyɛw a wɔde bɛhyɛ adwuma a ɛredi mfomso yi nyinaa mu no nkutoo ama nyansahufo Frederick Cuvier, Baron a wagye din no nua. Wɔ 1836 mu no, otintim Natural History of Whales, a ɔde nea ɔfrɛ no Sperm Whale mfonini mae wom. Ansa na wode saa mfonini no rekyerɛ Nantucketer biara no, anka ɛbɛyɛ papa sɛ wobɛhwɛ wo tiawa a wobɛsan akɔ akyi afi Nantucket no. Sɛ yɛbɛka no tiawa a, Frederick Cuvier Sperm Whale nyɛ Sperm Whale, na mmom squash. Nokwarem no, wannya mfaso a ɛwɔ akwantu a wɔde yi mpataa mu da (mmarima a wɔte saa ntaa nnya bi), nanso ɛhe na onyaa saa mfonini no, hena na obetumi ahu? Ebia onyaa bi bere a nyansahu mu nea odii n’anim wɔ saa adwuma koro no ara mu, Desmarest, nyaa ne nyinsɛn a oyii no ankasa no biako no; kyerɛ sɛ, efi Chinafo mfonini bi mu. Na sɛnea saa Chinafo no yɛ mmarimaa a wɔyɛ hyew a wokurakura pɛnsere no, nkuruwa ne nkuku a ɛyɛ nwonwa pii bɔ yɛn amanneɛ.

Ɛdefa nsɛnkyerɛnne-mfoniniyɛfo akraman a wohu wɔn wɔ mmɔnten so a wɔsensɛn wɔn a wɔtɔn ngo no sotɔɔ ahorow so no ho no, dɛn na wɔbɛka afa wɔn ho? Mpɛn pii no, wɔyɛ Richard III. whale, a wɔwɔ dromedary humps, na wɔyɛ atirimɔdenfo kɛse; anɔpaduan a wodi wɔ po so hyɛn mu adwumayɛfo tart abiɛsa anaa anan so, kyerɛ sɛ whale akorow a po so adwumayɛfo ahyɛ mu ma: wɔn dɛmdi ahorow a ɛrehuruhuruw wɔ po a mogya ne paint bruu ayɛ mu ma mu.

Nanso mfomso ahorow a wɔadi wɔ whale no ho mfonini mu yi nyɛ nwonwa kɛse wɔ ne nyinaa akyi. Hwɛ! Wɔafa nyansahu mu mfonini dodow no ara afi mpataa a wɔakɔda mu no mu; na eyinom teɛ bɛyɛ te sɛ hyɛn a asɛe, a n’akyi abubu ho mfonini a ɛbɛgyina hɔ ama aboa a ɔyɛ ɔdehye no ankasa wɔ n’ahantan a enni n’ahantan a ɛfa ne hyɛn no anim ne nea ɛyɛ mmerɛw nyinaa mu no yiye no. Ɛwom sɛ asono agyina hɔ ama wɔn tenten nyinaa de, nanso Lewiatan teasefo no nnya nyɛɛ ne ho wɔ ɔkwan a ɛfata so da mmaa ne mfonini no. Whale a ɔte ase, wɔ n’anuonyam ne nea ɛkyerɛ nyinaa mu no, ɛsɛ sɛ wohu no wɔ po so wɔ nsu a wontumi nhu mu nkutoo; na wɔ nsuo mu no, ne fã kɛseɛ no ara nni hɔ a wɔhunu, te sɛ hyɛn a wɔatow ahyɛn a ɛko; na ɛfiri saa element no mu no ɛyɛ adeɛ a ɛrentumi nyɛ yie daa sɛ onipa a ɔwuo bɛma no so wɔ nipadua mu akɔ wim, sɛdeɛ ɛbɛyɛ a ɔbɛkora ne ahonhon ne asorɔkye a ɛyɛ den nyinaa so. Na, sɛ yɛrenka nsonsonoe a wosusuw kɛse sɛ ɛwɔ contour mu wɔ whale kumaa a ɔnom nufu ne Platonia Leviathan a wanyin awie ntam no ho asɛm; nanso, wɔ saa akraman nkumaa a wɔnom nufu a wɔama so akɔ hyɛn bi atifi no mu biako mpo fam no, saa bere no na ne nsusuwii a ɛyɛ nwonwa, ɛte sɛ eel, a ɛyɛ mmerɛw, a ɛsono ne su te, ma enti ɔbonsam ankasa antumi ankyere n’anim pɛpɛɛpɛ.

Nanso ebia wobetumi asusuw ho, sɛ wobetumi anya nsɛm a ɛyɛ nokware afi akraman a wɔakɔda no nnompe a ɛda adagyaw mu a ɛka ne su ankasa ho. Nte saa koraa. Efisɛ ɛyɛ nneɛma a ɛyɛ nwonwa kɛse wɔ Leviatan yi ho no mu biako, sɛ ne nnompe no mma yenhu sɛnea ɛte nyinaa. Ɛwom sɛ Jeremy Bentham nnompe a ɛsensɛn kaneadua wɔ ne wudifo no mu biako nhomakorabea no da adwene a ɛne sɛ ɔbarima panyin a ɔyɛ mfaso ho adwuma a ne ho yɛ duru yɛ den, a Jeremy ankasa su atitiriw afoforo nyinaa ka ho no adi yiye de; nanso na wontumi nsusuw biribiara a ɛte sɛɛ ho mfi leviathan biara nnompe a wɔabɔ no mua no mu. Nokwarem no, sɛnea Ɔbɔmmɔfo kɛse no ka no, whale nnompe ara kwa ne aboa a wɔde sika ahyɛ mu koraa na wɔde ntama ahyɛ ne ho no wɔ abusuabɔ koro no ara te sɛ nkoekoemmoa no ne chrysalis a ɛkata no kurukuruwa saa no. Saa soronko yi da adi wɔ ti no mu ma ɛyɛ nwonwa, sɛnea wɔ nhoma yi fã bi mu no, wɔbɛda no adi wɔ akwanhyia mu no. Wɔda no adi wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ nwonwa kɛse so nso wɔ ɔfã ntaban no mu, a ɛkame ayɛ sɛ ne nnompe no bua onipa nsa nnompe pɛpɛɛpɛ, na wɔayi nsateaa nkutoo afi mu. Saa ntaban yi wɔ nsateaa anan a ɛyɛ nnompe a ɛyɛ daa, nsateaa a wɔde kyerɛ ade, mfinimfini, nkatabo, ne nsateaa ketewa. Nanso eyinom nyinaa akɔhyɛ wɔn honam nkataso no mu daa, te sɛ onipa nsateaa a ɛwɔ nkataso a wɔde nsa ayɛ mu no. Stubb a ɔyɛ serewfo no kae da bi sɛ: “Ɛmfa ho sɛnea ɛtɔ mmere bi a whale betumi asom yɛn anibiannaso no, wontumi nka ankasa da sɛ odi yɛn ho dwuma a onni nsateaa.”

Enti, esiane saa nneɛma yi nyinaa nti, ɔkwan biara a wobɛfa so ahwɛ no, ɛsɛ sɛ wuhia sɛ wode ba awiei sɛ Leviatan kɛse no ne saa abɔde biako a ɔwɔ wiase a ɛsɛ sɛ ɛkɔ so yɛ nea wɔmfa ho adwini biara nkɔ so kosi nea etwa to no. Nokwarem no, ebia mfonini biako bɛbɔ agyiraehyɛde no abɛn kɛse sen foforo, nanso emu biara ntumi mmɔ no pɛpɛɛpɛ kɛse. Enti asase so kwan biara nni hɔ a wobɛfa so ahu sɛnea whale no te ankasa. Na ɔkwan biako pɛ a wubetumi afa so anya ne contour a ɛte ase no ho adwene a wotumi gyina ano mpo, ne sɛ w’ankasa wobɛkɔ akɔkyere whale; nanso ɛnam saayɛ so no, wode wo ho to asiane ketewaa bi mu sɛ wobɛyɛ fononoo daa na no akɔmem. Enti, ɛte sɛ me sɛ anka ɛbɛyɛ papa sɛ wonyɛ fastidious dodo wɔ w’anigye a wowɔ sɛ wobɛka Leviatan yi ho.





TI 56. Ɛfa Akraman Mfonini a Ɛnyɛ Mfomso Kɛse Ho, ne Nokware Mfonini a Ɛfa Whale Ho Mfonini Ho.

Ɛdefa whale mfonini ahorow a ɛyɛ hu ho no, wɔsɔ me hwɛ denneennen wɔ ha sɛ mɛhyɛn saa wɔn ho nsɛm a ɛda so ara yɛ hu kɛse a wobehu wɔ nhoma ahorow bi mu, tete ne nnɛyi nyinaa mu, titiriw wɔ Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, etc. Nanso metwam saa asɛm no.

Sperm Whale kɛse no ho nsɛm anan pɛ a wɔatintim a minim; Colnett, Huggins, Frederick Cuvier, ne Beale de. Wɔ ti a atwam no mu no, wɔaka Colnett ne Cuvier ho asɛm. Huggins de no ye koraa sen wɔn de no; nanso, sɛnea ɛte biara no, Beale de no ne nea eye sen biara. Beale mfonini ahorow a ɛfa saa whale yi ho nyinaa ye, gye mfinimfini nipa a ɔwɔ whale abiɛsa a wɔwɔ suban ahorow mfonini mu no nkutoo, na ɔde ne ti a ɛto so abien no ano. N’anim mfonini, ahyɛmma a ɛtow hyɛ Sperm Whales so, ɛwom sɛ akyinnye biara nni ho sɛ wobu akontaa sɛ ɛbɛkanyan ɔmanfo adwenem naayɛ a mmarima bi a wɔda dan mu wɔ de, nanso ɛteɛ wɔ ɔkwan a ɛfata nkamfo so na ɛte sɛ nkwa wɔ ne nkɛntɛnso nyinaa mu. Sperm Whale mfonini ahorow a ɛwɔ J. Ross Browne mu no bi teɛ yiye wɔ nsusuwii mu; nanso wɔakurukyerɛw wɔn wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ awerɛhow so. Nanso ɛnyɛ ɛno ne ne mfomso.

Wɔ Right Whale ho no, mfonini ahorow a wɔayɛ no sɛnea wɔayɛ no yiye sen biara no wɔ Scoresby; nanso wɔatwe wɔn wɔ nsenia ketewaa bi so dodo sɛ wɔbɛda adwene a wɔpɛ adi. Ɔwɔ mfonini biako pɛ a ɛkyerɛ sɛnea wɔkyere whale, na eyi yɛ sintɔ a ɛyɛ awerɛhow, efisɛ ɛnam mfonini ahorow a ɛtete saa nkutoo so, bere a wɔayɛ no yiye koraa no, na wubetumi anya biribiara te sɛ whale a wɔte ase no ho adwene a ɛyɛ nokware sɛnea n’abɔmmɔfo a wɔte ase hu no.

Nanso, sɛ yɛka ne nyinaa bom a, nea eye sen biara, ɛwom sɛ wɔ nsɛm bi mu no ɛnyɛ nea ɛteɛ sen biara de, a wɔde akraman ne akraman a wɔkyere wɔn ho mfonini a wobetumi ahu wɔ baabiara ho nkyerɛkyerɛmu a wɔde ma no yɛ Fransefo nkyerɛwee akɛse abien, a wɔayɛ no yiye, na wɔafa afi mfonini ahorow a Garnery biako yɛe mu. Sɛnea ɛte biara no, wogyina hɔ ma ntua a wɔde ba Sperm ne Right Whale so. Wɔ nkyerɛwee a edi kan no mu no, wɔayɛ Sperm Whale a ɔyɛ ɔdehye bi ho mfonini wɔ ahoɔden kɛse mu, a ɔsɔre fii po no mu bun mu wɔ hyɛmma no ase ara pɛ, na ɔde fononoo mpuran a asɛe a ɛyɛ hu no soa soro wɔ wim wɔ n’akyi. Ɔkorow no anim fã bi ntumi mmubui, na wɔtwe no sɛnea ɛkari pɛ wɔ aboa kɛse no akyi berɛmo so ara kwa; na sɛ wugyina saa anim no mu, wɔ saa bere a ɛhyerɛn biako pɛ a wontumi mmu ho akontaa no nti, wuhu obi a ɔde ahyɛmma tu mmirika, a akraman no nsu a ɛrefɔw a abufuw ayɛ no akata ne fã so, na ɔrehuruw, te sɛ nea ofi ɔbotan bi mu. Adeyɛ a ade mũ no nyinaa yɛ no ye na ɛyɛ nokware anwonwakwan so. Line-tub a ɛda mpan fã no sensɛn po a ayɛ fitaa no so; nnua nnua a ɛwɔ harpoon ahorow a ahwie agu no mu no bob wɔ mu a ɛyɛ mmerɛw; asuguarefo no ti apete wɔ whale no ho wɔ ehu a ɛne ne ho bɔ abira mu; bere a wɔ ahum tuntum kwan no so no, hyɛn no rekɔ fam wɔ baabi a asiane no sii no. Ebia wobehu mfomso kɛse wɔ akraman yi nipadua ho nsɛm mu, nanso ma ɛno ntwam; efisɛ, me nkwa nti, mantumi antwe obi a eye saa.

NKYEKYƐMU 59. Squid.

Bere a Pequod no de nkakrankakra nam brit wuram so no da so ara kura ne kwan a ɛkɔ atifi fam apuei kɔ Java supɔw no so no mu; mframa a ɛyɛ brɛoo bi a ɛrekanyan ne keel, ma enti wɔ kommyɛ a atwa ho ahyia no mu no, ne nnua atenten abiɛsa a ɛyɛ teateaa no wosow brɛoo ma saa mframa a ɛyɛ mmerɛw no, te sɛ nsateaa abiɛsa a ɛyɛ mmerɛw wɔ asasetaw so. Na ɛda so ara, wɔ mmeae a ɛtrɛw wɔ anadwo a dwetɛ yɛ no mu no, na wobehu wimhyɛn a ɛyɛ ankonam na ɛtwetwe adwene no.

Nanso anɔpa bi a ɛyɛ bruu a ɛda adi pefee, bere a ɛkame ayɛ sɛ kommyɛ bi a ɛboro nnipa de so trɛwee wɔ po no so, ɛmfa ho sɛnea na obiara nni hɔ a na kommyɛ biara a enhinhim wom no; bere a na owia a ɛhyerɛn tenten a ɛwɔ nsu no so no te sɛ sika kɔkɔɔ nsateaa bi a wɔde ato wɔn so, a ɛhyɛ sɛ wɔmfa kokoamsɛm bi nyɛ adwuma no; bere a asorɔkye a ɛyɛ hwerɛma no boom bɔɔ nkɔmmɔ bere a na ɛretu mmirika brɛoo no; wɔ kurukuruwa a wotumi hu no mu kommyɛ a emu dɔ yi mu no, Daggoo huu ahonhonsɛmdi bi a ɛyɛ nwonwa fii mast-ti titiriw no so.

Wɔ akyirikyiri no, akuwakuw fitaa kɛse bi de ɔbrɛ sɔre, na ɛkɔɔ soro kɔɔ soro, na ɛtetew ne ho fi azure no ho, awiei koraa no ɛhyerɛn wɔ yɛn anim te sɛ sukyerɛmma a ɛrehuruhuruw, foforo a ɛtwetwe fi nkoko so. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
