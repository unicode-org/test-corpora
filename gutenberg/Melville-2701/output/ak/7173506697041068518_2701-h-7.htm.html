Nea ɛne anohyeto a ɛkame ayɛ sɛ wontumi nnyina ano ne tumidi a aniwa nhu a enni din a ɛwɔ ɔsraani panyin no pon so bɔ abira no, na tumi krataa a wɔmfa ahwɛyiye biara nni mu ne nea ɛyɛ mmerɛw no nyinaa, ɛkame ayɛ sɛ saa mfɛfo a wɔba fam a wɔyɛ harpooneers no demokrase a ɛyɛ hu no. Bere a na ɛte sɛ nea wɔn wuranom, ahokafo no, suro wɔn ankasa anom hinge nnyigyei no, na harpooneers no de anigye we wɔn aduan araa ma amanneɛbɔ bi wɔ hɔ. Wodidi sɛ awuranom; wɔhyɛɛ wɔn yafunu ma te sɛ Indiafo po so ahyɛn a wɔde nnuhuam ahyɛ mu ma da mũ nyinaa. Ná Queequeg ne Tashtego wɔ akɔnnɔ a ɛyɛ sɛnkyerɛnne a ɛte saa, araa ma sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ na wɔadi mmeae a ɛda hɔ a adidi a atwam no de mae no ma no, mpɛn pii no na Dough-Boy a ne ho yɛ bruu no ani gye ho sɛ ɔde nkyene-nwurade baron kɛse bi bɛba, a ɛte sɛ nea wɔatu afi nantwi a ɔyɛ den no mu. Na sɛ na ɔnyɛ obi a ɔyɛ nnam wɔ ho, sɛ wankɔ ne hop-skip-and-jump a ɛyɛ ntɛm a, ɛnde na Tashtego wɔ ɔkwan a ɛnyɛ fɛ a ɔfa so ma no yɛ ntɛmntɛm denam kyɛnsee a ɔde bɛbɔ n’akyi so, sɛnea ɛte wɔ harpoon mu no so. Na bere bi Daggoo a na aseresɛm a ɛyɛ mpofirim gyee no no boaa Dough-Boy ma ɔkaee no denam nipadua mu a ɔfaa no fii soro, na ɔde ne ti too nnua amena kɛse bi a hwee nni mu mu, bere a Tashtego a okura sekan no fii ase de kurukuruwa no too hɔ de dii kan twitwaa ne ti no. Ná ɔyɛ onipa ketewa bi a ne ho popo kɛse fi awosu mu, ofiehwɛfo a n’anim yɛ paanoo yi; paanootofo bi a wabɔ ka ne ayaresabea ɔyarehwɛfo bi asefo. Na dɛn ne Ahab a ɔyɛ abibifo a ne ho yɛ hu a ogyina hɔ hwɛbea, ne saa mmoa a wɔyɛ keka baasa yi nsrahwɛ a basabasayɛ wom bere ne bere mu no, na Dough-Boy asetra nyinaa yɛ anofafa a ɛwosow bere nyinaa. Mpɛn pii no, bere a ohu sɛ wɔde nneɛma a wɔhwehwɛ nyinaa asiesie wɔn a wɔbɔ sanku no akyi no, na oguan fi wɔn nsam kɔ ​​ne dan ketewa a ɛbɛn hɔ no mu, na ɔde ehu hwɛ wɔn mu fa ne pon ano anifuraefo mu, kosi sɛ ne nyinaa bɛba awiei.

Na ɛyɛ ade a ɛyɛ nwonwa sɛ wubehu Queequeg sɛ ɔte Tashtego anim, na ɔsɔre tia ne sẽ a wɔahyɛ no agyirae no atia Indiani no de: wɔ mmeamudua so wɔ wɔn ho no, Daggoo te fam, efisɛ anka benkyi bi de ne ti a wɔde afunu ahyɛ mu ma no bɛba carlines a ɛba fam no so; wɔ n’akwaa akɛse no kankan biara mu, na ɛma ɔdan no mu dan a ɛba fam no wosow, te sɛ bere a Afrika asono kɔ ɔkwantufo wɔ hyɛn mu no. Nanso eyi nyinaa akyi no, na negro kɛse no twe ne ho fi ho wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ nwonwa so, na ɛnyɛ sɛ yɛbɛka sɛ ɔyɛ dɛ. Na ɛte sɛ nea ɛkame ayɛ sɛ ɛrentumi nyɛ yiye sɛ ɛdenam ano nketenkete a ɛte saa so betumi akɔ so akura ahoɔden a ɛtrɛw wɔ onipa a ɔtrɛw, baronial, na ɔsen biara mu no mu. Nanso, akyinnye biara nni ho sɛ, saa aboa a ɔyɛ ɔdehye yi maa mframa a ɛdɔɔso no aduan denneennen na ɔnom kɔ akyiri; na ɛnam ne hwene a atrɛw a ɔde ahyɛ wiase ahorow no asetra a ɛkorɔn no mu no so. Ɛnyɛ nantwinam anaa abodoo so na wɔyɛ abran anaasɛ wɔma wɔn aduan. Nanso Queequeg, na ɔwɔ anofafa a ɛyɛ owu, atirimɔdensɛm wɔ adidi mu —nnyigyei a ɛyɛ abofono a ɛdɔɔso —araa ma ɛkame ayɛ sɛ Dough-Boy a ɔrepopo no hwɛɛ sɛ ebia ne sẽ agyiraehyɛde bi ahintaw wɔ n’ankasa nsa a ɛyɛ mmerɛw no mu anaa. Na sɛ ɔbɛte sɛ Tashtego reto dwom ama no sɛ ɔnyɛ ne ho, sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a wɔbɛtetew ne nnompe a, ɛkame ayɛ sɛ ofiehwɛfo a n’adwene mu yɛ den no bubuu nkuku a ɛsensɛn ne ho wɔ aduankorabea hɔ no nyinaa, denam ne mpofirim a ɛyɛɛ obubuafo no so. Saa ara nso na abo a wɔde bɔ akuturuku a na harpooneers no kura wɔ wɔn kotoku mu, de yɛɛ wɔn peaw ne akode afoforo no nso annyɛ saa; na abo a wɔde yɛ whetstone a, wɔ anwummere adidi mu no, na wɔde ayɛ wɔn nnade no sew wɔn nnade; saa grating nnyigyei no ntaa mma Dough-Boy ohiani no dwo koraa. Ɛbɛyɛ dɛn na ne werɛ afi sɛ wɔ ne Supɔw so nna no mu no, akyinnye biara nni ho sɛ na Queequeg dii fɔ wɔ awudisɛm, ayɔnkofa ho adwemmɔne bi ho. Awerɛhosɛm ne sɛ! Mmɔre-Abarimaa! hard fares the white waiter a ɔtwɛn nnipa a wodi nnipa nam. Ɛnyɛ napkin na ɛsɛ sɛ ɔde to ne nsa so, na mmom ɛsɛ sɛ ɔde buckler. Nanso, bere pa mu no, na n’ani gye kɛse ne sɛ na nkyene po so akofo baasa no bɛsɔre na wɔakɔ; wɔ n’aso a ennye nni na wɔka anansesɛm ho asɛm no ho, na wɔn akodi nnompe nyinaa rebɔ mu wɔ anammɔn biara mu, te sɛ Moorfo scimetars a wɔhyɛ abɔso.

Nanso, ɛwom sɛ saa atirimɔdenfo yi didi wɔ ɔdan ketewa no mu, na wɔ din mu no, na wɔte hɔ de; nanso, esiane sɛ na wɔyɛ biribiara gye sɛ wɔtra ase wɔ wɔn suban mu nti, na ɛkame ayɛ sɛ na wɔwɔ mu da gye sɛ adidi bere, na aka kakra ma wɔada, bere a wɔfaa mu kɔɔ wɔn ankasa atrae soronko no.

Wɔ asɛm biako yi mu no, na ɛte sɛ nea Ahab nyɛ soronko koraa wɔ Amerikafo akraman a wɔfrɛ wɔn whale dodow no ara ho, a sɛ́ nnipa dodow no ara no, wɔpɛ adwene a ɛne sɛ ɛdenam hokwan ahorow so no, hyɛn no fã yɛ wɔn dea mmom; na sɛ ɛnam animtew nkutoo so na wɔma obi foforo biara kwan, bere biara. Sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a, wɔ nokware ankasa mu no, wobetumi aka sɛnea ɛfata sɛ wɔka sɛ Pequod no ahokafo ne wɔn a wɔbɔ sanku no traa ɔdan no akyi sen sɛ wɔtraa mu. Na bere a wɔhyɛn mu no, na ɛyɛ biribi te sɛ abɔnten pon hyɛn fie mu; dannan kɔ mu bere tiaa bi, na nea edi hɔ no na wɔdannan no; na, sɛ́ ade a ɛtra hɔ daa no, ɛtra wim. Saa ara nso na wɔanhwere pii wɔ eyi so; na fekubɔ biara nni ɔdan ketewa no mu; wɔ asetra mu no, na wontumi nkɔ Ahab nkyɛn. Ɛwom sɛ wɔde din kaa Kristoman nnipakan ho de, nanso na ɔda so ara yɛ ɔhɔho ma Kristoman. Ɔtenaa wiase, sɛdeɛ Grisly Bears no mu a wɔtwa toɔ no tenaa Missouri a wɔatu akɔtena hɔ no. Na te sɛ bere a Ahohuru Bere ne Ahuhuro kɔe no, saa wuram Logan a ofi kwae mu no, a osiee ne ho wɔ dua bi tokuru mu no, traa awɔw bere mu wɔ hɔ, na ɔnom n’ankasa ne nan; enti, wɔ ne nkwakoraabere a ɛnyɛ papa na ɔteɛm mu no, Ahab kra, a wɔatoto ne nipadua dua a ɛwɔ ɔbodan mu no mu, na ɛhɔ na ɔredi ne sum kabii no nan a ɛyɛ kusuu no!





TI 35. Mast-Ti no.

Ɛyɛ bere a wim tebea yɛ anigye kɛse no, na bere a me ne po so hyɛn mu adwumayɛfo afoforo no di akɔneaba sɛnea ɛsɛ mu no, me mast-ti a edi kan no bae.

Wɔ Amerikafo a wɔkyere nsu mu mpataa dodow no ara mu no, ɛkame ayɛ sɛ wɔde nnipa hyɛ mast-heads no mu bere koro mu bere a hyɛn no fi ne hyɛn gyinabea no; ɛwom mpo sɛ ebia ɔwɔ akwansin mpem dunum, ne nea ɛboro saa, a ɔde bɛfa po so hyɛn ansa na wadu ne po so hyɛn gyinabea a ɛfata. Na sɛ, wɔ mfe abiɛsa, anan, anaa anum akwantu akyi no ɔrebɛn fie a biribiara nni ne mu —ka sɛ, toa a hwee nni mu mpo —a, ɛnde, wɔde nnipa ahyɛ ne mast-ti so kosi nea etwa to; na ɛnyɛ kosi sɛ ne wim po so ahyɛn no bɛkɔ hyɛn gyinabea no atifi fam no, na ogyae anidaso a ɔwɔ sɛ ɔbɛkyere akraman biako bio no koraa.

Afei, sɛnea adwuma a ɛne sɛ wobegyina mast-ti, wɔ mpoano anaasɛ wɔ nsu so no yɛ tete de na ɛyɛ anigye yiye no, momma yɛntrɛw mu kakra wɔ ha. Mefa no, sɛ wɔn a wodii kan gyinaa mast-ti ne Misrifo dedaw no; efisɛ, wɔ me nhwehwɛmu nyinaa mu no, minhu biara ansa na ɛreba. Efisɛ ɛwom sɛ akyinnye biara nni ho sɛ ɛbɛyɛ sɛ wɔn nananom, Babel adansifo, nam wɔn abantenten no so ayɛ wɔn adwene sɛ wɔbɛtete adum ti a ɛkorɔn sen biara wɔ Asia nyinaa, anaa Afrika nso de; nanso (bere a wɔde lɔre a etwa to no guu so) sɛnea wobetumi aka sɛ saa abo kɛse a wɔde yɛ wɔn de no faa kyerɛwpon no ho no, wɔ Onyankopɔn abufuw ahum a ɛyɛ hu no mu; enti, yɛrentumi mfa Babel adansifoɔ yi nni kan nsen Misrifoɔ. Na sɛ na Misrifo yɛ ɔman a wogyina hɔ ma wɔn ti, yɛ asɛm a wɔka a egyina gyidi a ɛwɔ fam tutufo mu nyinaa so, sɛ wɔde pyramid ahorow a edi kan no sii hɔ maa nsoromma mu hwɛ atirimpɔw ahorow: nsusuwii a wɔde afã anan no nyinaa a ɛte sɛ antweri soronko no foa so biako pɛ saa adan no; ɛnam so de wɔn nan atenten a wɔma so a ɛyɛ nwonwa so no, na saa nsoromma ho animdefo dedaw no taa foro kɔ soro, na wɔto dwom ma nsoromma foforo; sɛnea nnɛyi hyɛn bi a wɔhwɛ no to dwom ma po so hyɛn, anaasɛ whale a ɛrekɔ so ara kwa no mpo no. Wɔ Ɔhotefo Stylites mu no, Kristoni kokorafo a wagye din a ɔtraa ase tete no, a osii ɔbo adum a ɛkorɔn maa no wɔ sare so na ɔde n’asetra fã a etwa to nyinaa traa ne soro, na ɔde afiri maa n’aduan so fii fam; wɔ ne mu no yɛwɔ nhwɛso a ɛyɛ nwonwa a ɛfa obi a ogyina hɔ a ne ho nyɛ hu ho; a na ɛnsɛ sɛ sum anaa awɔw, osu, asukɔtweaa, anaa osutɔ bɛpam no afi ne tenabea; nanso ɔde akokoduru hwɛɛ biribiara anim kosii nea etwa to no, owui ankasa wɔ ne gyinabea. Wɔ nnɛyi standers-of-mast-heads mu no yɛwɔ set a nkwa nnim kɛkɛ; abo, dade, ne kɔbere nnipa ara kwa; a ɛwom sɛ wotumi gyina ahum a ano yɛ den ano yiye de, nanso wɔda so ara nnim koraa wɔ adwuma a ɛne sɛ wɔbɛto dwom bere a wɔahu ade a ɛyɛ nwonwa biara no mu. Napoleon wɔ hɔ; a, wɔ Vendome adum no atifi, gyina hɔ a ne nsa abɔ mu, bɛyɛ anammɔn ɔha aduonum wɔ wim; ahwɛyiye, afei, hena na odi deck ahorow a ɛwɔ ase hɔ no so; sɛ́ ebia Louis Philippe, Louis Blanc, anaa Louis Ɔbonsam no. Washington Kɛse nso gyina soro wɔ soro wɔ ne dua kɛse a ɛkorɔn a ɛwɔ Baltimore no so, na te sɛ Hercules adum no biako no, n’adum no hyɛ saa nnipa kɛseyɛ beae a nnipa kakraa bi a wowuwu bɛkɔ akyiri no agyirae. Admiral Nelson nso, a ɔte atuo-dade capstan so, gyina ne mast-ti wɔ Trafalgar Square; na bere biara a saa London wusiw no kata so kɛse no, wɔda so ara de sɛnkyerɛnne ma sɛ ɔbran bi a wahintaw wɔ hɔ; ɛfiri sɛ baabi a wusiw wɔ no, ɛsɛ sɛ ogya wɔ. Nanso Washington kɛse, anaa Napoleon, anaa Nelson, remmua asukɔtweaa biako mpo mfi ase, ɛmfa ho sɛnea wɔn afotufo de abɔdamfo frɛɛ wɔn sɛ wɔne deck ahorow a wɔatwetwe wɔn adwene a wɔhwɛ so no adamfo no; nanso wobetumi asusuw sɛ, wɔn ahonhom no hyɛn daakye ahum a ɛyɛ den no mu, na wɔkyerɛkyerɛ abon ahorow ne abotan a ɛsɛ sɛ wɔkwati mu.

Ebia ɛbɛyɛ te sɛ nea ntease nnim sɛ wɔde asase no so adum a wɔde gyina hɔ no bɛka po so de ho wɔ ɔkwan biara so; nanso sɛ nokwarem no ɛnte saa no da adi pefee wɔ ade bi a Obed Macy, Nantucket abakɔsɛm kyerɛwfo koro pɛ no bu ho akontaa no mu. Obed a ɔfata no ka kyerɛ yɛn, sɛ wɔ mfiase mmere a na wɔayi mpataa a wɔfrɛ no whale no, ansa na wɔde po so ahyɛn tow hyɛn mu daa de di agoru no akyi no, saa supɔw no sofo sisi spars a ɛkorɔn wɔ po no mpoano, na na wɔn a wɔhwɛ so no nam akwan so foro kɔ hɔ a wɔde nnadewa abɔ so, biribi a ɛte sɛ nnomaa kɔ soro wɔ nkokɔ fie mu. Mfe kakraa bi a atwam ni no, Bay whalemen a wɔwɔ New Zealand no gyee nhyehyɛe koro yi ara toom, na bere a wɔkaa agoru no ho asɛm no, wɔbɔɔ ahyɛmma a wɔasiesie wɔn ho a ɛbɛn mpoano no amanneɛ. Nanso mprempren amanne yi abɛyɛ nea ne bere atwam; afei yɛdan kɔ mast-head biako a ɛfata no so, nea ɛyɛ whale-ship a ɛwɔ po so no. Wɔde nnipa sie mast-ti abiɛsa no so fi owia apuei kosi owiatɔe; po so adwumayɛfo no a wɔdannan wɔn ho daa (te sɛ nea wɔyɛ hyɛn no kwankyerɛfo no), na wɔma wɔn ho wɔn ho ahotɔ nnɔnhwerew abien biara. Wɔ wim tebea a ɛyɛ komm a ɛwɔ mmeae a osu tɔ na owia bɔ kɛse mu no, ɛyɛ anigye dodo sɛ mast-head no; dabi, wɔ ɔbarima a odwennwen nneɛma ho a ɔyɛ dae fam no, ɛyɛ anigye. Ɛhɔ na wugyina hɔ, anammɔn ɔha wɔ abansoro a ɛso yɛ komm no atifi, na woretu anammɔn wɔ bun no mu, te sɛ nea mpoma no yɛ nnua akɛse, bere a sɛ yɛbɛka a, po mu mmoa akɛse a wɔyɛ keka no guare wɔ w’ase ne wo nan ntam, bere mpo a bere bi na po so ahyɛn tu no wɔ Kolose a wagye din wɔ Rhodes dedaw no mpaboa ntam. Ɛhɔ na wugyina hɔ, ayera wɔ po no ntoatoaso a enni ano mu, a biribiara nni hɔ a ɛyɛ basaa gye asorɔkye no. Hyɛn a ayɛ basaa no de brɛoo hwirew; aguadi mframa a ɛda hɔ no bɔ; biribiara siesie wo kɔ languor mu. Wɔ fã kɛse no ara mu no, wɔ ɔhyew mu mpataa a wɔkyere wɔn yi mu no, biribi a ɛkorɔn a ensi ansi de wo sika hyɛ mu; monte asɛm biara; kenkan nsɛmma nhoma biara; extras a ɛwɔ nsɛm a ɛyɛ nwonwa a ɛfa nneɛma a wɔtaa yɛ ho no nnaadaa wo da wɔ anigye a ɛho nhia mu; monte ofie amanehunu biara ho asɛm; sikakorabea ahorow a wɔabɔ ka; stock ahorow a ɛtɔ gu fam; no nhaw wo da wɔ nea wubenya wɔ anwummere aduan ho adwene ho —efisɛ wɔde w’aduan a wubedi mfe abiɛsa ne nea ɛboro saa nyinaa ahyɛ nsuhina mu fɛfɛɛfɛ, na wo ka a wode tua ho ka no ntumi nsakra.

Wɔ saa anafo fam mpataayifo no mu biako mu no, wɔ mfe abiɛsa anaa anan akwantu tenten bi mu, sɛnea ɛtaa ba no, nnɔnhwerew ahorow a wode bɛtra hyɛn no atifi no nyinaa bɛyɛ asram mũ pii. Na ɛyɛ awerɛhow kɛse sɛ beae a wode w’abɔde mu asetra mu bere nyinaa fã kɛse no ara kɔ hɔ no, ɛsɛ sɛ ɛyɛ nea awerɛhosɛm ne sɛ biribiara nni hɔ a ɛbɛn baabi a ɛhɔ yɛ fɛ, anaasɛ wɔayɛ nsakrae wɔ mpɔtam hɔfo a wɔte nka a ahotɔ wom, . te sɛ nea ɛfa mpa, hammock, funu kar, awɛmfo adaka, asɛnka agua, kar, anaa saa mfiri nketewa a ɛyɛ fɛ a mmarima tew wɔn ho bere tiaa bi no mu biara ho. Beae a wotaa tra ne t’ gallant-mast no ti, baabi a wugyina nnua abien a ɛyɛ tratraa a ɛne ne ho di nsɛ (ɛkame ayɛ sɛ ɛyɛ soronko ma whalemen) a wɔfrɛ no t’ gallant cross-trees so. Ɛha yi, bere a po no rebɔ no, nea ɔrefi ase no te nka sɛ ne ho tɔ no te sɛ nea ɔbɛyɛ bere a ogyina nantwinini mmɛn so no. Sɛ yɛbɛka no yiye a, sɛ wim yɛ nwini a, wubetumi de wo fie akɔ soro, a ɛte sɛ dɔn-tade; nanso sɛ yɛbɛka no yiye a, awɛmfo atade a ɛyɛ den sen biara no nyɛ ofie kɛse te sɛ nipadua a wɔankata so no; ɛfiri sɛ sɛdeɛ wɔde gluu abɔ ɔkra no mu wɔ ne honam ntamadan no mu, na ɔrentumi nkɔ baabiara wɔ mu, na ɔrentumi mfiri mu mpo, a ɔmfa ne ho nto asiane kɛseɛ mu sɛ ɔbɛyera (te sɛ ɔkwantufoɔ a onnim hwee a ɔtwa Alps mmepɔ a sukyerɛmma wɔ so wɔ awɔberɛ mu); enti watch-coat nyɛ ofie kɛse na mmom ɛyɛ envelope ara kwa, anaasɛ were foforo a ɛkata wo so. CHAPTER 36. The Quarter-Deck.

( Enter Ahab: Then, all. )

It was not a great while after the affair of the pipe, that one morning shortly after breakfast, Ahab, as was his wont, ascended the cabin-gangway to the deck. There most sea-captains usually walk at that hour, as country gentlemen, after the same meal, take a few turns in the garden.

Soon his steady, ivory stride was heard, as to and fro he paced his old rounds, upon planks so familiar to his tread, that they were all over dented, like geological stones, with the peculiar mark of his walk. Did you fixedly gaze, too, upon that ribbed and dented brow; there also, you would see still stranger foot-prints—the foot-prints of his one unsleeping, ever-pacing thought.

But on the occasion in question, those dents looked deeper, even as his nervous step that morning left a deeper mark. And, so full of his thought was Ahab, that at every uniform turn that he made, now at the main-mast and now at the binnacle, you could almost see that thought turn in him as he turned, and pace in him as he paced; so completely possessing him, indeed, that it all but seemed the inward mould of every outer movement.

“D'ye mark him, Flask?” whispered Stubb; “the chick that's in him pecks the shell. 'Twill soon be out.”

The hours wore on;—Ahab now shut up within his cabin; anon, pacing the deck, with the same intense bigotry of purpose in his aspect.

It drew near the close of day. Suddenly he came to a halt by the bulwarks, and inserting his bone leg into the auger-hole there, and with one hand grasping a shroud, he ordered Starbuck to send everybody aft.

“Sir!” said the mate, astonished at an order seldom or never given on ship-board except in some extraordinary case.

“Send everybody aft,” repeated Ahab. “Mast-heads, there! come down!”

When the entire ship's company were assembled, and with curious and not wholly unapprehensive faces, were eyeing him, for he looked not unlike the weather horizon when a storm is coming up, Ahab, after rapidly glancing over the bulwarks, and then darting his eyes among the crew, started from his standpoint; and as though not a soul were nigh him resumed his heavy turns upon the deck. With bent head and half-slouched hat he continued to pace, unmindful of the wondering whispering among the men; till Stubb cautiously whispered to Flask, that Ahab must have summoned them there for the purpose of witnessing a pedestrian feat. But this did not last long. Vehemently pausing, he cried:—

“What do ye do when ye see a whale, men?”

“Sing out for him!” was the impulsive rejoinder from a score of clubbed voices.

“Good!” cried Ahab, with a wild approval in his tones; observing the hearty animation into which his unexpected question had so magnetically thrown them.

“And what do ye next, men?”

“Lower away, and after him!”

“And what tune is it ye pull to, men?”

“A dead whale or a stove boat!”

More and more strangely and fiercely glad and approving, grew the countenance of the old man at every shout; while the mariners began to gaze curiously at each other, as if marvelling how it was that they themselves became so excited at such seemingly purposeless questions.

But, they were all eagerness again, as Ahab, now half-revolving in his pivot-hole, with one hand reaching high up a shroud, and tightly, almost convulsively grasping it, addressed them thus:—

“All ye mast-headers have before now heard me give orders about a white whale. Look ye! d'ye see this Spanish ounce of gold?”—holding up a broad bright coin to the sun—“it is a sixteen dollar piece, men. D'ye see it? Mr. Starbuck, hand me yon top-maul.”

While the mate was getting the hammer, Ahab, without speaking, was slowly rubbing the gold piece against the skirts of his jacket, as if to heighten its lustre, and without using any words was meanwhile lowly humming to himself, producing a sound so strangely muffled and inarticulate that it seemed the mechanical humming of the wheels of his vitality in him.

Receiving the top-maul from Starbuck, he advanced towards the main-mast with the hammer uplifted in one hand, exhibiting the gold with the other, and with a high raised voice exclaiming: “Whosoever of ye raises me a white-headed whale with a wrinkled brow and a crooked jaw; whosoever of ye raises me that white-headed whale, with three holes punctured in his starboard fluke—look ye, whosoever of ye raises me that same white whale, he shall have this gold ounce, my boys!”

“Huzza! huzza!” cried the seamen, as with swinging tarpaulins they hailed the act of nailing the gold to the mast.

“It's a white whale, I say,” resumed Ahab, as he threw down the topmaul: “a white whale. Skin your eyes for him, men; look sharp for white water; if ye see but a bubble, sing out.”

All this while Tashtego, Daggoo, and Queequeg had looked on with even more intense interest and surprise than the rest, and at the mention of the wrinkled brow and crooked jaw they had started as if each was separately touched by some specific recollection.

“Captain Ahab,” said Tashtego, “that white whale must be the same that some call Moby Dick.”

“Moby Dick?” shouted Ahab. “Do ye know the white whale then, Tash?”

“Does he fan-tail a little curious, sir, before he goes down?” said the Gay-Header deliberately.

“And has he a curious spout, too,” said Daggoo, “very bushy, even for a parmacetty, and mighty quick, Captain Ahab?”

“And he have one, two, three—oh! good many iron in him hide, too, Captain,” cried Queequeg disjointedly, “all twiske-tee be-twisk, like him—him—” faltering hard for a word, and screwing his hand round and round as though uncorking a bottle—“like him—him—”

“Corkscrew!” cried Ahab, “aye, Queequeg, the harpoons lie all twisted and wrenched in him; aye, Daggoo, his spout is a big one, like a whole shock of wheat, and white as a pile of our Nantucket wool after the great annual sheep-shearing; aye, Tashtego, and he fan-tails like a split jib in a squall. Death and devils! men, it is Moby Dick ye have seen—Moby Dick—Moby Dick!”

“Captain Ahab,” said Starbuck, who, with Stubb and Flask, had thus far been eyeing his superior with increasing surprise, but at last seemed struck with a thought which somewhat explained all the wonder. “Captain Ahab, I have heard of Moby Dick—but it was not Moby Dick that took off thy leg?”

“Who told thee that?” cried Ahab; then pausing, “Aye, Starbuck; aye, my hearties all round; it was Moby Dick that dismasted me; Moby Dick that brought me to this dead stump I stand on now. Aye, aye,” he shouted with a terrific, loud, animal sob, like that of a heart-stricken moose; “Aye, aye! it was that accursed white whale that razed me; made a poor pegging lubber of me for ever and a day!” Then tossing both arms, with measureless imprecations he shouted out: “Aye, aye! and I'll chase him round Good Hope, and round the Horn, and round the Norway Maelstrom, and round perdition's flames before I give him up. And this is what ye have shipped for, men! to chase that white whale on both sides of land, and over all sides of earth, till he spouts black blood and rolls fin out. What say ye, men, will ye splice hands on it, now? I think ye do look brave.”

“Aye, aye!” shouted the harpooneers and seamen, running closer to the excited old man: “A sharp eye for the white whale; a sharp lance for Moby Dick!”

“God bless ye,” he seemed to half sob and half shout. “God bless ye, men. Steward! go draw the great measure of grog. But what's this long face about, Mr. Starbuck; wilt thou not chase the white whale? art not game for Moby Dick?”

“I am game for his crooked jaw, and for the jaws of Death too, Captain Ahab, if it fairly comes in the way of the business we follow; but I came here to hunt whales, not my commander's vengeance. How many barrels will thy vengeance yield thee even if thou gettest it, Captain Ahab? it will not fetch thee much in our Nantucket market.”

“Nantucket market! Hoot! But come closer, Starbuck; thou requirest a little lower layer. If money's to be the measurer, man, and the accountants have computed their great counting-house the globe, by girdling it with guineas, one to every three parts of an inch; then, let me tell thee, that my vengeance will fetch a great premium here! ”

“He smites his chest,” whispered Stubb, “what's that for? methinks it rings most vast, but hollow.”

“Vengeance on a dumb brute!” cried Starbuck, “that simply smote thee from blindest instinct! Madness! To be enraged with a dumb thing, Captain Ahab, seems blasphemous.”

“Hark ye yet again—the little lower layer. All visible objects, man, are but as pasteboard masks. But in each event—in the living act, the undoubted deed—there, some unknown but still reasoning thing puts forth the mouldings of its features from behind the unreasoning mask. If man will strike, strike through the mask! How can the prisoner reach outside except by thrusting through the wall? To me, the white whale is that wall, shoved near to me. Sometimes I think there's naught beyond. But 'tis enough. He tasks me; he heaps me; I see in him outrageous strength, with an inscrutable malice sinewing it. That inscrutable thing is chiefly what I hate; and be the white whale agent, or be the white whale principal, I will wreak that hate upon him. Talk not to me of blasphemy, man; I'd strike the sun if it insulted me. For could the sun do that, then could I do the other; since there is ever a sort of fair play herein, jealousy presiding over all creations. But not my master, man, is even that fair play. Who's over me? Truth hath no confines. Take off thine eye! more intolerable than fiends' glarings is a doltish stare! So, so; thou reddenest and palest; my heat has melted thee to anger-glow. Ah! constrainings seize thee; I see! the billow lifts thee! Speak, but speak!—Aye, aye! thy silence, then, that voices thee. “God keep me!—keep us all!” murmured Starbuck, lowly.
