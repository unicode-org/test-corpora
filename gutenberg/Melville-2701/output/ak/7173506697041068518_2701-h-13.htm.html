Ɛyɛ saa bere no na wɔsesa mmeae; na ɔpanyin, adwinnan ketewa no so panyin no, fa ne gyinabea a ɛfata wɔ hyɛmma no anim.

Afei, ɛnyɛ nea ɔkura nea ɛne eyi bɔ abira no mu no mfa me ho, nanso eyi nyinaa yɛ nkwaseasɛm na ɛho nhia. Ɛsɛ sɛ ɔpanyin no tra agyan no mu fi nea edi kan kosi nea etwa to; ɛnsɛ sɛ ɔtow agyan ne peaw no nyinaa, na ɛnsɛ sɛ wɔhwɛ kwan sɛ ɔbɛtow ahyɛmma biara, gye sɛ tebea horow a ɛda adi pefee ma ɔpofoni biara. Minim sɛ ɛtɔ mmere bi a na eyi bɛhwehwɛ sɛ wɔhwere ahoɔhare kakra wɔ akyidi no mu; nanso osuahu a akyɛ wɔ aman a ɛboro biako mu a wɔkyere mpataa ahorow mu no ama magye adi sɛ wɔ mpataayi mu huammɔdi dodow no ara mu no, ɛnyɛ ɔkwan biara so no, ɛnyɛ ahoɔhare a whale no de ba no kɛse te sɛ ɔbrɛ a yɛadi kan aka ho asɛm wɔ harpooneer no mu no na ɛde aba no wɔn.

Sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ na wɔahwɛ ahu sɛ agyan no bɛyɛ adwuma yiye kɛse no, ɛsɛ sɛ wiase yi mu sankubɔfo fi ase gyina wɔn nan so fi adwuma a wɔnyɛ mu, na ɛnyɛ adwumaden mu.





TI 63. Crotch no ho asɛm.

Fi dua no mu na nkorabata no nyin; wɔn mu, nkorabata no. Enti, wɔ nsɛmti a ɛsow aba mu no, nyin ti ahorow no.

Crotch a yɛaka ho asɛm wɔ kratafa bi a atwam no fata sɛ yɛka ho asɛm wɔ ahofadi mu. Ɛyɛ dua a ɛwɔ nsensanee a ɛyɛ soronko, a ne tenten bɛyɛ anammɔn abien, a wɔde hyɛ nifa fam atuo a ɛbɛn agyan no mu tẽẽ, a atirimpɔw no ne sɛ wɔbɛma ahomegye ama harpoon a ne fã foforo a ɛda adagyaw na ɛyɛ nsɔe no awiei a ɛkɔ fam no nnwuma a efi prow no so. Ɛnam so ma akode no ba nea ɔtow tuo no nkyɛn ntɛm ara, na ɔfa no fi ne home mu ntɛm ara te sɛ nea obi a ɔbɔ akyi dua wosow n’atuo fi ɔfasu no so no. Ɛyɛ amanne sɛ wobenya agyan abien a wɔde gye wɔn ahome wɔ asen mu, a wɔfrɛ no dade a edi kan ne nea ɛto so abien.

Nanso saa harpoon abien yi a emu biara nam n’ankasa hama so no, ne nyinaa ne hama no wɔ abusuabɔ; botae no ne sɛ: sɛ ɛbɛyɛ yiye a, sɛ ɛbɛyɛ yiye a, wɔbɛtow wɔn baanu nyinaa akuturuku biako akyi biako akɔ akraman koro no ara mu ntɛm ara; sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a sɛ, wɔ twe a ɛreba no mu no, obiako twe fi mu a, ɔfoforo no betumi akɔ so akura mu. Ɛyɛ hokwan ahorow a ɛwɔ hɔ no mmɔho abien. Nanso ɛtaa ba sɛ esiane sɛnea whale no tu mmirika ntɛm ara, basabasa, na ɛma ne ho popo bere a onyaa dade a edi kan no nti, ɛbɛyɛ nea entumi nyɛ yiye sɛ nea ɔbɔ sanku no, ɛmfa ho sɛnea ne kankyee te sɛ anyinam no, bɛtow dade a ɛto so abien no agu ne mu. Ne nyinaa mu no, sɛnea dade a ɛto so abien no ne hama no abɔ dedaw, na nhama no retu mmirika no, enti ɛsɛ sɛ, wɔ tebea biara mu no, wɔhwɛ kwan sɛ wɔtow saa akode no fi hyɛmma no mu, ɔkwan bi so ne baabi; anyɛ saa a asiane a ɛyɛ hu sen biara no bɛfa nsa nyinaa ho. Bere a wɔahwe ase akɔ nsu no mu no, sɛnea ɛte no, ɛwɔ tebea horow a ɛtete saa mu; adaka mu nhama a wɔde aka ho (a yɛaka ho asɛm wɔ ti a edi eyi anim mu) a ɛma saa adeyɛ yi, wɔ nsɛm dodow no ara mu, yɛ nea nyansa wom yɛ nea wɔde di dwuma. Nanso ɛnyɛ bere nyinaa na adeyɛ a ɛho hia yi yɛ nea obiara nhwɛ so a nnipa a wowuwu a ɛyɛ awerɛhow na edi awu sen biara.

Bio nso: ɛsɛ sɛ wuhu sɛ sɛ wɔtow dade a ɛto so abien no gu po no mu a, efi saa bere no, ɛbɛyɛ ehu a ɛsensɛn hɔ, a ano yɛ nnam, na ɛde skittishly twitwa hyɛmma ne whale nyinaa ho, na ɛkyekyere nhama no, anaasɛ ɛtwitwa, na ɛyɛ atenka a ɛyɛ nwonwa wɔ afã horow nyinaa. Saa ara nso na mpɛn pii no, ɛrentumi nyɛ yiye sɛ wɔbɛsan abɔ no ho ban kosi sɛ wɔbɛkyere whale no yiye na wayɛ funu.

Afei susuw sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ sɛ ɛte wɔ akorow anan a ne nyinaa ne whale biako a ne ho yɛ den, ɔyɛ nnam, na onim ade soronko di nkitaho no ho hwɛ; bere a esiane saa su ahorow a ɛwɔ ne mu yi nti, ne akwanhyia apem a ɛba bere koro mu a adwuma a ɛte saa a ɛyɛ akokoduru de ba nti, ebia dade a ɛto so abien a ɛyɛ mmerɛw awotwe anaa du a ɛsensɛn ne ho bere koro mu. Efisɛ nokwarem no, wɔde harpoon pii ma hyɛmma biara sɛ sɛ wɔde mmirika a entu mpɔn wɔ nea edi kan no mu a ennya ahoɔden a, ɛbɛkotow wɔ hama no so. Wɔde nokwaredi aka saa nsɛm pɔtee yi nyinaa ho asɛm wɔ ha, efisɛ ɛrentumi mma nkyekyem ahorow pii a ɛho hia sen biara, ɛmfa ho sɛnea ɛyɛ den no mu nna hɔ wɔ nsɛm a ɛbɛba akyiri yi a wɔbɛyɛ ho mfonini no mu.





TI 64. Stubb Anwummere Adidi.

Ná wɔakum Stubb whale no wɔ akyirikyiri kakra wɔ hyɛn no ho. Ná ɛyɛ kommyɛ; enti, yɛyɛɛ ahyɛmma abiɛsa a wɔbom yɛ adwuma no, yefii adwuma a ɛyɛ brɛoo a ɛne sɛ yɛbɛtwe nkonimbo no akɔ Pequod no ase. Na afei, berɛ a yɛyɛ mmarima dunwɔtwe a yɛde yɛn nsa aduasa nsia, ne nsateaa ne nsateaa ɔha aduɔwɔtwe, de nkakrankakra yɛɛ adwuma dɔnhwerew biara wɔ saa funu a ɔnyɛ adwuma na ɔyɛ ɔkwasea wɔ ɛpo mu no so; na na ɛte sɛ nea ɛyɛ den sɛ ɛbɛhinhim koraa, gye sɛ ɛwɔ ntam tenten; wɔde adanse pa mae wɔ sɛnea nnipadɔm a yetu kɔɔ baabi foforo no sõ no ho. Efisɛ, wɔ Hang-Ho asubɔnten kɛse no so, anaasɛ nea wɔfrɛ no biara, wɔ China no, adwumayɛfo baanan anaa baanum a wɔwɔ nantew kwan no so bɛtwe nwura akɛse a wɔde fa nneɛma a ɛyɛ akwansin biako dɔnhwerew biara; nanso saa akasakasa kɛse a yɛtwee yi yɛɛ atoro kɛse, te sɛ nea mprako-kɔbere a ɛdɔɔso ahyɛ mu ma.

Esum bae; nanso akanea abiɛsa a ɛkɔ soro ne fam wɔ Pequod no main-rigging no mu no kyerɛɛ yɛn kwan wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ kusuu so; kosii sɛ yɛbɛn hɔ no yehuu Ahab sɛ ɔreto akanea afoforo pii no mu biako agu abankɛse no so. Ɔde n’ani kyerɛɛ akraman a ɔrehuruhuruw no so bere tiaa bi no, ɔde ahyɛde ahorow a wɔtaa de ma sɛ wɔmfa nsie anadwo no, na afei ɔde ne kanea maa po so hyɛn mu dwumayɛni bi, kɔɔ ɔdan no mu, na wankɔ n’anim bio kosii anɔpa.

Ɛwom sɛ, wɔ ɔsahene Ahab akyi a ɔhwɛɛ so no, na wada n’amammerɛ dwumadi adi, sɛ yɛbɛfrɛ no saa a; nanso afei a abɔde no awu no, na ɛte sɛ nea abotɔyam a enni hɔ, anaa abotare a wonni, anaa abasamtu bi reyɛ adwuma wɔ ne mu; te sɛ nea saa funu no a ohui no kae no sɛ wonnya nkum Moby Dick; na ɛwom sɛ wɔde akraman afoforo apem baa ne hyɛn no mu de, nanso ne nyinaa remma n’ade kɛse a ɛyɛ obiakofo no nkɔ anim. Ankyɛ koraa na wubesusuw afi nnyigyei a ɛwɔ Pequod no decks so no mu, sɛ nsa nyinaa resiesie wɔn ho sɛ wɔbɛtow ankora wɔ bun no mu; efisɛ wɔretwe nkɔnsɔnkɔnsɔn a emu yɛ duru afa hyɛn no atifi, na wɔrepiapia a ɛrebɔ denneennen afi hyɛn gyinabea ntokuru no mu. Nanso ɛdenam saa nhama a ɛrebɔ denneennen no so no, ɛsɛ sɛ wɔde funu kɛse no ankasa si hɔ, na ɛnyɛ hyɛn no. Esiane sɛ wɔde ne ti akyekyere no akɔ akyi, na wɔde ne dua akyekyere no akɔ n’anim nti, mprempren ɔda a ne ti tuntum no bɛn hyɛn no de na wohu no wɔ anadwo sum mu, a ɛkataa spars ne rigging a ɛwɔ soro no so, abien no—hyɛn ne whale, a na ɛte sɛ nea wɔakyekyere wɔn kɔndua te sɛ anantwinini akɛse, a wɔn mu biako tena fam bere a ɔfoforo no da so ara gyina hɔ.*

*Ebia ade ketewaa bi nso fa ho wɔ ha. Nneɛma a emu yɛ den na wotumi de ho to so sen biara a hyɛn no kura wɔ whale no so bere a wɔde ahyɛ nkyɛn no, ne flukes anaa dua no; na sɛnea efi ne kɛse a ɛyɛ den no mu no, saa fã no mu yɛ duru sen foforo biara (gye nkyɛnkyɛn no), sɛnea ɛyɛ mmerɛw sɛ ɛbɛdannan no wɔ owu mu mpo, ma ɛkɔ fam wɔ ɔfã no ase; sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a womfa nsa no ntumi mfi hyɛmma no mu nkɔ so, sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a wode nkɔnsɔnkɔnsɔn no bɛtwa ho ahyia. Nanso wɔde anifere di ɔhaw yi so: wosiesie hama ketewaa bi a ɛyɛ den a wɔde nnua a ɛsensɛn nsu mu wɔ n’akyi ano, na wɔde adesoa bi ahyɛ ne mfinimfini, bere a wɔde ano foforo no abɔ hyɛn no ho. Ɛdenam ahokokwaw a wɔde di dwuma so no, wɔma nnua a ɛsensɛn nsu so no sɔre wɔ mass no fã foforo, ma enti mprempren a wɔabɔ akraman no abɔso no, wɔma nkɔnsɔnkɔnsɔn no di akyi ntɛm ara; na sɛ wɔde wɔn ho hyɛ nipadua no mu a, awiei koraa no, wɔkyekyere no ntɛmntɛm twa dua no fã ketewaa no ho hyia, wɔ baabi a ɛne ne ntini a ɛtrɛw anaa ne ntini a ɛtrɛw no hyia no.

Sɛ afei de na Ahab a ne ho yɛ hu no nyinaa yɛ komm, anyɛ yiye koraa no sɛnea wobetumi ahu wɔ hyɛn no so a, Stubb, ne hokafo a ɔto so abien a nkonimdi ho ayɛ no hyew no daa anigye a ɛyɛ soronko nanso ɛda so ara yɛ suban pa adi. Na ɔyɛ gyegyeegye a na ɔntaa nyɛ saa efisɛ Starbuck a ɔyɛ pintinn, ne panyin a ɔyɛ aban no, gyaee adwuma komm maa no saa bere no na ɔno nkutoo na ɔhwɛ nsɛm so. Ankyɛ na wɔdaa ade ketewaa biako a ɛboa a ɛde anigye yi nyinaa bae wɔ Stubb no adi wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ nwonwa so. Ná Stubb yɛ mmerɛbo a ɛkɔ soro; na n’ani gye whale ho kakra sɛ ade a ɛma n’anom yɛ dɛ.

“Steak, steak, ere mada! Wo, Daggoo, wo! po so kɔ, na wotwa me biako fi ne ketewa no mu!”

Ɛha na wonim, sɛ ɛwom sɛ saa wuram apofofo yi mma, sɛ́ ade a ɛkɔ so wɔ ɔkwan a ɛkɔ akyiri so, na sɛnea asraafo asɛm kɛse no kyerɛ no, mma ɔtamfo no ntua ɔko no ho ka mprempren (anyɛ yiye koraa no ansa na wɔahu sika a wonya fi akwantu no mu) de, nanso mprempren na afei wuhu Nantucketfo yi bi a wɔwɔ anigye ankasa wɔ saa Sperm Whale no fã pɔtee a Stubb paw no no ho; a ɛyɛ nipadua no fã a ɛyɛ teateaa no.

Bɛyɛ anadwo fã no, wotwitwaa saa nam no na wɔnoaa; na na wɔde akanea abien a wɔde mmadwoa ngo ayɛ no sɔ no, Stubb de ahoɔden gyinaa hɔ maa ne mmadwoa anwummere aduan no wɔ capstan-ti no so, te sɛ nea saa capstan no yɛ sideboard. Saa ara nso na na Stubb nkutoo ne apontow a ɔdii whale nam saa anadwo no. Bere a wɔde wɔn nsɛm a wɔka no frafra n’ankasa anom no, akraman mpempem pii a wɔde wɔn ho ahyɛ leviathan a awu no ho ahyia no dii ne srade no ho apontow a ɛyɛ akuturuku. Ná nnipa kakraa bi a wɔda ase wɔ wɔn mpa so no ho dwiriw wɔn wɔ wɔn dua a wɔde bɔ hyɛn no anim denneennen, wɔ wɔn a wɔada no koma mu nsateakwaa kakraa bi mu no ho. Sɛ wohwɛ nkyɛn a, na wubetumi ahu wɔn ara kwa (te sɛ ansa na worete wɔn) sɛ wɔrehuruhuruw wɔ nsu a ɛyɛ kusuu na ɛyɛ tuntum no mu, na wɔredan wɔn ho wɔ wɔn akyi bere a wɔretutu akraman a ne kɛse te sɛ onipa ti no asinasin akɛse a ɛyɛ kurukuruwa no. Ɛte sɛ nea saa akraman no adeyɛ pɔtee yi yɛ anwonwade. Sɛnea wɔ beae a ɛte saa a ɛda adi sɛ wontumi ntua so no, wɔbɔ pɔw sɛ wɔbɛpopa ano a ɛte saa a ɛyɛ pɛpɛɛpɛ no da so ara yɛ amansan nyinaa haw a ɛfa nneɛma nyinaa ho no fã. Agyiraehyɛde a wɔnam saayɛ so gyaw wɔ whale no so no, yebetumi de atoto tokuru a duadwumfo bi yɛe wɔ countersinking mu de hwehwɛ skru no ho yiye.

Ɛwom sɛ wɔ po so akodi mu ahude a ɛyɛ wusiw ne ɔbonsam su nyinaa mu de, nanso wobehu akraman sɛ wɔde akɔnnɔ rehwɛ hyɛn no atifi, te sɛ akraman a ɔkɔm de wɔn atwa pon bi a wɔredi nam kɔkɔɔ ho ahyia, a wɔasiesie wɔn ho sɛ wɔbɛbɔ onipa biara a wɔakum no a wɔbɛtow no akɔ ne nkyɛn no wɔn; na nanso, bere a namkumfo akokodurufo a wɔwɔ deck-pon no so de nnipakum nam saa kwan yi so de adwemmɔne nnade a wɔde sika kɔkɔɔ ne tassell ayɛ ne nyinaa resen wɔn ho wɔn ho nam a nkwa wom no, akraman no nso, a wɔn ano a wɔde aboɔden abo ayɛ no, de ntɔkwaw retwa wɔn ho wɔn ho nam a nkwa wom wɔ pon no ase wɔ nam a awu; na nanso, sɛ wodan asɛm no nyinaa danee a, anka ɛda so ara bɛyɛ ade koro no ara, kyerɛ sɛ, adwuma a ɛyɛ ahodwiriw a ɛyɛ sharkish a ɛdɔɔso ma afã horow nyinaa; na ɛwom sɛ akraman nso yɛ nkoa po so ahyɛn a ɛtwa Atlantic po no nyinaa akyigyinafo a wontumi nsakra, a wɔde wɔn ho tu mmirika wɔ nhyehyɛe kwan so de, nanso sɛ ɛba sɛ wɔde nneɛma bi bɛkɔ baabiara, anaasɛ wobesie akoa a wawu wɔ ɔkwan a ɛfata so a; na ɛwom sɛ wobetumi de nsɛm foforo biako anaa abien a ɛte saa ato hɔ, a ɛka nsɛmfua, mmeae, ne mmere a wɔahyɛ ato hɔ, bere a akraman hyiam kɛse wɔ asetra mu, na wodi apontow a ɛyɛ serew kɛse no de; nanso so bere anaa bere biara nni hɔ a wubetumi asusuw ho a wubehu wɔn wɔ dodow a wontumi nkan saa mu, ne ahonhom a wɔyɛ gayer anaa anigye kɛse mu, sen sɛ wɔbɛtwa sperm whale a wawu ho ahyia, a wɔde no asie anadwo wɔ whale hyɛn bi mu wɔ po so. Sɛ wunhuu saa ade a wohu no da a, ɛnde gyae wo gyinaesi a ɛfa sɛnea ɔbonsam som fata, ne mfaso a ɛwɔ so sɛ wobɛpata ɔbonsam no.

Nanso, ɛde besi nnɛ no, Stubb antie apontow a na ɛrekɔ so wɔ baabi a ɛbɛn no saa no nwiinwii, na ɛnyɛ sɛnea akraman no antie n’ankasa epicurean anofafa a ɛrebɔ no no.

“Noa aduan, noa!—ɛhe na saa Nwi dedaw no wɔ?” osui kyɛe, na ɔtrɛw ne nan mu bio, te sɛ nea ɔreyɛ nnyinaso a ahobammɔ wom kɛse ama n’anwummere aduan; na, bere koro no ara mu no, ɔde ne kyɛnsee no rebɔ ahina no mu, te sɛ nea ɔde ne peaw rebɔ; “noa aduan, wo noa!—fa po so hyɛn fa saa kwan yi so, noa!”

Abibifo dedaw no, a na onni anigye kɛse biara sɛ wɔadi kan anyan no afi ne hammock a ɛyɛ hyew no mu wɔ dɔnhwerew a ɛnyɛ bere mu de koraa mu no, de ahopere fii ne galley no mu bae, efisɛ, te sɛ abibifo dedaw pii no, na biribi wɔ hɔ a ɛyɛ asɛm wɔ ne nkotodwe a wɔde hyɛ mu no ho, . a wankora so yiye sɛ ne kyɛnsee afoforo no; saa Nwoma dedaw yi, sɛnea na wɔfrɛ no no, bae a ɔrebɔ ne ho akɔneaba na ɔrebɔ ne nan ase, na ɔde ne nkɔnsɔnkɔnsɔn a, wɔ ɔkwan a ɛyɛ mmerɛw akyi no, wɔde dade nhama a wɔateɛ na ɛyɛe no boaa n’anammɔn; saa Ebony dedaw yi hwee ase, na esiane sɛ ɔyɛɛ osetie maa ahyɛde asɛm no nti, ogyinaa baabi a awu wɔ Stubb nkyɛnkyɛn no fã a ɛne no bɔ abira no; bere a ɔde ne nsa abien nyinaa bobɔw n’anim, na ɔde ne ho too ne poma a ɛwɔ nan abien so no, ɔde n’akyi a ɛyɛ abɔso no kotow kɔɔ akyiri, bere koro no ara mu no ɔde ne ti kɔ nkyɛnkyɛn, sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a ɔde n’aso a eye sen biara no bɛba agoru no mu.

CHAPTER 65. The Whale as a Dish.

That mortal man should feed upon the creature that feeds his lamp, and, like Stubb, eat him by his own light, as you may say; this seems so outlandish a thing that one must needs go a little into the history and philosophy of it.

It is upon record, that three centuries ago the tongue of the Right Whale was esteemed a great delicacy in France, and commanded large prices there. Also, that in Henry VIIIth's time, a certain cook of the court obtained a handsome reward for inventing an admirable sauce to be eaten with barbacued porpoises, which, you remember, are a species of whale. Porpoises, indeed, are to this day considered fine eating. The meat is made into balls about the size of billiard balls, and being well seasoned and spiced might be taken for turtle-balls or veal balls. The old monks of Dunfermline were very fond of them. They had a great porpoise grant from the crown.

The fact is, that among his hunters at least, the whale would by all hands be considered a noble dish, were there not so much of him; but when you come to sit down before a meat-pie nearly one hundred feet long, it takes away your appetite. Only the most unprejudiced of men like Stubb, nowadays partake of cooked whales; but the Esquimaux are not so fastidious. We all know how they live upon whales, and have rare old vintages of prime old train oil. Zogranda, one of their most famous doctors, recommends strips of blubber for infants, as being exceedingly juicy and nourishing. And this reminds me that certain Englishmen, who long ago were accidentally left in Greenland by a whaling vessel—that these men actually lived for several months on the mouldy scraps of whales which had been left ashore after trying out the blubber. Among the Dutch whalemen these scraps are called “fritters”; which, indeed, they greatly resemble, being brown and crisp, and smelling something like old Amsterdam housewives' dough-nuts or oly-cooks, when fresh. They have such an eatable look that the most self-denying stranger can hardly keep his hands off.

But what further depreciates the whale as a civilized dish, is his exceeding richness. He is the great prize ox of the sea, too fat to be delicately good. Look at his hump, which would be as fine eating as the buffalo's (which is esteemed a rare dish), were it not such a solid pyramid of fat. But the spermaceti itself, how bland and creamy that is; like the transparent, half-jellied, white meat of a cocoanut in the third month of its growth, yet far too rich to supply a substitute for butter. Nevertheless, many whalemen have a method of absorbing it into some other substance, and then partaking of it. In the long try watches of the night it is a common thing for the seamen to dip their ship-biscuit into the huge oil-pots and let them fry there awhile. Many a good supper have I thus made.

In the case of a small Sperm Whale the brains are accounted a fine dish. The casket of the skull is broken into with an axe, and the two plump, whitish lobes being withdrawn (precisely resembling two large puddings), they are then mixed with flour, and cooked into a most delectable mess, in flavor somewhat resembling calves' head, which is quite a dish among some epicures; and every one knows that some young bucks among the epicures, by continually dining upon calves' brains, by and by get to have a little brains of their own, so as to be able to tell a calf's head from their own heads; which, indeed, requires uncommon discrimination. And that is the reason why a young buck with an intelligent looking calf's head before him, is somehow one of the saddest sights you can see. The head looks a sort of reproachfully at him, with an “Et tu Brute!” expression.

It is not, perhaps, entirely because the whale is so excessively unctuous that landsmen seem to regard the eating of him with abhorrence; that appears to result, in some way, from the consideration before mentioned: ie that a man should eat a newly murdered thing of the sea, and eat it too by its own light. But no doubt the first man that ever murdered an ox was regarded as a murderer; perhaps he was hung; and if he had been put on his trial by oxen, he certainly would have been; and he certainly deserved it if any murderer does. Go to the meat-market of a Saturday night and see the crowds of live bipeds staring up at the long rows of dead quadrupeds. Does not that sight take a tooth out of the cannibal's jaw? Cannibals? who is not a cannibal? I tell you it will be more tolerable for the Fejee that salted down a lean missionary in his cellar against a coming famine; it will be more tolerable for that provident Fejee, I say, in the day of judgment, than for thee, civilized and enlightened gourmand, who nailest geese to the ground and feastest on their bloated livers in thy paté-de-foie-gras.

But Stubb, he eats the whale by its own light, does he? and that is adding insult to injury, is it? Look at your knife-handle, there, my civilized and enlightened gourmand dining off that roast beef, what is that handle made of?—what but the bones of the brother of the very ox you are eating? And what do you pick your teeth with, after devouring that fat goose? With a feather of the same fowl. And with what quill did the Secretary of the Society for the Suppression of Cruelty to Ganders formally indite his circulars? It is only within the last month or two that that society passed a resolution to patronize nothing but steel pens.





CHAPTER 66. The Shark Massacre.

When in the Southern Fishery, a captured Sperm Whale, after long and weary toil, is brought alongside late at night, it is not, as a general thing at least, customary to proceed at once to the business of cutting him in. For that business is an exceedingly laborious one; is not very soon completed; and requires all hands to set about it. Therefore, the common usage is to take in all sail; lash the helm a'lee; and then send every one below to his hammock till daylight, with the reservation that, until that time, anchor-watches shall be kept; that is, two and two for an hour, each couple, the crew in rotation shall mount the deck to see that all goes well.

But sometimes, especially upon the Line in the Pacific, this plan will not answer at all; because such incalculable hosts of sharks gather round the moored carcase, that were he left so for six hours, say, on a stretch, little more than the skeleton would be visible by morning. In most other parts of the ocean, however, where these fish do not so largely abound, their wondrous voracity can be at times considerably diminished, by vigorously stirring them up with sharp whaling-spades, a procedure notwithstanding, which, in some instances, only seems to tickle them into still greater activity. But it was not thus in the present case with the Pequod's sharks; though, to be sure, any man unaccustomed to such sights, to have looked over her side that night, would have almost thought the whole round sea was one huge cheese, and those sharks the maggots in it.

Nevertheless, upon Stubb setting the anchor-watch after his supper was concluded; and when, accordingly, Queequeg and a forecastle seaman came on deck, no small excitement was created among the sharks; for immediately suspending the cutting stages over the side, and lowering three lanterns, so that they cast long gleams of light over the turbid sea, these two mariners, darting their long whaling-spades, kept up an incessant murdering of the sharks,* by striking the keen steel deep into their skulls, seemingly their only vital part. But in the foamy confusion of their mixed and struggling hosts, the marksmen could not always hit their mark; and this brought about new revelations of the incredible ferocity of the foe. They viciously snapped, not only at each other's disembowelments, but like flexible bows, bent round, and bit their own; till those entrails seemed swallowed over and over again by the same mouth, to be oppositely voided by the gaping wound. Nor was this all. It was unsafe to meddle with the corpses and ghosts of these creatures. A sort of generic or Pantheistic vitality seemed to lurk in their very joints and bones, after what might be called the individual life had departed. Killed and hoisted on deck for the sake of his skin, one of these sharks almost took poor Queequeg's hand off, when he tried to shut down the dead lid of his murderous jaw.

*The whaling-spade used for cutting-in is made of the very best steel; is about the bigness of a man's spread hand; and in general shape, corresponds to the garden implement after which it is named; only its sides are perfectly flat, and its upper end considerably narrower than the lower. This weapon is always kept as sharp as possible; and when being used is occasionally honed, just like a razor. In its socket, a stiff pole, from twenty to thirty feet long, is inserted for a handle.

“Queequeg no care what god made him shark,” said the savage, agonizingly lifting his hand up and down; “wedder Fejee god or Nantucket god; but de god wat made shark must be one dam Ingin.”
