MUTU 101. Chophimba.

Sitima yapamadzi ya Chingerezi isanazimiririke, zikhale pansi pano, kuti adachokera ku London, ndipo adatchedwa malemu Samuel Enderby, wamalonda wa mzinda umenewo, choyambirira cha nyumba yodziwika bwino ya whaling ya Enderby & Sons; nyumba yomwe mwa lingaliro langa la whaleman wosauka, imabwera pafupi ndi nyumba zachifumu zogwirizana za Tudors ndi Bourbons, ponena za chidwi chenicheni cha mbiri yakale. Kwautali wotani, chisanafike chaka cha Ambuye wathu 1775, nyumba yosokera yamchere imeneyi inalipo, zolembedwa zanga zambiri za nsomba sizimamveketsa bwino; koma m’chaka chimenecho (1775) chinalinganiza zombo zoyambirira za Chingelezi zimene nthaŵi zonse zimasaka Nangumi wa Umuna; ngakhale kwa zaka zingapo zapitazo (kuyambira 1726) a Coffins athu olimba mtima ndi Maceys a Nantucket ndi Munda Wamphesa anali ndi zombo zazikulu zothamangitsa Leviathan, koma Kumpoto ndi Kumwera kwa Atlantic: osati kwina kulikonse. Zikhale zolembedwa momveka bwino apa, kuti a Nantucketers anali oyamba pakati pa anthu kulumikiza ndi chitsulo chotukuka kwambiri Whale Whale; ndi kuti kwa theka la zana anali anthu okhawo padziko lonse lapansi omwe adamuzunza.

Mu 1778, sitima yabwino, Amelia, inakonzekera cholinga chenichenicho, ndipo pamlandu wokhawo wa Enderbys wamphamvu, molimba mtima kuzungulira Cape Horn, ndipo inali yoyamba pakati pa mayiko kutsitsa bwato la whale lamtundu uliwonse m'nyanja. chachikulu South Sea. Ulendowu unali waluso komanso wamwayi; ndipo pobwerera kumalo ake ogonerako ndi kugwira kwake kodzala ndi umuna wamtengo wapatali, chitsanzo cha Amelia posapita nthaŵi chinatsatiridwa ndi zombo zina, Chingelezi ndi Chiamerika, ndipo chotero mabwalo aakulu a Sperm Whale a Pacific anatsegulidwa. Koma osakhutitsidwa ndi ntchito yabwinoyi, nyumba yosatopayo idakhazikikanso: Samuel ndi Ana ake onse - angati, amayi awo okha amadziwa - komanso mothandizidwa ndi iwo, ndipo pang'ono, ndikuganiza, chifukwa cha ndalama zawo, boma la Britain lidakopeka. kuti atumize Rattler woyenda pang'onopang'ono paulendo wamansomba wotulukira ku South Sea. Polamulidwa ndi Post-Captain wapamadzi, Rattler anayenda ulendo wokhotakhota, ndipo anachita ntchito ina; sizikuwoneka bwanji. Koma izi siziri zonse. Mu 1819, nyumba yomweyo inapanga sitima yapamadzi yotchedwa discovery whale yawoyawo, kuti ipite paulendo wokoma kumadzi akutali a Japan. Sitimayo, yotchedwa "Syren" - idayenda bwino kwambiri poyesera; ndipo zinali choncho pamene malo aakulu a Whaling a ku Japan anayamba kudziwika mofala. The Syren mu ulendo wotchuka umenewu analamulidwa ndi Captain Coffin, Nantucketer.

Ulemu wonse kwa a Enderbies, chifukwa chake, omwe nyumba yawo, ndikuganiza, ilipo mpaka lero; ngakhale kuti mosakayikira Samueli woyambirirayo ayenera kuti kalekalelo anazembetsa chingwe chake ku Nyanja Yakummwera yaikulu ya dziko lina.

Sitimayo yotchedwa pambuyo pake inali yoyenerera ulemu, pokhala wothamanga kwambiri komanso luso lapamwamba kwambiri. Ndinamukwera kamodzi pakati pausiku kwinakwake kugombe la Patagonia, ndikumwa madzi abwino m'malo owonetsera. Anali masewera abwino omwe tinali nawo, ndipo onse anali malipenga - aliyense m'bwalomo. Moyo waufupi kwa iwo, ndi imfa yosangalatsa. Ndipo masewera abwino aja amene ine ndinali nawo—nthawi yaitali, motalika kwambiri Ahabu wokalamba atagwira matabwa ake ndi chidendene cha minyanga—izo zimandikumbukira ine za kuchereza kolemekezeka, kolimba, kwa Saxon kwa ngalawayo; ndipo mulole mwana wangayo andiiwale ine, ndipo mdierekezi andikumbukire ine, ngati ine nditasiya konse kupenya kwa izo. Kutembenuza? Kodi ndati tinali ndi zopindika? Inde, ndipo tidachitembenuza pamlingo wa magaloni khumi pa ola; ndipo pamene chiphokosocho chinabwera (pakuti chinachoka kumeneko pafupi ndi Patagonia), ndipo manja onse—alendo ndi onse—anaitanidwa ku nsonga za pamwamba pa matanthwe, tinali olemera kwambiri kotero kuti tinkakwera m’mwamba wina ndi mnzake m’mwamba; ndipo mosadziwa tinaveka masiketi a jekete zathu m’matanga, kotero kuti tinapachikidwa pamenepo, tikumangirira mwamphamvu m’mphepo yamkuntho, chitsanzo chochenjeza kwa phula lonse loledzera. Komabe, milongotiyo sinapitirire; ndipo pang'ono ndi pang'ono tidatsika, osaganiza bwino, kotero kuti tidayenera kudutsanso, ngakhale utsi wonyezimira wamchere udasefukira pansi pa scuttle, m'malo mwake adasungunuka ndikuzifutsa zomwe ndimakonda.

Ng'ombeyo inali yabwino-yolimba, koma yokhala ndi thupi. Iwo anati iyo inali ng'ombe-ng'ombe; ena, kuti anali dromedary ng'ombe; koma sindikudziwa, motsimikiza, momwe zidakhalira. Iwo anali nawonso zinyenyeswazi; tinthu tating'ono, koma tokulirapo, tofanana ndi globular, ndi dumplings zosawonongeka. Ndinkalakalaka kuti mungazimvere, ndikuzigudubuza mwa inu zitamezedwa. Ngati mutawerama patsogolo kwambiri, mumayika pachiwopsezo kuti akutulutseni ngati mipira ya mabiliyoni. Mkate—koma zimenezo sizikanatheka; pambali pake, chinali chotsutsa-scorbutic; Mwachidule, mkatewo unali ndi ndalama yokhayo yomwe anali nayo. Koma malo achitetezowo sanali opepuka kwambiri, ndipo zinali zosavuta kuponda pakona yamdima mutadya. Koma zonse, kumutenga kuchokera ku galimoto kupita ku woyendetsa, poganizira za kukula kwa ma boilers a wophika, kuphatikizapo ma boilers ake amoyo; kutsogolo ndi kumbuyo, ndikunena, Samuel Enderby inali sitima yapamadzi; zabwino ndi zoipa; chopindika chabwino ndi cholimba; crack anzake onse, ndi likulu kuchokera nsapato zidendene kuti chipewa-band.

Koma nchifukwa ninji, mukuganiza kuti, Samuel Enderby, ndi amphaka ena Achingelezi omwe ndimawadziwa—osati onse—anali zombo zotchuka, zochereza; amene ankadutsa mozungulira ng'ombe, ndi mkate, ndi chitini, ndi nthabwala; ndipo sanatope posachedwa kudya, ndi kumwa, ndi kuseka? Ndikuuzani. Chisangalalo chochuluka cha nsomba za ku England zimenezi ndi nkhani yofufuza mbiri yakale. Komanso sindinasiye kufufuza za mbiri yakale ya whale, pamene zikuwoneka kuti ndizofunikira.

Angerezi adatsogoleredwa ndi kusodza anamgumi ndi Hollanders, Zealanders, ndi Danes; amene adachokera kwa mawu ambiri omwe adakalipobe pa usodzi; ndipo chowonjezerapo, zizolowezi zawo zakale zonenepa, zokhuza kudya ndi zakumwa zambiri. Pakuti, mwachizolowezi, sitima yapamadzi ya ku England imasokoneza antchito ake; koma sizinali choncho ndi whale wa Chingerezi. Choncho, mu Chingerezi, chinthu ichi cha whaling chimwemwe sichachilendo komanso chachibadwa, koma mwangozi komanso makamaka; ndipo, chifukwa chake, ziyenera kukhala ndi chiyambi chapadera, chomwe chasonyezedwa apa, ndipo chidzafotokozedwabe.

Pakufufuza kwanga mu mbiri ya Leviathan, ndidakumana ndi voliyumu yakale yachi Dutch, yomwe, chifukwa cha fungo lake lonunkhira bwino, ndidadziwa kuti iyenera kukhala yokhudzana ndi nsomba zam'madzi. Mutuwu unali wakuti, "Dan Coopman," motero ndinaganiza kuti ichi chiyenera kukhala chikumbutso chamtengo wapatali cha munthu wina wa ku Amsterdam wogwira ntchito pa usodzi, popeza sitima yamtundu uliwonse iyenera kunyamula cooper yake. Ndinalimbikitsidwa ndi lingaliro ili powona kuti inali kupanga "Fitz Swackhammer" imodzi. Koma bwenzi langa Dr. Snodhead, mwamuna wophunzira kwambiri, profesa wa Low Dutch ndi High German pakoleji ya Santa Claus ndi St. Dr. Snodhead yemweyu, atangoona bukulo, ananditsimikizira kuti “Dan Coopman” sanali kutanthauza “The Cooper,” koma “The Merchant.” Mwachidule, buku lakale ndi lophunzira la Low Dutch lomwe limafotokoza za malonda aku Holland; ndipo, mwa nkhani zina, munali nkhani yosangalatsa kwambiri ya usodzi wake wa anamgumi. Ndipo m'mutu uno munali, mutu wakuti, "Smeer," kapena "Fat," kuti ndinapeza mndandanda wautali watsatanetsatane wa zovala zosungiramo zosungiramo katundu ndi zosungiramo za 180 za Dutch whalemen; kuchokera pamndandanda womwe, monga momwe anamasuliridwa ndi Dr. Snodhead, ndikulemba zotsatirazi:

400,000 lbs. wa ng'ombe. 60,000 lbs. Friesland nkhumba. 150,000 lbs. nsomba zamasamba. 550,000 lbs. za biscuit. 72,000 lbs. wa mkate wofewa. Mafuta 2,800 a mafuta. 20,000 lbs. Texel & Leyden tchizi. 144,000 lbs. tchizi (mwinamwake nkhani yotsika). 550 ankers aku Geneva. 10,800 migolo ya mowa.

Matebulo ambiri owerengera amakhala owuma powerenga; sizili choncho m’nkhani ino, komabe, pamene woŵerenga amasefukira ndi mapaipi athunthu, migolo, quarts, ndi gills za jini yabwino ndi chisangalalo chabwino.

Panthawiyo, ndinathera masiku atatu ndikugayitsa mowa moŵa, nyama ya ng'ombe, ndi mkate wonsewo kwa masiku atatu, pamene malingaliro ozama anaperekedwa kwa ine, okhoza kugwiritsira ntchito mopitirira muyeso ndi Plato; ndipo, kuonjezera apo, ndinapanga ma tebulo enaake, okhudza kuchuluka kwa nsomba za nsomba, ndi zina zotero, zodyedwa ndi msodzi aliyense wa Low Dutch muusodzi wamakedzana wa Greenland ndi Spitzbergen. Poyambirira, kuchuluka kwa batala, ndi Texel ndi Leyden tchizi amadya, zikuwoneka zodabwitsa. Ndikunena, komabe, ku chikhalidwe chawo chonyansa, kuchititsidwa manyazi kwambiri chifukwa cha ntchito yawo, makamaka pochita masewera awo munyanja zozizira za Polar, m'mphepete mwa dziko la Esquimaux kumene anthu omwe ali ndi moyo amalonjeza. wina ndi mzake mu bumpers mafuta sitima.

Mowa nawonso ndi waukulu kwambiri, migolo 10,800. Tsopano, monga momwe nsomba za polar zikanakhoza kuimbidwa mlandu m'chilimwe chachifupi cha nyengoyo, kotero kuti ulendo wonse wa mmodzi wa anthu otchedwa Dutch whalemen, kuphatikizapo ulendo waufupi wopita ndi kuchokera ku nyanja ya Spitzbergen, sunapitirire miyezi itatu, nenani, ndipo kuwerengera amuna 30 pagulu lililonse la zombo zawo za 180, tili ndi amalinyero a Low Dutch 5,400 onse; chifukwa chake, ndikunena, tili ndi migolo iwiri ya mowa pa munthu aliyense, kwa malipiro a masabata khumi ndi awiri, kupatula gawo lake labwino la ma ankers 550 a gin. Tsopano, kaya ma gin ndi mowa wa harpooneers, ogwedezeka monga momwe wina angaganizire kuti anali, anali amuna oyenerera oimirira pamutu wa ngalawa, ndi kukhala ndi cholinga chabwino pa zinsomba zowuluka; izi zingawoneke ngati zosatheka. Koma iwo anawalondolera iwo, nawamenya iwonso. Koma uku kunali kutali kwambiri Kumpoto, zikumbukiridwe, kumene mowa umagwirizana bwino ndi malamulo; pa Equator, m'mwezi wathu wakumwera, moŵa ukhoza kupangitsa ng'ombeyo kugona pamutu wa mast ndi mowa m'ngalawa yake; ndipo kutayika komvetsa chisoni kungathe kuchitika ku Nantucket ndi New Bedford.

Koma osatinso; zokwanira zanenedwa kusonyeza kuti Dutch whalers akale a zaka mazana awiri kapena atatu zapitazo anali ziŵindi zazikulu; ndi kuti owomba namgumi achingelezi sananyalanyaze chitsanzo chabwino kwambiri. Pakuti, amati, poyenda m'chombo chopanda kanthu, ngati simungapeze chilichonse chabwinoko kuchokera kudziko lapansi, tengani chakudya chamadzulo chabwino, osachepera. Ndipo izi zimachotsa decanter.





MUTU 102. Woponya Mphepete mwa Nkhondo.

Mpaka pano, pofotokoza mofotokozera za Umuna Whale, ine makamaka ndakhala pa zodabwitsa za mawonekedwe ake akunja; kapena payekhapayekha komanso mwatsatanetsatane pazinthu zina zingapo zamkati. Koma pakumvetsetsa kwakukulu ndi kozama za iye, ndikuyenera tsopano kumumasula iye mopitirira, ndikumasula nsonga za payipi yake, kumasula nsonga zake, ndikumasula mbedza ndi maso a mafupa amkati mwake. iye patsogolo panu m’chimaliziro chake; ndiko kunena kuti, mu mafupa ake opanda malire.

Koma bwanji tsopano, Ismayeli? Nanga bwanji, iwe, wopalasa wongopalasa msodzi, ukuyesa ngati sukudziwa za m'nyanja za m'nyanja? Kodi erudite Stubb, atakwera pamwamba pa capstan wanu, adapereka maphunziro a anatomy a Cetacea; ndipo mothandizidwa ndi mphepo, mutenge nthiti yachitsanzo kuti muwonetsere? Dzifotokozere wekha, Ishmaeli. Kodi mungakwere nangumi wachikulire kuti ayesedwe, monga momwe wophika amawotcha nkhumba yowotcha? Ayi ndithu. Iwe ndiwe mboni yowona, Ismayeli mpaka pano; koma samalani kuti mulanda ukulu wa Yona yekha; mwayi wokambilana pa ma joists ndi matabwa; ndi denga, ndi zipinjiri, ndi zogona, ndi zansi, ndi zomangira za mdulidwe wa Leviatani; ndi kufanana ndi nkhokwe, zipinda zamkaka, zophika mafuta, ndi tchizi m'matumbo ake.

Ndikuvomereza, kuti kuyambira Yona, ansomba ochepa adalowa pansi pa khungu la namgumi wamkulu; komabe, ndadalitsidwa ndi mwayi womusweka pang'ono. M'sitima yomwe ndinali nayo, kamwana kakang'ono ka Sperm Whale kamodzi kamene kanakwezedwa ndi thupi kumtunda kuti apange poko kapena thumba lake, kuti apange masiketi a nthiti za makoko, ndi mitu ya mikondo. Mukuganiza kuti ndalola mwayi umenewo kupita, osagwiritsa ntchito chipewa changa cha bwato ndi mpeni, ndikuswa chisindikizo ndikuwerenga zonse za mwana wamng'onoyo?

Ndipo ponena za chidziwitso changa chenicheni cha mafupa a leviathan mu kukula kwawo kwakukulu, kwakukulu, chifukwa cha chidziwitso chosowacho ndili ndi ngongole kwa bwenzi langa lachifumu Tranquo, mfumu ya Tranque, mmodzi wa Arsacides. Chifukwa chokhala ku Tranque, zaka zapitazo, pamene ndinalumikizidwa ku sitima yamalonda ya Dey ya Algiers, ndinaitanidwa kuti ndikakhale ndi nthawi ya tchuthi cha Arsacidean ndi mbuye wa Tranque, ku nyumba yake ya kanjedza yopuma pantchito ku Pupella; Mphepete mwa nyanja yotalikirana kwambiri ndi imene amalinyero athu ankatcha Bamboo-Town, likulu lake.

Pakati pa mikhalidwe ina yambiri yabwino, bwenzi langa lachifumu Tranquo, pokhala ndi mphatso ya chikondi chodzipereka pa nkhani zonse za vertu zankhanza, anasonkhanitsa pamodzi mu Pupella zinthu zilizonse zosoŵa zimene anthu ake akanatulukira mwanzeru; makamaka mitengo yosema ya zipangizo zodabwitsa, zigoba zonyezimira, mikondo, zopalasa zamtengo wapatali, mabwato onunkhira; ndipo zonsezo zinagawika mwa zozizwa za cilengedwe, ndi mafunde a msonkho anaponya m'mbali mwace.

Mkulu pakati pa omalizirawa anali Nangumi wamkulu wa Umuna, yemwe, pambuyo pa chiwopsezo chambiri chowopsa, adapezeka atafa ndipo ali wosokonekera, mutu wake uli pamtengo wa koko, womwe mapiko ake okhala ngati nthenga, zopindika zidawoneka ngati ndege yake yobiriwira. Thupi lalikululo litatha kuvula zopindika mozama kwambiri, ndipo mafupawo atauma padzuwa, mafupawo ananyamulidwa mosamala kupita ku Pupella glen, komwe kunali kachisi wamkulu wa kanjedza.

Nthitizo zinapachikidwa ndi zikho; fupa la msanawo linajambulidwa ndi zolemba za Arsacidean, m’zolembedwa zachilendo; m'chigaza, ansembe ankasunga lawi lonunkhira losazimitsidwa, kotero kuti mutu wachinsinsi unatulutsanso mpweya wake; pamene, ataimitsidwa panthambi, nsagwada zapansi zowopsya zinkagwedezeka pa onse odzipereka, monga lupanga lopachikidwa tsitsi lomwe linachititsa mantha kwambiri Damocles.

Zinali zochititsa chidwi kwambiri. Mtengowo unali wobiriwira ngati mosses wa Icy Glen; mitengo inaima pamwamba ndi yodzikuza, ikumva kuyamwa kwawo kwamoyo; Pansi pake nthaka yogwira ntchitoyo inali ngati nsalu yoluka nsalu, yokhala ndi kapeti yokongola kwambiri, imene nthambi za mpesawo zinkapanga ulusi wopota ndi ulusi, ndipo maluwa amoyo ankapanga zithunzizo. Mitengo yonse, ndi nthambi zake zonse zolemedwa; zitsamba zonse, ndi ferns, ndi udzu; mpweya wofalitsa uthenga; zonsezi zinali zogwira ntchito mosalekeza. Kupyolera mu kutsetsereka kwa masamba, dzuwa lalikulu linkawoneka ngati shuttle yowuluka yoluka verdure wosatopa. O, woluka wotanganidwa! Woluka nsalu wosaoneka!—Imani kaye!—mawu amodzi! nyumba yachifumu iti? chifukwa chiyani ntchito zosatha zonsezi? Lankhula iwe mmisiri, gwira dzanja lako, koma mawu amodzi ndi iwe! Ayi, ntchentche ziuluka—zithunzi ziyandama kuchokera pansalu; kapeti wothamanga wa freshet nthawi zonse amachoka. Mulungu woluka, iye amaluka; ndi kuluka kumeneko agontha, kuti asamve mau a munthu; ndi kung’ung’udza kumeneko, ifenso, amene tiyang’ana pa nsalu yoluka, timagontha; ndipo pokha pamene ife tidzauthawa ife tidzamva mawu chikwi amene amalankhula kupyolera mwa icho. Pakuti ngakhale zili choncho m'mafakitale onse zinthu. Mawu olankhulidwa omwe samveka pakati pa zopota zowuluka; mawu omwewo amamveka momveka bwino popanda makoma, kuphulika kuchokera ku mazenera otsegulidwa. Potero apezeka oyipa. Ah, munthu! pamenepo, samalani; pakuti kotero, mu kulira konseku kwa dziko lalikulu, maganizo anu ochenjera angamveke patali.

Tsopano, pakati pa matabwa obiriŵira, opanda mpumulo a mtengo wa Arsacidean umenewo, chigoba chachikulu, choyera, cholambiridwa chinali kulira—chizilombo chachikulu! Komabe, pamene ulusi wobiriŵira wolukidwa nthaŵi zonse unkasanganikirana ndi kumvekera momuzungulira, walesi wamphamvuyo anawoneka ngati mmisiri waluso; iye yekha analukidwa ndi mpesa; mwezi uliwonse kuganiza zobiriwira, zatsopano; koma iye mwini mafupa. Moyo wopindidwa Imfa; Imfa trellised Moyo; mulungu woyipa adakhala ndi Moyo wachinyamata, ndipo adamuberekera ulemerero wamutu wopindika.

Tsopano, pamene ndi Tranquo yachifumu ndinayendera chinsomba chodabwitsa ichi, ndipo ndinawona chigaza cha guwa la nsembe, ndi utsi wochita kupanga ukukwera kuchokera pamene ndege yeniyeni inatulutsa, ndinadabwa kuti mfumu iyenera kuona tchalitchi ngati chinthu cha vertu. Iye anaseka. Koma ndinadabwa kwambiri kuti ansembe ayenera kulumbira kuti jeti yake yofuka inali yowona. Ndidayenda uku ndi uku ndisanagonjetse chigoba ichi - chinaphwanya mipesa pambali - idathyola nthiti - komanso ndi mpira wa twine wa Arsacidean, woyendayenda, wodumphira pakati pa makhonde ake ambiri okhotakhota, okhala ndi mithunzi ndi mabwalo. Koma posakhalitsa mzere wanga unatha; ndikuyitsatira mmbuyo, ndidatulukira pachitseko chomwe ndidalowa. Sindinaona zamoyo mkati; panalibe koma mafupa basi.

Pondidula ndodo yobiriwira, ndinadumphiranso m'mafupa. Ansembewo atang’ambika m’chigaza chamutu, anandiona ndikukwera pamwamba pa nthiti yomaliza kuti, “Bwanji tsopano!” iwo anafuula; “Kodi muyesa mulungu wathu uyu! Ndizo zathu.” "Inde, ansembe - chabwino, mudzamupanga mpaka liti?" Koma pamenepo panabuka mkangano woopsa pakati pawo, wa mapazi ndi mainchesi; Anathyolana ma sconces wina ndi mzake ndi ndodo zawo pabwalo - chigaza chachikulu chinamvekera - ndipo nditapeza mwayi umenewo, ndinamaliza kuyesa kwanga mwamsanga.

Zoyezetsa izi tsopano ndikufuna kuziyika pamaso panu. Koma choyamba, zilembedwe, kuti, pankhani iyi, sindine womasuka kunena muyeso wamtundu uliwonse womwe ndikufuna. CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. He had lighted with such energy upon a thwart of his boat that his ivory leg had received a half-splintering shock. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
