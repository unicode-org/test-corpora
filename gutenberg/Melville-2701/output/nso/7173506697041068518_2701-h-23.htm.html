KGAOLO YA 124. Nalete.

Mesong ye e latelago lewatle leo le sa hlwago le kokobela le ile la kgokologa ka gare ga maphoto a matelele a go nanya a bontši bjo bogolo, gomme le katanela tseleng ya go tšholla ya Pequod, la mo kgoromeletša pele bjalo ka meomo ya ditšhaba tše dikgolo yeo e phatlaletšego. Moya o matla, o sa thekeselago o be o atile kudu, moo leratadima le moya di be di bonagala e le diseila tše dikgolo tša ka ntle ga mpa; lefase ka moka le ile la duma pele ga phefo. A thibetšwe ke seetša se se tletšego sa mesong, letšatši le le sa bonagalego le be le tsebja feela ka matla a go phatlalala a lefelo la gagwe; moo mahlasedi a gagwe a bayonet a ilego a tšwela pele ka mekgobo. Mekgabišo, bjalo ka ya dikgoši le dikgošigadi tša Babilona tšeo di rwešitšwego mphapahlogo, di be di buša godimo ga selo se sengwe le se sengwe. Lewatle le be le swana le sebjana sa gauta ye e qhibidišitšwego, seo se tlolago ka go bubble ka seetša le phišo.

Ka nako e telele a boloka setu sa go loiwa, Ahaba o ile a ema ka thoko; gomme nako le nako ge sekepe seo se thekeselago se be se theoga fase ka go theoga lešoba la sona la bora, o be a retologa go lebelela mahlasedi a letšatši a phadimago ao a tšweleditšwego ka pele; gomme ge a dula ka mo go tseneletšego ka morago ga sekepe, o ile a retologa ka morago, gomme a bona lefelo la letšatši la ka morago, le ka fao mahlasedi a swanago a masehla a bego a kopana le go tsoga ga gagwe mo go sa fapogego.

“Ha, ha, sekepe sa ka! o ka tšewa gabotse bjale ka baka la koloi ya lewatle ya letšatši. Ho, ho! lena ditšhaba ka moka pele ga sekepe sa ka, ke le tlišetša letšatši! Joko ka billows e eketsehileng; dumelang! a tandem, ke otlela lewatle!”

Eupša gateetee a laotšwe morago ke kgopolo ye nngwe ya go ganetša, o ile a kitimela go leba lebatong la go otlela, ka go tšhoga a nyaka gore sekepe se lebile bjang.

“Bohlabela-borwa-bohlabela, mong wa ka,” a realo mohlankedi wa go otlela yo a tšhogilego.

“O bolela maaka!” a mo otla ka seatla sa gagwe se se kgomaretšwego. “O lebile Bohlabela ka iri ye mesong, gomme letšatši le le ka morago?”

Ka se moya o mongwe le o mongwe o ile wa gakanega; ka gobane tiragalo yeo e ilego ya bonwa ka nako yeo feela ke Ahaba e be e phonyokgile ba bangwe ka moka ka mo go sa ikarabelego; eupša go kgoma ga yona mo go foufatšago kudu go swanetše go ba e bile sebaki.

A kgoromeletša hlogo ya gagwe seripagare ka gare ga binnacle, Ahaba o ile a bona dikhamphase gatee; letsogo la gagwe leo le emišitšwego le ile la wa ganyenyane-ganyenyane; ka motsotswana o ile a nyakile a bonagala a thekesela. A eme ka morago ga gagwe Starbuck o ile a lebelela, gomme bona! dikhamphase tše pedi di be di šupa Bohlabela, gomme Pequod e be e eya Bodikela ka mo go sa fošegego go swana le.

Eupša ere alamo ya mathomo ya hlaga e ka tšwa dinageng tša ka ntle gare ga sehlopha sa basepediši, mokgalabje ka setshego se se thata o ile a goeletša, “Ke na le yona! Go diregile pele. Morena Starbuck, seaduma sa bošego bja maabane se ile sa retološa dikhamphase tša rena—ke sona feela. O kile wa kwa ka taba ye bjalo, ke a e tšea.”

“Ae; eupša ga se gwa ka gwa direga pele go nna, mong wa ka,” a realo molekane yo a phadimago, a nyamile.

Mo, go swanetše go bolelwa gore dikotsi tša go swana le tše di diregile maemong a fetago a tee dikepeng ka madimo a šoro. Matla a makenete, bjalo ka ge a hlamilwe ka gare ga nalete ya mosesiši wa sekepe, bjalo ka ge bohle ba tseba, ge e le gabotse ke a tee le mohlagase wo o bonwago legodimong; ka fao ga se mo go swanetšego go makatšwa kudu, gore dilo tše bjalo di swanetše go ba bjalo. Maemo ao go ona legadima le ilego la hlaba sekepe e le ka kgonthe, e le gore le thule tše dingwe tša di- spar le di- rigging, mafelelo ao a bilego le ona godimo ga nalete ka dinako tše dingwe e sa dutše e le a bolayago kudu; bokwala bja yona ka moka bja leswika la morwalo bo fedišwa, mo e lego gore tšhipi ya makenete ya pele e be e se na mohola go feta nalete ya go loga ya mosadi wa kgale. Eupša maemong le ge e le afe, nalete ga e sa hlwa, ka boyona, e tsošološa bokwala bja mathomo bjo bo sentšwego goba bjo bo lahlegilego ka tsela yeo; gomme ge e ba dikhamphase tša binnacle di kgongwa, pheletšo e swanago e fihla go tše dingwe ka moka tšeo di ka bago di le ka sekepeng; gaešita le e be e le yeo e lego ka tlase kudu yeo e tsentšwego ka gare ga kelson.

Ka boomo a eme pele ga binnacle, gomme a lebeletše dikhamphase tše di fetotšwego, mokgalabje, ka bogale bja seatla sa gagwe se se otlolotšwego, bjale o ile a tšea go rwala mo go nepagetšego ga letšatši, gomme a kgotsofetše gore dinalete di be di fetotšwe gabotse, a goeletša ditaelo tša gagwe tša tsela ya sekepe go fetošwa go ya ka fao. Dijarata di be di le thata godimo; gomme gape Pequod e ile ya kgoromeletša bora bja yona bjo bo sa tšhogego phefong ye e ganetšanago, ka gobane yeo go thwego e lokile e be e no mo juggling.

Go sa le bjalo, go sa šetšwe gore dikgopolo tša gagwe ka noši tša sephiri e be e le dife, Starbuck ga se a ka a bolela selo, eupša ka setu o ile a ntšha ditaelo ka moka tše di nyakegago; mola Stubb le Flask—bao ka tekanyo e itšego e nyenyane ka nako yeo ba bego ba bonagala ba abelana maikwelo a gagwe—ka mo go swanago ba ile ba dumela ka ntle le go ngongorega. Ge e le banna bao, le ge ba bangwe ba bona ba be ba duma ka maemo a tlase, poifo ya bona go Ahaba e be e le ye kgolo go feta poifo ya bona ya Mafelelo. Eupša go etša pele, bašomi ba di-harpoone ba baheitene ba ile ba dula ba nyakile ba sa kgahlwe ka mo go feletšego; goba ge e ba e be e kgahlilwe, e be e le feela ka makenete a itšego ao a thuntšhitšwego dipelong tša bona tše di kgahlišago go tšwa go tša Ahaba tšeo di sa feto-fetogego.

Bakeng sa sekgoba mokgalabje o ile a sepela mokatong wa sekepe ka ditoro tše di kgokologago. Eupša ka sebaka sa go thelela ka serethe sa gagwe sa manaka a tlou, o ile a bona diphaephe tša go bona tša koporo tše di pšhatlagantšwego tša kotara yeo a bego a e-na le yona letšatšing pele ga moo di kitimela mokatong wa sekepe.

“Wena modiidi, yo a ikgantšhago yo a lebelelago legodimo le mokgweetsi wa sefofane sa letšatši! maabane ke go thubetše, gomme lehono dikhamphase di be di tla fokola di nthubetše. Ka fao, ka fao. Eupša Ahaba ke mong wa leswika la morwalo le le lekanago le bjale. Morena Starbuck—lerumo leo le se nago kota; a top-maul, le ye nnyane kudu ya dinalete tša modiri wa seila. Potlako!"

Sedirišwa, mohlomongwe, go tutuetšo yeo e laelago selo seo bjale a bego a le kgauswi le go se dira, e be e le maikemišetšo a itšego a temogo, ao morero wa ona e ka bago e bile go tsošološa meoya ya sehlopha sa gagwe ka go otla bokgoni bja gagwe bjo bo sa lemogegego, tabeng yeo e makatšago kudu go swana le ya dikhamphase tše di fetotšwego. Ka ntle le moo, mokgalabje o be a tseba gabotse gore go otlela ka dinalete tše di fetotšwego, gaešita le ge go be go kgonega ka tsela e sa kgahlišego, e be e se selo seo se swanetšego go fetišetšwa ke basesiši ba dikepe ba tumela-khwele, ka ntle le go thothomela mo gongwe le dipontšho tše mpe.

“Banna,” a realo, a retologela godimo ga sehlopha sa basepediši ka go se kgaotše, ge molekane a mo nea dilo tšeo a bego a di nyaka, “banna ba ka, seaduma se ile sa fetoša dinalete tša mokgalabje Ahaba; eupša ka tšhipi ye Ahaba a ka itirela ya gagwe, yeo e tlago go šupa e le ya therešo go swana le le ge e le efe.”

Go gadima ka dihlong ga go makala ga bokgoba go ile gwa fapantšhwa ke basesiši ba dikepe, bjalo ka ge se se be se bolelwa; gomme ka mahlo a kgahlilwego ba ile ba letela maleatlana le ge e le afe ao a bego a ka latela. Eupša Starbuck o ile a lebelela thoko.

Ka go otlwa go tšwa go top-maul Ahaba o ile a wiša hlogo ya tšhipi ya lerumo, gomme ka morago a neela molekane lepara le letelele la tšhipi leo le šetšego, a mo laela gore le le sware thwii, ntle le go kgoma mokatong wa lona. Ke moka, ka maul, ka morago ga go itia kgafetšakgafetša ntlha ya ka godimo ya molamu wo wa tšhipi, o ile a bea nalete yeo e sa bonagalego ka mafelelo godimo ga yona, gomme a otla ka hamore ka thata kudu gore, ka makga a mmalwa, molekane a sa swere molamu bjalo ka pele. Ke moka go feta ditšhišinyegong tše dingwe tše dinyenyane tše di makatšago le yona—e ka ba e sa nyakegego bakeng sa go dira gore tšhipi e be makenete, goba e fo ba e reretšwe go oketša poifo ya sehlopha sa basepediši, ga go kgonthišege—o ile a bitša tlhale ya linene; gomme a šuthelela go binnacle, a thelela ka ntle dinalete tše pedi tše di bušeditšwego morago moo, gomme ka go rapalala a fega nalete ya seila ka bogareng bja yona, godimo ga ye nngwe ya dikarata tša khamphase. Mathomong, tšhipi e be e dikologa le go dikologa, e thothomela le go thothomela mafelelong ka bobedi; eupša mafelelong e ile ya dula lefelong la yona, ge Ahaba, yo a bego a dutše a lebeletše ka kelohloko mafelelo a, a boela morago ka go se pepetletše go tšwa go binnacle, gomme a šupa letsogo la gagwe le otlolotšwego go yona, a goeletša,—“Itebeleleng, ge e ba Ahaba e se yena morena wa maemo a loadstone! Letšatši ke Bohlabela, gomme khamphase yeo e a e ikana!”

Ba ile ba lebelela ka gare ka go latelana, ka gobane ga go selo ge e se mahlo a bona ka noši seo se bego se kgona go kgodiša go hloka tsebo mo go swanago le ga bona, gomme ka go latelana ba ile ba tšholla.

Mahlong a gagwe a mollo a go nyatša le phenyo, ka morago o ile wa bona Ahaba ka boikgogomošo bja gagwe ka moka bjo bo bolayago.





KGAOLO YA 125. Log le Mola.

Le ge bjale Pequod yeo e rerilwego e be e le nako e telele gakaakaa e phaphametše leetong le, kota le mothaladi di be di dirišwa ka sewelo kudu. Ka baka la go ithekga ka kgodišego ka ditsela tše dingwe tša go laetša lefelo la sekepe, bagwebi ba bangwe le banna ba bantši ba maruarua, kudu-kudu ge ba sesa ka sekepe, ba hlokomologa ka mo go feletšego go emiša kota; le ge ka nako e swanago, gomme gantši kudu ka baka la sebopego go feta selo le ge e le sefe se sengwe, ka mehla a bea fase godimo ga letlapa la tlwaelo tsela yeo e laolwago ke sekepe, gotee le tekanyo yeo e akanyetšwago ya palogare ya tšwelopele iri e nngwe le e nngwe. E be e bile ka tsela yeo ka Pequod. Reel ya kota le kota ya sekhutlong yeo e kgomareditšwego di be di fegilwe, nako ye telele di sa kgongwa, ka fase ga seporo sa dibo tša ka morago. Dipula le go gaša di be di e kokobeditše; letšatši le phefo di be di e sothetše; dielemente ka moka di be di kopane go bola selo seo se bego se fegilwe ka go se dire selo. Eupša ka go se šetše tše ka moka, maikutlo a gagwe a ile a swara Ahaba, ge go ile gwa direga gore a gadime godimo ga reel, e sego diiri tše ntši ka morago ga lefelo la makenete, gomme a gopola ka fao kotara ya gagwe e bego e se sa le gona, gomme a gopola keno ya gagwe ya go tšhoga ka ga kota ya maemo le mothaladi. Sekepe se be se sesa ka go nwelela; ka morago maphoto a ile a kgokologa ka meferefere.

“Pele, fao! Emiša kota!”

Basesiši ba babedi ba ile ba tla. Mo-Tahiti wa mmala wa gauta le Manxman wa grizzly. “Tšea reele, yo mongwe wa lena, ke tla phagamiša.”

Ba ile ba leba ka morago ga sekepe ka mo go feteletšego, ka lehlakoreng la lee la sekepe, moo mokatong wa sekepe, ka maatla a go sekama a phefo, bjale o bego o nyakile go ina ka lewatleng la tranelate, leo le kitimelago ka thoko.

Manxman o ile a tšea reel, gomme a e swara godimo, ka mafelelo a ditshwaro a go tšwelela a spindle, yeo go dikologa yona spool ya mothaladi e bego e dikologa, ka fao e ile ya ema ka kota ya sekhutlong e fegilwe fase, go fihlela Ahaba a tšwela pele go yena.

Ahaba o ile a ema pele ga gagwe, gomme o be a lokolla gabonolo go retologa mo go ka bago masometharo goba masomenne go bopa khoile ya pele ya seatla go e lahlela ka lewatleng, ge Manxman wa kgale, yo a bego a mo lebeletše ka kelohloko bobedi le mothaladi, a dira sebete sa go bolela.

“Mohlomphegi, ga ke e tshepe; mothaladi wo o bonala o ile kgole, phišo e telele le go kolobega di o sentše.”

“‘Twill swara, mohlomphegi wa kgale. Na ba go senyeditše phišo ye telele le ye e kolobilego? O bonala o swere. Goba, therešo kudu mohlomongwe, bophelo bo a go swara; e sego wena.”

“Ke swere spool, mong wa ka. Eupša go no swana le ge molaodi wa ka a bolela. Ka meriri ye e mehwibidu ya-ka ‘tis ga e swanelwe ke go ngangišana, ‘kudu-kudu le motho yo a phagamego, yo a ka se kego a ipobola.”

“Ke eng seo? Bjale go na le moprofesara wa go ba le diphaphathi Kholetšheng ya Kgošigadi Tlhago yeo e hlomilwego ka granite; eupša methinks o ikokobeletša kudu. O belegetšwe kae?”

“Ka Sehlakahlakeng se sennyane sa maswika sa Man, mong wa ka.”

"Gabotse kudu! O otla lefase ka seo.”

“Ga ke tsebe, mong wa ka, eupša ke belegetšwe fao.”

“Ka Sehlakahlakeng sa Man, hei? Go lokile, ka tsela ye nngwe, e botse. Monna yo ke yo go tšwa go Monna; monna yo a belegwego ka Motho yo a kilego a ikemela, gomme bjale a se na motho go Motho; yeo e monywago ka gare—ke eng? Godimo ka reel! Leboto le le hwilego, le le foufetšego le thula dihlogo ka moka tše di botšišago mafelelong. Godimo le yona! Ka gona."

Kota e be e emišitšwe. Dikhoile tše di lokologilego di ile tša otlologa ka lebelo ka mothaladi o motelele wa go goga ka morago, gomme ka morago, ka yona nako yeo, reel ya thoma go dikologa. Ka lehlakoreng le lengwe, e phagamišitšwe ka go šikinyega le go theošetšwa fase ke maphoto a go kgokologa, go ganetša ga go goga ga kota go ile gwa dira gore reelman ya kgale e thekesele ka tsela ye e makatšago.

“Swara ka thata!”

Kgaola! mothaladi wo o gateletšwego ka mo go feteletšego o ile wa theoga ka festoon e tee e telele; kota ya go goga e be e se gona.

“Ke pšhatlaganya quadrant, duma e retološa dinalete, gomme bjale lewatle le le hlanyago le aroganya mothaladi wa dikota. Eupša Ahaba a ka lokiša bohle. Gogela ka mo, Motahiti; reel godimo, Manxman. Gomme lena, a mmetli a dire kota ye nngwe, a lokiše mothaladi. Le bone.”

“Ke moo o yago bjale; go yena ga go selo seo se diregilego; eupša go nna, skewer e bonala e lokologana go tšwa bogareng bja lefase. Goga ka gare, goga ka gare, Motahiti! Methaladi ye e kitima ka moka, gomme e dikologa ka ntle: e tsena e robegile, gomme e goga ka go nanya. Ha, Pip? tla go thuša; eh, Pip?”

“Pip? ke mang yo le bitšago Pip? Pip o ile a tlola go tšwa seketswaneng sa maruarua. Pip o timetše. A re boneng bjale ge eba ga se la mo rea dihlapi godimo mo, morei wa dihlapi. E goga ka thata; Ke nagana gore o swere. Mo tšhošetše, Tahiti! Mo tšhošetše; ga re goge makgopo mo. Ho! go na le letsogo la gagwe le no thuba meetse. Sethunya sa go thunya! sethunya sa go thunya! e kgaole—ga re goge makgopo mo. Mokapotene Ahaba! mong wa ka, mohlomphegi! ke Pip yo, a leka go namela gape.”

“Khutšo, wena loon ya lehlanya,” gwa goeletša Manxman, a mo swara ka letsogo. “Kgole le mokatong wa kotara!”

Ahaba o ile a ngunanguna, a tšwela pele ka gore: “Setlatla se segolo se kile sa omanya yo monyenyane.” “Matsogo a tlogile bokgethwa bjoo! O re Pip o be o le kae, mošemane?

“Ka morago kua, mong wa ka, ka morago! Bona! bona!”

“Gomme o mang, mošemane? Ga ke bone ponagatšo ya ka dipupiles tše di se nago selo tša mahlo a gago. Ao Modimo! motho yoo e swanetše go ba selo seo meoya e sa hwego e se sefego! O mang, mošemane?”

“Mošemane wa Bell, mong wa ka; sekepe-se-crier; ding, dong, ding! Pip! Pip! Pip! Dikhilograma tše lekgolo tša moputso wa letsopa bakeng sa Pip; bophagamo bja dimithara tše hlano—e bonagala e le makgopo—e tsebja ka pela kudu ke seo! Ding, dong, ding! Ke mang yo a bonego Pip lekgopo?”

“Go ka se be le dipelo ka godimo ga mothaladi wa lehlwa. Oo, lena magodimo a gatsetšego! lebelela fase mo. Le belege ngwana yo wa go hloka mahlatse, gomme le mo lahlile, lena ba tokologo ba tlholo. Mo, mošemane; Ntlo ya Ahaba e tla ba legae la Pipe go tloga bjale, mola Ahaba a sa phela. O kgoma bogare bja ka bja ka gare, mošemane; o tlemilwe go nna ka dithapo tše di logilwego ka dithapo tša ka tša pelo. Etlang, a re theogele fase.”

“Ke eng se? mo ke letlalo la shaka ya velvet,” a lebelela seatla sa Ahaba ka kelohloko, gomme a se kwa. “Ah, bjale, o be a na le Pip wa modiidi eupša a ikwa a le selo se botho kudu bjalo ka se, mohlomongwe o be a se a ka a lahlega! Se se bonala go nna, mong wa ka, bjalo ka thapo ya motho; selo seo meoya e fokolago e ka se swarago. Oo, mong wa ka, a Perth ya kgale bjale e tle gomme a rivet diatla tše tše pedi mmogo; yo motsothwa le yo mošweu, ka gobane nka se tlogele se se sepela.”

“Oo, mošemane, le nna nka se wena, ntle le ge ka go realo nka go gogela go dilo tše di tšhošago tše mpe go feta tše di lego mo. Etla, ka gona, ka khabineng ya ka. Bona! lena badumedi ba medimo botse ka moka, le batho ba ba babjago ka moka, lona! bona medimo e tsebago tšohle e sa hlokomele motho yo a tlaišegago; le motho, le ge e le setlatla, gomme a sa tsebe seo a se dirago, eupša a tletše ka dilo tše bose tša lerato le tebogo. Tla! Ke ikwa ke ikgantšha kudu ka go go eta pele ka seatla sa gago se se ntsho, go feta le ge ke swere sa Mmušiši!”

“Go ya tše pedi tša go se loke bjale,” gwa ngunanguna Manxman wa kgale. “Yo mongwe o daft ka matla, yo mongwe o daft ka bofokodi. Eupša mo ke mafelelo a mothaladi wo o bodilego—ka moka di rotha, le tšona. E lokiše, eh? Ke nagana gore go be go kaone gore re be le mothaladi o mofsa ka mo go feletšego. Ke tla bona Mna Stubb ka yona.”





KGAOLO YA 126. Bophelo-Buoy.

Go otlela bjale ka borwa-bohlabela ka tšhipi ya Ahaba yeo e lekaleditšwego, gomme tšwelopele ya gagwe e laolwa feela ke kota le mothaladi wa Ahaba wa go lekalekana; Pequod e ile ya swara tseleng ya yona go leba Equator. Go dira tsela ye telele gakaakaa ka meetseng a bjalo ao a sa tlwaelegago, go sa hlaloše dikepe, gomme ere e telele, ka thoko e tutuetšwa ke diphefo tše di sa fetogego tša kgwebo, godimo ga maphoto a boleta ka mo go sa fetogego; tše ka moka di be di bonagala e le dilo tše di makatšago tše di ikemetšego tšeo di bego di etelela pele pono e itšego ya meferefere le e itlhobogilego.

Mafelelong, ge sekepe se be se batamela ka ntle ga ka ntle, bjalo ka ge eka ke lefelo la go rea dihlapi la Equatorial, gomme lefsifsing le le tseneletšego leo le yago pele ga mahube, se be se sesa ke sehlopha sa dihlakahlaka tša maswika; sešupanako—ka nako yeo se bego se eteletšwe pele ke Flask—se ile sa tšhošwa ke sello se se hlagago kudu le seo e sego sa lefaseng—go swana le dillo tšeo di hlalositšwego ka seripa-gare tša dipoko tša Batho ka moka bao ba se nago molato bao ba bolailwego ke Heroda—moo e lego gore o tee le ka moka, ba thomile go tšwa ditoropong tša bona, le bakeng sa sebaka sa ba bangwe metsotso e ile ya ema, goba ya dula, goba ya ithekga ka moka ba theeleditše ka go fetofetoga, go swana le lekgoba la Roma leo le betlilwego, mola sello seo sa hlaga se be se dula ka gare ga go kwa. Karolo ya Bokriste goba ye e hlabologilego ya sehlopha sa basepediši e boletše gore ke di-mermaid, gomme ya thothomela; eupša bašomi ba di-harpoone ba baheitene ba ile ba dula ba sa tšhoge. But by her still halting course and winding, woeful way, you plainly saw that this ship that so wept with spray, still remained without comfort. She was Rachel, weeping for her children, because they were not.

CHAPTER 130. The Hat.

And now that at the proper time and place, after so long and wide a preliminary cruise, Ahab,—all other whaling waters swept—seemed to have chased his foe into an ocean-fold, to slay him the more securely there; now, that he found himself hard by the very latitude and longitude where his tormenting wound had been inflicted; now that a vessel had been spoken which on the very day preceding had actually encountered Moby Dick;—and now that all his successive meetings with various ships contrastingly concurred to show the demoniac indifference with which the white whale tore his hunters, whether sinning or sinned against; now it was that there lurked a something in the old man's eyes, which it was hardly sufferable for feeble souls to see. As the unsetting polar star, which through the livelong, arctic, six months' night sustains its piercing, steady, central gaze; so Ahab's purpose now fixedly gleamed down upon the constant midnight of the gloomy crew. It domineered above them so, that all their bodings, doubts, misgivings, fears, were fain to hide beneath their souls, and not sprout forth a single spear or leaf.

In this foreshadowing interval too, all humor, forced or natural, vanished. Stubb no more strove to raise a smile; Starbuck no more strove to check one. Alike, joy and sorrow, hope and fear, seemed ground to finest dust, and powdered, for the time, in the clamped mortar of Ahab's iron soul. Like machines, they dumbly moved about the deck, ever conscious that the old man's despot eye was on them.

But did you deeply scan him in his more secret confidential hours; when he thought no glance but one was on him; then you would have seen that even as Ahab's eyes so awed the crew's, the inscrutable Parsee's glance awed his; or somehow, at least, in some wild way, at times affected it. Such an added, gliding strangeness began to invest the thin Fedallah now; such ceaseless shudderings shook him; that the men looked dubious at him; half uncertain, as it seemed, whether indeed he were a mortal substance, or else a tremulous shadow cast upon the deck by some unseen being's body. And that shadow was always hovering there. For not by night, even, had Fedallah ever certainly been known to slumber, or go below. He would stand still for hours: but never sat or leaned; his wan but wondrous eyes did plainly say—We two watchmen never rest.

Nor, at any time, by night or day could the mariners now step upon the deck, unless Ahab was before them; either standing in his pivot-hole, or exactly pacing the planks between two undeviating limits,—the main-mast and the mizen; or else they saw him standing in the cabin-scuttle,—his living foot advanced upon the deck, as if to step; his hat slouched heavily over his eyes; so that however motionless he stood, however the days and nights were added on, that he had not swung in his hammock; yet hidden beneath that slouching hat, they could never tell unerringly whether, for all this, his eyes were really closed at times; or whether he was still intently scanning them; no matter, though he stood so in the scuttle for a whole hour on the stretch, and the unheeded night-damp gathered in beads of dew upon that stone-carved coat and hat. The clothes that the night had wet, the next day's sunshine dried upon him; and so, day after day, and night after night; he went no more beneath the planks; whatever he wanted from the cabin that thing he sent for.

He ate in the same open air; that is, his two only meals,—breakfast and dinner: supper he never touched; nor reaped his beard; which darkly grew all gnarled, as unearthed roots of trees blown over, which still grow idly on at naked base, though perished in the upper verdure. But though his whole life was now become one watch on deck; and though the Parsee's mystic watch was without intermission as his own; yet these two never seemed to speak—one man to the other—unless at long intervals some passing unmomentous matter made it necessary. Though such a potent spell seemed secretly to join the twain; openly, and to the awe-struck crew, they seemed pole-like asunder. If by day they chanced to speak one word; by night, dumb men were both, so far as concerned the slightest verbal interchange. At times, for longest hours, without a single hail, they stood far parted in the starlight; Ahab in his scuttle, the Parsee by the mainmast; but still fixedly gazing upon each other; as if in the Parsee Ahab saw his forethrown shadow, in Ahab the Parsee his abandoned substance.

And yet, somehow, did Ahab—in his own proper self, as daily, hourly, and every instant, commandingly revealed to his subordinates,—Ahab seemed an independent lord; the Parsee but his slave. Still again both seemed yoked together, and an unseen tyrant driving them; the lean shade siding the solid rib. For be this Parsee what he may, all rib and keel was solid Ahab.

At the first faintest glimmering of the dawn, his iron voice was heard from aft,—“Man the mast-heads!”—and all through the day, till after sunset and after twilight, the same voice every hour, at the striking of the helmsman's bell, was heard—“What d'ye see?—sharp! sharp!”

But when three or four days had slided by, after meeting the children-seeking Rachel; and no spout had yet been seen; the monomaniac old man seemed distrustful of his crew's fidelity; at least, of nearly all except the Pagan harpooneers; he seemed to doubt, even, whether Stubb and Flask might not willingly overlook the sight he sought. But if these suspicions were really his, he sagaciously refrained from verbally expressing them, however his actions might seem to hint them.

“I will have the first sight of the whale myself,”—he said. “Aye! Ahab must have the doubloon!” and with his own hands he rigged a nest of basketed bowlines; and sending a hand aloft, with a single sheaved block, to secure to the main-mast head, he received the two ends of the downward-reeved rope; and attaching one to his basket prepared a pin for the other end, in order to fasten it at the rail. This done, with that end yet in his hand and standing beside the pin, he looked round upon his crew, sweeping from one to the other; pausing his glance long upon Daggoo, Queequeg, Tashtego; but shunning Fedallah; and then settling his firm relying eye upon the chief mate, said,—“Take the rope, sir—I give it into thy hands, Starbuck.” Then arranging his person in the basket, he gave the word for them to hoist him to his perch, Starbuck being the one who secured the rope at last; and afterwards stood near it. And thus, with one hand clinging round the royal mast, Ahab gazed abroad upon the sea for miles and miles,—ahead, astern, this side, and that,—within the wide expanded circle commanded at so great a height.

When in working with his hands at some lofty almost isolated place in the rigging, which chances to afford no foothold, the sailor at sea is hoisted up to that spot, and sustained there by the rope; under these circumstances, its fastened end on deck is always given in strict charge to some one man who has the special watch of it. Because in such a wilderness of running rigging, whose various different relations aloft cannot always be infallibly discerned by what is seen of them at the deck; and when the deck-ends of these ropes are being every few minutes cast down from the fastenings, it would be but a natural fatality, if, unprovided with a constant watchman, the hoisted sailor should by some carelessness of the crew be cast adrift and fall all swooping to the sea. So Ahab's proceedings in this matter were not unusual; the only strange thing about them seemed to be, that Starbuck, almost the one only man who had ever ventured to oppose him with anything in the slightest degree approaching to decision—one of those too, whose faithfulness on the look-out he had seemed to doubt somewhat;—it was strange, that this was the very man he should select for his watchman; freely giving his whole life into such an otherwise distrusted person's hands.

Now, the first time Ahab was perched aloft; ere he had been there ten minutes; one of those red-billed savage sea-hawks which so often fly incommodiously close round the manned mast-heads of whalemen in these latitudes; one of these birds came wheeling and screaming round his head in a maze of untrackably swift circlings. Then it darted a thousand feet straight up into the air; then spiralized downwards, and went eddying again round his head.

But with his gaze fixed upon the dim and distant horizon, Ahab seemed not to mark this wild bird; nor, indeed, would any one else have marked it much, it being no uncommon circumstance; only now almost the least heedful eye seemed to see some sort of cunning meaning in almost every sight.

“Your hat, your hat, sir!” suddenly cried the Sicilian seaman, who being posted at the mizen-mast-head, stood directly behind Ahab, though somewhat lower than his level, and with a deep gulf of air dividing them.

But already the sable wing was before the old man's eyes; the long hooked bill at his head: with a scream, the black hawk darted away with his prize.

An eagle flew thrice round Tarquin's head, removing his cap to replace it, and thereupon Tanaquil, his wife, declared that Tarquin would be king of Rome. But only by the replacing of the cap was that omen accounted good. Ahab's hat was never restored; the wild hawk flew on and on with it; far in advance of the prow: and at last disappeared; while from the point of that disappearance, a minute black spot was dimly discerned, falling from that vast height into the sea.
