KGAOLO YA 89. Hlapi ya go itima dijo le Dihlapi tše di lokologilego.

Tšhupetšo ya waif le waif-poles kgaolong ya mafelelo eupša e tee, e nyaka pego ye nngwe ya melao le melawana ya go rea dihlapi tša maruarua, yeo waif e ka tšewago bjalo ka leswao le legolo le betšhe.

Gantši go direga gore ge dikepe tše mmalwa di sepela ka sekepe, leruarua le ka thulwa ke sekepe se tee, ke moka la tšhaba, gomme mafelelong la bolawa gomme la thopša ke sekepe se sengwe; gomme mo go bopilwe ka mo go sa lebanyego ditiragalo tše dintši tše dinyenyane tše di sa letelwago, ka moka di tšea karolo go seka se se tee se segolo. Ka mohlala,—ka morago ga go kitimišwa le go thopša ga leruarua ka mo go lapilego le mo go lego kotsi, setopo se ka lokologana sekepeng ka baka la ledimo le šoro; gomme e gogolega kgole go ya leeward, e tšewe gape ke mohlapi wa bobedi wa maruarua, yo, ka go iketla, ka go snugly a e gogago ka thoko, ntle le kotsi ya bophelo goba mothaladi. Ka go rialo gantši dingangišano tše di tshwenyago kudu le tše šoro di be di tla tšwelela magareng ga barei ba dihlapi, ge nkabe go se na molao o itšego wo o ngwadilwego goba wo o sa ngwalwago, wa legohle, wo o sa ganetšwego wo o šomago melatong ka moka.

Mohlomongwe molao o nnoši wa semmušo wa go swara maruarua wo o dumeletšwego ke molao wa molao, e be e le wa Holland. E ile ya laelwa ke States-General ka AD 1695. Eupša le ge go se na setšhaba se sengwe seo se kilego sa ba le molao le ge e le ofe wo o ngwadilwego wa go swara maruarua, le ge go le bjalo barei ba dihlapi ba Amerika e bile baphethi ba bona ba melao le boramolao tabeng ye. Ba file tshepedišo yeo ka go akaretša ka boripana e fetago Justinian’s Pandects le Melawana ya Ka fasana ya Mokgatlo wa China wa go Gatelela go Tsenya Kgwebo ya Batho ba bangwe. Ee; melao ye e ka betlilwe godimo ga farthing ya Mohumagadi Anne, goba barb ya harpoon, gomme ya aparwa molaleng, ke ye nnyane kudu.

I. A Fast-Fish ke ya mokga fast go yona.

II. A Loose-Fish ke papadi ye e lokilego go mang le mang yo a ka e swarago ka pela.

Eupša seo se bapalago bošoro ka molao wo wa bokgoni ke go ba ga wona mo go kgahlišago, mo go nyakago bophagamo bjo bogolo bja ditlhaloso bakeng sa go o hlalosa.

Sa pele: Hlapi ya go itima dijo ke eng? Hlapi yeo e phelago goba e hwile e na le lebelo ka tsela ya setegeniki, ge e kgokagantšwe le sekepe goba seketswana seo se dutšwego, ka sedirišwa le ge e le sefe seo se laolwago le gatee ke moagi goba bao ba dulago go sona,—maste, sekepe sa go sesa, thapo ya disenthimithara tše senyane, terata ya thelekramo goba legare la cobweb, ka moka di a swana. Ka mo go swanago hlapi e na le lebelo ka setegeniki ge e rwele waif, goba seka se sengwe le se sengwe seo se lemogwago sa go ba le selo; ge feela mokgatlo wo o e tlogelago o bontšha gabotse bokgoni bja bona ka nako le ge e le efe ya go e tšea gotee, gotee le maikemišetšo a bona a go dira bjalo.

Tše ke ditlhaloso tša thutamahlale; eupša ditlhaloso tša banna ba maruarua ka bobona ka dinako tše dingwe di bopilwe ka mantšu a thata le go kokota mo go thata kudu—Coke-upon-Littleton ya seatla. Ke therešo gore gare ga banna ba maruarua bao ba lokilego kudu le bao ba hlomphegago ka mehla dikabo di dirwa bakeng sa melato e sa tlwaelegago, moo e tla bago go hloka toka mo go šiišago ga boitshwaro gore mokgatlo o mongwe o bolele gore o na le maruarua ao a kilego a kitimišwa goba a bolailwe ke mokgatlo o mongwe. Eupša ba bangwe ga se ba hlokolosi gakaakaa le gatee.

Nywageng e ka bago e masomehlano e fetilego go bile le molato o makatšago wa go rata maruarua wo o ilego wa sekišwa kua Engelane, moo basekiši ba ilego ba hlagiša seo ka morago ga go kitimiša maruarua ka thata mawatleng a Leboa; le ge ka nnete bona (baseki) ba atlegile go swara dihlapi ka harpoon; mafelelong, ka kotsi ya maphelo a bona, ba ile ba gapeletšega go tlogela e sego feela methaladi ya bona, eupša sekepe sa bona ka bosona. Mafelelong basekišwa (bašomi ba sekepe se sengwe) ba ile ba tla ka leruarua, ba otla, ba bolaya, ba le thopa gomme mafelelong ba le tšea pele ga mahlo a basekiši. Gomme ge basekišwa bao ba be ba ganetšwa le, molaodi wa bona o ile a kgaola menwana ya gagwe ka meno a basekiši, gomme a ba kgonthišetša gore ka tsela ya doxology go lengwalo leo a le dirilego, bjale o be a tla boloka mothaladi wa bona, diharpoon, le sekepe, tšeo di bego di dutše di kgomaretše go leruarua ka nako ya go thopša. Ka fao bjale basekiši ba ile ba sekiša bakeng sa go hwetša boleng bja maruarua a bona, mothaladi, diharpoon le sekepe.

Morena Erskine e be e le ramolao wa basekišwa; Morena Ellenborough e be e le moahlodi. Ge a dutše a itšhireletša, Erskine yo a nago le bokgoni bja go hlalefetša o ile a tšwela pele go bontšha boemo bja gagwe, ka go šupa bosenyi bja morago bjale. con. taba, yeo go yona mohlomphegi, ka morago ga go leka lefeela go laola sehlogo sa mosadi wa gagwe, mafelelong a mo lahlile mawatleng a bophelo; eupša ge nywaga e dutše e e-ya, a sokologa mogatong woo, o ile a thoma tiro ya go mo hwetša gape. Erskine o be a le ka lehlakoreng le lengwe; gomme ka morago a e thekga ka go bolela, gore le ge mohlomphegi mathomong a be a swere mohumagadi ka harpoon, gomme a kile a mo ikona dijo, gomme fela ka lebaka la kgateletšego ye kgolo ya bošoro bja gagwe bjo bo nwelelago, mafelelong o be a mo lahlile; le ge go le bjalo a mo lahla o ile a dira bjalo, mo e lego gore a fetoga hlapi ya go lokologana; gomme ka fao ge mohlomphegi yo a ilego a mo latela a mo swara gape ka dithapo, mohumagadi ka morago o ile a ba thoto ya mohlomphegi yoo yo a ilego a latela, gotee le harpoon efe goba efe yeo e ka bago e hweditšwe e kgomaretše ka go yena.

Bjale tabeng ya gona bjale Erskine o ile a phegelela gore mehlala ya leruarua le mohumagadi e be e bontšhana ka go fetolana.

Dikgopelo tše, le dikgopelo tša go ganetša, ge di theeleditšwe ka tshwanelo, moahlodi yo a rutegilego kudu ka dipeelano tše di beilwego o ile a tšea sephetho, go bolela,—Gore ge e le sekepe, o ile a se nea basekiši, ka gobane ba be ba fo se tlogela gore ba phološe maphelo a bona; eupša gore mabapi le maruarua ao go ngangišanwago ka wona, di-harpoon le mothaladi, e be e le tša basekišwa; leruarua, ka gobane e be e le Loose-Fish ka nako ya go thopša ga mafelelo; le diharpoon le mothaladi ka gobane ge hlapi e be e tloga le tšona, yona (hlapi) e ile ya hwetša thoto ka gare ga dihlogo tšeo; gomme ka fao mang le mang yo ka morago a ilego a tšea dihlapi o be a na le tokelo go tšona. Bjale basekišwa ka morago ba ile ba tšea dihlapi; ergo, dihlogo tšeo di boletšwego ka mo godimo e be e le tša bona.

Monna yo a tlwaelegilego ge a lebelela phetho ye ya Moahlodi yo a rutegilego kudu, a ka kgona go e ganetša. Eupša e lemetšwe go fihla leswikeng la mathomo la taba ye, melawana ye mebedi ye megolo yeo e beilwego ka melaong ya mafahla ya go rema maruarua yeo e tsopotšwego peleng, gomme ya dirišwa le go hlathollwa ke Morena Ellenborough molatong wo o tsopotšwego ka mo godimo; melao ye mebedi yeo e kgomago Fast-Fish le Loose-Fish, ke re, ge go naganišišwa, e tla hwetšwa e le metheo ya thuto ka moka ya molao ya batho; ka gobane go sa šetšwe go latišiša ga yona mo go raraganego ga diswantšho tše di betlilwego, Tempele ya Molao, go swana le Tempele ya Bafilisita, e na le dilo tše pedi feela tšeo di ka emago go tšona.

Na ga se polelo yeo e lego ka ganong ga motho yo mongwe le yo mongwe ya rego: Go ba le selo ke seripa sa molao: ke gore, go sa šetšwe gore selo seo se thomile bjang? Eupša gantši go ba le dilo ke molao ka moka. Ke mešifa efe le meoya ya makgoba a Russia le makgoba a Repabliki ge e se Fast-Fish, yeo go yona e lego molao ka moka? Ke eng go mong wa ntlo yo a hlaselago e lego tšhošane ya mafelelo ya mohlologadi eupša e le Fast-Fish? Seo se lego kua ntlo e kgolo ya mmabole ya mobe yeo e sa lemogwego yeo e nago le poleiti ya mojako bakeng sa waif; seo ke eng ge e se Fast-Fish? Ke phokoletšo efe ye e senyago yeo Morodekai, mogwebi wa dithoto, a e hwetšago go Woebegone wa modiidi, yo a phuhlamego, ka kadimo ya go boloka lapa la Woebegone gore le se bolawe ke tlala; ke eng theolelo yeo e senyago ge e se Fast-Fish? Ke letseno lefe la Mopišopo-mogolo wa Savesoul la £ 100,000 yeo e thopilwego go tšwa go borotho bjo bonyenyane le tšhese ya bašomi ba dikete tše makgolo bao ba robegilego thekgo (ka moka ba kgonthišegile ka legodimo ntle le thušo le ge e le efe ya Savesoul) ke eng £100,000 yeo ya nkgokolo eupša e le Fast-Fish? Ke metse efe ya leabela ya Duke of Dunder le metsana ya Fast-Fish? Ke eng go mohlapi yoo wa harpoone yo a belaetšago, John Bull, Ireland yo a diilago, eupša ke Fast-Fish? Go thwe’ng go mohlapi yoo wa moapostola, Ngwanabo rena Jonathan, ke Texas eupša ke Fast-Fish? Gomme mabapi le tše ka moka, na go ba ga se molao ka moka?

Eupša ge e ba thuto ya Fast-Fish e šoma ka kakaretšo kudu, thuto ya leloko ya Loose-Fish e sa dutše e le bjalo ka mo go apareditšego kudu. Seo se šoma boditšhabatšhaba le lefaseng ka bophara.

Amerika e be e le eng ka 1492 ge e se Loose-Fish, yeo go yona Columbus a ilego a otla tekanyetšo ya Sepania ka tsela ya go e tlogela bakeng sa mong wa gagwe wa bogoši le mong wa gagwe wa mosadi? Poland e be e le eng go Czar? Ke eng Gerika go Turk? Ke eng India go ya Engelane? Mafelelong Mexico e tla ba eng go United States? Dihlapi ka moka tše di lokologilego.

Ditokelo tša Motho le Ditokologo tša Lefase ke dife ge e se Dihlapi tše di lokologilego? Ke eng menagano le dikgopolo tša banna ka moka ge e se Loose-Fish? Molao wa motheo wa tumelo ya bodumedi go bona ke ofe ge e se Hlapi e Lokologilego? Ke eng go bo-radipolelo ba go ikgantšha ba go sepetša dilo ka sephiring ke dikgopolo tša batho bao ba naganago eupša e le tša Loose-Fish? Lefase le legolo ka bolona ke eng ge e se Hlapi ye e Lokologilego? Gomme o eng, mmadi, ge e se Hlapi ye e Lokologilego le Hlapi ya ka Potlako, le wena?





KGAOLO YA 90. Dihlogo goba Mesela.

“De balena vero e lekane, si rex habeat caput, et regina caudam.” B r a c t o n , l . 3, c. 3. 3. .

Selatine go tšwa dipukung tša Melao ya Engelane, yeo e tšerwego mmogo le seemo, e ra gore, ya maruarua ka moka ao a thopilwego ke mang le mang lebopong la naga yeo, Kgoši, bjalo ka Honorary Grand Harpooneer, e swanetše go ba le hlogo, gomme Kgošigadi e be ka tlhompho hlahiswa le mosela. Karoganyo yeo, ka go leruarua, e swanago kudu le go fokotša apole ka seripagare; ga go na mašaledi a magareng. Bjale ka ge molao wo, ka fase ga sebopego se se fetotšwego, go fihla le lehono o šoma kua Engelane; gomme ka ge e nea ka ditsela tše di fapa-fapanego go se swane mo go makatšago mo go kgomago molao wa kakaretšo wa Fast and Loose-Fish, mo e swarwa ka yona kgaolong e arogilego, molaong o swanago wa molao wa maitshwaro wo o hlohleletšago ditimela tša Seisemane go ba ka ditshenyagalelo tša koloi e arogilego , yeo e beetšwego ka mo go kgethegilego bakeng sa madulo a bogoši. Sa mathomo, ka bohlatse bja go rata go tseba bja taba ya gore molao wo o boletšwego ka mo godimo o sa šoma, ke tšwela pele go bea pele ga lena maemo ao a diregilego mo mengwageng ye mebedi ya go feta.

Go bonagala gore basesiši ba bangwe ba dikepe ba botegago ba Dover, goba Sandwich, goba yo mongwe wa Maemakepe a Cinque, ka morago ga go kitimiša ka thata ba atlegile go bolaya le go bea lebopong la lewatle leruarua le lebotse leo mathomong ba le hlalositšego kgole le lebopo. Bjale Maemakepe a Cinque a karolo goba ka tsela ye nngwe a ka fase ga taolo ya mohuta wa lephodisa goba beadle, yeo e bitšwago Lord Warden. Go swara ofisi ka go lebanya go tšwa mphapahlogong, ke a dumela, tiragalo ka moka ya di-emoluments tša bogoši go ya mafelong a Boemakepe bja Cinque e ba ka kabelo ya gagwe. Ke bangwadi ba bangwe ofisi ye e bitšwa sinecure. Eupša ga go bjalo. Ka gobane Lord Warden o thwetšwe ka go swarega ka dinako tše dingwe go fobbing di-perquisite tša gagwe; tšeo e lego tša gagwe kudu-kudu ka baka la yona fobbing yeo ya tšona.

Bjale ge basesiši ba ba diilago bao ba fišitšwego ke letšatši, ba sa rwala dieta, gomme ba kgokologetše marokgo a bona godimo maotong a bona a eely, ba be ba gogile dihlapi tša bona tše di nonnego ka go lapa godimo le go oma, ba ikholofetša £150 ye botse go tšwa go oli ya bohlokwa le lerapong; gomme ka boikgopolelo ba nwa teye ya sewelo le basadi ba bona, le ale ye botse le bagwera ba bona, ka maatla a dikarolo tša bona ka go latelana; godimo ga dikgato mohlomphegi yo a rutegilego kudu le wa Mokriste kudu le yo a nago le botho, ka khopi ya Blackstone ka fase ga letsogo la gagwe; gomme a e bea hlogong ya leruarua, o re—“Matsogo a tloge! hlapi ye, beng ba ka, ke Hlapi ya go itima dijo. Ke e thopa bjalo ka ya Morena Mohlapetši.” Godimo ga se basesiši ba dikepe ba diilago ka go tšhoga ga bona ka tlhompho—ka gona e le ka kgonthe ba Seisemane—ba sa tsebe seo ba ka se bolelago, ba wela go ngwatha dihlogo tša bona ka matla go dikologa; go sa le bjalo a gadima ka manyami go tšwa go leruarua go ya go motho yo a sa mo tsebego. Eupša seo ga se sa lokiša taba yeo ka tsela le ge e le efe, goba go le gatee sa nolofatša pelo e thata ya mohlomphegi yo a rutegilego ka kopi ya Blackstone. Mafelelong yo mongwe wa bona, ka morago ga go ngwatha ka nako e telele bakeng sa dikgopolo tša gagwe, o ile a dira gore a be le sebete sa go bolela, .

“Hle, mohlomphegi, Morena Mohlapetši ke mang?”

“Mokgoma.”

“Eupša kgošana o be a se na selo le ge e le sefe seo a se dirago ka go tšea hlapi ye?”

“Ke ya gagwe.”

“Re bile mathateng a magolo, le kotsing, le ditshenyagalelo tše dingwe, gomme ke tšeo ka moka go ya go hola Duke; re sa hwetše selo le gatee bakeng sa dihlabi tša rena ge e se makhopho a rena?”

“Ke ya gagwe.”

“Na Duke o diila kudu moo a ka gapeletšwago go dira mokgwa wo o itlhobogilego wa go hwetša mošomo wa boiphedišo?”

“Ke ya gagwe.”

“Ke ile ka nagana go imolla mma wa-ka wa kgale yo a robetšego malaong ka karolo ya kabelo ya-ka ya leruarua le.”

“Ke ya gagwe.”

“Na Duke a ka se kgotsofale ka kotara goba seripa?”

“Ke ya gagwe.”

Ka lentšu le tee, leruarua le ile la thopša gomme la rekišwa, gomme Mogau wa gagwe Duke wa Wellington o ile a amogela tšhelete yeo. Ka go nagana gore ge go lebelelwa ka mabone a mangwe a itšego, molato wo ka kgonagalo ye e se nago selo ka tekanyo e itšego e nyenyane o ka lebelelwa, ka tlase ga maemo, e le o thata kudu, moruti yo a botegago wa toropo ka tlhompho o ile a lebiša lengwalo go Mogau wa gagwe, a mo kgopela gore a tšee taba ya bao basesiši ba dikepe ba madimabe go elwa hloko ka botlalo. Go seo Morena wa ka Duke ka nnete a ilego a araba (mangwalo ka bobedi a gatišitšwe) gore o šetše a dirile bjalo, gomme o amogetše tšhelete, gomme o tla gapeletšega go mohlomphegi yo a hlomphegago ge e ba ka bokamoso yena (mohlomphegi yo a hlomphegago) a be a tla gana go tsena ditaba gare le tša batho ba bangwe kgwebo. Na yo ke mokgalabje yo a sa dutšego a le mohlabani, yo a emego dikhutlong tša mebušo ye meraro, ka diatla ka moka a gapeletša dimpho tša ditšhelete tša bakgopedi?

Go tla bonwa gabonolo gore tabeng ye tokelo yeo go thwego e na le yona ya Duke go leruarua e be e le yeo e abetšwego go tšwa go Mmuši. Re swanetše go nyaka go botšiša ka nako yeo gore ke molao wa motheo ofe wo Mmuši a beeleditšwego mathomong ka tokelo yeo. Molao ka bowona o šetše o hlagišitšwe. Eupša Plowdon o re fa lebaka la yona. Plowdon o re, leruarua leo le swerwego gakaakaa ke la Kgoši le Kgošigadi, “ka baka la bokgoni bja lona bjo bo phagamego.” Le gona ke bahlalosi ba kwagalago kudu se se kile sa swarwa e le ngangišano e kgodišago ditabeng tše bjalo.

Eupša ke ka baka la’ng Kgoši e swanetše go ba le hlogo, gomme Kgošigadi e be le mosela? Lebaka la seo, lena boramolao!

Lengwalong la gagwe la go bolela ka “Queen-Gold,” goba Queen-pinmoney, mongwadi wa kgale wa King’s Bench, William Prynne yo mongwe, o bolela ka tsela yeo: “Lena mosela ke wa Kgošigadi, gore lena oteropo ya Kgošigadi le tlamelwe le lerapo la maruarua.” Bjale se se ngwadilwe nakong ya ge lerapo le lentsho la limber la Greenland goba Right whale le be le dirišwa kudu ka gare ga di- bodice tša mafumahadi. Eupša lona lerapo le ga le moseleng; ke hlogong, e lego phošo e nyamišago go ramolao yo a nago le bohlale bjalo ka Prynne. Eupša na Kgošigadi ke mermaid, yeo e swanetšego go tšweletšwa ka mosela? Tlhaloso ya tshwantšhišo e ka lalela mo.

Go na le dihlapi tše pedi tša bogoši tšeo di hlamilwego ka tsela yeo ke bangwadi ba molao ba Maisemane—leruarua le sturgeon; bobedi thoto ya bogoši ka fase ga mellwane ye e itšego, le ka leina go aba lekala la lesome la letseno le le tlwaelegilego la mphapahlogo. Ga ke tsebe gore mongwadi ofe goba ofe yo mongwe yo a šišinyago taba ye; eupša ka phetho go bonagala go nna gore sturgeon e swanetše go arolwa ka tsela e swanago le ya leruarua, Kgoši e amogela hlogo e kitlanego kudu le e elastic yeo e kgethegilego go hlapi yeo, yeo ge e lebelelwa ka tsela ya seswantšhetšo, e ka bago e theilwe ka metlae godimo ga go amogelwa mo gongwe mo go akanyetšwago. Gomme ka go realo go bonala go na le lebaka dilong ka moka, gaešita le molaong.





KGAOLO YA 91. Pequod e Kopana le Rose-Bud.

“Ka lefeela e be e le go rake bakeng sa Ambergriese ka paunch ya Leviathan ye, fetor e sa kgotlelelegego e gana go se nyakišiše.” Mohlomphegi T. Browne, V. E.

E bile beke goba tše pedi ka morago ga ge pono ya mafelelo ya go tsoma maruarua e anegetšwe, gomme ge re be re sesa ganyenyane-ganyenyane godimo ga lewatle leo le robetšego, leo le nago le mouwane, la gare ga letšatši, moo dinko tše dintši tšeo di bego di le mokatong wa sekepe sa Pequod di ilego tša ipontšha e le dilo tše di phafogilego kudu tšeo di utolotšego go feta dipara tše tharo tša mahlo ao a lego ka godimo. Monkgo o makatšago le wo o sa kgahlišego kudu o ile wa nkga ka lewatleng.

Stubb o itše: “Ke tla petšha selo se itšego bjale gore felotsoko mo go na le tše dingwe tša maruarua ao a dirišitšwego dihlare-tagi tšeo re di kitimišitšego letšatšing le lengwe. Ke be ke nagana gore ba tla kelela godimo go se go ye kae.”

Ga bjale, mouwane e sa le pele o ile wa thelela ka thoko; gomme moo kgojana go be go robetše sekepe, seo diseila tša sona tše di kobegilego di bego di bontšha gore mohuta o itšego wa maruarua o swanetše go ba o le ka thoko. Ge re dutše re thelela kgaufsi, motho yo a sa mo tsebego o ile a bontšha mebala ya Sefora go tšwa tlhoreng ya gagwe; gomme ka leru le le dikologago la dinonyana tša lewatle tša manong tšeo di bego di dikologa, gomme di phaphamala, gomme di mo tšholla, go be go le molaleng gore leruarua leo le lego ka thoko e swanetše go ba seo barei ba dihlapi ba se bitšago leruarua leo le thuthupišitšwego, ke gore, leruarua leo le hwilego le sa tlaišwe lewatleng , gomme ka go rialo gwa phaphamala setopo seo se sa swanelegego. Go ka naganwa gabotse, ke monkgo o sa kgahlišego gakaakang wo boima bjo bjalo bo swanetšego go o ntšha; e mpe go feta motse wa Asiria wa leuba, ge ba ba phelago ba se na bokgoni bja go boloka bao ba hwilego. Ka kgonthe e lebelelwa e le yeo e sa kgotlelelegego kudu ke ba bangwe, moo e lego gore ga go na kganyogo yeo e bego e ka ba kgodiša gore ba kgomaretše go bapa le yona. Le ge go le bjalo na go na le bao ba sa tlogo e dira; go sa šetšwe taba ya gore oli yeo e hwetšwago go tšwa go batho ba bjalo ke ya boleng bja tlase kudu, gomme le gatee ga se ya tlhago ya attar-of-rose.

Ge re be re sa dutše re batamela le phefo yeo e bego e fela, re ile ra bona gore Mofora o be a e-na le leruarua la bobedi ka thoko; gomme leruarua le la bobedi le be le bonagala e le la nosegay le go feta la pele. KGAOLO YA 92. Ambergris.

Bjale ambergris ye ke selo se se makatšago kudu, gomme se bohlokwa kudu bjalo ka selo sa kgwebo, mo e lego gore ka 1791 Mokapotene Coffin yo a itšego yo a belegwego Nantucket o ile a hlahlobja ka bareng ya Ntlo ya Ditona ya Engelane ka taba yeo. Gobane ka nako yeo, gomme ka nnete go fihla letšatšing la morago ga nako ge le bapetšwa, setlogo se se nepagetšego sa ambergris se ile sa dula, go swana le amber ka boyona, e le bothata go barutegi. Gaešita le ge lentšu ambergris e fo ba motswako wa Sefora bakeng sa amber e talalerata, lega go le bjalo dilo tše tše pedi di fapane kudu. Gobane amber, gaešita le ge ka dinako tše dingwe e hwetšwa lebopong la lewatle, e epilwe gape mobung o mongwe wa kgole wa ka gare ga naga, mola ambergris ga e ke e hwetšwa ka ntle le lewatleng. Ka ntle le moo, amber ke selo se thata, se se bonagalago, se se brittle, se se se nago monkgo, se šomišwa go dira dikotwana tša molomo go ya go diphaephe, bakeng sa dipheta le mekgabišo; eupša ambergris e boleta, e na le boka e bile e nkga bose kudu e bile e na le dinoko, moo e lego gore e dirišwa kudu go dira dinkgiša-bose, di- pastile, dikerese tše bohlokwa, diphofo tša moriri le pomatum. Maturkey a e šomiša go apea, gomme gape a e rwala go ya Mecca, ka morero wo o swanago le wo diorelo di išwago go St. Peter's kua Roma. Bagwebi ba bangwe ba beine ba lahlela mabele a sego kae ka gare ga claret, go e latswa.

Ka gona, ke mang yo a ka naganago gore mafumahadi le bahlomphegi ba bjalo ba babotse ba swanetše go ipshina ka selo sa motheo seo se hwetšwago ka maleng a sa kgahlišego a leruarua leo le babjago! Lega go le bjalo go bjalo. Go ba bangwe, go thwe ambergris ke sebaki, gomme ke ba bangwe e le mafelelo, a dyspepsia ka gare ga leruarua. Tsela ya go fodiša dyspepsia ye bjalo go be go le thata go bolela, ntle le ge ka go fa merwalo ye meraro goba ye mene ya sekepe ya dipilisi tša Brandreth, gomme ka morago ya felelwa ke tsela ya kotsi, bjalo ka ge bašomi ba dira ka go thuthupiša maswika.

Ke lebetše go bolela gore go be go hweditšwe ka gare ga ambergris ye, dipoleiti tše itšego tše thata, tša nkgokolo, tša marapo, tšeo mathomong Stubb a bego a nagana gore e ka ba dikonope tša marokgo a basesiši; eupša ka morago go ile gwa hwetšwa gore e be e se selo ge e se diripana tša marapo a manyenyane a squid ao a tloditšwego ka mokgwa woo.

Bjale ka ge go se senyege ga ambergris ye e nkgago bose kudu go swanetše go hwetšwa pelong ya go bola mo go bjalo; na se ga se selo? Nagana ka polelo yeo ya Mokgethwa Paulo go Bakorinthe, mabapi le go senyega le go se senyege; re bjetšwe ka lenyatšo, eupša ra tsošwa ka letago. I should like to conclude the chapter with the above appeal, but cannot, owing to my anxiety to repel a charge often made against whalemen, and which, in the estimation of some already biased minds, might be considered as indirectly substantiated by what has been said of the Frenchman's two whales. Elsewhere in this volume the slanderous aspersion has been disproved, that the vocation of whaling is throughout a slatternly, untidy business. But there is another thing to rebut. They hint that all whales always smell bad. Now how did this odious stigma originate?

I opine, that it is plainly traceable to the first arrival of the Greenland whaling ships in London, more than two centuries ago. Because those whalemen did not then, and do not now, try out their oil at sea as the Southern ships have always done; but cutting up the fresh blubber in small bits, thrust it through the bung holes of large casks, and carry it home in that manner; the shortness of the season in those Icy Seas, and the sudden and violent storms to which they are exposed, forbidding any other course. The consequence is, that upon breaking into the hold, and unloading one of these whale cemeteries, in the Greenland dock, a savor is given forth somewhat similar to that arising from excavating an old city grave-yard, for the foundations of a Lying-in Hospital.

I partly surmise also, that this wicked charge against whalers may be likewise imputed to the existence on the coast of Greenland, in former times, of a Dutch village called Schmerenburgh or Smeerenberg, which latter name is the one used by the learned Fogo Von Slack, in his great work on Smells, a text-book on that subject. As its name imports (smeer, fat; berg, to put up), this village was founded in order to afford a place for the blubber of the Dutch whale fleet to be tried out, without being taken home to Holland for that purpose. It was a collection of furnaces, fat-kettles, and oil sheds; and when the works were in full operation certainly gave forth no very pleasant savor. But all this is quite different with a South Sea Sperm Whaler; which in a voyage of four years perhaps, after completely filling her hold with oil, does not, perhaps, consume fifty days in the business of boiling out; and in the state that it is casked, the oil is nearly scentless. The truth is, that living or dead, if but decently treated, whales as a species are by no means creatures of ill odor; nor can whalemen be recognised, as the people of the middle ages affected to detect a Jew in the company, by the nose. Nor indeed can the whale possibly be otherwise than fragrant, when, as a general thing, he enjoys such high health; taking abundance of exercise; always out of doors; though, it is true, seldom in the open air. I say, that the motion of a Sperm Whale's flukes above water dispenses a perfume, as when a musk-scented lady rustles her dress in a warm parlor. What then shall I liken the Sperm Whale to for fragrance, considering his magnitude? Must it not be to that famous elephant, with jewelled tusks, and redolent with myrrh, which was led out of an Indian town to do honor to Alexander the Great?





CHAPTER 93. The Castaway.

It was but some few days after encountering the Frenchman, that a most significant event befell the most insignificant of the Pequod's crew; an event most lamentable; and which ended in providing the sometimes madly merry and predestinated craft with a living and ever accompanying prophecy of whatever shattered sequel might prove her own.

Now, in the whale ship, it is not every one that goes in the boats. Some few hands are reserved called ship-keepers, whose province it is to work the vessel while the boats are pursuing the whale. As a general thing, these ship-keepers are as hardy fellows as the men comprising the boats' crews. But if there happen to be an unduly slender, clumsy, or timorous wight in the ship, that wight is certain to be made a ship-keeper. It was so in the Pequod with the little negro Pippin by nick-name, Pip by abbreviation. Poor Pip! ye have heard of him before; ye must remember his tambourine on that dramatic midnight, so gloomy-jolly.

In outer aspect, Pip and Dough-Boy made a match, like a black pony and a white one, of equal developments, though of dissimilar colour, driven in one eccentric span. But while hapless Dough-Boy was by nature dull and torpid in his intellects, Pip, though over tender-hearted, was at bottom very bright, with that pleasant, genial, jolly brightness peculiar to his tribe; a tribe, which ever enjoy all holidays and festivities with finer, freer relish than any other race. For blacks, the year's calendar should show naught but three hundred and sixty-five Fourth of Julys and New Year's Days. Nor smile so, while I write that this little black was brilliant, for even blackness has its brilliancy; behold yon lustrous ebony, panelled in king's cabinets. But Pip loved life, and all life's peaceable securities; so that the panic-striking business in which he had somehow unaccountably become entrapped, had most sadly blurred his brightness; though, as ere long will be seen, what was thus temporarily subdued in him, in the end was destined to be luridly illumined by strange wild fires, that fictitiously showed him off to ten times the natural lustre with which in his native Tolland County in Connecticut, he had once enlivened many a fiddler's frolic on the green; and at melodious even-tide, with his gay ha-ha! had turned the round horizon into one star-belled tambourine. So, though in the clear air of day, suspended against a blue-veined neck, the pure-watered diamond drop will healthful glow; yet, when the cunning jeweller would show you the diamond in its most impressive lustre, he lays it against a gloomy ground, and then lights it up, not by the sun, but by some unnatural gases. Then come out those fiery effulgences, infernally superb; then the evil-blazing diamond, once the divinest symbol of the crystal skies, looks like some crown-jewel stolen from the King of Hell. But let us to the story.

It came to pass, that in the ambergris affair Stubb's after-oarsman chanced so to sprain his hand, as for a time to become quite maimed; and, temporarily, Pip was put into his place.

The first time Stubb lowered with him, Pip evinced much nervousness; but happily, for that time, escaped close contact with the whale; and therefore came off not altogether discreditably; though Stubb observing him, took care, afterwards, to exhort him to cherish his courageousness to the utmost, for he might often find it needful.

Now upon the second lowering, the boat paddled upon the whale; and as the fish received the darted iron, it gave its customary rap, which happened, in this instance, to be right under poor Pip's seat. The involuntary consternation of the moment caused him to leap, paddle in hand, out of the boat; and in such a way, that part of the slack whale line coming against his chest, he breasted it overboard with him, so as to become entangled in it, when at last plumping into the water. That instant the stricken whale started on a fierce run, the line swiftly straightened; and presto! poor Pip came all foaming up to the chocks of the boat, remorselessly dragged there by the line, which had taken several turns around his chest and neck.

Tashtego stood in the bows. He was full of the fire of the hunt. He hated Pip for a poltroon. Snatching the boat-knife from its sheath, he suspended its sharp edge over the line, and turning towards Stubb, exclaimed interrogatively, “Cut?” Meantime Pip's blue, choked face plainly looked, Do, for God's sake! All passed in a flash. In less than half a minute, this entire thing happened.

“Damn him, cut!” roared Stubb; and so the whale was lost and Pip was saved.

So soon as he recovered himself, the poor little negro was assailed by yells and execrations from the crew. Tranquilly permitting these irregular cursings to evaporate, Stubb then in a plain, business-like, but still half humorous manner, cursed Pip officially; and that done, unofficially gave him much wholesome advice. The substance was, Never jump from a boat, Pip, except—but all the rest was indefinite, as the soundest advice ever is. Now, in general, Stick to the boat , is your true motto in whaling; but cases will sometimes happen when Leap from the boat , is still better. Moreover, as if perceiving at last that if he should give undiluted conscientious advice to Pip, he would be leaving him too wide a margin to jump in for the future; Stubb suddenly dropped all advice, and concluded with a peremptory command, “Stick to the boat, Pip, or by the Lord, I won't pick you up if you jump; mind that. We can't afford to lose whales by the likes of you; a whale would sell for thirty times what you would, Pip, in Alabama. Bear that in mind, and don't jump any more.” Hereby perhaps Stubb indirectly hinted, that though man loved his fellow, yet man is a money-making animal, which propensity too often interferes with his benevolence.

But we are all in the hands of the Gods; and Pip jumped again. It was under very similar circumstances to the first performance; but this time he did not breast out the line; and hence, when the whale started to run, Pip was left behind on the sea, like a hurried traveller's trunk. Alas! Stubb was but too true to his word. It was a beautiful, bounteous, blue day; the spangled sea calm and cool, and flatly stretching away, all round, to the horizon, like gold-beater's skin hammered out to the extremest. Bobbing up and down in that sea, Pip's ebon head showed like a head of cloves. No boat-knife was lifted when he fell so rapidly astern. Stubb's inexorable back was turned upon him; and the whale was winged. In three minutes, a whole mile of shoreless ocean was between Pip and Stubb. Out from the centre of the sea, poor Pip turned his crisp, curling, black head to the sun, another lonely castaway, though the loftiest and the brightest.

Now, in calm weather, to swim in the open ocean is as easy to the practised swimmer as to ride in a spring-carriage ashore. But the awful lonesomeness is intolerable. The intense concentration of self in the middle of such a heartless immensity, my God! who can tell it? Mark, how when sailors in a dead calm bathe in the open sea—mark how closely they hug their ship and only coast along her sides.

But had Stubb really abandoned the poor little negro to his fate? No; he did not mean to, at least. Because there were two boats in his wake, and he supposed, no doubt, that they would of course come up to Pip very quickly, and pick him up; though, indeed, such considerations towards oarsmen jeopardized through their own timidity, is not always manifested by the hunters in all similar instances; and such instances not unfrequently occur; almost invariably in the fishery, a coward, so called, is marked with the same ruthless detestation peculiar to military navies and armies.

But it so happened, that those boats, without seeing Pip, suddenly spying whales close to them on one side, turned, and gave chase; and Stubb's boat was now so far away, and he and all his crew so intent upon his fish, that Pip's ringed horizon began to expand around him miserably. By the merest chance the ship itself at last rescued him; but from that hour the little negro went about the deck an idiot; such, at least, they said he was. The sea had jeeringly kept his finite body up, but drowned the infinite of his soul. Not drowned entirely, though. Rather carried down alive to wondrous depths, where strange shapes of the unwarped primal world glided to and fro before his passive eyes; and the miser-merman, Wisdom, revealed his hoarded heaps; and among the joyous, heartless, ever-juvenile eternities, Pip saw the multitudinous, God-omnipresent, coral insects, that out of the firmament of waters heaved the colossal orbs. He saw God's foot upon the treadle of the loom, and spoke it; and therefore his shipmates called him mad. So man's insanity is heaven's sense; and wandering from all mortal reason, man comes at last to that celestial thought, which, to reason, is absurd and frantic; and weal or woe, feels then uncompromised, indifferent as his God.

For the rest, blame not Stubb too hardly. The thing is common in that fishery; and in the sequel of the narrative, it will then be seen what like abandonment befell myself.
