KGAOLO YA 94. Kgaolo ya Letsogo.

Leruarua leo la Stubb, leo le rekilwego ka tšhelete e ntši gakaakaa, le ile la tlišwa ka mo go swanetšego ka lehlakoreng la Pequod, moo ditiro tšeo ka moka tša go sega le go emiša tšeo di bego di hlalositšwe ka botlalo pele, di bego di feta ka mehla, gaešita le go baling ya Heidelburgh Tun, goba Case.

Le ge ba bangwe ba be ba swaregile ka mošomo wo wa mafelelo, ba bangwe ba ile ba thwala go goga di- tub tše dikgolo, gatee-tee ge di tladitšwe ka peu; gomme ge nako ye e swanetšego e fihla, yona peu ye e ile ya laolwa ka kelohloko ere going to the try-works, of which anon.

E be e fodile le go kristale go fihla bokgoleng bjo bogolo, mo e lego gore ge, le ba bangwe ba mmalwa, ke dutše fase pele ga go hlapa mo gogolo ga Constantine ka yona, ke ile ka e hwetša e le ka sekontiri ka tsela ye e makatšago go ba dithotobolo, mo le mo e kgokologa ka karolong ya seela. E be e le kgwebo ya rena go pitlela dithotobolo tše morago ka gare ga seela. Mošomo o bose le o sa kgahlišego! Ga go makatše ge mehleng ya kgale peu ye e be e le setlolo seo se ratwago gakaakaa. E hlakilego gakaakaa! senotši se se bjalo! senolofatši se se bjalo! sedirišwa se se bose gakaakaa sa go fetoša dilo! Ka morago ga go ba le diatla tša ka ka go yona metsotso ye mmalwa fela, menwana ya ka e ile ya ikwa bjalo ka di-eel, gomme ya thoma, bjalo ka ge go ka thwe, go serpentine le go spiralise.

Ge ke dutše fao ke iketlile, ke sefapano maoto godimo ga mokatong wa sekepe; ka morago ga go itapiša mo go babago ka windlass; ka tlase ga leratadima le le khutšitšego le letala-lerata; sekepe ka tlase ga seila se se sa šomego, gomme se thelela ka khutšo gakaakaa go ya pele; ge ke be ke hlapa diatla tša-ka gare ga di- globule tšeo tše boleta le tše boleta tša ditlhalenama tše di tsentšwego, tšeo di logilwego mo e nyakilego go ba ka gare ga iri; bjalo ka ge ba be ba robega menwaneng ya ka ka mo go humilego, ba tšholla mahumo a bona ka moka, bjalo ka morara wo o butšwitšego ka botlalo beine ya bona; ge ke be ke tšhuma monkgo woo o sa šilafatšwago,—ka kgonthe le ka kgonthe, go swana le monkgo wa di- violet tša selemo; Ke le tsebatša gore ka nako yeo ke phetše bjalo ka mohlakeng wa musky; Ke lebetše ka moka ka keno ya rena ye e šiišago; ka peu yeo e sa hlalosegego, ke ile ka hlapa diatla tša ka le pelo ya ka ka yona; Ke nyakile ke thoma go reta tumela-khwele ya kgale ya Paracelsan ya gore peu e na le bokwala bjo bo sa tlwaelegago bja go okobatša phišo ya pefelo; ge ke be ke hlapa ka bateng yeo, ke ile ka ikwa ke lokologile go tšwa go Modimo go tšwa go go se rate ka moka, goba petulance, goba lehufa, la mohuta le ge e le ofe.

Pitlela! pitlela! pitlela! mesong ka moka; Ke ile ka pitlela peu yeo go fihlela nna ka noši ke nyakile ke qhibidiga ka gare ga yona; Ka pitlaganya peu yeo go fihlela mohuta wo o makatšago wa bohlanya o etla godimo ga ka; gomme ke ile ka ikhwetša ke pitlaganya diatla tša bašomi-gotee le nna ka go se lemoge, ke foša diatla tša bona ka di-globule tše boleta. Maikwelo a bjalo a atile, a lerato, a bogwera, a lerato a pitšo ye e a belegile; gore mafelelong ke be ke dula ke ba pitlaganya diatla, gomme ke lebelela godimo mahlong a bona ka maikutlo; go fihla bokgoleng bja go bolela,—Oo! batho-gotee le nna ba rategago, ke ka baka la’ng re swanetše go dula re tšeela godimo di- acerbities le ge e le dife tša leago, goba re tsebe metlae e mebe goba mona o monyenyane! Tla; a re pitleleng diatla gohle; aowa, a ka moka ga rena re ipitlela ka gare ga yo mongwe le yo mongwe; a re ipitlela ka gohle ka gare ga yona maswi le peu ya botho.

Nkabe nka tšwela pele ke pitlaganya peu yeo ka mo go sa felego! Ga bjale, ka ge ka diphihlelo tše dintši tše di tšeago nako e telele, tšeo di boeletšwago, ke lemogile gore maemong ka moka motho o swanetše go feleletša a theošitše, goba bonyenyane a šuthiše, boikgogomošo bja gagwe bja lethabo leo le fihlelelwago; go se e bee kae goba kae tlhaologanyong goba ya go makatša; eupša mo mosadi, pelong, malaong, tafoleng, saddle, thoko ga mollo, naga; bjale ka ge ke lemogile tše ka moka, ke ikemišeditše go pitlela molato ka mo go sa felego. Ka dikgopolo tša dipono tša bošego, ke ile ka bona methaladi e metelele ya barongwa paradeiseng, yo mongwe le yo mongwe a swere diatla tša gagwe ka nkgong ya spermaceti.

Bjale, ge go bolelwa ka peu, go swanetše go bolela ka dilo tše dingwe tše di swanago le yona, kgwebong ya go lokišetša leruarua la peu bakeng sa di- try-works.

Sa pele go tla pere ye tšhweu, yeo e bitšwago bjalo, yeo e hwetšwago go tšwa karolong ye e tshesane ya hlapi, le gape go tšwa dikarolong tše koto tša di-fluke tša gagwe. E thata ka ditšhika tše di pitlagantšwego—e lego sehlopha sa mešifa—eupša e sa dutše e e-na le makhura a itšego. Ka morago ga go kgaolwa go tšwa go leruarua, pere ye tšhweu e thoma ka go segwa ka di-oblong tše di rwalago ere e ya go mincer. Di swana kudu le diboloko tša mmabole wa Berkshire.

Plum-pudding ke lereo leo le filwego dikarolo tše itšego tša dikarolwana tša nama ya leruarua, mo le mo di kgomarela kobo ya blubber, gomme gantši e tšea karolo ka tekanyo e kgolo go unctuousness ya yona. Ke selo se se lapološago kudu, seo se nago le bogwera, se sebotse go se bona. Bjalo ka ge leina la yona le tšwa dinageng tša ka ntle, ke ya mmala wo o humilego ka mo go feteletšego, wa maronthontho, ka mobu wa lehlwa le wa gauta wo o nago le methalo, wo o nago le mabala a bohwibidu bjo bo tseneletšego kudu le bophepolo. Ke di-plum tša dirubi, ka diswantšho tša citron. Go sa šetšwe mabaka, go thata go ithibela go e ja. Ke a ipolela, gore gatee ke utswitše ka morago ga foremast go e leka. E ile ya latswa selo se sengwe bjalo ka ge ke swanetše go emola cutlet ya bogoši go tšwa seropeng sa Louis le Gros e ka ba e ile ya latswa, re tšea gore o bolailwe letšatšing la mathomo ka morago ga sehla sa nama ya phoofolo ya naga, le sehla seo se itšego sa nama ya phoofolo ya naga ya sebjalebjale le vintage ye botse ka mo go sa tlwaelegago ya dirapa tša merara tša Champagne ya go dira bjalo.

Go na le selo se sengwe, le sa botee kudu, seo se tšwelelago mo tseleng ya kgwebo ye, eupša seo ke ikwago se gakantšha kudu ka mo go lekanego go se hlaloša. E bitšwa slobgollion; leina la mathomo le banna ba maruarua, gomme gaešita le go bjalo le ka tlhago ya selo seo. Ke taba yeo e sa hlalosegego yeo e nago le oozy, ya dithapo, yeo gantši e hwetšwago ka gare ga ditub tša peu, ka morago ga go pitlela ka nako e telele, le go tšhollwa ga morago ga moo. Ke e swara e le di- membrane tše di sesane ka mo go makatšago, tše di phatlogilego tša kgetse, tšeo di kopanago.

Gurry, yeo e bitšwago bjalo, ke lereo leo ka mo go swanetšego e lego la barei ba maruarua ba le letona, eupša ka dinako tše dingwe ka tshoganetšo le dirišwa ke barei ba peu. E hlaola selo se se lefsifsi, se se kgomaretšego seo se gogwago ka morago ga Greenland goba leruarua la go ja, gomme bontši bja sona bo khupetša di-deck tša meoya yeo ya maemo a tlase yeo e tsomago Leviathan yeo e sa hlomphegego.

Di-nipper tša go swara. Ka go tia lentšu le ga se la setlogo go tlotlontšu ya leruarua. Eupša bjalo ka ge e dirišwa ke banna ba maruarua, e ba bjalo. Nipper ya monna wa maruarua ke lešela le le kopana le le tiilego la dilo tša tendinous tšeo di segilwego go tšwa karolong ye e tshesane ya mosela wa Leviathan: e na le palogare ya botenya bja intšhi, gomme go tše dingwe ka moka, e ka ba bogolo bja karolo ya tšhipi ya lešoba. Edgewise sepela go bapa le mokatong wa makhura, e šoma bjalo ka squilgee ya letlalo; gomme ka di- blandishment tše di se nago maina, bjalo ka tša maleatlana, di goketša gotee le yona ditšhila ka moka.

Eupša go ithuta ka moka ka ditaba tše tša recondite, tsela ya gago ye kaone ke go theogela ka gare ga blubber-room, gomme o be le poledišano ye telele le bagolegwa ba yona. Lefelo le le boletšwe peleng bjalo ka sebjana sa dikobo-diripana, ge di hlobolwa le go phagamišwa go tšwa go leruarua. Ge nako e swanetšego e fihla ya go sega dikagare tša yona, folete ye ke lefelo la letšhogo go di- tyro ka moka, kudu-kudu bošego. Ka lehlakoreng le lengwe, le bonešitšwe ke lebone le le lerootho, go tlogetšwe sekgoba se se hlwekilego bakeng sa bašomi. Ka kakaretšo di sepela ka bobedi,—motho wa pike-and-gaffman le monna wa spade. Whale-pike e swana le sebetša sa go namela sa frigate sa leina le le swanago. Gaff ke selo se sengwe se se swanago le sekgoketšane sa sekepe. Ka gaff ya gagwe, gaffman o goketša letlakala la blubber, gomme o katanela go le swara gore le se thelele, ge sekepe se tšholla le go šikinyega go dikologa. Go sa le bjalo, monna wa go swara dipere o eme godimo ga letlakala ka bolona, ​​a le sega ka go otlologa gore e be diripana tša dipere tše di rwalago. Sepetlele se se bogale bjalo ka ge hone e ka se dira; maoto a spademan ga a na dieta; selo seo a emego go sona ka dinako tše dingwe se tla thelela ka mo go sa ganetšegego go tloga go yena, bjalo ka selei. Ge a ka kgaola le lengwe la menwana ya gagwe ya maoto, goba le lengwe la bathuši ba gagwe, na o tla makala kudu? Menwana ya maoto e a hlaelela gare ga banna ba kgale ba blubber-room.





KGAOLO YA 95. Ba Cassock.

Ge nkabe o ile wa gata sekepe sa Pequod nakong e itšego ya go bolawa mo ga maruarua ka morago ga lehu; gomme ge nkabe o ile wa sepelasepela pele kgauswi le windlass, ke na le bonnete bjo bogolo gore o be o tla skena ka ntle le go rata go tseba mo gonyenyane selo se se makatšago kudu, sa sephiri, seo o bego o tla se bona moo, se rapaletše ka botelele ka gare ga di- lee scuppers. E sego sediba se se makatšago seo se lego hlogong e kgolo ya leruarua; e sego prodigy ya mohlagare wa gagwe wa ka tlase wo o sa hlwago; e sego mohlolo wa mosela wa gagwe wa go lekana; ga go le e tee ya tše yeo e ka go makatšago gakaakaa, bjalo ka seripa-gare sa go bona khoune yeo e sa ikarabelego,—e telele go feta mo-Kentuck e telele, e nyakile go ba leoto ka bophara motheong, gomme e le e ntsho ya jet-black go swana le Yojo, modingwana wa ebony wa Queequeg. Gomme modingwana, ka nnete, ke wona; goba, go e na le moo, mehleng ya kgale, go swana ga yona e be e le. Modingwana wo o swanago le wo o hweditšwego dithokgweng tša ka sephiring tša Mohumagadi Maaka kua Judea; gomme ka baka la go rapela seo, Kgoši Asa, morwa wa gagwe, o ile a mo raka, gomme a senya modingwana, gomme a o fiša e le makgapha moeding wa Kedrone, bjalo ka ge go hlalošitšwe ka leswiswi kgaolong ya bo-15 ya Puku ya Pele ya Dikgoši.

Lebelela mosesiši wa sekepe, yo a bitšwago mo-mincer, yo bjale a tlago, gomme a thušwa ke balekane ba babedi, a thekga grandissimus ka boima, bjalo ka ge basesiši ba dikepe ba e bitša, gomme ka magetla a inamišitšwego, a thekesela le yona bjalo ka ge eka ke mo-grenadier yo a rwelego modirišani yo a hwilego go tšwa tšhemong. A e katološa godimo ga mokatong wa forecastle, bjale o tšwela pele ka silindara go tloša letlalo la yona le lefsifsi, bjalo ka motsomi wa Moafrika letlalo la boa. Se se dirilwe o retološa letlalo ka gare ka ntle, bjalo ka leoto la pantaloon; e e fa go otlolla mo go botse, e le gore e nyakile go ba bophara bja yona gabedi; gomme mafelelong e e fega, e phatlaletše gabotse, ka gare ga rigging, gore e ome. Ere long, e tlošwa fase; ge a tloša mo e ka bago maoto a mararo go yona, a lebile ntlheng e šupago, gomme ka morago a sega mašoba a mabedi bakeng sa mekoti ya matsogo ntlheng e nngwe, ka botelele o ithelela mmeleng ka gare ga yona. Mincer bjale e eme pele ga ge o beeleditše ka go di-canonicals ka botlalo tša pitšo ya gagwe. E sa gopole taelo ya gagwe ka moka, investiture ye e nnoši e tla mo šireletša ka mo go lekanego, mola a thwetšwe mešomong ye e kgethegilego ya ofisi ya gagwe.

Ofisi yeo e bopilwe ka go mincing dipere-diripana tša blubber bakeng sa dipitša; tshepedišo yeo e dirwago ka pere ya kota yeo e ratago go tseba, yeo e bjetšwego ka mafelelo kgahlanong le dibo, gomme ka tub ye kgolo ka fase ga yona, yeo go yona diripana tše di segilwego di welago, ka lebelo bjalo ka matlakala go tšwa tafoleng ya seboledi sa rapt. Arrayed ka hlomphehang ba batsho; go dula sefaleng se se bonagalago; maikemišetšo a matlakala a beibele; ke nkgetheng gakaakang bakeng sa bopišopo-mogolo, ke mošemane gakaakang bakeng sa Mopapa e be e le mo-mincer yo!*

*Mahlaku a Beibele! Matlakala a Beibele! Ye ke sello se se sa fetogego go tšwa go balekane go ya go mincer. E mo laela gore a be šedi, gomme a sege mošomo wa gagwe ka dilae tše sesane ka mo go kgonegago, ka ge ka go dira bjalo kgwebo ya go bela oli e akgofišwa kudu, gomme bontši bja yona bo oketšegile kudu, ntle le mohlomongwe go e kaonafatša ka boleng.





KGAOLO YA 96. Di-Try-Mešomo.

Ka ntle le diketswana tša gagwe tše di phagamišitšwego, mohlapi wa maruarua wa Amerika o hlaolwa ka ntle ka ditiro tša gagwe tša go leka. O tšweletša go se swane mo go makatšago ga masenke a tiilego kudu ao a kopanyago le oak le hemp go bopeng sekepe seo se phethilwego. Go bjalo ka ge eka go tšwa tšhemong e bulegilego go be go išwa sebjana sa ditena mapolanka a gagwe.

Di- try-work di bjetšwe magareng ga foremast le mainmast, e lego karolo e nago le phapoši e kgolo kudu ya mokatong wa sekepe. Dikota tšeo di lego ka tlase ke tša matla a makatšago, tšeo di loketšego go tšwetša pele boima bja boima bja ditena le sekontiri tšeo di nyakilego di tiile, tšeo di ka bago dimithara tše lesome ka disekwere tše seswai le tše hlano ka botelele. Motheo ga o tsenelele mokatong wa sekepe, eupša masenke a kgomareditšwe ka go tia godimo ka mangwele a boima a tšhipi ao a o tiišago ka mahlakoreng ka moka, gomme a o kgokološetša fase go fihla dikota. Ka mahlakoreng e cased ka kota, gomme ka godimo e khupeditšwe ka botlalo ke tsela ye kgolo, ye e sekamego, ye e nago le mapheko. Go tloša hatch ye re pepentšha dipitša tše kgolo tša go leka, tše pedi ka palo, le ye nngwe le ye nngwe ya bokgoni bja megopo ye mmalwa. Ge di sa dirišwe, di bolokwa di hlwekile ka mo go makatšago. Ka nako ye nngwe di phadima ka leswika la sesepa le santa, go fihlela di phadima ka gare bjalo ka megopo ya silifera ya go phunya. Nakong ya go lebelela bošego basesiši ba bangwe ba kgale bao ba nyatšago ba tla gagabela ka gare ga tšona gomme ba ikgokološa moo bakeng sa go robala boroko bjo bonyenyane. Ge di dutše di thwetšwe go di phopholetšwa—monna o tee ka pitšeng e nngwe le e nngwe, a bapile—dikgokagano tše dintši tša sephiri di a tšwetšwa pele, godimo ga melomo ya tšhipi. Ke lefelo gape la go naganišiša ka mo go tseneletšego ga dipalo. E be e le ka pitšeng ya go leka ya letsogo la nngele ya Pequod, ka leswika la sesepa le le dikologa ka mafolofolo, moo ke ilego ka thoma ka go hlabja ka go se lebanye ke therešo ye e makatšago, gore ka thutafase mebele ka moka yeo e thelelago go bapa le cycloid, leswika la ka la sesepa ka mohlala, e tla theoga go tšwa go yona ntlha efe goba efe ka nako e swanago tlwaa.

Go tloša fire-board go tšwa ka pele ga try-works, masenke a se nago selo a lehlakore leo a pepentšhwa, a tsenelela ke melomo ye mebedi ya tšhipi ya dibešo, thwii ka fase ga dipitša. Melomo ye e tsentšwe mamati a boima a tšhipi. Mogote o mogolo wa mollo o thibelwa go ikgokagana le mokatong wa sekepe, ka go diriša letamo le le sa tsepamago leo le atologelago ka tlase ga bokagodimo ka moka bjo bo tswaletšwego bja mediro. Ka thanele yeo e tsentšwego ka morago, letamo le le bolokwa le tladitšwe gape ka meetse ka lebelo ge le moyafala. Ga go na ditšhimini tša ka ntle; di bula thwii go tšwa lebotong la ka morago. Gomme mo a re boele morago motsotswana.

E be e le mo e ka bago ka iri ya senyane bošego moo ditiro tša go leka tša Pequod di ilego tša thongwa la mathomo leetong le la bjale. E be e le ya Stubb go hlokomela kgwebo.

“Ka moka ba itokišitše fao? Off hatch, ka gona, gomme o mo thome. O apea, o tšhume mediro.” Se e be e le selo se bonolo, ka gobane mmetli o be a dutše a kgoromeletša ditšhitswana tša gagwe ka sebešong go ralala le tsejana. Mo go bolelwe gore leetong la go tsoma maruarua mollo wa mathomo ka go try-works o swanetše go fepša ka nako e itšego ka dikgong. Ka morago ga moo ga go na kota yeo e dirišwago, ka ntle le e le mokgwa wa go gotetša ka pela go makhura a motheo. Ka lentšu le tee, ka morago ga go lekwa, blubber ye e hlwekilego le ye e onyegilego, yeo ga bjale e bitšwago di- scraps goba fritters, e sa dutše e e-na le dithoto tša yona tše dintši tše di sa kgahlišego. Di- fritter tše di fepa dikgabo tša mollo. Go swana le mohwelatumelo yo a tukago kudu, goba mohwela-tumelo yo a ijago, ge a šetše a gotetšwe, leruarua le aba makhura a lona gomme le fiša ka mmele wa lona. Eka nkabe a ile a ja muši wa gagwe ka noši! gobane muši wa gagwe o a šiiša go o hema, gomme o o heme o swanetše, gomme e sego seo feela, eupša o swanetše go phela ka go wona bakeng sa nako yeo. E na le monkgo wo o sa bolelwago, wa hlaga, wa ma-Hindu ka wona, wo o ka lalelago tikologong ya dipire tša poloko. E nkga bjalo ka lephego la nngele la letšatši la kahlolo; ke ngangišano ya molete.

Bošegogare mediro e be e thoma go šoma ka botlalo. Re be re hlakile go tšwa setopong; seila se be se dirilwe; phefo e be e fokotšega; leswiswi la lewatle la lešoka e be e le le legolo. Eupša leswiswi leo le ile la latswa ke dikgabo tše šoro, tšeo ka dikgaotšo di bego di forked go tšwa go diphaephe tša mouta, gomme tša boneša thapo ye nngwe le ye nngwe ye e phagamego ka gare ga rigging, bjalo ka mollo wo o tumilego wa Segerika. Sekepe seo se tukago se ile sa tšwela pele, bjalo ka ge eka se ile sa laelwa ka go se itshole go dira tiro e itšego ya tefetšo. Ka gona di- brig tšeo di rwelego sebete le sebabole tša sekepe sa Hydriote seo se nago le sebete, e lego Canaris, tšeo di bego di e-tšwa maemakepeng a tšona a bošegogare, di e-na le matlakala a sephara a kgabo bakeng sa diseila, di ile tša rwala dikepe tša go sepela ka lewatleng tša Turkey gomme tša di phutha ka mollo.

Lešoba, leo le tlošitšwego godimo ga mediro, bjale e be e nea lefelo le le nabilego la go fiša ka pele ga tšona. Go eme godimo ga se e be e le dibopego tša Tartarean tša bašomi ba di-harpoone ba baheitene, bao ka mehla e bego e le di- stoker tša sekepe sa maruarua. Ka dikota tše kgolo tša ditlhale ba be ba lahlela mašaba a go šišinya a ditšhitswana ka gare ga dipitša tše di fišago, goba ba hlohleletša mello ka fase, go fihlela dikgabo tša noga di kitimela, di kobega, ka ntle ga mejako go di swara ka maoto. Muši o ile wa kgokologa ka mekgobo e nyamilego. Go segalo se sengwe le se sengwe sa sekepe go be go e-na le segalo sa makhura a go bela, ao a bego a bonagala e le phišego ka moka ya go tlolela difahlegong tša bona. Go lebana le molomo wa mediro, ka lehlakoreng le lengwe la lefelo le le sephara la go fiša la kota, go be go e-na le sedirišwa sa go tšhuma moya. Se se ile sa sebeletsa bakeng sa leoatle-sofa. Mona go be go robala sešupanako, ge se be se sa thwetšwe ka tsela ye nngwe, se lebelela ka gare ga phišo ye khubedu ya mollo, go fihlela mahlo a bona a ikwa a fišitšwe dihlogong tša bona. Dibopego tša bona tša tawny, bjale ka moka di begrimed ke muši le mofufutšo, ditedu tša bona tše di matted, le go phadima mo go fapanego ga barbaric ga meno a bona, tše ka moka di ile tša utollwa ka mo go makatšago ka emblazonings ya capricious ya mediro. KGAOLO YA 99. Ba-Doubloon.

Ere bjale go anegwa ka fao Ahaba a bego a tlwaetše go sepela ka lebelo la kotara ya gagwe, a retologela ka mehla mollwaneng ofe goba ofe, binnacle le mainmast; eupša ka bontši bja dilo tše dingwe tšeo di nyakago kanegelo ga se gwa okeletšwa ka fao gore ka nako ye nngwe maetong a, ge bontši a nweletše maikutlong a gagwe, o be a tlwaetše go ema ka go latelana lefelong le lengwe le le lengwe, gomme a ema fao ka tsela ye e makatšago a lebeletše selo se se itšego pele ga gagwe. Gomme bohlokwa bjo bongwe bjo bo itšego bo lalela dilong ka moka, go sego bjalo dilo ka moka ga di na mohola o monnyane, gomme lefase la nkgokolo ka bolona eupša e le cipher ye e se nago selo, ntle le go rekiša ka koloyana, bjalo ka ge ba dira meboto ka Boston, go tlatša morass ye nngwe ka Milky Way.

Bjale doubloon ye e be e le ya gauta ye e hlwekilego kudu, ya kgarebe, e raked felotsoko go tšwa pelong ya meboto ye mebotse kudu, moo, bohlabela le bodikela, godimo ga santa ya gauta, meetse a dihlogo a Pactolus ye ntši a elelago. Le gona, le ge e be e beilwe gare ga sehlopha sa basepediši ba go hloka kwelobohloko gomme iri ye nngwe le ye nngwe e feta ka diatla tše di se nago kwelobohloko, gomme go ralala le mašego a go phela a apešitšwe ke leswiswi le le koto leo le ka khupetšago mokgwa ofe goba ofe wa go utswa, le ge go le bjalo go hlaba ga letšatši go gongwe le go gongwe go ile gwa hwetša doubloon moo go sobela ga letšatši go e tlogetšego gona la mafelelo. Gobane e be e beilwe ka thoko gomme ya kgethagatšwa go fihla mafelelong a tee a makatšago; gomme le ge e le gore e be e sa kgahliše ditseleng tša bona tša basesiši, e tee le ka moka, basesiši ba dikepe ba be ba e hlompha e le seširo sa maruarua a mašweu. CHAPTER 100. Leg and Arm.

The Pequod, of Nantucket, Meets the Samuel Enderby, of London.

“Ship, ahoy! Hast seen the White Whale?”

So cried Ahab, once more hailing a ship showing English colours, bearing down under the stern. Trumpet to mouth, the old man was standing in his hoisted quarter-boat, his ivory leg plainly revealed to the stranger captain, who was carelessly reclining in his own boat's bow. He was a darkly-tanned, burly, good-natured, fine-looking man, of sixty or thereabouts, dressed in a spacious roundabout, that hung round him in festoons of blue pilot-cloth; and one empty arm of this jacket streamed behind him like the broidered arm of a hussar's surcoat.

“Hast seen the White Whale?”

“See you this?” and withdrawing it from the folds that had hidden it, he held up a white arm of sperm whale bone, terminating in a wooden head like a mallet.

“Man my boat!” cried Ahab, impetuously, and tossing about the oars near him—“Stand by to lower!”

In less than a minute, without quitting his little craft, he and his crew were dropped to the water, and were soon alongside of the stranger. But here a curious difficulty presented itself. In the excitement of the moment, Ahab had forgotten that since the loss of his leg he had never once stepped on board of any vessel at sea but his own, and then it was always by an ingenious and very handy mechanical contrivance peculiar to the Pequod, and a thing not to be rigged and shipped in any other vessel at a moment's warning. Now, it is no very easy matter for anybody—except those who are almost hourly used to it, like whalemen—to clamber up a ship's side from a boat on the open sea; for the great swells now lift the boat high up towards the bulwarks, and then instantaneously drop it half way down to the kelson. So, deprived of one leg, and the strange ship of course being altogether unsupplied with the kindly invention, Ahab now found himself abjectly reduced to a clumsy landsman again; hopelessly eyeing the uncertain changeful height he could hardly hope to attain.

It has before been hinted, perhaps, that every little untoward circumstance that befell him, and which indirectly sprang from his luckless mishap, almost invariably irritated or exasperated Ahab. And in the present instance, all this was heightened by the sight of the two officers of the strange ship, leaning over the side, by the perpendicular ladder of nailed cleets there, and swinging towards him a pair of tastefully-ornamented man-ropes; for at first they did not seem to bethink them that a one-legged man must be too much of a cripple to use their sea bannisters. But this awkwardness only lasted a minute, because the strange captain, observing at a glance how affairs stood, cried out, “I see, I see!—avast heaving there! Jump, boys, and swing over the cutting-tackle.”

As good luck would have it, they had had a whale alongside a day or two previous, and the great tackles were still aloft, and the massive curved blubber-hook, now clean and dry, was still attached to the end. This was quickly lowered to Ahab, who at once comprehending it all, slid his solitary thigh into the curve of the hook (it was like sitting in the fluke of an anchor, or the crotch of an apple tree), and then giving the word, held himself fast, and at the same time also helped to hoist his own weight, by pulling hand-over-hand upon one of the running parts of the tackle. Soon he was carefully swung inside the high bulwarks, and gently landed upon the capstan head. With his ivory arm frankly thrust forth in welcome, the other captain advanced, and Ahab, putting out his ivory leg, and crossing the ivory arm (like two sword-fish blades) cried out in his walrus way, “Aye, aye, hearty! let us shake bones together!—an arm and a leg!—an arm that never can shrink, d'ye see; and a leg that never can run. Where did'st thou see the White Whale?—how long ago?”

“The White Whale,” said the Englishman, pointing his ivory arm towards the East, and taking a rueful sight along it, as if it had been a telescope; “there I saw him, on the Line, last season.”

“And he took that arm off, did he?” asked Ahab, now sliding down from the capstan, and resting on the Englishman's shoulder, as he did so.

“Aye, he was the cause of it, at least; and that leg, too?”

“Spin me the yarn,” said Ahab; “how was it?”

“It was the first time in my life that I ever cruised on the Line,” began the Englishman. “I was ignorant of the White Whale at that time. Well, one day we lowered for a pod of four or five whales, and my boat fastened to one of them; a regular circus horse he was, too, that went milling and milling round so, that my boat's crew could only trim dish, by sitting all their sterns on the outer gunwale. Presently up breaches from the bottom of the sea a bouncing great whale, with a milky-white head and hump, all crows' feet and wrinkles.”

“It was he, it was he!” cried Ahab, suddenly letting out his suspended breath.

“And harpoons sticking in near his starboard fin.”

“Aye, aye—they were mine— my irons,” cried Ahab, exultingly—“but on!”

“Give me a chance, then,” said the Englishman, good-humoredly. “Well, this old great-grandfather, with the white head and hump, runs all afoam into the pod, and goes to snapping furiously at my fast-line!

“Aye, I see!—wanted to part it; free the fast-fish—an old trick—I know him.”

“How it was exactly,” continued the one-armed commander, “I do not know; but in biting the line, it got foul of his teeth, caught there somehow; but we didn't know it then; so that when we afterwards pulled on the line, bounce we came plump on to his hump! instead of the other whale's; that went off to windward, all fluking. Seeing how matters stood, and what a noble great whale it was—the noblest and biggest I ever saw, sir, in my life—I resolved to capture him, spite of the boiling rage he seemed to be in. And thinking the hap-hazard line would get loose, or the tooth it was tangled to might draw (for I have a devil of a boat's crew for a pull on a whale-line); seeing all this, I say, I jumped into my first mate's boat—Mr. Mounttop's here (by the way, Captain—Mounttop; Mounttop—the captain);—as I was saying, I jumped into Mounttop's boat, which, d'ye see, was gunwale and gunwale with mine, then; and snatching the first harpoon, let this old great-grandfather have it. But, Lord, look you, sir—hearts and souls alive, man—the next instant, in a jiff, I was blind as a bat—both eyes out—all befogged and bedeadened with black foam—the whale's tail looming straight up out of it, perpendicular in the air, like a marble steeple. No use sterning all, then; but as I was groping at midday, with a blinding sun, all crown-jewels; as I was groping, I say, after the second iron, to toss it overboard—down comes the tail like a Lima tower, cutting my boat in two, leaving each half in splinters; and, flukes first, the white hump backed through the wreck, as though it was all chips. We all struck out. To escape his terrible flailings, I seized hold of my harpoon-pole sticking in him, and for a moment clung to that like a sucking fish. But a combing sea dashed me off, and at the same instant, the fish, taking one good dart forwards, went down like a flash; and the barb of that cursed second iron towing along near me caught me here” (clapping his hand just below his shoulder); “yes, caught me just here, I say, and bore me down to Hell's flames, I was thinking; when, when, all of a sudden, thank the good God, the barb ript its way along the flesh—clear along the whole length of my arm—came out nigh my wrist, and up I floated;—and that gentleman there will tell you the rest (by the way, captain—Dr. Bunger, ship's surgeon: Bunger, my lad,—the captain). Now, Bunger boy, spin your part of the yarn.”

The professional gentleman thus familiarly pointed out, had been all the time standing near them, with nothing specific visible, to denote his gentlemanly rank on board. His face was an exceedingly round but sober one; he was dressed in a faded blue woollen frock or shirt, and patched trowsers; and had thus far been dividing his attention between a marlingspike he held in one hand, and a pill-box held in the other, occasionally casting a critical glance at the ivory limbs of the two crippled captains. But, at his superior's introduction of him to Ahab, he politely bowed, and straightway went on to do his captain's bidding.

“It was a shocking bad wound,” began the whale-surgeon; “and, taking my advice, Captain Boomer here, stood our old Sammy—”

“Samuel Enderby is the name of my ship,” interrupted the one-armed captain, addressing Ahab; “go on, boy.”

“Stood our old Sammy off to the northward, to get out of the blazing hot weather there on the Line. But it was no use—I did all I could; sat up with him nights; was very severe with him in the matter of diet—”

“Oh, very severe!” chimed in the patient himself; then suddenly altering his voice, “Drinking hot rum toddies with me every night, till he couldn't see to put on the bandages; and sending me to bed, half seas over, about three o'clock in the morning. Oh, ye stars! he sat up with me indeed, and was very severe in my diet. Oh! a great watcher, and very dietetically severe, is Dr. Bunger. (Bunger, you dog, laugh out! why don't ye? You know you're a precious jolly rascal.) But, heave ahead, boy, I'd rather be killed by you than kept alive by any other man.”

“My captain, you must have ere this perceived, respected sir”—said the imperturbable godly-looking Bunger, slightly bowing to Ahab—“is apt to be facetious at times; he spins us many clever things of that sort. But I may as well say—en passant, as the French remark—that I myself—that is to say, Jack Bunger, late of the reverend clergy—am a strict total abstinence man; I never drink—”

“Water!” cried the captain; “he never drinks it; it's a sort of fits to him; fresh water throws him into the hydrophobia; but go on—go on with the arm story.”

“Yes, I may as well,” said the surgeon, coolly. “I was about observing, sir, before Captain Boomer's facetious interruption, that spite of my best and severest endeavors, the wound kept getting worse and worse; the truth was, sir, it was as ugly gaping wound as surgeon ever saw; more than two feet and several inches long. I measured it with the lead line. In short, it grew black; I knew what was threatened, and off it came. But I had no hand in shipping that ivory arm there; that thing is against all rule”—pointing at it with the marlingspike—“that is the captain's work, not mine; he ordered the carpenter to make it; he had that club-hammer there put to the end, to knock some one's brains out with, I suppose, as he tried mine once. He flies into diabolical passions sometimes. Do ye see this dent, sir”—removing his hat, and brushing aside his hair, and exposing a bowl-like cavity in his skull, but which bore not the slightest scarry trace, or any token of ever having been a wound—“Well, the captain there will tell you how that came here; he knows.”

“No, I don't,” said the captain, “but his mother did; he was born with it. Oh, you solemn rogue, you—you Bunger! was there ever such another Bunger in the watery world? Bunger, when you die, you ought to die in pickle, you dog; you should be preserved to future ages, you rascal.”

“What became of the White Whale?” now cried Ahab, who thus far had been impatiently listening to this by-play between the two Englishmen.

“Oh!” cried the one-armed captain, “oh, yes! Well; after he sounded, we didn't see him again for some time; in fact, as I before hinted, I didn't then know what whale it was that had served me such a trick, till some time afterwards, when coming back to the Line, we heard about Moby Dick—as some call him—and then I knew it was he.”

“Did'st thou cross his wake again?”

“Twice.”

“But could not fasten?”

“Didn't want to try to: ain't one limb enough? What should I do without this other arm? And I'm thinking Moby Dick doesn't bite so much as he swallows.”

“Well, then,” interrupted Bunger, “give him your left arm for bait to get the right. Do you know, gentlemen”—very gravely and mathematically bowing to each Captain in succession—“Do you know, gentlemen, that the digestive organs of the whale are so inscrutably constructed by Divine Providence, that it is quite impossible for him to completely digest even a man's arm? And he knows it too. So that what you take for the White Whale's malice is only his awkwardness. For he never means to swallow a single limb; he only thinks to terrify by feints. But sometimes he is like the old juggling fellow, formerly a patient of mine in Ceylon, that making believe swallow jack-knives, once upon a time let one drop into him in good earnest, and there it stayed for a twelvemonth or more; when I gave him an emetic, and he heaved it up in small tacks, d'ye see. No possible way for him to digest that jack-knife, and fully incorporate it into his general bodily system. Yes, Captain Boomer, if you are quick enough about it, and have a mind to pawn one arm for the sake of the privilege of giving decent burial to the other, why in that case the arm is yours; only let the whale have another chance at you shortly, that's all.”

“No, thank ye, Bunger,” said the English Captain, “he's welcome to the arm he has, since I can't help it, and didn't know him then; but not to another one. No more White Whales for me; I've lowered for him once, and that has satisfied me. There would be great glory in killing him, I know that; and there is a ship-load of precious sperm in him, but, hark ye, he's best let alone; don't you think so, Captain?”—glancing at the ivory leg.

“He is. But he will still be hunted, for all that. What is best let alone, that accursed thing is not always what least allures. He's all a magnet! How long since thou saw'st him last? Which way heading?”

“Bless my soul, and curse the foul fiend's,” cried Bunger, stoopingly walking round Ahab, and like a dog, strangely snuffing; “this man's blood—bring the thermometer!—it's at the boiling point!—his pulse makes these planks beat!—sir!”—taking a lancet from his pocket, and drawing near to Ahab's arm.

“Avast!” roared Ahab, dashing him against the bulwarks—“Man the boat! Which way heading?”

“Good God!” cried the English Captain, to whom the question was put. “What's the matter? He was heading east, I think.—Is your Captain crazy?” whispering Fedallah.

But Fedallah, putting a finger on his lip, slid over the bulwarks to take the boat's steering oar, and Ahab, swinging the cutting-tackle towards him, commanded the ship's sailors to stand by to lower.

In a moment he was standing in the boat's stern, and the Manilla men were springing to their oars. In vain the English Captain hailed him. With back to the stranger ship, and face set like a flint to his own, Ahab stood upright till alongside of the Pequod.
