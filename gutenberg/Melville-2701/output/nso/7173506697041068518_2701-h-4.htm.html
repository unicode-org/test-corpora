Ge ke sepela, ke be ke tletše ka go nagana; seo se bego se utollotšwe go nna ka tshoganetšo sa Mokapotene Ahaba, se ile sa ntlatša ka go se kwagale mo go itšego ga hlaga ga bohloko mabapi le yena. Gomme ka tsela ye nngwe, ka nako yeo, ke ile ka kwa kwelobohloko le manyami ka yena, eupša ka baka la ga ke tsebe gore ke eng, ntle le ge e be e le tahlegelo ye sehlogo ya leoto la gagwe. Gomme le ge go le bjalo gape ke ile ka kwa poifo ye e makatšago ka yena; eupša mohuta woo wa poifo, yeo ke sa kgonego go e hlalosa le gatee, e be e se poifo gabotse; Ga ke tsebe gore e be e le eng. Eupša ke ile ka e kwa; gomme ga se ya nthibela go yena; le ge ke ile ka ikwa ke hloka kgotlelelo go seo se bego se bonagala e le sephiri go yena, ka mo go sa phethagalago kudu bjalo ka ge a be a tsebja ke nna ka nako yeo. Lega go le bjalo, dikgopolo tša-ka mafelelong di ile tša išwa ka mahlakoreng a mangwe, moo e lego gore go lefsifsi la gona bjale Ahaba o ile a thelela monagano wa-ka.





KGAOLO YA 17. Ramadan.

Bjalo ka ge Ramadan ya Queequeg, goba Go ikona dijo le go Kokobetšwa, e be e swanetše go tšwela pele letšatši ka moka, ga se ka kgetha go mo tshwenya go fihlela go leba bošego; ka gobane ke tšeela godimo tlhompho e kgolo kudu go ditlamo tša bodumedi tša motho yo mongwe le yo mongwe, le ka mohla ke sa nagane kamoo e lego ya metlae ka gona, gomme ga se ka kgona go e hwetša ka pelong ya-ka go tšeela fase gaešita le phuthego ya ditšhošwane yeo e rapelago setulo sa ditšhošane; goba dibopiwa tšeo tše dingwe dikarolong tše itšego tša lefase la rena, tšeo ka tekanyo e itšego ya go sepela ka maoto yeo e sa kago ya ba gona pele dipolaneteng tše dingwe, di khunama pele ga setho sa mmele sa mong wa naga yo a hwilego feela ka baka la dithoto tše di sa leka-lekanego tšeo di sa dutšego di e-na le tšona le tšeo di hirišwago ka leina la gagwe.

Ke re, rena Bakriste ba ba botse ba Presbyterian re swanetše go ba ba botho dilong tše, gomme re se ke ra ikgantšha kudu go feta batho ba bangwe ba ba hwago, baheitene le seo e sego, ka lebaka la boikgogomošo bja bona bja seripagare sa bohlanya ka ditaba tše. Go be go na le Queequeg, bjale, ruri a thabiša dikgopolo tše di sa kwagalego kudu ka Yojo le Ramadan ya gagwe;—eupša go thwe’ng ka seo? Queequeg o be a nagana gore o tseba seo a bego a le ka sona, ke nagana gore; o be a bonagala a kgotsofetše; a khutše moo. Go ngangišana ga rena ka moka le yena go be go ka se holege; a a be, ke re: gomme Legodimo le re gaugele ka moka—Mapresbyterian le Baheitene ka go swana—ka gobane ka moka ga rena ka tsela ye nngwe re phatlogile ka tsela ye e tšhošago ka hlogo, gomme ka manyami re hloka go lokišwa.

Go ya mantšiboa, ge ke ikwa ke kgonthišeditšwe gore ditiragatšo tša gagwe ka moka le meetlo di swanetše go ba di fedile, ke ile ka rotogela phapošing ya gagwe gomme ka kokota mojakong; eupša ga go na karabo. Ke ile ka leka go e bula, eupša e be e kgomaretšwe ka gare. “Queequeg,” ka realo ka boleta ka lešoba la senotlelo:—ka moka ba homotše. “Ke re, Queequeg! ke ka baka la’ng o sa bolele? Ke nna—Ishmaele.” Eupša ka moka di ile tša dula di sa šišinyege go etša pele. Ke ile ka thoma go tšhoga kudu. Ke be ke mo dumeletše nako e ntši gakaakaa; Ke be ke nagana gore a ka ba a bile le bolwetši bja apoplectic fit. Ka lebelela ka gare ga lešoba la senotlelo; eupša lebati le bulega sekhutlong se se makatšago sa phapoši, tebelelo ya lešoba la senotlelo e be e fo ba e kgopamego le e mpe. Ke be ke kgona go bona fela karolo ya foot-board ya mpete le mothaladi wa lebota, eupša go se selo se se fetago seo. Ke ile ka makatšwa ke go bona e khuditše lebotong shaft ya kota ya harpoon ya Queequeg, yeo mong wa ntlo mantšiboeng a go feta a bego a e tšere go yena, pele ga ge re namela ka phapošing. Seo se a makatša, ke ile ka nagana; eupša ka tekanyo efe goba efe, ka ge harpoon e eme kua, gomme e le ka sewelo goba ga e ke e ya dinageng tša ka ntle ntle le yona, ka fao o swanetše go ba ka gare mo, gomme ga go na phošo ye e kgonegago.

“Queequeg!—Queequeg!”—ka moka di sa dutše di sa le gona. Go swanetše go ba go diregile selo se sengwe. Apoplexy ya go ba le mmele! Ke ile ka leka go thunya lebati; eupša e ile ya ganetša ka manganga. Ge ke kitima ka manamelo, ke ile ka botša dipelaelo tša-ka ka pela go motho wa mathomo yo ke kopanego le yena—mohlanka wa ka phapošing. “La! la!” a lla, “Ke be ke nagana gore se sengwe e swanetše go ba taba. Ke ile ka ya go dira malao ka morago ga dijo tša mesong, gomme lebati le be le notletšwe; gomme e sego towe yeo e swanetšego go kwewa; gomme e be e no homola kudu ga e sa le go tloga ka nako yeo. Eupša ke ile ka nagana, e ka ba, bobedi bja lena le be le tlogile gomme la notlela merwalo ya gago ka gare gore e bolokwe ka polokego. La! la, mohumagadi!—Mong wa mosadi! polao! Mohumagadi Hussey! apoplexy!”—gomme ka dillo tše, o ile a kitimela ka khitšhing, ke latela.

Mohumagadi Hussey o ile a tšwelela kapejana, a swere pitša ya mosetareta ka seatleng se sengwe le sebjana sa asene ka go se sengwe, a sa tšwa go kgaogana le mošomo wa go hlokomela di-castor, gomme a omanya mošemane wa gagwe yo monnyane wa mothomoso mo nakong ye.

“Ntlo ya dikota!” ka goeletša, “ke tsela efe go ya go yona? Kitima ka baka la Modimo, gomme o tliše selo seo o tlago go se bula lebati—selepe!—selepe! o bile le seterouku; ithekga ka yona!”—gomme ke bolela bjalo ke be ke kitimela godimo ka manamelo ka mo go sa swanelago gape ke se na selo, ge Mohumagadi Hussey a tsena gare ga pitša ya mosetareta le sebjana sa asene, le castor ka moka ya sefahlego sa gagwe.

“Bothata ke eng ka wena lesogana?”

“Tšea selepe! Ka baka la Modimo, kitimela ngaka, yo mongwe, mola ke e phumola!”

“Bona mo,” a realo mong wa ntlo, a bea fase ka pela sebjana sa asene, gore seatla se tee se lokologe; “lebelela mo; na o bolela ka go bula le ge e le efe ya mejako ya-ka ka go phuruphutša?”—gomme ka seo o ile a swara letsogo la-ka. “Bothata ke eng ka wena? Taba ke eng ka wena, modirišani wa sekepe?”

Ka mokgwa o iketlilego, eupša o akgofilego ka mo go kgonegago, ke ile ka mo nea gore a kwešiše molato ka moka. A opa diatla ka go sa lemoge sebjana sa asene ka lehlakoreng le lengwe la nko ya gagwe, o ile a ruminated ka ponyo ya leihlo; ke moka a goeletša ka gore—“Aowa! Ga se ka e bona ga e sa le ke e bea moo.” A kitimela ka phapošing ye nnyane ya go bea diaparo ka fase ga lefelo la go kotama la manamelo, o ile a gadima ka gare, gomme a boa, a mpotša gore harpoon ya Queequeg e be e se gona. O ile a lla ka gore: “O ipolaile.” “Ke madimabe Stiggs a dira gape—go ya counterpane ye nngwe—Modimo a kwela mmagwe yo a diilago bohloko!—e tla ba tshenyo ya ntlo ya ka. Na mošemane wa modiidi o na le kgaetšedi? Ngwanenyana yoo o kae?—gona, Betty, eya go Snarles the Painter, gomme o mmotše gore a npenta leswao, ka—“ga go na go ipolaya mo go dumeletšwego mo, le go se kgoge ka phapošing ya go jela;”—go ka ba gabotse go bolaya dinonyana tše ka bobedi ka nako e tee. Bolaya? Morena a gaugele moya wa gagwe! Ke eng lešata leo? Wena lesogana, avast kua!”

Gomme a kitimela godimo ka morago ga ka, o ile a ntshwara ge ke be ke leka gape go bula lebati ka kgapeletšo.

“Ga ke e dumelele; Nka se be le lefelo la ka le sentšwe. Eya go mošomi wa dinotlelo, go na le ye tee ye e ka bago maele go tloga mo. Eupša avast!” a tsenya seatla sa gagwe ka potleng ya gagwe ya ka thoko, “se senotlelo se se tlago go lekana, ke a gopola; a re bone.” Gomme ka seo, a e retološa ka gare ga senotlelo; eupša, go madimabe! Bolt ya tlaleletšo ya Queequeg e ile ya dula e sa gogelwe ka gare.

“Ke swanetše go e thunya,” ka realo, gomme ke be ke kitima go theoga ka tsela ya go tsena gannyane, go thoma gabotse, ge mong wa ntlo a ntshwara, gape a ikana gore ga se ka swanela go thuba meago ya gagwe; eupša ke ile ka mo gagola, gomme ka go kitimela ga tšhoganetšo ga mmele ka kitimela ka botlalo kgahlanong le leswao.

Ka lešata le le makatšago lebati le ile la fofa la bulega, gomme konotswana ya thula lebota, ya romela polasetara siling; gomme fao, magodimo a mabotse! go be go dutše Queequeg, ka moka ga gagwe yo a fodilego e bile a ikgobokeditše; thwi bogareng bja phapoši; a khukhunela godimo ga di-ham tša gagwe, gomme a swere Yojo ka godimo ga hlogo ya gagwe. O be a sa lebelele ka tsela ye nngwe goba ka tsela ye nngwe, eupša o be a dutše bjalo ka seswantšho se se betlilwego seo se nago le leswao le le hlaelelago la bophelo bjo bo mafolofolo.

“Queequeg,” ka realo, ka ya godimo go yena, “Queequeg, bothata ke eng ka wena?”

“Ga se a ba sittin’ so letšatši ka moka, akere?” a realo mong wa ntlo.

Eupša tšohle tšeo re di boletšego, ga go lentšu le tee leo re bego re ka go goga go yena; Ke ile ka nyakile ke ikwa ke nyaka go mo kgoromeletša godimo, gore ke fetoše maemo a gagwe, ka gobane e be e nyakile e sa kgotlelelege, e be e bonagala e thibetšwe ka tsela ye bohloko kudu le yeo e sego ya tlhago; kudu-kudu, bjalo ka ge go kgonega ka moka a be a dutše bjalo ka diiri tše di fetago tše seswai goba tše lesome, a sepela le yena ntle le dijo tša gagwe tša ka mehla.

"Mdi. Hussey,” ka realo, “o a phela ditiragalong ka moka; ka gona, re tlogele, ge o rata, ke tla kgonthišetša taba ye e makatšago ka bonna.”

Ge ke tswalela mong wa ntlo mojako, ke ile ka leka go fenya Queequeg gore a tšee setulo; eupša e le lefeela. O ile a dula moo; gomme sohle seo a bego a ka se dira—bakeng sa bokgabo bja-ka ka moka bja maitshwaro le di- blandishment—o be a ka se šišinye peg, goba go bolela le lentšu le tee, gaešita le go ntebelela, goba go lemoga go ba gona ga-ka ka tsela e nyenyane.

Ke ipotšiša, ke ile ka nagana, ge e ba se se ka kgona go ba karolo ya Ramadan ya gagwe; na ba ikona dijo ka di- ham tša bona ka tsela yeo sehlakahlakeng sa gabo. Go swanetše go ba bjalo; ee, ke karolo ya tumelo ya gagwe, ke a nagana; gabotse, gona, a a khutše; o tla tsoga kapejana goba ka morago, ga go pelaelo. E ka se swarelele go ya go ile, re leboga Modimo, gomme Ramadan ya gagwe e tla gatee fela ka ngwaga; gomme ga ke dumele gore e swara nako kudu ka nako yeo.

Ke ile ka theogela dijong tša mantšiboa. Ka morago ga go dula nako ye telele a theeleditše dikanegelo tše telele tša basesiši ba bangwe bao ba bego ba sa tšwa go tšwa leetong la plum-pudding, bjalo ka ge ba be ba le bitša (ke gore, leeto le lekopana la go tsoma maruarua ka schooner goba brig, le tswaletšwe ka leboa la mothaladi , ka Lewatleng la Atlantic feela); ka morago ga go theeletša di-plum-puddinger tše go fihla mo e nyakilego go ba iri ya lesometee, ke ile ka namela manamelo go yo robala, ke ikwa ke kgodišegile kudu ka nako ye Queequeg ruri o swanetše go ba a tlišitše Ramadan ya gagwe mafelelong. Eupša aowa; fao o be a no ba fao ke mo tlogetšego gona; o be a se a šišinya le intšhi. Ke ile ka thoma go mo tshwenyega; go be go bonagala go se na tlhaologanyo ka mo go feletšego le go hlanya go ba a dutše fao letšatši ka moka le seripa sa bošego godimo ga diham tša gagwe ka phapošing ye e tonyago, a swere seripa sa kota hlogong ya gagwe.

“Ka baka la legodimo, Queequeg, tsoga o itšhišinye; tsoga o je dijo tša mantšiboa. O tla bolawa ke tlala; o tla ipolaya, Queequeg.” Eupša ga go lentšu le tee leo a ilego a le araba.

Ka gona, ka ge ke be ke mo itlhobogile, ke ile ka ikemišetša go robala le go robala; gomme ka ntle le pelaelo, pele ga nako ye kgolo, o be a tla ntatela. Eupša pele ga go retologela ka gare, ke ile ka tšea baki ya ka ye boima ya letlalo la bere, gomme ka e lahlela godimo ga gagwe, ka ge e be e holofetša go ba bošego bjo bo tonyago kudu; gomme o be a se na selo ge e se baki ya gagwe ye e tlwaelegilego ya nkgokolo. Ka nako e itšego, dira sohle seo ke bego ke tla se dira, ke be ke sa kgone go tsena ka gare ga boroko bjo bo fokolago kudu. Ke be ke timetše kerese; le kgopolo feela ya Queequeg—e sego dimithara tše nne go tloga—a dutše moo boemong bjoo bjo bo sa iketlago, a le noši a nnoši go tonya le leswiswing; se se ile sa ntira gore ke be yo a nyamilego e le ka kgonthe. Nagana ka yona; a robala bošego ka moka ka phapošing e tee le moheitene yo a phafogilego kudu godimo ga di- ham tša gagwe Ramadan ye e nyamišago, yeo e sa ikarabelego!

Eupša ka tsela ye nngwe ke ile ka theoga mafelelong, gomme ka se tsebe selo se sengwe go fihla go hlaba ga mosegare; ge, a lebelela ka thoko ga malao, fao Queequeg a be a khukhunetše, bjalo ka ge eka o be a kgokološitšwe fase fase. Eupša gateetee ge go bona letšatši la mathomo go tsena ka lefasetereng, godimo o ile a fihla, ka malokololo a thata le a go grating, eupša ka tebelelo ya lethabo; a golofala a lebile go nna moo ke robetšego gona; a gatelela phatla ya gagwe gape kgahlanong le ya ka; gomme a re Ramadan ya gagwe e fedile.

Bjale, bjalo ka ge ke ile ka šišinya pele, ga ke na kganetšo go bodumedi bja motho le ge e le ofe, e ka ba seo e ka bago sona, ge feela motho yoo a sa bolaye goba a goboša motho yo mongwe, ka gobane motho yoo yo mongwe le yena ga a bo dumele. Eupša ge bodumedi bja monna bo thoma go tšhoga e le ka kgonthe; ge e le tlhokofatšo e botse go yena; gomme, mafelelong, e dira lefase le la rena ntlo ya baeti yeo e sa kgahlišego go robala go yona; ke moka ke nagana gore ke nako ya godimo ya go tšea motho yoo ka thoko gomme ke ngangišane le yena ntlha yeo.

Gomme go no ba bjalo bjale ke dirile ka Queequeg. “Queequeg,” ka realo, “kena malaong bjale, gomme o robale gomme o ntheeletše.” Ke moka ke ile ka tšwela pele, go thoma ka go hlatloga le tšwelopele ya madumedi a pele, gomme ka theogela madumeding a fapa-fapanego a nako ya gona bjale, nakong yeo ke ile ka šoma ka thata go bontšha Queequeg gore Dilente tše ka moka, Ramadan le di-ham-squatting tše telele ka go tonya , diphapoši tše di se nago lethabo e be e le bošilo bjo bo feletšego; e mpe bakeng sa bophelo bo botle; e se nago mohola bakeng sa moya; kgahlanong, ka boripana, le melao e lego molaleng ya Bohlweki le tlhaologanyo e tlwaelegilego. Ke ile ka mmotša, gape, gore ka ge a le dilong tše dingwe e le sehlogo se se nago le tlhaologanyo ye e feteletšego le ya bohlale, go ile gwa nkweša bohloko, go ile gwa nkweša bohloko kudu, go mmona bjale a le setlaela ka mo go nyamišago kudu ka Ramadan ye ya gagwe ya go segiša. Ntle le moo, ke ile ka phega kgang, go ikona dijo go dira gore mmele o tsene ka gare; ke ka baka leo moya o gaga ka gare; gomme dikgopolo ka moka tšeo di belegwego ke go ikona dijo di swanetše go bolawa ke tlala ka seripa-gare. Le ke lebaka leo ka lona bontši bja badumedi ba dyspeptic ba tšeelago godimo dikgopolo tše bjalo tše di nyamišago mabapi le mahu a bona. Ka lentšu le tee, Queequeg, ka realo, go e na le go aroga; dihele ke kgopolo yeo e belegwego la mathomo godimo ga apole-dumpling yeo e sa šilegego; gomme ga e sa le go tloga ka nako yeo e tšwetšwa pele ka dyspepsias ya leabela yeo e godišitšwego ke Ramadan.

Ke moka ke ile ka botšiša Queequeg ge e ba yena ka noši a kile a tshwenywa ke dyspepsia; go hlagiša kgopolo yeo ka mo go kwagalago kudu, gore a kgone go e tšea ka gare A re aowa; feela tiragalong e tee yeo e sa lebalegego. E be e le ka morago ga monyanya o mogolo wo o filwego ke tatagwe kgoši, ka go hwetša ntwa ye kgolo yeo go yona ba masomehlano ba lenaba ba bego ba bolailwe mo e ka bago ka iri ya bobedi thapama, gomme ka moka ba apeilwe le go jewa mantšiboeng ao.

“Ga go sa na, Queequeg,” ka realo, ke thothomela; “seo se tla dira;” ka gobane ke be ke tseba diphetho tšeo ntle le go di šišinya gape. Ke be ke bone mosesiši yo a etetšego sona sehlakahlaka seo, gomme a mpotša gore e be e le mokgwa, ge ntwa ye kgolo e hweditšwe moo, go beša bohle bao ba bolailwego ka jarateng goba serapeng sa mofenyi; gomme ka morago, ka e tee ka e tee, di be di bewa ka gare ga diforo tše kgolo tša kota, gomme tša kgabišwa go dikologa bjalo ka pilau, ka dienywa tša borotho le dikhokhonate; gomme ka parsley ye nngwe ka melomong ya bona, ba ile ba romelwa go dikologa ka ditheto tša mofenyi go bagwera ba gagwe ka moka, go no swana le ge dimpho tše e be e le di-turkey tše ntši kudu tša Keresemose.

Taba ke gore, ga ke nagane gore dipolelo tša-ka mabapi le bodumedi di ile tša kgoma Queequeg kudu. Ka gobane, lefelong la pele, ka tsela e itšego o be a bonagala a le lerootho go kwa ka taba yeo e bohlokwa, ka ntle le ge e ka lebelelwa go ya ka pono ya gagwe ka noši; gomme, lefelong la bobedi, ga se a ka a nkwešiša go feta tee-tharong, a sofa dikgopolo tša ka feela bjalo ka ge ke be ke tla dira; gomme, mafelelong, ka ntle le pelaelo o be a nagana gore o tseba mo gontši kudu ka bodumedi bja therešo go feta nna. O ile a ntebelela ka mohuta wa go tshwenyega le kwelobohloko ya go ikokobetša, bjalo ka ge eka o be a nagana gore e le mo go kwešago bohloko kudu gore lesogana le le bjalo le le nago le tlhaologanyo le swanetše go lahlega ka mo go se nago kholofelo gakaakaa borapedi bja boheitene bja boebangedi.

Mafelelong re ile ra tsoga gomme ra apara; le Queequeg, re tšea dijo tša mesong tše di makatšago ka mo go feletšego tša di- chowder tša mehuta ka moka, e le gore mong wa ntlo a se dire poelo e ntši ka baka la Ramadan ya gagwe, re ile ra sallied ntle go namela Pequod, re sauntering gotee, gomme ra kgetha meno a rena ka marapo a halibut.





KGAOLO YA 18. Letshwao la gagwe.

Ge re be re sepela go theoga mafelelong a boemakepe go leba sekepeng, Queequeg a rwele harpoon ya gagwe, Mokapotene Peleg ka lentšu la gagwe le le makgwakgwa o ile a re tumiša ka go hlaboša go tšwa go wigwam ya gagwe, a re ga se a belaela gore mogwera wa ka ke motho yo a jago batho, gomme go feta fao a tsebiša gore o dumeletše aowa batho ba go ja batho bao ba bego ba le ka sekepeng seo sa diatla, ntle le ge ba be ba tšweleditše dipampiri tša bona peleng.

“O ra go reng ka seo, Mokapotene Peleg?” a realo nna, bjale ke tlola godimo ga dibo, gomme ka tlogela modirišani wa ka a eme boemakepeng.

O ile a araba ka gore: “Ke ra gore o swanetše go bontšha dipampiri tša gagwe.”

“Ee,” a realo Mokapotene Bildad ka lentšu la gagwe la sekoti, a kgomaretša hlogo ya gagwe go tšwa ka morago ga ya Peleg, ka ntle ga wigwam. “O swanetše go bontšha gore o sokologile. Morwa wa leswiswi,” a tlaleletša, a retologela go Queequeg, “na ga bjale o kopanela le kereke le ge e le efe ya Bokriste?”

“Ka baka la eng,” ka realo, “ke leloko la Kereke ya Phuthego ya mathomo.” Mona a go bolelwe, gore batho ba bantši ba sehlogo bao ba nago le di- tattoo bao ba sesago ka dikepeng tša Nantucket mafelelong ba tla go fetošetšwa dikereke.

Bildad o ile a goeletša ka gore: “Kereke ya Phuthego ya Pele, eng! yeo e rapelago ka ntlong ya kopano ya Motiakone Doiteronomio Coleman?” gomme a bolela bjalo, a ntšha digalase tša gagwe tša mahlo, a di silila ka sakatuku ya gagwe ye kgolo ye serolane ya bandana, gomme a di apara ka kelohloko kudu, a tšwa ka gare ga wigwam, gomme a ithekga ka go tia godimo ga dibo, a lebelela Queequeg ka nako ye telele gabotse.

“E bile setho ka nako e kaaka’ng?” ke moka a re, a retologela go nna; “e sego ye telele kudu, ke rata go phopholetša, lesogana.”

“Aowa,” a realo Peleg, “gomme le yena ga se a kolobetšwa gabotse, goba e be e tla hlatswa ye nngwe ya botala bja leratadima bjoo bja diabolo sefahlegong sa gagwe.”

Bildad o ile a goeletša ka gore: “Bolela, bjale, na Mofilisita yo ke setho sa ka mehla sa kopano ya Motiakone Doiteronomio? Ga se nke ka mmona a eya moo, gomme ke e fetiša letšatši le lengwe le le lengwe la Morena.”

KGAOLO YA 20. Bohle Astir.

Letšatši goba a mabedi le ile la feta, gomme gwa ba le modiro o mogolo ka sekepeng sa Pequod. Ga se fela gore diseila tša kgale di be di lokišwa, eupša diseila tše mpsha di be di etla ka sekepeng, le dibolutu tša lešela, le dikhoile tša rigging; ka boripana, se sengwe le se sengwe se be se bontšha gore ditokišetšo tša sekepe di be di akgofela go fihla mafelelong. Eupša go bonagala ka mehla ba nea tsebišo e telele kudu maemong a, gomme sekepe ga se sa ka sa sesa ka matšatši a mmalwa. Eupša ga go makatše; go be go na le kgwebišano ye botse yeo e swanetšego go dirwa, gomme ga go na go bolela gore ke dilo tše kae tšeo di swanetšego go naganwa, pele ga ge Pequod e be e hlometšwe ka botlalo.

Yo mongwe le yo mongwe o tseba gore ke dilo tše dintši gakaakang—diphate, dipane tša sose, dithipa le diforoko, digarafo le di- tong, di- napkin, di- nut-cracker le seo e sego bjalo, e lego tšeo di sa nyakegego kgwebong ya go hlokomela ntlo. Go fo bjalo le ka go tsoma maruarua, mo go nyakago go hlokomelwa ga ntlo ka nywaga e meraro lewatleng le le nabilego, kgole le barekiši ka moka ba dijo, barekiši ba di- coster, dingaka, bapeki ba dinkgwa le bo-ra-dipanka. Le gona gaešita le ge se se swarelela le ka dikepe tša bagwebi, le ge go le bjalo e sego ka tsela le ge e le efe go fihla bokgoleng bjo bo swanago le bja banna ba maruarua. Gobane ka ntle le botelele bjo bogolo bja leeto la go tsoma maruarua, dilo tše dintši tšeo di kgethegilego go sekišweng ga boreahlapi, le go se kgonege ga go di tšeela legato maemakepeng a kgole ao gantši a etelwago gantši, go swanetše go gopolwa gore go dikepe ka moka, dikepe tša go rema maruarua ke tšona di pepentšhitšwego kudu go dikotsi tša mehuta ka moka, gomme kudu-kudu go tshenyo le go lahlegelwa ke tšona dilo tšeo katlego ya leeto e ithekgilego kudu ka tšona. Ka fao, dikepe tša go se šomišwe, di-spar tša go se šomišwe, le mela ya go se šomišwe le di-harpoon, le dilo ka moka tša go se šomišwe, mo e nyakilego go ba, eupša Mokapotene wa go se šomišwe le sekepe sa go ipoeletša.

Nakong ya go fihla ga rena Sehlakahlakeng, polokelo e boima kudu ya Pequod e be e nyakile e phethilwe; yeo e akaretšago nama ya gagwe ya kgomo, borotho, meetse, makhura le di- hoop le dikota tša tšhipi. Eupša, bjalo ka ge go šišinywa pele, ka nako ye nngwe go be go na le go lata le go rwala ka sekepeng ga divers odds le mafelelo a dilo, bobedi tše kgolo le tše nnyane.

Kgoši gare ga bao ba dirilego go lata le go rwala mo e be e le kgaetšedi ya Mokapotene Bildad, mohumagadi wa kgale yo a se nago makhura wa moya wo o ikemišeditšego kudu le wo o sa lapego, eupša withal wa pelo ye botho kudu, yo a bego a bonagala a ikemišeditše gore, ge a ka kgona go e thuša, ga go selo seo se swanetšego go hwetšwa se hloka ka Pequod , ka morago ga go fihla lewatleng ka toka gatee. Ka nako e nngwe o be a tla namela sekepe a swere nkgo ya dipikiri bakeng sa lefelo la go bolokela dijo la mohlokomedi; nako e nngwe ka sehlopha sa di- quill bakeng sa tafola ya molekane yo mogolo, moo a bego a boloka kota ya gagwe; a third time with a roll of flannel for the small of some one's rheumatic back. Never did any woman better deserve her name, which was Charity—Aunt Charity, as everybody called her. And like a sister of charity did this charitable Aunt Charity bustle about hither and thither, ready to turn her hand and heart to anything that promised to yield safety, comfort, and consolation to all on board a ship in which her beloved brother Bildad was concerned, and in which she herself owned a score or two of well-saved dollars.

But it was startling to see this excellent hearted Quakeress coming on board, as she did the last day, with a long oil-ladle in one hand, and a still longer whaling lance in the other. Nor was Bildad himself nor Captain Peleg at all backward. As for Bildad, he carried about with him a long list of the articles needed, and at every fresh arrival, down went his mark opposite that article upon the paper. Every once in a while Peleg came hobbling out of his whalebone den, roaring at the men down the hatchways, roaring up to the riggers at the mast-head, and then concluded by roaring back into his wigwam.

During these days of preparation, Queequeg and I often visited the craft, and as often I asked about Captain Ahab, and how he was, and when he was going to come on board his ship. To these questions they would answer, that he was getting better and better, and was expected aboard every day; meantime, the two captains, Peleg and Bildad, could attend to everything necessary to fit the vessel for the voyage. If I had been downright honest with myself, I would have seen very plainly in my heart that I did but half fancy being committed this way to so long a voyage, without once laying my eyes on the man who was to be the absolute dictator of it, so soon as the ship sailed out upon the open sea. But when a man suspects any wrong, it sometimes happens that if he be already involved in the matter, he insensibly strives to cover up his suspicions even from himself. And much this way it was with me. I said nothing, and tried to think nothing.

At last it was given out that some time next day the ship would certainly sail. So next morning, Queequeg and I took a very early start.





CHAPTER 21. Going Aboard.

It was nearly six o'clock, but only grey imperfect misty dawn, when we drew nigh the wharf.

“There are some sailors running ahead there, if I see right,” said I to Queequeg, “it can't be shadows; she's off by sunrise, I guess; come on!”

“Avast!” cried a voice, whose owner at the same time coming close behind us, laid a hand upon both our shoulders, and then insinuating himself between us, stood stooping forward a little, in the uncertain twilight, strangely peering from Queequeg to me. It was Elijah.

“Going aboard?”

“Hands off, will you,” said I.

“Lookee here,” said Queequeg, shaking himself, “go 'way!”

“Ain't going aboard, then?”

“Yes, we are,” said I, “but what business is that of yours? Do you know, Mr. Elijah, that I consider you a little impertinent?”

“No, no, no; I wasn't aware of that,” said Elijah, slowly and wonderingly looking from me to Queequeg, with the most unaccountable glances.

“Elijah,” said I, “you will oblige my friend and me by withdrawing. We are going to the Indian and Pacific Oceans, and would prefer not to be detained.”

“Ye be, be ye? Coming back afore breakfast?”

“He's cracked, Queequeg,” said I, “come on.”

“Holloa!” cried stationary Elijah, hailing us when we had removed a few paces.

“Never mind him,” said I, “Queequeg, come on.”

But he stole up to us again, and suddenly clapping his hand on my shoulder, said—“Did ye see anything looking like men going towards that ship a while ago?”

Struck by this plain matter-of-fact question, I answered, saying, “Yes, I thought I did see four or five men; but it was too dim to be sure.”

“Very dim, very dim,” said Elijah. “Morning to ye.”

Once more we quitted him; but once more he came softly after us; and touching my shoulder again, said, “See if you can find 'em now, will ye?

“Find who?”

“Morning to ye! morning to ye!” he rejoined, again moving off. “Oh! I was going to warn ye against—but never mind, never mind—it's all one, all in the family too;—sharp frost this morning, ain't it? Good-bye to ye. Shan't see ye again very soon, I guess; unless it's before the Grand Jury.” And with these cracked words he finally departed, leaving me, for the moment, in no small wonderment at his frantic impudence.

At last, stepping on board the Pequod, we found everything in profound quiet, not a soul moving. The cabin entrance was locked within; the hatches were all on, and lumbered with coils of rigging. Going forward to the forecastle, we found the slide of the scuttle open. Seeing a light, we went down, and found only an old rigger there, wrapped in a tattered pea-jacket. He was thrown at whole length upon two chests, his face downwards and inclosed in his folded arms. The profoundest slumber slept upon him.

“Those sailors we saw, Queequeg, where can they have gone to?” said I, looking dubiously at the sleeper. But it seemed that, when on the wharf, Queequeg had not at all noticed what I now alluded to; hence I would have thought myself to have been optically deceived in that matter, were it not for Elijah's otherwise inexplicable question. But I beat the thing down; and again marking the sleeper, jocularly hinted to Queequeg that perhaps we had best sit up with the body; telling him to establish himself accordingly. He put his hand upon the sleeper's rear, as though feeling if it was soft enough; and then, without more ado, sat quietly down there.

“Gracious! Queequeg, don't sit there,” said I.

“Oh! perry dood seat,” said Queequeg, “my country way; won't hurt him face.”

“Face!” said I, “call that his face? very benevolent countenance then; but how hard he breathes, he's heaving himself; get off, Queequeg, you are heavy, it's grinding the face of the poor. Get off, Queequeg! Look, he'll twitch you off soon. I wonder he don't wake.”

Queequeg removed himself to just beyond the head of the sleeper, and lighted his tomahawk pipe. I sat at the feet. We kept the pipe passing over the sleeper, from one to the other. Meanwhile, upon questioning him in his broken fashion, Queequeg gave me to understand that, in his land, owing to the absence of settees and sofas of all sorts, the king, chiefs, and great people generally, were in the custom of fattening some of the lower orders for ottomans; and to furnish a house comfortably in that respect, you had only to buy up eight or ten lazy fellows, and lay them round in the piers and alcoves. Besides, it was very convenient on an excursion; much better than those garden-chairs which are convertible into walking-sticks; upon occasion, a chief calling his attendant, and desiring him to make a settee of himself under a spreading tree, perhaps in some damp marshy place.

While narrating these things, every time Queequeg received the tomahawk from me, he flourished the hatchet-side of it over the sleeper's head.

“What's that for, Queequeg?”

“Perry easy, kill-e; oh! perry easy!”

He was going on with some wild reminiscences about his tomahawk-pipe, which, it seemed, had in its two uses both brained his foes and soothed his soul, when we were directly attracted to the sleeping rigger. The strong vapor now completely filling the contracted hole, it began to tell upon him. He breathed with a sort of muffledness; then seemed troubled in the nose; then revolved over once or twice; then sat up and rubbed his eyes.

“Holloa!” he breathed at last, “who be ye smokers?”

“Shipped men,” answered I, “when does she sail?”

“Aye, aye, ye are going in her, be ye? She sails to-day. The Captain came aboard last night.”

“What Captain?—Ahab?”

“Who but him indeed?”

I was going to ask him some further questions concerning Ahab, when we heard a noise on deck.

“Holloa! Starbuck's astir,” said the rigger. “He's a lively chief mate, that; good man, and a pious; but all alive now, I must turn to.” And so saying he went on deck, and we followed.

It was now clear sunrise. Soon the crew came on board in twos and threes; the riggers bestirred themselves; the mates were actively engaged; and several of the shore people were busy in bringing various last things on board. Meanwhile Captain Ahab remained invisibly enshrined within his cabin.
