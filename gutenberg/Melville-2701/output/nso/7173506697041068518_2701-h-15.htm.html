KGAOLO YA 74. Hlogo ya Leruarua la Peu—Pono ye e Fapanego.

Mo, bjale, ke maruarua a mabedi a magolo, a beile dihlogo tša ona mmogo; a re ba kopanele, re bee mmogo tša rena.

Go tatelano e kgolo ya di- leviathan tša folio, Leruarua la Peu le Leruarua la Tokelo ke tšeo di lemogegago kudu go fihla ga bjale. Ke tšona feela maruarua ao a tsongwago ka mehla ke motho. Go Nantucketer, di tšweletša dilo tše pedi tše di feteletšego tša mehuta ka moka e tsebjago ya maruarua. Bjalo ka ge phapano ya ka ntle magareng ga bona e bonagala kudu dihlogong tša bona; gomme bjalo ka hlogo ya ye nngwe le ye nngwe ke motsotso wo o fegilwe ka lehlakoreng la Pequod; gomme bjalo ka ge re ka tloga ka bolokologi go tloga go e nngwe go ya go e nngwe, ka go fo gata go putla mokatong wa sekepe:—ke kae, ke swanetše go rata go tseba, na o tlago go hwetša sebaka se sekaone sa go ithuta thuto ya di-cetology ye e šomago go feta mo?

Sa pele, o kgahlwa ke phapano ya kakaretšo magareng ga dihlogo tše. Bobedi bja tšona ke tše dikgolo ka mo go lekanego letswalong ka moka; eupša go na le tekatekano ye e itšego ya dipalo ka go Sperm Whale yeo ya Right Whale e e hlokago ka manyami. Go na le semelo se sengwe hlogong ya Sperm Whale. Ge o dutše o e bona, ka go se rate o ineela go phagama mo gogolo go yena, ka ntlha ya seriti se se apareditšego. Boemong bja bjale, le gona, seriti se se godišwa ke mmala wa pherefere le letswai wa hlogo ya gagwe tlhoreng, o nea leswao la nywaga e tšofetšego le phihlelo e kgolo. Ka boripana, ke seo ka tsela ya setegeniki barei ba dihlapi ba se bitšago “leruarua la dihlogo tše talalerata.”

Bjale anke re ela hloko seo se sa swanego kudu dihlogong tše—e lego ditho tše pedi tše bohlokwa kudu, leihlo le tsebe. Kgole morago ka lehlakoreng la hlogo, le fase fase, kgauswi le khutlo ya mohlagare wa maruarua le ge e le ofe, ge o ka phuruphutša ka tsela ye tshesane, mafelelong o tla bona leihlo le le se nago dintshi, leo o ka ratago go ba leihlo la pokolo ye nnyane; ka gona go tšwa tekanyong ka moka e lekana le bogolo bja hlogo.

Bjale, go tšwa boemong bjo bjo bo makatšago bja ka thoko bja mahlo a leruarua, go molaleng gore a ka se tsoge a bone selo seo se lego ka pele tlwaa, e sego go feta kamoo a ka kgonago ka gona ka morago. Ka lentšu le tee, boemo bja mahlo a leruarua bo sepelelana le bja ditsebe tša motho; gomme o ka ikgantšha, ka bowena, ka fao e bego e tla sepela ka gona le wena, na o ile wa hlahloba dilo ka thoko ka ditsebe tša gago. O be o tla hwetša gore o be o ka laela feela dikelo tše ka bago tše masometharo tša pono e sa le pele ga mothaladi o otlologilego wa ka thoko wa pono; le tše dingwe tše ka bago tše masometharo ka morago ga yona. Ge nkabe lenaba la gago le le babago kudu le be le sepela thwii le lebile go wena, le emišitšwe tšhoša ka letšatši le le nabilego, o be o ka se kgone go le bona, go feta ge nkabe le be le go utswa ka morago. Ka lentšu le tee, o be o tla ba le mekokotlo ye mebedi, ge re ka bolela bjalo; eupša, ka nako ye e swanago, gape, mahlakore a mabedi a ka pele (mahlakore a ka thoko): ka gobane ke eng seo se dirago gore motho a ka pele—ke eng, ka nnete, ge e se mahlo a gagwe?

Go feta moo, gaešita le ge bontšing bja diphoofolo tše dingwe tšeo ga bjale ke kgonago go di nagana, mahlo a bjetšwe ka tsela yeo e sa lemogegego gore a tswake matla a ona a go bona, e le gore a tšweletše seswantšho se tee e sego tše pedi bjokong; boemo bjo bo kgethegilego bja mahlo a leruarua, ao a arotšwego ka mo go atlegago bjalo ka ge a le bjalo ka dikhubikimitha tše dintši tša hlogo e tiilego, yeo e phagametšego magareng ga ona bjalo ka thaba e kgolo yeo e aroganyago matsha a mabedi ka meeding; se, go ba gona, se swanetše go aroganya ka botlalo dikgopolo tšeo setho se sengwe le se sengwe se se ikemetšego se di fetišago. Ka gona, leruarua le swanetše go bona seswantšho se tee se se fapanego ka lehlakoreng le, gomme seswantšho se sengwe se se fapanego ka lehlakoreng leo; mola tšohle magareng e swanetše go ba leswiswi le le tseneletšego le go se be le selo go yena. Ge e le gabotse go ka thwe motho o lebelela lefase a le ka lepokising la baleti leo le nago le dithapo tše pedi tše di kopantšwego bakeng sa lefasetere la gagwe. Eupša ka leruarua, di- sash tše tše pedi di tsenywa ka thoko, di dira mafasetere a mabedi a fapanego, eupša ka manyami di senya pono. Go ikgetha mo ga mahlo a leruarua ke selo seo se swanetšego go gopolwa ka mehla ge go rea dihlapi; le go gopolwa ke mmadi ditiragalong tše dingwe tše di latelago.

Potšišo e makatšago le e gakantšhago kudu e ka thongwa mabapi le taba ye ya pono e le yeo e kgomago Leviathane. Eupša ke swanetše go kgotsofala ka tšhupetšo. Ge feela mahlo a motho a bulegile seetšeng, tiro ya go bona ga se ya boithatelo; ke gore, ka nako yeo a ka se kgone go thuša go bona ka motšhene dilo le ge e le dife tšeo di lego pele ga gagwe. Lega go le bjalo, phihlelo ya motho le ge e le ofe e tla mo ruta, gore gaešita le ge a ka kgona go tšea go sweep mo go sa kgethollego ga dilo ka go lebelela ga tee, go tloga go sa kgonege gore, ka kelohloko, le ka mo go feletšego, a hlahlobje dilo le ge e le dife tše pedi—go sa šetšwe gore ke tše dikgolo gakaakang goba tše dinyenyane gakaakang—ka go lebelela e tee le motsotso o swanago wa nako; never mind ge ba robala ba bapile gomme ba kgomana. Eupša ge bjale o ka tla go aroganya dilo tše tše pedi, gomme wa dikologa se sengwe le se sengwe ka sediko sa leswiswi le le tseneletšego; ke moka, e le gore o bone e nngwe ya tšona, ka mokgwa wo o tlago go tliša monagano wa gago gore o e kgotlelele, e nngwe e tla kgaphelwa ka ntle ka mo go feletšego temogo ya gago ya mehleng yeno. Ka gona, go bjang ka leruarua? Ke therešo gore mahlo a gagwe ka bobedi, ka bowona, a swanetše go gata mogato ka nako e tee; eupša na bjoko bja gagwe bo akaretša kudu, bo kopanya le bjo bo sa lemogegego go feta bja motho, moo e lego gore ka motsotso o tee wa nako a ka hlahloba ka kelohloko ditebelelo tše pedi tše di fapanego, e nngwe e le ka lehlakoreng le lengwe la gagwe gomme e nngwe e le ka lehlakoreng le le fapanego tlwaa? Ge e ba a kgona, gona na ke selo se se makatšago go yena, bjalo ka ge eka monna o kgonne go feta ka nako e tee dipontšhong tša mathata a mabedi a fapanego go Euclid. Le gona, ge go nyakišišitšwe ka go tia, ga go na go se dumelelane le ge e le gofe papišong ye.

E ka ba feela go se dire selo, eupša go be go dutše go bonagala go nna, gore go thekesela mo go sa tlwaelegago ga go sepela mo go bontšhwago ke maruarua a mangwe ge a hlasetšwe ke dikepe tše tharo goba tše nne; go ba le dihlong le boikarabelo bja go tšhoga ga queer, mo go tlwaelegilego kudu go maruarua a bjalo; Ke nagana gore tše ka moka di tšwela pele ka mo go sa lebanyego go tšwa go kgakanego ye e se nago thušo ya boithatelo, yeo go yona maatla a bona a pono ao a aroganego le ao a fapanego ka mo go feletšego a swanetšego go ba akaretša.

Eupša tsebe ya leruarua e tletše go rata go tseba go swana le leihlo. Ge e ba o le moeng ka mo go feletšego morafong wa bona, o ka tsoma godimo ga dihlogo tše tše pedi ka diiri tše dintši, gomme wa se tsoge o utollotše setho seo. Tsebe ga e na letlakala la ka ntle le ge e le lefe; gomme ka moleteng ka bowona o ka se kgone go tsenya quill, ka fao e makatšago motsotso ke yona. E lodged go se nene ka morago ga leihlo. Mabapi le ditsebe tša bona, phapano ye ya bohlokwa e swanetše go bonwa magareng ga leruarua la peu le la le letona. Le ge tsebe ya ya pele e e-na le lešoba la ka ntle, ya ya mafelelo e khupeditšwe ka mo go feletšego le ka go lekana ka lera, e le gore e be yeo e sa lemogegego ka mo go feletšego go tšwa ka ntle.

Na ga se go rata go tseba, gore sephedi se segolo bjalo ka leruarua se bone lefase ka leihlo le lennyane gakaakaa, gomme se kwe go duma ka tsebe yeo e lego ye nnyane go feta ya mmutla? Eupša ge nkabe mahlo a gagwe a be a nabile bjalo ka lense ya thelesekoupu ye kgolo ya Herschel; gomme ditsebe tša gagwe di phatlaletše bjalo ka mathudi a dikereke tše dikgolo; na seo se be se tla mo dira gore a kgone go bona, goba a be bogale go kwa? Le gatee.—Bjale ke ka baka la’ng o leka go “godiša” monagano wa gago? E subtilize.

A re bjale ka di-lever dife goba dife le dientšene tša mouwane tšeo re nago le tšona ka seatleng, cant over the sperm whale’s head, that it may road bottom up; ke moka, o rotogela ka lere go ya tlhoreng, dira gore o pepe fase molomong; gomme ge nkabe e se gore mmele bjale o arogantšwe ka botlalo le wona, ka lebone re ka theogela ka Leweng le legolo la Kentucky Mammoth la mpa ya gagwe. Eupša a re swareleng mo ka leino le, gomme re lebelele go dikologa rena moo re lego gona. A molomo o mobotse e le ka kgonthe le wo o bonagalago o hlwekile! go tloga fase go fihla siling, e na le mašela goba go e na le moo e ngwadilwe ka pampiri ka lera le lešweu le le phadimago, le phadimago bjalo ka di- satin tša banyadi.

Eupša tšwela ka ntle bjale, gomme o lebelele mohlagare wo wa ka fase wa go bontšha, wo o bonagalago bjalo ka sekhurumetšo se setelele se se sesane sa lepokisi le legolo la go tšhuma, ka hinge ntlheng e tee, go e na le ka lehlakoreng le tee. Ge o ka e pry godimo, gore o e hwetše godimo ga hlogo, gomme wa pepentšha methaladi ya yona ya meno, e bonala e le portcullis ye e šiišago; le tše bjalo, go madimabe! e itlhatsela go ba bantši e le wight e fokolago boreahlapi, yeo ditlhale tše di welago godimo ga yona ka matla a go kokotela. Eupša se se šiišago kudu ke go bona, ge o fathoms fase ka lewatleng, o bona maruarua a mangwe a sulky, a phaphametše fao a fegilwe, ka mohlagare wa gagwe wa go makatša, wo mongwe wa botelele bja dimithara tše lesomehlano, o fegilwe thwii fase ka dikhutlo tše di lokilego le mmele wa gagwe, bakeng sa ka moka lefase bjalo ka jib-boom ya sekepe. Leruarua le ga se la hwa; o nyamile feela; ka ntle ga mehuta, mohlomongwe; hypochondriac ya go ba le mmele; gomme a rapaletše kudu, mo e lego gore dihinge tša mohlagare wa gagwe di lokologile, di mo tlogela moo ka gare ga mohuta woo o sa kgahlišego wa maemo, e lego kgobošo go morafe wa gagwe ka moka, wo o swanetšego, ka ntle le pelaelo, go mo sola ka mehlagare ya go notlela.

Mabakeng a mantši mohlagare wo wa ka tlase—ka ge o lokollwa gabonolo ke motaki yo a itlwaetšago—o a kgaolwa gomme wa phagamišetšwa mokatong wa sekepe ka morero wa go ntšha meno a manaka a tlou, le go nea kabo ya lerapo leo le thata le lešweu la maruarua leo ka lona barei ba dihlapi ba bopago mehuta ka moka ya dilo tšeo di ratago go tseba, . go akaretša le dithupa, di-ambrella-stock le ditshwaro go ya go dikotlo tša go namela.

Ka sedirišwa se setelele sa go emiša se se lapilego mohlagare o gogwa ka sekepeng, bjalo ka ge eka ke tshetledi; gomme ge nako e swanetšego e fihla—matšatši a mangwe a sego kae ka morago ga mošomo o mongwe—Queequeg, Daggoo le Tashtego, ka ge ka moka e le dingaka tša meno tše di nago le bokgoni, ba ikemišeditše go goga meno. Ka go sega-spade e bogale, Queequeg o lance marinini; ke moka mohlagare o tlemilwe go fihla go di- ringbolt, gomme sedirišwa se rigged go tšwa godimo, di goga meno a, bjalo ka ge dipholo tša Michigan di goga dikutu tša di-oak tša kgale go tšwa dinageng tša dikota tša lešoka. Ka kakaretšo go na le meno a masomenne-pedi ka moka; maruarua a kgale, ao a onaletšego kudu, eupša a sa bola; goba go tlatšwa ka morago ga fešene ya rena ya maitirelo. Mohlagare ka morago o segwa go ba matlapa, gomme wa kgoboketšwa bjalo ka di-joist tša go aga dintlo.





KGAOLO YA 75. Hlogo ya Leruarua la Letona—Pono ye e Fapanego.

Ge re tshela mokatong wa sekepe, bjale a re be le pono ye botse ye telele hlogong ya Right Whale.

Bjalo ka sebopego ka kakaretšo hlogo ya Sperm Whale ye e hlomphegago e ka bapetšwa le koloi ya ntwa ya Roma (kudu ka pele, moo e lego nkgokolo ka bophara gakaakaa); ka gona, ge o lebelela ka mo go nabilego, hlogo ya Right Whale e rwele go swana mo go sa kgahlišego kudu le seeta se segolo sa menwana ya galliot. Nywageng e makgolo a mabedi e fetilego mosepedi wa kgale wa mo-Dutch o ile a swantšha sebopego sa yona le sa mafelelo sa modiri wa dieta. Gomme ka go sona se sa mafelelo goba seeta, mokgekolo yoo wa kanegelo ya bana, ka bana ba go tšhoša, ba ka no robala ka boiketlo kudu, yena le ditlogolo tša gagwe ka moka.

Eupša ge o batamela hlogo ye kgolo ye e thoma go tšea dibopego tše di fapanego, go ya ka pono ya gago. Ge o ka ema godimo ga tlhora ya yona gomme wa lebelela mašoba a a mabedi a sebopego sa F, o be o tla tšea hlogo ka moka bakeng sa bass-viol ye kgolo kudu, le di-spiracle tše, mašoba ka gare ga sounding-board ya yona. Ke moka, gape, ge e ba o tsepamiša leihlo la gago godimo ga go tsepama mo mo go makatšago, mo go nago le tlhora, mo go swanago le sekhafo godimo ga lešaba—selo se se setala, sa barnacled, seo ma-Greenland a se bitšago “mphapahlogo,” gomme barei ba dihlapi ba Borwa ke “bonete” ya sona Leruarua la Letona; o tsepamišitše mahlo a gago go se feela, o be o tla tšea hlogo bakeng sa kutu ya moeike o mongwe o mogolo, o e-na le sehlaga sa nonyana ka gare ga setho sa wona. Ka tekanyo le ge e le efe, ge o bogela digagabi tšeo tše di phelago tšeo di dulago mo godimo ga bonete ye, kgopolo e bjalo e tla nyakile e tla go tlela; ntle le ge, ka nnete, go ikgabiša ga gago go beakantšwe ke lereo la setegeniki la “mphapahlogo” le lona leo le neilwego go lona; boemong bjoo o tla kgahlegela kudu go nagana kamoo phoofolo ye e matla ge e le gabotse e lego kgoši ya lewatle yeo e nago le mphapahlogo, yeo mphapahlogo wa yona o motala o mo kopantšwego ka mokgwa wo o makatšago. Eupša ge e ba leruarua le e le kgoši, ke thaka yeo e bonagalago e le sulky kudu go kgabiša mphapahlogo. Lebelela molomo woo wa ka tlase wo o fegilwego! ke sulk le pout e kgolo gakaakang yeo e lego gona! sulk le pout, ka tekanyo ya mmetli, botelele bjo bo ka bago dimithara tše masomepedi le botebo bja dimithara tše hlano; sulk le pout tšeo di tlago go go tšweletša dilitara tše ka bago tše 500 tša oli le tše dingwe.

Maswabi a magolo, bjale, gore leruarua le la madimabe le swanetše go ba le melomo ya mmutla. Phapano e ka ba leoto ka mošola. Mohlomongwe mma nakong ya nako e bohlokwa o be a sesa ka sekepe go theoga lebopong la Peru, ge ditšhišinyego tša lefase di be di dira gore lebopong le bule. Ka godimo ga molomo wo, bjalo ka godimo ga moedi wo o thelelago, bjale re thelela ka ganong. Godimo ga lentšu la ka ke be ke le kua Mackinaw, ke swanetše go tšea se e le ka gare ga wigwam ya India. Morena yo mobotse! ke tsela yeo Jona a ilego a sepela ka yona? Marulelo a ka ba dimithara tše lesomepedi godimo, gomme a kitima go ya khutlo ye bogale ye botse, bjalo ka ge eka go na le kota ya molala ya ka mehla fao; mola mahlakore a a ribbed, a arched, a boya, a re tšweletša ka di-slats tšeo tše di makatšago, tše di emego seripa-gare, tša sebopego sa scimetar tša lerapo la maruarua, re re makgolo a mararo ka lehlakoreng, tšeo go ithekgilego ka karolo ya ka godimo ya hlogo goba lerapo la mphapahlogo, di bopago difofu tšeo tša Venetian tšeo di boletšwe mafelong a mangwe ka go se kgaotše. Mapheko a marapo a a na le ditlhale tša boya, tšeo ka tšona Leruarua la go ja le sefa meetse, gomme ka go raragana ga lona le boloka dihlapi tše nnyane, ge di bulegile molomo o feta mawatleng a brit ka nako ya go ja. Difoufatšong tša bogareng tša marapo, ge di eme ka thulaganyo ya tšona ya tlhago, go na le maswao a itšego a go rata go tseba, go kobega, dikoti le meboto, yeo ka yona banna ba bangwe ba maruarua ba balago nywaga ya sebopiwa, e le nywaga ya oak ka dipalamonwana tša yona tša nkgokolo. Le ge kgonthišetšo ya tekanyetšo ye e kgole le go bontšhwa, le ge go le bjalo e na le tatso ya kgonagalo ya go swantšha. Ka tekanyo le ge e le efe, ge e ba re ineela go yona, re swanetše go nea Right Whale nywaga e megolo kudu go feta kamoo ge o e lebelela la mathomo e tla bonagalago e le e kwagalago.

Mehleng ya kgale, go bonagala go be go buša dikgopolo tše di makatšago kudu tša go rata go tseba mabapi le difoufatšo tše. Mosepedi yo mongwe wa basepedi kua Purchas o di bitša “maledu” a makatšago ka gare ga molomo wa leruarua;* yo mongwe, “mašoba a dikolobe”; mohlomphegi wa boraro wa kgale kua Hackluyt o diriša polelo e latelago ya maemo a godimo: “Go na le maphegwana a ka bago makgolo a mabedi-masomehlano ao a melago ka lehlakoreng le lengwe le le lengwe la chop ya gagwe ya ka godimo , ao a khupetšago leleme la gagwe ka lehlakoreng le lengwe le le lengwe la molomo wa gagwe.”

*Se se re gopotša gore Right Whale e tloga e na le mohuta wa maledu, goba go e na le moo ditedu, tšeo di bopilwego ka meriri ye mmalwa ye mešweu ye e phatlaletšego karolong ya ka godimo ya ntlha ya ka ntle ya mohlagare wa ka fase. Ka dinako tše dingwe di- tuft tše di nea ponagalo e itšego ya boradia sefahlegong sa gagwe seo ka tsela e nngwe se bego se hlomphega.

Bjalo ka ge yo mongwe le yo mongwe a tseba, ona “mašoba a dikolobe,” “maphegwana,” “maledu,” “difofu” goba selo le ge e le sefe seo o se ratago, di nea mafumahadi di-busk tša ona le didirišwa tše dingwe tša go thatafatša. Eupša tabeng ye e kgethegilego, nyakego ke kgale e fokotšega. E bile mehleng ya Kgošigadi Anne moo lerapo le bego le le letagong la lona, ​​farthingale e be e le fešene ka moka ka nako yeo. Gomme bjalo ka ge di-dame tšeo tša bogologolo di be di sepelasepela ka lethabo, le ge e le ka mehlagare ya maruarua, bjalo ka ge o ka bolela; le ge go le bjalo, ka shawara, ka go se nagane mo go swanago, na mehleng yeno re fofa ka fase ga mehlagare ye e swanago bakeng sa tšhireletšo; seširo e le tente yeo e phatlaletšego godimo ga lerapo le tee.

Eupša bjale lebala ka moka ka difoufatšo le maledu motsotswana, gomme, o eme ka ganong ga Leruarua la Tokelo, lebelela go go dikologa lefsa. Ge o bona dikholonate tše ka moka tša marapo di le ka mokgwa wo o fapanego, na o be o ka se nagane gore o ka gare ga setho se segolo sa Haarlem, gomme o lebeletše diphaephe tša sona tše sekete? Bakeng sa khapete go setho re na le rug ya Turkey e boleta kudu—leleme, leo le kgomaretšwego, bjalo ka ge eka, lebatong la molomo. E nonne kudu e bile e boleta, gomme e swanetše go gagola diripana ka go e phagamiša mokatong wa sekepe. Leleme le le itšego bjale pele ga rena; ka go lebelela ka go feta ke swanetše go bolela gore e be e le sethunya sa megopo e tshela; ke gore, e tla go tšweletša mo e ka bago tekanyo yeo ya makhura.

Ere se, o swanetše go ba o bone gabotse therešo ya seo ke thomilego ka sona—gore Sperm Whale le Right Whale di na le dihlogo tše di nyakilego di fapane ka mo go feletšego. Ka gona, ge re akaretša: ka go Right Whale’s ga go na sediba se segolo sa peu; ga go na meno a manaka a tlou le gatee; ga se mandible ye telele, ye sesane ya mohlagare wa ka fase, go swana le ya Sperm Whale. Le gona ka go Sperm Whale ga go na le ge e le efe ya difofu tšeo tša marapo; ga go na molomo o mogolo wa ka tlase; gomme ka sewelo go se na selo sa leleme. Le mo, Leruarua la Tokelo le na le mašoba a mabedi a ka ntle a go ntšha meetse, Leruarua la Peu le le tee feela.

Matsogo a mangwe bjale a ile a tlolela ka seketswaneng ka thoko, gomme a kgorometša go se nene go tloga sekepeng.

“Ha! ha!” gwa goeletša Daggoo, ka nako e tee, go tšwa go setulo sa gagwe seo bjale se homotšego, se se šikinyegago godimo ga hlogo; gomme ge re lebelela kgole go tšwa ka thoko, re ile ra bona letsogo le kgorometšwa thwii go tšwa maphotong a matala-lerata; pono yeo e makatšago go e bona, bjalo ka letsogo leo le kgoromeleditšwego go tšwa bjanyeng godimo ga lebitla.

"Bobedi! bobedi!—ke bobedi!”—Dagoo a goeletša gape ka mokgoši wa lethabo; and soon after, Queequeg was seen boldly striking out with one hand, and with the other clutching the long hair of the Indian. Drawn into the waiting boat, they were quickly brought to the deck; but Tashtego was long in coming to, and Queequeg did not look very brisk.

Now, how had this noble rescue been accomplished? Why, diving after the slowly descending head, Queequeg with his keen sword had made side lunges near its bottom, so as to scuttle a large hole there; then dropping his sword, had thrust his long arm far inwards and upwards, and so hauled out poor Tash by the head. He averred, that upon first thrusting in for him, a leg was presented; but well knowing that that was not as it ought to be, and might occasion great trouble;—he had thrust back the leg, and by a dexterous heave and toss, had wrought a somerset upon the Indian; so that with the next trial, he came forth in the good old way—head foremost. As for the great head itself, that was doing as well as could be expected.

And thus, through the courage and great skill in obstetrics of Queequeg, the deliverance, or rather, delivery of Tashtego, was successfully accomplished, in the teeth, too, of the most untoward and apparently hopeless impediments; which is a lesson by no means to be forgotten. Midwifery should be taught in the same course with fencing and boxing, riding and rowing.

I know that this queer adventure of the Gay-Header's will be sure to seem incredible to some landsmen, though they themselves may have either seen or heard of some one's falling into a cistern ashore; an accident which not seldom happens, and with much less reason too than the Indian's, considering the exceeding slipperiness of the curb of the Sperm Whale's well.

But, peradventure, it may be sagaciously urged, how is this? We thought the tissued, infiltrated head of the Sperm Whale, was the lightest and most corky part about him; and yet thou makest it sink in an element of a far greater specific gravity than itself. We have thee there. Not at all, but I have ye; for at the time poor Tash fell in, the case had been nearly emptied of its lighter contents, leaving little but the dense tendinous wall of the well—a double welded, hammered substance, as I have before said, much heavier than the sea water, and a lump of which sinks in it like lead almost. But the tendency to rapid sinking in this substance was in the present instance materially counteracted by the other parts of the head remaining undetached from it, so that it sank very slowly and deliberately indeed, affording Queequeg a fair chance for performing his agile obstetrics on the run, as you may say. Yes, it was a running delivery, so it was.

Now, had Tashtego perished in that head, it had been a very precious perishing; smothered in the very whitest and daintiest of fragrant spermaceti; coffined, hearsed, and tombed in the secret inner chamber and sanctum sanctorum of the whale. Only one sweeter end can readily be recalled—the delicious death of an Ohio honey-hunter, who seeking honey in the crotch of a hollow tree, found such exceeding store of it, that leaning too far over, it sucked him in, so that he died embalmed. How many, think ye, have likewise fallen into Plato's honey head, and sweetly perished there?





CHAPTER 79. The Prairie.

To scan the lines of his face, or feel the bumps on the head of this Leviathan; this is a thing which no Physiognomist or Phrenologist has as yet undertaken. Such an enterprise would seem almost as hopeful as for Lavater to have scrutinized the wrinkles on the Rock of Gibraltar, or for Gall to have mounted a ladder and manipulated the Dome of the Pantheon. Still, in that famous work of his, Lavater not only treats of the various faces of men, but also attentively studies the faces of horses, birds, serpents, and fish; and dwells in detail upon the modifications of expression discernible therein. Nor have Gall and his disciple Spurzheim failed to throw out some hints touching the phrenological characteristics of other beings than man. Therefore, though I am but ill qualified for a pioneer, in the application of these two semi-sciences to the whale, I will do my endeavor. I try all things; I achieve what I can.

Physiognomically regarded, the Sperm Whale is an anomalous creature. He has no proper nose. And since the nose is the central and most conspicuous of the features; and since it perhaps most modifies and finally controls their combined expression; hence it would seem that its entire absence, as an external appendage, must very largely affect the countenance of the whale. For as in landscape gardening, a spire, cupola, monument, or tower of some sort, is deemed almost indispensable to the completion of the scene; so no face can be physiognomically in keeping without the elevated open-work belfry of the nose. Dash the nose from Phidias's marble Jove, and what a sorry remainder! Nevertheless, Leviathan is of so mighty a magnitude, all his proportions are so stately, that the same deficiency which in the sculptured Jove were hideous, in him is no blemish at all. Nay, it is an added grandeur. A nose to the whale would have been impertinent. As on your physiognomical voyage you sail round his vast head in your jolly-boat, your noble conceptions of him are never insulted by the reflection that he has a nose to be pulled. A pestilent conceit, which so often will insist upon obtruding even when beholding the mightiest royal beadle on his throne.

In some particulars, perhaps the most imposing physiognomical view to be had of the Sperm Whale, is that of the full front of his head. This aspect is sublime.

In thought, a fine human brow is like the East when troubled with the morning. In the repose of the pasture, the curled brow of the bull has a touch of the grand in it. Pushing heavy cannon up mountain defiles, the elephant's brow is majestic. Human or animal, the mystical brow is as that great golden seal affixed by the German emperors to their decrees. It signifies—“God: done this day by my hand.” But in most creatures, nay in man himself, very often the brow is but a mere strip of alpine land lying along the snow line. Few are the foreheads which like Shakespeare's or Melancthon's rise so high, and descend so low, that the eyes themselves seem clear, eternal, tideless mountain lakes; and all above them in the forehead's wrinkles, you seem to track the antlered thoughts descending there to drink, as the Highland hunters track the snow prints of the deer. But in the great Sperm Whale, this high and mighty god-like dignity inherent in the brow is so immensely amplified, that gazing on it, in that full front view, you feel the Deity and the dread powers more forcibly than in beholding any other object in living nature. For you see no one point precisely; not one distinct feature is revealed; no nose, eyes, ears, or mouth; no face; he has none, proper; nothing but that one broad firmament of a forehead, pleated with riddles; dumbly lowering with the doom of boats, and ships, and men. Nor, in profile, does this wondrous brow diminish; though that way viewed its grandeur does not domineer upon you so. In profile, you plainly perceive that horizontal, semi-crescentic depression in the forehead's middle, which, in man, is Lavater's mark of genius.

But how? Genius in the Sperm Whale? Has the Sperm Whale ever written a book, spoken a speech? No, his great genius is declared in his doing nothing particular to prove it. It is moreover declared in his pyramidical silence. And this reminds me that had the great Sperm Whale been known to the young Orient World, he would have been deified by their child-magian thoughts. They deified the crocodile of the Nile, because the crocodile is tongueless; and the Sperm Whale has no tongue, or at least it is so exceedingly small, as to be incapable of protrusion. If hereafter any highly cultured, poetical nation shall lure back to their birth-right, the merry May-day gods of old; and livingly enthrone them again in the now egotistical sky; in the now unhaunted hill; then be sure, exalted to Jove's high seat, the great Sperm Whale shall lord it.

Champollion deciphered the wrinkled granite hieroglyphics. But there is no Champollion to decipher the Egypt of every man's and every being's face. Physiognomy, like every other human science, is but a passing fable. If then, Sir William Jones, who read in thirty languages, could not read the simplest peasant's face in its profounder and more subtle meanings, how may unlettered Ishmael hope to read the awful Chaldee of the Sperm Whale's brow? I but put that brow before you. Read it if you can.





CHAPTER 80. The Nut.

If the Sperm Whale be physiognomically a Sphinx, to the phrenologist his brain seems that geometrical circle which it is impossible to square.

In the full-grown creature the skull will measure at least twenty feet in length. Unhinge the lower jaw, and the side view of this skull is as the side of a moderately inclined plane resting throughout on a level base. But in life—as we have elsewhere seen—this inclined plane is angularly filled up, and almost squared by the enormous superincumbent mass of the junk and sperm. At the high end the skull forms a crater to bed that part of the mass; while under the long floor of this crater—in another cavity seldom exceeding ten inches in length and as many in depth—reposes the mere handful of this monster's brain. The brain is at least twenty feet from his apparent forehead in life; it is hidden away behind its vast outworks, like the innermost citadel within the amplified fortifications of Quebec. So like a choice casket is it secreted in him, that I have known some whalemen who peremptorily deny that the Sperm Whale has any other brain than that palpable semblance of one formed by the cubic-yards of his sperm magazine. Lying in strange folds, courses, and convolutions, to their apprehensions, it seems more in keeping with the idea of his general might to regard that mystic part of him as the seat of his intelligence.

It is plain, then, that phrenologically the head of this Leviathan, in the creature's living intact state, is an entire delusion. As for his true brain, you can then see no indications of it, nor feel any. The whale, like all things that are mighty, wears a false brow to the common world.

If you unload his skull of its spermy heaps and then take a rear view of its rear end, which is the high end, you will be struck by its resemblance to the human skull, beheld in the same situation, and from the same point of view. Indeed, place this reversed skull (scaled down to the human magnitude) among a plate of men's skulls, and you would involuntarily confound it with them; and remarking the depressions on one part of its summit, in phrenological phrase you would say—This man had no self-esteem, and no veneration. And by those negations, considered along with the affirmative fact of his prodigious bulk and power, you can best form to yourself the truest, though not the most exhilarating conception of what the most exalted potency is.

But if from the comparative dimensions of the whale's proper brain, you deem it incapable of being adequately charted, then I have another idea for you. If you attentively regard almost any quadruped's spine, you will be struck with the resemblance of its vertebræ to a strung necklace of dwarfed skulls, all bearing rudimental resemblance to the skull proper. It is a German conceit, that the vertebræ are absolutely undeveloped skulls. But the curious external resemblance, I take it the Germans were not the first men to perceive. A foreign friend once pointed it out to me, in the skeleton of a foe he had slain, and with the vertebræ of which he was inlaying, in a sort of basso-relievo, the beaked prow of his canoe. Now, I consider that the phrenologists have omitted an important thing in not pushing their investigations from the cerebellum through the spinal canal. For I believe that much of a man's character will be found betokened in his backbone. I would rather feel your spine than your skull, whoever you are. A thin joist of a spine never yet upheld a full and noble soul. I rejoice in my spine, as in the firm audacious staff of that flag which I fling half out to the world.

Apply this spinal branch of phrenology to the Sperm Whale. His cranial cavity is continuous with the first neck-vertebra; and in that vertebra the bottom of the spinal canal will measure ten inches across, being eight in height, and of a triangular figure with the base downwards. As it passes through the remaining vertebræ the canal tapers in size, but for a considerable distance remains of large capacity. Now, of course, this canal is filled with much the same strangely fibrous substance—the spinal cord—as the brain; and directly communicates with the brain. And what is still more, for many feet after emerging from the brain's cavity, the spinal cord remains of an undecreasing girth, almost equal to that of the brain. Under all these circumstances, would it be unreasonable to survey and map out the whale's spine phrenologically? For, viewed in this light, the wonderful comparative smallness of his brain proper is more than compensated by the wonderful comparative magnitude of his spinal cord.

But leaving this hint to operate as it may with the phrenologists, I would merely assume the spinal theory for a moment, in reference to the Sperm Whale's hump. This august hump, if I mistake not, rises over one of the larger vertebræ, and is, therefore, in some sort, the outer convex mould of it. From its relative situation then, I should call this high hump the organ of firmness or indomitableness in the Sperm Whale. And that the great monster is indomitable, you will yet have reason to know.
