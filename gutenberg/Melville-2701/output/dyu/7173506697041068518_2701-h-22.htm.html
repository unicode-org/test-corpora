ƝƐFƆLI 114. Gilidi.

Komi Pequod tun be donna ka taga Zapɔn ka kurunw boliyɔrɔ cɛmancɛ la, dɔɔni o kɔ, a tun be jɛgɛminɛyɔrɔ bɛɛ la. Tuma caaman na, wagati sumaninw na, wagati ɲumanw na, lɛrɛ tan ni fila, tan ni duuru, tan ni seegi ani mugan kɔnɔ kurunw kɔnɔ, u tun be kurunw sama dɔɔni dɔɔni, wala u tun be kurun boli, wala u tun be kurun boli balɛnw kɔ, wala miniti biwɔɔrɔ wala biwolonfila kɔnɔ u tun be u ka wuli makɔnɔna ni dusu sumanin ye; hali ni u ye ɲɛtaga dɔɔni sɔrɔ u ka dimiw kosɔn.

O wagati ɲɔgɔnw na, tile min ka surun; tile kuru bɛɛ kɔnɔ, funu nɔgɔmanw kan, minw be yɛlɛ dɔɔni dɔɔni; a siginin bɛ a ta kurun kɔnɔ, a ka fɛgɛ i ko bɛrɛ; ani u be ɲagami ɲɔgɔn na ni jikuru nɔgɔmanw yɛrɛ ye, fɔɔ u be pɛrɛn i n’a fɔ tasuma kabakuruw, u be pɛrɛn gunwale kan; o ye siko sumanin wagatiw ye, ni mɔgɔ ye kɔgɔji fari cɛɲumanya sumanin n’a ka manamana ye, mɔgɔ be ɲinɛ jara dusukun kɔ min be pɛrɛnna a jukɔrɔ; ani a tun tɛna sɔn k’a hakili to a la, ko nin velouri senkala be ɲinɛ dɔ dogo min tɛ nimisa.

O wagatiw lo, a ka balɛni-kurun kɔnɔ, kurun kɔnɔ, nɛgɛsobolikɛla be dencɛnya, lannaya, dugukolo ɲɔgɔnna dusukunnakow dɔ mɛn dɔɔni dɔɔni baji fan fɛ; ko a b’a jati i n’a fɔ dugukolo filɛrifɛn caman; ani kurun yɔrɔjan min b’a ka kurunw sanfɛla dɔrɔn lo yira, a be komi a be jijali la ka taga ɲɛfɛ, a ma kɛ jikurubaw fɛ minw be yɛlɛmayɛlɛma, nka a be tɛmɛna kongokolon binjanw fɛ minw be yɛlɛmayɛlɛma: i n’a fɔ tuma min na tilebenyanfan mɔgɔw ka soow b’u tulo lɔninw dɔrɔn lo yira, k’a sɔrɔ u farikolo dogoninw be yɛlɛmayɛlɛma yɔrɔ bɛɛ la binkɛnɛ kabakomanw cɛma.

Sugurunw ka kongokolonw; kulu gɛrɛfɛla bulaman nɔgɔmanw; i n’a fɔ olugu sanfɛ, pɛrɛnkan bɛ sonya; i be ɲini ka kali ko denmisɛn minw sɛgɛnin lo tulon na, olu be sinɔgɔra o yɔrɔw la, mɛkalo ninsɔndiya wagati dɔw la, n’u be kungo kɔnɔ feerenw tigɛ. Ani nin bɛɛ ɲagaminin lo n’i ka miiriya gundomanba ye; o cogo la, tiɲɛn ni fani, ɲɔgɔnye tilancɛ, be don ɲɔgɔn na ani ka kɛ fɛɛn kelen ye min tɛ ɲɔgɔn minɛ.

O koo minw be mɔgɔ dusu saalo, hali n’u tun be wagati dɔɔni dɔrɔn lo kɛ, u ma nɔɔ to Akabu kan hali wagati dɔɔni dɔrɔn. Nka n’a tun be komi o sanu gundo kilɛw tun b’a yɛrɛ ka sanu nafolo gundo dayɛlɛla a kɔnɔ, o bɛɛ n’a ta, a ka ninakali min tun be u kan, o tun be nɔgɔ dɔrɔn lo la.

O, binyɔrɔw! oh, tuma bɛɛ vernal banbali yɔrɔw niin kɔnɔ; aw kɔnɔ, —hali ni a jalen lo kabini wagati jan dugukolo ɲanamaya ja salen fɛ, —aw kɔnɔ, mɔgɔw bɛ se ka yɛlɛma hali bi, i n’a fɔ sodennin minw bɛ sɔgɔma yiriden kuraw kɔnɔ; ani wagati dɔɔni kɔnɔ, ɲɛnamaya banbali ka nɛnɛ nɛnɛman mɛn u kan. Ni Ala tun b’a fɛ o hɛɛrɛ barikamanw tun bena to yen. Nka ɲanamaya juru minw ɲagaminin lo, minw ɲagaminin lo, olu be tigɛ ni fanikolon ye: sanfiɲɛw be yɔrɔ sumaninw tigɛ, sanfiɲɛ be sumanin bɛɛ la. Ɲɛtaga min tɛ se ka kɛ, o tɛ yen nin ɲɛnamaya kɔnɔ; an tɛ taga ɲɛ ni yɛlɛmani jɔnjɔnw ye, ani laban na, an be lɔ dɔɔni:—denmisɛnw ka hakilitanw fɛ, cɛdenya ka limaniya min tɛ miiri, kanbelenya ka sigasigali (o ye halakili ye min be kɛ mɔgɔw bɛɛ fɛ), o kɔ, sigasigali, o kɔ, lannayabaliya, ka lafiɲɛ laban na cɛɛya ka miiri lafiɲɛ na min be weele ko If. Nka ni an tɛmɛna ka ban, an bɛ tugu yɔrɔ lamini kɔ; ani ye denmisɛnw, cɛdenw, ani cɛw, ani Ifs banbari. Kurun lɔyɔrɔ laban be min, an tɛ kurun bɔ yɔrɔ min na tugun? Duniɲa be tagama yɔrɔ juman lo la, min sɛgɛnin lo ka tɛmɛ tɔɔw bɛɛ kan, o tɛna sɛgɛ abada? Denɲɛnin facɛ dogonin be min? An niinw be i n’a fɔ o denmisɛnw, u bamuso minw ma furu, olu be sa u wolotuma na: an ka facɛya gundo b’u ka kaburu kɔnɔ, an ka ɲi ka kɛ yen k’o kalan.

O loon kelen na fana, k’a to a be filɛli kɛra duguma yɔrɔjan ka bɔ a ka kurun gɛrɛfɛ ka don o baji sanulaman kelen na, Starbuck y’a daminɛ ka ŋunuŋunu ko:—

“Kanu min tɛ se ka faamu, i n’a fɔ kanubaga y’a ye cogo min na a ka furumuso dencɛ ɲɛɛ na!—I kana i ka jɛgɛ ɲinw n’i ka mɔgɔfaga siraw fɔ n ye.

Ani Stubb, min tun be komi jɛgɛ, ani a ka fanikolonw be mɛnɛmɛnɛ, ale panna ka yɛlɛ o yeelen sanulaman kelen kɔnɔ:—

“Ne ye Stubb ye, ani Stubb ka tariki be yen, nka yan, Stubb be kali ko a tun be ninsɔndiya la tuma bɛɛ!”





ƝƐFƆLI 115. Pequod be ɲɔgɔn kunbɛn ni cɛkɔnɔ ye.

Ani, lɔgɔkun damanin o kɔ, Akabu ka kɛlɛkɛminan sɔgɔnin kɔ, u tun be fɛɛn minw yera ani mankan minw tun be mɛnna fɔɲɔ ɲɛfɛ, u tun be mɔgɔ ninsɔndiya kosɔbɛ.

Nantucket ka kurun dɔ lo, o min tɔgɔ ko Bachelor, o min tun y’a ka tulubɔlan laban don sisan sisan, k’a ka dakunw sɔgɔ ni bolow ye minw tun be pɛrɛnna; ani sisan, ni ninsɔndiya fani ye, a tun be kurun bolila ka kurunw cɛma dugukolo kan minw tun be yɔrɔjan, sanni a k’a ɲɛfɛla yira ka taga soo.

Cɛɛ saba minw tun b’a ka kurun kunfɛ, olu tun be fani gwɛmanw don u ka fugulanw na; ka bɔ kurun kɔfɛ, baleni kurun dɔ tun sirinin bɛ, duguma duguma; ani u tun ye balɛn laban min faga, o duguma dakun jan tun sirinin be bowsprit la. Tagamasiɲɛw, darapow ani jaki kulɛri sifa bɛɛ tun be panna ka bɔ a ka minanw na, fan bɛɛ la. A ka fanikolon saba nunu kelen kelen bɛɛ tun be ni muso ka ji barigo fila ye; o sanfɛ, a ka yiriw sanfɛla la, i tun be jii sɔngɔ gwɛlɛ kelen karifɛn misɛnw ye; ani fintɛni ziralaman dɔ tun nɔrɔnin be a ka mɔbiliba la.

I n’a fɔ a fɔra o kɔfɛ cogo min na, Bacɛri tun ye ɲɔgɔn sɔrɔ ni kabako ye ka tɛmɛ fɛn bɛɛ kan; o tun be mɔgɔ kabakoya kosɔbɛ sabu k’u to kurun boli la baji kelenw kan, kurun caaman wɛrɛw tun ye kalo caaman kɛ k’a sɔrɔ u ma jɛgɛ kelen sɔrɔ. O tun ma misisogo ni buru barigow dɔrɔn di walisa ka yɔrɔ di muso ka jilafɛnw ma minw nafa ka bon kosɔbɛ, nka u tun ye barigo wɛrɛw falen, ka bɔ kurunw kɔnɔ a tun ye minw kunbɛn; o fɛnw tun bɛ kurun kunna, ani kurun kuntigi ni sɔrɔdasi kuntigiw ta sow kɔnɔ. Hali kabini tabali yɛrɛ tun gosira ka kɛ tasuma yiri ye; ani kabini kɔnɔfɛnw tun be dumuni kɛ tulu-bugu dɔ kungolo la, min tun be gosi duguma ka kɛ cɛmancɛfɛn ye. Forocastle kɔnɔ, kurunbolilaw tun y’u ka disiw nɔrɔ ani k’u yɛlɛma tiɲɛn na, k’u fa; a y’a fara a kan ni yɛlɛko ye, ko tobilikɛla tun ye kungolo gosi a ka nɛgɛdaga belebeleba kan, k’a fa; ko baarakɛla tun k’a ta kafedaga tɔw datugu k’a fa; ko nɛgɛkunbɛnnanw tun ka o ta nɛgɛkunbɛnnanw kunkolo tigɛ, ka o fa; ko tiɲɛ na, fɛɛn bɛɛ tun fanin be muso ka ji la, fɔɔ kapitɛni ka pantalon juruw, ani a tun be minw mara k’a bolow don u kɔnɔ, k’a yɛrɛ wasa seereya kɛ a ka wasa bɛɛ koo la.

O kurun ninsɔndiyanin tun be jigira Pequod kan min tun be ni dusukunnakow ye tuma min na, dundun belebelebaw mankan tun be bɔra a ka kurun ɲɛfɛ; ani k’a to a be surunyana tugun, a ka cɛɛw jamaba dɔ yera u lɔnin b’a ka sɛgɛsɛgɛrikɛdaga belebelebaw lamini na, minw tun datugunin lo ni jɛgɛ finman ka pɔkɔ wala a kɔnɔgolo ye min be komi sɛbɛnin, o tun be mankanba bɔ kurun kɔnɔ baarakɛlaw bolo nɔrɔnin kelen kelen bɛɛ la. O kurun naaninan na, teriw ni nɛgɛsotigiw tun be dɔn kɛra ni sunguruw ye minw tun bolila n’u ye ka bɔ Polinezi gunw kan; k’u to kurun dɔ kɔnɔ min tun masirinin lo, ani u tun sirinin be sanfɛ koɲuman kurun ɲɛfɛla ni kurunba cɛ, Long Island ka mɔgɔ nɛrɛmuguman saba, minw ka violon-bow tun be mɛnɛmɛnɛ ni baleine sama ɲin ye, olu lo tun be yɛlɛko jig ɲɛminɛna. O wagati la, kurun kɔnɔ mɔgɔ wɛrɛw tun be mankan kɛra sɛgɛsɛgɛrikɛyɔrɔw ka kabakuruw lɔli la, u tun ye daga belebelebaw bɔ yɔrɔ minw na. I tun bena a miiri k’u be Bastille danganin samana ka jigi, u be kulekan juguw lawuli, i n’a fɔ biriki ni bɔgɔ minw nafa tɛ yen sisan, u be filila baji kɔnɔ.

Matigi ani kuntigi min tun be nin koow bɛɛ kan, kurun kuntigi tun lɔnin be kurun sanfɛla la, fɔɔ ninsɔndiyako bɛɛ tun fanin be a ɲɛfɛ, ani a tun be komi u y’o labɛn dɔrɔn a yɛrɛ ka ɲɛnagwɛko kama.

Akabu fana tun lɔnin bɛ a ta sanbon kan, a fari tun ka ɲi, a tun ka fin, a tun bɛ ni dibi ye; ani tuma min na kurun fila nunu tun be ɲɔgɔn ka yɛlɛyɔrɔw tigɛra—kelen tun be ninsɔndiyakow lo fɔra koo dɔw kosɔn minw tɛmɛna, tɔ kelen tun be koo nataw yira—u ka kurun kuntigi fila tun be danfara kabakoman min kɛra o koo la, u yɛrɛ lo tun b’o kɛra.

“Aw ka don kurun kɔnɔ, aw ka don kurun kɔnɔ!” cɛɲɔgɔnɲini Bacɛli ka komandan kulera, k’a to a be nɛgɛdaga ni buteli dɔ kɔrɔta fiɲɛ fɛ.

“I ye Balɛn Gwɛman ye wa?” Akabu y’o fɔ ni pɛrɛn ye k’a jaabi.

Tɔɔ kelen y’a fɔ ni nisɔndiya ye ko: “Ɔn ɔn, n’ y’a tɔgɔ mɛn dɔrɔn, nka n’ kana la a la fewu.” “Aw ka don kurun kɔnɔ!”

"I nisɔndiyanin lo kojugu. Taga kurun kɔnɔ. Yala mɔgɔ si tununa wa?"

"A ma dan ka kuma—gun mɔgɔ fila, o dɔrɔn lo;—nka aw ka don kurun kɔnɔ, aw dusukun kɔrɔw, aw ka na. N’ bena o finman bɔ aw ɲɛda la joona. Aw ka na, aw (merry's the play); kurun fanin be ani aw be taga soo."

“Naloman ye mɔgɔ kabakoman ye min b’a lɔn!” Akabu ka ŋunuŋunu; o kɔ, ni kaanba ye ko: "E ye kurun fanin ye, i be taga i ka soo, i ko; o tuma na, i ka ne weele ko kurun lankolon, min be taga kɛnɛma. O la, taga i ka siraw fɛ, ne fana bena ne ta siraw ta. Taga ɲɛfɛ yen! Aw ka kurunw bɛɛ bila, k'a to fɔɲɔ ma!"

O cogo ra, k’a sɔrɔ kurun kelen tun bɛ tagamana ni nisɔndiya ye fiɲɛ ɲa kɔrɔ, tɔ kelen tun b’a kɛlɛra ni kunkolo ye; O cogo ra, o minan fla farala ɲɔgɔn kan; Pequod kurun kɔnɔ baarakɛlaw tun be filɛra ni kaburu ye, ani u tun be to ka filɛli kɛ Bachelor min tun be kɔsegira; nka Bachelor ka cɛɛw tun tɛ u ka filɛri lamɛn abada u tun be ɲɛnagwɛ min na. Ani, tuma min na Akabu tun b’a yɛrɛ sɔgɔra taffrail kan, ka kurun filɛ min tun be taga soo, a ye bɔgɔdaga fitinin dɔ bɔ a ka bɔrɔ kɔnɔ, ka sɔrɔ ka bɔ kurun kɔnɔ ka filɛri kɛ ka taga tasa la, a tun be komi o be na ni Nackete fila ye yɔrɔjan, so villeual for finding to vintuud.





ƝƐFƆLI 116. Balɛn min be sa.

A man teli ka kɛ nin ɲɛnamaya kɔnɔ, ni, kinin fɛ, nafolo ka kurunw be kurun bolila ka gwɛrɛ an na, an, hali n’an bɛɛ be yɛlɛla ka kɔn, an be fiɲɛba dɔ minɛ ani an be ninsɔndiya k’a ye ko an ka kurunw be fa. A tun be komi o lo kɛra Pequod ka mɔgɔw koo la. Sabu o dugusagwɛ, u ye cɛɲɔgɔnɲini cɛɲɔgɔn kunbɛn ka ban, u ye baleniw ye ani u ye mɔgɔ naani faga; o dɔ le tun bɛ Akabu fɛ.

A tun be yɔrɔjan wulafɛ; ani tuma min na kɛlɛ wuleman ka tankiw bɛɛ banna: ani u tun be yɛlɛla tileben cɛɲumannin kan kɔgɔji ni sankolo la, tile ni balɛni fila bɛɛ sara ɲɔgɔn fɛ; o kɔ, o ɲɔgɔnna ɲumanya ani o ɲɔgɔnna ŋunuŋunu ɲɔgɔn, o ɲɔgɔnna fɛnɲɛnamafagalanw minw tun be ɲɔgɔn sɔgɔra o fiɲɛ nɛrɛmuguman kɔnɔ, fɔɔ a tun be komi yɔrɔjan ka bɔ Manila gunw ka kowɛntiw ka kuluwow la, Ɛsipaɲi dugukolo fiɲɛ, min tun be yɛlɛma ka kɛ kurunbolila ye, o tun tagara n’o hysperm, freight ye baji kan.

A dusu tun ka di tugun, nka a tun dusu tun ka dibi dɔrɔn lo la, Akabu min tun bɔra balɛni na, ale tun siginin lo k’a ɲɛɛ sin a ka jigi labanw ma ka bɔ kurun kɔnɔ min tun sumanin lo sisan. Sabu o kabako kabakoman min be ye sperme baleines bɛɛ la minw be sa—u kungolo be yɛlɛma tile fan fɛ, ani u be sa o cogo la—o kabakofɛn, min be ye o wulafɛ hɛɛrɛman na, cogo dɔ la, o ye kabako dɔ yira Akabu la min tun ma deli ka kɛ.

“A b’a yɛrɛ yɛlɛma k’a ɲɛsin a ma, dɔɔni dɔɔni, nka a be to ka bonya la a kan ani k’a wele, n’a ka saya tagamasiɲɛ labanw ye. Ale fana be tasuma bato; tile ka baarakɛden kantigiman, min ka bon, min ka bon! bɛɛ ka adamadenw ka nafolo wala bɔnɛw; ka suu lamini, a be taga sira wɛrɛ fɛ.

“O, e dibi Ɛndu tilancɛ, min y’i ka masasigilan danfaranin lɔ yɔrɔ dɔ la nin baji nunu cɛma, e ye kafiri ye, e masamuso, ani tiɲɛn na, i be kuma ne fɛ tilebɔyɔrɔ la min be mɔgɔ faga ka taga Norha ka sutarali kɔ, ani yɔrɔ min suturanin lo. a y’a kuun min tun be satɔ yɛlɛma, o kɔ, a tagara munumunu tugun, k’a sɔrɔ a ma kalan foyi sɔrɔ ne fɛ.

“O, fanga min be ni fani saba ye ani a be nɔrɔnin lo! O, jet min be ni sankaba ye!—o kelen be jijali kɛ, nin be jet bɛɛ kɛ gwansan! O, baleine, i be delili ɲini i fɛ tile fɛ min be fɛɛn bɛɛ lakunu, min be ɲɛnamaya dɔrɔn lo weele, nka i t’a di ne ma tugun. lanaya min ka dibi ka tɛmɛ, i ta fɛn ɲagamininw bɛɛ bɛ ne jukɔrɔ yan, fɛnɲanaman minw tun bɛ niin na fɔlɔ, o bɛ ne fiyɛ i ko fiɲɛ, nka sisan, o bɛ ne fiyɛ.

«O tuma na, sanbɛrɛ, sanbɛrɛ banbari, e kɔgɔji, kongo kɔnɔ kɔnɔw kelenpe bɛ lafiɲɛ sɔrɔ i ta fili banbaliw le ra.





ƝƐFƆLI 117. Balɛnw kɔlɔsili.

Balɛn naani minw fagara o wulafɛ, olu tun sara yɔrɔjan; kelen, yɔrɔjan fɔɲɔ fan fɛ; kelen, min man jan, ka taga fɔɲɔ fan fɛ; kelen bɛ ɲa fɛ; kelen ye kɔfɛla ye. O mɔgɔ saba labanw nana ɲɔgɔn fɛ sanni su ka ben; nka fɔɲɔ ta fan fɛ, mɔgɔ tun tɛ se ka se fɔ fɔ sɔgɔma; Kurun min tun k’a faga, o tun lanin bɛ a kɛrɛ fɛ su bɛɛ; o kurun tun ye Akabu ta le ye.

Waif-pole tun be sɔgɔ tilennin na baleine salen ka ji bɔyɔrɔ kɔnɔ; ani lanpan min tun sirinin be a sanfɛ, o tun be yeelen yɛlɛmayɛlɛmanin dɔ bɔra a kɔfɛla fiman kan, min tun be mɛnɛmɛnɛ, ani yɔrɔjan suu tilancɛ jikuruw kan, minw tun be balɛn yɔrɔbaw sɔgɔsɔgɔra dɔɔni dɔɔni i n’a fɔ jikuruw nɔgɔmanw baji daa la.

A tun bɛ i n’a fɔ Akabu ni a ta kurun kɔnɔmɔgɔw bɛɛ tun bɛ sinɔgɔra, fɔ Parse; minw tun be yɛlɛla kurun ɲɛfɛ, u tun be sigi ka jɛgɛw filɛ, minw tun be tulon kɛra jɛgɛba lamini na, ani u tun be sɛdiriyiri nɔgɔmanw gosi n’u kukala ye. Mankan dɔ i n’a fɔ Gomɔri ka jinaw minw ma yafa, olu ka kulekan min be sɔrɔ sɛrɛkiliw kɔnɔ, o tun be bolila ka yɛrɛyɛrɛ fiɲɛ fɛ.

A daminɛna a ka sinɔgɔ la, Akabu, ɲɛda ni ɲɛda, a ye Parsee ye; ani suu dibi tun b’u lamini, u tun be komi mɔgɔ labanw lo duniɲa kɔnɔ min be jii la. A ko: “Ne y’o siko kɛ tugun.”

«Suwotorow ta fan fɛ? Yala ne m’a fɔ, cɛkɔrɔba, ko suwotoro ni sukesu tɛ se ka kɛ i ta ye wa?»

“Jɔn lo be sa baji kan?”

“Nga ne y’a fɔ, cɛkɔrɔba, ko sanni i ka sa nin tagama nin na, i ka kan ka suu fila ye baji kan tiɲɛ na, fɔlɔ ma dilan mɔgɔ satɔ bolo la, ani laban ka yiri yetaw ka kan ka falen Ameriki.”

“Ɔnhɔn, ɔnhɔn! yelifɛn kabakoman min, Parsee:—sufɛwotoro n’a ka faniw be yɛlɛla baji sanfɛ ni jikuruw ye suuw tabagaw kama. Ha! O ɲɔgɔnna yelifɛn tɛna an ye joona.”

“I ka la a la walima i kana la a la, i ti se ka sa fɔɔ a ka ye, cɛkɔrɔba.”
