UMUTWE WA 4. Counterpane.

Nkibyuka bukeye bwaho nko ku manywa, nasanze ukuboko kwa Queequeg kuntera hejuru muburyo bwuje urukundo kandi bwuje urukundo. Wari warigeze gutekereza ko nabaye umugore we. Counterpane yari yububiko, yuzuye udusimba duto duto duto duto twinshi na mpandeshatu; kandi uku kuboko kwe kwishushanyijeho hirya no hino hamwe na labyrint ya Cretan ihuza ishusho, nta bice bibiri byayo byari bifite igicucu kimwe - bitewe nuko ndakeka ko kuba yarakomeje ukuboko kwe mu nyanja mu buryo butemewe n'izuba ndetse n'igicucu, amaboko y'ishati ye yazungurutse bidasanzwe. mu bihe bitandukanye - uku kuboko kwe, ndavuga, yashakishije isi yose nkumugozi wiyo mwenda umwe. Mubyukuri, igice kimwe kuryamyeho nkuko ukuboko kwabikoze nkibyuka bwa mbere, sinashoboraga kubivuga kuva mugitanda, nuko bahuza indabyo zabo; kandi numvise uburemere nigitutu gusa nashoboraga kubwira ko Queequeg yampobeye.

Ibyiyumvo byanjye byari bidasanzwe. Reka ngerageze kubisobanura. Nkiri umwana, nibuka neza ibintu bisa nkanjye byambayeho; yaba impamo cyangwa inzozi, ntabwo nigeze nshobora gutuza rwose. Ibintu byari bimeze. Nari narimo gutema caper cyangwa izindi - Ndatekereza ko yagerageje kunyerera hejuru ya chimney, nkuko nabonye siporo nkeya ikora iminsi mike ishize; na mama wa mama wanjye, mu buryo runaka cyangwa ubundi, buri gihe yankubitaga, cyangwa akanyohereza kuryama ku mugoroba, - mama yankuruje amaguru muri chimney anshira kuryama, nubwo hari saa mbiri gusa. nyuma ya saa sita zo ku ya 21 Kamena, umunsi muremure mu mwaka mu gice cyacu. Numvise mfite ubwoba. Ariko nta mfashanyo yabyo, nuko hejuru yintambwe njya mucyumba cyanjye gito mu igorofa rya gatatu, niyambura buhoro buhoro kugira ngo nice igihe, maze ndumirwa cyane mbona hagati y'amabati.

Naryamyeyo mbara nabi ko amasaha cumi nagatandatu yose agomba kurangira mbere yuko nizera ko nzuka. Amasaha cumi n'atandatu mu buriri! umuto wumugongo wanjye urababara kubitekereza. Kandi byari byoroshye cyane; izuba rimurika mu idirishya, hamwe no gutontoma gukomeye kw'abatoza mu mihanda, n'ijwi ry'amajwi y'abahuje ibitsina hirya no hino mu rugo. Numvise meze nabi kandi ndushijeho kuba mubi - amaherezo narahagurutse, nambara, kandi njya hasi njya mu birenge byanjye, nshakisha mama wanjye, maze mpita njugunya ku birenge bye, ndamwinginga ngo ampe kunyerera neza. imyitwarire idahwitse; ikintu cyose rwose ariko kuncira urubanza kubeshya abed igihe kirekire kidashoboka. Ariko yari mwiza kandi witonda cyane mubabyeyi ba mama, kandi inyuma byabaye ngombwa ko njya mucyumba cyanjye. Mumasaha menshi naryamye ndakangutse, numva ibintu bibi cyane kuruta uko nigeze kubikora kuva, ndetse no mubyago bikomeye byakurikiyeho. Ubwanyuma ngomba kuba naguye mubitotsi bibi bya doze; maze kubyuka buhoro buhoro - kimwe cya kabiri cyuzuye mu nzozi - nakinguye amaso, icyumba mbere cyaka izuba cyari cyuzuye umwijima w'inyuma. Ako kanya numvise ihungabana rinyuze kumurongo wanjye wose; nta kintu na kimwe cyagombaga kugaragara, kandi nta kintu na kimwe cyagombaga kumvikana; ariko ikiganza ndengakamere cyasaga nkicyashyizwe mubyanjye. Ukuboko kwanjye kumanitse kuri kaburimbo, kandi izina ritagira izina, ridashobora gutekerezwa, ryicecekeye cyangwa fantom, ukuboko kwarimo, byasaga naho bicaye hafi yigitanda cyanjye. Kubintu byasaga naho imyaka yarundanyije imyaka, naryamyeyo, nkonje ubwoba bwinshi buteye ubwoba, sinatinyuka gukuramo ikiganza; nyamara burigihe utekereza ko niba mbishoboye ariko nkabyutsa santimetero imwe, amarozi ateye ubwoba yari gucika. Ntabwo nari nzi uburyo iyi myumvire amaherezo yaranyobeye; ariko mbyutse mugitondo, mpinda umushyitsi nibuka byose, maze iminsi, ibyumweru n'amezi nyuma yaho nabuze uko ngerageza kugerageza gusobanura ibanga. Oya, kugeza kuriyi saha nyine, akenshi ndumiwe.

Noneho, kura ubwoba buteye ubwoba, kandi ibyiyumvo byanjye byo kumva ukuboko ndengakamere kwanjye byari bisa cyane, mubitangaje, nabiboneye kubyuka nkabona ukuboko kwa gipagani kwa Queequeg kuntera impande zose. Ariko muburebure ibyabaye mwijoro ryakeye byagarutsweho cyane, umwe umwe, mubyukuri, hanyuma ndaryama nzima gusa mubibazo bisekeje. Kuberako nagerageje kunyeganyeza ukuboko - gukingura umukwe umukwe - nyamara, asinziriye uko yari ameze, aracyampobera cyane, nkaho nta kindi uretse urupfu rukwiye kudutandukanya. Ubu nihatiye kumukangura - “Queequeg!” - ariko igisubizo cye cyonyine cyari igituba. Nahise nzunguruka, ijosi ryanjye numva ari nk'ifarashi; hanyuma ahita yumva akantu gato. Kujugunya kuruhande, haryamye tomahawk aryamye kuruhande rwumunyamahane, nkaho ari uruhinja rufite isura. Umutobe mwiza, mubyukuri, natekereje njye; abed hano munzu idasanzwe kumunsi mugari, hamwe numuntu urya umuntu na tomahawk! “Queequeg! —Mu izina ry'ibyiza, Queequeg, kanguka!” Mu burebure, nkoresheje amagambo menshi yo guterana amagambo, hamwe n'amajwi aranguruye kandi adahwema kwerekana ku buryo butagaragara bwo guhobera umugabo mugenzi we muri ubwo buryo bwo gushyingiranwa, nashoboye gukuramo induru; kandi ubungubu, asubiza inyuma ukuboko, yinyeganyeza hirya no hino nk'imbwa ya Newfoundland avuye mu mazi, maze yicara mu buriri, akomeye nk'abakozi ba pike, arandeba, anamukubita amaso nk'aho atibuka na gato. uko naje kuhaba, nubwo ubwenge buke bwo kumenya ikintu kuri njye byasaga naho bucya buhoro buhoro. Hagati aho, naryamye ncecetse ndamureba, nta kibazo gikomeye mfite ubu, maze nunama nitegereza gato ikiremwa gifite amatsiko. Igihe, amaherezo, ibitekerezo bye byasaga nkibigize gukora ku miterere ya mugenzi we baryamanye, maze ahinduka, nkaho, yiyunze nukuri; yahise asimbukira hasi, kandi ku bimenyetso n'amajwi amwe yampaye kumva ko, niba binshimishije, yabanza kwambara hanyuma akansiga nambaye nyuma, asiga inzu yose. Tekereza I, Queequeg, mubihe, iyi ni impinduramatwara cyane; ariko, ukuri nukuri, aba banyarugomo bafite imyumvire ivuka yo kuryoherwa, vuga icyo ushaka; biratangaje uburyo mubyukuri bafite ikinyabupfura. Ndashimira byumwihariko Queequeg, kuko yangaragarije ubupfura bwinshi no kubitekerezaho, mugihe nari nagize ikinyabupfura gikomeye; kumwitegereza avuye ku buriri, akareba uko umusarani we wose; kumwanya amatsiko yanjye arushaho kuba mwiza mubworozi bwanjye. Nubwo bimeze bityo, umugabo nka Queequeg utabona burimunsi, we n'inzira ze byari bikwiye rwose bidasanzwe kubijyanye.

Yatangiye kwambara hejuru yambara ingofero ye yinzuki, ndende cyane, hafi ya, hanyuma - aracyagabanya umutego we - ahiga inkweto. Niki munsi y'ijuru yabikoreye, sinshobora kubivuga, ariko urugendo rwe rwakurikiyeho kwari ukwikubita hasi - inkweto mu ntoki, n'ingofero - munsi yigitanda; mugihe, uhereye kumatiku yubugizi bwa nabi bukabije no kunanirwa, nasanze yari umunyamwete mukazi ko kwikorera wenyine; nubwo nta tegeko ryigenga nigeze numva, hari umuntu usabwa kwiherera mugihe yambaye inkweto. Ariko Queequeg, urabona, yari ikiremwa mugihe cyinzibacyuho - yaba inyenzi cyangwa ikinyugunyugu. Yari afite umuco uhagije kugirango yerekane ubwiyandarike bwe muburyo budasanzwe bushoboka. Amashuri ye yari atararangira. Yari umunyeshuri wa kaminuza. Niba atarigeze agira umuco muto, birashoboka cyane ko ataba yarigeze yikanga na gato inkweto; ariko rero, iyo ataba akiri umunyamahane, ntabwo yigeze arota kwinjira munsi yigitanda ngo abambare. Amaherezo, yagaragaye afite ingofero ye yunamye cyane kandi yunamye hejuru y'amaso, atangira kunyeganyega no gucumbagira hafi y'icyumba, nkaho, atamenyereye inkweto, inkweto ze zuzuye, zinyeganyeza inka - birashoboka ko zitakozwe. tegeka na none - ahubwo kumukubita no kumubabaza mugitangira bwa mbere mugitondo gikonje gikaze.

Kubona, ubungubu, nta mwenda uhari ku idirishya, kandi ko umuhanda ari muto cyane, inzu ihanganye yategetse kureba mu cyumba cyeruye, kandi ukareba byinshi kandi biteye isoni Queequeg yakoze, yiziritse ku bindi bike ariko ingofero ye n'inkweto; Namusabye uko nshoboye, kwihutisha umusarani mu buryo runaka, cyane cyane kwinjira mu ipantaro ye vuba bishoboka. Yarabyubahirije, hanyuma atangira kwiyuhagira. Icyo gihe mugitondo umukristo wese yaba yarogeje mumaso; ariko Queequeg, natangaye, anyuzwe no kugabanya kwiyuhagira mu gituza, amaboko, n'amaboko. Yahise yambara ikoti rye, afata isabune ikomeye ku meza yo gukaraba, ayijugunya mu mazi atangira kumupfuka mu maso. Narebaga kugirango ndebe aho yabitse urwembe, dore ko, akuramo inanga mu mfuruka yigitanda, asohora ibiti birebire bikozwe mu giti, atiyogoshesha umutwe, abizunguza gato kuri boot, maze agenda yerekeza kuri bito y'indorerwamo kurukuta, itangira gusakara cyane, cyangwa kuvuza imisaya. Tekereza I, Queequeg, iyi ikoresha ibikoresho byiza bya Rogers hamwe no kwihorera. Nyuma yaho, nibajije bike kuriyi mikorere ubwo naje kumenya icyuma cyiza umutwe wa inanga ikozwe, nuburyo bukaze cyane impande ndende zigororotse zihora zibitswe.

Ubwiherero bwe busigaye bwaragezweho bidatinze, maze yishimira gusohoka mu cyumba, yizingira mu ikoti rye rinini ry'indege, kandi akinisha inanga ye nk'ikibando cya marshal.





UMUTWE WA 5. Ifunguro rya mu gitondo.

Nahise nkurikira iyo nzira, maze manuka mu cyumba cyo mu kabari nandika nyir'inzu aseka cyane. Ntabwo nigeze nkunda kumugirira nabi, nubwo yari yaranyiboneye hamwe na gato mu kibazo cya mugenzi wanjye dusangiye.

Ariko, guseka neza nikintu cyiza gikomeye, kandi ni gake cyane ikintu cyiza; byinshi birababaje. Noneho, niba umuntu umwe, muburyo bwe bwite, agura ibintu byurwenya rwiza kumuntu uwo ari we wese, ntasubire inyuma, ahubwo yemere yishimye gukoresha kandi akoreshe muri ubwo buryo. Kandi umugabo ufite ikintu cyose gisekeje kuri we, menya neza ko muri uriya mugabo hari byinshi birenze ibyo utekereza.

Icyumba cy'akabari cyari cyuzuye abicumbikiraga bari baraye mu ijoro ryakeye, kandi uwo nari ntarabona neza. Bose bari hafi ya baleine; abo bashakanye bakuru, hamwe nabandi bashakanye, hamwe nabagatatu, hamwe nababaji bo mu nyanja, hamwe nabafatanyabikorwa bo mu nyanja, hamwe n'abacuzi bo mu nyanja, n'abacuranga inanga, n'abashinzwe ubwato; isosiyete yijimye kandi yikaraga, ifite ubwanwa bwa bosky; ishati idashwanyaguje, shaggy, bose bambaye amakoti yinguge kumyenda ya mugitondo.

Urashobora kuvuga neza igihe buri umwe yari amaze ku nkombe. Uyu musore ukiri muto umusaya muzima ni nkamapera yizuba ryizuba, kandi byasa nkimpumuro nziza nkimitsi; ntashobora kuba amaze iminsi itatu avuye murugendo rwe rwo mubuhinde. Uwo mugabo ukurikiraho asa nigicucu gito; ushobora kuvuga ko gukoraho ibiti bya satin biri muri we. Mu isura ya gatatu iracyafite ubushyuhe bwo mu turere dushyuha, ariko byoroheje gato; ntagushidikanya ko yamaze ibyumweru byose ku nkombe. Ariko ninde washobora kwerekana umusaya nka Queequeg? ibyo, bibujijwemo amabara atandukanye, byasaga nkiburengerazuba bwa Andes, kugirango bigaragare muburyo bumwe, butandukanye nikirere, akarere kuntara.

“Grub, ho!” noneho nyir'inzu yarize, akingura urugi, maze tujya gufata ifunguro rya mu gitondo.

Bavuga ko abagabo babonye isi, bityo bakoroherwa muburyo, bakitunga rwose. Ntabwo buri gihe, nubwo: Ledyard, umugenzi ukomeye wa New England, na Mungo Park, umwe muri Scotch; mubagabo bose, bari bafite ibyiringiro bike muri salle. Ariko birashoboka ko kwambuka Siberiya gusa mu muhigo washushanijwe n'imbwa nkuko Ledyard yabigenje, cyangwa gufata urugendo rurerure wenyine ku gifu cyuzuye ubusa, mu mutima wa negro wo muri Afurika, ibyo bikaba byari igiteranyo cy'imikorere mibi ya Mungo - nk'urugendo, Ndabivuze, ntibishobora kuba uburyo bwiza cyane bwo kugera kuri polish yo hejuru. Biracyaza, kubice byinshi, ibintu nkibi bigomba kuba ahantu hose.

Ibi bitekerezo hano gusa bigaragazwa nigihe tumaze kwicara kumeza, kandi nkaba nitegura kumva inkuru nziza zijyanye na baleine; Ntibitangaje, hafi ya buri mugabo yakomeje guceceka cyane. Kandi sibyo gusa, ahubwo basaga nisoni. Nibyo, hano hari imbwa-mbwa zo mu nyanja, inyinshi muri zo zitagira isoni na gato zari zinjiye mu nyanja nini ku nyanja ndende - abantu bose batazi kuri bo - maze zibapfira nta guhumbya; kandi nyamara, hano bicaye kumeza yo gusangira ifunguro rya mugitondo - bose bahamagaye kimwe, uburyohe bwa bene wabo - bareba nk'intama zintama nkaho batigeze babona intama zimwe ziri mumisozi yicyatsi. Kubona amatsiko; ibi bidubu biteye isoni, aba barwanyi b'intwari batinyuka!

Ariko kubijyanye na Queequeg - kubera iki, Queequeg yicaye hagati yabo - ku meza yameza, nayo yarahindutse; nkibyiza nkicicle. Kugira ngo mumenye neza ko ntashobora kuvuga byinshi kubworozi bwe. Umukunzi we ukomeye ntiyashoboraga kwerekana neza ko yazanye inanga ye mu gitondo, kandi akayikoresha nta muhango; kugera hejuru yameza hamwe nayo, mukaga kari hafi yimitwe myinshi, no gufata inyama zinka zimugana. Ariko ibyo rwose byakozwe neza na we, kandi buriwese azi ko mubitekerezo byabantu benshi, gukora ikintu cyiza ari ukubikora witonze.

Ntabwo tuzavuga umwihariko wa Queequeg hano; uburyo yirinze ikawa hamwe nudupapuro dushyushye, kandi akoresha ibitekerezo bye bitavangiye kuri beefsteaks, byakozwe bidasanzwe. Birahagije, ko ifunguro rya mu gitondo rirangiye asubira inyuma nk'abandi basigaye mu cyumba rusange, acana umuyoboro we wa tomahawk, maze yicara aho atuje kandi anywa itabi yambaye ingofero ye itandukanijwe, ubwo nashakaga gutembera.





UMUTWE WA 6. Umuhanda.

Iyaba nari natangajwe no kubanza kubona umuntu ku giti cye nka Queequeg yazengurukaga muri societe yubupfura yo mumujyi utuwe, ibyo byahise bitangara nyuma yo gutembera kumanywa yambere mumihanda ya New Bedford.

Mu nzira nyabagendwa yegereye ikivuko, icyambu icyo aricyo cyose gishobora gutanga kenshi kugirango turebe ikibazo cyanditse kidafite inyandiko ziva mu bice by'amahanga. Ndetse no mu mihanda ya Broadway na Chestnut, abasare bo mu nyanja ya Mediterane rimwe na rimwe bazajya basetsa abadamu bafite ubwoba. Umuhanda wa Regent ntabwo uzwi na Lascars na Maleziya; no kuri Bombay, muri Green Apollo, Yankees bazima bakunze gutera ubwoba kavukire. Ariko New Bedford ikubita Street Street yose na Wapping. Muri izi mpigi zavuzwe haruguru urabona abasare gusa; ariko muri New Bedford, abantu barya abantu bahagaze baganira kumihanda; abanyarugomo rwose; benshi muribo nyamara bitwaza amagufwa yabo inyama zanduye. Bituma umuntu utazi yitegereza.

Ariko, usibye Feegeeans, Tongatobooarrs, Erromanggoans, Pannangians, na Brighggians, kandi, usibye ingero zo mwishyamba zubukorikori butitondewe kumihanda, uzabona ahandi hantu hakiri amatsiko, rwose birasekeje. Hano buri cyumweru ugera muri uyu mujyi amanota menshi yicyatsi cya Vermonters nabagabo ba New Hampshire, bose bafite inyota yo kunguka nicyubahiro muburobyi. Ahanini ni bato, ya stalwart frame; bagenzi bacu batemye amashyamba, none bagashaka guta ishoka no kunyaga balale. Benshi ni icyatsi nkimisozi yicyatsi aho baturutse. Mubintu bimwe wabitekereza ariko amasaha make. Reba hano! icyo gice kizunguruka mu mfuruka. Yambaye ingofero yinzuki hamwe namira ikote umurizo, akenyeye umukandara-umukandara nicyuma. Hano haje undi ufite sou'-wester n'umwenda wa bombazine.

Nta dandy zororerwa mu mujyi uzagereranya n’uwororerwa mu gihugu - ndavuga dandy yuzuye igicucu - mugenzi wanjye, mu minsi y’imbwa, azaca hegitari ebyiri mu ntoki za buckskin kubera gutinya kumanika amaboko. Noneho iyo igihugu cyijimye nkibi kimujyanye mumutwe kugirango cyamamare, kandi kifatanije nuburobyi bukomeye bwa baleine, ugomba kubona ibintu bisekeje akora amaze kugera ku cyambu. Mu kwambika imyambaro ye yo mu nyanja, ategeka inzogera-buto ku ikoti rye; imishumi kumurongo wa canvas. Ah, umukene-Imbuto! mbega ukuntu bizaturika cyane iyo mishumi muri gale ya mbere yo gutaka, mugihe utwarwa, imishumi, buto, nibindi byose, kumuhogo wumuyaga.

Ariko ntutekereze ko uyu mujyi uzwi cyane ufite inanga, abantu barya abantu, hamwe nuduseke kugirango bereke abashyitsi be. Ntabwo ari rwose. Biracyaza New Bedford ni ahantu habi. Iyo tutaba abacuzi b'inyamanswa, ako gace k'ubutaka uyu munsi wenda kari kumera nk'inyanja ya Labrador. Nkuko biri, ibice byigihugu cye cyinyuma birahagije kugirango umuntu atere ubwoba, birasa neza. Umujyi ubwawo birashoboka ko ari ahantu heza cyane ho gutura, mu Bwongereza bwose. Nigihugu cyamavuta, cyukuri gihagije: ariko ntameze nka Kanani; igihugu, nacyo, cy'ibigori na vino. Imihanda ntigenda n'amata; eka no mu gihe c'impeshyi ntibayitera amagi mashya. Nyamara, nubwo bimeze gurtyo, ntahantu na hamwe muri Amerika uzasangamo amazu asa na patrician; parike n'ubusitani byiza cyane, kuruta muri New Bedford. Baturutse he? nigute cyatewe kuriyi scoria ya scraggy yigihugu?

Genda witegereze ibyuma byerekana ibimenyetso byicyuma kizengurutse inzu ndende, kandi ikibazo cyawe kizasubizwa. Yego; aya mazu yose yintwari nubusitani bwindabyo byaturutse mu nyanja ya Atalantika, Pasifika, nu Buhinde. Umwe muri bose, bavuzaga inanga bakururwa hano kuva munsi yinyanja. Ese Herr Alexander ashobora gukora ibikorwa nkibyo?

Bavuga ko muri New Bedford, ba se, baha abakobwa babo amafi ya baleine, bakagabana na bishywa babo hamwe na pisine nkeya. Ugomba kujya muri New Bedford kureba ubukwe bwiza; kuberako, bavuga ko bafite ibigega byamavuta muri buri nzu, kandi buri joro utitonze utwika uburebure bwa buji ya spermaceti.

Mu gihe cyizuba, umujyi uraryoshye kubona; yuzuye amakarita meza-inzira ndende yicyatsi na zahabu. Muri Kanama, hejuru cyane mu kirere, amafarashi meza cyane kandi menshi cyane-igituba, ubwenge bwa candelabra, uhindura umuhisi ukoresheje imishino yabo igororotse yuzuye indabyo. Ishoborabyose rero ni ubuhanzi; mu turere twinshi two muri New Bedford yarengeje amaterasi y’indabyo hejuru y’indabyo zitagira ubutayu zajugunywe ku munsi wanyuma w’irema.

N'abagore ba New Bedford, barabya nka roza zabo zitukura. Ariko roza zirabya gusa mu cyi; mugihe karnasi nziza yimisaya yabo ihoraho nkumucyo wizuba mwijuru rya karindwi. Ahandi hose bihuye nuburabyo bwabo, ntushobora, usibye muri Salem, aho bambwira ko abakobwa bakiri bato bahumeka imitsi nkiyi, umukunzi wabo wabasare babahumuriza kubirometero byinshi kuruhande, nkaho bashushanya hafi ya Moluccasi ihumura aho kuba umusenyi wa Puritanic.





UMUTWE WA 7. Shapeli.

Muri iyi New Bedford nyine hari Chapel ya Whaleman, kandi bake ni abarobyi bameze neza, bidatinze berekeza mu nyanja y'Ubuhinde cyangwa Pasifika, bananiwe gusura icyumweru aho hantu. Nzi neza ko ntabikoze.

Nsubiye mu rugendo rwanjye rwa mbere mu gitondo, nongeye gusuhuza iki gikorwa kidasanzwe. Ijuru ryari ryahindutse riva mu mbeho ikonje, izuba, rihinduka ibicu n'ibicu. Nipfunyitse mu ikoti ryanjye rya shaggy y'igitambara cyitwa bearskin, narwanye inzira yanjye kurwanya umuyaga winangiye. Ninjiye, nasanze itorero rito ryatatanye ry'abasare, n'abagore b'abasare n'abapfakazi. Guceceka gucecetse byategetse, gusa byacitse rimwe na rimwe n'induru y'umuyaga. Buri wese usenga acecetse yasaga nkuwicaye nkana atandukanijwe nundi, nkaho buri gahinda kicecekeye kari insular kandi ntigishobora kuvugana. Padiri Mapple yarahagurutse, maze mu ijwi ryoroheje ry'ubutegetsi budasuzugura ategeka abantu batatanye guterana. “Starboard gangway, ngaho! kuruhande rwibibaho - larboard gangway to starboard! Midship! hagati! ”

Hafi ya bose bifatanije kuririmba iyi ndirimbo, yabyimbye hejuru hejuru yo gutaka kwumuyaga. Haca akanya gato; umubwiriza yahinduye buhoro buhoro amababi ya Bibiliya, hanyuma, arambura ukuboko hasi ku rupapuro rukwiye, yagize ati: “Bakundwa bo mu bwato mukundwa, fata umurongo wa nyuma w'igice cya mbere cya Yona - 'Kandi Imana yari yateguye ifi nini kuri kumira Yona. '”

“Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

“With this sin of disobedience in him, Jonah still further flouts at God, by seeking to flee from Him. He thinks that a ship made by men will carry him into countries where God does not reign, but only the Captains of this earth. He skulks about the wharves of Joppa, and seeks a ship that's bound for Tarshish. There lurks, perhaps, a hitherto unheeded meaning here. By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
