SABABU 33. Specksnyder ka mɔgɔw.

Balema-baarakɛlaw ka sɔrɔdasiw ko la, a bɛ iko nin ye yɔrɔ ɲuman ye i n’a fɔ yɔrɔ tɔw bɛɛ walasa ka sokɔnɔna danfara dɔɔnin sigi kurun kɔnɔ, min bɛ bɔ sɔrɔdasikulu ka harpooneer kulu ka sɔrɔli la, kalasi min tɛ dɔn tiɲɛ na kɔgɔjida la ni balema-kurunw ka kurunba.

Nafaba min bɛ harpooneer weleli la, o bɛ jira ni a ye, ko a daminɛ na Hollande mɔni kɔrɔ la, a bɛ san kɛmɛ fila ni kɔ bɔ, balema kurunba dɔ ɲɛmɔgɔya tun ma don pewu mɔgɔ la min bɛ wele sisan ko kapitɛni, nka a tun tilalen don u ni ɲɔgɔn cɛ ale ni sɔrɔdasi dɔ min tɔgɔ ye ko Specksnyder. Daɲɛ kɔrɔ la, nin daɲɛ in kɔrɔ ye Fasa-Tigɛbaga; baarakɛcogo kɔni, waati tɛmɛnen kɔfɛ, a y’a kɛ ka bɛn Harpooneer kuntigi ma. O waatiw la, kapitɛni ka fanga tun bɛ dan kurun bolicogo n’a ɲɛnabɔli bɛɛ ma; k’a sɔrɔ balema ɲinini baarada n’a haminankow bɛɛ kan, Specksnyder walima Chief Harpooneer de tun bɛ fanga la. Angletɛri Gronland jɛgɛko la, Specksioneer tɔgɔ tiɲɛnen kɔnɔ, nin Hollande jamanatigi kɔrɔ in tora hali bi, nka a ka danbe kɔrɔ bɛ surunya dusukasi la. Sisan a bɛ jɔyɔrɔ la dɔrɔn i n’a fɔ Harpooneer kɔrɔba; ani o cogo la, a ye kapitɛni ka subalterns dɔgɔmanninw dɔ dɔrɔn de ye. O bɛɛ n’a ta, i n’a fɔ harponkɛlaw ka taamacogo ɲuman de kan, balema-ɲini taama ɲɛtaa bɛ bɔ kosɛbɛ, wa ikomi Ameriki jɛgɛko la, a tɛ sɔrɔdasi nafama dɔrɔn ye kurun kɔnɔ, nka a ye ko dɔw la (su kɔlɔsili balema-ɲiniyɔrɔ la) komandan kurunba kɔnɔ fana ye ale ta ye; o de kama, kɔgɔji ka politiki kumasenba b’a ɲini, ko a ka kan ka sigi tɔgɔ la ka bɔ cɛw la minnu bɛ masɔrɔ ɲɛfɛ, ani ka danfara don cogo dɔ la i n’a fɔ u ka baarakɛlaw ɲɛmɔgɔ hali n’a y’a sɔrɔ tuma bɛɛ, u fɛ, u bɛ jate u ka sigida bɛnkan ye cogo min na.

Sisan, danfaraba min bɛ sɔrɔdasi ni cɛ cɛ kɔgɔji la, o ye nin ye — fɔlɔ bɛ balo kɔfɛ, laban bɛ ɲɛnamaya ɲɛfɛ. O la, balema-kurunw ni jagokɛlaw bɛɛ la, furuɲɔgɔnmaw bɛ u sigiyɔrɔ sɔrɔ kapitɛni fɛ; ani o cogo kelen na fana, Ameriki balemakɛla fanba la, harpooneerw bɛ sigi kurun kɔfɛyɔrɔ la. O kɔrɔ ye k’u b’u ka dumuni ta kapitɛni ka kabini kɔnɔ, ka sunɔgɔ yɔrɔ la k’u bɛ kumaɲɔgɔnya kɛ n’a ye cogo la min tɛ ɲɛ.

Hali ni Saheli balema ɲinini waati jan (a ka jan ni taama tɔw bɛɛ ye sisan walima hadamaden ye minnu kɛ), o farati kɛrɛnkɛrɛnnenw, ani nafa jɛkulu min bɛ sɔrɔ tɔn dɔ cɛ, minnu bɛɛ bɛ bɔ u la, u ka bon walima u ka dɔgɔ k’a sababu kɛ u ka tɔnɔw tɛ sara latigɛlen na, nka u ka ɲɔgɔn ka ɲɛnajɛ de kan, ka fara u ka kɔrɔsili, u ka jagɛlɛya ani u ka baara gɛlɛn kan; hali ni nin ko ninnu bɛɛ bɛ kɛ tuma dɔw la, u ka teli ka kololi dɔ bange min man gɛlɛn ka tɛmɛ jagokɛlaw kan caman na; o bɛɛ n’a ta, a kana kɛ abada ko nin balemakɛlaw bɛ se ka sigi ɲɔgɔn fɛ cogo min na i n’a fɔ Mezopotami denbaya kɔrɔ dɔ; o bɛɛ kama, kɛnɛmafɛnw, a dɔgɔyalenba la, quarter-deck (dakun naaninan) man teli ka lafiya farikolo ta fan fɛ, wa u tɛ ban cogo si la. Tiɲɛ na, u caman ye Nantucket kurunw ye, i bɛna kurunba ye minnu kɔnɔ, a bɛ ka a ka quarter-deck parade ni nisɔndiyaba ye min tɛ tɛmɛ sɔrɔdasi kɔgɔjidala si kan; ayi, ka kɛnɛma bonya caman ɲini i n’a fɔ a bɛ masakɛ ka fini jɛman don, wa a tɛ piloti fini nɔgɔlenba ye.

Wa hali ni cɛw bɛɛ la, Pequod kapitɛni min tun bɛ miiri kosɛbɛ, o de tun ka dɔgɔ o miirili sugu la min tun ka dun kosɛbɛ; ani hali ni bonya kelenpe min tun bɛ a la, o tun ye kanminɛli ye min bɛ kɛ cogo jɛlen na, min bɛ kɛ yɔrɔnin kelen; hali n'a tun t'a ɲini mɔgɔ si fɛ a ka sanbara bɔ a sen na sanni a ka senna-tɛgɛrɛ kɛ quarter-deck kan; ani hali ni waati dɔw tun bɛ yen, k’a sababu kɛ ko kɛrɛnkɛrɛnnenw ye minnu bɛ tali kɛ ko kɛlenw na kɔfɛ minnu bɛna ɲɛfɔ ka ɲɛ, a y’u ɲɛfɔ ni kumasenw ye minnu tɛ deli ka kɛ, a kɛra majigilenya ye wo, a kɛra terrorem ye wo, walima cogo wɛrɛ la o bɛɛ n’a ta, hali Kapiteni Akabu tun tɛ kɔgɔji cogoyaw n’a baarakɛcogo bɛɛ la belebelew kɔlɔsi cogo si la.

Laala, a tɛna laban fana k’a dɔn, ko o cogoyaw ni baarakɛcogo ninnu kɔfɛ, n’a bɛ fɔ cogo min na, a y’a yɛrɛ masike tuma dɔw la; a kɛra cogo o cogo, ka baara kɛ n’u ye kuntilenna wɛrɛw kama ani kuntilenna wɛrɛw kama minnu ka ca ni u tun laɲinin ye ka baara kɛ ni u ye sariya siratigɛ la. O a hakili sultanism dɔ, min tun bɛ cogo wɛrɛ la cogo ɲuman na, o ma jira; o cogoyaw sababu fɛ o sultanisme kelen in kɛra farikolo ye dictature (jamanatigiya) kɔnɔ min tɛ se ka bali. Bari ka kɛ cɛ ka hakili ta fan fɛ sanfɛla ye a bɛna min kɛ, a tɛ se ka kɛ abada ka fanga ta min bɛ se ka kɛ, min bɛ sɔrɔ cɛ tɔw kan, ni kɛnɛma seko ni dɔnko sugu dɔw dɛmɛni tɛ, tuma bɛɛ, u yɛrɛ la, ka caya walima ka dɔgɔ, ka kɛ fɛn fitinin ye ani ka kɛ basigi ye. Nin de ye, min bɛ Ala ka Mansamara kuntigi lakikaw tanga diɲɛ ka bɔnɛw ma fo abada; ani ka bonyaba to o fiɲɛ bɛ se ka min di, cɛ minnu bɛ tɔgɔ bɔ kosɛbɛ u ka dɔgɔya danma fɛ ka tɛmɛn Ala ka Inert bolo dogolen sugandilen kan, ka tɛmɛ u ka sanfɛla salen kan siga t’a la ka tɛmɛ jama hakɛ salen kan. O jogo ɲumanba suguw bɛ dogo o fɛn misɛnnin ninnu na ni politiki dannayabaliya juguw bɛ u bila, fo masakɛ ka ko dɔw la hali hakilintan nalonmaw la, u ye fanga di u ma. Nka tuma min na, i n’a fɔ Nikola Zar ta, dugukoloko mansamara masafugulan bololanɛgɛ bɛ mansamara hakili dɔ lamini; o kɔfɛ, plebeian sagakuluw bɛ u kunbiri gwan ka kɔn cɛmancɛlafanga belebeleba ɲɛ. Dramakɛla dusukasibaga fana min tun bɛna mɔgɔ salenw ka sebaliya jira a ka sɛgɛsɛgɛli dafalen na ani a ka wulicogo tilennen na, o tɛna ɲinɛ abada hakilina dɔ kɔ, min nafa ka bon kosɛbɛ a ka seko la, i n’a fɔ a bɛ fɔ min kan sisan.

Nka Akab, n ka Kapiteni, bɛ lamaga hali bi ne ɲɛfɛ a ka Nantucket grimness ni shagginess bɛɛ la; ani nin yɔrɔ in na min bɛ maga Mansamaraw ni Masakɛw la, ne man kan k’a dogo ko ne bɛ tali kɛ ale bɔɲɔgɔnko balema-ɲinina kɔrɔ faantan dɔ dɔrɔn de la; o de kosɔn, kɛnɛma bonya jaanw ni sow bɛɛ bɛ ban ne la. Oh, Akabu! min bɛna kɛ fɛnba ye i kɔnɔ, o ka kan ka bɔ sankolo la, ka jigin ka taa a fɛ dugujukɔrɔ, ka jira fiɲɛ farikolontan na!





SABABU 34. Kabini-Tabali.

Tilelafana don; ani Dough-Boy, n’o ye sotigi ye, k’a ɲɛda buruburu jɛman bɔ kabini-sɔgɔsɔgɔninjɛ la, ka sufɛdumuni laseli a matigi n’a makɛ ma; min sigilen bɛ lee quarter-boat kɔnɔ, a bɛ ka tile kɔlɔsili kɛ sisan; ani sisan a bɛ ka latitude jate ni bobo ye tablɛti nɔgɔlen kan, min bɛ i n’a fɔ medayi, min maralen bɛ o don o don baara kama a sen sanfɛla la. K’a sababu kɛ a ma a janto o kibaruyaw la pewu, i bɛna a miiri ko Akab min dusu tiɲɛna, o ma a ka dɔgɔyali mɛn. Nka sisan, a kɛlen ka mizen ka finiw minɛ, a b’a yɛrɛ wuli ka taa deki la, ka kan kelen, min tɛ nisɔndiya, k’a fɔ ko “Sufɛdumuni, Mr. Starbuck,” a tununna kabini kɔnɔ.

Ni a ka sultan senna-tɛgɛrɛ laban sara, ni Starbuck, n’o ye Emir fɔlɔ ye, dalilu bɛɛ b’a bolo k’a miiri ko a sigilen bɛ, o tuma na, Starbuck bɛ kunun ka bɔ a ka lafiya la, ka wuli damadɔw kɛ pankurunw kan, ani, kaburu ɲɛjibɔ kɔfɛ ka don binnakili kɔnɔ, a b’a fɔ, ni nisɔndiya dɔ ye, “Sufɛdumuni, Mr. Stubb,” ka jigin scuttle kɔnɔ. Emir filanan bɛ lafiɲɛ dɔɔnin rigging ko la, ka sɔrɔ ka brace kunba lamaga dɔɔnin, k’a lajɛ n’a bɛna ɲɛ ni o juru nafama in ye, a fana bɛ doni kɔrɔ ta o cogo kelen na, ani ni teliya ye “Sufɛdumuni, Mr Flask,” bɛ tugu a ɲɛfɛmɔgɔw kɔ.

Nka Emir sabanan, n’a y’a yɛrɛ ye sisan a kelen na quarter-deck kan, a bɛ iko a lafiyalen don ka bɔ ɲininkali dɔw la minnu bɛ a yɛrɛ minɛ; sabu, a bɛ dɔnniya ɲɛjibɔ sugu bɛɛ wuli fan bɛɛ fɛ, k’a ka sanbaraw bɔ, a bɛ burufiyɛkan dɔ gosi ka don Turkiba kunkolo sanfɛ yɛrɛ, nka mankan tɛ min na o kɔfɛ, ni sɛgɛnlafiɲɛbɔlan ŋana dɔ ye, k’a ka fini wuli san fɛ ka don mizentop kɔnɔ walasa ka tabali dɔ kɛ, a bɛ jigin ka wuli ka taa fo yɔrɔ jan a dɔgɔyalenba la, n’a bɛ to ka ye ka bɔ kurunba kɔnɔ, ka taama tɔw bɛɛ kɔsegin, k’a kɛ kɔfɛla lase san fɛ ni dɔnkili ye. Nka sanni a ka sen don kabini da la min bɛ duguma, a bɛ jɔ dɔɔnin, ka ɲɛda kura bila kurun kɔnɔ pewu, o kɔfɛ, Flask fitinin yɛrɛmahɔrɔnyalen, nisɔndiyalen bɛ don masakɛ Akab ɲɛkɔrɔ, Abjectus, walima Jɔn jogo la.

Kɔgɔjida-baarakɛcogo kɛlenw ka kɛwalew kɛcogo jugu ye minnu lamɔ, o tɛ fɛn fitinin ye, k’a sɔrɔ k’u to kurunba kɔnɔ, sɔrɔdasi dɔw bɛna u yɛrɛ muɲu ni jagɛlɛya ye ani ni sɔsɔli ye u ka komandan fɛ o bɛɛ n’a ta, tan ni kelen, o sɔrɔdasi yɛrɛw ka jigin waati wɛrɛ la u ka laadala dumuni na o komandan kelen in ka so kɔnɔ, ka o yɔrɔnin bɛɛ u ka juguya kɛ, k’a fɔ ko fiɲɛ min bɛ mɔgɔ mafilɛ ni majigin ye a fan fɛ, k’a sigilen to tabali kun na nin ye kabako ye, tuma dɔw la, a bɛ kɛ nisɔndiyako ye kosɛbɛ. Mun na nin danfara in bɛ yen? Gɛlɛya dɔ don wa? Laala, o tɛ. Ka kɛ Babilonɛ masakɛ Bɛlsazar ye; ani ka kɛ Bɛlsazar ye, a ma kɛ kuncɛbaya ye, nka ni bonya ye, siga t’a la ko a ka kan ka kɛ ko diɲɛ bonya dɔ tun bɛ o kɔnɔ. Nka min b’a yɛrɛ ka dunan welelenw ka dumunikɛtabali ɲɛminɛ ni masaya ni hakilitigiya hakili ɲuman ye, o cɛ ka fanga ni a ka fanga min tɛ sɔsɔ, n’o ye mɔgɔ kelen-kelen bɛɛ ka fanga ye o waati kɔnɔ o cɛ ka masaya bɛ tɛmɛ Bɛlsazar ta kan, bawo Bɛlsazar tun tɛ mɔgɔba ye. Min ye a teriw dun siɲɛ kelen dɔrɔn, o ye Sesar ye min ye, o dege. O ye sosiyali czarship ka jatɔya ye min tɛ se ka muɲu. Sisan, n’i ye kuruntigi dɔ ka fanga ofisiyali fara o jateminɛ kan, o tuma na, ni jateminɛ ye, i bɛna o kɔgɔji-fɛnw danfaralen sababu sɔrɔ min kofɔra sisan.

A ka tabali kan, nɛgɛfinw tun bɛ min kan, Akabu tun bɛ ɲɛmɔgɔya ta i n’a fɔ kɔgɔji waraba bobo, min kunsigi tun bɛ a la, korali finman bajida la, a denw tun bɛ kɛlɛ la, nka u tun bɛ bonya da u kan hali bi. A yɛrɛ ka waati bɛnnen na, sɔrɔdasi kelen-kelen bɛɛ tun bɛ makɔnɔni kɛ walasa ka baara kɛ u ye. U tun bɛ iko denmisɛnninw ka kɔn Akabu ɲɛ. o bɛɛ n’a ta, Akabu kɔnɔ, a tun bɛ iko sigida kuncɛbaya fitinin tun tɛ yen. Ni hakili kelen ye, u ɲɛw laɲinitaw bɛɛ sirilen bɛ cɛkɔrɔba ka muru la, k’a sɔrɔ a bɛ minɛnba in ja a ɲɛfɛ. N t’a miiri ko diɲɛ ta fan fɛ, u tun bɛna o waati nɔgɔ ni kɔlɔsili fitinin ye, hali ni barokun ye min tɛ mɔgɔ si ta ye i n’a fɔ waati cogoya. Ayi! Wa tuma min na a y’a bolo bɔ a ka muru ni foroko la, misisogo tigɛlen tun sirilen bɛ minnu ni ɲɔgɔn cɛ, Akabu ye Starbuck ka tasa jira a fan fɛ o cogo la, furuɲɔgɔn y’a ka sogo sɔrɔ i n’a fɔ a bɛ saraka sɔrɔ ka a tigɛ ni dususuma ye; ani dɔɔnin daminɛna ni, n’a sɔrɔla, muru ye baganmara kɛ tasa la; k'a ɲimi mankan tɛ min na; a y'a munumunu, a ma kɛ k'a sɔrɔ a ma a janto a yɛrɛ la. Sabula, i n’a fɔ Masakɛya seli min kɛra Frankfort, Alemaɲi masakɛ bɛ dumuni kɛ yɔrɔ min na kosɛbɛ ni Mansamara Sugandilikɛla wolonwula ye, o cogo la, o kabini dumuniw tun ye dumuni ye cogo dɔ la, minnu tun bɛ dun makun juguba kɔnɔ o bɛɛ n'a ta, tabali kan, cɛkɔrɔba Akabu ma baro bali; ale yɛrɛ dɔrɔn de tun ye bobo ye. O kɛra lafiya ye dɛ Stubb sɔgɔli la, tuma min na soso dɔ ye bɔgɔdaga barikama kɛ minɛyɔrɔ la min bɛ duguma. Ani Flask fitinin faantan, ale tun ye denkɛ fitinin ye, ani cɛnin fitinin nin denbaya ka ɲɛnajɛ sɛgɛnnen in na. A ta tun ye misisogo kɔgɔlen senkɔniw ye; a ta tun bɛna kɛ dununw ye. Walasa Flask tun b’a miiri ko a b’a yɛrɛ dɛmɛ, n’a sɔrɔ o tun bɛ iko a bɛ bɛn sonyali ma degere fɔlɔ la. Ni a tun y’a yɛrɛ dɛmɛ o tabali kan, siga t’a la, a tun tɛna se k’a kun kɔrɔta ka tɛmɛ o kan abada nin diɲɛ lakika in kɔnɔ; o bɛɛ n'a ta, kabako don k'a fɔ ko Akabu ma a bali abada. Wa ni Flask tun y’a yɛrɛ dɛmɛ, a tun bɛ se ka kɛ ko Akabu tun ma deli k’o kɔlɔsi fo ka se a dan na. Min ka dɔgɔ ni o bɛɛ ye, yala Flask y’a yɛrɛ jate k’a yɛrɛ dɛmɛ ka tulu kɛ wa. Yala a tun b’a miiri ko kuruntigiw banna o la a ma, k’a sababu kɛ a ɲɛda jɛlen, tile bɛ min na, o sɔgɔli kosɔn; walima n’a y’a jate ko, taama jan in na o ji suguw la, tulu bɛ sɔngɔba la, o de kosɔn a tɛ a bolo, subaltern ye; o bɛɛ n'a ta, Flask, bɔnɛ ye a sɔrɔ! tun ye mɔgɔ ye min tɛ tulu sɔrɔ!

Fɛn wɛrɛ. Flask de tun ye mɔgɔ laban ye min jiginna sufɛdumuni na, ani Flask ye cɛ fɔlɔ ye min wulila. Ka jate! Sabu nin sababu fɛ Flask ka sufɛdumuni tun bɛ jam kosɛbɛ waati dɔ la. Starbuck ni Stubb fila bɛɛ de y’a daminɛ; o bɛɛ n’a ta, nɛɛma fana b’u bolo ka lafiɲɛ kɔfɛ. Ni Stubb yɛrɛ, min ka bon ni Flask ye peg kelen dɔrɔn, a kɛra cogo o cogo, dumuni nege fitinin dɔrɔn de bɛ a la, wa a ma mɛn, a ye a ka dumuni kuncɛ taamasiɲɛw jira, o tuma na, Flask ka kan k’a yɛrɛ wuli, a tɛna tɛmɛ da saba kan o don sabu a bɛ baara senuma kɛlɛ Stubb ka kɔn Flask ɲɛ ka taa deki la. O de kama a kɛra ko Flask sɔnna a ma siɲɛ kelen a dan na, ko kabini a wulila ka kɛ sɔrɔdasi danbe ye, kabini o waati, a ma deli k’a dɔn a ye min ye n’o tɛ kɔngɔ tɛ, ka caya walima ka dɔgɔya. Sabu a ye min dun, o ma kɔngɔ lajɔ kosɛbɛ, fo k'a to a la sa. Hɛrɛ ni wasa, Flask y’a miiri, o bɔra ne kɔnɔ fo abada. Ne ye sɔrɔdasi ɲɛmɔgɔ ye; nka, n b’a fɛ cogo min na n ka se ka misisogo kɔrɔ dɔɔni minɛ foro kɔnɔ, i n’a fɔ n tun b’a kɛ cogo min na n tun bɛ masɔrɔ ɲɛfɛ. Promotion denw bɛ yen sisan; nɔɔrɔ nafantan bɛ yen : ɲɛnamaya hakilintan bɛ yen ! O tɛmɛnen kɔ, n’a kɛra ko Pequod kurunbolila gansan o kuruntigi gansan tun bɛ Flask dimi Flask ka fanga la, o kuruntigi tun ka kan ka min kɛ, walasa ka waleɲumandɔn caman sɔrɔ, o tun ye ka taa kɔfɛ sufɛdumuni waati, ka filɛli kɛ at Flask through the cabin sky-light, sigilen hakilintan ni dusukasilen Akab jugu ɲɛkɔrɔ.

Sisan, Akabu n’a furuɲɔgɔn saba ye fɛn dɔ sigi sen kan min bɛ se ka wele ko tabali fɔlɔ Pequod ka so kɔnɔ. U bɔlen kɔ, k’a kɛ cogo la min bɛ wuli ka taa u nali la, finimugu min tun bɛ kɛ ni fini ye, o tun bɛ saniya, walima n’a fɔra ka ɲɛ, a seginna cogo kɔrɔtɔlen dɔ la, sotigi fariman fɛ. Wa o kɔfɛ, u ye harpooneer saba wele ka na seli in na, u kɛra a residuary legatees ye. U ye waati dɔɔnin baarakɛlaw ka so sugu dɔ kɛ kabini sanfɛla ni barikama in na.

O danfara kabakoma ni kapitɛni ka tabali ka gɛlɛya yebali ni tɔgɔ tɛ min na, o tun ye lase ni nɔgɔya ye min tɛ hami, o tun ye demokarasi ye min tun bɛ ɲini ka kɛ jɔrɔnanko ye, o mɔgɔ fitininw ka demokarasi min tun ye harpooneers ye. K’a sɔrɔ u makɛw, furuɲɔgɔnmaw, tun bɛ i n’a fɔ u bɛ siran u yɛrɛ ka dawolo sɔgɔlenw mankan ɲɛ, harpoonkɛlaw tun b’u ka dumuni ɲimi ni diyanye ye fo kunnafoni dɔ tun bɛ o kan. U ye dumuni kɛ i ko matigiw; u y’u kɔnɔbara fa i n’a fɔ Ɛndu kurunw tile bɛɛ ka doni kɛ ni tulumafɛnw ye. SABABU 35. Mast-Kunkolo.

Waati min tun ka di kosɛbɛ, o waati de la, n ka ɲɔgɔn falen-falen cogo bɛnnen na ni kɔgɔjidala tɔw ye, n ka masɔrɔ-kun fɔlɔ nana lamini.

Ameriki balemakɛla fanba la, masti-kunkolow bɛ baara kɛ waati kelen na ni kurun bɔli ye a ka kurunbonkarila la; hali ni a bɛ se ka kilomɛtɛrɛ ba tan ni duuru sɔrɔ, ani ka tɛmɛ o kan, ka kurun ta sani a ka se a ka kurunbokariyɔrɔ bɛnnen na. Wa ni, san saba, san naani walima duuru taama kɔfɛ, a bɛ ka gɛrɛ so ni fɛn lankolon dɔ bɛ a kɔnɔ—a fɔ, hali fiyɛlilan lankolon dɔ—o tuma na, a ka masɔrɔ-kunkolow bɛ mara ni mɔgɔ ye fo ka se laban ma ani fo a ka sankolola-kurunw ka don kurunbonkarila kuluw cɛma, a tɛ jigiya dabila pewu ka balema kelen wɛrɛ minɛ.

Sisan, i n’a fɔ jago min ye ka jɔ mast-kunkolow la, kɔgɔjida la walima ji la, o ye jago ye min kɔrɔlen don kosɛbɛ, wa a ka di, an ka dɔ fara a kan yan. N b’a ta, ko mast-kunkolo jɔbaga fɔlɔw tun ye Misira kɔrɔw ye; sabu, n ka ɲininiw bɛɛ la, n ma foyi sɔrɔ ka kɔn u ɲɛ. Sabu hali n’u bɛnbaw, Babɛl jɔbagaw, siga t’a la, u ka sankanso fɛ, u tun b’a fɛ ka masti kunkolo kɔrɔtalenba lamɔ Azi jamana bɛɛ la, walima Afiriki fana na; o bɛɛ n'a ta (sani kamiyɔn laban bilala a la) i n'a fɔ u ka kabakurunba min bɛ se k'a fɔ ko a tɛmɛna pankurun fɛ, Ala ka diminya fiɲɛba kɔnɔ; o de kosɔn , an tɛ se ka o Babɛl jɔbagaw bila jɔyɔrɔ fɔlɔ la Misirakaw kan . Wa ko Misirakaw tun ye jamana ye min tun bɛ jɔ ni masti kunkolo ye, o ye sɔsɔli ye min sinsinnen bɛ dannayaba kan min tun bɛ fɛnkɔrɔw dɔnbagaw cɛma, ko piramidi fɔlɔw sigira sen kan sankololafɛnw dɔnni kama: miiriya min bɛ dɛmɛ kelen na ni a fan naani bɛɛ ka sigicogo danma ye min bɛ i n’a fɔ ɛrɛzɛnsun o sow jɔlenw; o cogo la, n’u senw kɔrɔtacogo jan kabakomaw ye, o sankololafɛnw dɔnbaga kɔrɔw tun delila ka wuli ka taa u kuncɛ la, ka dɔnkili da dolow kura kama; hali i n’a fɔ bi kurunba kɔlɔsilikɛlaw bɛ dɔnkili da ka ɲɛsin kurunba ma, walima balema min bɛ ye dɔrɔn. Saint Stylites kɔnɔ, Krecɛn ermit tɔgɔba kɔrɔlen, min ye kabakurun jɔlan kɔrɔtalen dɔ jɔ a ye kungo kɔnɔ, k’a ka ɲɛnamaya laban bɛɛ kɛ a kuncɛ la, k’a ka dumuni kɔrɔta ka bɔ duguma ni minɛn ye ale kɔnɔ an bɛ misali kabakoma dɔ sɔrɔ min ye siranbaliya ye min bɛ jɔ-masti-kunkolow kan; min tun man kan ka gɛn ka bɔ a yɔrɔ la ni sisi walima nɛnɛ, sanji, sanbɛlɛ walima nɛnɛ ye; nka a kɛlen ka fɛn bɛɛ ɲɛnabɔ ni jagɛlɛya ye fo ka se laban ma, a sara tiɲɛ yɛrɛ la a ka jɔyɔrɔ la. Bi standers-of-mast-heads la an bɛ ni seti ɲɛnama ye dɔrɔn; kabakurun ni nɛgɛ ani nɛgɛso mɔgɔw dɔrɔn; minnu, hali n’u bɛ se ka fiɲɛba dɔ kunbɛn kosɛbɛ, hali bi, u tɛ se hali bi fewu dɔnkilida jago la, n’u ye yecogo kabakoma o kabakoma sɔrɔ. CHAPTER 36. The Quarter-Deck.

( Enter Ahab: Then, all. )

It was not a great while after the affair of the pipe, that one morning shortly after breakfast, Ahab, as was his wont, ascended the cabin-gangway to the deck. There most sea-captains usually walk at that hour, as country gentlemen, after the same meal, take a few turns in the garden.

Soon his steady, ivory stride was heard, as to and fro he paced his old rounds, upon planks so familiar to his tread, that they were all over dented, like geological stones, with the peculiar mark of his walk. Did you fixedly gaze, too, upon that ribbed and dented brow; there also, you would see still stranger foot-prints—the foot-prints of his one unsleeping, ever-pacing thought.

But on the occasion in question, those dents looked deeper, even as his nervous step that morning left a deeper mark. And, so full of his thought was Ahab, that at every uniform turn that he made, now at the main-mast and now at the binnacle, you could almost see that thought turn in him as he turned, and pace in him as he paced; so completely possessing him, indeed, that it all but seemed the inward mould of every outer movement.

“D'ye mark him, Flask?” whispered Stubb; “the chick that's in him pecks the shell. 'Twill soon be out.”

The hours wore on;—Ahab now shut up within his cabin; anon, pacing the deck, with the same intense bigotry of purpose in his aspect.

It drew near the close of day. Suddenly he came to a halt by the bulwarks, and inserting his bone leg into the auger-hole there, and with one hand grasping a shroud, he ordered Starbuck to send everybody aft.

“Sir!” said the mate, astonished at an order seldom or never given on ship-board except in some extraordinary case.

“Send everybody aft,” repeated Ahab. “Mast-heads, there! come down!”

When the entire ship's company were assembled, and with curious and not wholly unapprehensive faces, were eyeing him, for he looked not unlike the weather horizon when a storm is coming up, Ahab, after rapidly glancing over the bulwarks, and then darting his eyes among the crew, started from his standpoint; and as though not a soul were nigh him resumed his heavy turns upon the deck. With bent head and half-slouched hat he continued to pace, unmindful of the wondering whispering among the men; till Stubb cautiously whispered to Flask, that Ahab must have summoned them there for the purpose of witnessing a pedestrian feat. But this did not last long. Vehemently pausing, he cried:—

“What do ye do when ye see a whale, men?”

“Sing out for him!” was the impulsive rejoinder from a score of clubbed voices.

“Good!” cried Ahab, with a wild approval in his tones; observing the hearty animation into which his unexpected question had so magnetically thrown them.

“And what do ye next, men?”

“Lower away, and after him!”

“And what tune is it ye pull to, men?”

“A dead whale or a stove boat!”

More and more strangely and fiercely glad and approving, grew the countenance of the old man at every shout; while the mariners began to gaze curiously at each other, as if marvelling how it was that they themselves became so excited at such seemingly purposeless questions.

But, they were all eagerness again, as Ahab, now half-revolving in his pivot-hole, with one hand reaching high up a shroud, and tightly, almost convulsively grasping it, addressed them thus:—

“All ye mast-headers have before now heard me give orders about a white whale. Look ye! d'ye see this Spanish ounce of gold?”—holding up a broad bright coin to the sun—“it is a sixteen dollar piece, men. D'ye see it? Mr. Starbuck, hand me yon top-maul.”

While the mate was getting the hammer, Ahab, without speaking, was slowly rubbing the gold piece against the skirts of his jacket, as if to heighten its lustre, and without using any words was meanwhile lowly humming to himself, producing a sound so strangely muffled and inarticulate that it seemed the mechanical humming of the wheels of his vitality in him.

Receiving the top-maul from Starbuck, he advanced towards the main-mast with the hammer uplifted in one hand, exhibiting the gold with the other, and with a high raised voice exclaiming: “Whosoever of ye raises me a white-headed whale with a wrinkled brow and a crooked jaw; whosoever of ye raises me that white-headed whale, with three holes punctured in his starboard fluke—look ye, whosoever of ye raises me that same white whale, he shall have this gold ounce, my boys!”

“Huzza! huzza!” cried the seamen, as with swinging tarpaulins they hailed the act of nailing the gold to the mast.

“It's a white whale, I say,” resumed Ahab, as he threw down the topmaul: “a white whale. Skin your eyes for him, men; look sharp for white water; if ye see but a bubble, sing out.”

All this while Tashtego, Daggoo, and Queequeg had looked on with even more intense interest and surprise than the rest, and at the mention of the wrinkled brow and crooked jaw they had started as if each was separately touched by some specific recollection.

“Captain Ahab,” said Tashtego, “that white whale must be the same that some call Moby Dick.”

“Moby Dick?” shouted Ahab. “Do ye know the white whale then, Tash?”

“Does he fan-tail a little curious, sir, before he goes down?” said the Gay-Header deliberately.

“And has he a curious spout, too,” said Daggoo, “very bushy, even for a parmacetty, and mighty quick, Captain Ahab?”

“And he have one, two, three—oh! good many iron in him hide, too, Captain,” cried Queequeg disjointedly, “all twiske-tee be-twisk, like him—him—” faltering hard for a word, and screwing his hand round and round as though uncorking a bottle—“like him—him—”
