A tun man kan ka bɔ yen ko sanni a ka leviyatan farikolo bɔ pewu, a kunkolo tigɛra. Sisan, balema-banakisɛ kunkolo tigɛ ye dɔnniya farikolo-ɲɛnajɛ-ko ye, balema operelikɛla ŋanaw bɛ waso kosɛbɛ min na: wa a tɛ kɛ kun tɛ.

Aw k’a jateminɛ ko foyi tɛ balema na min bɛ se ka wele ka ɲɛ ko kɔ; o kɔ fɛ , a bɛ iko a kunkolo ni a farikolo bɛ fara ɲɔgɔn kan yɔrɔ min na , o yɔrɔ yɛrɛ la , a yɔrɔ min ka bon kosɛbɛ , o de bɛ yen . O la sa, yala i tɛ kabakoya Stubb ka yɛrɛbonya la, k’a fɔ ko a ye miniti tan dɔrɔn de ɲini walasa ka balema cɛya dɔ kunkolo tigɛ wa?

Wa yen ni bololabaarakɛminɛn gɛlɛn in y’i biri a ma kosɛbɛ, k’a sababu kɛ jiginɛba ye ka bɔ duguma-masi-kunkolo la, ani yaala-bolo kelen-kelen bɛɛ min bɛ o fan fɛ, o bɛ bɔ i n’a fɔ kurunba jikuruw kan yen, o kunkolo jolibɔlen tun sirilen bɛ Pequod cɛmancɛ la i n’a fɔ Holofernes cɛba ta ka bɔ Judith cɛsirilan na.

Nin baara laban in kɛra tuma min na, o kɛra tilelafana ye, kɔgɔjilamɔgɔw taara duguma ka taa u ka sufɛdumuni na. Makun tun bɛ deki kan min tun bɛ ɲagami fɔlɔ, nka sisan a tun lakolon don. Nɛgɛ lafiyalenba dɔ, i n’a fɔ diɲɛ bɛɛ ka lotus jɛman, tun bɛ ka a bulu sumanikɛbaliw dabɔ ka taa a fɛ kɔgɔji kan, mankan tɛ minnu na.

E kunkolo! i ye fɛn caman ye ka dugukolo tila ka Ibrahima kɛ kafiri ye, kumasen kelen tɛ i ta ye!”

“A ka fisa ani a ka fisa, cɛ. Yala sisan Paul senuma tun bɛna na o sira fɛ, ka na ni a ka fiɲɛ ye ne ka fiɲɛbɔbaliya la wa! E Nature, ani E hadamaden ni! aw ka ɲɔgɔndan minnu bɛ tali kɛ ɲɔgɔn na, olu bɛ tɛmɛ kumasen bɛɛ kan cogo di! atomi fitinin tɛ fɛn lamaga walima ka ɲɛnamaya kɛ fɛn kan, nka a bɛ a hakili la a ka nanbarako fila-fila.”





SABABU 71. Jeroboam ka maana.

Bolo ni bolo, kurun ni fiɲɛ ye fiɲɛ bila; nka fiɲɛ nana teliya la ka tɛmɛ kurun kan, a ma mɛn, Pequod y’a daminɛ ka yɛrɛyɛrɛ.

Dɔɔni dɔɔni, galasi fɛ, dunan in ka kurunw ni masti-kunkolo minnu bɛ mɔgɔ kɔnɔ, olu y’a jira ko a ye balema-kurun ye. Nka k’a sɔrɔ a tun yɔrɔ ka jan fiɲɛ fɛ, ani a tun bɛ marifa ci ka tɛmɛ, a bɛ iko a tun bɛ tɛmɛsira kɛ ka taa dugukolo wɛrɛ la, Pequod tun tɛ se ka jigiya sɔrɔ ka se a ma. O la sa, taamasiyɛn bilala walasa k’a dɔn jaabi min bɛna kɛ.

A fɔra yan, ko i n’a fɔ sɔrɔdasi kɔgɔjidala kurunw, Ameriki balema kulu ka kurunw kelen-kelen bɛɛ bɛ ni taamasiyɛn kɛrɛnkɛrɛnnen ye ; o bɛɛ taamasiyɛnw bɛ lajɛ gafe dɔ kɔnɔ ni kurun kelen-kelen bɛɛ tɔgɔw nɔrɔlen bɛ a la, kapitɛni bɛɛ bɛ di a ma. O cogo la, balema kuntigiw bɛ se ka ɲɔgɔn dɔn kɔgɔji kan, hali ni u yɔrɔ ka jan kosɛbɛ, wa ni yɔrɔ fitinin tɛ yen.

Pequod ka taamasiyɛn labanna ka jaabi sɔrɔ dunan in ka a yɛrɛ ta sigili fɛ; min y’a jira ko kurun in tun ye Jeroboam ye min bɛ bɔ Nantucket. A y’a ka forow kɛ square la, a ye kurunba jigin, ka rangement abeam Pequod ka lee jukɔrɔ, ka kurun dɔ jigin; a ma mɛn, a surunyara; nka, k’a sɔrɔ kɛrɛfɛla ladɛrɛsi tun bɛ ka sɛgɛsɛgɛli kɛ Starbuck ka yamaruya fɛ walasa ka kapitɛni min tun bɛ taa bɔ a ye, o ladon, dunan min kofɔlen don, o y’a bolo wuli ka bɔ a ka kurun kɔfɛ k’a jira ko o ko in tɛ mako ɲɛ fewu. A y’a jira ko bana jugu dɔ tun bɛ Jeroboam kurun kɔnɔ, wa ko Mayhew, a ka kapitɛni tun bɛ siran ka Pequod ka sosiyete minɛ. Sabu, hali n’a yɛrɛ ni kurun kɔnɔmɔgɔw ma nɔgɔ, ani hali n’a ka kurun tun bɛ marifa tilancɛ bɔ, ani kɔgɔji ni fiɲɛ min tɛ tiɲɛ, o tun bɛ ka girin ka woyo u ni ɲɔgɔn cɛ; o bɛɛ n’a ta, a dusukun y’a to jamana ka maloya la, a ma sɔn ka na ni Pequod ye k’a ɲɛsin a yɛrɛ ma.

Nka o ma kumaɲɔgɔnya bɛɛ bali cogo si la. K’a to mɛtɛrɛ damadɔw cɛma a ni kurun cɛ, Jeroboam ka kurun tun bɛ baara kɛ n’a ka kurunw ye tuma dɔw la, a ye fɛɛrɛ tigɛ walasa a ni Pequod ka kɛ ɲɔgɔn fɛ, k’a sɔrɔ a tun bɛ ka kurunba tigɛ kosɛbɛ kɔgɔji kɔnɔ (k’a masɔrɔ nin waati in na, a tun bɛ fiɲɛ kura bɔ kosɛbɛ), ni a ka main-topsail kabako; nka, tiɲɛ na, tuma dɔw la, jikuruba dɔ daminɛni barikama fɛ, kurun tun bɛ gɛrɛn ka taa ɲɛfɛ yɔrɔ dɔ la; nka sɔɔni, a tun bɛna na ni seko ye a ka fɛn bɛnnenw na tugun. Ka da o kan, ani o ɲɔgɔnna tigɛtigɛ wɛrɛw la sisan ni sisan, baro dɔ tun bɛ senna mɔgɔ fila ninnu cɛ; nka waati dɔw la, o tɛ ni tigɛ wɛrɛ ma kɛ min suguya wɛrɛ tɛ kelen ye kosɛbɛ.

Ka kurunba sama Jeroboam ka kurun kɔnɔ, o tun ye cɛ ye min ɲɛda tun ye kelen ye, hali o kungo kɔnɔ balema ɲinini ɲɛnamaya kɔnɔ, yɔrɔ min na mɔgɔ kelen-kelen kɛrɛnkɛrɛnnenw bɛ kɛ fɛn bɛɛ lajɛlen ye. A tun ye cɛ fitinin ye, a tun ka surun, a tun ye kamalennin ye, a ɲɛda bɛɛ tun bɛ seri ni wuluwuluw ye, wa a kunsigi jɛman tun tɛ foyi la. A ka fini janyalen dɔ, min tigɛlen don ni kaba ye, n’a ɲɛ ye noye ye min ɲɛnama don, o y’a datugu; bolokɔnincinin minnu bɛ ɲɔgɔn kan, olu tun sirilen bɛ a bolokɔninw na. Delirium jugumanba dɔ tun bɛ a ɲɛ na, min tun sigilen bɛ, a tun bɛ mɔgɔ bila ka miiri.

O la sa, nin jate in ɲɛfɔra fɔlɔ dɔrɔn, Stubb tun ye kulekanba bɔ ko: “Ale de don! o de ye ale de ye!—Town-Ho ka sosiyete y’a fɔ an ye ko scaramuuch jan togged!” Stubb tun bɛ kuma maana kabakoma dɔ kan yan min fɔra Jeroboam ko la, ani cɛ dɔ a ka kurun kɔnɔ, waati dɔ ka kɔn o ɲɛ, waati min na Pequod tun bɛ Town-Ho fɔ. Ka kɛɲɛ ni o maana ye ani min kalanna o kɔfɛ, a kɛra i n’a fɔ scaramuuch min kofɔlen don, o ye fanga kabakoma sɔrɔ Jeroboam kɔnɔ mɔgɔ bɛɛ kan. A ka maana tun ye nin ye:

A tun lamɔna fɔlɔ Neskyeuna Shakers ka jɛkulu dibi la, a tun ye kiraba ye yɔrɔ min na; u ka gundolajɛw la minnu karilen don, k’a sɔrɔ a jigira siɲɛ caman ka bɔ sankolo la jaan-da dɔ fɛ, k’a jira ko daga wolonwulanan dabɔli teliya la, a tun bɛ min ta a ka vest-pocket kɔnɔ nka, min, sanni marifakisɛ ka kɛ a la, a tun ka kan ka jalaki laudanum na. Cidenw ka ŋaniya kabakoma dɔ y’a minɛ, a tun bɔra Neskyeuna ka taa Nantucket, yɔrɔ min na, ni o namara ye min danfaralen don ni dibi ye, a y’a ta kɛnɛma yɔrɔ sabatilen, hakili ɲuman na, k’a yɛrɛ di i n’a fɔ bolo jɛman kandida Jeroboam ka balema ɲinini taama na. U ye furusiri kɛ a la; nka o yɔrɔnin bɛɛ kurun bɔlen kɔ dugukolo ɲɛ na, a ka hakilintan bɔra freshet kɔnɔ. A y’a jira ko ale ye mɛlɛkɛba Gabiriyɛli ye, ka ci bila kapitɛni ma ko a ka pan ka don baji kɔnɔ. A y’a ka manifesto bɔ, a y’a yɛrɛ bila o cogo la i n’a fɔ kɔgɔji gunw kisibaga ani Oceanica bɛɛ ka vikari-jenerali. A ye nin kow fɔ ni timinandiya ye min tɛ wuli;—a ka sunɔgɔbaliya, nisɔndiya miirili ka tulon dibi la, ni jagɛlɛya ye, ani deliriya lakika ka siranfɛnw bɛɛ minnu tɛ hadamadenw ta ye, olu bɛɛ farala ɲɔgɔn kan ka nin Gabiriyɛli in bila kurunba kɔnɔmɔgɔw fanba hakili la, minnu tɛ fɛn dɔn, ni an ye senumaw ka fiɲɛ. Ka fara o kan, u siranna a ɲɛ. Nka, ikomi o cɛ sugu tun tɛ nafa caman sɔrɔ kurun kɔnɔ, kɛrɛnkɛrɛnnenya la, a tun bɛ ban ka baara kɛ fo ni a diyara a ye, kapitɛni min tun dalen tɛ a la, o tun bɛna fain ka bɔ a bolo nka a y’a dɔn ko o tigi ka laɲini tun ye k’a jigin kurunbonkariyɔrɔ fɔlɔ la min bɛnnen don, mɛlɛkɛba y’a ka taamasiyɛnw ni a ka dagaw bɛɛ da wuli o yɔrɔnin bɛɛ — a ye kurun ni a bolow bɛɛ di halakili ma, n’a sɔrɔla o ŋaniya in kɛra. A ye baara kɛ a ka kalandenw kan kosɛbɛ kurun kɔnɔ baarakɛlaw cɛma, fo a laban na, farikolo dɔ kɔnɔ u taara kurun ɲɛmɔgɔ fɛ k’a fɔ a ye ko ni Gabiriyɛli bilala ka bɔ kurun kɔnɔ, u cɛ si tɛna to. O la sa, a wajibiyara k’a ka laɲini bila. U tun tɛna sɔn fana Gabiriyɛli ka tɔɔrɔ cogo si la, k’a fɔ walima k’a sago kɛ; fo a kɛra ko Gabiriyɛli ye kurun hɔrɔnya dafalen sɔrɔ. O bɛɛ kɔlɔlɔ kɛra, ko mɛlɛkɛba tun t’a janto kosɛbɛ kapitɛni n’a furuɲɔgɔnw la kosɛbɛ walima ko a tun t’a janto foyi la; wa ikomi banakisɛ in bɔra kɛnɛ kan, a ye bolo sanfɛla ta ka tɛmɛn fɔlɔ kan; k'a jira ko banajugu in, i n'a fɔ a y'a wele cogo min na, o tun bɛ a kelenpe de bolo; a man kan ka to fana fo ka kɛɲɛ n'a diyanyeko ye. Kuruntigiw, minnu fanba ye jinɛ faantanw ye, olu ye u yɛrɛ sɔgɔsɔgɔ, u dɔw ye u yɛrɛ sɔgɔsɔgɔ a ɲɛkɔrɔ; a ka cikanw labatoli la, tuma dɔw la, k’a yɛrɛ bonya, i n’a fɔ ala dɔ. O ko suguw bɛ se ka kɛ i n’a fɔ u tɛ se ka da a la; nka, hali ni u ye kabako ye cogo o cogo, u ye tiɲɛ ye. Fanatikaw ka tariku fana tɛ tilancɛ ye ka ɲɛsin fanatika yɛrɛ ka yɛrɛnafili sumanikɛbali ma, i n’a fɔ a ka fanga sumanbali min bɛ mɔgɔ caman wɛrɛw lafili ani k’u lafili. Nka waati sera ka segin Pequod kan.

“Ne tɛ siran i ka bana ɲɛ, cɛ,” Akabu y’o fɔ ka bɔ kogow la, ka taa Kapiteni Mayhew ma, min jɔlen tun bɛ kurun kɔfɛ; “na don kurun kɔnɔ.”

Nka sisan, Gabiriyɛli y’a daminɛ ka jɔ.

“Miiri, miiri sumaya la, jɛmanw ni bilisi! Aw k’aw yɛrɛ kɔlɔsi tɔɔrɔ juguba in ma!”

“Gabriɛl! Gabiriyɛli!” Kapiteni Mayhew kulela; “i ka kan fana—” Nka o yɔrɔnin bɛɛ la, jikuru kunkolo dɔ ye kurunba ci ka taa ɲɛfɛ yɔrɔ jan, a ka wulilenw ye kuma bɛɛ su ji la.

“Yala i ye Balema Blanc ye wa?” Akabu ye ɲininkali kɛ, tuma min na kurun seginna ka segin.

“Miiri, miiri i ka balema-kurun na, min bɛ tasuma na ani min jiginna! Aw k’aw yɛrɛ kɔlɔsi o kɔ juguba la!”

CHAPTER 72. The Monkey-Rope.

In the tumultuous business of cutting-in and attending to a whale, there is much running backwards and forwards among the crew. Now hands are wanted here, and then again hands are wanted there. There is no staying in any one place; for at one and the same time everything has to be done everywhere. It is much the same with him who endeavors the description of the scene. We must now retrace our way a little. It was mentioned that upon first breaking ground in the whale's back, the blubber-hook was inserted into the original hole there cut by the spades of the mates. But how did so clumsy and weighty a mass as that same hook get fixed in that hole? It was inserted there by my particular friend Queequeg, whose duty it was, as harpooneer, to descend upon the monster's back for the special purpose referred to. But in very many cases, circumstances require that the harpooneer shall remain on the whale till the whole flensing or stripping operation is concluded. The whale, be it observed, lies almost entirely submerged, excepting the immediate parts operated upon. So down there, some ten feet below the level of the deck, the poor harpooneer flounders about, half on the whale and half in the water, as the vast mass revolves like a tread-mill beneath him. On the occasion in question, Queequeg figured in the Highland costume—a shirt and socks—in which to my eyes, at least, he appeared to uncommon advantage; and no one had a better chance to observe him, as will presently be seen.

Being the savage's bowsman, that is, the person who pulled the bow-oar in his boat (the second one from forward), it was my cheerful duty to attend upon him while taking that hard-scrabble scramble upon the dead whale's back. You have seen Italian organ-boys holding a dancing-ape by a long cord. Just so, from the ship's steep side, did I hold Queequeg down there in the sea, by what is technically called in the fishery a monkey-rope, attached to a strong strip of canvas belted round his waist.

It was a humorously perilous business for both of us. For, before we proceed further, it must be said that the monkey-rope was fast at both ends; fast to Queequeg's broad canvas belt, and fast to my narrow leather one. So that for better or for worse, we two, for the time, were wedded; and should poor Queequeg sink to rise no more, then both usage and honor demanded, that instead of cutting the cord, it should drag me down in his wake. So, then, an elongated Siamese ligature united us. Queequeg was my own inseparable twin brother; nor could I any way get rid of the dangerous liabilities which the hempen bond entailed.

So strongly and metaphysically did I conceive of my situation then, that while earnestly watching his motions, I seemed distinctly to perceive that my own individuality was now merged in a joint stock company of two; that my free will had received a mortal wound; and that another's mistake or misfortune might plunge innocent me into unmerited disaster and death. Therefore, I saw that here was a sort of interregnum in Providence; for its even-handed equity never could have so gross an injustice. And yet still further pondering—while I jerked him now and then from between the whale and ship, which would threaten to jam him—still further pondering, I say, I saw that this situation of mine was the precise situation of every mortal that breathes; only, in most cases, he, one way or other, has this Siamese connexion with a plurality of other mortals. If your banker breaks, you snap; if your apothecary by mistake sends you poison in your pills, you die. True, you may say that, by exceeding caution, you may possibly escape these and the multitudinous other evil chances of life. But handle Queequeg's monkey-rope heedfully as I would, sometimes he jerked it so, that I came very near sliding overboard. Nor could I possibly forget that, do what I would, I only had the management of one end of it.*

*The monkey-rope is found in all whalers; but it was only in the Pequod that the monkey and his holder were ever tied together. Who's afraid of him, except the old governor who daresn't catch him and put him in double-darbies, as he deserves, but lets him go about kidnapping people; aye, and signed a bond with him, that all the people the devil kidnapped, he'd roast for him? There's a governor!”

“Do you suppose Fedallah wants to kidnap Captain Ahab?”

“Do I suppose it? You'll know it before long, Flask. But I am going now to keep a sharp look-out on him; and if I see anything very suspicious going on, I'll just take him by the nape of his neck, and say—Look here, Beelzebub, you don't do it; and if he makes any fuss, by the Lord I'll make a grab into his pocket for his tail, take it to the capstan, and give him such a wrenching and heaving, that his tail will come short off at the stump—do you see; and then, I rather guess when he finds himself docked in that queer fashion, he'll sneak off without the poor satisfaction of feeling his tail between his legs.”

“And what will you do with the tail, Stubb?”

“Do with it? Sell it for an ox whip when we get home;—what else?”

“Now, do you mean what you say, and have been saying all along, Stubb?”

“Mean or not mean, here we are at the ship.”

The boats were here hailed, to tow the whale on the larboard side, where fluke chains and other necessaries were already prepared for securing him.

“Didn't I tell you so?” said Flask; “yes, you'll soon see this right whale's head hoisted up opposite that parmacetti's.”

In good time, Flask's saying proved true. As before, the Pequod steeply leaned over towards the sperm whale's head, now, by the counterpoise of both heads, she regained her even keel; though sorely strained, you may well believe. So, when on one side you hoist in Locke's head, you go over that way; but now, on the other side, hoist in Kant's and you come back again; but in very poor plight. Thus, some minds for ever keep trimming boat. Oh, ye foolish! throw all these thunder-heads overboard, and then you will float light and right.

In disposing of the body of a right whale, when brought alongside the ship, the same preliminary proceedings commonly take place as in the case of a sperm whale; only, in the latter instance, the head is cut off whole, but in the former the lips and tongue are separately removed and hoisted on deck, with all the well known black bone attached to what is called the crown-piece. But nothing like this, in the present case, had been done. The carcases of both whales had dropped astern; and the head-laden ship not a little resembled a mule carrying a pair of overburdening panniers.

Meantime, Fedallah was calmly eyeing the right whale's head, and ever and anon glancing from the deep wrinkles there to the lines in his own hand. And Ahab chanced so to stand, that the Parsee occupied his shadow; while, if the Parsee's shadow was there at all it seemed only to blend with, and lengthen Ahab's. As the crew toiled on, Laplandish speculations were bandied among them, concerning all these passing things.
