SABABU 114. Sanuman.

A dontɔ ka taa a fɛ Japon kurunbokariyɔrɔ dusukun na, a ma mɛn, Pequod bɛɛ ye wuli ka taa mɔni na. SABABU 115. Pequod bɛ Bacelor kunbɛn.

Wa, yelifɛnw ni mankan minnu tun bɛ na ka jigin sani fiɲɛ ka se, dɔgɔkun damadɔw Akabu ka nɛgɛso sɔgɔlen kɔfɛ, o tun bɛ nisɔndiya kosɛbɛ.

O tun ye Nantucket kurun ye, n’o ye Bachelor ye, min tun y’a ka tulubɔlan laban don a la sisan dɔrɔn, k’a ka kurunba pɛrɛnlenw siri ka jigin; ani sisan, seli fini nisɔndiyalenw na, a tun bɛ nisɔndiya la, hali ni nɔɔrɔ fu tun bɛ a la dɔɔnin, a tun bɛ ka taama ka yaala kurunw cɛma minnu faralen don ɲɔgɔn kan kosɛbɛ duguma, sanni a k’a ɲɛfɛla jira ka taa so.

Cɛ saba minnu tun bɛ a ka masti kunkolo la, olu tun bɛ jirisun janw don u ka finiw na, minnu ye bunting bilenman fitininw ye; ka bɔ kɔfɛ, balema-kurun dɔ tun sirilen bɛ, duguma ka jigin; ani u ye balema laban min faga, o duguma dawolo jan yelen ye jɔnya la. Siginidenw, pankurunw ani jakiw kulɛri bɛɛ tun bɛ ka pan ka bɔ a ka rigging kɔnɔ, fan bɛɛ fɛ. Kɛrɛfɛla minnu sirilen bɛ a ka segi saba sanfɛla saba kelen-kelen bɛɛ la, o tun ye cɛya barili fila ye; o sanfɛ, a ka sanfɛla-masti jiribolo-jiriw la, i ye o ji nafama kelen in karifɛn nɔgɔlenw ye; ani fitinɛ dɔ tun sirilen bɛ a ka kamiyɔnba la.

I n’a fɔ a dɔnna o kɔfɛ cogo min na, Bakalawɔri tun ye ɲɛtaa kabakoma sɔrɔ min ka bon kosɛbɛ; o tun ye kabako ye ka tɛmɛn fɔlɔ kan, bawo k’u to kurunba kɔnɔ kɔgɔji kelenw kɔnɔ, kurun caman wɛrɛw tun tɛmɛna kalo kuuru bɛɛ kɔnɔ k’a sɔrɔ u ma jɛgɛ kelen sɔrɔ. Misisogo ni buru bariliw tun ma di dɔrɔn walasa ka yɔrɔ di cɛya nafamaba ma, nka, a tun ye kurun minnu sɔrɔ, olu tun ye bɔgɔdaga wɛrɛw fara ɲɔgɔn kan, ka bɔ kurun minnu na; o fɛnw tun bɛ bila kurunba kɔnɔ, ani kapitɛni ni sɔrɔdasi ɲɛmɔgɔw ka jamana so kɔnɔ. Hali kabini tabali yɛrɛ tun gosira ka kɛ tasuma-jiri ye; ani kabini kɔnɔnafili ye tulu-bugu kunkolo janba dɔ dun, min sirilen bɛ duguma walasa ka cɛmancɛ-fɛn dɔ sɔrɔ. Forocastle kɔnɔ, kurunbolilaw tun ye caulke tiɲɛ na k’u disi wuli, k’u fa; a farala nisɔndiya kan , ko tobilikɛla ye kunkolo dɔ da a ka bɔgɔdaga belebeleba kan , k' a fa ; ko kafemarala y’a ka kafeminkutu min tun bɛ a la, o datugu k’a fa; ko nɛgɛsotigiw tun ye u ka nɛgɛw dawolo kuncɛ k'u fa; ko tiɲɛ na, fɛn bɛɛ tun falen bɛ cɛya la, fo kapitɛni ka pantalonw, ani a y’a bolo minnu bila k’a bolow don minnu kɔnɔ, k’a kɛ a wasa bɛɛ seereya ye a yɛrɛ la.

Nin nisɔndiya kurunba in tun bɛ ka jigin Pequod kan min tun bɛ dusukunnataw la, dunun belebelebaw mankan barbari bɔra a ka ɲɛfɛla la; ani ka gɛrɛ a la hali bi, a cɛw jamaba dɔ y’a ye k’u jɔlen to a ka try-pot belebelebaw lamini na, minnu datugulen don ni jɛgɛ nɛrɛmuguma ka poke walima kɔnɔbara fari ye min bɛ i n’a fɔ sɛbɛnfura, ka mankanba bɔ bolow sirilen bɛɛ la kurun kɔnɔ baarakɛlaw. Quar-deck kan, furuɲɔgɔnmaw ni harpooneerw tun bɛ dɔn kɛ ni npogotigininw ye minnu ɲɛ ye oliviye ye, minnu bolila n’u ye ka bɔ Polinezi gunw kan; k’a sɔrɔ u sirilen bɛ kurun masirilen dɔ kɔnɔ, min sirilen don kosɛbɛ sanfɛ ɲɛfɛla ni kurunba cɛ, Long Island nɛrɛma saba, ni balema nɛgɛberew ye minnu bɛ manamana, olu tun bɛ ka nisɔndiya jig ɲɛminɛ. O waati kɔnɔna na, kurunba kɔnɔ mɔgɔ wɛrɛw tun bolo degunnen don ni ɲagami ye try-works ka kabakurunw dilanni na, daga belebelebaw tun bɔra yɔrɔ minnu na. I tun bɛna ɲini k’a miiri k’u bɛ Bastille dangalen sama ka jigin, kungo kulekan suguw u ye minnu lawuli, k’a sɔrɔ biriki ni bɔgɔ nafa tɛ minnu na sisan, olu bɛ ka fili kɔgɔji kɔnɔ.

Matigi ni kuntigi nin ko bɛɛ la, kapitɛni jɔlen tora kurunba kɔnɔna na, min kɔrɔtalen don, fo nisɔndiya dramu bɛɛ tun falen bɛ a ɲɛkɔrɔ, wa a kɛra i n’a fɔ a dabɔra dɔrɔn a yɛrɛ kelen-kelen bɛɛ ka fɛnɲɛnɛmafagalanw kama.

Ahab, ale fana tun jɔlen bɛ a ka quarter-deck kan, a tun bɛ sɔgɔsɔgɔ ani a tun ye nɛrɛ ye, ni dibi kuncɛba ye; ani tuma min na kurun fila ninnu tun bɛ ɲɔgɔn ka kunun tigɛ — kelen ye nisɔndiya bɛɛ ye fɛnw tɛmɛnen kosɔn, tɔ kelen tun ye ko nataw kofɔlenw bɛɛ ye — u ka kapitɛni fila yɛrɛ y’u kɛ i n’a fɔ o yɔrɔ danfara kabakoma bɛɛ.

“Na don kurun kɔnɔ, na don kurun kɔnɔ!” gayi Bacelor ka komandan kulela, ka galasi ni buteli kɔrɔta fiɲɛ na.

“Yala Balema Blanc ye wa?” Akabu ye sɔgɔsɔgɔ kɛ k’a jaabi.

"Ayi; a dɔrɔn de ye a ko mɛn; nka aw kana da a la fewu », tɔ kelen y’o fɔ ni nisɔndiya ye. “Na don kurun kɔnɔ!”

“I ye jolly dangalen ye kojugu. Sail on. Cɛ si tununna wa?”

“A tɛ se ka fɔ—gunden fila, o dɔrɔn de don;—nka na don kurun kɔnɔ, dusukun kɔrɔ, na. N bɛna o nɛrɛma in ta i ɲɛda la sɔɔni. Aw ka na, yala aw (merry's the play); kurun dafalen don ani min bɛ taa so.”

“Naloman ye kabako ye dɛ!” Akabu ye sɔgɔsɔgɔ kɛ; o kɔfɛ ni kanba ye ko: “I ye kurun dafalen ye, i bɛ taa so, i b’a fɔ; a ɲɛ, o tuma na fɛ, i ka n wele kurun lankolon, ani kɛnɛma-taama. O la, taa i ka siraw la, ne na ne ta. Aw ka taa ɲɛfɛ yen! Aw bɛɛ ka kurun ta, ka a to fiɲɛ fɛ!”

O cogo la, k’a sɔrɔ kurun kelen tun bɛ taa ni nisɔndiya ye fiɲɛ ɲɛfɛ, tɔ kelen y’a kɛlɛ ni kɔnɔnajɛya ye; O cogo la, o minɛn fila farala ɲɔgɔn kan. Pequod kurunba kɔnɔ baarakɛlaw bɛ ka u ɲɛw lajɛ ni kaburu ye, minnu bɛ mɛn ka taa Bacelor min bɛ kɔsegin; nka Bacelor cɛw ma u ɲɛmajɔ abada ɲɛnajɛ ɲɛnamaw la u tun bɛ min na. Wa, Akabu, a y’a yɛrɛ biri tafiri kan, ka bololabaarakɛla min bɛ taa so, a ɲɛ bɛ o la tuma min na, a ye cɛncɛn fiyɛli fitinin dɔ bɔ a ka bɔrɛ kɔnɔ, ka tila ka a ɲɛw bɔ kurun kɔnɔ ka taa o fiyɛli, a kɛra i n’a fɔ o cogo la, a bɛ yɔrɔjan jɛkulu fila fara ɲɔgɔn kan, bawo o fiyɛli tun falen bɛ Nantucket mankanw na.





SABABU 116. Balema min bɛ ka sa.

A man teli ka kɛ nin ɲɛnamaya in kɔnɔ, ni, kinin fɛ, nafolo kanubagaw bɛ kurun kɔnɔ ka surunya an na, an, hali ni an bɛɛ bɛ sɛgɛn ka kɔn o ɲɛ, an bɛ fiɲɛ kɔrɔtɔlen dɔ minɛ, ka nisɔndiya la k’a ye ko an ka bɔrɔw bɛ fa. A tun bɛ iko a tun bɛ ten ni Pequod ye. Bari o dugujɛ, u kɛlen kɔ ka Bacelor gay sɔrɔ, balemaw yelen don, naani fagara; U dɔ fana bɔra Akabu fɛ.

A tun yɔrɔ ka jan wulada fɛ; kɛlɛ bilenman tama bɛɛ kɛra tuma min na: ka panpan tilebin kɔgɔji ni sankolo kanulen na, tile ni balema fila bɛɛ sara ɲɔgɔn fɛ hali bi; o kɔfɛ, o dumuni duman ni o nisɔndiyaba, orizɔnw minnu bɛ mɔgɔ kɔnɔnafili, olu kurulen don o fiɲɛ roso la, fo a bɛ ɲini ka kɛ i n’a fɔ a yɔrɔ ka jan Manila gunw ka komini kɔjiw la minnu ɲɛ ye binkɛnɛ ye, Ɛsipaɲi dugukolo fiɲɛ, min kɛra kuruntigi ye k’a sɔrɔ a ma kɛ ni kɔnɔnafili ye, o taara ka taa kɔgɔji la, ka doni ta ni nin vesper dɔnkiliw ye.

Ahab min tun ye a kɔfɛla bɔ balema na, o dusu sumana tugun, nka a dusu sumana dɔrɔn fo ka se dibi ma min tun ka bon kosɛbɛ, a sigilen tora k’a ka dɔgɔya labanw kɔlɔsi kosɛbɛ ka bɔ kurun kɔnɔ min lafiyalen don sisan. Bari o ɲɛnajɛ kabakoma min bɛ ye balema sperme bɛɛ la minnu bɛ sa — kunkolo jiginni tile fan fɛ, ani a waati bɛ ban ten — o ɲɛnajɛ kabakoma in, min ye o wulada lafiyalen sugu la, cogo dɔ la, o ye kabako dɔ lase Akabu ma min tun ma dɔn fɔlɔ.

“A bɛ wuli k’a wuli k’a ɲɛsin o ma,—a bɛ kɛ dɔɔnin dɔɔnin cogo min na, nka a jɔlen don cogo min na, a ka bonya jirali n’a ka ɲɛda weleli, n’a ka saya lamaga labanw ye. Ale fana bɛ tasuma bato; tile vassal kantigiba, belebele, baronial!—Oh, nin ɲɛ minnu ka di kojugu, olu ka kan ka nin yelifɛnw ye minnu ka di kojugu. Ka filɛ! yan, yɔrɔ jan ji-kɔlɔsili; ka tɛmɛ hadamadenw ka nafolo walima bɔnɛ bɛɛ kan; nin kɔgɔjiw la minnu bɛ kɛ kɛnɛ kan kosɛbɛ ani minnu tɛ mɔgɔ bɔ mɔgɔ la; yɔrɔ min na laadalakow la, fara si tɛ sɛbɛnfura di; yɔrɔ min na kabini Sinuwaw ka waati jan, jikuruw bɛ girin hali bi kumabaliw ni kumabaliw fɛ, i n’a fɔ dolow minnu bɛ yeelen bɔ Nizɛri bɔyɔrɔ dɔnbali kan; yan fana, ɲɛnamaya bɛ sa tile fan fɛ ka fa dannaya la; nka a filɛ! ni a sara dɔrɔn, saya bɛ girin ka su lamini, a bɛ taa sira wɛrɛ fɛ.

“E, e dibi la Hindou tilancɛ, min ye i ka masasigilan danfaralen jɔ yɔrɔ dɔ la nin kɔgɔjiw kɔnɔ minnu ma ban; i ye kafiri ye, i masamuso, wa i bɛ kuma ne fɛ tiɲɛ na kojugu sanjiba la min bɛ ka mɔgɔ faga kosɛbɛ, ani a suturali makunlen kɔfɛ hakilisigi kɔfɛ. Nin i ka balema in fana ma a kunkolo salen wuli, ka sɔrɔ ka taa a lamini tugun, k’a sɔrɔ kalan ma kɛ ne bolo.

“O, trebly hooped ani welded hip fanga! Oh, high aspiring, rainbowed jet!—o kelen bɛ cɛsiri, nin kelen in bɛ jettest bɛɛ kɛ fu! Furu, e balema, yala i bɛ delili ɲini ni yon ye min bɛ ɲɛnamaya fɛn bɛɛ la, min bɛ ɲɛnamaya wele dɔrɔn, nka a tɛ a di tugun. O bɛɛ n'a ta, i, dibi tilancɛ, i bɛ ne lamaga ni dannaya ye min ka bon ni kuncɛbaya ye, ni dannaya min ka di ka tɛmɛ o kan. I ka fɛnɲɛnɛma minnu tɔgɔ ma fɔ, olu bɛɛ bɛ panpan ne jukɔrɔ yan; Fɛn ɲɛnamaw ninakiliw bɛ ne lawuli fɔlɔ, minnu bɛ fiɲɛ bɔ i n’a fɔ fiɲɛ, nka ji bɛ bɔ sisan.

“O tuma na fɛ, sanbɛlɛ, sanbɛlɛ fo abada, kɔgɔji, kungo kɔnɔ kɔnɔw b’a ka lafiɲɛ kelenpe sɔrɔ min ka wuli banbaliw la. A bangera dugukolo kan, o bɛɛ n'a ta, u bɛ sin di kɔgɔji fɛ; hali ni kulu ni kɔw ye ne bange, bibulu jɛmanw ye ne lamɔbagaw ye!”





SABABU 117. Balema kɔlɔsili.

Balema naani minnu fagara o wulada fɛ, olu tun sara ka bɔ ɲɔgɔn na kosɛbɛ; kelen, yɔrɔ jan na ka taa fiɲɛ fɛ; kelen, min yɔrɔ ka jan kosɛbɛ, ka taa leeward la; kelen bɛ ɲɛfɛ; kelen bɛ kɔfɛ. Nin saba laban ninnu Nàna ni ɲɔgɔn ye ere su fɛ; nka fiɲɛba ta ma se ka sɔrɔ fo sɔgɔma; Kurun min y'a faga, o tun dalen bɛ a kɛrɛ fɛ su bɛɛ. O kurun tun ye Akabu ta ye.

CHAPTER 123. The Musket.

During the most violent shocks of the Typhoon, the man at the Pequod's jaw-bone tiller had several times been reelingly hurled to the deck by its spasmodic motions, even though preventer tackles had been attached to it—for they were slack—because some play to the tiller was indispensable.

In a severe gale like this, while the ship is but a tossed shuttlecock to the blast, it is by no means uncommon to see the needles in the compasses, at intervals, go round and round. It was thus with the Pequod's; at almost every shock the helmsman had not failed to notice the whirling velocity with which they revolved upon the cards; it is a sight that hardly anyone can behold without some sort of unwonted emotion.

Some hours after midnight, the Typhoon abated so much, that through the strenuous exertions of Starbuck and Stubb—one engaged forward and the other aft—the shivered remnants of the jib and fore and main-top-sails were cut adrift from the spars, and went eddying away to leeward, like the feathers of an albatross, which sometimes are cast to the winds when that storm-tossed bird is on the wing.

The three corresponding new sails were now bent and reefed, and a storm-trysail was set further aft; so that the ship soon went through the water with some precision again; and the course—for the present, East-south-east—which he was to steer, if practicable, was once more given to the helmsman. For during the violence of the gale, he had only steered according to its vicissitudes. But as he was now bringing the ship as near her course as possible, watching the compass meanwhile, lo! a good sign! the wind seemed coming round astern; aye, the foul breeze became fair!

Instantly the yards were squared, to the lively song of “ Ho! the fair wind! oh-ye-ho, cheerly men! ” the crew singing for joy, that so promising an event should so soon have falsified the evil portents preceding it.

In compliance with the standing order of his commander—to report immediately, and at any one of the twenty-four hours, any decided change in the affairs of the deck,—Starbuck had no sooner trimmed the yards to the breeze—however reluctantly and gloomily,—than he mechanically went below to apprise Captain Ahab of the circumstance.

Ere knocking at his state-room, he involuntarily paused before it a moment. The cabin lamp—taking long swings this way and that—was burning fitfully, and casting fitful shadows upon the old man's bolted door,—a thin one, with fixed blinds inserted, in place of upper panels. The isolated subterraneousness of the cabin made a certain humming silence to reign there, though it was hooped round by all the roar of the elements. The loaded muskets in the rack were shiningly revealed, as they stood upright against the forward bulkhead. Starbuck was an honest, upright man; but out of Starbuck's heart, at that instant when he saw the muskets, there strangely evolved an evil thought; but so blent with its neutral or good accompaniments that for the instant he hardly knew it for itself.
