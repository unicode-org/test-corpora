BUNG 101. Decanter-a chu.

English lawng chu hmuh theih loha a bo hma chuan, hetah hian dah pawh ni se, London atanga lo piang a ni a, chu khawpuia sumdawng, Enderby & Sons-a whaling house hmingthang tak bulpui ber Samuel Enderby-a hming chawia sak a ni a in pakhat chu ka whaleman rethei tak ngaihdanah chuan Tudor leh Bourbon-ho lal in inzawmkhawm aiin a hnungtawlh hla vak lo a, in point of real historical interest. Kan Lalpa kum 1775 hma khan eng chen nge he whale man in ropui tak hi a awm tawh tih ka fish-document tam tak hian a tichiang lo; mahse chu kum (1775) chuan Sperm Whale man fo thin English lawng hmasa berte chu a fit out ta a; mahse, a hma kum sawmhnih engemaw zat (kum 1726 a\ang khan) kan Coffins leh Maceys huaisen tak takte chuan Nantucket leh Vineyard-a awmte chuan lawng lian tak takah chu Leviathan chu an lo zui tawh a, mahse North leh South Atlantic-ah chauh an zui a: hmun dangah ni lovin. Hetah hian chiang takin ziak ni se, Nantucketer-ho hi mihring zingah chuan civilized steel hmanga Sperm Whale ropui tak harpoon hmasa ber an ni tih; tin, kum zabi chanve chhung zet chu khawvel pum huapa hetiang taka harpoon-tu mite chauh an nih thu pawh a sawi bawk.

Kum 1778-ah chuan lawng ṭha tak, Amelia chu chutiang thiltum atan chuan a insiam a, Enderby-ho chak tak takte chauhin a enkawl a, huaisen takin Cape Horn chu a rawn vel a, hnam zingah chuan whale-boat chi hrang hrang, the South Sea ropui tak. Khualzinna chu thiam tak leh vanneihna a ni a; tin, a chenna hmuna sperm hlu tak tak nen a vawn lai chu a kir leh a, Amelia-i entawn tur chu rei lo teah lawng dang, English leh American-te pawhin an zui ve ta a, chutiang chuan Pacific tuipui kama Sperm Whale hmun zau tak chu an paih chhuak ta a ni. Mahse, he thil \ha tihnaah hian lungawi lovin, in chau lo tak chuan a inhnukdawk leh ta a: Samuela leh a Fapa zawng zawngte—engzat nge, an nu chauhin a hriat—chuan an kaihhruaina nghal hnuaiah, a then chu, ka ngaihdan chuan, an sum senso hmangin, British sorkar chu a hruai lut ta a ni sloop-of-war Rattler chu South Sea-a whaling-a thil hmuhchhuahna zin chhuahnaah tirh turin. Naval Post-Captain kaihhruaina hnuaiah Rattler lawng chuan rattling travel a nei a, service engemaw zat a thawk bawk a; engzat nge a lang lo. Mahse hei hi a ni lo. Kum 1819 khan chu in vek chuan anmahni ngeia discovery whale lawng an siam a, Japan tuipui hla tak takah tasting cruise-a kal turin. Chu lawng—“Syren” tia koh ṭha tak—chuan experimental cruise ropui tak a siam a; tin, chutiang chuan Japanese Whaling Ground ropui tak chu mipui hriat hlawh hmasa ber a ni ta a ni. He lawng hmingthang takah hian Syren lawng hi Captain Coffin, Nantucketer pakhatin a kaihruai a.

Chuvangin, Enderbies-ho chawimawina zawng zawng, an in chu tun thleng hian a awm tawh niin ka hria; mahse, rinhlelh rual lohvin Samuela hmasa ber chuan khawvêl dang South Sea ropui tak atân a cable chu a slip tawh ngei ang.

A hming chawia lawng hming vuah chu chawimawi tlak a ni a, lawng khalh chak tak leh kawng tinrenga kutchhuak ropui tak a ni. Patagonia tuipui kam hmun khatah zanlai pelh hnuah vawi khat chu ka chuang a, forecastle-ah chuan flip down tha tak ka in bawk. Kan neih gam tha tak a ni a, an vai hian trump an ni vek a—chuta chuang thlarau zawng zawng. An tan dam rei lo, leh thihna hlim tak. Tin, chu gam tha tak ka neih chu—Ahaba upain a sakeibaknei kekawrte hmanga a thingphel a khawih hnu rei tak, rei tak hnuah—chu lawnga Saxon-ho mikhualna ropui tak, nghet tak, Saxon-ho mikhualna chu min ngaihtuah tir a tin, ka parson chuan min theihnghilh sela, Diabola chuan min hre reng ang che, ka hmuh loh chuan. Vai? Flip kan nei tih ka sawi em? Ni e, darkar khata gallon sawm velin kan flip bawk a; tin, thlipui a lo thlen chuan (for it’s squally off there by Patagonia), kut zawng zawng—tlawhtu leh mi zawng zawng—reef topsail-a koh an nih chuan, kan top-heavy em em a, bowline-ah kan inswing a ngai ta a tin, hriat lohvin kan jacket-te kawrfual chu lawng lianah chuan kan thun lut a, chuvang chuan chutah chuan kan innghat a, thlipui au thawm karah chuan kan reefed chak hle a, zu rui tar zawng zawngte tana vaukhânna entawn tur a ni. Mahse, mast-te chu a kal lutuk lo va; tin, rei lo teah kan tlan thla a, kan fimkhur em em a, flip chu kan paltlang leh a ngai ta a, forecastle scuttle-a savage salt spray puak chhuak chuan, rather too much diluted and pickled it to my taste.

Bawngsa chu a tha hle—a khauh hle a, mahse taksa a awm thung. Bull-beef a ni an ti a; mi dangte chuan, dromedary bawnghnute a nih thu; mahse, chu chu engtin nge a nih tih erawh ka hre lo, chiang takin. Dumplings an nei bawk a; dumplings te tak te, mahse a lian tham, symmetrically globular, leh tihchhiat theih loh dumplings. I hre thei ang tih ka ngaihtuah a, an ei hnuah i chhungah i roll kual thei bawk. Hmalam pan lutuka i kun chuan billiard-ball ang maiin an pitch out che chu i risk a ni. Chhang chu—mahse chu chu a pui thei lo; chu bâkah, anti-scorbutic a ni bawk; a tawi zawngin, chhang chhungah chuan an fare thar awmchhun a awm a. Mahse forecastle chu a eng vak lo va, i ei chuan kil thim takah chuan ke pen a awlsam hle. Mahse a vaiin, truck atanga helm dangah hruaiin, cook-a boiler-te dimension ngaihtuah chungin, a mah ngeiin live parchment boiler-te pawh a tel a; hma leh hnung lamah, ka ti a, Samuel Enderby chu lawng hlim tak a ni a; fare tha leh tam tak; fine flip leh chak tak; crack fellows zawng zawng, leh boot heels atanga hat-band thlengin capital.

Mahse, engvangin nge, in ngaihtuah teh, Samuel Enderby-a te, leh English whaller dang ka hriat ṭhenkhat—a zawng zawng ni lo mah se—chutiang lawng hmingthang, mikhual lawm thei tak an nih; chu chuan bawngsa, leh chhang, leh can, leh fiamthu chu a vel a; tin, ei leh in leh nuih te kha an chau thuai lo em ni? Ka hrilh ang che. Hêng English whaller-te hlimna ṭha tak hi history zir chianna atân thil pawimawh tak a ni. Chutiang bawkin, historical whale research pawh ka hmang lo, a tul nia a lan lai pawhin.

English-ho hma hian whale man kawngah Hollander, Zealander leh Dane-ho an awm a; chuta ṭang chuan sangha man lama la awm thumal tam tak an la chhuak a; tin, chu aia nasa zawk chu, an fakna hlui thau tak takte, ei leh in tur tam tak khawih te hi a ni. A chhan chu, thil tlangpui angin, English sumdawng lawng chuan a thawktute chu a scrimp a; mahse, English whaler-te erawh chu chutiang chu a ni lo. Chuvangin, English-ah chuan he whaling good cheer tih hi thil pangngai leh thilsiam a ni lo va, incidental leh particular a ni zawk a; tin, chuvangin, bul tanna bik engemaw zat a nei tur a ni a, chu chu hetah hian tarlan a ni a, chu chu chiang zawkin kan la chiang zawk ang.

Leviathanic history-a ka zirchiannaah khan Dutch volume hlui pakhat ka hmu a, chu chu, a rim hnim hring tak avang chuan, whaler-te chungchang a ni ngei ang tih ka hria. A thupui chu, “Dan Coopman” a ni a, chuvangin hei hi Amsterdam cooper thenkhatte hriatrengna hlu tak, sangha mantute hriatrengna hlu tak a ni ngei ang, whale lawng tin hian an cooper an phur vek tur a ni tih ka thutlukna siam ta a ni. He ngaihdan hi “Fitz Swackhammer” pakhat siam a nih thu ka hmuh hian min tichak lehzual a ni. Mahse ka thianpa Dr. Snodhead, mi thiam tak, Santa Claus leh St. Pott’s college-a Low Dutch leh High German professor, lehlin hna chu ka hlan a, a harsatna atan sperm candle box khat a pe a— he Dr. Snodhead ngei hian lehkhabu a enthla meuh chuan “Dan Coopman” tih hian “The Cooper” tihna a ni lo va, “The Merchant” tihna a ni tih min tiam ta a ni. A tawi zawngin, he Low Dutch lehkhabu hlui leh zir chhuah tak hian Holland sumdawnna chungchang a sawi a; tin, thupui dangte zîngah chuan a whale man chungchâng chanchin ngaihnawm tak tak a keng tel bawk. Tin. chu list atang chuan Dr. Snodhead-a lehlin angin a hnuaia mi hi ka transcribe a ni:

Friesland vawk sa a ni. 150,000 lbs a ni. of stock fish a ni. 550,000 lbs a ni. biscuit a ni. 72,000 lbs a ni. chhang nêm tak a ni. butter firkin 2,800 a ni. 20,000 lbs a ni. Statistical table tam zawk hi chhiarnaah parchingly dry a ni a; mahse, tûn laiah chuan chutiang chu a ni lo, chhiartute chu gin ṭha tak leh hlimna ṭha tak pipe pum pui, barrel, quart, leh gills-a tuilianin a tuam a ni.

Chutih lai chuan, ni thum chhung chu heng beer, bawnghnute, leh chhangphut zawng zawngte hi zir chiang taka ei chhuah nan ka hmang a, chutih lai chuan ngaihtuahna thuk tak tak tam tak ka ngaihtuah a, chu chu thil danglam bik leh Plato-a hman theih a ni a tin, chu bâkah, chu hmân lai Greenland leh Spitzbergen whale man chhuahna hmuna Low Dutch harpooneer tinte ei tûr stock-fish, etc. awm thei zat chu ka khawih a, chu bâkah, keimah ngei pawhin supplementary table ka siam bawk. A hmasa berah chuan butter tam zia, leh Texel leh Leyden cheese ei zat hi mak tak a ni. Mahse, an pianphungah chuan an rilru natna (naturally unctuous natures)-ah ka puh a, an kohna nihna avang leh a bik takin, chu Polar Seas lum takah chuan, chu Esquimaux ram tuipui kamah, convivial native-te thutiamna hmuna an game an zawm avang chuan train oil bumper-ah an inhmukhawm a.

Beer pawh a tam hle a, barrel 10,800 a ni. Tunah chuan, chu polar fisheries te chu chu climate thlasik rei lo te chhung chauha thubuai siamsak theih a nih avangin, chutiang Dutch whalemen zinga pakhat cruise zawng zawng, Spitzbergen tuipui atanga lo haw leh hawna te pawh telin, thla thum aia rei lo, sawi ta ila, tin, an lawng 180 chuang zinga pakhat theuh mipa 30 chhiarin, a vaiin Low Dutch lawng khalhtu 5,400 kan nei a; chuvangin, ka ti a, mi pakhat tan beer barrel hnih chiah kan nei a, kar sawm leh pahnih allowance atan, chu gin ankers 550 atanga a zat dik tak chu telh loh chuan. Tunah chuan, heng gin leh beer harpooneer-te hi, mi pakhatin a ngaihtuah ang maia fuddled tak takte hi lawng lu chunga ding tur leh flying whale-te tum tha tak nei tur mi dik tak an ni em? hei hi eng emaw chen chu thil theih loh angin a lang ang. Mahse, an tum ve tho a, anmahni pawh an kap ve bawk. Mahse hei hi North hla tak a ni a, hriatreng ni se, beer hi constitution nen a inmil hle a; Equator-ah chuan, kan khawthlang lam sangha mannaah chuan, beer chuan harpooneer chu mast-head-ah muthilh tir leh a lawnga boozy-a siam turin a tling ang; leh Nantucket leh New Bedford-te tan hlohna nasa tak a thleng thei bawk.

Mahse, a awm tawh lo; kum zabi hnih emaw, kum zabi thum emaw kalta a Dutch whaller hluite chu liver sang tak an ni tih tilang thei khawpa sawi a ni tawh a; tin, English whaller-te chuan hetiang entawn tur tha tak hi an ngaihthah lo tih pawh a sawi bawk. A chhan chu, an ti a, lawng khar chhunga i zin hian khawvel a\ang hian thil tha zawk i hmu thei lo a nih chuan, a tlem berah chuan zanriah tha tak i ei chhuak ang. Tin, hei hian decanter chu a ti hring vek bawk.





BUNG 102. Arsacides-a Bower pakhat.

Tun thleng hian Sperm Whale chungchang sawifiahna hmanga ka enkawl danah hian a pawn lam thil mak tak takte ka sawi ber a; emaw, a hranpaa leh chipchiar takin interior structural feature tlemte thenkhatah emaw a ni thei bawk. Mahse a ultimatum-ah i hmaah a awm a; chu chu a ruhro (unconditional skeleton)-ah tihna a ni.

Mahse, engtin nge tunah, Ismaela? Engtin nge, nang, sangha man lama lawng khalhtu mai mai hian, whale-te lei hnuaia thil awmte chungchâng engmah hre lo anga inlan? I capstan-a chuang, erudite Stubb-a chuan Cetacea taksa peng hrang hrang chungchang zirtirna a pe em; tin, windlass puihnain, exhibition atan specimen rib pakhat chu han chawi sang em? Ismaela, inhrilhfiah rawh. I deck-ah whale puitling tak chu enfiah turin i thlawh tir thei ang em, chaw siamtuin vawk roast a dish angin? Ni lo tak zet a ni. Tun thleng hian thuhretu dik tak i ni tawh a, Ismaela; mahse, Jona chauh chanvo i hman dan chu fimkhur rawh; joist leh beam-a thusawi theihna chanvo; rafters, ridge-pole, sleepers, leh under-pinnings te hian leviathan frame-work a siam a; leh a rilru chhunga tallow-vat, dairy-room, buttery leh cheeseries te ang bawk.

Jona a\ang khan whale puitling vun hnuaiah hian whaleman tlemte chauh an lut tawh tih ka inpuang a; chuti chung pawh chuan, miniature-a dissect theihna hun remchang ka dawng a ni. Ka awmna lawng pakhatah chuan vawi khat chu Sperm Whale naupang te tak te chu a poke emaw bag emaw atan, harpoon barb atan leh lance lu atan sheath siam nan deck-ah taksain an chawi sang a. Chu hun remchang chu ka thlahthlam ta niin i ngai em, ka lawng-hatchet leh jack-knife hmang lovin, seal chu ka tichhia a, chu naupang no chhunga thil awm zawng zawng chu chhiar lovin?

Tin. Tranque-a ka awm avangin, kum tam tak kal ta khan, Algiers-a sumdawnna lawng Dey-a ka inzawm lai khan, Arsacidean holidays thenkhat chu Tranque lal nen, Pupella-a a palm villa pension tawhah hmang turin min sawm a kan lawng khalhtute’n a khawpui Bamboo-Town an tih aṭanga hla vak lo tuipui kama luipui pakhat.

Mize ṭha dang tam tak zîngah chuan ka lal ṭhian Tranquo-a chu barbaric vertu thil zawng zawnga hmangaihna rilru pu tak pêk a nih avângin, a mite zînga mi fing zâwkte thil hmuh chhuah theih tûr thil tlemte chu Pupella-ah a rawn hruai khâwm a a bik takin hmanraw mak tak tak thing phun, chiselled shell, inlaid spears, paddle man to tak tak, aromatic canoes; tin, chûng zawng zawng chu thilsiam thilmak eng pawh, thilmak phur, chhiah pe thei thlipuiin a tuipui kamah a rawn thlak a, chu chu an sem darh vek a ni.

Chung hnuhnung zawk zinga langsar ber chu Sperm Whale ropui tak a ni a, chu chu, a danglam bik taka thlipui na tak hnuah, thi leh tangkhang anga hmuh a ni a, a lu chu cocoa-nut thing chungah a nghat a, a hmul ang maia, tufted droopings chu a verdant jet ang mai a ni. A tawpah chuan taksa zau tak chu a fathom-dep enfoldings te chu an hlihsak a, ruhte chu ni chhuahnaah leivut a lo vawt ta a, chutah chuan ruhro chu uluk takin Pupella glen-ah chuan an phur chho a, chutah chuan tunah chuan lordly palms temple ropui takin a khuh ta a ni.

Rib-ah chuan trophy an tar a; vertebræ-te chu Arsacidean annals-a phun a ni a, hieroglyphics mak tak tak hmanga ziah a ni a; luruh chhungah chuan puithiamte chuan meialh rimtui tak, tihmit loh chu an vawng reng a, chutiang chuan thuruk lu chuan a vapory spout chu a rawn thawn leh a; chutih lai chuan, thinghnah pakhata innghatin, a hnuai lam hmui mak tak chu devotee zawng zawng chungah chuan a rawn nghing a, Damocles-a ti hlauhawm em emtu sam hrual khandaih ang maiin.

Thil mak tak mai a ni. Thing chu Icy Glen-a mosses ang maiin a green a; thingte chu sang tak leh chapo takin an ding a, an tui nung chu an hre ta a; a hnuaia lei taima tak chu puan puan sin ang mai a ni a, a chungah chuan carpet mawi tak a awm a, chu chu leia grep hrui hringte chuan warp leh woof a siam a, pangpar nungte chuan lem a siam bawk. Thingkung zawng zawng, a hnah phur zawng zawng nen; thinghnah te, fern te, thlai chi hrang hrang te; thuchah kengtu boruak; heng zawng zawng hi chawlhsan lovin an thawk chhuak vek a ni. A hnah lacings hmang chuan ni ropui tak chu thlawk chhuak shuttle ang maiin, chau lo verdure chu a hrual a. Aw, puan siamtu buai tak! hmuh loh puan siamtu!—pause!—thumal pakhat!—khawiah nge puan chu a luang? eng palace nge a deck theih ang? engvangin nge heng thawhrimna tawp lo zawng zawng hi? Thu sawi rawh, puan siamtu!—i kut chu awm rawh!—mahse, thu pakhat chauh sawi rawh! Ni lo—shuttle chu a thlawk a—a lemte chu loom hma lam aṭangin a lo thlawk chhuak a; freshet-rushing carpet chu chatuan atan a tlanbo ta a ni. Puan siamtu pathian, a puan a; chu puan siamna chuan a beng a tichhe a, thi thei aw engmah a hre lo; tin, chu hum hum avang chuan keini pawh, loom entu te pawh kan beng a tichhe ta a; tin, chuta kan tlanchhuah hunah chauh chuan a hmanga thusawi aw sang tam takte chu kan hre ang. Chuti chung pawh chuan material factory zawng zawngah pawh a awm vek si a. Thlawhna spindle zinga hriat theih loh thu sawite; chu thute tho chu bang tel lo pawhin chiang takin a ri a, casement hawn a\angin a puak chhuak a ni. Chutiang chuan villainies an hmuchhuak ta a ni. Ah, thi thei tak! tichuan, fimkhur rawh; chutiang chuan khawvel ropui tak lo thleng tur ri zawng zawngah hian i ngaihtuahna fiah lo ber berte chu hla tak atangin hriat theih a ni si a.

Tunah chuan, chu Arsacidean thing hring, nunna hahdam thei lo loom karah chuan, ruhro lian tak, var, biak thin chu a lo muhil ta a—midler lian tak mai! Mahse, a ever-woven verdant warp leh woof chu a inhmeh tawn a, a hum lai chuan, idler chak tak chu puan siamtu fing tak ang maiin a lang a; amah ngei pawh grep hrui nen a inthuam vek a; thla tin greener, fresher verdure anga ngaihin; mahse amah ngei chu ruhro a ni. Nunna folded Thihna; Thihna trellised Nunna; pathian lungchhiatthlak tak chuan tleirawl Nunna nen a nupui a, lu hrual ropuina a hring a.

Tunah chuan lal Tranquo nen he whale mak tak hi ka tlawh a, a luruh chu maicham, leh jet tak tak chhuahna hmun atanga meikhu siam chhuah chu ka hmuh chuan lal chuan chapel chu thil (object of vertu) anga a ngaih tur chu mak ka ti hle a ni. A nui suk a. Mahse, chu aia nasa zawk chu puithiamte chuan a smoky jet chu a tak tak a ni tih an tiam tur a ni. He ruhro hmaah hian ka kal kual a—grep hnahte chu ka brush a—a ruhte chu a tikehsawm a—Arsacidean twine ball nen chuan, a colonnade leh arbour tam tak, hrual, thlamuang tak tak karah chuan ka vak kual a, rei tak ka vak kual a. Mahse rei lo teah ka line chu a chhuak ta a; tin, chu chu ka zui leh a, ka luhna kawngkhar atang chuan ka chhuak ta a. A chhungah hian thil nung reng reng ka hmu lo; ruh tih loh chu engmah a awm lo.

Green measuring-rod min cut a, vawi khat chu ruhro chhungah chuan ka dive leh ta a. Luruh chhunga an thal hrual atang chuan puithiamte chuan ka ruh hnuhnung ber san zawng ka lak lai chu an hmu a, “Engtin nge tunah chuan!” an au chhuak a; “He kan pathian hi i teh thei em! Chu chu kan tan a ni.” “A ni, puithiamte u—a tha e, eng chen nge in siam ang, chuti a nih chuan?” Nimahsela, hetih lai hian an zîngah inelna nasa tak a lo chhuak a, ke leh inches chungchângah; an yard-stick hmangin an sconces an tikehsawm a—luruh ropui tak chu a ri chhuak ta a—chu vanneihna hun remchang chu hmang tangkaiin ka admeasurements chu rang takin ka titawp ta a ni.

Tunah hian heng admeasurements te hi in hmaah dah ka rawt a ni. Mahse, a hmasa berin, record ni rawh se, he thu-ah hian ka duh ang tehfung (fancied measurement) engmah sawi turin ka zalen lo. A chhan chu skeleton authority i refer theih a awm a, ka dikna test nan. Chu rama whale man theihna hmun pakhat, England ram Hull-ah chuan Leviathanic Museum a awm tih min hrilh a, chutah chuan fin-back leh whale dang specimen tha tak tak an nei a ni. Chutiang bawkin, New Hampshire-a Manchester museum-ah pawh, a neitute’n “United States-a Greenland emaw River Whale emaw specimen ṭha famkim awmchhun” an tih chu an nei tih ka hre tawh bawk. Chu bâkah, Yorkshire, England-a hmun pakhatah chuan, a hming Burton Constable-ah chuan Sir Clifford Constable pakhat chuan Sperm Whale ruhro chu a nei a, mahse a lian tawk lo va, ka thianpa King Tranquo-a ruhro ang kha a puitling lo tak zet a ni .

Heng pahnihah hian heng ruhro pahnih awmna, stranded whale te hi a tir chuan an neitute chuan chhan inang chiah chiah hmangin an claim a ni. King Tranquo chuan a duh avangin a ta chu a man a; leh Sir Clifford-a te, chu hmun hrang hranga seignories-te lal a nih avangin. Sir Clifford-a whale hi a pumpuiah articulated a ni tawh a; chutiang chuan, chest of drawer lian tak ang maiin, i hawng thei a, i khar thei a, a ruh hring zawng zawngah—a ruhte chu fan lian tak ang maiin i theh darh thei a—a hmui hnuai lam chungah nilengin i inher thei bawk. A trap-door leh shutter thenkhatah chuan lock dah tur a ni a; tin, footman pakhat chuan a bulah chabi bunch khat kengin round future visitors a entir ang. CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
