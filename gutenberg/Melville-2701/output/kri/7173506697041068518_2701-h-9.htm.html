CHAPTER 43. Hark!

“HIST! Yu yɛri da nɔys de, Kabako?”

Na bin di midul-watch: wan fayn mun layt; di siman dɛn bin tinap na wan kɔdon, we bin de kɔmɔt frɔm wan pan di fresh wata bɔt dɛn we de na di wes, te to di skata-bɔt nia di tafrail. Dis we ya, dɛn pas di bɔkit dɛn fɔ ful-ɔp di skata-bɔt. We dɛn bin tinap, bɔku tɛm, na di oli ples dɛn na di kwata-dɛk, dɛn bin de tek tɛm mek dɛn nɔ tɔk ɔ rɔsh dɛn fut. Frɔm wan an to an, di bɔkit dɛn bin de go insay di dip saylɛns, dɛn bin jɔs de brok bay di flap we wan sel de flap wan wan tɛm, ɛn di stedi hum we di kil we de go bifo nɔ de stɔp.

Na insay dis rɛst, na in Archy, we na wan pan di kɔdɔn, we in post bin de nia di afta-hatch, wispa to in neba, wan Cholo, di wɔd dɛn we de ɔp.

“Hist! yu yɛri da nɔys de, Kabako?”

“Tek di bɔkit, yu go du am, Archy? wetin nois d’ye min?”

“Na de i de bak—ɔnda di hatch dɛn—yu nɔ yɛri am—kɔf—i bin tan lɛk kɔf.”

“Kof bi damned! Pas along da ritɔn bɔkit de.”

“Na de bak—na de!—i tan lɛk se tu ɔ tri pipul dɛn we de slip de tɔn, naw!”

“Karamba! dɔn du, shipmet, yu go du am? Na di tri sok biskit dɛn we una de it fɔ sup de tɔn ova insay una—natin nɔ de. Luk to di bɔkit!”

“Se wetin yu want, shipmet; A gɛt shap yes.”

“Aye, yu na di chap, nɔto so, we yɛri di hum we di ol Kwakeress in nitin-nidul dɛn de mek fifti mayl na di si frɔm Nantucket; yu na di chap.”

“Grin go; wi go si wetin de ton op. Hark ye, Cabaco, sɔmbɔdi de dɔŋ na di afta-hold we dɛn nɔ si yet na dɛk; ɛn a saspek se wi ol Mogul sabi sɔntin bɔt am bak. A yɛri Stubb tɛl Flask, wan mɔnin wach, se sɔntin lɛk dat de na di briz.”

“Tish, ɛn tɔk bɔt am! di bɔkit!”





CHAPTER 44. Di Chat.

If yu bin fala Kpɛtɛn Eab dɔŋ insay in kebin afta di skwal we apin di nɛt we afta dat wayl ratifyeshɔn fɔ in purpose wit in kru, yu fɔ dɔn si am go na wan lɔka na di transom, ɛn briŋ wan big wrinkled rol ɔf yɔlɔsh si chɛt dɛn, spre dɛn bifo am pan in tebul we dɛn skru dɔŋ. Dɔn we i sidɔm bifo am, yu fɔ dɔn si am gud gud wan de stɔdi di difrɛn layn dɛn ɛn shed dɛn we de mit in yay; ɛn wit slo bɔt stedi pensil tray ɔda kɔs dɛn oba spɛs dɛn we bifo bin blank. Wan wan tɛm, i kin tɔk bɔt bɔku bɔku ol lɔg buk dɛn we de nia am, usay dɛn kin put di sizin ɛn ples dɛn usay, pan difrɛn difrɛn ship dɛn we dɛn bin de travul trade, dɛn bin dɔn kech ɔ si sperm wɛl dɛn.

We dɛn bin de yuz am so, di ebi ebi piɔta lamp we bin de hang chen oba in ed, i bin de rɔk ɔltɛm wit di muv we di ship de muv, ɛn fɔ ɔltɛm i bin de trowe di shado dɛn we de chenj chenj ɛn shado dɛn we gɛt layn dɛn pan in brɔw we bin rɔtin, te i bin tan lɛk se we insɛf de mak aut layn ɛn kɔs dɛn na di chɛt dɛn we gɛt wrinkled, sɔm pensil we pɔsin nɔ de si bin de tray bak layn ɛn kɔs dɛn na di chɔt we dɛn dɔn mak dip dip wan na in fɔɛd.

Bɔt nɔto dis nɛt mɔ, we Eab bin de in wan, i bin de tink gud wan bɔt in chɛt dɛn. Klosap ɛvri nɛt, dɛn bin de briŋ dɛn kɔmɔt na do; klos to ɛvri nɛt, dɛn bin de pul sɔm pensil mak dɛn, ɛn dɛn bin de chenj ɔda wan dɛn. Bikɔs wit di chɛt dɛn fɔ ɔl di 4 si dɛn we bin de bifo am, Eab bin de thread wan mazi we gɛt kɔrɛnt ɛn ed, wit di atɛnshɔn fɔ di mɔ shɔ se i go ebul fɔ du da monomaniac tink bɔt in sol de.

Naw, to ɛnibɔdi we nɔ rili no di we aw di livatan dɛn de du, i go tan lɛk se na wok we nɔ mek sɛns fɔ fɛn wan tin we de in wan na di si dɛn we nɔ gɛt hop na dis planɛt. Bɔt nɔto so i bin tan lɛk to Eab, we bin no aw ɔl di tayd ɛn kɔrɛnt dɛn kin kam; ɛn bay dat, dɛn kin kɔl di drɛf we di sperm wɛl in it de drɛf; ɛn, bak, fɔ mɛmba di rɛgyula, ascertained sizin dɛn fɔ hunt am insay patikyula latitud dɛn; kin rich to rizin fɔ tink, i kin lɛf smɔl fɔ lɛ i kam nia sɔntin we i fɔ no, bɔt di de we i fɔ de na dis ɔ da grɔn de fɔ go fɛn in animal dɛn we i want fɔ it.

Fɔ tru, wi rili biliv di tru tin bɔt di tɛm we di sperm wɛl kin yuz wata we dɛn gi am, dat bɔku pipul dɛn we de ɔntin biliv se, dɛn go ebul fɔ wach am gud gud wan ɛn stɔdi am ɔlsay na di wɔl; if dɛn bin tek tɛm kɔl di lɔg dɛn fɔ wan trip fɔ di wan ol wɛl grup, dɔn dɛn go si se di we aw di sperm wɛl de muf go ɔdasay, nɔ go chenj to di wan dɛn we di hɛri-shɔl ɔ di flay we di swɛlin dɛn de flay. Na dis hint, dɛn dɔn tray fɔ mek fayn fayn chɔt dɛn fɔ di sperm wɛl we de muf go ɔdasay.*

Apat frɔm dat, we dɛn de mek rod frɔm wan ples fɔ it to ɔda ples, di sperm wɛl dɛn, we sɔm instinkt we nɔ de mek mistek de gayd dɛn—lɛ wi se, sikrit intɛlijɛns frɔm di Gɔd—bɔku pan dɛn kin swim insay veins , lɛk aw dɛn kin kɔl dɛn; kɔntinyu fɔ waka along wan givɛn ocean-line wit so undeviating exactitude, dat no ship nɔ ɛva sel in kɔs, bay ɛni chɔt, wit wan tɛn pan dis kayn wɔndaful prɛsishɔn. Pan ɔl we, insay dɛn kes ya, di dairekshɔn we ɛni wan wɛl tek fɔ bi stret as sɔvayva in paralel, ɛn pan ɔl we di layn fɔ go bifo fɔ bi strikt wan fɔ in yon wek we nɔ go ebul fɔ avɔyd, we stret, yet di arbitri vein we dɛn se i de insay dɛn tɛm dɛn ya swim, jεnarali i kin embras sכm fכ mayl in waid (mכ כ lεs, as dεn kin tink se di vein de εkspכnd כ kכntrik); bɔt i nɔ de ɛva pas di swip we pɔsin kin si frɔm di wɛl-ship in mast-hɛd dɛn, we i de glid fayn fayn wan along dis majik zon. Di sɔm na, se na patikyula sizin dɛn we de insay da brayt de ɛn along da rod de, dɛn kin luk fɔ wɛl dɛn we de muf go ɔdasay wit big kɔnfidɛns.

Ɛn dat mek nɔto jɔs di tɛm dɛn we dɛn gɛt pruf, na difrɛn say dɛn we pipul dɛn sabi fɔ it, Eab bin ebul fɔ op fɔ mit di animal we i want fɔ it; bɔt we i de krɔs di wata we big pas ɔl bitwin dɛn grɔn dɛn de, i bin ebul, bay in art, so ples ɛn tɛm insɛf na in rod, as ivin da tɛm de nɔ fɔ bi ɔltin we nɔ gɛt op fɔ mit.

Wan sikɔstɛms bin de we we yu si am fɔs, i bin tan lɛk se i de miks in delirious bɔt stil mɛtɔdikal skim. Bɔt nɔto so insay di rial tin, sɔntɛm. Pan ɔl we di gregarious sperm whales gɛt dɛn rɛgyula sizin fɔ patikyula grɔn, yet in jɔnal yu nɔ go ebul fɔ kɔnklud se di herds we bin de haunt so ɛn so latitude ɔ longitude dis ia, lɛ wi se, go tɔn aut fɔ bi di sem wit di wan dɛn we dɛn bin fɛn de di sizin we bin dɔn pas; pan ɔl we sɔm patikyula tin dɛn de we nɔ gɛt wan dawt usay dis dɔn sho se na tru. In jɛnɛral, di sem tɔk, jɔs insay wan less waid limit, de aplay to di solitary ɛn hermits bitwin di machɔ, ol sperm wɛl. So dat pan ɔl we dɛn bin dɔn si Mobi Dik insay wan ia trade, fɔ ɛgzampul, na wetin dɛn kɔl di Sishɛl grɔn na di Indian si, ɔ Vɔlkɛnɔ Bay na di Japan Kɔst; yet i nɔ bin fala, se if di Pequod go visit ɛni wan pan dɛn ples dɛn de na ɛni kɔrɛspɔndɛns sizin we go kam afta dat, i go mit am de we nɔ go mek mistek. So, tu, wit sɔm ɔda ples dɛn fɔ it, usay i bin dɔn sho insɛf sɔntɛnde. Bɔt ɔl dɛn tin ya bin tan lɛk se na di say dɛn nɔmɔ we i kin stɔp fɔ stɔp ɛn di say dɛn we i kin slip na di si, so fɔ se, nɔto di say dɛn we i kin de fɔ lɔng tɛm. Ɛn usay dɛn dɔn tɔk bɔt Eab in chans fɔ akɔmplit in ɔbjɛkt te naw, dɛn dɔn jɔs mek allusion to ɛni we-sayd, antecedent, ɛkstra prɔspɛkt dɛn we na in yon, ere wan patikyula sɛt tɛm ɔ ples bin atɛnd, we ɔl di pɔsibul dɛn go bi prɔbabiliti, ɛn , lɛk aw Eab bin de tink wit gladi at, ɔl di tin dɛn we pɔsin kin du na di nɛks tin we pɔsin kin no. Dɛn bin jɔyn da patikyula tɛm ɛn ples de we dɛn bin dɔn sɛt insay di wan tɛknik frayz—di Sizin-ɔn-di-Layn. Fɔ de ɛn afta dat, fɔ sɔm ia kɔnsɛkutiv, dɛn bin dɔn de tɔk bɔt Mobi Dik wan wan tɛm, i bin de de na dɛn wata dɛn de fɔ sɔm tɛm, as di san, insay in ɛni ia rawnd, de loit fɔ wan prɛdikted intaval insay ɛni wan sayn fɔ di Zodiac. Na de bak, bɔku pan di bad bad tin dɛn we dɛn bin de mit wit di wayt wɛl bin dɔn apin; na de dɛn bin de tɔk bɔt di wef dɛn wit di tin dɛn we i du; na de bak da trayjik ples de usay di monomaniac ol man bin dɔn fɛn di bad bad rizin fɔ in blem. Bɔt insay di tek tɛm we Eab bin tek tɛm fɔ ɔndastand ɛn nɔ bin de wach in sol we bin de tink gud wan insay dis ɔntin we nɔ bin de chenj, i nɔ bin fɔ alaw insɛf fɔ put ɔl in op dɛn pan di wan krawn tru tin we wi bin dɔn tɔk bɔt ɔp, ilɛksɛf i fayn fɔ dɛn op dɛn de; ɛn insay di vaw we i nɔ bin ebul fɔ slip, i nɔ bin ebul fɔ mek in at we nɔ kwayɛt so kol dat i go put ɔl di tin dɛn we i bin de du fɔ go bifo.

Naw, di Pequod bin dɔn kɔmɔt na Nantucket di tɛm we di Sizin-ɔn-di-Layn bigin. No pɔsibul tray da tɛm de nɔ bin ebul fɔ mek in kɔmanda ebul fɔ mek di big pasej we de go na di sawt, we de dubl Kep Hɔn, ɛn afta dat we i rɔn dɔŋ siksti digri latitud rich na di ekwetorial Pasifik insay di tɛm fɔ kruz de. So, i fɔ wet fɔ di nɛks sizin we go kam. Bɔt stil, sɔntɛm Eab bin dɔn pik di tɛm we di Pɛkud bin de go bifo tɛm, ɛn i bin dɔn pik am kɔrɛkt wan, wit di atɛnshɔn fɔ dis kayn we aw tin dɛn tan. Bikɔs, wan intaval we na tri ɔndrɛd ɛn siksti fayv de ɛn nɛt bin de bifo am; wan intaval we, instead fɔ bia we i nɔ peshɛnt fɔ bia na di si, i go spɛn fɔ fɛn difrɛn difrɛn tin dɛn; if bay chans di Wait Wɛl, we de spɛn in vaykashun na si dɛn we de fa frɔm di say dɛn we i kin it ɔltɛm, fɔ tɔn in wrinkled brɔw ɔp ɔf di Pashian Galf, ɔ na di Bɛngal Bay, ɔ Chaina Si, ɔ na ɛni ɔda wata we in yon de haunt res. So dat Monsɔn, Pampas, Nɔ’-Wɛsta, Hamatan, Tred; ɛni briz pas di Levanter ɛn Simoon, kin blo Moby Dick insay di devious zig-zag wɔl-sɛklɔ na di Pequod in sirkumnavigating wek.

Bɔt fɔ gi ɔl dis; yet, we yu tek am wit sɛns ɛn kol, i tan lɛk se nɔto bɔt na mad aidia, dis; dat insay di brayt si we nɔ gɛt bɔda, dɛn fɔ tink se wan wɛl we de in wan, ilɛksɛf dɛn mit am, i go ebul fɔ no wan wan pɔsin frɔm in ɔntinman, ivin lɛk Mufti we gɛt wayt biad na di rod dɛn we bɔku pipul dɛn kin pas na Kɔnstantinopl? Yɛs. Bikɔs di pekul wayt sno brɔw we Mobi Dik gɛt, ɛn in sno wayt hump, nɔ bin ebul fɔ bi tin we pɔsin nɔ go ebul fɔ mistek. Ɛn a nɔ tink se a dɔn kɔnt di wɛl, Eab go de mɔna to insɛf, lɛk se afta we i dɔn luk in chɛt dɛn te lɔng afta midnayt i go trowe insɛf bak wit drɛg—i go kɔnt am, ɛn i go rɔnawe? In brayt fin dɛn dɔn bost, ɛn skel kɔmɔt lɛk ship in yes we dɔn lɔs! Ɛn na ya, in mad maynd go rɔn go bifo pan wan res we nɔ de blo; te wan taya ɛn taya fɔ tink gud wan kam oba am; ɛn na di opin ɛj na di dɛk i go tray fɔ gɛt in trɛnk bak. A, Gɔd! wetin trances of torments dat man de endure we de konsum wit wan unachieved revengeful desire. I kin slip wit in an dɛn we dɛn tay; ɛn wek wit in yon blɔd nel dɛn na in an.

Bɔku tɛm, we dɛn kin fos am fɔ kɔmɔt na in hamak bay drim dɛn we de mek i taya ɛn we nɔ go ebul fɔ bia wit drim dɛn bɔt di nɛt, we, we i bigin fɔ tink bak bɔt in yon strɔng tink dɛn tru di de, i kin kɛr dɛn go bifo midul wan krash pan frensi, ɛn rɔn dɛn rawnd ɛn rawnd ɛn rawnd insay in bren we de bɔn, . te di we aw in layf-spɔt bin de bit, bi pen we pɔsin nɔ go ebul fɔ bia; ɛn we, lɛk aw i kin bi sɔntɛnde, dɛn spiritual trɔbul dɛn ya we bin de insay am bin de es in bɔdi ɔp frɔm in bays, ɛn i bin tan lɛk se wan chasm de opin insay am, usay fɔk faya ɛn laytin dɛn bin de shot ɔp, ɛn swɛ dɛbul dɛn bin de mek sayn fɔ am fɔ jomp dɔŋ bitwin dɛn; we dis ɛl insɛf de yawn ɔnda am, dɛn go yɛri wan wayl kray tru di ship; ɛn wit yay we de shayn Eab go bɔs kɔmɔt na in stet rum, lɛk se i de rɔnawe kɔmɔt na bed we gɛt faya. Bɔt stil, dɛn tin ya, sɔntɛm, instead fɔ bi di sayn dɛn we nɔ go ebul fɔ stɔp fɔ sɔm kayn wikɛdnɛs we ayd, ɔ fɔ fred fɔ insɛf, na bin jɔs di klia sayn dɛn fɔ di kayn we aw i bin rili tranga. Bikɔs, na dɛn kayn tɛm dɛn de, krayzi Eab, di wan we de fɛn di wayt wɛl we nɔ de chenj ɛn we nɔ de mek i gladi; dis Eab we bin dɔn go na in hamak, nɔto di ɛjɛn we so mek i bɔs kɔmɔt de wit fred bak. Di las wan na bin di prinsipul ɔ sol we de sote go, we de alayv insay am; ɛn we i de slip, bikɔs fɔ di tɛm i nɔ bin de nia di maynd we de sho di pɔsin, we ɔda tɛm dɛn bin de yuz am fɔ in ɔda motoka ɔ ɛjɛn, i bin de tray fɔ rɔnawe bay insɛf frɔm di skɔnch kɔntigyushɔn we di tin we de mek pɔsin fred, we, fɔ di tɛm, i nɔ bin bi longer wan intagral. Bɔt as di maynd nɔ de pas i lig wit di sol, so i fɔ dɔn bi dat, insay Eab in kes, fɔ gi ɔl in tink ɛn fansi to in wan impɔtant tin we i want fɔ du; da rizin de, bay in yon sheer inveteracy of will, fos insɛf agens gɔd ɛn dɛbul fɔ bi wan kayn self-assumed, indipɛndɛnt biin fɔ in yon. Nay, kin grimly liv ɛn bɔn, we di kɔmɔn vitality we i bin jɔyn to, rɔnawe horror-stricken frɔm di unbidden ɛn unfathered bɔn. So, di spirit we bin de sɔfa we bin de shayn kɔmɔt na in bɔdi in yay, we wetin bin tan lɛk Eab rɔsh kɔmɔt na in rum, na bin fɔ di tɛm bɔt na tin we nɔ gɛt pɔsin, na bin somnambulist we nɔ gɛt fɔm, na layt we gɛt layf, fɔ tru, bɔt we nɔ gɛt ɛnitin fɔ du wit kɔlɔ, ɛn dat mek na blanknɛs insɛf. Gɔd ɛp yu, ol man, yu tinkin dɔn mek wan krichɔ insay yu; ɛn di wan we in strɔng tinkin so mek i bi Prometheus; wan vayl de it da at de sote go; dat vulture di very krichɔ we i mek.





CHAPTER 45. Di Afidavit.

So fa as wetin kin de pan wan narrative in dis buk; ɛn, fɔ tru, as i tɔch wan ɔ tu patikyula tin dɛn we rili intrestin ɛn we kin mek pɔsin want fɔ no mɔ bɔt di abit dɛn we sperm wɛl gɛt, di chapta we wi bin dɔn tɔk bɔt, insay in fɔs pat, impɔtant wan lɛk aw wi go si insay dis volyum; bɔt di lida tin bɔt am nid fɔ mek dɛn stil big pan am mɔ ɛn mɔ, fɔ mek dɛn ɔndastand am fayn fayn wan, ɛn mɔ fɔ pul ɛnitin we pɔsin nɔ biliv we wan dip ignɔrans bɔt di ɔl tɔpik kin mek sɔm pipul dɛn maynd, as fɔ di natura l tru tin pan di men pɔynt dɛn we dis afɛkt de tɔk bɔt.

A bisin fɔ nɔ du dis pat pan mi wok di rayt we; bɔt i fɔ satisfay fɔ prodyuz di impreshɔn we i want bay we i de sayt difrɛn say dɛn fɔ tin dɛn, we a prɛktikal ɔ rili sabi as wɛlman; ɛn frɔm dɛn saytayshɔn ya, a tek am—di kɔnklushɔn we dɛn aim fɔ go fala insɛf.

Fɔs: Mi sɛf dɔn no tri tɛm dɛn we wɛl, afta i dɔn gɛt harpun, dɔn mek i rɔnawe kpatakpata; ɛn, afta wan intaval (insay wan instans fɔ tri ia), dɛn dɔn bit am bak wit di sem an, ɛn kil am; we dɛn dɔn pul di tu ayɛn dɛn, we dɛn ɔl tu mak wit di sem prayvet sayfa, na di bɔdi. Insay di instans usay tri ia bin de bitwin di flinging fɔ di tu harpun dɛn; ɛn a tink se i go bi se na sɔntin we pas dat; di man we dart dɛn apin, insay di intaval, fɔ go insay wan tredin ship fɔ wan voyage to Afrika, go na di shore de, jɔyn wan pati we de fɛn pipul dɛn, ɛn go insay fa fawe insay di intɛriɔ, usay i travul fɔ lɛk tu ia so, bɔku tɛm dɛn kin de pan denja bay snek, savayj, tayga, pɔyzin miasma, wit ɔl di ɔda kɔmɔn prɔblɛm dɛn we kin apin we pɔsin de waka waka na di at na di say dɛn we wi nɔ no. Dis tɛm ya, di wɛl we i bin dɔn bit go dɔn de travul bak; i go mɔs bi se i bin dɔn rawnd di wɔl tri tɛm, ɛn i bin de brus wit in flank dɛn ɔl di kɔst dɛn na Afrika; bɔt na fɔ natin. Dis man ɛn dis wɛl kam togɛda bak, ɛn di wan win di ɔda wan. A se mi, misɛf, dɔn no tri instans dɛn we fiba dis; dat na insay tu pan dɛn a si di wɛl dɛn bit; ɛn, we dɛn atak di sɛkɔn tɛm, i si di tu ayɛn dɛn we dɛn kɔt di wan ol mak dɛn insay, afta dat dɛn tek dɛn frɔm di fish we dɔn day. Insay di tri ia instans, i so fɔdɔm dat a bin de insay di bot ɔl tu di tɛm, fɔs ɛn las tɛm, ɛn di las tɛm, a bin no klia wan wan kayn big big mol ɔnda di wɛl in yay, we a bin dɔn si de tri ia bifo dis tɛm. A se tri ia, bɔt a rili shɔ se i bin pas dat. So, na tri ɛgzampul dɛn ya, we mi sɛf no di tru bɔt; bɔt a dɔn yɛri bɔt bɔku ɔda instans frɔm pipul dɛn we dɛn veracity in di mata nɔ gɛt gud grɔn fɔ impeach.

Sɛkɔn: Dɛn no gud gud wan na di Sperm Whale Fishery, pan ɔl we di wɔl we de na di si nɔ no bɔt am, se sɔm istri instans dɛn dɔn de we pɔsin kin mɛmba usay wan patikyula wɛl na di si dɔn de fa fa tɛm ɛn ples dɛn we pipul dɛn kin no. Wetin mek dɛn bin mak dis kayn wɛl, nɔto ɔltogɛda ɛn fɔs bikɔs ɔf in bɔdi we difrɛn frɔm ɔda wɛl dɛn; bikɔs ilɛksɛf ɛni chans wɛl kin spɛshal pan dat, i nɔ tu te, dɛn kin dɔn in spɛshal tin dɛn bay we dɛn kil am, ɛn bɔyl am dɔŋ to wan ɔyl we gɛt valyu pasmak. Nɔ: di rizin na bin dis: se frɔm di bad bad tin dɛn we bin apin to di fisha biznɛs, wan bad bad prestij we bin de pan denja bin de hang bɔt da kayn wɛl de lɛk aw i bin de bɔt Rinaldo Rinaldini, sote bɔku fishaman dɛn bin satisfay fɔ no am bay we dɛn jɔs tɔch dɛn tapɔlin we i want bi discovered lounging by them on the sea, witout tray fɔ kultivayt wan mɔ intimate acquaintance. Lɛk sɔm po dɛbul dɛn we de na di si we kin apin fɔ no wan big man we kin vɛks bad bad wan, dɛn kin salut am fa fawe na strit, so dat if dɛn rɔnata di pɔsin we dɛn sabi mɔ, dɛn go gɛt sɔmari thump fɔ dɛn presumption.

Bɔt nɔto jɔs ɛni wan pan dɛn wɛl ya we pipul dɛn sabi bin ɛnjɔy fɔ gɛt bɔku nem fɔ dɛnsɛf—Nɔ, yu kin kɔl am wan ples we pipul dɛn sabi ɔlsay na di si; nɔto jɔs i bin gɛt nem na layf ɛn naw i nɔ de day insay fɔkast stori dɛn afta i day, bɔt dɛn bin admit am insay ɔl di rayt, prɛvilɛj, ɛn difrɛns dɛn fɔ wan nem; bin gɛt bɔku nem fɔ tru lɛk Kambis ɔ Siza. Yu tink se i nɔ bin bi so, O Timor Tom! yu famed leviathan, we gɛt skata lɛk ays blɔk, we so lɔng nɔ bin de lurk na di Oriental straits we gɛt da nem de, we dɛn bin de si in spɔt bɔku tɛm frɔm di palmy bich na Ombay? Yu nɔ tink se na so i bi, O Nyu Ziland Jɛk! yu terror of ol kruiz we kros dem wek na di vicinity of di Tattoo Land? Yu nɔ tink se na so i bi, O Mɔkwan! Kiŋ na Jepan, we dɛn se sɔntɛnde in ay ay jet bin tan lɛk krɔs we wayt lɛk sno agens di skay? Yu nɔ tink se na so i bi, O Dɔn Migul! yu Chiilian wɛl, we dɛn mak lɛk ol tɔt wit mistik hayoglifik dɛn na di bak! Insay klia proz, na 4 wɛl dɛn we di studɛnt dɛn we de stɔdi bɔt Sita Istri no gud gud wan as Marius ɔ Sylla to di klas masta sabi bukman.

Bɔt nɔto ɔl dis. Nyu Ziland Tɔmɔs ɛn Dɔn Migul, afta difrɛn tɛm dɛn dɔn mek big big pwɛl pwɛl bitwin di bot dɛn we gɛt difrɛn bot dɛn, dɛn bin dɔn go fɔ fɛn, dɛn bin dɔn ɔnt dɛn sistamatically, chase ɛn kil dɛn bay valiant wɛl kapten dɛn, we bin hev dɛn anka dɛn wit da ɛkspɛs ɔbjɛkt de as bɔku tin we wi bin de tink bɔt, lɛk we i bin de go tru di Naragansett Wuds, Kpɛntin Bɔtlar we bin de trade bin gɛt am na in maynd fɔ kech da bad bad savayj we nem Anawon we bin de kil pipul dɛn, we na di edman fɔ di Indian Kiŋ Filip.

A nɔ no usay a go fɛn bɛtɛ ples pas jɔs ya, fɔ mek mɛsej bɔt wan ɔ tu ɔda tin dɛn, we to mi tan lɛk se i impɔtant, lɛk insay print fɔm we de establish pan ɔltin di rizin we mek di wan ol stori bɔt di Wait Wɛl, . mɔ mɔ di bad bad tin we apin. Bikɔs dis na wan pan dɛn tin dɛn we kin mek pɔsin in at pwɛl usay trut nid fɔ ful-ɔp wit bɔku bɔku tin dɛn lɛk fɔ mek mistek. So ignorant na most landsmen of som of di plainest and most palpable wonders of di world, dat if som hints touch di plain facts, historical and otherwise, of di fishery, dem kin skaut pan Moby Dick as wan monstrous fable, or stil worse en mɔ detestable, wan hideous ɛn intolerable allegory.

Fɔs: Pan ɔl we bɔku man dɛn gɛt sɔm vague flitting aidia dɛn bɔt di jenɛral prɔblɛm dɛn we di big big fisha biznɛs kin gɛt, bɔt stil dɛn nɔ gɛt natin lɛk wan fiks, klia kɔnsɛpshɔn bɔt dɛn prɔblɛm dɛn de, ɛn di frɛkuɛns we dɛn kin kam bak. Wan rizin sɔntɛm na, dat nɔto wan pan ɛvri fifti pan di rial disasta ɛn day we pipul dɛn kin day na di fisha wok, nɔ kin ɛva fɛn pɔblik rɛkɔd na os, ilɛksɛf i fɔ shɔt tɛm ɛn fɔgɛt da rɛkɔd de wantɛm wantɛm. Yu tink se da po man de, we dis mɔnt sɔntɛm di wɛl-layn we de nia di kɔst na Nyu Gini, na in di leviathan we de mek sawnd de kɛr go dɔŋ na di si—yu tink se da po man de in nem go apia na di nyuspepa obituary yu go rid tumara na yu brekfas? Nɔ: bikɔs di mɛyl dɛn rili irɛgyula bitwin ya ɛn Nyu Gini. Infakt, yu dɔn ɛva yɛri wetin dɛn kin kɔl rɛgyula nyus dairekt ɔ indayrɛkt frɔm Nyu Gini? Bɔt stil a de tɛl una se pan wan patikyula waka we a bin travul go na di Pasifik, pan bɔku ɔda wan dɛn wi bin tɔk bɔt tati difrɛn ship dɛn, ɛn ɛni wan pan dɛn bin dɔn day bay wan wɛl, sɔm pan dɛn pas wan, ɛn tri we ɛni wan pan dɛn lɔs wan bot in kru. Fɔ Gɔd in sek, una nɔ spɛn bɔku mɔni wit una lamp ɛn kandul dɛn! nɔto wan galɔn yu bɔn, bɔt at le wan drɔp pan mɔtalman blɔd bin tɔn fɔ am.

Sɛkɔn: Pipul dɛn we de na di si gɛt sɔm aidia we nɔ de dɔn fɔ tru se wɛl na big big tin we gɛt bɔku pawa; bɔt a dɔn ɛva si se we a de tɛl dɛn sɔm patikyula ɛgzampul bɔt dis tu-fɔl big big tin, dɛn dɔn rili kɔmplimɛnt mi pan mi fes; we, a de tɔk pan mi sol, a nɔ bin gɛt ɛni aidia fɔ bi fes-to-fes pas Mozis, we i rayt di istri bɔt di bad bad tin dɛn we bin apin na Ijipt.

Bɔt fɔtunate wan di spɛshal pɔynt we a de luk fɔ ya kin establish pan tɛstimoni we nɔ gɛt ɛnitin fɔ du wit mi yon. Dat pɔynt na dis: Sɔntɛnde, di Spɛm Wɛl gɛt pawa, i no, ɛn i kin tink bɔt bad tin, lɛk aw i kin tink bifo tɛm fɔ mek i nɔ go insay, pwɛl kpatakpata, ɛn sink big ship; ɛn wetin pas dat, di Spɛm Wɛl dɔn du am.

Fɔs: Insay di ia 1820, di ship we dɛn kɔl Ɛsɛks, we nem Kpɛntin Pɔlad, we kɔmɔt na Nantɔkɛt, bin de travul na di Pasifik Oshɔn. Wan de, i si spɔt, i put in bot dɛn dɔŋ, ɛn rɔnata wan grup we gɛt sperm wɛl. Ere long, sɔm pan di wɛl dɛn bin wund; we, wantɛm wantɛm, wan rili big wɛl rɔnawe kɔmɔt na di bot dɛn, kɔmɔt na di shoal, ɛn go dɔŋ di ship dairekt wan. I bin de das in fɔɛd pan in bɔdi, so stov am insay, dat i nɔ rich “tɛn minit” i setul ɛn fɔdɔm. Frɔm da tɛm de, dɛn nɔ si wan plang pan am we stil de alayv. Afta di bad bad tin we bin apin to di bot, sɔm pan di wan dɛn we de wok na di bot bin rich na di land. We dɛn bin dɔn kam bak na os las las, Kpɛntin Pɔlad bin tek wan tɛm bak fɔ go na di Pasifik in kɔmand fɔ ɔda ship, bɔt di gɔd dɛn bin pwɛl am bak pan rɔk dɛn ɛn brɛka dɛn we dɛn nɔ no; fɔ di sɛkɔn tɛm we in ship lɔs kpatakpata, ɛn wantɛm wantɛm i swɛ di si, i nɔ ɛva tɛmpt am frɔm da tɛm de. Na dis de, Kpɛntin Pollard na pɔsin we de na Nantucket. A dɔn si Owen Chace, we na bin di chif mɛt fɔ di Essex di tɛm we di bad bad tin apin; A dɔn rid in klia ɛn fetful stori; A dɔn tɔk to in pikin; ɛn ɔl dis insay sɔm mayl frɔm di say we di bad bad tin apin.*

*Dis tin dɛn we de dɔŋ ya na sɔm pat dɛn frɔm Chace in narrative: “I bin tan lɛk se ɔl di tru tin dɛn we a bin de du bin mek a fɔ dɔn se na ɛnitin pas chans we bin de dayrɛkt in opareshɔn dɛn; i mek tu difrɛn atak dɛn pan di ship, na shɔt tɛm bitwin dɛn, we dɛn ɔl tu, akɔdin to dɛn dairekshɔn, dɛn kɔl fɔ du wi di mɔs injuri, bay we dɛn mek dɛn bifo, ɛn so dɛn jɔyn di spid fɔ di tu tin dɛn fɔ di shɔk we pɔsin kin gɛt; to effect which, di ekzak manœuvres we i mek bin nid. In aspek bin rili bad, ɛn lɛk se i bin de sho se i vɛks ɛn i bin vɛks. I kɔmɔt dairekt frɔm di shoal we wi bin dɔn go insay jɔs bifo, ɛn we wi bin dɔn bit tri pan in kɔmpin dɛn insay, lɛk se dɛn faya fɔ blem fɔ di sɔfa we dɛn bin de sɔfa.” Agen: “At ɔl di tin dɛn we apin, di wan ol sikɔstɛms we dɛn tek togɛda, ɔl de apin bifo mi yon yay, ɛn prodyuz, da tɛm de, impreshɔn dɛn na mi maynd fɔ disayd, kɔlkyul mischief, pan di pat pan di wɛl (bɔku pan dɛn impreshɔn ya a nɔ ebul naw mɛmba), mek a satisfay se a kɔrɛkt pan mi opinion.”

Na dis na di tin dɛn we i bin de tink bɔt sɔm tɛm afta i dɔn kɔmɔt na di ship, insay wan blak nɛt insay wan bot we opin, we i bin lɛf smɔl fɔ lɛ i nɔ gɛt op fɔ rich na ɛni sho we pipul dɛn kin wɛlkɔm pipul dɛn. “Di dak si ɛn di wata we bin de swel nɔ bin natin; di fred fɔ mek sɔm bad bad briz go swɛla mi, ɔ fɔ rɔn pan rɔk dɛn we ayd, wit ɔl di ɔda ɔdinari tin dɛn we pɔsin kin tink bɔt wit fred, i bin tan lɛk se i nɔ gɛt rayt fɔ tink bɔt am fɔ smɔl tɛm; di ship we bin pwɛl we i bin de luk bad, ɛn di bad bad tin we di wɛl bin de du ɛn di revaŋg we di wɛl bin de du , bin mek a de tink gud wan, te di de kam bak.”

Sɛkɔn: Di ship Yuniɔn, we na Nantucket bak, bin lɔs insay di ia 1807 ɔltogɛda ɔf di Azores bay wan sem kayn biginin, bɔt di ɔtɛnɛtik patikyula tin dɛn bɔt dis bad bad tin we a nɔ ɛva chans fɔ mit, pan ɔl we frɔm di wɛl ɔnta dɛn we a dɔn yɛri naw ɛn naw keshɔl allusions to am.

Tɔd: Sɔm ettin ɔ twɛnti ia so dɔn pas we Kɔmodɔ J——, we bin de kɔmand wan Amɛrikan sloop-ɔf-wɔ we na di fɔs klas da tɛm de, bin apin se i bin de it wit wan pati we gɛt kapten dɛn we de kech wɛl, na wan Nantucket ship we de na di haba na Oahu, Sandwich Ayland dɛn. Di tɔk we bin de tɔn pan wɛl, di Kɔmwɔd bin gladi fɔ dawt fɔ tɔch di wɔndaful trɛnk we di prɔfɛshɔnal jentlman dɛn we bin de de bin gi dɛn. I bin dinay fɔ ɛgzampul, se ɛni wɛl kin so smit in strɔng slɔp-ɔf-wɔ so dat i go mek i lik so lɛk wan thimbleful. Rili gud; bɔt ɔda tin dɛn de kam. Sɔm wiks afta dat, di Kɔmwɔd bin tek dis bot we nɔ go ebul fɔ win fɔ go na Valparaiso. Bɔt wan portly sperm whale bin stɔp am na di rod, we bin beg fɔ sɔm mɔnt kɔnfidɛns biznɛs wit am. Dat biznɛs bin de fɔ mek di Kɔmwɔd in kraft so thwack, dat wit ɔl in pɔmp dɛn we de go i mek stret fɔ di pɔt we de nia fɔ hev dɔŋ ɛn ripɛnt. A nɔ biliv lay lay tin dɛn, bɔt a kin tek di intavyu we di Kɔmwɔd bin gɛt wit da wɛl de as tin we dɛn kin du. Yu nɔ tink se Sɔl we kɔmɔt na Tasɔs bin chenj bikɔs i nɔ bin biliv Gɔd bikɔs ɔf di sem kayn fred? A de tel yu, di sperm whale go tinap no nonsense.

A go naw rifer yu to Langsdorff’s Voyages fɔ smɔl sikɔstɛms in pɔynt, we pekul intrestin to di rayta fɔ dis. Langsdorff, yu go no bay di we, bin atak di Rɔshian Admiral Krusenstern in fambul Discovery Expedition insay di biginin fɔ di prɛsɛn sɛnti. Na so Kpɛntin Langsdɔf bigin in sɛvintin chapta:

“Bay di de we mek tɛn insay Me, wi ship bin dɔn rɛdi fɔ go, ɛn di nɛks de wi bin de na di opin si, we wi de go na Ɔkɔtsh. Di wɛda bin rili klia ɛn fayn, bɔt i bin so kol we wi nɔ bin ebul fɔ bia dat wi bin gɛt fɔ wɛr wi klos dɛn we gɛt fut. Fɔ sɔm dez, wi nɔ bin gɛt bɛtɛ briz; na te di nayntin de we wan big big briz we kɔmɔt na di nɔt-wɛst bigin fɔ blo. Wan big wɛl we nɔ kɔmɔn, we in bɔdi bin big pas di ship sɛf, bin ledɔm klos to di wata, bɔt ɛnibɔdi we bin de insay di ship nɔ bin no am te di tɛm we di ship we bin dɔn ful-ɔp wit ful sel, bin dɔn lɛf smɔl fɔ kam am, so i nɔ bin pɔsibul fɔ mek i nɔ atak am. So dɛn bin put wi na di denja we bin de nia pas ɔl, as dis big big animal, we bin de mek in bak, bin es di ship ɔp tri fit at least kɔmɔt na di wata. Di mast dɛn bin de rɔn, ɛn di sel dɛn bin fɔdɔm ɔltogɛda, we wi we bin de dɔŋ ɔl bin rɔn wantɛm wantɛm na di dɛk, ɛn kam fɔ no se wi dɔn nak sɔm rɔk; insted of dis wi si di monsta de sel go wit di utmost gravity en solemnity. Kpɛntin D’Wɔlf bin aplay wantɛm wantɛm to di pɔmp dɛn fɔ chɛk if di bot dɔn gɛt ɛni damej frɔm di shɔk ɔ nɔto so, bɔt wi bin si se i rili gladi fɔ se i dɔn rɔnawe atɔl we i nɔ wund.”

Naw, di Kpɛntin D’Wɔlf we dɛn tɔk bɔt ya se na in de kɔmand di ship we dɛn de tɔk bɔt, na Nyu Ingland man, we, afta lɔng layf we i dɔn du tin dɛn we nɔ kɔmɔn as si-kapten, dis de de na di vilej we nem Dɔchɛsta nia Bɔston. A gɛt di ɔnɔ fɔ bi in brɔda in pikin. A dɔn aks am mɔ kwɛstyɔn bɔt dis pat na Langsdɔf. I de sɔpɔt ɛni wɔd. Bɔt di ship nɔ bin big atɔl: na Rɔshian kraf we dɛn bil na di Sayberian kɔst, ɛn mi ɔnkul bin bay am afta we i chenj di ship we i bin de kɔmɔt na os.

Insay da ɔp ɛn dɔŋ man buk we de tɔk bɔt ol-fashin advantej, we ful-ɔp, bak, wit ɔnɛs wɔndaful tin dɛn—di voyage we Layɔnɛl Wafa, we na wan pan di ol padi dɛn we Dampier bin gɛt trade trade—a bin fɛn wan smɔl tin we dɛn put dɔŋ so lɛk dat we dɛn jɔs kot frɔm Langsdorff, dat a nɔ go ebul fɔ bia fɔ put am ya fɔ ɛgzampul we de sɔpɔt am, if dɛn nid fɔ du dat.

I tan lɛk se Layɔnɛl bin de go to “Jɔn Fɛdinando,” as i de kɔl di pɔsin we de naw, Juan Fɛnandez. I se: “We wi bin de go de, lɛk 4 oklɔk na mɔnin, we wi bin de lɛk wan ɔndrɛd ɛn fifti lig frɔm di Men ɔf Amɛrika, wi ship bin fil wan bad bad shɔk, we mek wi man dɛn so dat dɛn nɔ bin ebul fɔ no usay dɛn de ɔ wetin fɔ tink; bɔt ɔlman bigin fɔ rɛdi fɔ day. Ɛn, fɔ tru, di shɔk bin so wantɛm wantɛm ɛn i bin gɛt fɛt-fɛt, dat wi bin tek am se na sɔntin we di ship bin dɔn bang pan wan rɔk; bɔt we di wɔndaful tin dɔn smɔl, wi trowe di lid, ɛn sawnd, bɔt wi nɔ si ɛni grɔn. * * * * * Di shɔk we dɛn shok wantɛm wantɛm mek di gɔn dɛn jomp insay dɛn kariɔt, ɛn sɔm pan di man dɛn shek kɔmɔt na dɛn hamak. Dɛn bin trowe Kpɛntin Devids, we bin ledɔm wit in ed pan gɔn, kɔmɔt na in kebin!” Dɔn Layɔnɛl go bifo fɔ se di shɔk na atkwek, ɛn i tan lɛk se i pruv se na atkwek apin bay we i tɔk se wan big atkwek, sɔmsay lɛk da tɛm de, bin rili du big bad tin along di Spanish land. Bɔt a nɔ fɔ rili wɔnda if, insay di daknɛs na da ali awa de na mɔnin, di shɔk bin apin afta ɔl na bikɔs wan wɛl we a nɔ si bin de bang di bot vertikal wan frɔm ɔnda.

A kin go bifo wit sɔm ɔda ɛgzampul dɛn, wan we ɔ ɔda we we a no, bɔt di big pawa ɛn bad we aw di sperm wɛl kin gɛt sɔntɛnde. I pas wan tɛm, pipul dɛn dɔn no am, nɔto jɔs fɔ rɔnata di bot dɛn we de atak am bak to dɛn ship dɛn, bɔt fɔ rɔnata di ship insɛf, ɛn fɔ lɔng tɛm fɔ bia ɔl di lans dɛn we dɛn kin trowe pan am frɔm in dɛk. Di Inglish ship we nem Pusie Hall kin tɛl wan stori pan da ed de; ɛn, as fɔ in trɛnk, lɛ a se, ɛgzampul dɛn dɔn de usay di layn dɛn we dɛn tay pan wan sperm wɛl we de rɔn dɔn, insay wan kol, transfa to di ship, ɛn sikyuɔr de; di wɛl de pul in big big bot tru di wata, lɛk aw ɔs de waka wit kat. Bak, dɛn kin si bɔku tɛm se, if dɛn alaw di sperm wɛl, wans dɛn dɔn bit am, tɛm fɔ gɛda, i kin du sɔntin, nɔto so ɔltɛm wit blaynd wamat, lɛk wit wilful, bay wilful plan fɔ pwɛl di wan dɛn we de rɔnata am; nor is it without conveying some eloquent indication of his character, that upon being attacked he will frequently open his mouth, and retain it in that dread expansion for several consecutive minutes. But I must be content with only one more and a concluding illustration; a remarkable and most significant one, by which you will not fail to see, that not only is the most marvellous event in this book corroborated by plain facts of the present day, but that these marvels (like all marvels) are mere repetitions of the ages; so that for the millionth time we say amen with Solomon—Verily there is nothing new under the sun.

In the sixth Christian century lived Procopius, a Christian magistrate of Constantinople, in the days when Justinian was Emperor and Belisarius general. As many know, he wrote the history of his own times, a work every way of uncommon value. By the best authorities, he has always been considered a most trustworthy and unexaggerating historian, except in some one or two particulars, not at all affecting the matter presently to be mentioned.

Now, in this history of his, Procopius mentions that, during the term of his prefecture at Constantinople, a great sea-monster was captured in the neighboring Propontis, or Sea of Marmora, after having destroyed vessels at intervals in those waters for a period of more than fifty years. A fact thus set down in substantial history cannot easily be gainsaid. Nor is there any reason it should be. Of what precise species this sea-monster was, is not mentioned. But as he destroyed ships, as well as for other reasons, he must have been a whale; and I am strongly inclined to think a sperm whale. And I will tell you why. For a long time I fancied that the sperm whale had been always unknown in the Mediterranean and the deep waters connecting with it. Even now I am certain that those seas are not, and perhaps never can be, in the present constitution of things, a place for his habitual gregarious resort. But further investigations have recently proved to me, that in modern times there have been isolated instances of the presence of the sperm whale in the Mediterranean. I am told, on good authority, that on the Barbary coast, a Commodore Davis of the British navy found the skeleton of a sperm whale. Now, as a vessel of war readily passes through the Dardanelles, hence a sperm whale could, by the same route, pass out of the Mediterranean into the Propontis.

In the Propontis, as far as I can learn, none of that peculiar substance called brit is to be found, the aliment of the right whale. But I have every reason to believe that the food of the sperm whale—squid or cuttle-fish—lurks at the bottom of that sea, because large creatures, but by no means the largest of that sort, have been found at its surface. If, then, you properly put these statements together, and reason upon them a bit, you will clearly perceive that, according to all human reasoning, Procopius's sea-monster, that for half a century stove the ships of a Roman Emperor, must in all probability have been a sperm whale.





CHAPTER 46. Surmises.

Though, consumed with the hot fire of his purpose, Ahab in all his thoughts and actions ever had in view the ultimate capture of Moby Dick; though he seemed ready to sacrifice all mortal interests to that one passion; nevertheless it may have been that he was by nature and long habituation far too wedded to a fiery whaleman's ways, altogether to abandon the collateral prosecution of the voyage. Or at least if this were otherwise, there were not wanting other motives much more influential with him. It would be refining too much, perhaps, even considering his monomania, to hint that his vindictiveness towards the White Whale might have possibly extended itself in some degree to all sperm whales, and that the more monsters he slew by so much the more he multiplied the chances that each subsequently encountered whale would prove to be the hated one he hunted. But if such an hypothesis be indeed exceptionable, there were still additional considerations which, though not so strictly according with the wildness of his ruling passion, yet were by no means incapable of swaying him.

To accomplish his object Ahab must use tools; and of all tools used in the shadow of the moon, men are most apt to get out of order. He knew, for example, that however magnetic his ascendency in some respects was over Starbuck, yet that ascendency did not cover the complete spiritual man any more than mere corporeal superiority involves intellectual mastership; for to the purely spiritual, the intellectual but stand in a sort of corporeal relation. Starbuck's body and Starbuck's coerced will were Ahab's, so long as Ahab kept his magnet at Starbuck's brain; still he knew that for all this the chief mate, in his soul, abhorred his captain's quest, and could he, would joyfully disintegrate himself from it, or even frustrate it. It might be that a long interval would elapse ere the White Whale was seen. During that long interval Starbuck would ever be apt to fall into open relapses of rebellion against his captain's leadership, unless some ordinary, prudential, circumstantial influences were brought to bear upon him. Not only that, but the subtle insanity of Ahab respecting Moby Dick was noways more significantly manifested than in his superlative sense and shrewdness in foreseeing that, for the present, the hunt should in some way be stripped of that strange imaginative impiousness which naturally invested it; that the full terror of the voyage must be kept withdrawn into the obscure background (for few men's courage is proof against protracted meditation unrelieved by action); that when they stood their long night watches, his officers and men must have some nearer things to think of than Moby Dick. For however eagerly and impetuously the savage crew had hailed the announcement of his quest; yet all sailors of all sorts are more or less capricious and unreliable—they live in the varying outer weather, and they inhale its fickleness—and when retained for any object remote and blank in the pursuit, however promissory of life and passion in the end, it is above all things requisite that temporary interests and employments should intervene and hold them healthily suspended for the final dash.

Nor was Ahab unmindful of another thing. In times of strong emotion mankind disdain all base considerations; but such times are evanescent. The permanent constitutional condition of the manufactured man, thought Ahab, is sordidness. Granting that the White Whale fully incites the hearts of this my savage crew, and playing round their savageness even breeds a certain generous knight-errantism in them, still, while for the love of it they give chase to Moby Dick, they must also have food for their more common, daily appetites. For even the high lifted and chivalric Crusaders of old times were not content to traverse two thousand miles of land to fight for their holy sepulchre, without committing burglaries, picking pockets, and gaining other pious perquisites by the way. Had they been strictly held to their one final and romantic object—that final and romantic object, too many would have turned from in disgust. I will not strip these men, thought Ahab, of all hopes of cash—aye, cash. They may scorn cash now; but let some months go by, and no perspective promise of it to them, and then this same quiescent cash all at once mutinying in them, this same cash would soon cashier Ahab.

Nor was there wanting still another precautionary motive more related to Ahab personally. Having impulsively, it is probable, and perhaps somewhat prematurely revealed the prime but private purpose of the Pequod's voyage, Ahab was now entirely conscious that, in so doing, he had indirectly laid himself open to the unanswerable charge of usurpation; and with perfect impunity, both moral and legal, his crew if so disposed, and to that end competent, could refuse all further obedience to him, and even violently wrest from him the command. From even the barely hinted imputation of usurpation, and the possible consequences of such a suppressed impression gaining ground, Ahab must of course have been most anxious to protect himself. That protection could only consist in his own predominating brain and heart and hand, backed by a heedful, closely calculating attention to every minute atmospheric influence which it was possible for his crew to be subjected to.

For all these reasons then, and others perhaps too analytic to be verbally developed here, Ahab plainly saw that he must still in a good degree continue true to the natural, nominal purpose of the Pequod's voyage; observe all customary usages; and not only that, but force himself to evince all his well known passionate interest in the general pursuit of his profession.

Be all this as it may, his voice was now often heard hailing the three mast-heads and admonishing them to keep a bright look-out, and not omit reporting even a porpoise. This vigilance was not long without reward.





CHAPTER 47. The Mat-Maker.

It was a cloudy, sultry afternoon; the seamen were lazily lounging about the decks, or vacantly gazing over into the lead-coloured waters. Queequeg and I were mildly employed weaving what is called a sword-mat, for an additional lashing to our boat. So still and subdued and yet somehow preluding was all the scene, and such an incantation of reverie lurked in the air, that each silent sailor seemed resolved into his own invisible self.

I was the attendant or page of Queequeg, while busy at the mat. As I kept passing and repassing the filling or woof of marline between the long yarns of the warp, using my own hand for the shuttle, and as Queequeg, standing sideways, ever and anon slid his heavy oaken sword between the threads, and idly looking off upon the water, carelessly and unthinkingly drove home every yarn: I say so strange a dreaminess did there then reign all over the ship and all over the sea, only broken by the intermitting dull sound of the sword, that it seemed as if this were the Loom of Time, and I myself were a shuttle mechanically weaving and weaving away at the Fates. There lay the fixed threads of the warp subject to but one single, ever returning, unchanging vibration, and that vibration merely enough to admit of the crosswise interblending of other threads with its own. This warp seemed necessity; and here, thought I, with my own hand I ply my own shuttle and weave my own destiny into these unalterable threads. Meantime, Queequeg's impulsive, indifferent sword, sometimes hitting the woof slantingly, or crookedly, or strongly, or weakly, as the case might be; and by this difference in the concluding blow producing a corresponding contrast in the final aspect of the completed fabric; this savage's sword, thought I, which thus finally shapes and fashions both warp and woof; this easy, indifferent sword must be chance—aye, chance, free will, and necessity—nowise incompatible—all interweavingly working together. The straight warp of necessity, not to be swerved from its ultimate course—its every alternating vibration, indeed, only tending to that; free will still free to ply her shuttle between given threads; and chance, though restrained in its play within the right lines of necessity, and sideways in its motions directed by free will, though thus prescribed to by both, chance by turns rules either, and has the last featuring blow at events.

Thus we were weaving and weaving away when I started at a sound so strange, long drawn, and musically wild and unearthly, that the ball of free will dropped from my hand, and I stood gazing up at the clouds whence that voice dropped like a wing. High aloft in the cross-trees was that mad Gay-Header, Tashtego. His body was reaching eagerly forward, his hand stretched out like a wand, and at brief sudden intervals he continued his cries. To be sure the same sound was that very moment perhaps being heard all over the seas, from hundreds of whalemen's look-outs perched as high in the air; but from few of those lungs could that accustomed old cry have derived such a marvellous cadence as from Tashtego the Indian's.

As he stood hovering over you half suspended in air, so wildly and eagerly peering towards the horizon, you would have thought him some prophet or seer beholding the shadows of Fate, and by those wild cries announcing their coming.

“There she blows! there! there! there! she blows! she blows!”

“Where-away?”

“On the lee-beam, about two miles off! a school of them!”

Instantly all was commotion.

The Sperm Whale blows as a clock ticks, with the same undeviating and reliable uniformity. And thereby whalemen distinguish this fish from other tribes of his genus.

“There go flukes!” was now the cry from Tashtego; and the whales disappeared.

“Quick, steward!” cried Ahab. “Time! time!”

Dough-Boy hurried below, glanced at the watch, and reported the exact minute to Ahab.

The ship was now kept away from the wind, and she went gently rolling before it. Tashtego reporting that the whales had gone down heading to leeward, we confidently looked to see them again directly in advance of our bows. For that singular craft at times evinced by the Sperm Whale when, sounding with his head in one direction, he nevertheless, while concealed beneath the surface, mills round, and swiftly swims off in the opposite quarter—this deceitfulness of his could not now be in action; for there was no reason to suppose that the fish seen by Tashtego had been in any way alarmed, or indeed knew at all of our vicinity. One of the men selected for shipkeepers—that is, those not appointed to the boats, by this time relieved the Indian at the main-mast head. The sailors at the fore and mizzen had come down; the line tubs were fixed in their places; the cranes were thrust out; the mainyard was backed, and the three boats swung over the sea like three samphire baskets over high cliffs. Outside of the bulwarks their eager crews with one hand clung to the rail, while one foot was expectantly poised on the gunwale. So look the long line of man-of-war's men about to throw themselves on board an enemy's ship.

But at this critical instant a sudden exclamation was heard that took every eye from the whale. With a start all glared at dark Ahab, who was surrounded by five dusky phantoms that seemed fresh formed out of air.
