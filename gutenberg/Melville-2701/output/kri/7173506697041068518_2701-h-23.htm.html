CHAPTER 124. Di Nidul.

Neks mɔnin di si we nɔ bin dɔn go dɔŋ yet bin rol insay lɔng slo slo billows of mighty bulk, ɛn we i bin de tray tranga wan na di Pequod in gurgling track, push am go bifo lɛk jayant dɛn palm dɛn we opin. Di strɔng briz we nɔ de shek bin de blo so dat di skay ɛn di briz bin tan lɛk big big sel dɛn we de kɔmɔt na di bɛlɛ; di wan ol wɔl bin de bɔm bifo di briz. We i bin de muf insay di ful mɔnin layt, di san we wi nɔ de si bin jɔs no bay di we aw in ples bin de skata; usay in bayonet rayt bin de muf go bifo insay stak. Emblazonings, as of Babilonian kiŋ ɛn kwin dɛn we dɛn dɔn krawn, bin de rul oba ɔltin. Di si bin tan lɛk kruk we dɛn mek wit gold we dɔn rɔtin, we de jomp bɔb wit layt ɛn wam.

Fɔ lɔng tɛm we Eab bin de mek i nɔ tɔk natin, ɛn i bin tinap apat; ɛn ɛvri tɛm we di ship we de shek shek de dɔŋ in bowsprit dɔŋ, i kin tɔn fɔ yay di brayt san in rayt we de kɔmɔt bifo; ɛn we i sidɔm dip wan nia di stɛp, i tɔn biɛn, ɛn si di say we di san de biɛn, ɛn aw di sem yɔlɔ rayt dɛn de miks wit in wek we nɔ de kɔmɔt biɛn.

“Ha, ha, mi ship! yu go ebul fɔ tek yu naw fɔ di si-chariot we di san de yuz. Ho, ho! ɔl una neshɔn dɛn bifo mi prow, a de briŋ di san to una! Yok pan di ɔda bilɔ dɛn; hallo! a tandem, na mi de drayv di si!”

Bɔt wantɛm wantɛm we sɔm kɔntra tinkin bin de kɔntrol am bak, i rɔsh go nia di edman, ɛn i bin de aks am wit swɛt aw di ship de go.

Di stiarman we bin de fred bin tɔk se: “Ist-sɔ-ist, sa.”

“Yu de lay!” i de bit am wit in fist we i dɔn klin. “Yu de go na Is dis awa na mɔnin, ɛn di san go biɛn?”

Pan dis, ɔlman bin kɔnfyus; bikɔs di tin we bin apin jɔs da tɛm de we Eab bin si, bin dɔn rɔnawe pan ɔlman we nɔ gɛt wan kɔnfidɛns; bɔt di we aw i bin rili blaynd fɔ si am, go mɔs dɔn bi di rizin.

We Eab bin de trowe in ed af-af insay di binnakul, i si di kɔmpas dɛn wan tɛm; in an we i bin es ɔp bin fɔdɔm smɔl smɔl; fɔ smɔl tɛm i bin tan lɛk se i de stɛp. Stand bihain am Starbuck luk, en lo! di tu kɔmpas dɛn bin de sho di Is, ɛn di Pɛkɔd bin de go na di Wɛst we nɔ gɛt mistek.

Bɔt bifo di fɔs wayl alam go kɔmɔt na ɔda kɔntri bitwin di kru, di ol man we bin de laf tranga wan bin ala se, “A gɛt am! I dɔn apin bifo dis tɛm. Mista Starbuck, las nɛt in tɛnda tɔn wi kɔmpas dɛn—na dat nɔmɔ. Yu dɔn yɛri bɔt dis kayn tin bifo naw, a de tek am.”

“Aye; bɔt i nɔ ɛva apin to mi bifo, sa,” di pale mate bin tɔk, wit pwɛl at.

Na ya, wi fɔ se, aksidɛnt dɛn lɛk dis dɔn apin insay pas wan kes to ship dɛn we big big briz de blo. Di magnɛtik ɛnaji, as i de divɛlɔp insay di mariner in nidul, na, as ɔlman no, essentially wan wit di ilɛktrishɔn we dɛn de si na ɛvin; na dat mek i nɔ fɔ sɔprayz bad bad wan, se dɛn kayn tin ya fɔ bi. Instans we di laytin dɔn rili slap di vessel, so dat i go smit dɔŋ sɔm ​​pan di spars ɛn rigging, di ifɛkt pan di nidul dɔn stil de kil mɔ; ɔl in lodston gud kwaliti dɛn bin de dɔnawe wit am, so dat di bifo magnɛtik stɛl nɔ bin gɛt ɛni yus pas wan ol wɛf in nidul fɔ nit. Bɔt pan ɔl dis, di nidul nɔ de ɛva igen, insɛf, gɛt bak di fɔs gud kwaliti we dɛn bin dɔn pwɛl ɔ we dɔn lɔs so; ɛn if dɛn afɛkt di binakl kɔmpas dɛn, di sem tin kin apin to ɔl di ɔda wan dɛn we kin de na di ship; ivin dɛn bin de put di wan we de dɔŋ pas ɔl insay di kelson.

We i bin dɔn tinap bifo di binakl bay wilful, ɛn de luk di kɔmpas dɛn we dɛn transpɔynt, di ol man, wit di shap shap an we i dɔn es, naw tek di prɛsis bia we di san de bia, ɛn satisfay se di nidul dɛn dɔn invayt ɛksaktɔli, ala in ɔda fɔ di ship in kɔs fɔ chenj akɔdin to dat. Di yad dɛn bin at fɔ go ɔp; ɛn wan tɛm mɔ di Pɛkud bin push in bɔw dɛn we nɔ bin de fred insay di briz we bin de agens am, bikɔs di wan we dɛn se i fayn bin jɔs de jɔg am.

Dis tɛm ya, ilɛk wetin na in yon sikrit tin dɛn, Starbuck nɔ tɔk natin, bɔt kwayɛt wan i gi ɔl di ɔda ɔda tin dɛn we i nid; we Stubb ɛn Flask—we insay sɔm smɔl digri, i bin tan lɛk se dɛn de sheb in filin da tɛm de—sɛf bin gri wit wetin dɛn de tɔk ɛn dɛn nɔ bin de grɔmbul. As fɔ di man dɛn, pan ɔl we sɔm pan dɛn bin de rɔm smɔl smɔl, di fred we dɛn bin de fred Eab bin pas di fred we dɛn bin de fred Fate. Bɔt jɔs lɛk aw i bin de trade, di pegan harpooneer dɛn nɔ bin rili kɔle dɛn; ɔ if dɛn kɔle am, na jɔs wit wan patikyula magnɛt we dɛn shot insay dɛn fayn at frɔm Eab in at we nɔ de chenj.

Fɔ wan ples di ol man bin de waka na di dɛk insay rɔlin rεvεri. Bɔt we i chans fɔ slip wit in ayvri il, i si di krɔs kɔpa sayt-tyub dɛn na di kwata we i bin gɛt di de bifo de rɔn go na di dɛk.

“Yu po, prawd we de wach ɛvin ɛn payɔt fɔ di san! yestede a bin brok yu, ɛn tide di kɔmpas dɛn bin fɔ dɔn pwɛl mi. So, so, so. Bɔt Eab na masta oba di lɛvul lodston yet. Mista Stabɔk—na lans we nɔ gɛt pol; wan top-maul, ɛn di smɔl wan pan di nidul dɛn we di wan we de mek sel kin yuz. Kwik!"

Aksesɔri, sɔntɛm, to di impuls we bin de dikte di tin we i bin de kam fɔ du naw, na bin sɔm prudential motives, we in objek go dɔn bi fɔ rivaiv di spirit dɛn fɔ in kru bay wan strok fɔ in subtil skil, insay wan tin we so wɔndaful lɛk dat di kɔmpas dɛn we dɛn dɔn invayt. Apat frɔm dat, di ol man bin no gud gud wan se fɔ stiar wit nidul dɛn we dɛn transpɔynt, pan ɔl we i nɔ izi fɔ du, nɔto sɔntin we pipul dɛn we de wok na di bot we biliv lay lay tin dɛn fɔ pas, we nɔ gɛt sɔm shek shek ɛn bad bad tin dɛn.

I se, “Man dɛn,” i de tɔn pan di kru ɔltɛm, as di patna de gi am di tin dɛn we i bin dɔn aks fɔ, “mi man dɛn, di tɛnda tɔn ol Eab in nidul dɛn; bɔt Eab kin yuz dis smɔl stik fɔ mek in yon, we go sho se na tru lɛk ɛni ɔda wan.”

Di pipul dɛn we de wok na di bot bin de chenj di we aw dɛn bin de luk dɛn we dɛn bin de mek lɛk se dɛn na slev, as dɛn bin de tɔk dis; ɛn wit yay dɛn we bin de mek dɛn gladi, dɛn bin de wet fɔ ɛnitin we majik go fala. Bɔt Starbuck bin luk ɔdasay.

Wit wan blow frɔm di top-maul Eab knock off di stɛl ed fɔ di lans, ɛn afta dat i gi di mate di lɔng ayɛn stik we lɛf, tɛl am fɔ ol am stret, witout i tɔch di dɛk. Dɔn, wit di maul, afta i dɔn bit di ɔp ɛnd pan dis ayɛn stik bɔku tɛm, i put di nidul we dɔn blunt ɛndways pan am, ɛn i nɔ bin hama am bɛtɛ so dat, bɔku tɛm, di man we i de mared stil ol di stik lɛk aw i bin de bifo. Dɔn fɔ go tru sɔm smɔl smɔl strenj muvmɛnt dɛn wit am—ilɛksɛf i impɔtant fɔ mek di stɛl magnetiz, ɔ jɔs fɔ mek di wan dɛn we de wok na di bot fred mɔ, i nɔ shɔ—i kɔl fɔ linin trɛd; ɛn muf go na di binnakul, i slip di tu nidul dɛn we dɛn dɔn tɔn bak de, ɛn stɔp di sel-nidul ɔrizɔntal wan bay in midul, oba wan pan di kɔmpas-kad dɛn. Fɔs, di stɛl bin de go rawnd ɛn rawnd, i bin de shek shek ɛn shek shek na di tu ɛnd dɛn; bɔt las las i setul na in ples, we Eab, we bin dɔn de wach gud gud wan fɔ dis rizɔlt, step bak klia wan frɔm di binnakul, ɛn pɔynt in an we i strɛch to am, ala se,—“Una luk fɔ unasɛf, if Eab nɔto so masta fɔ di lɛvɛl lodston! Di san de na Is, ɛn da kɔmpas de swɛ am!”

Dɛn bin de luk insay wan afta di ɔda wan, bikɔs natin nɔ bin ebul fɔ mek pipul dɛn we nɔ no natin lɛk dɛn yon, pas dɛn yon yay, ɛn dɛn bin de slip wan afta di ɔda wan.

Insay in faya yay dɛn we de provok ɛn win, yu si Eab insay ɔl in prawd we de kil am.





CHAPTER 125. Di Lɔg ɛn Layn.

Pan ɔl we naw di fated Pequod bin dɔn so lɔng fɔ flot dis voyage, di log ɛn layn nɔ bin rili de yuz. Bikɔs dɛn kin abop pan ɔda we dɛn fɔ no usay di bot de, sɔm biznɛsman dɛn, ɛn bɔku pipul dɛn we de rayd wɛl, mɔ we dɛn de go na di bot, nɔ kin tek tɛm fɔ es di tik atɔl; pan ɔl we di sem tɛm, ɛn bɔku tɛm mɔ fɔ fɔm pas ɛni ɔda tin, ɔltɛm dɛn kin put dɔŋ pan di kɔstɔm slet di kɔs we di ship de stiar, ɛn bak di avɛrej rit we dɛn kin tink se fɔ go bifo ɛvri awa. I bin dɔn bi so wit di Pequod. Di wud ril ɛn angul lɔg we dɛn bin ataya bin de hang, fɔ lɔng tɛm we dɛn nɔ bin tɔch am, jɔs ɔnda di relin fɔ di aftabulwɔk dɛn. Ren ɛn sprɛy bin dɔn mek i nɔ fayn; san ɛn briz bin dɔn mek i wɔs; ɔl di ɛlimɛnt dɛn bin dɔn jɔyn fɔ rɔtin wan tin we bin de hang so idɔl. Bɔt we i nɔ pe atɛnshɔn to ɔl dis, in maynd bin tek Eab, as i apin fɔ glan pan di riɛl, nɔto bɔku awa afta di magnet scene, ɛn i mɛmba aw in kwata nɔ bin de igen, ɛn mɛmba in frantik ɔth bɔt di lɛvɛl lɔg ɛn layn. Di ship bin de go insay di wata we de go dɔŋ; astern di billows bin de rol insay rɔsh.

“Fɔ go bifo, de! Hev di lod!”

Tu pipul dɛn we de wok na di si bin kam. Di Tahitian we gɛt golden-hued ɛn di grizly Manxman. “Tek di riɛl, wan pan una, a go hev.”

Dɛn go to di ekstrim stern, na di ship in lee sayd, usay di dɛk, wit di oblique ɛnaji we di briz de gi, bin dɔn lɛf smɔl naw fɔ dip insay di krim, sayd-lɔng-rɔsh si.

Di Manxman tek di ril, ɛn ol am ay ɔp, bay di projɛkt handel-ɛnd dɛn na di spindle, rawnd we di spɔl ɔf layn bin de rɔn, so tinap wit di angul lɔg we hang dɔŋ, te Eab go bifo to am.

Eab bin tinap bifo am, ɛn i bin de opin layt wan lɛk tati ɔ fɔti tɔn fɔ mek wan fɔs an-kɔyl fɔ tos oba di wata, we di ol Manksman, we bin de luk am ɛn di layn gud gud wan, mek bold fɔ tɔk.

“Sa, a nɔ trɔst am; dis layn luk fa fawe, lɔng ɔt ɛn wet dɔn pwɛl am.”

“‘Twil hold, ol jentlman. Lɔng ɔt ɛn wet, dɛn dɔn pwɛl yu? I tan lɛk se yu de ol. Ɔ, sɔntɛm, layf de ol yu; nɔto yu.”

“A de ol di spɔl, sa. Bɔt jɔs lɛk aw mi kapten se. Wit dis grey hairs of mai ‘tis not worth while disputing, ‘specially wit a superior, who’ll ne’er confess.”

“Wetin na dat? Naw, wan patch profesa de na Kwin Nature in granit-founded College; bɔt methinks se i tu subservient. Usay dɛn bɔn yu?”

“Na di smɔl Ayland ɔf Man we gɛt rɔk, sa.”

"Wɛl dɔn! Yu don hit di wold by dat.”

“A nɔ no, sa, bɔt na de dɛn bɔn mi.”

“Na di Ayland ɔf Man, hey? Wɛl, di ɔda we, i fayn. Na wan man we kɔmɔt na Man; man we dɛn bɔn insay wan tɛm indipɛndɛnt Man, ɛn naw i nɔ gɛt pɔsin frɔm Man; we dɛn de sok insay—wetin? Up wit di riel! Di day, blaynd wɔl de but ɔl di ed dɛn we de aks kwɛstyɔn las las. Up wit am! So."

Di tik bin es. Di kɔyl dɛn we nɔ bin tay bin stret kwik kwik wan insay wan lɔng layn we de drɛg na di bak, dɔn, wantɛm wantɛm, di ril bigin fɔ rɔn. Na in bak, we di bilɔ dɛn we de rɔl bin de jɔk am ɔp ɛn dɔŋ, di we aw di tik nɔ bin de pul am, bin mek di ol rilman stɛp strenj wan.

“Hold tranga wan!”

Snap! di layn we bin strɛch pasmak bin sag dɔŋ insay wan lɔng fes; di tugging log bin dɔn go.

“A de krɔs di kwadrant, di tɛnda de tɔn di nidul dɛn, ɛn naw di mad si de pat di lɔg-layn. Bɔt Eab kin mek ɔltin fayn. Haul in ya, Tahitian; riɛl ɔp, Manxman. Una luk, mek di kapɛnta mek ɔda tik, ɛn mek di layn fayn. Luk fɔ du am.”

“Na de i de go naw; to am natin nɔ apin; bɔt to mi, i tan lɛk se di skewa de lus kɔmɔt na di midul pat na di wɔl. Haul in, haul in, Tahitian! Dɛn layn ya de rɔn ol, ɛn de rɔn kɔmɔt: kam insay brok, ɛn drɛg sloslo. Ha, Pip, yu? kam fɔ ɛp; eh, Pip?”

“Pip? udat de kɔl una Pip? Pip bin jomp frɔm di wɛl-bɔt. Pip de mis. Lɛ wi si naw if una nɔ fishin am ɔp ya, fishaman. I de drɛg tranga wan; A guess se i de hol on. Jɛk am, Tahiti! Jerk am ɔf; wi haul in no cowards ya. Ho! na de in an jɔs de brok wata. Wan hatchet! wan hatchet! kɔt am—wi nɔ de pul ɛni kɔvayd ya. Kpɛntin Eab! sa, sa, sa! na Pip de ya, we de tray fɔ go insay di bot bak.”

“Pis, yu krayzi loon,” di Manxman kray, ɛn ol am na in an. “Away frɔm di kwata-dɛk!”

Eab bin de mɔna as i de go bifo se: “Di big big pɔsin we nɔ gɛt sɛns kin ɛva kɔl di smɔl wan.” “An dɛn kɔmɔt frɔm da oli we de! Usay yu se yu Pip bin de, bɔy?

“Astern de, sa, astern! Lo! lo!”

“Ɛn udat yu na, bɔy pikin? A nɔ de si mi riflɛkshɔn na di vakɛnt pupil dɛn na yu yay. Oh Gɔd! dat man fɔ bi tin fɔ sol dɛn we nɔ de day fɔ siev tru! Udat yu na, bɔy?”

“Bɛl-bɔy, sa; ship in-kray; ding, dong, ding! Pip we yu de du! Pip we yu de du! Pip we yu de du! Wan ɔndrɛd paund kle riwɔd fɔ Pip; fayv fit ay—i luk lɛk se i de fred—we dɛn no kwik kwik wan bay dat! Ding, dong, ding! Udat dɔn si Pip di kɔva?”

“No at nɔ go ebul fɔ de ɔp di sno-layn. O, una ɛvin dɛn we dɔn frɔz! luk dɔŋ ya. Una bɔn dis pikin we nɔ gɛt laki, ɛn una dɔn lɛf am, una we de mek fridɔm. Na ya, bɔy; Eab in kebin go bi Pip in os frɔm naw, we Eab de liv. Yu tɔch mi inmost sɛnt, bɔy; yu tay to mi wit kɔd dɛn we dɛn mek wit mi at-string. Kam, lɛ wi dɔŋ.”

“Wetin na dis? na velvet shak-skin ya,” i de luk Eab in an gud gud wan, ɛn fil am. “Ah, naw, bin gɛt po Pip bɔt i bin fil so kayn tin lɛk dis, sɔntɛm i nɔ bin dɔn lɔs! Dis tan lɛk to mi, sa, lɛk man-rop; sɔntin we wik sol dɛn kin ol. Oh, sa, mek ol Pɛt naw kam ɛn rivet dɛn tu an ya togɛda; di blak wan wit di wayt wan, bikɔs a nɔ go lɛf dis fɔ go.”

“O, bɔy, a nɔ go du yu, pas nɔmɔ a fɔ drɛg yu to bad bad tin dɛn we de mek yu fred pas aw dɛn de ya. So, kam na mi kebin. Lo! una we biliv pan gɔd ɔl gudnɛs, ɛn pan mɔtalman ɔl sik, lo una! si di gɔd dɛn we no ɔltin we nɔ no bɔt mɔtalman we de sɔfa; ɛn man, pan ɔl we i nɔ gɛt sɛns, ɛn i nɔ no wetin i de du, bɔt i ful-ɔp wit di swit swit tin dɛn we gɛt fɔ du wit lɔv ɛn tɛnki. Kam! A kin fil prawd fɔ lid yu wit yu blak an, pas we a ol wan Emparɔ in yon!”

CHAPTER 126. Di Layf-Buoy.

I de stiar naw fɔ go na di sawt-ist bay Eab in lɛvul stɛl, ɛn na Eab in lɛvul lɔg ɛn layn nɔmɔ de sho aw i go go bifo; di Pequod bin ol pan in rod we de go na di Ikweta. Fɔ mek so lɔng pasej tru dɛn kayn wata dɛn de we nɔ kin bɔku, we nɔ de tɔk bɔt no ship, ɛn ere lɔng, saydway we dɛn de push wit tred briz we nɔ de chenj, oba wef dɛn monotonously mild; ɔl dɛn tin ya bin tan lɛk di strenj kol at tin dɛn we bin de bifo sɔm ritɔs ɛn desperate scene.

Las las, we di ship kam nia di ɔdasay, lɛk se, na di Ikwetorial fishin-grɔn, ɛn insay di dip daknɛs we kin go bifo do klin, i bin de go nia wan grup we gɛt ayland dɛn we gɛt ston dɛn; di wach—we Flask bin de bifo da tɛm de—bin sɔprayz wit wan kray we so kray we bin de kray we na wayl ɛn we nɔ de na di wɔl—lɛk aw di gɔst dɛn fɔ ɔl di Inosɛnt dɛn we Ɛrɔd bin kil bin de kray af-af—dat wan ɛn ɔl, dɛn bigin frɔm dɛn tin dɛn we dɛn bin de tink bɔt, ɛn fɔ sɔm pipul dɛn fɔ lɔng tɛm mɔnt dɛn bin tinap, ɔ sidɔm, ɔ ledɔm ɔl transfixedly lisin, lɛk di Roman slev we dɛn kɔt, we da wayl kray de stil de insay di yɛri. Di Kristian ɔ sivilizayt pat pan di kru bin se na mɛrmɛid, ɛn i bin de shek shek; bɔt di pegan hapun dɛn nɔ bin fred. Bɔt stil di grey Manxman—we na di wan we dɔn ol pas ɔl di ɔda wan dɛn we de wok na di si—bin tɔk se di wayl sawnd dɛn we dɛn kin yɛri we kin mek pɔsin gladi, na di vɔys dɛn we man dɛn we jɔs drawn na di si kin mek.

Dɔŋ insay in hamak, Eab nɔ yɛri bɔt dis te grey dok, we i kam na di dɛk; afta dat, Flask bin tɛl am bak, nɔto we nɔ bin de wit hinted dak minin dɛn. I laf hol, ɛn na so i ɛksplen di wɔndaful tin.

Dɛn ayland dɛn we gɛt rɔk dɛn we di ship bin dɔn pas na bin ples usay bɔku bɔku sil dɛn bin de go, ɛn sɔm yɔŋ sil dɛn we bin dɔn lɔs dɛn dɛm, ɔ sɔm dɛm we bin dɔn lɔs dɛn pikin dɛn, go mɔs dɔn rayz nia di ship ɛn kip kɔmpin wit am, de kray ɛn kray wit dɛn mɔtalman kayn kray. Bɔt dis bin jɔs afɛkt sɔm pan dɛn mɔ, bikɔs bɔku pan di wan dɛn we de wok na di si kin valyu wan rili lay lay filin bɔt sil, we nɔ kin jɔs kɔmɔt frɔm dɛn spɛshal tɔyn dɛn we dɛn gɛt prɔblɛm, bɔt i kin kɔmɔt bak frɔm di we aw mɔtalman de luk dɛn rawnd ed ɛn dɛn fes we gɛt sɛmi-intɛligent, we dɛn kin si wit luk uprising frɔm di wata nia. Insay di si, ɔnda sɔm tin dɛn, dɛn dɔn mistek pas wan tɛm se sil na man.

Bɔt di bodings fɔ di kru bin destin fɔ gɛt wan mɔs plausibul kɔnfɔmeshɔn insay di fate fɔ wan pan dɛn nɔmba da mɔnin de. We di san kɔmɔt, dis man kɔmɔt na in hamak ɛn go na in mast-ed we de bifo; ɛn if na dat i nɔ bin dɔn wek af-af yet frɔm in slip (bikɔs sɔntɛnde di wan dɛn we de wok na di bot kin go ɔp we dɛn de chenj chenj), if na so i bin de wit di man, dɛn nɔ ebul fɔ tɛl am naw; bɔt, ilɛksɛf i bi, i nɔ bin dɔn te na in sidɔm ples, we dɛn yɛri wan kray—kray ɛn rɔsh—ɛn we dɛn luk ɔp, dɛn si wan phantom we de fɔdɔm na di ays; ɛn luk dɔŋ, wan smɔl tos hip we gɛt wayt bɔbul dɛn we de na di blu si.

Dɛn bin de drɔp di layf-buoy—na wan lɔng slɛnd bɔtul—frɔm di stern, usay i bin de hang ɔltɛm fɔ obe wan kɔni spring; bɔt no an nɔ bin grap fɔ ol am, ɛn we di san dɔn de bit dis bɔtul fɔ lɔng tɛm, i bin dɔn shrink, so i ful-ɔp smɔl smɔl, ɛn da dray wud de ful-ɔp bak na in ɛvri pore; ɛn di bɔks we dɛn tay wit ayɛn we gɛt stɔd bin fala di man we de wok na di bot fɔ go dɔŋ, lɛk se i de gi am in pilo, pan ɔl we i bin kol bɔt i at.

Ɛn na so di fɔs man na di Pequod we bin de mawnt di mast fɔ luk fɔ di Wait Wɛl, na di Wait Wɛl in yon pekul grɔn; dɛn bin swɛla da man de na di dip dip ples. Bɔt sɔntɛm na smɔl pipul dɛn nɔmɔ bin tink bɔt dat da tɛm de. Fɔ tru, insay sɔm kayn we, dɛn nɔ bin fil bad fɔ dis tin we apin, at ɔl as sɔntin we de sho se dɛn gɛt sɔntin fɔ du wit am; bikɔs dɛn nɔ bin de si am as sɔntin we de sho se bad tin go apin tumara bambay, bɔt dɛn bin de si am as bad tin we dɛn bin dɔn tɔk se go apin. Dɛn bin deklare se naw dɛn dɔn no di rizin fɔ dɛn wayl kray we dɛn bin yɛri di nɛt bifo. Bɔt bak di ol Manxman se nɔ.

Di layf-buoy we bin dɔn lɔs bin gɛt fɔ chenj naw; Dɛn bin dayrɛkt Starbuck fɔ si to am; bɔt as dɛn nɔ bin ebul fɔ fɛn ɛni bɔtul we gɛt bɛtɛ layt, ɛn lɛk di fiva we dɛn bin want fɔ du fɔ wetin bin tan lɛk se di prɔblɛm we de kam nia di voyage, ɔl di an dɛn nɔ bin gɛt peshɛnt fɔ ɛni wok we dɛn de du pas wetin gɛt fɔ du wit in las ɛnd, ilɛk wetin dat go bi ; so, dɛn bin de go lɛf di ship in stɛp we dɛn nɔ gɛt bɔy, we bay sɔm strenj sayn dɛn ɛn inuendo dɛn Kwik bin hint wan hint bɔt in kɔfin.

“Na layf-buoy fɔ kɔfin!” Starbuck kray, i bigin.

Stubb bin se: “Rather queer, dat, a fɔ se.”

Flask bin tɔk se: “I go mek gud wan, di kapɛnta we de ya kin arenj am izi wan.”

“Bring am ɔp; no ɔda tin nɔ de fɔ am,” na so Starbuck tɔk, afta we i dɔn stɔp wit pwɛl at. “Rig am, kapɛnta; nɔ luk mi so—di kɔfin, a min. Yu de yɛri mi? Rig am.”

“Ɛn a go nel di lid dɔŋ, sa?” we i de muv in an lɛk aw i de muv hama.

“Aye.”

“Ɛn a go kɔl di sim dɛn, sa?” we i de muv in an lɛk aw i de muv kɔl-ayɔn.

“Aye.”

“Ɛn a go pe ova di sem wit pitch, sa?” we i de muv in an lɛk se i de muv pitch-pɔt.

"Go! wetin possess yu to dis? Mek layf-bɔy wit di kɔfin, ɛn nɔ mek igen.—Mista. Stubb, Mista Flask, kam bifo wit mi.”

“I de go ɔf wit wan huff. Di wan ol tin we i ebul fɔ bia; na di pat dɛn we i de baul. Naw a nɔ lɛk dis. A mek wan leg fɔ Kpɛntin Eab, ɛn i wɛr am lɛk jentlman; ba a mek wan bandboks fo Queequeg, en i no go put in ed insay. Ɔl mi pen dɛn fɔ go fɔ natin wit da kɔfin de? Ɛn naw dɛn dɔn ɔda mi fɔ mek layf-buoy wit am. I tan lɛk se yu de tɔn ol kot; go briŋ di bɔdi na di ɔda say naw. A nɔ lɛk dis kayn biznɛs we dɛn kin kɔb—a nɔ lɛk am atɔl; i nɔ gɛt rɛspɛkt; nɔto mi ples. Mek tinkers’ brats du tinkerings; wi na dɛn bɛtɛ pipul dɛn. A lɛk fɔ tek ɛni wan na mi an pas klin, vajin, fayn ɛn skwea mɛtemat wok dɛn, sɔntin we kin bigin ɔltɛm na di biginin, ɛn we kin de na di midul we i de midul, ɛn kin dɔn we i dɔn; nɔto kɔblɛs wok, dat de na wan ɛnd na di midul, ɛn na di biginin na di ɛnd. Na di ol uman in trik fɔ de gi kɔblin wok. Lɔd! wetin ɔl ol uman dɛn gɛt fɔ tink bɔt tin dɛn. A sabi wan ol uman we ol siksti fayv ia we rɔnawe wit wan yɔŋ tinka we gɛt bold ed wan tɛm. Ɛn na di rizin we mek a nɔ go ɛva wok fɔ ol uman dɛn we dɛn man dɔn day we dɛn nɔ gɛt wanwɔd na di sho, we a bin de kip mi wok-shɔp na di Vayn gadin; dɛn kin dɔn tek am na dɛn ol ed we de wansay fɔ rɔn go wit mi. Bɔt heigh-ho! no kap nɔ de na si bɔt sno-kap de. Mek a si. Nel dɔŋ di lid; kol di sim dɛn; pe ova di sem wit pitch; batten dɛn dɔŋ tayt, ɛn hang am wit di snap-spring oba di ship in stɛp. Yu tink se dɛn bin dɔn ɛva du dɛn kayn tin ya bifo wit kɔfin? Sɔm ol kapɛnta dɛn we biliv lay lay tin dɛn, naw, dɛn go tay dɛn na di rig, bifo dɛn du di wok. Bɔt dɛn mek mi wit knotty Aroostook hemlock; A nɔ de budge. Krɔp am wit kɔfin! Sel fɔ go wit wan grev-yad tre! Bɔt nɔ ɛva tink bɔt dat. Wi we de wok na bush de mek bridal-bedstead ɛn kad-tebul, ɛn wi de mek kɔfin ɛn bɔdi we dɛn kin yuz fɔ kil pipul dɛn. Wi de wok bay di mɔnt, ɔ bay di wok, ɔ bay di prɔfit; nɔto fɔ mek wi aks wetin mek ɛn wetin mek wi wok, pas nɔmɔ i tu kɔnfyus fɔ kɔb, ɛn afta dat wi kin stash am if wi ebul. Hem! A go du di wok, naw, wit sɔri-at. A go mek a—lɛ wi si—ɔmɔs pipul dɛn de na di ship in kɔmni, dɛn dɔn tɛl dɛn ɔl? Bɔt a dɔn fɔgɛt. Eni we, a go get mi tati seperet, Turk ed layf-layn, each tri fit long hang ol roun to di kofin. Dɔn, if di bot go dɔŋ, na tati pipul dɛn we gɛt layf go de we dɛn ɔl de fɛt fɔ wan kɔfin, we dɛn nɔ kin si bɔku tɛm ɔnda di san! Kam hama, kol-ayɔn, pitch-pɔt, ɛn marling-spayk! Mek wi du am.”





CHAPTER 127. Di Dɛk.

Di kɔfin bin ledɔm pan tu layn-tɔb dɛn, bitwin di vays-bɛnch ɛn di opin hatchway; di Kapɛnta de kɔl in sim dɛn; di string we dɛn twist oakum de kɔmɔt smɔl smɔl frɔm wan big rol pan am we dɛn put insay in frɔk in bɔdi.—Eab kɔmɔt sloslo frɔm di kabin-gangway, ɛn yɛri Pip de fala am.

“Bak, bɔbɔ; A go de wit una bak naw. I de go! Nɔto dis an de fala mi kɔmɛnt mɔ genially pas da bɔbɔ de.—Middle aisle of a church! Wetin de ya?”

“Laif-buoy, sa. Mista Starbuck in ɔda dɛn. Oh, luk, sa! Una tek tɛm wit di hatchway!”

“Tɛnki, man. Yu kɔfin de nia di vault.”

"Sa? Di hatchway? O! So i de du, sa, na so i de du.”

“Nɔto yu na di wan we mek leg? Luk, nɔto yu shɔp dis stɔp kɔmɔt?”

“A biliv se i bin du am, sa; di ferrule tinap, sa?”

“Wɛl inof. Bɔt nɔto yu de du di ɔndatak?”

“Aye, sa; A patch dis tin ya as kɔfin fɔ Kwik; bɔt dɛn dɔn sɛt mi naw fɔ tɔn am to ɔda tin.”

“Dɔn tɛl mi; yu nɔto arrant, ɔl-grasping, intermeddling, monopolising, heathenish ol scamp, fɔ bi wan de mek leg, ɛn di nɛks de kɔfin fɔ klap dɛn insay, ɛn yet bak layf-buoys kɔmɔt na dɛn sem kɔfin dɛn de? Yu nɔ gɛt prinsipul jɔs lɛk di gɔd dɛn, ɛn yu na pɔsin we de du ɔltin.”

“Bɔt a nɔ min ɛnitin, sa. A de du wetin a de du.”

“Di gɔd dɛn bak. Hark ye, yu no eva sing wok abaut kofin? Dɛn se di Taytan dɛn bin de hum snatch we dɛn de chip di krata dɛn fɔ mek volkeno dɛn; ɛn di pɔsin we de dig grev na di ple de siŋ, i ol spayd in an. Yu nɔ de ɛva du dat?”

“Sing, sa? A kin siŋ? Oh, a indiferent inof, sa, fo dat; bɔt di rizin we mek di grev-dig mek myuzik go mɔs bi bikɔs nɔbɔdi nɔ bin de na in spayd, sa. Bɔt di kɔl malet ful-ɔp wit am. Hark to am.”

“Aye, ɛn dat na bikɔs di lid de na sawnd-bɔd; ɛn wetin insay ɔltin we de mek di sawnd-bɔd na dis—natin nɔ de ɔnda. Ɛn pan ɔl dat, kɔfin we gɛt bɔdi insay de ring lɛk di sem, Kapɛntɔ. Yu dɔn ɛva ɛp fɔ kɛr bɛlɛ, ɛn yɛri di kɔfin nak na di chɔch yad get, de go insay?

“Fɛt, sa, a dɔn——”

"Fet? Wetin na dat?”

“Wetin mek, fet, sa, na jɔs wan kayn we we tan lɛk se i de ala—na dat nɔmɔ, sa.”

“Um, um, yu de tɔk bɔt am; go bifo.”

“A bin de kam se, sa, se——”

“Yu na silk-wɔm? Yu de spin yu yon shroud kɔmɔt pan yusɛf? Luk yu bɔdi! Dispatch fɔ di pɔsin! ɛn pul dɛn trap dɛn ya kɔmɔt na di yay.”

“I kin go biɛn. Dat na bin wantɛm wantɛm, naw; bɔt skwal kin kam wantɛm wantɛm na hot latitud. a don yehri se di Isle of Albemarle, wan pan di Gallipagos, na di Ikweta kot am rait na di midul. I tan lɛk to mi sɔm kayn Ikweta kɔt yon ol man, tu, rayt na in midul. I de ɔnda di Layn ɔltɛm—faya wam, a de tɛl una! I de luk dis we—kam, oakum; kwik. Na ya wi de go bak. Dis wud malet na di kɔk, ɛn mi na di prɔfɛsɔ fɔ myuzik glas—tap, tap!”

“Na wan sight de! Na sawnd de! Di grey ed wudpɛk we de tap di ol tik! Blaynd ɛn mumu kin rili jɛlɔs naw. Si! dat tin de rɛst pan tu layn-tɔb, we ful-ɔp wit to-layn. Wan most malicious wag, dat felo. Rat-tat na! So man in sɛkɔn dɛn tik! O! aw ɔl di tin dɛn we dɛn de yuz nɔ impɔtant! Us tin dɛn we rial de, bɔt tin dɛn we pɔsin nɔ go ebul fɔ tink bɔt? Na naw na di rili dreaded simbul fɔ grim day, bay wan mere hap, mek di ɛksprɛsiv sayn fɔ di ɛp ɛn op fɔ mɔs layf we de pan denja. Wan layf-buoy fɔ wan kɔfin! Yu tink se i de go fa fawe? Yu tink se i go bi se insay sɔm spiritual we dɛn, di kɔfin na, afta ɔl, bɔt na sɔntin we de protɛkt pɔsin we nɔ de day! A go tink bɔt dat. Bɔt nɔto so. So fa a dɔn go na di dak say na di wɔl, dat in ɔda say, di tiori brayt wan, tan lɛk bɔt twilayt we nɔ shɔ. Yu nɔ tink se yu nɔ go ɛva du, Kapɛntɔ, wit da swɛ sawnd de? A de go dɔŋ ya; mek a nɔ si da tin de ya we a kam bak. Naw, den, Pip, wi go tok dis ova; A de sok mɔs wɔndaful filɔsofi dɛn frɔm yu! Sɔm kɔnduit dɛn we yu nɔ no bɔt frɔm di wɔl dɛn we yu nɔ no, go ɛmti insay yu!”





CHAPTER 128. Di Pequod Mit Di Rechɛl.

Di nɛks de, dɛn bin tɔk bɔt wan big ship we nem Rechɛl, we bin de go dɔŋ di Pɛkud, ɛn ɔl in spay dɛn bin tik tik wan wit man dɛn. Da tɛm de di Pequod bin de mek gud spid tru di wata; bɔt as di strenja we gɛt brayt wing we di briz de blo shot nia am, di sel dɛn we de bost ɔl fɔdɔm togɛda lɛk blank blad dɛn we de bɔs, ɛn ɔl layf rɔnawe kɔmɔt na di bot we dɛn dɔn bit.

“Bad nyus; i de briŋ bad nyus,” na so di ol Manxman bin de mɔna. Bɔt bifo in kɔmanda, we, wit trɔmpɛt to mɔt, tinap insay in bot; ere i kin op se ays blɔk, dɛn yɛri Eab in vɔys.

“Yu dɔn si di Wait Wɛl?”

“Aye, yestede. Una si wan wɛl-bot de drɛf?”

We Eab bin de stɔp in gladi at, i bin ansa dis kwɛstyɔn we i nɔ bin de tink se i go bi; ɛn afta dat i bin fɔ dɔn fain bɔd di strenja, we dɛn si di strenja kapten insɛf, we dɔn stɔp in bot in rod, de kam dɔŋ in sayd. Sɔm keen pul dɛn, ɛn i nɔ tu te, in bot-huk bin klin di Pequod in men-chen dɛn, ɛn i jomp go na di dɛk. Wantɛm wantɛm, Eab bin no am fɔ wan Nantucketer we i sabi. Bɔt dɛn nɔ bin chenj ɛni fɔmal salut.

“Usay i bin de?—nɔto kil am!—nɔto kil am!” Eab bin kray, i bin de go bifo klos wan. “Aw i bin bi?”

I bin tan lɛk se let smɔl na di aftanun fɔ di de bifo, we tri pan di strenja in bot dɛn bin de ɛnjɔy wit wan grup we gɛt wɛl, we bin dɔn kɛr dɛn go lɛk 4 ɔ 5 mayl so frɔm di ship; ɛn we dɛn bin stil de rɔnata kwik kwik wan fɔ go na di say we di briz de blo, di wayt hump ɛn di ed fɔ Mobi Dik bin dɔn kɔmɔt na di wata wantɛm wantɛm, i nɔ bin tu fa fɔ go na di say we di briz de blo; we, dɛn bin dɔn put di nɔmba 4 bot we gɛt rig—we na wan we dɛn dɔn kip—dat wantɛm wantɛm fɔ rɔnata am. Afta we dɛn dɔn tek wan sel bifo di briz, i bin tan lɛk se dis bot we mek 4—we gɛt di kil we swit pas ɔl—i bin ebul fɔ tay am—at ɔl, ɛn di man we bin de na di mast-ed bin ebul fɔ tɛl ɛnitin bɔt am. We i de fa, i si di bot we gɛt dɔti dɔt we dɔn stɔp; ɛn afta dat, wan wayt wata we de bɔbul de shayn kwik kwik wan; ɛn afta dat, natin nɔ de igen; na de dɛn kam fɔ no se di wɛl we dɛn bit fɔ dɔn rɔnawe fɔ lɔng tɛm wit di wan dɛn we de rɔnata am, jɔs lɛk aw i kin apin bɔku tɛm. Sɔm fred bin de, bɔt no pɔsitiv alam, as yet. Di rikol signal dɛn bin de na di rig; daknɛs bin kam; ɛn fos am fɔ tek in tri bot dɛn we de fa fawe we di briz de blo—we i de go fɔ fɛn di nɔmba 4 wan na di ɔda say—nɔto jɔs di ship bin nid fɔ lɛf da bot de fɔ in fate te nia midulnɛt, bɔt, fɔ da tɛm de, . fɔ mek i go fa frɔm am mɔ. Bɔt di ɔda wan dɛn we bin de wok na in bot bin dɔn sef fɔ go insay di bot, i bin krawd ɔl di sel dɛn—stunsel pan stunsail—afta di bot we bin dɔn lɔs; we i de bɔn faya na in tray-pɔt dɛn fɔ mek wan bikɔs; ɛn ɔl ɔda man dɛn we de ɔp na di say we dɛn de luk fɔ. Bɔt pan ɔl we we i bin dɔn sidɔm so so fɔ gɛt di ples we dɛn tink se di wan dɛn we nɔ bin de we dɛn bin si am las tɛm; pan ɔl we i bin stɔp fɔ put in spay bot dɛn dɔŋ fɔ pul ɔlsay rawnd am; ɛn we i nɔ fɛn natin, i bin dɔn rɔn go bak; i stɔp smɔl bak, ɛn put in bot dɛn dɔŋ; ɛn pan ɔl we i bin kɔntinyu fɔ du so te di de shayn; yet dɛn nɔ bin dɔn si di smɔl smɔl tin bɔt di kil we nɔ bin de.

Di stori we dɛn tɔk, di strenja Kpɛtɛn go bifo wantɛm wantɛm fɔ sho wetin i want we i de go insay di Pequod. I bin want mek da ship de jɔyn wit in yon fɔ luk fɔ am; bay we dɛn de sel oba di si we de lɛk 4 ɔ 5 mayl frɔm dɛnsɛf, pan layn dɛn we paralel, ɛn so i de swip tu ɔrayzin, lɛk se.

Stubb wispa to Flask se: “A go bet sɔntin naw, se sɔmbɔdi we bin de insay da bot we bin dɔn mis bin wɛr da Kpɛntin in bɛst kot de; mayhap, in wach—i so swɛ wɔri fɔ gɛt am bak. Udat dɔn ɛva yɛri bɔt tu wɛl-ship dɛn we de wɔship Gɔd we bin de krɔs afta wan wɛl-bot we bin dɔn lɔs we di sizin we dɛn bin de kech wɛl bin de ayt? Si, Flask, onli si aw i luk pale—pale in di very buttons of in ai—luk—no bi di kot—i go mɔs bi se na di—”

“Mi bɔy, mi yon bɔbɔ de wit dɛn. And now that at the proper time and place, after so long and wide a preliminary cruise, Ahab,—all other whaling waters swept—seemed to have chased his foe into an ocean-fold, to slay him the more securely there; now, that he found himself hard by the very latitude and longitude where his tormenting wound had been inflicted; now that a vessel had been spoken which on the very day preceding had actually encountered Moby Dick;—and now that all his successive meetings with various ships contrastingly concurred to show the demoniac indifference with which the white whale tore his hunters, whether sinning or sinned against; now it was that there lurked a something in the old man's eyes, which it was hardly sufferable for feeble souls to see. As the unsetting polar star, which through the livelong, arctic, six months' night sustains its piercing, steady, central gaze; so Ahab's purpose now fixedly gleamed down upon the constant midnight of the gloomy crew. It domineered above them so, that all their bodings, doubts, misgivings, fears, were fain to hide beneath their souls, and not sprout forth a single spear or leaf.

In this foreshadowing interval too, all humor, forced or natural, vanished. Stubb no more strove to raise a smile; Starbuck no more strove to check one. Alike, joy and sorrow, hope and fear, seemed ground to finest dust, and powdered, for the time, in the clamped mortar of Ahab's iron soul. Like machines, they dumbly moved about the deck, ever conscious that the old man's despot eye was on them.

But did you deeply scan him in his more secret confidential hours; when he thought no glance but one was on him; then you would have seen that even as Ahab's eyes so awed the crew's, the inscrutable Parsee's glance awed his; or somehow, at least, in some wild way, at times affected it. Such an added, gliding strangeness began to invest the thin Fedallah now; such ceaseless shudderings shook him; that the men looked dubious at him; half uncertain, as it seemed, whether indeed he were a mortal substance, or else a tremulous shadow cast upon the deck by some unseen being's body. And that shadow was always hovering there. For not by night, even, had Fedallah ever certainly been known to slumber, or go below. He would stand still for hours: but never sat or leaned; his wan but wondrous eyes did plainly say—We two watchmen never rest.

Nor, at any time, by night or day could the mariners now step upon the deck, unless Ahab was before them; either standing in his pivot-hole, or exactly pacing the planks between two undeviating limits,—the main-mast and the mizen; or else they saw him standing in the cabin-scuttle,—his living foot advanced upon the deck, as if to step; his hat slouched heavily over his eyes; so that however motionless he stood, however the days and nights were added on, that he had not swung in his hammock; yet hidden beneath that slouching hat, they could never tell unerringly whether, for all this, his eyes were really closed at times; or whether he was still intently scanning them; no matter, though he stood so in the scuttle for a whole hour on the stretch, and the unheeded night-damp gathered in beads of dew upon that stone-carved coat and hat. The clothes that the night had wet, the next day's sunshine dried upon him; and so, day after day, and night after night; he went no more beneath the planks; whatever he wanted from the cabin that thing he sent for.

He ate in the same open air; that is, his two only meals,—breakfast and dinner: supper he never touched; nor reaped his beard; which darkly grew all gnarled, as unearthed roots of trees blown over, which still grow idly on at naked base, though perished in the upper verdure. But though his whole life was now become one watch on deck; and though the Parsee's mystic watch was without intermission as his own; yet these two never seemed to speak—one man to the other—unless at long intervals some passing unmomentous matter made it necessary. Though such a potent spell seemed secretly to join the twain; openly, and to the awe-struck crew, they seemed pole-like asunder. If by day they chanced to speak one word; by night, dumb men were both, so far as concerned the slightest verbal interchange. At times, for longest hours, without a single hail, they stood far parted in the starlight; Ahab in his scuttle, the Parsee by the mainmast; but still fixedly gazing upon each other; as if in the Parsee Ahab saw his forethrown shadow, in Ahab the Parsee his abandoned substance.

And yet, somehow, did Ahab—in his own proper self, as daily, hourly, and every instant, commandingly revealed to his subordinates,—Ahab seemed an independent lord; the Parsee but his slave. Still again both seemed yoked together, and an unseen tyrant driving them; the lean shade siding the solid rib. For be this Parsee what he may, all rib and keel was solid Ahab.

When in working with his hands at some lofty almost isolated place in the rigging, which chances to afford no foothold, the sailor at sea is hoisted up to that spot, and sustained there by the rope; under these circumstances, its fastened end on deck is always given in strict charge to some one man who has the special watch of it. Because in such a wilderness of running rigging, whose various different relations aloft cannot always be infallibly discerned by what is seen of them at the deck; and when the deck-ends of these ropes are being every few minutes cast down from the fastenings, it would be but a natural fatality, if, unprovided with a constant watchman, the hoisted sailor should by some carelessness of the crew be cast adrift and fall all swooping to the sea. So Ahab's proceedings in this matter were not unusual; the only strange thing about them seemed to be, that Starbuck, almost the one only man who had ever ventured to oppose him with anything in the slightest degree approaching to decision—one of those too, whose faithfulness on the look-out he had seemed to doubt somewhat;—it was strange, that this was the very man he should select for his watchman; freely giving his whole life into such an otherwise distrusted person's hands.
