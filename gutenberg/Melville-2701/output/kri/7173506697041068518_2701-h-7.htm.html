CHAPTER 33. Di Spɛksnayda.

Konsain di ofisa dem fo di wel-kraft, dis luk laik se na gud ples laik eni wan fo set daun likl domestik pekuliariti fo ship-bod, we kom from di egzistens of di harpooneer klas of ofisa dem, wan klas we dem no sabi of kos fo eni oda marin pas di wɛl-flit dɛn.

Di big impɔtants we dɛn gi di harpooneer in wok de sho bay di tru tin, se fɔs na di ol Dutch Fishery, tu sɛnti ia ɛn mɔ bifo, di kɔmand fɔ wan wɛl ship nɔ bin de ɔlsay na di pɔsin we dɛn de kɔl naw di kapten, bɔt dɛn bin sheb am bitwin in ɛn wan ɔfisa we dɛn kɔl di Speksnayda. Literally dis wɔd min Fat-Cutter; bɔt aw dɛn bin de yuz am, as tɛm de go, i bin mek i ikwal to Chif Harpuoneer. Dɛn tɛm dɛn de, di kapten in pawa bin jɔs de fɔ di we aw di bot de go ɛn di jenɛral manejmɛnt fɔ di bot; we i bin de oba di dipatmɛnt fɔ fɛn wɛl ɛn ɔl di tin dɛn we bin de mɔna am, di Spɛksnayda ɔ Chif Hapun bin de rul. Insay di British Grinland Fishery, ɔnda di kɔrɔpt taytul Specksioneer, dɛn stil kip dis ol Dutch ɔfisa, bɔt i sɔri fɔ no se dɛn dɔn shɔt di rɛspɛkt we i bin gɛt trade. Naw i rank jɔs as sinia Harpooneer; ɛn as a so, na jɔs wan pan di kapten in mɔ infɛriɔ subalterns. Bɔt pan ɔl dat, as pan di gud kɔndɔkt we di harpooneers gɛt di sakrifays fɔ wan wɛl voyage de dipen mɔ, ɛn bikɔs insay di Amɛrikan Fishery i nɔ jɔs bi impɔtant ɔfisa na di bot, bɔt ɔnda sɔm sikɔstɛms (nayt wach na wan wɛl ples) di kɔmand di ship in dɛk na in bak; so di big big pɔlitikal maksim fɔ di si de aks fɔ, se i fɔ nominally liv apat frɔm di man dɛn bifo di mast, ɛn bi in sɔm we difrɛns as dɛn prɔfɛshɔnal supia; pan ɔl we ɔltɛm, bay dɛn, dɛn kin tek am as dɛn soshal ikwal.

Naw, di big difrɛns we dɛn drɔ bitwin ɔfisa ɛn man na si, na dis—di fɔs wan de liv biɛn, di las wan de go bifo. So, insay wɛl-ship ɛn biznɛsman dɛn, di mared pipul dɛn kin gɛt dɛn kwata wit di kapten; ɛn so, bak, insay bɔku pan di Amɛrikan pipul dɛn we de kech wɛl di harpuoneers dɛn kin lod na di afta pat na di ship. Dat min se, dɛn kin tek dɛn it na di kapten in kabin, ɛn slip na ples we dɛn de tɔk to am indaykt wan.

Pan ɔl we di lɔng tɛm we pɔsin kin travul na di Sawt wɛl (we na di lɔng pas ɔl di waka dɛn we mɔtalman dɔn travul naw ɔ we i dɔn ɛva waka), di spɛshal prɔblɛm dɛn we kin apin to am, ɛn di kɔmyuniti we dɛn kin intres pan wan kɔmni, we dɛn ɔl, ay ɔ smɔl, kin dipen pan dɛn fɔ dɛn prɔfit, nɔto pan fiks pe, bɔt pan dɛn kɔmɔn lɔk, togɛda wit dɛn kɔmɔn wach, nɔ fred, ɛn wok tranga wan; pan ɔl we ɔl dɛn tin ya kin du insay sɔm kes dɛn kin bɔn wan less rigorous disiplin pas in biznɛsman dɛn jɔs; yet, nɔ ɛva tink bɔt aw lɛk wan ol Mɛsopotemia famili, dɛn wɛlman ya kin, insay sɔm fɔstɛm instans dɛn, liv togɛda; fɔ ɔl dat, di punctilious externals, at least, of di kwata-dɛk nɔ kin rili rilaks wit matirial, ɛn in no instans dɛn kin dɔn am. Fɔ tru, bɔku pan dɛn na di Nantucket ship dɛn we yu go si di skipa de parade in kwata-dɛk wit wan elated grandeur we nɔ pas ɛni soja nevi; nay, extorting almost as much outward homage as if i wɛr di imperial pepul, ɛn nɔto di shabbiest of pilot-cloth.

Ɛn pan ɔl we pan ɔl di man dɛn di kapten we bin de mud di Pequod na bin di lɛst giv to da kayn shalɔs asɔmpshɔn de; ɛn pan ɔl we di wangren ɔmajɛ we i ɛva aks fɔ, na fɔ obe am wantɛm wantɛm; pan ɔl we i nɔ bin nid ɛnibɔdi fɔ pul di sus na in fut bifo i step pan di kwata-dɛk; ɛn pan ɔl we tɛm dɛn bin de we, bikɔs ɔf spɛshal tin dɛn we gɛt fɔ du wit di tin dɛn we go apin afta dis we dɛn go tɔk bɔt, i bin de tɔk to dɛn wit wɔd dɛn we nɔ kɔmɔn, ilɛksɛf na fɔ put insɛf dɔŋ ɔ fɔ mek pipul dɛn fred , ɔ ɔda we; yet ivin Kpɛntin Eab nɔ bin de si di impɔtant we dɛn ɛn di we aw dɛn de yuz di si.

Sɔntɛm, dɛn nɔ go ebul fɔ no bak se biɛn dɛn fɔm ɛn yus dɛn de, lɛk se, sɔntɛnde i bin de mask insɛf; incidentally mek yus of dem for oda en mo prayvet ends dan dem legitimately intend to subserve. Dat sɔm sultanism na in bren, we bin dɔn ɔdasay in gud digri nɔ bin de; tru dɛn fɔm dɛn de da sem sultanism bin kam fɔ bi bɔdi insay wan diktetaship we nɔ go ebul fɔ stɔp. Fɔ bi man in intɛlektual supiriɔriti wetin i go, i nɔ go ɛva ebul fɔ tek di prɛktikal, avaylabl suprimi pas ɔda man dɛn, if i nɔ gɛt di ɛp fɔ sɔm kayn ɛksternal art ɛn entrenchmɛnt, ɔltɛm, insay dɛnsɛf, mɔ ɔ lɛs paltry ɛn bays. Dis na, we fɔ sote go kip Gɔd in tru tru prins dɛn na di Ɛmpaya frɔm di wɔl in hustings; ɛn lɛf di ays ɔna we dis ɛya kin gi, to dɛn man dɛn we kin bi fambul mɔ tru dɛn infinite inferiority to di choice hiden handful of di Divine Inert, pas tru dɛn undoubted superiority ova di ded level of di mass. Dis kayn big gud kwaliti de lurk insay dɛn smɔl tin ya we ekstrim pɔlitikal lay lay biliv dɛn de invɛst dɛn, dat insay sɔm kiŋ instans dɛn ivin to idiot imbecility dɛn dɔn gi pawa. Bɔt we, lɛk Nikolas di Zar in kes, di krawn we gɛt ring fɔ di jografik ɛmpaya de rawnd wan impyɔrian bren; den, di plebeian herds crouch abased bifo di tremendous sentralization. Nɔto, di trayjik dramatis we go sho mɔtalman indomitableness in in ful swip ɛn dairekt swing, nɔ go ɛva fɔgɛt wan hint, incidentally so impɔtant insay in art, as di wan we dɛn de tɔk bɔt naw.

Bɔt Eab, mi Kaptɛn, stil de muf bifo mi wit ɔl in Nantucket grimness ɛn shagginess; ɛn insay dis episɔd we de tɔch Emparɔ ɛn Kiŋ dɛn, a nɔ fɔ ayd se a jɔs gɛt fɔ du wit wan po ol wɛl-ɔntin lɛk am; ɛn, bikɔs ɔf dat, dɛn nɔ de gi mi ɔl di ɔda fayn fayn trap dɛn ɛn os dɛn we de na do. Oh, Eab! wetin go bi grand insay yu, i fɔ nid fɔ pul am frɔm di skay, ɛn dayv fɔ am na di dip, ɛn sho am na di briz we nɔ gɛt bɔdi!





CHAPTER 34. Di Kabin-Tebul.

Na midulnɛt; ɛn Dough-Boy, di stiwɔd, we de push in pale bred-brɛd fes frɔm di kabin-skata, de anawns dina to in masta ɛn masta; we, we sidɔm na di lee kwata-bɔt, jɔs dɔn de tek wan ɔbshɔbishɔn fɔ di san; ɛn naw i de kɔl di latitud we de na di smɔl smɔl tablɛt we tan lɛk mɛdal, we dɛn kip fɔ da tin de ɛvride na di ɔp pat pan in ayvri leg. Frɔm di we aw i nɔ bin pe atɛnshɔn to di nyus atɔl, yu go tink se Eab we bin de fil bad nɔ bin yɛri in ɔmbul. Bɔt naw, we i ol di mizen shroud dɛn, i swing insɛf go na di dɛk, ɛn wit wan ivin, nɔ gladi vɔys, se, “Dina, Mista Stabɔk,” i lɔs insay di kabin.

We di las ɛko fɔ in sultan in stɛp dɔn day, ɛn Starbuck, di fɔs Emir, gɛt ɔl di rizin fɔ tink se i sidɔm, dɔn Starbuck de wek frɔm in kwayɛt, tek sɔm tɔn dɛn along di plank dɛn, ɛn, afta wan grev peep insay di binakl, i se, wit sɔm tɔch fɔ mek i gladi, “Dina, Mista Stɔb,” ɛn i de kam dɔŋ di skata. Di sɛkɔn Emir lounge bɔt di rigging sɔm tɛm, ɛn afta dat i shek di men bres smɔl, fɔ si if i go fayn wit da impɔtant rop de, insɛf tek di ol lod, ɛn wit wan kwik “Dinner, Mr. Flask,” . de fala afta di wan dɛn we bin de bifo am.

Bɔt di tɔd Emir, we naw de si insɛf ɔl in wan na di kwata-dɛk, i tan lɛk se i fil fri frɔm sɔm kɔriɔs kɔntrol; bikɔs, we i de tip ɔlkayn wink dɛn we i no na ɔlkayn say, ɛn kik in sus, i de slap insay wan shap bɔt nɔys swɛlin we de mek wan ɔnpaip rayt oba di Grɛnd Turk in ed; ɛn afta dat, bay wan dexterous sleight, we i de pitch in kap ɔp insay di mizentop fɔ wan shelf, i go dɔŋ rollicking so fa at least as i de kɔntinyu fɔ si am frɔm di dɛk, rivɛns ɔl di ɔda prushɔn dɛn, bay we i de briŋ ɔp di riya wit myuzik. Bɔt bifo i step insay di kabin domɔt we de dɔŋ, i stɔp, ship nyu fes ɔltogɛda, ɛn, afta dat, indipɛndɛnt, hilarious smɔl Flask go insay Kiŋ Eab in fes, insay di karakta we Abjectus, ɔ di Slev.

I nɔto di smɔl wan pan di strenj tin dɛn we di intens artificialness of si-usages brid, se we dɛn de na di opin ɛya na di dɛk sɔm ɔfisa dɛn, we dɛn provokeshɔn, bia dɛnsɛf wit maynd ɛn defyingly inof to dɛn kɔmanda; yet, tɛn to wan, lɛ dɛn ɔfisa dɛn de di nɛks mɔnt go dɔŋ to dɛn kɔstɔm dina na da sem kɔmanda in kebin de, ɛn wantɛm wantɛm dɛn inofɛns, nɔ fɔ se deprecatory ɛn ɔmbul ɛya to am, as i sidɔm na di ed fɔ di tebul; dis na wɔndaful tin, sɔntɛnde i kin mek pɔsin kɔmɛnt pas ɔl. Wetin mek dis difrɛns? Prɔblɛm? Sɔntɛm nɔto so. Fɔ bi Bɛlshaza, we na bin Kiŋ na Babilɔn; ɛn fɔ bi Bɛlshaza, nɔto fɔ prawd bɔt fɔ sho rɛspɛkt, fɔ tru, i go mɔs bi se i bin gɛt sɔm tin dɛn we gɛt fɔ du wit di wɔl. Bɔt di wan we insay di rayt rigal ɛn intɛligent spirit de bifo in yon prayvet dina-tebul fɔ invayt gɔst dɛn, da man de in pawa we nɔ gɛt chalenj ɛn dominɔn fɔ wan wan inflɔɛns fɔ di tɛm; dat man in kiŋdɔm pas Bɛlshaza in yon, bikɔs Bɛlshaza nɔto bin di bigman. We dɔn it in padi dɛn wan tɛm nɔmɔ, dɔn test wetin i bi fɔ bi Siza. Na witchery of soshal czarship we no withstanding nɔ de. Naw, if to dis kɔnsidareshɔn yu superadd di ɔfishal suprimi fɔ wan ship-masta, den, bay infɔmeshɔn, yu go derive di kɔz fɔ da peculiarity of si-layf we wi jɔs tɔk bɔt.

Ova in tebul we dɛn mek wit ayvri, Eab bin de bifo lɛk si layɔn we nɔ de tɔk ɛn we gɛt bɔdi na di wayt kɔral bich, ɛn in pikin dɛn we lɛk fɛt-fɛt bɔt we stil de rɛspɛkt bin de rawnd am. Insay in yon rayt tɔn, ɛni ɔfisa bin de wet fɔ mek dɛn sav am. Dɛn bin tan lɛk smɔl pikin dɛn bifo Eab; ɛn pan ɔl dat, insay Eab, i bin tan lɛk se di smɔl smɔl soshal prawd nɔ bin de. Wit wan maynd, dɛn intent yay dɛn ɔl bin tay pan di ol man in nɛf, as i de kɔt di chif dish bifo am. A nɔ tink se fɔ di wɔl dɛn bin fɔ dɔn dɔti da tɛm de wit di smɔl ɔbshɔbishɔn, ivin pan tɔpik we nɔ gɛt wan bɔt lɛk di wɛda. Nɔ! Ɛn we Eab es in nɛf ɛn fɔk, we dɛn lɔk di slais bif bitwin dɛn, dat mek Eab mek sayn to Stabɔk in plet to am, di pɔsin we i mared gɛt in mit lɛk se i de gɛt alms; ɛn kɔt am fayn fayn wan; ɛn smɔl bigin if, sɔntɛm, di nɛf gras agens di plet; ɛn chew am we nɔ gɛt nɔys; ɛn swɛla am, nɔto we i nɔ tek tɛm. Bikɔs, lɛk di Koroneshɔn bankwet na Frankfɔt, usay di Jaman Emparɔ de it dip dip wan wit di sɛvin Impɛrial Ilɛktɔ dɛn, so dɛn kebin it ya na bin sɔm kayn we fɔ it dɛn we dɛn bin de it, we dɛn bin de it insay bad bad silɛns; ɛn yet na tebul ol Eab nɔ bin ban fɔ tɔk; na insɛf nɔmɔ bin mumu. I bin rili fil fri fɔ chok Stub, we wan rat mek wan rɛkɛt wantɛm wantɛm na di hold we de dɔŋ. Ɛn po smɔl Flask, na in na bin di yɔŋ bɔy pikin, ɛn smɔl bɔbɔ fɔ dis famili pati we bin taya. In yon na bin di shinbon dɛn fɔ di salin bif; in yon fɔ dɔn bi di drɔmstik dɛn. Fɔ mek Flask tink se i de ɛp insɛf, dis go dɔn tan lɛk se i tan lɛk tif insay di fɔs digri. If i bin ɛp insɛf na da tebul de, wi nɔ gɛt wan dawt, i nɔ bin fɔ dɔn ɛva ebul fɔ ol in ed ɔp na dis ɔnɛs wɔl; pan ɔl dat, i strenj fɔ se, Eab nɔ ɛva ban am. Ɛn if Flask bin ɛp insɛf, di chans bin de fɔ se Eab nɔ bin dɔn ɛva notis am. Di tin we nɔ pas ɔl, na dat Flask bin tink se i ɛp insɛf fɔ mek bɔta. If i bin tink se di wan dɛn we gɛt di ship nɔ gri fɔ gi am am, bikɔs i bin de klɔt in klia, san san; ɔ if i tink se, we i de travul fɔ lɔng tɛm na dɛn kayn wata dɛn de we nɔ gɛt makit, bɔta de na prɛmiɔm, ɛn dat mek i nɔto fɔ am, na subaltern; pan ɔl we i bin bi, Flask, alas! na bin man we nɔ gɛt bɔta!

Wan ɔda tin. Flask na bin di las pɔsin we bin de dɔŋ na di dina, ɛn Flask na di fɔs man we bin kam ɔp. Tink bɔt! Fɔ dis, Flask in dina bin bad bad wan jam insay pɔynt fɔ tɛm. Starbuck ɛn Stubb ɔl tu bin gɛt di stat fɔ am; ɛn pan ɔl dat, dɛn gɛt di ɔnɔ bak fɔ slip na di bak. If Stubb ivin, we na jɔs wan peg ay pas Flask, apin fɔ gɛt bɔt smɔl apɛtit, ɛn i nɔ tu te i sho simptom dɛn fɔ dɔn in it, den Flask fɔ bestir insɛf, i nɔ go gɛt pas tri mɔtful da de de; bikɔs i agens oli yus fɔ mek Stubb bifo Flask to di dɛk. So na dat Flask bin admit wan tɛm insay prayvet, se frɔm we i dɔn rayz to di rɛspɛkt fɔ ɔfisa, frɔm da tɛm de i nɔ ɛva no wetin i bi fɔ bi ɔda we pas angri, mɔ ɔ smɔl. Bikɔs wetin i it nɔ bin mek i nɔ angri, bɔt i bin mek i nɔ day insay am. Flask bin tink se pis ɛn satisfayshɔn dɔn kɔmɔt na mi bɛlɛ fɔ ɔltɛm. Mi na ɔfisa; bɔt, aw a wish se a kin fish smɔl ol-fashin bif na di fɔkas, lɛk aw a bin de du we a bin de bifo di mast. Na di frut dɛm fɔ promoshɔn de naw; na di vaniti fɔ glori de: na di krayse na layf de! Apat frɔm dat, if i bin so dat ɛni jɔs selɔ na di Pequod bin gɛt grɔj agens Flask insay Flask in ɔfishal kapasiti, ɔl wetin da selɔ de bin gɛt fɔ du, fɔ mek i go gɛt bɔku blɛsin, na fɔ go aft we dɛn de it dina, ɛn gɛt wan peep na Flask tru di kabin skay-layt, sidɔm sili ɛn dufounded bifo awful Eab.

Naw, Eab ɛn in tri kɔmpin dɛn bin mek wetin dɛn go kɔl di fɔs tebul na di Pequod in kebin. Afta dɛn kɔmɔt, we bin de apin insay invayt ɔda te dɛn kam, dɛn bin de klia di kanvas klos, ɔ fɔ se, di pallid stiwɔd bin mek am bak to sɔm ɔda we aw dɛn bin de mek am kwik kwik wan. Ɛn afta dat, dɛn bin kɔl di tri pipul dɛn we de wok wit harpun fɔ go na di fɛstival, ɛn na dɛn na bin di wan dɛn we lɛf na di fɛstival. Dɛn mek wan kayn tɛmporari savant dɛn ɔl fɔ di ay ɛn pawaful kebin.

In strenj kɔntrast to di hardly tolerable constraint ɛn nameless invisible domineerings of di kapten in tebul, na di ɔl kia-fri laysens ɛn izi, di ɔlmost frantic dimokrasi fɔ dɛn inferior felo dɛn di harpooneers. Pan ɔl we i bin tan lɛk se dɛn masta dɛn, we na di man ɛn uman dɛn, bin de fred di sawnd we di hinj dɛn na dɛn yon jaw de mek, di wan dɛn we de mek harpoon bin de it dɛn it wit gladi at so dat ripɔt bin de to am. Dɛn bin de it lɛk masta dɛn; dɛn bin de ful-ɔp dɛn bɛlɛ lɛk Indian ship dɛn ɔl di de we de lod wit spays. Dɛn kayn portentous apɛtit dɛn ya bin gɛt Queequeg ɛn Tashtego, dat fɔ ful-ɔp di vakɛns dɛn we di fɔs it we dɛn bin dɔn it bin mek, bɔku tɛm di pale Dough-Boy bin fain fɔ briŋ wan big baron fɔ sɔl-jɔnk, we i tan lɛk se dɛn dɔn kɔt am kɔmɔt na di sɔlid ɔks. Ɛn if i nɔ bin gɛt layf bɔt am, if i nɔ bin go wit wan nimbul hop-skip-ɛn-jɔmp, den Tashtego bin gɛt wan we we nɔ gɛt sɛns fɔ aksɛleret am bay we i de dart wan fɔk na in bak, we i de yuz harpun. Ɛn wan tɛm, Daggoo, we dɛn bin seiz wit wan sɔdɛn kɔmɛnt, ɛp Dɔg-bɔy in mɛmori bay we i snɛp am ɔp bɔdi, ɛn push in ed insay wan big ɛmti wud trencha, we Tashtego, we ol nɛf na in an, bigin fɔ layt di sɛklɔ fɔs fɔ skel am. Na insɛf na bin smɔl pɔsin we bin rili de fred, we bin de shek shek, dis stiwɔd we gɛt fes we tan lɛk bred; di pikin dɛn we wan bankrupt baker ɛn wan ɔspitul nɔs bɔn. Ɛn wetin wit di tinap spɛktakl we di blak wɔndaful Eab bin de si, ɛn di tɛm we dɛn tri savayj dɛn ya bin de kam fɛn am wit bɔku bɔku pipul dɛn, Dɔ-bɔy in wan ol layf na bin wan kɔntinyu lip-kwɛv. Bɔku tɛm, afta we i si di harpuoneers dɛn ful-ɔp wit ɔl di tin dɛn we dɛn de aks fɔ, i kin rɔnawe kɔmɔt na dɛn an ɛn go insay in smɔl pantri we de nia am, ɛn i kin fred fɔ luk dɛn tru di blaynd dɛn na in domɔt, te ɔltin dɔn.

Na bin fayn tin fɔ si Kwik sidɔm oba agens Tashtego, de agens in fayl tit dɛn to di Indian in yon: krɔs to dɛn, Daggoo sidɔm na grɔn, bikɔs wan bɛnch fɔ dɔn briŋ in ed we gɛt bɔdi-plum to di lɔw karlayn dɛn; pan ɛvri muvmɛnt we in big big an dɛn de muv, we de mek di lɔw kabin fremwɔk shek, lɛk we Afrikan ɛlifant de go pasɛnja na ship. Bɔt fɔ ɔl dis, di big nigro bin rili abstemious, nɔto fɔ se dainty. I bin tan lɛk se i nɔ pɔsibul fɔ mek bay dɛn kayn smɔl smɔl mɔt dɛn de, i go ebul fɔ kip di vitality we dɛn de skata tru so brayt, baronial, ɛn superb pɔsin. Bɔt, wi nɔ gɛt wan dawt fɔ se dis nobul savayj bin de it strɔng ɛn drink dip dip pan di bɔku bɔku tin dɛn we de na di briz; ɛn tru in dilayt nos dɛn we i snuf insay di sublaym layf fɔ di wɔl dɛn. Nɔto bay bif ɔ bay bred, dɛn mek jayant dɛn ɔ dɛn de gi dɛn tin fɔ it. Bɔt Kwik, i bin gɛt wan mɔtalman, barbaric smack na di lip we i de it—wan ɔgly sawnd inof—sote di Dough-Boy we bin de shek shek bin lɛf smɔl fɔ luk fɔ si if ɛni mak pan tit de lurk insay in yon slim an dɛn. Ɛn we i go yɛri Tashtego de siŋ fɔ mek i prodyuz insɛf, so dat dɛn go pik in bon dɛn, di stiwɔd we gɛt simpul sɛns bin jɔs brok brok di krok we bin de hang rawnd am na di pantri, bay di we aw i bin gɛt di palsi we i bin de fil wantɛm wantɛm. Nɔto di wɛtston we di wan dɛn we de mek hapun bin de kɛr na dɛn poket, fɔ dɛn lans ɛn ɔda wɛpɔn dɛn; ɛn we dɛn kin yuz wetston dɛn, we dɛn de it dina, dɛn kin shap dɛn nɛf dɛn fɔ sho se dɛn de shap dɛn nɛf dɛn; dat gratin sawnd nɔ bin atɔl fɔ mek po Dɔg-Bɔy kwayɛt. Aw i go fɔgɛt se insay in Ayland tɛm, Kwik, fɔ wan, go mɔs dɔn gilti fɔ sɔm kil, kɔnvivial indiskrɛshɔn dɛn. Alas! Dɔti-Bɔy! hard fares di wayt weita we de wet pan kanibal. Nɔto napkin i fɔ kɛr na in an, bɔt na bukla. Bɔt as tɛm de go, i bin rili gladi we di tri sɔl-si wɔman dɛn go grap ɛn kɔmɔt; to in yes dɛn we nɔ biliv ɛn we de tɔk lay lay stori dɛn, ɔl dɛn mared bon dɛn de jingl insay dɛn ɛvri stɛp, lɛk Moorish scimetars we de insay skab.

Bɔt, pan ɔl we dɛn barbarian ya bin de it na di kebin, ɛn nominally bin de liv de; stil, bikɔs dɛn nɔ bin de sidɔm na dɛn abit, dɛn nɔ bin de ɛva de insay am pas we dɛn de it, ɛn jɔs bifo dɛn slip, we dɛn pas de go na dɛn yon spɛshal say.

Insay dis wan tin, i bin tan lɛk se Eab nɔ bin de pan bɔku pan di Amɛrikan wɛl kapten dɛn, we, as sɛt, dɛn kin rili tink se bay rayt di ship in kabin na dɛn yon; ɛn na bay kɔtisti nɔmɔ, ɛni ɔda pɔsin, ɛni tɛm, dɛn kin alaw am fɔ de de. So dat, fɔ tru, di mates ɛn harpooneers fɔ di Pequod go mɔs se dɛn bin de liv na do na di kebin pas insay de. Bikɔs we dɛn go insay de, na sɔntin we tan lɛk strit domɔt we de go insay os; tɔn insay fɔ smɔl tɛm, na fɔ tɔn aut di nɛks wan; ɛn, as tin we go de sote go, fɔ de na di opin ples. Dɛn nɔ bin lɔs bɔku tin bak pan dis; insay di kebin, nɔbɔdi nɔ bin de wit am; soshal, Eab nɔ bin ebul fɔ go de. Pan ɔl we dɛn bin de kɔl am insay di sɛns we dɛn bin de kɔnt Kristian Kristian dɛn, i bin stil bi strenja to di Kristian rilijɔn. I bin de liv na di wɔl, lɛk aw di las wan pan di Grisly Bears bin de liv na Misɔri we dɛn bin dɔn sɛtul. Ɛn lɛk we Spring ɛn Sɔma dɔn go, da wayl Lɔgan we de na di bush, we de bɛr insɛf na di ol na wan tik, bin de liv aut di winta de, de sok in yon fut; so, insay in ol ej we nɔ bin fayn ɛn we bin de ala, Eab in sol, bin lɔk insay di kev trɔnk na in bɔdi, de it di swɛlin fut dɛn we i dak!





CHAPTER 35. Di Mast-Hɛd.

Na di tɛm we di wɛda bin fayn mɔ, na in we a bin de rɔtin di rayt we wit di ɔda siman dɛn, mi fɔs mast-hɛd bin kam rawnd.

Insay bɔku pan di Amɛrikin wɛlman dɛn di mast-hɛd dɛn kin gɛt pipul dɛn klos wan tɛm wit di bot we de kɔmɔt na in pɔt; ivin if i kin gɛt fayvtin tawzin mayl, ɛn mɔ, fɔ sel bifo i rich na in rayt kruiz grɔn. Ɛn if, afta tri, 4, ɔ fayv ia travul, i de kam nia os wit ɛnitin we ɛmti insay am—lɛ wi se, ɛmti vayl ivin—dɛn, dɛn kin kip in mast-hed dɛn te to di las wan; ɛn nɔto te in skay-sɛl-pol dɛn sel insay midul di spia dɛn na di pɔt, i kin lɛf di op ɔltogɛda fɔ kech wan wɛl mɔ.

Naw, as di biznɛs fɔ tinap mast-hɛd, na di sho ɔ na wata, na wan we rili ol ɛn intrestin, lɛ wi in sɔm mɛzhɔ expatiate ya. A tek am, se di fɔs pipul dɛn we bin tinap fɔ mast-hɛd na di ol Ijipshian dɛn; bikɔs, insay ɔl mi risach dɛn, a nɔ si ɛni wan bifo dɛn. Bikɔs pan ɔl we dɛn gret gret granpa dɛn, we na di wan dɛn we bil Bebɛl, go mɔs dɔn, bay dɛn tawa, bin dɔn mekɔp dɛn maynd fɔ mek di mast-ed we ay pas ɔl na ɔl Eshia, ɔ Afrika sɛf; yet (ere dɛn put di las trak pan am) as dɛn kin se da big ston mast we dɛn gɛt bin go nia di bod, insay di bad bad briz we Gɔd in wamat bin de blo; so, wi nɔ go ebul fɔ gi dɛn Bebɛl bilda dɛn ya fɔs pas di Ijipshian dɛn. Ɛn se di Ijipshian dɛn na bin neshɔn we tinap wit mast-hɛd, na wan asɛsmɛnt we dɛn bays pan di jenɛral biliv we di wan dɛn we de stɔdi bɔt aw pipul dɛn bin de du trade trade bin biliv, se dɛn bin bil di fɔs piramid dɛn fɔ astronomical purposes: wan tiori we dɛn sɔpɔt wan wan bay di peculiar stej-layk fɔmɛshɔn fɔ ɔl di 4 say dɛn dɛn bildin dɛn de; we, wit prodigious long upliftings of their legs, dem ol astronomers bin yus fɔ go ɔp to di apex, ɛn siŋ fɔ nyu sta dɛm; ivin lɛk aw di say dɛn we pipul dɛn kin luk fɔ wan ship we de naw kin siŋ fɔ mek dɛn ebul fɔ go sel, ɔ wɛl we jɔs de na di say we dɛn de si. Insay Sɛnt Stayl, di Kristian ɛmit we bin gɛt nem trade trade, we bil am wan ay ay ston pila na di dɛzat ɛn spɛn di wan ol las pat pan in layf na in mawnten, ɛn i bin de es in it frɔm di grɔn wit takɛl; insay am wi gɛt wan wɔndaful ɛgzampul bɔt wan pɔsin we nɔ de fred fɔ tinap fɔ mast-ed; we nɔ fɔ mek fɔg ɔ frɔst, ren, ays blɔk, ɔ slit drɛb kɔmɔt na in ples; bɔt valiantly fes ɔltin aut to di las, rili day na in post. Fɔ di mɔdan standers-of-mast-heads wi gɛt bɔt wan sɛt we nɔ gɛt layf; na jɔs ston, ayɛn, ɛn brɔnz man dɛn; we pan ɔl we dɛn ebul fɔ fes wan big big briz, dɛn stil nɔ ebul fɔ du di biznɛs fɔ siŋ we dɛn fɛn ɛni strenj tin we dɛn de si. Napoleɔn de de; we, pan di tap pan di kɔlɔn na Vendome, tinap wit in an dɛn we dɛn fold, lɛk wan ɔndrɛd ɛn fifti fit na di ays; careless, naw, udat de rul di dɛk dɛn we de dɔŋ; ilɛksɛf na Luis Filip, Luis Blank, ɔ Luis di Dɛbul. Gret Washinton, insɛf, tinap ay ay wan pan in ay ay men-mast na Baltimo, ɛn lɛk wan pan Hɛkyul in pila dɛn, in kɔlɔm de mak da pɔynt de we mɔtalman gɛt big big wan we na smɔl mɔtalman dɛn nɔmɔ go go pas. Admiral Nelson, insɛf, pan wan kapstan we dɛn mek wit gɔn-mɛtal, tinap in mast-ed na Trafalgar Square; ɛn ɛva we dɛn mɔs ɔbskɔr bay da Lɔndɔn smok de, dɛn de gi token yet se wan hiro we ayd de de; bikɔs usay smok de, faya fɔ de. Bɔt nɔto big Washinton, ɔ Nɛpɔlyɔn, ɔ Nɛlson, nɔ go ansa wan singl ays blɔk frɔm dɔŋ, ilɛksɛf dɛn advays dɛn kɔl dɛn fɔ mek padi wit di dɛk dɛn we de ambɔg dɛn we dɛn de luk pan; ɔltogɛda dɛn kin tink se dɛn spirit kin go insay di tik tik ays we go kam, ɛn diskray us shoal ɛn us rɔk dɛn fɔ avɔyd.

I kin tan lɛk se i nɔ mek sɛns fɔ kɔpl di wan dɛn we de tinap na di land we gɛt mast-hed wit di wan dɛn we de na di si pan ɛni we; bɔt se fɔ tru, i nɔ so, de sho klia wan bay wan tin we Obed Macy, we na di wangren istri man bɔt Nantucket, tinap fɔ ansa fɔ. Di worthy Obed tɛl wi, se insay di fɔs tɛm we dɛn bin de fishin wɛl, ere ship dɛn bin de lanch ɔltɛm fɔ rɔnata di gem, di pipul dɛn na da ayland de bin de bil ay ay spars along di si-kɔst, to usay di luk-ɔut dɛn bin de go ɔp bay we na klit we dɛn nel, sɔntin lɛk aw fɔl dɛn de go ɔp stej na wan ɔn-os. Sɔm ia bifo dis tɛm, di Bay wɛlman dɛn na Nyu Ziland bin tek dis sem plan, ɛn we dɛn tɔk bɔt di gem, dɛn gi notis to di bot dɛn we rɛdi fɔ pipul dɛn we de nia di bich. Bɔt dis kɔstɔm dɔn nɔ de igen naw; tɔn wi den to di wan prɔpa mast-hɛd, we na wɛl-ship we de na si. Dɛn kin kip pipul dɛn na di tri mast-hɛd dɛn frɔm we di san kɔmɔt te di san go dɔŋ; di siman dɛn de tek dɛn rɛgyula tɔn (lɛk na di edman), ɛn rili fri dɛnsɛf ɛvri tu awa. Insay di serene wɛda na di tropiks i kin rili fayn di mast-hɛd; nɔto so, to man we de drim we de tink gud wan, i kin mek pɔsin gladi. Na de yu tinap, wan ɔndrɛd fit ɔp di dɛk dɛn we nɔ gɛt wan vɔys, de strit along di dip, lɛk se di mast dɛn na big big stik dɛn, we ɔnda yu ɛn bitwin yu leg dɛn, lɛk se, yu de swim di big big big big animal dɛn na di si, ivin lɛk aw ship dɛn bin de travul trade bitwin di but dɛn we di fambul we nem Kɔlɔs bin gɛt na ol Rɔds. Na de yu tinap, yu lɔs insay di siriɔs si we nɔ gɛt ɛnd, wit natin we rɔf pas di wef dɛn. Di ship we dɔn trance de rol indolently; di tred briz we de slip de blo; ɔltin de sɔlv yu insay languor. Fɔ di mɔs pat, insay dis tropik wɛl layf, wan sublaym uneventfulness de invɛst yu; yu nɔ de yɛri ɛni nyuz; nɔ rid ɛni gazɛt; ekstra dɛn wit sɔprayz akɔn dɛn bɔt kɔmɔn tin dɛn nɔ de ɛva ful yu fɔ ɛnjɔy yusɛf we yu nɔ nid; yu nɔ de yɛri bɔt ɛnitin we de mek yu sɔfa na os; bankrupt sikyɔriti dɛn; di stok dɛn we de fɔdɔm; nɔ de ɛva wɔri wit di tink bɔt wetin yu go it fɔ dina—bikɔs ɔl di it dɛn we yu de it fɔ tri ia ɛn mɔ, dɛn kin kip dɛn fayn fayn wan insay bɔtul dɛn, ɛn yu bil fɔ pe nɔ kin chenj.

Insay wan pan dɛn wɛl man dɛn de we de na di sawt, we yu de travul fɔ lɔng tɛm fɔ tri ɔ 4 ia, lɛk aw i kin apin bɔku tɛm, di sɔm pan di difrɛn awa dɛn we yu kin spɛn na di mast-hɛd go rich sɔm wan ol mɔnt. Ɛn i rili sɔri fɔ no se di ples we yu de devote so bɔku pan di wan ol tɛm fɔ yu natura layf, fɔ so sadly destitute fɔ ɛnitin we de kam nia wan cosy inhabitiveness, ɔ adapt fɔ brid wan kɔmfyut lokalness fɔ filin, lɛk we gɛt fɔ du wit bed, hamak, motoka we dɛn kin yuz fɔ kil pipul dɛn, bɔks we de wach pipul dɛn, pulpit, kɔch, ɔ ɛni ɔda wan pan dɛn smɔl ɛn fayn fayn tin dɛn de we man dɛn kin put dɛnsɛf ayd fɔ sɔm tɛm. Di say we yu kin sidɔm pas ɔl na di ed fɔ di t’ gallant-mast, usay yu tinap pan tu tin paralel stik dɛn (we na ɔlmost pekul to wɛlman dɛn) we dɛn kɔl di t’ gallant krɔs-tri dɛn. Na ya, we di si de tos rawnd, di pɔsin we de bigin fɔ fil kin fil fayn lɛk aw i kin fil fayn we i tinap pan kaw in ɔn. Fɔ tru, we kol weta yu kin kɛr yu os go ɔp wit yu, we tan lɛk wach-kot; bɔt fɔ tɔk di rayt we, di wach-kot we tik pas ɔl nɔto os pas di bɔdi we nɔ gɛt klos; bikɔs lɛk aw dɛn glu di sol insay in tabanakul we gɛt bɔdi, ɛn i nɔ ebul fɔ muv fri wan insay de, ɔ ivin muf kɔmɔt de, we i nɔ de rɔn big risk fɔ day (lɛk pilgrim we nɔ no natin we de krɔs di Alps we gɛt sno insay winta); so wach-kot nɔto so mɔ na os bɔt na jɔs ɛnvilɔp, ɔ ɔda skin we de kɔba yu. Yu nɔ go ebul fɔ put shelf ɔ bɔks fɔ drɔwa na yu bɔdi, ɛn yu nɔ go ebul fɔ mek fayn kloz fɔ yu wach-kot igen.

We i kam pan ɔl dis, i rili sɔri fɔ no se di mast-hɛd dɛn na wan wɛl ship we de na di sawt nɔ gɛt dɛn smɔl smɔl tɛnt ɔ pulpit dɛn de we pɔsin kin jɛlɔs, we dɛn kɔl kraw-nɛst , usay dɛn kin protɛkt di say dɛn we pɔsin we de kech wɛl na Grinland kin luk fɔ frɔm di bad tin dɛn we kin apin to am wɛda fɔ di si dɛn we dɔn frɔz. Insay di fayasayd stori we Kpɛntin Slit bin rayt, we gɛt di taytul “A Voyage among the Icebergs, in quest of the Greenland Whale, ɛn i apin fɔ mek dɛn fɛn bak di Lost Icelandic Colonies of Old Greenland;” insay dis volyum we wi kin admaya, ɔl di wan dɛn we tinap fɔ mast-hɛd dɛn dɔn ful-ɔp wit wan fayn fayn sikɔstɛms akɔdin to di kraw-nɛst we dɛn jɔs mek da tɛm de na di Gleshia, we na bin di nem fɔ Kpɛntin Slit in gud kraft. I kɔl am di Sleet in krawd-nɛst , fɔ ɔnɔ insɛf; i bi di ɔrijinal invintɔ ɛn patɛnt, ɛn fri frɔm ɔl ridikul lay lay delikasi, ɛn ol se if wi kɔl wi yon pikin dɛn afta wi yon nem (wi papa dɛn bi di ɔrijinal invintɔ ɛn patɛnt), so semweso wi fɔ denominate afta wisɛf ɛni ɔda aprɛshɔn wi kin bɔn pikin. We i shep, di Slit in kraw-nɛst na sɔntin lɛk big tierce ɔ paip; bɔt i opin ɔp usay dɛn dɔn mek am wit sayd-skrin we yu kin muv fɔ mek yu nɔ ebul fɔ blo na yu ed we big big briz de blo. Bikɔs yu fiks na di mawnten fɔ di mast, yu kin go insay de tru wan smɔl trap-hatch we de dɔŋ. Na di afta sayd, ɔ sayd we de nia di stɛp pat na di ship, dɛn gɛt fayn sidɔm ples, ɛn dɛn gɛt lɔka ɔnda fɔ put ambɔla, kɔmfɔta, ɛn kot. Insay di frɔnt, dɛn put lɛda rɛk, usay yu kin kip yu tɔk trɔmpɛt, paip, tɛliskɔp, ɛn ɔda tin dɛn we yu kin yuz na di si. We Kpɛntin Slit insɛf tinap in mast-ed insay dis kraw-nɛst we i gɛt, i tɛl wi se i bin gɛt rayf wit am ɔltɛm (we dɛn kin fiks bak na di rɛk), togɛda wit paoda flask ɛn shot, fɔ di rizin fɔ pop ɔf di narwɛl dɛn we nɔ de waka, ɔ si yunikɔn dɛn we de waka waka we de kam insay dɛn wata dɛn de; bikɔs yu nɔ go ebul fɔ shot dɛn fayn fayn wan frɔm di dɛk bikɔs di wata de tinap tranga wan, bɔt fɔ shot dɛn dɔŋ na sɔntin we rili difrɛn. Naw, i klia se na wok we Kpɛtɛn Slit bin de du fɔ lɛk fɔ tɔk bɔt, lɛk aw i de du, ɔl di smɔl smɔl tin dɛn we de mek i izi fɔ in krawd-nɛst; bɔt pan ɔl we i so big pan bɔku pan dɛn tin ya, ɛn pan ɔl we i de trit wi wit wan rili sayɛns stori bɔt di tin dɛn we i bin de du na dis krawd-nɛst, wit wan smɔl kɔmpas we i bin kip de fɔ di rizin fɔ kɔba di mistek dɛn we bin de kɔmɔt frɔm wetin dɛn kɔl di “ . lokal atrakshɔn” fɔ ɔl di binakl magnet dɛn; wan mistek we dɛn kin se na di ɔrizɔntal say we di ayɛn bin de na di ship in plank dɛn, ɛn insay di Gleshia in kes, sɔntɛm, bikɔs bɔku bɔku blaksmit dɛn we dɔn brok brok bin de bitwin in kru dɛn; A se, pan ɔl we di Kpɛntin rili gɛt sɛns ɛn sayɛnsman ya, bɔt stil, fɔ ɔl di tin dɛn we i dɔn lan “binnacle deviations,” “azimuth compass observations,” ɛn “approximate errors,” i no gud gud wan, Kpɛntin Slit, se i nɔ bin de so bɔku i bin de insay dɛn dip magnɛtik mɛditeishɔn dɛn de, as fɔ mek dɛn nɔ atrak am wan wan tɛm to da smɔl kes-bɔtul we dɛn dɔn ful-ɔp fayn fayn wan, we dɛn tuck so fayn na wan say na in krawd in nɛst, we i izi fɔ rich na in an. Pan ɔl we, pan ɔl, a rili lɛk ɛn ivin lɛk di Kpɛntin we gɛt maynd, we ɔnɛs, ɛn we lan buk; yet a tek am rili bad fɔ am se i fɔ so rili ignore da kes-bɔtul de, si aw i go dɔn bi fetful padi ɛn kɔmfɔt, we i bin de stɔdi di matematiks ɔp de na da bɔd in nɛst de insay tri ɔ 4 ples dɛn fɔ sidɔm na di tik.

Bɔt if wi Sawt wɛl-fisha dɛn nɔ de so fayn fɔ de ɔp lɛk aw Kpɛntin Slit ɛn in Grinland pipul dɛn bin de; yet dat disavantej de rili kɔntra-balans bay di bɔku bɔku kɔntrast sereniti fɔ dɛn sɛduktiv si dɛn we wi Sawt fishaman dɛn kin flɔt mɔ. Fɔ wan, a bin de yus fɔ lounge up di rigging rili leisurely, rɛst na di top fɔ gɛt chat wit Queequeg, ɔ ɛni ɔda pɔsin we nɔ de du di wok we a go fɛn de; dɔn a go ɔp smɔl fa, ɛn trowe wan les leg oba di yad we de ɔp di sel, tek wan fɔs we fɔ si di ples dɛn we gɛt wata, ɛn so las las a go mawnt to di say we a de go.

Mek a mek klin brest of am ya, en frankly admit se a kip bot sorry guard. Wit di prɔblɛm we di yunivas de rɔn insay mi, aw mi go—we dɛn lɛf mi ɔltogɛda na misɛf na dis kayn ayt we de mek pɔsin tink—aw a go bɔt layt fɔ ol mi ɔbligayshɔn fɔ fala ɔl di ɔda we aw di wɛl-ship dɛn de tinap, “Kip yu wɛda yay opin, ɛn siŋ ɔltɛm.”

Ɛn lɛ a na dis ples advays una wit muv, una we gɛt ship na Nantucket! Ɛn dɛn monishɔn ya nɔto tin we nɔ nid atɔl. Fɔ tide, di wɛl-fisheri de gi ples fɔ bɔku yɔŋ man dɛn we lɛk dɛnsɛf, we gɛt pwɛl at, ɛn we nɔ de tink bɔt ɛnitin, we nɔ lɛk di kayn we aw di wɔl de kia fɔ dɛn, ɛn we de luk fɔ sɛntimɛnt insay tar ɛn blubber. Childe Harold not unfrequently perches himself upon the mast-head of some luckless disappointed whale-ship, and in moody phrase ejaculates:—

“Roll on, thou deep and dark blue ocean, roll!
Ten thousand blubber-hunters sweep over thee in vain.”

Very often do the captains of such ships take those absent-minded young philosophers to task, upbraiding them with not feeling sufficient “interest” in the voyage; half-hinting that they are so hopelessly lost to all honorable ambition, as that in their secret souls they would rather not see whales than otherwise. But all in vain; those young Platonists have a notion that their vision is imperfect; they are short-sighted; what use, then, to strain the visual nerve? They have left their opera-glasses at home.

“Why, thou monkey,” said a harpooneer to one of these lads, “we've been cruising now hard upon three years, and thou hast not raised a whale yet. Whales are scarce as hen's teeth whenever thou art up here.” Perhaps they were; or perhaps there might have been shoals of them in the far horizon; but lulled into such an opium-like listlessness of vacant, unconscious reverie is this absent-minded youth by the blending cadence of waves with thoughts, that at last he loses his identity; takes the mystic ocean at his feet for the visible image of that deep, blue, bottomless soul, pervading mankind and nature; and every strange, half-seen, gliding, beautiful thing that eludes him; every dimly-discovered, uprising fin of some undiscernible form, seems to him the embodiment of those elusive thoughts that only people the soul by continually flitting through it. In this enchanted mood, thy spirit ebbs away to whence it came; becomes diffused through time and space; like Cranmer's sprinkled Pantheistic ashes, forming at last a part of every shore the round globe over.

There is no life in thee, now, except that rocking life imparted by a gently rolling ship; by her, borrowed from the sea; by the sea, from the inscrutable tides of God. But while this sleep, this dream is on ye, move your foot or hand an inch; slip your hold at all; and your identity comes back in horror. Over Descartian vortices you hover. And perhaps, at mid-day, in the fairest weather, with one half-throttled shriek you drop through that transparent air into the summer sea, no more to rise for ever. Heed it well, ye Pantheists!





CHAPTER 36. The Quarter-Deck.

( Enter Ahab: Then, all. )

It was not a great while after the affair of the pipe, that one morning shortly after breakfast, Ahab, as was his wont, ascended the cabin-gangway to the deck. There most sea-captains usually walk at that hour, as country gentlemen, after the same meal, take a few turns in the garden.

Soon his steady, ivory stride was heard, as to and fro he paced his old rounds, upon planks so familiar to his tread, that they were all over dented, like geological stones, with the peculiar mark of his walk. Did you fixedly gaze, too, upon that ribbed and dented brow; there also, you would see still stranger foot-prints—the foot-prints of his one unsleeping, ever-pacing thought.

But on the occasion in question, those dents looked deeper, even as his nervous step that morning left a deeper mark. And, so full of his thought was Ahab, that at every uniform turn that he made, now at the main-mast and now at the binnacle, you could almost see that thought turn in him as he turned, and pace in him as he paced; so completely possessing him, indeed, that it all but seemed the inward mould of every outer movement.

“D'ye mark him, Flask?” whispered Stubb; “the chick that's in him pecks the shell. 'Twill soon be out.”

The hours wore on;—Ahab now shut up within his cabin; anon, pacing the deck, with the same intense bigotry of purpose in his aspect.

It drew near the close of day. Suddenly he came to a halt by the bulwarks, and inserting his bone leg into the auger-hole there, and with one hand grasping a shroud, he ordered Starbuck to send everybody aft.

“Sir!” said the mate, astonished at an order seldom or never given on ship-board except in some extraordinary case.

“Send everybody aft,” repeated Ahab. “Mast-heads, there! come down!”

When the entire ship's company were assembled, and with curious and not wholly unapprehensive faces, were eyeing him, for he looked not unlike the weather horizon when a storm is coming up, Ahab, after rapidly glancing over the bulwarks, and then darting his eyes among the crew, started from his standpoint; and as though not a soul were nigh him resumed his heavy turns upon the deck. With bent head and half-slouched hat he continued to pace, unmindful of the wondering whispering among the men; till Stubb cautiously whispered to Flask, that Ahab must have summoned them there for the purpose of witnessing a pedestrian feat. But this did not last long. Vehemently pausing, he cried:—

“What do ye do when ye see a whale, men?”

“Sing out for him!” was the impulsive rejoinder from a score of clubbed voices.

“Good!” cried Ahab, with a wild approval in his tones; observing the hearty animation into which his unexpected question had so magnetically thrown them.

“And what do ye next, men?”

“Lower away, and after him!”

“And what tune is it ye pull to, men?”

“A dead whale or a stove boat!”

More and more strangely and fiercely glad and approving, grew the countenance of the old man at every shout; while the mariners began to gaze curiously at each other, as if marvelling how it was that they themselves became so excited at such seemingly purposeless questions.

But, they were all eagerness again, as Ahab, now half-revolving in his pivot-hole, with one hand reaching high up a shroud, and tightly, almost convulsively grasping it, addressed them thus:—

“All ye mast-headers have before now heard me give orders about a white whale. Look ye! d'ye see this Spanish ounce of gold?”—holding up a broad bright coin to the sun—“it is a sixteen dollar piece, men. D'ye see it? Mr. Starbuck, hand me yon top-maul.”

While the mate was getting the hammer, Ahab, without speaking, was slowly rubbing the gold piece against the skirts of his jacket, as if to heighten its lustre, and without using any words was meanwhile lowly humming to himself, producing a sound so strangely muffled and inarticulate that it seemed the mechanical humming of the wheels of his vitality in him.

Receiving the top-maul from Starbuck, he advanced towards the main-mast with the hammer uplifted in one hand, exhibiting the gold with the other, and with a high raised voice exclaiming: “Whosoever of ye raises me a white-headed whale with a wrinkled brow and a crooked jaw; whosoever of ye raises me that white-headed whale, with three holes punctured in his starboard fluke—look ye, whosoever of ye raises me that same white whale, he shall have this gold ounce, my boys!”

“Huzza! huzza!” cried the seamen, as with swinging tarpaulins they hailed the act of nailing the gold to the mast.

“It's a white whale, I say,” resumed Ahab, as he threw down the topmaul: “a white whale. Skin your eyes for him, men; look sharp for white water; if ye see but a bubble, sing out.”

All this while Tashtego, Daggoo, and Queequeg had looked on with even more intense interest and surprise than the rest, and at the mention of the wrinkled brow and crooked jaw they had started as if each was separately touched by some specific recollection.

“Captain Ahab,” said Tashtego, “that white whale must be the same that some call Moby Dick.”

“Moby Dick?” shouted Ahab. “Do ye know the white whale then, Tash?”

“Does he fan-tail a little curious, sir, before he goes down?” said the Gay-Header deliberately.

“And has he a curious spout, too,” said Daggoo, “very bushy, even for a parmacetty, and mighty quick, Captain Ahab?”

“And he have one, two, three—oh! good many iron in him hide, too, Captain,” cried Queequeg disjointedly, “all twiske-tee be-twisk, like him—him—” faltering hard for a word, and screwing his hand round and round as though uncorking a bottle—“like him—him—”

“Corkscrew!” cried Ahab, “aye, Queequeg, the harpoons lie all twisted and wrenched in him; aye, Daggoo, his spout is a big one, like a whole shock of wheat, and white as a pile of our Nantucket wool after the great annual sheep-shearing; aye, Tashtego, and he fan-tails like a split jib in a squall. Death and devils! men, it is Moby Dick ye have seen—Moby Dick—Moby Dick!”

“Captain Ahab,” said Starbuck, who, with Stubb and Flask, had thus far been eyeing his superior with increasing surprise, but at last seemed struck with a thought which somewhat explained all the wonder. “Captain Ahab, I have heard of Moby Dick—but it was not Moby Dick that took off thy leg?”

“Who told thee that?” cried Ahab; then pausing, “Aye, Starbuck; aye, my hearties all round; it was Moby Dick that dismasted me; Moby Dick that brought me to this dead stump I stand on now. Aye, aye,” he shouted with a terrific, loud, animal sob, like that of a heart-stricken moose; “Aye, aye! it was that accursed white whale that razed me; made a poor pegging lubber of me for ever and a day!” Then tossing both arms, with measureless imprecations he shouted out: “Aye, aye! and I'll chase him round Good Hope, and round the Horn, and round the Norway Maelstrom, and round perdition's flames before I give him up. And this is what ye have shipped for, men! to chase that white whale on both sides of land, and over all sides of earth, till he spouts black blood and rolls fin out. What say ye, men, will ye splice hands on it, now? I think ye do look brave.”

“Aye, aye!” shouted the harpooneers and seamen, running closer to the excited old man: “A sharp eye for the white whale; a sharp lance for Moby Dick!”

“God bless ye,” he seemed to half sob and half shout. “God bless ye, men. Steward! go draw the great measure of grog. But what's this long face about, Mr. Starbuck; wilt thou not chase the white whale? art not game for Moby Dick?”

“I am game for his crooked jaw, and for the jaws of Death too, Captain Ahab, if it fairly comes in the way of the business we follow; but I came here to hunt whales, not my commander's vengeance. How many barrels will thy vengeance yield thee even if thou gettest it, Captain Ahab? it will not fetch thee much in our Nantucket market.”

“Nantucket market! Hoot! But come closer, Starbuck; thou requirest a little lower layer. If money's to be the measurer, man, and the accountants have computed their great counting-house the globe, by girdling it with guineas, one to every three parts of an inch; then, let me tell thee, that my vengeance will fetch a great premium here! ”

“He smites his chest,” whispered Stubb, “what's that for? methinks it rings most vast, but hollow.”

“Vengeance on a dumb brute!” cried Starbuck, “that simply smote thee from blindest instinct! Madness! To be enraged with a dumb thing, Captain Ahab, seems blasphemous.”

“Hark ye yet again—the little lower layer. All visible objects, man, are but as pasteboard masks. But in each event—in the living act, the undoubted deed—there, some unknown but still reasoning thing puts forth the mouldings of its features from behind the unreasoning mask. If man will strike, strike through the mask! How can the prisoner reach outside except by thrusting through the wall? To me, the white whale is that wall, shoved near to me. Sometimes I think there's naught beyond. But 'tis enough. He tasks me; he heaps me; I see in him outrageous strength, with an inscrutable malice sinewing it. That inscrutable thing is chiefly what I hate; and be the white whale agent, or be the white whale principal, I will wreak that hate upon him. Talk not to me of blasphemy, man; I'd strike the sun if it insulted me. For could the sun do that, then could I do the other; since there is ever a sort of fair play herein, jealousy presiding over all creations. But not my master, man, is even that fair play. Who's over me? Truth hath no confines. Take off thine eye! more intolerable than fiends' glarings is a doltish stare! So, so; thou reddenest and palest; my heat has melted thee to anger-glow. But look ye, Starbuck, what is said in heat, that thing unsays itself. There are men from whom warm words are small indignity. I meant not to incense thee. Let it go. Look! see yonder Turkish cheeks of spotted tawn—living, breathing pictures painted by the sun. The Pagan leopards—the unrecking and unworshipping things, that live; and seek, and give no reasons for the torrid life they feel! The crew, man, the crew! Are they not one and all with Ahab, in this matter of the whale? See Stubb! he laughs! See yonder Chilian! he snorts to think of it. Stand up amid the general hurricane, thy one tost sapling cannot, Starbuck! And what is it? Reckon it. 'Tis but to help strike a fin; no wondrous feat for Starbuck. What is it more? From this one poor hunt, then, the best lance out of all Nantucket, surely he will not hang back, when every foremast-hand has clutched a whetstone? Ah! constrainings seize thee; I see! the billow lifts thee! Speak, but speak!—Aye, aye! thy silence, then, that voices thee. ( Aside ) Something shot from my dilated nostrils, he has inhaled it in his lungs. Starbuck now is mine; cannot oppose me now, without rebellion.”

“God keep me!—keep us all!” murmured Starbuck, lowly.

But in his joy at the enchanted, tacit acquiescence of the mate, Ahab did not hear his foreboding invocation; nor yet the low laugh from the hold; nor yet the presaging vibrations of the winds in the cordage; nor yet the hollow flap of the sails against the masts, as for a moment their hearts sank in. For again Starbuck's downcast eyes lighted up with the stubbornness of life; the subterranean laugh died away; the winds blew on; the sails filled out; the ship heaved and rolled as before. Ah, ye admonitions and warnings! why stay ye not when ye come? But rather are ye predictions than warnings, ye shadows! Yet not so much predictions from without, as verifications of the foregoing things within. For with little external to constrain us, the innermost necessities in our being, these still drive us on.

“The measure! the measure!” cried Ahab.

Receiving the brimming pewter, and turning to the harpooneers, he ordered them to produce their weapons. Then ranging them before him near the capstan, with their harpoons in their hands, while his three mates stood at his side with their lances, and the rest of the ship's company formed a circle round the group; he stood for an instant searchingly eyeing every man of his crew. But those wild eyes met his, as the bloodshot eyes of the prairie wolves meet the eye of their leader, ere he rushes on at their head in the trail of the bison; but, alas! only to fall into the hidden snare of the Indian.

“Drink and pass!” he cried, handing the heavy charged flagon to the nearest seaman. “The crew alone now drink. Round with it, round! Short draughts—long swallows, men; 'tis hot as Satan's hoof. So, so; it goes round excellently. It spiralizes in ye; forks out at the serpent-snapping eye. Well done; almost drained. That way it went, this way it comes. Hand it me—here's a hollow! Men, ye seem the years; so brimming life is gulped and gone. Steward, refill!

“Attend now, my braves. I have mustered ye all round this capstan; and ye mates, flank me with your lances; and ye harpooneers, stand there with your irons; and ye, stout mariners, ring me in, that I may in some sort revive a noble custom of my fisherman fathers before me. O men, you will yet see that—Ha! boy, come back? bad pennies come not sooner. Hand it me. Why, now, this pewter had run brimming again, wer't not thou St. Vitus' imp—away, thou ague!

“Advance, ye mates! Cross your lances full before me. Well done! Let me touch the axis.” So saying, with extended arm, he grasped the three level, radiating lances at their crossed centre; while so doing, suddenly and nervously twitched them; meanwhile, glancing intently from Starbuck to Stubb; from Stubb to Flask. It seemed as though, by some nameless, interior volition, he would fain have shocked into them the same fiery emotion accumulated within the Leyden jar of his own magnetic life. The three mates quailed before his strong, sustained, and mystic aspect. Stubb and Flask looked sideways from him; the honest eye of Starbuck fell downright.

“In vain!” cried Ahab; “but, maybe, 'tis well. For did ye three but once take the full-forced shock, then mine own electric thing, that had perhaps expired from out me. Perchance, too, it would have dropped ye dead. Perchance ye need it not. Down lances! And now, ye mates, I do appoint ye three cupbearers to my three pagan kinsmen there—yon three most honorable gentlemen and noblemen, my valiant harpooneers. Disdain the task? What, when the great Pope washes the feet of beggars, using his tiara for ewer? Oh, my sweet cardinals! your own condescension, that shall bend ye to it. I do not order ye; ye will it. Cut your seizings and draw the poles, ye harpooneers!”

Silently obeying the order, the three harpooneers now stood with the detached iron part of their harpoons, some three feet long, held, barbs up, before him.

“Stab me not with that keen steel! Cant them; cant them over! know ye not the goblet end? Turn up the socket! So, so; now, ye cup-bearers, advance. The irons! take them; hold them while I fill!” Forthwith, slowly going from one officer to the other, he brimmed the harpoon sockets with the fiery waters from the pewter.

“Now, three to three, ye stand. Commend the murderous chalices! Bestow them, ye who are now made parties to this indissoluble league. Ha! Starbuck! but the deed is done! Yon ratifying sun now waits to sit upon it. Drink, ye harpooneers! drink and swear, ye men that man the deathful whaleboat's bow—Death to Moby Dick! God hunt us all, if we do not hunt Moby Dick to his death!” The long, barbed steel goblets were lifted; and to cries and maledictions against the white whale, the spirits were simultaneously quaffed down with a hiss. Starbuck paled, and turned, and shivered. Once more, and finally, the replenished pewter went the rounds among the frantic crew; when, waving his free hand to them, they all dispersed; and Ahab retired within his cabin.
