MOKAPO 4. Panneau ya contre.

Ntango nalamukaki na ntongo elandi penepene na moi, nakutaki loboko ya Queequeg ebwakami likolo na ngai na lolenge ya bolingo mpe ya bolingo mingi koleka. Ozalaki pene ya kokanisa ete nazalaki mwasi na ye. Contre-pane ezalaki ya ba patchwork, etondi na ba carrés mpe ba triangles ya mikemike ya langi ya parti ya bizarre; mpe loboko oyo ya ye esalemaki na tatoo na esika mobimba na labyrinthe ya Crétain oyo esilaka te ya elilingi, oyo biteni mibale te na yango ezalaki ya elili moko ya sikisiki —mpo nakanisi ete na ndenge azalaki kobatela loboko na ye na mbu na ndenge ya kozanga mayele na moi mpe na elili, manzaka ya chemise na ye ekangamaki mbala na mbala na bantango ndenge na ndenge —lobɔkɔ oyo kaka ya ye, nalobi, elukaki mokili mobimba lokola eteni ya quilt wana kaka ya kosala ba patchwork. Ya solo, ndambo kolala likolo na yango lokola loboko esalaki ntango nalamukaki mpo na mbala ya yambo, nakokaki mpenza te koyeba yango uta na quilt, basangisaki mpenza langi na bango esika moko; mpe ezalaki bobele na liyoki ya kilo mpe mpasi nde nakokaki koyeba ete Queequeg azalaki koyamba ngai.

Mayoki na ngai ezalaki ya kokamwa. Tiká nameka kolimbola bango. Ntango nazalaki mwana moke, namikundoli malamu likambo moko ya mwa ndenge wana oyo ekómelaki ngai; ezala likambo ya solo to ndɔtɔ, nakokaki ata mokolo moko te kofanda mobimba. Ezalela ezalaki boye. Nazalaki kokata mwa caper to mosusu —nakanisi ezalaki komeka komata na cheminée, lokola namonaki mwa kosukola kosala mwa mikolo liboso; mpe mama mobali na ngai oyo, na lolenge moko to mosusu, azalaki ntango nyonso kobɛta ngai fimbo, to kotinda ngai na mbeto kozanga kolya bilei ya mpokwa, —mama na ngai abendaki ngai na makolo libanda ya litumbu ya mɔtɔ mpe akangaki ngai na mbeto, atako ezalaki kaka ngonga ya mibale na nsima ya midi ya mokolo ya 21 Yuni, mokolo oyo eleki molai na mbula na hémisphère na biso. Namiyokaki na ndenge ya nsɔmɔ. Kasi lisalisi ezalaki te mpo na yango, yango wana namatamaki na eskalye nakendaki na mwa shambre na ngai na etaze ya misato, namilongolaki bilamba malɛmbɛmalɛmbɛ lokola ekokaki mpo na koboma ntango, mpe na kopema ya bololo nakɔtaki kati na nkasa.

Nalalaki wana na mawa nyonso kosalaka calcul ete ngonga zomi na motoba mobimba esengeli koleka yambo nakoka kolikya lisekwa. Ngonga zomi na motoba na mbeto! moke ya mokɔngɔ na ngai ezalaki kosala ngai mpasi mpo na kokanisa yango. Mpe ezalaki pete mingi mpe; moi kongenga na kati na lininisa, mpe koganga monene ya ba coach na balabala, mpe makelele ya mingongo ya ba homosexuels na ndako mobimba. Nayokaki mabe mpe mabe koleka —nsukansuka natelemaki, nalataki, mpe nakitaki na malembe na makolo na ngai ya ba bas, nalukaki mama mobali na ngai ya mobali, mpe na mbalakaka namibwakaki na makolo na ye, kobondelaka ye lokola boboto moko ya sikisiki mpo na kopesa ngai glissement ya malamu mpo na ngai bizaleli mabe; eloko nyonso ya solo kasi kokweisa ngai na lokuta abed bolai ya ntango molai boye oyo ekoki koyika mpiko te. Kasi azalaki moto ya malamu koleka mpe ya lisosoli mingi koleka bamama mosusu, mpe na nsima nasengelaki kokende na shambre na ngai. Na boumeli ya bangonga mingi nalalaki wana na mpɔngi monene, koyokaka mabe mingi koleka oyo nasalaki uta wana, ata uta na mawa minene oyo elandaki. Nsukansuka nasengeli kozala ete nakweyaki na ndɔtɔ ya mpasi ya mpasi ya mpɔngi; mpe malembe malembe kolamuka uta na yango —ndambo nazindamaki na bandoto —nafungolaki miso na ngai, mpe shambre oyo ezalaki liboso oyo moi ezalaki kongenga ezingamaki sikawa na molili ya libanda. Na mbala moko nayokaki nsɔmɔ moko ezalaki koleka na kati ya kadre na ngai mobimba; eloko moko te esengelaki komonana, mpe eloko moko te esengelaki koyokama; kasi loboko moko oyo eleki ya bomoto emonanaki lokola etiamaki na oyo ya ngai. Loboko na ngai ekangamaki likolo ya contre-porte, mpe lolenge to phantom oyo ezangi nkombo, oyo ekoki kokanisama te, ya kimia, oyo loboko ezalaki ya yango, emonanaki lokola efandaki penepene pembeni ya mbeto na ngai. Mpo oyo emonanaki lokola mibu etondi na mibu, nalalaki wana, nakangamaki na malili na bobangi ya nsomo koleka, na mpiko te ya kobenda loboko na ngai mosika; nzokande kokanisa mokolo moko ete soki nakokaki kaka koningisa yango santimetele moko, likambo ya nsɔmɔ ekobukana. Nayebaki te lolenge nini bososoli oyo nsukansuka elongwaki mosika na ngai; kasi kolamuka na ntongo, naninganaki namikundolaki yango nyonso, mpe na boumeli ya mikolo mpe baposo mpe basanza nsima namibungisaki na milende ya kobulunganisa mpo na kolimbola libombami. Te, kino na ngonga oyo mpenza, mbala mingi namibulunganisaka na yango.

Sikawa, longola bobangi ya nsomo, mpe mayoki na ngai na koyoka loboko oyo eleki ya bomoto na oyo ya ngai ekokanaki mpenza, na kokamwa na yango, na oyo nakutanaki na yango na kolamuka mpe komona loboko ya bapakano ya Queequeg ebwakama zingazinga na ngai. Kasi na nsuka makambo nyonso ya butu eleki ezongaki na bososoli, moko na moko, na bosolo oyo ebongwani, mpe nsima nalalaki kaka na bomoi na mokakatano ya kosekisa. Mpo atako namekaki koningana na loboko na ye —kofungola mokaba na ye ya mobali ya libala —nzokande, kolala lokola azalaki, ayambaki ngai kaka makasi, lokola nde eloko mosusu te longola se liwa esengeli kokabola biso mibale. Nasalaki sikawa makasi mpo na kolamusa ye —“Queequeg!”—kasi eyano na ye kaka moko ezalaki konguluma. Na nsima, nabalukaki, nkingo na ngai ezalaki koyoka lokola nde ezali na ntolo ya mpunda; mpe na mbalakaka ayokaki mwa kozoka. Kobwaka pembeni panneau ya contre, kuna tomahawk elalaki kolala pembeni ya sauvage, lokola nde ezali bebe ya elongi ya hatchet. Pickle moko kitoko, ya solo, nakanisaki; abed awa na ndako moko ya mopaya na mokolo ya monene, na cannibal mpe tomahawk! “Queequeg!—na nkombo ya bolamu, Queequeg, lamuka!” Na nsuka, na nzela ya kobalubaluka mingi, mpe maloba ya makasi mpe ya kozanga kotika likolo ya kozanga kobonga ya koyamba moninga na ye ya mobali na lolenge wana ya lolenge ya libala, nalongaki kobimisa konguluma; mpe na ntango wana, azongisaki loboko na ye nsima, aminingisi nzoto mobimba lokola mbwa ya Terre-Neuve kaka uta na mai, mpe afandaki likolo na mbeto, makasi lokola nzete ya pike, kotalaka ngai, mpe kopangusa miso na ye lokola soki amikundolaki mobimba te lolenge nini nayaki kozala wana, atako bososoli ya molili ya koyeba likambo moko na ntina na ngai emonanaki lokola kongenga mokemoke likolo na ye. Na ntango wana, nalalaki na kimia kotalaka ye na miso, nazalaki na ntembe ya monene te sikawa, mpe nagumbamaki mpo na kotala na moke ekelamu moko oyo ezalaki na mposa ya koyeba makambo mingi. Ntango, na nsuka, makanisi na ye emonanaki lokola esalemi mpo na kosimba bizaleli ya moninga na ye ya mbeto, mpe akomaki, lokola, koyokana na likambo yango; apumbwaki libanda na nse, mpe na bilembo mpe makelele mosusu epesaki ngai kososola ete, soki esepelisi ngai, akolata liboso mpe na nsima akotika ngai nalata nsima, kotikaka ndako mobimba mpo na ngai moko. Akanisaka ngai, Queequeg, na se ya ba circonstances, oyo ezali ouverture moko très civilisée; kasi, bosolo ezali ete, ba sauvages oyo bazali na sens innée ya délicatesse, loba oyo olingi; ezali likambo ya kokamwa ndenge oyo bazali mpenza na bonkonde. Nafutaka longonya oyo en particulier na Queequeg, mpo a traité ngai na civilité mpe na considération mingi, alors que nazalaki na faute ya rudeness makasi; kotala ye na miso uta na mbeto, mpe kotalaka ba mouvements nionso ya toilette na ye; mpo na ntango wana mposa na ngai ya koyeba makambo ekómaki malamu koleka na kobokola na ngai. Pourtant, mobali lokola Queequeg oyo omonaka mikolo nionso te, ye na ba façons na ye ezalaki bien valeur inhabituelle considération.

Abandaki kolata na likoló na kolata shario na ye ya castor, oyo ezalaki molai mpenza, pembenipembeni, mpe na nsima —naino kolongola pantalon na ye —alukaki basapato na ye. Nini na nse ya lola asalaki yango mpo na yango, nakoki koyeba te, kasi mobulungano na ye oyo elandaki ezalaki ya kominyata —basapato na loboko, mpe shario na —na nse ya mbeto; ntango, uta na kopema ya mobulu ndenge na ndenge mpe kolɛmba nzoto, nakanisaki ete azalaki kosala mosala makasi mpo na komipesa na basapato; atako na mobeko moko te ya bosembo oyo nayokaki naino te, mobali moko asengeli kozala ye moko ntango azali kolata basapato na ye. Kasi Queequeg, omoni, azalaki ekelamu oyo ezalaki na eteni ya mbongwana —ezala nsɛlɛlɛ to papillon te. Azalaki kaka civilisé suffisant mpo na kolakisa outlandishness na ye na ba manières ya somo koleka. Boyekoli na ye esilaki naino te. Azalaki moyekoli ya mbala ya liboso. Soki azalaki na mwa degré civilisé te, mbala mosusu alingaki komitungisa ata moke te na basapato; kasi na nsima, soki azalaki naino moto ya zamba te, alingaki kolɔta ata mokolo moko te ete akokɔta na nse ya mbeto mpo na kolata yango. Na nsuka, abimaki na shario na ye oyo epanzani mingi mpe enyatamaki na nse likolo ya miso na ye, mpe abandaki koganga mpe kotɛngatɛnga zingazinga ya shambre, lokola nde, amesanaki mingi te na basapato, mibale na ye ya mposo ya ngombe oyo ezalaki na mai mpe ya kofinafina —mbala mosusu basalaki yango te ordre soit —efinafinaki nde mpe enyokolaki ye na mbala ya liboso ya ntongo ya malili makasi.

Komona, sikawa, ete ezalaki na ba rido te na lininisa, mpe ete balabala ezalaki moke mpenza, ndako oyo ezalaki na ngámbo mosusu epesaki mitindo ya kotala polele na kati ya shambre, mpe kotalaka mingi mpe mingi elilingi ya mabe oyo Queequeg asalaki, kokangama na nse na eloko mosusu mingi te kasi shario na ye mpe basapato na ye alataki; Nabondelaki ye malamu lokola nakokaki, mpo na kosala mwa mbangu na twalɛti na ye, mpe mingimingi akɔta na pantalon na ye nokinoki. Atosaki, mpe na nsima abandaki komisukola. Na ngonga wana na ntɔngɔ moklisto nyonso alingaki kosukola elongi; kasi Queequeg, na kokamwa na ngai, asepelaki na kopekisa ba ablutions na ye na ntolo, maboko, mpe maboko. Na nsima alataki kazaka na ye ya loketo, mpe azwaki eteni ya sabuni ya makasi na mesa ya katikati ya esika ya kosukola, azindisaki yango na mai mpe abandaki kosala mobulu na elongi. Nazalaki kotala mpo na komona esika wapi abombaki rasoir na ye, ntango lo mpe tala, azwi harpoon na coin ya mbeto, alongoli libanda ya stock molai ya mabaya, alongoli motó, abɛti yango mwa moke na sapato na ye, mpe azali komata na makolo tii na bit ya talatala na efelo, abandi kokatakata na makasi, to koloba malamu kobɛta matama na ye na harpoon. Akanisaka ngai, Queequeg, oyo ezali kosalela ba couvertures ya malamu koleka ya Rogers na vengeance. Na nsima namitunaki mingi te na lipaso oyo ntango nayaki koyeba bibende nini ya malamu motó ya harpoon esalemaka, mpe ndenge nini bansɔngɛ milai ya semba ebombamaka ntango nyonso na makasi mpenza.

Etikalaki ya twalɛti na ye eumelaki te, mpe abimaki na lolendo nyonso na shambre, azingamaki na jacket na ye monene ya nyama ya pilote, mpe abɛtaki harpoon na ye lokola lingenda ya maréchal.





MOKAPO 5. Na ntongo.

Nalandaki nokinoki, mpe kokita na shambre ya bar accosted nkolo ndako oyo azalaki kosɛka na esengo mpenza. Nazalaki kolinga mabe moko te epai na ye, atako azalaki kosala skylarking elongo na ngai mwa moke te na likambo ya moninga na ngai ya mbeto.

Nzokande, koseka malamu ezali likambo ya malamu ya nguya, mpe plutôt eloko ya malamu oyo ezali mingi te; mawa mingi koleka. Na yango, soki mobali moko, na moto na ye moko, akoki kosomba biloko mpo na liseki malamu epai ya moto nyonso, tika ete azonga nsima te, kasi tika na esengo nyonso amitika kolekisa mpe kolekisama na lolenge wana. Mpe mobali oyo azali na eloko moko ya koseka mingi na ntina na ye, zalá na ntembe te ete ezali na makambo mingi kati na mobali wana koleka oyo mbala mosusu ozali kokanisa.

Salle ya bar etondaki sikawa na ba boarders oyo bazalaki kokita na butu eleki, mpe oyo nazalaki naino kotala malamu te. Pene na bango nyonso bazalaki bato oyo balukaka baleine; baninga minene, mpe baninga ya mibale, mpe baninga ya misato, mpe basali-mabaya na mbu, mpe basali-mabaya na mbu, mpe basali-bibende ya mbu, mpe ba-harpooneers, mpe bakɛngɛli ya masuwa; kompanyi moko ya langi ya motane mpe ya makasi, oyo ezali na mandefu ya bosky; ensemble moko ya kokatakata te, ya shaggy, bango nyonso balati ba vestes ya singe mpo na ba robes ya tongo.

Okokaki mpenza koyeba ntango boni mokomoko na yango eumelaki na libongo. Litama ya elenge mobali oyo oyo ezali malamu ezali lokola poire oyo batumbaki na moi na langi, mpe ekomonanaka lokola ete ezali koyoka nsolo pene na lokola musky; akoki kozala te mikolo misato oyo akitaki na mabele uta na mobembo na ye ya Inde. Mobali wana oyo azali pembeni na ye azali komonana mwa ba nuances ya pete; okoki koloba ete mwa ndambo ya nzete ya satin ezali kati na ye. Na loposo ya ndambo ya misato ezali naino koumela tawnal moko ya mikili ya molunge, kasi oyo ekómi mwa moke na langi ya motane; na ntembe te aumelaki bapɔsɔ mobimba na libongo. Kasi nani akokaki kolakisa litama lokola Queequeg? oyo, oyo ekangamaki na langi ndenge na ndenge, emonanaki lokola nse ya wɛsti ya bangomba ya Andes, mpo na komonisa na molɔngɔ moko, klima oyo ekeseni, zone na zone.

“Grub, ho!” sikawa nkolo ndako agangaki, kobwaka kofungola ekuke, mpe na kati tokendeki kolia bilei ya ntongo.

Balobi ete mibali oyo bamoni mokili, na ndenge yango bakómaka mpenza na kimya na bizaleli, bamipelaka mpenza na bato. Kasi, ntango nyonso te: Ledyard, mobembo monene ya Nouvelle-Angleterre, mpe Mungo Park, oyo ya Écosse; kati na mibali nyonso, bazalaki na elikya moke koleka na salon. Kasi mbala mosusu bobele kokatisa Sibérie na luge oyo bambwa babendaka lokola Ledyard asalaki, to kotambola molai ya kotambola ye moko na libumu mpamba, na motema ya moindo ya Afrika, oyo ezalaki motuya ya masano ya mobola Mungo —lolenge oyo ya mobembo, . Nalobi, ekoki kozala te mode ya malamu mingi ya kozua polish social ya likolo. Atako bongo, mingimingi, likambo ya ndenge wana esengeli kozala na esika nyonso.

Makanisi oyo kaka awa euti na likambo oyo nsima ya kofanda biso banso na mesa, mpe nazalaki komibongisa mpo na koyoka mwa masolo ya malamu na ntina ya koboma baleine; na kokamwa na ngai ya moke te, pene na mobali nyonso abatelaki kimia ya mozindo. Mpe kaka yango te, kasi bamonanaki lokola bayokaki nsoni. Ee, awa ezalaki na etuluku ya bambwa ya mbu, oyo mingi na bango kozanga ata nsɔni ata moke te bamataki na baleine minene na mbu monene —bapaya mpenza mpo na bango —mpe babundaki na bango bakufaki kozanga kokanga miso; mpe atako bongo, awa bafandaki na mesa ya bilei ya ntongo ya bato —nyonso ya libiangi moko, nyonso ya elengi ya libota —kotalaka zingazinga lokola nsɔni moko na mosusu lokola nde bazalaki naino te na miso ya mwa etonga ya bampate na kati ya Ngomba ya Vert. Likambo moko ya kokamwa; ba ours oyo ya soni, ba baleines guerriers oyo ya soni!

Kasi mpo na Queequeg —mpo na nini, Queequeg afandaki wana kati na bango —na motó ya mesa, mpe, esalemaki na mbalakaka mpenza; malili lokola glace. Mpo na kozala na ntembe te nakoki koloba mingi te mpo na kobokola na ye. Moto oyo azalaki kosepela na ye mingi akokaki te kolongisa na bolingo nyonso ete amemaki harpoon na ye na bilei ya ntɔngɔ elongo na ye, mpe asalelaki yango kuna kozanga molulu; kosimbaka likolo ya mesa na yango, na likama oyo ezalaki pene ya mitó mingi, mpe kosimbasimba nyama ya ngombe epai na ye. Kasi yango esalemaki mpenza na malili mingi na ye, mpe moto nyonso ayebi ete na estimation ya bato mingi, kosala eloko nyonso na malili ezali kosala yango na boboto.

Tokoloba te ba peculiarités nionso ya Queequeg awa; ndenge azalaki kokima kafe mpe ba rulo ya mɔtɔ, mpe azalaki kotya likebi na ye oyo ekabolamaki te na nyama ya ngombe, oyo esalemaka mingi te. Ekoki, ete ntango bilei ya ntongo esilaki amilongolaki lokola basusu na kati ya shambre ya bato banso, apelisaki pipe na ye ya tomahawk, mpe afandaki wana na kimia kosilisaka mpe komela likaya na shario na ye oyo ekoki kokabwana te kolata, ntango na sallied libanda mpo na kotambola.





MOKAPO 6. Balabala.

Soki nakamwaki na ebandeli komona elili ya moto moko ya kokamwa lokola Queequeg oyo azalaki kotambola na kati ya bato ya bonkonde ya engumba moko ya sivilizasio, kokamwa wana eumelaki te ntango nasalaki kotambola na ngai ya liboso na moi na balabala ya New Bedford.

Na banzela oyo ezali pene na ba quais, libongo nyonso ya mbu ekopesa mbala mingi nzela ya kotala ba nondescripts oyo ezali komonana lokola queerest uta na bisika ya bapaya. Ata na balabala Broadway mpe Chestnut, bato oyo basalaka na masuwa na Méditerranée bakobɛta ntango mosusu na basi oyo bazali kobanga. Balabala Regent eyebani te mpo na bato ya Lascar mpe ya bato ya Malaisie; mpe na Bombay, na Apollo Green, mbala mingi ba Yankees oyo bazali na bomoi babangisaka bato ya mboka yango. Kasi New Bedford ebeti Water Street na Wapping nionso. Na bisika wana oyo tolobeli mpo na mbala ya nsuka omoni kaka bato ya masuwa; kasi na New Bedford, bato ya solosolo oyo balyaka bato batɛlɛmi kosolola na ba coins ya balabala; bato ya zamba ya zamba polele; mingi kati na bango bazali naino komema mikuwa na bango mosuni mosantu te. Esalaka ete mopaya atala na miso.

Kasi. Kuna poso na poso bakomaka na engumba oyo ebele ya mibali ya Vermont mpe ya New Hampshire ya vert, bango nyonso bazali na mposa ya litomba mpe nkembo na mosala ya koboma mbisi. Bazali mingimingi bilenge, ya bakadre ya makasi; baninga oyo bakati bazamba, mpe sikawa bazali koluka kobwaka likɔnga mpe kobɔtɔla lance ya baleine. Mingi bazali na vert lokola bangomba ya Vert esika bautaki. Na makambo mosusu okokanisa yango kasi mwa bangonga ya kala. Talá kuna! chap wana azali ko strutting na coin. Alati shario ya castor mpe kazaka ya mokila ya komela, ekangami na mokaba ya mokumbi masuwa mpe mbeli ya gaine. Awa eyei mosusu na sou'-wester na elamba ya bombazine.

Dandy moko te oyo abotami na engumba akokokana na oyo abotami na mboka —nalingi koloba dandy ya bumpkin ya solo —moninga oyo, na mikolo ya mbwa, akokata ba acres na ye mibale na ba gants ya mposo ya nyama mpo na kobanga kokata maboko na ye. Sikawa ntango dandy ya mboka lokola oyo azwi yango na motó na ye mpo na kozwa lokumu ya lokumu, mpe azali kosangana na mosala monene ya koboma baleine, osengeli komona makambo ya kosɛkisa oyo asalaka ntango akómi na libongo ya mbu. Na kolobaka elamba na ye ya mbu, azali kopesa mitindo ete bátya ba boutons ya ngonga na ba kazaka na ye ya loketo; ba sangles na ba pantalon na ye ya toile. Ah, mobola Hay-Seed! boniboni bololo ekopasuka bansinga wana na mopepe ya yambo ya koganga, ntango ozali kobengana, bansinga, bouton, mpe nyonso, na nse ya zolo ya mopepe makasi.

Kasi kanisá te ete engumba yango oyo eyebani mingi ezali kaka na bato oyo basalaka harpoon, bato oyo balyaka bato, mpe ba bumpkins mpo na kolakisa bapaya na ye. Ata moke te. Encore New Bedford ezali esika moko ya queer. Soki ezalaki biso bato oyo balukaka baleine te, mbala mosusu lelo oyo eteni wana ya mabele elingaki kozala na ezalela ya koganga lokola libongo ya Labrador. Ndenge ezali, biteni ya mboka na ye ya sima ekoki mpo na kobangisa moto, emonanaka lokola mikuwa. Engumba yango moko ezali mbala mosusu esika oyo bato balingaka mingi kofanda, na Nouvelle-Angleterre mobimba. Ezali mboka ya mafuta, ya solo ekoki: kasi lokola Kanana te; mboka, lisusu, ya masangu mpe vinyo. Balabala etambolaka na miliki te; mpe na eleko ya prɛnta, batyaka yango makei ya sika te. Kasi, atako bongo, esika moko te na Amerika mobimba okokuta bandako mingi oyo ezali lokola ba patriciens; baparke mpe bilanga oyo ezali na bozwi mingi, koleka na New Bedford. Bautaki wapi? ndenge nini balonaki likolo ya scoria oyo kala ezalaki scraggy ya mboka?

Kende kotala na ba harpoons ya emblema ya ebende zingazinga kuna ndako monene ya likolo, mpe motuna na yo ekozwa eyano. Iyo; bandako wana nyonso ya mpiko mpe bilanga ya bafololo eutaki na mbu ya Atlantique, ya Pasifike mpe ya Inde. Moko mpe nyonso, bakangamaki na nsinga mpe babendamaki likolo awa uta na nse ya mbu. “Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

“With this sin of disobedience in him, Jonah still further flouts at God, by seeking to flee from Him. He thinks that a ship made by men will carry him into countries where God does not reign, but only the Captains of this earth. He skulks about the wharves of Joppa, and seeks a ship that's bound for Tarshish. There lurks, perhaps, a hitherto unheeded meaning here. By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
