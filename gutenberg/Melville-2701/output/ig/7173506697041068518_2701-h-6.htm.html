Ugbua a na-eji obi ike malite n'elu omimi; ma n’oge na-adịghị anya, anyị ga-efunahụ ya n’oke oke mmiri ya na-enweghị oke. Ere nke ahu meru; t'ẹphe gbushia ụlo-ẹkwa-ndzukọ ono l'ẹnya-ufu Pekodu; na mmalite, ọ dị mma ịga n'ihe fọrọ nke nta ka ọ bụrụ ihe dị mkpa iji nweta nghọta zuru oke maka mkpughe leviathanic pụrụ iche na ntutụ aka nke ụdị niile ga-eso.

Ọ bụ ụfọdụ systematized ngosi nke whale ya sara mbara genera, na m ga-now fain tinye n'ihu gị. Ma ọ bụghị ọrụ dị mfe. Nkewa nke ihe mejupụtara ọgba aghara, ọ dịghị ihe ọ bụla ka edepụtara ebe a. Gee ntị n'ihe kacha mma na ndị ọchịchị kacha ọhụrụ tọgbọrọ.

Captain Scoresby, AD 1820 na-ekwu, "Onweghị alaka nke Zoology nke na-etinye aka na ya dịka nke a kpọrọ Cetology."

“Ọ bụghị ebumnobi m, ma ọ bụ n'ike m, ịbanye n'ajụjụ maka ezi ụzọ esi ekesa cetacea n'ime otu na ezinaụlọ. * * * Nnukwu mgbagwoju anya dị n’etiti ndị ọkọ akụkọ ihe mere eme nke anụmanụ a” (sperm whale), Surgeon Beale na-ekwu, AD 1839.

"Enweghị mma ịchụso nyocha anyị na mmiri na-enweghị ike ịghọta." "Mkpuchi na-enweghị atụ na-ekpuchi ihe ọmụma anyị banyere cetacea." “Ubi nke ahịhịa juru.” "Ngosipụta ndị a niile na-ezughị ezu ma na-ata anyị ahụhụ."

Otu a na-ekwu maka whale, nnukwu Cuvier, na John Hunter, na nkuzi, ọkụ ndị ahụ nke anụmanụ na anụ ahụ. Otú o sina dị, ọ bụ ezie na ezi ihe ọmụma dị ntakịrị, ma n'ime akwụkwọ e nwere ọtụtụ; ya mere na ụfọdụ obere ogo, na cetology, ma ọ bụ sayensị nke whale. Ọtụtụ bụ ndị ikom, ndị nta na ndị ukwu, ndị agadi na ndị ọhụrụ, ndị ọrụ ala na ndị ọrụ ụgbọ mmiri, ndị nwere nnukwu ma ọ bụ nke nta, e dere banyere whale. Na-achụpụ mmadụ ole na ole:—Ndị dere Bible; Aristotle; Pliny; Aldrovandi; Sir Thomas Browne; Gesner; Ray; Linæus; Rondeletius; Willoughby; Akwụkwọ ndụ akwụkwọ ndụ; Artedi; Sibbald; Brisson; Marten; Lacépède; Bonneterre; Desmarest; Baron Cuvier; Frederick Cuvier; John Hunter; Owen; Scoresby; Beale; Bennett; J. Ross Browne; Onye edemede Miriam Coffin; Olmstead; na Rev. T. Cheever. Mana maka ebumnuche zuru oke nke ihe ndị a niile deworo, ihe ndị a kpọtụrụ aha n'elu ga-egosi.

N'ime aha ndị dị na ndepụta a nke ndị edemede whale, ọ bụ naanị ndị na-eso Owen hụrụ whale dị ndụ; ma otu n'ime ha bụ ezigbo onye na-agba ụbọ akwara na whaleman. M pụtara Captain Scoresby. Na isiokwu dị iche iche nke Greenland ma ọ bụ aka nri-whale, ọ bụ ikike kacha mma dị. Ma Scoresby amaghị ihe ọ bụla ma ọ dịghị ekwu ihe ọ bụla banyere nnukwu sperm whale, ma e jiri ya tụnyere nke Greenland whale fọrọ nke nta ka erughị eru ịkọ. Ma ebe a ka ekwuru, na Greenland whale bụ onye na-anapụ n'oche-eze nke oke osimiri. Ọ bụghịdị n'ụzọ ọ bụla ọ bụ ya kasị ukwuu n'ime whale. N'agbanyeghị nke ahụ, n'ihi ogologo oge nke nkwuputa ya, na amaghị ama nke, ruo ihe dị ka afọ iri asaa gara aga, tinyere nnukwu sperm-whale ma ọ bụ nke a na-amaghị nke ọma, na nke amaghị ihe ruo ugbu a ka na-achị n'ime ihe niile ma ọ bụ ole na ole nlegharị anya sayensị. na ọdụ ụgbọ mmiri whale; nraranye a ezuola n'ụzọ niile. Ntụtụ aka n’ihe fọrọ nke nta ka ọ bụrụ ntutụ aka nile nke leviathan dị na nnukwu ndị na-ede uri n’oge gara aga, ga-emeju gị afọ na whale Greenland, na-enweghị otu onye mgba, bụụrụ ha eze nke oke osimiri. Ma oge eruola n'ikpeazụ maka mkpọsa ọhụrụ. Nke a bụ Charing Cross; nuru unu! ezi ndị mmadụ niile, — Greenland whale ewepụla, — nnukwu sperm whale na-achị ugbu a!

Enwere naanị akwụkwọ abụọ n'ime nke na-eme ka à ga-asị na ọ na-etinye ndụ spam whale n'ihu gị, ma n'otu oge ahụ, n'ogo dịpụrụ adịpụ ịga nke ọma na mgbalị ahụ. Akwụkwọ ndị ahụ bụ nke Beale na Bennett; ma na oge ha dọkịta na-awa na English South-Sea whale-ships, na ma kpọmkwem na ndị a pụrụ ịdabere na ndị ikom. Okwu mbụ na-emetụ sperm whale ga-ahụ na mpịakọta ha bụchaghị obere; mana ruo n'ókè ọ na-aga, ọ dị oke mma, n'agbanyeghị na ọ bụ naanị na nkọwa sayensị. Otú ọ dị, ka ọ dị ugbu a, sperm whale, ndị ọkà mmụta sayensị ma ọ bụ uri, adịghị ndụ zuru oke na akwụkwọ ọ bụla. Karịa whale ndị ọzọ a na-achụgharị, nke ya bụ ndụ edeghị ede.

Ugbu a ụdị dị iche iche nke whales chọrọ ụfọdụ ụdị nhazi ọkwa zuru oke, ma ọ bụrụ na ọ bụ naanị nkọwa dị mfe maka ugbu a, nke a ga-ejupụta na ngalaba ya niile site na ndị ọrụ na-esote. Ebe ọ bụ na ọ nweghị onye ka mma na-aga n'ihu iburu okwu a n'aka, m na-enye mbọ m adịghị mma. M na-ekwe nkwa ọ dịghị ihe zuru ezu; n'ihi na ihe ọ bụla mmadụ nke kwesịrị izu okè, n'ihi ya aghaghị ịbụ ihe na-ezighị ezi na-ezighị ezi. Agaghị m eme ka à ga-asị na ọ bụ nkọwa nke ụdị dị iche iche nke otu nkeji, ma ọ bụ—n'ebe a opekata mpe—n'ọtụtụ nkọwa ọ bụla. Ihe m mere ebe a bụ naanị imepụta usoro nhazi nke cetology. Abụ m onye na-ese ụkpụrụ ụlọ, ọ bụghị onye wuru ụlọ.

Ma ọ bụ ọrụ dị egwu; ọ dịghị nkịtị akwụkwọ ozi-sorter na Post-Office bụ hà ya. Iso ha n'azu ruda n'okpuru oké osimiri; inwe aka n’etiti ntọ-ala nile, ọgịrịga, na oke pelvis nke ụwa; nka bu ihe di egwu. Kedu ihe m bụ na m ga-ede edemede iji kpoo imi nke leviathan a! Njakịrị ndị ahụ jọgburu onwe ha dị na Job nwere ike ịmasị m. Ọ̀ (Leviathan) ọ̀ gāb͕a ndu; Le, olile-anya-Ya bu ihe-efu! Ma esiwo m n’ụlọ akwụkwọ gwuo mmiri wee banye n’oké osimiri; Ejila aka a na-ahụ anya mee ka m jiri whale mee; M na-anụ ọkụ n'obi; na m ga-agbalị. Enwere ụfọdụ mbido iji dozie.

Nke mbụ: Ọnọdụ a na-ejighị n'aka, ọnọdụ ejighị n'aka nke sayensị nke Cetology dị n'ime oghere nke eziokwu ahụ gosipụtara, na n'akụkụ ụfọdụ ọ ka na-anọgide na-enweghị isi ma whale ọ bụ azụ. N'ime usoro okike ya, AD 1776, Linnæus kwuru, "Ekewapụrụ m whale na azụ." Ma n’ihe m matara, amaara m na ruo n’afọ 1850, a ka hụrụ shark na shad, ndị na-azụ anụ na azụ asa, megidere iwu Linnæus, ka ha na Leviathan kesara ala nke otu osimiri ahụ.

Ihe mgbakwasị ụkwụ nke Linnæus gaara ada na achụpụwo whale n'ime mmiri, o kwuru dị ka ndị a: "N'ihi obi ha na-ekpo ọkụ, ngụgụ ha, nkuanya ha na-emegharị emegharị, ntị ha nwere oghere, penem intrantem feminam mammis lactantem," na n'ikpeazụ, "Ex lege naturæ jure meritoque." Enyere m ndị enyi m Simeon Macey na Charley Coffin, ndị Nantucket, ma ndị mụ na ha na-emekọ ihe n’otu njem njem, ma ha jikọtara ọnụ n’uche na ihe ndị e wepụtara ezughị ezu. Charley kwuru n'ụzọ rụrụ arụ na-egosi na ha na-akpa ike.

Ka amara na, na-ahapụ arụmụka nile, a na m ewere ezigbo ala ochie nke na whale bụ azụ, na-akpọku Jona dị nsọ ka ọ kwado m. Ihe a bụ isi ihe edoziri, isi ihe na-esote bụ, n'ụzọ dị n'ime, whale dị iche na azụ ndị ọzọ. N'elu, Linnæus enyela gị ihe ndị ahụ. Ma na nkenke, ha bụ ndị a: ngụgụ na ọbara ọkụ; ebe, azụ ndị ọzọ niile enweghị ngụgụ na ọbara oyi.

Na-esote: kedu ka anyị ga-esi kọwaa whale, site na mpụga ya doro anya, dị ka conspicuously ịkpọ ya maka oge niile na-abịa? Iji dị mkpụmkpụ, ya mere, whale bụ azụ na-agba agba nke nwere ọdụ kwụ ọtọ . N'ebe ahụ ka ị nwere ya. Agbanyeghị nkwekọrịta, nkọwa ahụ bụ nsonaazụ ntụgharị uche gbasaa. Walrus na-agbapụta dị ka whale, mana walrus abụghị azụ, n'ihi na ọ na-ama jijiji. Mana okwu ikpeazụ nke nkọwa ahụ ka na-amakarị nke ọma, dịka ya na nke mbụ. Ọ fọrọ nke nta ka ọ bụrụ na onye ọ bụla achọpụtala na azụ niile maara ndị na-arụ ọrụ ugbo enweghị ebe dị larịị, kama ọ bụ ọdụ kwụ ọtọ ma ọ bụ elu na ala. Ebe, n'etiti azụ ndị na-agbapụta, ọdụ ahụ, ọ bụ ezie na ọ nwere ike ịdị n'ụdị otu ahụ, na-ebukarị ọnọdụ kwụ ọtọ.

Site na nkọwa a dị n'elu nke ihe whale bụ, anaghị m ewepu n'òtù ụmụnna leviathan ọ bụla ihe e kere eke dị n'oké osimiri ruo ugbu a nke ndị Nantucketers mara mma mara na whale; ma ọ bụ, n'aka nke ọzọ, jikọta azụ ọ bụla a na-ewere dị ka ọbịa ruo ugbu a, na ya. Ugbu a, mgbe ahụ, bịa na nnukwu ngalaba nke ndị ọbịa whale niile.

*Amaara m na ruo ugbu a, ọtụtụ ndị ọkà n'okike na-esonye n'ime azụ̀ ndị a na-akpọ Lamatins na Dugongs (Pig-fish na Sow-fish of the Coffins of Nantucket). Ma dị ka azụ-ezi ndị a bụ ihe na-eme mkpọtụ, na-eleda anya, na-abụkarị ndị na-ezo n'ọnụ osimiri, na-eri nri na ahihia mmiri, na karịsịa ka ha na-adịghị apụ apụ, m na-agọnarị nzere ha dị ka whale; ma nyekwa ha akwụkwọ ikike ngafe ha ka ha hapụ ala eze Cetology.

Mbụ: Dị ka ịdị ukwuu si dị, m na-ekewa whale na akwụkwọ atọ bụ isi (nke a na-ekewa n'ime ISI), ndị a ga-aghọta ha niile, ma ndị nta ma ndị ukwu.

AKWỤKWỌ I. ( Folio ), ISI NKE I. ( Sperm Whale ) - whale a, n'etiti ndị Bekee nke oge ochie nke a na-amaghị dị ka Trumpa whale, na Physeter whale, na Anvil Headed whale, bụ Cachalot nke French ugbu a, na Pottsfich nke ndị German, na Macrocephalus nke ogologo Okwu. O doro anya na ọ bụ ya bụ onye kasị ukwuu n'ụwa; nke kachasi egwu nke whale niile ga-ezute; nke kachasị ebube n'akụkụ; na n'ikpeazụ, site na anya kasị baa uru na azụmahịa; ọ bụ naanị ihe e kere eke sitere na ihe ahụ bara uru, spermaceti, nwetara. A ga-agbasawanye ihe ya niile n'ọtụtụ ebe ndị ọzọ. Ọ bụ aha ya ka m ga-eme ugbu a. N'ihe gbasara nkà mmụta uche, ọ bụ ihe nzuzu. Ụfọdụ narị afọ gara aga, mgbe Sperm whale fọrọ nke nta ka a na-amaghị na nke ya kwesịrị ekwesị n'otu n'otu, na mgbe mmanụ ya bụ naanị na mberede nwetara site na tọrọ atọ azụ; N'oge ahụ, spermaceti, ọ ga-adị ka, a na-ewu ewu na-esi na ihe e kere eke yiri nke a maara na England dị ka Greenland ma ọ bụ Right Whale. Ọ bụkwa echiche ahụ, na otu spermaceti a bụ ihe ọchị nke Greenland Whale nke mkpụrụokwu mbụ nke okwu ahụ na-egosipụta n'ụzọ nkịtị. N'oge ndị ahụ, kwa, spermaceti dị ụkọ nke ukwuu, ọ bụghị iji ọkụ, kama ọ bụ naanị dị ka mmanụ na ọgwụ. Ọ bụ naanị ndị na-ahụ maka ọgwụ ga-enweta ya ka ị na-azụta otu ounce rhubarb n'oge a. Mgbe, dị ka m opine, na N'ezie nke oge, ezi ọdịdị nke spermaceti bịara mara, aha mbụ ya ka nọgidere na-ere ahịa; Obi abụọ adịghị ya na ọ ga-eme ka uru ya dịkwuo elu site n'echiche dị ịtụnanya dị ịrịba ama nke ụkọ ya. Ya mere, n'ikpeazụ, a ga-enyerịrị arịrịọ ahụ n'elu whale nke esitere na spermaceti a n'ezie.

AKWỤKWỌ I. ( Folio ), ISI II. ( Right Whale ) - N'otu akụkụ nke a bụ nke a kasị asọpụrụ nke ndị leviathans, ebe ọ bụ nke mbụ mmadụ na-achụgharị mgbe nile. Ọ na-ewepụta isiokwu a na-akpọkarị whalebone ma ọ bụ baleen; na mmanụ a maara nke ọma dị ka “mmanụ whale,” isiokwu dị ala na azụmahịa. N'etiti ndị na-akụ azụ̀, aha ndị a niile kpọpụtara ya n'amaghị ama: Whale; Greenland Whale; Oji whale; Nnukwu Whale; Ezi Whale; Whale ziri ezi. Enwere ihe dị mgbagwoju anya gbasara njirimara nke ụdị dị iche iche nke e mere baptizim n'ọtụtụ ebe. Kedu ihe bụ whale, nke m gụnyere na ụdị nke abụọ nke Folios m? Ọ bụ Great Mysticetus nke ndị na-ahụ maka ọdịdị ala bekee; Greenland Whale nke English whalemen; Baleine Ordinaire nke French whalemen; Walfish Growlands nke Swedes. Ọ bụ whale nke ndị Dutch na ndị Bekee na-achụgharị ihe karịrị narị afọ abụọ gara aga n'oké osimiri Arctic; ọ bụ whale nke ndị ọkụ azụ America na-achụ ogologo oge na oke osimiri India, na Brazil Banks, na Nor' West Coast, na akụkụ dị iche iche nke ụwa, nke ha họpụtara Right Whale Cruising Grounds.

Ụfọdụ na-eme ka hà na-ahụ ihe dị iche n'etiti Greenland whale nke Bekee na whale ziri ezi nke ndị America. Ma ha kwekọrịtara nke ọma n'ihe niile ha dị ebube; ma ọ bụ ka ewepụtabeghị otu eziokwu doro anya nke ga-esi na ya pụta. Ọ bụ site na nkewa na-adịghị agwụ agwụ dabere na ndịiche na-enweghị njedebe, na ngalaba ụfọdụ nke akụkọ ihe mere eme sitere n'okike na-agbagwoju anya nke ukwuu. A ga-agwọkwa whale ziri ezi n'ebe ọzọ n'ogologo ụfọdụ, na-ekwu maka ịkọwapụta sperm whale.

AKWỤKWỌ I. ( Folio ), ISI III. ( Fin-Back ) - N'okpuru isi a, m na-ewere nnukwu anụ ọhịa nke, site n'aha dị iche iche nke Fin-Back, Tall-Spout, na Long-John, a na-ahụ ihe fọrọ nke nta ka ọ bụrụ n'oké osimiri ọ bụla ma bụrụkarị whale nke ụgbọ elu ya dị anya. Ndị njem na-agafe Atlantic na-akọwakarị, na ngwugwu egwu New York. N'ogologo ọ na-erute, na n'ime baleen ya, Fin-azụ yiri whale ziri ezi, mana ọ nwere obere obere oghere, yana agba dị nfe, na-abịarute olive. Egbugbere ọnụ ya dị ukwuu na-enye akụkụ dị ka eriri, nke a na-emepụta site na njikọ na-ejikọta ọnụ, nke na-egbuke egbuke nke nnukwu wrinkles. Ihe ngosi ya dị ebube, nku, nke o si nweta aha ya, na-abụkarị ihe a na-ahụ anya. Ntụ a dị ihe dị ka kubit atọ ma ọ bụ anọ n'ogologo, na-eto n'ụzọ kwụ ọtọ site n'akụkụ azụ nke azụ, nwere ọdịdị akụkụ, yana njedebe nwere ntụ dị nkọ. Ọbụlagodi na a naghị ahụ akụkụ nke ọzọ nke ihe e kere eke, a ga-ahụ nku a dịpụrụ adịpụ, mgbe ụfọdụ, ka ọ na-apụta n'elu. Mgbe oké osimiri ahụ dị jụụ nke ukwuu, ma nwee obere ọnyà dị okirikiri, ma nhịhịa a dị ka gnomon guzoro ọtọ ma na-ekpudo n'elu ala ahụ wrinkled, ọ nwere ike iche na okirikiri mmiri gbara ya gburugburu yiri nke ọkpụkpọ, na ụdị ya na ya. ahịrị awa na-efegharị efegharị nke a kanyere na ya. Na Ahaz-ịkpọ oku, onyinyo na-agakarị azụ. The Fin-Back abụghị nnukwu. Ọ dị ka onye na-akpọ whale asị, dị ka ụfọdụ ndị ikom na-akpọ mmadụ asị. OCTAVOES.*—These embrace the whales of middling magnitude, among which present may be numbered:—I., the Grampus ; II., the Black Fish ; III., the Narwhale ; IV., the Thrasher ; V., the Killer .

*Why this book of whales is not denominated the Quarto is very plain. Because, while the whales of this order, though smaller than those of the former order, nevertheless retain a proportionate likeness to them in figure, yet the bookbinder's Quarto volume in its dimensioned form does not preserve the shape of the Folio volume, but the Octavo volume does.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER I. ( Grampus ).—Though this fish, whose loud sonorous breathing, or rather blowing, has furnished a proverb to landsmen, is so well known a denizen of the deep, yet is he not popularly classed among whales. But possessing all the grand distinctive features of the leviathan, most naturalists have recognised him for one. He is of moderate octavo size, varying from fifteen to twenty-five feet in length, and of corresponding dimensions round the waist. He swims in herds; he is never regularly hunted, though his oil is considerable in quantity, and pretty good for light. By some fishermen his approach is regarded as premonitory of the advance of the great sperm whale.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER II. ( Black Fish ).—I give the popular fishermen's names for all these fish, for generally they are the best. Where any name happens to be vague or inexpressive, I shall say so, and suggest another. I do so now, touching the Black Fish, so-called, because blackness is the rule among almost all whales. So, call him the Hyena Whale, if you please. His voracity is well known, and from the circumstance that the inner angles of his lips are curved upwards, he carries an everlasting Mephistophelean grin on his face. This whale averages some sixteen or eighteen feet in length. He is found in almost all latitudes. He has a peculiar way of showing his dorsal hooked fin in swimming, which looks something like a Roman nose. When not more profitably employed, the sperm whale hunters sometimes capture the Hyena whale, to keep up the supply of cheap oil for domestic employment—as some frugal housekeepers, in the absence of company, and quite alone by themselves, burn unsavory tallow instead of odorous wax. Though their blubber is very thin, some of these whales will yield you upwards of thirty gallons of oil.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER III. ( Narwhale ), that is, Nostril whale .—Another instance of a curiously named whale, so named I suppose from his peculiar horn being originally mistaken for a peaked nose. The creature is some sixteen feet in length, while its horn averages five feet, though some exceed ten, and even attain to fifteen feet. Strictly speaking, this horn is but a lengthened tusk, growing out from the jaw in a line a little depressed from the horizontal. But it is only found on the sinister side, which has an ill effect, giving its owner something analogous to the aspect of a clumsy left-handed man. What precise purpose this ivory horn or lance answers, it would be hard to say. It does not seem to be used like the blade of the sword-fish and bill-fish; though some sailors tell me that the Narwhale employs it for a rake in turning over the bottom of the sea for food. Charley Coffin said it was used for an ice-piercer; for the Narwhale, rising to the surface of the Polar Sea, and finding it sheeted with ice, thrusts his horn up, and so breaks through. But you cannot prove either of these surmises to be correct. My own opinion is, that however this one-sided horn may really be used by the Narwhale—however that may be—it would certainly be very convenient to him for a folder in reading pamphlets. The Narwhale I have heard called the Tusked whale, the Horned whale, and the Unicorn whale. He is certainly a curious example of the Unicornism to be found in almost every kingdom of animated nature. From certain cloistered old authors I have gathered that this same sea-unicorn's horn was in ancient days regarded as the great antidote against poison, and as such, preparations of it brought immense prices. It was also distilled to a volatile salts for fainting ladies, the same way that the horns of the male deer are manufactured into hartshorn. Originally it was in itself accounted an object of great curiosity. Black Letter tells me that Sir Martin Frobisher on his return from that voyage, when Queen Bess did gallantly wave her jewelled hand to him from a window of Greenwich Palace, as his bold ship sailed down the Thames; “when Sir Martin returned from that voyage,” saith Black Letter, “on bended knees he presented to her highness a prodigious long horn of the Narwhale, which for a long period after hung in the castle at Windsor.” An Irish author avers that the Earl of Leicester, on bended knees, did likewise present to her highness another horn, pertaining to a land beast of the unicorn nature.

The Narwhale has a very picturesque, leopard-like look, being of a milk-white ground colour, dotted with round and oblong spots of black. His oil is very superior, clear and fine; but there is little of it, and he is seldom hunted. He is mostly found in the circumpolar seas.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER IV. ( Killer ).—Of this whale little is precisely known to the Nantucketer, and nothing at all to the professed naturalist. From what I have seen of him at a distance, I should say that he was about the bigness of a grampus. He is very savage—a sort of Feegee fish. He sometimes takes the great Folio whales by the lip, and hangs there like a leech, till the mighty brute is worried to death. The Killer is never hunted. I never heard what sort of oil he has. Exception might be taken to the name bestowed upon this whale, on the ground of its indistinctness. For we are all killers, on land and on sea; Bonapartes and Sharks included.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER V. ( Thrasher ).—This gentleman is famous for his tail, which he uses for a ferule in thrashing his foes. He mounts the Folio whale's back, and as he swims, he works his passage by flogging him; as some schoolmasters get along in the world by a similar process. Still less is known of the Thrasher than of the Killer. Both are outlaws, even in the lawless seas.

Thus ends BOOK II. ( Octavo ), and begins BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ).

DUODECIMOES.—These include the smaller whales. I. The Huzza Porpoise. II. The Algerine Porpoise. III. The Mealy-mouthed Porpoise.

To those who have not chanced specially to study the subject, it may possibly seem strange, that fishes not commonly exceeding four or five feet should be marshalled among WHALES—a word, which, in the popular sense, always conveys an idea of hugeness. But the creatures set down above as Duodecimoes are infallibly whales, by the terms of my definition of what a whale is— ie a spouting fish, with a horizontal tail.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER 1. ( Huzza Porpoise ).—This is the common porpoise found almost all over the globe. The name is of my own bestowal; for there are more than one sort of porpoises, and something must be done to distinguish them. I call him thus, because he always swims in hilarious shoals, which upon the broad sea keep tossing themselves to heaven like caps in a Fourth-of-July crowd. Their appearance is generally hailed with delight by the mariner. Full of fine spirits, they invariably come from the breezy billows to windward. They are the lads that always live before the wind. They are accounted a lucky omen. If you yourself can withstand three cheers at beholding these vivacious fish, then heaven help ye; the spirit of godly gamesomeness is not in ye. A well-fed, plump Huzza Porpoise will yield you one good gallon of good oil. But the fine and delicate fluid extracted from his jaws is exceedingly valuable. It is in request among jewellers and watchmakers. Sailors put it on their hones. Porpoise meat is good eating, you know. It may never have occurred to you that a porpoise spouts. Indeed, his spout is so small that it is not very readily discernible. But the next time you have a chance, watch him; and you will then see the great Sperm whale himself in miniature.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER II. ( Algerine Porpoise ).—A pirate. Very savage. He is only found, I think, in the Pacific. He is somewhat larger than the Huzza Porpoise, but much of the same general make. Provoke him, and he will buckle to a shark. I have lowered for him many times, but never yet saw him captured.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER III. ( Mealy-mouthed Porpoise ).—The largest kind of Porpoise; and only found in the Pacific, so far as it is known. The only English name, by which he has hitherto been designated, is that of the fishers—Right-Whale Porpoise, from the circumstance that he is chiefly found in the vicinity of that Folio. In shape, he differs in some degree from the Huzza Porpoise, being of a less rotund and jolly girth; indeed, he is of quite a neat and gentleman-like figure. He has no fins on his back (most other porpoises have), he has a lovely tail, and sentimental Indian eyes of a hazel hue. But his mealy-mouth spoils all. Though his entire back down to his side fins is of a deep sable, yet a boundary line, distinct as the mark in a ship's hull, called the “bright waist,” that line streaks him from stem to stern, with two separate colours, black above and white below. The white comprises part of his head, and the whole of his mouth, which makes him look as if he had just escaped from a felonious visit to a meal-bag. A most mean and mealy aspect! His oil is much like that of the common porpoise.

Beyond the DUODECIMO, this system does not proceed, inasmuch as the Porpoise is the smallest of the whales. Above, you have all the Leviathans of note. But there are a rabble of uncertain, fugitive, half-fabulous whales, which, as an American whaleman, I know by reputation, but not personally. I shall enumerate them by their fore-castle appellations; for possibly such a list may be valuable to future investigators, who may complete what I have here but begun. If any of the following whales, shall hereafter be caught and marked, then he can readily be incorporated into this System, according to his Folio, Octavo, or Duodecimo magnitude:—The Bottle-Nose Whale; the Junk Whale; the Pudding-Headed Whale; the Cape Whale; the Leading Whale; the Cannon Whale; the Scragg Whale; the Coppered Whale; the Elephant Whale; the Iceberg Whale; the Quog Whale; the Blue Whale; etc. From Icelandic, Dutch, and old English authorities, there might be quoted other lists of uncertain whales, blessed with all manner of uncouth names. But I omit them as altogether obsolete; and can hardly help suspecting them for mere sounds, full of Leviathanism, but signifying nothing.

Finally: It was stated at the outset, that this system would not be here, and at once, perfected. You cannot but plainly see that I have kept my word. But I now leave my cetological System standing thus unfinished, even as the great Cathedral of Cologne was left, with the crane still standing upon the top of the uncompleted tower. For small erections may be finished by their first architects; grand ones, true ones, ever leave the copestone to posterity. God keep me from ever completing anything. This whole book is but a draught—nay, but the draught of a draught. Oh, Time, Strength, Cash, and Patience!
