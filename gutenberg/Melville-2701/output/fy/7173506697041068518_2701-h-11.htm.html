HAADSTIK 53. De Gam.

De skynbere reden wêrom't Achab net oan board gie fan 'e walfiskfarder dy't wy sprutsen hienen, wie dit: de wyn en de see tsjutten op stoarmen. Mar sels as dat net it gefal west hie, soe er ommers miskien net by har oan board west hawwe - nei syn folgjende hâlden en dragen by ferlykbere gelegenheden te oardieljen - as it sa west hie dat er troch it proses fan oprop in negatyf antwurd krigen hie op de fraach dy't hy stelde. Want, sa't it úteinlik die bliken, joech er him net om, sels foar fiif minuten, net mei in frjemde kaptein te konsortearjen, útsein dat hy wat bydrage koe fan dy ynformaasje dy't er sa ynnimmend socht. Mar dit alles mocht ûnfoldwaande skatten bliuwe, waerden hjir net hwat sein fan 'e eigenaardige gebrûken fan walfiskfarders by it moetsjen fan inoar yn frjemde seeën, en benammen op in mienskiplik krúsfjild.

As twa frjemdlingen oerstekke de Pine Barrens yn New York State, of de like desolate Salisbury Plain yn Ingelân; as se inoar tafallich tsjinkomme yn sokke ûngastfrije wylden, kinne dizze twaen, foar it libben fan har, in ûnderlinge groetnis net goed ûntkomme; en efkes stopje om it nijs út te wikseljen; en, faaks, in skoft sitten en rêste yn konsert: dan, hoefolle natuerliker dat op 'e ûnbeheinde Pine Barrens en Salisbury Plains fan' e see, twa walfiskfarders inoar beskriuwe oan 'e ein fan' e ierde - út iensume Fanning's Island , of de fiere King's Mills; hoefolle natuerliker, sis ik, dat dy skippen ûnder sokke omstannichheden net allinne hagel útwikselje, mar yn noch nauwer, freonliker en gesellicher kontakt komme. En binammen soe dat fanselssprekkend lykje, yn it gefal fan skippen dy't yn ien seehaven eigendom binne, en wêrfan de kapteins, ofsieren en net in pear fan 'e manlju inoar persoanlik bekend binne; en dêrtroch, hawwe allerhanne leave húslike dingen te praten oer.

Foar it lang ôfwêzige skip hat de bûtengrins, miskien, letters oan board; yn elts gefal, se sil der wis fan wêze dat se har wat papieren fan in datum in jier as twa letter hawwe litte as de lêste op har wazige en thumb-wearde triemmen. En yn ruil foar dy hoflikheid soe it nei bûten ta bûge skip de nijste walfiskfeartyntelliginsje krije fan 'e krúsgrûn dêr't se foar ornearre wêze kin, in ding fan it grutste belang foar har. En yn graad sil dit alles wier wêze oangeande walfiskfarders dy't elkoars spoar oerstekke op 'e krúsgrûn sels, ek al binne se likegoed lang ôfwêzich fan hûs. Want ien fan harren hat miskien in oerdracht fan brieven krigen fan in tredde, en no fier fuorthelle skip; en guon fan dy brieven kinne wêze foar de minsken fan it skip dat se no moetet. Boppedat soene se it walfisknijs útwikselje en in noflik petear ha. Want se soene net allinnich alle sympatyen fan seelju moetsje, mar ek mei alle eigenaardige noflikheden dy't fuortkomme út in mienskiplik stribjen en ûnderling dielde ûntefredenens en gefaren.

Ek soe it ferskil fan lân gjin heul wêzentlik ferskil meitsje; dat wol sizze, salang't beide partijen ien taal prate, lykas it gefal is mei Amerikanen en Ingelsk. Al komme, fansels, fan it lytse tal Ingelske walfiskfarders sokke gearkomsten net sa faak foar, en as se foarkomme, is der te skik om in soarte fan ferlegenens tusken har te wêzen; want dyn Ingelsman is nochal reservearre, en dyn Yankee, hy hat net fan soksoarte dingen yn immen oars as himsels. Boppedat beynfloedzje de Ingelske walfiskfarders soms in soarte fan metropoalyske oermacht boppe de Amerikaanske walfiskfarders; oangeande de lange, meagere Nantucketer, mei syn ûnbeskreaune provinsjalismen, as in soarte fan seeboer. Mar wêr't dizze oermacht yn 'e Ingelske walfiskfarders eins bestiet, soe it min te sizzen wêze, sjoen dat de Yankees op ien dei, kollektyf, yn tsien jier mear walfisken deadzje as alle Ingelsken, kollektyf. Mar dit is by de Ingelske walfiskjagers in ûnskuldich lyts kwea, dêr't de Nantucketer net folle oan it hert nimt; wierskynlik, om't hy wit dat hy hat in pear foibles sels.

Sa, dan sjogge wy dat fan alle skippen dy't apart de see farre, de walfiskfarders de measte reden hawwe om gesellich te wêzen - en dat binne dat ek. Wylst guon keapfardijskippen dy't inoars wekker yn 'e midden fan 'e Atlantyske Oseaan oerstekke, faaks sûnder safolle as ien wurd fan erkenning foarbygean sille, inoar op 'e hege see snije, as in beugel fan dandies yn Broadway; en de hiele tiid, miskien, oergean mei finyske krityk op elkoars rig. Wat Kriichslju oanbelanget, as se kâns hawwe om op see te moetsjen, dan geane se earst troch sa'n string fan dwaze bûgingen en skrabben, sa'n ducking fan fântekens, dat der net folle rjochts-down hertlike wolwêzen liket te wêzen en bruorrenleafde der hielendal oer. As oandwaanlike Slaveskippen gearkomste, wêrom, se binne sa'n geweldige haast, se rinne sa gau mooglik fan inoar ôf. En wat Piraten oangiet, as se de kâns hawwe om elkoars dwersbonken oer te stekken, is de earste hagel - "Hoefolle skulls?" - deselde manier as walfiskfarders hagel - "Hoefolle tonnen?" En dy fraach ienris beäntwurde, piraten stjoere daliks útinoar, want se binne infernale smjunten oan beide kanten, en sjogge net graach in protte fan inoars skandelike likenissen.

Mar sjoch nei de godlike, earlike, ûnferstannige, gastfrije, gesellige, frij-en-maklike walfiskfarder! Wat docht de walfiskfarder as se in oare walfiskfarder moetet yn elke soarte fan fatsoenlik waar? Se hat in " Gam ", in ding sa folslein ûnbekend foar alle oare skippen, dat se nea heard fan de namme sels; en as se der tafallich fan hearre soene, dan gnize se der allinnich mar om, en herhelje spultsjes oer "spouters" en "blubberboilers", en sa as moaie útroppen. Wêrom is it dat alle Keapman-seamers, en ek alle Piraten en Man-of-War syn mannen, en Slave-skip matroazen, koesterje sa'n spotsk gefoel foar Walfisk-skippen; dit is in fraach it soe wêze lestich te beantwurdzjen. Want by piraten, sis mar, ik soe graach witte wolle, oft dat berop fan har eigensinnige gloarje oer hat. It einiget wolris op ûngewoane ferheging; mar allinnich by de galge. En boppedat, as in man op dy frjemde manier ferhege wurdt, hat er gjin goede basis foar syn superieure hichte. Dêrfandinne, konkludearje ik, dat yn dy bewearing de piraat gjin fêste basis hat om op te stean, troch himsels te praetsje om heech boppe in walfiskman te stean.

Mar wat is in Gam? Jo kinne jo wiisfinger op en del rinne troch de kolommen fan wurdboeken, en it wurd nea fine. Dr. Johnson hat dy erudysje nea berikt; Noah Webster syn arke hâldt it net. Dochs is ditselde ekspressive wurd no in protte jierren yn konstant gebrûk west ûnder sa'n fyftjintûzen wier berne Yankees. Wis, it hat in definysje nedich, en moat wurde opnaam yn it Lexicon. Mei dy werjefte lit my it leard definiearje.

GAM. NAAM- In sosjale gearkomste fan twa ( of mear ) walfiskskippen, yn 't algemien op in krúsfjild; as se, nei it wikseljen fan hagel, besites útwikselje fan boatbemanningen: de twa kapteins bliuwe foarearst oan board fan it iene skip, en de twa haadmaten op it oare.

D'r is in oar lyts item oer Gamming dat hjir net fergetten wurde moat. Alle beroppen hawwe har eigen lytse eigenaardichheden fan detail; sa hat de walfiskfiskerij. Yn in piraten-, kriichs- of slaveskip, as de kaptein oeral yn syn boat roeide wurdt, sit er altyd yn 'e efterstekken op in noflike, soms dikke stoel dêr, en stjoert er him faaks mei in moai lyts molentsjertsje. fersierd mei gay koarden en linten. Mar de walfiskboat hat gjin stoel achterop, gjin sofa fan sa'n soart ek, en hielendal gjin helmstok. Hege tiden yndied, as walfiskfarders oer it wetter rieden waarden op rôlen as jichte âlde wethâlders yn patintstuollen. En wat in helmstok oanbelanget, de walfiskboat jout noait sa'n froulikens ta; en dêrom, lykas by it spieljen, moat in folsleine boatbemanning it skip ferlitte, en dêrom, om't de boatstuurder of harpoonier fan it nûmer is, is dy ûndergeskikte de stjoerman by de gelegenheid, en de kaptein, dy't gjin plak hat om yn te sitten, wurdt ôflutsen oan syn besite stiene allegearre as in pinebeam. En faaks sille jo fernimme dat bewust fan 'e eagen fan' e hiele sichtbere wrâld dy't op him rêste fan 'e kanten fan' e beide skippen, dizze steande kaptein is allegear libben foar it belang fan it behâld fan syn weardichheid troch it behâld fan syn skonken. Dit is ek gjin hiel maklike saak; want yn 'e efterkant slacht de ûnbidich útspringende stjoeroare him sa no en dan yn 't lyts fan 'e rêch, de nei-oar slacht tsjinoer troch syn knibbels foar te slaan. Hy sit dus foar en efter alhiel ynkeard, en kin him allinne mar sydlings útwreidzje troch del te setten op syn útrekkene skonken; mar in ynienen, gewelddiedich steech fan 'e boat sil faaks fier gean om him te fallen, om't lingte fan stifting neat is sûnder oerienkommende breedte. Meitsje gewoan in spriedingshoek fan twa peallen, en jo kinne se net stean. Dan, wer, it soe nea dwaen yn it sicht fan 'e wrald syn klinkende eagen, it soe nea dwaan, sis ik, foar dizze straddling kaptein te sjen sjen himsels steady it minste dieltsje troch it fangen fan wat mei syn hannen; ommers, as teken fan syn hiele, fleurige selsbehearsking, draacht er yn 't algemien syn hannen yn 'e bûsen fan 'e trowsers; mar miskien oer it algemien tige grutte, swiere hannen, draacht er se dêr foar ballast. Dochs hawwe der gefallen west, ek goed authentisearre, wêrby't de kaptein bekend wie foar in ûngewoan kritysk momint of twa, yn in hommelse bui sizze - om it hier fan 'e tichtstbyste roeier te pakken en dêr as in grimmitige dea fêst te hâlden.





HAADSTIK 54. It ferhaal fan 'e Town-Ho.

( As ferteld by de Gouden Herberch. )

De Kaap de Goede Hoop, en al it wettergebiet dêr omhinne, is in protte op guon opmurken fjouwer hoeken fan in grutte autodyk, dêr't jo mear reizgers treffe as yn in oar diel.

It duorre net lang nei it praten fan 'e Goney dat in oare thúsbûne walfiskman, de Town-Ho,* tsjinkaam. Se waard hast hielendal bemanne troch Polynesiërs. Yn de koarte gam dat folge se joech ús sterk nijs fan Moby Dick. Foar guon waard de algemiene belangstelling foar de Wite Wal no wyld ferhege troch in omstannichheid fan it ferhaal fan 'e Town-Ho, dy't ûndúdlik like te belûken by de walfisk in beskate wûnderlike, omkearde besite fan ien fan dy saneamde oardielen fan God dy't soms binne sei in ynhelje guon manlju. Dizze lêste omstannichheid, mei syn eigen bysûndere begeliedings, dy't it geheime diel foarmje fan 'e trageedzje dy't op it punt stiet te fertellen, hat nea de earen berikt fan kaptein Achab of syn maten. Want dat geheime diel fan it ferhaal wie de kaptein fan de Town-Ho sels ûnbekend. It wie it partikuliere eigendom fan trije konfederearre blanke seelju fan dat skip, fan wa't, sa't it liket, it oan Tashtego meidielde mei Roomske geheimhâldingsferbod, mar de folgjende nacht raffele Tashtego yn 'e sliep, en iepenbiere dêr safolle fan op dy wize. , dat er, as er wekker waerd, de rêst net goed tsjinhâlde koe. Dochs, sa'n machtige ynfloed hie dit ding op dy seelju yn 'e Pequod dy't der ta de folsleine kennis fan kamen, en troch sa'n frjemde delikatesse, om it sa te neamen, waarden se regele yn dizze saak, dat se it geheim hâlde tusken harsels, sadat it noait achter de haadmêst fan 'e Pequod barde. Troch dizze dûnkere tried op syn goede plak te ferweven mei it ferhaal sa't it iepenbier ferteld op it skip, de hiele nuvere affêre gean ik no fierder om op bliuwend rekord te setten.

*De âlde walfiskkreet by it earste sjen fan in walfisk fan 'e mêstkop, dy't noch altyd brûkt wurdt troch walfiskfarders by it jacht op 'e ferneamde Gallipagos-terrapin.

Om myn humor sil ik de styl behâlde wêryn't ik it ienris fertelde yn Lima, oan in lounging rûnte fan myn Spaanske freonen, ien hilligejûn, smokend op it dik-fergulde betegele piazza fan 'e Gouden Herberch. Fan dy moaie kavaliers wiene de jonge Dons, Pedro en Sebastiaan, op 'e nauwere ferhâldingen mei my; en dêrom de tuskenlizzende fragen dy't se sa no en dan stelle, en dy't op 'e tiid goed beäntwurde wurde.

"Sowat twa jier foarôfgeand oan myn earste it learen fan 'e barrens wêr't ik oan it repetearje foar jo, hearen, de Town-Ho, spermawalfisker fan Nantucket, krúste yn jo Stille Oseaan hjir, net in protte dagen nei it easten fan' e dak fan dizze goede Gouden Inn. Se wie earne nei it noarden fan 'e Line. Op in moarn by it behanneljen fan de pompen, neffens deistich gebrûk, waard konstatearre dat se mear wetter yn har hold makke dan gewoan. Se mienden dat in swurdfisk har stutsen hie, hearen. Mar de kaptein, dy't wat ûngewoane reden hie om te leauwen dat seldsume gelok him op dy breedtegraden wachte; en dêrom tige ôfkear fan har te ferlitten, en it lek waard doe hielendal net as gefaarlik beskôge, al koenen se it yndied net fine, nei't se it ruim sa leech as mooglik by frij swier waar socht hiene, gie it skip noch troch har cruises , de seefarders wurkje by de pompen mei brede en maklike yntervallen; mar der kaam gjin gelok; mear dagen gongen foarby, en net allinnich wie it lek noch net ûntdutsen, mar it ferstannich tanommen. Safolle noch, dat de kapitein, no't er wat alarmearre, alle seilen makke, fuort stie foar de tichtstby lizzende haven tusken de eilannen, om dêr syn romp út te hingjen en te reparearjen.

"Hoewol't der gjin lytse trochgong foar har wie, doch, as de meast foarkommende kâns begeunstige, hy wie hielendal net bang dat syn skip soe stifte troch de wei, om't syn pompen wiene fan de bêste, en wurde periodyk ûntlast op harren, dy seis- en tritich man fan him koene it skip maklik frij hâlde; it makket neat út as it lek op har ferdûbelje soe. Yn wierheid, tichtby de heule passaazje dy't bywenne waard troch heul bloeiende bries, wie de Town-Ho sa goed as wis yn perfekte feilichheid yn har haven oankommen sûnder it foarkommen fan 'e minste deaden, as it net wie foar de brutale oerhearsking fan Radney , de maat, in Wyngert, en de bitter útlokte wraak fan Steelkilt, in Lakeman en desperado út Buffalo.

''Lakeman! - Buffel! Bid, wat is in Lakeman, en wêr is Buffalo?' sei Don Sebastian, oprinnend yn syn swaaiende gersmatte.

"Op 'e eastlike igge fan ús Lake Erie, Don; mar — ik begear dyn hoflikens — kin wêze, dêr scille jimme gau fierder fan hearre. No, hearen, yn fjouwerkantseilbriggen en trijemêste skippen, sawat sa grut en stoer as elk dy't oait út dyn âlde Callao nei fier Manilla farre; dizze Lakeman, yn it lân-sluten hert fan ús Amearika, wie noch koestere troch al dy agraryske freebooting yndrukken folksmûle ferbûn mei de iepen oseaan. Want yn har trochrinnende aggregaat hawwe dy grutte swietwetterseeën fan ús, - Erie, en Ontario, en Huron, en Superior en Michigan, - in oseaan-like útwreiding, mei in protte fan 'e ealste eigenskippen fan 'e oseaan; mei in protte fan syn rimmed fariëteiten fan rassen en klimaten. Se befetsje rûne arsjipels fan romantyske eilannen, lykas de Polynesyske wetters dat dogge; foar in grut part, wurde shored troch twa grutte kontrastearjende folken, lykas de Atlantyske Oseaan is; se biede lange maritime oanpak nei ús tal fan territoriale koloanjes út it Easten, ferspraat oer har iggen; hjir en dêr wurde ferneatige troch batterijen, en troch de geit-like krappe gewearen fan hege Mackinaw; hja hawwe heard de float donderen fan marine-oerwinningen; mei tuskenskoften jouwe se har strannen oer oan wylde barbaren, waans read beskildere gesichten flitse út har peltry wigwams; want kompetysjes en kompetysjes wurde flankearre troch âlde en net-yngongen bosken, dêr't de magere dennen steane as serried linen fan keningen yn Goatyske genealogyen; dyselde bosken dy't wylde Afrikaanske rôfdieren herbergje, en seide skepsels wêrfan de eksportearre pels klean jouwe oan Tartarske keizers; se spegelje de ferhurde haadstêden fan Buffalo en Cleveland, lykas Winnebago-doarpen; hja driuwe likegoed it folopstutsen keapskip, de wapene kruser fan de Steat, de stoomboat en de beukenkano; se wurde swaaid troch Boreaanske en ûnthjittende klappen, sa eangstich as elk dy't de sâlte weach slacht; hja witte hwat skipswrakken binne, hwant út it sicht fen lân, ek yn it binnenlân, hawwe hja mannich middernachtskip mei al syn gûlende bemanning ferdronken. Sa, hearen, al wie in binnenlâner, Steelkilt wie wyld-osean berne, en wyld-osean koestere; safolle fan in dappere mariner as eltse. En foar Radney, hoewol't er yn syn bernejierren him miskien dellein hat op it ienige Nantucket-strân, om te ferplegen by syn memmesee; al hie er nei it libben lang ús strakke Atlantyske Oseaan en dyn kontemplative Stille Oseaan folge; dochs wie hy like wraaksinnich en fol sosjale rûzje as de eftergrûnse seeman, farsk fan 'e breedtegraden fan bokkehoarnen behannele Bowie-messen. Dochs wie dizze Nantucketer in man mei wat goedhertige trekken; en dizze Lakeman, in seeman, dy't, hoewol in soarte fan duvel, dochs troch ûnfleksibele fêstichheid, allinnich temperearre wurde kin troch dat mienskiplike fatsoen fan minsklike erkenning, dat it minste slaafsrjocht is; sa behannele, dizze Steelkilt wie lang ûnskuldich en docile bewarre bleaun. Yn alle gefallen hie er oant no ta bewiisd; mar Radney waerd fordoemd en gek makke, en Steelkilt — mar hearen, jimme scille hearre.

"It wie net mear as in dei as twa op it fierste nei't se har bocht foar har eilânhaven oanwiisde, dat it lek fan 'e Town-Ho wer tanimme, mar allinich om elke dei in oere of mear by de pompen te freegjen. Jo moatte witte dat yn in fêstige en beskaafde oseaan lykas ús Atlantyske Oseaan bygelyks guon skippers der net folle oan tinke om har hiele paad oer te pompen; hoewol fan in stille, sliepende nacht, soe de offisier fan it dek tafallich syn plicht yn dat opsicht ferjitte, de kâns soe wêze dat hy en syn skipsgenoaten it noait wer ûnthâlde, fanwegen alle hannen dy't sêft nei de boaiem sakke. Ek yn 'e iensume en wylde seeën fier fan jimme nei it westen ôf, hearen, is it alhiel ûngewoan dat skippen sels foar in reis fan oansjenlike lingte yn fol koar by har pompestokken bliuwe klotsjen; dat is, as it lizze lâns in tolerabel tagonklike kust, of as in oare ridlike retreat wurdt oanbean harren. It is pas as in lekkend skip yn guon hiel út 'e wei in part fan dy wetters is, guon echt lânleaze breedtegraad, dat har kaptein begjint te fiele in bytsje eangst.

“In protte sa hie it west mei de Town-Ho; dus doe't har lek waard fûn wer winst, der wie yn wierheid wat lytse soarch manifestearre troch ferskate fan har selskip; benammen troch Radney de mate. Hy gebea dat de boppeseilen goed ophelle wurde, op 'e nij nei hûs sketten en alle wizen útwreide nei de wyn. No, dizze Radney, nim ik, wie like min fan in leffert, en like min oanstriid ta elke soarte fan senuweftige eangst dy't syn eigen persoan oanrekke as elke eangstleaze, ûntinkende skepsel op lân of op see dat jo jo maklik foarstelle kinne, hearen. Dêrom, doe't er ferriede dizze soarch oer de feilichheid fan it skip, guon fan 'e seelju ferklearre dat it wie allinnich op rekken fan syn wêzen in part eigner yn har. Sa doe't se dy jûns by de pompen wurken, wie der op dizze kop gjin lyts spultsje slûchslimens ûnder har, as se stiene mei de fuotten hieltyd oerstreamd troch it kabbeljende kleare wetter; klear as alle berchmaiijen, hearen - dat bubbeljen fan 'e pompen rûn oer it dek en gie him yn fêste túten út by de lee skuorgaten.

"Nou, sa't jo goed witte, is it net selden it gefal yn dizze gewoane wrâld fan ús - wetterich of oars; dat as in persoan dy't it befel oer syn meiminsken ynsteld is, ien fan har fynt dat hy syn superieur is yn 'e algemiene grutskens fan 'e manlikheid, hy daliks tsjin dy man in ûnoerwinlike ôfkear en bitterens opfettet; en as er in kâns hat, sil er de toer fan dy subaltern dellûke en ferpulverje, en der in bytsje stof fan meitsje. Wês dizze eigensinnigens fan my, hearen, yn alle gefallen wie Steelkilt in heech en eal bist mei in holle as in Romein en in floeiend gouden burd as de kwastjes fan jo lêste vicekoning syn snoarjende lader; en in harsens, en in hert en in siel yn him, hearen, dy't Steelkilt Karel de Grutte makke hiene, as er soan berne wie fan Karel de Grutte. Mar Radney, de maat, wie ûnsjoch as in mul; dochs like hurd, as koppich, as kwea-aardich. Hy hâldde net fan Steelkilt, en Steelkilt wist it.

"Sjoch nei de maat dy't tichterby kaam doe't er mei de rest oan 'e pomp wurke, de Lakeman beynfloede him net te merken, mar ûnferwachts, gie troch mei syn homo's.

"'Ja, ja, myn fleurige jonges, it is in libben lekke dit; hâld in cannikin, ien fan jim, en lit ús hawwe in smaak. By de Heare, it is it botteljen wurdich! Ik sis jim wat, manlju, de ynvestearring fan âlde Rad moat der foar gean! hy hie it bêste syn part fan 'e romp ôfsnien en nei hûs slepe. “Upon hearing the frantic project of their leader, each in his own separate soul had suddenly lighted, it would seem, upon the same piece of treachery, namely: to be foremost in breaking out, in order to be the first of the three, though the last of the ten, to surrender; and thereby secure whatever small chance of pardon such conduct might merit. “But as the junior mates were hurrying to execute the order, a pale man, with a bandaged head, arrested them—Radney the chief mate. Ever since the blow, he had lain in his berth; but that morning, hearing the tumult on the deck, he had crept out, and thus far had watched the whole scene. “'You are a coward!' hissed the Lakeman.

“'So I am, but take that.' The mate was in the very act of striking, when another hiss stayed his uplifted arm. The three men were then cut down, all hands were turned to, and, sullenly worked by the moody seamen, the iron pumps clanged as before.

“Just after dark that day, when one watch had retired below, a clamor was heard in the forecastle; and the two trembling traitors running up, besieged the cabin door, saying they durst not consort with the crew. Entreaties, cuffs, and kicks could not drive them back, so at their own instance they were put down in the ship's run for salvation. Still, no sign of mutiny reappeared among the rest. On the contrary, it seemed, that mainly at Steelkilt's instigation, they had resolved to maintain the strictest peacefulness, obey all orders to the last, and, when the ship reached port, desert her in a body. But in order to insure the speediest end to the voyage, they all agreed to another thing—namely, not to sing out for whales, in case any should be discovered. For, spite of her leak, and spite of all her other perils, the Town-Ho still maintained her mast-heads, and her captain was just as willing to lower for a fish that moment, as on the day his craft first struck the cruising ground; and Radney the mate was quite as ready to change his berth for a boat, and with his bandaged mouth seek to gag in death the vital jaw of the whale.

“But though the Lakeman had induced the seamen to adopt this sort of passiveness in their conduct, he kept his own counsel (at least till all was over) concerning his own proper and private revenge upon the man who had stung him in the ventricles of his heart. He was in Radney the chief mate's watch; and as if the infatuated man sought to run more than half way to meet his doom, after the scene at the rigging, he insisted, against the express counsel of the captain, upon resuming the head of his watch at night. Upon this, and one or two other circumstances, Steelkilt systematically built the plan of his revenge.

“During the night, Radney had an unseamanlike way of sitting on the bulwarks of the quarter-deck, and leaning his arm upon the gunwale of the boat which was hoisted up there, a little above the ship's side. In this attitude, it was well known, he sometimes dozed. There was a considerable vacancy between the boat and the ship, and down between this was the sea. Steelkilt calculated his time, and found that his next trick at the helm would come round at two o'clock, in the morning of the third day from that in which he had been betrayed. At his leisure, he employed the interval in braiding something very carefully in his watches below.

“'What are you making there?' said a shipmate.

“'What do you think? what does it look like?'

“'Like a lanyard for your bag; but it's an odd one, seems to me.'

“'Yes, rather oddish,' said the Lakeman, holding it at arm's length before him; 'but I think it will answer. Shipmate, I haven't enough twine,—have you any?'

“But there was none in the forecastle.

“'Then I must get some from old Rad;' and he rose to go aft.

“'You don't mean to go a begging to him! ' said a sailor.

“'Why not? Do you think he won't do me a turn, when it's to help himself in the end, shipmate?' and going to the mate, he looked at him quietly, and asked him for some twine to mend his hammock. It was given him—neither twine nor lanyard were seen again; but the next night an iron ball, closely netted, partly rolled from the pocket of the Lakeman's monkey jacket, as he was tucking the coat into his hammock for a pillow. Twenty-four hours after, his trick at the silent helm—nigh to the man who was apt to doze over the grave always ready dug to the seaman's hand—that fatal hour was then to come; and in the fore-ordaining soul of Steelkilt, the mate was already stark and stretched as a corpse, with his forehead crushed in.

“But, gentlemen, a fool saved the would-be murderer from the bloody deed he had planned. Yet complete revenge he had, and without being the avenger. For by a mysterious fatality, Heaven itself seemed to step in to take out of his hands into its own the damning thing he would have done.

“It was just between daybreak and sunrise of the morning of the second day, when they were washing down the decks, that a stupid Teneriffe man, drawing water in the main-chains, all at once shouted out, 'There she rolls! there she rolls!' Jesu, what a whale! It was Moby Dick.

“'Moby Dick!' cried Don Sebastian; 'St. Dominic! Sir sailor, but do whales have christenings? Whom call you Moby Dick?'

“'A very white, and famous, and most deadly immortal monster, Don;—but that would be too long a story.'

“'How? how?' cried all the young Spaniards, crowding.

“'Nay, Dons, Dons—nay, nay! I cannot rehearse that now. Let me get more into the air, Sirs.'

“'The chicha! the chicha!' cried Don Pedro; 'our vigorous friend looks faint;—fill up his empty glass!'

“No need, gentlemen; one moment, and I proceed.—Now, gentlemen, so suddenly perceiving the snowy whale within fifty yards of the ship—forgetful of the compact among the crew—in the excitement of the moment, the Teneriffe man had instinctively and involuntarily lifted his voice for the monster, though for some little time past it had been plainly beheld from the three sullen mast-heads. All was now a phrensy. 'The White Whale—the White Whale!' was the cry from captain, mates, and harpooneers, who, undeterred by fearful rumours, were all anxious to capture so famous and precious a fish; while the dogged crew eyed askance, and with curses, the appalling beauty of the vast milky mass, that lit up by a horizontal spangling sun, shifted and glistened like a living opal in the blue morning sea. Gentlemen, a strange fatality pervades the whole career of these events, as if verily mapped out before the world itself was charted. The mutineer was the bowsman of the mate, and when fast to a fish, it was his duty to sit next him, while Radney stood up with his lance in the prow, and haul in or slacken the line, at the word of command. Moreover, when the four boats were lowered, the mate's got the start; and none howled more fiercely with delight than did Steelkilt, as he strained at his oar. After a stiff pull, their harpooneer got fast, and, spear in hand, Radney sprang to the bow. He was always a furious man, it seems, in a boat. And now his bandaged cry was, to beach him on the whale's topmost back. Nothing loath, his bowsman hauled him up and up, through a blinding foam that blent two whitenesses together; till of a sudden the boat struck as against a sunken ledge, and keeling over, spilled out the standing mate. That instant, as he fell on the whale's slippery back, the boat righted, and was dashed aside by the swell, while Radney was tossed over into the sea, on the other flank of the whale. He struck out through the spray, and, for an instant, was dimly seen through that veil, wildly seeking to remove himself from the eye of Moby Dick. But the whale rushed round in a sudden maelstrom; seized the swimmer between his jaws; and rearing high up with him, plunged headlong again, and went down.

“Meantime, at the first tap of the boat's bottom, the Lakeman had slackened the line, so as to drop astern from the whirlpool; calmly looking on, he thought his own thoughts. But a sudden, terrific, downward jerking of the boat, quickly brought his knife to the line. He cut it; and the whale was free. But, at some distance, Moby Dick rose again, with some tatters of Radney's red woollen shirt, caught in the teeth that had destroyed him. All four boats gave chase again; but the whale eluded them, and finally wholly disappeared.

“In good time, the Town-Ho reached her port—a savage, solitary place—where no civilized creature resided. There, headed by the Lakeman, all but five or six of the foremastmen deliberately deserted among the palms; eventually, as it turned out, seizing a large double war-canoe of the savages, and setting sail for some other harbor.

“The ship's company being reduced to but a handful, the captain called upon the Islanders to assist him in the laborious business of heaving down the ship to stop the leak. But to such unresting vigilance over their dangerous allies was this small band of whites necessitated, both by night and by day, and so extreme was the hard work they underwent, that upon the vessel being ready again for sea, they were in such a weakened condition that the captain durst not put off with them in so heavy a vessel. After taking counsel with his officers, he anchored the ship as far off shore as possible; loaded and ran out his two cannon from the bows; stacked his muskets on the poop; and warning the Islanders not to approach the ship at their peril, took one man with him, and setting the sail of his best whale-boat, steered straight before the wind for Tahiti, five hundred miles distant, to procure a reinforcement to his crew.

“On the fourth day of the sail, a large canoe was descried, which seemed to have touched at a low isle of corals. He steered away from it; but the savage craft bore down on him; and soon the voice of Steelkilt hailed him to heave to, or he would run him under water. The captain presented a pistol. With one foot on each prow of the yoked war-canoes, the Lakeman laughed him to scorn; assuring him that if the pistol so much as clicked in the lock, he would bury him in bubbles and foam.

“'What do you want of me?' cried the captain.

“'Where are you bound? and for what are you bound?' demanded Steelkilt; 'no lies.'

“'I am bound to Tahiti for more men.'

“'Very good. Let me board you a moment—I come in peace.' With that he leaped from the canoe, swam to the boat; and climbing the gunwale, stood face to face with the captain.

“'Cross your arms, sir; throw back your head. Now, repeat after me. As soon as Steelkilt leaves me, I swear to beach this boat on yonder island, and remain there six days. If I do not, may lightnings strike me!'

“'A pretty scholar,' laughed the Lakeman. 'Adios, Senor!' and leaping into the sea, he swam back to his comrades.

“Watching the boat till it was fairly beached, and drawn up to the roots of the cocoa-nut trees, Steelkilt made sail again, and in due time arrived at Tahiti, his own place of destination. There, luck befriended him; two ships were about to sail for France, and were providentially in want of precisely that number of men which the sailor headed. They embarked; and so for ever got the start of their former captain, had he been at all minded to work them legal retribution.

“Some ten days after the French ships sailed, the whale-boat arrived, and the captain was forced to enlist some of the more civilized Tahitians, who had been somewhat used to the sea. Chartering a small native schooner, he returned with them to his vessel; and finding all right there, again resumed his cruisings.

“Where Steelkilt now is, gentlemen, none know; but upon the island of Nantucket, the widow of Radney still turns to the sea which refuses to give up its dead; still in dreams sees the awful white whale that destroyed him. * * * *

“'Are you through?' said Don Sebastian, quietly.

“'I am, Don.'

“'Then I entreat you, tell me if to the best of your own convictions, this your story is in substance really true? It is so passing wonderful! Did you get it from an unquestionable source? Bear with me if I seem to press.'

“'Also bear with all of us, sir sailor; for we all join in Don Sebastian's suit,' cried the company, with exceeding interest.

“'Is there a copy of the Holy Evangelists in the Golden Inn, gentlemen?'

“'Nay,' said Don Sebastian; 'but I know a worthy priest near by, who will quickly procure one for me. I go for it; but are you well advised? this may grow too serious.'

“'Will you be so good as to bring the priest also, Don?'

“'Though there are no Auto-da-Fés in Lima now,' said one of the company to another; 'I fear our sailor friend runs risk of the archiepiscopacy. Let us withdraw more out of the moonlight. I see no need of this.'

“'Excuse me for running after you, Don Sebastian; but may I also beg that you will be particular in procuring the largest sized Evangelists you can.'

* * * * * *

“'This is the priest, he brings you the Evangelists,' said Don Sebastian, gravely, returning with a tall and solemn figure.

“'Let me remove my hat. Now, venerable priest, further into the light, and hold the Holy Book before me that I may touch it.

“'So help me Heaven, and on my honor the story I have told ye, gentlemen, is in substance and its great items, true. I know it to be true; it happened on this ball; I trod the ship; I knew the crew; I have seen and talked with Steelkilt since the death of Radney.'”
