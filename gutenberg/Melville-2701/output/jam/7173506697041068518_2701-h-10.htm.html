CHAPTER 49. Di Hyena.

Deh deh certain queer times an occasions inna dis strange mixed affair weh we call life wen a man tek dis whole universe fi a vast practical joke, though di wit deh him but dimly discern, an more dan suspects seh di joke is at nobody's expense but him own. “Queequeg,” seh mi, wen dem did drag mi, di laas man, to di deck, an mi did still a shake miself inna mi jacket fi fling off di wata; “Queequeg, mi fine fren, dis sort a ting often happen?” Widout much emotion, though soaked through jus like mi, him gi mi fi andastan seh such tings did often happen.

Yahso den, fram three impartial witnesses, mi did have a deliberate statement a di entire case. Mi survive miself; mi death an burial did lock up inna mi chest. Mi look roun mi tranquilly an contentedly, like a quiet ghost wid a clean conscience sitting inside di bars a one snug family vault.

CHAPTER 50. Ahab Boat an Crew. Fedallah.

“A who woulda did tink it, Flask!” Stubb baal out; “if mi did have only one leg yuh wouldn't ketch mi inna a boat, unless maybe fi stop di plug-hole wid mi timber toe. Oh! him a one wonderful old man!”

Among whale-wise people it have often been argued whether, considering di paramount importance a him life to di success a di voyage, it is right fi a whaling captain jeopardize dat deh life inna di active perils a di chase. So Tamerlane's soldiers often argue wid tears inna dem eyes, weda dat deh invaluable life a him shuda be carryed into di thickest a di fight.

But wid Ahab di kweschan did tek a modified aspect. As a general ting, di joint-owners a di Pequod mus did plainly tink not.

Ahab did well know seh although him fren dem a yaad woulda tink likkle bout him enterin a boat inna certain comparatively harmless vicissitudes a di chase, fi di sake a being near di scene a action an gi him orders in person, yet fi Captain Ahab fi have a boat actually apportioned to him as a regular headsman inna di hunt—above fi Captain Ahab fi get extra man wid five supplied dat deh same boat's crew, him well know seh such generous conceits neva did enter di head a di owners a di Pequod. So him neva did a solicit a boat's crew fram dem, an him neva did in any way hint him desires pan dat deh head. Nevertheless him did tek private measures a him own touching all dat matter. Til Cabaco's published discovery, di sailors dem did likkle foreseen it, though fi be sure wen, afta dem did a likkle while outa port, all hands did conclude di customary business a fitting di whaleboats fi service; wen some time afta dis Ahab did now an den find himself bestirring inna di matter a mek thole-pins wid him own han dem fi weh dem did tink seh a one a di spare boat dem, an even solicitously cutting di small wooden skewers dem, weh wen di line a run out dem pin ova di groove inna di bow: wen all dis did a observe inna him an particularly solicitously inna him haat coat sheathing inna di bottom a di boat, as if fi mek it beta widstand di pointed pressure a him ivory limb; an also di anxiety him evinced inna exactly shaping di thigh board, or clumsy cleat, as it is sometimes called, di horizontal piece inna di boat's bow fi brace di knee against inna darting or stabbing at di whale; wen dem did a observe how often him did a stan up inna dat deh boat wid him solitary knee fix inna di semi-circular depression inna di cleat, an wid di carpenter's chisel gouged out a likkle yah an straighten it a likkle deh; all dem ting deh, mi seh, did awaken plenty interest an curiosity at di time. But almost everybody did suppose seh dis particular preparative heedfulness inna Ahab mus only be wid a view to di ultimate chase a Moby Dick; fi him did already reveal him intention fi hunt dat deh mortal monster inna person. But such a supposition did by no means involve di remotest suspicion as to any boat's crew being assigned to dat deh boat.

Now, wid di subordinate phantoms, weh wonder did remain soon waned weh; fi inna a whaler wonders soon wane. Besides, now an den such unaccountable odds an ends a strange nations come up fram di unknown nooks an ash-holes a di earth fi man dem floating outlaws a whalers; an di ship dem demself often pick up such queer castaway creatures weh dem find a tossing bout di open sea pan planks, bits a wreck, oars, whaleboats, canoes, blown-off Japanese junks, an weh not; dat Beelzebub himself might climb up di side an step dung inna di cabin fi chat wid di captain, an it wouldn't create any unsubduable excitement inna di forecastle.

But be all dis as it may, certain it is dat while di subordinate phantoms soon find dem place among di crew, though still as it was somehow distinct fram dem, yet dat hair-turbaned Fedallah remained a muffled mystery to di last. Wen him come inna a mannerly worl like dis, by what sort a unaccountable tie him soon evinced himself fi be linked wid Ahab's peculiar fortunes; nay, so far as fi have some sort a half-hinted influence; Heaven know, but it mighta did even authority ova him; all dis nobadi neva know. But one cyaan sustain an indifferent air concerning Fedallah. Im was such a creature as civilized, domestic people inna di temperate zone only see inna dem dreams, an dat but dimly; but di like a who now an den glide mongst di unchanging Asiatic communities, especially di Oriental isles to di east a di continent—dem insulated, immemorial, unalterable countries, weh even inna dem modern days deh still preserve much a di ghostly aboriginalness a earth's primal generations, wen di memory a di first man was a distinct, an recollection all him descendants, unknowning weh him come fram, look pan each ada as real phantoms, an ask di sun an di moon why dem did mek dem an to weh end; wen though, according to Genesis, di angels indeed consorted wid di daughta dem a man, di devils dem also, add di uncanonical Rabbins, indulged in mundane amours.





CHAPTER 51. Di Spirit-Spout.

Days, weeks pass, an unda easy sail, di ivory Pequod did slowly sweep across four several cruising-grounds; dat off di Azores; off di Cape de Verdes; pan di Plate (so called), being off di mout a di Rio de la Plata; an di Carrol Ground, an unstaked, watery locality, southerly fram St. Helena.

A did while mi did a glide thru dem latter waters deh dat one serene an moonlight night, wen all di waves dem did a roll by like scrolls a silver; an, by dem soft, suffusing seethings, mek weh did look like a silvery silence, not a solitude; pan such a silent night a silvery jet did deh far inna di white bubbles dem at di bow. Lit up by di moon, it look celestial; did look like some plumed an glittering god did a up fram di sea. Fedallah fos diskraib dis jet. Fi dem moonlight nights deh, it was him wont fi mount to di main-mast head, an stand a look-out deh, wid di same precision as if it was day. An yet, though herds a whales did si by night, not one whaleman inna a hundred woulda venture a lowering fi dem. Yuh may tink wid weh emotions, den, di seamen dem did a si dis old Oriental perched aloft at such unusual hours; him turban an di moon, companions inna one sky. But wen, afta him spen him uniform interval deh fi several successive nights widout uttering a single sound; wen, afta all dis silence, him unearthly voice did hear a announce dat silvery, moon-lit jet, every reclining mariner staat up him foot dem as if some winged spirit did light inna di rigging, an hail di mortal crew. “A deh so she blow!” If di trump a judgment did blow, dem couldn't have quiver more; yet still dem neva feel no terror; radda pleasure. For though it was a most unwonted hour, yet so impressive was di cry, an so deliriously exciting, dat almost every soul on board instinctively desire a lowering.

Walking di deck wid quick, side-lunging strides, Ahab command di t'gallant sails an royals fi set, an every stunsail spread. Di bes man inna di ship haffi tek di helm. Den, wid every mast-head manned, di piled-up craft roll dung before di wind. Di strange, upheaving, lifting tendency a di taffrail breeze weh did a full di hollows a so many sails, mek di buoyant, hovering deck feel like air unda di feet dem; while still she rush along, as if two antagonistic influences did a struggle inna har—one fi mount direct to heaven, di other fi drive yawingly to some horizontal goal. An if yuh did watch Ahab face dat deh night, yuh woulda tink seh inna him too different tings did a war. While him one live leg did a mek lively echoes along di deck, every stroke a him dead limb did a sound like a coffin-tap. Pan laif an det dis uol man waak. But though di ship so swiftly sped, an though fram every eye, like arrows, di eager glances shot, yet di silvery jet neva seen more dat deh night. Every sailor swear seh him see it once, but not a secan time.

Dis midnight-spout did almost grow a forgetten ting, wen, some days afta, lo! at di same silent hour, it did announce again: again it did describe by all; but wen dem mek sail fi overtake it, once more it disappear as if it neva deh deh. An so it did a serve wi nait afta nait, til nobadi neva ier it bot fi wanda bout it. Mysteriously jetted inna di clear moonlight, or starlight, as di case might be; disappearing again fi one whole day, or two days, or three; an somehow seeming at every distinct repetition fi be advancing still further an further inna wi van, dis solitary jet seemed fi ever alluring us on.

Nor wid di immemorial superstition a dem race, an in accordance wid di preternaturalness, as it did look like, weh inna nuff tings did invest di Pequod, dem did deh deh wanting some a di seamen dem weh did swore dat whenever an weh dem did describe; at however remote times, or in however far apart latitudes and longitudes, dat unnearable spout was cast by one self-same whale; an dat deh whale, Moby Dick. Fi a time, deh reign, too, a sense a peculiar dread at dis flitting apparition, as if it did a treacherously beckoning wi on an on, in order dat di monster coulda turn round upon wi, an rend wi at last inna di remotest an most savage seas.

Dem temporary apprehensions, so vague but so awful, derived a wondrous potency fram di contrasting serenity a di weather, in which, beath all it blue blandness, some tink seh deh did a lurk a devilish charm, as fi days an days we voyage along, thru seas so wearily, lonesomely mild, dat all space, in vengageful, fi revenge did look like it did a vacate itself a life before wi urn-like prow.

But, at last, wen wi turn to di eastward, di Cape winds staat howling roun wi, an wi rise an fall pan di long, troubled seas dem weh deh deh; wen di ivory-tusked Pequod sharply bow to di blast, an gore di dark waves inna har madness, til, like showers a silver chips, di foam-flakes fly ova har bulwarks; den all dis desolate vacuity a life gaan weh, but gi place to sights more dismal dan before.

Close to wi bows, strange forms inna di wata did a darted hither an thither before wi; while thick inna wi rear did a fly di inscrutable sea-ravens. An every morning, perched pan wi stays, rows a dem birds deh did si; an spite a wi hootings, fi a long time obstinately clung to di hemp, as if dem did a consider wi ship some drifting, uninhabited craft; a ting weh appoint to desolation, an therefore fit roosting-place fi dem homeless selves. An heaved an heaved, still unrestingly heaved di black sea, as if its vast tides was a conscience; an di great mundane soul did inna anguish an remorse fi di long sin an suffering it did breed.

Cape a Good Hope, dem call yuh? Rather Cape Tormentoso, as called of yore; fi lang allured by di perfidious silences weh before did attend wi, wi did find wiself launched inna dis tormented sea, weh guilty beings did transform inna dem fowls an dem fish deh, did look like dem did a swim on everlastingly widout any haven inna store, or beat dat black air widout any horizon. But calm, snow-white, an unvarying; still a direct it fountain a feathers to di sky; still beckoning wi on fram before, di solitary jet woulda at times be described.

Durin all dis blackness a di elements, Ahab, though assuming fi di time di almost continual command a di drenched an dangerous deck, manifested di gloomiest reserve; an more seldom dan eva address him mates. Inna tempestuous times like dem yah, afta everyting above an aloft get secure, nutten more can be done but passively fi wait di issue a di gale. Den Captain an crew become practical fatalists. So, wid him ivory leg insert inna it accustomed hole, an wid one han firmly grasping a shroud, Ahab fi hours an hours woulda stan up a look dead to windward, while an occasional squall a sleet or snow woulda all but congeal him very eyelashes tugeda. Meantime, di crew weh did a drive fram di forward part a di ship by di perilous seas weh burstingly break ova it bows, did a stan up inna one line along di bulwarks inna di waist; an di beta fi guard against di leaping waves, each man did slip himself inna a sort a bowline secure to di rail, in which him swing as inna a loosened belt. Few or no words did a seh; an di silent ship, as if manned by painted sailors inna wax, day afta day tore on through all di swift madness an gladness a di demoniac waves. By night di same muteness a humanity before di shrieks a di ocean prevail; still inna silence di man dem swing inna di bowlines; still wordless Ahab did stan up to di blast. Even wen wearied nature did a demand repose him wouldn't seek dat deh repose inna him hammock. Neva coulda Starbuck forget di ole man's aspect, wen one night wen him did a go dung inna di cabin fi mark how di barometer did a stan, him si him wid closed eyes a sidong straight inna him floor-screwed chair; di rain an half-melted sleet a di storm fram weh him did have some time before did come out, still slowly dripping fram di unremoved hat an coat. Pan di table beside him lay unrolled one a dem charts a tides an currents weh dem did a taak bout before. Him lantern swing fram him tightly clenched han. Though di body did erect, di head did fling back so dat di closed eyes dem did a point towards di needle a di tell-tale weh did a swing fram a beam inna di ceiling.*

*Di cabin-compass dem call di tell-tale, cause widout going to di compass at di helm, di Captain, while below, can inform himself a di course a di ship.

Terrible old man! tink Starbuck wid a shier, sleeping inna dis gale, still yuh steadfastly a look pan yuh purpose.





CHAPTER 52. Di Albatross.

South-eastward fram di Cape, off di distant Crozetts, a good cruising ground fi Right Whalemen, a sail loomed ahead, di Goney (Albatross) by name. As she slowly did a draw nigh, fram mi lofty perch at di fore-mast-head, mi did have a good view a dat deh sight so remarkable to a tyro inna di far ocean fisheries—a whaler at sea, an long absent fram home.

As if di waves dem did fullers, dis craft did bleach like di skeleton a one stranded walrus. All dung har sides, dis spectral appearance did trace wid long channels a reddened rust, while all har spars an har rigging did like di thick branch a trees furred ova wid hoar-frost. Only har lower sails dem did set. A wild sight it was fi see har long-bearded look-outs at dem three mast-heads deh. Dem did look like dem did a wier di skin a beast, so dem did torn an bepatch di raiment weh did survive nearly four years a cruising. Dem did a stan op inna iron hoops weh did nail to di mast, an dem did a swing an swing ova a fathomless sea; an though, wen di ship slowly glide close unda wi stern, we six man inna di air come so nier to each other dat we might almost have leap fram di mast-heads a one ship to dem a di nex one; yet, dem forlorn-looking fishermen deh, mildly eyeing wi as dem pass, neva seh one word to wi own look-outs, while di quarter-deck hail did a hear fram below.

“Ship ahoy! Yuh si di White Whale?”

But as di strange captain, weh did a lean ova di pallid bulwarks, did a put him trumpet to him mout, it somehow fall fram him han inna di sea; an di wind now a rise amain, him inna vain strive fi mek himself heard widout it. Meantime him ship did still a increase di distance between. While inna various silent ways di seamen dem a di Pequod did a evincing dem observance a dis ominous incident at di first mere mention a di White Whale's name to anodda ship, Ahab fi a moment paused; it almost did look like him woulda did lower a boat fi board di stranger, if di threatening wind neva forbad. But tek advantage a him windward position, him again seize him trumpet, an knowing by har aspect seh di stranger vessel was a Nantucketer an shortly bound home, him loudly hailed—“Ahoy deh! Dis a di Pequod, bound round di worl! Tell dem fi address all future letters to di Pacific ocean dis time an if mi nuh deh home, tell dem fi address dem! tuh --"

At dat deh moment di two wake dem did fairly cross, an instantly, den, in accordance wid dem singular ways, shoals a small harmless fish, weh fi some days before did a swim placidly by wi side, did a darted weh wid weh did a look like dem did a shuddering fins, an did a range demself fore an aft wid di stranger's flanks. Though inna di course a him continual voyagings Ahab mus often before have notice a similar sight, yet, to any monomaniac man, di veriest trifles capriciously carry meanings.

“Swim weh fram mi, yuh?” murmured Ahab, gazing ova inna di wata. It did look like likkle inna di words, but di tone did a convey more a deep helpless sadness dan di insane old man did eva before evinced. Bot wen im ton tu di stierman, uu so far did a uol di ship ina di briiz fi diminish ar hedway, im baal out ina im uol laiyan vais,—“Up helm! Kip ar aaf roun di worl!”

Round di worl! A nuff ting deh inna dat deh sound fi inspire proud feelings; but weh all dat circumnavigation a gwaan? Only chruu numbaless perils to di very point weh wi staat, weh dem weh wi lef behind secure, did deh all di time before wi.

If dis worl did a endless plain, an by sailing eastward we coulda fi eva reach new distances, an discover sights more sweet an strange dan any Cyclades or Islands of King Solomon, den deh did have promise inna di voyage. But in pursuit a dem far mysteries we dream bout, or in tormented chase a dat demon phantom weh, some time or other, swim before all human hearts; wile dem a chase such ova dis round globe, dem either lead wi on inna barren mazes or midway lef wi whelmed.
