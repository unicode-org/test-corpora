CHAPTER 114. Di Gilder.

Penetrating further an further inna di heart a di Japanese cruising ground, di Pequod did soon all astir inna di fishery. Often, inna mild, pleasant weather, fi twelve, fifteen, eighteen, an twenty hours pan di stretch, dem did a engage inna di boats dem, steadily pulling, or sailing, or paddling afta di whales, or fi an interlude a sixty or seventy minutes calmly waiting dem uprising; though wid but likkle success fi dem pains.

At such times, unda an abated sun; afloat all day upon smooth, slow heaving swells; sidong inna him boat, light as a birch canoe; an so sociably mixing wid di soft waves demself, dat like hearth-stone cats dem purr against di gunwale; dem a di times a dreamy quietude, wen yuh behold di tranquil beauty an brilliancy a di ocean's skin, yuh forget di tiger heart weh pants unda it; an wouldn't willingly memba, dat dis velvet paw but conceals a remorseless fang.

Dis a di times, wen inna him whale-boat di rover softly feel a certain filial, confident, land-like feeling towards di sea; dat him regard it as so much flowery earth; an di distant ship weh did a show only di tops a har masts, look like it a struggle forward, not chruu high rolling waves, but chruu di tall grass a one rolling prairie: as wen di western emigrants dem horses only show dem erected ears, while dem hidden bodies widely wade through di amazing verdure.

Di long-drawn virgin vales; di mild blue hill-sides; as ova dem deh steal di hush, di hum; yuh almost swear seh play-wearied children lie sleeping inna dem solitudes, inna some glad May-time, wen di flowers a di woods dem pluck. An all dis mix wid yuh most mystic mood; so dat fact an fancy, half-way meeting, interpenetrate, an form one seamless whole.

Nor did such soothing scenes, however temporary, fail of at least as temporary an effect on Ahab. But if dem secret golden keys yah did look like dem did a open inna him him own secret golden treasuries, yet did him breath pan dem prove but tarnishing.

Oh, grassy glades! oh, ever vernal endless landscapes inna di soul; inna yuh,—though long parched by di dead drought a di earthy life,—in yuh, man yet may roll, like young horses inna new morning clover; an fi some few fleeting moments, feel di cool dew a di life immortal pan dem. Would to God dem blessed calms yah woulda last. But di mixed, mingle threads a life dem woven by warp an woof: calms crossed by storms, a storm fi every calm. No steady unretracing progress nuh deh inna dis life; wi no advance chruu fixed gradations, an at di laas wan pause:—thru infancy's unconscious spell, boyhood's thoughtless faith, adolescence's doubt (di common doom), den scepticism, den disbelief, resting at last inna manhood's pondering repose a If. But once wi go chuu, wi trace di round again; an dem a infant, bwoy, an man, an Ifs fi eva. Weh di final harbor deh, weh wi no unmoor no more? Inna weh rapt ether sail di worl, weh di weariest dem naa go eva weary? Weh di foundling faada hide? Wi soul dem a like dem orphan deh weh dem mada dem weh neva marid ded inna dem bear dem: di secret a wi paternity deh inna dem grave, an wi haffi deh deh fi laan it.

An dat deh same day, too, gazing far dung fram him boat's side inna dat deh same golden sea, Starbuck lowly murmured:—

“Loveliness unfathomable, as ever lover saw inna him young bride's eye!—Not tell mi bout yuh teet-tiered sharks, an yuh kidnapping cannibal ways. Mek faith oust fact; mek fancy oust memory; mi look deep dung an do believe.”

An Stubb, fish-like, wid sparkling scales, leap up inna dat deh same golden light:—

“Mi a Stubb, an Stubb have him history; but yah Stubb tek oaths seh him always did jolly!”





CHAPTER 115. Di Pequod Meets Di Bachelor.

An jolly enough was di sights an di sounds dem weh did a come bearing dung before di wind, some few weeks afta Ahab's harpoon did welded.

A did a Nantucket ship, di Bachelor, weh did jus wedge inna har last cask a oil, an bolt dung har bursting hatches; an now, inna glad holiday apparel, did joyously, though somewhat vain-gloriously, a sailing round mongst di widely-separated ships dem pan di ground, before shi did a point har prow fi home.

“Come abord, come abord!” di gay Bachelor's commander baal out, a lif op a glaas an a botel inna di ier.

“Yuh si di White Whale?” gritted Ahab in reply.

“Nuo, mi onggl ier bout im, bot no biliiv iina im at aal,” di ada wan se wid nof pien. “Come abord!”

“Not enough fi taak bout—two islanders, dat's all;—but come abord, old hearty, come along. Mi ago soon tek dat deh black fram yuh brow. Come along, will ye (merry's di play); a full ship an homeward-bound.”

“How wondrous familiar is a fool!” muttered Ahab; den loud, "Yuh a one full ship an homeward bound, yuh seh; well, den, call mi an empty ship, an outward-bound. So go yuh ways, an mi ago mine. Forward deh! Set all sail, an keep har to di wind!"

An so, while di one ship did a go cheerly before di breeze, di nex one stubbornly did a fight against it; an so di two vessels dem break; di crew a di Pequod a look wid grave, lingering glances towards di receding Bachelor; but di Bachelor's man dem neva did a heeding dem gaze fi di lively revelry dem did deh inna. An as Ahab, weh did a lean ova di taffrail, did a look pan di homeward-bound craft, him tek fram him pocket a likkle vial a sand, an den look fram di ship to di vial, an him did a look like dem did a bring two remote associations tugeda, fi dat deh vial did full a Nantuck sounds.





CHAPTER 116. Di Dying Whale.

Not seldom inna dis life, wen, pan di right side, fortune's favorites sail close by wi, wi, though all adroop before, catch somewhat a di rushing breeze, an joyfully feel wi bagging sails fill out. So it did look wid di Pequod. Fi di neks day afta dem did encounter di gay Bachelor, dem did si whales an four did get slain; an one a dem by Ahab.

It did far dong di aftanuun; an wen all di spearings a di crimson fight did done: an floating inna di lovely sunset sea an sky, sun an whale both stilly dead together; den, such a sweetness an such plaintiveness, such inwreathing orisons curled up inna dat deh rosy air, dat it almost did look like far ova fram di deep green convent valleys a di Manilla isles, di Spanish land-breeze, wantonly turned sailor, did gone to sea, freighted wid dem vesper hymns deh.

Soothed again, but only soothed to deeper gloom, Ahab, who did stern off fram di whale, did a sidong intently watching him final wanings fram di now tranquil boat. Fi dat strange spectacle weh yuh can observe inna all sperm whales dem a dead—di turning sunwards a di head, an so expiring—dat strange spectacle, beheld a such a placid evening, somehow to Ahab conveyed a wondrousness unknown before.

“Im turn an turn him to it,—how slowly, but how steadfastly, him homage-rendering an invoking brow, wid him last dying motions. Him too worship fire; most faithful, broad, baronial vassal of di sun!—Oh dat dem too-favouring eyes shuda see dem too-favouring sights yah! beyond all hum a human weal or woe; det whirl roun di corpse, an it head some other way.

“Oh, yuh dark Hindoo half a nature, who a drowned bones yuh build yuh separate throne somwe inna di heart a dem unverdured seas yah; yuh a one infidel, yuh queen, an yuh too truly a taak to mi inna di wide-slaughter Typhoon, an di hushed burial a it afta Northy don turn weh him ded. head, an den gone round again, widout a lesson to mi.

“Oh, trebly hooped an welded hip a power! Oh, high aspiring, rainbowed jet!—dat one strivest, dis one jettest all in vain! In vain, oh whale, dost thou seek intercedings wid yon all-quickening sun, dat only call out life, but give it not again. Yet dost thou, darker, rocker, prouder wid a half if mi darker faith. All thy unname imminglings float unda mi yah; mi a buoyed by breaths of once living tings, exhaled as air, but water now.

“Den hail, fi eva hail, O sea, inna whose eternal tossings di wild fowl find dem only rest. Born pan earth, but sucked by di sea; though hill an valley mother mi, unu billows a mi foster-breddas!”





CHAPTER 117. Di Whale Watch.

Di four whales dem weh dem did kill dat deh evening did dead wide apart; one, far to windward; one, less distant, to leeward; one ahead; one astern. CHAPTER 118. Di Quadrant.

Di season fi di Line at length did a come near; an every day wen Ahab, coming fram him cabin, cast him eyes aloft, di vigilant helmsman woulda ostentatiously handle him spokes, an di eager mariners quickly run to di braces, an woulda stan deh wid all dem eyes centrally fixed pan di nailed doubloon; impatient fi di order fi point di ship's prow fi di equator. Now, inna dat deh Japanese sea, di days inna summer a as freshets a effulgences. Dat deh unblinkingly vivid Japanese sun seems di blazing focus a di glassy ocean's immeasurable burning-glass. CHAPTER 119. Di Candles.

Warmest climes but nurse di cruelest fangs: di tiger a Bengal crouches inna spiced groves a ceaseless verdure. Skies di most effulgent but basket di deadliest thunders: gorgeous Cuba knows tornadoes weh neva swept tame northern lands. So, too, it is, dat inna dem resplendent Japanese seas di mariner encounter di direst a all storms, di Typhoon. Towards evening a dat deh day, di Pequod did get torn a har canvas, an bare-poled did lef fi fight a Typhoon weh did hit har directly ahead.
