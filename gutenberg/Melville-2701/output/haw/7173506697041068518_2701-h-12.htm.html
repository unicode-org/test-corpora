MOKUNA 55. No na kii weliweli o na kohola.

E pena aku au ia oe e like me ka mea hiki ole i ke kapa, e like me ke ano maoli o ke kohola e like me kona ike maoli ana i ka maka o ke kohola i kona kino ponoi e paa ana ke kohola ma ka aoao o ka moku okohola. hiki iā ia ke hehi ʻia ma laila. No laila, he mea pono paha ke hoʻolaha mua ʻia i kēlā mau kiʻi noʻonoʻo ʻino nona a hiki i kēia lā me ka wiwo ʻole i ka manaʻoʻiʻo o ka mea ʻāina. ʻO ka manawa kēia e hoʻoponopono ai i ka honua i kēia mea, ma ka hōʻoia ʻana i nā kiʻi o ke koholā he hewa ʻole.

E ʻike ʻia paha ke kumu mua o kēlā mau kiʻi hoʻopunipuni ma waena o nā kiʻi kiʻi kahiko o Hindoo, ʻAigupita, a me Helene. No ka mea, mai kēlā mau manawa noʻonoʻo ʻole, i ka wā ma luna o nā papa pōhaku o nā luakini, nā paepae o nā kiʻi, a me nā pale, nā mekala, nā kīʻaha, a me nā ʻāpana kālā, ua huki ʻia ka dolphin i nā unahi kaulahao e like me ko Saladin, a me ke poʻo i uhi ʻia e like me ke poʻo. St. George's; Mai ia manawa mai, ua lanakila ka laikini like, ʻaʻole wale ma nā kiʻi kaulana loa o ke koholā, akā ma nā hōʻike ʻepekema he nui nona.

I kēia manawa, ʻo ke kiʻi kahiko loa i manaʻo ʻia he koholā, ʻo ia ke kiʻi i loko o ke ana kaulana o Elephanta, ma India. Manaʻo ka poʻe Brahmins i loko o nā kiʻi ʻaneʻane pau ʻole o kēlā pagoda immemorial, ʻo nā ʻoihana a me nā ʻimi a pau, nā manaʻo a pau o ke kanaka, ua hoʻohālikelike ʻia i nā makahiki ma mua o ka hiki ʻana mai o kekahi o lākou. No laila, ʻaʻole ia he mea kupanaha, ma kekahi ʻano o kā mākou ʻoihana hanohano o ka lawaiʻa ʻana ma laila i uhi ʻia. ʻO ka koholā Hindoo i ʻōlelo ʻia, aia ma kahi ʻāpana ʻokoʻa o ka pā, e hōʻike ana i ke ʻano o Vishnu ma ke ʻano o leviatana, i ʻike ʻia ʻo Matse Avatar. Akā, ʻoiai he hapa kanaka a he hapalua kohola kēia kiʻi, no ka hāʻawi ʻana i ka huelo o ka hope, ʻo kēlā ʻāpana liʻiliʻi ʻo ia ka hewa. ʻOi aku kona ʻano me ka huelo liʻiliʻi o kahi anaconda, ma mua o nā poho ākea o nā ʻōpala nani o ke kohola maoli.

Aka, e hele i na Hale Kii kahiko, a e nana i keia manawa i ke kii o keia i'a a ke kanaka pena Kalikiano nui; no ka mea, ʻaʻole i ʻoi aku kona lanakila ma mua o ka Hindoo kahiko. ʻO ia ke kiʻi a Guido o Perseus e hoʻopakele ana iā Andromeda mai ke kai-monster a i ʻole ke koholā. Ma hea i loaʻa ai iā Guido ke kumu hoʻohālike o kahi mea ʻē e like me kēlā? ʻAʻole hoʻi ʻo Hogarth, i ka pena ʻana i ka hiʻohiʻona hoʻokahi i kāna "Perseus Descending," e hana i kahi mea ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi. ʻO ke kino nui o kēlā monster Hogarthian e hoʻoheheʻe ʻia ma ka ʻili, ʻaʻole hiki ke huki i hoʻokahi ʻīniha wai. He ʻano ʻano howdah ma kona kua, a ʻo kona waha ʻōpuʻu i loko o kahi e kaʻa ai nā ale, hiki ke lawe ʻia no ka Puka Kumakaia e alakaʻi ana mai Thames ma ka wai a hiki i ka Hale Kiaʻi. A laila, aia nā koholā Prodromus o Scotch Sibbald kahiko, a me Jonah's Whale, e like me ka mea i hōʻike ʻia ma nā paʻi o nā Baibala kahiko a me nā ʻokiʻoki o nā kumu mua. Pehea e olelo ai no keia mau mea? ʻO ke kohola o ka mea hoʻopaʻa puke e wiliwili ana e like me ke kumu waina a puni ke kumu o kahi heleuma e iho mai ana—e like me ka hoʻopaʻa ʻia ʻana a me ke gula ma ke kua a me nā ʻaoʻao poʻomanaʻo o nā puke kahiko a me nā puke hou—he mea nani loa ia akā nani maoli. hoʻohālike, lawe au iā ia, mai nā kiʻi like ma nā vases kahiko. ʻOiai ka inoa o ke ao holoʻokoʻa he dolphin, ua kapa naʻe au i kēia iʻa hoʻopaʻa puke he hoʻāʻo i ke koholā; no ka mea, ua manaʻo ʻia i ka wā i hoʻokomo mua ʻia ai ka mea hana. Ua hoʻolauna ʻia e kekahi mea hoʻopuka Italia kahiko ma kahi o ke kenekulia 15, i ka wā o ka Revival of Learning; a i kēlā mau lā, a hiki i ka manawa like ʻole, ua manaʻo nui ʻia nā iʻa he ʻano o ka Leviatana.

I loko o nā kiʻi a me nā mea hoʻonani ʻē aʻe o kekahi mau puke kahiko e hui ai ʻoe i kekahi manawa me nā mea hoihoi loa i ke koholā, kahi e puka mai ai nā ʻano puna like ʻole, nā jet dʻeau, nā pūnāwai wela a me ke anu, ʻo Saratoga a me Baden-Baden. lolo pau ole. Ma ka ʻaoʻao poʻomanaʻo o ka paʻi kumu o ka "Advancement of Learning" e ʻike ai ʻoe i kekahi mau koholā.

Akā, ke haʻalele nei i kēia mau hoʻāʻo pono ʻole, e nānā kākou i kēlā mau kiʻi o leviatana i manaʻo ʻia he ʻano kuoʻo, wehewehe ʻepekema, e ka poʻe ʻike. I loko o ka hōʻiliʻili o nā huakaʻi kahiko a Harris aia kekahi mau papa koholā i unuhi ʻia mai kahi puke huakaʻi Dutch, AD 1671, i kapa ʻia ʻo "A Whaling Voyage to Spitzbergen in the ship Jonas in the Whale, Peter Peterson of Friesland, master." Ma kekahi o ia mau papa, ua hoikeia na kohola, e like me na laau laau nui, e moe ana mawaena o na mokupuni hau, me na bea keokeo e holo ana ma ko lakou kua. Ma kekahi papa ʻē aʻe, hana ʻia ka blunder prodigious no ka hōʻike ʻana i ke koholā me nā flukes perpendicular.

Eia hou, aia kekahi quarto hanohano, i kakauia e kekahi Kapena Colnett, he Kapena Leta ma ka mokukaua Enelani, i kapaia "A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, for the purpose of extending the Spermaceti Whale Fisheries." Aia i loko o kēia puke kekahi papa kuhikuhi i manaʻo ʻia he "Picture of a Physeter or Spermaceti whale, i kaha ʻia ma ka unahi mai kekahi i pepehi ʻia ma kahakai o Mekiko, ʻAukake, 1793, a kau ʻia ma luna o ka papa." ʻAʻole oʻu kanalua ua kiʻi ʻia kēia kiʻi ʻoiaʻiʻo i ke kapena no ka pono o kāna mau koa. Hoʻokahi wale nō mea e pili ana i ia mea, e ʻae iaʻu e ʻōlelo aku he maka i pili, e like me ka unahi e pili pū ana, i ke kohola sperm ulu piha, e hoʻolilo i ka maka o kēlā koholā i puka aniani ma kahi o ʻelima kapuaʻi ka lōʻihi. Auē, e kuʻu kapena koa, no ke aha lā ʻoe i hāʻawi ʻole mai ai iā Iona e nānā i waho o kēlā maka!

ʻAʻole nō hoʻi nā hui ʻoi loa o ka Natural History no ka pono o ka ʻōpio a me ka palupalu, ʻaʻole i kaʻawale i ka hewa like ʻole. E nānā i kēlā hana kaulana "Goldsmith's Animated Nature." Ma ka paʻi pōkole ʻo Lākana o ka makahiki 1807, aia nā papa o ka "whale" a me ka "narwhale." ʻAʻole oʻu makemake e ʻike ʻole ʻia, akā, ua like kēia koholā maikaʻi ʻole me ka puaʻa i ʻoki ʻia; a no ka narwhale, ua lawa ka nana aku i keia mea e kahaha ai, i keia keneturia umikumamaiwa, ua hiki ke hoohana ia keia hippogriff i ka lima maoli maluna o kekahi lehulehu naauao o na keiki kula.

A laila, i ka makahiki 1825, ua paʻi ʻo Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, he kanaka ʻike kūlohelohe, i kahi puke koholā i hoʻonohonoho ʻia i ka ʻepekema, aia nā kiʻi he nui o nā ʻano like ʻole o ka Leviathan. ʻAʻole hewa wale kēia mau mea a pau, akā ʻo ke kiʻi o ke koholā Mysticetus a i ʻole Greenland (ʻo ia hoʻi, ʻo ke kohola ʻākau), ʻoiai ʻo Scoresby, he kanaka ʻike lōʻihi e pili ana i kēlā ʻano, ʻōlelo ʻo ia ʻaʻole i loaʻa kona ʻano like.

Akā ʻo ke kau ʻana i ka puʻu pāpale i kēia ʻoihana blundering āpau i mālama ʻia no ka ʻepekema ʻo Frederick Cuvier, ke kaikaina o ka Baron kaulana. I ka makahiki 1836, ua paʻi ʻo ia i kahi Natural History of Whales, kahi āna i hāʻawi ai i ke kiʻi o ka Sperm Whale. Ma mua o ka hōʻike ʻana i kēlā kiʻi i kekahi Nantucketer, pono ʻoe e hoʻolako i kāu hōʻuluʻulu hoʻi ʻana mai Nantucket. ʻO ka huaʻōlelo, ʻaʻole ʻo Frederick Cuvier's Sperm Whale he Sperm Whale, akā he ʻōpae. ʻOiaʻiʻo, ʻaʻole i loaʻa iā ia ka pōmaikaʻi o ka holo ʻana i ke koholā (kakaikahi ka poʻe i loaʻa i kēlā mau kāne), akā no hea mai ia kiʻi i loaʻa mai ai, ʻo wai ka mea e ʻike? Ua loaʻa paha iā ia ma ke ʻano he mea ʻepekema mua ma ia kahua hoʻokahi, ʻo Desmarest, ua loaʻa i kekahi o kāna mau hoʻohemo ʻoiaʻiʻo; ʻo ia hoʻi, mai kahi kiʻi Pake. A he aha ke ʻano o nā keiki ʻoliʻoli me ka penikala ʻo ia mau Pake, nui nā kīʻaha a me nā kīʻaha e hōʻike mai iā mākou.

No na kohola kii hoailona i ikeia ma na alanui e kau ana maluna o na hale kuai aila, pehea la lakou? ʻO lākou ʻo Richard III. nā koholā, me nā puʻu dromedary, a me nā mea ʻino loa; ʻO ka ʻaina kakahiaka i ʻekolu a ʻehā paha luina tart, ʻo ia hoʻi nā waʻa kohola i piha i nā luina: ʻo ko lākou mau deformations e kahe ana i ke kai o ke koko a me ka pena uliuli.

Akā, ʻaʻole ia he mea kupanaha loa kēia mau hewa he nui i ka hōʻike ʻana i ke koholā. E noʻonoʻo! Ua lawe ʻia ka hapa nui o nā kiʻi ʻepekema mai ka iʻa i pae ʻia; a ua like loa keia mau mea me ke kaha kii o ka moku nahaha, me ke kua i haki, e hoike pololei ana i ka holoholona hanohano iloko o kona haaheo pau ole o ka hull a me na spars. ʻOiai ua kū nā ʻelepani no ko lākou mau lōʻihi, ʻaʻole i lana maikaʻi ka Leviatana ola iā ia iho no kāna kiʻi. ʻO ke koholā ola, i kona nani a me kona ʻano nui, ʻike ʻia ma ke kai i nā wai hiki ʻole ke ʻike ʻia; a e lana ana ka hapa nui o ia i ka ike ole ia, me he moku kaua la; a mailoko mai o ia mea he mea hiki ole i ke kanaka kino ke hookiekie ia ia ma ke kino i ka lewa, i mea e malama ai i kona mau pehu a me na ohe. A ʻaʻole hoʻi e ʻōlelo i ka ʻokoʻa i manaʻo nui ʻia ma waena o kahi kohola omo ʻōpiopio a me kahi Leviatana Platonian nui; aka, ina no kekahi o kela mau kohola omo waiu opio i kauia maluna o ka papa moku, pela no ke ano okoa, like me ka puhi, a me kona ano like ole, i hiki ole i ka diabolo ke hopu i kana olelo pololei.

Akā, manaʻo ʻia, mai ka iwi ʻōlohelohe o ke koholā i pae ʻia, hiki ke loaʻa nā ʻōlelo hōʻike pololei e pili ana i kona ʻano maoli. ʻaʻole iki. No ka mea, ʻo ia kekahi o nā mea ʻē aʻe e pili ana i kēia Leviatana, ʻo kona iwi iwi e hāʻawi liʻiliʻi loa i kona ʻano nui. ʻOiai ʻo ka iwi o Jeremy Bentham, e kau ana no ka ipukukui i loko o ka hale waihona puke o kekahi o kāna mau mea hoʻokō, hōʻike pololei i ka manaʻo o kahi kanaka kahiko utilitarian ʻulaʻula, me nā hiʻohiʻona pilikino ʻē aʻe o Jeremy; ʻAʻohe mea o kēia ʻano i hiki ke hoʻomaopopo ʻia mai nā iwi o ka leviatana. ʻO ka ʻoiaʻiʻo, e like me ka ʻōlelo a ka Hunter nui, ʻo ka ʻiʻo wale nō o ke koholā e pili like ana me ka holoholona i hoʻolako piha ʻia a hoʻopaʻa ʻia e like me ka hana ʻana o ka ʻiniko i ka chrysalis e hoʻopuni puni ana iā ia. Hōʻike ʻia kēia ʻano ʻokoʻa ma ke poʻo, ʻoiai e hōʻike ʻia ana ma kekahi ʻāpana o kēia puke. Hōʻike ʻia nō hoʻi ma ka ʻaoʻao hope, ʻaneʻane pane pololei nā iwi i nā iwi o ka lima o ke kanaka, a koe wale nō ka manamana lima. ʻEhā mau manamana lima maʻamau o kēia peʻe, ʻo ka kuhikuhi, waena, apo, a me ka manamana liʻiliʻi. Akā, ua hoʻopaʻa mau ʻia kēia mau mea a pau i loko o ko lākou uhi ʻiʻo, e like me nā manamana lima o ke kanaka i kahi uhi hana. "Akā naʻe me ka noʻonoʻo ʻole e lawelawe mai ka koholā iā mākou i kekahi manawa," wahi a Stubb ʻakaʻaka i kekahi lā, "ʻaʻole hiki ke ʻōlelo maoli ʻia ʻo ia e lawelawe iā mākou me ka ʻole o nā miteni."

No kēia mau kumu a pau, no laila, i kēlā me kēia ʻano āu e nānā ai, pono ʻoe e hoʻoholo ʻo ka Leviatana nui ka mea hoʻokahi o ka honua e noho ʻole i pena ʻia a hiki i ka hope. ʻOiaʻiʻo, hiki i kekahi kiʻi ke paʻi i ka māka ma mua o kekahi, akā ʻaʻole hiki i kekahi ke paʻi iā ia me kahi kiʻekiʻe o ka pololei. No laila, ʻaʻohe ala honua e ʻike pono ai i ke ʻano o ke koholā. A ʻo ke ʻano wale nō e hiki ai iā ʻoe ke loaʻa i kahi manaʻo ʻoluʻolu o kona ʻano ola, ʻo ka hele ʻana i ke koholā iā ʻoe iho; akā, ma ka hana ʻana pēlā, ʻaʻole liʻiliʻi ʻoe e lilo i kapuahi mau loa a hoʻopaʻa ʻia e ia. No laila, i koʻu manaʻo, ʻaʻole pono ʻoe e hoʻokē ʻai i ka hoʻopā ʻana i kēia Leviatana.





MOKUNA 56. No na kii lalau iki o na kohola, a me na kii oiaio o na kii kohola.

I ka hui pu ana me na kii weliweli o na kohola, ua hoowalewale loa au maanei e komo i kela mau moolelo weliweli loa o lakou e ikeia ana ma kekahi mau buke kahiko a me keia wa, oi aku hoi ma Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, etc. Akā, ke hele nei au i kēlā mea.

ʻEhā wale nō kaʻu i ʻike i nā papa kuhikuhi o ka Sperm Whale nui; Na Colnett, Huggins, Frederick Cuvier, a me Beale. Ma ka mokuna mua, ua kuhikuhi ʻia ʻo Colnett lāua ʻo Cuvier. ʻOi aku ka maikaʻi o kā Huggins ma mua o kā lākou; aka, ma ka nui loa, o Beale ka oi. Maikaʻi nā kaha kiʻi a pau a Beale no kēia koholā, koe naʻe ka helu waena ma ke kiʻi o nā koholā ʻekolu ma nā ʻano ʻano like ʻole, e pani ana i kāna mokuna ʻelua. ʻO kāna mua, ʻo nā waʻa e hoʻouka ana i ka Sperm Whales, ʻoiai ʻaʻohe kānalua i manaʻo ʻia e hoʻonāukiuki i ka manaʻo kānalua kīwila o kekahi poʻe hale noho, he pololei a like me ke ola i kona hopena maʻamau. ʻO kekahi o nā kaha kiʻi ʻo Sperm Whale ma J. Ross Browne he pololei loa i ka contour; aka, ua kalai ino ia. ʻAʻole naʻe kāna hewa.

ʻO ka Whale Pono, aia nā kiʻi outline maikaʻi loa ma Scoresby; akā, ua huki ʻia lākou ma kahi pālākiō liʻiliʻi e hōʻike i kahi manaʻo i makemake ʻia. Hookahi wale no kii o ke kohola, a he hemahema keia, no ka mea, ma ia mau kii wale no, ke hana maikai ia, e hiki ai ia oe ke loaa ka manao oiaio o ke kohola ola e like me ka mea i ike ia e kona poe hahai holoholona.

Akā, i lawe ʻia no nā mea a pau, ʻo ka mea maikaʻi loa, ʻoiai ma kekahi mau kikoʻī ʻaʻole pololei loa, nā hōʻike o nā koholā a me nā hiʻohiʻona koholā e ʻike ʻia ma nā wahi āpau, ʻelua mau kaha kiʻi Farani nui, i hana maikaʻi ʻia, a lawe ʻia mai nā kiʻi pena e kekahi Garnery. Ma muli o kēia, hōʻike lākou i ka hoʻouka ʻana i ka Sperm and Right Whale. Ma ke kaha mua, ua hōʻike ʻia ka Sperm Whale me ka hanohano piha o ka mana, e kū ana ma lalo o ka waʻa mai ka hohonu o ka moana, a e kau ana i ka lewa ma luna o kona kua i ka pōʻino weliweli o nā papa kapuahi. ʻAʻole nahā ʻia ka ihu o ka waʻa, a ua huki ʻia me ke kaulike ʻana ma luna o ka iwi kuamoʻo; a e ku ana iloko o kela ihu, no ka manawa hookahi hiki ole ke helu, aia oe i ka hoe waapa, ua uhiia ka hapalua e ka hu'i hu'ihu o ke kohola, a me ka lele ana, me he pali la. ʻO ka hana o ka mea holoʻokoʻa he maikaʻi a he ʻoiaʻiʻo. E lana ana ka ipu laina hapalua i ke kai keokeo; ʻO nā ʻauamo lāʻau o nā harpoons i hoʻoheheʻe ʻia i loko o ia mea; ʻO nā poʻo o ka poʻe ʻauʻau ʻauʻau ʻaʻole i hoʻopuehu ʻia e pili ana i ke koholā ma nā ʻano ʻokoʻa o ka weliweli; oiai ma ka mamao ino eleele e ku ana ka moku maluna o ke kahua. Loaʻa ka hewa nui i nā kikoʻī anatomical o kēia koholā, akā e hoʻokuʻu i kēlā; no ka mea, no koʻu ola ʻana, ʻaʻole hiki iaʻu ke huki i kahi mea maikaʻi loa.

I ka lua o ke kahakaha ana, e kaha ana ka waapa ma ka aoao o ka aoao o kekahi Whale Akau e holo ana, e olokaa ana i kona puu weede eleele i ke kai e like me ka pahee pohaku mossy mai na pali Patagonian. ʻO kona mau jet ua kū pololei, piha, a ʻeleʻele e like me ka ʻōpala; no laila, no ka nui ʻana o ka uahi i loko o ke kapuahi, manaʻo paha ʻoe he ʻahaʻaina koa e kuke ana i ka ʻōpū nui ma lalo. Ke paʻi nei nā moa o ke kai i nā pāpaʻi liʻiliʻi, nā pūpū-iʻa, a me nā lole kai ʻē aʻe a me ka macaroni, a ka Whale ʻĀkau e hali ana i kona kua i ka maʻi ahulau. A i nā manawa a pau e holo wikiwiki ana ka leviatana lehelehe mānoanoa i ka hohonu, e waiho ana i nā tona o nā ʻeke keʻokeʻo haunaele ma kona ʻaoʻao, a e haʻalulu ana ka waʻa liʻiliʻi i loko o ka pehu me he waʻa lā i hopu ʻia i nā huila hoe o ka mokuahi moana. No laila, ʻo ka mua ka haunaele nui; aka, aia ma hope, ma ke ano o ka nani nani, aia ka ilikai aniani o ke kai i hoonioniia, o na pe'a maloo ole o ka moku mana ole, a me ka nui o ke kohola make, he pakaua lanakila, me ka hae o ka hopu e kau molowa ana mai ke kohola- pole i hookomoia iloko o kona puka-waha.

Aka, i kekahi kakahiaka uliuli alohilohi, i ka wa i pahola mai ai ka malie ma ke kai, aka nae me ka malama ole ia me ka malie malie; i ka wā i ʻā ai ka lā ma luna o ka wai me he mea lā ua kau ʻia kahi manamana gula ma waena o lākou, e kauoha ana i kahi huna; i ka hāwanawana ʻana o nā nalu paheʻe i ka holo mālie; i loko o kēia haʻahaʻa hohonu o ka pōʻai i ʻike ʻia, ua ʻike ʻia kahi mea ʻē aʻe e Daggoo mai ke poʻo nui.

Ma kahi lōʻihi aku, piʻi aʻe kekahi puʻupuʻu keʻokeʻo nui, a piʻi aʻe i luna a kiʻekiʻe aʻe, a hoʻokaʻawale iā ia iho mai ka azure, ma hope o ka ʻālohilohi ʻana i mua o kā mākou mua e like me ka hau-paheʻe, hou mai nā puʻu. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
