Ua lele wiwo ʻole ʻia mākou ma ka hohonu; aka, e nalo koke ana kakou iloko o kona mau wahi awa ole. Mamua o ka hiki ana mai; ma mua o ka holo ʻana o ka hull weedy o ka Pequod i ka ʻaoʻao me ka ʻaoʻao o ka leviatana; i ka hoʻomaka ʻana, ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi o ka mālama ʻana i kahi mea i makemake nui ʻia i ka hoʻomaopopo piha ʻana i nā hōʻike leviathanic kūikawā a me nā ʻōlelo hoʻohālikelike o nā ʻano āpau e hiki mai ana.

ʻO ia kahi hōʻike hōʻikeʻike o ke koholā i loko o kāna ʻano ākea, aʻu e hāʻawi ai i mua o ʻoukou. ʻAʻole naʻe he hana maʻalahi. ʻO ka hoʻokaʻawale ʻana o nā mea o ka haunaele, ʻaʻohe mea liʻiliʻi ma ʻaneʻi i kākau ʻia. E hoʻolohe i nā mea a nā mana maikaʻi a me nā mana hou i waiho ai.

"ʻAʻohe lālā o ka Zoology i komo nui e like me ka mea i kapa ʻia ʻo Cetology," wahi a Kāpena Scoresby, AD 1820.

"ʻAʻole koʻu manaʻo, inā i loko o koʻu mana, e komo i ka nīnau no ke ʻano maoli o ka māhele ʻana i ka cetacea i nā hui a me nā ʻohana. * * * Aia ka huikau nui i waena o nā mea kākau moʻolelo o kēia holoholona” (sperm whale), wahi a Surgeon Beale, AD 1839.

"ʻAʻole kūpono e hoʻomau i kā mākou noiʻi i nā wai hiki ʻole ke ʻike." "Ka pale hiki ʻole ke uhi i ko mākou ʻike no ka cetacea." “He mahinaai i paapu i ke kakalaioa.” "ʻO kēia mau hōʻailona piha ʻole akā e hoʻomāinoino iā mākou nā naturalists."

Pela e olelo ai no ke kohola, ka Cuvier nui, a me John Hunter, a me ka Lesson, kela mau kukui o ka zoology a me ke anatomy. Eia naʻe, ʻoiai he ʻuʻuku ka ʻike maoli, akā he nui nā puke; a pela no ma kekahi kekee liilii, me ka cetology, a me ka epekema o na kohola. He nui na kanaka, liilii a nui, kahiko a me na mea hou, na aina aina a me na luina, ua kakau nui lakou i ke kohola. Holo i kekahi mau mea:—Ka poe kakau Baibala; Aristotle; Pliny; Aldrovandi; Sir Thomas Browne; Gesner; Ray; Linnæus; Rondeletius; Willoughby; ʻōmaʻomaʻo; Artedi; Sibbald; Brisson; Marten; Lacépède; Bonneterre; Desmarest; Baron Cuvier; Frederick Cuvier; John Hunter; Owen; Scoresby; Beale; Bennett; J. Ross Browne; ka mea kākau o Miriam Coffin; Olmstead; a me Rev. T. Cheever. Akā i ke kumu hoʻohālikelike hope loa i kākau ai kēia mau mea āpau, e hōʻike ʻia nā unuhi i ʻōlelo ʻia ma luna.

ʻO nā inoa ma kēia papa inoa o nā mea kākau koholā, ʻo ka poʻe ma hope o Owen wale nō i ʻike i nā koholā ola; a ʻo kekahi o lākou, he kanaka harpoone ʻoihana maoli a he kanaka koholā. ʻO kaʻu ʻōlelo ʻo Kāpena Scoresby. Ma ke kumuhana ʻokoʻa o ka Greenland a i ʻole koholā ʻākau, ʻo ia ka mana maikaʻi loa e kū nei. Akā, ʻaʻole ʻike ʻo Scoresby a ʻaʻole ʻōlelo iki no ke kohola sperm nui, ke hoʻohālikelike ʻia me kahi kohola Greenland ʻaʻole kūpono e haʻi ʻia. A eia ka mea i olelo ia, o ke kohola Greenland he mea lawe pio ia ma ka nohoalii o na kai. ʻAʻole ʻo ia ka mea nui o nā koholā. Eia naʻe, ma muli o ka lōʻihi o kāna mau koi, a me ka naʻaupō nui, a hiki i kahi kanahiku mau makahiki i hala aku nei, ua hoʻolilo i ka sperm whale maikaʻi ʻole a ʻike ʻole ʻia paha, a ʻo ia ka naʻaupō a hiki i kēia lā ke noho aliʻi nei i loko o nā mea ʻepekema liʻiliʻi. a me na awa kohola; ua pau loa keia lawe pio ana. ʻO ka ʻike ʻana i ka ʻaneʻane o nā leviathanic allusions i loko o nā haku mele nui o nā lā i hala, e hōʻoluʻolu iā ʻoe ʻo ke kohola Greenland, ʻaʻohe mea hoʻokūkū hoʻokahi, iā lākou ke mōʻī o ke kai. Akā, ua hiki mai ka manawa no kahi hoʻolaha hou. ʻO Charing Cross kēia; e lohe oukou! na kanaka maikai a pau,—ua hoopauia ke kohola Greenland,—ke alii nui nei ka sperm whale!

Aia wale nō ʻelua mau puke i loko o ka mea i hoʻohālike i ka waiho ʻana i ke kohola sperm ola i mua o ʻoe, a ma ka manawa like, ma ka pae mamao loa e kūleʻa i ka hoʻāʻo. Na Beale lāua ʻo Bennett kēlā mau puke; i ko lakou manawa, he mau kauka lapaau i na moku okohola Enelani Hema, a me na kanaka pololei a hilinai. ʻO ka mea mua e pili ana i ka sperm whale i loaʻa i ko lākou mau puke he liʻiliʻi; akā, i ka wā e hele ai, ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi o ka maikaʻi, ʻoiai ka hapa nui o ka wehewehe ʻepekema. I kēia manawa naʻe, ʻaʻole i piha ka sperm whale, ʻepekema a poetic paha i nā palapala. Ma mua o nā koholā ʻē aʻe a pau, he ola kākau ʻole kona.

I kēia manawa, pono i nā ʻano koholā like ʻole kekahi ʻano hoʻokaʻawale ākea kaulana, inā he kikoʻī maʻalahi wale nō no kēia manawa, a ma kēia hope aku e hoʻopiha ʻia i kāna mau keʻena āpau e nā limahana ma hope. No ka mea, ʻaʻohe kanaka ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi e lawe i kēia mea ma ka lima, ke hāʻawi nei au i kaʻu mau hana ʻilihune. ʻAʻole au e hoʻohiki piha; no ka mea, ʻo nā mea a ke kanaka i manaʻo ʻia ua piha, ʻo ia ke kumu hewa ʻole. ʻAʻole wau e hoʻohālike i kahi wehewehe anatomical minute o nā ʻano ʻano like ʻole, a i ʻole - ma kēia wahi ma ka liʻiliʻi loa - i ka nui o nā wehewehe. ʻO kaʻu mea i ʻaneʻi ʻo ka hoʻolālā wale ʻana i ke kiʻi o ka systematization o ka cetology. ʻO wau ka mea kākau, ʻaʻole ka mea kūkulu hale.

Akā, he hana koʻikoʻi ia; ʻaʻohe mea hoʻoponopono leka maʻamau ma ka Hale Leta e like me ia. E hahau i lalo i lalo o ke kai ma hope o lākou; ka lima i waena o nā kumu hiki ʻole ke haʻi ʻia, nā iwi ʻaoʻao, a me ke pelvis loa o ka honua; he mea weliweli keia. He aha kaʻu e kākau ai e hoʻopaʻa i ka ihu o kēia leviatana! ʻO nā ʻōlelo hoʻomāinoino ʻino ma Ioba paha iaʻu. E hana anei ia (ka leviatana) i berita me oe? Aia hoʻi, ua makehewa ka manaʻolana iā ia! Akā, ua ʻau au ma nā hale waihona puke a ua holo au ma nā moana; Ua pili au i nā koholā me kēia mau lima ʻike ʻia; Ke hooikaika nei au; a e hoao au. Aia kekahi mau hoʻoholo mua.

ʻO ka mea mua: ʻO ke kūlana paʻa ʻole o kēia ʻepekema Cetology aia i loko o ka vestibule i hōʻike ʻia e ka ʻoiaʻiʻo, aia ma kekahi mau wahi ke waiho mau nei ia i kahi moot point inā he iʻa ke koholā. I loko o kāna System of Nature, AD 1776, ua ʻōlelo ʻo Linnæus, "Ke hoʻokaʻawale nei au i nā koholā mai ka iʻa." Akā, ma koʻu ʻike ponoʻī, ʻike wau a hiki i ka makahiki 1850, ua ʻike mau ʻia nā manō a me nā shad, nā alewives a me nā herring, e kūʻē i ke kauoha a Linnæus, e hoʻokaʻawale ana i ka waiwai o nā kai like me ka Leviatan.

ʻO ke kumu i makemake ai ʻo Linnæus e kipaku aku i nā koholā mai ka wai, ua ʻōlelo ʻo ia penei: "Ma muli o ko lākou puʻuwai puʻuwai pumehana, ko lākou māmā, ko lākou mau lihilihi hoʻoneʻe, ko lākou mau pepeiao puha, penem intrantem feminam mammis lactantem," a hope loa, “ex lege naturæ jure meritoque.” Ua waiho aku au i keia mau mea a pau i ko'u mau hoaloha Simeon Macey a me Charley Coffin, o Nantucket, o a'u mau hoa hele ma kekahi huakai, a ua lokahi laua me ka manao aole i lawa na kumu i hoikeia mai. Ua ʻōlelo haʻahaʻa ʻo Charley he humbug lākou.

E ʻike ʻia, me ka haʻalele ʻana i nā hoʻopaʻapaʻa a pau, lawe wau i ka ʻāina kahiko maikaʻi ʻo ke koholā he iʻa, a ke kāhea aku nei au iā Iona hemolele e kākoʻo mai iaʻu. Hoʻoholo ʻia kēia mea nui, ʻo ka mea hou aʻe, he aha ka ʻokoʻa o ke koholā mai nā iʻa ʻē aʻe. Ma luna, ua hāʻawi ʻo Linnæus iā ʻoe i kēlā mau mea. Akā ma ka pōkole, ʻo ia kēia: nā māmā a me ke koko mahana; akā, ʻo nā iʻa ʻē aʻe a pau, ʻaʻole maʻi a me ke koko anu.

Aʻe: pehea mākou e wehewehe ai i ke koholā, ma kāna mau mea i ʻike ʻia i waho, i mea e hōʻailona ai iā ia no ka wā e hiki mai ana? No ka pōkole, ʻo ke koholā, he iʻa ʻōpuʻu me ka huelo pae . Aia iā ʻoe. Eia naʻe i ʻaelike ʻia, ʻo ia wehewehe ka hopena o ka noʻonoʻo ʻana. Ua like loa ka walrus me he kohola, akā, ʻaʻole ia he iʻa, no ka mea, he amphibious ia. Akā ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi o ka huaʻōlelo hope o ka wehewehe, e like me ka mea mua. Aneane paha ua ʻike ka poʻe ʻaʻole he palahalaha nā iʻa a pau i kamaʻāina i ka poʻe kamaʻāina, akā he huelo kū pololei, a i ʻole i luna a lalo. ʻOiai, ma waena o ka iʻa ʻana i ka iʻa, ʻo ka huelo, ʻoiai ua like ke ʻano o kona ʻano, he kūlana paʻa mau.

Ma ka wehewehe ʻana o ke koholā i luna, ʻaʻole au e hoʻokaʻawale i nā mea kai i ʻike ʻia i kēia manawa me ke koholā e nā Nantucketers ʻike maikaʻi loa; ʻaʻole hoʻi e hoʻopili me ia i kekahi iʻa i manaʻo ʻia he malihini. No laila, pono e hoʻokomo ʻia nā iʻa liʻiliʻi a pau, puʻu, a me nā iʻa huelo i kēia papa honua o Cetology. ʻĀnō, e hele mai nā māhele nui o ka pūʻali koholā holoʻokoʻa.

*Ua ʻike au a hiki i kēia manawa, ua hoʻokomo ʻia nā iʻa i kapa ʻia ʻo Lamatins a me Dugongs (Pig-fish and Sow-fish of the Coffins of Nantucket) e nā poʻe loea maoli i waena o nā kohola. Aka, no ka mea, o keia mau i'a puaa, he ano walaau, hoowahawaha, e pee ana ma ka nuku o na muliwai, a e ai ana i ka mauu pulu, a no ka mea aole lakou e huahua, ke hoole aku nei au i ko lakou mau palapala kohola; a ua hāʻawi aku iā lākou i kā lākou palapala passport e haʻalele i ke Aupuni Cetology.

Akahi: E like me ka nui, puunaue au i na kohola i ekolu BUKE kumu (mahele i MOKUNA), a e maopopo ia lakou a pau, ka liilii a me ka nui.

I. KA FOLIO WALE; II. ka OCTAVO WHALE; III. ka DUODECIMO WALE.

E like me ke ʻano o ka FOLIO ke hōʻike nei au i ka Sperm Whale ; o ka OCTAVO, ka Grampus ; o ka DUODECIMO, ka Porpoise .

FOLIO. Mawaena o keia mau mokuna:—I. ʻO ka Sperm Whale ; II. ke kohola akau ; III. ke kohola hope hope ; IV. ke kohola hump-backed ; V. ke kohola pahi pahi ; VI. ke kohola Sulfur Bottom .

BUKE I. ( Folio ), MOKUNA I. ( Sperm Whale ).—O keia kohola, iwaena o ka poe Pelekane o ka wa kahiko i ike maopopo ole ia o ke kohola Trumpa, a me ke kohola Physeter, a me ka Anvil Headed whale, oia ka Cachalot o na Farani i keia wa. a me ka Pottsfich o na Geremania, a me ka Macrocephalus o na huaolelo loihi. ʻO ia, me ke kanalua, ʻo ia ka mea nui loa o ka honua; ka mea weliweli loa o na kohola a pau e halawai ai; ka mea hanohano loa ma ke ano; a ʻo ka hope loa, ʻo ia ka mea waiwai loa ma ke kālepa; ʻo ia wale nō ka mea i loaʻa mai kēlā mea waiwai, ʻo spermaceti. E hoʻonui ʻia kāna mau mea ʻokoʻa a pau, ma nā wahi ʻē aʻe. ʻO ka mea nui me kona inoa kaʻu e hana ai i kēia manawa. Ma ka noonoo ana, he mea lapuwale. I kekahi mau keneturia i hala aku nei, i ka wa i aneane maopopo ole ai ka Sperm whale i kona ano ponoi ponoi, a i ka wa i loaa wale mai ai kana aila mai ka i'a i paeia; I ia mau lā, ua manaʻo ʻia ʻo spermaceti ka mea i loaʻa mai kahi mea i like me ka mea i ʻike ʻia ma ʻEnelani i ia manawa ʻo ka Greenland a i ʻole ʻO ke kohola ʻākau. ʻO ia hoʻi ka manaʻo, ʻo kēia spermaceti hoʻi ʻo ia ka hoʻohenehene wikiwiki o ka Whale ʻo Greenland i hōʻike maoli ʻia e ka syllable mua o ka huaʻōlelo. I kēlā mau manawa hoʻi, ua ʻuʻuku loa ka spermaceti, ʻaʻole i hoʻohana ʻia no ka māmā, akā i mea ʻaila a me ka lāʻau lapaʻau. Loaʻa wale ia mai ka poʻe kūʻai lāʻau lapaʻau ʻoiai ʻoe e kūʻai nei i hoʻokahi auneke rhubarb. I koʻu manaʻo, i ka wā i ʻike ʻia ai ke ʻano maoli o ka spermaceti, ua paʻa mau kona inoa kumu e nā mea kūʻai aku; ʻaʻohe kanalua e hoʻonui i kona waiwai ma o ka manaʻo ʻano ʻē loa o kona hemahema. A no laila ua hiki mai ka inoa i hāʻawi ʻia i ke kohola i loaʻa maoli ai kēia spermaceti.

BUKE I. ( Folio ), MOKUNA II. ( Right Whale ).—Ma kekahi mea, o keia ka mea hanohano o na leviatana, oia ka mea i hahai mau ia e ke kanaka. Hāʻawi ia i ka ʻatikala i ʻike nui ʻia ʻo ka whalebone a i ʻole baleen; a ʻo ka ʻaila i ʻike nui ʻia ʻo "ʻaila koholā," he ʻatikala haʻahaʻa ma ke kālepa. Mawaena o ka poe lawaia, ua kapaia oia e keia mau inoa malalo iho nei: O ke kohola; ke kohola Greenland; ke kohola ʻEleʻele; ka Whale Nui; ka Whale Oiaio; ke kohola akau. Nui ka pohihihi e pili ana i ke ʻano o ia ʻano i bapetizo ʻia me ka lehulehu. He aha ke kohola, aʻu i hoʻokomo ai i loko o ka lua o kaʻu mau Folios? ʻO ia ka Mysticetus Nui o ka poʻe Hawaiʻi maoli; ke kohola Greenland o na kanaka kohola Enelani; ka Baleine Ordinaire o na kanaka kohola Farani; ka Growlands Walfish o na Suedena. ʻO ia ka koholā i ʻimi ʻia e ka Holani a me ka ʻEnelani ma ke kai ʻAtika no nā kenekulia ʻelua i hala aku nei; ʻO ia ka koholā a ka poʻe lawaiʻa ʻAmelika i hahai lōʻihi ai ma ka moana ʻĪnia, ma nā Banks Brazil, ma ke kahakai ʻo Nor' West Coast, a me nā wahi like ʻole o ka honua, i koho ʻia e lākou ʻo Right Whale Cruising Grounds.

Manaʻo kekahi e ʻike i ka ʻokoʻa ma waena o ke koholā ʻo Greenland o ka ʻEnelani a me ke kohola ʻākau o nā ʻAmelika. Akā ua ʻae pololei lākou i ko lākou mau hiʻohiʻona nui; ʻaʻole hoʻi i hōʻike ʻia kahi ʻoiaʻiʻo hoʻokahi e hoʻokumu ai i kahi ʻokoʻa koʻikoʻi. Ma muli o nā ʻāpana pau ʻole i hoʻokumu ʻia ma luna o nā ʻokoʻa like ʻole, e lilo ai kekahi mau keʻena o ka mōʻaukala kūlohelohe i mea paʻakikī loa. E mālama ʻia ka koholā ʻākau ma kahi ʻē aʻe ma kahi lōʻihi, e pili ana i ka wehewehe ʻana i ke kohola sperm.

BUKE I. ( Folio ), MOKUNA III. ( Fin-Back ).—Ma lalo o kēia poʻo ke manaʻo nei au he monster i ʻike ʻia ma nā inoa like ʻole ʻo Fin-Back, Tall-Spout, a me Long-John, kokoke i kēlā me kēia kai a ʻo ia ka kohola nona ka mokulele mamao. i wehewehe pinepine ʻia e nā poʻe e holo ana i ka ʻAkelanika, ma nā ala packet-track o New York. I ka lōʻihi i loaʻa iā ia, a i loko o kāna baleen, ua like ka Fin-back me ke kohola ʻākau, akā ʻoi aku ka liʻiliʻi o kona apo, a me ka waihoʻoluʻu māmā, kokoke i ka ʻoliva. ʻO kona mau lehelehe nui e hōʻike mai i kahi ʻano like me ke kaula, i hana ʻia e nā ʻāpana wiliwili o nā wrinkles nui. ʻO kāna hiʻohiʻona nui, ʻo ka ʻili, kahi i loaʻa ai kona inoa, he mea ʻike pinepine ʻia. ʻEkolu a ʻehā paha kapuaʻi ka lōʻihi o kēia peʻa, e ulu kū pololei ana mai ka ʻaoʻao hope o ke kua, he ʻano kihi, a ʻoi aku ka ʻoi loa. ʻOiai ʻaʻole ʻike ʻia kahi ʻāpana ʻē aʻe o ka mea i ʻike ʻia, ʻike ʻia kēia ʻili kaʻawale i kekahi manawa, e ʻike ʻia ana mai ka ʻili. I ka malie malie o ke kai, a hoailona iki ia me na ohe poepoe, a ku mai keia hope me he gnomon la a hoolulu i ka malu maluna o ka ili wiliwili, e manaoia paha ua like ka poai wai e puni ana me ka dial, me kona ano a me kona ano. ua kahakaha ʻia nā laina hola wavy. Ma kēlā ʻAhaza-dial e hoʻi pinepine ke aka. ʻAʻole pili ka Fin-Back. Me he mea lā he inaina koholā ʻo ia, ʻoiai he poʻe inaina kanaka kekahi. Hilahila loa; hele mehameha; ka piʻi ʻole ʻana i luna i ka ʻili i ka wai mamao loa a me nā wai ʻino loa; ʻO kāna mokulele kiʻekiʻe pololei a hoʻokahi e piʻi aʻe me he ihe misanthropic lōʻihi ma luna o kahi pāpū maloʻo; i hāʻawi ʻia me ka mana kupaianaha a me ka wikiwiki i ka ʻauʻau ʻana, e pale aku i ka hahai ʻana mai ke kanaka mai; ʻO kēia leviatana me he mea lā ʻo Kaina i hoʻokuke ʻia a lanakila ʻole ʻia o kona lāhui, e kau ana i kāna hōʻailona ma kona kua. Mai ka loaʻa ʻana o ka baleen i loko o kona waha, ua hoʻokomo ʻia ka Fin-Back i kekahi manawa me ke koholā ʻākau, ma waena o nā ʻano manaʻo i kapa ʻia ʻo Whalebone whale , ʻo ia hoʻi, nā koholā me ka baleen. ʻO kēia mau koholā i kapa ʻia ʻo Whalebone, me he mea lā he nui nā ʻano like ʻole, ʻaʻole naʻe i ʻike ʻia ka hapa nui. Nā koholā ihu ākea a me nā koholā nuku; nā koholā poʻo pike; nā kohola hui; ʻO nā koholā ʻauwae lalo a me nā kohola rostrate, ʻo ia nā inoa o nā lawaiʻa no kekahi mau ʻano.

Ma ka pili ana i keia huaolelo o "Whalebone whale," he mea nui e hai aku, aka nae ua kupono keia ano inoa no ka hooikaika ana i na olelo hoakaka no kekahi ano kohola, aka, he mea makehewa ka hoao ana e wehewehe maopopo i ka Leviatana. i hoʻokumu ʻia ma luna o kona baleen, a i ʻole kona puʻu, a i ʻole ʻili, a i ʻole nā ​​niho; ʻoiai ʻo kēlā mau ʻāpana a i ʻole nā ​​hiʻohiʻona i kaha ʻia, ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi o ka hoʻololi ʻana i ke kumu no ka ʻōnaehana maʻamau o Cetology ma mua o nā ʻano ʻokoʻa kino ʻē aʻe, a ke koholā, ma kāna ʻano, e hōʻike nei. Pehea la? ʻO ka baleen, hump, back-fin, a me nā niho; ʻO kēia nā mea i hoʻopuehu wale ʻia ma waena o nā ʻano koholā a pau, me ka nānā ʻole i ke ʻano o ko lākou ʻano ma nā mea ʻē aʻe. No laila, ʻo ka sperm whale a me ka humpbacked whale, he hump ko kēlā me kēia; aka, ua pau ka hoohalike ana. A laila, ʻo kēia koholā humpbacked a me ke kohola Greenland, he baleen kēlā me kēia; aka, ua pau hou keia hoohalike ana. A ua like ia me nā ʻāpana ʻē aʻe i ʻōlelo ʻia ma luna. Ma nā ʻano koholā like ʻole, hana lākou i nā hui like ʻole; a i ʻole, inā kaʻawale kekahi o lākou, kahi kaʻawale ʻole; i mea e kue loa ai i na hana ma'amau a pau i hanaia ma ia kumu. Ma luna o kēia pōhaku ua moku kēlā me kēia o nā koholā-naturalists.

Akā, hiki paha ke manaʻo ʻia, ma nā ʻāpana o loko o ke koholā, i kona anatomia-ma laila, ma ka liʻiliʻi loa, hiki iā mākou ke paʻi i ka hoʻokaʻawale kūpono. ʻAʻole; He aha ka mea i ʻoi aku ka maikaʻi o ka anatomy o ke kohola Greenland ma mua o kāna baleen? A inā ʻoe e iho i loko o ka ʻōpū o nā leviatana like ʻole, no ke aha ʻoe e ʻike ʻole ai i kahi hapa kanalima i loaʻa i ka systematizer e like me nā mea waho i helu mua ʻia. BUKE I. ( Folio ) MOKUNA IV. ( Hump Back ).—Ua ike pinepine ia keia kohola ma ka aoao akau o Amerika. Ua hopu pinepine ʻia ʻo ia ma laila, a huki ʻia i ke awa. He pūʻolo nui kona ma luna ona e like me ka mea kūʻai aku; a i ʻole e kapa ʻia ʻo ia ʻo ka Elephant and Castle Whale. Eia naʻe, ʻaʻole lawa ka ʻokoʻa ʻana o ka inoa kaulana nona, no ka mea, he puʻu ko ka sperm whale ʻoiai he ʻuʻuku. ʻAʻole waiwai nui kāna ʻaila. He baleen kona. ʻO ia ka ʻoi loa o ka pāʻani a me ka puʻuwai māmā o nā koholā a pau, e hana ana i ka huʻa gay a me ka wai keʻokeʻo ma mua o nā kohola ʻē aʻe.

BUKE I. ( Folio ), MOKUNA V. ( Razor Back ).—No keia kohola i ike iki ia kona inoa. Ua ʻike au iā ia ma kahi mamao mai Cape Horn. ʻO ke ʻano hoʻomaha, ʻalo ʻo ia i ka poʻe hahai holoholona a me ka poʻe akeakamai. ʻAʻole naʻe he makaʻu, ʻaʻole ʻo ia i hōʻike i kekahi ʻāpana o kona ʻaoʻao, ʻo kona kua wale nō, e piʻi aʻe ana ma kahi kuamoʻo lōʻihi. E hoʻokuʻu iā ia. ʻIke liʻiliʻi wau iā ia, ʻaʻole hoʻi i kekahi.

BUKE I. ( Folio ), MOKUNA VI. ( Sulfur Bottom ).—He keonimana hou e hoomaha ana, me ka opu luaipele, he kanalua ole i loaa ma ka wili ana ma na tile Tartarian ma kekahi o kona mau luu hohonu. ʻAʻole ʻike ʻia ʻo ia; ʻaʻole au i ʻike iā ia, koe wale nō ma ke kai mamao loa o ka hema, a laila ma kahi mamao loa e nānā i kona helehelena. Thus ends BOOK I. ( Folio ), and now begins BOOK II. ( Octavo ).

OCTAVOES.*—These embrace the whales of middling magnitude, among which present may be numbered:—I., the Grampus ; II., the Black Fish ; III., the Narwhale ; IV., the Thrasher ; V., the Killer .

*Why this book of whales is not denominated the Quarto is very plain. Because, while the whales of this order, though smaller than those of the former order, nevertheless retain a proportionate likeness to them in figure, yet the bookbinder's Quarto volume in its dimensioned form does not preserve the shape of the Folio volume, but the Octavo volume does.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER I. ( Grampus ).—Though this fish, whose loud sonorous breathing, or rather blowing, has furnished a proverb to landsmen, is so well known a denizen of the deep, yet is he not popularly classed among whales. But possessing all the grand distinctive features of the leviathan, most naturalists have recognised him for one. He is of moderate octavo size, varying from fifteen to twenty-five feet in length, and of corresponding dimensions round the waist. He swims in herds; he is never regularly hunted, though his oil is considerable in quantity, and pretty good for light. By some fishermen his approach is regarded as premonitory of the advance of the great sperm whale.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER II. ( Black Fish ).—I give the popular fishermen's names for all these fish, for generally they are the best. Where any name happens to be vague or inexpressive, I shall say so, and suggest another. I do so now, touching the Black Fish, so-called, because blackness is the rule among almost all whales. So, call him the Hyena Whale, if you please. His voracity is well known, and from the circumstance that the inner angles of his lips are curved upwards, he carries an everlasting Mephistophelean grin on his face. This whale averages some sixteen or eighteen feet in length. He is found in almost all latitudes. He has a peculiar way of showing his dorsal hooked fin in swimming, which looks something like a Roman nose. When not more profitably employed, the sperm whale hunters sometimes capture the Hyena whale, to keep up the supply of cheap oil for domestic employment—as some frugal housekeepers, in the absence of company, and quite alone by themselves, burn unsavory tallow instead of odorous wax. Though their blubber is very thin, some of these whales will yield you upwards of thirty gallons of oil.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER III. ( Narwhale ), that is, Nostril whale .—Another instance of a curiously named whale, so named I suppose from his peculiar horn being originally mistaken for a peaked nose. The creature is some sixteen feet in length, while its horn averages five feet, though some exceed ten, and even attain to fifteen feet. Strictly speaking, this horn is but a lengthened tusk, growing out from the jaw in a line a little depressed from the horizontal. But it is only found on the sinister side, which has an ill effect, giving its owner something analogous to the aspect of a clumsy left-handed man. What precise purpose this ivory horn or lance answers, it would be hard to say. It does not seem to be used like the blade of the sword-fish and bill-fish; though some sailors tell me that the Narwhale employs it for a rake in turning over the bottom of the sea for food. Charley Coffin said it was used for an ice-piercer; for the Narwhale, rising to the surface of the Polar Sea, and finding it sheeted with ice, thrusts his horn up, and so breaks through. But you cannot prove either of these surmises to be correct. My own opinion is, that however this one-sided horn may really be used by the Narwhale—however that may be—it would certainly be very convenient to him for a folder in reading pamphlets. The Narwhale I have heard called the Tusked whale, the Horned whale, and the Unicorn whale. He is certainly a curious example of the Unicornism to be found in almost every kingdom of animated nature. From certain cloistered old authors I have gathered that this same sea-unicorn's horn was in ancient days regarded as the great antidote against poison, and as such, preparations of it brought immense prices. It was also distilled to a volatile salts for fainting ladies, the same way that the horns of the male deer are manufactured into hartshorn. Originally it was in itself accounted an object of great curiosity. Black Letter tells me that Sir Martin Frobisher on his return from that voyage, when Queen Bess did gallantly wave her jewelled hand to him from a window of Greenwich Palace, as his bold ship sailed down the Thames; “when Sir Martin returned from that voyage,” saith Black Letter, “on bended knees he presented to her highness a prodigious long horn of the Narwhale, which for a long period after hung in the castle at Windsor.” An Irish author avers that the Earl of Leicester, on bended knees, did likewise present to her highness another horn, pertaining to a land beast of the unicorn nature.

The Narwhale has a very picturesque, leopard-like look, being of a milk-white ground colour, dotted with round and oblong spots of black. His oil is very superior, clear and fine; but there is little of it, and he is seldom hunted. He is mostly found in the circumpolar seas.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER IV. ( Killer ).—Of this whale little is precisely known to the Nantucketer, and nothing at all to the professed naturalist. From what I have seen of him at a distance, I should say that he was about the bigness of a grampus. He is very savage—a sort of Feegee fish. He sometimes takes the great Folio whales by the lip, and hangs there like a leech, till the mighty brute is worried to death. The Killer is never hunted. I never heard what sort of oil he has. Exception might be taken to the name bestowed upon this whale, on the ground of its indistinctness. For we are all killers, on land and on sea; Bonapartes and Sharks included.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER V. ( Thrasher ).—This gentleman is famous for his tail, which he uses for a ferule in thrashing his foes. He mounts the Folio whale's back, and as he swims, he works his passage by flogging him; as some schoolmasters get along in the world by a similar process. Still less is known of the Thrasher than of the Killer. Both are outlaws, even in the lawless seas.

Thus ends BOOK II. ( Octavo ), and begins BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ).

DUODECIMOES.—These include the smaller whales. I. The Huzza Porpoise. II. The Algerine Porpoise. III. The Mealy-mouthed Porpoise.

To those who have not chanced specially to study the subject, it may possibly seem strange, that fishes not commonly exceeding four or five feet should be marshalled among WHALES—a word, which, in the popular sense, always conveys an idea of hugeness. But the creatures set down above as Duodecimoes are infallibly whales, by the terms of my definition of what a whale is— ie a spouting fish, with a horizontal tail.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER 1. ( Huzza Porpoise ).—This is the common porpoise found almost all over the globe. The name is of my own bestowal; for there are more than one sort of porpoises, and something must be done to distinguish them. I call him thus, because he always swims in hilarious shoals, which upon the broad sea keep tossing themselves to heaven like caps in a Fourth-of-July crowd. Their appearance is generally hailed with delight by the mariner. Full of fine spirits, they invariably come from the breezy billows to windward. They are the lads that always live before the wind. They are accounted a lucky omen. If you yourself can withstand three cheers at beholding these vivacious fish, then heaven help ye; the spirit of godly gamesomeness is not in ye. A well-fed, plump Huzza Porpoise will yield you one good gallon of good oil. But the fine and delicate fluid extracted from his jaws is exceedingly valuable. It is in request among jewellers and watchmakers. Sailors put it on their hones. Porpoise meat is good eating, you know. It may never have occurred to you that a porpoise spouts. Indeed, his spout is so small that it is not very readily discernible. But the next time you have a chance, watch him; and you will then see the great Sperm whale himself in miniature.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER II. ( Algerine Porpoise ).—A pirate. Very savage. He is only found, I think, in the Pacific. He is somewhat larger than the Huzza Porpoise, but much of the same general make. Provoke him, and he will buckle to a shark. I have lowered for him many times, but never yet saw him captured.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER III. ( Mealy-mouthed Porpoise ).—The largest kind of Porpoise; and only found in the Pacific, so far as it is known. The only English name, by which he has hitherto been designated, is that of the fishers—Right-Whale Porpoise, from the circumstance that he is chiefly found in the vicinity of that Folio. In shape, he differs in some degree from the Huzza Porpoise, being of a less rotund and jolly girth; indeed, he is of quite a neat and gentleman-like figure. He has no fins on his back (most other porpoises have), he has a lovely tail, and sentimental Indian eyes of a hazel hue. But his mealy-mouth spoils all. Though his entire back down to his side fins is of a deep sable, yet a boundary line, distinct as the mark in a ship's hull, called the “bright waist,” that line streaks him from stem to stern, with two separate colours, black above and white below. The white comprises part of his head, and the whole of his mouth, which makes him look as if he had just escaped from a felonious visit to a meal-bag. A most mean and mealy aspect! His oil is much like that of the common porpoise.

* * * * * *

Beyond the DUODECIMO, this system does not proceed, inasmuch as the Porpoise is the smallest of the whales. Above, you have all the Leviathans of note. But there are a rabble of uncertain, fugitive, half-fabulous whales, which, as an American whaleman, I know by reputation, but not personally. I shall enumerate them by their fore-castle appellations; for possibly such a list may be valuable to future investigators, who may complete what I have here but begun. If any of the following whales, shall hereafter be caught and marked, then he can readily be incorporated into this System, according to his Folio, Octavo, or Duodecimo magnitude:—The Bottle-Nose Whale; the Junk Whale; the Pudding-Headed Whale; the Cape Whale; the Leading Whale; the Cannon Whale; the Scragg Whale; the Coppered Whale; the Elephant Whale; the Iceberg Whale; the Quog Whale; the Blue Whale; etc. From Icelandic, Dutch, and old English authorities, there might be quoted other lists of uncertain whales, blessed with all manner of uncouth names. But I omit them as altogether obsolete; and can hardly help suspecting them for mere sounds, full of Leviathanism, but signifying nothing.

Finally: It was stated at the outset, that this system would not be here, and at once, perfected. You cannot but plainly see that I have kept my word. But I now leave my cetological System standing thus unfinished, even as the great Cathedral of Cologne was left, with the crane still standing upon the top of the uncompleted tower. For small erections may be finished by their first architects; grand ones, true ones, ever leave the copestone to posterity. God keep me from ever completing anything. This whole book is but a draught—nay, but the draught of a draught. Oh, Time, Strength, Cash, and Patience!
