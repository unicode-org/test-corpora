SURA YA 55. Ya Picha za Kutisha za Nyangumi.

Nitakupaka rangi ndefu na vile vile mtu anaweza bila turubai, kitu kama sura halisi ya nyangumi kama anavyoonekana kwa jicho la nyangumi wakati nyangumi akiwa ndani ya mwili wake kamili amewekwa kando ya meli ya nyangumi ili anaweza kupitiwa kwa haki huko. Huenda ikafaa, kwa hivyo, hapo awali kutangaza kwa picha hizo za kuvutia za kuwaziwa kwake ambazo hata hadi leo hii zinapinga imani ya mwananchi huyo kwa ujasiri. Ni wakati wa kuweka ulimwengu sawa katika suala hili, kwa kuthibitisha picha kama hizo za nyangumi zote sio sawa.

Huenda ikawa kwamba chanzo kikuu cha udanganyifu huo wote wa picha kitapatikana kati ya sanamu za kale zaidi za Wahindoo, Wamisri, na Wagiriki. Tangu nyakati hizo za uvumbuzi lakini zisizo na adabu, wakati pomboo huyo alichorwa kwa mizani ya silaha za minyororo kama ya Saladin, kwenye safu za marumaru za mahekalu, misingi ya sanamu, na ngao, medali, vikombe na sarafu. St. George; tangu wakati huo ina kitu cha aina hiyo ya leseni ilishinda, si tu katika picha nyingi maarufu za nyangumi, lakini katika maonyesho mengi ya kisayansi yake.

Sasa, kwa vyovyote vile, picha ya kale zaidi iliyopo inayodaiwa kuwa ya nyangumi, inapatikana katika pango maarufu la pango la Elephanta, nchini India. Wabrahmin wanashikilia kwamba katika karibu sanamu zisizo na mwisho za pagoda hiyo ya kale, biashara na shughuli zote, kila utetezi unaowazika wa mwanadamu, ulikuwa ni enzi zilizotangulia kabla hata mojawapo haijatokea. Si ajabu basi, kwamba kwa namna fulani taaluma yetu nzuri ya kuvua nyangumi inapaswa kuwa imefichwa. Nyangumi wa Hindoo anayerejelewa, anatokea katika idara tofauti ya ukuta, inayoonyesha mwili wa Vishnu katika umbo la leviathan, anayejulikana sana kama Matse Avatar. Lakini ingawa sanamu hii ni nusu mtu na nusu nyangumi, ili tu kutoa mkia wa mwisho, lakini sehemu hiyo ndogo yake ni makosa. Inaonekana zaidi kama mkia wa anaconda, kuliko viganja vikubwa vya nyangumi wa kweli.

Lakini nenda kwenye Matunzio ya kale, na utazame sasa picha ya mchoraji mkuu wa Kikristo ya samaki huyu; kwa maana hafaulu vizuri kuliko Hindoo ya kabla ya gharika. Ni picha ya Guido ya Perseus akiokoa Andromeda kutoka kwa mnyama wa baharini au nyangumi. Guido alipata wapi mfano wa kiumbe wa ajabu kama huyo? Wala Hogarth, katika kuchora onyesho lile lile katika "Perseus Descending" yake mwenyewe, hafanyi sauti moja bora zaidi. Uzito mkubwa wa mnyama huyo wa Hogarthian unateleza juu ya uso, kwa shida sana kuchora inchi moja ya maji. Ina aina ya howdah mgongoni mwake, na mdomo wake uliopindika ambao mawimbi hutiririka, unaweza kuchukuliwa kwa Lango la Wasaliti linalotoka Mto Thames kwa maji hadi kwenye Mnara. Kisha, kuna nyangumi aina ya Prodromus wa zamani wa Scotch Sibbald, na nyangumi wa Yona, kama inavyoonyeshwa kwenye chapa za Biblia za zamani na mipasuko ya nyangumi za zamani. Je, itasemwa nini juu ya hawa? Kuhusu nyangumi wa mfungaji wa vitabu anayejipinda kama shina la mzabibu kuzunguka mkia wa nanga inayoshuka—kama ilivyopigwa mhuri na kupambwa kwenye migongo na kurasa za mada za vitabu vingi vya zamani na vipya—huyo ni kiumbe mzuri sana lakini wa ajabu kabisa, kuiga, ninaichukua, kutoka kwa takwimu kama hizo kwenye vases za kale. Ingawa ulimwenguni pote ni pomboo, hata hivyo ninaita samaki huyu wa binder-vitabu kuwa jaribio la kumtafuta nyangumi; kwa sababu ilikusudiwa sana wakati kifaa kilipoanzishwa. Ilianzishwa na mchapishaji wa zamani wa Kiitaliano mahali fulani kuhusu karne ya 15, wakati wa Uamsho wa Kujifunza; na katika siku hizo, na hata chini kwa comparatively marehemu kipindi, dolphins walikuwa maarufu walidhani kuwa aina ya Leviathan.

Katika tasnifu na mapambo mengine ya baadhi ya vitabu vya kale wakati fulani utakutana na miguso ya kuvutia sana ya nyangumi, ambapo kila aina ya spouts, jets d'eau, chemchemi za maji ya moto na baridi, Saratoga na Baden-Baden, hububujika kutoka kwake. ubongo usio na uchovu. Katika ukurasa wa kichwa wa toleo la asili la "Maendeleo ya Kujifunza" utapata nyangumi wadadisi.

Lakini tukiacha majaribio haya yote yasiyo ya kitaalamu, hebu tuangalie hizo picha za lewiathani zinazodai kuwa za kiasi, ufafanuzi wa kisayansi, na wale wanaojua. Katika mkusanyo wa safari za mzee Harris kuna baadhi ya sahani za nyangumi zilizotolewa katika kitabu cha Uholanzi cha safari, AD 1671, chenye kichwa “A Whaling Voyage to Spitzbergen in the ship Jonas in the Whale, Peter Peterson wa Friesland, bwana.” Katika moja ya sahani hizo nyangumi, kama safu kubwa ya magogo, wanawakilishwa wakiwa wamelala kati ya visiwa vya barafu, na dubu weupe wakikimbia juu ya migongo yao hai. Katika sahani nyingine, kosa la kushangaza linafanywa kwa kuwakilisha nyangumi na flukes perpendicular.

Kisha tena, kuna quarto yenye kustaajabisha, iliyoandikwa na Kapteni Colnett, Kapteni wa Posta katika jeshi la wanamaji la Kiingereza, yenye kichwa “A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, kwa kusudi la kupanua Uvuvi wa Nyangumi wa Spermaceti.” Katika kitabu hiki kuna muhtasari unaodaiwa kuwa "Picha ya Nyangumi wa Physeter au Spermaceti, iliyochorwa kwa mizani kutoka kwa yule aliyeuawa kwenye pwani ya Mexico, Agosti, 1793, na kupandishwa kwenye sitaha." Sina shaka kwamba nahodha alipigwa picha hii ya kweli kwa faida ya majini wake. Kutaja lakini jambo moja kuhusu hilo, napenda kusema kwamba ina jicho ambayo kutumika, kwa mujibu wa wadogo kuandamana, kwa nyangumi mzima mzima manii, bila kufanya jicho la nyangumi kwamba dirisha upinde baadhi ya futi tano kwa muda mrefu. Ah, nahodha wangu hodari, kwa nini hukutupa Yona kutazama kwa jicho hilo!

Wala mkusanyiko wa uangalifu zaidi wa Historia ya Asili kwa manufaa ya vijana na wapole, usio na uovu sawa wa makosa. Angalia kazi hiyo maarufu “Hali ya Uhuishaji ya Fundi wa Dhahabu.” Katika toleo lililofupishwa la London la 1807, kuna mabamba ya anayedaiwa kuwa “nyangumi” na “nyangumi.” Sipendi kuonekana mzembe, lakini nyangumi huyu asiyependeza anaonekana kama nguruwe aliyekatwa; na, kama kwa nyangumi, moja glimpse saa yake ni wa kutosha amaze moja, kwamba katika karne hii ya kumi na tisa kiboko vile inaweza palmd kwa ajili ya kweli juu ya umma wowote akili ya schoolboys.

Kisha, tena, mwaka wa 1825, Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, mwanasayansi mkuu, alichapisha kitabu cha kisayansi cha mfumo wa nyangumi, ambamo kuna picha kadhaa za aina mbalimbali za Leviathan. Haya yote si sahihi tu, lakini picha ya Mysticetus au nyangumi wa Greenland (hiyo ni kusema, nyangumi wa kulia), hata Scoresby, mtu mwenye uzoefu wa muda mrefu kuhusu kugusa aina hiyo, anatangaza kutokuwa na mwenzake katika asili.

Lakini kuweka mganda wa kofia kwa biashara hii yote ya kupotosha kuliwekwa kwa mwanasayansi Frederick Cuvier, ndugu wa Baron maarufu. Mnamo 1836, alichapisha Historia Asilia ya Nyangumi, ambamo anatoa kile anachoita picha ya Nyangumi wa Manii. Kabla ya kuonyesha picha hiyo kwa Nantucketer yoyote, ungetoa vyema kwa muhtasari wako wa kujiondoa kutoka Nantucket. Kwa neno moja, Nyangumi wa Manii ya Frederick Cuvier sio Nyangumi wa Manii, bali ni boga. Bila shaka, hakuwahi kupata faida ya safari ya kuvua nyangumi (wanaume kama hao huwa na mara chache), lakini ni wapi alipopata picha hiyo, ni nani awezaye kujua? Labda aliipata kama mtangulizi wake wa kisayansi katika uwanja huo, Desmarest, alipata moja ya utoaji mimba wake halisi; yaani, kutoka kwa mchoro wa Kichina. Na hao Wachina ni vijana wa aina gani wenye penseli, vikombe na visahani vingi vya kuchekesha vinatufahamisha.

Ama nyangumi wa wachora alama wanaoonekana barabarani wakining'inia kwenye maduka ya wauza mafuta, itasemwaje juu yao? Kwa ujumla wao ni Richard III. nyangumi, na nundu dromedary, na savage sana; kifungua kinywa kwenye tarti tatu au nne za baharia, hiyo ni boti za nyangumi zilizojaa mabaharia: ulemavu wao unaoelea katika bahari ya damu na rangi ya buluu.

Lakini makosa haya mengi katika kuonyesha nyangumi sio ya kushangaza sana. Fikiria! Michoro mingi ya kisayansi imechukuliwa kutoka kwa samaki waliokwama; na hizi ni sawa sawa na mchoro wa meli iliyovunjika, iliyovunjika mgongo, ungewakilisha kwa usahihi mnyama mtukufu katika majivuno yake yote ya mwili na spars. Ingawa tembo wamesimama kwa urefu wao kamili, Leviathan hai bado hajawahi kuelea mwenyewe kwa picha yake. Nyangumi aliye hai, katika ukuu na umuhimu wake kamili, ataonekana tu baharini katika maji yasiyopimika; na afloat wingi mkubwa wake ni nje ya mbele, kama ilizindua line-ya-vita meli; na kutoka katika kipengele hicho ni jambo lisilowezekana milele kwa mwanadamu anayeweza kufa kumpandisha kimwili hewani, ili kuhifadhi uvimbe wake wote wenye nguvu na machafuko. Na, bila kusema juu ya tofauti inayowezekana ya mtaro kati ya nyangumi mchanga anayenyonya na Leviathan wa Platonia; hata hivyo, hata katika kesi ya mmoja wa wale nyangumi vijana kunyonya hoisted kwa sitaha ya meli, vile ni basi outlandish, eel-kama, limbered, tofauti sura yake, kwamba kujieleza yake sahihi shetani mwenyewe hakuweza kupata.

Lakini inaweza kuwa fancied, kwamba kutoka kwa mifupa uchi ya nyangumi stranded, vidokezo sahihi inaweza kuwa inayotokana kugusa fomu yake ya kweli. Hapana kabisa. Kwa maana ni moja ya mambo ya ajabu zaidi kuhusu Leviathan hii, kwamba mifupa yake inatoa wazo kidogo sana la sura yake ya jumla. Ingawa mifupa ya Jeremy Bentham, ambayo huning'inia kwa candelabra kwenye maktaba ya mmoja wa watekelezaji wake, inawasilisha kwa usahihi wazo la mtu mzee wa utumishi aliye na uso, pamoja na sifa zingine zote za kibinafsi za Jeremy; lakini hakuna kitu cha aina hii ambacho kinaweza kudhaniwa kutoka kwa mifupa yoyote iliyotamkwa ya lewiathani. Kwa kweli, kama Mwindaji mkuu anavyosema, mifupa ya nyangumi ina uhusiano sawa na mnyama aliyewekeza kikamilifu na aliyejaa kama vile mdudu anavyofanya kwa chrysalis ambayo huifunika kwa mviringo. Upekee huu umedhihirika kwa kushangaza kichwani, kwani katika baadhi ya sehemu ya kitabu hiki itaonyeshwa kwa bahati mbaya. Pia inaonyeshwa kwa kushangaza sana kwenye pezi la upande, mifupa ambayo karibu hujibu kwa mifupa ya mkono wa mwanadamu, ukiondoa kidole gumba tu. Pezi hili lina vidole vinne vya kawaida vya mfupa, index, kati, pete na kidole kidogo. Lakini hizi zote hukaa kwa kudumu katika kifuniko chao chenye nyama, kama vidole vya binadamu katika kifuniko cha bandia. “Hata hivyo, nyakati fulani nyangumi anaweza kututumikia bila kujali,” alisema Stubb mcheshi siku moja, “hawezi kamwe kusemwa kwamba anatushughulikia bila mende.”

Kwa sababu hizi zote, basi, kwa njia yoyote unayoweza kuiangalia, lazima uhitimishe kwamba Leviathan mkuu ni yule kiumbe mmoja ulimwenguni ambaye lazima abakie bila kupakwa rangi hadi mwisho. Ni kweli, picha moja inaweza kugonga alama karibu zaidi kuliko nyingine, lakini hakuna inayoweza kuipiga kwa usahihi wa hali ya juu sana. Kwa hivyo hakuna njia ya kidunia ya kujua haswa jinsi nyangumi anavyofanana. Na njia pekee ambayo unaweza kupata hata wazo la kustahimili la contour yake ya kuishi, ni kwa kwenda kwa whaling mwenyewe; lakini kwa kufanya hivyo, unakuwa na hatari kubwa ya kuwa jiko la milele na kuzamishwa naye. Kwa hivyo, inaonekana kwangu haungekuwa mwepesi sana katika udadisi wako kugusa Leviathan hii.





SURA YA 56. Ya Picha Ndogo za Nyangumi, na Picha za Kweli za Matukio ya Kuvua Nyangumi.

Kwa kuunganishwa na picha za kutisha za nyangumi, ninajaribiwa sana hapa kuangazia hadithi hizo za kutisha zaidi ambazo zinapatikana katika vitabu fulani vya zamani na vya kisasa, haswa katika Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, nk Lakini ninapitisha jambo hilo.

Ninajua maelezo manne tu yaliyochapishwa ya Nyangumi mkubwa wa Manii; Colnett's, Huggins's, Frederick Cuvier's, na Beale's. Katika sura iliyotangulia Colnett na Cuvier wamerejelewa. Huggins's ni bora zaidi kuliko wao; lakini, kwa tabia mbaya, Beale ni bora zaidi. Michoro zote za Beale za nyangumi hii ni nzuri, isipokuwa takwimu ya kati katika picha ya nyangumi tatu katika mitazamo mbalimbali, akiweka sura yake ya pili. Sehemu yake ya mbele, boti zinazoshambulia Nyangumi manii, ingawa bila shaka zilihesabiwa kusisimua mashaka ya kiraia ya baadhi ya wanaume wa chumba cha kulala, ni sahihi sana na ni kama maisha katika athari yake ya jumla. Baadhi ya michoro ya Nyangumi wa Manii katika J. Ross Browne ni sahihi sana kwenye kontua; lakini zimechongwa kwa unyonge. Hilo si kosa lake ingawa.

Kati ya Nyangumi wa Kulia, picha bora za muhtasari ziko katika Scoresby; lakini zimechorwa kwa kipimo kidogo sana ili kutoa hisia inayotamanika. Ana picha moja tu ya matukio ya nyangumi, na huu ni upungufu wa kusikitisha, kwa sababu ni kwa picha kama hizo tu, wakati umefanya vizuri, ndipo unaweza kupata chochote kama wazo la kweli la nyangumi hai kama inavyoonekana na wawindaji wake wanaoishi.

Lakini, ikichukuliwa kwa wote katika yote, bora zaidi, ingawa katika baadhi ya maelezo si sahihi zaidi, maonyesho ya nyangumi na matukio ya nyangumi yanaweza kupatikana popote, ni michoro mbili kubwa za Kifaransa, zilizopigwa vizuri, na kuchukuliwa kutoka kwa uchoraji na Gari moja. Mtawaliwa, wao huwakilisha mashambulizi ya Sperm na Right Whale. Katika maandishi ya kwanza, Nyangumi wa Manii anaonyeshwa kwa ukuu kamili wa nguvu, akiinuka tu chini ya mashua kutoka kwa kina cha bahari, na kubeba juu ya hewa juu ya mgongo wake ajali mbaya ya mbao zilizochomwa. Sehemu ya mbele ya mashua haijavunjika, na inachorwa tu kusawazisha juu ya mgongo wa monster; na wamesimama katika prow kwamba, kwa kuwa moja incomputable flash ya muda, unaweza tazama kasia, nusu yamefunikwa na kuchomwa spout ya nyangumi, na katika tendo la kurukaruka, kama kutoka genge. Kitendo cha jambo zima ni nzuri ajabu na kweli. Bafu ya mstari iliyo na nusu-tupu huelea kwenye bahari iliyotiwa nyeupe; miti ya mbao ya harpoons iliyomwagika kwa oblique bob ndani yake; wakuu wa wafanyakazi wa kuogelea wametawanyika juu ya nyangumi kwa maneno tofauti ya hofu; huku katika umbali mweusi wenye dhoruba meli inashuka kwenye eneo la tukio. Kosa kubwa linaweza kupatikana kwa maelezo ya anatomical ya nyangumi huyu, lakini acha hilo lipite; kwani, kwa maisha yangu, sikuweza kuchora nzuri sana.

Katika mchongo wa pili, mashua iko katika harakati ya kuchora kando ya ubavu wa Nyangumi mkubwa anayekimbia, ambaye huviringisha magugu yake meusi baharini kama mtelezo fulani wa mwamba kutoka kwenye miamba ya Patagonia. Jeti zake zimesimama, zimejaa, na ni nyeusi kama masizi; ili kutokana na moshi mwingi sana kwenye bomba la moshi, ungefikiri lazima kuwe na kupika chakula cha jioni kwa ujasiri katika matumbo makubwa hapa chini. Ndege wa baharini wanawachuna kaa wadogo, samaki-gamba, na peremende nyinginezo za baharini na makaroni, ambayo Nyangumi wa Kulia huwabeba nyakati fulani kwenye mgongo wake wenye magonjwa. Na wakati wote leviathan yenye midomo minene inapita ndani ya kina kirefu, na kuacha tani nyingi za matope meupe katika wake wake, na kusababisha mashua kidogo kuyumba kwenye swells kama skiff iliyokamatwa karibu na magurudumu ya paddle ya meli ya bahari. Hivyo, mbele ni ghasia zote; lakini nyuma, kwa tofauti ya kisanii ya kupendeza, ni kiwango cha glasi cha bahari iliyotulia, tanga zisizo na wanga za meli isiyo na nguvu, na wingi wa ajizi wa nyangumi aliyekufa, ngome iliyoshindwa, na bendera ya kukamata ikining'inia kwa uvivu kutoka kwa nyangumi - nguzo kuingizwa katika spout-shimo yake.

Garnery mchoraji ni nani, au alikuwa, sijui. Lakini maisha yangu kwa ajili yake alikuwa ama kivitendo kuzungumza na somo lake, au mwingine ajabu tutored na baadhi whaleman uzoefu. Wafaransa ni vijana wa hatua ya uchoraji. Nenda ukaangalie michoro yote ya Uropa, na utapata wapi jumba la sanaa la kuishi na kupumua kwenye turubai, kama katika ukumbi wa ushindi huko Versailles; ambapo mtazamaji anapigana kwa njia yake, pell-mell, kupitia vita vikubwa vilivyofuatana vya Ufaransa; ambapo kila upanga unaonekana kuwaka kwa Miale ya Kaskazini, na wafalme wenye silaha waliofuatana na Wafalme wanapita, kama malipo ya centaurs yenye taji? SURA YA 57. Ya Nyangumi katika Rangi; katika meno; katika Wood; katika Karatasi-Iron; katika Stone; katika Milima; katika Stars.

Kwenye Tower-hill, unaposhuka kwenye kizimba cha London, huenda umemwona mwombaji kiwete (au mchungaji , kama mabaharia wanavyosema) akiwa ameshikilia ubao uliopakwa rangi mbele yake, unaowakilisha tukio lenye kuhuzunisha ambalo alipoteza mguu wake. Kuna nyangumi watatu na boti tatu; na boti moja (inayodhaniwa kuwa na mguu uliokosekana katika uadilifu wake wote wa asili) inakandamizwa na taya za nyangumi wa mbele. SURA YA 59. Squid.

Polepole wading kupitia Meadows ya brit, Pequod bado uliofanyika katika njia yake ya kaskazini-mashariki kuelekea kisiwa cha Java; hewa mpole impelling keel yake, hivyo kwamba katika utulivu jirani yake tatu tapering milingoti upole kutikiswa kwa upepo kwamba languid, kama mitende tatu kali juu ya wazi. Na bado, kwa vipindi virefu katika usiku wa rangi ya fedha, ndege ya upweke, ya kuvutia ingeonekana.

Lakini asubuhi moja ya uwazi ya bluu, wakati utulivu karibu kabla ya asili kuenea juu ya bahari, hata hivyo unattended na utulivu wowote palepale; wakati muda mrefu kuchomwa jua-glade juu ya maji walionekana kidole dhahabu kuweka juu yao, enjoining baadhi ya usiri; wakati mawimbi slippered alinong'ona pamoja kama wao softly mbio juu; katika ukimya huu wa kina wa tufe inayoonekana Specter ya ajabu ilionekana na Daggoo kutoka kwa kichwa kikuu cha mlingoti.

Kwa mbali, umati mkubwa mweupe uliinuka kwa uvivu, na kupanda juu na juu, na kujitenga kutoka kwa azure, mwishowe iling'aa mbele ya uso wetu kama mteremko wa theluji, utelezi mpya kutoka kwa vilima. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
