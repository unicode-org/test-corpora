Queequeg na mimi tulikuwa tumetoka tu Pequod, na tulikuwa tukienda mbali na maji, kwa wakati huo kila mmoja akiwa na mawazo yake mwenyewe, wakati maneno hapo juu yalipowekwa kwetu na mgeni, ambaye, akisimama mbele yetu, aliweka kidole chake kikubwa cha mbele. chombo husika. Alikuwa amevaa koti lililofifia na suruali yenye viraka; tamba ya leso nyeusi akiwekeza shingo yake. Mchanganyiko mdogo wa pox alikuwa katika pande zote alitiririka juu ya uso wake, na akaiacha kama kitanda ngumu cha kijito, wakati maji ya kukimbilia yamekaushwa.

"Umesafirisha ndani yake?" alirudia.

"Unamaanisha Pequod ya meli, nadhani," nilisema, nikijaribu kupata muda zaidi wa kumtazama.

"Ndiyo, Pequod - meli hiyo huko," alisema, akirudisha mkono wake wote, na kisha akausukuma moja kwa moja kutoka kwake, na bayonet iliyowekwa ya kidole chake kilichochongoka ikijaa kitu hicho.

“Ndiyo,” nikasema, “tumetia sahihi tu makala hizo.”

"Chochote huko chini juu ya roho zako?"

"Kuhusu nini?"

"Oh, labda huna lolote," alisema haraka. "Haijalishi, najua chaps nyingi ambazo hazina yoyote, - bahati nzuri kwa 'em; na wote ni bora zaidi kwa hilo. Nafsi ni aina ya gurudumu la tano kwa gari."

"Unashangaa nini, mwenzangu?" alisema mimi.

" Anatosha , hata hivyo, kufanya upungufu wote wa aina hiyo katika chaps zingine," ghafla alisema mgeni, akiweka msisitizo wa neva juu ya neno hilo .

“Queequeg,” nikasema, “twende; jamaa huyu ametoka mahali fulani; anazungumza kitu na mtu ambaye hatumjui.”

"Acha!" Alilia mgeni. "Ulisema kweli - bado haujaona Ngurumo ya Mzee, sivyo?"

"Ngurumo ya zamani ni nani?" Alisema mimi, tena riveted kwa bidii mwendawazimu ya namna yake.

"Kapteni Ahabu."

"Nini! nahodha wa meli yetu, Pequod?"

"Ndiyo, miongoni mwa baadhi yetu mabaharia wazee, anajulikana kwa jina hilo. Haujamuona bado, je!

"Hapana, hatuna. Wanasema ni mgonjwa, lakini anaendelea kupata nafuu, na atakuwa sawa tena muda si mrefu.”

“Sawa tena muda si mrefu!” alicheka mgeni, na aina wameweka dhihaka ya laugh. “Angalieni ninyi; Wakati Kapteni Ahabu yuko sawa, basi mkono wa kushoto wa mgodi utakuwa sawa; Sio hapo awali. ”

“Unajua nini kumhusu?”

" Walikuambia nini juu yake? Sema hivyo! "

“Hawakueleza mengi juu yake; Nimesikia tu kwamba yeye ni mwindaji mzuri wa nyangumi, na nahodha mzuri kwa wafanyakazi wake.

“Hiyo ni kweli, hiyo ni kweli—ndiyo, zote mbili ni kweli vya kutosha. Lakini lazima kuruka wakati anatoa agizo. Hatua na growl; kunguruma na uende—hilo ndilo neno la Kapteni Ahabu. Lakini hakuna kitu kuhusu jambo hilo lililompata kutoka Cape Horn, zamani sana, alipokuwa amelala kama mfu kwa siku tatu mchana na usiku; Hakuna kitu juu ya skrimmage mbaya na Spaniard hapo awali madhabahu huko Santa? - hakujali chochote juu ya hilo, eh? Hakuna kitu kuhusu kibuyu cha fedha alichotema? Na hakuna chochote kuhusu kupoteza kwake mguu katika safari yake ya mwisho, kulingana na unabii. Je! hamkusikia neno kuhusu mambo hayo na kitu kingine zaidi, eh? Hapana, sidhani kama mlifanya; Je! Ungewezaje? Nani anajua? Sio Nantucket yote, nadhani. Lakini hows'ever, mayhap, mmesikia habari juu ya mguu, na jinsi alivyopoteza; Ninyi, mmesikia habari hiyo, nathubutu kusema. Ah ndio, kwamba kila mtu anajua kabisa - ninamaanisha wanajua yeye ni mguu mmoja tu; Na kwamba Parmacetti aliondoa nyingine. "

"Rafiki yangu," nilisema, "ni nini gibberish yako yote ni juu, sijui, na sijali sana; kwa maana inaonekana kwangu kwamba lazima umeharibiwa kidogo kichwani. Lakini kama unazungumza juu ya Kapteni Ahabu, wa meli ile pale, Pequod, basi hebu niambie, kwamba ninajua yote kuhusu kupoteza mguu wake.

" Yote juu yake, eh - utakayofanya? - yote?"

"Hakika."

Kwa kidole kilichoelekezwa na jicho lililowekwa kwenye pequod, mgeni-kama-mwendeshaji alisimama kwa muda, kana kwamba katika reverie iliyokuwa na shida; kisha akianza kidogo, akageuka na kusema:—“Umesafirisha, sivyo? Majina kwenye karatasi? Vema, kilicho sainiwa, kimetiwa saini; na kitakachokuwa, kitakuwa; na kisha tena, labda haitakuwa, baada ya yote. Vyovyote vile, yote yamewekwa na yamepangwa tayari; na baadhi ya mabaharia au wengine lazima kwenda pamoja naye, nadhani; Vile vile hawa kama watu wengine wowote, huruma ya Mungu! Asubuhi kwenu, wasafiri wa meli, asubuhi; mbingu zisizoelezeka zinawabariki; Samahani nimekuzuia.”

"Angalia hapa, rafiki," nilisema, "ikiwa una jambo lolote muhimu la kutuambia, toka nalo; Lakini ikiwa unajaribu kututambulisha tu, umekosea katika mchezo wako; hilo ndilo ninalopaswa kusema.”

“Na imesemwa vizuri sana, na napenda kusikia chap akiongea hivyo; Wewe ndiye mtu tu kwake - anapenda. Asubuhi na wewe, wenzake, asubuhi! Ah! Unapofika hapo, sema nimehitimisha kutofanya moja ya 'em. "

“Ah, mwenzangu, huwezi kutudanganya hivyo—huwezi kutudanganya. Ni jambo jepesi zaidi duniani kwa mwanaume kuonekana kana kwamba ana siri kubwa ndani yake.”

"Asubuhi kwenu, wenzi wa meli, asubuhi."

"Asubuhi ni," alisema I. "Njoo, Queequeg, tumuache mtu huyu kichaa. Lakini acha, niambie jina lako, utaweza?”

Eliya! mawazo mimi, na sisi kutembea mbali, wote kutoa maoni, baada ya mtindo wa kila mmoja, juu ya hii chakavu baharia zamani; Na akakubaliana kuwa yeye sio kitu lakini ni Humbug, akijaribu kuwa bugbear. Lakini hatukuwa tumepita labda zaidi ya yadi mia, wakati tulipopiga kona, na kuangalia nyuma nilipofanya hivyo, ni nani angeonekana ila Eliya akitufuata, ingawa kwa mbali. Namna fulani, mbele yake akampiga yangu hivyo, kwamba mimi alisema chochote Queequeg ya kuwa nyuma yake, lakini kupita juu na rafiki yangu, wasiwasi kuona kama mgeni bila kugeuka kona hiyo kwamba sisi alivyofanya. Alifanya; na kisha ilionekana kwangu kwamba alikuwa akituzuia, lakini kwa nia gani sikuweza kufikiria maisha yangu. Hali hii, pamoja na utata wake, nusu-dokezo, nusu-akifichua, yamefunikwa aina ya mazungumzo, sasa kuzaa ndani yangu kila aina ya maajabu hazieleweki na nusu-hofu, na wote kushikamana na Pequod; na Kapteni Ahabu; na mguu alikuwa amepoteza; na Cape Pembe inafaa; na fedha za fedha; na kile Kapteni Peleg alikuwa amesema juu yake, wakati niliacha meli siku iliyotangulia; na utabiri wa squaw tistig; na safari tulikuwa tumejifunga kwa meli; na mambo mengine mia ya kivuli.

Niliazimia kujiridhisha kama Eliya huyu mbovu alikuwa anatusumbua au la, na kwa nia hiyo alivuka njia na Queequeg, na upande huo alifuata hatua zetu tena. Lakini Eliya alipita, bila kuonekana kutuona. Hii ilinituliza; na kwa mara nyingine, na hatimaye kama ilionekana kwangu, nilitamka moyoni mwangu, humbug.





Sura ya 20. Wote Astir.

Siku moja au mbili zilipita, na kulikuwa na shughuli kubwa ndani ya Pequod. Sio tu kwamba matanga ya zamani yalikuwa yakirekebishwa, lakini tanga mpya zilikuwa zikija kwenye ubao, na bolts za turubai, na safu za wizi; kwa ufupi, kila kitu kiliashiria kuwa maandalizi ya meli yalikuwa yanakaribia kumalizika. Kapteni Peleg mara chache au hakuwahi kwenda pwani, lakini alikaa katika wigwam yake akiweka machozi juu ya mikono: Bildad alifanya ununuzi wote na kutoa kwenye duka; na watu walioajiriwa katika ngome na kwenye wizi walikuwa wakifanya kazi hadi muda mrefu baada ya usiku kuingia.

Siku iliyofuata Queequeg kutia saini vifungu hivyo, neno lilitolewa katika nyumba zote za wageni ambapo kampuni ya meli ilikuwa ikisimama, kwamba vifua vyao lazima viwe ndani kabla ya usiku, kwa maana hakukuwa na habari jinsi meli inaweza kusafiri upesi. Kwa hivyo Queequeg na mimi tukashuka mitego yetu, kusuluhisha, hata hivyo, kulala pwani hadi mwisho. Lakini inaonekana kila wakati wanapeana taarifa ndefu katika kesi hizi, na meli haikusafiri kwa siku kadhaa. Lakini haishangazi; kulikuwa na mpango mzuri wa kufanywa, na hakuna kuwaambia jinsi mambo mengi ya kufikiriwa, kabla ya Pequod ilikuwa kikamilifu vifaa.

Kila mtu anajua mambo mengi-beds, sufuria za mchuzi, visu na uma, koleo na matako, leso, vibamba vya lishe, na nini sio, ni muhimu kwa biashara ya utunzaji wa nyumba. Kwa hivyo tu na whaling, ambayo inahitajika kutunza nyumba ya miaka tatu juu ya bahari pana, mbali na wauzaji wote, costermonger, madaktari, waokaji, na mabenki. Na ingawa hii pia inatumika kwa vyombo vya wafanyabiashara, lakini si kwa njia yoyote kwa kiwango sawa na whalemen. Kwa maana mbali na urefu mkubwa wa safari ya whaling, vifungu vingi vya kipekee kwa mashtaka ya uvuvi, na kutowezekana kwa kuzibadilisha kwenye bandari za mbali kawaida, lazima ikumbukwe, ya meli zote, vyombo vya whaling ndio vilivyo wazi zaidi kwa ajali za kila namna, na hasa kwa uharibifu na upotevu wa mambo yale yale ambayo mafanikio ya safari yanategemea zaidi. Kwa hivyo, boti za vipuri, spars za vipuri, na mistari ya vipuri na vibanda, na vipuri kila kitu, karibu, lakini nahodha wa vipuri na meli mbili.

Katika kipindi cha kuwasili kwetu katika Kisiwa, hifadhi nzito zaidi ya Pequod ilikuwa karibu kukamilika; Inajumuisha nyama yake, mkate, maji, mafuta, na hoops za chuma na miti. Lakini, kama ilivyodokezwa hapo awali, kwa muda fulani kulikuwa na kuchota na kubeba mara kwa mara kwenye bodi ya tabia mbaya na mwisho wa mambo, makubwa na madogo.

Mkuu kati ya wale ambao walifanya hii kuchota na kubeba alikuwa dada ya Kapteni Bildad, mwanamke mzee wa roho aliyedhamiria zaidi na asiyeweza kufikiwa, lakini mwenye moyo mzuri sana, ambaye alionekana kutatuliwa kuwa, ikiwa angeweza kusaidia, hakuna kitu kinachopaswa kupatikana katika Pequod , baada ya mara moja kupata bahari. Wakati mmoja angekuja kwenye bodi na jarida la kachumbari kwa pantry ya msimamizi; wakati mwingine na rundo la quills kwa dawati mate mkuu, ambapo aliweka logi yake; a third time with a roll of flannel for the small of some one's rheumatic back. Never did any woman better deserve her name, which was Charity—Aunt Charity, as everybody called her. And like a sister of charity did this charitable Aunt Charity bustle about hither and thither, ready to turn her hand and heart to anything that promised to yield safety, comfort, and consolation to all on board a ship in which her beloved brother Bildad was concerned, and in which she herself owned a score or two of well-saved dollars.

But it was startling to see this excellent hearted Quakeress coming on board, as she did the last day, with a long oil-ladle in one hand, and a still longer whaling lance in the other. Nor was Bildad himself nor Captain Peleg at all backward. As for Bildad, he carried about with him a long list of the articles needed, and at every fresh arrival, down went his mark opposite that article upon the paper. Every once in a while Peleg came hobbling out of his whalebone den, roaring at the men down the hatchways, roaring up to the riggers at the mast-head, and then concluded by roaring back into his wigwam.

During these days of preparation, Queequeg and I often visited the craft, and as often I asked about Captain Ahab, and how he was, and when he was going to come on board his ship. To these questions they would answer, that he was getting better and better, and was expected aboard every day; meantime, the two captains, Peleg and Bildad, could attend to everything necessary to fit the vessel for the voyage. If I had been downright honest with myself, I would have seen very plainly in my heart that I did but half fancy being committed this way to so long a voyage, without once laying my eyes on the man who was to be the absolute dictator of it, so soon as the ship sailed out upon the open sea. But when a man suspects any wrong, it sometimes happens that if he be already involved in the matter, he insensibly strives to cover up his suspicions even from himself. And much this way it was with me. I said nothing, and tried to think nothing.

At last it was given out that some time next day the ship would certainly sail. So next morning, Queequeg and I took a very early start.





CHAPTER 21. Going Aboard.

It was nearly six o'clock, but only grey imperfect misty dawn, when we drew nigh the wharf.

“There are some sailors running ahead there, if I see right,” said I to Queequeg, “it can't be shadows; she's off by sunrise, I guess; come on!”

“Avast!” cried a voice, whose owner at the same time coming close behind us, laid a hand upon both our shoulders, and then insinuating himself between us, stood stooping forward a little, in the uncertain twilight, strangely peering from Queequeg to me. It was Elijah.

“Going aboard?”

“Hands off, will you,” said I.

“Lookee here,” said Queequeg, shaking himself, “go 'way!”

“Ain't going aboard, then?”

“Yes, we are,” said I, “but what business is that of yours? Do you know, Mr. Elijah, that I consider you a little impertinent?”

“No, no, no; I wasn't aware of that,” said Elijah, slowly and wonderingly looking from me to Queequeg, with the most unaccountable glances.

“Elijah,” said I, “you will oblige my friend and me by withdrawing. We are going to the Indian and Pacific Oceans, and would prefer not to be detained.”

“Ye be, be ye? Coming back afore breakfast?”

“He's cracked, Queequeg,” said I, “come on.”

“Holloa!” cried stationary Elijah, hailing us when we had removed a few paces.

“Never mind him,” said I, “Queequeg, come on.”

But he stole up to us again, and suddenly clapping his hand on my shoulder, said—“Did ye see anything looking like men going towards that ship a while ago?”

Struck by this plain matter-of-fact question, I answered, saying, “Yes, I thought I did see four or five men; but it was too dim to be sure.”

“Very dim, very dim,” said Elijah. “Morning to ye.”

Once more we quitted him; but once more he came softly after us; and touching my shoulder again, said, “See if you can find 'em now, will ye?

“Find who?”

“Morning to ye! morning to ye!” he rejoined, again moving off. “Oh! I was going to warn ye against—but never mind, never mind—it's all one, all in the family too;—sharp frost this morning, ain't it? Good-bye to ye. Shan't see ye again very soon, I guess; unless it's before the Grand Jury.” And with these cracked words he finally departed, leaving me, for the moment, in no small wonderment at his frantic impudence.

At last, stepping on board the Pequod, we found everything in profound quiet, not a soul moving. The cabin entrance was locked within; the hatches were all on, and lumbered with coils of rigging. Going forward to the forecastle, we found the slide of the scuttle open. Seeing a light, we went down, and found only an old rigger there, wrapped in a tattered pea-jacket. He was thrown at whole length upon two chests, his face downwards and inclosed in his folded arms. The profoundest slumber slept upon him.

“Those sailors we saw, Queequeg, where can they have gone to?” said I, looking dubiously at the sleeper. But it seemed that, when on the wharf, Queequeg had not at all noticed what I now alluded to; hence I would have thought myself to have been optically deceived in that matter, were it not for Elijah's otherwise inexplicable question. But I beat the thing down; and again marking the sleeper, jocularly hinted to Queequeg that perhaps we had best sit up with the body; telling him to establish himself accordingly. He put his hand upon the sleeper's rear, as though feeling if it was soft enough; and then, without more ado, sat quietly down there.

“Gracious! Queequeg, don't sit there,” said I.

“Oh! perry dood seat,” said Queequeg, “my country way; won't hurt him face.”

“Face!” said I, “call that his face? very benevolent countenance then; but how hard he breathes, he's heaving himself; get off, Queequeg, you are heavy, it's grinding the face of the poor. Get off, Queequeg! Look, he'll twitch you off soon. I wonder he don't wake.”

Queequeg removed himself to just beyond the head of the sleeper, and lighted his tomahawk pipe. I sat at the feet. We kept the pipe passing over the sleeper, from one to the other. Meanwhile, upon questioning him in his broken fashion, Queequeg gave me to understand that, in his land, owing to the absence of settees and sofas of all sorts, the king, chiefs, and great people generally, were in the custom of fattening some of the lower orders for ottomans; and to furnish a house comfortably in that respect, you had only to buy up eight or ten lazy fellows, and lay them round in the piers and alcoves. Besides, it was very convenient on an excursion; much better than those garden-chairs which are convertible into walking-sticks; upon occasion, a chief calling his attendant, and desiring him to make a settee of himself under a spreading tree, perhaps in some damp marshy place.

While narrating these things, every time Queequeg received the tomahawk from me, he flourished the hatchet-side of it over the sleeper's head.

“What's that for, Queequeg?”

“Perry easy, kill-e; oh! perry easy!”

He was going on with some wild reminiscences about his tomahawk-pipe, which, it seemed, had in its two uses both brained his foes and soothed his soul, when we were directly attracted to the sleeping rigger. The strong vapor now completely filling the contracted hole, it began to tell upon him. He breathed with a sort of muffledness; then seemed troubled in the nose; then revolved over once or twice; then sat up and rubbed his eyes.

“Holloa!” he breathed at last, “who be ye smokers?”

“Shipped men,” answered I, “when does she sail?”

“Aye, aye, ye are going in her, be ye? She sails to-day. The Captain came aboard last night.”

“What Captain?—Ahab?”

“Who but him indeed?”

I was going to ask him some further questions concerning Ahab, when we heard a noise on deck.

“Holloa! Starbuck's astir,” said the rigger. “He's a lively chief mate, that; good man, and a pious; but all alive now, I must turn to.” And so saying he went on deck, and we followed.

It was now clear sunrise. Soon the crew came on board in twos and threes; the riggers bestirred themselves; the mates were actively engaged; and several of the shore people were busy in bringing various last things on board. Meanwhile Captain Ahab remained invisibly enshrined within his cabin.
