UPOKO 55. Mo nga Pikitia Manawa o Te Tohora.

Ka roa ahau e peita ki a koe me te tangata kaore he kānawehi, he rite tonu ki te ahua o te tohorā i te mea ka kitea e ia ki te kanohi o te tohorā i te wa e mau ana te tohorā ki te taha o te kaipuke tohorā i roto i tona tinana ake. ka taea e ia te takahi tika ki reira. No reira, he pai pea te panui i mua atu ki aua whakaahua pohewa nanakia nei, tae noa mai ki enei ra, e kaha ana ki te wero i te whakapono o te tangata whenua. Kua tae ki te wa ki te whakatika i te ao mo tenei take, ma te whakaatu he he katoa nga whakaahua o te tohorā.

Tera pea ko te puna tuatahi o aua pohehe whakaahua katoa ka kitea i roto i nga whakairo tawhito Hindoo, Ihipa, me nga Kariki. Mai ano i nga wa whakaaro noa, engari i runga i nga papa mapere o nga temepara, i runga i nga turanga o nga whakapakoko, i runga i nga whakangungu rakau, i nga metara, i nga kapu, i nga moni, ka toia te aihe ki roto ki nga unahi patu mekameka pera i to Saladin, me te upoko potae rite tonu. St. George's; Mai i tera wa he rite tonu te mana o te raihana, ehara i te mea i te nuinga o nga pikitia rongonui o te tohorā, engari i roto i te maha o nga whakaaturanga putaiao mona.

Inaianei kei te kitea te whakaahua tawhito o mua mo te tohorā, kei te ana rongonui o Elephanta, i Inia. Ko nga Brahmins e kii ana i roto i nga whakairo tata tonu o taua pagoda tawhito, ko nga mahi hokohoko me nga mahi katoa, nga mahi katoa e whakaarohia ana e te tangata, i tohuhia i nga tau i mua i te hanganga o tetahi o enei. No reira kare he mea whakamiharo, i etahi ahua o ta tatou mahi rangatira tohorā ka taumarumaru iho ki reira. Ko te tohorā Hindoo e kiia ana, ka puta i roto i tetahi tari motuhake o te pakitara, e whakaatu ana i te whakaurunga o Vishnu ki te ahua o te rewiatana, e mohiotia ana ko te Matse Avatar. Engari ahakoa he haurua tangata me te haurua tohorā tenei whakairo, kia hoatu noa te hiku o muri, heoi kei te he katoa tena wahanga iti o ia. He rite tonu te ahua ki te hiku o te anaconda, i nga nikau whanui o nga rewharewha nui o te tohorā.

Engari haere ki nga Taiwhanga tawhito, titiro inaianei ki te whakaahua a tetahi kaitoi Karaitiana rongonui mo tenei ika; no te mea kaore ia i pai ake i te Hindoo o mua. Ko te pikitia a Guido mo Perseus e whakaora ana i a Andromeda mai i te taniwha moana, tohorā ranei. No hea a Guido i whiwhi ai i te tauira o te mea hanga kee penei? Kaore ano hoki a Hogarth, i te peita i te ahua ano i roto i tana ake "Perseus Descending," he pai ake te ahua. Ko te tinana nui o taua taniwha Hogarthian e korikori ana i runga i te mata, karekau e tohia he inihi wai. Kei tona tuara te ahua o te hodah, a ko tona waha e pupuhi ana i roto i nga ngaru e huri haere ana, tera pea ka haria atu mo te keti o nga Kaituku e ahu atu ana i Thames ma te wai ki te Pourewa. I muri mai, ko nga tohorā Prodromus o Scotch Sibbald tawhito, me te tohorā a Jonah, e whakaatuhia ana i roto i nga tuhinga o nga Paipera tawhito me nga tapahi o nga pukapuka tuatahi. Ka pehea te korero mo enei? Ko te tohorā a te kaipupuri pukapuka e awhiowhio ana ano he kakau waina i te taha o te punga e heke iho ana—he mea hiri, he mea whakakikoruatia ki muri me nga wharangi taitara o nga pukapuka maha o nga pukapuka tawhito me nga pukapuka hou—he mea tino ataahua, engari he mea tino ataahua. Imitated, I take it, from the like figures on antique vases. Ahakoa he aihe te ingoa o te ao, ka kiia e ahau tenei ika a te pukapuka pukapuka he ngana ki te tohorā; na te mea i peratia i te wa tuatahi i whakaurua ai te taputapu. I tukuna mai e tetahi kaiwhakaputa Itari tawhito i tetahi waahi mo te rautau 15, i te wa o te Revival of Learning; a i aua ra, tae noa ki te taima tata nei, i kiia ko te aihe he momo o te Leviatana.

I roto i nga tohu me etahi atu whakapaipai o etahi pukapuka tawhito ka tutaki koe i etahi wa ki nga pa tino mohio ki te tohorā, kei reira nga momo pupuhi katoa, nga jet d'eau, nga puna wera me te makariri, Saratoga me Baden-Baden, ka puta mai i tana roro korekore. Kei te wharangi taitara o te putanga tuatahi o te “Te Ahutanga o te Ako” ka kitea e koe etahi tohorā.

Engari ka mutu enei nganatanga kore ngaio, me titiro tatou ki nga pikitia o te rewiatana e kii ana he ahua mohio, he tohu putaiao, na te hunga mohio. Kei roto i nga kohinga haerenga a Harris tawhito he papa tohorā i tangohia mai i te pukapuka haerenga Tatimana, AD 1671, ko te taitara “A Whaling Voyage to Spitzbergen in the ship Jonas in the Whale, Peter Peterson of Friesland, master.” I roto i tetahi o aua pereti ko nga tohorā, he rite ki nga rakau nui, kei te takoto i waenga i nga motu hukapapa, me nga pea ma e rere ana i runga i o ratou tuara ora. I tetahi atu pereti, ko te pohehe nui he tohu mo te tohorā me nga rewharewha tika.

Ka mutu ano, tera ano tetahi roopu nui, i tuhia e tetahi Kapene Colnett, he Kapene Poutapeta i roto i te moana o Ingarangi, ko te taitara “A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, for the purpose of extending the Spermaceti Whale Fisheries.” Kei roto i tenei pukapuka tetahi whakahuahua e kiia ana he "Picture of a Physeter or Spermaceti whale, i tuhia ma te tauine mai i te tangata i mate i te takutai o Mexico, Akuhata, 1793, ka whakairihia ki runga i te papa." Kare au i pohehe i tangohia e te kapene tenei pikitia pono hei painga mo ana hoia. Kotahi tonu te korero mo taua mea, me kii ahau he kanohi tona e pa ana, i runga i te tauine e whai ake nei, ki te tohora paoa kua tino tupu, ka waiho te kanohi o taua tohora hei matapihi kopere kia rima putu te roa. Aue, e toku rangatira toa, he aha koe te tuku mai ai ia Hona ki a matou kia titiro atu i tera kanohi!

Kaore ano hoki nga kohinga tino mohio o Natural History mo te painga o te taiohi me te ngawari, kaore i te kino o te he. A hi‘o na i tera ohipa tuiroo “Goldsmith’s Animated Nature.” I roto i te whakaputanga poto o Ranana i te tau 1807, kei reira nga papa o te “tohorā” me te “narwhale” e kiia ana. Kare au e pai kia ahua pohehe, engari he rite te ahua o tenei tohorā kino ki te poaka kua tapahia; A, mo te narwhale, he mea whakamiharo te titiro ki tetahi, i tenei rautau tekau ma iwa ka taea te hanga i taua hippogriff ki runga i nga tangata mohio o nga tamariki kura.

Na, ano, i te tau 1825, ka whakaputahia e Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, he tohunga maori, he pukapuka tohorā kua oti te raupapa pūtaiao, kei reira etahi pikitia o nga momo rereke o te Leviathan. Ehara enei katoa i te he anake, engari ko te pikitia o te tohorā Mysticetus, Greenland ranei (ara, te tohorā Matau), tae noa ki a Scoresby, he tangata mohio kua roa nei e pa ana ki taua momo, e kii ana karekau he rite ki te ahua.

Engari ko te whakatakoto i te paihere potae ki enei mahi pohehe katoa i rahuitia mo te tohunga pūtaiao a Frederick Cuvier, teina o te Baron rongonui. I te tau 1836, ka whakaputahia e ia he Natural History of Whales, i roto i tana whakaatu i tana e kiia nei ko te pikitia o te Sperm Whale. I mua i te whakaatu i taua pikitia ki tetahi Nantucketer, he pai ki a koe te whakarite mo to rerenga whakarāpopototanga mai i Nantucket. Ko te kupu, ehara ko Frederick Cuvier's Sperm Whale i te Sperm Whale, engari he kamokamo. Ae ra, kare rawa ia i whai hua mo te haerenga patu tohorā (he iti noa nga tangata penei), engari no hea ia i puta ai taua pikitia, ma wai e mohio? Akene pea i riro i a ia te mea i puta mai i a ia i mua i te taha pūtaiao i roto i te mara kotahi, a Desmarest, i whiwhi tetahi o ana materoto pono; ara, mai i te tuhi Hainamana. He aha te ahua o nga tamariki ora me te pene he Hainamana, he maha nga kapu me nga hoeha e whakaatu mai ana ki a matou.

Mo nga tohora a nga kaipeita haina e kitea ana i nga tiriti e iri ana i runga i nga toa hoko hinu, ka pehea te korero mo ratou? Ko te tikanga ko Richard III ratou. he tohorā, he hump dromedary, he tino mohoao; E parakuihi ana i runga i te toru, e wha ranei nga tarati heramana, ara he poti tohorā ki tonu i nga kaihoe moana: ko o ratou ahua e rere ana i roto i nga moana toto me te peita kahurangi.

Engari ko enei he maha ki te whakaatu i te tohorā ehara i te mea tino miharo. Whakaarohia! Ko te nuinga o nga whakaahua pūtaiao i tangohia mai i nga ika kua paea; He rite tonu enei ki te tuhi o te kaipuke kua pakaru, kua pakaru te tuara, e tohu tika ana mo te kararehe rangatira i roto i tona whakapehapeha katoa o te takere me te mokowhiti. Ahakoa kua tu nga arewhana mo o ratou roa, kare ano te rewiatana ora i rewa mo tana whakaahua. Ko te tohorā ora, i runga i tona tino nui me tona hiranga, ka kitea noa i te moana i roto i nga wai e kore e kitea; a ka manu te nuinga o ia kua kore e kitea, ano he kaipuke whawhai kua whakarewahia; e no roto mai i taua tumu ra e mea e ore roa e tia i te taata tahuti ia huti i to ’na tino i nia i te reva, no te faaherehere i to ’na mau pupuhi puai atoa e to ’na mau haapueraa. A, ehara i te korero mo te tino rerekee o te ahua o te ahua o te kuao tohorā ngote me te Rewiatana Platonian kua pakeke; Heoi, ahakoa ko tetahi o nga tamariki ngote tohorā ka mau ki runga i te papa o te kaipuke, he ahua ke, he tuna, he tuna, he rereke te ahua, kaore e taea e te rewera te hopu.

Engari tera pea te whakaaro, mai i te kōiwi tahanga o te tohorā kua paea, ka puta mai he tohu tika mo tona ahua. Kaore rawa. No te mea ko tetahi o nga mea tino miharo mo tenei rewiatana, he iti noa te whakaaro o tana koiwi ki tona ahua whanui. Ahakoa ko te kohiwi o Jeremy Bentham, e iri ana mo te rama rama i roto i te whare pukapuka o tetahi o ana kaihautu, e whakaatu tika ana i te whakaaro o te koroua utilitarian rae-rae, me era atu ahuatanga o Jeremy; heoi karekau he mea penei i taea te whakatau mai i nga koiwi whai kiko o rewiatana. Inaa, e kii ana te Kaiwhaiwhai nui, he rite tonu te hononga o te kōiwi noa o te tohorā ki te kararehe kua whakangaohia me te mea hoe, ki ta te pepeke ki te chrysalis e kopaki ana i a ia. Ko tenei ahuatanga e tino kitea ana i roto i te mahunga, no te mea kei etahi waahanga o tenei pukapuka ka whakaatuhia. Ka tino kitea ano hoki i roto i te tara o te taha, ko nga koiwi e tata tika ana ki nga koiwi o te ringa tangata, haunga te koromatua anake. E wha nga maihao maihao o tenei tara, te tohu, te waenganui, te mowhiti, me te maihao iti. Ko enei katoa ia e mau tonu ana ki o ratou hipoki kikokiko, pera me nga maihao tangata i roto i te uhi horihori. "Engari ka mahi poauau te tohorā ki a tatou i etahi wa," ko ta Stubb katakata i tetahi ra, "kaore rawa e taea te kii ki te hapai ia tatou me te kore he mitini."

Mo enei take katoa, ahakoa he aha te titiro ki a koe, me whakaaro koe ko te Rewiatana nui ko te mea hanga kotahi o te ao e kore e peita ki te mutunga. Pono, ka tata atu tetahi whakaahua ki te tohu i tetahi atu, engari karekau e taea e tetahi te pa ki te tino tika. No reira karekau he huarahi o te whenua ki te kimi i te ahua o te tohorā. Ko te tikanga anake e taea ai e koe te whai whakaaro mo tona ahua ora, ko te haere koe ki te patu tohorā; engari ma te mahi pera, ka tupono noa koe ki te noho mo ake tonu atu, ka totohuhia e ia. Na, ki taku whakaaro me kaua koutou e ngakau nui ki te pa ki tenei rewiatana.





UPOKO 56. Mo nga Pikitia He iti ake te pohehe o nga Tohorā, me nga Pikitia Tuturu o nga Whakaaturanga Tohorā.

I runga i te hono atu ki nga pikitia nanakia o nga tohorā, e kaha ana ahau ki konei ki te uru atu ki era atu korero nanakia mo ratou e kitea ana i roto i etahi pukapuka, o mua, o naianei, otira ki Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, me etahi atu. Engari ka paahitia e ahau tena mea.

E wha noa nga waahanga kua whakaputaina e au mo te Tohora Paa nui; Ko Colnett's, Huggins's, Frederick Cuvier's, and Beale's. I roto i te upoko o mua kua korerohia a Colnett raua ko Cuvier. He pai ake a Huggins i a raua; engari, na te mea nui, ko Beale te mea pai rawa atu. He pai nga whakaahua katoa a Beale mo tenei tohorā, haunga te ahua o waenganui i te pikitia o nga tohorā e toru he maha nga waiaro, e kati ana i tana upoko tuarua. Ko tana ahua o mua, ko nga poti e patu ana i te Sperm Whales, ahakoa karekau he whakaaro hei whakaohooho i te ngakau pohehe o etahi o nga tangata o te whare noho, he tino tika, he rite tonu te ahua ki te oranga o tona paanga. Ko etahi o nga pikitia Sperm Whale i J. Ross Browne he tino tika te ahua; engari he kino te whaowhao. E hara tera i a ia te he.

O te Tohora Matau, ko nga pikitia tino pai kei Scoresby; engari ka toia ki runga i te tauine iti rawa hei kawe i te ahua pai. Kotahi tonu tana pikitia mo te hopu tohorā, he pouri tenei, na te mea ma enei pikitia anake, ina oti pai te mahi, ka taea e koe te whai whakaaro pono mo te tohorā ora e kitea ana e ana tangata whaiwhai ora.

Engari, i tangohia mo te katoa, ko te mea pai rawa atu, ahakoa ko etahi korero ehara i te mea tino tika, ko nga whakaaturanga o nga tohorā me nga tirohanga tohorā ka kitea ki nga waahi katoa, e rua nga whakairo nui a te Wiwi, he pai te mahi, he mea tango mai i nga peita na tetahi Garnery. Arā, e tohu ana i ngā whakaekenga o te Sperm and Right Whale. I te whakairo tuatahi e whakaatuhia ana he Tohora Sperm Whale i roto i te tino nui o te kaha, i ara ake i raro i te poti mai i te hohonutanga o te moana, me te kawe i runga i te rangi ki runga i tona tuara te pakarutanga o nga papa tahu. Ko te ihu o te poti kaore i pakaru tetahi wahi, ka kumea ki runga i te tuara o te taniwha; Ka tu koe i runga i taua ihu, mo te ra kotahi e kore e taea te tatau, ka kite koe i te kaihoe, he haurua kua hipokina e te pupuhi koropupu o te tohorā, me te pekepeke, me te mea mai i te pari. Ko te mahi o te katoa he tino pai, he pono. Ka rewa te haurua o te tapu raina i runga i te moana kua ma; ko nga pou rakau o nga hapo kua maringi, ka kopikopiko ki roto; ka marara nga upoko o nga kauhoe ki te tohorā i roto i nga korero rerekee o te wehi; I te wa o te awha pango kei te heke te kaipuke ki runga i te waahi. Ka kitea pea te he nui o nga korero mo te tinana o tenei tohorā, engari waiho tera; no te mea, mo toku oranga, kaore au e kaha ki te utu i tetahi mea pai.

I te mahi whakairo tuarua, kei te mahi te poti ki te tuhi ki te taha o te kaokao o te Tohora Matau nui e rere ana, e huri ana i tana puranga taru mangu i te moana ano he toka pukohu mai i nga pari o Patagonian. Ko ona rerenga he mea hangai, kapi tonu, he mangu ano he pungarehu; na te nui o te paoa i roto i te tumere, ka whakaaro koe me he kai maia te kai i roto i nga whekau o raro. Kei te pupuhi nga manu o te moana ki nga papaka iti, ki te anga-ika, ki etahi atu monamona moana me te macaroni, ka mauria e te Tohora Matau i runga i tona tuara mate. A, i nga wa katoa e rere ana te rewiatana ngutu matotoru ki roto i te rire, ka mahue te tini o nga pata ma te ngangau i tona ara, ka aki te poti iti i roto i te ngawhatanga ano he poti e mau ana ki te taha o nga wira hoe o te tima moana. No reira, ko te papa o mua he ngangau katoa; engari kei muri, he tino rerekee nga mahi toi, ko te taumata karaehe o te moana e marumaru ana, ko nga ra o te kaipuke karekau e mimiti, me te papatipu o te tohorā mate, he pa kaha, me te kara hopu e iri ana i runga i te tohorā- ka kuhua te pou ki roto i tana kohao.

Ko wai a Garnery te kaipeita, ko wai ranei, kaore au e mohio. Engari ko taku oranga mo tenei, he tino mohio ia ki tana kaupapa, ki te kore ranei i tino whakaakona e tetahi tangata mohio tohorā. Ko te Wīwī ngā tamariki mō te mahi peita. Haere ki te titiro ki nga peita katoa o Uropi, kei hea koe e kite ai i te taiwhanga ora me te ngangau i runga i te konae, pera i tera whare toa i Versailles; te wahi e whawhai ana te kaititiro ki tana ara, pell-mell, i roto i nga pakanga nunui o Parani; kei hea nga hoari katoa e rite ana ki te uira o te Raki o te Raki, a ko nga kingi mau patu me nga Emepera e rere mai ana, ano he karaehe keneta? Ehara i te mea e kore e tika mo tetahi waahi i roto i taua taiwhanga, ko enei taonga whawhai moana o Garnery.

Ko te mohiotanga o te Wīwī ki te hopu i te ataahua o nga mea ka kitea i roto i nga peita me nga whakairo o a ratou mahi tohorā. Karekau he whakatekau o Ingarangi ki te mahi ika, kaore ano hoki i te mano o nga wahi o Amerika, heoi kua hoatu e ratou ki nga iwi e rua nga huahua kua oti katoa te kaha ki te kawe i te tino wairua o te hopu tohorā. Ko te nuinga, ko te ahua o nga kaihopu tohorā o Ingarangi me Amerika kei te tino pai ki te whakaatu i te whakahuahua miihini o nga mea, penei i te ahua watea o te tohorā; he rite tonu ki te tuhi i te ahua o te tara. Ahakoa ko Scoresby, te tangata tohorā matau rongonui, i muri i tana homaitanga ki a matou te roa tino maro o te tohorā Greenland, me te toru, e wha ranei nga ahua iti o te narwhales me te porepoises, ka tukuna e matou he raupapa o nga whakairo tawhito o nga matau poti, nga maripi tapatapahi, me nga karepe; me te kaha moroiti o te Leuwenhoeck ka tuku ki te tirotiro i te ao wiri e iwa tekau ma ono nga ahua o nga tioata hukarere Arctic whakanuia. Ko taku tikanga karekau he whakahawea ki te kaihoroi pai (ka whakanui ahau ia ia mo te hoia), engari i roto i tetahi take nui, he pohehe te kore e oatitia mo ia karaihe he oati i oatitia ki mua i tetahi Kai-whakawa mo te rangimarie o Greenland.

I tua atu i era whakairo pai mai i Garnery, e rua atu ano nga whakairo Wīwī e tika ana kia tirohia, na tetahi i ohauru ki a ia "H. Durand.” Ko tetahi o ratou, ahakoa kaore i tino rite ki ta tatou kaupapa o naianei, engari e tika ana kia whakahuahia mo etahi atu korero. He ata ata noho i waenganui i nga motu o Te Moananui-a-Kiwa; he tangata whai tohorā Wīwī ka punga, ki uta, i roto i te ata noho, me te mangere ki te kawe wai ki runga; ko nga ra o te kaipuke kua wetekina, me nga rau roroa o nga nikau i muri, e topa tahi ana i te hau hau. He tino pai te paanga, ina whakaarohia mo te whakaatu i nga kaihao hii pakari i raro i tetahi o nga ahuatanga iti o te okiokinga ki te rawhiti. He rereke te ahua o te mahi whakairo: ko te kaipuke e rere ana ki runga i te moana, kei roto tonu i te ngakau o te oranga o Rewiatana, me te Tohora Matau ki te taha; ko te waka (i te mahi tope) ka toro atu ki te taniwha me te mea ki te waaawa; a ko tetahi poti e tere tere atu ana i tenei waahi mahi, he whaiwhai tohorā i tawhiti. Ko nga haoro me nga tao e takoto ana hei whakamahi; Tokotoru nga kaihoe e whakanoho ana i te rewa ki roto i tona rua; mai i te hurihanga ohorere o te moana, ka tu te waka iti i te haurua o te wai, ano he hoiho whakatipu. Mai i te kaipuke, ka kake ake te paoa o nga mamae o te tohora koropupu ano he paoa ki runga i te kainga parapara; a ki te hau, he kapua mangu e kake ake ana me te kaha o te tukinga me te ua, te ahua nei e tere ake ana te mahi a nga kaimaa hikaka.





UPOKO 57. Mo nga Tohora i te Peita; i roto i nga Niho; i te Rakau; i te Rau-rino; i roto i te Kohatu; i nga Maunga; i nga Whetu.

I runga i te Tower-hill, i a koe e heke ana ki te tauranga o Raanana, kua kite pea koe i tetahi kopa ( kedger ranei, e ai ki nga heramana) e pupuri ana i tetahi papa peita ki tona aroaro, e tohu ana i te ahua kino i ngaro ai tona waewae. E toru nga tohora, e toru nga poti; a ko tetahi o nga poti (e kiia ana kei roto te waewae kua ngaro i roto i tona tuturutanga o mua) kei te ngongohia e nga kauae o te tohorā tuatahi. I nga wa katoa i enei tau tekau, ka ki mai ratou ki ahau, kua mau taua tangata i taua pikitia, ka whakaatu i taua tumu ki te ao whakaponokore. Otira kua tae mai te wa e whakatikaia ai ia. Ko ana tohorā e toru he rite tonu te pai o nga tohorā i panuitia ki Wapping, ahakoa te aha; a ko tona tumu ano he tumu e kore e taea te whakapae pera me etahi ka kitea e koe i nga maara o te hauauru. Ka tere haere i roto i nga wiwi o brit, ka mau tonu te Pequod ki te raki-ma-rawhiti ki te motu o Java; na te hau ngawari e akiaki ana i tana reera, na i te ata noho a tawhio noa, ka tawhiri marietia e ia e toru nga rangatira teitei e toru ki taua hau ngawari, ano he nikau ngawari e toru i runga i te mania. Heoi ano, i nga wa whanui i te po hiriwa, ka kitea te mokemoke, te mokemoke jet.

Engari i tetahi ata kikorangi kikorangi, ka horapa te ata noho tata ki runga i te moana, ahakoa karekau i arohia me te ata noho; i te wa e whiti ana te ra i runga i nga wai i te ahua o tetahi maihao koura i whakatakotoria ki runga i a ratou, e kii ana kia huna; i te wa e komuhumuhu ana nga ngaru pahekeheke i a ratou e oma marie ana; i roto i tenei wahangu hohonu o te ao e kitea ana he ahua kee i kitea e Daggoo mai i te upoko-matua.

I tawhiti, ka ara ake he papatipu ma ma te mangere, ka piki ki runga ake, ka mawehe atu i te azure, i te mutunga ka kanapa mai i mua i to matou ihu ano he hukarere, he paheke hou mai i nga puke. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
