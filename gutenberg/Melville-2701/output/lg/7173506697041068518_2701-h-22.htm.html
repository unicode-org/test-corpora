ESSUULA 114. Omugagga.

Nga yeeyongera okuyingira mu mutima gw’ekifo eky’e Japan we baali batambulira ku nnyanja, mu bbanga ttono emmeeri ya Pequod yonna yali etabuse mu by’obuvubi. Emirundi mingi, mu mbeera y’obudde ennyogovu, ennungi, okumala essaawa kkumi na bbiri, kkumi na ttaano, kkumi na munaana, n’amakumi abiri ku luguudo, baali beenyigira mu maato, nga basika obutasalako, oba nga basaabala, oba nga bavuba oluvannyuma lw’ennyanja ekika kya whale, oba okumala eddakiika nkaaga oba nsanvu nga balindirira mu bukkakkamu okwegugunga kwabwe; wadde nga balina naye obuwanguzi obutono olw’obulumi bwabwe.

Mu biseera ng’ebyo, wansi w’enjuba ekendedde; nga batengejja olunaku lwonna ku bizimba ebiseeneekerevu ebiwanvuwa mpola; ng’atudde mu lyato lye, nga mutangaavu ng’akaato ka birch; era nga batabula mu ngeri ey’omukwano n’amayengo amagonvu gennyini, ne kiba nti ng’embwa ez’amayinja ag’omuliro ziwuuma ku gunwale; bino bye biseera eby’okusirika okw’ekirooto, omuntu bw’alaba obulungi obutebenkevu n’okumasamasa kw’olususu lw’ennyanja, yeerabira omutima gw’engo ogufuuwa wansi waalyo; era teyandijjukira kyeyagalire, nti kino ekiwaawaatiro kya velvet naye kikweka amannyo agataliimu kwejjusa.

Bino bye biseera, mu lyato lye erya whale-boat rover mw’awulira mpola okuwulira okumu okw’omwana, okwekkiririzaamu, okulinga okw’oku lukalu ng’ayolekera ennyanja; nti akitwala ng’ettaka eririmu ebimuli bingi nnyo; era emmeeri ey’ewala ng’ebikkula waggulu w’ebikondo byayo byokka, erabika ng’egenda mu maaso, si mu mayengo amawanvu agayiringisibwa, wabula ng’eyita mu muddo omuwanvu ogw’ekiwonvu ekiyiringisibwa: ng’embalaasi z’abasenguka ab’amaserengeta bwe ziraga amatu gazo gokka agasimbiddwa, ate nga emibiri gyazo egyekwekeddwa gigazi wade through the verdure ekyewuunyisa.

Ebiwonvu eby’embeerera ebiwanvuye; ensozi za bbululu ennyogovu; nga ku bino we wabba okusirika, okuwuuma; kumpi olayira nti abaana abakooye okuzannya bagalamira nga beebase mu bifo bino eby’okwewummuzaamu, mu kiseera ekimu eky’essanyu ekya May-time, ng’ebimuli by’ensiko bisimbuddwa. Era bino byonna bitabuddwamu n’embeera yo esinga okuba ey’ekyama; bwe kityo ensonga n’eby’omulembe, ekitundu ky’ekkubo ne bisisinkana, ne biyingira, ne bikola ekintu kimu ekitaliiko buzibu.

Era n’ebifaananyi ng’ebyo ebikkakkanya, ka bibe nga bya kaseera buseera, tebyalemererwa waakiri okukola akaseera katono ku Akabu. Naye bwe kiba nti ebisumuluzo bino eby’ekyama ebya zaabu byalabika ng’ebimuggulirawo amawanika ge ag’ekyama aga zaabu, naye omukka gwe gwali ku byo gwalaga nti gwali gwonoona.

Oh, ebifo ebirimu omuddo! oh, ever vernal endless landscapes mu mwoyo; mu mmwe, — newakubadde nga mumaze ebbanga nga mukala olw’ekyeya ekifu eky’obulamu obw’oku nsi, — mu mmwe, abantu bayinza okuyiringisibwa, ng’embalaasi ento mu clover empya ey’oku makya; era okumala akaseera katono akayitawo, wulira omusulo omunyogovu ogw’obulamu obutafa ku bo. Yandibadde Katonda obukkakkamu buno obw’omukisa bwandiwangaala. Naye obuwuzi bw’obulamu obutabuddwatabuddwa, obutabuddwatabuddwa bulukibwa n’okuwuguka n’okuwuuta: obukkakkamu obusalako emiyaga, omuyaga ku buli bukkakkamu. Tewali nkulaakulana etakyukakyuka etaddirira mu bulamu buno; tetugenda mu maaso nga tuyita mu nsengeka ezitebenkedde, era ku nkomerero esembayo tuyimirira: —okuyita mu kuloga kw’obuwere obutategeera, okukkiriza okw’obulenzi okutaliimu kulowooza, okubuusabuusa kw’obuvubuka (okuzikirizibwa okwa bulijjo), olwo okubuusabuusa, olwo obutakkiriza, okuwummulako ku nkomerero mu kuwummula kw’obusajja okufumiitiriza ku Singa. Naye bwe tumala okuyita, tuddamu okulondoola okwetooloola; era bawere, balenzi, n’abasajja, era Ifs emirembe gyonna. Omwalo ogusembayo guli ludda wa, gye tutaddamu kusumulula? ESSUULA 115. Pequod Asisinkana Bachelor.

Era essanyu ekimala byali bifaananyi n’amaloboozi ebyajja nga bikka wansi ng’empewo tennatandika, nga wayise wiiki ntono ng’engoye ya Akabu emaze okuwebwa.

Yali mmeeri ya Nantucket, Bachelor, eyali yaakayingira mu kibbo kyayo eky’amafuta ekisembayo, n’ekuba obuuma obusiba wansi mu bikuta byayo ebyakutuka; era kati, mu ngoye z’ennaku enkulu ez’essanyu, yali mu ssanyu, wadde nga mu kitiibwa ekitaliimu nsa, ng’agenda yeetooloola wakati w’emmeeri ezaawuddwamu ennyo ku ttaka, nga tannasonga mu maaso ge okudda eka.

Abasajja abasatu abaali ku mutwe gwe ogwa mast-head baali bambadde emiguwa emiwanvu egya bunting emmyufu enfunda ku nkofiira zaabwe; okuva emabega w’eryato, eryato erya whale lyali liwanikiddwa, wansi wansi; era nga bawanikiddwa mu buwambe ku bbuutu yalabibwa akawanga akawanvu aka wansi ak’ennyanja ekika kya whale gye baasembayo okutta. Siginini, ensigns, ne jacks eza langi zonna byali bibuuka okuva mu rigging ye, ku buli ludda. Ebbali ezikubiddwa mu buli emu ku ngulu ze essatu ezaali zisiddwamu ebisero mwalimu ebipipa bibiri eby’ensigo; waggulu w’ekyo, mu miti gye egy’omusalaba egy’oku ntikko, walaba ebimenya ebigonvu eby’amazzi ge gamu ag’omuwendo; era nga ku loole ye enkulu kwaliko ettaala ey’ekikomo.

Nga bwe kyategeezeddwa oluvannyuma, Bachelor yali asisinkanye obuwanguzi obusinga okwewuunyisa; n’okusingawo, kubanga ekyo bwe baali batambulira mu nnyanja ze zimu amaato amalala mangi gaali gagenze emyezi gyonna nga tegafunye n’akatono. Tewaali bipipa bya nnyama ya nte n’omugaati byokka bye byali biweereddwayo okusobola okuteekawo ekifo ky’ensigo ez’omuwendo ennyo, naye n’ebidomola ebirala eby’okugatta byali biwanyisiganyizibwa, okuva ku mmeeri ze yali asisinkanye; era bino ne biterekebwa ku mabbali g’olutimbe, ne mu bisenge bya kapiteeni n’eby’abaserikale. N’emmeeza y’omu kabina yennyini yali ekooneddwa mu mbaawo ezikuma omuliro; era akavuyo ka kabina kaalidde ku mutwe omugazi ogw’ekikuta ky’amafuta, nga kikubiddwa wansi wansi olw’ekintu ekiri wakati. Mu forecastle, abalunnyanja mu butuufu baali bafunye caulk ne basuula ebifuba byabwe, ne babijjuza; kyayongerwako mu ngeri ey’okusekerera, nti omufumbi yali akubye omutwe ku bboyiyira ye esinga obunene, n’agijjuza; nti omuwanika yali asibye ekiyungu kye ekya kaawa ekya sipeeya n’akijjuza; nti abavuzi b’engoye baali bakutte emitwe gy’ebifo eby’ebyuma byabwe ne babijjuza; nti ddala buli kimu kyali kijjudde ensigo, okuggyako ensawo za kapiteeni eza pantaloons, n’ezo ze yatereka okuyingiza emikono gye, mu bujulizi obw’okwematiza obw’okumatizibwa kwe kwonna.

Nga emmeeri eno ey’omukisa ennungi egenda wansi ku Pequod eyali alina embeera, eddoboozi ery’obukambwe ery’engooma ennene ennyo lyava mu kifo kye eky’omu maaso; era nga basemberedde nnyo, ekibinja ky’abasajja be kyalabibwa nga bayimiridde okwetooloola ebiyungu bye ebinene ennyo eby’okugezesa, nga bino, nga bibikkiddwa n’olususu olulinga olususu oba olususu lw’olubuto olw’ebyennyanja ebiddugavu, nga biwa eddoboozi ery’omwanguka buli kukuba kw’emikono egyali ginywezeddwa abakozi b’oku mmeeri. Ku ddeeke ya kwata, abafumbo n’abavuzi b’engoye baali bazina n’abawala abaali ba langi y’omuzeyituuni abaali badduse nabo okuva mu bizinga by’e Polynesia; bwe baali bawaniriddwa mu lyato eryali liyooyooteddwa, nga linywezeddwa bulungi waggulu wakati w’olutindo lw’omu maaso n’olutindo olukulu, abaddugavu basatu ab’oku kizinga Long Island, nga balina ebivuga ebimasamasa eby’amasanga g’ennyanja whale, baali bakulembeddemu jig eyali esesa. Mu kiseera ekyo, abalala mu kkampuni y’emmeeri baali banyiikivu nnyo mu kuzimba amayinja mu bifo eby’okugezaako, ebiyungu ebinene mwe byali biggyiddwa. Kumpi wandirowoozezza nti basika wansi Bastille eyakolimirwa, emiranga egy’omu nsiko bwe gino gye gyasitula, ng’amabaati n’amayinja kati ebitaliimu mugaso bisuulibwa mu nnyanja.

Mukama era kafulu ku kifo kino kyonna, kapiteeni yayimirira nga yeegolodde ku ddeeke y’emmeeri eyali egulumiziddwa, katemba yenna ow’essanyu n’ajjula mu maaso ge, era n’alabika ng’ayiiya kwokka olw’okuwugula kwe ssekinnoomu.

Era Akabu, naye yali ayimiridde ku ddeeke ye ey’okuna, nga muddugavu era nga muddugavu, ng’alina ekizikiza ekikakanyavu; era ng’emmeeri zombi zisalagana okuzuukuka kwa munne —emu nga zonna za ssanyu olw’ebintu nga ziyitawo, endala zonna zaali ziteebereza ku bintu ebigenda okujja —ba kapiteeni baazo ababiri ku bwabyo beefuula enjawulo yonna ey’ekitalo ey’ekifo ekyo.

“Jjangu ku lyato, jjangu ku lyato!” omuduumizi wa Bachelor omugagga bwe yakaaba ng’asitula egiraasi n’eccupa mu bbanga.

“Olabye ku White Whale?” Akabu yasikambula ng’addamu.

"Nedda; yamuwulirako yekka; naye tomukkiririzaamu n’akatono,” omulala bwe yagamba mu ngeri ey’okusesa obulungi. “Jjangu olinnye ku lyato!”

“Oli too damned jolly. Sail on. Ofiiriddwa abasajja yenna?”

“Tekimala kwogera ku —babiri ku bizinga, ekyo kyokka;—naye jjangu ku mmeeri, omutima omukadde, mujje. Nja kuggyako ekiddugavu ekyo mu bbanga ttono ku kyenyi kyo. Mujje, mujja (merry's the play); emmeeri ejjude era edda eka.”

“Omusirusiru nga kyewuunyisa okumanyibwa!” Akabu bwe yayomba; Awo mu ddoboozi ery’omwanguka nti, “Ggwe emmeeri enzijuvu era ng’odda eka, ogamba nti; bulungi, n’olwekyo, mpite emmeeri etaliimu kintu kyonna, era egenda ebweru. Kale genda mu makubo go, nange nja gange. Mu maaso awo! Byonna mutambule, mumukuume ku mpewo!”

Era bwe kityo, emmeeri emu bwe yali egenda n’essanyu mu maaso g’empewo, endala n’alwana n’obukakanyavu; era bwe kityo ebibya byombi ne byawukana; abakozi b’e Pequod nga batunuulira n’amaaso ag’entaana, agawangaala nga boolekedde Bachelor eyali agenda emabega; naye abasajja ba Bachelor tebaassaayo mwoyo ku maaso gaabwe olw’okujaguza okw’obulamu kwe baali mu ekibbo, kyalabika ng’ekyo kigatta ebibiina bibiri eby’ewala, kubanga ekibbo ekyo kyali kijjudde amaloboozi ga Nantucket.





ESSUULA 116. Ennyama y’ennyanja (Whale) efa.

Si kya bulijjo mu bulamu buno, nga, ku ludda olwa ddyo, abasinga okwagalibwa omukisa basaabala okumpi naffe, ffe, wadde nga ffenna tugwa emabega, tukwata ekitono ku mpewo efubutuka, era n’essanyu tuwulira amaato gaffe ag’ensawo nga gajjula. Bwe kityo bwe kyalabika ne Pequod. Kubanga enkeera oluvannyuma lw’okusisinkana omusajja omugagga Bachelor, ennyanja ekika kya whale zaalabibwa era nnya ne zittibwa; n’ekimu ku byo ekya Akabu.

Yali wala nnyo emisana; era amafumu gonna ag’olutalo olw’erangi emmyufu bwe gaggwa: era nga balengejja mu nnyanja ennungi ennyo eyagwa enjuba n’eggulu, enjuba n’ennyanja ekika kya whale byombi byafa wamu; awo, obuwoomi obw’engeri eyo n’okwemulugunya ennyo, ebiwujjo ebiwunyiriza ennyo nga bizingiddwa mu mpewo eyo eya rose, ne kiba nti kumpi kyalabika ng’ewala nnyo okuva mu biwonvu by’ekigo ebya kiragala ebizito eby’ebizinga by’e Manilla, empewo y’oku lukalu ey’e Spain, eyafuuka omuvubi mu ngeri ey’ekibogwe, yali egenze ku nnyanja, nga zitikkiddwa n’ennyimba zino ez’omusana.

Nga azzeemu okukkakkana, naye n’akkakkana okutuuka mu kizikiza eky’amaanyi, Akabu, eyali avudde ku nnyanja ekika kya whale, yatuula n’obwegendereza ng’atunuulira okukendeera kwe okusembayo okuva ku lyato kati eryali literedde. Kubanga ekyewuunyo ekyo ekyewuunyisa ekirabika mu sperm whale zonna nga zifa —okukyuka kw’enjuba okudda ku mutwe, era nga ziggwaako bwe zityo —ekyewuunyo ekyo ekyewuunyisa, ekyalabibwa akawungeezi akateredde bwe kiti, mu ngeri emu oba endala eri Akabu kyatuusa ekyewuunyo ekyali tekimanyiddwa.

“Akyuka n’amukyusa n’agitunuulira, —nga mpola, naye nga munywevu, ekibatu kye eky’okuwa ekitiibwa n’okukoowoola, n’ebikolwa bye ebisembayo okufa. Naye asinza omuliro; asinga omwesigwa, omugazi, baronial vassal of the sun!—Oh singa amaaso gano agasinga okuganyulwa galina okulaba ebifo bino ebisukkiridde okuganyulwa. Laba! wano, ewala nga zisibiddwa amazzi; okusukka okuwuuma kwonna okw’obugagga oba ennaku z’omuntu; mu nnyanja zino ezisinga okuba ez’obwesimbu era ezitaliimu kyekubiira; awali ennono tewali njazi ziwa bipande; awali okumala emyaka mingi egy’Abachina, amayengo gakyayiringisibwa nga tegalina kwogera era nga tegayogerwako, ng’emmunyeenye ezimasamasa ku nsibuko ya Niger etamanyiddwa; wano era, obulamu bufa enjuba nga bujjudde okukkiriza; naye laba! amangu ddala nga kifudde, okufa kwetoloola omulambo, ne gwolekera ekkubo eddala.

“Oo, ggwe ekitundu ky’obutonde eky’ekizikiza eky’Abahindu, eyazimba entebe yo ey’obwakabaka ey’enjawulo awalala mu mutima gw’ennyanja zino ezitali za bulijjo; oli mukaafiiri, ggwe nnaabagereka, era oyogera nange mu mazima nnyo mu kibuyaga ekitta ennyo, n’okuziika mu kasirise okw’obukkakkamu bwagwo oluvannyuma lw’obukkakkamu. Era ono whale yo teyakyusizza mutwe gwe ogugenda okufa, n’oluvannyuma n’addamu okwetooloola, nga tewali ssomo gyendi.

“Oo, trebly hooped ne welded hip y’amaanyi! Oh, high aspiring, rainbowed jet! —oyo afuba, ono jettest byonna bya bwereere! Mu bwereere, oh whale, onoonya intercedings ne yon all-quickening sun, eyita obulamu bwokka, naye nga siddamu kubuwaayo. Naye ggwe, ekitundu ekiddugavu, onkankanya n’okukkiriza okusingako amalala, bwe kiba nga kya kizikiza. Ebitabuddwa byo byonna ebitamanyiddwa mannya bitengejja wansi wange wano; Nnyumirwa omukka gw’ebiramu edda, ebifulumizibwa ng’empewo, naye kati amazzi.

“Oluvannyuma wala, emirembe gyonna, ayi ennyanja, mu kusuula kwayo okw’olubeerera ekinyonyi eky’omu nsiko mwe musanga ekiwummulo kye kyokka. Yazaalibwa mu nsi, naye nga ayonka ku nnyanja; newankubadde nga olusozi n’ekiwonvu byanzaala, amayengo aga kyenvu be baganda bange abakuza!”





ESSUULA 117. Omukuumi w’ennyanja Whale.

Envubu ennya ezattibwa akawungeezi ako zaali zifudde nga zaawukana; omu, ewala okutuuka ku mpewo; ekimu, ekitali kya wala nnyo, okutuuka ku leeward; omu mu maaso; ekimu eky’emabega. Bino ebisatu ebisembayo byaleetebwa ku mabbali ere nightfall; naye eyali ewunya empewo teyasobola kutuukirirwa okutuusa ku makya; n'eryato eryali ligisse ne ligalamira ku mabbali gaalyo ekiro kyonna; Era eryato eryo lyali lya Akabu.

Waif-pole yasuulibwa nga yeegolodde mu kinnya kya whale eyali efudde; era ettaala eyali ewaniridde waggulu waayo, yasuula ekitangaala ekizibu ekiwunya ku mugongo omuddugavu, ogwamasamasa, era ewala ku mayengo ag’omu ttuntu, agaanyiga mpola oludda olugazi olw’ennyanja ekika kya whale, ng’amayengo amagonvu ku bbiici.

Akabu n’abakozi bonna abaali ku lyato lye baali balabika nga beebase naye ng’Abaparsee; abaali bafukamidde mu butaasa, nga batuula nga balaba sharks, ezaazannya mu ngeri ey’ekitalo okwetooloola whale, era nga zikuba ebipande by’emivule ebitangaavu n’emikira gyazo. Eddoboozi ng’okuwowoggana mu bibinja ku Asphaltites z’emizimu egy’e Ggomola egitasonyiyibwa, lyadduka nga likankana mu bbanga.

Yatandika okuva mu tulo, Akabu, maaso ku maaso, yalaba Omuparsee; era nga beetooloola ekizikiza ky’ekiro nga balabika ng’abasajja abasembayo mu nsi eyali ejjudde amazzi. Yagamba nti: “Nze nzzeemu okukiroota.”

“Ku mmotoka z’abafu? Sigambye, musajja mukulu, nti tewali n’omu ssanduuko oba ssanduuko y’abafu tebayinza kuba ggwe?”

“Era baani abawulirwa abafiira ku nnyanja?”

“Naye ne ŋŋamba, omukadde, nti tonnafa ku lugendo luno, ddala emmeeri bbiri ez’okuziika ziteekwa okulabibwa ku nnyanja; Ekisooka ekitakolebwa mu ngalo ezifa; era enku ezirabika ez’ezo ezisembayo zirina okulimibwa mu Amerika.”

“Aye, aye! ekifaananyi ekyewuunyisa nti, Parsee:—emmeeri y’abafu n’ebiwujjo byayo ebitengejja waggulu w’ennyanja n’amayengo eri abasitula omulambo. Ha! Okulaba ng’okwo tetujja kukiraba mangu.”

“Kkiriza oba gaana, toyinza kufa okutuusa nga kirabibwa, musajja mukulu.”

“Era ekyo kyali kyogera ki ku ggwe kennyini?”

“Ne bwe kinaatuuka ku nkomerero, nja kukusooka okukulembera omugoba wo.”

“Era bw’oba ​​ogenze bw’otyo mu maaso — ekyo bwe kiba kituuse — olwo nga sinnaba kugoberera, olina okulabika gye ndi, okunvuga n’okutuusa?—Tekyali bwe kityo? Kale, kale, nnakkiriza byonna bye mwogera, ayi omugoba wange! Nnina wano ebisuubizo bibiri nti nja kuddamu okutta Moby Dick ne mmuwona.”

“Twala obweyamo obulala, omusajja omukadde,” bw’atyo Parsee bwe yagamba, ng’amaaso ge gaaka ng’enseenene z’omuliro mu kizikiza —“hemp only can kill you.”

Bombi ne baddamu okusirika, ng’omusajja omu. Enkya enzirugavu yayaka, era abakozi abaali beebase ne basituka okuva wansi w’eryato, era ng’emisana teginnatuuka ennyanja ekika kya whale eyafudde n’ereetebwa ku mmeeri.





ESSUULA 118. Ekitundu eky’okuna.

Sizoni ya Line mu buwanvu yasemberera; era buli lunaku Akabu, ng’ava mu kabina ye, bwe yasuula amaaso ge waggulu, omuvuzi w’amaato eyali bulindaala yakwatanga mu ngeri ey’okwefuula amaloboozi ge, era abavubi abaali baagala ennyo badduka mangu okutuuka ku bikondo, ne bayimirira awo ng’amaaso gaabwe gonna gatunudde wakati ku doubloon eyali efumbiddwa emisumaali; nga tagumiikiriza kiragiro ky’okusonga omutwe gw’emmeeri eri ekyengulu. Mu kiseera ekirungi ekiragiro kyajja. Kyabanga kizibu ku ssaawa 12 ez’emisana; era Akabu, ng’atudde mu butaasa bw’eryato lye eryali liwanvuye, yali anaatera okutwala enjuba gye yali amanyidde buli lunaku okusobola okuzuula latitude ye.

Kati, mu nnyanja eyo ey’e Japan, ennaku mu kyeya ziba nga freshets of effulgences. Oh, oh, ayi! Naye nga nzibe amaaso, naye nja kwogera naawe. Newaakubadde nga oli musana, obuuka okuva mu kizikiza; naye ndi kizikiza ekibuuka okuva mu musana, nga kibuuka okuva mu ggwe! Abajama bakoma; amaaso agaggule; olaba, oba nedda? There burn the flames! Oh, thou magnanimous! now I do glory in my genealogy. But thou art but my fiery father; my sweet mother, I know not. Oh, cruel! what hast thou done with her? There lies my puzzle; but thine is greater. Thou knowest not how came ye, hence callest thyself unbegotten; certainly knowest not thy beginning, hence callest thyself unbegun. I know that of me, which thou knowest not of thyself, oh, thou omnipotent. There is some unsuffusing thing beyond thee, thou clear spirit, to whom all thy eternity is but time, all thy creativeness mechanical. Through thee, thy flaming self, my scorched eyes do dimly see it. Oh, thou foundling fire, thou hermit immemorial, thou too hast thy incommunicable riddle, thy unparticipated grief. Here again with haughty agony, I read my sire. Leap! leap up, and lick the sky! I leap with thee; I burn with thee; would fain be welded with thee; defyingly I worship thee!”

“The boat! the boat!” cried Starbuck, “look at thy boat, old man!”

Ahab's harpoon, the one forged at Perth's fire, remained firmly lashed in its conspicuous crotch, so that it projected beyond his whale-boat's bow; but the sea that had stove its bottom had caused the loose leather sheath to drop off; and from the keen steel barb there now came a levelled flame of pale, forked fire. As the silent harpoon burned there like a serpent's tongue, Starbuck grasped Ahab by the arm—“God, God is against thee, old man; forbear! 'tis an ill voyage! ill begun, ill continued; let me square the yards, while we may, old man, and make a fair wind of it homewards, to go on a better voyage than this.”

Overhearing Starbuck, the panic-stricken crew instantly ran to the braces—though not a sail was left aloft. For the moment all the aghast mate's thoughts seemed theirs; they raised a half mutinous cry. But dashing the rattling lightning links to the deck, and snatching the burning harpoon, Ahab waved it like a torch among them; swearing to transfix with it the first sailor that but cast loose a rope's end. Petrified by his aspect, and still more shrinking from the fiery dart that he held, the men fell back in dismay, and Ahab again spoke:—

“All your oaths to hunt the White Whale are as binding as mine; and heart, soul, and body, lungs and life, old Ahab is bound. And that ye may know to what tune this heart beats; look ye here; thus I blow out the last fear!” And with one blast of his breath he extinguished the flame.

As in the hurricane that sweeps the plain, men fly the neighborhood of some lone, gigantic elm, whose very height and strength but render it so much the more unsafe, because so much the more a mark for thunderbolts; so at those last words of Ahab's many of the mariners did run from him in a terror of dismay.





CHAPTER 120. The Deck Towards the End of the First Night Watch.

Ahab standing by the helm. Starbuck approaching him.

“We must send down the main-top-sail yard, sir. The band is working loose and the lee lift is half-stranded. Shall I strike it, sir?”

“Strike nothing; lash it. If I had sky-sail poles, I'd sway them up now.”

“Sir!—in God's name!—sir?”

“Well.”

“The anchors are working, sir. Shall I get them inboard?”

“Strike nothing, and stir nothing, but lash everything. The wind rises, but it has not got up to my table-lands yet. Quick, and see to it.—By masts and keels! he takes me for the hunch-backed skipper of some coasting smack. Send down my main-top-sail yard! Ho, gluepots! Loftiest trucks were made for wildest winds, and this brain-truck of mine now sails amid the cloud-scud. Shall I strike that? Oh, none but cowards send down their brain-trucks in tempest time. What a hooroosh aloft there! I would e'en take it for sublime, did I not know that the colic is a noisy malady. Oh, take medicine, take medicine!”





CHAPTER 121. Midnight.—The Forecastle Bulwarks.

Stubb and Flask mounted on them, and passing additional lashings over the anchors there hanging.

“No, Stubb; you may pound that knot there as much as you please, but you will never pound into me what you were just now saying. And how long ago is it since you said the very contrary? Didn't you once say that whatever ship Ahab sails in, that ship should pay something extra on its insurance policy, just as though it were loaded with powder barrels aft and boxes of lucifers forward? Stop, now; didn't you say so?”

“Well, suppose I did? What then? I've part changed my flesh since that time, why not my mind? Besides, supposing we are loaded with powder barrels aft and lucifers forward; how the devil could the lucifers get afire in this drenching spray here? Why, my little man, you have pretty red hair, but you couldn't get afire now. Shake yourself; you're Aquarius, or the water-bearer, Flask; might fill pitchers at your coat collar. Don't you see, then, that for these extra risks the Marine Insurance companies have extra guarantees? Here are hydrants, Flask. But hark, again, and I'll answer ye the other thing. First take your leg off from the crown of the anchor here, though, so I can pass the rope; now listen. What's the mighty difference between holding a mast's lightning-rod in the storm, and standing close by a mast that hasn't got any lightning-rod at all in a storm? Don't you see, you timber-head, that no harm can come to the holder of the rod, unless the mast is first struck? What are you talking about, then? Not one ship in a hundred carries rods, and Ahab,—aye, man, and all of us,—were in no more danger then, in my poor opinion, than all the crews in ten thousand ships now sailing the seas. Why, you King-Post, you, I suppose you would have every man in the world go about with a small lightning-rod running up the corner of his hat, like a militia officer's skewered feather, and trailing behind like his sash. Why don't ye be sensible, Flask? it's easy to be sensible; why don't ye, then? any man with half an eye can be sensible.”

“I don't know that, Stubb. You sometimes find it rather hard.”

“Yes, when a fellow's soaked through, it's hard to be sensible, that's a fact. And I am about drenched with this spray. Never mind; catch the turn there, and pass it. Seems to me we are lashing down these anchors now as if they were never going to be used again. Tying these two anchors here, Flask, seems like tying a man's hands behind him. And what big generous hands they are, to be sure. These are your iron fists, hey? What a hold they have, too! I wonder, Flask, whether the world is anchored anywhere; if she is, she swings with an uncommon long cable, though. There, hammer that knot down, and we've done. So; next to touching land, lighting on deck is the most satisfactory. I say, just wring out my jacket skirts, will ye? Thank ye. They laugh at long-togs so, Flask; but seems to me, a long tailed coat ought always to be worn in all storms afloat. The tails tapering down that way, serve to carry off the water, d'ye see. Same with cocked hats; the cocks form gable-end eave-troughs, Flask. No more monkey-jackets and tarpaulins for me; I must mount a swallow-tail, and drive down a beaver; so. Halloa! whew! there goes my tarpaulin overboard; Lord, Lord, that the winds that come from heaven should be so unmannerly! This is a nasty night, lad.”

CHAPTER 123. The Musket.

During the most violent shocks of the Typhoon, the man at the Pequod's jaw-bone tiller had several times been reelingly hurled to the deck by its spasmodic motions, even though preventer tackles had been attached to it—for they were slack—because some play to the tiller was indispensable.

In a severe gale like this, while the ship is but a tossed shuttlecock to the blast, it is by no means uncommon to see the needles in the compasses, at intervals, go round and round. It was thus with the Pequod's; at almost every shock the helmsman had not failed to notice the whirling velocity with which they revolved upon the cards; it is a sight that hardly anyone can behold without some sort of unwonted emotion.

Some hours after midnight, the Typhoon abated so much, that through the strenuous exertions of Starbuck and Stubb—one engaged forward and the other aft—the shivered remnants of the jib and fore and main-top-sails were cut adrift from the spars, and went eddying away to leeward, like the feathers of an albatross, which sometimes are cast to the winds when that storm-tossed bird is on the wing.

The three corresponding new sails were now bent and reefed, and a storm-trysail was set further aft; so that the ship soon went through the water with some precision again; and the course—for the present, East-south-east—which he was to steer, if practicable, was once more given to the helmsman. For during the violence of the gale, he had only steered according to its vicissitudes. But as he was now bringing the ship as near her course as possible, watching the compass meanwhile, lo! a good sign! the wind seemed coming round astern; aye, the foul breeze became fair!

Instantly the yards were squared, to the lively song of “ Ho! the fair wind! oh-ye-ho, cheerly men! ” the crew singing for joy, that so promising an event should so soon have falsified the evil portents preceding it.

In compliance with the standing order of his commander—to report immediately, and at any one of the twenty-four hours, any decided change in the affairs of the deck,—Starbuck had no sooner trimmed the yards to the breeze—however reluctantly and gloomily,—than he mechanically went below to apprise Captain Ahab of the circumstance.

Ere knocking at his state-room, he involuntarily paused before it a moment. The cabin lamp—taking long swings this way and that—was burning fitfully, and casting fitful shadows upon the old man's bolted door,—a thin one, with fixed blinds inserted, in place of upper panels. The isolated subterraneousness of the cabin made a certain humming silence to reign there, though it was hooped round by all the roar of the elements. The loaded muskets in the rack were shiningly revealed, as they stood upright against the forward bulkhead. Starbuck was an honest, upright man; but out of Starbuck's heart, at that instant when he saw the muskets, there strangely evolved an evil thought; but so blent with its neutral or good accompaniments that for the instant he hardly knew it for itself.

“He would have shot me once,” he murmured, “yes, there's the very musket that he pointed at me;—that one with the studded stock; let me touch it—lift it. Strange, that I, who have handled so many deadly lances, strange, that I should shake so now. Loaded? I must see. Aye, aye; and powder in the pan;—that's not good. Best spill it?—wait. I'll cure myself of this. I'll hold the musket boldly while I think.—I come to report a fair wind to him. But how fair? Fair for death and doom,— that’s fair for Moby Dick. It's a fair wind that's only fair for that accursed fish.—The very tube he pointed at me!—the very one; this one—I hold it here; he would have killed me with the very thing I handle now.—Aye and he would fain kill all his crew. Does he not say he will not strike his spars to any gale? Has he not dashed his heavenly quadrant? and in these same perilous seas, gropes he not his way by mere dead reckoning of the error-abounding log? and in this very Typhoon, did he not swear that he would have no lightning-rods? But shall this crazed old man be tamely suffered to drag a whole ship's company down to doom with him?—Yes, it would make him the wilful murderer of thirty men and more, if this ship come to any deadly harm; and come to deadly harm, my soul swears this ship will, if Ahab have his way. If, then, he were this instant—put aside, that crime would not be his. Ha! is he muttering in his sleep? Yes, just there,—in there, he's sleeping. Sleeping? aye, but still alive, and soon awake again. I can't withstand thee, then, old man. Not reasoning; not remonstrance; not entreaty wilt thou hearken to; all this thou scornest. Flat obedience to thy own flat commands, this is all thou breathest. Aye, and say'st the men have vow'd thy vow; say'st all of us are Ahabs. Great God forbid!—But is there no other way? no lawful way?—Make him a prisoner to be taken home? What! hope to wrest this old man's living power from his own living hands? Only a fool would try it. Say he were pinioned even; knotted all over with ropes and hawsers; chained down to ring-bolts on this cabin floor; he would be more hideous than a caged tiger, then. I could not endure the sight; could not possibly fly his howlings; all comfort, sleep itself, inestimable reason would leave me on the long intolerable voyage. What, then, remains? The land is hundreds of leagues away, and locked Japan the nearest. I stand alone here upon an open sea, with two oceans and a whole continent between me and law.—Aye, aye, 'tis so.—Is heaven a murderer when its lightning strikes a would-be murderer in his bed, tindering sheets and skin together?—And would I be a murderer, then, if”—and slowly, stealthily, and half sideways looking, he placed the loaded musket's end against the door.

“On this level, Ahab's hammock swings within; his head this way. A touch, and Starbuck may survive to hug his wife and child again.—Oh Mary! Mary!—boy! boy! boy!—But if I wake thee not to death, old man, who can tell to what unsounded deeps Starbuck's body this day week may sink, with all the crew! Great God, where art Thou? Shall I? shall I?—The wind has gone down and shifted, sir; the fore and main topsails are reefed and set; she heads her course.”

“Stern all! Oh Moby Dick, I clutch thy heart at last!”

Such were the sounds that now came hurtling from out the old man's tormented sleep, as if Starbuck's voice had caused the long dumb dream to speak.

The yet levelled musket shook like a drunkard's arm against the panel; Starbuck seemed wrestling with an angel; but turning from the door, he placed the death-tube in its rack, and left the place.

“He's too sound asleep, Mr. Stubb; go thou down, and wake him, and tell him. I must see to the deck here. Thou know'st what to say.”
