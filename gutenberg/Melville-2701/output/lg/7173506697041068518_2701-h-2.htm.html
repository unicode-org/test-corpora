ESSUULA 4. Ekintu ekiyitibwa Counterpane.

Bwe nnazuukuka enkeera ku makya ng’emisana, nnasanga omukono gwa Queequeg nga gusuuliddwa waggulu wange mu ngeri esinga okwagala n’omukwano. Kumpi wali olowooza nti nnali mukyala we. Counterpane yali ya patchwork, nga ejjudde odd little parti-colored squares ne triangles; era omukono guno ogwa ttatu wonna n’oluwuzi lw’e Kuleete olutakoma olwa ffiga, nga tewali bitundu bibiri byali bya kisiikirize kimu ekituufu —nsuubira olw’okukuuma omukono gwe ku nnyanja mu ngeri etaali ya nkola mu musana n’ekisiikirize, emikono gy’essaati ye nga gizingiddwa mu ngeri etaali ya bulijjo mu biseera eby’enjawulo —omukono guno gwe gumu ogwa ye, ngamba, gwanoonya ensi yonna ng’olutimbe lwa kkiiti eyo y’emu eya patchwork. Mazima ddala, ekitundu nga kigalamidde ku kyo ng’omukono bwe gwakola nga nsoose okuzuukuka, nnali sisobola kukitegeera bulungi okuva ku kkiiti, bwe batyo ne batabula langi zaabwe; era olw’okuwulira obuzito n’okunyigirizibwa kwe nnasobola okutegeera nti Queequeg yali annywegera.

Sensations zange zaali za kyewuunyo. Ka nfube okubinnyonnyola. Bwe nnali nkyali mwana muto, nzijukira bulungi embeera efaananako bwetyo eyantuukako; ka kibeere nti kyali kituufu oba kirooto, saasobola kutereera ddala. Embeera yali bweti. Nnali nsalako caper oba endala —ndowooza nti yali egezaako okwekulukuunya waggulu ku ssigiri, nga bwe nnali ndabye akasenya akatono nga kakola ennaku ntono emabega; ne maama wange omuto eyali, mu ngeri emu oba endala, buli kiseera ng’ankuba emiggo, oba ng’ansindika ku kitanda nga sirina kyaggulo, — maama yansika amagulu okuva mu ssigiri n’anpakira okugenda ku kitanda, wadde nga zaali ssaawa bbiri zokka akawungeezi ka nga 21st June, olunaku olusinga obuwanvu mu mwaka mu kitundu kyaffe eky’ensi. Nawulira nga ntya nnyo. Naye tewaaliwo buyambi bwonna, bwentyo okulinnya amadaala nagenda mu kasenge kange akatono akaali ku mwaliiro ogwokusatu, ne nneeyambula mpola nga bwe kisoboka nsobole okutta obudde, era n’okusinda okukaawa ne nnyingira wakati w’ebitambaala.

Nagalamira awo mu ngeri ey’ennaku nga mbalirira nti essaawa kkumi na mukaaga zonna zirina okuyitawo nga sinnasuubira kuzuukira. Essaawa kkumi na mukaaga mu buliri! obutono bw’omugongo gwange bwannuma okukirowoozaako. Era nakyo kyali kitangaavu nnyo; enjuba ng’eyaka mu ddirisa, n’okuwuuma okunene okw’abatendesi mu nguudo, n’eddoboozi ly’amaloboozi g’abasajja abagaala ebisiyaga mu nnyumba yonna. Nawulira bubi nnyo —ku nkomerero nnasituka, ne nnyambala, era nga ngenda wansi mpola mu bigere byange ebyabaddemu sitokisi, ne nnoonya maama wange omuto, era amangu ago ne nneesuula ku bigere bye, nga mmusaba ng’ekisa eky’enjawulo okumpa okuseerera okulungi ku lwange okweyisa obubi; ekintu kyonna ddala wabula okunsalira omusango okulimba abed so un endurable length of time. Naye ye yali asinga era asinga okubeera n’omuntu ow’omunda mu bamaama abato, era okuddayo nnalina okugenda mu kisenge kyange. Okumala essaawa eziwerako nnagalamira awo nga nzuukuse, nga mpulira bubi nnyo okusinga bwe nnali nkola okuva olwo, ne bwe nnava ku mikisa emibi egyaddirira. At last nteekwa okuba nga nnagwa mu kirooto ekizibu eky’okuzirika; era nga nzuukuka mpola okuva mu kyo —ekitundu nga nnyikiddwa mu birooto —nazibula amaaso gange, era ekisenge ekyali tekinnaba kutangaala musana kati kyali kizingiddwa mu kizikiza eky’ebweru. Amangu ago nawulira ensisi ng’edduka mu fuleemu yange yonna; tewali kintu kyonna kyali kigenda kulabibwa, era tewali kyali kiwulirwa; naye omukono ogusukkulumye ku butonde gwalabika nga guteekeddwa mu gwange. Omukono gwange gwali guwaniridde ku counterpane, era ekifaananyi oba phantom ekitaliiko linnya, ekitalowoozebwako, ekisirise, omukono gwe gwali, kyalabika nga kitudde kumpi ku mabbali g’ekitanda kyange. Kubanga ekyali kirabika ng’emyaka gitumbidde ku myaka, nnagalamira awo, nga nfuuse bbugumu olw’okutya okusinga obubi, nga sigumiikiriza kusika mukono gwange; naye nga ndowooza nti singa nsobola naye okugisika yinsi emu, ekirooto eky’entiisa kyandibadde kimenyese. Nnali simanyi ngeri kutegeera kuno gye kwasembayo okuseerera okuva gyendi; naye nga nzuukuka ku makya, byonna nnabijjukira nga nkankana, era okumala ennaku ne wiiki n’emyezi oluvannyuma ne nfiirwa mu kugezaako okutabula okunnyonnyola ekyama. Nedda, okutuuka ku ssaawa eno yennyini, ntera okwesobera nakyo.

Kati, ggyawo okutya okw’entiisa, n’okuwulira kwange okw’okuwulira omukono ogusukkulumye ku butonde mu gwange kwali kufaanagana nnyo, mu kwewuunya kwabwo, n’ebyo bye nnayitamu nga nzuukuse ne ndaba omukono gwa Queequeg ogw’ekikaafiiri nga gusuuliddwa okwetooloola. Naye mu kiseera ekiwanvu byonna ebyaliwo ekiro ekyayita byaddibwamu mu ngeri ey’obwegendereza, kimu ku kimu, mu butuufu obutakyukakyuka, olwo ne ngalamira nga ndi mulamu ku mbeera enzibu ey’okusekerera yokka. Kubanga newankubadde nga nagezaako okutambuza omukono gwe —okusumulula ekikwaso kye eky’omugole omusajja —naye, nga yeebase nga bwe yali, yali akyannywegera nnyo, ng’alinga agamba nti tewali kintu kirala kyonna okuggyako okufa kye kisaanidde okutwawulamu babiri. Kati nnafuba okumuzuukusa —“Queequeg!”—naye eky’okuddamu kye kyokka kyali kya kusinda. Olwo ne nneekulukuunya, ng’ensingo yange mpulira ng’eri mu kkoolaasi y’embalaasi; era amangu ago n’awulira ng’akutte akatono. Nga basuula counterpane ku bbali, awo weegalamidde tomahawk nga yeebase ku mabbali g’omusajja omukambwe, ng’alinga omwana ow’amaaso ag’ekiso. A pretty pickle, ddala, nnalowooza nti; abed wano mu nnyumba etali ya bulijjo mu lunaku olugazi, nga mulimu omulya abantu ne tomahawk! “Queequeg!—mu linnya ly’obulungi, Queequeg, zuukuka!” Mu buwanvu, olw’okuwuubaala ennyo, n’okunnyonnyola okw’amaanyi era okutasalako ku butasaana bwa kunywegera musajja munne mu sitayiro eyo ey’obufumbo, nnasobola okuggyamu okuwuuma; era mu kiseera kino, yazza omukono gwe emabega, ne yeekankanya wonna ng’embwa ya Newfoundland ng’evudde mu mazzi, n’atuula waggulu ku kitanda, ng’akalubye ng’omuggo gwa pike, ng’antunuulira, era ng’asika amaaso ge ng’alinga atajjukira ddala engeri gye nnatuuka okubeera eyo, wadde ng’okutegeera okuzibu okw’okumanya ekintu ku nze kwalabika ng’okumukya mpola. Mu kiseera ekyo, nnagalamira mu kasirise nga mmutunuulira, nga kati sirina kubuusabuusa kwonna kwa maanyi, era ne nfukamira ku kwetegereza mu bufunda ekitonde ekyetaaga okumanya ennyo. Ku nkomerero, ebirowoozo bye bwe byalabika ng’ebikoleddwa nga bikwata ku mpisa za munne ow’oku kitanda, era n’afuuka, nga bwe tuyinza okugamba, okutabagana n’ensonga; yabuuka n’afuluma wansi, era olw’obubonero n’amaloboozi ebimu ne bimpa okutegeera nti, bwe kiba nga kinsanyusa, yandisoose okwambala n’oluvannyuma n’anlekera okwambala oluvannyuma, omuzigo gwonna n’aguleka nzekka. Alowooza nze Queequeg, wansi w’embeera, eno overture ya mpisa nnyo; naye, ekituufu kiri nti, ensolo zino enkambwe zirina obuzaale obw’obuwoomi, yogera ky’oyagala; kyewuunyisa engeri mu bukulu gye balina empisa. Nsasula okusiima kuno okwenjawulo eri Queequeg, kubanga yanyisa mu ngeri ey’empisa n’okufaayo ennyo, so nga nze nnali n’omusango gw’obugwenyufu obw’amaanyi; ng’amutunuulira ng’ali ku kitanda, era ng’atunuulira entambula ye yonna eya kaabuyonjo; olw’ekiseera okwegomba kwange okw’okumanya kweyongera okunsinga okuzaala kwange. Wadde kiri kityo, omusajja nga Queequeg gw’otolaba buli lunaku, ye n’amakubo ge byali bisaanidde nnyo ebitali bya bulijjo okubitunuulira.

Yatandika okwambala waggulu ng’ayambala enkoofiira ye ey’ekika kya beaver, eyali empanvu ennyo, ku mabbali, olwo —ng’akyaggyako bbulawuzi ze —n’ayigga engatto ze. Kiki kye yakikolera wansi w’eggulu, siyinza kukimanya, naye ekikolwa kye ekyaddako kyali kya kwenyiga —obutto mu ngalo, n’enkoofiira —wansi w’ekitanda; bwe, okuva ku sundry violent gaspings and strainings, I inferred nti yali akola nnyo ng’akuba bbuutu; newankubadde nga tewali tteeka lya nkola lye nnali mpuliddeko, omusajja yenna yeetaagibwa okuba ow’ekyama ng’ayambala engatto ze. Naye Queequeg, olaba, yali kitonde mu mutendera gw’enkyukakyuka —si kiwuka wadde ekiwujjo. Yali wa mpisa ekimala okulaga obuswandi bwe mu ngeri ezisinga okwewuunyisa. Okusoma kwe kwali tekunnaggwa. Yali muyizi wa diguli esooka. Singa teyabadde mu mpisa ntono, osanga nnyo teyandibadde yeetawaanya na bbuutu n’akatono; naye olwo, singa teyabadde musajja mukambwe, teyandiloose kuyingira wansi w’ekitanda okuziteeka. Mu nkomerero, yavaayo ng’enkoofiira ye efuukuuse nnyo era ng’enyiganyiga wansi ku maaso ge, n’atandika okuwuuma n’okuwuubaala mu kisenge, ng’alinga, nga tamanyidde nnyo bbuutu, engatto ze ez’amaliba g’ente ennyogovu, eziriko ennyiriri —oboolyawo nga tezikoleddwa order either —okusinga yamunyiga n’okumubonyaabonya ku kusooka okugenda ku makya agaali gannyogovu ennyo.

Okulaba, kati, nti tewaaliwo kateni ku ddirisa, era nti oluguudo lwali lufunda nnyo, ennyumba eyali eyolekedde yalagira okulaba obulungi mu kisenge, era nga yeeyongera okwetegereza ekifaananyi ekitali kya kitiibwa Queequeg kye yakola, ng’adduka nga talina kirala kitono okuggyako enkoofiira ye n’engatto ze ayambadde; Namwegayirira nga bwe nsobola, ayanguyize kaabuyonjo ye katono, n’okusingira ddala okuyingira mu pantaloons ze amangu ddala nga bwe kisoboka. Yagondera, n’oluvannyuma n’agenda mu maaso n’okunaaba. Mu kiseera ekyo ku makya Omukristaayo yenna yandibadde anaaba mu maaso; naye Queequeg, ekyanneewuunyisa, yamatira n’okukoma ku wudhu ze ku kifuba, emikono n’emikono. Oluvannyuma yayambala ekiwato kye, n’akwata ssabbuuni omukalu ku mmeeza eyali wakati mu wash-stand, n’aginnyika mu mazzi n’atandika okufuumuula mu maaso. Nali ntunula okulaba gye yatereka enviiri ze, lo and behold, n’aggya ensambaggere mu nsonda y’ekitanda, n’aseerera n’afulumyayo sitokisi empanvu ey’embaawo, n’asumulula omutwe, n’agikuba katono ku bbuutu ye, n’agenda waggulu okutuuka ku kitundu wa ndabirwamu ku bbugwe, atandika okusenya n’amaanyi, oba okusingawo okukuba amatama ge. Thinks I, Queequeg, kino kikozesa cutlery za Rogers ezisinga obulungi nga zirina okwesasuza. Oluvannyuma nneebuuza ekitono ku kulongoosa kuno bwe nnatuuka okumanya ekyuma ekirungi omutwe gw’engoye gye gukolebwa, n’engeri empenda empanvu ezigolokofu gye zikuumibwamu nga nsongovu nnyo bulijjo.

Kaabuyonjo ye eyasigaddewo mu bbanga ttono yatuukirira, era n’atambula n’amalala n’afuluma ekisenge, ng’azingidde mu jaketi ye ennene ey’enkima eya pilot, era ng’azannya ensambaggere ye ng’omuggo gwa marshal.





ESSUULA 5. Ekyenkya.

Nagoberera mangu, era okukka mu kisenge ky’ebbaala accosted landiroodi eyali amwenya mu ngeri esanyusa nnyo. Nze saali nnyini bubi bwonna gy’ali, wadde nga yali abadde akuba skylarking nange si katono mu nsonga ya munnange ow’oku kitanda.

Kyokka, okuseka okulungi kintu kirungi eky’amaanyi, ate okusinga ekintu ekirungi eky’ebbula ennyo; gye kikoma okusaasira. Kale, singa omusajja yenna omu, mu muntu we omutuufu, asobola okwetuusaako ebintu olw’okusaaga okulungi eri omuntu yenna, aleme kudda mabega, wabula akkiriza n’essanyu okusaasaanya n’okusaasaanyizibwa mu ngeri eyo. Era omusajja alina ekintu kyonna ekisekererwa ennyo, kakasa nti mu musajja oyo mulimu bingi okusinga mpozzi by’olowooza.

Ekisenge ky’ebbaala kati kyali kijjudde aba boarder abaali basuula mu kiro ekyayita, era nga n’okutuusa kati nnali sinnaba kutunuulira bulungi. Kumpi bonna baali bavuzi ba nnyanja; abakulu, n’abafumbo ab’okubiri, n’abafumbo ab’okusatu, n’abaweesi b’ennyanja, n’abaweesi b’ennyanja, n’abaweesi b’ennyanja, n’abakubi b’engoye, n’abakuumi b’emmeeri; kkampuni ya kitaka ate nga ya maanyi, ng’erina ebirevu bya bosky; set etalina masala, eriko ebiwujjo, nga bonna bambadde obukooti bw’enkima obw’engoye z’oku makya.

Oyinza okutegeera bulungi ebbanga buli emu lye yali emaze ku lukalu. Ettama ly’omuvubuka ono eriramu liringa amapeera agasiigiddwa omusana mu langi, era yandirabise ng’ewunya kumpi nga musky; tayinza kuba nga yali amaze ennaku ssatu ku lukalu okuva ku lugendo lwe olw’e Buyindi. Omusajja oyo amuddirira alabika nga muweweevu ebisiikirize bitono; oyinza okugamba nti okukwata ku muti gwa satin kuli mu ye. Mu langi y’ekitundu eky’okusatu ekyasigalawo omusota ogw’obutiti, naye nga gumyufu katono withal; awatali kubuusabuusa amaze wiiki zonna ku lukalu. Naye ani ayinza okulaga ettama nga Queequeg? nga zino, nga ziriko langi ez’enjawulo, zaali zirabika ng’olusozi Andes olw’amaserengeta, okulaga mu nsengeka emu, embeera z’obudde ezaawukana, zoni ku zoni.

“Grub, ho!” kati landiroodi yakaaba, ng’asuula oluggi, ne tuyingira ne tugenda okulya ekyenkya.

Bagamba nti abasajja abalabye ensi, bwe batyo ne bafuuka ba mirembe nnyo mu ngeri, nga beefuula nnyo mu kibiina. Kyokka si bulijjo: Ledyard, omutambuze omukulu ow’e New England, ne Mungo Park, ow’e Scotland; mu basajja bonna, be baalina obukakafu obutono mu paalamenti. Naye mpozzi okusomoka Siberia kwokka mu sseddume eyasika embwa nga Ledyard bwe yakola, oba okutambula okuwanvu okw’omuntu omu nga tolina lubuto, mu mutima gw’abaddugavu mu Afirika, nga guno gwe gwali omugatte gw’ebikolwa bya Mungo omwavu —okutambula okw’ekika kino, . Nze ngamba, ayinza obutaba ngeri esinga nnyo ey’okutuuka ku ‘high social polish’. Wadde kiri kityo, okusinga obungi, ekintu eky’ekika ekyo kirina okubaawo wonna.

Ebifumiitiriza bino wano wennyini biva ku mbeera nti oluvannyuma lw’okutuula ffenna ku mmeeza, era nga nneetegekera okuwulira emboozi ennungi ezikwata ku kuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale; ekyanneewuunyisa nnyo, kumpi buli musajja yasirika nnyo. Era si ekyo kyokka, naye baali balabika nga baswadde. Yee, wano waaliwo ekibinja ky’embwa z’omu nnyanja, nga nnyingi ku zo awatali kuswala n’akatono ku zo ezaali zirinnye envubu ennene ku nnyanja ennene —nga zizitamanyidde ddala —ne zizikuba enduulu nga zifudde awatali kuziba maaso; era naye, wano baatuula ku mmeeza ey’ekyenkya ey’omukwano —bonna nga bayitibwa kwe kumu, bonna nga bawooma mu ngeri y’emu —nga buli omu atunuulira munne mu ngeri ey’ensonyi nga bwe batabangako mu maaso ga kisibo ky’endiga ekimu wakati mu nsozi za Green. Ekifaananyi ekyewuunyisa; zino eddubu ery’ensonyi, bano abalwanyi abavuba ennyanja ekika kya whale abalina ensonyi!

Naye nga ye Queequeg — lwaki, Queequeg yatuula awo wakati waabwe —ku mutwe gw’emmeeza, naye, kyali kya butanwa nnyo; nga nnyogovu nga icicle. Okukakasa siyinza kwogera bingi ku kulunda kwe. Omuwagizi we asinga teyayinza kuwa nsonga mu ngeri ey’omukwano nti yaleeta engoye ye mu ky’enkya naye, n’agikozesa eyo awatali mukolo; ng’atuuka waggulu w’emmeeza nayo, okutuuka ku kabi akaali kagenda okutuuka ku mitwe mingi, n’okukwata ennyama y’ente gy’ali. Naye ekyo mazima ddala kyakolebwa mu ngeri ennyogovu ye, era buli omu akimanyi nti mu kuteebereza kw’abantu abasinga obungi, okukola ekintu kyonna mu ngeri ennyogovu kwe kukikola mu ngeri ey’obuseegu.

Tetujja kwogera ku bintu byonna eby’enjawulo ebya Queequeg wano; engeri gye yeewala kaawa n’emizingo egyokya, n’okussaayo omwoyo gwe ogutagabanyizibwa ku nnyama y’ente, ezikolebwa nga tezitera kubaawo. Enough, nti ekyenkya bwe kyaggwa n’avaayo ng’abalala n’ayingira mu kisenge ky’olukale, n’akoleeza payipu ye eya tomahawk, era yali atudde awo mu kasirise ng’agaaya era ng’anywa sigala ng’ayambadde enkoofiira ye etayawukana, ne nfuluma okutambulako.





ESSUULA 6. Oluguudo.

Singa mu kusooka nnali nwuniikirira okukwata ku muntu ssekinnoomu ow’enjawulo ennyo nga Queequeg ng’atambula mu bantu ab’empisa ab’omu kibuga eky’empuku, okwewuunya okwo kwava mangu bwe nnasooka okutambula emisana mu nguudo z’omu New Bedford.

Mu nguudo eziyita okumpi n’emyalo, omwalo gwonna omunene ogw’ennyanja gujja kutera okuwaayo okulaba ebifo ebisinga okulabika ng’ebitali bitegeerekeka okuva mu bitundu by’ebweru. Ne mu nguudo za Broadway ne Chestnut, abavubi b’oku nnyanja Mediterranean oluusi bajja kusikambula ba ladies abatidde. Regent Street si ya butamanyibwa ba Lascars n’Abamalaaya; ate e Bombay, mu Apollo Green, aba Yankees abalamu batera okutiisa enzaalwa. Wabula New Bedford ekubye Water Street ne Wapping zonna. Mu bifo bino ebisembyeyo okwogerwako olaba abalunnyanja bokka; naye mu New Bedford, abalya abantu abatuufu bayimiridde nga banyumya ku nsonda z’enguudo; ensolo enkambwe mu ngeri ey’obwerufu; bangi ku bo bakyalina amagumba gaabwe omubiri ogutali mutukuvu. Kifuula omuntu gw’atamanyi okutunula butunuzi.

Naye, ng’oggyeeko aba Feegeeans, Tongatobooarrs, Erromanggoans, Pannangians, ne Brighggians, era, ng’oggyeeko ebika by’omu nsiko eby’eby’emikono eby’okuvuba ennyanja whale ebyali tebifaayo reel ku nguudo, ojja kulaba ebifo ebirala eby’okulaba nga bikyali bya kwewuunya, mazima ddala ebisinga okusesa. Buli wiiki mu kibuga kino batuuka abasajja bangi aba Vermonters ne New Hampshire aba kiragala, bonna nga balina ennyonta y’okufunamu n’ekitiibwa mu by’obuvubi. Zisinga kuba nto, za fuleemu ennywevu; bannaabwe abatemye ebibira, era kati banoonya okusuula embazzi ne banyaga emmundu ya whale-lance. Bangi ba kiragala nga Green Mountains gye baava. Mu bintu ebimu wandibirowoozezzaako naye nga biwezezza essaawa ntono. Laba awo! oyo chap strutting okwetoloola enkoona. Ayambala enkoofiira ya beaver n’ekkanzu eriko omukira gw’okumira, ng’asibiddwa omusipi gw’omuvubi n’akambe akayitibwa sheath-knife. Wano we wajja omulala ng’ayambadde sou’-wester n’ekyambalo kya bombazine.

Tewali dandy azaalibwa mu kibuga ajja kugeraageranyizibwa ku yazaalibwa mu nsi —ntegeeza dandy ow’ekika kya bumpkin ddala —munne nti, mu nnaku z’embwa, ajja kusala yiika ze ebbiri mu ggalavu z’amaliba g’embuzi olw’okutya okusiiga emikono gye. Kati dandy w’eggwanga nga ono bw’akitwala mu mutwe gwe okukola erinnya ery’enjawulo, n’ayingira mu nvuba ennene ey’ennyanja whale, wandibadde olaba ebintu ebisesa by’akola ng’atuuse ku mwalo gw’ennyanja. Mu kwogera ku ngoye ze ez’ennyanja, alagira obutambi bw’engoye ku kiwato kye; emiguwa ku bbulawuzi ze eza kanvaasi. Ah, Hay-Seed omwavu! engeri enkaawa gye zinaakutuka emiguwa egyo mu muyaga ogusooka ogw’okuwowoggana, ng’ogobeddwa, emiguwa, obutambi, ne byonna, wansi mu mumiro gw’omuyaga.

Naye tolowooza nti ekibuga kino ekimanyiddwa ennyo kirimu abakuba enkoba, abalya abantu, n’ebiwuka ebiyitibwa bumpkins byokka okulaga abagenyi baakyo. Nedda. Still New Bedford kifo kya queer. Singa tetwali ffe abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale, ekitundu ekyo eky’ettaka oboolyawo leero kyandibadde mu mbeera ey’okuwowoggana ng’olubalama lw’ennyanja Labrador. Nga bwe kiri, ebitundu by’ensi ye ey’emabega bimala okutiisa omuntu, birabika nga bya magumba nnyo. Ekibuga kyennyini oboolyawo kye kifo ekisinga okwagalibwa okubeeramu, mu New England yonna. “Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
