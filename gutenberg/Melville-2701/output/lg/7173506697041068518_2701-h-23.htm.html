ESSUULA 124. Empiso.

Enkeera ku makya ennyanja eyali tennakendeera yayiringisibwa mu biwujjo ebiwanvu ebiwanvu eby’amaanyi amangi, era ng’efuba mu luguudo lwa Pequod olwali luwuuma, n’emusika okugenda mu maaso ng’engalo z’abanene ezibunye. Empewo ey’amaanyi, etawunyiriza yali nnyingi nnyo, ne kiba nti eggulu n’empewo byalabika ng’amayengo amanene ennyo ag’ebweru w’olubuto; ensi yonna yabuukabuuka nga empewo tennatandika. Nga azibiddwa mu kitangaala ekijjuvu eky’oku makya, enjuba etalabika yali emanyiddwa nnyo olw’amaanyi agasaasaana g’ekifo kye; emisinde gye egya bayonet gye gyagenda mu maaso mu ntuumu. Ebifaananyi eby’okusiiga, ng’ebya bakabaka ne bakabaka b’e Babulooni abaali batikkiddwa engule, byafuganga buli kimu. Ennyanja yali ng’ekibya kya zaabu asaanuuse, ekibuuka mu ngeri efuumuuka olw’ekitangaala n’ebbugumu.

Nga amaze ebbanga ng’asirise mu ngeri ey’amagezi, Akabu yayimirira nga yeetongodde; era buli emmeeri eyali ewuubaala lwe yakka wansi n’omukono gwayo ogw’obutaasa, yakyukanga n’atunuulira emisinde gy’enjuba egyali gitangalijja egyali givaamu mu maaso; era bwe yasenga mu buziba ku mabbali g’emmeeri, n’akyuka emabega, n’alaba ekifo enjuba we yali emabega, n’engeri emisinde gye gimu egya kyenvu gye gyali gitabula n’okuzuukuka kwe okutakyuka.

“Ha, ha, emmeeri yange! oyinza bulungi okutwalibwa kaakano olw'eggaali ly'ennyanja ery'enjuba. Ho, ho! mmwe amawanga gonna mu maaso g'omugoba gwange, mbaleetera enjuba! Ekikoligo ku biyiriro ebirala; mulamu bulungi! a tandem, nvuga ennyanja!”

Naye amangu ago ng’aziyiziddwa endowooza ezimu ezikontana, yayanguwa ng’ayolekera omugoba, ng’asaba emmeeri mu ngeri ey’amaanyi engeri emmeeri gye yali egenda.

“East-sou-east, ssebo,” omuvuzi w’emmotoka eyali atidde ennyo bwe yagamba.

“Olimba!” ng’amukuba n’ekikonde kye ekinywezeddwa. “Oyolekera Ebuvanjuba mu ssaawa eno ey’oku makya, n’enjuba emabega?”

Ku kino buli mmeeme yasobeddwa; kubanga ekintu ekyo Akabu kye yalaba mu kiseera ekyo kyali kisimattuse buli omu mu ngeri etategeerekeka; naye okukwata kwayo okuziba amaaso ennyo kuteekwa okuba nga kwe kwavaako.

Nga asika omutwe gwe wakati mu binnacle, Akabu yalaba akabonero kamu ku kkampasi; omukono gwe ogwasitulwa gwagwa mpola; okumala akaseera katono yalabika ng’agenda okuwuubaala. Nga ayimiridde emabega we Starbuck yatunula, era laba! kkampasi zombi zaali zisonga mu Buvanjuba, era Pequod yali ng’egenda mu Bugwanjuba mu ngeri etasobya.

Naye ere alamu y’ensiko esooka esobola okufuluma ebweru w’eggwanga mu bavuzi b’ennyonyi, omusajja omukadde eyalina enseko enkakali yayomba nti, “Nnina! Kibaddewo emabegako. Mwami Starbuck, okubwatuka kw’ekiro ekyakeesezza olwaleero kwakyusa kkampasi zaffe — ekyo kyokka. Kaakano tonnawuliranga ku kintu ng’ekyo, nkitwala.”

“Aye; naye tekinnatuukangako, ssebo,” munne omuddugavu bwe yagamba ng’aliko ennaku.

Wano, kiteekwa okwogerwa, nti obubenje nga buno mu mbeera ezisukka mu emu bubadde ku mmeeri mu kibuyaga ow’amaanyi. Amasoboza ga magineeti, nga bwe gakula mu mpiso y’omuvubi, nga bonna bwe bakimanyi, mu bukulu gali gamu n’amasannyalaze agatunuulirwa mu ggulu; kye kiva tekikwewuunyisa nnyo, nti ebintu ng’ebyo bibeerewo. Emirundi ng’omulabe gukubye ddala emmeeri, ne gukuba wansi ebimu ku biwujjo n’ebikondo, ebikosa empiso oluusi bibadde bikyasinga okutta; empisa zaayo zonna ez’amayinja ag’omugugu nga zisaanawo, ne kiba nti ekyuma kya magineeti ekyasooka tekyali kya mugaso nnyo okusinga empiso y’omukyala omukadde ey’okuluka. Naye mu ngeri zombi, empiso teddamu kuddamu, ku bwayo, okuddamu empisa ennungi eyasooka bwetyo eyonoonese oba eyabula; era singa kkampasi za binnacle zikosebwa, enkomerero y’emu etuuka ku ndala zonna eziyinza okuba mu mmeeri; wadde nga n’ezo ezaali eza wansi zaayingizibwa mu kelson.

Mu bugenderevu ng’ayimiridde mu maaso ga binnacle, era ng’atunuulira kkampasi ezaali zikyusiddwa, omukadde, n’omukono gwe omusongovu ogwagoloddwa, kati yakwata entambula entuufu ey’enjuba, era ng’amatidde nti empiso zaali zikyusiddwa ddala, n’aleekaana ebiragiro bye ku kkubo ly’emmeeri okukyusibwa okusinziira ku ekyo. Yaadi zaali nkalu waggulu; era omulundi omulala Pequod n’esuula obutaasa bwe obutatya mu mpewo eyali evuganya, kubanga eyali agambibwa okuba omulungi yali abadde amujooga.

Mu kiseera kino, ka kibeere nti ebirowoozo bye eby’ekyama byali biruwa, Starbuck teyayogera kintu kyonna, naye mu kasirise yafulumya ebiragiro byonna ebyetaagisa; ate nga Stubb ne Flask —nga mu kiseera ekyo mu kigero ekitono baali balabika ng’abagabana enneewulira ze —mu ngeri y’emu ne bakkiriza awatali kwemulugunya. Ate ku basajja, wadde ng’abamu ku bo baali bawuuma mu ngeri ya wansi, okutya kwabwe Akabu kwali kusinga okutya kwabwe eri Enkomerero. Naye nga bwe kyali kibadde, abakaafiiri aba Harpooneers baasigala kumpi nga tebakwatibwako ddala; oba bwe kiba nga kyakwatibwako, kyali kya magineeti emu yokka eyakubwa mu mitima gyabwe egy’omukwano okuva ku gya Akabu egitakyukakyuka.

Okumala ekifo omukadde yatambula ku ddeeke mu birooto ebiyiringisibwa. Naye ng’afuna omukisa okuseerera n’ekisinziiro kye eky’amasanga, yalaba ebipimo by’ekikomo ebibetenteddwa eby’ekitundu eky’okuna kye yalina olunaku olwasooka nga bidduka ku ddeeke.

“Ggwe, Ggulu-Gazer ow’amalala n’omugoba w’ennyonyi ya Sun! Eggulo nakumenyese, era leero kkampasi zandibadde fain have wrecked me. Kale, bwe kityo. Naye Akabu ye mukama ku jjinja ly’omugugu erya level yet. Mwami Starbuck —effumu eritaliiko kikondo; ekyuma ekiyitibwa top-maul, era nga kye kisinga obutono mu mpiso z’omukozi w’amaato. Mangu!"

Accessory, mpozzi, eri impulse dictating ekintu kye yali anaatera okukola kati, were certain prudential motives, nga ekigendererwa kyabwe kyandibadde kuzuukiza myoyo gy’abakozi be nga stroke y’obukugu bwe subtile, mu matter so wonderous as that of kkampasi ezikyusiddwa. N’ekirala, omusajja omukulu yali akimanyi bulungi nti okulung’amya empiso ezikyusiddwa, wadde nga mu ngeri ey’ekikugu, tekyali kintu kya kuyisibwa balunnyanja ba nzikiriza enkyamu, awatali kukankana n’obubonero obubi.

“Abasajja,” bwe yagamba, ng’akyuka buli lukya ku bavuzi, nga munne amukwasa ebintu bye yali asabye, “abasajja bange, okubwatuka kwakyusa empiso za Akabu omukadde; naye mu kyuma kino Akabu asobola okukola ekimu ekikye, ekijja okusonga ekituufu ng’ekyo kyonna.”

Amaaso ag’okuswala ag’okwewuunya okw’obuddu gaawanyisiganyizibwa abalunnyanja, nga kino bwe kyayogerwa; Era n’amaaso agasikiriza baali balindirira obulogo bwonna obuyinza okuddirira. Naye Starbuck yatunula ebbali.

Olw’okukubwa okuva eri omusajja ow’oku ntikko Akabu yakoona omutwe gw’ekyuma ogw’effumu, n’oluvannyuma n’akwasa munne omuggo omuwanvu ogw’ekyuma ogwasigalawo, n’amulagira okugukwata nga gwegolodde, nga tegukwata ku ddeeke. Olwo, n’omuggo, oluvannyuma lw’okukuba enfunda n’enfunda enkomerero ey’okungulu ey’omuggo guno ogw’ekyuma, yateeka empiso eyali efuukuuse enkomerero waggulu waagwo, era n’akuba ennyondo mu ngeri etali ya maanyi nnyo nti, emirundi egiwerako, munne ng’akyakutte omuggo nga bwe gwali edda. Awo ng’ayita mu ntambula ezimu entonotono ezeewuunyisa —ka kibeere nga tekyetaagisa ku magineeti y’ekyuma, oba nga kigendereddwamu kyokka okwongera okutya kw’abakozi, tekikakasibwa —yayita obuwuzi bwa bafuta; era ng’agenda ku binnacle, n’aseerera okuvaamu empiso ebbiri ezaali zikyusiddwa awo, n’awanirira mu ngeri ey’okwesimbye empiso y’amaato wakati waayo, waggulu w’emu ku kaadi za kkampasi. Mu kusooka, ekyuma kyagenda kyetooloola, nga kikankana era nga kikankana ku buli nkomerero; Naye ku nkomerero kyatuuka mu kifo kyakyo, Akabu, eyali abadde atunuulidde n’obwegendereza ekivaamu kino, n’adda mu bwesimbu okuva mu binnacle, n’asonga omukono gwe ogwagololwa nga gugenda ku gwo, bwe yayomba,—“Mutunuulire, kubanga Abaza babeere nga si babeere nga . mukama w'ejjinja ly'omugugu erya level! Enjuba eri mu Buvanjuba, era kkampasi eyo elayira!”

Omu oluvannyuma lw’omulala gwe batunulamu, kubanga tewali kirala okuggyako amaaso gaabwe nga gasobola okusikiriza obutamanya ng’obwo, ate obumu oluvannyuma lw’obulala ne buwuubaala.

Mu maaso ge ag’omuliro ag’okunyooma n’obuwanguzi, olwo n’olaba Akabu mu maanyi ge gonna agatta.





ESSUULA 125. Ekiwandiiko ne Layini.

Nga kati Pequod eyali efunye enkomerero yali emaze ebbanga ddene ng’egenda ku lugendo luno, ekikondo n’olunyiriri byali tebitera kukozesebwa. Olw’okwesigama n’obwesige ku ngeri endala ez’okuzuula ekifo ky’eryato, abasuubuzi abamu, n’abavubi bangi naddala nga batambulira ku nnyanja, balagajjalira ddala okusitula ekikondo ekyo; wadde nga mu kiseera kye kimu, era emirundi mingi nnyo olw’obulungi bwa ffoomu okusinga ekintu ekirala kyonna, bulijjo nga bateeka wansi ku slate eya bulijjo ekkubo erivugirwa emmeeri, awamu n’omutindo oguteeberezebwa okuba ogwa wakati ogw’okukulaakulana buli ssaawa. Bwe kityo bwe kyali kibadde ne Pequod. Ekikondo ky’embaawo n’omuti ogw’enkoona ogwagattibwako byali biwanikiddwa, okumala ebbanga nga tebikwatibwako, wansi ddala w’olukomera lw’ebigo eby’oluvannyuma. Enkuba n’okufuuyira byali bigifudde; enjuba n’empewo byali bigikyusizza; Elements zonna zaali zigatta okuvunda ekintu ekyali kiwanikiddwa nga idly. Naye nga tafaayo ku bino byonna, mood ye yakwata Akabu, nga bwe yatuuka n’okutunula ku reel, si ssaawa nnyingi oluvannyuma lw’ekifo kya magineeti, era n’ajjukira engeri ekitundu kye gye kyali tekikyaliyo, era n’ajjukira ekirayiro kye eky’obusungu ku tlog ya level ne layini. Emmeeri yali egenda ng’egwa wansi; emabega amayengo gayiringisibwa mu buvuyo.

“Mu maaso, awo! Situla ekikondo!”

Abavubi babiri bajja. Omutahiti eya langi ya zaabu n’omu Manxman ow’ekika kya grizzly. “Twala reel, omu ku mmwe, nja kusitula.”

Bagenda ku ludda olw’emabega ennyo, ku ludda lw’emmeeri olwa lee, nga ddeeke, n’amaanyi ag’empewo ag’ekyekulungirivu, kati katono enyweredde mu nnyanja eyali efuuse ebizigo, ng’efubutuka ku mabbali.

Omumanxman yakwata reel, n’agikwata waggulu, ku mikono egyafuluma egy’enkomerero z’omusipi, okwetooloola ekipooli kya layini kye yeetooloola, bwe kityo ne kiyimirira ng’omuti ogw’enkoona guwanikiddwa wansi, okutuusa Akabu lwe yagenda mu maaso gy’ali.

Akabu yayimirira mu maaso ge, era yali asumulula mu ngeri ennyangu enkyukakyuka nga amakumi asatu oba amakumi ana okukola kkooyilo y’omukono esooka okusuula waggulu w’ennyanja, Omumankisi omukadde, eyali atunuulidde nnyo bombi bombi ne layini, n’akola obuvumu okwogera.

“Ssebo, sikyesiga; olunyiriri luno lulabika nga lugenze wala, ebbugumu eddene n’obunnyogovu bigwonoonye.”

“‘Twill hold, omwami omukadde. Ebbugumu eddene n'obunnyogovu, bakuyonoonye? Olabika okwata. Oba, ekituufu mpozzi, obulamu bukukwata; si ggwe.”

“Nze nkwata ekyuma ekiyitibwa spool, ssebo. Naye nga kapiteeni wange bw’agamba. Nga nnina enviiri zino enzirugavu ezange ‘tis not worth while disputing, ‘naddala n’omuntu asinga, who’er ne’er confess.”

“Ekyo kye ki? Kati waliwo pulofeesa alina ebitundutundu mu ttendekero lya Queen Nature eryali litandise okukola ku granite; naye methinks nti asukkiridde okugondera. Wazaalibwa wa?”

“Mu kizinga ekitono eky’amayinja ekiyitibwa Isle of Man, ssebo.”

"Suffu! Ekyo okikubye ensi.”

“Simanyi ssebo, naye nazaalibwa eyo.”

“Mu kizinga kya Man, hey? Wamma, mu ngeri endala, kirungi. Wuuno omusajja okuva mu Man; omusajja eyazaalibwa mu Muntu edda eyeetongodde, era kati nga talina muntu mu Muntu; ekisonseka mu —ki ki? Waggulu ne reel! Bbugwe omufu, omuzibe w’amaaso akuba emitwe gyonna egy’okubuuza ku nkomerero. Up ne kyo! Ekituufu."

Ekikondo kyali kizitowa. Koyilo ezaali zitambula bulungi zagolola mangu mu layini empanvu ey’okusika emabega, era awo, mu kaseera ako, ekiwujjo ne kitandika okuwuguka. Mu ngeri y’emu, olw’okusitulwa n’okukka mu ngeri ey’okusika omuguwa olw’amayengo agayiringisibwa, okuziyiza okusika kw’omuti kwaleetera omukozi w’omuti omukadde okuwuubaala mu ngeri eyeewuunyisa.

“Mukwate nnyo!”

Ekifananyi! layini eyali esukkiridde okunyigirizibwa yagwa wansi mu festoon emu empanvu; ekikondo ky’okusika kyali kiweddewo.

“Nbetenta ekitundu eky’okuna, okubwatuka kukyusa empiso, era kati ennyanja egwa eddalu ekutula layini y’emiti. Naye Akabu asobola okutereeza byonna. Haul in wano, Omutahiti; reel up, Manxman, omusajja ayitibwa Manxman. Era laba, omubazzi akole omuti omulala, olongoose omuguwa. Mulabe.”

“Eyo gy’agenda kati; gy’ali tewali kibaddewo; naye gyendi, siketi eringa esumululwa okuva wakati mu nsi. Haul in, haul in, Omutahiti! Ennyiriri zino zidduka nga zonna, era nga ziyiringisibwa ebweru: ziyingira nga zimenyese, era nga zisika mpola. Ha, Pip, nga bwe kiri? mujje okuyamba; eh, Pip?”

“Pip? ani gwe muyita Pip? Pip yabuuka okuva ku lyato erya whale-boat. Pip's abuze. Ka tulabe kati oba temumuvubye waggulu wano, omuvubi. Kisika nnyo; Ntebereza nti akwata ku. Musikaasikanya, Tahiti! Mujere ku mugongo; tetuyingiza mukodo gwonna wano. Ho! waliwo omukono gwe nga gumenya mazzi gokka. Ekyuma ekiyitibwa hatchet! ekyuma ekiyitibwa hatchet! kiteme —tetuyingiza mukodo gwonna wano. Kapiteeni Akabu! ssebo, ssebo! wuuno Pip, ng’agezaako okuddamu okulinnya ku lyato.”

“Emirembe, ogwa eddalu,” omu Manxman bwe yakaaba ng’amuwamba ku mukono. “Ewala okuva ku ddeeke ya kwata!”

Akabu bwe yayomba ng’agenda mu maaso nti: “Omusiru omukulu aboggolera omutono.” “Emikono okuva ku butukuvu obwo! Ogamba nti Pip yali wa, mulenzi?

“Emabega awo, ssebo, emabega! Laba! laba!”

“Era ggwe ani, mulenzi? Siraba kweyoleka kwange mu bitundu by’amaaso go ebitalimu kintu kyonna. Ayi Katonda! Omuntu oyo yandibadde kintu kya myoyo egitafa okusengeka! Ggwe ani, mulenzi?”

“Bell-Boy, ssebo; emmeeri-omumenyi w’amateeka; ding, dong, ding, omusajja omulala! Pip! Pip! Pip! Pawundi kikumi ez’empeera y’ebbumba eri Pip; Obugulumivu bwa ffuuti ttaano —erabika ng’omukodo —esinga okumanyibwa olw’ekyo! Ding, dong, ding, nga bwe kiri! Ani alabye Pip omukodo?”

“Tewali mitima waggulu wa layini y’omuzira. Oh, mmwe eggulu erya bbugumu! tunuulira wansi wano. Ye yakola okuzaala omwana ono ow'omukisa, era mumusuddewo, mmwe abayiiya libertines. Wano, omulenzi; Ennyumba ya Akabu ejja kuba maka ga Pipu okuva kati, so nga Akabu abeera. Okwata ku makkati gange ag’omunda, mulenzi; Osibiddwa ku miguwa egyalukibwa mu miguwa gyange egy’omutima. Jjangu tugende wansi.”

“Kino kye ki? wuuno olususu lwa velvet shark-skin,” ng’atunuulira nnyo omukono gwa Akabu, era ng’aguwulira. “Ah, kati, yalina Pip omwavu naye yawulira ekintu eky’ekisa ennyo nga kino, mpozzi nga teyabula! Kino kirabika gyendi ssebo, ng’omuguwa gw’omuntu; ekintu emyoyo enafu kye giyinza okukwata. Oh, ssebo, leka kati Perth ajje akwate emikono gino ebiri nga gikutte wamu; omuddugavu n’omuzungu, kubanga kino sijja kukireka kugenda.”

“Oo, mulenzi, nange sijja ggwe, okuggyako nga bwe ntyo nkusikambula okutuuka mu bitiisa ebibi okusinga ebiri wano. N’olwekyo, jangu mu kabina yange. Laba! mmwe abakkiriza mu bakatonda ebirungi byonna, ne mu muntu abalwadde bonna, laba mmwe! laba bakatonda abamanyi byonna nga tebamanyi muntu abonaabona; n’omuntu, wadde nga musiru, era nga tamanyi ky’akola, naye ng’ajjudde ebintu ebiwooma eby’okwagala n’okwebaza. Jangu! Mpulira nga nneenyumiriza mu kukulembera n’omukono gwo omuddugavu, okusinga newankutte ogwa Empula!”

“Waliwo bagenda babiri abasiru kati,” Manxman omukadde bwe yayomba. “Omu daft olw’amaanyi, omulala daft olw’obunafu. Naye wuuno enkomerero ya layini evunze — byonna nga bitonnya, nabyo. Kitereeze, eh? Ndowooza twandibadde kirungi okuba ne layini empya yonna. Nja kulaba Mwami Stubb ku nsonga eyo.”





ESSUULA 126. Ekintu ekiyitibwa Life-Buoy.

Okuvuga kati okudda mu bukiikaddyo-buvanjuba n’ekyuma kya Akabu ekitereevu, era enkulaakulana ye esalibwawo ekyuma kya Akabu ekitereevu n’olunyiriri lwokka; Pequod yakwata ku kkubo lye eryolekera Equator. Okukola okuyita okumala ebbanga eddene bwe lityo okuyita mu mazzi agatali ga bulijjo bwe gatyo, nga tegasukkuluma mmeeri, era nga gaali gawanvu, nga gasikirizibwa empewo z’obusuubuzi ezitakyukakyuka, ku mayengo nga galimu akawoowo kamu; bino byonna byalabika ng’ebintu ebyewuunyisa ebikkakkamu nga bikulembera ekifo ekimu eky’akavuyo n’okuggwaamu essuubi.

Mu nkomerero, emmeeri bwe yasemberera ebweru, nga bwe tuyinza okugamba, eky’ekifo eky’okuvubiramu eky’e Equatorial, era mu kizikiza ekinene ekigenda nga tebunnakya, yali etambulira ku kibinja ky’ebizinga eby’amayinja; essaawa —mu kiseera ekyo nga ekulemberwa Flask —yakankana olw’okukaaba okw’ensiko ennyo era okutali kwa nsi —ng’okukaaba okwa kitundu okutegeerekeka okw’emizimu gy’Abataliiko musango bonna abattibwa Kerode —ne kiba nti omu na bonna, baatandika okuva mu kuloota kwabwe, era okumala ebbanga ly’abamu ebiseera byayimirira, oba byatuula, oba ne byesigamira bonna nga bawuliriza mu ngeri etakyukakyuka, ng’omuddu Omuruumi eyayolwa, ate ng’okukaaba okwo okw’ensiko kusigala mu kuwulira. Ekitundu ky’abakozi b’oku nnyanja ekya Christian oba Civilized kyagamba nti baali ba mermaids, era nga bakankana; naye abakubi b’ensambaggere abakaafiiri baasigala nga tebakwatibwa ntiisa. Naye Manxman enzirugavu —omuvubi asinga obukulu mu bonna —yalangirira nti amaloboozi ag’omu nsiko agasanyusa agaali gawulirwa, gaali maloboozi ga basajja abaali baakabbira mu nnyanja.

Wansi mu hammock ye, Akabu kino teyakiwulira okutuusa obudde lwe bwakya, bwe yatuuka ku ddeeke; olwo ne kimuddibwamu Flask, si nga tekiwerekerwako makulu ga kizikiza agateekeddwako. Yaseka mu ngeri ey’ekituli, era bw’atyo n’annyonnyola ekyewuunyo ekyo.

Ebizinga ebyo eby’amayinja Emmeeri bye yali eyiseewo byali kifo eky’okuwummuliramu ennyo eky’envubu, n’envubu ezimu ento ezaali zifiiriddwa ebigoma byabwe, oba ebigo ebimu ebyali bifiiriddwa abaana baabwe, biteekwa okuba nga byasituka okumpi n’emmeeri ne bikuuma wamu naye, okukaaba n’okukaaba n’ekika kyabwe eky’okukaaba okw’obuntu. “I will not go,” said the stranger, “till you say aye to me. Do to me as you would have me do to you in the like case. “Avast,” cried Ahab—“touch not a rope-yarn”; then in a voice that prolongingly moulded every word—“Captain Gardiner, I will not do it. Even now I lose time. Good-bye, good-bye. Hurriedly turning, with averted face, he descended into his cabin, leaving the strange captain transfixed at this unconditional and utter rejection of his so earnest suit. But starting from his enchantment, Gardiner silently hurried to the side; more fell than stepped into his boat, and returned to his ship.

Soon the two ships diverged their wakes; and long as the strange vessel was in view, she was seen to yaw hither and thither at every dark spot, however small, on the sea. This way and that her yards were swung round; starboard and larboard, she continued to tack; now she beat against a head sea; and again it pushed her before it; while all the while, her masts and yards were thickly clustered with men, as three tall cherry trees, when the boys are cherrying among the boughs.

But by her still halting course and winding, woeful way, you plainly saw that this ship that so wept with spray, still remained without comfort. She was Rachel, weeping for her children, because they were not.

CHAPTER 130. The Hat.

And now that at the proper time and place, after so long and wide a preliminary cruise, Ahab,—all other whaling waters swept—seemed to have chased his foe into an ocean-fold, to slay him the more securely there; now, that he found himself hard by the very latitude and longitude where his tormenting wound had been inflicted; now that a vessel had been spoken which on the very day preceding had actually encountered Moby Dick;—and now that all his successive meetings with various ships contrastingly concurred to show the demoniac indifference with which the white whale tore his hunters, whether sinning or sinned against; now it was that there lurked a something in the old man's eyes, which it was hardly sufferable for feeble souls to see. As the unsetting polar star, which through the livelong, arctic, six months' night sustains its piercing, steady, central gaze; so Ahab's purpose now fixedly gleamed down upon the constant midnight of the gloomy crew. It domineered above them so, that all their bodings, doubts, misgivings, fears, were fain to hide beneath their souls, and not sprout forth a single spear or leaf.

In this foreshadowing interval too, all humor, forced or natural, vanished. Stubb no more strove to raise a smile; Starbuck no more strove to check one. Alike, joy and sorrow, hope and fear, seemed ground to finest dust, and powdered, for the time, in the clamped mortar of Ahab's iron soul. Like machines, they dumbly moved about the deck, ever conscious that the old man's despot eye was on them.

But did you deeply scan him in his more secret confidential hours; when he thought no glance but one was on him; then you would have seen that even as Ahab's eyes so awed the crew's, the inscrutable Parsee's glance awed his; or somehow, at least, in some wild way, at times affected it. Such an added, gliding strangeness began to invest the thin Fedallah now; such ceaseless shudderings shook him; that the men looked dubious at him; half uncertain, as it seemed, whether indeed he were a mortal substance, or else a tremulous shadow cast upon the deck by some unseen being's body. And that shadow was always hovering there. For not by night, even, had Fedallah ever certainly been known to slumber, or go below. He would stand still for hours: but never sat or leaned; his wan but wondrous eyes did plainly say—We two watchmen never rest.

Nor, at any time, by night or day could the mariners now step upon the deck, unless Ahab was before them; either standing in his pivot-hole, or exactly pacing the planks between two undeviating limits,—the main-mast and the mizen; or else they saw him standing in the cabin-scuttle,—his living foot advanced upon the deck, as if to step; his hat slouched heavily over his eyes; so that however motionless he stood, however the days and nights were added on, that he had not swung in his hammock; yet hidden beneath that slouching hat, they could never tell unerringly whether, for all this, his eyes were really closed at times; or whether he was still intently scanning them; no matter, though he stood so in the scuttle for a whole hour on the stretch, and the unheeded night-damp gathered in beads of dew upon that stone-carved coat and hat. The clothes that the night had wet, the next day's sunshine dried upon him; and so, day after day, and night after night; he went no more beneath the planks; whatever he wanted from the cabin that thing he sent for.

He ate in the same open air; that is, his two only meals,—breakfast and dinner: supper he never touched; nor reaped his beard; which darkly grew all gnarled, as unearthed roots of trees blown over, which still grow idly on at naked base, though perished in the upper verdure. But though his whole life was now become one watch on deck; and though the Parsee's mystic watch was without intermission as his own; yet these two never seemed to speak—one man to the other—unless at long intervals some passing unmomentous matter made it necessary. Though such a potent spell seemed secretly to join the twain; openly, and to the awe-struck crew, they seemed pole-like asunder. If by day they chanced to speak one word; by night, dumb men were both, so far as concerned the slightest verbal interchange. At times, for longest hours, without a single hail, they stood far parted in the starlight; Ahab in his scuttle, the Parsee by the mainmast; but still fixedly gazing upon each other; as if in the Parsee Ahab saw his forethrown shadow, in Ahab the Parsee his abandoned substance.

And yet, somehow, did Ahab—in his own proper self, as daily, hourly, and every instant, commandingly revealed to his subordinates,—Ahab seemed an independent lord; the Parsee but his slave. Still again both seemed yoked together, and an unseen tyrant driving them; the lean shade siding the solid rib. For be this Parsee what he may, all rib and keel was solid Ahab.

At the first faintest glimmering of the dawn, his iron voice was heard from aft,—“Man the mast-heads!”—and all through the day, till after sunset and after twilight, the same voice every hour, at the striking of the helmsman's bell, was heard—“What d'ye see?—sharp! sharp!”

But when three or four days had slided by, after meeting the children-seeking Rachel; and no spout had yet been seen; the monomaniac old man seemed distrustful of his crew's fidelity; at least, of nearly all except the Pagan harpooneers; he seemed to doubt, even, whether Stubb and Flask might not willingly overlook the sight he sought. But if these suspicions were really his, he sagaciously refrained from verbally expressing them, however his actions might seem to hint them.

“I will have the first sight of the whale myself,”—he said. “Aye! Ahab must have the doubloon!” and with his own hands he rigged a nest of basketed bowlines; and sending a hand aloft, with a single sheaved block, to secure to the main-mast head, he received the two ends of the downward-reeved rope; and attaching one to his basket prepared a pin for the other end, in order to fasten it at the rail. This done, with that end yet in his hand and standing beside the pin, he looked round upon his crew, sweeping from one to the other; pausing his glance long upon Daggoo, Queequeg, Tashtego; but shunning Fedallah; and then settling his firm relying eye upon the chief mate, said,—“Take the rope, sir—I give it into thy hands, Starbuck.” Then arranging his person in the basket, he gave the word for them to hoist him to his perch, Starbuck being the one who secured the rope at last; and afterwards stood near it. And thus, with one hand clinging round the royal mast, Ahab gazed abroad upon the sea for miles and miles,—ahead, astern, this side, and that,—within the wide expanded circle commanded at so great a height.

When in working with his hands at some lofty almost isolated place in the rigging, which chances to afford no foothold, the sailor at sea is hoisted up to that spot, and sustained there by the rope; under these circumstances, its fastened end on deck is always given in strict charge to some one man who has the special watch of it. Because in such a wilderness of running rigging, whose various different relations aloft cannot always be infallibly discerned by what is seen of them at the deck; and when the deck-ends of these ropes are being every few minutes cast down from the fastenings, it would be but a natural fatality, if, unprovided with a constant watchman, the hoisted sailor should by some carelessness of the crew be cast adrift and fall all swooping to the sea. So Ahab's proceedings in this matter were not unusual; the only strange thing about them seemed to be, that Starbuck, almost the one only man who had ever ventured to oppose him with anything in the slightest degree approaching to decision—one of those too, whose faithfulness on the look-out he had seemed to doubt somewhat;—it was strange, that this was the very man he should select for his watchman; freely giving his whole life into such an otherwise distrusted person's hands.

Now, the first time Ahab was perched aloft; ere he had been there ten minutes; one of those red-billed savage sea-hawks which so often fly incommodiously close round the manned mast-heads of whalemen in these latitudes; one of these birds came wheeling and screaming round his head in a maze of untrackably swift circlings. Then it darted a thousand feet straight up into the air; then spiralized downwards, and went eddying again round his head.

But with his gaze fixed upon the dim and distant horizon, Ahab seemed not to mark this wild bird; nor, indeed, would any one else have marked it much, it being no uncommon circumstance; only now almost the least heedful eye seemed to see some sort of cunning meaning in almost every sight.

“Your hat, your hat, sir!” suddenly cried the Sicilian seaman, who being posted at the mizen-mast-head, stood directly behind Ahab, though somewhat lower than his level, and with a deep gulf of air dividing them.

But already the sable wing was before the old man's eyes; the long hooked bill at his head: with a scream, the black hawk darted away with his prize.

An eagle flew thrice round Tarquin's head, removing his cap to replace it, and thereupon Tanaquil, his wife, declared that Tarquin would be king of Rome. But only by the replacing of the cap was that omen accounted good. Ahab's hat was never restored; the wild hawk flew on and on with it; far in advance of the prow: and at last disappeared; while from the point of that disappearance, a minute black spot was dimly discerned, falling from that vast height into the sea.
