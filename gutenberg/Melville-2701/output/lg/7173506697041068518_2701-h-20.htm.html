ESSUULA 101. Omuwandiisi w’ebitabo.

Ere emmeeri y’Olungereza egenda okuggwaawo okuva mu maaso, kabeere ng’eteekeddwa wansi wano, nti yava e London, era n’etuumibwa erinnya ly’omugenzi Samuel Enderby, omusuubuzi w’ekibuga ekyo, eyasooka ey’ennyumba emanyiddwa ennyo ey’okuvuba envubu eya Enderby & Sons; ennyumba nga mu ndowooza y’omuvuzi wange omwavu whale, ejja si wala mabega wa nnyumba z’obwakabaka ezigatta ez’Abatudor ne Bourbon, mu nsonga ey’ebyafaayo ebya nnamaddala. Ebbanga ki, nga omwaka gwa Mukama waffe 1775 tegunnatuuka, ennyumba eno ennene ey’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale yaliwo, ebiwandiiko byange ebingi eby’ebyennyanja tebitegeeza bulungi; naye mu mwaka ogwo (1775) yateekawo emmeeri z’Abangereza ezaasooka okuyigga bulijjo Sperm Whale; newankubadde okumala emyaka egiwerako emabega (okuva mu 1726) abazira baffe aba Coffins ne Maceys ab’e Nantucket ne Vineyard baali mu bibinja ebinene nga bagoberera Leviathan eyo, naye mu North ne South Atlantic yokka: si awalala. Kibeere nga kiwandiikiddwa mu ngeri ey’enjawulo wano, nti aba Nantucketers be baasooka mu bantu okukuba enkoba n’ekyuma eky’empuku The great Sperm Whale; era nti okumala ekitundu ky’ekyasa be bantu bokka mu nsi yonna abaamukuba ensambaggere bwe batyo.

Mu 1778, emmeeri ennungi, Amelia, yateekebwateekebwa olw’ekigendererwa eky’amangu, era ku buvunaanyizibwa bwokka obw’aba Enderby ab’amaanyi, n’obuvumu yeetooloola Cape Horn, era ye yasooka mu mawanga okukka wansi eryato erya whale-eryato ery’engeri yonna mu Ennyanja ennene ey’obugwanjuba. Olugendo luno lwabadde lwa bukugu era lwa mukisa; era ng’adda mu kifo we yali asimba ng’akutte ejjudde ensigo ez’omuwendo, ekyokulabirako kya Amelia mu bbanga ttono kyagobererwa emmeeri endala, ez’Olungereza n’ez’Abamerika, era bwe kityo ebibangirizi ebinene ebya Sperm Whale eby’omu Pacific ne bisuulibwa. Naye nga tematidde na kikolwa kino ekirungi, ennyumba etakoowa yaddamu okwecamula: Samuel ne Batabani be bonna —bameka, maama waabwe yekka y’amanyi —era wansi w’obukulembeze bwabwe obw’amangu, era ekitundu, ndowooza, ku ssente zaabwe, gavumenti ya Bungereza yasendebwasendebwa okusindika emmeeri eyitibwa sloop-of-war Rattler ku lugendo lw’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale olw’okuzuula mu nnyanja ey’obugwanjuba. Nga eduumirwa Post-Captain y’amagye g’oku mazzi, Rattler yakola olugendo oluwuuma, era n’ekola obuweereza obumu; bwe bungi obutalabika. Naye kino si kye kyokka. Mu 1819, ennyumba y’emu yassaawo emmeeri yaabwe eya discovery whale, okugenda ku lugendo lw’okuwooma okugenda mu mazzi ag’ewala mu Japan. Emmeeri eyo —eyitibwa obulungi “Syren” —yakola olugendo olw’ekitiibwa olw’okugezesa; era bwe kityo ekifo ekinene eky’okuvuba envubu ekya Japan kyasooka okumanyibwa okutwalira awamu. Syren mu lugendo luno olw’ettutumu yali eduumirwa Captain Coffin, eyali Nantucketer.

Ekitiibwa kyonna eri aba Enderbies, n’olwekyo, ennyumba yaabwe, ndowooza, eriwo n’okutuusa kati; wadde nga awatali kubuusabuusa Samwiri ow’olubereberye ateekwa okuba nga yaseerera edda waya ye olw’Ennyanja ennene ey’Obugwanjuba ey’ensi endala.

Emmeeri eyatuumibwa erinnya lye yali esaanira ekitiibwa ekyo, ng’evuga mangu nnyo era nga ya kitiibwa buli kkubo. Namulinnya omulundi gumu mu ttumbi awalala ku lubalama lw’ennyanja Patagonia, ne nnywa flip ennungi wansi mu forecastle. Yali gam nnungi gye twalina, era bonna baali ba trumps —buli mwoyo ogwali ku lyato. Obulamu obumpi gye bali, n’okufa okw’essanyu. Era gam eyo ennungi gye nnalina — ebbanga ddene nnyo nga Akabu omukadde amaze okukwata ku bipande bye n’ekisinziiro kye eky’amasanga — kinnuma ku kusembeza abagenyi okw’ekitiibwa, okunywevu, okw’Abasaksoni okw’emmeeri eyo; era omusumba wange anneerabire, era sitaani anzijukire, singa nkibulako. Okukyuusa? Nze nnagambye nti tulina flip? Yee, era twagifuumuula ku sipiidi ya ggaloni kkumi buli ssaawa; era omuyaga bwe gwajja (kubanga guli squally off there by Patagonia), era emikono gyonna —abagenyi na bonna —nga bayitibwa ku reef topsails, twali top-heavy nnyo ne tulina okuwuuba buli omu waggulu mu bowlines; era mu butamanya ne tufuumuula sikaati z’obukooti bwaffe mu mazzi, ne tuwanika awo, nga tusiba ebiyiriro mu muyaga ogwali guwowoggana, ekyokulabirako eky’okulabula eri ttaala zonna ezitamidde. Kyokka, ebikondo tebyasukkiridde ku nnyanja; era mu kaseera katono twafubutuka wansi, nga tuli balongoofu nnyo, ne tulina okuddamu okuyita ku flip, wadde ng’ekifuuyira ky’omunnyo eky’obukambwe nga kibutuka wansi mu forecastle scuttle, rather too much diluted and pickled it to my taste.

Ennyama y’ente yali nnungi —nga nkalu, naye ng’omubiri gulimu. Bagamba nti yali nnyama ya nte; abalala, nti yali nnyama ya nte eya dromedary; naye simanyi, mu butuufu, engeri ekyo gye kyali. Nabo baali balina dumplings; obuwunga obutono, naye obunene, obw’enkulungo mu ngeri ya symmetrically, era nga tebusaanawo. Nalowooza nti osobola okuziwulira, n’okuziyiringisiza mu ggwe nga zimaze okumira. Bw’ofukamira wala nnyo mu maaso, oteeka mu kabi okukuba kwabwe okuva mu ggwe ng’emipira gya bbiiru. Omugaati —naye ekyo tekyasobola kuyambibwa; ng’oggyeeko ekyo, yali ddagala eriziyiza scorbutic; mu bufunze, omugaati gwali gulimu ebisale byokka ebipya bye baalina. Naye forecastle teyali kitangaala nnyo, era kyali kyangu nnyo okulinnya mu nsonda enzikivu ng’ogirya. Naye byonna awamu, okumutwala okuva ku loole okudda ku mulala, ng’alowooza ku bipimo by’ebiyumba by’omufumbi, nga mw’otwalidde n’ebyuma bye eby’amaliba ebiramu; mu maaso n’emabega, ngamba, emmeeri ya Samuel Enderby yali mmeeri ya ssanyu; eby’ebisale ebirungi n’ebingi; fine flip ate nga ya maanyi; crack fellows bonna, ne capital okuva ku boot heels okutuuka ku hat-band.

Naye lwaki kyali, mulowooze, nti Samuel Enderby, n’abavubi abalala Abazungu be mmanyi — si bonna wadde — baali mmeeri ez’ettutumu bwe zityo, ezisembeza abagenyi; ekyayita okwetooloola ennyama y’ente, n’omugaati, n’ekibbo, n’okusaaga; era tebaakoowa mangu okulya, n'okunywa, n'okuseka? Nja kukubuulira. Essanyu eringi ery’abavubi bano Abangereza, nsonga ya kunoonyereza ku byafaayo. Era sifuddeeyo n’akatono okunoonyereza ku byafaayo ku nnyanja ekika kya whale, so nga kirabika kyetaagisa.

Abangereza baakulemberwa mu nvuba y’ennyanja ekika kya whale aba Holland, Aba Zealand, n’Abadan; mwe baaggya ebigambo bingi ebikyaliyo mu by’obuvubi; n’ekisingawo, emisono gyabwe egy’amasavu egy’edda, nga gikwata ku bingi eby’okulya n’okunywa. kubanga, ng’ekintu eky’awamu, emmeeri y’Abangereza ey’abasuubuzi ekuba abakozi be; naye si bwe kiri omuvubi w’ennyanja Omuzungu. N’olwekyo, mu Lungereza, ekintu kino eky’okuvuba envubu si kya bulijjo era kya butonde, wabula kya butanwa era kya njawulo; era, n’olwekyo, erina okuba n’ensibuko ey’enjawulo, wano ekiragiddwa wano, era ejja kwongera okunnyonnyolwa.

Mu kunoonyereza kwange mu byafaayo by’Abaleviathanic, nnasanga omuzingo ogw’edda ogw’Oludaaki, nga, olw’akawoowo kaago akaali kavuba, nnamanya nti guteekwa okuba nga gukwata ku bavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale. Omutwe gwali, “Dan Coopman,” n’olwekyo nnamaliriza nti kino kiteekwa okuba nga kye bijjukizo eby’omuwendo ennyo eby’emmeeri ezimu eza Amsterdam cooper mu by’obuvubi, nga buli mmeeri ya whale erina okutwala cooper yaayo. Nnanywezebwa mu ndowooza eno bwe nnalaba nti yali okufulumya “Fitz Swackhammer” emu. Naye mukwano gwange Dr. Snodhead, omusajja omuyivu ennyo, pulofeesa w’Oludaaki eya wansi n’Olugirimaani olwa waggulu mu ttendekero lya Santa Claus ne St. Pott’s, gwe nnakwasa omulimu gw’okuvvuunula, nga mmuwa ekibokisi ky’emimuli egy’ensigo olw’obuzibu bwe — ono yennyini Dr. Snodhead, amangu ddala bwe yamala okuketta ekitabo ekyo, yankakasa nti “Dan Coopman” yali tategeeza “The Cooper,” wabula “Omusuubuzi.” Mu bufunze, ekitabo kino eky’edda era eky’okuyiga ekya Low Dutch kyakwata ku by’obusuubuzi bya Holland; era, mu nsonga endala, yalimu emboozi ennyuvu ennyo ku nvuba yaayo ey’ennyanja ekika kya whale. Era mu ssuula eno, omutwe, “Smeer,” oba “Fat,” mwe nnasanga olukalala oluwanvu olukwata ku bintu eby’enjawulo eby’engoye ez’okutereka n’ebiyumba by’abavubi 180 abavuba ennyanja ekika kya Sail of Dutch whalemen; okuva ku lukalala olwo, nga bwe lwavvuunulwa Dr. Snodhead, mpandiika bino wammanga:

Firkins 2,800 eza butto. 20,000 lbs. Cheese ya Texel & Leyden. 144,000 lbs. kkeeki (oyinza okuba nga kiwandiiko kya wansi). Ankers 550 eza Geneva. Ebipipa bya bbiya 10,800.

Emmeeza z’ebibalo ezisinga zikalu mu kusoma; naye si bwe kiri mu mbeera eriwo kati, omusomi gy’abooga payipu enzijuvu, ebipipa, lita, ne gills za gin ennungi n’essanyu eddungi.

Mu kiseera ekyo, nnamala ennaku ssatu nga nfuba okugaaya bbiya ono yenna, ennyama y’ente, n’omugaati mu ngeri ey’okunoonyereza, mu kiseera ekyo ebirowoozo bingi eby’amaanyi ne binteesebwako mu butanwa, nga bisobola okukozesebwa mu ngeri esukkulumye ku ya bulijjo era eya Plato; era, n’ekirala, nakuŋŋaanya emmeeza ez’okugatta ezange, nga nkwata ku bungi obuyinza okuba obw’ebyennyanja ebiyitibwa stock-fish, n’ebirala, ebyaliibwa buli muvubi wa low Dutch harpooneer mu nvuba eyo ey’edda eya Greenland ne Spitzbergen whale. Mu kusooka, obungi bwa butto, ne kkeeki ya Texel ne Leyden ezikozesebwa, kirabika kyewuunyisa. Kyokka, nkiteeka ku butonde bwabwe obw’obutonde obutali bwa kimpowooze, nga bweyongera okufuulibwa obutaliimu olw’obutonde bw’omulimu gwabwe, n’okusingira ddala olw’okugoberera omuzannyo gwabwe mu Nnyanja ezo eza Polar ezitonnya, ku lubalama lw’ennyanja zennyini ez’ensi eyo ey’Abaesquimaux enzaalwa z’omukwano gye beeyama buli omu mu bumpers za woyiro w’eggaali y’omukka.

Omuwendo gwa bbiya nagwo munene nnyo, ebipipa 10,800. ESSUULA 102. Omu Bower mu Arsacides.

N’okutuusa kati, mu kujjanjaba mu ngeri ey’ennyonnyola ku Sperm Whale, okusinga ntunuulidde ebyewuunyo by’oludda lwe olw’ebweru; oba okwawukana era mu bujjuvu ku bimu ku bintu ebitono eby’enzimba y’omunda. Naye mu kutegeera okunene era okujjuvu okw’okusenya, kintuukako kati okusumulula ewala n’okumusumulula, n’okusumulula ensonga za hoosi ye, okusumulula ebisiba bye, n’okusuula ebikoola n’amaaso g’ennyondo z’amagumba ge ag’omunda, ye mu maaso go mu ultimatum ye; kwe kugamba, mu magumba ge agataliiko bukwakkulizo.

Naye kati otya Isimayiri? Kiba kitya, ggwe, omuvubi omukka mu by’obuvubi, weefuula nti olina ky’omanyi ku bitundu by’ennyanja ekika kya whale ebiri wansi w’ettaka? yakola Erudite Stubb, eyateekebwa ku capstan yo, yatuusa emisomo ku nsengeka y’omubiri gwa Cetacea; Era ng’oyambibwako Windlass, kwata olubavu olw’ekyokulabirako olw’omwoleso? Nnyonnyola ggwe kennyini, Ishmael. Osobola okukka ku ddeeke yo ku ddeeke ng’ogikuze, ng’omufumbi anywa embizzi eyokeddwa? Mazima ddala si bwe kiri. omujulizi omutuufu gwe mubadde okutuusa kati, Ishmael; Naye mubeere n’obwegendereza engeri gy’okwatamu enkizo ya Yona yekka; enkizo ey’okwogera ku bikondo n’ebikondo; ebikondo, ebikondo by’omugongo, ebisula, n’ebipima wansi, nga bikola fuleemu ya leviathan; era ng’alinga ebibya eby’omuti, ebisenge by’amata, butto, ne kkeeki mu byenda bye.

Njatula, nti okuva ku Yona, abavubi ba whale batono abayingidde wala nnyo wansi w’olususu lw’ennyanja ekika kya whale enkuze; wadde kiri kityo, nfunye omukisa okumusalasala mu butonotono. Mu mmeeri gye nnali, lumu akaana akatono akayitibwa Sperm Whale kaasitulwa mu mubiri okutuuka ku ddeeke okusobola okumukuba oba ensawo ye, okukola ebikoola by’emiggo gy’engoye, n’emitwe gy’amafumu. Olowooza omukisa ogwo naguleka, nga sikozesezza boat-hatchet yange ne jack-knife yange, ne nmenya seal ne nsoma byonna ebiri mu mwana oyo omuto?

Era ku ky’okumanya kwange okutuufu ku magumba ga leviathan mu nkulaakulana yaago ennene ennyo, ekuze mu bujjuvu, olw’okumanya okwo okutatera kubaawo mbanja mukwano gwange ow’obwakabaka omugenzi Tranquo, kabaka wa Tranque, omu ku ba Arsacides. Olw’okuba nnali e Tranque, emyaka egiyise, bwe nnali nkwatagana n’emmeeri y’ebyobusuubuzi Dey ey’e Algiers, nnayitibwa okumala ekitundu ky’ennaku enkulu ez’e Arsacidean ne mukama wa Tranque, mu nnyumba ye ey’enkindu eyawummula e Pupella; ekiwonvu ekiri ku mabbali g’ennyanja ekitali wala nnyo okuva ku ekyo abalunnyanja baffe kye baayita Bamboo-Town, ekibuga kye ekikulu.

Mu ngeri endala nnyingi ennungi, mukwano gwange ow’obwakabaka Tranquo, olw’okuba yali aweereddwa ekirabo ky’okwagala okw’amaanyi eri ensonga zonna ez’obukambwe, yali agatta mu Pupella ebintu byonna ebitali bimu abantu be abaali abagezi ennyo bye baali basobola okuyiiya; okusinga embaawo eziyooleddwa ez’ebyuma ebyewuunyisa, ebisusunku ebitemeddwa, amafumu agateekeddwamu, ebiwujjo eby’ebbeeyi, amaato ag’akawoowo; era bino byonna ne bigabibwa mu byewuunyo byonna eby’obutonde, amayengo agaali gatikkiddwa mu byewuunyo, agasasula omusolo ge gaali gasudde ku lubalama lwe.

Omukulu mu bino eby’oluvannyuma mwalimu envubu ennene ey’ensigo, oluvannyuma lw’omuyaga ogw’amaanyi ogutatera kubaawo mu ngeri etaali ya bulijjo, gwali gusangiddwa nga gufudde era nga gusibye, ng’omutwe gwe gukutte ku muti gwa cocoa-nut, ng’ebiwujjo bye ebiringa eby’amabaati, ebitungiddwa byalabika ng’ennyonyi ye eyali efuuse ebimera. Omulambo omunene bwe gwamala okuggyibwako ebizibiti byagwo ebiwanvu okutuuka ku ssa, era amagumba ne gakala enfuufu mu musana, olwo amagumba ne gatambuzibwa n’obwegendereza okulinnya mu kiwonvu kya Pupella, kati yeekaalu ennene ey’enkindu ez’obwakabaka gye yali egakuumibwa.

Embavu zaali ziwanikiddwa ku bikopo; vertebræ zaali ziyooleddwa n’ebitabo by’ebyafaayo eby’ekika kya Arsacidean, mu biwandiiko eby’enjawulo eby’ennukuta; mu kiwanga, bakabona baakuuma ennimi z’omuliro ez’akawoowo ezitazikiddwa, omutwe ogw’ekyama ne guddamu okusindika omudumu gwagwo ogw’omukka; ate nga, nga kiwanikiddwa ku ttabi, akawanga aka wansi ak’entiisa kakankana ku bagoberezi bonna, ng’ekitala ekyali kiwaniridde enviiri ekyatiisa ennyo Damocles.

Kyali kifaananyi kya kitalo. ESSUULA 103. Okupima Amagumba ga The Whale.

Mu kifo ekisooka, njagala okuteeka mu maaso gammwe ekigambo ekitongole, ekitegeerekeka obulungi, ekikwata ku kitundu ekinene ekiramu ekya leviathan eno, amagumba gaayo ge tugenda okwolesa mu bufunze. Ekigambo ng’ekyo kiyinza okuba eky’omugaso wano.

Okusinziira ku kubalirira okw’obwegendereza kwe nkoze, era nga ekitundu kye nsinziira ku kuteebereza kwa Kapiteeni Scoresby, okwa ttani nsanvu ku nnyanja Greenland whale esinga obunene ey’obuwanvu bwa ffuuti nkaaga; okusinziira ku kubalirira kwange okw’obwegendereza, ngamba nti, Envubu ya Sperm ey’obunene obusinga obunene, wakati wa ffuuti kinaana mu ttaano n’ekyenda mu buwanvu, n’ekintu ekitakka wansi wa ffuuti amakumi ana mu kwetooloola kwayo okujjuvu, ennyanja ekika kya whale ng’eyo ejja kuzitowa waakiri ttani mwenda; bwe kityo, bw’abala abasajja kkumi na basatu ku ttani, yandibadde asinga nnyo omuwendo gw’abantu bonna awamu ogw’ekyalo kyonna eky’abantu lukumi mu kikumi.

Olwo tolowooza nti obwongo, ng’ente eziriko ekikoligo, bulina okuteekebwa ku leviathan ono, okumuleetera okuwuguka n’akatono ku ndowooza y’omuntu yenna ow’ettaka?

Nga mmaze mu ngeri ez’enjawulo okuteeka mu maaso go ekiwanga kye, ekituli ky’omusulo, akawanga, amannyo, omukira, ekyenyi, ebiwaawaatiro, n’abavubi ebitundu ebirala, kati nja kumala kulaga ekisinga okunyumira mu kitundu ekinene eky’amagumba ge agatali gaziyiziddwa. Naye ng’ekiwanga ekinene ennyo bwe kikwata ekitundu ekinene ennyo eky’obunene bwonna obw’amagumba; nga bwe kiri ekitundu ekisinga okukaluba; era nga bwe watali kintu kyonna kiddibwamu ku kyo mu ssuula eno, tolina kulemererwa kukitwala mu birowoozo byo, oba wansi w’omukono gwo, nga bwe tugenda mu maaso, bwe kitaba ekyo tojja kufuna ndowooza enzijuvu ku nsengeka ey’awamu gye tunaatera okulaba .

Mu buwanvu, amagumba g’ensigo agayitibwa whale ku Tranque gaapima ffuuti nsanvu mu bbiri; bwe kityo bwe yateekebwamu ssente mu bujjuvu era n’agaziwa mu bulamu, ateekwa okuba nga yalina obuwanvu bwa ffuuti kyenda; kubanga mu nnyanja ekika kya whale, amagumba gafiirwa nga kimu kya kutaano mu buwanvu bw’ogeraageranya n’omubiri omulamu. Ku ffuuti zino nsanvu mu bbiri, ekiwanga kye n’akawanga byalimu ffuuti nga amakumi abiri, ne bireka ffuuti nga ataano ez’amagumba ag’emabega agataliimu. Ku ggumba lino ery’omugongo, okumala ekintu ekitakka wansi wa kimu kya kusatu eky’obuwanvu bwagwo, kwaliko ekibbo eky’amaanyi ekyekulungirivu eky’embavu edda ekyali kizingiramu ebitundu bye ebikulu.

Eri nze ekifuba kino ekinene eky’amasanga, nga kiriko omugongo omuwanvu, ogutaawummudde, nga gugoloddwa wala okuva ku gwo mu layini engolokofu, nga tegufaanana katono ku kisenge ekinene eky’emmeeri empya ku sitooki Ensaano y’obusaale ziteekebwamu, era ekisumuluzo kibeera mu ngeri endala, okumala ekiseera, naye embaawo empanvu, ezikutuddwa.

Embavu zaali kkumi ku ludda olumu. Esooka, okutandika okuva mu bulago, yali kumpi ffuuti mukaaga obuwanvu; ekyokubiri, eky’okusatu, n’eky’okuna buli kimu kyali kiwanvuwa mu lunyiriri, okutuusa lwe watuuka ku ntikko y’ekyokutaano, oba emu ku mbavu ez’omu makkati, ezaali zipima ffuuti munaana ne yinsi ezimu. Okuva ku kitundu ekyo, embiriizi ezaali zisigaddewo zakendeera, okutuusa ez’ekkumi n’ezisembayo lwe zaawanvuwa ffuuti ttaano zokka ne yinsi ezimu. Mu bugumu okutwalira awamu, zonna zaali zirina ekintu ekirabika ng’ekikwatagana n’obuwanvu bwazo. Embiriizi eza wakati ze zaali zisinga okubeera nga zirimu ebikonde. Mu bimu ku Arsacides zikozesebwa okukola ebikondo kwe bateeka ebibanda by’amakubo g’ebigere ku migga emitonotono.

Mu kulowooza ku mbavu zino, saasobola buta kukubwa buto n’embeera, eddiddwamu mu ngeri ez’enjawulo ennyo mu kitabo kino, ne kiba nti amagumba g’ennyanja ekika kya whale si kibumbe kya ffoomu ye gye yateekamu ssente. Embavu ezisinga obunene ku za Tranque, emu ku za wakati, zaali zikwata ekitundu ekyo eky’ebyennyanja nga mu bulamu, ekisinga obunene mu buziba. Kati, obuziba obusinga obunene obw’omubiri ogwateekebwamu ssente ogw’ennyanja eno entongole buteekwa okuba nga bwali waakiri ffuuti kkumi na mukaaga; so nga, olubavu olukwatagana lwapima naye nga lusukka mu ffuuti munaana. bwe kityo embiriizi eno yatuusa kitundu kyokka ku ndowooza entuufu ey’obunene obulamu obw’ekitundu ekyo. N’ekirala, mu ngeri emu oba endala, kati gye nnalaba naye omugongo ogw’obwereere, byonna ebyali bizingiddwa edda okwetooloola ttani z’obunene obwongezeddwa mu nnyama, ebinywa, omusaayi, n’ekyenda. Ekirala, olw’ebiwaawaatiro ebingi, wano nnalaba ennyondo ntono ezitategekeddwa bulungi; era mu kifo ky’ebiwuka ebizitowa era eby’ekitiibwa, naye nga tebirina magumba, ekintu ekitaliimu kintu kyonna!

N’olwekyo, nga bwe nnalowooza nti kya bwereere era kya busirusiru, omuntu ow’ensonyi atatambula okugezaako okutegeera obulungi ennyanja eno ey’ekitalo, ng’agenda akuba enduulu yokka ku magumba gaayo agafudde agakendedde, agagoloddwa mu muti guno ogw’emirembe. Nedda.Mu mutima gwokka ogw’akabi akasinga amangu; nga munda mu biwujjo by’ebiwujjo bye eby’obusungu byokka; ku nnyanja enzito etaliiko nsalo yokka, ennyanja ekika kya whale essiddwamu ssente mu bujjuvu y’esobola okuzuulibwa mu butuufu era mu bulamu.

Naye omugongo. Ku lw’ekyo, engeri esinga obulungi gye tuyinza okukirowoozaamu, nga tulina crane, okutuuma amagumba gaayo waggulu ku nkomerero. Tewali kitongole kya mangu. Naye kati kiwedde, kifaanana nnyo Empagi ya Pompey.

Ekisinga obutono, omugongo we gukendeera okutuuka mu mukira, gubeera gwa yinsi bbiri zokka mu bugazi, era gufaanana ekintu ekiringa omupiira gwa bbiiru omweru. Nategeezebwa nti wakyaliwo obutonotono, naye nga bufiiriddwa obuwundo obumu obutono obulya abantu, abaana ba kabona, obwali bubibba okuzannya nabyo amayinja amabajje. Bwe tutyo tulaba engeri omugongo gw’ebiramu n’ebiramu ebisinga obunene gye gukendeera ku nkomerero ne gufuuka omuzannyo gw’omwana omungu.

Nor must there be omitted another strange attestation of the antiquity of the whale, in his own osseous post-diluvian reality, as set down by the venerable John Leo, the old Barbary traveller.

“Not far from the Sea-side, they have a Temple, the Rafters and Beams of which are made of Whale-Bones; for Whales of a monstrous size are oftentimes cast up dead upon that shore. The Common People imagine, that by a secret Power bestowed by God upon the Temple, no Whale can pass it without immediate death. But the truth of the Matter is, that on either side of the Temple, there are Rocks that shoot two Miles into the Sea, and wound the Whales when they light upon 'em. They keep a Whale's Rib of an incredible length for a Miracle, which lying upon the Ground with its convex part uppermost, makes an Arch, the Head of which cannot be reached by a Man upon a Camel's Back. This Rib (says John Leo) is said to have layn there a hundred Years before I saw it. Their Historians affirm, that a Prophet who prophesy'd of Mahomet, came from this Temple, and some do not stand to assert, that the Prophet Jonas was cast forth by the Whale at the Base of the Temple.”

In this Afric Temple of the Whale I leave you, reader, and if you be a Nantucketer, and a whaleman, you will silently worship there.





CHAPTER 105. Does the Whale's Magnitude Diminish?—Will He Perish?

Inasmuch, then, as this Leviathan comes floundering down upon us from the head-waters of the Eternities, it may be fitly inquired, whether, in the long course of his generations, he has not degenerated from the original bulk of his sires.

But upon investigation we find, that not only are the whales of the present day superior in magnitude to those whose fossil remains are found in the Tertiary system (embracing a distinct geological period prior to man), but of the whales found in that Tertiary system, those belonging to its latter formations exceed in size those of its earlier ones.

Of all the pre-adamite whales yet exhumed, by far the largest is the Alabama one mentioned in the last chapter, and that was less than seventy feet in length in the skeleton. Whereas, we have already seen, that the tape-measure gives seventy-two feet for the skeleton of a large sized modern whale. And I have heard, on whalemen's authority, that Sperm Whales have been captured near a hundred feet long at the time of capture.

But may it not be, that while the whales of the present hour are an advance in magnitude upon those of all previous geological periods; may it not be, that since Adam's time they have degenerated?

Assuredly, we must conclude so, if we are to credit the accounts of such gentlemen as Pliny, and the ancient naturalists generally. For Pliny tells us of whales that embraced acres of living bulk, and Aldrovandus of others which measured eight hundred feet in length—Rope Walks and Thames Tunnels of Whales! And even in the days of Banks and Solander, Cooke's naturalists, we find a Danish member of the Academy of Sciences setting down certain Iceland Whales (reydan-siskur, or Wrinkled Bellies) at one hundred and twenty yards; that is, three hundred and sixty feet. And Lacépède, the French naturalist, in his elaborate history of whales, in the very beginning of his work (page 3), sets down the Right Whale at one hundred metres, three hundred and twenty-eight feet. And this work was published so late as AD 1825.

But will any whaleman believe these stories? No. The whale of to-day is as big as his ancestors in Pliny's time. And if ever I go where Pliny is, I, a whaleman (more than he was), will make bold to tell him so. Because I cannot understand how it is, that while the Egyptian mummies that were buried thousands of years before even Pliny was born, do not measure so much in their coffins as a modern Kentuckian in his socks; and while the cattle and other animals sculptured on the oldest Egyptian and Nineveh tablets, by the relative proportions in which they are drawn, just as plainly prove that the high-bred, stall-fed, prize cattle of Smithfield, not only equal, but far exceed in magnitude the fattest of Pharaoh's fat kine; in the face of all this, I will not admit that of all animals the whale alone should have degenerated.

But still another inquiry remains; one often agitated by the more recondite Nantucketers. Whether owing to the almost omniscient look-outs at the mast-heads of the whale-ships, now penetrating even through Behring's straits, and into the remotest secret drawers and lockers of the world; and the thousand harpoons and lances darted along all continental coasts; the moot point is, whether Leviathan can long endure so wide a chase, and so remorseless a havoc; whether he must not at last be exterminated from the waters, and the last whale, like the last man, smoke his last pipe, and then himself evaporate in the final puff.

Comparing the humped herds of whales with the humped herds of buffalo, which, not forty years ago, overspread by tens of thousands the prairies of Illinois and Missouri, and shook their iron manes and scowled with their thunder-clotted brows upon the sites of populous river-capitals, where now the polite broker sells you land at a dollar an inch; in such a comparison an irresistible argument would seem furnished, to show that the hunted whale cannot now escape speedy extinction.

But you must look at this matter in every light. Though so short a period ago—not a good lifetime—the census of the buffalo in Illinois exceeded the census of men now in London, and though at the present day not one horn or hoof of them remains in all that region; and though the cause of this wondrous extermination was the spear of man; yet the far different nature of the whale-hunt peremptorily forbids so inglorious an end to the Leviathan. Forty men in one ship hunting the Sperm Whales for forty-eight months think they have done extremely well, and thank God, if at last they carry home the oil of forty fish. Whereas, in the days of the old Canadian and Indian hunters and trappers of the West, when the far west (in whose sunset suns still rise) was a wilderness and a virgin, the same number of moccasined men, for the same number of months, mounted on horse instead of sailing in ships, would have slain not forty, but forty thousand and more buffaloes; a fact that, if need were, could be statistically stated.

Nor, considered aright, does it seem any argument in favour of the gradual extinction of the Sperm Whale, for example, that in former years (the latter part of the last century, say) these Leviathans, in small pods, were encountered much oftener than at present, and, in consequence, the voyages were not so prolonged, and were also much more remunerative. Because, as has been elsewhere noticed, those whales, influenced by some views to safety, now swim the seas in immense caravans, so that to a large degree the scattered solitaries, yokes, and pods, and schools of other days are now aggregated into vast but widely separated, unfrequent armies. That is all. And equally fallacious seems the conceit, that because the so-called whale-bone whales no longer haunt many grounds in former years abounding with them, hence that species also is declining. For they are only being driven from promontory to cape; and if one coast is no longer enlivened with their jets, then, be sure, some other and remoter strand has been very recently startled by the unfamiliar spectacle.

Furthermore: concerning these last mentioned Leviathans, they have two firm fortresses, which, in all human probability, will for ever remain impregnable. And as upon the invasion of their valleys, the frosty Swiss have retreated to their mountains; so, hunted from the savannas and glades of the middle seas, the whale-bone whales can at last resort to their Polar citadels, and diving under the ultimate glassy barriers and walls there, come up among icy fields and floes; and in a charmed circle of everlasting December, bid defiance to all pursuit from man.

But as perhaps fifty of these whale-bone whales are harpooned for one cachalot, some philosophers of the forecastle have concluded that this positive havoc has already very seriously diminished their battalions. But though for some time past a number of these whales, not less than 13,000, have been annually slain on the nor' west coast by the Americans alone; yet there are considerations which render even this circumstance of little or no account as an opposing argument in this matter.

Natural as it is to be somewhat incredulous concerning the populousness of the more enormous creatures of the globe, yet what shall we say to Harto, the historian of Goa, when he tells us that at one hunting the King of Siam took 4,000 elephants; that in those regions elephants are numerous as droves of cattle in the temperate climes. And there seems no reason to doubt that if these elephants, which have now been hunted for thousands of years, by Semiramis, by Porus, by Hannibal, and by all the successive monarchs of the East—if they still survive there in great numbers, much more may the great whale outlast all hunting, since he has a pasture to expatiate in, which is precisely twice as large as all Asia, both Americas, Europe and Africa, New Holland, and all the Isles of the sea combined.

Moreover: we are to consider, that from the presumed great longevity of whales, their probably attaining the age of a century and more, therefore at any one period of time, several distinct adult generations must be contemporary. And what that is, we may soon gain some idea of, by imagining all the grave-yards, cemeteries, and family vaults of creation yielding up the live bodies of all the men, women, and children who were alive seventy-five years ago; and adding this countless host to the present human population of the globe.

Wherefore, for all these things, we account the whale immortal in his species, however perishable in his individuality. He swam the seas before the continents broke water; he once swam over the site of the Tuileries, and Windsor Castle, and the Kremlin. In Noah's flood he despised Noah's Ark; and if ever the world is to be again flooded, like the Netherlands, to kill off its rats, then the eternal whale will still survive, and rearing upon the topmost crest of the equatorial flood, spout his frothed defiance to the skies.





CHAPTER 106. Ahab's Leg.

The precipitating manner in which Captain Ahab had quitted the Samuel Enderby of London, had not been unattended with some small violence to his own person. He had lighted with such energy upon a thwart of his boat that his ivory leg had received a half-splintering shock. And when after gaining his own deck, and his own pivot-hole there, he so vehemently wheeled round with an urgent command to the steersman (it was, as ever, something about his not steering inflexibly enough); then, the already shaken ivory received such an additional twist and wrench, that though it still remained entire, and to all appearances lusty, yet Ahab did not deem it entirely trustworthy.

And, indeed, it seemed small matter for wonder, that for all his pervading, mad recklessness, Ahab did at times give careful heed to the condition of that dead bone upon which he partly stood. For it had not been very long prior to the Pequod's sailing from Nantucket, that he had been found one night lying prone upon the ground, and insensible; by some unknown, and seemingly inexplicable, unimaginable casualty, his ivory limb having been so violently displaced, that it had stake-wise smitten, and all but pierced his groin; nor was it without extreme difficulty that the agonizing wound was entirely cured.

Nor, at the time, had it failed to enter his monomaniac mind, that all the anguish of that then present suffering was but the direct issue of a former woe; and he too plainly seemed to see, that as the most poisonous reptile of the marsh perpetuates his kind as inevitably as the sweetest songster of the grove; so, equally with every felicity, all miserable events do naturally beget their like. Yea, more than equally, thought Ahab; since both the ancestry and posterity of Grief go further than the ancestry and posterity of Joy. For, not to hint of this: that it is an inference from certain canonic teachings, that while some natural enjoyments here shall have no children born to them for the other world, but, on the contrary, shall be followed by the joy-childlessness of all hell's despair; whereas, some guilty mortal miseries shall still fertilely beget to themselves an eternally progressive progeny of griefs beyond the grave; not at all to hint of this, there still seems an inequality in the deeper analysis of the thing. For, thought Ahab, while even the highest earthly felicities ever have a certain unsignifying pettiness lurking in them, but, at bottom, all heartwoes, a mystic significance, and, in some men, an archangelic grandeur; so do their diligent tracings-out not belie the obvious deduction. To trail the genealogies of these high mortal miseries, carries us at last among the sourceless primogenitures of the gods; so that, in the face of all the glad, hay-making suns, and soft cymballing, round harvest-moons, we must needs give in to this: that the gods themselves are not for ever glad. The ineffaceable, sad birth-mark in the brow of man, is but the stamp of sorrow in the signers.

Unwittingly here a secret has been divulged, which perhaps might more properly, in set way, have been disclosed before. With many other particulars concerning Ahab, always had it remained a mystery to some, why it was, that for a certain period, both before and after the sailing of the Pequod, he had hidden himself away with such Grand-Lama-like exclusiveness; and, for that one interval, sought speechless refuge, as it were, among the marble senate of the dead. Captain Peleg's bruited reason for this thing appeared by no means adequate; though, indeed, as touching all Ahab's deeper part, every revelation partook more of significant darkness than of explanatory light. But, in the end, it all came out; this one matter did, at least. That direful mishap was at the bottom of his temporary recluseness. And not only this, but to that ever-contracting, dropping circle ashore, who, for any reason, possessed the privilege of a less banned approach to him; to that timid circle the above hinted casualty—remaining, as it did, moodily unaccounted for by Ahab—invested itself with terrors, not entirely underived from the land of spirits and of wails. So that, through their zeal for him, they had all conspired, so far as in them lay, to muffle up the knowledge of this thing from others; and hence it was, that not till a considerable interval had elapsed, did it transpire upon the Pequod's decks.

But be all this as it may; let the unseen, ambiguous synod in the air, or the vindictive princes and potentates of fire, have to do or not with earthly Ahab, yet, in this present matter of his leg, he took plain practical procedures;—he called the carpenter.

And when that functionary appeared before him, he bade him without delay set about making a new leg, and directed the mates to see him supplied with all the studs and joists of jaw-ivory (Sperm Whale) which had thus far been accumulated on the voyage, in order that a careful selection of the stoutest, clearest-grained stuff might be secured. This done, the carpenter received orders to have the leg completed that night; and to provide all the fittings for it, independent of those pertaining to the distrusted one in use. Moreover, the ship's forge was ordered to be hoisted out of its temporary idleness in the hold; and, to accelerate the affair, the blacksmith was commanded to proceed at once to the forging of whatever iron contrivances might be needed.
