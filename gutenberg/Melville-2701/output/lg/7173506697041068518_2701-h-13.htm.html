ESSUULA 61. Stubb Atta Ennyonyi Eyitibwa Whale.

Bwe kiba nti eri Starbuck okwolesebwa kwa Squid kwali kintu kya bubonero, eri Queequeg kyali kintu kya njawulo nnyo.

“Bw’omulaba ‘quid,” omusajja omukambwe bwe yagamba, ng’alongoosa ekyuma kye mu bbanga ly’eryato lye eryali liwaniriddwa, “olwo n’omulaba mangu ‘parm whale.”

Enkeera lwali lusirise nnyo era nga luli mu bbugumu, era nga tewali kintu kya njawulo kiyinza kubakwatako, abakozi ba Pequod baali tebasobola kuziyiza tulo otulo olwaleetebwa ennyanja eyali etaliimu kintu kyonna. Kubanga ekitundu kino eky’ennyanja Buyindi kye twali tuyitamu mu kiseera ekyo si kye bayita ettaka eririmu obulamu; kwe kugamba, tesobola kulaba bitono nnyo ku nnyonyi eziyitibwa porpoises, dolphins, flying-fish, n’ebisolo ebirala ebirabika obulungi ebibeera mu mazzi agasinga okusikaasikanya, okusinga ago agali ku lubalama lw’omugga Rio de la Plata, oba ku ttaka eriri ku lubalama lw’ennyanja ku nnyanja Peru.

Yali ssaawa yange okuyimirira ku mutwe gw’omu maaso; era ng’ebibegabega byange byesigamye ku bitambaala by’obwakabaka ebyali bigonvu, nga ngenda n’okudda nga nwuubaala mu mpewo eyali eringa ey’okuloga. Tewali kiteeso kyali kisobola kugumira; mu muudu eyo ey’ekirooto nga nfiirwa okutegeera kwonna, ku nkomerero emmeeme yange yava mu mubiri gwange; newankubadde omubiri gwange gwakyagenda mu maaso n’okuwuubaala nga pendulum bw’ejja, nga wayiseewo ebbanga ddene ng’amaanyi agaasooka okugitambuza gaggyiddwawo.

Ere forgetfulness altogether came over me, nali nkirabye nti abavubi ku main ne mizzen-mast-heads baali bafunye dda otulo. Bwe kityo ku nkomerero ffenna abasatu twawuubaala nga tetulina bulamu okuva ku biwujjo, era ku buli kuwuubaala kwe twakola waaliwo okunyeenya omutwe okuva wansi okuva eri omuvuzi w’emmotoka eyali yeebase. Amayengo nago gaanyenya omutwe ku bikondo byabwe ebizibu; era emitala w’ennyanja eyali egazi, ebuvanjuba ne yeetooloola omutwe okudda mu maserengeta, n’enjuba n’ekwata byonna.

Amangu ago ebiwujjo byalabika ng’ebikutuka wansi w’amaaso gange agaali gazibye; ng’ebikolwa eby’obugwenyufu emikono gyange gye gyakwata ebibikka; ekitongole ekimu ekitalabika, eky’ekisa kyankuuma; nga nfunye ensisi nakomawo mu bulamu. Era laba! okumpi wansi w’omusulo gwaffe, nga tetulina ffuuti amakumi ana okuva, ennyonyi ennene ennyo eya Sperm Whale yali egalamidde ng’eyiringisibwa mu mazzi ng’ekisenge ky’ennyonyi ekika kya frigate eyagwa, omugongo gwe omugazi era ogwakaayakana, ogwa langi y’e Ethiopia, ng’eyakaayakana mu masasi g’enjuba ng’endabirwamu. Naye nga mugayaavu ng’amayengo mu kiyumba ky’ennyanja, era nga buli kiseera mu mirembe ng’afuuwa ennyonyi ye ey’omukka, ennyanja ekika kya whale yali eringa portly burgher ng’afuuwa payipu ye ey’emisana egy’ebbugumu. Naye payipu eyo, whale omwavu, ye yali esembayo. Nga balinga abakubiddwa omuggo gw’omulogo omu, emmeeri eyali yeebase ne buli eyeebase mu yo byonna omulundi gumu byatandika okuzuukuka; era amaloboozi agasukka mu makumi okuva mu bitundu byonna eby’ekibya, mu kiseera kye kimu n’ennukuta essatu ezaali waggulu, ne galeekaana okukaaba okwali kumanyidde, ng’ebyennyanja ebinene bifuuwa mpola era bulijjo omunnyo omubisi ogwakaayakana mu bbanga.

“Mugogole amaato! Luff!” Akabu bwe yayomba. Era ng’agondera ekiragiro kye, yadduka ekyuma ekivuga wansi ng’omugoba w’emmotoka tannakwata bipimo.

Okuwoggana okw’amangu okwakolebwa abakozi ku mmeeri kuteekwa okuba nga kwatiisa ennyanja ekika kya whale; era nga amaato tegannaba wansi, nga gakyuka mu kitiibwa, yawuga n’agenda mu kifo ekirimu empewo, naye n’obutebenkevu obutakyukakyuka, era ng’akola amayengo matono nnyo ng’awuga, ne kiba nti ng’alowooza nti oluvannyuma lw’ebyo byonna ayinza obutatya, Akabu n’alagira nti aleme ekyuma ekiyitibwa oar kisaana okukozesebwa, era tewali muntu yenna alina kwogera wabula mu kuwuubaala. Bwe tutyo bwe twatudde ng’Abayindi b’omu Ontario ku bikondo by’amaato, twavuga mangu naye nga tusirise; obukkakkamu obutakkiriza bwa sails ezitaliimu maloboozi eziteekebwawo. Mu kiseera kino, bwe twaseeyeeya bwe tutyo nga tuyigga, ekisolo ekyo kyabuuka omukira gwakyo mu bbanga mu ngeri eyeesimbye mu bbanga, n’oluvannyuma ne kibbira nga tetulaba ng’omunaala ogumira.

“Eyo egenda flukes!” ye yali emiranga, okulangirira okwaddirira amangu ddala Stubb okufulumya omupiira gwe n’okukuma omuliro mu payipu ye, kubanga kati ekiwummulo kyaweebwa. Oluvannyuma lw’ekiseera ekijjuvu eky’okuwulikika kwe okuggwaako, ennyanja ekika kya whale yaddamu okusituka, era nga kati eri mu maaso g’eryato ly’omunywa sigala, era nga liri kumpi nnyo okusinga ku malala gonna, Stubb yabalirira ekitiibwa ky’okukwatibwa. Kyalabika bulungi, kati, nti ennyanja ekika kya whale yali emaze okutegeera abaagigoberera. N’olwekyo okusirika kwonna okw’obwegendereza kwali tekukyalina mugaso. Ebiwujjo byasuulibwa, era ebiwujjo ne bijja mu ddoboozi ery’omwanguka mu muzannyo. Era ng’akyafuuwa ku payipu ye, Stubb yasanyukira abakozi be okutuuka ku kulumba.

Yee, enkyukakyuka ey’amaanyi yali ezze ku byennyanja. Bonna nga balamu mu kabi ke, yali agenda “head out”; ekitundu ekyo nga kifuluma mu ngeri ey’ekyekulungirivu okuva mu kizimbulukusa eky’eddalu kye yafumba.*

*Kijja kulabibwa mu kifo ekirala nga ekintu ekitangaavu ennyo munda yonna ey’omutwe omunene ennyo ogwa sperm whale mwe gulimu. Wadde nga kirabika kye kisinga obunene, kye kitundu ekisinga okumusanyusa. N’olwekyo mu ngeri ennyangu n’agisitula mu bbanga, era bulijjo akikola ng’agenda ku sipiidi ye esinga. Ng’oggyeeko ekyo, bwe butyo obugazi bw’ekitundu eky’okungulu eky’omu maaso g’omutwe gwe, era bwe butyo n’okutondebwa kw’amazzi agasaliddwa okukendeera okw’ekitundu ekya wansi, ne kiba nti bw’asitula omutwe gwe mu ngeri ey’ekyekulungirivu, bw’atyo ayinza okugambibwa nti yeekyusa okuva mu bluff-bowed sluggish galliot mu lyato erya New York erya pilot-boat eryali lisongovu.

“Mutandike, mutandike basajja bange! Temweyanguwa mwekka; twala obudde bungi —naye mutandike; mutandike ng’okubwatuka kw’enkuba, ekyo kyokka,” Stubb bwe yakaaba ng’afuuwa omukka ng’ayogera. “Mutandike, kati; bawe stroke empanvu era ey'amaanyi, Tashtego. Mutandike, Tash, omulenzi wange — mutandike, byonna; naye mubeere nga muyonjo, mubeere nga muyonjo — cucumber kye kigambo — kyangu, kyangu — kyokka mutandike ng’okufa okw’ennaku n’abasitaani abanyeenya, era muzuukuse abafu abaziikiddwa nga beesimbye okuva mu ntaana zaabwe, abalenzi — ekyo kyokka. Mutandikirewo!”

“Woo-hoo! Wa-hee!” yaleekaana Gay-Header ng’addamu, ng’asitula ebiwujjo by’olutalo ebikadde okutuuka mu bbanga; nga buli muvuzi w’eryato mu lyato eryali lifunye obuzibu bw’abuuka mu maaso mu ngeri etaali ya kyeyagalire n’omupiira gumu ogw’amaanyi ogw’okukulembera Omuyindi eyali ayagala ennyo gwe yawa.

Naye enduulu ze ez’ensiko zaddibwamu abalala nga za nsiko nnyo. “Kee-hee! Kee-hee!” Daggoo bwe yaleekaana ng’asikambula mu maaso n’emabega ku ntebe ye, ng’engo etambula mu kiyumba kye.

“Ka-la! Koo-loo!” Queequeg bwe yayomba, ng’alinga akuba emimwa gye ku kamwa k’ente ya Grenadier. Era bwe kityo n’amaloboozi n’okuleekaana ebiwujjo bisala ennyanja. Mu kiseera kino, Stubb ng’asigazza ekifo kye mu kabangali, yakyakubiriza basajja be okutuuka ku ntandikwa, ebbanga lyonna ng’afuuwa omukka okuva mu kamwa ke. Ng’abantu abaggwaamu essuubi baasika ne basika omuguwa, okutuusa eddoboozi ery’okwaniriza lwe lyawulirwa —“Yimiriza, Tashtego! —muwe!” Harpoon yasuulibwa. “Stern byonna!” Abavuzi b’amaato baawagira amazzi; mu kaseera ke kamu ekintu kyagenda kyokya era nga kiwuuma ku buli emu ku bisambi byabwe. Yali layini ey’amagezi. Akaseera katono emabega, Stubb yali akutte mangu enkyukakyuka endala bbiri nayo okwetooloola omutwe gw’emiti, era, olw’okwetoloola kwayo okw’amangu okweyongera, omukka ogwa bbululu wa hempen kati gwafuumuuka waggulu ne gwetabula n’omukka ogutakyukakyuka ogwava mu payipu ye. Nga layini eyita okwetooloola n’okwetooloola loggerhead; bwe kityo era, nga tekinnatuuka mu kifo ekyo, kyayita mu ngeri ey’ebizimba mu mikono gya Stubb gyombi era n’eyita mu mikono gyombi, nga mu butanwa engoye z’emikono, oba square za kanvaasi eziriko ebyuma oluusi ezaayambalwa mu biseera bino, zaali zigudde mu butanwa. Kyalinga okukwata ekitala ky’omulabe ekisongovu eky’amasasi abiri ku kyuma, era omulabe oyo buli kiseera ng’afuba okukiggya mu mukono gwo.

“Fukirira layini! okufukirira layini!” Stubb bwe yakaaba eri omuvuzi wa bbaafu (ye yali atudde ku mabbali ga bbaafu) eyasikambula enkoofiira ye, n’adduka amazzi g’ennyanja mu yo.* Enkyukakyuka endala zaakyusibwa, layini n’etandika okukwata ekifo kyayo. Eryato kati lyabuuka mu mazzi agabuguma nga shark all fins. Stubb ne Tashtego wano baakyusa ebifo —ekikolo okudda ku stern —bizineesi eyeewuunyisa ddala mu kavuyo ako akakankana.

*Ekitundu okulaga obuteetaagisa bw’ekikolwa kino, wano kiyinza okugambibwa, nti, mu nvuba y’Abadaaki enkadde, mopu yakozesebwanga okumenya layini y’okudduka n’amazzi; mu mmeeri endala nnyingi, embizzi oba bailer ey’embaawo eteekebwawo olw’ekigendererwa ekyo. Kyokka enkoofiira yo y’esinga okubeera ennyangu.

Okuva ku layini ekankana eyawanvuya obuwanvu bwonna obw’ekitundu eky’okungulu eky’eryato, era okuva kati bwe yali enywezeddwa okusinga omuguwa gw’ennanga, wandirowoozezza nti emmeeri eyo yalina ebikondo bibiri —ekimu kyasala amazzi, ekirala empewo —ng’eryato churned on okuyita mu bintu byombi ebivuganya omulundi gumu. Omuggalo ogutasalako gwazannyibwanga ku butaasa; eddy ewunyiriza etakoma mu kuzuukuka kwe; era, ku kutambula okutono okuva munda, wadde naye nga kwa lunwe olutono, emmeeri eyali ekankana, eyatika yalengejja waggulu w’emmundu ye eyali ewunyiriza n’eyingira mu nnyanja. Bwe batyo ne bafubutuka; buli musajja ng’alina amaanyi n’omusingi nga yeesibye ku ntebe ye, okutangira okusuulibwa ku kifuba; ne ffoomu empanvu eya Tashtego ku siteeringi ng’efukamidde kumpi emirundi ebiri, okusobola okukka wansi ekifo kye eky’amaanyi ag’ekisikirize. Ennyonyi zonna eza Atlantic ne Pacific zaali zirabika nga ziyise nga bwe zikuba amasasi mu kkubo, okutuusa mu kiseera ekiwanvu envubu n’ekendeeza ku nnyonyi ye.

“Haul in — haul in!” Stubb bwe yakaaba eri omuteebi! era, nga batunudde okwetooloola eri ennyanja ekika kya whale, emikono gyonna gyatandika okusika eryato okutuuka gy’ali, ng’eryato likyasimbulwa. Mu bbanga ttono ng’agenda waggulu ku mabbali ge, Stubb, ng’asimbye okugulu kwe mu cleat eyali efuuse ekizibu, n’adduka emisinde oluvannyuma lw’omusinde n’ayingira mu byennyanja ebyali bibuuka; ku kigambo ky’ekiragiro, eryato ne likyukakyuka okuva mu kkubo ly’okuwuuma okw’entiisa okw’ennyanja ekika kya whale, n’oluvannyuma ne ligenda waggulu okusuula ekirala.

Omugga omumyufu kati gwayiwa okuva ku njuyi zonna ez’ekisolo ng’emigga egyakka ku lusozi. Omulambo gwe ogwali gubonyaabonyezebwa tegwayiringisibwa mu mubisi gw’enjuki wabula mu musaayi, ogwabuguma era nga gufuumuuka okumala ffuuti eziwera emabega mu kuzuukuka kwabwe. Enjuba eyali eserengese ng’ezannyira ku kidiba kino ekimyufu mu nnyanja, n’ezzaayo ekifaananyi kyayo mu buli maaso, bonna ne bamasamasa buli omu ng’abasajja abamyufu. Era ebbanga eryo lyonna, omukka omweru ogwa jet ku jet gwakubwa mu ngeri eruma ennyo okuva mu spiracle ya whale, n’okufuuwa okw’amaanyi oluvannyuma lw’okufuuwa okuva mu kamwa k’omukulu w’omutwe eyali acamuse; nga ku buli musaale, ng’asikambula effumu lye eryali likoona (ku layini eyagikwatako), Stubb yagigolola enfunda n’enfunda, ng’akuba emiggo mitono egy’amangu ku gunwale, olwo enfunda n’enfunda n’agisindika mu whale.

“Simbula waggulu —sika waggulu!” kati yakaabira omuteebi, ng’ennyanja ekika kya whale eyali egenda ekendeera ewummudde mu busungu bwe. “Simbula waggulu!—kumpi ne!” era eryato lyali ligenda ku mabbali g’ebyennyanja. Bwe yatuuka ewala waggulu w’obutaasa, Stubb yasika mpola effumu lye empanvu ensongovu mu byennyanja, n’agikuuma awo, ng’akuba n’obwegendereza n’okuwuuta, ng’alinga anoonya n’obwegendereza okuwulira oluvannyuma lw’essaawa ezimu eza zaabu nti ennyanja ekika kya whale yandiba nga yamira, era gye yali atya wa kumenya ere yali asobola okugikuba hook out. Naye essaawa eyo eya zaabu gye yali anoonya ye yali obulamu obw’omunda obw’ebyennyanja. Era kati kikubiddwa; kubanga, okutandika okuva mu kuwuuma kwe mu kintu ekyo ekitayinza kwogerwa ekiyitibwa “okufuukuuka” kwe, ekisolo ekyo kyawuubaala mu ngeri ey’entiisa mu musaayi gwe, ne kyezinga mu kufuuyira okutayitamu, okw’eddalu, okubuguma, bwe kityo emmeeri ey’emikono eyali mu kabi, ng’egwa mu kaseera ako emabega, n’efuna akavuyo kungi mu ngeri ey’amaaso okulwana okuva mu ttuntu eryo erya phrensied mu mpewo entangaavu ey’emisana.

Era kati ng’ekendedde mu bbugumu lye, ennyanja ekika kya whale yaddamu okwekulukuunya mu maaso; okukulukuta okuva ku ludda olumu okudda ku lulala; spasmodically dilating and contracting his spout-hole, nga alina okussa okusongovu, okwatika, okulumwa. Mu nkomerero, gush after gush of clotted red gore, nga bwe yali lees za purple wine, zaakubwa mu mpewo eyali etidde; era n’addamu okugwa emabega, n’adduka ng’atonnya wansi ku mabbali ge agatatambula mu nnyanja. Omutima gwe gwali gukutuse!

“Afudde, Mwami Stubb,” Daggoo bwe yagamba.

"Yee; payipu zombi zaavaamu omukka!” era ng’aggyayo eyiye mu kamwa ke, Stubb n’asaasaanya evvu eryafu ku mazzi; era, okumala akaseera, n’ayimirira ng’alowooza ng’atunudde mu mulambo omunene ennyo gwe yali akoze.





ESSUULA 62. Omusipi.

Ekigambo ekikwata ku kintu ekyaliwo mu ssuula esembayo.

Okusinziira ku nkozesa etakyukakyuka ey’okuvuba, eryato eriyitibwa whale-boat lisika okuva ku mmeeri, ng’omukulu oba omutemu whale ye muvuzi ow’ekiseera, ate omuvubi wa harpooneer oba whale-fastener y’asika ekyuma ekisinga okuba eky’omu maaso, ekyo ekimanyiddwa nga harpooneer-oar . Kati kyetaaga omukono ogw’amaanyi era ogw’obusimu okukuba ekyuma ekisooka mu byennyanja; kubanga emirundi mingi, mu kye bayita omusasi omuwanvu, ekikozesebwa ekizito kirina okusuulibwa mu bbanga lya ffuuti amakumi abiri oba amakumi asatu. Naye ne bwe kiba nga kiwanvuye era nga kikooye okuyigga, omukubi w’ensambaggere asuubirwa okusika emmeeri ye mu kiseera kino okutuuka ku nkomerero; mazima ddala, asuubirwa okuteekawo ekyokulabirako ky’emirimu egisukkulumye ku muntu eri abalala, si mu kuvugira mu ngeri etasuubirwa kwokka, naye n’okuleekaana okw’amaanyi era okw’obuzira enfunda n’enfunda; era kye kitegeeza okusigala ng’oleekaana waggulu ku kkampasi y’omuntu, ng’ate ebinywa ebirala byonna binywezeddwa era nga kitundu kitandise —ekyo tewali akimanyi wabula abo abakigezezzaako. Ku lw’ekimu, sisobola kuwoggana nnyo n’omutima gwange era nkola mu ngeri ey’obulagajjavu ennyo mu kiseera kye kimu. N’olwekyo, mu mbeera eno ey’okunyigirizibwa, ey’okuwoggana, ng’omugongo gwe gutunudde mu byennyanja, omulundi gumu omukubi w’ennanga akooye awulira okukaaba okusanyusa —“Yimiridde, muwe!” Kati alina okugwa n’okunyweza ekiwato kye, okwetooloola wakati we wakati mu kkubo, n’akwata ekyuma kye okuva mu kisambi, era n’amaanyi matono ge gayinza okusigala, awandiika emboozi okukisuula mu ngeri emu oba endala mu nnyanja ekika kya whale. Tekyewuunyisa, okutwala emmeeri yonna ey’abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale mu mubiri, nti ku mikisa ataano egy’obwenkanya egy’okukuba omusaale, si etaano gye gifuna obuwanguzi; tekyewuunyisa nti abavubi bangi abatalina mukisa bakolimirwa mu ngeri ey’eddaalu era ne banyiiga; tekyewuunyisa nti ddala abamu ku bo baakutuka emisuwa gyabwe mu lyato; tekyewuunyisa nti abamu ku ba sperm whalemen tebaliiwo emyaka ena nga balina ebipipa bina; tekyewuunyisa nti eri bannannyini mmeeri bangi, okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale kweraliikirira okufiirwa; kubanga omukubi w’ennanga y’akola olugendo, era bw’oggya omukka mu mubiri gwe oyinza otya okusuubira okugusangayo ng’abasinga baagala!

Nate, singa omusipi guba guwangudde, olwo mu kaseera ak’okubiri akakulu, kwe kugamba, ennyanja ekika kya whale bw’etandika okudduka, omuvuzi w’amaato n’omuvuzi w’amaato mu ngeri y’emu batandika okudduka mu maaso n’emabega, mu kabi akali kumpi eri bo bennyini ne buli muntu omulala. Olwo lwe bakyusa ebifo; era omukulu w’eryato, omuserikale omukulu ow’eryato ettono, atwala ekifo kye ekituufu mu bikondo by’eryato.

Kati, sifaayo ani akuuma ekikontana n’ekyo, naye bino byonna bya busirusiru era tebyetaagisa. Omukulu alina okusigala mu butaasa okuva ku kusooka okutuuka ku nkomerero; bombi yandibadde akuba ensambaggere n’effumu, era tewali kuvugira kwonna kulina kumusuubirwamu, okuggyako mu mbeera eyeeyolese eri omuvubi yenna. Nkimanyi nti kino oluusi kyandibadde kizingiramu okufiirwa sipiidi katono mu kuyigga; naye obumanyirivu obw’ekiseera ekiwanvu mu bavubi ab’enjawulo ab’amawanga agasukka mu limu bunkakasa nti mu kulemererwa okusinga obungi mu nvuba, mu ngeri yonna tekubadde nnyo sipiidi ya whale ng’okukoowa okwayogerwako emabegako okw’omuvubi w’ennyanja ekika kya harpooneer kwe kuvuddeko bbo.

Okusobola okukakasa obulungi obusinga obunene mu musinde, abavuzi b’engoye z’ensi eno balina okutandika okuyimirira okuva mu butakola, so si okuva mu kukola ennyo.





ESSUULA 63. Ensigo.

Okuva mu kikolo, amatabi gakula; okuva mu byo, amatabi. Kale, mu masomo agavaamu ebibala, kuza essuula.

Ensigo eyogeddwako ku lupapula oluyise esaana okwogerwako mu ngeri eyeetongodde. Guno muggo oguliko enkokola ogw’engeri ey’enjawulo, nga guweza ffuuti nga bbiri, nga guyingizibwa mu ngeri eyeesimbye mu kisenge ky’emmundu eky’oku ddyo okumpi n’obutaasa, n’ekigendererwa eky’okufunira ekifo eky’okuwummuliramu enkomerero y’embaawo ey’engoye, ng’enkomerero yaayo endala ey’obwereere, ey’emiguwa ekoma nga eserengese pulojekiti okuva ku prow. Mu ngeri eyo eky’okulwanyisa ekyo kiba kituuse mangu eri oyo akisuula, n’akinyagulula amangu okuva mu kifo we kiwummudde ng’omusajja ow’emabega w’emmundu bw’akuba emmundu ye okuva ku bbugwe. Kimanyiddwa okuba n’engoye bbiri eziwummudde mu kisambi, nga ziyitibwa ekyuma ekisooka n’ekyokubiri.

Naye enkoba zino ebbiri, buli emu n’omuguwa gwayo, zombi ziyungiddwa ku layini; ekigendererwa nga kino: okuzikuba emisinde gyombi, bwe kiba kisoboka, emu amangu ago oluvannyuma lw’endala okuyingira mu nnyanja y’emu; bwe kityo singa, mu kusika okujja, omu yandisika ebweru, omulala asobole okusigala ng’akwata. Kwe kubisaamu emirundi ebiri emikisa. Naye kitera nnyo okubaawo nti olw’okudduka okw’amangu, okw’amaanyi, okw’okukonziba kw’ennyanja ekika kya whale ng’afunye ekyuma ekisooka, kifuuka ekitasobokera ddala omukubi wa harpoone, wadde ng’alinga omulabe mu ntambula ze, okumusuula ekyuma ekyokubiri mu ye. Hold it there now, and pay attention.”

“All 'dention,” said the old black, with both hands placed as desired, vainly wriggling his grizzled head, as if to get both ears in front at one and the same time.

“Well then, cook, you see this whale-steak of yours was so very bad, that I have put it out of sight as soon as possible; you see that, don't you? Well, for the future, when you cook another whale-steak for my private table here, the capstan, I'll tell you what to do so as not to spoil it by overdoing. Hold the steak in one hand, and show a live coal to it with the other; that done, dish it; d'ye hear? And now to-morrow, cook, when we are cutting in the fish, be sure you stand by to get the tips of his fins; have them put in pickle. As for the ends of the flukes, have them soused, cook. There, now ye may go.”

But Fleece had hardly got three paces off, when he was recalled.

“Cook, give me cutlets for supper to-morrow night in the mid-watch. D'ye hear? away you sail, then.—Halloa! stop! make a bow before you go.—Avast heaving again! Whale-balls for breakfast—don't forget.”

“Wish, by gor! whale eat him, 'stead of him eat whale. I'm bressed if he ain't more of shark dan Massa Shark hisself,” muttered the old man, limping away; with which sage ejaculation he went to his hammock.





CHAPTER 65. The Whale as a Dish.

That mortal man should feed upon the creature that feeds his lamp, and, like Stubb, eat him by his own light, as you may say; this seems so outlandish a thing that one must needs go a little into the history and philosophy of it.

It is upon record, that three centuries ago the tongue of the Right Whale was esteemed a great delicacy in France, and commanded large prices there. Also, that in Henry VIIIth's time, a certain cook of the court obtained a handsome reward for inventing an admirable sauce to be eaten with barbacued porpoises, which, you remember, are a species of whale. Porpoises, indeed, are to this day considered fine eating. The meat is made into balls about the size of billiard balls, and being well seasoned and spiced might be taken for turtle-balls or veal balls. The old monks of Dunfermline were very fond of them. They had a great porpoise grant from the crown.

The fact is, that among his hunters at least, the whale would by all hands be considered a noble dish, were there not so much of him; but when you come to sit down before a meat-pie nearly one hundred feet long, it takes away your appetite. Only the most unprejudiced of men like Stubb, nowadays partake of cooked whales; but the Esquimaux are not so fastidious. We all know how they live upon whales, and have rare old vintages of prime old train oil. Zogranda, one of their most famous doctors, recommends strips of blubber for infants, as being exceedingly juicy and nourishing. And this reminds me that certain Englishmen, who long ago were accidentally left in Greenland by a whaling vessel—that these men actually lived for several months on the mouldy scraps of whales which had been left ashore after trying out the blubber. Among the Dutch whalemen these scraps are called “fritters”; which, indeed, they greatly resemble, being brown and crisp, and smelling something like old Amsterdam housewives' dough-nuts or oly-cooks, when fresh. They have such an eatable look that the most self-denying stranger can hardly keep his hands off.

But what further depreciates the whale as a civilized dish, is his exceeding richness. He is the great prize ox of the sea, too fat to be delicately good. Look at his hump, which would be as fine eating as the buffalo's (which is esteemed a rare dish), were it not such a solid pyramid of fat. But the spermaceti itself, how bland and creamy that is; like the transparent, half-jellied, white meat of a cocoanut in the third month of its growth, yet far too rich to supply a substitute for butter. Nevertheless, many whalemen have a method of absorbing it into some other substance, and then partaking of it. In the long try watches of the night it is a common thing for the seamen to dip their ship-biscuit into the huge oil-pots and let them fry there awhile. Many a good supper have I thus made.

In the case of a small Sperm Whale the brains are accounted a fine dish. The casket of the skull is broken into with an axe, and the two plump, whitish lobes being withdrawn (precisely resembling two large puddings), they are then mixed with flour, and cooked into a most delectable mess, in flavor somewhat resembling calves' head, which is quite a dish among some epicures; and every one knows that some young bucks among the epicures, by continually dining upon calves' brains, by and by get to have a little brains of their own, so as to be able to tell a calf's head from their own heads; which, indeed, requires uncommon discrimination. And that is the reason why a young buck with an intelligent looking calf's head before him, is somehow one of the saddest sights you can see. The head looks a sort of reproachfully at him, with an “Et tu Brute!” expression.

It is not, perhaps, entirely because the whale is so excessively unctuous that landsmen seem to regard the eating of him with abhorrence; that appears to result, in some way, from the consideration before mentioned: ie that a man should eat a newly murdered thing of the sea, and eat it too by its own light. But no doubt the first man that ever murdered an ox was regarded as a murderer; perhaps he was hung; and if he had been put on his trial by oxen, he certainly would have been; and he certainly deserved it if any murderer does. Go to the meat-market of a Saturday night and see the crowds of live bipeds staring up at the long rows of dead quadrupeds. Does not that sight take a tooth out of the cannibal's jaw? Cannibals? who is not a cannibal? I tell you it will be more tolerable for the Fejee that salted down a lean missionary in his cellar against a coming famine; it will be more tolerable for that provident Fejee, I say, in the day of judgment, than for thee, civilized and enlightened gourmand, who nailest geese to the ground and feastest on their bloated livers in thy paté-de-foie-gras.

But Stubb, he eats the whale by its own light, does he? and that is adding insult to injury, is it? Look at your knife-handle, there, my civilized and enlightened gourmand dining off that roast beef, what is that handle made of?—what but the bones of the brother of the very ox you are eating? And what do you pick your teeth with, after devouring that fat goose? With a feather of the same fowl. And with what quill did the Secretary of the Society for the Suppression of Cruelty to Ganders formally indite his circulars? It is only within the last month or two that that society passed a resolution to patronize nothing but steel pens.





CHAPTER 66. The Shark Massacre.

When in the Southern Fishery, a captured Sperm Whale, after long and weary toil, is brought alongside late at night, it is not, as a general thing at least, customary to proceed at once to the business of cutting him in. For that business is an exceedingly laborious one; is not very soon completed; and requires all hands to set about it. Therefore, the common usage is to take in all sail; lash the helm a'lee; and then send every one below to his hammock till daylight, with the reservation that, until that time, anchor-watches shall be kept; that is, two and two for an hour, each couple, the crew in rotation shall mount the deck to see that all goes well.

But sometimes, especially upon the Line in the Pacific, this plan will not answer at all; because such incalculable hosts of sharks gather round the moored carcase, that were he left so for six hours, say, on a stretch, little more than the skeleton would be visible by morning. In most other parts of the ocean, however, where these fish do not so largely abound, their wondrous voracity can be at times considerably diminished, by vigorously stirring them up with sharp whaling-spades, a procedure notwithstanding, which, in some instances, only seems to tickle them into still greater activity. But it was not thus in the present case with the Pequod's sharks; though, to be sure, any man unaccustomed to such sights, to have looked over her side that night, would have almost thought the whole round sea was one huge cheese, and those sharks the maggots in it.

Nevertheless, upon Stubb setting the anchor-watch after his supper was concluded; and when, accordingly, Queequeg and a forecastle seaman came on deck, no small excitement was created among the sharks; for immediately suspending the cutting stages over the side, and lowering three lanterns, so that they cast long gleams of light over the turbid sea, these two mariners, darting their long whaling-spades, kept up an incessant murdering of the sharks,* by striking the keen steel deep into their skulls, seemingly their only vital part. But in the foamy confusion of their mixed and struggling hosts, the marksmen could not always hit their mark; and this brought about new revelations of the incredible ferocity of the foe. They viciously snapped, not only at each other's disembowelments, but like flexible bows, bent round, and bit their own; till those entrails seemed swallowed over and over again by the same mouth, to be oppositely voided by the gaping wound. Nor was this all. It was unsafe to meddle with the corpses and ghosts of these creatures. A sort of generic or Pantheistic vitality seemed to lurk in their very joints and bones, after what might be called the individual life had departed. Killed and hoisted on deck for the sake of his skin, one of these sharks almost took poor Queequeg's hand off, when he tried to shut down the dead lid of his murderous jaw.

*The whaling-spade used for cutting-in is made of the very best steel; is about the bigness of a man's spread hand; and in general shape, corresponds to the garden implement after which it is named; only its sides are perfectly flat, and its upper end considerably narrower than the lower. This weapon is always kept as sharp as possible; and when being used is occasionally honed, just like a razor. In its socket, a stiff pole, from twenty to thirty feet long, is inserted for a handle.

“Queequeg no care what god made him shark,” said the savage, agonizingly lifting his hand up and down; “wedder Fejee god or Nantucket god; but de god wat made shark must be one dam Ingin.”
