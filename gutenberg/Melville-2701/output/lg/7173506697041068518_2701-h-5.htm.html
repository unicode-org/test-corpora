ESSUULA 22. Ssekukkulu ennungi.

Mu bbanga eritali ly’ewala, nga tunaatera okutuuka ku ssaawa 12 ez’emisana, ku kugobwa okusembayo kw’abakozi b’emmeeri, era nga Pequod emaze okusimbulwa okuva ku mwalo, era nga Charity eyali elowoozebwanga bulijjo emaze okuva mu lyato erya whale, n’ekirabo kye ekisembayo —ekiro -cap eri Stubb, munne owookubiri, muggya we, ne Bayibuli ya sipeeya eri omuwanika —oluvannyuma lwa bino byonna, Kapiteeni ababiri, Peleg ne Bildad, baafuluma okuva mu kabina, ne bakyukira munne omukulu, Peleg bwe yagamba :

“Kati, Mwami Starbuck, okakasa nti buli kimu kituufu? Kapiteeni Akabu yenna mwetegefu —yamala kwogera naye —tewali kintu kirala kya kufuna okuva ku lukalu, eh? Kale, muyite emikono gyonna, olwo. Muster ’em aft wano —bakuŋŋaanyizza!”

Bildad yagamba nti: “Tekyetaagisa bigambo bivvoola, ne bwe biba byangu, Peleg, naye wala naawe, mukwano gwange Starbuck, okole bye twagala.”

Nga kati! Wano ku kifo kyennyini we baatandikira olugendo, Kapiteeni Pelegi ne Kapiteeni Bildad baali balugenda n’omukono ogwa waggulu ku ddeeke ya kwata, nga balinga abaduumizi ab’awamu ku nnyanja, awamu n’okulabika kwonna ku mwalo . Era, ye Kapiteeni Akabu, tewali kabonero konna akalaga; kyokka, baagamba nti yali mu kabina. Naye olwo, ekirowoozo kyali nti, okubeerawo kwe tekwali kwetaagisa n’akatono mu kugipima emmeeri, n’okugivuga obulungi okugenda ku nnyanja. Mazima ddala, nga ekyo bwe kyali si bizinensi ye entuufu n’akatono, wabula ey’omugoba w’ennyonyi; era nga bwe yali tannawona ddala —bwe bagamba —n’olwekyo, Kapiteeni Akabu yasigala wansi. Era bino byonna byalabika ng’eby’obutonde ekimala; naddala nga mu mpeereza y’abasuubuzi bakapiteeni bangi tebalaga ku ddeeke okumala ebbanga ddene oluvannyuma lw’okusitula ennanga, wabula basigala waggulu w’emmeeza y’akayumba, nga basiibula essanyu ne mikwano gyabwe egy’oku lubalama lw’ennyanja, nga tebannava ku mmeeri for good with the omuvuzi w'ennyonyi.

Naye tewaaliwo mukisa munene kulowooza ku nsonga eyo, kubanga kati Kapiteeni Peleg yenna yali mulamu. Yalabika ng’okusinga okwogera n’okuduumira, so si Birudaadi.

“Aft here, mmwe batabani ba bachelors,” bwe yakaaba, ng’abalunnyanja balwawo ku kikondo ekikulu. “Mw. Starbuck, zivuge emabega.”

“Mukube weema eyo!” —kye kyali ekiragiro ekyaddako. Nga bwe nnalambika emabegako, marquee eno ey’amagumba g’ennyanja ekika kya whalebone teyasimbibwangako okuggyako ku mwalo; era ku lyato erya Pequod, okumala emyaka amakumi asatu, ekiragiro ky’okukuba weema kyali kimanyiddwa bulungi nti kye kintu ekiddako okusitula ennanga.

“Musajja wa kapisita! Omusaayi n’okubwatuka! —buuka!” —kye kyali ekiragiro ekyaddako, era abakozi ne babuuka okunoonya emisinde gy’emikono.

Kati mu kupimibwa, siteegi okutwalira awamu ebeera omugoba w’emmeeri kye kitundu eky’omu maaso eky’emmeeri. Era wano Bildad, nga, ne Peleg, kimanyiddwa, ng’oggyeeko abaserikale be abalala, yali omu ku bavuzi b’ennyonyi abaali bafunye layisinsi ku mwalo —nga ateeberezebwa okuba nga yeefunira omuvuzi w’ennyonyi asobole okutaasa ssente z’omuvuzi w’ennyonyi eza Nantucket emmeeri zonna ze yali afaayo, kubanga teyavugako nnyonyi ndala yonna —Bildad, ngamba, kati ayinza okulabibwa ng’akola nnyo mu kutunula waggulu w’obutaasa okunoonya ennanga eyali esemberera, era mu biseera ebitali bimu ng’ayimba ekyalabika ng’omuggo ogw’ennaku ogwa zabbuli, eri cheer the hands at the windlass, eyawuluguma ekika ky’oluyimba olukwata ku bawala mu Booble Alley, n’okwagala okulungi okw’omu mutima. Wadde kyali kityo, nga tewannayita nnaku ssatu, Bildad yali abagambye nti tewali nnyimba za kivve zijja kukkirizibwa ku mmeeri ya Pequod naddala mu kupima; era Charity, mwannyina, yali atadde kkopi entono eya Watts erongooseddwa mu kifo buli omuvubi w’oku nnyanja.

Mu kiseera ekyo, ng’alabirira ekitundu ekirala eky’emmeeri, Kapiteeni Peleg yasikambula era n’alayira okudda emabega mu ngeri esinga okutiisa. Kumpi nnalowooza nti ajja kubbira emmeeri nga ennanga tennasituka; mu ngeri etali ya kyeyagalire nayimirira ku handspike yange, ne ŋŋamba Queequeg akole kye kimu, nga ndowooza ku kabi ffembi ke twadduka, mu kutandika olugendo ne sitaani ng’oyo eri omugoba w’ennyonyi. Naye nnali nneebudaabuda n’ekirowoozo nti mu Birudaadi omutya Katonda ayinza okusangibwamu obulokozi obumu, wadde nga yali layeri ye ey’ebikumi musanvu mu nsanvu mu musanvu; bwe nawulira okukuba okw’amangu okw’amaanyi mu mugongo, era nga nkyuka, ne ntya nnyo olw’okwolesebwa kwa Kapiteeni Peleg mu kikolwa eky’okuggya okugulu kwe okuva mu bitundu byange eby’okumpi. Ekyo kye kyali ekigwo kyange ekyasooka.

“Eyo y’engeri gye basitula mu kusaba kw’okusuubula?” bwe yawuluguma. “Ensulo, ggwe omutwe gw’endiga; ensulo, era omenye omugongo gwo! Lwaki temusenya, ngamba, mwenna — ensulo! Quohog! ensulo, ggwe chap n'enviiri emmyuufu; ensulo eyo, Scotch-cap; ensulo, ggwe empale eya kiragala. Ensulo, ngamba mmwe mwenna, era n’amaaso gammwe gafulumye!” Era bw’ayogera bw’atyo, yatambula ng’ayita ku ekyuma ekikuba empewo, wano ne wali ng’akozesa ekigere kye mu ddembe nnyo, ate nga Biludad ataataaganyizibwa n’asigala akulembera n’oluyimba lwe olwa zabbuli. Alowooza nze, Captain Peleg nteekwa okuba nga leero nnina kye nnywa.

Oluvannyuma ennanga yali esimbye, amaato ne gasimbiddwa, era ne tugenda ne tuseeyeeya. Yali Ssekukkulu nnyimpi era ennyogovu; era olunaku olumpi olw’obukiikakkono bwe lwagenda lwegatta ne lufuuka ekiro, twesanga kumpi nga tugazi ku nnyanja eyali efuuse ekyeya, ng’okufuuyira kwayo okutonnya ennyo kwatuteeka mu bbalaafu, nga mu byambalo ebirongooseddwa. Ennyiriri empanvu ez’amannyo ku bigo zaayakaayakana mu musana gw’omwezi; era okufaananako amasanga amazungu ag’enjovu ezimu ennene, ebiwujjo ebinene ennyo ebikoonagana byali byesigamye ku butaasa.

Lank Bildad, ng’omugoba w’ennyonyi, yakulembera essaawa esooka, era buli kiseera, ng’emmeeri enkadde ebbira mu buziba mu nnyanja enzirugavu, n’esindika omuzira ogukankana wonna, era empewo ne ziwoggana, era omuguwa ne guvuga, ennyimba ze ezitakyukakyuka bwe zaali awulira,-

“Ennimiro enwoomu ezisukka amataba agazimba, .
Yimirirako ng’oyambadde engoye za kiragala omulamu.
Bwatyo eri Abayudaaya Kanani omukadde n'ayimirira;
Nga Jordan yeekulukuunya wakati.”

Ebigambo ebyo ebiwooma tebyawulikika nga biwooma okusinga mu kiseera ekyo. Baali bajjudde essuubi n’ebibala. Wadde nga ekiro kino eky’obutiti kyali kibuguma mu Atlantic eyali efuuse enduulu, wadde ng’ebigere byange bibisi n’ekikooti ekibisi, waaliwo, mu kiseera ekyo kyalabika gyendi, ebifo bingi ebisanyusa eby’okuddukiramu; n’ebimera ebiyitibwa meads ne glades ebibeera mu vernal emirembe gyonna, ne kiba nti omuddo ogwakubwa ensulo, nga tegulinnyiddwa, nga teguwoze, gusigala mu makkati g’obutiti.

At last we gained such an offing, nti abavuzi b’ennyonyi bombi baali tebakyakyetaagisa. Eryato erya sail-boat eryali lituwerekeddeko lyatandika okutambula ku mabbali.

Kyaali kya kwewuunya era nga tekisanyusa, engeri Pelegi ne Bildad gye baakosebwamu mu kiseera kino naddala Kapiteeni Bildad. Kubanga mukyawa okugenda, naye; okwetamwa nnyo okuva, olw’obulungi, emmeeri eyali esibiddwa ku lugendo oluwanvu ennyo era olw’akabi —okusukka Capes zombi ez’omuyaga; emmeeri mwe yateekamu enkumi n’enkumi z’ensimbi ze yakola n’amaanyi; emmeeri, nga munne munne omukadde atambulirako nga kapiteeni; omusajja kumpi omukadde nga ye, omulundi omulala ng’atandika okusisinkana entiisa zonna ez’akawanga akatalina kisa; okwetamwa okusiibula ekintu bwe kityo buli ngeri nga kijjudde buli kimu ky’ayagala, — Birudaadi omukadde omwavu yalwawo ebbanga ddene; yatambula ku ddeeke n’emitendera egy’okweraliikirira; yadduka wansi mu kabina okwogera ekigambo ekirala eky’okusiibula eyo; nate n’ajja ku ddeeke, n’atunuulira empewo; yatunudde mu mazzi amagazi era agataliiko nkomerero, nga gakoma ku Ssemazinga z’Ebuvanjuba ezitalabika ewala zokka; yatunula ng’ayolekera ensi; yatunula waggulu; yatunudde ku ddyo ne kkono; yatunula buli wamu era nga tewali we yatunula; era ku nkomerero, ng’asika omuguwa mu makanika ku ppini yaayo, n’akwata Peleg omugumu mu ngeri ey’okukonziba ku mukono, era ng’akutte ettaala waggulu, okumala akaseera katono n’ayimirira ng’atunudde mu maaso ge mu ngeri ey’obuzira, okutuuka n’okugamba nti, “Wadde kiri kityo, mukwano gwange Peleg, nsobola kiyimirize; weewaawo, nsobola.”

Ate ye Peleg yennyini, yasinga kukitwala ng’omufirosoofo; naye olw’obufirosoofo bwe bwonna, waaliwo amaziga agaakaayakana mu liiso lye, ettaala bwe yasemberera ennyo. Era naye, teyadduka katono okuva ku kabina okudda ku ddeeke —kati ekigambo wansi, era kati ekigambo ne Starbuck, munne omukulu.

Naye, ku nkomerero, yakyuka n’atunuulira munne, ng’atunuulira ekika ekisembayo ku ye, —“Kapiteeni Bildad — jangu, munnange omukadde, tulina okugenda. Ddayo mu luggya olukulu awo! Eryato ahoy! Yimirirako okusemberera ku mabbali, kati! Weegendereze, weegendereze! —jjangu, Bildad, mulenzi —gamba ekisembayo. Omukisa eri mmwe, Starbuck — omukisa eri mmwe, Mwami Stubb — omukisa eri mmwe, Mwami Flask — musiibule era mukisa mwenna — era leero emyaka esatu nja kuba n’ekyeggulo ekyokya nga nnywa sigala ku lwammwe mu Nantucket enkadde . Yanguwa ogende!”

“Katonda abawe omukisa, era mubeere mu kukuumibwa kwe okutukuvu, abasajja,” omukadde Birudaadi bwe yeemulugunya, kumpi nga takwatagana. “Nsuubira nti mujja kuba n’obudde obulungi kati, Kapiteeni Akabu asobole okutambula mu mmwe mu bbanga ttono — enjuba ennyuvu ye yokka gy’ayagala, era mujja kuba nazo nnyingi mu lugendo lw’obutiti lwe mugenda. Mwegendereze mu kuyigga, mmwe abafumbo. Temuziyiza maato nga temulina kye mukola, mmwe abavuzi b’ebikondo; olubaawo lw’emivule olweru olulungi lusitulwa nga lujjudde ebitundu bisatu ku buli kikumi. mu mwaka. Naawe tewerabira essaala zo. Mr. Starbuck, mind nti cooper toyonoona miggo gya sipeeya. Oh! empiso za sail-needles ziri mu locker eya kiragala! Temugikuba nnyo whale a' ennaku za Mukama, abasajja; naye naawe tosubwa mukisa gwa bwenkanya, ekyo kwe kugaana ebirabo ebirungi ebya Ggulu. Mubeere n’eriiso ku tierce ya molasses, Mwami Stubb; kyali kikulukuta katono, bwe nnalowooza. Bwe mukwata ku bizinga, Mwami Flask, weegendereze obwenzi. Musiibule, musiibule! Tokuuma kkeeki eyo bbanga ddene nnyo wansi mu kifo ekikuumirwamu, Mwami Starbuck; kijja kwonooneka. Weegendereze butto —sente amakumi abiri ku pawundi gye yali, era mulowooze, singa —”

“Jjangu, jjangu, Kapiteeni Bildad; mulekere awo okukuba enduulu, —ewala!” era bwe yayogera ebyo, Pelegi n’amuyanguwa okuyita ku mabbali, bombi ne bagwa mu lyato.

Emmeeri n’eryato byawukana; empewo ennyogovu era ennyogovu ey’ekiro yafuuwa wakati; ennyonyi ekika kya gull eyali ekuba enduulu yabuuka waggulu; ebisenge ebibiri byayiringisibwa mu ngeri ey’ekigwenyufu; twawa enduulu ssatu ez’omutima omuzito, era nga tuzibe amaaso ne tubbira ng’enkomerero mu Atlantic eyali yokka.





ESSUULA 23. Olubalama lw’ennyanja Lee.

Essuula ezimu emabega, Bulkington omu yayogerwako, omuvubi omuwanvu, eyatuuka ku nnyanja empya, eyasangibwa mu New Bedford mu kiyumba ky’abagenyi.

Bwe yali mu kiro ekyo eky’obutiti ekyali kikankana, Pequod bwe yasuula obutaasa bwe obw’okwesasuza mu mayengo agannyogoga ag’obulabe, ani gwe nnandiraba ng’ayimiridde ku mugongo gwe okuggyako Bulkington! Natunuulira n’okutya okw’okusaasira n’okutya omusajja oyo, mu makkati g’obutiti eyaakatuuka ku lukalu okuva ku lugendo olw’akabi olw’emyaka ena, ng’asobola okuddamu okusimbula mu ngeri etawummudde okumala ekisanja ekirala eky’omuyaga. Ettaka lyalabika ng’eryokya okutuuka ku bigere bye. Ebintu ebisinga okwewuunyisa bye bitayogerwako; ebijjukizo ebizito tebivaamu biwandiiko ebiyitibwa epitaphs; essuula eno eya yinsi mukaaga ye ntaana ya Bulkington etaliiko mayinja. Ka njogere kyokka nti kyatambula naye nga bwe kyali ku mmeeri eyakubwa omuyaga, evuga mu ngeri ey’ennaku ku ttaka eririmu empewo. Omwalo gwandibadde fain okuwa obuyambi; omwalo gusaasira; mu mwalo mulimu obukuumi, obuweerero, ejjinja ly’omuliro, ekyeggulo, emifaliso egy’ebbugumu, emikwano, byonna ebyo eby’ekisa eri abantu baffe abafa. Naye mu muyaga ogwo, omwalo, ettaka, gwe kabi akasinga obubi emmeeri eyo; alina okubuuka ennyonyi yonna ey’okusembeza abagenyi; okukwata ku ttaka omulundi gumu, wadde nga lyali lirya kikolo, kyandimuleetedde okukankana okuyita n’okuyita. N’amaanyi ge gonna ebibiina byonna bigenda ku nnyanja; mu kukola ekyo, alwana ‘n’empewo zennyini fain ze yandimufuuwa okudda eka; anoonya obutaba na ttaka bwonna obw’ennyanja efumbiddwa nate; olw’obuddukiro nga bafubutuka mu kabi; mukwano gwe yekka omulabe we asinga okukaawa!

Omanyi kati, Bulkington? Mulabika nga mulaba obubonero obw’amazima ago agatagumiikiriza; nti okulowooza kwonna okw’obuziba, okunyiikivu, kaweefube yenna ow’obuzira ow’omwoyo okukuuma obwetwaze obw’olubeerera obw’ennyanja yaayo; ate ng’empewo ezisinga okuba ez’omu nsiko ez’eggulu n’ensi zikola olukwe okumusuula ku lubalama lw’ennyanja olw’enkwe, olw’obuddu?

Naye nga mu butabeera na ttaka bwokka bwe mubeera amazima agasinga obukulu, agataliiko lubalama, agataliiko kkomo nga Katonda —bwe kityo, kisingako okuzikirizibwa mu kuwowoggana okwo okutaliiko kkomo, okusinga okufuumuulwa mu ngeri etali ya kitiibwa ku bbugumu, ne bwe kiba nti ekyo kyali kya bukuumi! Kubanga eringa ensowera, olwo, oh! eyandibadde yeegomba okwekulukuunya okutuuka ku lukalu! Entiisa z'ebitiisa! obulumi buno bwonna bwa bwereere bwe butyo? Fuma omutima, kwata omutima, Ayi Bulkington! Kugumiikiriza nnyo, katonda omulala! Waggulu okuva mu kufuuyira kw’ennyanja yo esaanawo — butereevu waggulu, ebuuka apotheosis yo!





ESSUULA 24. Omuwolereza.

Nga nze ne Queequeg kati bwe tutandise bulungi mu bizinensi eno ey’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale; era nga bizinensi eno ey’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale bw’etuuse mu ngeri emu okutwalibwa mu bannansi b’ettaka ng’ekintu ekitali kya bitontome era ekitali kya kitiibwa; n’olwekyo, nneeraliikirivu kwonna okubamatiza mmwe, mmwe bannansi, obutali bwenkanya obukoleddwa wano ku ffe abayizzi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale.

Mu kifo ekisooka, kiyinza okutwalibwa ng’ekitali kya bulijjo okuteekawo ensonga, nti mu bantu okutwalira awamu, bizinensi y’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale tebalibwa ku mutendera n’ebyo ebiyitibwa emirimu egy’eddembe. Singa omuntu gw’atamanyi yayingizibwa mu kibiina kyonna eky’ebibuga ebinene eby’enjawulo, kyandikulembeddemu katono endowooza ey’awamu ku bulungibwansi bwe, singa yayanjulwa eri kkampuni ng’omukubi w’engoye, tugambe; era singa mu kukoppa abaserikale b’amagye g’oku mazzi yandibadde agattako ennukuta ezisooka SWF (Sperm Whale Fishery) ku kaadi ye ey’okukyalira ensiko, enkola ng’eyo yanditwaliddwa ng’esinga okwetwala era ey’okusaaga.

Awatali kubuusabuusa ensonga emu enkulu lwaki ensi egaana okussa ekitiibwa mu ffe abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale, ye eno: balowooza nti, mu kiseera ekisinga, omulimu gwaffe guwera bizinensi y’okusala ennyama; era nti bwe twenyigira ennyo mu kyo, twetooloddwa ebicaafu ebya buli ngeri. Tuli bakanyama, ekyo kituufu. Naye abakanyama, nabo, n’abakanyama ba badge esinga omusaayi bonna babadde ba Martial Commanders ensi b’esanyuka obutakyuka okuwa ekitiibwa. Era ku nsonga y’obutali bulongoofu obugambibwa nti bwa bizinensi yaffe, mujja kutandikibwa mangu mu nsonga ezimu okutuusa kati ezitamanyiddwa nnyo, era nga, okutwalira awamu, zijja kusimba n’obuwanguzi emmeeri ya sperm whale-ship waakiri mu bintu ebisinga obuyonjo eby’obuyonjo buno ensi. Naye n’okukkiriza omusango ogwogerwako okuba omutuufu; ddeeke ki eziseerera ezitategekeddwa bulungi ez’emmeeri ya whale ezigeraageranyizibwa ku carrion etayogerwako ey’ebifo ebyo eby’olutalo abaserikale bangi mwe badda okunywa mu kuwaana kwonna okw’abakyala? Era singa endowooza y’akabi eyongera nnyo ku kwegulumiza kw’abantu abangi mu mulimu gw’omujaasi; ka mbakakase nti bangi omuzirwanako atambudde mu ddembe okutuuka ku bbaatule, yandizze emabega mangu olw’okwolesebwa kw’omukira omunene ogw’ennyanja sperm whale, ng’afuuwa empewo waggulu w’omutwe gwe mu biwujjo. Kubanga ntiisa ki ezitegeerekeka ez’omuntu bw’ogeraageranya n’entiisa n’ebyewuunyo bya Katonda ebikwatagana!

Naye, newankubadde ensi etusikambula ffe abayizzi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale, naye mu butamanya etuwa ekitiibwa ekisinga obunene; weewaawo, okusinza okuyitiridde! kubanga kumpi taper zonna, amataala, n’emimuli egyaka okwetooloola ensi yonna, byokya, nga mu maaso g’amasabo amangi bwe gatyo, olw’ekitiibwa kyaffe!

Naye ensonga eno mutunuulire mu bitangaala ebirala; kipime mu minzaani eza buli ngeri; laba ffe abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale kye tuli, era kye tubadde.

Lwaki Abadaaki mu kiseera kya De Witt baalina ba admirals b’amaato gaabwe agavuba ennyanja ekika kya whale? Lwaki Louis XVI yakola. wa Bufalansa, ku ssente ze ez’obuntu, n’ateekateeka emmeeri ezivuba ennyanja ekika kya whale okuva e Dunkirk, era mu mpisa n’ayita mu kibuga ekyo amaka agamu oba abiri okuva ku kizinga kyaffe ekya Nantucket? Lwaki Bungereza wakati w’emyaka 1750 ne 1788 yasasula abavubi baayo abaali bavuba ennyanja ekika kya whale mu ssente ezisukka mu pawundi 1,000,000? Era ekisembayo, kijja kitya nti ffe abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale mu Amerika kati tusinga abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya bbandi bonna mu nsi yonna; okusaabala eggye ly’oku mazzi eryalimu amaato agasukka mu bikumi musanvu; nga balina abasajja emitwalo kkumi na munaana; buli mwaka okukozesa ddoola 4,000,000; emmeeri ezo zaali zibalirirwamu, mu kiseera we zaatambulira, doola 20,000,000! era buli mwaka nga tuyingiza mu myalo gyaffe amakungula agakungula obulungi aga doola 7,000,000. Bino byonna bijja bitya, bwe kiba nga tewali kintu kizibu mu kuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale?

Naye kino si kye kitundu; ddamu otunule.

Nkakasa mu ddembe, nti omufirosoofo ow’enjawulo tayinza, mu bulamu bwe, kulaga buyinza bumu obw’emirembe, mu myaka nkaaga egiyise obukoze mu ngeri esinga ku nsi yonna engazi, nga butwaliddwa mu mugatte gumu, okusinga bizinensi ey’okuvuba envubu ey’ekika ekya waggulu era ey’amaanyi . Mu ngeri emu n’endala, kizadde ebintu ebyewuunyisa ennyo ku bwabyo, era ebikulu ennyo mu nsonga zaabyo eziddiriŋŋana, ne kiba nti okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale kuyinza bulungi okutwalibwa nga maama oyo Omumisiri, eyazaala abaana bennyini nga bali lubuto okuva mu lubuto lwe. Kyandibadde mulimu ogutalina ssuubi, ogutaggwaawo okuwandiika ebintu bino byonna mu katabo. Leka omukono omutono gumala. Okumala emyaka mingi emmeeri eno eyitibwa whale-ship ebadde ntandikwa mu kuvuga ebitundu by’ensi ebisinga ewala era ebitamanyiddwa nnyo. Abadde anoonyezza ennyanja n’ebizinga ebitaliiko chati, nga tewali Cook oba Vancouver yali atambuddeko. What, perhaps, with other things, made Stubb such an easy-going, unfearing man, so cheerily trudging off with the burden of life in a world full of grave pedlars, all bowed to the ground with their packs; what helped to bring about that almost impious good-humor of his; that thing must have been his pipe. For, like his nose, his short, black little pipe was one of the regular features of his face. You would almost as soon have expected him to turn out of his bunk without his nose as without his pipe. He kept a whole row of pipes there ready loaded, stuck in a rack, within easy reach of his hand; and, whenever he turned in, he smoked them all out in succession, lighting one from the other to the end of the chapter; then loading them again to be in readiness anew. For, when Stubb dressed, instead of first putting his legs into his trowsers, he put his pipe into his mouth.

I say this continual smoking must have been one cause, at least, of his peculiar disposition; for every one knows that this earthly air, whether ashore or afloat, is terribly infected with the nameless miseries of the numberless mortals who have died exhaling it; and as in time of the cholera, some people go about with a camphorated handkerchief to their mouths; so, likewise, against all mortal tribulations, Stubb's tobacco smoke might have operated as a sort of disinfecting agent.

The third mate was Flask, a native of Tisbury, in Martha's Vineyard. A short, stout, ruddy young fellow, very pugnacious concerning whales, who somehow seemed to think that the great leviathans had personally and hereditarily affronted him; and therefore it was a sort of point of honor with him, to destroy them whenever encountered. So utterly lost was he to all sense of reverence for the many marvels of their majestic bulk and mystic ways; and so dead to anything like an apprehension of any possible danger from encountering them; that in his poor opinion, the wondrous whale was but a species of magnified mouse, or at least water-rat, requiring only a little circumvention and some small application of time and trouble in order to kill and boil. This ignorant, unconscious fearlessness of his made him a little waggish in the matter of whales; he followed these fish for the fun of it; and a three years' voyage round Cape Horn was only a jolly joke that lasted that length of time. As a carpenter's nails are divided into wrought nails and cut nails; so mankind may be similarly divided. Little Flask was one of the wrought ones; made to clinch tight and last long. They called him King-Post on board of the Pequod; because, in form, he could be well likened to the short, square timber known by that name in Arctic whalers; and which by the means of many radiating side timbers inserted into it, serves to brace the ship against the icy concussions of those battering seas.

Now these three mates—Starbuck, Stubb, and Flask, were momentous men. They it was who by universal prescription commanded three of the Pequod's boats as headsmen. In that grand order of battle in which Captain Ahab would probably marshal his forces to descend on the whales, these three headsmen were as captains of companies. Or, being armed with their long keen whaling spears, they were as a picked trio of lancers; even as the harpooneers were flingers of javelins.

And since in this famous fishery, each mate or headsman, like a Gothic Knight of old, is always accompanied by his boat-steerer or harpooneer, who in certain conjunctures provides him with a fresh lance, when the former one has been badly twisted, or elbowed in the assault; and moreover, as there generally subsists between the two, a close intimacy and friendliness; it is therefore but meet, that in this place we set down who the Pequod's harpooneers were, and to what headsman each of them belonged.

First of all was Queequeg, whom Starbuck, the chief mate, had selected for his squire. But Queequeg is already known.

Next was Tashtego, an unmixed Indian from Gay Head, the most westerly promontory of Martha's Vineyard, where there still exists the last remnant of a village of red men, which has long supplied the neighboring island of Nantucket with many of her most daring harpooneers. In the fishery, they usually go by the generic name of Gay-Headers. Tashtego's long, lean, sable hair, his high cheek bones, and black rounding eyes—for an Indian, Oriental in their largeness, but Antarctic in their glittering expression—all this sufficiently proclaimed him an inheritor of the unvitiated blood of those proud warrior hunters, who, in quest of the great New England moose, had scoured, bow in hand, the aboriginal forests of the main. But no longer snuffing in the trail of the wild beasts of the woodland, Tashtego now hunted in the wake of the great whales of the sea; the unerring harpoon of the son fitly replacing the infallible arrow of the sires. To look at the tawny brawn of his lithe snaky limbs, you would almost have credited the superstitions of some of the earlier Puritans, and half-believed this wild Indian to be a son of the Prince of the Powers of the Air. Tashtego was Stubb the second mate's squire.

Third among the harpooneers was Daggoo, a gigantic, coal-black negro-savage, with a lion-like tread—an Ahasuerus to behold. Suspended from his ears were two golden hoops, so large that the sailors called them ring-bolts, and would talk of securing the top-sail halyards to them. In his youth Daggoo had voluntarily shipped on board of a whaler, lying in a lonely bay on his native coast. And never having been anywhere in the world but in Africa, Nantucket, and the pagan harbors most frequented by whalemen; and having now led for many years the bold life of the fishery in the ships of owners uncommonly heedful of what manner of men they shipped; Daggoo retained all his barbaric virtues, and erect as a giraffe, moved about the decks in all the pomp of six feet five in his socks. There was a corporeal humility in looking up at him; and a white man standing before him seemed a white flag come to beg truce of a fortress. Curious to tell, this imperial negro, Ahasuerus Daggoo, was the Squire of little Flask, who looked like a chess-man beside him. As for the residue of the Pequod's company, be it said, that at the present day not one in two of the many thousand men before the mast employed in the American whale fishery, are Americans born, though pretty nearly all the officers are. Herein it is the same with the American whale fishery as with the American army and military and merchant navies, and the engineering forces employed in the construction of the American Canals and Railroads. The same, I say, because in all these cases the native American liberally provides the brains, the rest of the world as generously supplying the muscles. No small number of these whaling seamen belong to the Azores, where the outward bound Nantucket whalers frequently touch to augment their crews from the hardy peasants of those rocky shores. In like manner, the Greenland whalers sailing out of Hull or London, put in at the Shetland Islands, to receive the full complement of their crew. Upon the passage homewards, they drop them there again. How it is, there is no telling, but Islanders seem to make the best whalemen. They were nearly all Islanders in the Pequod, Isolatoes too, I call such, not acknowledging the common continent of men, but each Isolato living on a separate continent of his own. Yet now, federated along one keel, what a set these Isolatoes were! An Anacharsis Clootz deputation from all the isles of the sea, and all the ends of the earth, accompanying Old Ahab in the Pequod to lay the world's grievances before that bar from which not very many of them ever come back. Black Little Pip—he never did—oh, no! he went before. Poor Alabama boy! On the grim Pequod's forecastle, ye shall ere long see him, beating his tambourine; prelusive of the eternal time, when sent for, to the great quarter-deck on high, he was bid strike in with angels, and beat his tambourine in glory; called a coward here, hailed a hero there!





CHAPTER 28. Ahab.

For several days after leaving Nantucket, nothing above hatches was seen of Captain Ahab. The mates regularly relieved each other at the watches, and for aught that could be seen to the contrary, they seemed to be the only commanders of the ship; only they sometimes issued from the cabin with orders so sudden and peremptory, that after all it was plain they but commanded vicariously. Yes, their supreme lord and dictator was there, though hitherto unseen by any eyes not permitted to penetrate into the now sacred retreat of the cabin.

Every time I ascended to the deck from my watches below, I instantly gazed aft to mark if any strange face were visible; for my first vague disquietude touching the unknown captain, now in the seclusion of the sea, became almost a perturbation. This was strangely heightened at times by the ragged Elijah's diabolical incoherences uninvitedly recurring to me, with a subtle energy I could not have before conceived of. But poorly could I withstand them, much as in other moods I was almost ready to smile at the solemn whimsicalities of that outlandish prophet of the wharves. But whatever it was of apprehensiveness or uneasiness—to call it so—which I felt, yet whenever I came to look about me in the ship, it seemed against all warrantry to cherish such emotions. For though the harpooneers, with the great body of the crew, were a far more barbaric, heathenish, and motley set than any of the tame merchant-ship companies which my previous experiences had made me acquainted with, still I ascribed this—and rightly ascribed it—to the fierce uniqueness of the very nature of that wild Scandinavian vocation in which I had so abandonedly embarked. But it was especially the aspect of the three chief officers of the ship, the mates, which was most forcibly calculated to allay these colourless misgivings, and induce confidence and cheerfulness in every presentment of the voyage. Three better, more likely sea-officers and men, each in his own different way, could not readily be found, and they were every one of them Americans; a Nantucketer, a Vineyarder, a Cape man. Now, it being Christmas when the ship shot from out her harbor, for a space we had biting Polar weather, though all the time running away from it to the southward; and by every degree and minute of latitude which we sailed, gradually leaving that merciless winter, and all its intolerable weather behind us. It was one of those less lowering, but still grey and gloomy enough mornings of the transition, when with a fair wind the ship was rushing through the water with a vindictive sort of leaping and melancholy rapidity, that as I mounted to the deck at the call of the forenoon watch, so soon as I levelled my glance towards the taffrail, foreboding shivers ran over me. Reality outran apprehension; Captain Ahab stood upon his quarter-deck.

There seemed no sign of common bodily illness about him, nor of the recovery from any. He looked like a man cut away from the stake, when the fire has overrunningly wasted all the limbs without consuming them, or taking away one particle from their compacted aged robustness. His whole high, broad form, seemed made of solid bronze, and shaped in an unalterable mould, like Cellini's cast Perseus. Threading its way out from among his grey hairs, and continuing right down one side of his tawny scorched face and neck, till it disappeared in his clothing, you saw a slender rod-like mark, lividly whitish. It resembled that perpendicular seam sometimes made in the straight, lofty trunk of a great tree, when the upper lightning tearingly darts down it, and without wrenching a single twig, peels and grooves out the bark from top to bottom, ere running off into the soil, leaving the tree still greenly alive, but branded. Whether that mark was born with him, or whether it was the scar left by some desperate wound, no one could certainly say. By some tacit consent, throughout the voyage little or no allusion was made to it, especially by the mates. But once Tashtego's senior, an old Gay-Head Indian among the crew, superstitiously asserted that not till he was full forty years old did Ahab become that way branded, and then it came upon him, not in the fury of any mortal fray, but in an elemental strife at sea. Yet, this wild hint seemed inferentially negatived, by what a grey Manxman insinuated, an old sepulchral man, who, having never before sailed out of Nantucket, had never ere this laid eye upon wild Ahab. Nevertheless, the old sea-traditions, the immemorial credulities, popularly invested this old Manxman with preternatural powers of discernment. So that no white sailor seriously contradicted him when he said that if ever Captain Ahab should be tranquilly laid out—which might hardly come to pass, so he muttered—then, whoever should do that last office for the dead, would find a birth-mark on him from crown to sole.

So powerfully did the whole grim aspect of Ahab affect me, and the livid brand which streaked it, that for the first few moments I hardly noted that not a little of this overbearing grimness was owing to the barbaric white leg upon which he partly stood. It had previously come to me that this ivory leg had at sea been fashioned from the polished bone of the sperm whale's jaw. “Aye, he was dismasted off Japan,” said the old Gay-Head Indian once; “but like his dismasted craft, he shipped another mast without coming home for it. He has a quiver of 'em.”

I was struck with the singular posture he maintained. Upon each side of the Pequod's quarter deck, and pretty close to the mizzen shrouds, there was an auger hole, bored about half an inch or so, into the plank. His bone leg steadied in that hole; one arm elevated, and holding by a shroud; Captain Ahab stood erect, looking straight out beyond the ship's ever-pitching prow. There was an infinity of firmest fortitude, a determinate, unsurrenderable wilfulness, in the fixed and fearless, forward dedication of that glance. Not a word he spoke; nor did his officers say aught to him; though by all their minutest gestures and expressions, they plainly showed the uneasy, if not painful, consciousness of being under a troubled master-eye. And not only that, but moody stricken Ahab stood before them with a crucifixion in his face; in all the nameless regal overbearing dignity of some mighty woe.

Ere long, from his first visit in the air, he withdrew into his cabin. But after that morning, he was every day visible to the crew; either standing in his pivot-hole, or seated upon an ivory stool he had; or heavily walking the deck. As the sky grew less gloomy; indeed, began to grow a little genial, he became still less and less a recluse; as if, when the ship had sailed from home, nothing but the dead wintry bleakness of the sea had then kept him so secluded. And, by and by, it came to pass, that he was almost continually in the air; but, as yet, for all that he said, or perceptibly did, on the at last sunny deck, he seemed as unnecessary there as another mast. But the Pequod was only making a passage now; not regularly cruising; nearly all whaling preparatives needing supervision the mates were fully competent to, so that there was little or nothing, out of himself, to employ or excite Ahab, now; and thus chase away, for that one interval, the clouds that layer upon layer were piled upon his brow, as ever all clouds choose the loftiest peaks to pile themselves upon.

Nevertheless, ere long, the warm, warbling persuasiveness of the pleasant, holiday weather we came to, seemed gradually to charm him from his mood. For, as when the red-cheeked, dancing girls, April and May, trip home to the wintry, misanthropic woods; even the barest, ruggedest, most thunder-cloven old oak will at least send forth some few green sprouts, to welcome such glad-hearted visitants; so Ahab did, in the end, a little respond to the playful allurings of that girlish air. More than once did he put forth the faint blossom of a look, which, in any other man, would have soon flowered out in a smile.
