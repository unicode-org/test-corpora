Stubb bwe yamala okugenda, Akabu n’ayimirira okumala akaseera nga yeesigamye ku bigo; era awo, nga bwe kyali kibadde naye ennaku zino, ng’ayita omuvubi w’essaawa, n’amusindika wansi olw’omusulo gwe ogw’amasanga, era ne payipu ye. Mu biseera by’Abanorse eby’edda, entebe za bakabaka ba Denmark abaali baagala ennyo ennyanja zaali ziyiiya, ennono bw’egamba, ku mannyo g’ennyonyi ekika kya narwhale. Omuntu yanditunuulidde atya Akabu olwo, ng’atudde ku magumba ago ag’essatu, nga tamulowoozezza ku bwakabaka bwe bwali bulaga? Kubanga Kani ow'olubaawo, ne kabaka w'ennyanja, ne mukama omukulu ow'Abaleeviyasani yali Akabu.

ESSUULA 32. Ensengekera y’ebika (Cetology).

Edda twasindikibwa n’obuvumu ku buziba; naye mu bbanga ttono tujja kubula mu bunene bwayo obutali ku mwalo, obutaliiko mwalo. Ere ekyo okutuukirira; ere ekisenge kya Pequod ekirimu omuddo nga kiyiringisibwa ku mabbali n’ebisenge eby’ekika kya leviathan ebiriko barnacled; ku ntandikwa kirungi nnyo okufaayo ku nsonga kumpi ezeetaagisa okutegeera obulungi okusiima okubikkulirwa okw’enjawulo ennyo okwa leviathanic n’okujuliza okw’engeri zonna okulina okugoberera.

It is some systematized exhibition of the whale mu kika kye ekigazi, kye nnandibadde kati fain okuteeka mu maaso gammwe. Naye si mulimu mwangu. Ensengeka y’ebintu ebikola akavuyo, tewali kisingako wano essayed. Wuliriza ab’obuyinza abasinga obulungi n’abasembyeyo bye bataddewo.

“Tewali ttabi lya Zoology lyenyigiddemu nnyo ng’eryo eriyitibwa Cetology,” bw’atyo Captain Scoresby, AD 1820 bw’agamba.

“Si kigendererwa kyange, singa kyali mu buyinza bwange, okuyingira mu kunoonyereza ku nkola entuufu ey’okugabanyaamu ebiwuka ebiyitibwa cetacea mu bibinja n’amaka. * * * Okutabulwa okw’amaanyi kuliwo mu bannabyafaayo b’ensolo eno” (sperm whale), bw’atyo Surgeon Beale, AD 1839 bw’agamba.

“Obutasaana kukola kunoonyereza kwaffe mu mazzi agatategeerekeka.” “Ekibikka ekitayitamu nga kibikka okumanya kwaffe ku biwuka ebiyitibwa cetacea.” “Ennimiro esaasaanidde amaggwa.” “Bino byonna ebiraga nti tebituukiridde naye bikola okututulugunya ffe abakugu mu by’obutonde.”

Bw’otyo yogera ku nnyanja ekika kya whale, Cuvier omukulu, ne John Hunter, ne Lesson, amataala ago ag’eby’ensolo n’ensengekera y’omubiri. Wadde kiri kityo, wadde ng’okumanya okwa nnamaddala kutono, naye ebitabo bingi; era bwe kityo mu ddaala eritali limu, n’eby’obutonde (cetology), oba ssaayansi w’ennyanja ekika kya whale. Bangi be basajja, abato n’abanene, abakadde n’abapya, abakubi b’ettaka n’abavubi, abalina okutwalira awamu oba mu kitono, ebiwandiiko ku nnyanja ekika kya whale. Dduka ku bitonotono: —Abawandiisi ba Baibuli; Aristotle, era nga ye; Pliny, era nga ye; Aldrovandi ne banne; Sir Thomas Browne, omuwandiisi w’ebitabo; Gesner, ne banne; Ray; Linnæus, era nga ye; Rondeletius nga ye; Willoughby, ne banne; Kiragala; Artedi, ne banne; Sibbald, ne banne; Brisson, ne banne; Marten, ne banne; Lacépède ow’ekika kya Lacépède; Bonneterre, ne banne; Desmarest, omusajja omulala; Baron Cuvier ne banne; Frederick Cuvier ne munne; John Hunter, omuyimbi; Owen, ne banne; Scoresby nga; Beale; Bennett, ne banne; J. Ross Browne, omuwandiisi w’ebitabo; Omuwandiisi w’ekitabo Miriam Coffin; Olmstead, ne banne; ne Rev. T. Cheever. Naye ku kigendererwa ekisembayo eky’okugatta ebintu bino byonna bye bawandiise, ebitundu ebijuliziddwa waggulu bijja kulaga.

Ku mannya agali ku lukalala luno olw’abawandiisi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale, abo bokka abagoberera Owen be baalaba ennyanja ekika kya whale enamu; era naye omu ku bo yali muvuzi wa harpooneer ow’ekikugu ddala era nga muvuzi wa whale. Ntegeeza Kapiteeni Scoresby. Ku nsonga ey’enjawulo eya Greenland oba right-whale, ye mukulu asinga obulungi aliwo. Naye Scoresby yali talina ky’amanyi era talina ky’ayogera ku nnyonyi ennene ey’ensigo, bw’ogigeraageranya n’ennyanja Greenland whale kumpi tekisaanira kwogerwako. Era wano kigambibwa, nti Greenland whale muwambi ku ntebe y’ennyanja. Mu ngeri yonna si y’asinga obunene mu nnyanja ekika kya whale. Naye, olw’okukulembeza okumala ebbanga eddene kw’ebyo bye yayogera, n’obutamanya obw’amaanyi obwali, okutuusa emyaka nga nsanvu emabega, obwateekamu ennyama y’ensigo eyali emanyiddwa ennyo oba eyali temanyiddwa ddala mu kiseera ekyo, era obutamanya obwo n’okutuusa leero bukyafuga mu bifo byonna eby’okudda emabega ebya ssaayansi okuggyako ebitonotono n’emyalo gy’ennyanja ekika kya whale; okuwamba kuno kubadde kujjuvu mu buli ngeri. Okwogera kumpi ku bigambo byonna ebya leviathanic allusions mu bayimbi abakulu ab’ennaku eziyise, kijja kukumatiza nti Greenland whale, nga terina muvuganya omu, gye bali kabaka w’ennyanja. Naye ku nkomerero ekiseera kituuse eky’okulangirira okupya. Ono ye Charing Cross; muwulire mmwe! abantu abalungi bonna, —ennyanja Greenland whale egobeddwa mu ntebe, —kati sperm whale ennene efugira!

Waliwo ebitabo bibiri byokka mu kubeerawo nga n’akatono byefuula ebiteeka sperm whale enamu mu maaso go, ate mu kiseera kye kimu, mu ddaala erisinga ewala ne bituuka ku buwanguzi mu kugezaako. Ebitabo ebyo bya Beale ne Bennett; bombi mu kiseera kyabwe baali abalongoosa ku mmeeri z’e Bungereza eza South-Sea whale-ships, era bombi abasajja abatuufu era abeesigika. Ekintu ekisooka ekikwata ku sperm whale okusangibwa mu voliyumu zaabwe kiteekwa okuba nga kitono; naye okutuuka we kigenda, kya mutindo mulungi nnyo, wadde ng’okusinga kikoma ku kunnyonnyola kwa ssaayansi. Kyokka, n’okutuusa kati, sperm whale, eya ssaayansi oba ey’ebitontome, ewangaala nga tetuukiridde mu bitabo byonna. Okusinga wala ennyanja ekika kya whale endala zonna eziyiggibwa, eyiye bulamu obutawandiikiddwa.

Kati ebika by’ennyanja ekika kya whale eby’enjawulo byetaaga ekika ky’okusengeka okujjuvu okumanyiddwa ennyo, bwe kiba nga kyangu okulambika mu kiseera kino, oluvannyuma okujjibwamu mu bitongole byayo byonna abakozi abaddako. Nga tewali musajja asinga agenda mu maaso kutwala nsonga eno mu ngalo, wano nwaayo kaweefube wange omubi. Sisuubiza kintu kyonna kijjuvu; kubanga ekintu kyonna eky’obuntu ekiteeberezebwa okuba ekijjuvu, olw’ensonga eyo yennyini kiteekwa okuba nga kikyamu awatali nsobi. Sijja kwefuula nga nnina ennyonyola ya ddakiika ey’omubiri ku bika eby’enjawulo, oba —mu kifo kino waakiri —ku nnyonyola yonna. Ekigendererwa kyange wano kwe kukola pulojekiti yokka ebbago ly’ensengeka y’eby’obutonde (systematization of cetology). Nze mukubi wa pulaani, so si muzimbi.

Naye mulimu munene nnyo; tewali muntu wa bulijjo asunsula bbaluwa mu Post-Office yenkana nayo. Okukomba wansi wansi mu nnyanja nga bagoberera; okubeera n’emikono gy’omuntu wakati mu misingi, embiriizi, n’ekisambi kyennyini eby’ensi ebitayogerwa; kino kintu kya ntiisa. Nze kiki nti nsaanidde essay okusiba ennyindo ya leviathan eno! Okujooga okw’entiisa okwali mu Yobu kuyinza okunkuba encukwe. Anaakola endagaano naawe (omuleviyatani? Laba essuubi lye lya bwereere! Naye nwuze nga mpita mu materekero g’ebitabo era n’amaato nga mpita mu nnyanja; Nfunye akakwate ku nnyanja ekika kya whale ezirina emikono gino egirabika; Ndi mu bujjuvu; era nja kugezaako. Waliwo ebimu ebisookerwako okusenga.

Ekisooka: Embeera etali nkakasa, etali nnywevu eya ssaayansi ono ow’Eby’Ensi (Cetology) eri mu kisenge kyennyini ekikakasibwa ensonga, nti mu bitundu ebimu kikyali nsonga ya kukubaganya birowoozo oba ennyanja ekika kya whale eba kyennyanja. Mu kitabo kye ekiyitibwa System of Nature, AD 1776, Linnæus alangirira nti, “Njawula ennyanja ekika kya whale ku byennyanja.” Naye okusinziira ku kye mmanyi, nkimanyi nti okutuukira ddala mu mwaka gwa 1850, shark ne shad, alewives ne herring, nga zimenya ekiragiro kya Linnæus ekitegeerekeka, zaasangibwa nga zigabanya obuyinza bw’ennyanja ze zimu ne Leviathan.

Ensonga Linnæus kwe yandifudde yandigobye ennyanja ekika kya whale mu mazzi, agamba bw’ati: “Olw’omutima gwazo ogw’obugumu obw’amaaso abiri, amawuggwe gazo, ebikowe byazo ebitambula, amatu gazo agalimu ebituli, penem intrantem feminam mammis lactantem,” n’okusembayo, . “ex lege obutonde bw’ensi mu mateeka agayitibwa meritoque.” Bino byonna nabiwaayo eri mikwano gyange Simeon Macey ne Charley Coffin, ab’e Nantucket, bombi baali ba messmate zange mu lugendo olumu, era ne beegatta mu ndowooza nti ensonga ezaateekebwawo zonna zaali tezimala. Charley mu ngeri ey’obugwenyufu yalaga nti baali ba humbug.

Kimanyiddwa nti, nga nvaako okuyomba kwonna, nkwata ettaka eddungi ery’omulembe nti ennyanja ekika kya whale, ne mpita Yona omutukuvu okumpagira. Ekintu kino ekikulu kyatereera, ensonga eddako eri nti, mu ngeri ki ey’omunda ennyanja ekika kya whale gye yawukana ku byennyanja ebirala. Waggulu, Linnæus akuwadde ebintu ebyo. Naye mu bufunze, ze zino: amawuggwe n’omusaayi ogubuguma; so nga, ebyennyanja ebirala byonna tebirina mawuggwe era nga birimu omusaayi omunnyogovu.

Ekiddako: tunaannyonnyola tutya ennyanja ekika kya whale, nga tuyita mu bintu bye eby’ebweru ebyeyoleka, bwe tutyo mu ngeri eyeeyolese okugiwandiikako akabonero ak’ebiseera byonna ebijja? N’olwekyo, okubeera omumpi, ennyanja ekika kya whale kye kyennyanja ekifulumya amazzi nga kirina omukira ogw’okwebungulula . Eyo gy’olina. Wadde nga ekoleddwa, ennyonyola eyo eva mu kufumiitiriza okugaziyiziddwa. Walrus efuuwa nnyo ng’ennyanja ekika kya whale, naye walrus si kyenyanja, kubanga ebeera mu mazzi n’omu ttaka. Naye ekigambo ekisembayo mu nnyonyola kikyali kikakafu nnyo, nga bwe kigattibwako n’ekisooka. Kumpi omuntu yenna ateekwa okuba nga yakitegedde nti ebyennyanja byonna abakugu mu by’ettaka bye bamanyi tebirina mukira gwa fulaati, wabula omukira ogwesimbye, oba ogugenda waggulu ne wansi. So nga mu byennyanja ebifuuwa omukira, wadde nga guyinza okuba n’enkula y’emu, bulijjo gutwala ekifo ekiwanvu.

Olw’ennyonnyola eyo waggulu ku kiki ennyanja ekika kya whale, siggyako n’akatono mu sseruganda wa leviathanic ekitonde kyonna eky’omu nnyanja okutuusa kati ekimanyiddwa n’ennyanja whale aba Nantucketers abasinga okumanya; wadde, ku ludda olulala, okuyunga nayo ebyennyanja byonna okutuusa kati ebitunuulirwa mu buyinza ng’eby’amawanga amalala.* N’olwekyo, ebyennyanja byonna ebitono, ebifuuwa, era ebirina omukira ogw’okwebungulula birina okuteekebwa mu pulaani eno ey’oku ttaka ey’Eby’Ensi. Kati, n’olwekyo, wajja enjawulo ennene ez’ekifo kyonna ekikyaza ennyanja ekika kya whale.

*Nkimanyi nti okutuukira ddala mu kiseera kino, ebyennyanja ebiyitibwa Lamatins ne Dugongs (Pig-fish and Sow-fish of the Coffins of Nantucket) biyingizibwamu abakugu mu by’obutonde bangi mu nnyanja ekika kya whale. Naye engeri ebyennyanja bino eby’embizzi gye biri seti ezikola amaloboozi, ezinyoomebwa, ng’okusinga zikweka mu mimwa gy’emigga, era nga zirya omuddo omubisi, n’okusingira ddala nga tezifuuwa mafuta, nneegaana ebiwandiiko byabwe nga ennyanja ekika kya whale; era nga babawadde paasipooti zaabwe okuva mu Bwakabaka bwa Cetology.

EKITABO I. ( Folio ), ESSUULA I. ( Sperm Whale ).—Whale eno, mu Lungereza olw’edda emanyiddwa mu ngeri etategeerekeka nga Trumpa whale, ne Physeter whale, ne Anvil Headed whale, ye Cachalot y’Abafaransa mu kiseera kino, ne Pottsfich y’Abagirimaani, ne Macrocephalus y’Ebigambo Ebiwanvu. Awatali kubuusabuusa, ye muntu asinga obunene mu nsi yonna; ekisinga okutiisa mu nnyanja ekika kya whale zonna okusisinkana; ekisinga obukulu mu nsonga; era ekisembayo, ewala nnyo ekisinga okuba eky’omuwendo mu by’obusuubuzi; ye nga ye kitonde kyokka ekintu ekyo eky’omuwendo, spermaceti, mwe kifunibwa. Ebintu bye byonna eby’enjawulo, mu bifo ebirala bingi, bijja kugaziwa. Okusinga ku linnya lye kati kye nnina okukola. Mu by’ennimi, tekiba kya busirusiru. Ebyasa ebimu emabega, nga Sperm whale kumpi temanyiddwa ddala mu ngeri yaayo entuufu, era ng’amafuta ge gafunibwa mu butanwa okuva mu byennyanja ebyasibirwa; mu biseera ebyo, kyandirabise ng’ensigo (spermaceti) abantu baali bateekwa okuba nga zisibuka mu kitonde ekifaanagana n’ekyo ekyali kimanyiddwa mu Bungereza mu kiseera ekyo nga Greenland oba Right Whale. Yali kirowoozo era, nti spermaceti eno yennyini ye yali enseko eyo eyanguya eya Greenland Whale ennukuta esooka ey’ekigambo ky’eyogera butereevu. Mu biseera ebyo, era, spermaceti yali ntono nnyo, nga tekozesebwa ku kitangaala, wabula ng’ekizigo n’eddagala lyokka. Yali ya kubeera na ddagala lyokka nga ensangi zino ogula ounce ya rhubarb. Nga bwe ndowooza, mu bbanga, obutonde obw’amazima obwa spermaceti bwe bwamanyibwa, erinnya lyayo eryasooka lyali likyakuumibwa abasuubuzi; awatali kubuusabuusa okwongera ku muwendo gwayo olw’endowooza ey’amakulu ennyo mu ngeri eyeewuunyisa olw’ebbula lyayo. Era bwe kityo erinnya eryo ku nkomerero liteekwa okuba nga lyatuuka okuweebwa ennyanja ekika kya whale mwe yava ddala spermaceti eno.

EKITABO I. ( Folio ), ESSUULA II. ( Right Whale ).—Mu ngeri emu eno y’esinga okuweebwa ekitiibwa mu leviathans, nga ye yasooka okuyigganyizibwa buli kiseera abantu. Kivaamu ekintu ekimanyiddwa ennyo nga whalebone oba baleen; n’amafuta agamanyiddwa mu ngeri ey’enjawulo nga “amafuta g’ennyanja ekika kya whale,” ekintu eky’omutindo ogwa wansi mu by’obusuubuzi. Mu bavubi, ayitibwa awatali kusosola n’ebitiibwa bino byonna: The Whale; ennyonyi ekika kya Greenland Whale; ennyonyi ekika kya Black Whale; ennyonyi ekika kya Great Whale; ennyonyi ekika kya Whale Entuufu; ennyonyi ya Right Whale. Waliwo okuzibuwalirwa okunene ku bikwata ku bika by’ebisolo ebibatizibwa bwe bityo mu bungi. Kati olwo ennyanja ekika kya whale kye ki, kye nteka mu kika eky’okubiri ekya Folios zange? Ye Mysticetus Omukulu ow’abakugu mu by’obutonde Abazungu; ennyonyi ekika kya Greenland Whale ey’abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale Abangereza; Baleine Ordinaire ow’abavubi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale mu Bufalansa; envubu ya Growlands Walfish ey’Abasweden. Ye whale okumala ebyasa ebisukka mu bibiri emabega ebadde eyiggibwa Abadaaki n’Abangereza mu nnyanja za Arctic; ye whale abavubi b’Abamerika gye baludde nga bagoberera mu nnyanja Buyindi, ku Brazil Banks, ku Nor’ West Coast, n’ebitundu ebirala eby’enjawulo eby’ensi, bye baalonda Right Whale Cruising Grounds.

Abamu beefuula abalaba enjawulo wakati w’ennyanja Greenland whale ey’Abangereza n’ey’Abamerika eya ddyo. Naye bakkiriziganya ddala mu bintu byabwe byonna ebikulu; era tewannaba kwanjulwa nsonga emu enkakafu kwe tuyinza okusinziirako enjawulo ey’amaanyi. Okuyita mu kugabanyaamu okutaggwaawo nga kwesigamiziddwa ku njawulo ezisinga obutakoma, ebitongole ebimu eby’ebyafaayo by’obutonde mwe bifuuka ebizibu ennyo mu ngeri egoba. Whale eya ddyo ejja kujjanjabwa awalala mu buwanvu obumu, nga twogera ku kunnyonnyola sperm whale.

EKITABO I. ( Folio ), ESSUULA III. ( Fin-Back ).—Wansi w’omutwe guno mbalirira ekisolo ekinene ennyo, nga kiyitibwa amannya ag’enjawulo aga Fin-Back, Tall-Spout, ne Long-John, ekibadde kirabibwa kumpi mu buli nnyanja era nga kitera okuba ennyanja ekika kya whale ng’ennyonyi yaayo ey’ewala eri bwe kityo kitera okunnyonnyolwa abasaabaze abasala Atlantic, mu New York packet-tracks. Mu buwanvu bw’atuukako, ne mu baleen ye, Fin-back eringa ennyanja ekika kya right whale, naye nga ya less portly girth, ate nga ya langi enzirugavu, esemberera omuzeyituuni. Emimwa gye eminene giraga ekifaananyi ekiringa waya, ekikolebwa ebizimba ebiwanvu ebiserengese eby’enviiri ennene. Ekintu ekinene eky’enjawulo kye, ekiwaawaatiro, mw’aggya erinnya lye, kitera okuba ekintu ekyeyoleka. Ekiwaawaatiro kino kiwanvu ffuuti nga ssatu oba nnya, nga kikula mu vertikal okuva mu kitundu eky’emabega eky’omugongo, nga kya ngeri ya nkoona, era nga kiriko enkomerero ensongovu ennyo. Ne bwe kiba nti ekitundu ekirala ekitono eky’ekitonde ekyo tekirabika, ekiwaawaatiro kino ekyetongodde, oluusi, kijja kulabibwa nga kifuluma bulungi okuva kungulu. Ennyanja bw’eba ekkakkamu mu kigero, era ng’eriko akabonero katono n’amayengo ag’enkulungo, era ng’ekiwaawaatiro kino ekiringa gnomon kiyimirira ne kisuula ebisiikirize ku ngulu eriko ennyiriri, kiyinza bulungi okulowoozebwa nti enzirugavu ey’amazzi egyetoolodde eringa ekintu ekifaanana dial, n’omusono gwayo era layini z’essaawa eziriko amayengo eziyooleddwa ku yo. Ku Akazi-dial eyo ekisiikirize kitera okudda emabega. Fin-Back si ya mukwano. Alabika nga mukyayi wa whale, anti abasajja abamu bwe bakyawa abantu. Ensonyi nnyo; bulijjo okugenda nga bali bokka; okulinnya ku mazzi mu ngeri etasuubirwa mu mazzi agasinga ewala era agasinga okubeera amazibu; ennyonyi ye egolokofu era emu empanvu ng’esituka ng’effumu eddene erya misanthropic ku lusenyi olutaliiko bimera; ekirabo ky’amaanyi ag’ekitalo n’embiro mu kuwuga, okusomooza byonna ebiriwo kati okuva eri omuntu; leviathan ono alabika nga Kayini eyagobebwa era atawangulwa ow’ekika kye, ng’alina akabonero ke sitayiro eyo ku mugongo gwe. Okuva ku kuba ne baleen mu kamwa ke, Fin-Back oluusi eyingizibwa ne whale eya ddyo, mu kika eky’enzikiriziganya ekiyitibwa Whalebone whales , kwe kugamba, whale ezirina baleen. Ku nnyanja zino eziyitibwa Whalebone whale, kyandirabise nga waliwo ebika ebiwerako, naye ebisinga obungi ku byo tebimanyiddwa nnyo. Ennyonyi ekika kya whale ez’ennyindo empanvu n’ennyanja ekika kya beaked whales; ennyanja ekika kya pike-headed whale; ennyanja ekika kya whale ezikubiddwa mu bibinja; under-jawed whales ne rostrated whales, ge mannya g’abavubi abalina ebika ebitonotono.

Mu bikwatagana n’ekigambo kino eky’okuyita “Whalebone whales,” kikulu nnyo okwogera, nti wadde ng’okutuuma amannya ng’okwo kuyinza okuba okwangu mu kwanguyiza okwogera ku kika ky’ennyanja ekika kya whale, naye nga bwereere okugezaako okugabanya obulungi Leviathan, . yasimbibwa ku baleen ye, oba ku hump, oba fin, oba amannyo ge; wadde ng’ebitundu ebyo ebiteekeddwako akabonero oba ebifaananyi byeyoleka bulungi nnyo birabika nga bituukiridde bulungi okusobola okusasula omusingi gw’enkola eya bulijjo ey’Eby’Ensi okusinga enjawulo endala zonna ez’omubiri ezitali zimu, envubu, mu bika byayo, z’eyanjula. Kati olwo otya? Ensigo za baleen, hump, back-fin, n’amannyo; bino bye bintu eby’enjawulo byabyo ebibunye awatali kusosola mu bika byonna eby’ennyanja ekika kya whale, awatali kulowooza kwonna ku kiki ekiyinza okuba obutonde bw’ensengekera yazo mu bintu ebirala era ebikulu ennyo. Bwe kityo, sperm whale ne humpbacked whale, buli emu erina ekiwujjo; naye eyo okufaanagana we kukoma. Olwo, eno y’emu ekika kya humpbacked whale ne Greenland whale, buli emu ku zino erina baleen; naye eyo nate okufaanagana kukoma. Era kye kimu n’ebitundu ebirala ebyogeddwako waggulu. Mu bika by’ennyanja ekika kya whale ez’enjawulo, zikola okugatta ng’okwo okutali kwa bulijjo; oba, mu mbeera y’omu ku bo eyeekutudde, okweyawula okutali kwa bulijjo ng’okwo; nga okusoomooza ddala enkola zonna ez’awamu ezikoleddwa ku musingi ng’ogwo. Ku lwazi luno buli omu ku ba whale-naturalists yayawukanye.

Naye kiyinza okulowoozebwa nti, mu bitundu eby’omunda eby’ennyanja ekika kya whale, mu nsengeka ye ey’omubiri —ewo, waakiri, tujja kusobola okukuba ensengeka entuufu. Nedda; okugeza, kintu ki ekiri mu nsengeka y’ennyanja Greenland whale ekisinga okukwata ennyo okusinga baleen ye? Naye tulabye nti okusinziira ku baleen ye tekisoboka kugabanya bulungi nnyonyi ya Greenland whale. Era bw’okka mu byenda bya leviathans ez’enjawulo, lwaki eyo tojja kusanga njawulo kitundu kya makumi ataano nga bwe kiri eri omutegesi ng’ebyo eby’ebweru ebyabalibwa edda. Olwo kiki ekisigaddewo? tewali kirala wabula okukwata ennyanja ekika kya whale mu mubiri, mu voliyumu yazo yonna ey’eddembe, n’okuzisunsulamu n’obuvumu bwe zityo. Era eno y’enkola ya Bibliographical wano ekwatibwa; era ye yokka eyinza okutuuka ku buwanguzi, kubanga yokka y’esobola okukolebwa. Okugenda mu maaso.

EKITABO I. ( Folio ), ESSUULA V. ( Razor Back ).—Ku nnyanja eno kitono ekimanyiddwa okuggyako erinnya lye. Nze mmulabye ng’ali wala nnyo okuva e Cape Horn. Olw’engeri y’okuwummula, yeewala abayizzi n’abafirosoofo. Wadde nga talina mukodo, tannaba kulaga kitundu kye okuggyako omugongo gwe, ogusituka mu luwonko oluwanvu olusongovu. Muleke agende. Nze mmanyi kitono nnyo ku ye, era n’omuntu omulala yenna tamanyi.

( Sulphur Bottom ).—Omusajja omulala eyali awummudde, ng’alina olubuto olw’ekibiriiti, awatali kubuusabuusa yafuna ng’asenya ku tile za Tartarian mu bimu ku bifo bye eby’okubbira mu mazzi ebiwanvu. Tatera kulabibwa; waakiri simulabangako okuggyako mu nnyanja ez’ewala ez’obugwanjuba, ate olwo bulijjo mu bbanga eddene ennyo okusoma ffeesi ye. Tagoberwa n’akatono; yaddukanga n’emiguwa-okutambula kwa layini. Prodigies are told of him. Adieu, Sulphur Bottom! I can say nothing more that is true of ye, nor can the oldest Nantucketer.

OCTAVOES.*—These embrace the whales of middling magnitude, among which present may be numbered:—I., the Grampus ; II., the Black Fish ; III., the Narwhale ; IV., the Thrasher ; V., the Killer .

*Why this book of whales is not denominated the Quarto is very plain. Because, while the whales of this order, though smaller than those of the former order, nevertheless retain a proportionate likeness to them in figure, yet the bookbinder's Quarto volume in its dimensioned form does not preserve the shape of the Folio volume, but the Octavo volume does.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER I. ( Grampus ).—Though this fish, whose loud sonorous breathing, or rather blowing, has furnished a proverb to landsmen, is so well known a denizen of the deep, yet is he not popularly classed among whales. But possessing all the grand distinctive features of the leviathan, most naturalists have recognised him for one. He is of moderate octavo size, varying from fifteen to twenty-five feet in length, and of corresponding dimensions round the waist. He swims in herds; he is never regularly hunted, though his oil is considerable in quantity, and pretty good for light. By some fishermen his approach is regarded as premonitory of the advance of the great sperm whale.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER II. ( Black Fish ).—I give the popular fishermen's names for all these fish, for generally they are the best. Where any name happens to be vague or inexpressive, I shall say so, and suggest another. I do so now, touching the Black Fish, so-called, because blackness is the rule among almost all whales. So, call him the Hyena Whale, if you please. His voracity is well known, and from the circumstance that the inner angles of his lips are curved upwards, he carries an everlasting Mephistophelean grin on his face. This whale averages some sixteen or eighteen feet in length. He is found in almost all latitudes. He has a peculiar way of showing his dorsal hooked fin in swimming, which looks something like a Roman nose. When not more profitably employed, the sperm whale hunters sometimes capture the Hyena whale, to keep up the supply of cheap oil for domestic employment—as some frugal housekeepers, in the absence of company, and quite alone by themselves, burn unsavory tallow instead of odorous wax. Though their blubber is very thin, some of these whales will yield you upwards of thirty gallons of oil.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER III. ( Narwhale ), that is, Nostril whale .—Another instance of a curiously named whale, so named I suppose from his peculiar horn being originally mistaken for a peaked nose. The creature is some sixteen feet in length, while its horn averages five feet, though some exceed ten, and even attain to fifteen feet. Strictly speaking, this horn is but a lengthened tusk, growing out from the jaw in a line a little depressed from the horizontal. But it is only found on the sinister side, which has an ill effect, giving its owner something analogous to the aspect of a clumsy left-handed man. What precise purpose this ivory horn or lance answers, it would be hard to say. It does not seem to be used like the blade of the sword-fish and bill-fish; though some sailors tell me that the Narwhale employs it for a rake in turning over the bottom of the sea for food. Charley Coffin said it was used for an ice-piercer; for the Narwhale, rising to the surface of the Polar Sea, and finding it sheeted with ice, thrusts his horn up, and so breaks through. But you cannot prove either of these surmises to be correct. My own opinion is, that however this one-sided horn may really be used by the Narwhale—however that may be—it would certainly be very convenient to him for a folder in reading pamphlets. The Narwhale I have heard called the Tusked whale, the Horned whale, and the Unicorn whale. He is certainly a curious example of the Unicornism to be found in almost every kingdom of animated nature. From certain cloistered old authors I have gathered that this same sea-unicorn's horn was in ancient days regarded as the great antidote against poison, and as such, preparations of it brought immense prices. It was also distilled to a volatile salts for fainting ladies, the same way that the horns of the male deer are manufactured into hartshorn. Originally it was in itself accounted an object of great curiosity. Black Letter tells me that Sir Martin Frobisher on his return from that voyage, when Queen Bess did gallantly wave her jewelled hand to him from a window of Greenwich Palace, as his bold ship sailed down the Thames; “when Sir Martin returned from that voyage,” saith Black Letter, “on bended knees he presented to her highness a prodigious long horn of the Narwhale, which for a long period after hung in the castle at Windsor.” An Irish author avers that the Earl of Leicester, on bended knees, did likewise present to her highness another horn, pertaining to a land beast of the unicorn nature.

The Narwhale has a very picturesque, leopard-like look, being of a milk-white ground colour, dotted with round and oblong spots of black. His oil is very superior, clear and fine; but there is little of it, and he is seldom hunted. He is mostly found in the circumpolar seas.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER IV. ( Killer ).—Of this whale little is precisely known to the Nantucketer, and nothing at all to the professed naturalist. From what I have seen of him at a distance, I should say that he was about the bigness of a grampus. He is very savage—a sort of Feegee fish. He sometimes takes the great Folio whales by the lip, and hangs there like a leech, till the mighty brute is worried to death. The Killer is never hunted. I never heard what sort of oil he has. Exception might be taken to the name bestowed upon this whale, on the ground of its indistinctness. For we are all killers, on land and on sea; Bonapartes and Sharks included.

BOOK II. ( Octavo ), CHAPTER V. ( Thrasher ).—This gentleman is famous for his tail, which he uses for a ferule in thrashing his foes. He mounts the Folio whale's back, and as he swims, he works his passage by flogging him; as some schoolmasters get along in the world by a similar process. Still less is known of the Thrasher than of the Killer. Both are outlaws, even in the lawless seas.

Thus ends BOOK II. ( Octavo ), and begins BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ).

DUODECIMOES.—These include the smaller whales. I. The Huzza Porpoise. II. The Algerine Porpoise. III. The Mealy-mouthed Porpoise.

To those who have not chanced specially to study the subject, it may possibly seem strange, that fishes not commonly exceeding four or five feet should be marshalled among WHALES—a word, which, in the popular sense, always conveys an idea of hugeness. But the creatures set down above as Duodecimoes are infallibly whales, by the terms of my definition of what a whale is— ie a spouting fish, with a horizontal tail.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER 1. ( Huzza Porpoise ).—This is the common porpoise found almost all over the globe. The name is of my own bestowal; for there are more than one sort of porpoises, and something must be done to distinguish them. I call him thus, because he always swims in hilarious shoals, which upon the broad sea keep tossing themselves to heaven like caps in a Fourth-of-July crowd. Their appearance is generally hailed with delight by the mariner. Full of fine spirits, they invariably come from the breezy billows to windward. They are the lads that always live before the wind. They are accounted a lucky omen. If you yourself can withstand three cheers at beholding these vivacious fish, then heaven help ye; the spirit of godly gamesomeness is not in ye. A well-fed, plump Huzza Porpoise will yield you one good gallon of good oil. But the fine and delicate fluid extracted from his jaws is exceedingly valuable. It is in request among jewellers and watchmakers. Sailors put it on their hones. Porpoise meat is good eating, you know. It may never have occurred to you that a porpoise spouts. Indeed, his spout is so small that it is not very readily discernible. But the next time you have a chance, watch him; and you will then see the great Sperm whale himself in miniature.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER II. ( Algerine Porpoise ).—A pirate. Very savage. He is only found, I think, in the Pacific. He is somewhat larger than the Huzza Porpoise, but much of the same general make. Provoke him, and he will buckle to a shark. I have lowered for him many times, but never yet saw him captured.

BOOK III. ( Duodecimo ), CHAPTER III. ( Mealy-mouthed Porpoise ).—The largest kind of Porpoise; and only found in the Pacific, so far as it is known. The only English name, by which he has hitherto been designated, is that of the fishers—Right-Whale Porpoise, from the circumstance that he is chiefly found in the vicinity of that Folio. In shape, he differs in some degree from the Huzza Porpoise, being of a less rotund and jolly girth; indeed, he is of quite a neat and gentleman-like figure. He has no fins on his back (most other porpoises have), he has a lovely tail, and sentimental Indian eyes of a hazel hue. But his mealy-mouth spoils all. Though his entire back down to his side fins is of a deep sable, yet a boundary line, distinct as the mark in a ship's hull, called the “bright waist,” that line streaks him from stem to stern, with two separate colours, black above and white below. The white comprises part of his head, and the whole of his mouth, which makes him look as if he had just escaped from a felonious visit to a meal-bag. A most mean and mealy aspect! His oil is much like that of the common porpoise.

Beyond the DUODECIMO, this system does not proceed, inasmuch as the Porpoise is the smallest of the whales. Above, you have all the Leviathans of note. But there are a rabble of uncertain, fugitive, half-fabulous whales, which, as an American whaleman, I know by reputation, but not personally. I shall enumerate them by their fore-castle appellations; for possibly such a list may be valuable to future investigators, who may complete what I have here but begun. If any of the following whales, shall hereafter be caught and marked, then he can readily be incorporated into this System, according to his Folio, Octavo, or Duodecimo magnitude:—The Bottle-Nose Whale; the Junk Whale; the Pudding-Headed Whale; the Cape Whale; the Leading Whale; the Cannon Whale; the Scragg Whale; the Coppered Whale; the Elephant Whale; the Iceberg Whale; the Quog Whale; the Blue Whale; etc. From Icelandic, Dutch, and old English authorities, there might be quoted other lists of uncertain whales, blessed with all manner of uncouth names. But I omit them as altogether obsolete; and can hardly help suspecting them for mere sounds, full of Leviathanism, but signifying nothing.

Finally: It was stated at the outset, that this system would not be here, and at once, perfected. You cannot but plainly see that I have kept my word. But I now leave my cetological System standing thus unfinished, even as the great Cathedral of Cologne was left, with the crane still standing upon the top of the uncompleted tower. For small erections may be finished by their first architects; grand ones, true ones, ever leave the copestone to posterity. God keep me from ever completing anything. This whole book is but a draught—nay, but the draught of a draught. Oh, Time, Strength, Cash, and Patience!
