ESSUULA 81. Pequod Asisinkana Bikira Maria.

Olunaku olwali lutegekeddwa lwatuuka, era mu ngeri entuufu twasisinkana emmeeri Jungfrau, Derick De Deer, master, ow’e Bremen.

Mu kiseera ekimu abantu abasinga okuvuba envubu mu nsi yonna, Abadaaki n’Abagirimaani kati be bamu ku basinga obutono; naye wano ne wali mu bbanga erigazi ennyo erya latitude ne longitude, oluusi n’oluusi okyasisinkana ne bendera yaabwe mu Pacific.

Olw’ensonga ezimu, Jungfrau yalabika ng’ayagala nnyo okumuwa ekitiibwa. Nga akyali wala okuva ku Pequod, yeetooloola okutuuka, n’asuula eryato, kapiteeni we yasindikibwa okututuukako, ng’ayimiridde mu maaso mu kifo ky’emabega w’eryato nga talina bugumiikiriza.

“Alina ki mu ngalo ze eyo?” Starbuck bwe yakaaba ng’asonga ku kintu Omugirimaani gwe yali akutte mu ngeri ey’okuwuuba. “Tekisoboka! —ekintu ekigabula ettaala!”

“Si ekyo,” Stubb bwe yagamba, “nedda, nedda, kiyungu kya kaawa, Mwami Starbuck; agenda kutukolera kaawa waffe, ye Yarman; tolaba ekibbo ekyo ekinene eky’ebbaati awo ku mabbali ge?—ago ge mazzi ge agabuguma. Oh! ali bulungi, ye Yarman.”

“Genda naawe,” Flask bwe yakaaba, “kiliisa ttaala n’ekibbo ky’amafuta. Amafuta gamuweddeko, era azze a-asabiriza.”

Wadde nga kiyinza okulabika ng’ekyewuunyisa emmeeri ey’amafuta okwewola amafuta ku ttaka ly’ennyanja ekika kya whale, era ne bwe kiyinza okukontana mu ngeri ey’ekifuulannenge olugero olukadde olukwata ku kutwala amanda e Newcastle, naye oluusi ekintu ng’ekyo ddala kibaawo; era mu musango guno Captain Derick De Deer ddala yakola ekyuma ekigabula ettaala nga Flask bwe yalangirira.

Nga bw’alinnya ku ddeeke, Akabu yamukwata mu ngeri ey’ekikangabwa, nga tafuddeeyo n’akatono ekyo kye yalina mu ngalo ze; naye mu lingo ye eyamenyese, mu bbanga ttono Omugirimaani yalaga obutamanya bwe bwonna ku White Whale; amangu ago ng’akyusa emboozi n’agitwala ku kifo kye eky’okuliisa ettaala n’ekibbo kye eky’amafuta, ng’ebigambo ebimu bikwata ku kuba nti alina okukyuka mu hammock ye ekiro mu kizikiza ekinene —ettondo lye erisembayo ery’amafuta ga Bremen nga liweddewo, era nga tewali n’ekyennyanja ekibuuka n’ekimu ekyakwatibwa okusobola okugagabira obutaba na buzibu; ng’amaliriza ng’alaga nti ddala emmeeri ye yali ekyo mu Buvubi kye kiyitibwa mu by’ekikugu ekiyonjo (kwe kugamba, ekyerere), nga kisaanidde bulungi erinnya lya Jungfrau oba Bikira Maria.

Ebyetaago bye bwe byamuweebwa, Derick n’agenda; naye yali tannafuna ludda lwa mmeeri ye, nga kumpi ennyanja ekika kya whale zisitulwa okuva ku mitwe gy’amaato gombi; era Derick yali ayagala nnyo okuyigga, ne kiba nti awatali kuyimirira kuteeka kibbo kye eky’amafuta n’ekirya ettaala ku lyato, n’asala okwetooloola eryato lye n’akola ng’agoberera eby’okuliisa ettaala ya leviathan.

Kati, omuzannyo bwe gwali gulinnye okutuuka ku bbugumu, ye n’amaato amalala asatu ag’Abagirimaani agaamugoberera mu bbanga ttono, baalina entandikwa nnyo ey’ebisenge bya Pequod. Waaliwo ennyanja ekika kya whale munaana, nga zino za kikuta kya wakati. Olw’okuba baali bamanyi akabi kaabwe, bonna baali bagenda nga bakwatagana ku sipiidi ey’amaanyi butereevu ng’empewo tennatandika, nga basikasika ku mabbali gaabwe okumpi ng’ebiwanvu by’embalaasi bingi bwe bityo ebiri mu bbugumu. Baalekawo okuzuukuka okunene, okugazi, nga balinga abasumulula buli kiseera olugoye olunene olugazi ku nnyanja.

Nga ejjudde mu kuzuukuka kuno okw’amangu, era nga mu bbanga eritali ddene emabega, yawuga ente ennume enkadde ennene ennyo, eyali efuukuuse, nga olw’okukulaakulana kwayo okwali kugeraageranyizibwa, awamu n’ebikuta ebya kyenvu ebitali bya bulijjo ebyali bimukula, yalabika ng’etawaanyizibwa obulwadde bwa jaundice, oba obunafu obulala. Oba whale eno yali ya pod nga bukyali, kyalabika nga kibuusibwabuusibwa; kubanga si kya mpisa nti ba leviathans ab’ekitiibwa ng’abo okubeera n’abantu n’akatono. Wadde kyali kityo, yanywerera ku kuzuukuka kwabwe, wadde nga ddala amazzi gaabwe ag’emabega gateekwa okuba nga gaamuddirira, kubanga eggumba eryeru oba okuzimba ku mumwa gwe omugazi kwali kwa bbugumu, ng’okuzimba okwakolebwa ng’emigga ebiri egy’obulabe gisisinkanye. Omusulo gwe gwali mumpi, nga gugenda mpola era nga gukola nnyo; okuvaayo n’ekika ky’amazzi agaziyira, ne yeemala mu bitundutundu ebikutuse, nga kigobererwa akajagalalo ak’ekyewuunyo ak’omu ttaka mu ye, akaali kalabika ng’akafuluma ku nkomerero ye endala eyaziikibwa, ne kireetera amazzi emabega we okuwuuma.

“Ani alina paregoric?” Stubb bwe yagamba, “alina obulumi bw’olubuto, ntya. Mukama lowooza ku ky’okulumwa olubuto ekitundu kya yiika! Empewo embi zimukutte Ssekukkulu ey’eddalu, abalenzi. Y’empewo encaafu esoose gye nnali mmanyi okufuuwa okuva emabega; naye laba, waliwo whale yaw bwetyo emabegako? kiteekwa okuba nga, abuze tiller ye.”

Ng’Omuyindi azitoowereddwa ng’asituka wansi ku lubalama lw’ennyanja Hindostan ng’alina omugugu gw’embalaasi ezitidde ku ddeeke, careens, buries, rolls, and wallows in her way; bwe kityo n’envubu eno enkadde n’esitula ekitundu kyayo ekinene ekyakaddiye, era kati n’oluvannyuma ekitundu n’ekyuka ku mbavu zaayo ezizibu, ne kibikkula ekivaako okuzuukuka kwe okw’obulimba mu kikonge ekitali kya butonde eky’ekiwaawaatiro kyayo eky’oku ddyo. Oba yali afiiriddwa ekiwaawaatiro ekyo mu lutalo, oba yazaalibwa nga tekirina, kyali kizibu okwogera.

“Lindako katono, chap omukadde, era nja kukuwa sling olw’omukono ogwo ogwali gufunye ebisago,” Flask omukambwe bwe yakaaba, ng’asonga ku layini y’ennyanja ekika kya whale okumpi naye.

“Mind takukuba na kyo,” Starbuck bwe yakaaba. “Muwe ekkubo, oba Omugirimaani ajja kumufuna.”

Mu kigendererwa kimu amaato gonna agavuganya agagatta gaali gasonga ku kyennyanja kino ekimu, kubanga teyakoma ku kuba nti ye yali asinga obunene, n’olwekyo nga ye whale esinga okuba ey’omuwendo, naye yali asinga okumpi nabo, era whales endala zaali zigenda n’embiro ennene bwe zityo, n’ekirala, . nga kumpi okusomooza okuyigga olw’ekiseera ekyo. Mu kiseera kino ebikondo bya Pequod byali bikubiddwa amasasi amaato asatu ag’e Girimaani agaasembayo okukka; naye okuva ku ntandikwa ennene gye yali afunye, eryato lya Derick lyali likyakulembera okuyigga, wadde nga buli kaseera kasembereranga abavuganya be ab’ebweru. Ekintu kyokka kye baali batya, kyali nti, okuva ku kuba nti yali amaze okumpi nnyo n’akabonero ke, yandisobozeseddwa okukuba ekyuma kye nga tebannasobola kumutuukira ddala n’okumuyita. Ate ye Derick, yalabika nga mukakafu nnyo nti kino kyandibadde bwe kityo, era oluusi n’oluusi n’akabonero ak’okusekerera yakankanya ekyuma kye eky’ettaala ku maato amalala.

“Embwa etali ya kisa era etasiima!” Starbuck bwe yakaaba; “ansekerera era n’angumiikiriza n’ekibokisi eky’obwavu ennyo kye nnamujjuza si ddakiika ttaano eziyise!”—olwo mu kuwuubaala kwe okw’amaanyi okw’edda —“Muwe ekkubo, ennyonyi ekika kya greyhounds! Embwa ku kyo!”

“Mbuulira kye kiri, abasajja” —Stubb bwe yakaaba eri abakozi be —“kimenya eddiini yange okugwa eddalu; naye nandyagadde okulya Yarman oyo omubi — Pull — si bwe kiri? Mugenda kuleka omubbi oyo akube? Mwagala nnyo brandy? N’olwekyo, ekikuta ky’embizzi ekya brandy eri omusajja asinga. Mujje, lwaki abamu ku mmwe temukutula musuwa? Ani oyo abadde asuula ennanga ku nnyanja —tetuwuuba yinsi yonna —tukkakkana. Halloo, wuuno omuddo ogumera wansi w’eryato —era ku lwa Mukama, ekikondo ekiri eyo kimera. Kino tekijja kukola, abalenzi. Laba Yarman oyo! Ekimpi n’ekiwanvu kye kiri, abasajja, munaafuuwa omuliro oba nedda?”

"Oh! laba ebiwujjo by’akola!” Flask bwe yakaaba ng’azina waggulu ne wansi — “Nga kiwujjo —Oo, kola entuumu ku nnyama y’ente —egalamira ng’omuti! Oh! abalenzi bange, mukole spring — slap-jacks ne quahogs ku kijjulo, omanyi, ba lads bange — baked clams ne muffins — oh, do , do , spring,—ye barreller kikumi—tomufiirwa kati—tomufiirwa oh , tokola! —laba Yarman oyo —Oo, temujja kusika duff yammwe, ba lads bange — sog bwetyo! sogger bwetyo! Temwagala nsigo? Waliwo ddoola enkumi ssatu ezigenda, abasajja! —bbanka! —bbanka yonna! Banka ya Bungereza!—Oo, kola , kola , kola! —Oyo Yarman ayogera ki kati?”

Mu kaseera kano Derick yali mu kikolwa eky’okusuula ekintu kye eky’okuliisa ettaala ku maato agaali gagenda mu maaso, era n’ekibbo kye eky’amafuta; mpozzi n’endowooza ey’emirundi ebiri ey’okuzza emabega ekkubo ly’abavuganya be, ate mu kiseera kye kimu mu by’enfuna n’ayanguyiza eyiye olw’okukubirizibwa okw’akaseera katono okw’okusuula emabega.

“Omuyimbi Omudaaki atalina mpisa!” Stubb bwe yakaaba. “Ssika kati, abasajja, ng’emigugu emitwalo ataano egy’olunyiriri lw’emmeeri z’olutalo egya sitaani ez’enviiri emmyuufu. Kye d'ye kwogera, Tashtego; ggwe musajja okukutula omugongo gwo mu bitundu bibiri n'amakumi abiri olw'ekitiibwa kya Gayhead omukadde? Kiki d’ye kwogera?”

“Nze ngamba, sika nga katonda-dam,” — Omuyindi bwe yakaaba.

Mu bukambwe, naye nga zikubirizibwa kyenkanyi olw’okusekererwa kw’Omugirimaani, amaato asatu aga Pequod kati gaatandika okutambula kumpi nga gakwatagana; era, olw’okwagala bwe kityo, n’amusemberera akaseera katono. Mu ndowooza eyo ennungi, etali nnywevu, ey’embalaasi ey’omukulu w’omutwe bwe yali asemberera omuyiggo gwe, bannaabwe abasatu baayimiriranga n’amalala, oluusi n’oluusi nga bawagira omuvuzi w’amaato oluvannyuma n’okukaaba okusanyusa nti, “Eyo gy’aseerera, kati! Hurrary olw'empewo y'evvu eryeru! Wansi ne Yarman! Mutambulire ku mazzi!”

Naye bwe kityo yasalawo entandikwa ey’olubereberye singa Derick yalina, nti wadde nga balina obuzira bwabwe bwonna, yandibadde akakasa nti ye muwanguzi mu mpaka zino, singa tewaali musango mutuukirivu gwamukka mu crab eyakwata ekiso ky’omuvuzi we ow’omu makkati g’emmeeri. Nga lubber eno etali nnungi efuba okusumulula evvu lye eryeru, era nga, mu kivaamu, eryato lya Derick lyali linaatera okugwa, era n’abwatuka n’agenda ku basajja be mu busungu obw’amaanyi;—ekyo kyali kiseera kirungi eri Starbuck, Stubb, ne Flask. Nga baleekaana, baatutte entandikwa ey’okufa mu maaso, era mu ngeri ey’okwekulukuunya ne bagenda waggulu ku kwata y’Omugirimaani. Akaseera akalala, era amaato gonna ana gaali mu diagonically mu whale’s immediate wake, ate nga gagoloddwa okuva ku go, ku njuyi zombi, we waali okuzimba okufuumuuka kwe yakola.

Kyali kifaananyi kya ntiisa, ekisinga okusaasira, era eddalu. Whale kati yali egenda omutwe, era ng’esindika ekifuba kyayo mu maaso ge mu nnyonyi eyali etulugunyizibwa buli kiseera; ate ekiwaawaatiro kye ekimu ekyavu kyakuba oludda lwe mu bulumi obw’okutya. Kati ku mukono guno, kati ku ogwo, yawuubaala mu nnyonyi ye eyali ewuguka, era nga akyali ku buli kiwujjo kye yamenya, mu ngeri ey’okusannyalala n’abbira mu nnyanja, oba ku mabbali n’ayiringisibwa ng’ayolekera eggulu ekiwaawaatiro kye ekikuba. Bwentyo ndabye ekinyonyi ekirina ekiwawaatiro ekisaliddwa nga kikola enzirugavu ezimenyese mu ngeri ey’entiisa mu bbanga, nga kifuba bwereere okutoloka mu nnyonyi z’ababbi. Naye ekinyonyi kirina eddoboozi, era n’okukaaba okw’okwemulugunya kijja kumanyisa okutya kwakyo; naye okutya kw’ensolo eno ennene ennyo ensirifu ey’ennyanja, kwasibibwa enjegere ne kulogebwa mu ye; teyalina ddoboozi, okuggyako okussa okwo okuziyira okuyita mu spiracle ye, era kino kyamufuula okusaasira mu ngeri etayinza kwogerwa; ate nga akyali, mu bungi bwe obw’ekyewuunyo, akawanga ke aka portcullis, n’omukira ogw’amaanyi gonna, waaliwo ebimala okutiisa omusajja asinga obugumu eyasaasira ennyo.

Okulaba kati nti naye akaseera katono nnyo okusingawo kwandiwadde amaato ga Pequod enkizo, era mu kifo ky’okulemesebwa bw’atyo omuzannyo gwe, Derick yasalawo okuteeka mu kabi ekyo gy’ali ekiteekwa okuba nga kyalabika ng’omusinde omuwanvu ennyo mu ngeri etaali ya bulijjo, ere omukisa ogusembayo tegunnabaawo emirembe gyonna okudduka.

Naye amangu ddala ng’omuvuzi we ow’engoye ayimiriddewo olw’okusannyalala, enjovu zonsatule —Queequeg, Tashtego, Daggoo —mu butonde ne zibuuka ne ziyimirira, era nga ziyimiridde mu lunyiriri oluwanvu, mu kiseera kye kimu ne zisonga emiggo gyazo; era ne badduka ku mutwe gw’omuvuzi w’ennyonyi Omugirimaani, ebyuma byabwe ebisatu ebya Nantucket ne biyingira mu nnyanja ekika kya whale. Omukka oguziba amaaso ogw’ekifuumuuka n’omuliro ogweru! Amaato ago asatu, mu busungu obwasooka obw’okudduka kw’ennyanja ekika kya whale n’omutwe, gaakuba ag’Omugirimaani ku bbali n’amaanyi mangi nnyo, ne kiba nti bombi Derick n’omuvuzi we eyali asobeddwa ne bayiwa ebweru, ne basaabala okuyita ku biwujjo ebisatu ebyabuuka.

“Temutya, bbokisi zange eza butto,” Stubb bwe yakaaba, ng’abasuula amaaso agayitawo ng’akuba amasasi; “mujja kusimbulwa mu kiseera kino —kirungi — nnalaba enseenene ezimu emabega —St. Embwa za Bernard, omanyi —ziwummuza abatambuze abali mu nnaku. Hurrah! eno y’engeri y’okusaabala kati. Buli keel a sunbeam! Hurrah!—Wano tugenda ng’ebibbo by’amabaati bisatu ku mukira gwa cougar eddalu! Kino kinteeka mu birowoozo by’okusiba ku njovu mu tilbury ku lusenyi —kireetera emisinde gya nnamuziga okubuuka, abalenzi, bwe mumusibako bwe mutyo; era waliwo akabi k'okusuulibwa ebweru nakyo, ng'okubye olusozi. Hurrah! eno y’engeri munne gy’awulira ng’agenda ewa Davy Jones —byona bifubutuka wansi mu nnyonyi eserebye etakoma! Hurrah! whale eno etwala ebbaluwa etaggwaawo!”

Naye emisinde gy’ekisolo ekyo gyali gya kaseera katono. Ng’awa omukka ogw’amangu, yavuga mu ngeri ey’akajagalalo. Nga zifubutuka, layini essatu zaabuuka okwetooloola emitwe gy’emiggo n’amaanyi mangi nnyo ne zikuba emimwa emiwanvu mu zo; wadde ng’abavuzi b’engoye baali batya nnyo ne kiba nti okuwulikika kuno okw’amangu kwandikooye mu bbanga ttono layini, ne kiba nti nga bakozesa amaanyi gaabwe gonna ag’obukugu, baakwata enkyukakyuka eziddiŋŋana nga bafuuwa sigala n’omuguwa gwe baali bagenda okunywerera; okutuusa ku nkomerero —olw’okunyigirizibwa okw’okwesimbye okuva ku bikoola by’amaato ebiriko layini y’omusulo, emiguwa esatu gye gyava butereevu wansi mu bbululu —ebikondo by’emmundu eby’obutaasa kumpi byali bikwatagana n’amazzi, ate ng’embaawo essatu ez’emabega zaali ziserengese waggulu mu empewo. Era mu bbanga ttono ennyanja ekika kya whale bwe yalekera awo okuwulikika, okumala ekiseera yasigala mu ndowooza eyo, nga batya okusaasaanya layini endala, wadde ng’ekifo kyali kiwunya katono. Naye newankubadde nga amaato gaggyiddwa wansi ne gabula mu ngeri eno, naye kino kye “kukwata,” nga bwe kiyitibwa; kino ng’akwatagana n’ebiwujjo ebisongovu eby’ennyama ye enamu okuva emabega; kino kye kitera okubonyaabonya Leviyasani mu bbanga ttono n’asituka nate okusisinkana effumu ery’amaanyi ery’abalabe be. Naye nga tetulina kwogera ku kabi k’ekintu ekyo, kibuusibwabuusibwa oba bulijjo ekkubo lino lye lisinga obulungi; kubanga kya magezi okulowooza, nti whale ekubiddwa gy’ekoma okumala wansi w’amazzi, gy’ekoma okukoowa. Kubanga, olw’obugulumivu bwe —mu sperm whale ekuze mu bujjuvu ekintu ekitasukka square feet 2000 — puleesa y’amazzi nnene nnyo. Ffenna tumanyi obuzito bw’empewo obw’ekyewuunyo ffe kennyini bwe tuyimiridde wansi; ne wano, waggulu w’ettaka, mu bbanga; n’olwekyo, omugugu gw’ennyanja ekika kya whale nga munene nnyo, ng’etisse ku mugongo gwe empagi ya ffuuti ebikumi bibiri ez’ennyanja! Kiteekwa waakiri okwenkana obuzito bw’empewo amakumi ataano. Omuvuzi w’ennyanja ekika kya whale omu akibalirira obuzito bw’emmeeri amakumi abiri ezitambula mu layini y’olutalo, nga zirina emmundu zazo zonna, n’amaduuka, n’abasajja abali ku mmeeri.

Nga amaato asatu gagalamidde awo ku nnyanja eyo eyali yeekulukuunya mpola, nga gatunudde wansi mu misana gyayo egya bbululu egy’olubeerera; era nga tewali kusinda wadde okukaaba okw’engeri yonna, nedda, nga n’amayengo oba ekiwujjo bwe kyava mu buziba bwakyo; nga omukugu mu by’ettaka yandirowoozezza, nti wansi w’okusirika okwo kwonna n’obutebenkevu, ekisolo ekisinga obunene eky’ennyanja kyali kiwuuma era nga kiwunya olw’obulumi! Tewali yinsi munaana ez’omuguwa ogw’okwesimbye ogwalabika ku butaasa. Kirabika kyesigika nti olw’obuwuzi busatu obugonvu bwe butyo Leviathan omukulu yali awaniriddwa ng’obuzito obunene ku ssaawa ey’ennaku munaana. Okuyimiriza? era ku ki? Ku bitundutundu bisatu eby’olubaawo. Kino kye kitonde kye kyayogerwako edda n’obuwanguzi obw’amaanyi nti —“Oyinza okujjuza olususu lwakyo ebyuma eby’emiguwa? oba omutwe gwe nga guliko amafumu g’ebyennyanja? Ekitala ky'oyo amugalamidde tekiyinza kukwata, effumu, n'obusaale, newakubadde engoye: ekyuma akitwala ng'essubi; akasaale tekayinza kumuddusa; emisinde gibalibwa ng’ebisusunku; aseka olw’okukankana kw’effumu!” Kino ekitonde? ono ye? Oh! nti obutatuukirira kugoberera bannabbi. Kubanga olw’amaanyi g’ebisambi lukumi mu mukira gwe, Leviathan yali adduse omutwe gwe wansi w’ensozi z’ennyanja, okumukweka amafumu g’ebyennyanja aga Pequod!

Mu musana ogwo ogw’emisana ogwali guserengese, ebisiikirize amaato asatu bye gaasindika wansi wansi w’amazzi, biteekwa okuba nga byali biwanvu ekimala era nga bigazi ekimala okusiikiriza ekitundu ky’eggye lya Zakisi. Ani ayinza okutegeera engeri ennyanja ekika kya whale eyali efunye ebisago gye yateekwa okuba nga yali ewunyiriza ennene ennyo bwe zityo ezaali ziwuguka ku mutwe gwe!

“Muyimirire awo, abasajja; asikasika,” Starbuck bwe yakaaba, nga layini essatu zikankana mu mazzi mu bwangu, nga ziyita mu ngeri ey’enjawulo waggulu gye ziri, nga bwe ziyita mu waya za magineeti, okukuba kw’obulamu n’okufa kw’ennyanja ekika kya whale, buli muvuzi w’amaato n’abiwulira mu ntebe ye. Akaseera akaddako, nga gawummudde ekitundu ekinene olw’okunyigirizibwa okukka wansi ku butaasa, amaato gaawa okubuuka okw’amangu waggulu, ng’ekifo ekitono eky’omuzira bwe kinaakola, ekisibo ekinene eky’eddubu eryeru bwe litiisibwa okuva mu kyo ne liyingira mu nnyanja.

“Haul in! Haul in!” Starbuck bwe yazzeemu okukaaba; “asituka.”

Ennyiriri, nga, mu kaseera katono emabega, nga tewali bugazi bwa mukono gumu, kati zaali mu koyilo empanvu ez’amangu ezaasuulibwa emabega zonna nga zitonnya mu maato, era mu bbanga ttono ennyanja ekika kya whale yamenya amazzi mu buwanvu bw’emmeeri bbiri okuva ku bayizzi.

Ebiteeso bye byali biraga bulungi nti yali akooye nnyo. Mu bisolo ebisinga obungi eby’oku lukalu wabaawo obuuma obumu oba emiryango gy’amataba mu misuwa gyabyo mingi, nga bwe buba bufunye ebisago, omusaayi mu ddaala eritali limu waakiri guggalwa mangu mu njuyi ezimu. ESSUULA 82. Ekitiibwa n’Ekitiibwa ky’Okuvuba Envubu.

Waliwo ebitongole ebimu nga mu bino okutabangula n’obwegendereza y’enkola entuufu.

Gy’okoma okubbira mu nsonga eno ey’okuvuba ennyanja ekika kya whale, n’okusika okunoonyereza kwange okutuuka ku mutwe gwennyini ogw’ensulo yaayo gye nkoma okukwatibwako nnyo olw’ekitiibwa kyayo ekinene n’obukadde bwayo; era naddala bwe nsanga bakatonda abakulu bangi nnyo n’abazira, bannabbi ab’engeri zonna, mu ngeri emu oba endala abayiwako enjawulo, ntambuzibwa n’okufumiitiriza nti nze kennyini ndi wa, wadde naye nga wansi, mu kibiina ky’obwasseruganda ekimanyiddwa ennyo .

Omuzira Perseus, mutabani wa Jupiter, ye yali omuvuzi w’ennyanja ekika kya whale eyasooka; era eri ekitiibwa eky’olubeerera eky’okuyitibwa kwaffe kigambibwa, nti ennyanja ekika kya whale eyasooka okulumbibwa obwasseruganda bwaffe teyattibwa na kigendererwa kyonna kibi. Ezo zaali nnaku za knightly ez’omulimu gwaffe, nga twetikka emmundu zokka okuyamba abaali mu nnaku, so si kujjuza biyumba bya basajja. Buli omu amanyi emboozi ennungi eya Perseus ne Andromeda; engeri Andromeda omwagalwa, muwala wa kabaka, gye yali asibiddwa ku lwazi ku lubalama lw’ennyanja, era nga Leviathan ali mu kikolwa kyennyini eky’okumutwala, Perseus, omulangira w’abavuzi b’ennyanja ekika kya whale, ng’agenda mu maaso n’obuvumu, n’akuba ekisolo ekyo, era yazaala n’awasa omukozi w’awaka. Yali mulimu gwa by’emikono ogw’okusiimibwa, nga tegutera kutuukibwako bavubi ba harpoone abasinga obulungi mu kiseera kino; kubanga Leeviyasani ono yattibwa ku musaale ogwasooka ennyo. Era tewali muntu yenna abuusabuusa emboozi eno ey’Abaarki; kubanga mu Yopa ey’edda, kati Yaffa, ku lubalama lw’ennyanja Busuuli, mu emu ku yeekaalu z’Abakaafiiri, waaliwo okumala emyaka mingi amagumba amanene ennyo ag’ennyanja ekika kya whale, enfumo z’ekibuga n’abatuuze bonna ge baagamba nti ge magumba agafaanagana ag’ekisolo ekyo nti Perseus yatta. Abaruumi bwe baatwala Yopa, amagumba ge gamu gaatwalibwa e Yitale mu buwanguzi. Ekisinga okulabika ng’ekisinga obumu era ekikulu mu mboozi eno, kiri nti: Yona mwe yasimbula okuva e Yopa.

Okufaananako n’akabenje ka Perseus ne Andromeda —mazima ddala, abamu bwe balowooza nti kaava mu ngeri etali butereevu —ye mboozi eyo emanyiddwa ennyo eya St. George n’Omusota; ekisota ki kye nkuuma nti kyali kiwuka ekiyitibwa whale; kubanga mu bitabo by’ebyafaayo bingi eby’edda ennyanja ekika kya whale n’ebisota bitabuddwatabuddwa wamu mu ngeri eyeewuunyisa, era emirundi mingi biyimiriddewo. Ezeekyeri agamba nti: “Oli ng’empologoma ey’amazzi, era ng’ekisota eky’omu nnyanja; wano, kitegeeza mu bulambulukufu ennyanja ekika kya whale; mu mazima, enkyusa za Baibuli ezimu zikozesa ekigambo ekyo kyennyini. Ng’oggyeeko ekyo, kyandiggye nnyo ku kitiibwa ky’okukola ennyo yalina St. George naye n’asisinkana ekisolo ekiseeyeeya eky’ensi, mu kifo ky’okukola olutalo n’ekisolo ekinene eky’omu buziba. Omusajja yenna ayinza okutta omusota, naye Perseus, St. George, Coffin bokka be balina omutima mu bo okutambula n’obuvumu okutuuka ku whale.

Ebifaananyi eby’omulembe eby’ekifo kino bireme kutubuzaabuza; kubanga newankubadde ekitonde ekyasisinkana omusajja oyo omuzira w’ennyanja ow’edda kikiikirira mu ngeri etategeerekeka nga kiringa griffin, era newankubadde ng’olutalo lulagibwa ku lukalu ate omutukuvu ng’ali ku mbalaasi, naye ng’olowooza ku butamanya obunene obw’ebiseera ebyo, ng’ekifaananyi ekituufu eky’ whale yali tamanyiddwa bayimbi; era bw’olowooza nti nga bwe kyali mu mbeera ya Perseus, ennyanja ekika kya St. George’s whale yandibadde yeekulukuunya okuva mu nnyanja ku bbiici; era nga tulowooza nti ensolo eyali yeebagalwa St. George yandiba nga yali seal ennene yokka, oba embalaasi y’ennyanja; nga bino byonna bikutte mu birowoozo, tekijja kulabika nga tekikwatagana n’akatono n’olugero olutukuvu n’ebifaananyi eby’edda eby’ekifo ekyo, okukwata kino kye bayita ekisota nga si mulala okuggyako Leviathan omukulu yennyini. Mu butuufu, bwe kiteekeddwa mu maaso g’amazima amakakali era agafumita, emboozi eno yonna ejja kutambula ng’ekifaananyi ekyo eky’ebyennyanja, ennyama, n’ebinyonyi eky’Abafirisuuti, erinnya lya Dagoni; eyasimbibwa mu maaso g’essanduuko ya Isiraeri, omutwe gw’embalaasi ye n’engalo ze zombi ne bimugwako, era ekikonge oba ekitundu kye eky’ebyennyanja kyokka ne kisigalawo. Bwe kityo, n’olwekyo, omu ku sitampu yaffe ey’ekitiibwa, wadde omuvuzi w’ennyanja whale, ye mukuumi wa Bungereza omukuumi; era olw’eddembe eddungi, ffe aba harpooneers ba Nantucket tusaanidde okuwandiisibwa mu order esinga ekitiibwa eya St. George. Era n’olwekyo, baleme ku ba knights b’ekibiina ekyo eky’ekitiibwa (tewali n’omu ku bo, nfuba okugamba, abaddeko na kakwate na whale ng’omuyambi waabwe omukulu), baleme kutunuulira Nantucketer n’okunyooma, okuva ne mu frocks zaffe ez’ebyoya by’endiga n’empale eziriko kolaasi tusinga nnyo okuyooyoota St. George okusinga bo.

Oba okukkiriza Hercules mu ffe oba nedda, ku nsonga eno nnasigala nga nbuusabuusa: kubanga wadde okusinziira ku nfumo z’Abayonaani, Crockett oyo ow’edda ne Kit Carson —omukozi oyo ow’amaanyi ow’okusanyuka ebikolwa ebirungi, yamira wansi n’asuulibwa waggulu ennyanja ekika kya whale; still, oba ekyo strictly kimufuula whaleman, ekyo kiyinza okuba nga kibuusibwabuusibwa. Tewali we kirabika nti mu butuufu yakubye ebyennyanja bye, okuggyako, ddala, okuva munda. Wadde kiri kityo, ayinza okutwalibwa ng’ekika ky’omuvuzi w’ennyanja ekika kya whale atali kyeyagalire; mu ngeri yonna whale yamukwata, singa teyakwata whale. Nze mmuwozaako lwa kimu ku bika byaffe.

Naye, ab’obuyinza abasinga okukontana, emboozi eno ey’Oluyonaani eya Hercules n’ennyanja ekika kya whale etwalibwa ng’evudde mu mboozi y’Olwebbulaniya ekyali ey’edda ennyo eya Yona n’ennyanja ekika kya whale; ne vice versâ; mazima ddala bafaanagana nnyo. Bwemba nga nneewozaako nti demi-god olwo, lwaki si nnabbi?

Era n’abazira, abatukuvu, bakatonda abatonotono, ne bannabbi bokka si be bakola omuzingo gwonna ogw’ekibiina kyaffe. Grand master waffe akyalina okutuumibwa erinnya; kubanga okufaananako bakabaka ab’obwakabaka ab’edda, tusanga amazzi g’omutwe ag’obwasseruganda bwaffe mu kintu kyonna ekitali kitono okusinga bakatonda abakulu bennyini. Emboozi eyo ey’ekitalo ey’omu buvanjuba kati ya kwegezaamu okuva mu Shaster, etuwa Vishnoo ow’entiisa, omu ku bantu abasatu mu bwakatonda bw’Abahindu; atuwa Vishnoo ono ow’obwakatonda yennyini ku lwa Mukama waffe;—Vishnoo, olw’okufuuka omubiri gwe ekkumi ogw’oku nsi, eyawula emirembe gyonna era n’atukuza ennyanja ekika kya whale. CHAPTER 83. Jonah Historically Regarded.

Reference was made to the historical story of Jonah and the whale in the preceding chapter. Now some Nantucketers rather distrust this historical story of Jonah and the whale. But then there were some sceptical Greeks and Romans, who, standing out from the orthodox pagans of their times, equally doubted the story of Hercules and the whale, and Arion and the dolphin; and yet their doubting those traditions did not make those traditions one whit the less facts, for all that.

One old Sag-Harbor whaleman's chief reason for questioning the Hebrew story was this:—He had one of those quaint old-fashioned Bibles, embellished with curious, unscientific plates; one of which represented Jonah's whale with two spouts in his head—a peculiarity only true with respect to a species of the Leviathan (the Right Whale, and the varieties of that order), concerning which the fishermen have this saying, “A penny roll would choke him”; his swallow is so very small. But, to this, Bishop Jebb's anticipative answer is ready. It is not necessary, hints the Bishop, that we consider Jonah as tombed in the whale's belly, but as temporarily lodged in some part of his mouth. And this seems reasonable enough in the good Bishop. For truly, the Right Whale's mouth would accommodate a couple of whist-tables, and comfortably seat all the players. Possibly, too, Jonah might have ensconced himself in a hollow tooth; but, on second thoughts, the Right Whale is toothless.

Another reason which Sag-Harbor (he went by that name) urged for his want of faith in this matter of the prophet, was something obscurely in reference to his incarcerated body and the whale's gastric juices. But this objection likewise falls to the ground, because a German exegetist supposes that Jonah must have taken refuge in the floating body of a dead whale—even as the French soldiers in the Russian campaign turned their dead horses into tents, and crawled into them. Besides, it has been divined by other continental commentators, that when Jonah was thrown overboard from the Joppa ship, he straightway effected his escape to another vessel near by, some vessel with a whale for a figure-head; and, I would add, possibly called “The Whale,” as some craft are nowadays christened the “Shark,” the “Gull,” the “Eagle.” Nor have there been wanting learned exegetists who have opined that the whale mentioned in the book of Jonah merely meant a life-preserver—an inflated bag of wind—which the endangered prophet swam to, and so was saved from a watery doom. Poor Sag-Harbor, therefore, seems worsted all round. But he had still another reason for his want of faith. It was this, if I remember right: Jonah was swallowed by the whale in the Mediterranean Sea, and after three days he was vomited up somewhere within three days' journey of Nineveh, a city on the Tigris, very much more than three days' journey across from the nearest point of the Mediterranean coast. How is that?

But was there no other way for the whale to land the prophet within that short distance of Nineveh? Yes. He might have carried him round by the way of the Cape of Good Hope. But not to speak of the passage through the whole length of the Mediterranean, and another passage up the Persian Gulf and Red Sea, such a supposition would involve the complete circumnavigation of all Africa in three days, not to speak of the Tigris waters, near the site of Nineveh, being too shallow for any whale to swim in. Besides, this idea of Jonah's weathering the Cape of Good Hope at so early a day would wrest the honor of the discovery of that great headland from Bartholomew Diaz, its reputed discoverer, and so make modern history a liar.

But all these foolish arguments of old Sag-Harbor only evinced his foolish pride of reason—a thing still more reprehensible in him, seeing that he had but little learning except what he had picked up from the sun and the sea. I say it only shows his foolish, impious pride, and abominable, devilish rebellion against the reverend clergy. For by a Portuguese Catholic priest, this very idea of Jonah's going to Nineveh via the Cape of Good Hope was advanced as a signal magnification of the general miracle. And so it was. Besides, to this day, the highly enlightened Turks devoutly believe in the historical story of Jonah. And some three centuries ago, an English traveller in old Harris's Voyages, speaks of a Turkish Mosque built in honor of Jonah, in which Mosque was a miraculous lamp that burnt without any oil.





CHAPTER 84. Pitchpoling.

To make them run easily and swiftly, the axles of carriages are anointed; and for much the same purpose, some whalers perform an analogous operation upon their boat; they grease the bottom. Nor is it to be doubted that as such a procedure can do no harm, it may possibly be of no contemptible advantage; considering that oil and water are hostile; that oil is a sliding thing, and that the object in view is to make the boat slide bravely. Queequeg believed strongly in anointing his boat, and one morning not long after the German ship Jungfrau disappeared, took more than customary pains in that occupation; crawling under its bottom, where it hung over the side, and rubbing in the unctuousness as though diligently seeking to insure a crop of hair from the craft's bald keel. He seemed to be working in obedience to some particular presentiment. Nor did it remain unwarranted by the event.

Towards noon whales were raised; but so soon as the ship sailed down to them, they turned and fled with swift precipitancy; a disordered flight, as of Cleopatra's barges from Actium.

Nevertheless, the boats pursued, and Stubb's was foremost. By great exertion, Tashtego at last succeeded in planting one iron; but the stricken whale, without at all sounding, still continued his horizontal flight, with added fleetness. Such unintermitted strainings upon the planted iron must sooner or later inevitably extract it. It became imperative to lance the flying whale, or be content to lose him. But to haul the boat up to his flank was impossible, he swam so fast and furious. What then remained?

Of all the wondrous devices and dexterities, the sleights of hand and countless subtleties, to which the veteran whaleman is so often forced, none exceed that fine manœuvre with the lance called pitchpoling. Small sword, or broad sword, in all its exercises boasts nothing like it. It is only indispensable with an inveterate running whale; its grand fact and feature is the wonderful distance to which the long lance is accurately darted from a violently rocking, jerking boat, under extreme headway. Steel and wood included, the entire spear is some ten or twelve feet in length; the staff is much slighter than that of the harpoon, and also of a lighter material—pine. It is furnished with a small rope called a warp, of considerable length, by which it can be hauled back to the hand after darting.

But before going further, it is important to mention here, that though the harpoon may be pitchpoled in the same way with the lance, yet it is seldom done; and when done, is still less frequently successful, on account of the greater weight and inferior length of the harpoon as compared with the lance, which in effect become serious drawbacks. As a general thing, therefore, you must first get fast to a whale, before any pitchpoling comes into play.

Look now at Stubb; a man who from his humorous, deliberate coolness and equanimity in the direst emergencies, was specially qualified to excel in pitchpoling. Look at him; he stands upright in the tossed bow of the flying boat; wrapt in fleecy foam, the towing whale is forty feet ahead. Handling the long lance lightly, glancing twice or thrice along its length to see if it be exactly straight, Stubb whistlingly gathers up the coil of the warp in one hand, so as to secure its free end in his grasp, leaving the rest unobstructed. Then holding the lance full before his waistband's middle, he levels it at the whale; when, covering him with it, he steadily depresses the butt-end in his hand, thereby elevating the point till the weapon stands fairly balanced upon his palm, fifteen feet in the air. He minds you somewhat of a juggler, balancing a long staff on his chin. Next moment with a rapid, nameless impulse, in a superb lofty arch the bright steel spans the foaming distance, and quivers in the life spot of the whale. Instead of sparkling water, he now spouts red blood.

“That drove the spigot out of him!” cried Stubb. “'Tis July's immortal Fourth; all fountains must run wine today! Would now, it were old Orleans whiskey, or old Ohio, or unspeakable old Monongahela! Then, Tashtego, lad, I'd have ye hold a canakin to the jet, and we'd drink round it! Yea, verily, hearts alive, we'd brew choice punch in the spread of his spout-hole there, and from that live punch-bowl quaff the living stuff.”

Again and again to such gamesome talk, the dexterous dart is repeated, the spear returning to its master like a greyhound held in skilful leash. The agonized whale goes into his flurry; the tow-line is slackened, and the pitchpoler dropping astern, folds his hands, and mutely watches the monster die.





CHAPTER 85. The Fountain.

That for six thousand years—and no one knows how many millions of ages before—the great whales should have been spouting all over the sea, and sprinkling and mistifying the gardens of the deep, as with so many sprinkling or mistifying pots; and that for some centuries back, thousands of hunters should have been close by the fountain of the whale, watching these sprinklings and spoutings—that all this should be, and yet, that down to this blessed minute (fifteen and a quarter minutes past one o'clock PM of this sixteenth day of December, AD 1851), it should still remain a problem, whether these spoutings are, after all, really water, or nothing but vapor—this is surely a noteworthy thing.

Let us, then, look at this matter, along with some interesting items contingent. Every one knows that by the peculiar cunning of their gills, the finny tribes in general breathe the air which at all times is combined with the element in which they swim; hence, a herring or a cod might live a century, and never once raise its head above the surface. But owing to his marked internal structure which gives him regular lungs, like a human being's, the whale can only live by inhaling the disengaged air in the open atmosphere. Wherefore the necessity for his periodical visits to the upper world. But he cannot in any degree breathe through his mouth, for, in his ordinary attitude, the Sperm Whale's mouth is buried at least eight feet beneath the surface; and what is still more, his windpipe has no connexion with his mouth. No, he breathes through his spiracle alone; and this is on the top of his head.

If I say, that in any creature breathing is only a function indispensable to vitality, inasmuch as it withdraws from the air a certain element, which being subsequently brought into contact with the blood imparts to the blood its vivifying principle, I do not think I shall err; though I may possibly use some superfluous scientific words. Assume it, and it follows that if all the blood in a man could be aerated with one breath, he might then seal up his nostrils and not fetch another for a considerable time. That is to say, he would then live without breathing. Anomalous as it may seem, this is precisely the case with the whale, who systematically lives, by intervals, his full hour and more (when at the bottom) without drawing a single breath, or so much as in any way inhaling a particle of air; for, remember, he has no gills. How is this? Between his ribs and on each side of his spine he is supplied with a remarkable involved Cretan labyrinth of vermicelli-like vessels, which vessels, when he quits the surface, are completely distended with oxygenated blood. So that for an hour or more, a thousand fathoms in the sea, he carries a surplus stock of vitality in him, just as the camel crossing the waterless desert carries a surplus supply of drink for future use in its four supplementary stomachs. The anatomical fact of this labyrinth is indisputable; and that the supposition founded upon it is reasonable and true, seems the more cogent to me, when I consider the otherwise inexplicable obstinacy of that leviathan in having his spoutings out , as the fishermen phrase it. This is what I mean. If unmolested, upon rising to the surface, the Sperm Whale will continue there for a period of time exactly uniform with all his other unmolested risings. Say he stays eleven minutes, and jets seventy times, that is, respires seventy breaths; then whenever he rises again, he will be sure to have his seventy breaths over again, to a minute. Now, if after he fetches a few breaths you alarm him, so that he sounds, he will be always dodging up again to make good his regular allowance of air. And not till those seventy breaths are told, will he finally go down to stay out his full term below. Remark, however, that in different individuals these rates are different; but in any one they are alike. Now, why should the whale thus insist upon having his spoutings out, unless it be to replenish his reservoir of air, ere descending for good? How obvious is it, too, that this necessity for the whale's rising exposes him to all the fatal hazards of the chase. For not by hook or by net could this vast leviathan be caught, when sailing a thousand fathoms beneath the sunlight. Not so much thy skill, then, O hunter, as the great necessities that strike the victory to thee!

In man, breathing is incessantly going on—one breath only serving for two or three pulsations; so that whatever other business he has to attend to, waking or sleeping, breathe he must, or die he will. But the Sperm Whale only breathes about one seventh or Sunday of his time.

It has been said that the whale only breathes through his spout-hole; if it could truthfully be added that his spouts are mixed with water, then I opine we should be furnished with the reason why his sense of smell seems obliterated in him; for the only thing about him that at all answers to his nose is that identical spout-hole; and being so clogged with two elements, it could not be expected to have the power of smelling. But owing to the mystery of the spout—whether it be water or whether it be vapor—no absolute certainty can as yet be arrived at on this head. Sure it is, nevertheless, that the Sperm Whale has no proper olfactories. But what does he want of them? No roses, no violets, no Cologne-water in the sea.

Furthermore, as his windpipe solely opens into the tube of his spouting canal, and as that long canal—like the grand Erie Canal—is furnished with a sort of locks (that open and shut) for the downward retention of air or the upward exclusion of water, therefore the whale has no voice; unless you insult him by saying, that when he so strangely rumbles, he talks through his nose. But then again, what has the whale to say? Seldom have I known any profound being that had anything to say to this world, unless forced to stammer out something by way of getting a living. Oh! happy that the world is such an excellent listener!

Now, the spouting canal of the Sperm Whale, chiefly intended as it is for the conveyance of air, and for several feet laid along, horizontally, just beneath the upper surface of his head, and a little to one side; this curious canal is very much like a gas-pipe laid down in a city on one side of a street. But the question returns whether this gas-pipe is also a water-pipe; in other words, whether the spout of the Sperm Whale is the mere vapor of the exhaled breath, or whether that exhaled breath is mixed with water taken in at the mouth, and discharged through the spiracle. It is certain that the mouth indirectly communicates with the spouting canal; but it cannot be proved that this is for the purpose of discharging water through the spiracle. Because the greatest necessity for so doing would seem to be, when in feeding he accidentally takes in water. But the Sperm Whale's food is far beneath the surface, and there he cannot spout even if he would. Besides, if you regard him very closely, and time him with your watch, you will find that when unmolested, there is an undeviating rhyme between the periods of his jets and the ordinary periods of respiration.

But why pester one with all this reasoning on the subject? Speak out! You have seen him spout; then declare what the spout is; can you not tell water from air? My dear sir, in this world it is not so easy to settle these plain things. I have ever found your plain things the knottiest of all. And as for this whale spout, you might almost stand in it, and yet be undecided as to what it is precisely.

The central body of it is hidden in the snowy sparkling mist enveloping it; and how can you certainly tell whether any water falls from it, when, always, when you are close enough to a whale to get a close view of his spout, he is in a prodigious commotion, the water cascading all around him. And if at such times you should think that you really perceived drops of moisture in the spout, how do you know that they are not merely condensed from its vapor; or how do you know that they are not those identical drops superficially lodged in the spout-hole fissure, which is countersunk into the summit of the whale's head? For even when tranquilly swimming through the mid-day sea in a calm, with his elevated hump sun-dried as a dromedary's in the desert; even then, the whale always carries a small basin of water on his head, as under a blazing sun you will sometimes see a cavity in a rock filled up with rain.

Nor is it at all prudent for the hunter to be over curious touching the precise nature of the whale spout. It will not do for him to be peering into it, and putting his face in it. You cannot go with your pitcher to this fountain and fill it, and bring it away. For even when coming into slight contact with the outer, vapory shreds of the jet, which will often happen, your skin will feverishly smart, from the acridness of the thing so touching it. And I know one, who coming into still closer contact with the spout, whether with some scientific object in view, or otherwise, I cannot say, the skin peeled off from his cheek and arm. Wherefore, among whalemen, the spout is deemed poisonous; they try to evade it. Another thing; I have heard it said, and I do not much doubt it, that if the jet is fairly spouted into your eyes, it will blind you. The wisest thing the investigator can do then, it seems to me, is to let this deadly spout alone.

Still, we can hypothesize, even if we cannot prove and establish. My hypothesis is this: that the spout is nothing but mist. And besides other reasons, to this conclusion I am impelled, by considerations touching the great inherent dignity and sublimity of the Sperm Whale; I account him no common, shallow being, inasmuch as it is an undisputed fact that he is never found on soundings, or near shores; all other whales sometimes are. He is both ponderous and profound. And I am convinced that from the heads of all ponderous profound beings, such as Plato, Pyrrho, the Devil, Jupiter, Dante, and so on, there always goes up a certain semi-visible steam, while in the act of thinking deep thoughts. While composing a little treatise on Eternity, I had the curiosity to place a mirror before me; and ere long saw reflected there, a curious involved worming and undulation in the atmosphere over my head. The invariable moisture of my hair, while plunged in deep thought, after six cups of hot tea in my thin shingled attic, of an August noon; this seems an additional argument for the above supposition.

And how nobly it raises our conceit of the mighty, misty monster, to behold him solemnly sailing through a calm tropical sea; his vast, mild head overhung by a canopy of vapor, engendered by his incommunicable contemplations, and that vapor—as you will sometimes see it—glorified by a rainbow, as if Heaven itself had put its seal upon his thoughts. For, d'ye see, rainbows do not visit the clear air; they only irradiate vapor. And so, through all the thick mists of the dim doubts in my mind, divine intuitions now and then shoot, enkindling my fog with a heavenly ray. And for this I thank God; for all have doubts; many deny; but doubts or denials, few along with them, have intuitions. Doubts of all things earthly, and intuitions of some things heavenly; this combination makes neither believer nor infidel, but makes a man who regards them both with equal eye.
