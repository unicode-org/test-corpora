MOAKÃHA 89. Pira Pya’e ha Pira Suelto.

Pe alusión pe waif ha waif-poles rehegua pe capítulo pahape ha katu peteĩ, oikotevẽ algún relato umi léi ha reglamento pesca ballena rehegua, umíva apytégui ikatu ojehecha pe waif ha’eha pe gran símbolo ha insignia.

Py’ỹi ojehu heta barco ohóramo compañía-pe, peteĩ ballena ikatu oinupã peteĩ mba’yru, upéi okañy ha ipahápe ojuka ha ojejagarra ambue mba’yru; ha ko’ápe oñecompone indirectamente heta contingencia michĩva, opavave oparticipáva ko peteĩ rasgo tuichaitévagui. Techapyrã, —peteĩ persecución ha captura ikane’õ ha ipeligroso rire peteĩ ballena, ikatu ojepoi pe barco-gui peteĩ tormenta violenta rupi; ha oñemomombyrývo mombyry yvytu gotyo, ojejagarra jey peteĩ ballenador mokõiha, ha’e, peteĩ py’aguapýpe ha snugly orremolca ijykére, riesgo’ỹre tekove ni línea. Péicha umi disputa ombopy'aroryvéva ha violento-véva heñóita jepi umi pescador-kuéra apytépe, ndaipóririre algún léi ojehaíva térã ndojehaíriva, universal, indiscutible ojeporúva opavave kásope.

Oiméne pe código formal caza de ballenas añoite oautorisáva promulgación legislativa, ha'e kuri Holanda-pegua. Odecreta Estados Generales ary 1695. Ha katu jepémo ndaipóri ambue tetã oguerekóva mba’eveichagua léi ojehaíva ballena rehegua, jepémo upéicha umi pescador norteamericano ha’ekuri legislador ha abogado imba’éva ko mba’épe. Ome'ê hikuái peteî sistema por conciso integral ohasáva umi Pandecto Justiniano ha umi Estatuto Sociedad China de Supresión de Injerencia ambue Negocio Pueblo-pe. Heẽ; ko’ã léi ikatu ojegrava peteĩ farthing rréina Ana-pe, térã peteĩ arpón barba rehe, ha ojeporu ijajúrare, michĩeterei.

I. Peteĩ Pira Pya’e ha’e pe partido pya’e chupe mba’e.

II. Peteĩ Loose-Fish ha’e ñembosarái hekopete oimeraẽvape ĝuarã ikatúva pya’eve ojagarra.

Ha katu pe ombopuva’ekue pe travesura ko código magistral reheve ha’e pe admirable brevedad orekóva, oikotevẽva peteĩ volumen tuicháva comentario-kuéra oñemyesakã haĝua.

Peteĩha: Mba’épa peteĩ Pira Pya’e? Peteĩ pira oikovéva térã omanóva técnicamente ipya’e, oñembojoajúramo peteĩ barco térã barco ocupado ndive, oimeraẽ medio rupive mba’eveichavérõ ocontroláva pe ocupante térã ocupante, —peteĩ mástil, peteĩ remo, peteĩ cable de nueve pulgadas, peteĩ alambre de telégrafo térã peteĩ telaraña ryru, opa mba’e peteĩchaite. Péicha avei peteî pira técnicamente ipya'e ogueraháva waif, térã oimeraêva ambue símbolo ojehechakuaáva posesión; pe partido odenunsiáva ohechauka porã aja ikatuha oimeha ára ogueraha ijykére, avei intención orekóva ojapo haguã.

Ko’ãva ha’e umi comentario científico; ha katu umi comentario umi ballena-gua voi sapy’ánte oconsisti ñe’ẽ hatã ha ombota hatãvévape —pe Coca-upon-Littleton puño rehegua. Añetehápe, umi ballena-kuéra hekojojáva ha oñemomba’eguasuvéva apytépe ojejapo meme umi subsidio umi káso peculiar-pe ĝuarã, ha’étahápe peteĩ injusticia moral indignante peteĩ parte oreklama haĝua oguerekoha peteĩ ballena opersegi térã ojukava’ekue yma ambue partido. Péro ótro katu ni mbaʼevéicharõ ndahaʼéi upéicha escrupuloso.

Ojapo cincuenta áño rupi oĩ hague peteĩ káso curioso de ballena-trover ojelitigava’ekue Inglaterra-pe, umi demandante omoĩhápe upéva peteĩ persecución mbarete rire peteĩ ballena-pe umi mar Norte-pe; ha añetehápe ha’ekuéra (umi demandante) osẽ porãrõ guare oarpón pe pira; ipahápe oĩkuri hikuái, peligro hekovépe, obligado oheja haĝua ndaha’éi ilínea añónte, ha katu ibarco voi. Ipahápe umi acusado (tripulación ambue barco-gua) oúva ballena rehe, oinupã, ojuka, ojagarra ha ipahápe oapropia umi demandante resa renondépe voi. Ha oñembohory jave umi acusado-pe, ikapitán oikytĩ ikuã umi demandante diente-pe, ha oasegura chupekuéra doxología rupive pe escritura ojapova’ekuépe, ko’áğa omantenetaha ilínea, arpón ha barco, opytáva ojejokóva pe ballena-pe ojeapresa jave. Upévare umi demandante ko'ágã odemanda ojerekupera haguã valor orekóva ballena, línea, arpón ha barco.

Karai Erskine ha’eva’ekue abogado umi acusado-kuérape ĝuarã; Lord Ellenborough ha’e va’ekue pe hués. Oñemotenondévo defensa, ingenioso Erskine oho ohechauka iposición, oaludivo peteî delito ndahi'aréi. con. káso, peteĩ karai, oñeha’ã rire en vano ojoko hembireko reko vai, ipahápe oheja hague chupe umi mar de vida ári; ha katu umi áño ohasávape, oñearrepenti upe paso-gui, oinstitui peteĩ acción ojerrekupera haĝua iposesión hese. Erskine oĩkuri ambue lado-pe; ha upéi oipytyvõ he’ívo, jepe pe karai iñepyrũmbýpe oarpona pe kuñakaraípe, ha peteĩ jey o’ajuna, ha pe tuicha estrés orekóvare añoite pe vicioso oñembohypýiva, ipahápe oheja hague chupe; jepeve oheja chupe ha’e ojapo, upéicha oiko chugui peteĩ pira suelto; ha upévare peteĩ karai oúva upe rire oarpón jeývo chupe, upéi pe kuñakaraigui oiko upe karai oúva upe rire mba’e, oimeraẽ arpón ndive ikatúva ojejuhu ojepysóva ipype.

Ko'ágã ko káso presente Erskine ocontende umi ejemplo ballena ha kuñakarai ha'éva recíprocamente ilustrativa ojuehe.

Ko’ã pleito, ha umi contra pleito, oñehendúvo debidamente, pe juez iñaranduetereíva término establecido-pe odesidi, ha’éva,—Pe barco rehegua katu, ome’ẽha umi demandante-pe, ohejágui hikuái osalva haĝuánte hekove; ha katu pe ballena polémica, arpón ha línea rehegua, ha’eha umi acusado mba’e; pe ballena, ha’égui peteĩ Pira Suelto ojejapyhy paha jave; ha umi arpón ha línea porque pe pira osẽvo hendivekuéra, ha’e (pe pira) ohupyty peteĩ propiedad umi artículo-pe; ha upégui oimeraêva oipyhýva upe rire pira oguereko derecho hesekuéra. Ko'ágã umi acusado upe rire ogueraha pira; ergo, umi artículo oje’eva’ekue ha’ekuri imba’ekuéra.

Peteî kuimba'e común omañáva ko decisión Juez iñaranduetereíva, ikatu posiblemente ombotove. Ha katu oñearado pe ita primario peve pe asunto-pe, umi mokõi principio tuicháva oñemoĩva umi léi gemela caza de ballena-pe oñecitava’ekue yma, ha o’aplika ha omyesakãva Lord Ellenborough pe káso oñecitava’ekuépe yvate; ko’ã mokõi léi opokóva Pira Pya’e ha Pira Suelto rehe, ha’e,, ojepy’amongetávo, ojejuhúta umi mba’e iñimportantevéva opa yvypóra jurisprudencia-pe; pórke jepe ikomplikado rastro de escultura, pe Léi Templo, umi Filistea Temploicha, oreko mokõi apoyo añónte oñemboʼy hag̃ua.

Ndaha'éi piko peteĩ ñe'ẽ opavave jurúpe: Posesión ha'e la mitad de la léi: he'iséva, taha'e ha'éva mba'éichapa oike pe mba'e posesión-pe? Péro heta vése pe posesión haʼe pe léi kompletoite. Mba’épa umi sinedo ha ánga umi siervo ruso ha tembiguái republicano-kuéra ha katu Fast-Fish, upévagui pe posesión ha’e pe léi tuichakue? Mba’épa pe propietario rapaz-pe ĝuarã ha’e pe viuda ácaro paha ha katu peteĩ Fast-Fish? Mba’épa oĩ upérupi mansión de mármol villano ndojejuhúiva orekóva peteĩ okẽ-chapa peteĩ waif-pe ĝuarã; mba’épa upéva ndaha’éiramo peteĩ Pira Pya’e? Mba’épa pe descuento ruinoso Mardoqueo, pe corredor, ohupytyva’ekue Woebegone mboriahúgui, pe quiebra-pe, peteĩ préstamo-pe ani haĝua omano ñembyahýigui Woebegone familia-pe; mba’épa upe descuento ruinoso ha katu peteĩ Fast-Fish? Mba’épa pe Arzobispo de Savesoul ingreso 100.000 libras esterlinas ojeapresáva pe escaso pan ha kesu cientos de miles de trabajadores de espalda rota-gui (opavave seguro yvágagui mba’eveichagua Savesoul pytyvõ’ỹre) mba’épa upe globular £100.000 ha katu peteĩ Fast-Fish? Mba'épa umi táva ha aldea hereditaria Duque de Dunder-pegua ha katu Fast-Fish? Mba’épa upe arpón ojedudávape ĝuarã, John Bull, ha’e Irlanda mboriahu, ha katu peteĩ Fast-Fish? Mba’épa upe lanzador apostólico, hermano Jonathan-pe ĝuarã, ha’e Texas ha katu peteĩ Fast-Fish? Ha opa koʼã mbaʼére, ¿ndahaʼéi piko pe Posesión pe Léi kompletoite?

Ha katu pe doctrina Fast-Fish rehegua ha’éramo bastante generalmente aplicable, pe doctrina pariente Loose-Fish rehegua ha’e gueteri tuichavéva upéicha. Upéva ojeporu internacional ha universalmente.

Mba’épa ha’e América 1492-pe ha katu peteĩ Loose-Fish, Colón oinupãhápe pe estándar español odenuncia rupi ipatrón ha ipatrón real-pe ĝuarã? Mbaʼépa rakaʼe Polonia pe zar-pe g̃uarã? Mba'e Grecia pe turco-pe? Mba'épa India Inglaterra-pe? Mba’épa ipahápe ha’éta México Estados Unidos-pe ĝuarã? Opavave Pira Suelto.

Mba’épa umi Derecho orekóva Yvypóra ha umi Libertad del Mundo ha katu Pira Suelto? Mba’épa opavave kuimba’e apytu’ũ ha opinión ndaha’éiramo Loose-Fish? Mba’épa pe principio jerovia religiosa hesekuéra ha katu peteĩ Pira Suelto? ¿Mba'e umi ostentoso contrabando verbalista-pe guarã ha'e umi pensamiento umi pensador pero Loose-Fish? Mba’épa pe globo tuicháva voi ndaha’éiramo peteĩ Pira Suelto? Ha mba’épa nde, moñe’ẽhára, ha katu peteĩ Pira Suelto ha peteĩ Pira Pya’e, avei?





MOAKÃHA 90. Akã térã jyva.

“De balena vero suficiente, si rex habeat caput, ha regina caudam”. Bracton, l. Ñe’ẽpoty ha ñe’ẽpoty. 3, c. 3. Ñe’ẽpoty ryru.

Latín umi aranduka Léi Inglaterra-pegua, ojegueraháva contexto ndive, he’ise, opaite ballena ojagarráva oimeraẽva upe yvy rembe’ýpe, Mburuvicha guasu, Gran Arponero Honorario ramo, oguerekova’erã iñakã, ha Rréina ha’eva’erã respeto reheve oñepresenta pe jyva reheve. Peteĩ división ha’éva, pe ballena-pe, ojoguávaiterei peteĩ manzana oñemboja’óva la mitad-pe; ndaipóri hembýva mbytépe. Ko'ágã ko léi, forma modificada guýpe, ko'ágã peve oî vigente Inglaterra-pe; ha oikuave’ẽháicha opáichagua mba’épe peteĩ anomalía extraña opokóva léi general Fast and Loose-Fish rehe, ko’ápe oñeñe’ẽ peteĩ capítulo aparte-pe, pe principio cortés-pe voi omomýiva umi ferrocarril ingles-pe oĩ haĝua peteĩ auto aparte costo-pe , especialmente reservado oñemohenda haguã umi realeza. Primero, prueba curiosa ramo pe léi yvate oñeñe’ẽva’ekue oĩha gueteri en vigor, aprocede amoĩ haĝua pene renondépe peteĩ circunstancia oikova’ekue ko’ã mokõi áño ohasava’ekuépe.

Ha’ete ku oĩva umi marinero honesto Dover-gua, térã Sandwich-gua, térã peteĩva umi Puerto Cinque-gua, peteĩ persecución mbarete rire osẽ porã ojuka ha omoĩ playa-pe peteĩ ballena porã ha’ekuéra iñepyrũmbýpe odescribíva mombyry pe orilla-gui. Ko'ágã umi Puerto Cinque oime parcialmente térã algún modo jurisdicción peteî especie de policía térã cuenta, ojeheróva Lord Warden. Omotenondévo oficina directamente corona guive, che arovia, opavave emolumento real incidente umi territorio Puerto Cinque-pe oiko por asignación imba'éva. Oĩ haihára ko ofisína ojeheróva sinecura. Péro ndahaʼéi upéicha. Pe Lord Warden omba’apo haguére hembiapo heta sapy’ánte ombopu haĝua iperquisitokuéra; ha’éva imba’éva principalmente upe fobbing peteĩchagua rupive.

Ko’áğa ko’ã marinero mboriahu kuarahy’ãgui, ipy’a’ỹva ha ikalsonsillo ojeroky yvate ipy eely-pe, ikane’õme ogueraha yvate ha iseko ipira ikyráva, oprometévo ijehe 150 libra porã pe aceite ha kangue hepyetereívagui; ha fantasía-pe o’u té ndahetáiva hembireko ndive, ha cerveza porã ikompañerokuéra ndive, imbarete rupi ipartekuéra; ojupi peteĩ karai iñaranduetereíva ha cristiano ha caritativovéva, orekóva peteĩ copia Blackstone-gui ijyva guýpe; ha omoĩvo pe ballena akã ári, he’i —“¡Po! ko pira, che jára, ha’e peteĩ Pira Pya’e. Che ajagarra pe Lord Warden mba’éicha”. Péva ári umi marinero mboriahu iñemondýi respetuoso-pe —upéicha añetehápe inglés —ndoikuaáigui mba’épa he’íta, ho’a oñakãity kyre’ỹme opa ijerére; upe aja ñembyasýpe omañávo pe ballena-gui pe extraño-pe. Ha katu upéva mba’eveichavérõ nomyatyrõi pe asunto, térã mba’eveichavérõ ombopiro’y pe karai arandu korasõ hatãva Blackstone copia reheve. Ipahápe peteĩva ijapytepekuéra, are rire oñakãity iñe’ẽre, ipy’aguasu oñe’ẽ haĝua,

Peteĩ ñe’ẽme, ojejagarra ha oñevende pe ballena, ha iGracia Duque de Wellington ohupyty pe pirapire. Opensávo ojehecháramo algunas luces particulares-pe, pe káso ikatu por una posibilidad desnuda en algún grado michĩva oñeconsidera, umi circunstancia-pe, peteĩ mba’e hasy’ỹva, peteĩ clérigo honesto upe távapegua ombohasa respeto reheve peteĩ nota iGracia-pe, ojerure’asývo chupe ogueraha haĝua pe káso umi marinero desafortunado en plena consideración. Upévape ombohovái che Ruvicha Duque en sustancia (mokõive kuatiañe’ẽ oñemoherakuã) ojapóma hague upéva, ha ohupyty hague pe pirapire, ha oîta obligado pe caballero reverendo-pe tenonderãme ĝuarã ha’e (pe caballero reverendo) ombotovéramo oñemboja ambue tapicha mba’épe ñemuharenda. ¿Kóva piko pe karai tuja militante gueteri, oñembo’ýva umi mbohapy rréino esquina-pe, opa ipo rehe oobligáva limosna umi mendigo-kuérape?

Tekotevê ñaporandu upéi mba'e principio-pepa oñeinverti iñepyrûhápe Soberano upe derecho reheve. Pe léi voi oñemoĩma. Péro Plowdon omeʼẽ ñandéve mbaʼérepa upéva. Plowdon heʼi pe ballena ojejapyhýva upéicha haʼeha pe Rréi ha Rréina mbaʼe, “iporãiterei rupi”. Ha umi comentarista hekoporãvéva rupive kóva oreko peteĩ argumento convincente koʼãichagua mbaʼépe.

Ha katu, ¿ha’épa pe Rréina peteĩ sirena, oñepresenta haĝua peteĩ cola reheve? Ikatu oñemo’ã ko’ápe peteĩ significado alegórico.

Oĩ mokõi pira mburuvicha guasu péicha oestilo umi léi inglés ohaíva —pe ballena ha esturión; mokõive propiedad real oîva ciertas limitaciones, ha nominalmente ome'êva décima rama ingreso ordinario corona-pe. Ndaikuaái ambue autor oinsinua hague pe asunto; ha katu inferencia rupive ha’ete chéve pe esturión ojedividiva’erãha pe ballena-icha, pe Rey orrecibívo pe iñakã denso ha elástico-itereíva peculiar upe pira-pe ĝuarã, ha’éva, simbólicamente ojehecháva, ikatu posiblemente oñemopyenda chistosamente algún presumida congenialidad-pe. Ha péicha ha’ete ku oĩva peteĩ razón opa mba’épe, léipe jepe.





MOAKÃHA 91. Pe Pequod Ojojuhu Pe Rosa-Bud ndive.

“En vano ha'e kuri rastrillo Ambergriese-pe guarã paunch ko Leviatán-pe, insoportable fetor onega ndaha'éi consulta”. Karai T. Browne, VE., ha ambuekuéra

Ha’ékuri peteĩ térã mokõi semana pe escena de ballena paha omombe’u rire, ha mbeguekatúpe ro’vela jave peteĩ mar oke, vapor, ára mbytépe, umi heta nariz oĩva Pequod cubierta-pe ohechauka descubridor vigilanteve umi mbohapy par de tesa yvateguágui. Peteĩ hyakuã peculiar ha ndaha’éiva iporãitereíva hyakuã pe mar-pe.

“Apostáta peteĩ mba’e ko’áğa”, he’i Stubb, “algún lugar ko’ápe oĩha algunas de esas ballenas drogadas ro’cosquillas ambue árape. Che apensa vaʼekue haʼekuéra okypitaha are rire”.

Ko’áĝaite, umi vapor de antemano ojedesliza peteĩ lado-pe; ha upépe mombyry oĩ peteĩ várko, ivela ojerrevolvéva ohechauka oiméne oĩha ijykére algún tipo de ballena. Roñemo’aguĩvo, pe extraño ohechauka umi color francés ipico guive; ha pe arai osyryrýva guyra marina buitre rehegua ojeréva, ha ojere ha ojere ha ojere hese, ojekuaa porã pe ballena oĩva ijykére ha’eva’erãha pe umi pescador ohenóiva ballena ojejapíva, he’iséva, peteĩ ballena omanóva nomolesta’ỹre pe mar ári , ha upéicha oveve peteĩ te’õngue noñeapropiáiva. Ikatu oñekonsepáma porã, mba’eichaitépa hyakuã vaiete peichagua masa oexhalava’erã; ivaivéva peteĩ táva asirio-gui oĩva pe plágape, umi oikovéva ndaikatúi jave oñotỹ umi omanóvape. Añetehápe intolerableiterei ningo oĩ ohechava, ni peteĩ cupididad ndaikatúiva okonvense chupekuéra oamarre haĝua ijykére. Upéicharõ jepe oĩ umi ojapótava gueteri; jepémo pe aceite ojehupytýva ko’ãichagua sujeto-gui ha’e peteĩ calidad inferior-itereíva, ha ni mba’evéicharõ ndaha’éi pe naturaleza attar-de-rosa-gui.

Roĝuahẽvo gueteri pe yvytu ovenséva ndive, rohecha pe francés orekoha peteĩ ballena mokõiha ijykére; ha ko ballena mokõiha ha’ete voi peteĩ nosegayvéva pe peteĩhagui. Añetehápe, ojekuaa ha’eha peteĩ umi ballena problemática ha’etévaicha iseko ha omanóva peteĩ especie de dispepsia prodigiosa, térã indigestión reheve; ohejávo hete omanóva haimete enteramente quiebra-pe oimeraê mba'e ojoguáva petróleo-pe. Upéicharõ jepe, hendaitépe jahecháta ndaiporiha pescador oikuaáva araka’eve ombojeremo’ãiha ijyva peteĩ ballena-pe péicha, jepe ikatu omboyke umi ballena ojejapívagui en general.

MOAKÃHA 92. Ambergris rehegua.

Ko’áğa ko ambergris ha’e peteĩ sustancia curiosoitereíva, ha iñimportanteterei peteĩ artículo de comercio-icha, upévare 1791-pe peteĩ cierto Capitán Coffin nacido Nantucket-pe oñehesa’ỹijo abogado Cámara de Comunas inglesa-pe upe tema rehe. Pórke upe tiémpope, ha añetehápe peteĩ ára comparativamente tarde peve, pe origen preciso orekóva pe ambergris opyta, pe ámbar-icha voi, peteĩ provléma umi iñarandúvape g̃uarã. Jepémo pe ñe’ẽ ambergris ha’e pe francés ñe’ẽme’ẽnte ámbar gris rehegua, upéicharõ jepe umi mokõi sustancia idiferenteterei. Pórke pe ámbar, sapyʼánte ojejuhúramo jepe pe mar rembeʼýpe, ojejoʼo avei unos kuánto yvy mombyry oĩvape hyepýpe, péro arakaʼeve ndojejuhúi pe ambergris ndahaʼéiramo pe mar ári. Avei, ámbar ha’e peteĩ mba’e hatã, hesakãva, frágil, hyakuã’ỹva, ojeporúva juru-pe guarã tubo-pe, cuenta ha adorno-pe g̃uarã; ha katu pe ambergris ningo ipiroʼy, ipiroʼy ha hyakuã ha ipiroʼyeterei, upévare ojepuruve ojejapo hag̃ua perfumería, umi pastil, vela iporãitereívape, umi akãrague polvo ha pomatum-pe. Turco-kuéra oipuru tembi'urã, ha avei ogueraha La Meca-pe, upevarã ojegueraha incienso San Pedro-pe, Rómape. Oĩ víno ñemuhára oityva’ekue mbovymi grano claret-pe, ombosa’y haĝua.

¡Máva piko oimo’ãta upéicharõ umi kuñakarai ha karai porãite oñembovy’ava’erãha peteĩ esencia ojejuhúva peteĩ ballena hasýva intestino inglorioso-pe! Upéicharõ jepe upéicha. Oĩ he’íva ambergris ha’eha pe káusa, ha ambue katu pe efecto, pe dispepsia oĩva ballena-pe. Mba’éichapa oñepohano ko’ãichagua dispepsia hasy ja’e haĝua, ndaha’éiramo oñeme’ẽvo mbohapy térã irundy carga barco-pe umi pastilla Brandreth rehegua, ha upéi opa mba’e vai, ojapoháicha umi mba’apohára ombokapúvo ita.

Cheresarái ha’e haĝua ojejuhu hague ko ambergris-pe, ciertas chapa hatã, redonda ha hueso, ñepyrũrã Stubb oimo’ãva ikatuha ha’e umi marinero pantalón botón; ha katu upe rire ojekuaa ndaha’eiha mba’eve ndaha’éirõ umi calamar kangue michĩva pehẽngue oñeembalsamáva upéicha.

Ko’ágã ojejuhuva’erã ko ambergris hyakuãvéva incorrupción ko’ãichagua decadencia korasõme; ¿ndaha’éi piko kóva mba’eve? Epensamína nde Pablo Marangatu he’iva’ekuére Corintios-pe, oñembyaíva ha oñembyai’ỹvare; Mba'éicha piko ñañeñotỹ ñemotĩme, ha ñañemopu'ã ñemomba'eguasúpe. Ha upéicha avei ñanemandu’a Paracelso he’ívare mba’épa ha’e pe ojapóva almizcle iporãvéva. Avei ani ñanderesarái pe hecho extraño opa mba’e mal sabor-gui, Colonia-y, umi etapa de fabricación rudimental-pe, ha’eha pe ivaivéva.

I should like to conclude the chapter with the above appeal, but cannot, owing to my anxiety to repel a charge often made against whalemen, and which, in the estimation of some already biased minds, might be considered as indirectly substantiated by what has been said of the Frenchman's two whales. Elsewhere in this volume the slanderous aspersion has been disproved, that the vocation of whaling is throughout a slatternly, untidy business. But there is another thing to rebut. They hint that all whales always smell bad. Now how did this odious stigma originate?

I opine, that it is plainly traceable to the first arrival of the Greenland whaling ships in London, more than two centuries ago. Because those whalemen did not then, and do not now, try out their oil at sea as the Southern ships have always done; but cutting up the fresh blubber in small bits, thrust it through the bung holes of large casks, and carry it home in that manner; the shortness of the season in those Icy Seas, and the sudden and violent storms to which they are exposed, forbidding any other course. The consequence is, that upon breaking into the hold, and unloading one of these whale cemeteries, in the Greenland dock, a savor is given forth somewhat similar to that arising from excavating an old city grave-yard, for the foundations of a Lying-in Hospital.

I partly surmise also, that this wicked charge against whalers may be likewise imputed to the existence on the coast of Greenland, in former times, of a Dutch village called Schmerenburgh or Smeerenberg, which latter name is the one used by the learned Fogo Von Slack, in his great work on Smells, a text-book on that subject. As its name imports (smeer, fat; berg, to put up), this village was founded in order to afford a place for the blubber of the Dutch whale fleet to be tried out, without being taken home to Holland for that purpose. It was a collection of furnaces, fat-kettles, and oil sheds; and when the works were in full operation certainly gave forth no very pleasant savor. But all this is quite different with a South Sea Sperm Whaler; which in a voyage of four years perhaps, after completely filling her hold with oil, does not, perhaps, consume fifty days in the business of boiling out; and in the state that it is casked, the oil is nearly scentless. The truth is, that living or dead, if but decently treated, whales as a species are by no means creatures of ill odor; nor can whalemen be recognised, as the people of the middle ages affected to detect a Jew in the company, by the nose. Nor indeed can the whale possibly be otherwise than fragrant, when, as a general thing, he enjoys such high health; taking abundance of exercise; always out of doors; though, it is true, seldom in the open air. I say, that the motion of a Sperm Whale's flukes above water dispenses a perfume, as when a musk-scented lady rustles her dress in a warm parlor. What then shall I liken the Sperm Whale to for fragrance, considering his magnitude? Must it not be to that famous elephant, with jewelled tusks, and redolent with myrrh, which was led out of an Indian town to do honor to Alexander the Great?





CHAPTER 93. The Castaway.

It was but some few days after encountering the Frenchman, that a most significant event befell the most insignificant of the Pequod's crew; an event most lamentable; and which ended in providing the sometimes madly merry and predestinated craft with a living and ever accompanying prophecy of whatever shattered sequel might prove her own.

Now, in the whale ship, it is not every one that goes in the boats. Some few hands are reserved called ship-keepers, whose province it is to work the vessel while the boats are pursuing the whale. As a general thing, these ship-keepers are as hardy fellows as the men comprising the boats' crews. But if there happen to be an unduly slender, clumsy, or timorous wight in the ship, that wight is certain to be made a ship-keeper. It was so in the Pequod with the little negro Pippin by nick-name, Pip by abbreviation. Poor Pip! ye have heard of him before; ye must remember his tambourine on that dramatic midnight, so gloomy-jolly.

In outer aspect, Pip and Dough-Boy made a match, like a black pony and a white one, of equal developments, though of dissimilar colour, driven in one eccentric span. But while hapless Dough-Boy was by nature dull and torpid in his intellects, Pip, though over tender-hearted, was at bottom very bright, with that pleasant, genial, jolly brightness peculiar to his tribe; a tribe, which ever enjoy all holidays and festivities with finer, freer relish than any other race. For blacks, the year's calendar should show naught but three hundred and sixty-five Fourth of Julys and New Year's Days. Nor smile so, while I write that this little black was brilliant, for even blackness has its brilliancy; behold yon lustrous ebony, panelled in king's cabinets. But Pip loved life, and all life's peaceable securities; so that the panic-striking business in which he had somehow unaccountably become entrapped, had most sadly blurred his brightness; though, as ere long will be seen, what was thus temporarily subdued in him, in the end was destined to be luridly illumined by strange wild fires, that fictitiously showed him off to ten times the natural lustre with which in his native Tolland County in Connecticut, he had once enlivened many a fiddler's frolic on the green; and at melodious even-tide, with his gay ha-ha! had turned the round horizon into one star-belled tambourine. So, though in the clear air of day, suspended against a blue-veined neck, the pure-watered diamond drop will healthful glow; yet, when the cunning jeweller would show you the diamond in its most impressive lustre, he lays it against a gloomy ground, and then lights it up, not by the sun, but by some unnatural gases. Then come out those fiery effulgences, infernally superb; then the evil-blazing diamond, once the divinest symbol of the crystal skies, looks like some crown-jewel stolen from the King of Hell. But let us to the story.

It came to pass, that in the ambergris affair Stubb's after-oarsman chanced so to sprain his hand, as for a time to become quite maimed; and, temporarily, Pip was put into his place.

The first time Stubb lowered with him, Pip evinced much nervousness; but happily, for that time, escaped close contact with the whale; and therefore came off not altogether discreditably; though Stubb observing him, took care, afterwards, to exhort him to cherish his courageousness to the utmost, for he might often find it needful.

Now upon the second lowering, the boat paddled upon the whale; and as the fish received the darted iron, it gave its customary rap, which happened, in this instance, to be right under poor Pip's seat. The involuntary consternation of the moment caused him to leap, paddle in hand, out of the boat; and in such a way, that part of the slack whale line coming against his chest, he breasted it overboard with him, so as to become entangled in it, when at last plumping into the water. That instant the stricken whale started on a fierce run, the line swiftly straightened; and presto! poor Pip came all foaming up to the chocks of the boat, remorselessly dragged there by the line, which had taken several turns around his chest and neck.

Tashtego stood in the bows. He was full of the fire of the hunt. He hated Pip for a poltroon. Snatching the boat-knife from its sheath, he suspended its sharp edge over the line, and turning towards Stubb, exclaimed interrogatively, “Cut?” Meantime Pip's blue, choked face plainly looked, Do, for God's sake! All passed in a flash. In less than half a minute, this entire thing happened.

“Damn him, cut!” roared Stubb; and so the whale was lost and Pip was saved.

So soon as he recovered himself, the poor little negro was assailed by yells and execrations from the crew. Tranquilly permitting these irregular cursings to evaporate, Stubb then in a plain, business-like, but still half humorous manner, cursed Pip officially; and that done, unofficially gave him much wholesome advice. The substance was, Never jump from a boat, Pip, except—but all the rest was indefinite, as the soundest advice ever is. Now, in general, Stick to the boat , is your true motto in whaling; but cases will sometimes happen when Leap from the boat , is still better. Moreover, as if perceiving at last that if he should give undiluted conscientious advice to Pip, he would be leaving him too wide a margin to jump in for the future; Stubb suddenly dropped all advice, and concluded with a peremptory command, “Stick to the boat, Pip, or by the Lord, I won't pick you up if you jump; mind that. We can't afford to lose whales by the likes of you; a whale would sell for thirty times what you would, Pip, in Alabama. Bear that in mind, and don't jump any more.” Hereby perhaps Stubb indirectly hinted, that though man loved his fellow, yet man is a money-making animal, which propensity too often interferes with his benevolence.

But we are all in the hands of the Gods; and Pip jumped again. It was under very similar circumstances to the first performance; but this time he did not breast out the line; and hence, when the whale started to run, Pip was left behind on the sea, like a hurried traveller's trunk. Alas! Stubb was but too true to his word. It was a beautiful, bounteous, blue day; the spangled sea calm and cool, and flatly stretching away, all round, to the horizon, like gold-beater's skin hammered out to the extremest. Bobbing up and down in that sea, Pip's ebon head showed like a head of cloves. No boat-knife was lifted when he fell so rapidly astern. Stubb's inexorable back was turned upon him; and the whale was winged. In three minutes, a whole mile of shoreless ocean was between Pip and Stubb. Out from the centre of the sea, poor Pip turned his crisp, curling, black head to the sun, another lonely castaway, though the loftiest and the brightest.

Now, in calm weather, to swim in the open ocean is as easy to the practised swimmer as to ride in a spring-carriage ashore. But the awful lonesomeness is intolerable. The intense concentration of self in the middle of such a heartless immensity, my God! who can tell it? Mark, how when sailors in a dead calm bathe in the open sea—mark how closely they hug their ship and only coast along her sides.

But had Stubb really abandoned the poor little negro to his fate? No; he did not mean to, at least. Because there were two boats in his wake, and he supposed, no doubt, that they would of course come up to Pip very quickly, and pick him up; though, indeed, such considerations towards oarsmen jeopardized through their own timidity, is not always manifested by the hunters in all similar instances; and such instances not unfrequently occur; almost invariably in the fishery, a coward, so called, is marked with the same ruthless detestation peculiar to military navies and armies.

But it so happened, that those boats, without seeing Pip, suddenly spying whales close to them on one side, turned, and gave chase; and Stubb's boat was now so far away, and he and all his crew so intent upon his fish, that Pip's ringed horizon began to expand around him miserably. By the merest chance the ship itself at last rescued him; but from that hour the little negro went about the deck an idiot; such, at least, they said he was. The sea had jeeringly kept his finite body up, but drowned the infinite of his soul. Not drowned entirely, though. Rather carried down alive to wondrous depths, where strange shapes of the unwarped primal world glided to and fro before his passive eyes; and the miser-merman, Wisdom, revealed his hoarded heaps; and among the joyous, heartless, ever-juvenile eternities, Pip saw the multitudinous, God-omnipresent, coral insects, that out of the firmament of waters heaved the colossal orbs. He saw God's foot upon the treadle of the loom, and spoke it; and therefore his shipmates called him mad. So man's insanity is heaven's sense; and wandering from all mortal reason, man comes at last to that celestial thought, which, to reason, is absurd and frantic; and weal or woe, feels then uncompromised, indifferent as his God.

For the rest, blame not Stubb too hardly. The thing is common in that fishery; and in the sequel of the narrative, it will then be seen what like abandonment befell myself.
