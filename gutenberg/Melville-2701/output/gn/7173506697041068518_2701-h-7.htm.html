MOAKÃHA 33. Pe Specksnyder rehegua.

Oñe'êvo umi oficial artesanía ballena-gua, kóva ha'ete iporãva tenda oimeraêvaicha omoî haguã peteî peculiaridad doméstica michîmi barco-board-pe, heñóiva oîgui clase de oficiales arponeer, clase ndojekuaáiva por supuesto ambue marina-pe ndaha'éiva pe ballena-flota rehegua.

Tuicha importancia oñeme’ẽva arpón vocación rehe ojehechauka pe hecho rupive, iñepyrũmbýpe Pesca Holandesa tujápe, ojapo mokõi siglo ha hetave, pe comando peteĩ barco ballena rehegua noñemoĩri enteramente pe tapicha ko’áĝa oñehenóiva capitán-pe, ha katu oñemboja’o ijapytépe ha’e ha peteĩ oficial hérava Specksnyder. Literalmente ko ñe’ẽ he’ise Fat-Cutter; jeporu katu, tiempo ohasávo ojapo chugui equivalente Chief Harpooneer-pe. Umi árape, capitán autoridad ojejoko navegación ha gestión general embarcación-pe; ha katu pe departamento de caza de ballenas ha opa mba’e oipy’apýva hese, pe Specksnyder térã Chief Harpooneer oisãmbyhy yvate. Pesca Británica Groenlandia-pe, pe título corrupto Specksioneer rupive, ko funcionario holandés tuja ojeguereko gueteri, ha katu ñembyasýpe oñemboty idignidad yma guare. Ko’áĝa ha’e oñemohenda simplemente Harpooneer senior ramo; ha upéicha, ha’e peteĩnte umi subalterno inferiorvéva capitán-pe. Upéicharõ jepe, umi arpón conductor porã reheguáicha pe éxito peteĩ viaje de ballena caza-pe tuicha odepende, ha pe Pesca Americana-pe ndaha’éigui peteĩ oficial importante añónte pe barco-pe, ha katu ciertas circunstancias-pe (ojesareko pyhare peteĩ ballena caza-pe) pe comando pe barco cubierta-gui ha’e avei imba’e; upévare pe gran máxima política mar-pegua ojerure, ha’e oiko haĝua nominalmente aparte umi kuimba’e mástil mboyveguágui, ha de alguna manera ojedistinguíva isuperior profesional ramo; jepe akóinte, ha’ekuéra rupive, ojehecha familiarmente ijoja social ramo.

Ko’áğa, pe gran distinción ojedibujáva oficial ha kuimba’e apytépe mar-pe, ha’e kóva —pe primero oiko popa, pe último delantero. Upévare, umi várko de ballena ha umi komersiántepe, umi ikompañero oreko icuartel pe kapitán ndive; ha upéicha avei, la mayoría umi ballena cazador norteamericano-pe umi arpónero opyta pe barco pehẽngue rireguápe. He'iséva, ogueraha hikuái hembi'u capitán cabaña-pe, ha oke peteî tendápe indirectamente oñe'êva hendive.

Jepémo pe periodo puku peteĩ viaje de ballenas Sur-pegua (mombyry ipukuvéva opa viaje ko’áĝa térã araka’eve yvypóra ojapova’ekuégui), umi peligro peculiar upévagui, ha pe comunidad de interés oñemomba’eguasúva peteĩ empresa apytépe, opavave, yvate térã ijyvate, odepende umi ganancia orekóvare, ndaha’éi salario fijo rehe, ha katu isuerte común rehe, oñondive pe vigilancia, intrepididad ha tembiapo pohýi común reheve; Jepe opa ko’ã mba’e ojapo sapy’ánte oreko tendencia omoheñói haĝua peteĩ disciplina menos rigurosa umi comerciante-gui en general; jepémo upéicha, araka’eve noimportái mba’éichapa ojogua peteĩ familia mesopotamia tujápe ko’ã ballena-kuéra ikatu, algunas instancias primitivas-pe, oiko oñondive; opa umívape ĝuarã, umi externo puntilio, por lo menos, pe cuarto cubierta-pegua sa’i oñembopiro’y materialmente, ha ni peteĩ instancia-pe ndojejapói. Añetehápe, heta ha’e umi barco Nantucket-pegua pehechátahápe pe capitán odesfila icuarto-cubierta peteĩ grandeza elocita reheve ndojehasáiva mba’eveichagua armada militar-pe; nahániri, oextorsionávo haimete heta homenaje okáguio oipurúvaicha pe púrpura imperial, ha ndaha’éi pe tela piloto-gui iky’avéva.

Ha jepe opa kuimba’égui pe capitán ipy’aporãva Pequod-pegua ha’e pe sa’ive oñeme’ẽva upe clase de suposición ijyvyku’ivévape; ha jepe pe único homenaje ha’e oexigiva’ekue, ha’e pe obediencia implícita, instantánea; jepe ndojeruréi avavépe oipe’a haĝua umi sapatu ipygui opyrũ mboyve pe cuarto cubierta-pe; ha jepe oĩ sapy’a, umi circunstancia peculiar ojoajúva umi acontecimiento ko’águi rire oñedetallava’erã rehe, oñe’ẽ chupekuéra término jepivegua’ỹvape, taha’e condescensión térã terrorem -pe , térã ambue mba’épe; jepémo upéicha, capitán Acab jepe ni mba’evéicharõ ndohechái umi forma ha jeporu iñimportantevéva pe mar-pe.

Ni, oiméne, ndohechakuaamo’ãi amo ipahápe, umi forma ha jeporu rapykuéri, ja’eporãsérõ, sapy’ánte oñemascará hague; casualmente oiporúvo ambue ha hetave fin privado-pe guarã, legítimamente oñeha'ãva'ekuégui osubservi. Upe cierto sultanismo iñakãme, ambue hendáicha peteĩ grado porãme opyta ndojehechái; umi forma rupive upe sultanismo voi oñembohete peteĩ dictadura irresistible-pe. Ha’égui peteĩ kuimba’e superioridad intelectual mba’épa oipota, araka’eve ndaikatúi o’asumi pe supremacía práctica, ojeguerekóva ambue kuimba’ekuéra ári, oipytyvõ’ỹre algún tipo de arte ha atrincheramiento externo, akóinte, ijehegui, hetave térã sa’ive insignificante ha base. Péva ha’e, oñongatúva tapiaite ĝuarã Ñandejára príncipe añetegua Imperio-pegua umi hustings mundo-peguagui; ha oheja umi honor ijyvatevéva ikatúva ome’ẽ ko aire, umi kuimba’e herakuãvévape inferioridad infinita rupive pe puñado kañymby ojeporavóva pe Divina Inerte-gui, pe superioridad indudable rupive pe nivel omanóva masa reheguágui. Péicha virtud tuicháva oime al acecho ko'ã mba'e michîmívape umi superstición política extrema oinvertívo, oî instancia real-pe jepe idiota imbecilidad-pe ome'ê potencia. Ha katu, Nicolás zar kásopeguáicha, pe corona anillo imperio geográfico rehegua ojerévo peteĩ cerebro imperial rehe; upéi, umi rebaño plebeo oñembo’y pe tremenda centralización renondépe. Ni, pe trágico dramaturgo ohechaukátava indomableidad mortal barrido completo ha columpio directo-pe, araka’eve ndahesaráimo’ãi peteĩ pista-gui, por casualidad iñimportantetereíva hi’arte-pe, pe ko’áğa oñeñe’ẽva hese.

Ha katu Acab, che Capitán, omýi gueteri che renondépe opa i Nantucket ñembyasy ha ikyrỹivape; ha ko episodio opokóva Emperador ha Reyes rehe, namokañýiva’erã arekoha peteĩ mba’e peteĩ ballena-cazador tuja mboriahu ha’eichagua ndive añoite; ha, upévare, opa umi trampa ha óga majestuoso okáguio oñenega chéve. ¡Oh, Acab! mba’épa tuicha mba’éta ndepype, tekotevẽ ojeipe’a yvágagui, ha oñembohypýi pypukúpe, ha ojehechauka pe yvytu hete’ỹvape!





MOAKÃHA 34. Pe Cabina-Mesa rehegua.

Ha’e asajepyte; ha Dough-Boy, pe mayordomo, omombo hova mbujape morotĩva pe cabaña-scuttle-gui, oikuaauka cena ijára ha ijárape; Mávapa, oguapýva cuarto de Lee-pe, ogueraha ramoite peteĩ observación kuarahy rehegua; ha ko’áğa oconta iñe’ẽngúpe pe latitud pe tabla lisova ha orekóva forma de medallón-pe, oñeñongatúva upe propósito ára ha ára-pe ĝuarã pe parte yvategua ipy marfil-gui. Pe noñatendéiguiete pe notísiare, repensáta Acab ipyʼaporãva nohendúi hague pe ivaíva. Ha katu ko’áĝaite, ojagarrávo umi sudario mizen-gui, oñembo’y pe cubierta-pe, ha peteĩ iñe’ẽ joja ha ndovy’áivape, he’ívo: “Cena, karai Starbuck”, okañy pe cabaña-pe.

Omanómarõ pe eco paha isultán paso-gui, ha Starbuck, pe primer Emir, oreko opa razón oimo’ã haĝua oguapyha, upéi Starbuck opu’ã ikirirĩhágui, ojapo mbovymi giro umi tablón pukukue, ha, peteĩ sepulcro jesareko rire Pe binacle-pe, he’i, algún toque de agradable reheve, “Cena, karai Stubb”, ha oguejy pe scuttle. Pe segundo Emir opytu’u sapy’ami pe aparejo rehe, ha upéi ombotyryry’imi pe corchete principal, ohecha haĝua oĩ porãtapa upe soga importante reheve, ha’e avei ojagarra pe carga tuja, ha peteĩ “Cena, karai Matraz” pya’e reheve. osegui umi antecesor-kuéra rire.

Ha katu pe mbohapyha Emir, ko’áğa ohechávo ha’eñoite pe cuarto de cubierta-pe, ha’ete oñeñandúva aliviado algún restricción curiosa-gui; pórke, ombojere opáichagua guiño oikuaáva opáichagua dirección-pe, ha oipe’ávo isapatu, oinupã peteĩ tormenta haimbe ha katu ndorekóiva ruido peteĩ bocina-gui pe Gran Turco akã ári voi; ha upéi, peteĩ trineo hábil rupive, omoĩvo igorro yvate pe mizentop-pe peteĩ estante-rã, oguejy rollicking mombyry por lo menos opyta aja ojehecha pe cubierta-gui, ombojere opa ambue procesión, oguerúvo yvate pe trasero música reheve. Ha katu ere oike pe cabina rokẽme oĩva iguýpe, opyta’imi, omondo peteĩ hova pyahu en conjunto, ha, upéi, Matraz michĩ independiente ha hilarante oike rréi Acab renondépe, Abjectus térã Tembiguái carácter-pe.

Ndaha’éi pe michĩvéva umi mba’e iñextraño apytépe omoheñóiva pe intensa artificialidad umi jeporu marítimo-pe, oĩ aja aire abierto cubierta-pe oĩta oficial, provocación jave, oaguanta py’aguasúpe ha desafiantemente ikomandante gotyo; jepeve, diez a uno, toguejy umi oficialite voi ambue momento-pe okaru jepiguáicha upe comandante cabaña-pe voi, ha pya’e inofensivo, ani haĝua ja’e aire deprecatorio ha humilde hese, oguapy aja mesa akãme; Kóva ha’e maravilloso, sapy’ánte cómico-véva. Mba’ére piko ko diferencia? ¿Peteĩ provléma? Ikatu ndaha’éi. Ha'e hague Belsasar, Babilonia ruvicha guasu; ha ha’égui Belsasar, ndaha’éi orgulloso ha katu cortésmente, katuete oĩkuri upépe algún toque de grandeza mundana. Ha katu pe espíritu hendaitépe real ha inteligente-pe opresidíva imesa de cena privada propia invitado invitado-kuéra rehegua, upe kuimba’e poder ha dominio indesafiaiva influencia individual rehegua upe época-pe ĝuarã; upe kuimbaʼe rréi de estado ohasa Belsasar-gui, Belsasar ndahaʼéigui pe tuichavéva. Mávapa peteĩ jeýnte okaru iñamigokuérape, oproba mba’épa ha’e César. Ha'e peteî brujería zarismo social ndaipóriva soporte. Ko’áğa, ko consideración-pe resuperlusa ramo pe supremacía oficial peteĩ buque-maestro-pe, upéicharõ, inferencia rupive, rederivata pe causa upe peculiaridad de vida marítima rehegua oñeñe’ẽ ramóva.

Imesa marfil incrustada ári, Acab opresidi peteĩ león marítimo iñe’ẽngúva ha iñakãrague morotĩvaicha pe playa de coral morotĩme, ojeréva imembykuéra ñorairõhárare ha katu oñemomba’eguasúva gueteri. Iturno propio-pe, káda oficial oha’arõ oñeservi chupe. Ha'e kuéra niko mitã'ícha Acab mboyve; ha katu, Acab-pe, ha’ete ku noĩriva’ekue pe arrogancia social michĩvéva. Peteĩ apytu’ũme, hesakuéra intención opavave ojejoko pe karai tuja kyse rehe, otallá aja henondépe pe tembi’u tenondegua. Nda’aimo’ãi ko mundo-pe ĝuarã oprofanta hague hikuái upe momento ni michĩmi observación reheve, jepe peteĩ tema neutral-pe pe tiempo-icha. Nahániri! Ha oipysóvo ikuã ha horquilla, ijapytépe oñemboty pe vaka ro’o rykue, Acab upéicha ojapo seña Starbuck pláto hendápe, pe iména ohupyty iso’o ohupytyramoguáicha limosna; ha oikytĩ tiernamente; ha oñepyrũ michĩmi, ikatu, pe kyse okaru pe pláto rehe; ha oisu’u tyapu’ỹre; ha omokõ, ndaha’éi circunspección’ỹre. Pórke, pe karu guasu Coronación rehegua Frankfort-peguáicha, pe Emperador alemán okaru pypukuhápe umi siete Elector Imperial ndive, upéicha ko’ã tembi’u cabina-pegua ha’eva’ekue de alguna manera tembi’u solemne, oje’úva kirirĩ vaietépe; Ha katu mesa Acab-pe ombotovéva ani haguã ñemongeta; ha’e voi iñe’ẽngúva. Mba’eichaitépa tuicha alivio oestrangulisa Stubb-pe, peteĩ rata ojapo jave peteĩ raqueta sapy’aitépe pe agarre iguýpe. Ha mboriahu Flask michĩ, ha’e ta’ýra imitãvéva, ha mitãkaria’y ko fiesta familiar ikane’õvape. Imba’éva ha’e va’ekue umi shinbones pe vaka ro’o salina rehegua; imba’éva ha’éta kuri umi mbaraka’i. Flask opresumi haguére oipytyvõha ijehe, oiméne ha’ete chupe péva ojoguáva ñemonda primer grado-pe. Oñepytyvõrire upe mesa-pe, katuete, araka’eve ndaikatumo’ãvéimakuri ojoko iñakã yvate ko mundo honesto-pe; upéicharõ jepe, iñextraño ja’e, Acab araka’eve ndoproiviri chupe. Ha Flask oipytyvõrire ijehe, umi posibilidad ha’e Acab araka’eve ndohechakuaáikuri upéva. Sa’ive mba’égui, opresumi piko Flask oipytyvõtaha mantequilla-pe. Oimo’ãpa umi barco jára onega chupe, ocoagula haguére itez hesakã ha kuarahy’ã; térã ohechápa, peteĩ viaje ipukuetereívape ko’ãichagua y ndorekóiva mercado-pe, mantequilla oĩha peteĩ prima-pe, ha upévare ndaha’eiha chupe ĝuarã, peteĩ subalterno; jepémo upéicha ha’e, Matraz, ¡ay! ha’eva’ekue peteĩ kuimba’e mantequilla’ỹva!

Ambue mba’e. Flask ha’e pe último persona oguejy pe cena-pe, ha Flask ha’e pe primer kuimba’e ojupíva. Hechakuaa! Pórke ko’ápe Flask cena ojejaho’i vaieterei punto de tiempo-pe. Starbuck ha Stubb mokõivéva oguereko iñepyrũrã; ha katu oreko avei hikuái privilegio opytu’u haĝua tapykuépe. Stubb jepe, ha’éva peteĩ clavija-nte ijyvatevéva Flask-gui, ojehúramo oguereko peteĩ apetito michĩmínte, ha pya’e ohechauka síntoma omohu’ãvo ikaru, upéicharõ Flask oñembotavyva’erã, ndohupytýi hetave mbohapy juru upe árape; ha’égui en contra jeporu marangatu Stubb oñemotenonde haĝua Flask-pe pe cubierta-pe. Upévare ha’e peteĩ jey Flask oadmiti en privado, opu’ã guive peteĩ oficial dignidad-pe, upe momento guive araka’eve ndoikuaái mba’épa ha’e ambue hendáicha iñembyahýigui, hetave térã sa’ive. Pe ho'úva nomboguevéi iñembyahýi, omantene haguã ipype nomano'ÿi. Py’aguapy ha satisfacción, oimo’ã Flask, tapiaite ĝuarã ojei che ryégui. Che ha’e peteĩ oficial; ha katu, mba’eichaitépa aipotaiterei apirakutu peteĩ vaka ro’o yma guare michĩmi pe forecastle-pe, ajapo haguéicha yma aime jave pe mástil renondépe. Oĩ umi yva promoción rehegua ko'ágã; Oĩ pe vanidad de gloria: ¡Oĩ pe tekove locura! Ko'ágã, Acab ha mbohapy iñirûnguéra omoheñói pe ikatúva oje'e peteîha mesa Pequod cabina-pe. Oho rire hikuái, oikóva orden invertido-pe og̃uahẽ meve hikuái, pe tela de lona oñembogue, térã ja’e porãsérõ, oñemoĩ jey algún orden pya’épe pe mayordomo pálido rupive. En contraste extraño pe constreñimiento apenas tolerable ha umi dominación invisible ndorekóiva téra capitán mesa-pe, ha'e kuri opavave licencia ha facilidad sin cuidado, democracia haimete frenética umi compañero inferior umi arpón-gua. Okaru hikuái karai kuéraicha; omyenyhẽ hikuái ijyva umi barco indio-icha ára pukukue okargáva especia-gui. Ko’ãichagua apetito portentoso oguereko Queequeg ha Tashtego, omyenyhẽ haĝua umi vacante ojapova’ekue pe repaso mboyvegua, heta jey pe masa-boy pálida ha’e fain ogueru haĝua peteĩ barón tuicháva salt-junk rehegua, ha’etévaicha ojeipe’áva pe buey sólido-gui. Ha’e peteĩ mba’e ojehecháva jahecha Queequeg oguapýva Tashtego rovái, ombocháke umi hatĩ archivado pe indio-pe: kurusu gotyo chupekuéra, Daggoo oguapýva yvýpe, peteĩ banco oguerútagui iñakã auto funerario-pe plumado umi carlínea ijyvatévape; opa movimiento umi ipehẽngue colosal-pe, ojapóvo pe marco de cabina bajo oryrýi, ha’ete ku peteĩ elefante africano oho jave pasajero peteĩ barco-pe. Ha katu opa ko’ã mba’ére, pe negro guasu ha’e maravillosamente abstemio, ndaha’éi ja’e haĝua delicioso. Ha’ete ku apenas ikatúva umi juru’i michĩva comparativamente rupive ikatuha omantene pe vitalidad ojedifundiva’ekue peteĩ persona tuichaitereíva, baronial ha iporãitereíva rupive. Capítulo 35. Pe mástil-akã.

Ha’ékuri pe tiempo iporãvéva aja, debida rotación-pe umi ambue marinero ndive che primer mástil-cabeza oĝuahẽ jere.

La mayor parte, ko vida de ballenas trópicas-pe, peteĩ sublime inconveniente peneinverti; napehendúi mba'eveichagua marandu; nomoñe’ẽi mba’eveichagua gaceta; Umi extra orekóva relato sorprendente umi común rehegua araka’eve nde’extrae umi emoción innecesaria-pe; napehendúi mba’eveichagua jehasa’asy ogapypegua; umi valor quiebra-pegua; ho’a umi stock; Araka’eve noĩri ojepy’apývo pe pensamiento mba’épa reguerekóta rekaru haĝua —opavave nde rembi’urã mbohapy áño aja ha hetave mba’e oñemoĩ porã umi barril-pe, ha nde factura de pasaje ha’e inmutable.

Peteĩva umi ballena sur gotyogua, peteĩ viaje pukukue tres térã cuatro áño aja, oikoháicha jepi, pe suma umi diferénte óra rehasáva pe mástil akãme ohupytýta heta mése entero. Nde punto de percha jepiveguavéva ha’e pe t’ gallant-mast akã, reñembo’yhápe mokõi yvyra paralelo ipire hũva ári (haimete peculiar umi ballena-pe ĝuarã) hérava t’ gallant cross-trees. Ko’ápe, oñembotyryrýva mar rembe’ýpe, pe oñepyrũva oñeñandu ipy’aguapýva oñembo’yháicha peteĩ tóro ratĩ ári. Añetehápe, ro’y jave ikatu regueraha nde róga yvate nendive, peteĩ reloj-abrigo-icha; Ha katu oñe’ẽvo hekopete pe vigilancia gruesavéva ndaha’éi peteĩ óga hetave pe tete ndojejokóivagui; pe ánga ojepegaháicha itavernáculo so’o ryepýpe, ha ndaikatúi omýi sãsõme ipype, ni jepe osẽ chugui, tuicha riesgo’ỹre oñehundi haĝua (peteĩ peregrino ignorante ohasávo Alpes nieve-icha ro’y jave); upévare peteĩ reloj-abrigo ndaha’éi peteĩ óga añónte, ha katu peteĩ sobre añónte, térã pire adicional ndembojere. Ndaikatúi remoĩ nde retepýpe peteĩ estante térã cómoda, ha ndaikatuvéima rejapo peteĩ armario conveniente nde vigilancia-gui.

Opa ko’ã mba’ére, heta ñambyasy umi mástil akã peteĩ barco de ballenas sur-pegua noñeme’ẽiha umi tenda térã púlpito michĩ envidiable, hérava cuervo-nido , oñeñangarekohápe umi mirada peteĩ ballena Groenlandia-gua mba’evaígui tiempo umi mar congelado-pe. Pe narrativa tata rembe’ýpe Capitán Sleet-pe, hérava “Peteĩ viaje umi iceberg apytépe, ohekávo pe ballena Groenlandia-pe, ha casualmente ojedescubri jey haĝua umi colonia islandesa okañýva Groenlandia Vieja-pe;” ko volumen admirable-pe, opavave standers de mástil-cabezas oreko peteĩ relato encantador circunstancial pe upérõ nda’aréi oñeinventáva cuervo-nido Glaciar-pe, ha’eva’ekue capitán Sleet artesanía porã réra. Ohenói chupe pe Sleet-pegua Cuervo-Nest , omomba’eguasúvo ijehe; ha’égui pe inventor ha patentado original, ha libre opa delicadeza japu ridícula-gui, ha oguerekóva ñahenóiramo ñane membykuérape ñande réra tee rupive (ñande túva ha’égui umi inventor ha patentado original), upéicha avei ñadenominava’erã ñandejehegui oimeraẽ ambue aparato ikatu ñamoheñói. En forma, pe Sleet’s Crow-Nest ha’e peteĩ mba’e ojoguáva peteĩ nivel tuichávape térã peteĩ tubo-pe; katu ojeabri yvate gotyo, upépe oñemoĩ peteĩ pantalla lateral oñemomýiva ikatu hag̃uáicha yvytu gotyo nde akãgui peteĩ yvytu atãme. Reñemboguapýgui pe mástil ru’ãme, rejupi ipype peteĩ trampa-hatch michĩmi rupive oĩva iguype. Pe lado rire, térã lado pe várko popa ykére, oĩ peteĩ apyka iporãva, ha iguype peteĩ armario oĩ hag̃ua umi paraguas, consolador ha abrigo. Henondépe oĩ peteĩ estante de cuero, ikatuhápe reñongatu nde trompeta oñe’ẽva, tubo, telescopio ha ambue mba’e porã náutico. Capitán Sleet en persona oñembo’ýrõ guare imastil-akã ko cuervo-nido imba’évape, omombe’u ñandéve akóinte oguerekoha hendive peteĩ fusil (oñemohenda avei pe estante-pe), oñondive peteĩ matraz de polvo ha odispara, ojepopete haĝua umi narwhales okañývagui, térã umi unicornio marítimo vagante oinfestáva umi y; Ndaikatúigui redispara porã hesekuéra pe cubierta guive pe y resistencia rupi, ha katu redispara haĝua hesekuéra ha’e peteĩ mba’e iñambuetereíva. Ko’áğa, hesakã porã ha’ékuri peteĩ mborayhu rembiapo capitán Sleet-pe ĝuarã odescribi haĝua, ha’e ojapoháicha, opa umi conveniencia michĩmi detallado icuervo-nido-pe; ha katu jepeve upéicha ombotuichave heta ko’ãva ári, ha jepe ñanetrata peteĩ relato científico-itereívape umi experimento ojapova’ekue ko cuervo-nido-pe, peteĩ brújula michĩva reheve oñongatu upépe ombohovái haĝua umi error oúva pe ojeheróvagui “ atracción local” opavave imán binnacle rehegua; peteĩ error oje’atribuíva pe hierro jerére horizontal oĩvare pe barco tablón-pe, ha pe Glaciar kásope, oiméne, oĩ haguére hetaiterei herrero oñembyaíva itripulación apytépe; Che ha’e, jepe pe Capitán ha’e discreto ha científicoiterei ko’ápe, jepeve, opa umi “desviaciones binnacle” o’aprendeva’ekuére, “observaciones brújula de acimut” ha “error aproximado”, oikuaa porãiterei, Capitán Sleet, ndaha’eiha upéicha heta oñeinmerso umi meditación magnética ipypukúvape, ani haĝua ojeatrae sapy’apy’a upe caja-botella michĩva oñemyenyhẽ porãva gotyo, oñemboty porãitereíva peteĩ lado icuervo nido-pe, ndahasýiva ohupyty haĝua ipo. Pehejami tajapo peteĩ pecho ipotĩva chugui ko’ápe, ha francamente arrekonose añongatu hague pero perdón guardia. Pe problema universo rehegua ojeréva chepype, mba’éicha piko che —ajehejávo completamente chejehe peteĩ altitud omoheñóiva pensamiento-pe —mba’éicha piko ikatu ajagarra ligero che obligaciones a’observa haĝua opa umi barco ballenas orden de pie, “Eñongatu nde resa tiempo rehegua Eipe’a, ha epurahéi cada vez”.

Ha pehejami ko tendápe pomomarandu pomokyre’ỹ, peẽ barco jára Nantucket-pegua! Peñangareko pe’alista haĝua pende pesca vigilante-pe oimeraẽ mitãkaria’y orekóva ceja magra ha tesa hueco; oñeme’ẽva meditación insazonable-pe; ha oikuave’ẽva omondo haĝua pe Phædon ndive Bowditch rangue iñakãme. Peñangarekóke peichagua rehe, ha’e; ojehechava’erã nde ballena-kuéra ikatu mboyve ojejuka; ha ko mitãrusu platonista hesa oñehundíva nde remolca diez despertar ko mundo jerére, ha araka’eve ndejapóta peteĩ pinta de espermatozoide ipirapire hetáva. Ni ko’ã monición mba’eveichavérõ natekotevẽi. Ko’áĝa peve, pe pesquero de ballena ome’ẽ peteĩ asilo heta mitãrusu romántico, melancólico ha mente ausente-pe ĝuarã, oñembojeguarúva umi cuidado de carcador de la Tierra-pe, ha ohekávo sentimiento alquitrán ha blubber-pe. Childe Harold not unfrequently perches himself upon the mast-head of some luckless disappointed whale-ship, and in moody phrase ejaculates:—

“Roll on, thou deep and dark blue ocean, roll!
Ten thousand blubber-hunters sweep over thee in vain.”

Very often do the captains of such ships take those absent-minded young philosophers to task, upbraiding them with not feeling sufficient “interest” in the voyage; half-hinting that they are so hopelessly lost to all honorable ambition, as that in their secret souls they would rather not see whales than otherwise. But all in vain; those young Platonists have a notion that their vision is imperfect; they are short-sighted; what use, then, to strain the visual nerve? They have left their opera-glasses at home.

“Why, thou monkey,” said a harpooneer to one of these lads, “we've been cruising now hard upon three years, and thou hast not raised a whale yet. Whales are scarce as hen's teeth whenever thou art up here.” Perhaps they were; or perhaps there might have been shoals of them in the far horizon; but lulled into such an opium-like listlessness of vacant, unconscious reverie is this absent-minded youth by the blending cadence of waves with thoughts, that at last he loses his identity; takes the mystic ocean at his feet for the visible image of that deep, blue, bottomless soul, pervading mankind and nature; and every strange, half-seen, gliding, beautiful thing that eludes him; every dimly-discovered, uprising fin of some undiscernible form, seems to him the embodiment of those elusive thoughts that only people the soul by continually flitting through it. In this enchanted mood, thy spirit ebbs away to whence it came; becomes diffused through time and space; like Cranmer's sprinkled Pantheistic ashes, forming at last a part of every shore the round globe over.

There is no life in thee, now, except that rocking life imparted by a gently rolling ship; by her, borrowed from the sea; by the sea, from the inscrutable tides of God. But while this sleep, this dream is on ye, move your foot or hand an inch; slip your hold at all; and your identity comes back in horror. Over Descartian vortices you hover. And perhaps, at mid-day, in the fairest weather, with one half-throttled shriek you drop through that transparent air into the summer sea, no more to rise for ever. Heed it well, ye Pantheists!





CHAPTER 36. The Quarter-Deck.

( Enter Ahab: Then, all. )

It was not a great while after the affair of the pipe, that one morning shortly after breakfast, Ahab, as was his wont, ascended the cabin-gangway to the deck. There most sea-captains usually walk at that hour, as country gentlemen, after the same meal, take a few turns in the garden.

Soon his steady, ivory stride was heard, as to and fro he paced his old rounds, upon planks so familiar to his tread, that they were all over dented, like geological stones, with the peculiar mark of his walk. Did you fixedly gaze, too, upon that ribbed and dented brow; there also, you would see still stranger foot-prints—the foot-prints of his one unsleeping, ever-pacing thought.

But on the occasion in question, those dents looked deeper, even as his nervous step that morning left a deeper mark. And, so full of his thought was Ahab, that at every uniform turn that he made, now at the main-mast and now at the binnacle, you could almost see that thought turn in him as he turned, and pace in him as he paced; so completely possessing him, indeed, that it all but seemed the inward mould of every outer movement.

“D'ye mark him, Flask?” whispered Stubb; “the chick that's in him pecks the shell. 'Twill soon be out.”

The hours wore on;—Ahab now shut up within his cabin; anon, pacing the deck, with the same intense bigotry of purpose in his aspect.

It drew near the close of day. Suddenly he came to a halt by the bulwarks, and inserting his bone leg into the auger-hole there, and with one hand grasping a shroud, he ordered Starbuck to send everybody aft.

“Sir!” said the mate, astonished at an order seldom or never given on ship-board except in some extraordinary case.

“Send everybody aft,” repeated Ahab. “Mast-heads, there! come down!”

When the entire ship's company were assembled, and with curious and not wholly unapprehensive faces, were eyeing him, for he looked not unlike the weather horizon when a storm is coming up, Ahab, after rapidly glancing over the bulwarks, and then darting his eyes among the crew, started from his standpoint; and as though not a soul were nigh him resumed his heavy turns upon the deck. With bent head and half-slouched hat he continued to pace, unmindful of the wondering whispering among the men; till Stubb cautiously whispered to Flask, that Ahab must have summoned them there for the purpose of witnessing a pedestrian feat. But this did not last long. Vehemently pausing, he cried:—

“What do ye do when ye see a whale, men?”

“Sing out for him!” was the impulsive rejoinder from a score of clubbed voices.

“Good!” cried Ahab, with a wild approval in his tones; observing the hearty animation into which his unexpected question had so magnetically thrown them.

“And what do ye next, men?”

“Lower away, and after him!”

“And what tune is it ye pull to, men?”

“A dead whale or a stove boat!”

More and more strangely and fiercely glad and approving, grew the countenance of the old man at every shout; while the mariners began to gaze curiously at each other, as if marvelling how it was that they themselves became so excited at such seemingly purposeless questions.

But, they were all eagerness again, as Ahab, now half-revolving in his pivot-hole, with one hand reaching high up a shroud, and tightly, almost convulsively grasping it, addressed them thus:—

“All ye mast-headers have before now heard me give orders about a white whale. Look ye! d'ye see this Spanish ounce of gold?”—holding up a broad bright coin to the sun—“it is a sixteen dollar piece, men. D'ye see it? Mr. Starbuck, hand me yon top-maul.”

While the mate was getting the hammer, Ahab, without speaking, was slowly rubbing the gold piece against the skirts of his jacket, as if to heighten its lustre, and without using any words was meanwhile lowly humming to himself, producing a sound so strangely muffled and inarticulate that it seemed the mechanical humming of the wheels of his vitality in him.

Receiving the top-maul from Starbuck, he advanced towards the main-mast with the hammer uplifted in one hand, exhibiting the gold with the other, and with a high raised voice exclaiming: “Whosoever of ye raises me a white-headed whale with a wrinkled brow and a crooked jaw; whosoever of ye raises me that white-headed whale, with three holes punctured in his starboard fluke—look ye, whosoever of ye raises me that same white whale, he shall have this gold ounce, my boys!”

“Huzza! huzza!” cried the seamen, as with swinging tarpaulins they hailed the act of nailing the gold to the mast.

“It's a white whale, I say,” resumed Ahab, as he threw down the topmaul: “a white whale. Skin your eyes for him, men; look sharp for white water; if ye see but a bubble, sing out.”

All this while Tashtego, Daggoo, and Queequeg had looked on with even more intense interest and surprise than the rest, and at the mention of the wrinkled brow and crooked jaw they had started as if each was separately touched by some specific recollection.

“Captain Ahab,” said Tashtego, “that white whale must be the same that some call Moby Dick.”

“Moby Dick?” shouted Ahab. “Do ye know the white whale then, Tash?”

“Does he fan-tail a little curious, sir, before he goes down?” said the Gay-Header deliberately.

“And has he a curious spout, too,” said Daggoo, “very bushy, even for a parmacetty, and mighty quick, Captain Ahab?”

“And he have one, two, three—oh! good many iron in him hide, too, Captain,” cried Queequeg disjointedly, “all twiske-tee be-twisk, like him—him—” faltering hard for a word, and screwing his hand round and round as though uncorking a bottle—“like him—him—”

“Corkscrew!” cried Ahab, “aye, Queequeg, the harpoons lie all twisted and wrenched in him; aye, Daggoo, his spout is a big one, like a whole shock of wheat, and white as a pile of our Nantucket wool after the great annual sheep-shearing; aye, Tashtego, and he fan-tails like a split jib in a squall. Death and devils! men, it is Moby Dick ye have seen—Moby Dick—Moby Dick!”

“Captain Ahab,” said Starbuck, who, with Stubb and Flask, had thus far been eyeing his superior with increasing surprise, but at last seemed struck with a thought which somewhat explained all the wonder. “Captain Ahab, I have heard of Moby Dick—but it was not Moby Dick that took off thy leg?”

“Who told thee that?” cried Ahab; then pausing, “Aye, Starbuck; aye, my hearties all round; it was Moby Dick that dismasted me; Moby Dick that brought me to this dead stump I stand on now. Aye, aye,” he shouted with a terrific, loud, animal sob, like that of a heart-stricken moose; “Aye, aye! it was that accursed white whale that razed me; made a poor pegging lubber of me for ever and a day!” Then tossing both arms, with measureless imprecations he shouted out: “Aye, aye! and I'll chase him round Good Hope, and round the Horn, and round the Norway Maelstrom, and round perdition's flames before I give him up. And this is what ye have shipped for, men! to chase that white whale on both sides of land, and over all sides of earth, till he spouts black blood and rolls fin out. What say ye, men, will ye splice hands on it, now? I think ye do look brave.”

“Aye, aye!” shouted the harpooneers and seamen, running closer to the excited old man: “A sharp eye for the white whale; a sharp lance for Moby Dick!”

“God bless ye,” he seemed to half sob and half shout. “God bless ye, men. Steward! go draw the great measure of grog. But what's this long face about, Mr. Starbuck; wilt thou not chase the white whale? art not game for Moby Dick?”

“I am game for his crooked jaw, and for the jaws of Death too, Captain Ahab, if it fairly comes in the way of the business we follow; but I came here to hunt whales, not my commander's vengeance. How many barrels will thy vengeance yield thee even if thou gettest it, Captain Ahab? it will not fetch thee much in our Nantucket market.”

“Nantucket market! Hoot! But come closer, Starbuck; thou requirest a little lower layer. If money's to be the measurer, man, and the accountants have computed their great counting-house the globe, by girdling it with guineas, one to every three parts of an inch; then, let me tell thee, that my vengeance will fetch a great premium here! ”

“He smites his chest,” whispered Stubb, “what's that for? methinks it rings most vast, but hollow.”

“Vengeance on a dumb brute!” cried Starbuck, “that simply smote thee from blindest instinct! Madness! To be enraged with a dumb thing, Captain Ahab, seems blasphemous.”

“Hark ye yet again—the little lower layer. All visible objects, man, are but as pasteboard masks. But in each event—in the living act, the undoubted deed—there, some unknown but still reasoning thing puts forth the mouldings of its features from behind the unreasoning mask. If man will strike, strike through the mask! How can the prisoner reach outside except by thrusting through the wall? To me, the white whale is that wall, shoved near to me. Sometimes I think there's naught beyond. But 'tis enough. He tasks me; he heaps me; I see in him outrageous strength, with an inscrutable malice sinewing it. That inscrutable thing is chiefly what I hate; and be the white whale agent, or be the white whale principal, I will wreak that hate upon him. Talk not to me of blasphemy, man; I'd strike the sun if it insulted me. For could the sun do that, then could I do the other; since there is ever a sort of fair play herein, jealousy presiding over all creations. But not my master, man, is even that fair play. Who's over me? Truth hath no confines. Take off thine eye! more intolerable than fiends' glarings is a doltish stare! So, so; thou reddenest and palest; my heat has melted thee to anger-glow. But look ye, Starbuck, what is said in heat, that thing unsays itself. There are men from whom warm words are small indignity. I meant not to incense thee. Let it go. Look! see yonder Turkish cheeks of spotted tawn—living, breathing pictures painted by the sun. The Pagan leopards—the unrecking and unworshipping things, that live; and seek, and give no reasons for the torrid life they feel! The crew, man, the crew! Are they not one and all with Ahab, in this matter of the whale? See Stubb! he laughs! See yonder Chilian! he snorts to think of it. Stand up amid the general hurricane, thy one tost sapling cannot, Starbuck! And what is it? Reckon it. 'Tis but to help strike a fin; no wondrous feat for Starbuck. What is it more? From this one poor hunt, then, the best lance out of all Nantucket, surely he will not hang back, when every foremast-hand has clutched a whetstone? Ah! constrainings seize thee; I see! the billow lifts thee! Speak, but speak!—Aye, aye! thy silence, then, that voices thee. ( Aside ) Something shot from my dilated nostrils, he has inhaled it in his lungs. Starbuck now is mine; cannot oppose me now, without rebellion.”

“God keep me!—keep us all!” murmured Starbuck, lowly.

But in his joy at the enchanted, tacit acquiescence of the mate, Ahab did not hear his foreboding invocation; nor yet the low laugh from the hold; nor yet the presaging vibrations of the winds in the cordage; nor yet the hollow flap of the sails against the masts, as for a moment their hearts sank in. For again Starbuck's downcast eyes lighted up with the stubbornness of life; the subterranean laugh died away; the winds blew on; the sails filled out; the ship heaved and rolled as before. Ah, ye admonitions and warnings! why stay ye not when ye come? But rather are ye predictions than warnings, ye shadows! Yet not so much predictions from without, as verifications of the foregoing things within. For with little external to constrain us, the innermost necessities in our being, these still drive us on.

“The measure! the measure!” cried Ahab.

Receiving the brimming pewter, and turning to the harpooneers, he ordered them to produce their weapons. Then ranging them before him near the capstan, with their harpoons in their hands, while his three mates stood at his side with their lances, and the rest of the ship's company formed a circle round the group; he stood for an instant searchingly eyeing every man of his crew. But those wild eyes met his, as the bloodshot eyes of the prairie wolves meet the eye of their leader, ere he rushes on at their head in the trail of the bison; but, alas! only to fall into the hidden snare of the Indian.

“Drink and pass!” he cried, handing the heavy charged flagon to the nearest seaman. “The crew alone now drink. Round with it, round! Short draughts—long swallows, men; 'tis hot as Satan's hoof. So, so; it goes round excellently. It spiralizes in ye; forks out at the serpent-snapping eye. Well done; almost drained. That way it went, this way it comes. Hand it me—here's a hollow! Men, ye seem the years; so brimming life is gulped and gone. Steward, refill!

“Attend now, my braves. I have mustered ye all round this capstan; and ye mates, flank me with your lances; and ye harpooneers, stand there with your irons; and ye, stout mariners, ring me in, that I may in some sort revive a noble custom of my fisherman fathers before me. O men, you will yet see that—Ha! boy, come back? bad pennies come not sooner. Hand it me. Why, now, this pewter had run brimming again, wer't not thou St. Vitus' imp—away, thou ague!

“Advance, ye mates! Cross your lances full before me. Well done! Let me touch the axis.” So saying, with extended arm, he grasped the three level, radiating lances at their crossed centre; while so doing, suddenly and nervously twitched them; meanwhile, glancing intently from Starbuck to Stubb; from Stubb to Flask. It seemed as though, by some nameless, interior volition, he would fain have shocked into them the same fiery emotion accumulated within the Leyden jar of his own magnetic life. The three mates quailed before his strong, sustained, and mystic aspect. Stubb and Flask looked sideways from him; the honest eye of Starbuck fell downright.

“In vain!” cried Ahab; “but, maybe, 'tis well. For did ye three but once take the full-forced shock, then mine own electric thing, that had perhaps expired from out me. Perchance, too, it would have dropped ye dead. Perchance ye need it not. Down lances! And now, ye mates, I do appoint ye three cupbearers to my three pagan kinsmen there—yon three most honorable gentlemen and noblemen, my valiant harpooneers. Disdain the task? What, when the great Pope washes the feet of beggars, using his tiara for ewer? Oh, my sweet cardinals! your own condescension, that shall bend ye to it. I do not order ye; ye will it. Cut your seizings and draw the poles, ye harpooneers!”

Silently obeying the order, the three harpooneers now stood with the detached iron part of their harpoons, some three feet long, held, barbs up, before him.

“Stab me not with that keen steel! Cant them; cant them over! know ye not the goblet end? Turn up the socket! So, so; now, ye cup-bearers, advance. The irons! take them; hold them while I fill!” Forthwith, slowly going from one officer to the other, he brimmed the harpoon sockets with the fiery waters from the pewter.

“Now, three to three, ye stand. Commend the murderous chalices! Bestow them, ye who are now made parties to this indissoluble league. Ha! Starbuck! but the deed is done! Yon ratifying sun now waits to sit upon it. Drink, ye harpooneers! drink and swear, ye men that man the deathful whaleboat's bow—Death to Moby Dick! God hunt us all, if we do not hunt Moby Dick to his death!” The long, barbed steel goblets were lifted; and to cries and maledictions against the white whale, the spirits were simultaneously quaffed down with a hiss. Starbuck paled, and turned, and shivered. Once more, and finally, the replenished pewter went the rounds among the frantic crew; when, waving his free hand to them, they all dispersed; and Ahab retired within his cabin.
