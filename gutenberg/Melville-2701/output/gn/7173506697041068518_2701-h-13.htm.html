Osẽvo pe tronco-gui, umi hakã okakuaa; Osẽ chuguikuéra, umi yvyra rakã. Upéicha, umi tema productivo-pe, okakuaa umi capítulo.

Avei: Reikuaava’erã pe segundo hierro ojeity jave yvate, upégui oiko chugui peteĩ terror colgante, filo de filo, skittishly oñecurva mokõive barco ha ballena rehe, oenreda umi línea, térã oikytĩvo, ha ojapo peteĩ sensación prodigiosa opa dirección-pe. Ni, en general, ndaikatúi ojeasegura jey ojejagarra peve ballena hekoitépe ha peteî te'õngue.

Ñapensamína, ko’áğa, mba’éichapa oiméne ha’eva’erã irundy barco kásope opavave o’engage peteĩ ballena imbarete jepivegua’ỹva, activo ha oikuaáva; ko’ã cualidades oĩva ipype rupi, ha avei umi mil accidente concurrente ko’ãichagua emprendimiento audaz-pe, ikatu ocho térã diez hierro segundo suelto simultáneamente oñemoĩ hese. Pórke, katuete, káda várkope oñemeʼẽ heta arpón ojedobla hag̃ua pe línea-pe ojedesviáramo ineficazamente pe primero ojerrekuperaʼỹre. Opa ko’ã particular oñemombe’u fielmente ko’ápe, ndofaltamo’ãigui omyesakã haĝua heta pasaje iñimportantevéva, jepe ikomplikadovéva, umi escena ko’águi rire oñepinta va’ekuépe.





Capítulo 64. Stubb cena rehegua.

Stubb ballena ojejuka kuri mombyrymi pe várkogui. Ha’e peteĩ py’aguapy; upévare, roformávo peteĩ tándem mbohapy barco rehegua, roñepyrũ pe negocio mbeguekatúpe rorremolca haĝua pe trofeo Pequod-pe. Ha ko’áğa, ore dieciocho kuimba’e ore po treinta y seis ore po reheve, ha ciento ochenta poguasu ha kuã reheve, mbeguekatúpe romba’apo aravo rire aravo upe te’õngue inerte ha imbeguevéva ári oĩva yguasúpe; Ha ha’ete ku apenas oñemomýiva mba’eveichavérõ, ndaha’éiramo intervalo pukukue; ko’ápe oñeme’ẽ evidencia porã pe masa romomýiva tuichaitereiha. Pórke, pe canal tuicháva Hang-Ho ári, térã taha’e ha’éva pe ohenóiva hikuái, China-pe, irundy térã cinco mba’apohára pe tape ojeguatahápe oguenohẽta peteĩ basura voluminosa cargada peteĩ milla por hora-pe; ha katu ko argosía tuichaitéva rorremolcáva tuichaiterei oñeforja, ha’ete ku cargado ryguasu plomo-gui a granel.

Pytũmby oúkuri; ha katu mbohapy tesape yvate ha yvýre pe Pequod principal-rigging-pe odirigí sa’i ore rape; Oñemoaguĩve peve rohecha Acab oity peteĩva heta linterna-gui hetave umi bulwark ári. Ojesarekóva vacantemente pe ballena pohýire sapy’ami, oguenohẽ umi órden jepiguáichagua oasegura haĝua pyharerã, ha upéi ome’ẽvo ilinterna peteĩ marinero-pe, oho hape pe cabina-pe, ha ndoúi jey tenonde gotyo pyhareve peve.

Jepe, oñangarekóvo ko ballena rapykuéri, capitán Acab ohechaukava’ekue hembiapo jepokuaa, ja’e haĝua upéicha; jepeve ko’áğa pe criatura omanóma, ha’ete omba’apóva ipype algún insatisfacción vaga, térã impaciencia, térã desesperación; ha’ete ku ohechávo upe tete omanóva omomandu’a chupe Moby Dick ojejukava’erãha gueteri; ha ojegueru ramo jepe ambue mil ballena ibarco-pe, opa mba’e ndomotenondemo’ãiva ni peteĩ jot pe objeto tuicha ha monomanía orekóva. Pya’eterei pepensa va’erãmo’ã pe tyapu oĩvagui umi Pequod cubiertas-pe, opavave po oñembosako’iha omombo haĝua ancla pe pypukúpe; ojegueraha haguére umi cadena pohýi pe cubierta pukukue, ha oñembotyryry ryrýi osẽvo umi puerto-agujero-gui. Péro umi eslabón okororõva rupive, oñeamarráta pe teʼongue tuichaitereíva voi, ndahaʼéi pe várko. Oñeñapytĩva iñakãme pe popa gotyo, ha pe cola umi arco peve, pe ballena ko’áğa oñeno icasco morotĩ hi’aguĩva pe mba’yru’s-gui ha ojehecha pyhare pytũmby rupi, omoypytũva umi esparcida ha aparejamiento yvate gotyo, umi mokõi —barco ha . ballena, haʼete ku oñembojoajúva oñondive umi tóro tuichaitereívaicha, peteĩva oñemboʼy ha pe ótro katu opyta oñemboʼy *.

*Ikatu avei ojoaju ko'ápe peteî mba'e michîmi. Pe jejopy imbaretevéva ha ojeroviavéva oguerekóva pe barco ballena ári oñeamarrávo ijykére, ha’e umi fluke térã cola rupive; ha idensidad tuichavévagui upe parte ipohýive relativamente ambuégui (excepto umi aleta lateral), iflexibilidad jepe ñemano jave, ojapo ohypýivo yvy guýpe; upéicha rupi nde po rupive ndaikatúi rejupi hese pe várkogui, ikatu hag̃uáicha remoĩ pe kadéna ijerére. Ha katu ko dificultad ojesupera ingeniosamente: oñembosako’i peteĩ línea michĩ ha imbaretéva peteĩ flotador yvyrágui ojejapóva reheve ijyke okápe, ha peteĩ peso mbytépe, ambue ijyke katu ojeasegura pe barco-pe. Gestión hábil rupive ojejapo pe flotador yvyrágui ojejapóva ojupi haĝua ambue lado pe masa-pe, upéicha ko’áğa ojecinturón rire pe ballena-pe, pe cadena ojejapo pya’e osegui haĝua; ha ojedesliza jave hete pukukue, ipahápe oñemboty pya’e pe jyva pehẽngue michĩvéva jerére, pe punto ojoajuhápe umi fluke térã lóbulo tuicháva ndive.

Moody Acab ha’érire ko’áğa opa quiescencia, por lo menos ikatuháicha ojekuaa Cubierta-pe, Stubb, icompañero mokõiha, oñembopiro’ýva conquista reheve, otraisiona peteĩ emoción inusual ha katu iporãva gueteri. Peichagua ajetreo ndojepokuaa’ỹva ha’e ha’e pe Starbuck estable, isuperior oficial, kirirĩháme orenunsia chupe upe tiempo-pe ĝuarã pe única gestión de asuntos. Peteĩ causa michĩva, oipytyvõva opa ko kyre’ỹ Stubb-pe, pya’e ojehechauka extrañamente. Stubb ha’eva’ekue peteĩ hígado yvate; ha’e ohayhu’imi intemperadamente pe ballena peteĩ mba’e saborizado ramo ipaladura-pe.

“Peteĩ filete, peteĩ filete, ere che ake! Nde, Daggoo! pe y rembe’ýre reho, ha eikytĩ chéve peteĩ michĩmigui!”.

Ko’ápe ojekuaa, jepémo ko’ã pescador salvaje ndojapói, peteĩ mba’e general ramo, ha según pe máxima máxima militar, ojapo pe enemigo odespega haĝua umi gasto ko’áĝagua pe ñorairõ rehegua (al menos ohechakuaa mboyve umi recaudación pe viaje-pe), jepe ko’áĝa . Ha upéi rejuhúta alguno ko’ã nantucketer orekóva peteĩ relish genuino upe parte particular ballena de espermatozoides odesignáva Stubb; oguerekóva pe extremidad cónica ñande rete rehegua.

Pyharepyte rupi oñeikytĩ ha oñembojy upe filete; ha ohesapéva mokõi linterna aceite de espermatozoide-gui, Stubb oñembo’y hatã pe cena de espermaceti-pe pe capstan-akãme, ha’ete ku upe capstan peteĩ aparador. Ni Stubb ndaha’éikuri pe banquete año pe ballena ro’o rehe upe pyharépe. Ombojehe’ávo iñe’ẽmbegue umi masticación imba’éva ndive, miles de miles de tiburones, ojere pe leviatán omanóva jerére, ovy’a ovy’a ikyrakuére. Umi mbovymi okeva’ekue oĩva iguype ikuatiahaipyrépe oñemondýi jepi pe ikolóna haimbe asývagui pe casco rehe, mbovymi centímetro umi okeva’ekue korasõgui. Ojesarekóramo pe lado ári ikatu rehechante chupekuéra (rehendu mboyveguáicha) oñembotapykue pe y ñembyasy ha morotĩvape, ha ojere ijyva ári oguenohẽvo hikuái umi pedazo globular tuichaitereíva pe ballena tuicha yvypóra akã rehegua. Ko hazaña particular tiburón rehegua ha’ete opa mba’e ha katu milagroso. Mba'éichapa péicha peteî superficie aparentemente incoso, oconstrui hikuái oguenohê haguã ko'ãichagua juru simétrico, opyta peteî parte problema universal opa mba'e rehe. Pe marca ohejáva hikuái upéicha pe ballena-pe, ikatu oñembojoja porãve pe hueco ojapova’ekue peteĩ carpintero contrahundido-pe peteĩ tornillo-pe ĝuarã.

Jepe opa pe terror opitáva ha diabolismo peteĩ ñorairõ marítimo-pegua apytépe, ojehecháta umi tiburón omañáva kyre’ỹme yvate pe barco cubiertas-pe, jagua iñembyahýivaicha peteĩ mesa jerére ojejapohápe so’o pytã, oĩva dispuesto oity haĝua opa kuimba’e ojejukava’ekue ojeityva’ekue hese hikuái; ha jepe, umi carnicero ipy’aguasúva pe mesa de cubierta ári péicha otallá aja ojupe so’o oikovéva umi cuchillo de tallado reheve opavave dorada ha borla reheve, umi tiburón, avei, ijuru joya-guigua reheve, otalla hína ñorairõme mombyry pe mesa guýpe pe so’o omanóva; ha jepe, pembojere ramo pe asunto tuichakue, ha’éta gueteri haimete peteĩ mba’énte, he’iséva, peteĩ negocio tiburón oporomondýiva suficiente opavave partido-pe ĝuarã; ha jepe umi tiburón ha’e avei umi outrider invariable opa umi barco tembiguái ohasáva Atlántico, sistemáticamente otrota ijykére, oĩ haĝua pópe en caso ojegueraha haĝua peteĩ paquete mamove, térã peteĩ tembiguái omanóva oñeñotỹ haĝua decentemente; Ha jepe ikatu oñemoĩ peteĩ térã mokõi ambue instancia-icha, opoko umi término, tenda ha ocasión oje’évare, umi tiburón ojapo jave karu guasu socialmente, ha hilarantevéva; Upéicharõ jepe, ndaipóri tiempo térã ocasión oñepensáva rejuhútahápe chupekuéra hetaiterei incontable-pe, ha umi espíritu gay térã jovial-vévape, peteĩ ballena de espermatozoides omanóva jerére rangue, oñeamarráva pyhare peteĩ ballena-pe yguasúpe. Oiméramo araka’eve ndapehechái upe jehecha, upéicharõ pesuspende pende decisión pe aña adoración propiedad rehegua, ha pe expediencia pekoncilia haĝua pe aña-pe.

Ha katu, ko’áĝa peve, Stubb nohenduiva’ekue umi ñe’ẽmbegue pe banquete-pegua ohóva upéicha rupi, ndohasái umi tiburón ohenduka pe smacking ijurúpe epicúreo imba’éva.

“¡Cocinar, ecocina!— ¿Moõpa oĩ upe polar tuja?” hasẽ puku, ombotuichave ipy, ha’ete ku ojapo haĝua peteĩ base isegurovéva ikarurã; ha, upe jave avei oityvyro itenedor pe tembi’úpe, ha’ete ku oinupãva ilánsa ndive; “embojy, nde rembojy!—eveve kóicha, embojy!”.

Pe morotĩ tuja, ndaha’éiva mba’eveichagua vy’a yvatetereívape oñemopu’ã haguére yma ihamaca haku asývagui peteĩ aravo ndaha’éivape sazonablevévape, ou otyryry igalería-gui, pórke, heta morotĩ tujaicha, oĩ peteĩ mba’e pe asunto umi ijyva’atã reheve, ha’e noñongatúi porãiva’ekue umi ambue sartén-icha; ko Fleece tuja, ohenóiháicha chupe, ou oñembojere ha opyrũ, oipytyvõvo ipype umi pinza reheve, ha’éva, peteĩ moda torpe rire, ojejapóva aros de hierro oñembo’ývagui; ko Ebony tuja oñemongu’e ohóvo, ha iñe’ẽrendúvo pe ñe’ẽ omandávape, og̃uahẽ peteĩ parada omanóvape Stubb aparador rovái; Oñembo’y jave mokõive ipo henondépe, ha opytu’u jave ijyva mokõi py rehe, oñakãity arco jeyve gueteri, upe jave lado-pe oinclinávo iñakã, ikatu haguãicha ogueru ijapysa iporãvéva ñembosaráipe.

“Cook”, he’i Stubb, pya’e ohupívo peteĩ mordida bastante pytã’i ijurúpe, “ani piko peimo’ã ko filete oje’exageraveha? Reinupãiterei ko filete, embojy; ha'e tiernoiterei. ¿Ndaʼéi piko siémpre iporã hag̃ua, peteĩ filete de ballena hasy vaʼerãha? Oĩ umi tiburón ko’áğa lado ári, ¿ndapehechái piko opreferiha hikuái hasy ha ndahetái? ¡Ajépa omimbipa hikuái! Embojy, tereho eñe'ẽ hendivekuéra; emombe'u chupekuéra oîha bienvenido oipytyvõ haguã civilmente, ha moderación-pe, pero okirirîva'erã. Chembopu, ikatúramo ahendu che ñe’ẽ tee. mombyry, ekosina ha emoguahẽ che marandu. Ko’ápe, ejagarra ko linterna”, oipe’ávo peteĩ iaparador-gui; “koʼág̃a upéicharõ, tereho epredika chupekuéra!”

Ogueraháva ñembyasýpe pe linterna oñeikuave’ẽva, pe polar tuja opyrũ pe cubierta ári umi balurk-pe; ha upéi, peteĩ ipo reheve omboguejy hesape yguasu ári, ohecha porã haĝua ikongregasión, ambue ipo reheve oñakãrapu’ã solemnemente umi pinza, ha oñembo’y mombyry pe lado ári peteĩ iñe’ẽ iñe’ẽmbeguépe oñepyrũ oñe’ẽ umi tiburón-pe, Stubb katu, otyryrýva mbeguekatu hapykuéri, ohendu opa mba’e oje’éva.

“Compañero-critters: Che añeordena ko'ápe ha'e haguã dat peẽ pejokova'erã dat ruido de represa atreve. ¿Rehendu piko? ¡Parán Dat Dam Smackin' Ob de Lip! Massa Stubb he'i dat ikatuha remyenyhẽ nde represa rye de eclosión peve, pero por Gor! ¡rejokova’erã dat raqueta de represa!”

“Cook”, ko’ápe ointerpone Stubb, omoirûvo pe ñe’ẽ peteĩ slap sapy’aitépe hombro-pe, —“Cook! mba’ére piko, maldición nde resa, nerehuraiva’erã upéicha repredika jave. ¡Upéva ndaha’éi mba’éichapa ikatu pekonverti umi pekadórpe, pekosina!”

“Máva piko dat? Den nde voi epredika chupe”, ojerévo ñembyasýpe oho haĝua.

“Nahániri, embojy; tereho, tereho”.

“Bueno, den, compañero-critters belubedos:”—

"Akatúa!" osapukái Stubb, omoneĩva, “coax ’em a él; eñeha’ã upéva”, ha Fleece osegi.

“¿Peẽ piko ha’e opavave tiburón, ha por naturaleza woracious, jepeve che zay peẽme, compañero-critters, dat dat woraciousness —‘top dat dam slappin’ ob de tail! Mba'éichapa pe tink pehendu, spose pemantene peichagua represa slappin' ha bitin' atreve?”

“Cocina”, osapukái Stubb, ocolla chupe, “ndarekomo’ãi upe juramento. Eñeʼẽ hendivekuéra karaípe”.

Peteĩ jey pe sermón oñemotenonde.

“Pende adoracion, compañero-critter, ndakulpái peẽme hetaiterei; Dat ha'e Natur, ha ndaikatúi oñepytyvõ; Pero a gobern dat wicked natur, dat ha'e de pint. Nde ha’e tiburones, sartin; Pero nde gobern de tiburón ndepype, mba'ére den nde ha'e ángel; opavave ángel ndaha'éigui hetave dan de tiburón porã goberned. Ko'ágã, emañamína ko'ápe, bred'ren, eñeha'ãnte would would ha'e haguã Cibil, peteĩ oipytyvõva umi ndeselbs de dat ballena. Ani redesgarra de blubber nde vecino mout-gui, ha'e. ¿Ndaha'éi piko peteĩ tiburón dood oike porãva toder to dat ballena-icha? Ha, Gor rupive, avave nderehe ndorekói de derecho dat ballena; dat ballena ha'e ambue mba'e. Che aikuaa algún o' nde reguereko Berry Brig Mout, Brigger Dan Oders; ha katu den de brig mouts sapy’ánte oreko de tyekue michĩva; Upéicha dat de brigness de de mout ndaha'éi Swaller Wid, sino oisu'u haguã de Blubber de dequeños ob tiburones, dat ndikatúi oike de scrouge oipytyvõ haguã desmalta-pe.”

“Ojejapo porã, ¡polar tuja!”. osapukái Stubb-pe, “Upéva ha’e cristianismo; esegíkena”.

“Ndaipóri mba'everã ohóva; de dam willains oñongatúta peteĩ scugin’ ha slappin’ cada oder, Massa Stubb; Dey ani rehendu peteĩ ñe'ẽ; no use a-preachin' ko'ãichagua dam g'uttons pehenóiháicha 'em, peve dare bellies henyhẽ, ha dare bellies ha'e fondo sin fondo; ha henyhẽ vove, nohendúi ndéve; porque den dey oñehundi de mar-pe, oho pya'e oke de coral ári, ha nohenduvéima no'ing mba'eveichavérõ, no más, por eber ha eber”.

“Che ánga ári, che aime peteĩ opinión-gui; Upévare emeʼẽ pe bendicción, polar, ha che asẽta che karurã”.

Péva ári, polar, ojokóva mokõive ipo pe pirakutuhára aty ári, ohupi iñe’ẽ kyre’ỹ ha hasẽ —

“Umi compañera de caminata encusada! Kick up de maldita fila ikatuháicha; emyenyhẽ nde represa’ rye ‘dey busta peve —ha den omano peve”.

“Ko’áğa, embojy”, he’i Stubb, oñepyrũ jeývo okaru pe capstan-pe; “peñembo’y peẽ peñembo’y haguépe voi, upépe, che rovái, ha peñatende particularmente”.

“Opa dención”, he’i Fleece, oñesũ jeývo ipiza ári pe posición oipotávape.

“Bueno”, he’i Stubb, oipytyvõvo ijehegui sãsõme upe aja; “Ko’áğa aha jeýta pe tema ko filete rehegua. Ñepyrũrã, mboy áñopa rereko, embojy?”.

“Mba’épa ojapo wid de 'teak”, he’i pe morotĩ tuja, testigo reheve.

"Kirirĩ! Mboy áñopa rereko, rembojy?”.

“‘Noventa rupi, ere”, he’i ñembyasýpe.

“Ha peẽ peiko mbarete ko mundo-pe cien áño ári, pekosina, ha ndapeikuaái gueteri mba’éichapa pembojy peteĩ ballena-pe?” pya’e ombojere ambue juru pe ñe’ẽ pahaitépe, upéicha upe mordida ha’ete peteĩ continuación pe porandu rehegua. “Moõ piko renase vaʼekue, ekosina?”.

“'Hind de Hatchway, en balsa-bote, goin' Ober de Roanoke.”

“Onase vaʼekue peteĩ várko de várkope! Upéva ha'e queer, avei. Péro aikuaase mbaʼe paíspepa renase, ekosina!”.

“¿Nda’éi piko de Roanoke tetã?” hasẽ hatã.

“Nahániri, nde nderejapói, rembojy; ha katu amombe'úta ndéve mba'épa aju, embojy. Reho va’erã nde rógape ha renase jey; Ndereikwaái gueteri mba'éichapa rembojyva'erã peteĩ ballena-paso”.

“Bress che ánga, acocina ramo noder peteĩ”, opukavy, pochýpe, ojerévo oho haĝua.

“Eju jey, embojy;—ko’ápe, eme’ẽ chéve umi pinza;—ko’áğa egueraha upépe upe filete pehẽngue, ha ere chéve reimo’ãpa upe filete oñembojy hague ha’eva’erãháicha? Pejagarra, ha’e” —ojokóvo umi pinza hendápe—“ejagarra, ha eproba”.

Omboja’o kangymi umi ijurúpe ipirúva hi’ári sapy’ami, pe Negro tuja osusurra, “Mejor cocido 'teak i eber sabor; joosy, baya joosy”.

“Cocina”, he’i Stubb, oñembo’y peteĩ jey; “¿Nde piko nde ha’e pe tupao mba’e?”

“Ohasa peteĩ jey Cabo-Down-pe”, he’i pe karai tuja ñembyasýpe.

“And you have once in your life passed a holy church in Cape-Town, where you doubtless overheard a holy parson addressing his hearers as his beloved fellow-creatures, have you, cook! And yet you come here, and tell me such a dreadful lie as you did just now, eh?” said Stubb. “Where do you expect to go to, cook?”

“Go to bed berry soon,” he mumbled, half-turning as he spoke.

“Avast! heave to! I mean when you die, cook. It's an awful question. Now what's your answer?”

“When dis old brack man dies,” said the negro slowly, changing his whole air and demeanor, “he hisself won't go nowhere; but some bressed angel will come and fetch him.”

“Fetch him? How? In a coach and four, as they fetched Elijah? And fetch him where?”

“Up dere,” said Fleece, holding his tongs straight over his head, and keeping it there very solemnly.

“So, then, you expect to go up into our main-top, do you, cook, when you are dead? But don't you know the higher you climb, the colder it gets? Main-top, eh?”

“Didn't say dat t'all,” said Fleece, again in the sulks.

“You said up there, didn't you? and now look yourself, and see where your tongs are pointing. But, perhaps you expect to get into heaven by crawling through the lubber's hole, cook; but, no, no, cook, you don't get there, except you go the regular way, round by the rigging. It's a ticklish business, but must be done, or else it's no go. But none of us are in heaven yet. Drop your tongs, cook, and hear my orders. Do ye hear? Hold your hat in one hand, and clap t'other a'top of your heart, when I'm giving my orders, cook. What! that your heart, there?—that's your gizzard! Aloft! aloft!—that's it—now you have it. Hold it there now, and pay attention.”

“All 'dention,” said the old black, with both hands placed as desired, vainly wriggling his grizzled head, as if to get both ears in front at one and the same time.

“Well then, cook, you see this whale-steak of yours was so very bad, that I have put it out of sight as soon as possible; you see that, don't you? Well, for the future, when you cook another whale-steak for my private table here, the capstan, I'll tell you what to do so as not to spoil it by overdoing. Hold the steak in one hand, and show a live coal to it with the other; that done, dish it; d'ye hear? And now to-morrow, cook, when we are cutting in the fish, be sure you stand by to get the tips of his fins; have them put in pickle. As for the ends of the flukes, have them soused, cook. There, now ye may go.”

But Fleece had hardly got three paces off, when he was recalled.

“Cook, give me cutlets for supper to-morrow night in the mid-watch. D'ye hear? away you sail, then.—Halloa! stop! make a bow before you go.—Avast heaving again! Whale-balls for breakfast—don't forget.”

“Wish, by gor! whale eat him, 'stead of him eat whale. I'm bressed if he ain't more of shark dan Massa Shark hisself,” muttered the old man, limping away; with which sage ejaculation he went to his hammock.





CHAPTER 65. The Whale as a Dish.

That mortal man should feed upon the creature that feeds his lamp, and, like Stubb, eat him by his own light, as you may say; this seems so outlandish a thing that one must needs go a little into the history and philosophy of it.

It is upon record, that three centuries ago the tongue of the Right Whale was esteemed a great delicacy in France, and commanded large prices there. Also, that in Henry VIIIth's time, a certain cook of the court obtained a handsome reward for inventing an admirable sauce to be eaten with barbacued porpoises, which, you remember, are a species of whale. Porpoises, indeed, are to this day considered fine eating. The meat is made into balls about the size of billiard balls, and being well seasoned and spiced might be taken for turtle-balls or veal balls. The old monks of Dunfermline were very fond of them. They had a great porpoise grant from the crown.

The fact is, that among his hunters at least, the whale would by all hands be considered a noble dish, were there not so much of him; but when you come to sit down before a meat-pie nearly one hundred feet long, it takes away your appetite. Only the most unprejudiced of men like Stubb, nowadays partake of cooked whales; but the Esquimaux are not so fastidious. We all know how they live upon whales, and have rare old vintages of prime old train oil. Zogranda, one of their most famous doctors, recommends strips of blubber for infants, as being exceedingly juicy and nourishing. And this reminds me that certain Englishmen, who long ago were accidentally left in Greenland by a whaling vessel—that these men actually lived for several months on the mouldy scraps of whales which had been left ashore after trying out the blubber. Among the Dutch whalemen these scraps are called “fritters”; which, indeed, they greatly resemble, being brown and crisp, and smelling something like old Amsterdam housewives' dough-nuts or oly-cooks, when fresh. They have such an eatable look that the most self-denying stranger can hardly keep his hands off.

But what further depreciates the whale as a civilized dish, is his exceeding richness. He is the great prize ox of the sea, too fat to be delicately good. Look at his hump, which would be as fine eating as the buffalo's (which is esteemed a rare dish), were it not such a solid pyramid of fat. But the spermaceti itself, how bland and creamy that is; like the transparent, half-jellied, white meat of a cocoanut in the third month of its growth, yet far too rich to supply a substitute for butter. Nevertheless, many whalemen have a method of absorbing it into some other substance, and then partaking of it. In the long try watches of the night it is a common thing for the seamen to dip their ship-biscuit into the huge oil-pots and let them fry there awhile. Many a good supper have I thus made.

In the case of a small Sperm Whale the brains are accounted a fine dish. The casket of the skull is broken into with an axe, and the two plump, whitish lobes being withdrawn (precisely resembling two large puddings), they are then mixed with flour, and cooked into a most delectable mess, in flavor somewhat resembling calves' head, which is quite a dish among some epicures; and every one knows that some young bucks among the epicures, by continually dining upon calves' brains, by and by get to have a little brains of their own, so as to be able to tell a calf's head from their own heads; which, indeed, requires uncommon discrimination. And that is the reason why a young buck with an intelligent looking calf's head before him, is somehow one of the saddest sights you can see. The head looks a sort of reproachfully at him, with an “Et tu Brute!” expression.

It is not, perhaps, entirely because the whale is so excessively unctuous that landsmen seem to regard the eating of him with abhorrence; that appears to result, in some way, from the consideration before mentioned: ie that a man should eat a newly murdered thing of the sea, and eat it too by its own light. But no doubt the first man that ever murdered an ox was regarded as a murderer; perhaps he was hung; and if he had been put on his trial by oxen, he certainly would have been; and he certainly deserved it if any murderer does. Go to the meat-market of a Saturday night and see the crowds of live bipeds staring up at the long rows of dead quadrupeds. Does not that sight take a tooth out of the cannibal's jaw? Cannibals? who is not a cannibal? I tell you it will be more tolerable for the Fejee that salted down a lean missionary in his cellar against a coming famine; it will be more tolerable for that provident Fejee, I say, in the day of judgment, than for thee, civilized and enlightened gourmand, who nailest geese to the ground and feastest on their bloated livers in thy paté-de-foie-gras.

But Stubb, he eats the whale by its own light, does he? and that is adding insult to injury, is it? Look at your knife-handle, there, my civilized and enlightened gourmand dining off that roast beef, what is that handle made of?—what but the bones of the brother of the very ox you are eating? And what do you pick your teeth with, after devouring that fat goose? With a feather of the same fowl. And with what quill did the Secretary of the Society for the Suppression of Cruelty to Ganders formally indite his circulars? It is only within the last month or two that that society passed a resolution to patronize nothing but steel pens.





CHAPTER 66. The Shark Massacre.

When in the Southern Fishery, a captured Sperm Whale, after long and weary toil, is brought alongside late at night, it is not, as a general thing at least, customary to proceed at once to the business of cutting him in. For that business is an exceedingly laborious one; is not very soon completed; and requires all hands to set about it. Therefore, the common usage is to take in all sail; lash the helm a'lee; and then send every one below to his hammock till daylight, with the reservation that, until that time, anchor-watches shall be kept; that is, two and two for an hour, each couple, the crew in rotation shall mount the deck to see that all goes well.

But sometimes, especially upon the Line in the Pacific, this plan will not answer at all; because such incalculable hosts of sharks gather round the moored carcase, that were he left so for six hours, say, on a stretch, little more than the skeleton would be visible by morning. In most other parts of the ocean, however, where these fish do not so largely abound, their wondrous voracity can be at times considerably diminished, by vigorously stirring them up with sharp whaling-spades, a procedure notwithstanding, which, in some instances, only seems to tickle them into still greater activity. But it was not thus in the present case with the Pequod's sharks; though, to be sure, any man unaccustomed to such sights, to have looked over her side that night, would have almost thought the whole round sea was one huge cheese, and those sharks the maggots in it.

Nevertheless, upon Stubb setting the anchor-watch after his supper was concluded; and when, accordingly, Queequeg and a forecastle seaman came on deck, no small excitement was created among the sharks; for immediately suspending the cutting stages over the side, and lowering three lanterns, so that they cast long gleams of light over the turbid sea, these two mariners, darting their long whaling-spades, kept up an incessant murdering of the sharks,* by striking the keen steel deep into their skulls, seemingly their only vital part. But in the foamy confusion of their mixed and struggling hosts, the marksmen could not always hit their mark; and this brought about new revelations of the incredible ferocity of the foe. They viciously snapped, not only at each other's disembowelments, but like flexible bows, bent round, and bit their own; till those entrails seemed swallowed over and over again by the same mouth, to be oppositely voided by the gaping wound. Nor was this all. It was unsafe to meddle with the corpses and ghosts of these creatures. A sort of generic or Pantheistic vitality seemed to lurk in their very joints and bones, after what might be called the individual life had departed. Killed and hoisted on deck for the sake of his skin, one of these sharks almost took poor Queequeg's hand off, when he tried to shut down the dead lid of his murderous jaw.

*The whaling-spade used for cutting-in is made of the very best steel; is about the bigness of a man's spread hand; and in general shape, corresponds to the garden implement after which it is named; only its sides are perfectly flat, and its upper end considerably narrower than the lower. This weapon is always kept as sharp as possible; and when being used is occasionally honed, just like a razor. In its socket, a stiff pole, from twenty to thirty feet long, is inserted for a handle.

“Queequeg no care what god made him shark,” said the savage, agonizingly lifting his hand up and down; “wedder Fejee god or Nantucket god; but de god wat made shark must be one dam Ingin.”
