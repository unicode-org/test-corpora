Aguata aja mombyry, che henyhẽ pensamiento-gui; pe ojerrevelava’ekue chéve casualmente capitán Acab-gui, chemyenyhẽ peteĩ cierto vaguedad salvaje hasývagui hese. Ha algún modo, upe jave, añandu peteĩ simpatía ha peteĩ ñembyasy hese, ha katu ndaikuaáigui mba’épa, ndaha’éiramo pe pérdida cruel ipy rehegua. Ha katu añandu avei peteĩ ñemondýi iñextraño hese; ha katu upeichagua ñemondýi, ndaikatúiva mba’eveichavérõ amombe’u, ndaha’éikuri exactamente ñemondýi; Ndaikuaái mba'épa raka'e. Ha katu añandu upéva; ha ndachedesinclinái hendápe; jepe añandu ipasiénsiaha pe ha’etévaicha misterio ipype, imperfectamente aikuaaháicha chupe upérõ. Ha katu, che pensamiento ipuku ojegueraha ambue dirección-pe, upéicha ko’áĝa ĝuarã Acab iñypytũva ombotyryry che akãme.





MOAKÃHA 17. Pe Ramadán.

Queequeg Ramadán, térã Ayuno ha Humillación, osegiva’erãháicha ára pukukue, ndaiporavói amolesta chupe pyhare gotyo peve; amomba’égui pe respeto tuichavéva opavave obligación religiosa rehe, araka’eve noimportái mba’éichapa cómico, ha ndaikatúi ajuhu che korasõme amomichĩ haĝua peteĩ congregación de hormigas jepe oadoráva peteĩ sapo-taburete; térã umi ambue criatura oĩva ciertas partes ñande yvype, peteĩ grado de pie de pie reheve ndojehecháiva gueteri ambue planeta-pe, oñesũva peteĩ propietario yvy rehegua omanóva torso renondépe umi mba’e inordinado oguerekóva ha oalkiláva gueteri hérape rupi añónte.

Che ha’e, ñande cristiano presbiteriano porã ha’eva’erã caritativo ko’ã mba’épe, ha ani ñañefantasia tuichaiterei ñandejehegui superior ambue mortal-kuéragui, pagano ha mba’e ndaha’éivagui, umi conceito medio loco orekóvare ko’ã tema rehe. Oĩkuri Queequeg, ko’áĝa, katuete oentretene umi noción absurdavéva Yojo ha iRamadán rehegua;—ha katu mba’épa upévagui? Queequeg oimo’ã oikuaaha mba’érehepa oñe’ẽ, aimo’ã; ha’ete ku oñekontentáva; ha upépe topytu'u. Opa ore discusión hendive ndovalemoʼãi; toiko, ha’e: ha Yvága ñandeporiahuvereko opavave —presbiteriano ha pagano-kuéra peteĩcha—opavave ñande jajejapígui algún modo kyhyjepópe ñane akãre, ha ñembyasýpe ñaikotevẽ oñemyatyrõ.

Ka’aru gotyo, añeñandu seguro opava’erãha opa umi actuación ha ritual ojapóva, ajupi ikotýpe ha ambota okẽ; ha katu ndaipóri ñembohovái. Añeha’ã aipe’a, ha katu ojejokua hyepýpe. “Queequeg”, ha’e mbeguekatu pe llave-agujero rupive:—opavave kirirĩ. “Che ha’e, ¡Quequeg! mba'ére piko nereñe'ẽi? Ha’e che —Ismael”. Péro opa mbaʼe opyta kirirĩháme yma guaréicha. Añepyrũ añemondýi. Che apermiti kuri chupe hetaiterei tiémpo; Aimo’ãkuri ikatuha oreko peteĩ ataque apopléctico. Amaña pe llave-agujero rupive; ha katu pe okẽ ojeabri peteĩ esquina impar-pe pe koty’ípe, pe perspectiva agujero de llave-pe ha’énte peteĩ torcido ha siniestro. Ahecha peteĩ parte pe tupa tabla de pie-gui ha peteĩ línea pe muro-gui añoite, ha katu mba’eveteve. Añesorprende ahechávo opytu’uha pe muro rehe pe eje de madera Queequeg arpón rehegua, pe óga jára oipe’ava’ekue chugui pe ka’aru mboyve, rojupi mboyve pe cámara-pe. Upéva ningo iñextraño, apensa; ha katu taha’e ha’éva, pe arpón oñembo’y rupi upérupi, ha sa’i térã araka’eve ndohói tetã ambuére hese’ỹ, upévare oiméne oĩ ko’ápe hyepýpe, ha ndaipóri javy ikatúva.

“¡Queequeg!—¡Queequeg!”—opa mba’e gueteri. Oiméne oiko raʼe peteĩ mbaʼe. ¡Apoplejía rehegua! Añeha’ã aipe’a pe okẽ; ha katu ipy’ahatãme oñemoĩ. Añani aja escalera-gui, pya’e amombe’u che sospecha pe primera persona aikuaava’ekuépe —pe sirvienta de cámara. “La! la!” hasẽ: “Aimo’ãkuri oiméne peteĩ mba’e ha’eha pe mba’e. Aha ajapo pe tupa akaru rire, ha pe okẽ oñemboty; ha ndaha’éi peteĩ ryguasu oñehendu haĝua; ha upe guive okirirĩeterei. Ha katu apensa, ikatu, mokõivéva peho ha pemboty pende equipaje oñeñongatu haĝua seguro. La! la, señora!—¡Señora! jejuka! ¡Kuñakarai Hussey! apoplejía!”—ha ko’ã sapukái reheve, oñani cocina gotyo, che asegui.

Pya’e ojekuaa kuñakarai Hussey, peteĩ ipópe peteĩ mostaza ryru ha ambuépe peteĩ vinagre-cruet, ojeipe’a ramoite pe ocupación oñatende haĝua umi ricino-gui, ha ojahéi imembykuimba’e morotĩ michĩvape upe aja.

“¡Yvyra-óga!”. che asapukái, “mba’e tapérepa? ¡Eñani Ñandejára rehehápe, ha egueru peteĩ mba’e reipe’a haĝua pe okẽ —pe hacha!—pe hacha! ha'e oguerekóma peteĩ derrame cerebral; odepende hese!”—ha upéicha ha’évo ajupi jey inmetodicamente escalera-pe po nandi, kuñakarai Hussey ointerponévo pe mostaza-olla ha vinagre-cruet, ha pe ricino entero hova rehegua.

“Mba’e piko nde rasy, mitãrusu?”.

“¡Egueraha pe hacha! Ñandejára rehehápe, peñani pe pohanohárape, algún, che aipe’a aja!”

“Emañamína ko’ápe”, he’i pe óga jára, pya’e omoĩvo pe vinagre-cruet, ikatu hağuáicha oreko peteĩ ipo sãsõme; “emañamína ko’ápe; ¿reñe’ẽpa reipe’a haĝua oimeraẽ che rokẽ?”—ha upéva he’ívo ojagarra che po. —Mba'e piko la oikóva? ¿Mbaʼe piko la oikóva, ne kompañero de barco?”.

Ikatuháicha ipy’aguapy, ha katu pya’e, ame’ẽ chupe ontende haĝua pe káso pukukue. Opopete inconscientemente pe vinagre-cruet peteĩ lado ijyva ári, ha’e o’rumina sapy’ami; upéi osapukái —“Nahániri! Amoĩ guive upépe ndahechái”. Oñani peteĩ armario michĩvape pe escalera aterrizaje guýpe, omaña hyepýpe, ha ojevy, he’i chéve ofaltaha Queequeg arpón. “Ojejuka”, hasẽ. “Ha’e lamentable Stiggs ojapo jey —oho ambue contrapane —Ñandejára oiporiahuvereko isy mboriahúpe!—ha’éta che róga ruina. ¿Oguerekópa peteĩ ermána pe mitãkariaʼy mboriahu? Moõpa oĩ upe mitãkuña?—upépe, Betty, tereho Snarles el Pintor rendápe, ha ere chupe opinta haĝua chéve peteĩ letrero, orekóva —“ndaipóri ojepermiti ojesuicida ko’ápe, ha ndojepitái salón-pe;”— ikatu avei ojuka mokõive guyra peteĩ jeýpe. Porojuka? ¡Ñandejára toiporiahuvereko ijespíritu! Mba'épa upe tyapu upépe? Nde, mitãrusu, avast upépe!”

Ha oñani che rapykuéri, chejagarra añeha’ã jeývo aipe’a mbaretépe pe okẽ.

“Ndahejái; Ndaguerekomo'ãi che local oñembyaíva. Tereho pe cerrajero rehehápe, oĩ peteĩ kilómetro rupi koʼágui. ¡Ha katu avast!”. omoĩvo ipo ibolsillo lateral-pe, “ko’ápe oĩ peteĩ llave oike porãtava, aimo’ã; jahechamína”. Ha upéva reheve, ombojere pe cerradura-pe; ha katu, ¡ay! Queequeg perno suplementario opyta ndojeretira hyepýpe.

“Tekotevẽ arreventá”, ha’e, ha añanimi hína kuri pe entrada-gui, peteĩ ñepyrũ porãrã, pe óga jára chejagarra, opromete jey nda’ipe’amo’ãiha ilokales; ha katu aikytĩ chugui, ha peteĩ tete jepopete sapy’a reheve añembotavy henyhẽ pe marca rehe.

Peteĩ tyapu prodigioso reheve pe okẽ oveve ojepe’a, ha pe perilla ojekutu pe pared rehe, omondo pe yeso techo-pe; ha upépe, yvága porã! upépe oguapy Queequeg, en conjunto ro’ysã ha oñembyatýva ijehegui; koty mbytépe voi; oñembo’y ijamón ári, ha ojoko Yojo-pe iñakã ári. Ha’e ndojesarekói ni peteĩ hendáicha ni ambue gotyo, ha katu oguapy peteĩ ta’anga tallada-icha sa’i orekóva peteĩ señal vida activa rehegua.

“Queequeg”, ha’e chupe, ajupívo hendápe, “Queequeg, mba’e piko nde rasy?”.

“Ha’e ndaha’éikuri peteĩ sittin’ upéicha opa ára pukukue, ¿ajépa?” he’i pe óga jára.

Ha katu opa mba’e ro’eva’ekue, ni peteĩ ñe’ẽ ndaikatúikuri roguenohẽ chugui; Haimete añandu amopu’ãse chupe, ikatu hağuáicha amoambue iposición, haimete intolerable haguére, ha’ete hasyetereíva ha innaturalmente ojejokóva; especialmente, opa probabilidad-pe oguapy haguéicha upéicha ocho térã diez hora yvate gotyo, oho avei okaru’ỹre jepi.

"Kuñakarai. Hussey”, ha’e che, “ha’e oikove opa mba’e oikóvape; upévare orereja, reipotáramo, ha che voi ahecháta ko mbaʼe iñextraño”.

Ambotývo pe okẽ pe óga jára ári, añeha’ã agana Queequeg-pe ajagarra haĝua peteĩ apyka; ha katu en vano. Upépe oguapy; ha opa ikatúva ojapo —opa che arte cortés ha che blandishments-pe ĝuarã —nomomýiri peteĩ clavija, ni nde’íri ni peteĩ ñe’ẽ, ni nomañamo’ãi cherehe, ni ndohechakuaamo’ãi che presencia ni michĩmi.

Añeporandu, apensa, ikatúpa kóva ha’e peteĩ parte iRamadán-gui; piko oajuna hikuái ijamón-pe upéicha iisla heñói haguépe. Oiméne upéicha; heẽ, ha’e parte icredo-gui, aimo’ã; bueno, upéicharõ, topytu’u; ha’e opu’ãta pya’e térã tarde, katuete. Ndaikatúi odura tapiaite ĝuarã, aguyje Ñandejárape, ha iRamadán oú peteĩ jeýnte al año; ha ndagueroviái ha'eha puntualiterei upérõ.

Aguejy akaru haguã. Oguapy rire are ohendu umi marinero rembiasakue puku oúva’ekue peteĩ viaje de ciruela-pudín-gui, ohenóiháicha hikuái (he’iséva, peteĩ viaje mbykymi ballena-pe peteĩ goleta térã brig-pe, oñeconfinava’ekue línea norte gotyo , paraguasu Atlántico-pe añoite); ahendu rire ko’ã ciruela-pudín haimete once aravo peve, ajupi escalera-pe ake haĝua, añeñandu seguroiterei ko’ã momento-pe Queequeg katuete ogueru ramadán peteĩ terminación-pe. Ha katu nahániri; upépe ha’e oĩ pe che aheja haguépe voi; ha’e noñemongu’éikuri ni peteĩ pulgada. Añepyrũ chembopy’arory hese; ha’ete ku añetehápe ndorekóiva sentido ha itavy oguapy haguére upépe ára pukukue ha mbyte pyhare ijamón ári peteĩ koty ro’ysãme, oguerekóva peteĩ yvyra pehẽngue iñakã ári.

“Yvága rehehápe, Queequeg, epu’ã ha eñemongu’e; epu’ã ha ekaru’imi. Remanóta ñembyahýigui; nde rejukáta, Queequeg”. Péro ni peteĩ ñeʼẽ nombohováiri.

Upévare, adesespera haguére chugui, adesidi añeno ha ake; ha katuete, heta tiempo mboyve, ha’e chemoirũta. Ha katu aike mboyve, ajagarra che chaqueta oso pire pohýi, ha amombo hi’ári, opromete haguéicha ha’étaha peteĩ pyhare ro’yeterei; ha ndorekói mba’eve ndaha’éirõ ijao redonda ordinario. Sapy’ami aja, ajapo opa mba’e ajapótava, ndaikatúi aike pe dormir michĩvévape. Che ambogue kuri pe vela; ha pe pensamiento añónte Queequeg rehegua —ndaha’éi irundy pie mombyry —oguapyha upépe upe posición ndahasýivape, ha’eño ro’ysã ha pytũmbýpe; péva chembopy’arory añete. Pepensamína hese; oke pyhare pukukue peteĩ koty’ípe peteĩ pagano amplio despierta reheve ijamón ári ko Ramadán ñembyasy ha ndorekóiva cuenta-pe!

Ha katu algún modo aguejy ipahápe, ha ndaikuaái mba’evete ko’ẽmbotávo peve; omañávo tupa ykére, upépe oñembo’y Queequeg, ha’ete ku ojetornillava’ekue chupe yvýpe. Ha katu pe kuarahy jehecha peteĩha oike ramoite pe ventána-pe, ojupi ojupi, umi articulación hatã ha rejilla reheve, ha katu peteĩ jesareko vy’ápe; oñembo’y che gotyo añenohápe; opresiona jey isyva che rehe; ha he’i opamaha iRamadán.

Ko’áğa, che ainsinua haguéicha yma, ndarekói objeción oimeraẽ tapicha religión rehe, taha’e ha’éva, upe tapicha ndojukái ni ndoinsultái aja ambue tapichápe, upe ambue tapicha ndogueroviáigui avei. Péro peteĩ kuimbaʼe rrelihión añetehápe oñembopyʼarory jave; ha’éramo peteĩ tormento positivo chupe ĝuarã; ha, ipahápe, ojapo ko ñande yvygui peteĩ posada incómoda japytu’u haĝua; upéi apensa ha’eha ára ha’eha ára jagueraha haĝua peteĩ lado upe individuo-pe ha adiscuti hendive pe punto.

Ha upéichaite ko’áğa ajapo Queequeg ndive. “Queequeg”, ha’e chéve, “ejupi ko’áĝa, ha eñeno ha cherendu”. Upérõ aha, añepyrũvo umi religión primitiva opu’ã ha progreso reheve, ha aguejy umi opáichagua religión ko’áĝaguápe, upe tiempo aja amba’apo ahechauka haĝua Queequeg-pe opa ko’ã Cuaresma, Ramadán ha jamón-squattings ipukúva ro’ysãme , umi koty vy’a’ỹva ha’ekuri peteĩ mba’e ndovaléiva mba’everã; ivaíva tesãirã; ndovaléiva mba’everã pe ángape ĝuarã; ombocháke, mbykyhápe, umi léi ojehechakuaáva Higiene ha sentido común rehe. Che ha’e chupe, avei, ha’égui ambue mba’épe peteĩ salvaje extremadamente sensato ha sagaz, chembohasy, chembohasy vaieterei, ahechávo chupe ko’áğa deplorablemente itavy ko ridículo Ramadán imba’évare. Avei, aargumenta che, pe ayuno ojapo pe tete oike haĝua; upégui pe espíritu oike; ha opa pensamiento heñóiva peteĩ ayuno-gui katuete oîva’erã medio ñembyahýigui. Péva ha’e pe mba’érepa la mayoría umi religioso dispéptico omomba’eguasu ko’ãichagua noción melancólica hembireko rehegua. Peteĩ ñe’ẽme, Queequeg, ha’e che, digresivamente; infierno ha’e peteĩ idea heñói ypyva’ekue peteĩ manzana-bollo nodigerido-pe; ha upe guive operpetúa dispepsia hereditaria rupive omongakuaáva Ramadán.

Upérõ aporandu Queequeg-pe ha’e voipa oipy’apýpa araka’eve dispepsia rehe; oikuaaukávo hesakã porãiterei pe idea, ikatu haguã ogueraha ipype He’i nahániri; peteĩ ocasión mandu’arãme añoite. Ha’e peteĩ karu guasu guasu rire ome’ẽva itúva mburuvicha guasu, ohupyty haguére peteĩ ñorairõ guasu ojejukahápe cincuenta enemigo-gui amo dos aravo asajepyte peve, ha opavave oñembojy ha oje’u upe ka’aruete voi.

“Ndaiporivéima, Queequeg”, ha’e che oryrýivo; “upéva ojapóta;” aikuaágui umi inferencia ha’e oinsinuave’ỹre. Ahechava’ekue peteĩ marinero ovisita va’ekue upe isla-pe voi, ha he’i chéve ojepokuaaha, ojehupyty jave upépe peteĩ ñorairõ tuicháva, ojejapo barbacoa opa umi ojejukávape pe victorioso korapýpe térã yvotytype; ha upéi, peteĩ teĩ, oñemoĩ umi zanja yvyrágui tuichávape, ha oñembojegua pilau-icha, mbujape ha coco reheve; ha ijurúpe perejil reheve, oñemondo ijerére pe victorioso elogio reheve opa iñamigokuérape, ha’ete ku ko’ã jopói hetaiterei pavo navideño rehegua.

Añetehápe, nachepensái umi mbaʼe añeʼẽva rrelihiónre tuicha oimpresiona hague Queequeg-pe. Pórke, primer lugar-pe, ha’etevaicha de alguna manera ohendúvo upe tema iñimportánteva, ndaha’éiramo oñekonsideráva ipuntos de vista-gui; ha, mokõiha tendápe, ha’e ndaha’éi hetave peteĩ tercio-gui chentende, sofá che ideakuéra simplemente che aipotaháicha; ha, ipahápe, katuete oimo’ã oikuaaveha heta mba’e pe religión añeteguágui chehegui. Ha’e omaña cherehe peteĩ especie de preocupación ha compasión condescendiente reheve, ha’ete ku oimo’ãva tuicha ñembyasy peteĩ mitãrusu sensato-icha okañy esperanza’ỹre piedad pagana evangélica-gui.

Ipahápe ropu’ã ha roñemonde; ha Queequeg, ro’u peteĩ desayuno prodigiosamente henyhẽva chowder opáichagua, ani haĝua pe óga jára ohupyty heta ganancia iRamadán rupi, rosẽ rojupi haĝua pe Pequod-pe, roguata ha roiporavo ore juru aleta rague reheve.





MOAKÃHA 18. IMarcos.

Roguata aja pe muelle rembe’ýgui pe barco gotyo, Queequeg ogueraháva iarpón, capitán Peleg iñe’ẽ hũme hatã oremomaitei iwigwam-gui, he’ívo ndosospechái hague che angirũ ha’eha peteĩ caníbal, ha hi’arive omoherakuãvo ohejaha nahániri umi caníbal oĩva upe artesanía-pe, ndaha’éiramo ojapova’ekue hikuái ikuatiakuéra.

“¿Mba’e piko ere upéva, capitán Péleg?”. ha’e che, ko’áğa atyryry umi baluarte ári, ha ahejávo che compañero-pe oñembo’y muelle-pe.

“Che ha’ese”, ombohovái, “ohechaukava’erã ikuatiakuéra”.

“Heẽ”, he’i capitán Bildad iñe’ẽ hueco-pe, omoĩvo iñakã Peleg rapykuéri guive, osẽvo pe wigwam-gui. “Ohechaukava'erã oñekonverti hague. Pytũmby ra’y”, ombojoapy ojerévo Queequeg-pe, “¿ko’áĝa piko reime comunión oimeraẽ tupao cristiana ndive?”.

“Mba’ére piko”, ha’e chupe, “ha’e miembro peteĩha Iglesia Congregacional-pe”. Ko’ápe oje’e, heta salvaje tatuado ohóva Nantucket barco-pe ipahápe oĝuahẽ oñekonverti haĝua tupaópe.

Bildad osapukái: “Primera Iglesia Congregacional, mba’e! upéva oadoráva Diácono Deuteronomio Coleman reunión rógape?”. ha péicha he’ívo, oguenohẽvo anteojo, ohetũ ipañuelo bandana hovy tuichaitéva reheve, ha omoĩ porãiterei, osẽ pe wigwam-gui, ha oñembo’y hatã umi baluarte ári, omaña porã puku Queequeg rehe.

“¿Mboy tiémpopa haʼe miembro?”. he’i upéi, ojerévo cherehe; “ndaha’éi ipukuetereíva, aimo’ã porãve, mitãrusu”.

“Nahániri”, he’i Peleg, “ha ha’e noñemongaraíri avei hekopete, térã ojohéi va’erãmo’ã hovagui michĩmi upe aña hovy”.

“Emombe’u, ko’áĝa”, osapukái Bildad, “ko filistéo piko miembro regular Diácono Deuteronomio reunión-pe? Araka’eve ndahecháiva chupe ohoha upépe, ha ahasa káda Ñandejára ára”.

“Ndaikuaái mba’eve Diácono Deuteronomio ni irreunión rehegua”, ha’e; “opa mba’e aikuaáva ha’e, Queequeg ko’ápe ha’eha peteĩ miembro nacido Primera Iglesia Congregacional-pe. Ha’e voi diácono, Queequeg ha’e”.

“Mitãrusu”, he’i Bildad ipy’ahatã, “nde reñemongu’e chendive —emyesakã, nde mitãrusu hitita. Mba'e tupao piko ere? embohovái chéve”.

Ajejuhúvo péicha añembotavy hatã, ambohovái. “Che ha’ese, karai, pe Iglesia Católica yma guare voi nde ha che, ha Capitán Peleg upépe, ha Queequeg ko’ápe, ha opavave ñande, ha opa sy ra’y ha ánga ñande jaha’éva; pe Primera Congregación tuicha ha opave’ỹva opa ko mundo de adoración-pe; opavave ñande ha’e upéva mba’e; oĩnte ñande apytépe omomba’éva algunos crotchets queer ni mba’éichapa opokóva pe gran jerovia rehe; upéicha rupi opavave ñañomoirũ”.

“Embojoaju, erese rembojoajuha po”, osapukái Peleg, oñemoaguĩvo. “Mitãrusu, iporãvéta remondo peteĩ misionero-pe ĝuarã, peteĩ po mástil delantero rangue; Araka’eve nahendúiva peteĩ sermón iporãvéva. Diácono Deuteronomio —mba’érepa pa’i Mapple voi ndaikatúi ipu’aka hese, ha ha’e oconta peteĩ mba’e. Eju a bordo, eju a bordo; araka’eve noimportái umi kuatia rehe. Che ha’e, ere Quohog-pe upépe —mba’épa upéva pehenói chupe? ere Quohog-pe toñemongu’e hese. Pe ancla guasu rupive, mba’eichaitépa oguereko upépe! ha’ete mba’e porã upéva; ha ha’e omaneja hekopete. Che ha’e, Quohog, térã taha’e ha’éva nde réra, ¿reñembo’y piko peteĩ ballena-barco akãme? ¿reinupã raʼe peteĩ pira?”.

MOAKÃHA 20. Opavave Astir.

Ohasa peteĩ térã mokõi ára, ha tuicha actividad pe Pequod-pe. Ndaha’éi umi vela tuja añónte oñemyatyrõva, ha katu oúkuri a bordo umi vela pyahu, ha umi perno de lona, ​​ha umi bobina de aparejo; en pocas palabras, opa mba’e ohechauka pe barco ñembosako’i pya’e oñemohu’ãmaha. Capitán Peleg sa’i térã araka’eve ndohói yvýpe, ha katu oguapy iwigwam-pe omantene peteĩ jesareko haimbe umi póre: Bildad ojapo opa mba’e ojoguáva ha ome’ẽva umi tenda; ha umi kuimba’e omba’apóva pe hold-pe ha pe aparejo-pe omba’apo hína kuri heta ára pyhare peve.

Pe ára Queequeg ofirma rire umi artículo, oñeme’ẽ ñe’ẽ opa umi posada-pe opytahápe pe barco compañía, ijyvakuéra oĩva’erãha a bordo pyhare mboyve, ndaipórigui oje’éva mba’éichapa pya’e ikatu oveve pe barco. Upévare Queequeg ha che roguejy ore ñuhãgui, rodesidi, jepe, roke yvýpe ipahaite peve. Péro haʼete ku siémpre omeʼẽva hikuái aviso ipukuetereíva koʼã kásope, ha pe várko ndohói heta díare. Ha katu ndaha’éi peteĩ mba’e sorpresa; oĩkuri peteĩ mba’e porã ojejapova’erã, ha ndaipóri oje’éva mboy mba’épa ojepy’amongetava’erã, pe Pequod oñembosako’ipa mboyve.

Maymáva oikuaa mba’eichaitépa hetaiterei mba’e —tupa, sartén, kyse ha tenedor, pala ha pinza, servilleta, galleta de nuez ha mba’épa nahániri, ha’e indispensable pe negocio de limpieza de casa-pe ĝuarã. Upéicha avei pe ballena jejopy rehe, oikotevẽva peteĩ óga tres áño pukukue pe océano tuicháva ári, mombyry opa umi comestible, costero, pohanohára, panadero ha banquero-gui. Ha jepe péva añete avei umi barco comerciante-pe, jepémo upéicha ndaha’éi mba’eveichavérõ peteĩcha umi ballena-kuéra ndive. Pórke además ipukueterei pe viaje de ballenas, umi hetaiterei artículo peculiar pe procesamiento pesca rehegua, ha ndaikatuiha oñemyengovia umi puerto mombyrýpe ojeho jepi jepi, ñanemandu’ava’erã, opaite barco apytépe, umi barco caza de ballena ha’eha pe ojehechaukavéva opaichagua accidente-pe, ha especialmente oñehundi ha ojeperdévo umi mba’eite odependevéva hese pe viaje éxito. Upévare, umi barco de repuesto, spare de repuesto ha línea ha arpón de repuesto, ha opa mba’e de repuesto, haimete, ha katu peteĩ Capitán de repuesto ha barco duplicado.

Pe periodo roĝuahẽvo Isla-pe, haimete oñemohu’ãma pe almacenamiento ipohýivéva Pequod-pe; oguerekóva ivaka ro’o, mbujape, y, combustible ha hierro aro ha yvyra. Ha katu, oñeinsinua haguéicha yma, peteĩ tiempo aja oĩkuri peteĩ jegueru ha jegueraha continuo a bordo umi buzo-kuéra odds ha ends de cosas, tuicháva ha michĩva.

Mburuvicha umi ojapóva ko jegueru ha jegueraha apytépe ha’e Capitán Bildad reindy, peteĩ kuñakarai tuja magra orekóva peteĩ espíritu determinado ha indefatigable, ha katu withal ipy’aporãitereíva, ha’etévaicha odesidíva, ikatúramo oipytyvõ, ndojejuhúi mba’eve ofaltáva pe Pequod-pe , peteĩ jey og̃uahẽ rire hekopete mar gotyo. Peteĩ momento-pe oúta a bordo peteĩ jarra de encurtidos reheve pe mayordomo despensa-pe ĝuarã; ambue jey peteĩ aty pluma reheve pe compañero principal escritorio-pe ĝuarã, ha’e oñongatuhápe itrebro; a third time with a roll of flannel for the small of some one's rheumatic back. Never did any woman better deserve her name, which was Charity—Aunt Charity, as everybody called her. And like a sister of charity did this charitable Aunt Charity bustle about hither and thither, ready to turn her hand and heart to anything that promised to yield safety, comfort, and consolation to all on board a ship in which her beloved brother Bildad was concerned, and in which she herself owned a score or two of well-saved dollars.

But it was startling to see this excellent hearted Quakeress coming on board, as she did the last day, with a long oil-ladle in one hand, and a still longer whaling lance in the other. Nor was Bildad himself nor Captain Peleg at all backward. As for Bildad, he carried about with him a long list of the articles needed, and at every fresh arrival, down went his mark opposite that article upon the paper. Every once in a while Peleg came hobbling out of his whalebone den, roaring at the men down the hatchways, roaring up to the riggers at the mast-head, and then concluded by roaring back into his wigwam.

During these days of preparation, Queequeg and I often visited the craft, and as often I asked about Captain Ahab, and how he was, and when he was going to come on board his ship. To these questions they would answer, that he was getting better and better, and was expected aboard every day; meantime, the two captains, Peleg and Bildad, could attend to everything necessary to fit the vessel for the voyage. If I had been downright honest with myself, I would have seen very plainly in my heart that I did but half fancy being committed this way to so long a voyage, without once laying my eyes on the man who was to be the absolute dictator of it, so soon as the ship sailed out upon the open sea. But when a man suspects any wrong, it sometimes happens that if he be already involved in the matter, he insensibly strives to cover up his suspicions even from himself. And much this way it was with me. I said nothing, and tried to think nothing.

At last it was given out that some time next day the ship would certainly sail. So next morning, Queequeg and I took a very early start.





CHAPTER 21. Going Aboard.

It was nearly six o'clock, but only grey imperfect misty dawn, when we drew nigh the wharf.

“There are some sailors running ahead there, if I see right,” said I to Queequeg, “it can't be shadows; she's off by sunrise, I guess; come on!”

“Avast!” cried a voice, whose owner at the same time coming close behind us, laid a hand upon both our shoulders, and then insinuating himself between us, stood stooping forward a little, in the uncertain twilight, strangely peering from Queequeg to me. It was Elijah.

“Going aboard?”

“Hands off, will you,” said I.

“Lookee here,” said Queequeg, shaking himself, “go 'way!”

“Ain't going aboard, then?”

“Yes, we are,” said I, “but what business is that of yours? Do you know, Mr. Elijah, that I consider you a little impertinent?”

“No, no, no; I wasn't aware of that,” said Elijah, slowly and wonderingly looking from me to Queequeg, with the most unaccountable glances.

“Elijah,” said I, “you will oblige my friend and me by withdrawing. We are going to the Indian and Pacific Oceans, and would prefer not to be detained.”

“Ye be, be ye? Coming back afore breakfast?”

“He's cracked, Queequeg,” said I, “come on.”

“Holloa!” cried stationary Elijah, hailing us when we had removed a few paces.

“Never mind him,” said I, “Queequeg, come on.”

But he stole up to us again, and suddenly clapping his hand on my shoulder, said—“Did ye see anything looking like men going towards that ship a while ago?”

Struck by this plain matter-of-fact question, I answered, saying, “Yes, I thought I did see four or five men; but it was too dim to be sure.”

“Very dim, very dim,” said Elijah. “Morning to ye.”

Once more we quitted him; but once more he came softly after us; and touching my shoulder again, said, “See if you can find 'em now, will ye?

“Find who?”

“Morning to ye! morning to ye!” he rejoined, again moving off. “Oh! I was going to warn ye against—but never mind, never mind—it's all one, all in the family too;—sharp frost this morning, ain't it? Good-bye to ye. Shan't see ye again very soon, I guess; unless it's before the Grand Jury.” And with these cracked words he finally departed, leaving me, for the moment, in no small wonderment at his frantic impudence.

At last, stepping on board the Pequod, we found everything in profound quiet, not a soul moving. The cabin entrance was locked within; the hatches were all on, and lumbered with coils of rigging. Going forward to the forecastle, we found the slide of the scuttle open. Seeing a light, we went down, and found only an old rigger there, wrapped in a tattered pea-jacket. He was thrown at whole length upon two chests, his face downwards and inclosed in his folded arms. The profoundest slumber slept upon him.

“Those sailors we saw, Queequeg, where can they have gone to?” said I, looking dubiously at the sleeper. But it seemed that, when on the wharf, Queequeg had not at all noticed what I now alluded to; hence I would have thought myself to have been optically deceived in that matter, were it not for Elijah's otherwise inexplicable question. But I beat the thing down; and again marking the sleeper, jocularly hinted to Queequeg that perhaps we had best sit up with the body; telling him to establish himself accordingly. He put his hand upon the sleeper's rear, as though feeling if it was soft enough; and then, without more ado, sat quietly down there.

“Gracious! Queequeg, don't sit there,” said I.

“Oh! perry dood seat,” said Queequeg, “my country way; won't hurt him face.”

“Face!” said I, “call that his face? very benevolent countenance then; but how hard he breathes, he's heaving himself; get off, Queequeg, you are heavy, it's grinding the face of the poor. Get off, Queequeg! Look, he'll twitch you off soon. I wonder he don't wake.”

Queequeg removed himself to just beyond the head of the sleeper, and lighted his tomahawk pipe. I sat at the feet. We kept the pipe passing over the sleeper, from one to the other. Meanwhile, upon questioning him in his broken fashion, Queequeg gave me to understand that, in his land, owing to the absence of settees and sofas of all sorts, the king, chiefs, and great people generally, were in the custom of fattening some of the lower orders for ottomans; and to furnish a house comfortably in that respect, you had only to buy up eight or ten lazy fellows, and lay them round in the piers and alcoves. Besides, it was very convenient on an excursion; much better than those garden-chairs which are convertible into walking-sticks; upon occasion, a chief calling his attendant, and desiring him to make a settee of himself under a spreading tree, perhaps in some damp marshy place.

While narrating these things, every time Queequeg received the tomahawk from me, he flourished the hatchet-side of it over the sleeper's head.

“What's that for, Queequeg?”

“Perry easy, kill-e; oh! perry easy!”

He was going on with some wild reminiscences about his tomahawk-pipe, which, it seemed, had in its two uses both brained his foes and soothed his soul, when we were directly attracted to the sleeping rigger. The strong vapor now completely filling the contracted hole, it began to tell upon him. He breathed with a sort of muffledness; then seemed troubled in the nose; then revolved over once or twice; then sat up and rubbed his eyes.

“Holloa!” he breathed at last, “who be ye smokers?”

“Shipped men,” answered I, “when does she sail?”

“Aye, aye, ye are going in her, be ye? She sails to-day. The Captain came aboard last night.”

“What Captain?—Ahab?”

“Who but him indeed?”

I was going to ask him some further questions concerning Ahab, when we heard a noise on deck.

“Holloa! Starbuck's astir,” said the rigger. “He's a lively chief mate, that; good man, and a pious; but all alive now, I must turn to.” And so saying he went on deck, and we followed.

It was now clear sunrise. Soon the crew came on board in twos and threes; the riggers bestirred themselves; the mates were actively engaged; and several of the shore people were busy in bringing various last things on board. Meanwhile Captain Ahab remained invisibly enshrined within his cabin.
