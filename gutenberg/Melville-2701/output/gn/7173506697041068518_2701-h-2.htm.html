MOAKÃHA 4. Pe Contrapanel.

Apu’ãvo ambue pyhareve ára resape rehe, ajuhu Queequeg po ojeity che ári mborayhu ha mborayhuvévape. Haimete reimo'ãkuri che ha'eha hembireko. Pe contrapalde ha’e kuri remiendo-gui, henyhẽva cuadrado ha triángulo michĩ impar-color parti-gui; ha ko ijyva ojetatuáva oparupiete peteĩ laberinto cretano interminable peteĩ figura-gui, ndaipóri mokõi parte ha’éva peteĩ sombra precisa —aimo’ã omantene haguére ipo mar-pe inmetódicamente kuarahy ha sombra-pe, umi manga kamisa ojeroky irregularmente opáichagua época-pe —ko ipo peteĩchagua, ha’e, oheka opa mundo peteĩ tira-icha upe edredón remiedo-gui voi. Añetehápe, en parte añenóvo hi’ári pe po ojapo haguéicha apu’ã ypy ramo guare, hasy aikuaa pe edredón-gui, ombojehe’aiterei hikuái umi tono oñondive; ha pe sentido de peso ha presión rupive añoite ikatu ahechakuaa Queequeg cheañuãha.

Che sensaciones ha’e kuri extraño. Añeha’ãta amombe’u umíva. Che mitãrõ guare, chemandu’a porã peteĩ circunstancia ojoguáva’ekue michĩmi cherehe; taha’e peteĩ realidad térã peteĩ sueño, araka’eve ndaikatúikuri añemohendapaite. Pe circunstancia ha’e kuri péicha. Aikytĩkuri algún alcaparra térã ambue —aimo’ã oñeha’ãha otyryry pe chimenea-pe, ahecha haguéicha peteĩ barrido michĩmi ojapo mbovy ára mboyve; ha che madrastra, de alguna manera térã ambue, cheinupãva meme, térã chemondóva tupape cena’ỹre, —che sy chegueraha che pygui pe chimenea-gui ha chembojehe’a añeno haĝua, jepe ha’e mokõi aravo añónte asaje 21 jasyrundy, ára ipukuvéva ko arýpe ñande hemisferio-pe. Añeñandu vaieterei. Ha katu ndaipóri pytyvõ upévarã, upévare ajupi escalera-pe aha che koty’ípe oĩva tercer piso-pe, añemonde mbeguekatu ikatuháicha ajuka haĝua tiempo, ha peteĩ suspiro amargo reheve aike umi sábana mbytépe.

Añeno upépe ñembyasýpe acalculávo ohasa va’erãha dieciséis hora entera ikatu mboyve aha’arõ peteĩ resurrección. ¡Dieciséis aravo tupape! che jyva michĩva hasy apensa haĝua hese. Ha ivevúiete avei; kuarahy omimbi oike ventána-pe, ha tuicha tyapu umi autocar umi tape rehe, ha umi gay ñe’ẽ ryapu opa ógape. Añeñandu vaive ha ivaive —ipahápe apu’ã, añemonde ha aguejy mbeguekatu che py medias-pe, aheka che madrastra-pe ha sapy’aitépe añemombo ipy gotyo, ajerure’asývo chupe peteĩ favor particular ramo ome’ẽ haĝua chéve peteĩ deslizamiento porã che-pe ĝuarã teko vai; oimeraẽ mba’e añetehápe ndaha’éirõ chekondenávo ijapu haĝua abed peichagua tiempo ipukúva insoportable. Ha katu ha’e pe iporãvéva ha ikonsiénsiavéva umi madrastra apytépe, ha aju jey aha va’erã che kotýpe. Heta hora aja añeno upépe añembopiro’y, añeñandu tuicha ivaiveha upe guive, jepe umi desgracia tuichavéva oúva upe rire. Ipahápe oiméne ho’a peteĩ pesadilla iproblema-pe peteĩ dormir-pe; ha mbeguekatúpe apu’ãvo chugui —medio añembohypýiva sueño-pe —aipe’a che resa, ha pe koty kuarahy resape mboyvegua ko’áĝa ojejokua pytũmbýpe okápe. Upepete añandu peteĩ ñemondýi oñani opa che marco rupi; mbaʼeve ndojehechái vaʼerã, ha mbaʼeve noñehendúi vaʼerã; ha katu ha’ete ku oñemoĩva che po ári peteĩ po sobrenatural. Che po oñemoĩ pe encimera ári, ha pe forma térã fantasma héra’ỹva, oñeimagina’ỹva, kirirĩháme, pe po ha’éva, ha’ete oguapy porãva che tupa ykére. Pe ha’etévaicha edades oñembyatýva edades ári, añeno upépe, congelado umi kyhyje ivaivéva reheve, nañanimái aipe’a che po; jepeve araka’eve apensa ikatúramo amongu’e peteĩ pulgada-nte, pe hechizo horrible oñembyaíta. Ndaikuaái mba’éichapa ipahápe ko conciencia ojedesliza chehegui; ha katu apu’ãvo pyhareve, oryrýivo chemandu’a opa mba’ére, ha ára ha semana ha jasy upe rire aperde umi ñeha’ã confuso-pe amombe’u haĝua pe misterio. Nahániri, ko aravo peve, py’ỹi añembotavy hese.

Ko’áğa, peipe’a pe kyhyje vaiete, ha che sensaciones añandu haĝua pe po sobrenatural che mba’évape ojoguaiterei, iñextraño-pe, umi ahasava’ekuépe apu’ãvo ha ahechávo Queequeg po pagano oñemombo che jerére. Ha katu ipahápe opa umi acontecimiento pyhare ohasava’ekuépe ojerrecurri sobriamente, peteĩ teĩ, realidad fija-pe, ha upéi añeno aikove pe dificultad cómica-pe añoite. Pórke añeha’ãramo jepe amomyi ipo —adesblokea pe novio cierre —ha katu, oke ha’e haguéicha, ha’e cheañuã mbarete gueteri, ha’ete ku mba’eve ndaha’éiva pe ñemano ñandemboja’óva mokõime. Ko’áğa añeha’ã amombáy chupe —“¡Queequeg!”— ha katu iñembohovái añoite ha’e peteĩ ronco. Upérõ ajeroky, che akã oñeñandu ha’ete ku oĩva peteĩ kavaju cuello-pe; ha sapy’aitépe oñandu peteĩ rasguño michĩmi. Omombo peteĩ lado pe contrapaldo, upépe oñeno pe tomahawk oke pe salvaje ykére, ha’ete ku peteĩ mitã hova hacha. Peteĩ encurtido iporãva, añetehápe, apensa; abed ko’ápe peteĩ óga iñextraño-pe ára pukukue, peteĩ caníbal ha peteĩ tomahawk ndive! “¡Queequeg!—mba’eporã rérape, Queequeg, epu’ã!” Ipahápe, heta revuelta rupive, ha expostulaciones hatã ha incesante rupi pe indekocuada oñañua haguére peteĩ hapicha kuimba’épe upe clase de estilo matrimonial-pe, ahupyty aipe’ávo peteĩ gruñido; ha ko’áĝaite, oipe’a jey ipo, oñemomýi oparupiete peteĩ jagua Terranova-peguaicha yguiite, ha oguapy tupape, hatã peteĩ lucio-icha, omañávo cherehe ha ohetũ hesa naimandu’áiramoguáichaite mba’éichapa aĝuahẽ aime upépe, jepe peteĩ conciencia hũ aikuaa haĝua peteĩ mba’e chehegui ha’ete mbeguekatúpe ko’ẽmbotáva hi’ári. Upe aja, añeno kirirĩháme amaña hese, ndarekóigui duda grave ko’áĝa, ha añembo’y ahecha haĝua estrechamente peteĩ criatura curiosoitereíva. Ipahápe, ha’ete ku iñakã ojejapóva opokóvo ikompañero de cama carácter rehe, ha ha’e, ja’eporãsérõ, oñemoĩ porã pe hecho ndive; osẽ yvýpe, ha ciertas señales ha sonidos rupive ome’ẽ chéve antende haĝua, chegustáramo, oñemonde raẽtaha ha upéi cherejataha añemonde upe rire, ohejávo chejehe pe apartamento tuichakue. Oimo’ã che, Queequeg, umi circunstancia-pe, kóva ha’e peteĩ ubertura civilizadaiterei; ha katu, pe añetegua ha’e, ko’ã salvaje oreko peteĩ sentido innata de delicadeza, ere pe peipotáva; ha’e maravilloso mba’éichapa esencialmente ikortés hikuái. Apaga ko elogio particular Queequeg-pe, ha’e chetrata haguére hetaiterei civilidad ha consideración reheve, che aime aja culpable tuicha grosero rehe; omañávo hese tupa guive, ha omaña opa umi movimiento inodoro-pe; pe tiempo-pe ĝuarã che curiosidad iporãvéva che cría-gui. Upéicharõ jepe, peteĩ kuimba’e Queequeg-icha nderehecháiva ára ha ára, ha’e ha hape vale la pena jepivegua’ỹva rehecha.

Oñepyrũ oñemonde yvate omondevo isombrero de castor, peteĩ ijyvatetereíva, pe by the by, ha upéi —omenos gueteri ikalsonsillo —ocaza ibotín. Mba’épa ojapo yvága guýpe, ndaikatúi amombe’u, ha katu pe movimiento oúva ha’e oñembo’y —bota ipópe, ha sombrero reheve — tupa guýpe; upérõ, opaichagua jadeo ha tensión violenta-gui, ainferi ha’e omba’apo mbareteha ombota haĝua ijehegui; jepe mba’eveichagua léi de propiedad ahenduva’ekue rupive, ndojejeruréi avave kuimba’épe ha’e haĝua privado omonde jave ibotín. Ha katu Queequeg, pehechápa, ha’eva’ekue peteĩ criatura oĩva etapa de transición-pe —ni oruga ni mariposa. Ha’e ningo civilizadoiterei ohechauka haĝua iextrañoha umi manera iñextrañovévape. Ne’ĩra gueteri oñemohu’ã iñehekombo’e. Ha’e ningo peteĩ pregrado-pe. Ndaha’éirire peteĩ grado michĩva civilizado, oiméne noñembopy’apýi va’erãmo’ã mba’eveichavérõ bota rehe; ha katu upéi, ndaha’éirire gueteri peteĩ salvaje, araka’eve noimo’ãikuri oike haĝua tupa guýpe omoĩ haĝua chupekuéra. Ipahápe, osê isombrero ojeabollaiterei ha oñemboja’o hesa ári, ha oñepyrũ okororõ ha opyrũ pe koty jerére, ha’ete ku, ndojepokuaái heta bota rehe, ipar de vaka pire húmeda ha arrugada —oiméne ndojejapói orden taha’e —oñapytĩ ha ombohasa’asyve chupe peteĩha pyhareve ro’ysã vaietépe.

Ohechávo, ko’áğa, ndaiporiha cortina pe ventána-pe, ha pe tape ijyvyku’ietereiha, pe óga hovake omanda peteĩ vista llana pe koty’ípe, ha ohechávo hetave pe figura indecorosa Queequeg ojapova’ekue, ojepysóva sa’i ambue mba’e reheve isombrero ha ibotín oñemoĩ; Ajerure’asy chupe ikatuháicha, a’acelerá haĝua michĩmi inodoro, ha particularmente oike haĝua ipantalón-pe pya’e ikatuháicha. Haʼe okumpli, ha upéi oprocede ojejohéi. Upe jave pyhareve oimeraẽ kristiáno ojohéi vaʼerãmoʼã hova; ha katu Queequeg, che sorprenderã, oñekontenta orrestringe haguére umi ablución ijyva, ijyva ha ipokuérape. Upéi omonde ichaleco, ha ojagarrávo peteĩ jabón hatã pehẽngue pe mesa centro de lavabo-pe, omoinge ýpe ha oñepyrũ oespuma hova. Amaña hína kuri ahecha haĝua moõpa oñongatu inavajas, lo ha ohecha jave, oipe’a pe arpón pe tupa esquina-gui, odesliza pe stock yvyrágui ojejapóva puku, oipe’a iñakã, ombopu’imi ibotín ári ha oñemongu’e pe pedazo peve de espejo pe pared rehe, oñepyrũ peteĩ raspador mbarete, térã ja’e porãsérõ arpón ijyva ári. Aimo’ã che, Queequeg, kóva oipuru hína Rogers cubiertos iporãvéva peteĩ venganza reheve. Upe rire sa’ive añeporandu ko operación-pe aikuaávo mba’e acero porãpa ojejapo peteĩ arpón akã, ha mba’éichapa akóinte oñeñongatu umi borde recto puku haimbeitereíva.

Pya’e ojehupyty pe hembýva inodoro-gui, ha orgulloso osẽ pe koty’ígui, ojejokua ichaqueta mono piloto tuichaitévape ha odeporta iarpón peteĩ mariscal bastón-icha.





MOAKÃHA 5. Pyhareve.

Pya’e asegui, ha aguejy pe bar-room-pe accosted pe propietario sonriente-pe vy’aiterei. Nda’amomba’éi mba’eveichagua maldad hese, jepe ha’e o’skylarking chendive ni michĩmi che compañero de cama asunto-pe.

Ha katu, peteĩ pukavy porã ha’e peteĩ mba’e porã ipu’akapáva, ha katu sa’ieterei peteĩ mba’e porã; hetave pe poriahuvereko. Upéicharõ, oiméramo peteĩ kuimba’e, ipersona hekopeteguápe, opermiti mba’e peteĩ broma porãrã oimeraẽvape, ani toime tapykuépe, ha katu toheja vy’ápe ogasta ha ojegasta upéicha. Ha pe kuimba’e orekóva oimeraẽ mba’e abundantemente opukava hese, eime seguro oĩha hetave upe kuimba’épe oiméne repensávagui.

Pe bar-koty ko’áĝa henyhẽ umi internado-gui oguejyva’ekue pe pyhare mboyve, ha ne’ĩra gueteri ahecha porã hesekuéra. Haimete opavave ha’ekuéra ha’ekuri ballena-kuéra; iñirũnguéra mburuvicha, ha mokõiha, ha mbohapyha, ha karpintero yguasúpe, ha yguasu ñemongu'e, ha yguasu herrero, ha arponero ha barco ñangarekohára; peteĩ compañía marrón ha ipy’aguasúva, barba bosky reheve; peteĩ conjunto noñeikytĩriva ha ikyrỹiva, opavave omonde chaqueta mono-pe ĝuarã umi vestido pyharevegua.

Ikatu reikuaa porãiterei mboy tiémpopa káda uno oĩ pe yvýpe. Ko mitãrusu jyva hesãiva ha’ete peteĩ pera kuarahy’ãme tostado de hueco, ha ha’etéta haimete hyakuã asýva almizcle-icha; ndaikatúikuri oguejy mbohapy ára iviaje indio-gui. Upe kuimba’e oĩva ijykére ojehecha mbovymi sombra ligero; ikatu ere peteĩ toque yvyra satén rehegua oĩha ipype. Peteĩ tercio tez-pe opyta gueteri peteĩ tawnal trópico, ha katu withal ojeblanquea’imi; ha’e katuete opyta semana pukukue yvýpe. Péro máva piko ikatu ohechauka peteĩ jyva Queequeg-icha? ha’éva, ojejokóva opáichagua tinte reheve, ha’ete ku pendiente occidental Andes-pegua, ojehechauka haĝua peteĩ matriz-pe, climas contrastante, zona por zona.

“¡Grub, ho!”. ko’áğa osapukái pe óga jára, oipe’ávo peteĩ okẽ, ha oike roho rodesayuna.

Heʼi hikuái umi kuimbaʼe ohechavaʼekue ko múndo, upéicha oikoha trankíloiterei ikompañíape, ojepytasoitereiha ijehe. Péro ndahaʼéi jepi: Ledyard, pe tuicha oviaháva Nueva Inglaterra-pe, ha Mungo Park, pe escocés; opavave kuimba’égui, ha’ekuéra oguereko pe sa’ive garantía pe salón-pe. Ha katu ikatu pe Siberia jehasa rei peteĩ trineo-pe ogueraháva jaguakuéra Ledyard ojapoháicha, térã pe jeguata puku ha’eño peteĩ tyekue nandi, África korasõ negro-pe, ha’éva pe suma Mungo mboriahu actuaciones-gui —ko’ãichagua viaje, Che ha’e, ikatu ndaha’éi pe modo iporãvéva ojehupyty haĝua peteĩ pulido social yvate. Upéicharõ jepe, la majoría, upeichagua mbaʼe ojeguerekovaʼerã mamove.

Ko’ã reflexión ko’ápe voi oje’ocasiona pe circunstancia opavave roguapy rire mesa-pe, ha añembosako’i ahendu haĝua unos cuantos tembiasakue porã ballena caza rehegua; che sorpresa ndaha’éiva michĩmi, haimete opa kuimba’e omantene peteĩ kirirĩ pypuku. Ha ndaha’éi upévante, ha katu ojehecha otĩha hikuái. Heẽ, ko’ápe oĩkuri peteĩ conjunto de jagua marítimo, heta ijapytepekuéra ni michĩmi otĩ’ỹre ojupi umi ballena tuichávape umi mar alto-pe —extraño entero chupekuéra ĝuarã —ha oduelo omanóva oñakãity’ỹre; ha katu, ko’ápe oguapy hikuái peteĩ mesa de desayuno social-pe —opavave peteĩchagua llamamiento-pe, opavave orekóva gusto pariente —omañávo ijerére ovechaicha ojuehe ha’ete ku araka’eve noĩriva’ekue hesa’ỹijógui algún ovecha korapy umi Montañas Verdes apytépe. Peteĩ jehecha curioso; ko’ã oso otĩva, ko’ã ballena guerrero tímido!

Ha katu Queequeg rehe katu —mba’ére piko, Queequeg oguapy upépe ijapytepekuéra —pe mesa akãme, avei, upéicha casualidad; ro’ysã peteĩ hieloicha. Aime haĝua seguro ndaikatúi ha’e heta mba’e iñemongakuaáre. Iadmirador tuichavéva ndaikatúikuri ohustifika cordialmente ogueru hague hendive iarpón desayuno-pe, ha oipuru upépe ceremonia’ỹre; ohupyty pe mesa ári hendive, pe peligro inminente heta akãme, ha ojagarrávo umi vaka ro’o hendápe. Ha katu upéva katuete ojapo porãiterei ha’e, ha opavave oikuaa la mayoría tapichakuéra estimación-pe, jajapo haĝua oimeraẽ mba’e ro’ysã ha’eha jajapo manso.

Nañañe’ẽmo’ãi opa Queequeg peculiaridad rehe ko’ápe; mba’éichapa ojehekýi café ha rollo haku asývagui, ha o’aplika iñatención individida umi carne de vaca-pe, ojejapóva ndahetái. Suficiente, opa vove desayuno ojeretira umi ambuéicha pe sala pública-pe, omyendy itomahawk-pipa, ha oguapy upépe kirirĩháme odigesti ha opita isombrero inseparable reheve, che asẽvo paseo-rã.





MOAKÃHA 6. Pe Tape.

Añesorprenderire ñepyrũrã ahechávo peteĩ individuo extraño-itereíva Queequeg-icha ocirculáva pe sociedad cortés peteĩ táva civilizado-pegua apytépe, upe asombro pya’e oho ajapóvo che primer paseo ára resape New Bedford rapére.

Umi vía hi’aguĩva umi muelle-gui, oimeraẽ puerto marítimo considerable py’ỹi oikuave’ẽta ohecha haĝua umi nondescripto ojehechavéva queerest umi parte extranjera-gui. Jepe umi tape Broadway ha Castaño-pe, umi marinero mediterráneo sapy’ánte oityvyróta umi kuñakarai okyhyjévape. Regent Street ndaha’éi ojekuaa’ỹva umi Lascar ha malayo-kuérape; ha Bombay-pe, Apollo Green-pe, umi Yankees oikovéva omondýi jepi umi nativo-pe. Ha katu New Bedford ipu’aka opavave Water Street ha Wapping-pe. Ko’ã tenda ipahaitépe oñeñe’ẽva’ekuépe rehecha umi marinero-kuérante; ha katu New Bedford-pe, umi caníbal añetegua oñembo’y oñemongeta umi tape esquina-pe; salvaje-kuéra directamente; heta ijapytepekuéra ogueraha gueteri ikangue ho’o imarangatu’ỹva. Ojapo peteĩ extraño omaña haĝua.

Ha katu, umi Feegeean, Tongatobooarrs, Erromanggoans, Pannangians ha Brighggians ykére, ha, umi especímen ka’aguy pe ballena-artesanía-gui noñatendéiva carrete rehe umi tape rehe ykére, rehecháta ambue mba’e ojehecháva curiosovéva gueteri, katuete cómicovéva. Oĩ semanal og̃uahẽ ko távape decenas de hombres verdes vermontes ha New Hampshire-gua, opavave y’uhéi ganancia ha gloria pesca-pe. Ha’ekuéra imitãvéva, umi marco mbarete; hapichakuéra oikytĩva’ekue ka’aguy, ha ko’áĝa oheka oity pe hacha ha oipe’a pe ballena-lanza. Heta oĩ hovy umi Yvyty Verde-icha ou haguégui hikuái. Oĩ mba’épe repensátava umíva ha katu mbovymi aravo’i. ¡Emañamína upépe! upe chap strutting pe esquina jerére. Omonde peteĩ sombrero de castor ha peteĩ abrigo de cola de golondrina, ojecinturón peteĩ cinturón de marinero ha peteĩ kyse de vaina reheve. Ko'ápe ou ambue orekóva sou'-wester ha peteî manto bombazine.

Ndaipóri dandy oñemongakuaava’ekue távape oñembojojátava peteĩ oñemongakuaava’ekue tetãme —che ha’ese peteĩ dandy topetín-gui —peteĩ hapicha, jagua ára jave, oikytĩtava mokõi hectárea guante de piel de torro-pe okyhyjégui ombopiro’y haĝua ipo. Ko’áğa peteĩ dandy tetãmegua péicha oipyhývo iñakãme ojapo haĝua peteĩ reputación distinguida, ha oike pe tuicha ballena-pesca-pe, pehechava’erã umi mba’e cómico ojapóva og̃uahẽvo puerto marítimo-pe. Oñe’ẽvo pe ao marítimo orekóvare, omanda campana-kuéra umi chaleco-pe; correas umi ikalsonsillo de lona-pe. ¡Ah, mboriahu Hay-Seed! mba’éichapa hasýta ojepe’áta umi correa pe primer tormenta opukavývape, reñemongu’évo, correa, botones ha opa mba’e, pe tormenta garganta-pe.

Péro ani pepensa ko puévlo herakuãitéva orekoha umi arpón, kanibal ha topetín añoite ohechauka hag̃ua umi ovisitávape chupe. Mba'eveichavérõ. Still New Bedford ha’e peteĩ tenda queer. Ndaha’éirire ore ballena-kuéra, upe yvy pehẽngue ko árape oiméne oĩta kuri en condición opukavýva Labrador costa-icha. Oĩháicha, umi parte hetã rapykuéri ha’e suficiente omondýi haĝua peteĩvape, ojehecha hu’ueterei. Ko táva voi oiméne ha’e pe tenda ojehayhuvéva ojeiko haĝua, opaite Nueva Inglaterra-pe. Ha’e peteĩ yvy petróleo rehegua, añeteguaiterei: ha katu ndaha’éi Canaánicha; peteĩ yvy, avei, mandi’o ha víno rehegua. Umi tape ndohói kamby reheve; ni primavera-pe ndojapói hikuái huevo pyahúpe. Upéicharõ jepe, jepémo upéicha, moõve opa América-pe ndapejuhúi hetave óga patricio-icha; umi parque ha yvotyty iporãvéva, New Bedford-gui. Moõguipa ou hikuái? mba’éichapa oñeñotỹ ko escoria peteĩ jey ikyráva ári peteĩ tetãme?

Tereho ha emaña umi arpón emblemático de hierro oĩva upérupi mansión yvate jerére, ha oñembohováita ne porandu. Heẽ; opa ko’ã óga py’aguasu ha yvotyty yvotyty oúva océano Atlántico, Pacífico ha Índico-gui. New Bedford-pe, umi túva, he’i hikuái, ome’ẽ ballenas dote ramo imembykuñakuérape, ha oporciona isobrino-kuérape mbovymi marsopas peteĩ pedazo reheve. Ha umi kuña New Bedford-gua, ipoty hikuái rosa pytã imba’évaicha. Ha katu rosa ipoty verano-pe añoite; ha katu pe clavel porã ijyvakuéra rehegua ha’e perenne kuarahy resapeicha yvága séptimo-pe. Ko New Bedford-pe voi oĩ peteĩ Capilla de Ballenas, ha mbovymi ha’e umi pescador ipy’aporãva, ohóva mbykymi océano Índico térã Pacífico gotyo, ndojapóiva visita dominical upe tendápe. Aime segúro ndajapói hague.

Ajevývo che primer paseo pyhareveguagui, asẽ jey ko encargo especial-pe. Pe yvága okambia kuri ro’y hesakã ha kuarahy’ãgui, ha niebla ha niebla omanehávape. Ko salvaje ha’e pe tapicha añoite oĩva upépe ha’ete ohechakuaáva che jeike; ha’e añoite ndaikatúigui olee, ha, upévare, nomoñe’ẽi hína umi inscripción frígida oĩva pe pared-pe. Oĩpa koʼág̃a pe kongregasión apytépe umi marinero héra osẽva upépe, pehẽngue, che ndaikuaái; ha katu hetaiterei oĩ umi accidente noñeregistráiva pe pesca-pe, ha hesakã porãiterei heta kuña oĩva upépe omonde pe hova ndaha’éiramo umi trampa algún ñembyasy ndopytu’úiva rehegua, ha upévare añeñandu seguro ko’ápe che renondépe oñembyaty hague umi, ikorasõ noñemongueráivape ohecha umi umi tableta sombrío simpáticamente ojapo umi herida tuja huguy pyahu.

Oh! peẽ peñenóva omanóva oñeñotỹva kapi’ipe hovy guýpe; mávapa oñembo’ýva yvoty apytépe ikatu he’i—ko’ápe, ko’ápe oñeno che ahayhuetéva; peẽ ndapeikuaái pe ñembyai oñemongáva koʼãvaicha. ¡Mba’eichaitépa umi blanco amargo umi mármol frontera morotĩvape ndojaho’íriva ceniza! ¡Ajépa pe desesperación umi inscripción inmóvil-pe! Mba’e vacío oporojukáva ha infidelidad noñehenóiva umi línea-pe ha’etévaicha oisu’úva opa Jerovia, ha ombotovéva resurrección umi ser omanóva tenda’ỹre peteĩ sepulcro’ỹre. Ikatu avei umi tabla oñembo’y Elephanta itakuápe ko’ápe guáicha.

Mba’e censo-pepa umi tekove oikovéva, oike yvypóra omanóva; mba’érepa peteĩ ñe’ẽpoty universal he’i hesekuéra, nomombe’úiha hikuái mombe’upy, jepémo oguereko hetave ñemigua umi Goodwin Sands-gui; mba’éichapa ha’e hérape kuehe ohova’ekue ambue mundo-pe, ñamoñepyrũ peteĩ ñe’ẽ tuicha mba’éva ha infiel, ha katu ndajaderetíri chupe upéicha, ha’e oiméramo ha’e katu oike umi Indies mombyryvévape ĝuarã ko yvy oikovévape; mba’érepa umi Compañía de Seguros de Vida opaga umi pérdida de muerte umi inmortal rehe; mba’e parálisis eterna, noñemomýivape, ha trance oporojukáva, esperanza’ỹvape, oĩ gueteri pe antiguo Adán omanóva ojapo sesenta siglo ronda; mba’éichapa ñambotove gueteri ñañekonsola umi ñamantenévare upéicharõ jepe oikoha vy’apavẽ oñemombe’u’ỹvape; mba’érepa opavave oikovéva oñeha’ãmbaite omokirirĩ opa omanóvape; upévare ha katu pe rumor oñembotáva peteĩ sepulcro-pe omondýita peteĩ tavaguasu tuichakue. Opa ko’ã mba’e ndaha’éi he’ise’ỹva.

Heẽ, Ismael, ikatu avei nde destino. Péro algún modo avyʼa jey. Methinks che rete ha’eha ha katu umi lees che ser iporãvéva rehegua. Añetehápe ejagarra che rete pe ojapótava, ejagarra che ha’e, ndaha’éi che. Ha upévare mbohapy vy’apavẽ Nantucket-pe ĝuarã; ha ou peteĩ várko estufa ha estufa rete oipota jave, oity haguére che ánga, Jove voi ndaikatúi.





MOAKÃHA 8. Pe Púlpito rehegua.

Nda’aréi aguapyva’ekue oike mboyve peteĩ kuimba’e orekóva peteĩ cierto robustez venerable; pya’e pe okẽ tormenta-gui oveve jeývo oadmiti jave chupe, peteĩ jesareko pya’e hese opa congregación, oatestigua suficientemente ko karai tuja porã ha’eha pe capellán. Heẽ, ha’eva’ekue pe herakuãitéva Padre Mapple, ohenóiva upéicha umi ballena-kuéra, ijapytepekuéra ha’e peteĩ favorito tuichaitereíva. Haʼe ningo imitãrusúpe haʼe vaʼekue peteĩ marinero ha arpóno, péro heta áño ohasavaʼekuépe odedika hekove predikasiónpe. Pe momento ko’áğa ahaihápe, pa’i Mapple oĩkuri pe ro’y ro’ysãme peteĩ tuja hesãivape; upeichagua tuja ha’etévaicha oñembojoajúva peteĩ mitãrusu yvoty mokõihápe, opa umi fisuras orekóvagui umi arruga apytépe, omimbi ciertos resplandor suave peteĩ yvoty oñedesarrolla ramóvagui —pe verdura primaveral ojesarekóva jepe febrero nieve guýpe. Avave nohendúiva’ekue hembiasakue, ndaikatúikuri primera vez ohecha pa’i Mapple-pe pe máximo interés’ỹre, oĩgui ciertas peculiaridades clericales injertadas hese, imputable upe vida marítima aventurera omotenondévape. Oike jave ahechakuaa ndoguerahái hague paraguas, ha katuete ndoúi hague icarro-pe, isombrero de lona oguejy haguére peteĩ sleet derretido reheve, ha pe chaqueta de tela piloto tuichaitéva ha’ete haimete ogueraháva chupe yvýpe pe y pohýi reheve ha’e oabsorbe kuri. Ha katu, ojepe’a peteĩ teĩ sombrero ha abrigo ha zapato, ha ojekoloka peteĩ espacio michĩmíme peteĩ esquina ojoykéregua; oñemonde jave peteĩ traje decente-pe, kirirĩháme oñemboja pe púlpito-pe.

La mayoría umi púlpito yma guareháicha, ha’e peteĩ ijyvatetereíva, ha peteĩ escalera regular péicha yvate gotyo, iángulo puku rupive pe piso ndive, ocontrata seriamente pe área michĩmava capilla-pe, pe arquitecto, ha’ete, o’actua Padre Mapple pista ári, ha omohu’ã pe púlpito escalera’ỹre, omyengoviávo peteĩ escalera lateral perpendicular, umi ojeporúvaicha ojemonta haĝua peteĩ barco peteĩ barco-gui mar-pe. Peteĩ capitán ballenas rembireko ome’ẽkuri pe capilla-pe peteĩ par de hombre-cuerdas de worsted pytã iporãva ko escalera-pe ĝuarã, ha’éva, ijehegui iñakã porã, ha oñemancha peteĩ color de caoba reheve, pe contrivancia pukukue, ñakonsiderávo mba’eichagua capilla ha’e, ha’ete ku mba’eveichavérõ ndoguerekóiva gusto vai. Opytáva sapy’ami pe escalera py gotyo, ha mokõive ipo ojagarrávo umi perilla ornamental umi kuimba’e soga rehegua, pa’i Mapple omaña yvate gotyo, ha upéi peteĩ destreza añetehápe marinero-icha ha katu reverencial gueteri reheve, ipo ári, ojupíva umi escalón ojupíramoguáicha imba’yru ru’ã principal-pe.

Umi párte perpendicular ko escalera lateral-pe, ojehuháicha jepi umi oscilante rehe, haʼe vaʼekue soga ojejahoʼíva télagui, umi rrédo añoite ojejapo yvyrágui, upévare káda pasope oĩ peteĩ junta. Che primera vista pe púlpito-gui, ndokañýikuri chehegui, taha’e ha’éva conveniente peteĩ barco-pe ĝuarã, ko’ã junta ko’áĝagua instancia-pe ha’ete natekotevẽiva. Che ndaha’éigui preparado ahecha haĝua pa’i Mapple-pe ohupyty rire pe altura, mbeguekatúpe ojere ha añesũ pe púlpito ári, deliberadamente aarrasávo pe escalera paso a paso, oñedeposita peve pe entero hyepýpe, ohejávo chupe impregnable iQuebec michĩmíme.

Ajepy’amongeta sapy’ami antende porã’ỹre mba’érepa oiko upéva. Padre Mapple odisfruta peteĩ reputación amplio sinceridad ha santidad rehe, ndaikatúi asospecha chupe ocortejaha notoriedad mba’eveichagua truco mera escenario-pe rupive. Nahániri, apensa, oiméne oĩ algún razón sobria ko mba’ére; hi’arive, ohechaukava’erã peteĩ mba’e ojehecha’ỹva. ¿Ikatu piko upéicharõ, upe acto de aislamiento físico rupive, ohechauka ojeretira espiritual upe tiempo-pe ĝuarã, opa vínculo ha conexión mundana okáguio? Heẽ, oñemyenyhẽ jeýgui pe ñe’ẽ so’o ha vínogui, Ñandejára kuimba’e fiel-pe ĝuarã, ko púlpito, ahecha, ha’e peteĩ mbarete ojejokóva ijehegui —peteĩ Ehrenbreitstein yvate, peteĩ ykua y perenne reheve umi muro ryepýpe.

Ha katu pe escalera lateral ndaha’éikuri pe rasgo extraño añoite upe tendápe, ojeprestáva umi capellán yma guarégui mar-pe. Umi cenotafio de mármol mbytépe mokõive ipo púlpito-pe, pe muro omoheñóiva ijyva oñembojegua peteĩ pintura tuicháva reheve orrepresentáva peteĩ barco gallante oinupãva peteĩ tormenta vaiete peteĩ costa lee ita morotĩ ha rompedor nieve-gui. Ha katu yvate pe ryguasu rupi’a oveve ha arai iñypytũva otyryrýva ári, oveve peteĩ isla michĩ kuarahy resape rehegua, upégui osẽ peteĩ ángel rova; ha ko hova omimbipáva oñohẽ peteĩ punto distinto de brillo pe barco cubierta ojeityva’ekuére, peteĩ mba’e ojoguáva upe chapa de plata-pe ko’áğa oñemoĩva pe Victoria tablón-pe ho’a haguépe Nelson. “Ah, barco noble”, ha’ete he’íva pe ángel, “einupã, einupã, nde barco noble ha egueraha peteĩ timón hatã; pórke péina! kuarahy oityvyro; umi arai ojeroky ohóvo —oĩma hi’aguĩ pe azul serenest”.

Ni pe púlpito voi ndorekói rastro pe sabor mar-pegua peteĩchagua ohupytyva’ekue pe escalera ha pe ta’anga. Pe frente orekóva paneles ojogua peteĩ várko arco bluff-pe, ha pe Santa Biblia opytuʼu peteĩ rróllo ojeproyectávare, ojejapóva peteĩ várko pico violín-icha.

Mba’épa ikatu henyhẽve significado-gui?—pe púlpito ha’égui tapiaite ko yvy pehẽngue tenondegua; opa hembýva ou hapykuépe; pe púlpito omotenonde ko mundo. Upégui ha’e pe tormenta Ñandejára pochy pya’e rehegua oñemombe’u raẽ, ha pe arco ogueraha va’erã pe mba’e pohýi iñepyrũrã. Upégui ha’e pe Tupã yvytu rehegua iporãva térã iky’áva oñehenói raẽ yvytu iporãvape ĝuarã. Heẽ, ko mundo ha’e peteĩ barco osẽva hína, ha ndaha’éi peteĩ viaje completo; ha pe púlpito ha’e iproa.





MOAKÃHA 9. Pe Sermón.

Pa’i Mapple opu’ã, ha peteĩ voz suave de autoridad despreciable-pe omanda umi tapicha isarambipávape oñecondensa haĝua. “Pandilla estribor, upépe! lado mombyry larboard-pe —larboard gangway guive estribor peve! ¡Umi barco mbytépe! umi várko mbytépe!”.

Oĩ peteĩ tyapu ijyvatéva umi bota marina pohýi rehegua umi banco apytépe, ha peteĩ kuña sapatu ñembojere michĩvéva gueteri, ha opa mba’e okirirĩ jey, ha opa tesa ojesareko pe predicador rehe.

Opytu’u’imi; upéi oñesũ pe púlpito arco-pe, ombojere ipo morotĩ tuicháva ijyva ári, ohupi hesa oñembotýva ha oikuave’ẽ peteĩ ñembo’e pypuku devoto ha’ete oñesũva ha oñembo’éva pe mar ruguápe.

Kóva opa, tono solemne ipukúvape, peteĩ campana ñembopu memeguáicha peteĩ barco-pe oñehundíva hína mar-pe peteĩ niebla-pe —ko’ãichagua tono-pe oñepyrũ omoñe’ẽ ko himno oúva; ha katu omoambuévo heko umi estrofa omohu’ãva gotyo, osẽ peteĩ vy’a ha vy’apavẽ pealing reheve—

“Umi costilla ha terror oĩva pe ballena-pe,
Arco che ári peteĩ ñembyasy ñembyasy, .
Opaite Ñandejára ola kuarahy ohesapéva ohasa aja,
Ha chemopu’ã pypukuvévo ñehundi peve.

“Ahecha pe infierno maw apertura,
Mba’asy ha ñembyasy opa’ỹva reheve upépe;
Avave ndaikatúiva omombe’u, ndaha’éirõ umi oñandúva...
Oh, che añembohysýikuri desesperación-pe.

“Añembyasy morotĩme, ahenói che Tupãme, .
Sa’i arovia jave chupe che mba’éva,
Ha’e oñakãity che reclamo-kuéra renondépe—
Ndaiporivéima pe ballena che confina.

“Pya’e reheve oveve che alivio-pe, .
Peteĩ delfín omimbipáva oguerahávapeguáicha;
Ivaieterei, ha katu omimbi, omimbiháicha rayo
Che Tupã oporosalvátava rova.

“Che purahéi tapiaite ĝuarã ograváta
Upe aravo vaiete, vy’apavẽ;
Ame'ẽ pe gloria che Tupãme,
Imba’e poriahuvereko ha pu’aka”.

Haimete opavave ojoaju opurahéi haĝua ko himno, opupu yvate pe tormenta sapukái ári. Oiko peteĩ pausa mbykymi; pe predicador ombojere mbeguekatu la Biblia rogue, ha ipahápe, ombojere ipo pe páhina hekopeteguáre, he’i: “Pe compañero de barco rohayhúva, pejagarra pe versículo paha pe primer capítulo Jonás-pe —‘Ha Ñandejára ombosako’íkuri peteĩ pira tuicháva petragáke Jonáspe’”.

“Shipmates, this book, containing only four chapters—four yarns—is one of the smallest strands in the mighty cable of the Scriptures. Yet what depths of the soul does Jonah's deep sealine sound! what a pregnant lesson to us is this prophet! What a noble thing is that canticle in the fish's belly! How billow-like and boisterously grand! We feel the floods surging over us; we sound with him to the kelpy bottom of the waters; sea-weed and all the slime of the sea is about us! But what is this lesson that the book of Jonah teaches? Shipmates, it is a two-stranded lesson; a lesson to us all as sinful men, and a lesson to me as a pilot of the living God. As sinful men, it is a lesson to us all, because it is a story of the sin, hard-heartedness, suddenly awakened fears, the swift punishment, repentance, prayers, and finally the deliverance and joy of Jonah. As with all sinners among men, the sin of this son of Amittai was in his wilful disobedience of the command of God—never mind now what that command was, or how conveyed—which he found a hard command. But all the things that God would have us do are hard for us to do—remember that—and hence, he oftener commands us than endeavors to persuade. And if we obey God, we must disobey ourselves; and it is in this disobeying ourselves, wherein the hardness of obeying God consists.

“With this sin of disobedience in him, Jonah still further flouts at God, by seeking to flee from Him. He thinks that a ship made by men will carry him into countries where God does not reign, but only the Captains of this earth. He skulks about the wharves of Joppa, and seeks a ship that's bound for Tarshish. There lurks, perhaps, a hitherto unheeded meaning here. By all accounts Tarshish could have been no other city than the modern Cadiz. That's the opinion of learned men. And where is Cadiz, shipmates? Cadiz is in Spain; as far by water, from Joppa, as Jonah could possibly have sailed in those ancient days, when the Atlantic was an almost unknown sea. Because Joppa, the modern Jaffa, shipmates, is on the most easterly coast of the Mediterranean, the Syrian; and Tarshish or Cadiz more than two thousand miles to the westward from that, just outside the Straits of Gibraltar. See ye not then, shipmates, that Jonah sought to flee world-wide from God? Miserable man! Oh! most contemptible and worthy of all scorn; with slouched hat and guilty eye, skulking from his God; prowling among the shipping like a vile burglar hastening to cross the seas. So disordered, self-condemning is his look, that had there been policemen in those days, Jonah, on the mere suspicion of something wrong, had been arrested ere he touched a deck. How plainly he's a fugitive! no baggage, not a hat-box, valise, or carpet-bag,—no friends accompany him to the wharf with their adieux. At last, after much dodging search, he finds the Tarshish ship receiving the last items of her cargo; and as he steps on board to see its Captain in the cabin, all the sailors for the moment desist from hoisting in the goods, to mark the stranger's evil eye. Jonah sees this; but in vain he tries to look all ease and confidence; in vain essays his wretched smile. Strong intuitions of the man assure the mariners he can be no innocent. In their gamesome but still serious way, one whispers to the other—“Jack, he's robbed a widow;” or, “Joe, do you mark him; he's a bigamist;” or, “Harry lad, I guess he's the adulterer that broke jail in old Gomorrah, or belike, one of the missing murderers from Sodom.” Another runs to read the bill that's stuck against the spile upon the wharf to which the ship is moored, offering five hundred gold coins for the apprehension of a parricide, and containing a description of his person. He reads, and looks from Jonah to the bill; while all his sympathetic shipmates now crowd round Jonah, prepared to lay their hands upon him. Frighted Jonah trembles, and summoning all his boldness to his face, only looks so much the more a coward. He will not confess himself suspected; but that itself is strong suspicion. So he makes the best of it; and when the sailors find him not to be the man that is advertised, they let him pass, and he descends into the cabin.

“'Who's there?' cries the Captain at his busy desk, hurriedly making out his papers for the Customs—'Who's there?' Oh! how that harmless question mangles Jonah! For the instant he almost turns to flee again. But he rallies. 'I seek a passage in this ship to Tarshish; how soon sail ye, sir?' Thus far the busy Captain had not looked up to Jonah, though the man now stands before him; but no sooner does he hear that hollow voice, than he darts a scrutinizing glance. 'We sail with the next coming tide,' at last he slowly answered, still intently eyeing him. 'No sooner, sir?'—'Soon enough for any honest man that goes a passenger.' Ha! Jonah, that's another stab. But he swiftly calls away the Captain from that scent. 'I'll sail with ye,'—he says,—'the passage money how much is that?—I'll pay now.' For it is particularly written, shipmates, as if it were a thing not to be overlooked in this history, 'that he paid the fare thereof' ere the craft did sail. And taken with the context, this is full of meaning.

“Now Jonah's Captain, shipmates, was one whose discernment detects crime in any, but whose cupidity exposes it only in the penniless. In this world, shipmates, sin that pays its way can travel freely, and without a passport; whereas Virtue, if a pauper, is stopped at all frontiers. So Jonah's Captain prepares to test the length of Jonah's purse, ere he judge him openly. He charges him thrice the usual sum; and it's assented to. Then the Captain knows that Jonah is a fugitive; but at the same time resolves to help a flight that paves its rear with gold. Yet when Jonah fairly takes out his purse, prudent suspicions still molest the Captain. He rings every coin to find a counterfeit. Not a forger, any way, he mutters; and Jonah is put down for his passage. 'Point out my state-room, Sir,' says Jonah now, 'I'm travel-weary; I need sleep.' 'Thou lookest like it,' says the Captain, 'there's thy room.' Jonah enters, and would lock the door, but the lock contains no key. Hearing him foolishly fumbling there, the Captain laughs lowly to himself, and mutters something about the doors of convicts' cells being never allowed to be locked within. All dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth, and finds the little state-room ceiling almost resting on his forehead. The air is close, and Jonah gasps. Then, in that contracted hole, sunk, too, beneath the ship's water-line, Jonah feels the heralding presentiment of that stifling hour, when the whale shall hold him in the smallest of his bowels' wards.

“Screwed at its axis against the side, a swinging lamp slightly oscillates in Jonah's room; and the ship, heeling over towards the wharf with the weight of the last bales received, the lamp, flame and all, though in slight motion, still maintains a permanent obliquity with reference to the room; though, in truth, infallibly straight itself, it but made obvious the false, lying levels among which it hung. The lamp alarms and frightens Jonah; as lying in his berth his tormented eyes roll round the place, and this thus far successful fugitive finds no refuge for his restless glance. But that contradiction in the lamp more and more appals him. The floor, the ceiling, and the side, are all awry. 'Oh! so my conscience hangs in me!' he groans, 'straight upwards, so it burns; but the chambers of my soul are all in crookedness!'

“Like one who after a night of drunken revelry hies to his bed, still reeling, but with conscience yet pricking him, as the plungings of the Roman race-horse but so much the more strike his steel tags into him; as one who in that miserable plight still turns and turns in giddy anguish, praying God for annihilation until the fit be passed; and at last amid the whirl of woe he feels, a deep stupor steals over him, as over the man who bleeds to death, for conscience is the wound, and there's naught to staunch it; so, after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep.

“And now the time of tide has come; the ship casts off her cables; and from the deserted wharf the uncheered ship for Tarshish, all careening, glides to sea. That ship, my friends, was the first of recorded smugglers! the contraband was Jonah. But the sea rebels; he will not bear the wicked burden. A dreadful storm comes on, the ship is like to break. But now when the boatswain calls all hands to lighten her; when boxes, bales, and jars are clattering overboard; when the wind is shrieking, and the men are yelling, and every plank thunders with trampling feet right over Jonah's head; in all this raging tumult, Jonah sleeps his hideous sleep. He sees no black sky and raging sea, feels not the reeling timbers, and little hears he or heeds he the far rush of the mighty whale, which even now with open mouth is cleaving the seas after him. Aye, shipmates, Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship—a berth in the cabin as I have taken it, and was fast asleep. But the frightened master comes to him, and shrieks in his dead ear, 'What meanest thou, O, sleeper! arise!' Startled from his lethargy by that direful cry, Jonah staggers to his feet, and stumbling to the deck, grasps a shroud, to look out upon the sea. But at that moment he is sprung upon by a panther billow leaping over the bulwarks. Wave after wave thus leaps into the ship, and finding no speedy vent runs roaring fore and aft, till the mariners come nigh to drowning while yet afloat. And ever, as the white moon shows her affrighted face from the steep gullies in the blackness overhead, aghast Jonah sees the rearing bowsprit pointing high upward, but soon beat downward again towards the tormented deep.

“Terrors upon terrors run shouting through his soul. In all his cringing attitudes, the God-fugitive is now too plainly known. The sailors mark him; more and more certain grow their suspicions of him, and at last, fully to test the truth, by referring the whole matter to high Heaven, they fall to casting lots, to see for whose cause this great tempest was upon them. The lot is Jonah's; that discovered, then how furiously they mob him with their questions. 'What is thine occupation? Whence comest thou? Thy country? What people? But mark now, my shipmates, the behavior of poor Jonah. The eager mariners but ask him who he is, and where from; whereas, they not only receive an answer to those questions, but likewise another answer to a question not put by them, but the unsolicited answer is forced from Jonah by the hard hand of God that is upon him.

“'I am a Hebrew,' he cries—and then—'I fear the Lord the God of Heaven who hath made the sea and the dry land!' Fear him, O Jonah? Aye, well mightest thou fear the Lord God then! Straightway, he now goes on to make a full confession; whereupon the mariners became more and more appalled, but still are pitiful. For when Jonah, not yet supplicating God for mercy, since he but too well knew the darkness of his deserts,—when wretched Jonah cries out to them to take him and cast him forth into the sea, for he knew that for his sake this great tempest was upon them; they mercifully turn from him, and seek by other means to save the ship. But all in vain; the indignant gale howls louder; then, with one hand raised invokingly to God, with the other they not unreluctantly lay hold of Jonah.

“And now behold Jonah taken up as an anchor and dropped into the sea; when instantly an oily calmness floats out from the east, and the sea is still, as Jonah carries down the gale with him, leaving smooth water behind. He goes down in the whirling heart of such a masterless commotion that he scarce heeds the moment when he drops seething into the yawning jaws awaiting him; and the whale shoots-to all his ivory teeth, like so many white bolts, upon his prison. Then Jonah prayed unto the Lord out of the fish's belly. But observe his prayer, and learn a weighty lesson. For sinful as he is, Jonah does not weep and wail for direct deliverance. He feels that his dreadful punishment is just. He leaves all his deliverance to God, contenting himself with this, that spite of all his pains and pangs, he will still look towards His holy temple. And here, shipmates, is true and faithful repentance; not clamorous for pardon, but grateful for punishment. And how pleasing to God was this conduct in Jonah, is shown in the eventual deliverance of him from the sea and the whale. Shipmates, I do not place Jonah before you to be copied for his sin but I do place him before you as a model for repentance. Sin not; but if you do, take heed to repent of it like Jonah.”

While he was speaking these words, the howling of the shrieking, slanting storm without seemed to add new power to the preacher, who, when describing Jonah's sea-storm, seemed tossed by a storm himself. His deep chest heaved as with a ground-swell; his tossed arms seemed the warring elements at work; and the thunders that rolled away from off his swarthy brow, and the light leaping from his eye, made all his simple hearers look on him with a quick fear that was strange to them.

There now came a lull in his look, as he silently turned over the leaves of the Book once more; and, at last, standing motionless, with closed eyes, for the moment, seemed communing with God and himself.

But again he leaned over towards the people, and bowing his head lowly, with an aspect of the deepest yet manliest humility, he spake these words:

“Shipmates, God has laid but one hand upon you; both his hands press upon me. I have read ye by what murky light may be mine the lesson that Jonah teaches to all sinners; and therefore to ye, and still more to me, for I am a greater sinner than ye. And now how gladly would I come down from this mast-head and sit on the hatches there where you sit, and listen as you listen, while some one of you reads me that other and more awful lesson which Jonah teaches to me , as a pilot of the living God. How being an anointed pilot-prophet, or speaker of true things, and bidden by the Lord to sound those unwelcome truths in the ears of a wicked Nineveh, Jonah, appalled at the hostility he should raise, fled from his mission, and sought to escape his duty and his God by taking ship at Joppa. But God is everywhere; Tarshish he never reached. As we have seen, God came upon him in the whale, and swallowed him down to living gulfs of doom, and with swift slantings tore him along 'into the midst of the seas,' where the eddying depths sucked him ten thousand fathoms down, and 'the weeds were wrapped about his head,' and all the watery world of woe bowled over him. Yet even then beyond the reach of any plummet—'out of the belly of hell'—when the whale grounded upon the ocean's utmost bones, even then, God heard the engulphed, repenting prophet when he cried. Then God spake unto the fish; and from the shuddering cold and blackness of the sea, the whale came breeching up towards the warm and pleasant sun, and all the delights of air and earth; and 'vomited out Jonah upon the dry land;' when the word of the Lord came a second time; and Jonah, bruised and beaten—his ears, like two sea-shells, still multitudinously murmuring of the ocean—Jonah did the Almighty's bidding. And what was that, shipmates? To preach the Truth to the face of Falsehood! That was it!

“This, shipmates, this is that other lesson; and woe to that pilot of the living God who slights it. Woe to him whom this world charms from Gospel duty! Woe to him who seeks to pour oil upon the waters when God has brewed them into a gale! Woe to him who seeks to please rather than to appal! Woe to him whose good name is more to him than goodness! Woe to him who, in this world, courts not dishonor! Woe to him who would not be true, even though to be false were salvation! Yea, woe to him who, as the great Pilot Paul has it, while preaching to others is himself a castaway!”

He dropped and fell away from himself for a moment; then lifting his face to them again, showed a deep joy in his eyes, as he cried out with a heavenly enthusiasm,—“But oh! shipmates! on the starboard hand of every woe, there is a sure delight; and higher the top of that delight, than the bottom of the woe is deep. Is not the main-truck higher than the kelson is low? Delight is to him—a far, far upward, and inward delight—who against the proud gods and commodores of this earth, ever stands forth his own inexorable self. Delight is to him whose strong arms yet support him, when the ship of this base treacherous world has gone down beneath him. Delight is to him, who gives no quarter in the truth, and kills, burns, and destroys all sin though he pluck it out from under the robes of Senators and Judges. Delight,—top-gallant delight is to him, who acknowledges no law or lord, but the Lord his God, and is only a patriot to heaven. Delight is to him, whom all the waves of the billows of the seas of the boisterous mob can never shake from this sure Keel of the Ages. And eternal delight and deliciousness will be his, who coming to lay him down, can say with his final breath—O Father!—chiefly known to me by Thy rod—mortal or immortal, here I die. I have striven to be Thine, more than to be this world's, or mine own. Yet this is nothing: I leave eternity to Thee; for what is man that he should live out the lifetime of his God?”

He said no more, but slowly waving a benediction, covered his face with his hands, and so remained kneeling, till all the people had departed, and he was left alone in the place.
