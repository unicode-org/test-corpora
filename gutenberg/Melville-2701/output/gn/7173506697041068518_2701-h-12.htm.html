Capítulo 55. umi ta’anga monstruosa de ballenas rehegua.

Che pinta ere puku peẽme ikatuháicha peteĩ ikatuháicha lona’ỹre, peteĩ mba’e ojoguáva pe forma añeteguávape pe ballena-pe añetehápe ojehechaukaháicha pe ballena resa renondépe hete absoluto-pe voi pe ballena oñeamarráramo pe ballena-barco ykére ikatu hağuáicha ikatu ojepyta hese hekopete upépe. Ikatu vale la pena, upévare, ñamoherakuã mboyve umi curioso retrato imaginario hesegua ko’áĝa peve jepe odesafía confianza reheve pe yvy jára jerovia. Oguahẽma ára ñamohenda porã haĝua ko mundo ko mba’épe, jahechaukávo ko’ãichagua ta’anga ballena rehegua ojavypaiteha.

Ikatu pe fuente primaria opa umi ilusión pictórica ojejuhúta umi escultura hindú, egipcia ha griega itujavéva apytépe. Ymaite guive umi época inventivo ha katu inescrupuloso oĩrõ guare umi paneles de mármol templo-pe, umi pedestal de estatua-pe ha umi escudo, medallón, copa ha moneda-pe, pe delfín ojedibuja balanza de armadura de cadena-pe Saladín-icha, ha peteĩ iñakã casco-icha San Jorge pegua; upe guive ipu’aka peteĩ mba’e peteĩchagua licencia-pe, ndaha’éi umi ta’anga ojeguerohoryvévape pe ballena rehegua añónte, ha katu heta presentación científica-pe hese.

Ko’áğa, opa mba’e ikatúvape, pe retrato ymavéva oĩva gueteri taha’e ha’éva he’íva ha’eha pe ballena mba’e, ojejuhu pe famoso caverna-pagoda Elephanta-pe, India-pe. Umi brahmanes omantene umi escultura haimete opa’ỹva upe pagoda inmemoria-pe, opa umi oficio ha persecución, opa avocación oñepensáva yvypóra rehe, oñeprefigura hague edad-kuéra oimeraẽva ijapytepekuéra añetehápe oike mboyve. Ndaipóri sorpresa upéicharõ, en algún tipo ñande profesión noble caza de ballenas oĩva’erãmo’ã upépe sombra. Pe ballena hindú oñeñe'êva rehe, ojehu peteî departamento aparte muro-pe, ohechaukáva Vishnu encarnación en forma de leviatán, ojekuaáva Matse Avatar ramo. Ha katu jepe ko escultura ha’e medio kuimba’e ha media ballena, ikatu hağuáicha ome’ẽnte pe última cola, upéicharõ jepe upe sección michĩva chugui ojavypaite. Ojoguave peteĩ Anaconda cola afilado-pe, umi palma ancha umi ballena añetegua majestuoso flukes-gui.

Ha katu tereho umi Galería tujápe, ha emaña ko’áğa peteĩ pintor cristiano tuicháva ra’anga ko pira rehegua; ha’e osẽ porãvégui pe hindú antediluvio-gui. Ha'e Guido ra'anga Perseo osalvávo Andrómeda-pe pe yguasu-monstruo térã ballena-gui. Moõpa ohupyty Guido pe modelo de criatura extraña peichagua? Ni Hogarth, opintávo pe escena peteĩchagua “Pérseo oguejyva’ekuépe”, ndojapói peteĩ mba’e iporãvéva. Pe corpulencia tuichaitereíva upe monstruo hogarthio-pegua ondula pe superficie-pe, apenas oguenohẽ peteĩ pulgada y. Oreko peteĩ espésie de howdah ijyva ári, ha ijuru distenso de cordón ojerokyhápe umi ondula, ikatu ojegueraha pe Traidor-kuéra Rokẽme g̃uarã ohóva pe Támesis guive y rupive pe Torre-pe. Upéi, oĩ umi ballena Prodromus Sibbald escocés yma guaréva, ha ballena Jonah, ojehechaukaháicha umi impresión de la Biblia tuja ha umi corte de imprimación tuja. Mba'épa oje'éta ko'ãva rehe? Pe libro-encuadernador ballena ojeréva peteĩ parral ra’ỹiicha peteĩ ancla oguejyva’ekue stock jerére —ojesella ha ojedoroháicha heta aranduka tuja ha ipyahúva rapykuéri ha título-pe —upéva ha’e peteĩ criatura pintorescaiterei ha katu puramente fabulosa, imitado, ajagarra, umi figura ojoguávagui umi jarrón antiguo-pe. Jepe universalmente oñedenomina peteĩ delfín, upéicharõ jepe ahenói ko pira encuadernador de libro-pe peteĩ intento peteĩ ballena rehe; oñeha’ãgui upéicha oñepresenta ypy ramo guare pe tembipuru. Oikuaauka peteĩ editor italiano tuja amo siglo XV rupi, Revival de Aprendizaje aja; ha umi tiémpope, ha peteĩ tiémpo rire jepe, ojeʼe umi delfín haʼeha peteĩ espésie de Leviatán.

Umi viñeta ha ambue adorno algunos libros yma guarépe sapy’ánte pejuhúta umi toque curiosoiterei pe ballena-pe, ko’ápe opáichagua esputo, jets d’eau, fuente haku ha ro’y, Saratoga ha Baden-Baden, oúva burbuja yvate imba’évagui cerebro no exhausted rehegua. Pe páhina título edición original “Avances del Aprendizaje”-pe rejuhúta unos kuánto ballenas curiosa.

Ha katu jahejávo opa ko’ã intento ndaha’éiva profesional, jajesareko umi ta’anga leviatán rehegua oje’éva ha’eha delineación sobria, científica, umi oikuaáva rupive. Harris tuja colección de viajes-pe oĩ unos cuantos chapa de ballenas ojeipe’ava’ekue peteĩ libro holandés de viajes-gui, 1671 d.C., hérava “Un viaje caza de ballenas a Spitzbergen en el barco Jonas en la ballena, Peter Peterson de Friesland, maestro”. Peteĩva umi plánchape oñerrepresenta umi ballena, umi balsa tuichávaicha, oñenoha umi ísla de hielo apytépe, ha umi oso morotĩ oñani ijyva oikovéva ári. Ambue chapa-pe, pe prodigioso error ojejapo orrepresentávo pe ballena umi casualidad perpendicular reheve.

Upéi jey, oĩ peteĩ cuarto imponente, ohaiva’ekue peteĩ Capitán Colnett, peteĩ Capitán de Postes armada inglesa-pe, hérava “Una Viaje ojere Cuerno del Cabo jerére umi Mar del Sur-pe, pe propósito ombopukúvo pe Pesca de Ballenas Spermaceti-pe”. Ko arandukápe oĩ peteĩ esquema he’íva ha’eha peteĩ “Ta’anga peteĩ ballena Physeter térã Spermaceti rehegua, ojejapóva escala rupive peteĩ ojejukava’ekuégui México rembe’ýpe, agosto, 1793-pe, ha ojeipysóva cubierta-pe”. Adudá pe capitán oguereko hague ko ta’anga añetegua ojejapo haĝua beneficio-rã imarines-kuérape. Amombe’u haĝua peteĩ mba’énte hese, ta’e oguerekoha peteĩ tesa oje’aplikava’ekue, según pe escala omoirûva, peteĩ ballena espermatozoide okakuaapaitévape, ojapóta upe ballena resagui peteĩ arco-ventana ipukúva cinco pie rupi. ¡Ah, che capitán gallante, mba’ére piko nereme’ẽi oréve Jonás omañáva upe teságui!

Ni umi recopilación conciencia-véva Historia Natural-gui ndaha’éi beneficio mitãrusu ha tierno-kuérape ĝuarã, libre pe mismo heinosidad de error-gui. Emañamína upe obra ojeguerohorýva “Naturaleza animada del marrón de oro” rehe. Pe edición Londres-pe oñembotapykuéva 1807-pe, oĩ umi pláncha peteĩ supuesta “ballena” ha peteĩ “narwhale”. Ndaipotái ha’ete inelegante, ha katu ko ballena ojehecha’ỹva ojoguaiterei peteĩ cerda amputada-pe; Ha, Narwale-pe guarã, peteĩ jehechami hese ha’e suficiente osorprende haĝua peteĩ, ko siglo XIX-pe peichagua hipogrogriff ikatuva’ekue opytáma palma-pe añetehápe oimeraẽ público inteligente de escuelares-pe.

Upéi, jey, ary 1825-pe, Bernard Germain, Conde de Lacépède, peteĩ naturalista tuicháva, omoherakuã peteĩ aranduka ballena sientífica científica, upépe ha’e heta ta’anga umi especie iñambuéva Leviatán-pegua. Opa ko’ã mba’e ndaha’éi hendape’ỹnte, ha katu pe ballena Mysticetus térã Groenlandia ra’anga (he’iséva, pe ballena Derecha), Scoresby jepe, peteĩ kuimba’e ymaite guive ohasava’ekue opokóva upe especie rehe, odeclara ndoguerekóiha ikompañero naturaleza-pe.

Ha katu pe gorra-gajo ñemoĩ opa ko negocio ojavývape ojereserva pe científico Frederick Cuvier-pe ĝuarã, pe famoso Barón joyke’y. Ary 1836-pe omoherakuã peteĩ Historia Natural de los Ballenas, upépe ome’ẽ pe ha’e ohenóiva peteĩ ta’anga Ballena Esperma rehegua. Pehechauka mboyve upe ta’anga oimeraẽ Nantucketer-pe, iporãvéta peme’ẽ pende retiro sumario Nantucket-gui. Peteĩ ñe’ẽme, Frederick Cuvier ballena de espermatozoides ndaha’éi peteĩ ballena de espermatozoides, ha katu peteĩ calabaza. Añetehápe araka’eve ndoguerekói pe beneficio peteĩ viaje de ballenas-pe (ko’ãichagua kuimba’e sa’i oreko), ha katu moõguipa oguenohẽ upe ta’anga, máva piko ikatu omombe’u? Oiméne ohupyty pe antecesor científico upe ámbito-pe voi, Desmarest, ohupyty haguéicha peteĩ umi aborto auténtico ojapova’ekue; he’iséva, peteĩ dibujo chino-gui. Ha mba’eichagua mitãkaria’y kyre’ỹ orekóva pe lápiz umi chino, heta taza ha platillo queer ñanemomarandu.

Umi ballena de letrero-pinturas ojehecháva umi tape rehe oñemoîva umi tenda ojehepyme'êhápe petrolero-kuéra ári, mba'épa oje'éta hesekuéra? Ha’ekuéra ha’e generalmente Ricardo III. ballenas, orekóva horca dromedaria, ha salvajeiterei; Ojedesayuna mbohapy térã irundy tarta marinero-pe, upéva ha’e umi ballena henyhẽva marinero-gui: ideformidadkuéra otyryry mares de sangre ha pintura hovy-pe.

Ha katu ko’ã múltiple javy ojerrepresentávo pe ballena ndaha’éi upéicha tuicha sorprendente opa mba’e rire. Hechakuaa! Hetave umi dibujo científico ojegueru umi pira oñembotývagui; ha ko’ãva oĩ porãiterei peteĩ dibujo peteĩ barco oñembyaívaicha, ijyva oñembyaíva, orrepresentáta hekopete pe mymba noble-pe voi opa iorgullo sin discontinuo de casco ha espalda-pe. Jepe umi elefante oñembo’y ipukukue pukukue, pe Leviatán oikovéva araka’eve ne’ĩra gueteri oveve hekopete irretrato rehe. Pe ballena oikovéva, imajestuosidad ha significación plena-pe, ojehechava’erãnte mar-pe umi y ndojekuaaporãivape; ha yvate gotyo pe tuicha gran parte oĩ ojehecha’ỹre, peteĩ barco línea de batalla lanzada-icha; ha upe elemento-gui ha’e peteĩ mba’e eternamente imposible yvypóra mortal-pe ĝuarã ohupi chupe hete reheve yvate, oñongatu haĝua opa umi hinchazón ha ondulación ipu’akapáva. Ha, ani haĝua ñañe’ẽ pe diferencia de contorno ojepresumiitereívare peteĩ ballena chupadora imitãva ha peteĩ Leviatán platonio okakuaapaitévare; jepe, jepe peteĩva umi ballena chupadora imitãva ojeipysóva peteĩ barco cubierta-pe, upéicha upérõ ha’e pe extraño, anguila-icha, limbered, variada forma hese, ha pe expresión precisa orekóva pe aña voi ndaikatúi ojagarra.

Ha katu ikatu oñefantasea, pe esqueleto desnudo pe ballena encallada-gui, umi pista exacta ikatu ojederiva opokóvo iforma añeteguáre. Mba'eveichavérõ. Ha’égui peteĩ mba’e curiosovéva ko Leviatán rehegua, ijesqueleto ome’ẽ sa’ieterei idea iforma general rehe. Jepénte Jeremy Bentham esqueleto, oñemoîva candelabro rehe biblioteca peteîva umi ejecutor, ombohasa hekoitépe idea orekóva caballero viejo utilitario cebado cebado, opavave ambue característica personal principal Jeremy; jepémo upéicha ndaikatúi oje’inferi mba’eve ko’ãichagua mba’eveichagua leviatán kangue articulado-gui. Añetehápe, he’iháicha pe cazador tuichaitéva, pe mera esqueleto ballena-pegua ogueraha pe relación pe mymba oñeinverti ha acolchado-ichaite pe insecto ojapoháicha umi crisalis-pe upéicha redondeosamente o’envolve. Ko peculiaridad ojehecha llamativamente iñakãme, péicha algún parte ko arandukápe ojehechaukáta casualmente. Avei ojehechauka curiosamente aleta lateral-pe, umi kangue haimete ombohovái exactamente yvypóra po kangue, menos pe pulgar añónte. Ko aleta oguereko irundy hueso-yva regular, pe índice, medio, anillo ha dedo michĩva. Péro entéro koʼã mbaʼe opyta tapiaite ijao soʼoitévape, umi yvypóra kuãicha peteĩ kuádro artificial-pe. “Ha katu imprudentemente pe ballena ikatu sapy’ánte ñandeservi”, he’i peteĩ ára Stubb chistoso, “araka’eve ndaikatúi oje’e añetehápe ñande manejaha guantes’ỹre”.

Opa ko’ã mba’ére, upéicharõ, oimeraẽ mba’e ikatúva remaña hese, tekotevẽ reikotevẽ pe Leviatán Guasu ha’eha peteĩ criatura ko mundo-pegua opytava’erã noñepintáiva ipahaitépe. Añete, peteĩ retrato ikatu ogolpea pe marca hi’aguĩvéva ambuégui, ha katu avave ndaikatúi oinupã chupe oimeraẽ grado de exactitud considerable-itereíva reheve. Upévare ndaipóri mbaʼéichapa ikatu jaikuaa añetehápe mbaʼéichapa añetehápe pe ballena. Ha pe único modo ikatúvape peguenohẽ peteĩ idea tolerable jepe pe contorno oikovéva rehegua, ha’e peho peteĩ ballenaje-pe pendejehe; ha katu pejapóvo upéicha, ndape’arriesga michĩmi peime haĝua eternamente estufa ha peñehundi hese. Upévare, ha’ete chéve iporãvéva ani haĝua reñembotavyeterei nde curiosidad-pe repokóvo ko Leviatán rehe.





MOAKÃHA 56. Umi ta’anga sa’ive ojejavyva’ekue ballena rehegua, ha umi ta’anga añetegua umi escena ballena rehegua.

Ojoajúvo umi ballena ra’anga monstruoso ndive, che aime mbarete ko’ápe aike haĝua umi tembiasakue monstruosovéva gueteri hesekuéra ojejuhúva ciertos libros-pe, yma ha ko’áĝaguápe, especialmente Plinio, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, etc. Ha katu ahasa upe asunto.

Aikuaa irundy esquema publicado añónte pe Ballena Esperma tuichaitéva rehegua; Colnett, Huggins mba'e, Frederick Cuvier ha Beale mba'éva. Pe capítulo ohasava’ekuépe oñeñe’ẽkuri Colnett ha Cuvier rehe. Huggins mba’éva iporãve mombyry chuguikuéra; ha katu, tuicha probabilidad rupive, Beale mba’e ha’e pe iporãvéva. Opa umi dibujo Beale ojapova’ekue ko ballena rehegua iporã, ndaha’éiramo pe figura mbytépegua oĩva ta’angápe mbohapy ballena opáichagua actitud-pe, omohu’ãva mokõiha capítulo. Ifrontispicio, umi barco oatakáva Ballenas Esperma-pe, jepe katuete oñecalcula omokyre’ỹ haĝua pe escepticismo civil algunos kuimba’e salón-pegua, ha’e admirablemente correcto ha tekoveichagua efecto general-pe. Oĩ umi dibujo Ballena Esperma rehegua J. Ross Browne-pe oĩporãitereíva contorno-pe; ha katu ojegraba vaieterei hikuái. Upéva ndahaʼéi ikulpa jepe.

Pe Ballena Derecha rehegua, umi ta’anga contorno iporãvéva oĩ Scoresby-pe; ha katu ojedibuja peteĩ escala michĩetereívape ombohasa haĝua peteĩ impresión ojeipotaitéva. Ha’e oguereko peteĩ ta’anga añónte umi escena ballena rehegua, ha kóva ha’e peteĩ deficiencia ñembyasy, ha’égui ko’ãichagua ta’anga rupive añoite, ojejapo porãramo jepe, ikatu reguenohẽ oimeraẽ mba’e ojoguáva peteĩ idea añeteguáva ballena oikovéva rehegua ohechaháicha umi icazador oikovéva.

Ha katu, ojegueraha opa mba’épe ĝuarã, mombyry guive umi iporãvéva, jepe algunos detalles-pe ndaha’éi hekopetevéva, umi presentación ballena ha escena ballena rehegua ojejuhúva mamove, ha’e mokõi grabado francés tuicháva, ojejapo porãva, ha ojegueru umi pintura peteĩ Garnery ojapova’ekuégui. Peteĩteĩ, ha’ekuéra orrepresenta umi ataque ojejapóva pe Espermatozoide ha Ballena Derecha rehe. Pe primer grabado-pe peteĩ noble Ballena Esperma ojehechauka plena majestad de poder-pe, opu’ã ramoite pe barco guýpe pe océano pypukuhágui, ha ogueraháva yvate pe aire-pe ijyva ári pe naufragio hechapyrãva umi tablón estufa-gui. Pe barco proa parcialmente noñeikytĩri, ha ojedibuja equilibrio-nte pe monstruo columna vertebral rehe; ha peñembo’y upe proa-pe, upe peteĩ tiempo incomputable destello-pe ĝuarã, pehecha peteĩ remo-pe ĝuarã, medio envuelto pe ballena boquilla opupuva ipochy reheve, ha pe acto de salto-pe, ha’ete ku peteĩ precipicio-gui. Pe mba’e tuichakue acción iporã ha añetegua maravillosamente. Pe tina de línea medio vacía oveve pe mar oñembojehe’áva ári; umi poste yvyrágui ojejapóva umi arpón oñeñohẽvape oblicuamente bob ipype; Umi tripulación de natación akã oñemosarambi pe ballena rehe umi expresión contrastante de afrípices-pe; pe distancia tormenta morotĩme aja pe barco oguejy ohóvo pe escena ári. Ikatu ojejuhu falla grave umi detalle anatómico ko ballena rehegua, ha katu tohasa upéva; che rekovére, ndaikatúigui adibuja peteĩ iporãitereíva.

Pe mokõiha grabado-pe, pe barco oĩ pe acto-pe odibujávo pe flanco barnacled ykére peteĩ Ballena Derecha tuicháva oñanihápe, ombotyryrýva igrabado ñana morotĩva mar-pe algún roca-deslizamiento musgoso-icha umi acantilado Patagonia-gui. Ijetkuéra opu’ã, henyhẽ ha morotĩ hollín-icha; upéicha hetaiterei tatatĩgui pe chimenea-pe, peimo’ãta oĩha peteĩ cena py’aguasu oñembojyva’ekue umi intestino guasúpe oĩva iguýpe. Umi guyra yguasúpegua oisu’u hína umi cangrejo michĩva, pira concha ha ambue dulce ha macarrones marina-pegua, sapy’ánte ogueraháva pe Ballena Derecha ijyva pestilente ári. Ha upe aja pukukue pe leviatán ijuru hũva otyryry ohóvo pe pypuku rupi, ohejávo tonelada de cuajada morotĩ tumultuosa hapykuéri, ha ojapo pe barco michĩva oryrýi umi hinchazón-pe peteĩ skiff ojejagarrávaicha umi rueda de remo ypýpe peteĩ barco de vapor océano-pegua. Péicha, primer plano ha'e opavave sarambi pochy; ha katu hapykuéri, admirable contraste artístico-pe, oĩ pe nivel vidrioso peteĩ mar oñembopy’aguapýva, umi vela sin almidonado oguejyva’ekue pe barco ipu’aka’ỹvagui, ha pe masa inerta peteĩ ballena omanóvagui, peteĩ fortaleza conquistada, pe bandera de captura oñemoĩva perezosamente pe ballena-gui- poste oñemoĩva ijyvyku’ípe.

Mávapa Garnery pe pintor, térã ha’eva’ekue, ndaikuaái. Ha katu che rekove upévarã ha’e prácticamente oikuaa porãkuri isujeto, térã katu maravillosamente oñetutorákuri algún ballena experimentado rupive. Umi francés ha'e umi mitãrusu opinta haguã acción. Tereho emaña opa umi pintura Europa-pegua rehe, ha moõpa rejuhúta peichagua galería de conmoción oikovéva ha orrespirava lona ári, upe salón triunfal Versalles-peguáicha; Pe ohecháva oñorairõhápe hape, Pell-Mell, umi ñorairõ guasu consecutivo rupive Francia-pe; opa kyse puku ha’etehápe peteĩ destello umi Luz del Norte-gui, ha umi rréi ha Emperador armado sucesivos ohasa pya’e, peteĩ carga de centauro coronado-icha? Ndaha’éi enteramente indigno peteĩ tenda upe galería-pe, ha’e ko’ã batalla-pieza marítima Garnery-pegua.

Pe aptitud natural orekóva umi francés ojagarra haĝua pe pintoresco umi mba’e rehegua ha’ete ojehechakuaáva peculiarmente mba’e pintura ha grabado-pa oreko hikuái umi escena ballena rehegua. Ndaipóri peteĩ décima Inglaterra experiencia pesca-pe, ha ndaha’éi pe milésima parte umi norteamericano-kuéra rehegua, upéicharõ jepe omoĩ hikuái mokõive tetãme umi boceto terminado añoite ikatúva ombohasa pe espíritu añetegua pe ballena caza-pe. En su mayor parte, umi dibujante ballena inglés ha estadounidense ha’ete enteramente contento opresentávo pe contorno mecánico umi mba’ére, ha’eháicha pe perfil vacante ballena rehegua; péva, oñeñe’ẽvo pintoresco efecto rehe, haimete ojogua ojejapo haguã boceto perfil peteĩ pirámide rehegua. Umi partitura jepe, pe justamente reconocida derecha Whaleman, ome’ẽ rire ñandéve peteĩ longitud completa rígida pe ballena Groenlandia-gui, ha mbohapy térã irundy delicado miniaturas de narwales ha marsopas, ñanetrata peteĩ serie de grabados clásicos de enganches de barcos, cuchillos de chopper ha grapnels; ha diligencia microscópica orekóva peteî Leuwenhoeck oñesomete inspección peteî mundo oryrýiva noventa y seis fac-similes de cristales de nieve ártico magnificado. Che ha’ese ndaiporiha desprecio pe excelente viajero-pe (amomba’eguasu chupe peteĩ veterano rehe), ha katu peteĩ asunto iñimportantetereívape katuete ha’e peteĩ supervisión ndaprocurái hague opa cristal-pe ĝuarã peteĩ declaración jurada jurada ojejapóva peteĩ Juez de Paz Groenlandia-gua renondépe.

Oĩve umi grabado porã Garnery-gui, oĩ ambue mokõi grabado francés digno de nota, algúno oabonáva ijehegui “H. Durand”. Peteĩva umívagui, ndaha’éiramo jepe ojeadaptáva precisamente ñane propósito ko’áĝaguápe, upéicharõ jepe omerece mención ambue relato-pe. MOAKÃHA 59. Calamar rehegua.

Mbeguekatúpe oike umi pradera británica-pe, pe Pequod ojejoko gueteri ohokuévo noreste gotyo isla Java gotyo; peteĩ aire suave oempulsáva quilla, upéicha pe serenidad ijerére oĩvape umi mbohapy mástil yvate cónico oñanduuka suavemente upe yvytu languido-pe, mbohapy palma suave-icha peteĩ llanura-pe. Ha upéicharõ jepe, intervalo tuichávape pe pyhare plátaicha, ojehecháta pe jet ha’eño ha seductora.

Ha katu peteĩ pyhareve hovy hesakãva, peteĩ kirirĩ haimete preternatural ojeipysórõ guare pe mar ári, jepeve noñangarekóiva mba’eveichagua py’aguapy oñembotapykuéva reheve; Pe kuarahy’ã puku ojepurúva y ári ha’ete ku peteĩ kuã órore oñemoĩva hi’arikuéra, omandava’ekue michĩmi ñemiháme; umi ola ojedeslizava’ekue osuspira jave oñondive oñani mbeguekatu aja; Ko kirirĩ profundo-pe pe esfera ojehechávape peteĩ espectro extraño ohecha Daggoo pe principal-mast-head-gui.

Mombyry, peteĩ masa morotĩ tuicháva opu’ã ipy’aju, ha ojupi yvateve ohóvo, ha ojedesenreda pe azul-gui, ipahápe omimbi ore proa renondépe peteĩ nieve-deslizante-icha, pyahu ojedesliza umi cerro-gui. Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
