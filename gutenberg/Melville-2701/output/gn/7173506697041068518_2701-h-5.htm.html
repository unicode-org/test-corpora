MOAKÃHA 22. Vy’apavê Navidad rehegua.

Ipahápe, asajepyte gotyo, oñemosê rire paha umi barco rigger-kuéra, ha ojegueru rire pe Pequod muelle-gui, ha pe Caridad ojepy’amongetáva jepi oguejy rire peteĩ ballena-barco-pe, ijopói paha reheve —peteĩ pyhare -cap Stubb-pe ĝuarã, pe segundo compañero, ikuñataĩ ha peteĩ Biblia de repuesto pe mayordomo-pe ĝuarã —opa ko’ã mba’e rire, umi mokõi Capitán, Peleg ha Bildad, osẽ pe cabina-gui, ha ojere pe compañero principal gotyo, he’i Peleg : 1 .

“Ko’áğa, karai Starbuck, ¿reime seguro opa mba’e oĩ porãha? Capitán Acab oĩma preparádo —oñe’ẽnte chupe —mba’eve ojehupytyveva’erã orilla-gui, ¿ajépa? Ha, ehenói opavave po, upéicharõ. ¡Embyaty chupekuéra popa ko’ápe —¡embopuka chupekuéra!”

“Natekotevẽi ñe’ẽ ky’a, taha’e ha’éva tuicha pya’e, Peleg”, he’i Bildad, “ha katu mombyry ndehegui, angirũ Starbuck, ha ejapo ore ñe’ẽme’ẽ”.

¡Mbaʼeichaitépa koʼág̃a! Ko’ápe pe puntoite oñepyrũ haĝua pe viaje, capitán Péleg ha capitán Bildad oho hína kuri peteĩ po yvate reheve pe cuarto cubierta-pe, ha’ete ku ha’éva comandante conjunto mar-pe, ha avei opa aparición puerto-pe . Ha, capitán Acab rehe katu, ndojehechái gueteri mba'eveichagua señal chugui; añoite, he’i hikuái oĩha pe cabaña-pe. Ha katu upéi, pe idea ha’e, ipresencia ni mba’evéicharõ natekotevẽiha ohupyty haĝua pe barco peso-pe, ha odirihi porã haĝua chupe mar gotyo. Añetehápe, ndaha’éi haguéicha upéva mba’eveichavérõ inegocio hekopete, ha katu pe piloto mba’éva; ha ne’ĩra gueteri ojerrekuperapaite —péicha he’i hikuái —upévare, capitán Acab opyta iguýpe. Ha opa ko’ã mba’e ha’ete ku suficientemente natural; especialmente pe servicio comercial-pe heta capitán araka’eve ndojehechái cubierta-pe peteĩ tiempo considerable ohupi rire pe ancla, ha katu opyta pe mesa de cabina ári, oguerekóvo peteĩ despedida vy’apavẽ iñamigokuéra orilla-pegua ndive, oheja mboyve hikuái pe barco-gui por siempre pe mba'yrumbovevehára.

Péro ndaipóri heta oportunida ñapensa hag̃ua ko mbaʼére, pórke koʼág̃a oikovepaite pe capitán Péleg. Haʼete voi ojapóva la majoría umi mbaʼe oñeʼẽva ha omandáva, ha ndahaʼéi Bildad.

“Ko’águi rire, peẽ soltero ra’y”, osapukái, umi marinero opyta aja pe mástil principal-pe. “Karai. Starbuck, emboguata chupekuéra popa gotyo”.

“¡Einupã pe tenda upépe!”— ha’e pe orden oúva. Ainsinua haguéicha yma, ko carpa de hueso de ballena araka’eve noñemoĩri ndaha’éiramo puerto-pe; ha Pequod-pe, treinta áño pukukue, ojekuaa porã pe orden ojekutu haĝua pe tenda ha’eha pe mba’e oúva ojehupi haĝua pe ancla.

“¡Kuimba’e pe capstan! ¡Tuguy ha arasunu!—¡ejupi!”— ha’e pe mandamiento oúva, ha pe tripulación otyryry umi po’a rehe.

Ko’áğa ojepesa haĝua, pe estación generalmente ocupada pe piloto ha’e pe parte tenondegua pe barco-pe. Ha ko’ápe Bildad, ha’éva, Peleg ndive, ojekuaa, ambue oficial-kuéra ári, ha’eva’ekue peteĩ umi piloto licenciado puerto-pegua —ojesospechávo ojapo hague ijehegui peteĩ piloto ikatu hağuáicha osalva pe piloto-cuota Nantucket-pe opa umi barco ojepy’apýva hese, araka’eve ndopilotáigui ambue artesanía —Bildad, che ha’e, ikatu ko’áĝa ojehecha activamente ojesarekóva umi arco ári pe ancla oñemoaguĩva rehe, ha intervalo-pe opurahéi pe ha’etévaicha peteĩ salmodia vara ñembyasy, to ombovy’a umi po pe ventilador-pe, okororõva’ekue algún tipo de coro umi mitãkuña Booble Alley-pegua rehegua, py’aite guive voluntad porã reheve. Upéicharõ jepe, ndaha’éi mbohapy ára mboyve, Bildad he’iva’ekue chupekuéra ndojehejamo’ãiha purahéi profana pe Pequod-pe, particularmente ojepesa haĝua; ha Charity, iñermána, omoĩkuri peteĩ Watts copia michĩva ojeporavóva káda marinero amarre-pe.

Upe aja, oñangarekóvo ambue parte del barco rehe, capitán Peleg oikytĩ ha ohura popa gotyo pe manera oporomondýivévape. Haimete aimo’ã oitytaha pe barco ikatu mboyve opu’ã pe ancla; involuntariamente apytu’u che mano-pe, ha ha’e Queequeg-pe ojapo haĝua avei upéicha, opensávo umi peligro mokõivéva roñaniva’ekuére, roñepyrũvo pe viaje peichagua aña ndive peteĩ piloto-pe ĝuarã. Che añembopy’aguapy hína kuri, jepe, pe pensamiento reheve ikatuha ojejuhu piadoso Bildad-pe algún salvación, jepeve ha’e setecientos setenta y siete laico; añandu jave peteĩ golpe haimbe sapy’a che rapykuéri, ha ajerévo, akyhyjeterei pe capitán Peleg aparición-gui pe acto oipe’ávo ipy che jerére inmediato-gui. Upéva ha’ekuri che primera patada.

“¿Upéicha piko oñemopuʼã hikuái pe servísio de marchante-pe?”. ha’e opukavy. “Primavera, nde ovecha akã; primavera, ha eity nde jyva! Mba’ére piko ndapepu’ãi, ha’e, opavave peẽ —primavera! ¡Quohog! primavera, nde chap umi iñakãrague pytã reheve; Primavera upépe, scotch-cap; primavera, nde pantalón hovy. ¡Primavera, ha’e, opavave peẽme, ha primavera pende resa!” Ha péicha he’ívo, omýi pe ventilador rupi, ápe ha pépe oipuru ipy sãsõiterei, Bildad imperturbable katu osegi omotenonde isalmodia reheve. Oimo’ã che, capitán Peleg oiméne hoy’u ra’e peteĩ mba’e ko árape.

Ipahápe pe ancla ojupi, umi vela oñemoĩ ha rosẽ rodesliza. Ha’ékuri peteĩ Navidad mbyky ha ro’ysã; ha pe ára mbyky norte gotyo oñembojoajúvo pyhare, rojejuhu haimete ipypuku pe océano ro’ysã ári, irocío congelante oremoĩ hielo-pe, armadura pulida-icha. Umi hatĩ fila puku oĩva umi baluarte ári omimbi jasy resape’ápe; ha umi ryguasu marfil morotĩvaicha algún elefante tuichaitereívape, umi hielo tuichaitereíva ojekurva odepende umi arco-gui.

Lank Bildad, piloto ramo, omyakã pe primer reloj, ha siempre ha anon, pe artesanía tuja oñembohypýi pypukúvo umi mar verde-pe, ha omondo pe escarcha oryrýiva opa hese, ha umi yvytu opukavy, ha pe cordón ipu, umi nota constante ha’e ohendúkuri,-

“Kokue dulce ohasáva pe inundación hinchazón, .
Eñembo’y oñemonde verde oikovévape.
Upémarõ umi hudíope Canaán tuja oñembo'y.
Jordan ojeroky aja ijapytépe”.

Araka’eve umi ñe’ẽ he’ẽ asýva noñehenduporãi chéve upérõ. Ha’ekuéra henyhẽ esperanza ha fructígui. Jepe ko pyhare ro’ysã ro’ysã pe Atlántico sarambikuépe, jepe che py húmedo ha che chaqueta hykuvéva, oĩ gueteri, upérõ ha’ete chéve, heta refugio agradable oñeñongatúva; ha umi meads ha glades eternamente vernal, pe kapi’ipe odispara yvate pe primavera rupive, ndojepytasóiva, ndojepy’apýiva, opyta verano mbytépe.

Ipahápe rohupyty peteĩ ofing tuichaitereíva, ha noñeikotevẽvéima umi mokõi piloto rehe. Pe várko de vela hatãva oremoirũvaʼekue oñepyrũ oñemonguʼe ijykére.

Ha’e kuri curioso ha ndaha’éi ndogustáiva, mba’éichapa ojeafectákuri Péleg ha Bildad-pe ko momento-pe, especialmente capitán Bildad-pe. Ombojeguaru haguére oho, upéicharõ jepe; ombojeguarueterei oheja haĝua, iporãvape ĝuarã, peteĩ barco ohóva peteĩ viaje ipuku ha ipeligrosoitévape —ohasávo mokõive Cabo tormenta-pe; peteĩ barco oñeinverti haguépe amo miles de dólares oganava’ekue heta mba’égui; peteĩ várko, peteĩ várko tuja ohohápe capitán ramo; peteĩ kuimba’e haimete itujáva ha’eicha, peteĩ jey oñepyrũvo ojuhu opa umi terror pe mandibula poriahuvereko’ỹva rehegua; ndogustái ojedespedi peteĩ mba’égui opa hendáicha henyhẽva opa interésgui chupe ĝuarã, —mboriahu tuja Bildad opyta are; o’ataka pe cubierta umi paso ansioso reheve; oguejy oñani pe cabaña-pe oñe’ẽ haĝua ambue ñe’ẽ despedida upépe; ou jey cubierta-pe, ha omaña yvytu gotyo; omaña y tuicha ha opa’ỹva gotyo, oñembotýva umi Continente Oriental mombyry ojehecha’ỹva rupive añoite; omaña pe yvy gotyo; omaña yvate gotyo; omaña akatúa ha asu gotyo; omaña oparupiete ha moõve; ha ipahápe, ombojere mecánicamente peteĩ soga ipin ári, ojagarra convulsivamente Peleg imbaretéva ipópe, ha ohupi peteĩ linterna, sapy’ami oñembo’y omañávo heroicamente hova rehe, he’i peve: “Upéicharõ jepe, angirũ Peleg, ikatu eñembotapykue; heẽ, ikatu”.

Peleg rehe katu, ha’e ogueraha hetave peteĩ filósofo-pe; ha katu opa ifilosofía-pe ĝuarã, oĩ peteĩ tesay omimbi hesápe, pe linterna oñemoaguĩeterei jave. Ha ha’e avei, ndoñani’imi peteĩ cabina guive ambuépe —ko’áğa peteĩ ñe’ẽ iguýpe, ha ko’áğa peteĩ ñe’ẽ Starbuck ndive, pe compañero principal.

Ha katu, ipahápe, ojere ikompañero gotyo, peteĩ jesareko paha reheve hese, —“Capitán Bildad— eju, compañero de barco tuja, jahava’erã. ¡Ejevy pe patio principal-pe upépe! Yga ahoy! ¡Eñembo’y reñemboja haĝua ijykére, ko’áĝa! ¡Eñangareko, eñangarekóke!—eju Bildad, mitãkaria’y— ere nde ipahaite. Suerte peẽme ĝuarã, Starbuck —suerte peẽme ĝuarã, karai Stubb —suerte peẽme ĝuarã, karai Flask —adiós ha suerte opavave peẽme ĝuarã —ha ko árape mbohapy áño aguerekóta peteĩ cena haku asýva peẽme ĝuarã apitáva Nantucket tujápe . ¡Pya’e ha mombyry!”.

“Ñandejára tapehovasa, ha peguereko Iñangareko marangatúpe, kuimba’ekuéra”, oñe’ẽmbegue tuja Bildad, haimete ndojoajúiva. “Aha’arõ ko’áĝa peguereko ára porã, ikatu haĝuáicha pya’e Capitán Acab omýi pende apytépe —peteĩ kuarahy iporãva ha’e opa mba’e oikotevẽva, ha peguerekóta heta umívagui pe viaje trópico pehohápe. Peñangarekóke pe caza-pe, peẽ pene irũnguéra. Ani pemboty umi várko tekotevẽʼỹre, peẽ arpón ombaʼapóva; tablón de cédro morotĩ porã ojehupi tres por ciento henyhẽva. ary ryepýpe. Ani avei penderesarái pene ñembo’égui. Karai Starbuck, eñatendéke pe cooper ani omombo umi vara de repuesto. Oh! umi vela-aguja oĩ pe armario verde-pe! Ani pe ballenaiterei a' Ñandejára ára, kuimba'ekuéra; ha katu ani avei peperdé peteĩ oportunidad justa, upéva ha’e pe ombotove Yvága don porãkuéra. Eguereko peteĩ tesa pe melaza tierce rehe, karai Stubb; ha’e kuri fuga’imi, apensa. Pepokóramo umi íslare, karai Flask, eñangarekóke pe tekovaígui. Adiós, adiós! Ani reñongatu are upe kesu oguejy pe retención-pe, karai Starbuck; oñembyaíta. Peñangareko pe mantequilla rehe —veinte céntimos pe libra ha’eva’ekue, ha penemandu’áke, oiméramo—”

“Eju, eju, Capitán Bildad; anivéma pe palavering, —mombyry!” ha upéva reheve, Péleg ombopya’e chupe ijykére, ha mokõivéva ho’a pe barco-pe.

Pe barco ha barco ojedivergi; pe yvytu ro’ysã ha húmedo pyhare oipeju ijapytépe; peteĩ gaviota osapukáiva oveve iñakã ári; umi mokõi casco ojeroky ka’aguýre; rome’ẽ mbohapy vy’apavẽ korasõ pohýi reheve, ha ciegamente roñembohysýi destino-icha pe Atlántico ha’eñoitépe.





MOAKÃHA 23. Pe Lee rembe’y.

Oĩ capítulo ohasava’ekuépe, oñeñe’ẽkuri peteĩ Bulkington rehe, peteĩ marinero yvate, yvy pyahu, ojejuhúva New Bedford-pe pe posada-pe.

Upe pyhare ro’y oryrýivape, pe Pequod oity umi arco vengativo umi ola ro’ysã maliciosa-pe, mávapepa ahecháta oñembo’ýva timón-pe ha katu Bulkington! Amaña ñemondýi ha kyhyje simpático reheve pe kuimba’ére, ro’y mbytépe oguejy ramóva peteĩ viaje ipeligroso irundy áñogui, ikatuva’ekue ipy’aguapy’ỹre oñemongu’e jey ambue término tormenta-pe ĝuarã. Pe yvy ha’ete ku okáiva ipy peve. Umi mba’e hechapyrãvéva ha’e umi araka’eve noñeñe’ẽiva; umi mandu’a pypuku ndohejái mba’eveichagua epitafio; ko capítulo seis pulgadas-pegua ha’e Bulkington sepulcro ita’ỹva. Che ha’énte oho hague hendive pe barco tormenta-pe ojeityháicha, omaneháva miserablemente pe yvy atãme. Pe puerto fain ome’ẽta pytyvõ; pe puerto ningo oporoporiahuvereko; puerto-pe oĩ seguridad, comodidad, ita tatakua, cena, manta hakuáva, angirũnguéra, opa mba’e ipy’aporãva ñande mortalidad-kuérape. Ha katu upe yvytu atãme, pe puerto, pe yvy, ha’e upe barco peligro ivaivéva; ha’e oveveva’erã opa hospitalidad; peteĩ yvy toque, jepe okaru pe quilla, ojapova’erã chupe oryrýi ha opa rupi. Opa ipu’aka reheve ha’e oñembyaty opavave oveve y rembe’ýgui; péicha ojapóvo, oñorairõ ‘umi yvytuite ndive, fain omboveveva’erãmo’ã chupe hógape; oheka jey opa pe mar lashed yvy’ỹ; refugio rehehápe desesperadamente ojejapura peligro-pe; ¡Iñangirũ peteĩnteva iñenemigo ivaivévape!

¿Peikuaa piko ko’áĝa, Bulkington? Umi vislumbre ha’ete pehecháva upe añetegua mortalmente intolerable-gui; opa pensamiento pypuku ha ipy’aite guive ha’eha pe ánga ñeha’ã intrépido añónte omantene haĝua pe independencia abierta imar rehegua; umi yvytu ka’aguyvéva yvága ha yvy rehegua okonspirávo omombo haĝua chupe pe orilla traicionero ha tembiguáiramo?

Ha katu pe yvy’ỹre añoite oikoháicha pe añetegua ijyvatevéva, y rembe’y’ỹva, indefinido Ñandejáraicha —upéicha, iporãve ñañehundi upe infinito opukavývape, ñañembotavy rangue ingloriosamente pe lee ári, jepe upéva ha’e seguridad! Gusano-icha, upéicharõ, ¡oh! mávapa oipotaitereíta otyryry oguejy haĝua! ¡Terrores umi mba'e vaiete rehegua! ¿mba’eve piko opa ko’ã mba’e hasýva? ¡Eñemokyre’ỹ, eñemokyre’ỹ, O Bulkington! ¡Egueropu'aka ndéve ñembyasýpe, semidios! Yvate pe rocío nde océano-pe oñehundívagui —derecto yvate gotyo, osaltá nde apoteosis!





MOAKÃHA 24. Pe Abogado.

Queequeg ha che ko’áĝa roñepyrũháicha bastante ko negocio de caza de ballenas-pe; ha ko negocio de caza de ballenas ojehechaháicha algún modo umi yvy jára apytépe peteĩ mba’e ndaha’éiva poético ha ojeguerohorýva; Upévare, che ha’e opa jepy’apy rekonvense haĝua peẽ, peẽ yvýpe, pe injusticia ojejapóva ko’ápe umi cazador de ballenas rehe.

Peteîha tendápe, ikatu ojehecha haimete superfluo omopyendávo hecho, umi tapicha en general apytépe, negocio caza de ballenas noñecontáiva peteî nivel umi ojeheróva profesiones liberales ndive. Oñemoingéramo peteĩ extraño oimeraẽ sociedad metropolitana opaichagua-pe, omotenondéta michĩmi pe opinión general imérito rehegua, oñepresentárire chupe pe empresa-pe arpón ramo, ja’e; ha emulación ramo umi oficial naval-pe omoîva'erã ramo umi inicial SWF (Pesca de Ballenas Espermas) itarjeta de visita-pe, ko'ãichagua procedimiento ojehecháta preeminentemente presunto ha ridículo.

Katuete peteĩ razón principal mba’érepa ko mundo ombotove omomba’eguasúvo umi ballena estadounidense-pe, ha’e kóva: oimo’ã hikuái, iporãvévape, ñande vocación osuma peteĩ clase de negocio carnicería-pe; ha ñañe’involucra jave activamente upépe, ñande jerére oĩha opaichagua mba’e ky’a. Carnicero ñande ha’e, upéva añete. Ha katu umi carnicero, avei, ha umi carnicero insignia huguypavéva ha’ékuri opavave Comandante Marcial ko mundo invariablemente ovy’áva omomba’eguasúvo. Ha pe asunto pe supuesto iky’áva ñane negocio-pe, pya’e peñepyrũta peteĩ hecho-pe ko’áĝa peve bastante generalmente desconocido, ha upéva, en general, oñotỹva’erã triunfantemente pe ballena esperma-pe por lo menos umi mba’e ipotĩvéva apytépe ko ordenado . yvy. Ha katu jepe ome’ẽvo pe cargo en cuestión añeteguaha; mba’e cubiertas resbaladizas desordenadas peteĩ barco de ballena-pe oñembojojáva pe carrión noñe’ẽivare umi campo de batalla-pe hetaiterei soldado ohojeyhágui hoy’u opa kuñanguéra alabanza-pe? Ha pe idea peligro rehegua omomba’eguasuetereíramo pe conceito popular pe soldado profesión rehegua; ta’asegura peẽme heta peteĩ veterano oguata va’ekue sãsõme peteĩ batería peve, pya’e ojeretirataha pe ballena espermatozoide cola tuichaitéva aparición rehe, ombohasávo remolino-pe pe aire iñakã ári. ¡Mba’épa umi yvypóra terror oñentendekuaáva oñembojojávo umi terror ha maravillas ojoajúva ojuehe Ñandejáragui!

Ha katu, jepe umi scouts mundial umi ballena cazador-kuéra rehe, jepeve ojapo inconscientemente ome’ẽ ñandéve pe homenaje ipypukuvéva; heẽ, peteĩ adoración opa mba’e henyhẽva! Haimete opa umi cónico, lámpara ha vela okáiva globo jerére, okáiva, hetaiterei santuario mboyveguáicha, ñande gloria-pe ĝuarã!

Ha katu pemaña ko mba’ére ambue tesape’ápe; opesa opaichagua balanza-pe; pehecha mba’épa ñande ballena-kuéra, ha ha’eva’ekue.

Mba'érepa umi holandés De Witt tiempo-pe oreko almirante-kuéra umi flota de ballenas orekóva? Mba’érepa Luis XVI. de Francia, igasto personal-pe, omohenda umi barco de ballenas Dunkerque-gui, ha cortésmente oinvita upe távape algún decena térã dos familia ñande isla Nantucket-gui voi? Mba'érepa Gran Bretaña ary 1750 ha 1788 mbytépe opaga umi ballena-pe umi abundante yvate gotyo 1.000.000 libras esterlinas? Ha ipahápe, mba’éichapa oúma ñande ballenas de América ko’áğa jasupera opa umi ballena bandida hembýva ko mundo-pe; oveve peteĩ armada orekóva sietecientos várko yvate gotyo; oguerekóva dieciocho mil kuimba’e; anualmente okonsumíva 4.000.000 de dólares; umi barco ovale, pe vela jave, 20.000.000 dólares! ha káda áño oimportávo ñande puerto-pe peteĩ cosecha oñemono’õ porãva 7.000.000 dólares. Mba’éicha piko ou opa ko’ã mba’e, ndaipóriramo mba’e puissant pe ballena caza-pe?

Ha katu kóva ndaha’éi pe mitad; emaña jey.

A’afirma sãsõme, pe filósofo cosmopolito ndaikatuiha, hekove pukukue, oapunta peteĩ influencia py’aguapýpe añoite, ko’ã sesenta áño ohasava’ekuépe o’operava’ekue potencialmente opa mundo amplio-pe, ojegueraháva peteĩ agregado-pe, pe negocio yvate ha ipu’akapáva caza de ballena-gui . Peteĩ ha ambue hendáicha, omoheñói umi acontecimiento hechapyrãitereíva ijehegui, ha iñimportantetereíva continuamente umi tema secuencial-pe, ikatu hağuáicha ojehecha porã pe ballena pe sy egipcio ramo, imembyva’ekue ijeheguiete imemby hyeguasúva isy guive. Ha’éta peteĩ tembiapo esperanza’ỹva, opa’ỹva ñakatalogávo opa ko’ã mba’e. Tove peteĩ po’a. Heta áño ohasavaʼekuépe pe várko de ballena haʼe pe prekursór osẽ hag̃ua umi párte mombyryvéva ha saʼive ojekuaáva ko yvy ape ári. MOAKÃHA 27. Kavaju’arigua ha escudero-kuéra.

Stubb ha’ekuri pe mokõiha iména térã hembireko. Ha’e niko Cape Cod retãygua; ha upévare, ojeporuháicha upe tendápe, oñembohéra Cape-Cod-man. Peteĩ vy’apavẽ-go-suerte; ni ndoipotái ni ipy’aguasu; ojagarrávo umi peligro oúvo peteĩ aire indiferente reheve; ha oime aja pe crisis inminentevéva pe persecución-pe, omba’apo mombyry, ipy’aguapy ha oñembyaty peteĩ carpintero viajero ramo oñembohapéva pe año pukukue. Iñembosarái porã, ndahasýi ha descuidado, ha’e opresidi ibarco de ballenas ha’ete ku pe encuentro oporojukáva ha’éva peteĩ cena añónte, ha itripulación opavave oinvita invitado-kuérape. Ha’e ha’e particularmente pe arreglo cómodo iparte barco-pe, peteĩ escenario-conductor tuja ha’eháicha pe snugness ikaja rehe. Hi’aguĩ jave pe ballena-gui, pe ñorairõ ñemano-pe voi, omaneja ilanza ndoiporiahuverekóiva ro’ysã ha ipy’a’ỹre, peteĩ silbato ombojereháicha imartíllo. Ha’e ozumbiva’erã umi melodía rigadig tuja ári oime aja flanco ha flanco pe monstruo exasperadovéva ndive. Pe jepuru puku, ko stubb-pe ĝuarã, omoambue umi ñemano jyva peteĩ apyka ndahasýivape. Pe ha’e opensáva ñemano rehe voi, ndaipóri ñemombe’u. Opensápa araka’eve hese mba’eveichavérõ, ikatu ha’e peteĩ porandu; but, if he ever did chance to cast his mind that way after a comfortable dinner, no doubt, like a good sailor, he took it to be a sort of call of the watch to tumble aloft, and bestir themselves there, about something which he would find out when he obeyed the order, and not sooner.

What, perhaps, with other things, made Stubb such an easy-going, unfearing man, so cheerily trudging off with the burden of life in a world full of grave pedlars, all bowed to the ground with their packs; what helped to bring about that almost impious good-humor of his; that thing must have been his pipe. For, like his nose, his short, black little pipe was one of the regular features of his face. You would almost as soon have expected him to turn out of his bunk without his nose as without his pipe. He kept a whole row of pipes there ready loaded, stuck in a rack, within easy reach of his hand; and, whenever he turned in, he smoked them all out in succession, lighting one from the other to the end of the chapter; then loading them again to be in readiness anew. For, when Stubb dressed, instead of first putting his legs into his trowsers, he put his pipe into his mouth.

I say this continual smoking must have been one cause, at least, of his peculiar disposition; for every one knows that this earthly air, whether ashore or afloat, is terribly infected with the nameless miseries of the numberless mortals who have died exhaling it; and as in time of the cholera, some people go about with a camphorated handkerchief to their mouths; so, likewise, against all mortal tribulations, Stubb's tobacco smoke might have operated as a sort of disinfecting agent.

The third mate was Flask, a native of Tisbury, in Martha's Vineyard. A short, stout, ruddy young fellow, very pugnacious concerning whales, who somehow seemed to think that the great leviathans had personally and hereditarily affronted him; and therefore it was a sort of point of honor with him, to destroy them whenever encountered. So utterly lost was he to all sense of reverence for the many marvels of their majestic bulk and mystic ways; and so dead to anything like an apprehension of any possible danger from encountering them; that in his poor opinion, the wondrous whale was but a species of magnified mouse, or at least water-rat, requiring only a little circumvention and some small application of time and trouble in order to kill and boil. This ignorant, unconscious fearlessness of his made him a little waggish in the matter of whales; he followed these fish for the fun of it; and a three years' voyage round Cape Horn was only a jolly joke that lasted that length of time. As a carpenter's nails are divided into wrought nails and cut nails; so mankind may be similarly divided. Little Flask was one of the wrought ones; made to clinch tight and last long. They called him King-Post on board of the Pequod; because, in form, he could be well likened to the short, square timber known by that name in Arctic whalers; and which by the means of many radiating side timbers inserted into it, serves to brace the ship against the icy concussions of those battering seas.

Now these three mates—Starbuck, Stubb, and Flask, were momentous men. They it was who by universal prescription commanded three of the Pequod's boats as headsmen. In that grand order of battle in which Captain Ahab would probably marshal his forces to descend on the whales, these three headsmen were as captains of companies. Or, being armed with their long keen whaling spears, they were as a picked trio of lancers; even as the harpooneers were flingers of javelins.

And since in this famous fishery, each mate or headsman, like a Gothic Knight of old, is always accompanied by his boat-steerer or harpooneer, who in certain conjunctures provides him with a fresh lance, when the former one has been badly twisted, or elbowed in the assault; and moreover, as there generally subsists between the two, a close intimacy and friendliness; it is therefore but meet, that in this place we set down who the Pequod's harpooneers were, and to what headsman each of them belonged.

First of all was Queequeg, whom Starbuck, the chief mate, had selected for his squire. But Queequeg is already known.

Next was Tashtego, an unmixed Indian from Gay Head, the most westerly promontory of Martha's Vineyard, where there still exists the last remnant of a village of red men, which has long supplied the neighboring island of Nantucket with many of her most daring harpooneers. In the fishery, they usually go by the generic name of Gay-Headers. Tashtego's long, lean, sable hair, his high cheek bones, and black rounding eyes—for an Indian, Oriental in their largeness, but Antarctic in their glittering expression—all this sufficiently proclaimed him an inheritor of the unvitiated blood of those proud warrior hunters, who, in quest of the great New England moose, had scoured, bow in hand, the aboriginal forests of the main. But no longer snuffing in the trail of the wild beasts of the woodland, Tashtego now hunted in the wake of the great whales of the sea; the unerring harpoon of the son fitly replacing the infallible arrow of the sires. To look at the tawny brawn of his lithe snaky limbs, you would almost have credited the superstitions of some of the earlier Puritans, and half-believed this wild Indian to be a son of the Prince of the Powers of the Air. Tashtego was Stubb the second mate's squire.

Third among the harpooneers was Daggoo, a gigantic, coal-black negro-savage, with a lion-like tread—an Ahasuerus to behold. Suspended from his ears were two golden hoops, so large that the sailors called them ring-bolts, and would talk of securing the top-sail halyards to them. In his youth Daggoo had voluntarily shipped on board of a whaler, lying in a lonely bay on his native coast. And never having been anywhere in the world but in Africa, Nantucket, and the pagan harbors most frequented by whalemen; and having now led for many years the bold life of the fishery in the ships of owners uncommonly heedful of what manner of men they shipped; Daggoo retained all his barbaric virtues, and erect as a giraffe, moved about the decks in all the pomp of six feet five in his socks. There was a corporeal humility in looking up at him; and a white man standing before him seemed a white flag come to beg truce of a fortress. Curious to tell, this imperial negro, Ahasuerus Daggoo, was the Squire of little Flask, who looked like a chess-man beside him. As for the residue of the Pequod's company, be it said, that at the present day not one in two of the many thousand men before the mast employed in the American whale fishery, are Americans born, though pretty nearly all the officers are. Herein it is the same with the American whale fishery as with the American army and military and merchant navies, and the engineering forces employed in the construction of the American Canals and Railroads. The same, I say, because in all these cases the native American liberally provides the brains, the rest of the world as generously supplying the muscles. No small number of these whaling seamen belong to the Azores, where the outward bound Nantucket whalers frequently touch to augment their crews from the hardy peasants of those rocky shores. In like manner, the Greenland whalers sailing out of Hull or London, put in at the Shetland Islands, to receive the full complement of their crew. Upon the passage homewards, they drop them there again. How it is, there is no telling, but Islanders seem to make the best whalemen. They were nearly all Islanders in the Pequod, Isolatoes too, I call such, not acknowledging the common continent of men, but each Isolato living on a separate continent of his own. Yet now, federated along one keel, what a set these Isolatoes were! An Anacharsis Clootz deputation from all the isles of the sea, and all the ends of the earth, accompanying Old Ahab in the Pequod to lay the world's grievances before that bar from which not very many of them ever come back. Black Little Pip—he never did—oh, no! he went before. Poor Alabama boy! On the grim Pequod's forecastle, ye shall ere long see him, beating his tambourine; prelusive of the eternal time, when sent for, to the great quarter-deck on high, he was bid strike in with angels, and beat his tambourine in glory; called a coward here, hailed a hero there!





CHAPTER 28. Ahab.

For several days after leaving Nantucket, nothing above hatches was seen of Captain Ahab. The mates regularly relieved each other at the watches, and for aught that could be seen to the contrary, they seemed to be the only commanders of the ship; only they sometimes issued from the cabin with orders so sudden and peremptory, that after all it was plain they but commanded vicariously. Yes, their supreme lord and dictator was there, though hitherto unseen by any eyes not permitted to penetrate into the now sacred retreat of the cabin.

Every time I ascended to the deck from my watches below, I instantly gazed aft to mark if any strange face were visible; for my first vague disquietude touching the unknown captain, now in the seclusion of the sea, became almost a perturbation. This was strangely heightened at times by the ragged Elijah's diabolical incoherences uninvitedly recurring to me, with a subtle energy I could not have before conceived of. But poorly could I withstand them, much as in other moods I was almost ready to smile at the solemn whimsicalities of that outlandish prophet of the wharves. But whatever it was of apprehensiveness or uneasiness—to call it so—which I felt, yet whenever I came to look about me in the ship, it seemed against all warrantry to cherish such emotions. For though the harpooneers, with the great body of the crew, were a far more barbaric, heathenish, and motley set than any of the tame merchant-ship companies which my previous experiences had made me acquainted with, still I ascribed this—and rightly ascribed it—to the fierce uniqueness of the very nature of that wild Scandinavian vocation in which I had so abandonedly embarked. But it was especially the aspect of the three chief officers of the ship, the mates, which was most forcibly calculated to allay these colourless misgivings, and induce confidence and cheerfulness in every presentment of the voyage. Three better, more likely sea-officers and men, each in his own different way, could not readily be found, and they were every one of them Americans; a Nantucketer, a Vineyarder, a Cape man. Now, it being Christmas when the ship shot from out her harbor, for a space we had biting Polar weather, though all the time running away from it to the southward; and by every degree and minute of latitude which we sailed, gradually leaving that merciless winter, and all its intolerable weather behind us. It was one of those less lowering, but still grey and gloomy enough mornings of the transition, when with a fair wind the ship was rushing through the water with a vindictive sort of leaping and melancholy rapidity, that as I mounted to the deck at the call of the forenoon watch, so soon as I levelled my glance towards the taffrail, foreboding shivers ran over me. Reality outran apprehension; Captain Ahab stood upon his quarter-deck.

There seemed no sign of common bodily illness about him, nor of the recovery from any. He looked like a man cut away from the stake, when the fire has overrunningly wasted all the limbs without consuming them, or taking away one particle from their compacted aged robustness. His whole high, broad form, seemed made of solid bronze, and shaped in an unalterable mould, like Cellini's cast Perseus. Threading its way out from among his grey hairs, and continuing right down one side of his tawny scorched face and neck, till it disappeared in his clothing, you saw a slender rod-like mark, lividly whitish. It resembled that perpendicular seam sometimes made in the straight, lofty trunk of a great tree, when the upper lightning tearingly darts down it, and without wrenching a single twig, peels and grooves out the bark from top to bottom, ere running off into the soil, leaving the tree still greenly alive, but branded. Whether that mark was born with him, or whether it was the scar left by some desperate wound, no one could certainly say. By some tacit consent, throughout the voyage little or no allusion was made to it, especially by the mates. But once Tashtego's senior, an old Gay-Head Indian among the crew, superstitiously asserted that not till he was full forty years old did Ahab become that way branded, and then it came upon him, not in the fury of any mortal fray, but in an elemental strife at sea. Yet, this wild hint seemed inferentially negatived, by what a grey Manxman insinuated, an old sepulchral man, who, having never before sailed out of Nantucket, had never ere this laid eye upon wild Ahab. Nevertheless, the old sea-traditions, the immemorial credulities, popularly invested this old Manxman with preternatural powers of discernment. So that no white sailor seriously contradicted him when he said that if ever Captain Ahab should be tranquilly laid out—which might hardly come to pass, so he muttered—then, whoever should do that last office for the dead, would find a birth-mark on him from crown to sole.

So powerfully did the whole grim aspect of Ahab affect me, and the livid brand which streaked it, that for the first few moments I hardly noted that not a little of this overbearing grimness was owing to the barbaric white leg upon which he partly stood. It had previously come to me that this ivory leg had at sea been fashioned from the polished bone of the sperm whale's jaw. “Aye, he was dismasted off Japan,” said the old Gay-Head Indian once; “but like his dismasted craft, he shipped another mast without coming home for it. He has a quiver of 'em.”

I was struck with the singular posture he maintained. Upon each side of the Pequod's quarter deck, and pretty close to the mizzen shrouds, there was an auger hole, bored about half an inch or so, into the plank. His bone leg steadied in that hole; one arm elevated, and holding by a shroud; Captain Ahab stood erect, looking straight out beyond the ship's ever-pitching prow. There was an infinity of firmest fortitude, a determinate, unsurrenderable wilfulness, in the fixed and fearless, forward dedication of that glance. Not a word he spoke; nor did his officers say aught to him; though by all their minutest gestures and expressions, they plainly showed the uneasy, if not painful, consciousness of being under a troubled master-eye. And not only that, but moody stricken Ahab stood before them with a crucifixion in his face; in all the nameless regal overbearing dignity of some mighty woe.

Ere long, from his first visit in the air, he withdrew into his cabin. But after that morning, he was every day visible to the crew; either standing in his pivot-hole, or seated upon an ivory stool he had; or heavily walking the deck. As the sky grew less gloomy; indeed, began to grow a little genial, he became still less and less a recluse; as if, when the ship had sailed from home, nothing but the dead wintry bleakness of the sea had then kept him so secluded. And, by and by, it came to pass, that he was almost continually in the air; but, as yet, for all that he said, or perceptibly did, on the at last sunny deck, he seemed as unnecessary there as another mast. But the Pequod was only making a passage now; not regularly cruising; nearly all whaling preparatives needing supervision the mates were fully competent to, so that there was little or nothing, out of himself, to employ or excite Ahab, now; and thus chase away, for that one interval, the clouds that layer upon layer were piled upon his brow, as ever all clouds choose the loftiest peaks to pile themselves upon.

Nevertheless, ere long, the warm, warbling persuasiveness of the pleasant, holiday weather we came to, seemed gradually to charm him from his mood. For, as when the red-cheeked, dancing girls, April and May, trip home to the wintry, misanthropic woods; even the barest, ruggedest, most thunder-cloven old oak will at least send forth some few green sprouts, to welcome such glad-hearted visitants; so Ahab did, in the end, a little respond to the playful allurings of that girlish air. More than once did he put forth the faint blossom of a look, which, in any other man, would have soon flowered out in a smile.
