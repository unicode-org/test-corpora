TA 55. Le Whale ƒe Nɔnɔmetata Dziŋɔwo Ŋu.

I shall ere long paint to you as well as one can without canvas, nane si le abe tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ƒe nɔnɔme ŋutɔŋutɔ ene abe alesi wòdzena ŋutɔŋutɔ na tɔmelãléla ƒe ŋku ene ne le eya ŋutɔ ƒe ŋutilã blibo me la, wobla tɔmelã la ɖe tɔmelã-melia xa ale be woate ŋu aɖo afɔ edzi le afima le mɔ dzɔdzɔe nu. Eyata ate ŋu anyo be míaɖe gbeƒã eƒe nɔnɔmetata mawo siwo wosusu be wodi be yewoanya nu tso eŋu siwo vaseɖe egbea gɔ̃ hã tsɔa kakaɖedzi tsia tre ɖe anyigbadzikpɔla ƒe xɔse ŋu la ŋu do ŋgɔ. Ɣeyiɣia de be míaɖɔ xexeame ɖo le nya sia me, to kpeɖodzi nana be tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ƒe nɔnɔmetata mawo katã mesɔ o me.

Ate ŋu anye be Hindutɔwo, Egiptetɔwo, kple Helatɔwo ƒe kpememe xoxotɔwo kekeake domee woakpɔ nɔnɔmetatawo ƒe alakpanuwɔwɔ mawo katã tsoƒe vevitɔ. Elabena tso keke ɣeyiɣi mawo siwo me woto nu yeyewo vɛ gake womewɔa nu nyuie o me esime wònɔa gbedoxɔwo ƒe kpe xɔasi siwo wotsɔ kpe xɔasiwo wɔe, kpememewo ƒe afɔtiwo, kple akpoxɔnuwo, medalẽwo, kpluwo, kple gakuwo dzi la, wotsɔa kɔsɔkɔsɔ ƒe nudanu siwo le abe Saladin tɔ ene, kple ta si wotsɔ gakuku wɔe la hea tɔmelã si woyɔna be dolphin la George Kɔkɔe ƒe suku; tso ɣemaɣi la, mɔɖegbalẽ ma ke ƒe nane xɔ aƒe ɖe eme, menye le tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ƒe nɔnɔmetata xɔŋkɔ akpa gãtɔ me ɖeɖeko o, ke le dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunya me nu geɖe siwo woɖe fia le eŋu hã me.

Fifia, le mɔ sia mɔ nu la, woakpɔ blematɔ kekeake si li fifia ƒe nɔnɔmetata si wogblɔ le mɔ sia mɔ nu be enye tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale tɔ la le do xɔŋkɔ si nye Elephanta, le India, me. Brahmintɔwo gblɔna be le pagoda ma si womeɖo ŋkui o ƒe kpememe siwo nuwuwu meli na o kloe me la, wowɔ aɖaŋudɔwo kple nusiwo katã woti, amegbetɔ ƒe dɔ ɖesiaɖe si woate ŋu abu la ƒe kpɔɖeŋu do ŋgɔ ƒe geɖe hafi wo dometɔ aɖeke va dzɔ ŋutɔŋutɔ. Ekema mewɔ nuku o be le mɔ aɖe nu la, ɖe wòle be míaƒe tɔmelãwo léle ƒe dɔ bubume la nado vɔvɔli ɖe afima hafi. Hindoo tɔmelã si ŋu woƒo nu tsoe la dzena le gli la ƒe akpa aɖe si le vovo, si ɖe Vishnu ƒe ŋutilã me nɔnɔme fia le leviathan, si wosrɔ̃ nu tsoe be Matse Avatar. Gake togbɔ be kpememe sia nye amegbetɔ afã kple tɔmelã afã, ale be wòana mamlɛtɔa ƒe asike ko hã la, eƒe akpa sue ma katã mesɔ o. Eɖi anaconda ƒe asike si le tsɛ, wu tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale vavãtɔ ƒe fluke gã la ƒe asibidɛ gbadzawo.

Gake yi Galleries xoxoawo me, eye nàlé ŋku ɖe Kristotɔ nutala gã aɖe ƒe nɔnɔmetata si ɖe tɔmelã sia ŋu ɖa azɔ; elabena mekpɔa dzidzedze nyuie wu Hindootɔ si nɔ anyi do ŋgɔ na tsiɖɔɖɔa o. Enye Guido ƒe nɔnɔmetata si me Perseus le Andromeda ɖem tso ƒumelã alo tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale si me. Afikae Guido kpɔ nuwɔwɔ wɔnuku sia tɔgbe ƒe kpɔɖeŋu tsoe? Eye Hogarth hã meɖe nukpɔkpɔ ma ke le eya ŋutɔ ƒe “Perseus Descending” me o. Hogarthian lã wɔadã ma ƒe kpekpeme gã la le ƒutsotsoewo dzi le tsia dzi, eye ƒã hafi wòhea tsi sentimeta ɖeka. Howdah ƒomevi aɖe le eƒe akɔta, eye woate ŋu atsɔ eƒe nu si keke si me ƒutsotsoeawo le ʋuʋum ɖo la na Alakpatɔ ƒe Agbo si tso Thames to tsi dzi yi Mɔ̃ kɔkɔa me. Emegbe, Scotch Sibbald xoxoa ƒe Prodromus tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whales, kple Jonah ƒe tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale, abe alesi woɖee fia le Biblia xoxowo ƒe tatawo kple gɔmedzenu xoxowo ƒe lãɖeɖewo me ene hã li. Nukae woagblɔ tso amesiawo ŋu? Le agbalẽblala ƒe tɔmelã si le tsatsam abe weintsetse ƒe aŋgba ene ƒo xlã seke si le ɖiɖim ƒe atikpo—abe alesi wotree hetsɔ sika ɖo agbalẽ xoxo kple yeye geɖe ƒe megbe kple tanya ene—la, ema nye nuwɔwɔ si ƒe nɔnɔme dzeani ŋutɔ gake wònye nyakpakpa dzro aɖe ko la, . wosrɔ̃e, mexɔe se, tso nɔnɔmetata mawo tɔgbe siwo le blema vasewo dzi me. Togbɔ be wotsɔ ŋkɔ nɛ le xexeame katã be tɔmelã si woyɔna be dolphin hã la, meyɔa agbalẽblala ƒe tɔmelã sia be tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ƒe agbagbadzedze; elabena woɖoe nenema esime woto mɔ̃a vɛ zi gbãtɔ. Italytɔ gbeƒãɖela xoxo aɖee to esia vɛ le afi aɖe le ƒe alafa 15 lia lɔƒo, le Nusɔsrɔ̃ ƒe Gbugbɔgadzɔɣi; eye le ŋkeke mawo me, kple vaseɖe ɣeyiɣi aɖe si tsi megbe gɔ̃ hã la, ame geɖe bua tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be dolphins be wonye Leviathan ƒomevi aɖe.

Le blemagbalẽ aɖewo ƒe vignettes kple atsyɔ̃ɖoɖo bubuwo me la, àdo go ɣeaɖewoɣi kple asikaka wo ŋu vevie ŋutɔ le tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale la ŋu, afisi spouts ƒomevi ɖesiaɖe, jets d’eau, tsidzɔƒe dzodzoewo kple vuvɔ, Saratoga kple Baden-Baden, dona le ʋuʋum tso etɔ gbɔ ahɔhɔ̃ si ŋu ɖeɖi mete o. Àkpɔ tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale aɖewo siwo di be yewoanya nu geɖe le “Nusɔsrɔ̃ ƒe Ŋgɔyiyi” ƒe tata gbãtɔ ƒe tanya ƒe axa dzi.

Gake esi míedzudzɔ agbagbadzedze siawo katã siwo menye dɔnyala o la, mina míalé ŋku ɖe leviathan ƒe nɔnɔmetata mawo siwo wogblɔ be wonye dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunya ƒe nɔnɔmetata siwo ŋu wobu nyuie, siwo amesiwo nya nu tsoe la ŋu. Le Harris xoxoa ƒe mɔzɔzɔ ŋuti nuƒoƒoƒu me la, tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale ƒe agbalẽvi aɖewo siwo woɖe tso Netherlandstɔwo ƒe mɔzɔzɔ ŋuti agbalẽ aɖe me, si nye ƒe 1671 M.Ŋ. Le agbalẽ mawo dometɔ ɖeka me la, woɖe tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale, abe ati gãwo ƒe tɔdziʋuwo ene, mlɔ tsikpe ƒukpowo dome, eye dzata ɣiwo le du dzi le woƒe akɔta gbagbewo dzi. Le agbalẽ bubu me la, wowɔa vodada wɔnukua le alesi wotsɔa ƒumelã si woyɔna be whale la tsi tre ɖi na ƒumelã si woyɔna be whale la kple fluke siwo le tsitrenu.

Emegbe le afisia hã la, quarto gã aɖe li, si Kapitan Colnett ɖeka, si nye Post Captain le Eŋlisiawo ƒe ƒudzisrafowo me, ŋlɔ, si ƒe tanyae nye “A Voyage round Cape Horn into the South Seas, for the purpose to extend the Spermaceti Whale Fisheries.” Le agbalẽ sia me la, woŋlɔ nɔnɔmetata aɖe si wogblɔ be enye “Physeter alo Spermaceti tɔmelã aɖe ƒe Nɔnɔmetata, si woɖe le nudanu nu tso esi wowu le Mexico ƒuta, le August, 1793 me, eye wokɔe ɖe ʋua dzi.” Meke ɖi be menye ƒudzisrafomegã la na woɖe foto nyateƒe sia be wòaɖe vi na eƒe ƒudzisrafowo o. Ne magblɔ nu ɖeka ko tso eŋu la, mina magblɔ be ŋku aɖe le esi si wotsɔ, le dzidzenu si kpe ɖe eŋu nu, ɖe sperm whale si tsi bliboe ŋu la, ana whale ma ƒe ŋku nanye bow-fesre si didi abe afɔ atɔ̃ ene. Ao, nye aʋafia kalẽtɔ, nukatae mètsɔ Yona na mí wòkpɔ ŋku ma dzi o!

Eye Dzɔdzɔmeŋutinya gbalẽ siwo woƒo ƒu le dzitsinya me wu hã meɖea vi na sɔhɛwo kple ame fatuwo, siwo me vodada nyɔŋu ma ke mele o. Kpɔ agbalẽ xɔŋkɔ ma si nye “Goldsmith’s Animated Nature” ɖa. Le London ƒe tata kpui si wota le ƒe 1807 me me la, woŋlɔ “ƒumelã si woyɔna be whale” kple “narwhale” ƒe agbalẽwo. Nyemedi be madze abe ɖe nyemedze tugbe o ene o, gake tɔmelã sia si ƒe nu medzea ame ŋu o la le abe nyitsu si wolã lãgbalẽ ene; eye le narwhale gome la, ekpɔkpɔ vie sɔ gbɔ be wòawɔ nuku na ame, be le ƒe alafa wuiadelia sia me la, woate ŋu atsɔ asi aƒo hippogriff ma tɔgbe ɖe sukuviwo ƒe dutoƒonunyala ɖesiaɖe si nye nyateƒe la dzi.

Emegbe le ƒe 1825 me hã la, Bernard Germain, Count de Lacépède, si nye dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunyala gã aɖe, ta dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunya me tɔmelãwo ŋuti gbalẽ aɖe si woɖo ɖe ɖoɖo nu, si me Leviathan ƒomevi vovovoawo ƒe nɔnɔmetata geɖe le. Menye ɖeko esiawo katã mesɔ o, ke boŋ Mysticetus alo Greenland tɔmelã (si fia be, Right whale) ƒe nɔnɔmetata, Scoresby, si si nuteƒekpɔkpɔ geɖe su ɣeyiɣi didi aɖe le asikaka lã ƒomevi ma ŋu gɔ̃ hã gblɔ be ye hatɔ mele ye si le dzɔdzɔme nu o.

Gake dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunyala Frederick Cuvier, si nye Baron xɔŋkɔa nɔviŋutsu, koe tsɔ kpo-kploa dede asitsatsa sia katã si me vodada le la me. Le ƒe 1836 me la, eta Natural History of Whales, si me wòna nusi wòyɔ be Sperm Whale ƒe nɔnɔmetata le. Hafi nàtsɔ nɔnɔmetata ma afia Nantuckettɔ ɖesiaɖe la, anyo wu be nàwɔ ɖoɖo ɖe wò gbɔɖeme kpui si nàɖe tso Nantucket ŋu. Ne míagblɔe kpuie la, Frederick Cuvier ƒe Sperm Whale menye Sperm Whale o, ke boŋ squash ye. Nyateƒee, viɖe aɖeke mesu esi le tɔmelãwo léle ƒe mɔzɔzɔ me kpɔ o (ƒã hafi ŋutsu mawo tɔgbe kpɔnɛ), gake afikae wòkpɔ nɔnɔmetata ma tsoe, amekae ate ŋu anya? Ðewohĩ exɔe esime dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunya me tɔ si do ŋgɔ nɛ le dɔ ma ke me, Desmarest, xɔ eƒe fuɖeɖe vavãwo dometɔ ɖeka; si fia be woɖee tso Chinatɔwo ƒe nɔnɔmetata aɖe me. Eye ŋutsuvi gbagbe siwo si pɛnsil le ka ƒomevie Chinatɔ mawo nye, kplu kple nugoe siwo mebɔ o geɖe na míenya.

Le dzesidelawo ƒe tɔmelã siwo wokpɔna le ablɔwo dzi le amidzralawo ƒe fiasewo dzi gome la, nya kae woagblɔ tso wo ŋu? Zi geɖe la, woawoe nye Richard III. tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whales, siwo ƒe dromedary humps le, eye wowɔa adã ŋutɔ; ŋdi nuɖuɖu le ƒudzidelawo ƒe tart etɔ̃ alo ene dzi, si nye tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale tɔdziʋu siwo me ƒudzidelawo yɔ fũu: woƒe nɔnɔme madeamedziwo nɔa ʋuʋum le ʋu kple amadede blɔ ƒe atsiaƒuwo me.

Gake vodada vovovo siawo siwo wowɔ le tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale la ƒe nɔnɔmetata me mewɔ nuku nenema gbegbe le nyateƒe me o. Bu eŋu! Woɖe dzɔdzɔmeŋutinunya me nɔnɔmetata akpa gãtɔ tso tɔmelã siwo tsi ka me; eye esiawo sɔ abe alesi meli si gbã, si ƒe akɔta gbã, ƒe nɔnɔmetata aɖe atsi tre ɖi na lã bubume la ŋutɔ le eƒe dada si katã megbã o le eƒe ʋua ƒe ʋuƒo kple eƒe ƒumelãwo ŋu me ene. Togbɔ be sɔwo tsi tre ɖe woƒe didime bliboa nu hã la, Lewiatan gbagbe la metsɔ eɖokui ƒu tsi dzɔdzɔe haɖe hena eƒe nɔnɔmetata la kpɔ o. Tsi siwo gɔme womate ŋu ase o me koe woate ŋu akpɔ tɔmelã gbagbe la le, le eƒe gãnyenye kple vevienyenye bliboa me; eye le tsia dzi la, eƒe akpa gãtɔ mele ŋku lém ɖe eŋu o, abe aʋawɔmeli si woda ɖe fli me ene; eye tso nu ma me la, enye nusi mate ŋu adzɔ tegbee na amegbetɔ kuku be wòakɔe ɖe yame le ŋutilã me o, ale be wòakpɔ eƒe ʋuʋudedi sesẽwo kple ƒutsotsoewo katã ta. Eye, ne míagblɔ vovototo si wosusu vevie le tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale vi si le no nom kple Platoniatɔwo ƒe Leviathan si tsi bliboe dome ƒe nɔnɔme ŋu o; ke hã le tɔmelãvi mawo siwo le no nom siwo wokɔ ɖe meli ƒe akpata gome gɔ̃ hã gome la, eƒe nɔnɔme si to vovo, si le abe eel ene, si le ʋuʋum, si ƒe nɔnɔme to vovo la nɔa ale gbegbe be eƒe dzedzeme si tututu abosam ŋutɔ mete ŋu lé o.

Gake woate ŋu asusui, be woate ŋu akpɔ nyatakaka siwo de pɛpɛpɛ tso tɔmelã si tsi ƒumelã si tsi ƒumelãwo me ƒe ƒunukpeƒe amama me ku ɖe eƒe nɔnɔme ŋutɔŋutɔ ŋu. Gbɛɖɛ. Elabena enye nusiwo doa dzidzɔ na ame wu le Leviatan sia ŋu dometɔ ɖeka, be eƒe ƒunukpeƒewo mena susu boo aɖeke tso eƒe nɔnɔme bliboa ŋu o. Togbɔ be Jeremy Bentham ƒe ƒunukpeƒe si wotsɔ ƒo ɖe eƒe amewulawo dometɔ ɖeka ƒe agbalẽdzraɖoƒe na akaɖiti la ɖe aƒetɔ tsitsi si ƒe mo le kpekpem si ƒe mo le kpekpem le viɖekpɔkpɔ me, kple Jeremy ƒe ame ŋutɔ ƒe nɔnɔme vevi bubuawo katã ƒe susua fia wòsɔ hã; ke hã womate ŋu aƒo nya ta le leviathan aɖeke ƒe ƒu siwo wotsɔ ƒo ƒui me o. Le nyateƒe me la, abe alesi Adela gã la gblɔe ene la, ƒomedodo si le tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ƒe ƒunukpeƒe dzro ko kple lã si wotsɔ ga de eme bliboe eye wotsɔ nu ƒoe la dome abe alesi nudzodzoe la do ƒome kple chrysalis si ƒo xlãe wòƒo xlãe ene. Etɔxɛ sia dze le ta me wòwɔ nuku ŋutɔ, abe alesi woaɖee afia le vo me le agbalẽ sia ƒe akpa aɖe ene. Woɖee fia le axadzi ƒe asike hã me wòwɔ nuku ŋutɔ, si ƒe ƒuwo ɖoa amegbetɔ ƒe asi ƒe ƒuwo ŋu pɛpɛpɛ kloe, eye woɖe asibidɛa ɖeɖeko ɖa. Ƒu ƒe asibidɛ ene siwo wozãna edziedzi, siwo nye asibidɛ si wotsɔ fiaa nu, titina, asigɛ, kple asibidɛ sue le asike sia ŋu. Gake amesiawo katã nɔa woƒe nutsyɔnu si me lã le me tegbee, abe alesi amegbetɔ ƒe asibidɛwo nɔa nutsyɔnu si wowɔ kple asi me ene. Stubb si nye nukokoedonamela gblɔ gbeɖeka be: “Aleke kee tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale ate ŋu asubɔ mí ɣeaɖewoɣi ŋumaɖɔɖotɔe o, womate ŋu agblɔe le nyateƒe me gbeɖe be ewɔa nu ɖe ​​mía ŋu asike manɔmee o.”

Eyata le susu siawo katã ta la, mɔ ka kee nèɖalé ŋku ɖe eŋu o la, ele be nàƒo nya ta be Lewiatan gã lae nye nuwɔwɔ ɖeka ma le xexeame si wòle be wòanɔ anyi si womede amae o vaseɖe mamlɛtɔ dzi. Nyateƒee, nɔnɔmetata ɖeka ate ŋu aƒo dzesia wòate ɖe eŋu wu bubu kura, gake ɖeke mate ŋu aƒoe wòade pɛpɛpɛ ale gbegbe o. Eyata mɔ aɖeke meli si le anyigba dzi si dzi woato anya alesi tututu tɔmelã si woyɔna be whale la le o. Eye mɔ ɖeka kolia si dzi nàto akpɔ eƒe nɔnɔme gbagbe ƒe susu si dzi woate ŋu alɔ̃ ɖo gɔ̃ hã, enye be wò ŋutɔ nàyi tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale dzi; gake to esia wɔwɔ me la, èɖoa afɔku sue aɖe me be yeazu dzodoƒe tegbee eye wòanyrɔ wò. Eyata edze nam be anyo wu be màgawɔ nu kpata akpa le wò didi be yeanya nu tso Lewiatan sia ŋu me o.





TA 56. Le Whale Ƒe Nɔnɔmetata Siwo Me Vodada Mele O Ŋu, Kple Whale Ƒe Nɔnɔmetata Nyateƒetɔwo.

Le tɔmelã siwo woyɔna be whale ƒe nɔnɔmetata dziŋɔwo gome la, woteam kpɔ vevie le afisia be mage ɖe wo ŋuti ŋutinya mawo siwo gavloe wu siwo woakpɔ le agbalẽ aɖewo me, blematɔwo kple egbegbetɔwo siaa, vevietɔ le Pliny, Purchas, Hackluyt, Harris, Cuvier, etc. Gake metoa nya ma ŋu.

Sperm Whale gã la ƒe nɔnɔmetata ene siwo wota koe menya; Colnett, Huggins tɔ, Frederick Cuvier tɔ, kple Beale tɔ. Le ta si do ŋgɔ me la, woƒo nu tso Colnett kple Cuvier ŋu. Huggins tɔ nyo wu wo tɔ sãsãsã; gake, le nyateƒe me la, Beale tɔe nye nyuitɔ kekeake. Beale ƒe tɔmelã sia ƒe nɔnɔmetatawo katã nyo, negbe titinatɔ si le tɔmelã etɔ̃ siwo ƒe nɔnɔme le vovovo ƒe nɔnɔmetata me koe susɔ, eye wòwu eƒe ta evelia nu. Eƒe ŋgɔgbekpa si nye tɔdziʋu siwo dzea Sperm Whales dzi, togbɔ be ɖikeke mele eme o be wobu akɔnta be woatsɔ ado dukɔmeviwo ƒe ɖikeke si le xɔdɔme me ŋutsu aɖewo si hã la, esɔ ŋutɔ eye wòsɔ kple agbe tɔ le eƒe ŋusẽkpɔɖeamedzi le goawo katã me. Sperm Whale ƒe nɔnɔmetata siwo le J. Ross Browne me dometɔ aɖewo sɔ nyuie le woƒe nɔnɔme me; gake wokpa wo nublanuitɔe. Gake menye eƒe vodadae nye ema o.

Le Right Whale gome la, nɔnɔmetata siwo woɖe fia nyuie wu le Scoresby; gake wota wo ɖe nudanu sue aɖe dzi akpa be woaɖe nukpɔsusu si wodi la afia. Whale léle ƒe nukpɔkpɔwo ƒe nɔnɔmetata ɖeka koe le esi, eye esia nye gbɔdzɔgbɔdzɔ wɔnublanui aɖe, elabena nɔnɔmetata mawo tɔgbe dzi koe nàte ŋu akpɔ nusianu abe tɔmelã gbagbe ƒe nukpɔsusu vavãtɔ ene abe alesi eƒe adela gbagbewo kpɔe ene.

Thus glistening for a moment, as slowly it subsided, and sank. Then once more arose, and silently gleamed. It seemed not a whale; and yet is this Moby Dick? thought Daggoo. Again the phantom went down, but on re-appearing once more, with a stiletto-like cry that startled every man from his nod, the negro yelled out—“There! there again! there she breaches! right ahead! The White Whale, the White Whale!”

Upon this, the seamen rushed to the yard-arms, as in swarming-time the bees rush to the boughs. Bare-headed in the sultry sun, Ahab stood on the bowsprit, and with one hand pushed far behind in readiness to wave his orders to the helmsman, cast his eager glance in the direction indicated aloft by the outstretched motionless arm of Daggoo.

Whether the flitting attendance of the one still and solitary jet had gradually worked upon Ahab, so that he was now prepared to connect the ideas of mildness and repose with the first sight of the particular whale he pursued; however this was, or whether his eagerness betrayed him; whichever way it might have been, no sooner did he distinctly perceive the white mass, than with a quick intensity he instantly gave orders for lowering.

The four boats were soon on the water; Ahab's in advance, and all swiftly pulling towards their prey. Soon it went down, and while, with oars suspended, we were awaiting its reappearance, lo! in the same spot where it sank, once more it slowly rose. Almost forgetting for the moment all thoughts of Moby Dick, we now gazed at the most wondrous phenomenon which the secret seas have hitherto revealed to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length and breadth, of a glancing cream-colour, lay floating on the water, innumerable long arms radiating from its centre, and curling and twisting like a nest of anacondas, as if blindly to clutch at any hapless object within reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token of either sensation or instinct; but undulated there on the billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition of life.

As with a low sucking sound it slowly disappeared again, Starbuck still gazing at the agitated waters where it had sunk, with a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had I seen Moby Dick and fought him, than to have seen thee, thou white ghost!”

“What was it, Sir?” said Flask.

“The great live squid, which, they say, few whale-ships ever beheld, and returned to their ports to tell of it.”

But Ahab said nothing; turning his boat, he sailed back to the vessel; the rest as silently following.

Whatever superstitions the sperm whalemen in general have connected with the sight of this object, certain it is, that a glimpse of it being so very unusual, that circumstance has gone far to invest it with portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, that though one and all of them declare it to be the largest animated thing in the ocean, yet very few of them have any but the most vague ideas concerning its true nature and form; notwithstanding, they believe it to furnish to the sperm whale his only food. For though other species of whales find their food above water, and may be seen by man in the act of feeding, the spermaceti whale obtains his whole food in unknown zones below the surface; and only by inference is it that any one can tell of what, precisely, that food consists. At times, when closely pursued, he will disgorge what are supposed to be the detached arms of the squid; some of them thus exhibited exceeding twenty and thirty feet in length. They fancy that the monster to which these arms belonged ordinarily clings by them to the bed of the ocean; and that the sperm whale, unlike other species, is supplied with teeth in order to attack and tear it.

There seems some ground to imagine that the great Kraken of Bishop Pontoppodan may ultimately resolve itself into Squid. The manner in which the Bishop describes it, as alternately rising and sinking, with some other particulars he narrates, in all this the two correspond. But much abatement is necessary with respect to the incredible bulk he assigns it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely heard rumors of the mysterious creature, here spoken of, it is included among the class of cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it would seem to belong, but only as the Anak of the tribe.





CHAPTER 60. The Line.

With reference to the whaling scene shortly to be described, as well as for the better understanding of all similar scenes elsewhere presented, I have here to speak of the magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

The line originally used in the fishery was of the best hemp, slightly vapored with tar, not impregnated with it, as in the case of ordinary ropes; for while tar, as ordinarily used, makes the hemp more pliable to the rope-maker, and also renders the rope itself more convenient to the sailor for common ship use; yet, not only would the ordinary quantity too much stiffen the whale-line for the close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen are beginning to learn, tar in general by no means adds to the rope's durability or strength, however much it may give it compactness and gloss.

Of late years the Manilla rope has in the American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material for whale-lines; for, though not so durable as hemp, it is stronger, and far more soft and elastic; and I will add (since there is an æsthetics in all things), is much more handsome and becoming to the boat, than hemp. Hemp is a dusky, dark fellow, a sort of Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian to behold.

The whale-line is only two-thirds of an inch in thickness. At first sight, you would not think it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one and fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred and twenty pounds; so that the whole rope will bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, the common sperm whale-line measures something over two hundred fathoms. Towards the stern of the boat it is spirally coiled away in the tub, not like the worm-pipe of a still though, but so as to form one round, cheese-shaped mass of densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers of concentric spiralizations, without any hollow but the “heart,” or minute vertical tube formed at the axis of the cheese. As the least tangle or kink in the coiling would, in running out, infallibly take somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, the utmost precaution is used in stowing the line in its tub. Some harpooneers will consume almost an entire morning in this business, carrying the line high aloft and then reeving it downwards through a block towards the tub, so as in the act of coiling to free it from all possible wrinkles and twists.

In the English boats two tubs are used instead of one; the same line being continuously coiled in both tubs. There is some advantage in this; because these twin-tubs being so small they fit more readily into the boat, and do not strain it so much; whereas, the American tub, nearly three feet in diameter and of proportionate depth, makes a rather bulky freight for a craft whose planks are but one half-inch in thickness; for the bottom of the whale-boat is like critical ice, which will bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much of a concentrated one. When the painted canvas cover is clapped on the American line-tub, the boat looks as if it were pulling off with a prodigious great wedding-cake to present to the whales.

Both ends of the line are exposed; the lower end terminating in an eye-splice or loop coming up from the bottom against the side of the tub, and hanging over its edge completely disengaged from everything. This arrangement of the lower end is necessary on two accounts. First: In order to facilitate the fastening to it of an additional line from a neighboring boat, in case the stricken whale should sound so deep as to threaten to carry off the entire line originally attached to the harpoon. In these instances, the whale of course is shifted like a mug of ale, as it were, from the one boat to the other; though the first boat always hovers at hand to assist its consort. Second: This arrangement is indispensable for common safety's sake; for were the lower end of the line in any way attached to the boat, and were the whale then to run the line out to the end almost in a single, smoking minute as he sometimes does, he would not stop there, for the doomed boat would infallibly be dragged down after him into the profundity of the sea; and in that case no town-crier would ever find her again.

Before lowering the boat for the chase, the upper end of the line is taken aft from the tub, and passing round the loggerhead there, is again carried forward the entire length of the boat, resting crosswise upon the loom or handle of every man's oar, so that it jogs against his wrist in rowing; and also passing between the men, as they alternately sit at the opposite gunwales, to the leaded chocks or grooves in the extreme pointed prow of the boat, where a wooden pin or skewer the size of a common quill, prevents it from slipping out. From the chocks it hangs in a slight festoon over the bows, and is then passed inside the boat again; and some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon the box in the bows, it continues its way to the gunwale still a little further aft, and is then attached to the short-warp—the rope which is immediately connected with the harpoon; but previous to that connexion, the short-warp goes through sundry mystifications too tedious to detail.

Thus the whale-line folds the whole boat in its complicated coils, twisting and writhing around it in almost every direction. All the oarsmen are involved in its perilous contortions; so that to the timid eye of the landsman, they seem as Indian jugglers, with the deadliest snakes sportively festooning their limbs. Nor can any son of mortal woman, for the first time, seat himself amid those hempen intricacies, and while straining his utmost at the oar, bethink him that at any unknown instant the harpoon may be darted, and all these horrible contortions be put in play like ringed lightnings; he cannot be thus circumstanced without a shudder that makes the very marrow in his bones to quiver in him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange thing! what cannot habit accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, and brighter repartees, you never heard over your mahogany, than you will hear over the half-inch white cedar of the whale-boat, when thus hung in hangman's nooses; and, like the six burghers of Calais before King Edward, the six men composing the crew pull into the jaws of death, with a halter around every neck, as you may say.

Perhaps a very little thought will now enable you to account for those repeated whaling disasters—some few of which are casually chronicled—of this man or that man being taken out of the boat by the line, and lost. For, when the line is darting out, to be seated then in the boat, is like being seated in the midst of the manifold whizzings of a steam-engine in full play, when every flying beam, and shaft, and wheel, is grazing you. It is worse; for you cannot sit motionless in the heart of these perils, because the boat is rocking like a cradle, and you are pitched one way and the other, without the slightest warning; and only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy and simultaneousness of volition and action, can you escape being made a Mazeppa of, and run away with where the all-seeing sun himself could never pierce you out.

Again: as the profound calm which only apparently precedes and prophesies of the storm, is perhaps more awful than the storm itself; for, indeed, the calm is but the wrapper and envelope of the storm; and contains it in itself, as the seemingly harmless rifle holds the fatal powder, and the ball, and the explosion; so the graceful repose of the line, as it silently serpentines about the oarsmen before being brought into actual play—this is a thing which carries more of true terror than any other aspect of this dangerous affair. But why say more? All men live enveloped in whale-lines. All are born with halters round their necks; but it is only when caught in the swift, sudden turn of death, that mortals realize the silent, subtle, ever-present perils of life. And if you be a philosopher, though seated in the whale-boat, you would not at heart feel one whit more of terror, than though seated before your evening fire with a poker, and not a harpoon, by your side.
