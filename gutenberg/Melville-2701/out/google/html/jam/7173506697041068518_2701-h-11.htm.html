Di ostensible reason why Ahab neva go pan di whaler weh wi did taak bout was dis: di wind an sea did a betoken storms. But all dis might remain inadequately estimated, if someting neva seh yah bout di peculiar usages a whaling-vessels wen dem meet each other inna foreign seas, an especially pan a common cruising-ground.

If two strangers a cross di Pine Barrens inna New York State, or di equally desolate Salisbury Plain inna England; if casually encounter each other in such inhospitable wilds, dem twoin yah, fi di life a dem, cyaan well avoid a mutual salutation; an stop fi a moment fi interchange di news; an, perhaps, sitting dung fi a while an resting in concert: den, how much more natural dat upon di illimitable Pine Barrens an Salisbury Plains a di sea, two whaling vessels a decrying each other at di ends a di earth—off lone Fanning's Island, or di far away King's Mills; how much more natural, mi seh, dat unda such circumstances dem ships yah shudn't only interchange hails, but come into still closer, more friendly an sociable contact. An especially dis woulda seem to be a matter of course, inna di case a vessels weh own inna one seaport, an whose captains, officers, an not a few a di man dem personally know to each other; an consequently, have all sorts a dear domestic tings fi talk bout.

An in return fi dat courtesy, di outward-bound ship woulda receive di latest whaling intelligence fram di cruising-ground to which she may be destined, a ting of the utmost important to har. An in degree, all dis ago hold true concerning whaling vessels crossing each other's track pan di cruising-ground itself, even though dem equally long absent fram home. Fi one a dem maita get a transfer a leta fram some third, an now far remote vessel; an some a dem letta deh maita be fi di people dem pan di ship weh she meet now. Besides, dem woulda exchange di whaling news, an have an agreeable chat. For not only dem woulda meet wid all di sympathies a sailors, but likewise wid all di peculiar congenialities arising fram a common pursuit an mutually shared privations an perils.

Nor woulda difference of country mek any very essential difference; dat is, so long as both parties speak one language, as is di case wid Americans an English. Though, fi be sure, fram di likl nomba a English whalers, such meetings no very often occur, an wen dem do occur deh too apt fi be a sort a shyness between dem; fi yuh Englishman is rather reserved, an yuh Yankee, him nuh fancy dat deh sort a ting inna nobody but himself. Besides, di English whalers dem sometimes affect a kind a metropolitan superiority ova di American whalers dem; regarding di long, lean Nantucketer, wid him nondescript provincialisms, as a sort a sea-peasant. But weh dis superiority inna di English whalemen dem really consist, it woulda be hard fi seh, seeing dat di Yankees dem inna one day, collectively, kill more whales dan all di English, collectively, inna ten years. But dis a one harmless likkle foible inna di English whale-hunters, weh di Nantucketer dem nuh tek much to heart; probably, cause him know seh him have a few foibles himself.

So, den, wi si seh a all ships separately weh a sail di sea, di whalers dem have most reason fi be sociable—an dem so. Whereas, some merchant ships crossing each other's wake inna di mid-Atlantic, will oftentimes pass on widout so much as a single word of recognition, mutually cutting each other on the high seas, like a brace of dandies in Broadway; an all di time indulging, perhaps, in finical criticism upon each other's rig. As fi Men-of-War, wen dem chance fi meet at sea, dem first go chruu such a string a silly bowings an scrapings, such a ducking a ensigns, dat it nuh look like yuh have much right-down hearty good-will an brotherly love bout it at all. As touching Slave-ships meeting, why, dem deh inna such a prodigious hurry, dem run weh fram each ada as soon as possible. An as fi Pirates, wen dem chance fi cross each ada cross-bones, di first hail is—“How many skulls?”—di same way how whalers hail—“How many barrels?” An dat deh kweschan once ansa, pirates straightway steer apart, cause dem a infernal villains pan both sides, an dem nuh like fi see overmuch a each other villanous likenesses.

But look pon di godly, honest, unostentatious, hospitable, sociable, free-an-easy whaler! Weh di whaler do wen she meet anodda whaler inna any sort a decent weather? Shi have a “ Gam ,” a ting so utterly unknown to all other ships dat dem neva hear bout di name even; an if by chance dem shuda hear bout it, dem only grin at it, an repeat gamesome stuff bout “spouters” an “blubber-boilers,” an such like pretty exclamations. Why it is dat all Merchant-seamen, an also all Pirates an Man-of-War's man, an Slave-ship sailors, cherish such a scornful feeling towards Whale-ships; dis a one kweschan it woulda be hard fi ansa. Bikaaz, inna di case a pirates, seh, mi shuda like fi know weda dat de profession a dem have any peculiar glory bout it. It sometimes end inna uncommon elevation, indeed; but only at di gallows. An besides, wen a man get elevate inna dat deh odd fashion, him nuh have no proper foundation fi him superior altitude. So, mi conclude, seh inna boasting himself fi be high lifted above a whaleman, inna dat deh assertion di pirate nuh have no solid basis fi stan pan.

But weh a Gam? Yuh might wear out yuh index-finger running up an dung di columns a dictionaries, an neva find di word. Dr. Johnson neva attain to dat deh erudition; Noah Webster's ark nuh hold it. Nevertheless, dis same expressive word now fi nuff years been inna constant use mongst some fifteen thousand true born Yankees. Certainly, it need a definition, an it shuda be incorporate inna di Lexicon. Wid dat deh view, mek mi learnedly define it.

GAM. NOUN— A social meeting a two ( or more ) Whaleships, generally pan a cruising-ground; wen, afta dem exchange hails, dem exchange visits by boats' crews: di two captains remaining, fi di time, pan board a one ship, an di two chief mates pan di ada one.

Deh have anodda likkle ting bout Gamming weh yuh no fi forget yah. All professions have dem own likkle peculiarities of detail; so di whale fishery. Inna a pirate, man-of-war, or slave ship, wen di captain get row anyweh inna him boat, him always sidong inna di stern sheets pan a comfortable, sometimes cushioned seat deh, an often steer himself wid a pretty likkle milliner's tiller weh dem decorate wid gay cords an ribbons. But di whale-boat nuh have no seat astern, no sofa a dat deh sort whatever, an no tiller at all. High times indeed, if whaling captains did a wheel bout di water pan castors like gouty old aldermen inna patent chairs. An as fi a tiller, di whale-boat neva admit a any such effeminacy; an so as inna gamming a complete boat's crew haffi lef di ship, an hence as di boat steerer or harpooneer is a di numba, dat deh subordinate a di steersman upon di occasion, an di captain, having no place fi sit inna, is pull off to him visit all standing like a pine tree. An often yuh wi notice seh being conscious a di eyes a di whole visible world resting pan him fram di sides a di two ships, dis standing captain is all alive to di importance a sustaining him dignity by maintaining him legs. Nor dis a nuh very easy matter; fi inna him rear a di immense projecting steering oar weh a hit him now an den inna di small a him back, di afta-oar a reciprocate by rapping him knees inna front. Im so completely wedged before an behind, an can only expand imself sideways by settle dung pan im stretched legs; but a sudden, violent pitch a di boat will often go far fi topple him, cause length a foundation a nutten widout corresponding breadth. Merely mek a spread angle a two poles, an yuh cyaan stan dem up. Den, again, it woulda neva do inna plain sight a di world's riveted eyes, it woulda neva do, mi seh, fi dis straddling captain fi be seen steadying himself di slightest particle by catching hold a anyting wid him hands; indeed, as token a him entire, buoyant self-command, him generally carry him hands inna him trowsers' pockets; but perhaps being generally very large, heavy hands, him carry dem deh fi ballast. Nevertheless deh have occurred instances, well authenticated ones too, weh di captain have been known fi an uncommonly critical moment or two, inna a sudden squall say—fi seize hold a di nearest oarsman's hair, an hold on deh like grim death.
