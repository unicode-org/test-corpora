Again di phantom go dung, but pan re-appearing once more, wid a stiletto-like cry weh startle every man fram him nod, di negro yell out—“Deh! deh again! deh she breaches! right ahead! Di White Whale, di White Whale!”

Pan dis, di seamen dem rush to di yard-arms, as inna swarming-time di bees dem rush to di boughs. Bare-headed inna di sultry sun, Ahab did a stan up pan di bowsprit, an wid one han push far behind inna readiness fi wave him orders to di helmsman, cast him eager glance inna di direction weh di outstretched motionless arm a Daggoo did indicate aloft.

Weda di flitting attendance a di one still an solitary jet did gradually work pan Ahab, so dat him did now ready fi connect di ideas a mildness an repose wid di first sight a di particular whale weh him did a pursue; however dis was, or weda him eagerness did betray him; whichever way it might been, no sooner did him distinctly perceive di white mass, dan wid a quick intensity him instantly gi orders fi lowering.

Di four boat dem did soon pan di wata; Ahab deh inna front, an all swiftly a pul towards dem prey. Soon it go dung, an while, wid oars suspended, we did a wait it reappearance, lo! inna di same spot weh it did sink, once more it slowly rise. Almost forget fi di moment all thoughts a Moby Dick, we now gazed at di most wondrous phenomenon weh di secret seas dem have hitherto reveal to mankind. A vast pulpy mass, furlongs in length an breadth, a glancing cream-colour, lay floating pan di wata, innumerable long arms radiating fram it centre, an curling an twisting like a nest a anaconda, as if blindly fi clutch at any hapless object widin reach. No perceptible face or front did it have; no conceivable token a either sensation or instinct; but undulated deh pan di billows, an unearthly, formless, chance-like apparition a life.

As wid a low sucking sound it slowly disappear again, Starbuck still gazing at di agitated waters weh it did sunk, wid a wild voice exclaimed—“Almost rather had mi seen Moby Dick an fight him, dan to have seen you, thou white ghost!”

“Weh it was, Sir?” Flask seh.

“Di great live squid, weh, dem seh, few whale-ships eva beheld, an go back to dem ports fi tell bout it.”

Bot Ehab neva se notn; ton him boat, him sail back to di vessel; di rest as silently following.

Wateva superstitions di sperm whalemen in general have connected wid di sight a dis object, certain it is, dat a glimpse a it being so very unusual, dat circumstance don gone far fi invest it wid portentousness. So rarely is it beheld, dat though one an all a dem declare it to be di largest animated ting inna di ocean, yet very few a dem have any but di most vague ideas concerning its true nature an form; notwithstanding, dem believe it fi furnish to di sperm whale him only food. For though ada species a whale find dem food above water, an may be seen by man inna di act a feeding, di spermaceti whale get him whole food inna unknown zones below di surface; an only by inference is it dat any one can tell of weh, precisely, dat food consist. Somtaim, wen yuh pursue im closely, im wi disgorge weh dem suppose to be di detached arms a di squid; some a dem did a exhibit exceeding twenty an thirty feet inna length. Dem fancy seh di monster weh dem arms yah belong to ordinarily clings by dem to di bed a di ocean; an dat di sperm whale, unlike ada species, get supply wid teet fi attack an tear it.

It look like some ground fi imagine seh di great Kraken a Bishop Pontoppodan maita ultimately resolve itself inna Squid. Di manner in which di Bishop describe it, as alternately rising an sinking, wid some other particulars him narrate, in all dis di two correspond. But much abatement is necessary wid respect to di incredible bulk weh him assign it.

By some naturalists who have vaguely hear rumors of di mysterious creature, weh wi a talk bout yah, it is included among di class a cuttle-fish, to which, indeed, in certain external respects it woulda seem fi belong, but only as di Anak a di tribe.





CHAPTER 60. Di Line.

Wid reference to di whaling scene shortly fi be described, as well as fi di better understanding a all similar scenes elsewhere present, mi have yah fi speak bout di magical, sometimes horrible whale-line.

Di line weh dem did use inna di fishery was a di bes hemp, slightly vapored wid tar, not impregnated wid it, as inna di case a ordinary ropes; fi while tar, as ordinarily used, mek di hemp more pliable to di rope-maker, an also renders di rope itself more convenient to di sailor fi common ship use; yet, not only woulda di ordinary quantity too much stiffen di whale-line fi di close coiling to which it must be subjected; but as most seamen a staat laan, tar in general by no means add to di rope's durability or strength, however much it may gi it compactness an gloss.

A late years di Manilla rope have inna di American fishery almost entirely superseded hemp as a material fi whale-lines; fi, though it no so durable as hemp, it stronger, an far more soft an elastic; an mi ago add (since deh have an æsthetics inna all tings), is much more handsome an becoming to di boat, dan hemp. Hemp a one dusky, dark fellow, a sort a Indian; but Manilla is as a golden-haired Circassian fi behold.

Di whale-line is only two-thirds a one inch inna thickness. At first sight, yuh wouldn't tink it so strong as it really is. By experiment its one an fifty yarns will each suspend a weight of one hundred an twenty pounds; so dat di whole rope ago bear a strain nearly equal to three tons. In length, di common sperm whale-line measure someting ova two hundred fathoms. Towards di stern a di boat it spirally coiled weh inna di tub, not like di worm-pipe a one still though, but so as fi form one round, cheese-shaped mass a densely bedded “sheaves,” or layers a concentric spiralizations, widout any hollow but di “heart,” or minute vertical tube form at di axis a di cheese. As di least tangle or kink inna di coiling woulda, inna running out, infallibly tek somebody's arm, leg, or entire body off, di utmost precaution is used inna stowing di line inna it tub. Some harpooneers ago consume almost a entire morning inna dis business, carrying di line high aloft an den reeving it dung through a block towards di tub, so as inna di act a coiling fi free it fram all possible wrinkles an twists.

Ina di Ingglish buot dem yuuz tuu tub insted a wan; di same line being continuously coiled inna both tubs. Som advantage deh inna dis; cause dem twin-tubs yah so small dem fit more readily inna di boat, an dem nuh strain it so much; whereas, di American tub, nearly three feet inna diameter an a proportionate depth, mek a rather bulky freight fi a craft weh planks dem a but one half-inch inna thickness; fi di bottom a di whale-boat is like critical ice, weh ago bear up a considerable distributed weight, but not very much a concentrated one. Wen di painted canvas cover clap pan di American line-tub, di boat look like it did a pull off wid a prodigious great wedding-cake fi present to di whales.

Both ends a di line dem expose; di lower end terminating inna an eye-splice or loop coming up fram di bottom against di side a di tub, an hanging ova it edge completely disengaged fram everyting. Dis arrangement a di lower end is necessary pan two accounts. Fos: Fi mek it iizi fi fasten tu it a wan ada lain fram wan neighboring boat, in kies di stricken whale shuda soun so diip az fi threaten fi carry aaf di entire line weh did orijinali attach tu di harpoon. Inna dem instances yah, di whale of course shift like a mug a ale, as it were, fram di one boat to di other; though di first boat always hover at han fi assist it consort. Sekant: Dis arrangement is indispensable fi common safety's sake; fi if di lower end a di line in any way attached to di boat, an if di whale den did a run di line out to di end almost inna a single, smoking minute as him sometimes do, him wouldn't stop deh, fi di doomed boat woulda infallibly be drag dung afta him inna di profundity a di sea; an inna dat deh case no town-crier woulda eva find har again.

Bifuo yuh lower di boat fi di chase, di upper end a di line tek aft fram di tub, an pass round di loggerhead deh, is again carry forward di entire length a di boat, resting crosswise upon di loom or handle a every man's oar, so dat it jogs against him wrist inna rowing; an also passing between di man dem, as dem alternately sit at di opposite gunwales, to di leaded chocks or grooves inna di extreme pointed prow a di boat, weh a wooden pin or skewer di size a one common quill, prevent it fram slip out. Fram di chocks it hang inna a slight festoon ova di bows, an den it pass inside di boat again; an some ten or twenty fathoms (called box-line) being coiled upon di box inna di bows, it continue it way to di gunwale still a likkle further aft, an is den attached to di short-warp—di rope weh immediately connect wid di harpoon; but before dat deh connexion, di short-warp go chuu sundry mystifications too tedious fi detail.

So di whale-line fold di whole boat inna it complicated coils, twisting an writhing roun it inna almost every direction. All di oarsmen dem involve inna it perilous contortions; so dat to di timid eye a di landsman, dem seem as Indian jugglers, wid di deadliest snakes sportively festooning dem limbs. Nor can any son a mortal woman, fi di first time, seat himself amid dem hempen intricacies, an while straining him utmost at di oar, betthink him seh at any unknown instant di harpoon may be darted, an all dem horrible contortions may be put in play like ringed lightnings; him cyaan be so circumstanced widout a shudder weh mek di very marrow inna him bones fi quiver inna him like a shaken jelly. Yet habit—strange ting! weh habit can't accomplish?—Gayer sallies, more merry mirth, better jokes, an brighter repartees, yuh neva hear ova yuh mahogany, dan yuh woulda hear ova di half-inch white cedar a di whale-boat, wen so hung inna hangman's nooses; an, like di six burgers a Calais before King Edward, di six man dem weh did a compose di crew pull inna di jaws a death, wid a halter roun every neck, as yuh may seh.

Miebi a very likkle thought wi now mek yuh can account fi dem repeated whaling disasters—some few a dem casually chronicled—a dis man or dat deh man weh get tek outa di boat by di line, an lost. For, wen di line a dart out, fi be seated den inna di boat, is like being seated inna di midst a di manifold whizzings a one steam-engine inna full play, wen every flying beam, an shaft, an wheel, a graze yuh. It worse; kaa yuh cyaan sit motionless inna di heart a dem perils yah, cause di boat a rock like a cradle, an yuh a pitch one way an di ada, widout di slightest warning; an only by a certain self-adjusting buoyancy an simultaneousness a volition an action, yuh can escape being mek a Mazeppa of, an run weh wid weh di all-seeing sun himself coulda neva pierce yuh out.

Again: as di profound calm weh only apparently precede an prophesies a di storm, is perhaps more awful dan di storm itself; fi, indeed, di calm a jus di wrapper an envelope a di storm; an contain it inna itself, as di seemingly harmless rifle hold di fatal powder, an di ball, an di explosion; so di graceful repose a di line, as it silently serpentines bout di oarsmen dem before dem bring it inna actual play—dis a one ting weh carry more a true terror dan any ada aspect a dis dangerous affair. But why yuh seh more? All man live enveloped inna whale-lines. All a dem born wid halters roun dem neck; but it is only wen dem ketch dem inna di swift, sudden turn a death, dat mortals realize di silent, subtle, ever-present perils a life. An if yuh a philosopher, though yuh did a sidong inna di whale-boat, yuh wouldn't feel one whit more a terror, dan if yuh did a sidong before yuh evening fire wid a poker, an not a harpoon, by yuh side.
