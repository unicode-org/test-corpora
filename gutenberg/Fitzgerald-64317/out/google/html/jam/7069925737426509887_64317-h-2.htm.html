V

Wen mi come home to West Egg dat deh night mi did fraid fi a moment seh mi house did a bon. Two o'clock an di whole corner a di peninsula did a blaze wid light, weh did a fall unreal pan di shrubbery an mek thin elongating glints pan di roadside wires. Wen mi turn a corner, mi si seh a did Gatsby's house, lit fram tower to cellar.

At first mi did tink seh a did anodda party, a wild rout weh did resolve itself inna “hide-and-go-seek” or “sardines-in-the-box” wid all di house throw open to di game. Bot no soun neva kom. Ongl briiz ina di chrii dem, we bluo di waya dem an mek di lait dem go aaf an aan agen laik se di ous did wink inna di daaknis. As mi taxi did a groan weh mi si Gatsby a walk toward mi across him lawn.

“Yu plies luk laik di Worl Fier,” mi se.

“It do?” Him turn him eyes toward it absently. “Mi a glance inna some a di rooms. Mek wi go a Coney Island, old sport. Inna mi car.”

“It tuu liet.”

“Well, suppose we tek a plunge inna di swimming pool? Mi neva mek use a it all summer.”

“Mi haffi go a bed.”

"Arite."

Him wait, looking pon mi wid suppressed eagerness.

“Mi talk wid Miss Baker,” mi seh afta a moment. “Mi a go kaal op Daisy tumaro an invait ar uova ya fi jringk tii.”

“Oh, dat's all right,” him seh carelessly. “Mi nuh waan put yuh to no trouble.”

“Weh day woulda suit yuh?”

“Weh day woulda suit yuh ?” him correct mi quickly. “Mi nuh waan put yuh inna no trouble, yuh see.”

“How bout di day afta tomorrow?”

Im tink fi a moment. Den, wid reluctance: “Mi waan get di grass cut,” him seh.

Wi both look dung pan di grass—a did a shaap line weh mi ragged lawn did end an di darker, well-kept expanse a him did staat. Mi did tink seh him did mean mi grass.

“A wan neks likkle ting,” him seh uncertainly, an hesitate.

“Yuh woulda radda put it off fi a few days?” Mi ask.

“Oh, it nuh bout dat. At least—” Im fumbled wid a series a beginnings. “Why, mi did tink—why, look yah, old sport, yuh nuh mek much money, yuh?”

“Not very much.”

Dis did look like it did reassure him an him continue more confidently.

“Mi did tink yuh neva, if yuh wi pardon mi—yuh si, mi carry on a likkle business pan di side, a sort a side line, yuh andastan. An mi did tink seh if yuh nuh mek very much—Yuh a sell bonds, noh true, old sport?”

“Trying to.”

“Well, dis woulda interest yuh. It wouldn't tek up much a yuh time an yuh might pick up a nice bit a money. It happen fi be a rather confidential sort a ting.”

Mi realize now seh unda different circumstances dat deh conversation mighta been one a di crises a mi life. But, because di offer was obviously an tactlessly fi a service fi be rendered, mi neva have no choice except fi cut him off deh.

“Mi have mi han dem full,” mi seh. “Mi much obliged but mi couldn't tek on no more work.”

“Yuh wouldn't haffi do no business wid Wolfshiem.” Evidently him did tink seh mi did a shy weh fram di “gonnegtion” weh dem mention at lunch, but mi assure him seh him did wrong. Im wait a moment longer, hoping seh mi woulda staat a conversation, but mi did too absorbed fi be responsive, so im go home unwillingly.

Di evening did mek mi lightheaded an happy; Mi tink mi walk into a deep sleep as mi enter mi front door. So mi no know weda or not Gatsby go a Coney Island, or fi how many hours him “glance into rooms” while him house blaze gaudily on. Mi call up Daisy fram di office next morning, an invite har fi come to tea.

“No bring Tom,” mi warn har.

"Wah?"

“No bring Tom.”

“A who is 'Tom'?” she ask innocently.

Di day weh wi did agree pan did a rain. At eleven o'clock a man inna a raincoat, a drag a lawn-mower, tap pan mi front door an seh dat Mr. Gatsby did sen him ova fi cut mi grass. Dis remind mi seh mi did forget fi tell mi Finn fi come back, so mi drive inna West Egg Village fi search fi har mongst soggy whitewashed alleys an fi buy some cups an lemons an flowers.

Di flowers dem neva need, cause at two o'clock a greenhouse did come fram Gatsby's, wid innumerable receptacles fi contain it. An hour later di front door open nervously, an Gatsby inna a white flannel suit, silver shirt, an gold-coloured tie, hurry in. Him did pale, an deh did have dark signs a sleeplessness unda him eyes.

“Evriting rait?” him ask immediately.

“Di graas luk gud, if a dat yu miin.”

“Weh grass?” him ask blankly. “Oh, di grass inna di yaad.” Im look outa di window pan it, but, judging fram im expression, mi nuh believe im si a ting.

“Look very good,” him remarked vaguely. “One a di papers dem seh dem did tink di rain woulda stop bout four. Mi tink a did The Journal . Yuh have everyting yuh need inna di shape a—of tea?”

Mi tek him inna di pantry, weh him look likkle reproachfully at di Finn. Tugeda wi scrutinize di twelve lemon cakes fram di delicatessen shop.

“Dem ago do?” Mi ask.

“A chuu, a chuu! Dem rait!” an him add hollowly, “… old sport.”

Di rain cool bout half-past three to a damp mist, through which occasional thin drops swim like dew. Gatsby look wid vacant eyes chruu a copy a Clay's Economics , staatin pan di Finnish tread weh did a shake di kitchen floor, an a luk tuwaadz di bleared windows dem fram taim tu taim laik se a siiriz a invisibl bot alarming happenings did a tek plies outsaid. Finally him get up an tell mi, inna one uncertain voice, seh him a goh home.

“Wa mek dat?”

“Nobadi naa kom fi tii. It tuu liet!” Him look pan him watch as if deh did have some pressing demand pan him time elsewhere. “Mi cyan wait all day.”

“No bada gwaan fuul-fuul, a jos tuu minit tu fuo.”

Him did sidong miserably, as if mi did push him, an simultaneously deh did have di sound a one motor a turn inna mi lane. Wi both jump up, an, a likkle harrowed miself, mi go out inna di yard.

Anda di dripping bare lilac-trees a big open car did a come up di drive. It stop. Daisy face, tipped sideways unda a three-cornered lavender hat, look out pan mi wid a bright ecstatic smile.

“A dis absolutely weh yuh live, mi dearest one?”

Di exhilarating ripple a har voice was a wild tonic inna di rain. Mi did afi fala di soun a it fi a moutn, op an dong, wid mi iez aluon, bifuo eni wod kom chuu. A damp streak a hair did deh like a dash a blue paint across har cheek, an har han did wet wid glistening drops as mi tek it fi help har fram di car.

“Yuh in love wid mi,” she seh low inna mi ear, “or why mi did haffi come alone?”

“A dat a di secret a Castle Rackrent. Tell yuh chauffeur fi go far weh an spen one hour.”

“Come back inna one hour, Ferdie.” Den inna a grave murmur: “Him name is Ferdie.”

“Di gasoline affect him nose?”

“Mi nuh tink so,” she seh innocently. "Wah mek?"

Wi go in. To mi overwhelming surprise di living-room did deserted.

“Well, dat funny,” mi exclaimed.

“Weh funny?”

Shi ton har head as deh did have a light dignified knocking at di front door. Mi go out an open it. Gatsby, pale as death, wid him han dem plunged like weights inna him coat pockets, did a stan up inna a puddle a water a glare tragically inna mi eyes.

Wid him han dem still inna him coat pocket him stalk by mi inna di hall, turn sharply as if him did deh pan a wire, an disappear inna di living-room. It neva did a bit funny. Aware a di loud beating a mi own heart mi pull di door to against di increasing rain.

Fi half a minute no sound neva did a come. Den fram di living-room mi hear a sort a choking murmur an part a laugh, followed by Daisy's voice pan a clear artificial note:

“Mi certainly glad fi si yuh again.”

A pause; it endure horribly. Mi neva have nutten fi do inna di hall, so mi go inna di room.

Gatsby, him han dem still inna him pocket, did a recline gens di mantelpiece inna a strained counterfeit a perfect ease, even a boredom. Him head did lean back so far dat it did rest against di face a one defunct mantelpiece clock, an fram dis position him distraught eyes did a stare dung at Daisy, weh did a sidong, fraitn but graceful, pan di edge a one stiff chair.

“Wi don meet before,” Gatsby muttered. Him eyes dem glance momentarily at mi, an him lips dem part wid an abortive attempt at a laugh. Luckily di clock tek dis moment fi tilt dangerously at di pressure a him head, whereupon him turn an ketch it wid trembling fingers, an set it back inna place. Den him sidong, rigidly, him elbow pan di arm a di sofa an him chin inna him han.

“Mi sorry bout di clock,” him seh.

Mi own face did now assume a deep tropical burn. Mi couldn't muster up a single commonplace out a di thousand inna mi head.

“A wan uol klak,” mi tel dem iidiatli.

Mi tink wi all did believe fi a moment seh it did smash inna pieces pan di floor.

“Wi neva meet fi nuff years,” Daisy seh, har voice as matter-of-fact as it coulda eva be.

“Five years next November.”

Di automatic quality a Gatsby's ansa set wi all back at least anodda minute. Mi did have dem both pan dem foot wid di desperate suggestion seh dem help mi mek tea inna di kitchen wen di demoniac Finn bring it in pan a tray.

Amid di welcome confusion a cups an cakes a certain physical decency establish itself. Gatsby get himself inna a shadow an, while Daisy an mi did a talk, him look concientiously fram one to di ada a wi wid tense, unhappy eyes. However, as calmness wasn't an end in itself, mi mek an excuse at di first possible moment, an get to mi feet.

“Weh yuh a go?” Gatsby demand in immediate alarm.

“Mi ago come back.”

“Mi haffi talk to yuh bout someting before yuh go.”

Im fala mi wildly inna di kitchen, close di door, an whisper: “Oh, God!” inna a miserable way.

“Weh di mata?”

“Dis a one terrible mistake,” him seh, a shake him head fram side to side, “a terrible, terrible mistake.”

“Yu jos shiem, dat a aal,” an laki mi ad se: “Daisy shiem tu.”

“She embarrassed?” him repeated incredulously.

“Just as much as yuh is.”

“No chat so loud.”

“Yu a gwaan laik wan likl bwai,” mi brok out impatientli. “No onggl dat, bot yu ruul. Daisy a sidong iin de aal a im wan.”

Im raise im han fi stop mi wod dem, look pan mi wid unforgettable reproach, an, opening di door cautiously, go back inna di ada room.

Mi walk out di back way—jus like Gatsby did do wen him did mek him nervous circuit a di house half an hour before—an run fi a huge black knotted tree, whose massed leaves mek a fabric against di rain. Once more it did a pour, an mi irregular lawn, well-shaved by Gatsby's gardener, abounded inna small muddy swamps an prehistoric marshes. Notn neva de fi luk pan fram anda di chrii ekssep Gatsby big big ous, so mi luk pan it, laik Kant a im choch stiipl, fi aaf a owa. A brewer did build it early inna di “period” craze, a decade before, an deh did have a story seh him did agree fi pay five years taxes pan all di neighboring cottages dem if di owna dem woulda have dem roofs datched wid straw. Miebi dem refusal tek di heart outa him plan fi Found a Family—im go inna wan immediate decline. Him pickney dem sell him house wid di black wreath weh still deh pan di door. Americans, while dem did willing, even eager, fi be serfs, dem always been obstinate bout being peasantry.

Afta aaf owa, di son shain agen, an di grosa otomobil ron roun Gatsby jraiv wid di raw matiirial fi im sorvant dem dina—mi did fiil shuor se im naa go iit no spuonful. A maid staat open di upper windows a him house, appear momentarily inna each, an, leaning fram di big central bay, spat meditatively inna di garden. A did time mi go back. While di rain did a gwaan it did look like di murmur a dem voices, a rise an a swelling likkle now an den wid gusts a emotion. But inna di new silence mi feel seh silence did fall widin di house too.

Mi go in—afta mi mek every possible noise inna di kitchen, short a pushing ova di stove—but mi nuh believe dem hear a sound. Dem did a sidong a di tuu en a di sofa, a luk pan dem wan aneda laik se som kweschan did aks, ar did de ina di ier, an evri vestige a embarassment did gaan. Daisy face did smear wid tears, an wen mi come in she jump up an staat wipe at it wid har handkerchief before a mirror. But a did a change inna Gatsby weh did simply confounding. Im did a glow; widout a word or a gesture a exultation a new well-being radiate fram him an fill di likkle room.

“Oh, hello, old sport,” him seh, as if him neva si mi fi years. Mi did tink fi a moment seh him did a go shake hands.

“It stap rien.”

“It?” Wen him realize weh mi did a talk bout, seh a did twinkle-bells a sunshine inna di room, him smile like a weather man, like a ecstatic patron a recurrent light, an repeat di news to Daisy. “Wa yu tingk bout dat, rien tap faal.”

“Mi glad, Jay.” Har throat, full a aching, grieving beauty, tell only bout har unexpected joy.

“Mi waahn yu an Daisy fi kom uova a mi yaad,” im se, “mi wuda laik fi shuo ar roun.”

“Yuh sure yuh waan mi fi come?”

“Absolutely, old sport.”

Daisy go opstairs fi wash har face—too late mi did tink wid humiliation a mi towels—while Gatsby an mi did a wait pan di lawn.

“Mi ous luk gud, iihn?” him demand. “Si how di whole front a it ketch di light.”

Mi agree seh it did splendid.

"Yea." Him eyes dem did a go ova it, every arched door an square tower. “It tek mi jus three years fi earn di money weh buy it.”

“Mi did tink yuh did inherit yuh money.”

“Mi did, old sport,” him seh automatically, “but mi lose most a it inna di big panic—di panic a di war.”

Mi tink him hardly did know weh him did a seh, cause wen mi ask him weh business him did inna him ansa: “A mi affair dat,” before him realize seh a neva an appropriate reply.

“Oh, mi been inna several tings,” him correct himself. “Mi did inna di drug business an den mi did inna di oil business. But mi nuh deh inna non a dem now.” Im look pon mi wid more attention. “Yuh mean yuh did a tink bout weh mi did a propose di neda nait?”

Bifuo mi kuda ansa, Daisy kom outa di ous an tuu ruod a braas boton pan ar jres did a shain inna di sonlait.

“Dat big place deh deh ?” shi baal out a point.

“Yuh like it?”

“Mi love it, but mi nuh see how yuh live deh all alone.”

“Mi keep it always full a interesting people, night an day. People weh do interesting tings. Celebrated people.”

Insted a tek di shortcut along di Sound we go dung to di road an enter by di big postern. Wid enchanting murmurs Daisy admire dis aspect or dat a di feudal silhouette gens di sky, admire di gardens, di sparkling odor a jonquils an di frothy odor a hawthorn an plum blossoms an di pale gold odor a kiss-me-at-the-gate. It was strange fi reach di marble steps an find no stir a bright dresses in an out di door, an hear no sound but bird voices inna di trees.

An inside, as we did a wander thru Marie Antoinette music-rooms an Restoration Salons, mi did feel seh deh did have guests weh did a hide behind every couch an table, unda order fi be breathlessly silent until wi did pass through. As Gatsby did a close di door a “di Merton College Library” mi coulda did sworn seh mi hear di owl-eyed man break inna ghostly laughter.

Wi go upstairs, chruu period bedrooms swathed inna rose an lavender silk an vivid wid new flowers, chruu dressing-rooms an poolrooms, an bathrooms wid sunken baths—intruding into one chamber weh a disheveled man inna pajamas did a do liver exercises pan di floor. A did Mr. Klipspringer, di “boarder.” Mi did si him a waak hungry bout di beach da maanin de. Finally wi come to Gatsby's own apartment, a bedroom an a bath, an a Adam's study, weh we did a sit dung an drink a glass a some Chartreuse weh him did tek fram a cupboard inna di wall.

Im neva did stop look pon Daisy, an mi tink im did revalue everyting inna im yaad according to di measure a response it did a draw fram har well-loved eyes. Somtaim tu, im did a luk roun pan im tingz dem ina wan dazed wie, laik se inna ar akchual an astounding prezans non a it neva riil no muor. Once him nearly topple dung a flight a stairs.

Him bedroom was di simplest room a all—except weh di dresser did garnish wid a toilet set a pure dull gold. Daisy tek di brush wid delight, an smooth har hair, weh Gatsby sidong an shade him eyes an staat laugh.

“A di funniest ting, old sport,” him seh hilariously. “Mi cyan—Wen mi try fi—”

Im did pass visiblely chruu two states an did a go inna a third. Afta him embarrassment an him unreasoning joy him did consume wid wonder at har presence. Im did full a di idea so lang, im did a dream it rait chuu tu di en, im did a wiet wid im teet dem set, so fi se it, a wan inconceivable pitch a intensity. Now, inna di reaction, him did a run dung like a over-wound clock.

Recovering himself inna a minute him open fi wi two hulking patent cabinets weh did hold him massed suits an dressing-gowns an ties, an him shirts, pailed like bricks inna stacks a dozen high.

“Mi have a man inna England weh buy mi clothes. Him sen ova a selection a tings at di beginning a each season, spring an fall.”

Im tek out a pile a shirt an staat throw dem, one by one, before wi, shirt a sheer linen an thick silk an fine flannel, weh did lose dem folds as dem did a fall an cover di table inna many-coloured disarray. While we did a admire him bring more an di soft rich heap mount higher—shirts wid stripes an scrolls an plaids inna coral an apple-green an lavender an faint orange, wid monograms a indian blue. Suddenly, wid a strained sound, Daisy ben har head inna di shirts an staat cry stormily.

“Dem a such beautiful shirts,” she sob, har voice muffled inna di thick folds. “It mek mi sad cause mi neva si such—such beautiful shirts before.”

Afta di house, wi did fi si di grounds an di swimming pool, an di hydroplane, an di midsummer flowers—but outside Gatsby's window it staat rain agen, so wi did a stan op inna a row a look pon di corrugated surface a di Sound.

“If it neva fi di mist wi coulda see yuh yaad across di bay,” seh Gatsby. “Yuh always have a green light weh burn all night at di end a yuh dock.”

Daisy put har arm chruu him abruptly, but him did look like him did absorb inna weh him did jus seh. Possibly it did come to him seh di colossal significance a dat deh light did now vanish fi eva. Compared to di great distance weh did separate him fram Daisy it did look very near to har, almost touching har. It did look as close as a star to di moon. Now it was again a green light pan a dock. Him count a enchanted objects did diminish by one.

Mi staat walk bout di room, a examine various indefinite objects inna di half darkness. A big photograph a one elderly man inna yachting costume attract mi, hang pan di wall ova him desk.

“A who dis?”

“Dat? Dat a Mr. Dan Cody, uol sport.”

Di name did sound faintly familiar.

“Im ded nou.Im yuus tu bi mi bes fren ierz ago.”

Deh did have a likkle picture a Gatsby, also inna yachting costume, pan di bureau—Gatsby wid him head throw back defiantly—tek apparently wen him was bout eighteen.

“Mi adore it,” Daisy seh. “Di pompadour! Yuh neva tell mi seh yuh have a pompadour—or a yacht.”

“Look pon dis,” Gatsby seh quickly. “Yahso a lot a clippings—bout yuh.”

Dem did a stan up side by side a examine it. Mi did a go aks fi si di rubi dem wen di fuon ring, an Gatsby tek op di risiiva.

“Yes... Well, mi cyan talk now... Mi cyan talk now, old sport... Mi seh a likkle town... Him mus know weh a likkle town is... Well, him nuh use to wi if Detroit a him idea a likkle town...”

Im ring off.

“Come yah quick !” Daisy baal out pan di window.

Di rien did stil a faal, bot di daaknis did don paat inna di wes, an a did wan pink an guol billow a foamy kloud uova di sii.

“Luk pan dat,” shi wispa, an den afta a moment: “Mi wuda laik fi jos get wan a dem pink kloud de an put yu iina it an push yu roun.”

Mi try fi go den, but dem neva waan hear bout it; perhaps mi presence mek dem feel more satisfactorily alone.

“Mi know weh wi a go do,” Gatsby seh, “wi a go mek Klipspringer play di piano.”

Im go outa di ruum an kaal “Ewing!” an come back inna a few minutes wid an embarrassed, slightly worn young man, wid shell-rimmed glasses an scanty blond hair. Im did now decently clothed inna a “sport shirt,” open at di neck, sneakers, an duck trousers a one nebulous hue.

“Wi did interrupt yuh exercise?” Daisy aks politely.

“Mi did a sleep,” Mr. Klipspringer baal out, inna a spasm a embarrassment. “Dat mean, mi did a sleep. Den mi get up...”

“Klipspringer play di piano,” seh Gatsby, cutting him off. “Yuh nuh, Ewing, old sport?”

“Mi no play well. Mi no—hardly play at all. Mi all out a prac—”

“Wi a go dungstairs,” Gatsby interrupted. Im flip a switch. Di grey windows dem disappear as di house did a glow full a light.

Inna di music-room Gatsby turn on a solitary lamp beside di piano. Im lait Daisy cigarette fram a trembling match, an sidong wid har pan a couch far across di room, weh neva have no light lef weh di gleaming floor did a bounce inna fram di hall.

Wen Klipspringer did play “The Love Nest” him turn roun pan di bench an search unhappily fi Gatsby inna di gloom.

“Mi all out a practice, yuh see. Mi did tell yuh seh mi couldn't play. Mi all out a prac—”

“No talk so much, old sport,” Gatsby command. "Ramp!"

“Ina di maanin,
Inna di evening,
Ain't we got fun—”

Outside di wind did loud an a did a likl flow a thunder along di Sound. All di lights dem did a go on inna West Egg now; di electric trains, man-carrying, did a plung home thru di rain fram New York. A did di hour a one profound human change, an excitement did a generate pan di air.

“Wan ting shuor an notn no shuor
Di rich get rich an di puor get—pikni.
Inna di meantime,
In between time—”

As mi go ova fi seh goodbye mi si seh di expression a bewilderment did come back inna Gatsby face, as if a faint doubt did occur to him as to di quality a him present happiness. Almost five years! It mosi did have somes even dat deh aftanoon wen Daisy did a fall short a him dreams—not chruu har own fault, but because a di colossal vitality a him illusion. It did gaan beyond har, beyond everyting. Him did throw himself inna it wid a creative passion, adding to it all di time, a deck it out wid every bright feather weh did a drift him way. No amount a fire or freshness can challenge weh a man can store up inna him ghostly heart.

As mi watch him him adjust himself likkle, visibly. Him han tek hold a hars, an as she seh someting low inna him ear him turn toward har wid a rush a emotion. Mi tink dat deh voice did a hold him most, wid it fluctuating, feverish warmth, cause it couldn't be over-dreamed—dat voice was a deathless song.

Dem did forget mi, but Daisy glance up an hold out har han; Gatsby neva know mi now at all. Mi look once more at dem an dem look back at mi, remotely, possessed by intense life. Den mi go outa di room an dong di marble step dem inna di rain, lef dem deh tugeda.

VI

Bout dis time a ambitious young reporter fram New York arrive one morning a Gatsby's door an ask him if him have anyting fi seh.

“Anyting fi seh bout weh?” Gatsby aks politely.

“Why—any statement fi gi out.”

It transpired afta a confused five minutes dat di man did hear Gatsby's name roun him office inna a connection weh him either wouldn't reveal or didn't fully understand. Dis was him day off an wid laudable initiative him did hurry out “fi see.”

A did a random shot, an yet di reporter's instinct did right. Gatsby's notoriety, spread bout by di hundreds who did accept him hospitality an so become authorities pan him past, did increase all summer until him fall jus short a being news. Contemporary legends such as di “underground pipeline to Canada” attached demself to him, an deh did have one persistent story dat him neva live inna a house at all, but inna a boat weh look like a house an dem did a move secretly up an dung di Long Island shore. Jus why dem inventions yah was a source a satisfaction to James Gatz a North Dakota, a nuh easy fi seh.

James Gatz—dat was really, or at least legally, him name. Im did change it at di age a seventeen an at di specific moment weh im did si di beginning a im career—wen im si Dan Cody's yacht drop anchor ova di most insidious flat pan Lake Superior. A did James Gatz weh did a loaf along di beach dat deh aftanuun inna a torn green jersey an a pair a canvas pants, but a did Jay Gatsby weh did a borrow a rowboat, pull out to di Tuolomee , an tell Cody seh a wind might ketch him an break him up inna half an hour.

Mi suppose him did have di name ready fi a long time, even den. Him parents dem was shiftless an unsuccessful farm people—him imagination neva really accept dem as him parents at all. Di truth was dat Jay Gatsby of West Egg, Long Island, sprang fram him Platonic conception a himself. Im was a son a God—a phrase weh, if it mean anyting, mean jus dat—an im mus be bout Him Fada business, di service a one vast, vulgar, an meretricious beauty. So him invent jus di sort a Jay Gatsby weh a seventeen-year-old bwoy woulda be likely fi invent, an to dis conception him did faithful to di end.

Fi ova a year him did a beat him way along di south shore a Lake Superior as a clam-digger an a salmon-fisher or inna any ada capacity weh bring him food an bed. Him brown, hardening body did live naturally chruu di half-fierce, half-lazy work a di bracing days. Him did know women early, an since dem spoil him him did become contemptuous a dem, a young virgins cause dem neva ignorant, a di others cause dem did hysterical bout tings weh inna him overwhelming self-absorption him tek fi granted.

But him heart did inna a constant, turbulent riot. Di most grotesque an fantastic conceits haunted him inna him bed at night. A universe a ineffable gaudiness spun itself out inna him brain while di clock did a tick pan di washstand an di moon did a soak wid wet light him tangled clothes pan di floor. Each night him add to di pattern a him fancies until drowsiness close dung upon some vivid scene wid an oblivious embrace. Fi a while dem reveries deh did provide an outlet fi him imagination; dem was a satisfactory hint a di unreality a reality, a promise dat di rock a di worl did found securely pan a fairy's wing.

Wan instinct toward him future glory did lead him, some months before, to di likkle Lutheran College a St. Olaf's inna southern Minnesota. Im stay deh fi two weeks, dismayed at it ferocious indifference to di drums a im destiny, to destiny itself, an despising di janitor's work wid which im did a go pay im way through. Den him drift back to Lake Superior, an him did still a look fi someting fi do pan di day wen Dan Cody's yacht did drop anchor inna di shallows alongshore.

Cody was fifty years old den, a product a di Nevada silver fields, a di Yukon, a every rush fi metal since seventy-five. Di transactions in Montana copper weh mek him many times a millionaire find him physically robust but pan di verge a soft-mindedness, an, suspecting dis, an infinite numba a women did a try separate him fram him money. Di none too savory ramifications by which Ella Kaye, di newspaper woman, did play Madame de Maintenon to him weakness an sen him to sea inna a yacht, was common property a di turgid journalism inna 1902.
