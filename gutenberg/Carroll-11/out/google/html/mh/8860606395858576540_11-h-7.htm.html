JAPTER VII.
Juon Mad Tea-Party

Ekar wor juon tebōl ekar bed iumin juon wijki iman mweo, im March Hare im Hatter ekar idaak tii ie: juon Dormouse ekar jijet ibwiljier, kiki, im ruo ro jet ekar kajerbale einwot juon jikin kiki, likit peier ion, im konono ion bōran. “Elukkun jab emman ñan Dormouse eo,” Alice ear lemnok; “ak, ke ej kiki, ij lemnok ejjab inebata.”

Tebōl eo ekar juon eo elap, ak jilu rar aolep kobatok ilo juon kona ie: “Ejjelok jikin! Ejelok jikin!” rar lamij ke rar lo an Alice itok. “ Elap jikin!” Alice ekar ba ilo an illu, innem ekar jijet ilo juon jea eo elap ilo juon jemlokin tebōl eo.

“Irak jidik wain,” March Hare eo ear ba ilo juon ainikien ej kōketak.

Alice ear kalimjōk tebōl eo, ak ejelok men ion ijellokin wōt tii. “Ijjab lo jabdewōt wain,” liin eaar ba.

“Ejjelok,” March Hare eo ear ba.

“Innem ear jab lukkun emman am lewoj,” Alice ear ba ilo an illu.

“Ekar jab lukkun emman am kar jijet im jab kir eok,” March Hare eo ear ba.

“Ikar jab jelā bwe ej tebōl eo am ,” Alice ekar ba; “ej kōmmane ñan elaplok jen jilu.”

“Bōro eo am ekōnaan mōttan,” Rikōmman Hat eo eaar ba. Ekar kalimjōk Alice iumwin jet ien kin an lukkun kōnan jelā, im ekar jinoin an konono.

“Kwoj aikuj katak ñan jab kōmman am make nan ko,” Alice ear ba ilo an kajoor; “elukkun nana.”

Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear lukkun kapeļļo k mejen ke ear roñ men in; ak men eo ear ba ej, “Etke juon raven ej einwot juon tebōl in jeje?”

“Itok, jenaaj ikkure kiiō!” Alice ear lemnok. “Ij mõnõnõ bwe rar jino kajitõk riddle ko.—Ij tõmak imaroñ in lemnak kake,” ear kobaiktok ilo ainikien elap.

“Kwoj melele ke bwe kwoj lemnok kwomaroñ lo uak eo ñan e?” March Hare eo ear ba.

“Emool,” Alice eaar ba.

“Innem kwoj aikuj ba ta eo kwoj melele kake,” March Hare eo ekar wōnmanlok.

“Ij,” Alice ekar uwaak ilo mōkaj; “eļaptata—eļaptata ij meļeļe ta eo ij ba—ej ejja men eo wōt, kwojeļā.”

“Ejjab juon wōt men!” rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba. “Kwomaroñ ba bwe ‘Ij lo ta eo ij mõñã’ ej einlok wõt ‘Ij mõñã ta eo ij loe’!”

“Kwomaroñ ba,” March Hare eo ear kobaiktok, “bwe 'Ij iakwe ta eo ij bōk' ej einwot 'Ij bōk ta eo ij bōk'!"

“Kwomaroñ ba,” Dormouse eo ear kobaiktok, eo ear einwõt ej konono ilo an kiki, “bwe ‘Ij menono ñe ij kiki’ ej einlok wõt ‘Ij kiki ñe ij menono’!”

“ Ej ejja men in wot ippam,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba, im ijin bwebwenato eo ear bōjrak, im kumi eo ear jijet im jab konono iumin juon minit, ke Alice ear lemnok kin aolep men ko ear maroñ kememej kin raven ko im tebōl in jeje ko, eo ear jab lap.

Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear moktata ñan rupe ainikien eo. “Ta raan in allōñ in?” eaar ba, oktakļo̧k n̄an Alice: eaar bōk watch eo an jān pāāk eo an, im kar kalimjōk e ilo an jab inepata, kōm̧akūtkūt e iien otemjej, im jibwe n̄an lo̧jiln̄in.

Alice ear lemnok jidik, innem ba “Kein kajilu.”

“Ruo raan bōd!” rikōmman hat eo ear mōk. “Ikar jiron yuk bwe butter ejamin jejjet ñan jerbal ko!” ear kobaiktok im kalimjōk March Hare eo ilo an illu.

“Ekar butter eo emman tata ,” March Hare eo ekar uwaak ilo ettā.

“Aet, ak jet mōttan ko rekar deloñ bareinwõt,” Ri Hatter eo ear inebata: “kwojjab aikuj kar likite ippāñ bread-knife eo.”

March Hare eo ear bōk watch eo im kalimjeke ilo an buromõj: innem ear likit e ilo kap in tii eo an, im bar kalimjeke: ak ear jab maroñ in lemnok kin bar juon men emmanlok jen an ba jen nan eo an moktata, “Ekar butter eo emman tata , kwojelā ke.”

Alice ekar kalimjōk ion bōran kin an kōnan jelā. “Ej juon wa eo emman!” ej ba. “Ej kwalok raan in allōñ eo, im ejjab kwalok ta awa eo ej!”

“Etke ej aikuj in?” rikōmman hat eo ekar mōñā. “Wat eo am ej ke kwalok ñan yuk ta yio in?”

“Emool jaab,” Alice ear uwaak ilo an mōkaj: “ak ej kinke ej bed wot ilo ejja yio eo wot iumin juon ien eo elap ibben don.”

“Eo ej baj einwot eo ippa ,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba.

Alice ear eñjake an lukkun bwilōñ, naan eo an The Hatter ear einwõt ejjelok melele ie, ak ear lukkun kajin English. “Ijjab lukkun melele kin yuk,” lio eo eaar ba, ilo joij joñan an maroñ.

“Dormouse eo ej bar kiki,” Hatter eo ear ba, im ear lutōk jidik tii eo ebwil ioon loñin.

Dormouse eo ear mōkaj an jab kijenmij, im ba, ilo an jab kapeļļo k mejen, “Emool, emol; ta eo ikar kōnaan ba make.”

“Kwar ke melele kin riddle eo?” Hatter eo ear ba, im oktaklok ñan Alice bar juon alen.

“Jaab, ij jolok,” Alice ekar uwaak: “ta uwaak eo?”

“Ejjelok aō jella,” rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba.

“Ak io,” March Hare eo ear ba.

Alice ear mōk ilo an mōk. “Ij lemnok kwomaroñ kōmmane juon men emmanlok ilo ien eo,” lio eo eaar ba, “jen am jolok ien eo ilo am kajitõk riddle ko ejelok uak ko ie.”

“Eļan̄n̄e kwaar jeļā Iien āinwōt aō jeļā,” Rikōm̧akoko eo eaar ba, “kwojjab naaj kar kōnono kōn joļo̧ke . Ej e .”

“Ijjab jelā ta meļeļein,” Alice eaar ba.

“Emool kwojjab!” rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba, im ukōt bōran ilo an kōjekdoon. “Imaroñ ba bwe kwar jab konono ibben Time!”

“Bōlen jaab,” Alice ear uwaak ilo kōjparok: “ak ijelā bwe ij aikuj in bōk ien ñe ij katak al.”

“A! men in ej melelen,” Rikōmman Hat eo ear ba. “Ejjab naaj jutak in m̧anm̧an. Kiiō, eļan̄n̄e kwaar wōr wōt am kōttōpar em̧m̧an ippān, enaaj kar kōm̧m̧ane jabdewōt men ko kwoj kōņaan kōn awa eo. N̄an waanjon̄ak, jen ba bwe ej ļalem awa jibbon̄, iien eo wōt n̄an jino katak ko: kwonaaj kar aikuj wōt m̧ōkaj in kōmeļeļeik Iien, im awa eo ej m̧ōkaj an m̧akūtkūt!

("Ij baj kōnaan bwe en kar," March Hare eo ear ba ñan e make ilo an ettōñ dikdik.)

“Enaaj kar lukkun emman,” Alice eaar ba ilo an lemnok: “ak innem—Ijjab aikuj in kwuli kake, kwojelā ke.”

“Ejjab ilo jinoin, bōlen,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba: “ak kwomaroñ kar kōmmane ñan jimettan-juon awa joñan wōt am kōnaan.”

“Eñin ke wāween eo kwoj kōmmane?” Alice ekar kajitok.

Ri kōmman nuknuk eo ear mōkaj an mōkaj an buromõj. Jab io! ekar uwaak. “Kōm kar akwāāl ilo Māe eo ļo̧k—m̧okta jān an kar m̧ōjņo̧, kwojeļā—” (ej kwaļo̧k kōn tea spoon eo an n̄an March Hare eo,) “—eaar ilo iien kōjan̄jan̄ eo eļap kar kōm̧m̧ane jān Queen of Hearts, im iaar aikuj in al

'Twinkle, twinkle, bat jidikdik!
Ewi joñan aō lemnok kin ta eo kwoj bed ie!'

Kwojelā ke al eo, bōlen?”

“Ikar roñ juon men einwot in,” Alice ekar ba.

“Ej wōnmanlok wōt, kwojelā,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear wōnmanlok, “ilo wewein in:—

'Ilōñ in lal in kwoj ettōr,
Einwot juon tea-tray ilo mejatoto.
Twinkle, twinkle—'”

Ilo ijin Dormouse eo ear mōkaj an mōkaj, im jino al ilo an kiki “ Twinkle, twinkle, twinkle, twinkle —” im ear wōnmanlok wōt bwe rar aikuj in jibwe bwe en bōjrak.

“Emmon, ikar jab kadedelok eon eo moktata,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ekar ba, “ke Kwin eo ekar ettōr im lamõj, 'Ej mõn ien eo! Jolok bõran!'”

“Ewi joñan an nana!” Alice ekar lamij.

“Im jen ien eo,” Hatter eo ear wōnmanlok ilo juon ainikien būromõj, “ejjab kōmmane juon men ij kajjitōk kake! Ej iien otemjej jiljino awa kiiō.”

Juon ļōmņak em̧m̧an eaar itok ilo bōran Alice. “Eñin ke unin an lõñ men in tii ko rej likiti ijin?” ej kajitōk.

“Aet, eñin,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba ilo an mōk: “ej iien otemjej ien idaak, im ejelok ad ien ñan kwale men ko ikõtan ien ko.”

“Innem kwoj wōnmanlok wōt im etal, ij lemnok?” Alice eaar ba.

“Emool,” Rikōmmane Hat eo eaar ba: “āinwōt an men ko kōjerbali.”

“Ak ta eo ej walok ñe kwoj bar itok ñan jinoin?” Alice ear kajjitōk.

“Jen ba bwe jen ukōt unin kōnono eo,” March Hare eo ear bōjrak, im mōñā. “Ij jino mōk kin men in. Ij vote bwe jiroñ eo ej kwalok juon bwebwenato ñan kij.”

“Ij mijak bwe ijjab jelā juon,” Alice eaar ba, eļap an inepata kōn kōttōpar eo.

“Innem Dormouse eo enaj!” erro jimor jañ. “Kwōn ruj, Dormouse!” Im rar dāpij e ilo turin ruo ilo juon wōt ien.

Dormouse eo ear jidik kin jidik kapelok mejen. “Ikar jab kiki,” ekar ba ilo juon ainikien eo ejjab kajoor: “Ikar roñ aolep nan ko kom kar ba.”

“Kwōn kwalok juon bwebwenato ñan kim!” March Hare eo ear ba.

“Aet, jouj im kōmmane!” Alice ear akwelap.

“Im mōkaj in kōmmane,” Hatter eo ear kobaiktok, “ak kwonaj bar kiki mokta jen an dedelok.”

“Juon ien ekar wor jilu leddik ro jatū,” Dormouse eo ekar jino ilo an lukkun mōkaj; “im etaer kar Elsie, Lacie, im Tillie; im raar jokwe ilo tōrerein juon aebōj—”

Ta eo rar mour kake? Alice eaar ba, eo eaar iien otemjej bōk juon itok limo eļap ilo kajjitōk ko kōn m̧ōn̄ā im idaak.

“Rekar mour kin treacle,” Dormouse eo ekar ba, elikin an kar lemnok juon ak ruo minit.

“Rejjab maroñ kar kōmmane men in, kwojelā ke,” Alice ekar ba ilo jidimkij; “ren kar nañinmij.”

“Āindein raar,” Dormouse eo eaar ba; “ lukkun nañinmij.”

Alice ear kajeoñ in lemnok ñan e make kin ta eo enaj kar einwõt juõn wãwen mour eo ekabwilõñlõñ, ak ear lukkun kabwilõñ e, innem ear wõnmanlok im ba: “Ak etke rar jokwe ilo tu-kadu in juõn aebõj?”

“Bōk bar jet tii,” March Hare eo ear ba ñan Alice, ilo an lukkun mol.

“Ejjelok men ippa kiõ,” Alice ear uak ilo juon ainikien inebata, “kin menin ijjab maroñ in bōk elaplok.”

“Kwoj melele bwe kwojjab maroñ bōk diiklok ,” ekar ba Ri Hatter eo: “elap an bin ñan bōk elaplok jen ejelok.”

“Ejjelok ear kajitok lemnok eo am ,” Alice ear ba.

“Wōn eo ej kōmman nan in kōmeļeļe ko an make kiiō?” rikōjan̄jan̄ eo eaar kajjitōk ilo an anjo̧.

Alice ear jab lukkun jelā ta eo en ba ñan men in: innem ear jipan e make ñan idaak tii im bread-im-butter, innem ear oktaklok ñan Dormouse eo, im bar bar kajjitōk. “Etke rar jokwe ilo turin juon aebōj?”

Dormouse eo ear bar bōk juon ak ruo minit ñan an lemnok kake, innem ear ba, “Ekar juon treacle-well.”

“Ejjelok men rot in!” Alice ekar jino an illu, ak Hatter eo im March Hare eo ekar ba “Sh! sh!” im Dormouse eo ear ba, “Elañe kwojjab maroñ in jouj, emmanlok am kadedelok bwebwenato eo ñan kwe make.”

Jaab, jouj im wōnmanlok wōt! Alice ear ba ilo ettā; “Ijjab bar bōbrae. Ij peran in ba bwe emaron wor juon .”

“Juon, emool!” Dormouse eo ear ba ilo an illu. Ak, ear errā bwe en wōnmanlok wōt. “Im innem leddik rein jilu—raar katak in je, kwojelā—”

“Ta eo rar je?” Alice ear ba, ilo an meloklok kallimur eo an.

“Treacle,” Dormouse eo ear ba, ilo an jab lemnok kin ien in.

“Ikõnan juon kap erreo,” rikōmman nuknuk eo ear bōjrak: “jen aolep etal ñan juon jikin.”

Ear etal wōt ke ej konono, im Dormouse eo ear lor e: March Hare ear etal ñan jikin Dormouse eo, im Alice ear jab kōnan bōk jikin March Hare eo. Hatter eo ej make wōt eo ear bōk tokjen jen oktak eo: im Alice ear lukkun nana jen mokta, ke March Hare eo ear baj jolok milk-jug eo ilo pileij eo an.

Alice ear jab kōnan bar kainebataik Dormouse eo, innem ear jino ilo an lukkun lale: “Ak ijjab melele. Ia eo rar bōk treacle eo jen e?”

“Kwomaroñ bōk den jen juon aebōj in dren,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba; “innem ij lemnok kwomaroñ in bōk treacle jen juon treacle-well—eh, bwebwe?”

“Ak rar bed ilo aebōj eo,” Alice ear ba ñan Dormouse eo, im jab kōnan kile nan in eliktata.

“Emool rar,” Dormouse eo ear ba; “—emman ilo.”

Uwaak in ear lukkun kainebataik Alice eo ejeramel, bwe ear kōtlok bwe Dormouse eo en etal wōt ilo an jab bōjrak.

“Rekar katak in je,” Dormouse eo ekar wōnmanlok, ej mōñā im mōñā mejen, bwe ekar lukkun kiki; “im rar je aolep kain men ko—aolep men ko rej jino kin juon M—”

“Etke kin juon M?” Alice eaar ba.

"Etke jab?" March Hare eo ear ba.

Alice ear jab konono.

Dormouse eo ear kilōk mejen ilo ien in, im ear etal im kiki; ak, ke kar jibwe jen Hatter eo, ear bar ruj kin jidik lamõj, im wõnmanlok: “—eo ej jino kin juon M, einwõt mouse-traps, im alliñ eo, im kememej, im muchness—kwojelā ke kwoj ba men ko rej “much of a muchness”—kwoj ke kar lo kain men rot in einwõt juõn pija in elaplok?”

“Emool, kiõ kwoj kajitõk ippa,” Alice ear ba, elap an inebata, “Ijjab lemnok—”

“Innem kwon jab konono,” Rikōmman nuknuk eo ear ba.

M̧ōttan in nana in eaar ļapļo̧k jān an Alice maron̄ in kijenmej: eaar jutak ilo an lukkuun inepata, im etal; Dormouse eo ear kiki ilo ien eo wot, im ejelok ian ro jet ear kile an etal, meñe ear reiliklok juon ak ruo alen, jimettan an kejatrikrik bwe renaj kar kir e: ien eo eliktata ear lo ir, rar kajeoñ in likit Dormouse eo ilo teapot eo.

“Ilo jabdewōt jekjek Ijjamin naaj bar etal ñan ijo !” Alice eaar ba ke eaar etal ilo iaļ eo an ilo wōjke eo. “Ej juon tea-party eo elaptata an bwebwe io kar bed ie ilo aolepen mour eo aõ!”

Ke ej ba men in, ej lo bwe juon ian wijki ko ewōr juon an kōjām ej tellok ñan lowaan. “Elukkuun in kabwilōñlōñ!” lio ekar lemnok. “Ak aolep men rej kabwilōñlōñ rainin. Ij lemnok bwe in maroñ in deloñ ilo juon ien.” Im lio ear deḷọñ.

Bar juon alen ear lo e make ilo jikin eo elap, im epaak ñan tebōl eo edik. “Kiō, enaj emmanlok aõ jerbal ilo ien in,” lio ear ba ñan e make, im jino ilo an bōk ki eo edik gold, im kapelok kejem eo ej tellok ñan jikin kallib eo. Innem ekar etal ñan jerbal im mōñā mushroom eo (ekar likit juon mõttan ilo pocket eo an) mae ien eo ekar juon ne utiejen: innem ekar etetal ilo ial eo edik: innem —ekar lo e make ilo jikin kallib eo eaiboojoj, ibwiljin jikin kallib ko remera im fountain ko rebin.
