CHAPTER V.
Advice fram a Caterpillar

Di Caterpillar an Alice look pan each ada fi some time inna silence: at last di Caterpillar tek di hookah outa it mout, an address har inna a languid, sleepy voice.

“A who yuh is? ” di Caterpillar seh.

Dis neva did a encouraging opening fi a conversation. Alice ansa, radda shyly, “Mi—mi hardly know, sir, jus at present—at least mi know who mi was wen mi get up dis morning, but mi tink mi mosi did change several times since den.”

“Weh yuh mean wid dat?” di Caterpillar seh sternly. “Explain yuhself!”

“Mi kyaahn explien miself , mi fried, sa,” Alis se, “kaaz mi a no miself, yu si.”

“Mi nuh see,” di Caterpillar seh.

“Mi fried mi kyaahn put it muo klier,” Alice ansa veri poliis, “kaa mi kyaahn andastan it miself fi staat wid; an fi bi so moch difrant saiz ina wan die a veri kanfiujan.”

“A no so,” di Caterpillar seh.

“Well, perhaps yuh neva find it so yet,” seh Alice; “but wen yuh haffi turn inna a chrysalis—yuh ago some day, yuh know—an den afta dat inna a butterfly, mi shuda tink yuh ago feel it a likkle queer, noh chruu?”

“Not a bit,” di Caterpillar seh.

“Well, perhaps yuh feelings maita be different,” seh Alice; “all mi know is, it woulda feel very queer to mi .”

"Yuh!" di Caterpillar seh contemptuously. "Hu yuh bi? "

Weh bring dem back again to di beginning a di conversation. Alice did feel a likkle irritated wen di Caterpillar did a mek such very short remarks, an she draw harself up an seh, very gravely, “Mi tink, yuh shuda tell mi who yuh is, first.”

"Wah mek?" di Caterpillar seh.

Yahso was anodda puzzling kweschan; an as Alice couldn't tink a no good reason, an as di Caterpillar did a look like him did deh inna a very unpleasant state a mind, she turn weh.

“Come back!” di Caterpillar call afta har. “Mi av sopm impuotant fi se!”

Dis did sound promising, certainly: Alice turn an come back again.

“Keep yuh temper,” di Caterpillar seh.

“A dat all?” seh Alice, a swallow dung har anger as well as she coulda.

“No,” di Caterpillar seh.

Alice did tink she woulda as well wait, as she neva have nutten else fi do, an perhaps afta all it might tell har someting worth hearing. Fi some minutes it puff weh widout speaking, but at last it unfold it arms, tek di hookah outa it mout again, an seh, “So yuh tink yuh change, yuh tink?”

“Mi fraid mi a, sa,” Alice seh; “Mi cyan memba tings as mi use—an mi nuh keep di same size fi ten minutes tugeda!”

“Chan't memba weh tings?” di Caterpillar seh.

“Well, mi did try fi seh “How doth di likkle busy bee,” but it all come different!” Alice ansa inna a very melancholy voice.

“Repeat, ' Yuh old, Fada William ,'” di Caterpillar seh.

Alice fold har han dem, an staat:—

“Yu uol, Faada Wiliam,” di yong man se,
“An yuh hair don get very white;
An yet yuh stan op pan yuh head aal di taim—
Yuh tink, at yuh age, it right?”

“Inna mi youth,” Fada William ansa im son,
“Mi did fraid it might injure di brain;
But, now dat mi perfectly sure mi nuh have none,
Why, mi do it again an again.”

“Yuh uol,” di youth seh, “as mi did menshan before,
An dem grow most uncommonly fat;
Yet yuh turn a back-somersault inna di door—
Pray, weh a di reason fi dat?”

“Inna mi youth,” di sage seh, as him shake him gray locks,
“Mi kip all a mi limb dem very supple
By di use a dis ointment—one shilling di box—
Mek mi sell yuh a couple?”

“Yu uol,” di yuut se, “an yu jakit dem tuu wiik
Fi anyting tougher dan suet;
Yet yuh finish di goose, wid di bones an di beak—
Pray, how yuh manage fi do it?”

“Wen mi did a likl bwai,” im faada se, “mi tek fala di Laa,
An mi did a argue each case wid mi wife;
An di muscular strength, weh it gi mi jaw,
It last di rest a mi life.”

“Yuh old,” di youth seh, “one woulda hardly suppose
Dat yuh eye did as steady as ever;
Yet yuh balance an eel pan di end a yuh nose—
Weh mek yuh so awfully clever?”

“Mi ansa three kweschan, an dat inof,”
Im faada seh; “no gi yuhself airs!
Yuh tink mi can lisn all day to dem deh ting deh?
Be off, or mi ago kick yuh dung stairs!”

“Dat a nuh seh right,” di Caterpillar seh.

“Not quite right, mi fraid,” seh Alice, timidly; “som a di wod dem get chienj.”

“It wrong fram beginning to end,” seh di Caterpillar decidedly, an deh did silence fi some minutes.

Di Caterpillar was di first fi taak.

“Weh size yuh waan be?” it ask.

“Oh, mi no particular as to size,” Alice hastily replied; “only one nuh like changing so often, yuh know.”

“Mi nuh know,” di Caterpillar seh.

Alice neva seh nutten: she neva get so much contradict inna har life before, an she did feel seh she did a lose har temper.

“Yuh content now?” di Caterpillar seh.

“Well, mi shuda like fi be a likkle larger, sir, if yuh wouldn't mind,” seh Alice: “three inches a such a wretched height fi be.”

“A wan very good height indeed!” seh di Caterpillar angryly, rearing itself upright as it did a talk (it did exactly three inches high).

“Bot mi no yuus tu it!” beg poor Alice inna a piteous tone. An shi tingk bout arself, “Mi wish di kricha dem wudn beks so iizi!”

“Yu wi get yuus tu it inna taim,” di Kaatapila se; an it put di hookah inna it mout an staat smoke agen.

Dis time Alice wait patiently until it choose fi speak again. Inna a minute or two di Caterpillar tek di hookah outa it mout an yawn once or twice, an shake itself. Den it get dung off di mushroom, an crawl weh inna di grass, merely remarking as it go, “One side ago mek yuh grow taller, an di other side ago mek yuh grow shorter.”

“Wan side a weh? Di ada side a weh? ” Alice did tink to harself.

“Of di mushroom,” di Caterpillar seh, jus as if she did ask it loud; an inna anodda moment it did out a sight.

Alice did a look pan di mushroom fi a minute, a try mek out which a di two sides a it; an as it did perfectly round, she find dis a very difficult question. However, at last she stretch har arms roun it as far as dem woulda go, an break off a bit a di edge wid each han.

“An now which is which?” she seh to harself, an nibble a likkle a di right-hand bit fi try di effect: di neks moment shi feel a violent blow unda har chin: it did struck har foot!

Shi did a gud deal fraitn fram dis very sudden change, but shi did feel seh a neva no time fi be lost, as she did a shrink rapidly; so she set fi work at once fi eat some a di ada bit. Har chin did press so closely gens har foot, dat it neva have no room fi open har mout; but she did it at last, an manage fi swallow a morsel a di lefthand bit.

“Kom, mi ed frii at laas!” seh Alice inna a tone a delight, weh change inna alarm inna anodda moment, wen she find out seh har shoulda dem neva deh noweh: all she coulda see, wen she look dung, was an immense length a neck, weh did look like it did a rise like a stalk outa a sea a green leaves weh did deh far below har.

“Weh all dat deh green stuff can be?” seh Alice. “An we mi shuolda dem gaan? An oh, mi puor han dem, ou kom mi kyaahn si yu?” Shi did a move dem bout as she did a taak, but no result neva did a follow, except a likkle shaking mongx di distant green leaves dem.

As it did look like it neva have no chance fi get har han dem up to har head, she did try fi get har head dung to dem, an did glad fi find out seh har neck woulda bend bout easy inna any direction, like a serpent. Shi did jus succeed inna curve it dung inna a graceful zigzag, an did a go dive inna di leaves dem, weh shi did find out seh a nutten but di tops a di trees dem unda weh she did a wander, wen a sharp hiss mek har draw back inna a hurry: a big pigeon did a fly inna har face, an did a beat har violently wid it wings.

“Serpent!” di Pigeon baal out.

“Mi a no no siniek!” seh Alice indignantly. “Let mi alone!”

“Serpent, mi seh again!” di Pigeon repeat, but inna a more subdued tone, an added wid a kind a sob, “Mi try every way, an notn nuh look like it suit dem!”

“Mi nuh have di least idea weh yuh a talk bout,” seh Alice.

“Mi try di roots a trees, an mi try banks, an mi try hedges,” di Pigeon seh, widout attending to har; “bot dem siniek de, notn kyaahn mek dem api!”

Alice did more an more puzzled, but she did tink seh nuh use notn more til di Pigeon did finish.

“As if it neva trouble enough fi hatch di eggs,” seh di Pigeon; “Bot mi afi wach out fi siniek nait an die, kaaz dem chrii wiik ya mi no get no sliip a wan wink!”

“Mi very sorry yuh did a get annoyed,” seh Alice, weh did a staat si it meaning.

“An jus as mi did tek di highest tree inna di wood,” di Pigeon continue, a raise it voice to a shriek, “an jus as mi did a tink seh mi shuda be free a dem at last, dem mus need fi come wriggling dung fram di sky! Ugh, Serpent!”

“Bot mi a no no siniek, mi tel unu!” seh Alice. “Mi a—mi a—”

“Well! Weh yuh is?” seh di Pigeon. “Mi can see yuh a try invent someting!”

“Mi—mi a one likkle gyal,” seh Alice, radda doubtfully, as she memba di amount a changes she did goh chruu dat deh day.

“A likely story indeed!” di Pigeon seh inna a tone a di deepest contempt. “Mi eva si a gud uol iip a likl gyal pikni iina mi taim, bot neva wan wid so a nek laik dat! Nuo, no! Yu a wan siniek, an no yuus fi se yu no nuo it. Mi tingk se yu a-go tel mi neks taim se yu neva ties no eg!”

“Mi did taste eggs, certainly,” seh Alice, weh was a very truthful pikni; “but likkle girls eat eggs quite as much as serpents do, yuh know.”

“Mi no biliiv it,” di Pijin se; “but if dem do, why den dem a one kain a sniek, dat a all mi can seh.”

Dis was such a new idea to Alice, dat she did quite silent fi a minute or two, weh gi di Pigeon di opportunity fi add, “Yuh a look fi eggs, mi know dat well enough; an weh it matta to mi weda yuh a likkle gyal or a serpent?”

“It matta a good deal to mi ,” seh Alice hastily; “bot mi naa luk fi eg, az it happen; an if mi did, mi shudn waahn yu wan : mi no laik dem raw.”

“Well, be off, den!” di Pigeon seh inna a sulky tone, as it settle dung again inna it nest. Alice crouch dung mongst di trees as well as she could, cause har neck did a get entangled mongx di branch dem, an every now an den she did haffi stop an untwist it. Afta a wail shi memba se shi stil did a uol di piis dem a mosl ina ar an dem, an shi set fi wok gud gud, a nibl fos a wan an den a di neks wan, an a gruo somtaim biga an somtaim shaata, til shi did soksesful fi bring arself dong tu ar yuujal ai.

It did so long since she neva have anyting near di right size, dat it did feel quite strange at first; but she get used to it inna a few minutes, an staat talk to harself, as usual. “Kom, aaf a mi plan don nou! Ou puzzling aal a dem chienj ya! Mi neva shuor wa mi a go bi, fram wan minit tu di neks! Bot, mi get bak tu mi rait saiz: di neks ting a, fi get ina da priti priti gyaadn de— ou dat fi du, mi wanda?” Wen shi did a se dis, shi riich suudn-suudn a wan uopm plies wid wan likl ous bout fuo fut ai. “Whoever live deh,” tink Alice, “it nah go eva do fi come pan dem dis size: why, mi shuda frighten dem outa dem wits!” So she staat nibbling pan di righthand bit again, an neva venture fi go near di house til she did bring harself dung to nine inches high.
