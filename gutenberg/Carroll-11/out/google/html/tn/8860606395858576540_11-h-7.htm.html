KGAOLO VII.
Moletlo wa Teye wa Botsenwa

Go ne go na le tafole e e neng e beilwe ka fa tlase ga setlhare fa pele ga ntlo, mme Mmutla wa Mopitlwe le Modiri wa Dihutshe ba ne ba nwa teye mo go yone: Dormouse e ne e ntse fa gare ga bone, e robetse thata, mme ba bangwe ba babedi ba ne ba e dirisa jaaka mosamo, ba ikhutsa ka dikgopo tsa bone mo go yone, mme ba bua mo godimo ga tlhogo ya yone. “Ga go phuthologe thata mo go Dormouse,” Alice o ne a akanya jalo; “fela, jaaka e robetse, ke akanya gore ga e na bothata.”

Tafole e ne e le e kgolo, mme boraro jwa tsone bo ne bo pitlagane mmogo mo sekhutlong sengwe sa yone: “Ga go na phaposi! Ga go na phaposi!” ba ne ba goa fa ba bona Alice a tla. “Go na le sebaka se sentsi !” Ga rialo Alice a galefile, mme a nna mo setulong se segolo sa diatla kwa ntlheng e nngwe ya tafole.

“Nwa beine,” Mmutla wa Mopitlwe o ne a bua jalo ka lentswe le le kgothatsang.

Alice o ne a leba gotlhe mo tafoleng, mme go ne go se na sepe mo go yone fa e se teye fela. O ne a akgela jaana: “Ga ke bone beine epe.”

“Ga go na epe,” ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

Alice o ne a galefa jaana: “Mme go ne go se botho jo bogolo mo go wena go e naya.”

“E ne e se setho se segolo mo go wena go nna fa fatshe o sa lalediwa,” ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

“Ke ne ke sa itse gore ke tafole ya gago ,” ga rialo Alice; “e beilwe bakeng sa ba bangata ba baholo ho feta ba bararo.”

“Moriri wa gago o batla go kutolwa,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe. O ne a ntse a lebile Alice ka nako e e rileng ka keletso e kgolo ya go itse, mme eno e ne e le puo ya gagwe ya ntlha.

“O tshwanetse go ithuta go se bue ka namana,” Alice o ne a bua jalo ka bogale bongwe; “e makgwakgwa thata.”

Modiri wa Hutshe a bula matlho thata fa a utlwa se; mme se a neng a se bua fela e ne e le gore, “Ke ka ntlha yang fa legakabe le tshwana le tafole ya go kwalela?”

“Tlang, re tla ijesa monate jaanong!” Alice o ne a akanya jalo. “Ke itumelela gore ba simolotse go botsa dithamalakane.—Ke dumela gore nka fopholetsa seo,” o ne a oketsa jalo kwa godimo.

“A o raya gore o akanya gore o ka kgona go bona karabo ya yone?” Ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

Alice o ne a re: “Go ntse fela jalo.

“Go tswa foo o tshwanetse go bua se o se rayang,” Mmutla wa Mopitlwe wa tswelela.

Alice o ne a araba ka bonako a re: “Ke a itse; “bonyane—bonyane ke bolela seo ke se buang—seo ke ntho e le nngwe, o a tseba.”

“E seng selo se le sengwe go sekae!” Ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe. “O ka nna wa bolela fela jalo gore ‘Ke bona se ke se jang’ ke selo se le sengwe fela le ‘Ke ja se ke se bonang’!”

March Hare o ne a oketsa jaana: “O ka nna wa bolela fela jalo gore ‘Ke rata se ke se bonang’ ke selo se le sengwe fela le ‘Ke bona se ke se ratang’!”

“O ka nna wa bua fela jalo,” ga oketsa jalo Dormouse, yo o neng a lebega a bua mo borokong jwa gagwe, “gore ‘Ke hema fa ke robetse’ ke selo se le sengwe le ‘Ke robala fa ke hema’!”

“ Go ntse fela jalo le ka wena,” ga rialo Motshwari wa Dihutshe, mme fano motlotlo wa wela tlase, mme lekoko la nna la didimala motsotso, fa Alice a ne a akanya ka tsotlhe tse a neng a ka di gakologelwa ka magakabe le ditafole tsa go kwala, tse di neng di se dintsi.

Modiri wa Hutshe e ne e le ene wa ntlha go thuba tidimalo. “Ke letsatsi lefe la kgwedi?” A bua jalo, a retologela kwa go Alice: o ne a ntshitse tshupanako ya gagwe mo kgetsaneng ya gagwe, mme a e lebile a sa phuthologa, a e tshikinya nako le nako, mme a e tshwara mo tsebeng.

Alice o ne a akanyetsa go sekae, mme morago a re “Ya bone.”

“Malatsi a mabedi a fositse!” ga fegelwa Modiri wa Hutshe. “Ke go boleletse gore botoro ga e kitla e tshwanela ditiro!” A tlaleletsa a lebile Mmutla wa Mopitlwe ka bogale.

“E ne e le botoro e e gaisang tsotlhe ,” Mmutla wa March o ne a araba ka bonolo.

“Ee, mme di-crumb dingwe di tshwanetse tsa bo di tsene le tsone,” Modiri wa Dihutshe a ngongorega jalo: “o ne o sa tshwanela go e tsenya ka thipa ya borotho.”

Mmutla wa Mopitlwe o ne a tsaya tshupanako mme a e leba ka tlhobaelo: morago ga moo a e ina mo koping ya gagwe ya teye, mme a e leba gape: mme o ne a sa kgone go akanya ka sepe se se botoka go se bua go na le kakgelo ya gagwe ya ntlha, “E ne e le botoro e e gaisang , o a itse.”

Alice o ne a ntse a lebile ka fa godimo ga legetla la gagwe ka go batla go itse. “A bo e le tshupanako e e tshegisang jang ne!” o ne a tshwaela jalo. “E bolela letsatsi la kgwedi, mme ga e bolele gore ke ura mang!”

“Ke ka ntlha yang fa e tshwanetse go nna jalo?” ga ngunanguna Modiri wa Hutshe. “A tshupanako ya gago e go bolelela gore ke ngwaga ofe?”

“Ee, nnyaa,” Alice o ne a araba jalo ka bonako: “mme seo ke ka gonne e nna mo ngwageng o le mongwe ka nako e telele jaana mmogo.”

“Se se ntseng jalo fela ka sa me ,” ga rialo Modiri wa Dihutshe.

Alice o ne a ikutlwa a gakgametse ka tsela e e tshosang, kakgelo ya ga The Hatter e ne e lebega e sena bokao bope mo go yone, mme le fa go ntse jalo e ne e le Seesemane eleruri. “Ga ke go tlhaloganye sentle,” o ne a bua jalo, ka maitseo ka moo a neng a ka kgona.

“Dormouse e robetse gape,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe, mme a tshela teye e nnye e e bolelo mo nkong ya yone.

Dormouse ya tshikinya tlhogo ya yone e sa fele pelo, mme ya re, e sa bula matlho a yone, “Ee, ee; fela se ke neng ke tlile go se tshwaela ka bonna.”

“A o setse o fopholeditse seane?” Modiri wa Dihutshe a rialo, a retologela kwa go Alice gape.

“Nnyaa, ke a e tlogela,” Alice o ne a araba jalo: “karabo ke eng?”

“Ga ke na kakanyo epe,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe.

“Le nna,” ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

Alice o ne a fegelwa ka letsapa. O ne a re: “Ke akanya gore o ka nna wa dira sengwe se se botoka ka nako eo go na le go e senya ka go botsa dithamalakane tse di senang dikarabo.”

“Fa o ne o itse Nako sentle jaaka nna,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe, “o ne o ka se bue ka go e senya . Ke ene .”

Alice o ne a re: “Ga ke itse gore o raya jang.

“Ee ruri ga o dire jalo!” Modiri wa Hutshe a bua jalo a tshikinya tlhogo ka lenyatso. “Ke na le sebete sa go re ga o ise o ko o bue le Time!”

Alice o ne a araba jaana ka kelotlhoko: “Gongwe nnyaa, mme ke a itse gore ke tshwanetse go fenya nako fa ke ithuta mmino.”

“Ah!seo se a e tlhalosa,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe. "Ga a kitla a itshokela go itewa. Jaanong, fa o ne o ka nna o dirisana sentle le ene, o ne a tla dira mo e ka nnang sengwe le sengwe se o se ratang ka tshupanako. Ka sekai, a re re e ne e le ka ura ya borobongwe mo mosong, e le nako fela ya go simolola dithuto: o ne o tla tshwanelwa ke go sebaseba fela go Nako, mme tshupanako e tsamaya ka nako e le nngwe, go tlhotlhora!"

(“Ke eletsa fela gore e ka bo e le jalo,” Mmutla wa Mopitlwe o ne a bua jalo ka pelo ka go seba.)

“Seo e tla bo e le se segolo thata, eleruri,” Alice o ne a rialo a akanya: “mme he—ga ke a tshwanela go bolawa ke tlala ya sone, o a itse.”

“E seng kwa tshimologong, gongwe,” ga rialo Modiri wa Dihutshe: “mme o ne o ka e boloka e le mo halofong ya ura ya bongwe ka lobaka lo lo ka lo ratang.”

“A ke yone tsela e o laolang ka yone?” Alice o ne a botsa.

Modiri wa Hutshe a tshikinya tlhogo ka khutsafalo. “E seng nna!” A araba jalo. “Re ne ra omana mo kgweding e e fetileng ya Mopitlwe—pele fela ga a tsenwa, o a itse—” (a supa ka leswana la gagwe la tee kwa March Hare,) “—e ne e le kwa khonsarateng e kgolo e e neng e neelwa ke Kgosigadi ya Dipelo, mme ke ne ka tshwanelwa ke go opela .

'Panya, phatsima, mmamanthwane yo monnye!
Ke ipotsa gore o mo go eng!'

O itse pina, gongwe?”

Alice o ne a re: “Ke kile ka utlwa sengwe se se tshwanang le seo.

“Go tswelela, o a itse,” Modiri wa Hutshe a tswelela jalo, “ka tsela eno:—

'Kwa godimo ga lefatshe o fofa, .
Jaaka terei ya tee mo loaping.
Phatsima, phatsima—’”

Fano Dormouse e ne ya itlhotlhora, mme ya simolola go opela mo borokong jwa yone “ Phatsima, phatsima, phatsima, phatsima —” mme ya tswelela ka nako e telele mo ba neng ba tshwanelwa ke go e tshwaratshwara go dira gore e eme.

“Ebu, ke ne ke ise ke fetse temana ya ntlha,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe, “fa Kgosigadi a tlola a bo a goa a re, ‘O bolaya nako!

“A bo e le setlhogo se se boitshegang jang ne!” Alice o ne a goa jalo.

“Mme fa e sale ka nako eo,” Modiri wa Hutshe a tswelela ka lentswe le le hutsafetseng, “ga a kitla a dira selo se ke se kopang! Ka metlha ke ura ya borataro jaanong.”

Kgopolo e e phatsimang e ne ya tla mo tlhogong ya ga Alice. “A ke lone lebaka la go bo go bewa dilo tse dintsi tsa teye kwa ntle fano?” O ne a botsa.

“Ee, ke gone,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe a fegelwa: “ka metlha ke nako ya teye, mme ga re na nako ya go tlhatswa dilo fa gare ga dinako.”

“Mme o nna o dikologa, ke akanya jalo?” ” Alice o boletse.

“Go ntse fela jalo,” ga rialo Modiri wa Dihutshe: “fa dilo di ntse di tlwaela.”

“Mme go diragalang fa o tla kwa tshimologong gape?” Alice o ne a itlhaganelela go botsa.

“A re re re fetola kgang,” Mmutla wa March o ne a mo kgaola, a tlhotlhora. “Ke lapisiwa ke se.Ke tlhopha lekgarebe le re bolelela kgang.”

“Ke tshaba gore ga ke itse epe,” ga rialo Alice, go na le moo a tshogile ka tshitshinyo eo.

“Mme Peba ya Borobalo e tla!” ba lela ka bobedi. “Tsoga, Peba ya Robala!” Mme ba e tshwaratshwara ka matlhakore oobedi ka nako e le nngwe.

Dormouse a bula matlho ka iketlo. O ne a bua jaana ka lentswe le le bokoa le le bokoa: “Ke ne ke sa robala: “Ke utlwile lefoko lengwe le lengwe le lo neng lo le bua.”

“Re bolelele kgang!” Ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

“Ee, tsweetswee dira jalo!” Alice o ne a kopa jalo.

“Mme o nne bonako ka gone,” ga oketsa jalo Modiri wa Hutshe, “kgotsa o tla bo o robetse gape pele ga go wediwa.”

“Ka nako nngwe go ne go na le bokgaitsadi ba bararo ba bannye,” Dormouse a simolola ka lepotlapotla le legolo; “mme maina a bone e ne e le Elsie, Lacie, le Tillie;mme ba ne ba nna kwa tlase ga sediba—”

“Ba ne ba tshela ka eng?” ga rialo Alice, yo ka metlha a neng a kgatlhegela thata dipotso tsa go ja le go nwa.

“Ba ne ba tshela ka treacle,” ga rialo Dormouse, morago ga go akanya motsotso kgotsa metsotso e mebedi.

“Ba ka bo ba sa kgona go dira jalo, o a itse,” Alice o ne a akgela jalo ka bonolo; “ba ka bo ba lwala.”

“Ba ne ba ntse jalo,” ga rialo Peba ya Borobalo; “ ke lwala thata .”

Alice o ne a leka go ikakanyetsa gore tsela e e sa tlwaelegang jalo ya go tshela e ne e tla nna jang, mme seno se ne sa mo gakgamatsa thata, ka jalo o ne a tswelela jaana: “Mme ke eng fa ba ne ba nna kwa tlase ga sediba?”

“Tsaya teye e nngwe gape,” Mmutla wa March o ne a raya Alice jalo, ka tlhoafalo e kgolo.

“Ga ke ise ke nne le sepe,” Alice o ne a araba ka lentswe le le kgopisitsweng, “ka jalo ga ke kgone go tsaya tse dingwe.”

“O raya gore ga o ka ke wa tsaya go le gonnye ,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe: “go motlhofo thata go tsaya go feta go tlhoka sepe.”

Alice o ne a re: “Ga go ope yo o neng a go botsa gore o akanya eng.

“Ke mang yo o dirang dikakgelo tsa botho jaanong?” Modiri wa Hutshe a botsa ka phenyo.

Alice o ne a sa itse sentle gore a reng ka seno: ka jalo o ne a ithusa ka teye le borotho-le-botoro, mme morago a retologela kwa go Dormouse, mme a boeletsa potso ya gagwe. “Ke ka ntlha yang fa ba ne ba nna kwa tlase ga sediba?”

Dormouse gape e ne ya tsaya motsotso kgotsa metsotso e mebedi go akanya ka gone, mme morago ga moo ya re, “E ne e le sediba sa treacle.”

“Ga go na sepe se se ntseng jalo!” Alice o ne a simolola a galefile thata, mme Modiri wa Dihutshe le Mmutla wa March ba ne ba tsamaya “Sh! sh!” mme Dormouse a akgela jaana ka bogale, “Fa o sa kgone go nna setšhaba, go botoka o fetse kgang ka bowena.”

“Nnyaa, ke kopa gore o tswelele!” Alice o ne a bua jalo ka boikokobetso thata; “Ga nkitla ke tsena ganong gape. Ke na le sebete sa go re go ka tswa go na le mongwe .”

“E le nngwe, eleruri!” Ga bua Dormouse a galefile. Le fa go ntse jalo, o ne a dumela go tswelela. “Mme ka jalo dikgaitsadi tse dinnye tse bararo tse—ba ne ba ithuta go thala, o a itse—”

“Ba ne ba thala eng?” Ga rialo Alice, a lebetse tsholofetso ya gagwe gotlhelele.

“Treacle,” ga rialo Dormouse, e sa akanyetse gotlhelele ka nako e.

“Ke batla kopi e e phepa,” ga kgaola Modiri wa Dihutshe: “a rotlhe re tsamaye lefelo le le lengwe go ya pele.”

O ne a tswelela pele fa a ntse a bua, mme Dormouse ya mo sala morago: Mmutla wa March o ne a fudugela mo lefelong la Dormouse, mme Alice go na le moo a sa batle go tsaya sebaka sa Mmutla wa March. Motshwari wa Hutshe e ne e le ene fela yo o neng a bona mosola ope mo phetogong: mme Alice o ne a le maswe thata go feta pele, ka Mmutla wa Mopitlwe o ne a sa tswa go kgoreletsa nkgwana ya mashi mo sejaneng sa gagwe.

Alice o ne a sa eletse go kgopisa Dormouse gape, ka jalo o ne a simolola ka kelotlhoko thata: “Mme ga ke tlhaloganye. Ba gogile kae treacle?”

“O ka ga metsi mo sedibeng sa metsi,” ga rialo Modiri wa Hutshe; “ka jalo ke tshwanetse go akanya gore o ka goga treacle mo sedibeng sa treacle—eh, sematla?”

“Mme ba ne ba le mo sedibeng,” Alice o ne a raya Dormouse jalo, a sa tlhophe go ela tlhoko kakgelo eno ya bofelo.

“Ee, ba ne ba le teng,” ga rialo Dormouse; “—go tsena sentle.”

Karabo e e ne ya tlhakanya Alice yo o humanegileng tlhogo thata, mo e leng gore o ne a letlelela Dormouse go tswelela ka nako nngwe a sa e kgaotse.

“Ba ne ba ithuta go thala,” Dormouse ya tswelela, e tlhotlhora le go itlotsa matlho, ka e ne e simolola go robala thata; “mme ba thala dilo tsa mefuta yotlhe—sengwe le sengwe se se simololang ka M—”

“Ke eng ka M?” ” Alice o boletse.

"Goreng?" Ga rialo Mmutla wa Mopitlwe.

Alice o ne a didimetse.

Dormouse e ne e tswetse matlho ka nako e, mme e ne e ya go robala; mme, fa e ne e tshwarwa ke Modiri wa Dihutshe, e ne ya tsoga gape ka go goa go le gonnye, mme ya tswelela: “—e e simololang ka M, jaaka dirai tsa dipeba, le ngwedi, le kgakologelo, le bontsi—o a itse gore o re dilo ke “bontsi jwa bontsi”—a o kile wa bona selo se se ntseng jalo sa bontsi jaaka setshwantsho sa?”

“Tota, jaanong o a mpotsa,” ga rialo Alice, a tlhakane tlhogo thata, “Ga ke akanye gore—”

“Fa go le jalo ga o a tshwanela go bua,” ga rialo Modiri wa Dihutshe.

Sengwathwana seno sa go tlhoka maitseo se ne se feta se Alice a neng a ka se itshokela: o ne a ema ka temogo e kgolo, mme a tsamaya; Dormouse e ne ya robala ka ponyo ya leitlho, mme ga go ope wa ba bangwe yo o neng a ela tlhoko go tsamaya ga gagwe, le fa a ne a leba kwa morago gangwe kgotsa gabedi, a solofetse ka halofo gore ba tla mo bitsa morago ga gagwe: lekgetlo la bofelo fa a ba bona, ba ne ba leka go tsenya Dormouse mo sejaneng sa tee.

“Le fa go ka nna jang ga nkitla ke tlhola ke ya koo gape!” Ga rialo Alice fa a ntse a tlhopha tsela ya gagwe mo dikgong. “Ke phathi ya tee ya boeleele go gaisa tsotlhe tse nkileng ka nna kwa go tsone mo botshelong jwa me jotlhe!”

Fela fa a bua jalo, o ne a lemoga gore sengwe sa ditlhare se na le lebati le le neng le tsena mo go sone. “Seo se batla go itse thata!” O ne a akanya jalo. "Mme sengwe le sengwe se batla go itse gompieno. Ke akanya gore go ka nna molemo gore ke tsene ka yone nako eo." Mme a tsena.

O ne a iphitlhela gape a le mo holong e telele, mme gaufi le tafole e nnye ya galase. “Jaanong, ke tla kgona botoka mo nakong eno,” a bua jalo ka pelo, mme a simolola ka go tsaya selotlolo se sennye sa gauta, le go lotlolola lebati le le neng le tsena mo tshingwaneng. Morago ga moo o ne a ya tirong a tlhotlhora mushroom (o ne a bolokile karolo ya one mo kgetsaneng ya gagwe) go fitlhela a nna bogodimo jwa leoto le le ka nnang: morago ga moo a tsamaya a fologa phaseje e nnye: mme morago ga moo —a iphitlhela kgabagare a le mo tshingwaneng e ntle, mo gare ga dithunya tse di phatsimang le metswedi e e tsidifetseng.
