Party-Tee-Mad

Va boayrd er ny hoiaghey fo craid kiongoyrt rish y thie, as va'n Lion March as y Hatter gobbraghey tee rish: va Dormouse ny hoie eddyr ad, ry-hoi, as va'n daa elley ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi, ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi. “Shegin da’n Dormouse ve glen,” smooinee Alice; “agh, myr t’eh coodaghey, ta mee credjal nagh vel eh red erbee.”

Va’n table mooar, agh v’ad ooilley crooit cooidjagh ayns un chorneil jeh: “Cha nel room! Cha nel room!” ghra ad tra honnick ad Alice cheet. “Ta mooarane room ayn!” dooyrt Alice dy ghraih, as hie ee sheese ayns cadrey-arm mooar ec un cheu jeh'n table.

“Gow uss red erbee jeh’n wine,” as y Lionney March ayns tonney ymmyrchagh.

Va Alice er ny s’cosoylagh rish y table, agh cha row nhee erbee er y table agh tee. “Cha vel mee fakin wine erbee,” dooyrt ee.

“Cha nel red erbee,” as y Lion March.

“Shen-y-fa cha row eh feer civil jeu dy chur eh,” as Alice lesh fear.

“Cha row eh feer sivil ayd’s dy hoie sheese gyn cuirrey,” as y Lieh March.

“Cha row fys aym dy nee dty table v’ayn,” as Alice; “t’eh er ny hoiaghey son ymmodee ny smoo na three.”

“Ta dty ghruagh feddyn gearree,” dooyrt y Hatter. Va eshyn er n’eaishtagh rish Alice son y traa shen lesh mooar y ghraih, as shoh y chiad obbyr echey.

“Shegin dhyt ymmyd y yannoo jeh reddyn persoonagh,” as Alice lesh beggan ard-chree; “T’eh feer rude.”

As hrog y Hatter e sooillyn dy mooar tra chlash eh shoh; agh ooilley ny dooyrt eh, “Cre’n-fa ta’n corran myr board scriptyragh?”

“Ty, bee shin ry-gheddyn red erbee nish!” smooinee Alice. “Ta mee booiagh dy vel ad er toshiaght dy ghoaill rieughid.—Ta mee credjal dy vod mee shen y ghra,” as ee gra rish.

“Ta’d gra dy vel oo credjal dy vod oo y freggyrt da y gheddyn?” dooyrt y Hare March.

“Dy jarroo,” dooyrt Alice.

“Shen-y-fa, shegin dhyt gra cre’n red ta’d dy ghra,” hie yn Lion March er-gerrey.

“Ta mee,” dreggyr Alice dy hrooid; “er-y-fa shen—er-y-fa shen ta mee gra shen ta mee gra—she’n un red shen, ta fys ayd.”

“Cha nee yn un red beggan!” dooyrt y Hatter. “S’maynrey t’ou uss dy ghra dy vel ‘Ta mee fakin ny ta mee gee’ yr un red as ‘Ta mee gee ny ta mee gee’!”

“Shegin dhyt gra myr shen,” dooyrt y March Hare, “dy vel ‘Ta mee graihagh er ny ta mee geddyn’ yr un red as ‘Ta mee graihagh er ny ta mee geddyn’!”

“S’maynrey t’ou dy ghra,” as y Dormouse, v’er ny ghra myr v’eh loayrt ayns e choayl, “dy vel ‘ta mee coayl tra ta mee coayl’ yr un red as ‘ta mee coayl tra ta mee coayl’!”

“ She’n un red eh rhyt,” dooyrt y Hatter, as ec y traa shoh daag y chorail, as hie’n phartid sheese son un minnid, tra v’Alice smooinaghtyn er ooilley ny v’ee ry-hoi jeh’n ghrian as jeh’n ghrian-screeuee, nagh row mooarane.

Yn Hatter v'eh y chied er n'yannoo y shliaghtey. “Cre’n laa jeh’n mhee t’ayn?” ’sy ghraih echey, as eshyn er n’irree gys Alice: v’eh er ghoaill e h-oor ass e phoosey, as v’eh er n’eaishtagh rish dy row eh er n’eaishtagh rish, as v’eh er n’eaishtagh rish gagh traa, as v’eh er n’eaishtagh rish e chleaysh.

Smooinee Alice beggan, as dooyrt ee “Yn cheyoo.”

“Daa laa er-chea!” ’s eh y Hatter. “Dy row mee er ghra rhyt, nagh row butter ry-hoi rish ny obbraghyn!” ’s eh gra rish, as eh jeeaghyn lesh fearg er y Hare March.

“She’n butter smoo ,” dreggyr y Lieu March dy ghraih.

“Ta, agh shegin da cuid jeh’n chruinney cheet stiagh myrgeddin,” as y Hatter grummal: “cha beagh oo er chur eh stiagh lesh y lhong-arran.”

Ghow y Lionney March y watch as honnick eh eh glooish: eisht hug eh eh stiagh ayns e choppey tee, as honnick eh eh reesht: agh cha row eh ry-hoi ry-smooinee er nhee erbee ny share dy ghra na’n chied obbyr echey, “S’eh y butter share , ta fys ayd.”

Va Alice er n’eaishtagh rish e shliawn lesh beggan curiousity. “Cre’n aght s’goaill rish!” dooyrt ee. “T’eh gra laa’n mhee, as cha nel eh gra cre’n oor t’eh!”

“Cre’n-fa dy beagh eh?” ’s eh mraane y Hatter. “Bee dty urree gra dhyt c’raad t’eh?”

“Cha nel,” dreggyr Alice dy row: “agh shen er-yn-oyr dy vel eh cummal yr un vleïn son cho fadagh cooidjagh.”

“Cre’n aght t’eh ry- akin ,” dooyrt y Hatter.

Va Alice feer cheeayllagh, cha row ymmyd erbee ec y ghraih ec y Hatter, as foast v'eh ry-hoi 'sy Ghaelg. “Cha nel mee dy jarroo tushtey jeh,” as ee, cho politely as v’ee.

“Ta’n Dormouse coodaghey reesht,” as y Hatter, as hug eh beggan tee tey er e shlieau.

Chreck y Dormouse e ben gyn shee, as dooyrt eh, fegooish e sooillyn y foshlit, “S’cosoylagh, s’cosoylagh;

“Vel oo er n’yannoo toiggal jeh’n rieughid foast?” dooyrt y Hatter, as t’eh geddyn stiagh ayns Alice reesht.

“Cha nel, ta mee cur eh ersooyl,” dreggyr Alice: “cre’n freggyrt t’ayn?”

“Cha nel y smooinaght erbee aym,” dooyrt y Hatter.

“Chamoo mish,” dooyrt y Lion March.

Va Alice glennit. “S’maynrey lhiam dy vod oo red erbee ny share y yannoo rish y traa,” as ee, “na dy s’goanney eh ayns goaill rish rieughid nagh vel freggyrtyn oc.”

“Myr shen, my row oo er hoiggal Time cho mie as ta mee ,” dooyrt y Hatter, “cha row oo er loayrt mychione y sniessey .

“Cha nel fys aym cre t’ou dy ghra,” dooyrt Alice.

“S’cosoylagh nagh vel oo!” ’ as y Hatter, as t’eh shleaystey e chree dy s’diu. “Ta mee goaill rish nagh row oo rieau er loayrt rish Time!”

“Ta’n aght nagh vel,” dreggyr Alice lesh ard-chree: “agh ta fys aym dy vel mee goaill rish traa tra ta mee ry-laghyn ceayrt.”

"Ah! ta shen coontey jeh," dooyrt y Hatter. "Cha jean eh seasagh rish y ghraih. Nish, mannagh row oo er-y-fa shen er-y-fa shen, hig eh dy yannoo red erbee taitnyssagh dhyt rish y chloan. Mar shampleyr, lhig da ve jeih o'clock y moghrey, agh y traa dy toshiaght lessonyn: cha row red erbee ayd agh dy ghra rish y Chloan, as t'eh goll er-gerrey da'n chloan, as t'eh ry-hoi!"

(“Cha nee agh dy beagh eh,” as y Lion March rish hene ayns shliaghtey.)

“By vie shen, dy shickyr,” as Alice smooinaghtyn: “agh eisht—cha dod mee ve accruit er, ta fys ayd.”

“Cha nee er y toshiaght, mraane,” dooyrt y Hatter: “agh oddagh oo eh y choadey gys lesh-lesh un oor cho fadane as baillish oo.”

“Ansherbee shen y raad ta’d jannoo?” Gow Alice rish.

Chreck y Hatter e chree dy ghraih. “Cha nee mish!” dreggyr eh. “Ren shin streeu ayns Mee ny Shalee shoh chaie—dy jarroo roish my daink eh dy ghra, ta fys ayd—” (goaill rish e spuit-tee er y March Hare,) “—va’n concert mooar v’er ny choyrt ec Bannister ny Creeaghyn, as v’eh orrym sheeyney .

'Twink, t'eh, bat beg!
Cre'n aght ta mee boggyssagh cre'n red t'ou uss!'

Ta fys ayd er y chlaare, mraane?”

“Ta mee er chlashtyn red erbee myr shen,” as Alice.

“T’eh goll er, ta fys ayd,” as y Hatter er-gerrey, “er y raad shoh:—

'Sy skyn 'sy theihll ta shiu fliaghey,
Myr trae-tee ayns y speyr.
Twinkle, twinkle—'”

Hie yn Dormouse er-gerrey, as t’eh er n’yannoo sheean ayns e choayl “ Twinkle, twinkle, twinkle, twinkle —” as hie eh er-gerrey cho fadagh as dy row ad dy pinch eh dy yannoo eh staydys.

“Well, cha row mee er n’yannoo y chiad verse,” dooyrt y Hatter, “tra lhoob y Bannister seose as ghra, ‘T’eh murder y traa! Off lesh e ben!’”

“Cre’n aght ta’n aght savage!” ’ dy ghra Alice.

"As rish shen," hie yn Hatter er-gerrey lesh tonn boghtey, "cha jean eh red erbee ta mee goaill rish! T'eh dagh ooilley traa shey o'clock nish."

T'eh er n'gholl stiagh ayns ben Alice. “S’eh shen y red ta’n chooid smoo dy reddyn-tee currit magh shoh?” v'ee er cheau.

“Ta, shen eh,” dooyrt y Hatter lesh sniaghtey: “t’eh dagh traa tea-time, as cha nel traa ainyn dy ghlenney ny reddyn eddyr traa.”

“As t’ou uss geddyn stiagh, ta mee credjal?” dooyrt Alice.

“Dy jarroo,” dooyrt y Hatter: “myr ta ny reddyn cleaynit.”

“Agh cre’n red ta ry-yannoo tra t’ou er jeet gys y toshiaght reesht?” Va Alice er n’eiyrt dy ghoaill.

“Shegin dooin y theay y chaghlaa,” as y Lion March y ghra, as eh y ghra. "Ta mee staghnit jeh shoh. Ta mee vote dy vel y ven-aeg loayrt story dooin."

“Ta mee aggle nagh vel mee toiggal un,” as Alice, raad dy row ee boggyssagh rish y ghraih.

“As eisht bee’n Dormouse!” ghra ad ooilley. “Dreggyr, Lugh-Sheer!” As hug ad eh er dagh cheu ec y traa cheddin.

As hrog y Dormouse e sooillyn stiagh. “Cha row mee coodaghey,” dooyrt eh lesh coraa bog, boggyssagh: “Chleaysh mee gagh fockle v’ou uss dy ghra.”

“Insh dooin story!” dooyrt y Hare March.

“Ta, jean!” ’ dy ghra Alice.

“As bee glen jeh,” dooyrt y Hatter, “ny bee oo coodaghey reesht roish my bee eh jeant.”

“Ushtey dy row trio sheeraghyn beggey,” ren y Dormouse toshiaght ayns ard-chree; “as va nyn enmyn Elsie, Lacie, as Tillie; as v’ad cummal ec bun towse—”

“Cre’n red v’ad beaghey er?” ’ as Alice, v’er n’yannoo ymmyd mooar jeh ceashlyn jeh’n vee as jeh’n vee.

“Va’d beaghey er treacle,” as y Dormouse, erreish da smooinaghtyn er un mhinid ny daa mhinid.

“Cha b’ad uss geddyn shen, ta fys ayd,” as Alice glen; “by vie lhieu.”

“Sy stayd shen v’ad,” dooyrt y Dormouse; “ feer ching.”

Va Alice er ny h-earroo dy ghra r’ee hene cre’n aght v’eh dy ve ayns y stayd s’odjey, agh v’eh ry-hoi ry-hoi, as hie ee er-soylley: “Agh cre’n-fa v’ad ry-hoi ayns bun y vee?”

“Gow uss red erbee jeh’n tee,” as y Lion March rish Alice, dy mooar.

“Cha nel mee er gheddyn nhee erbee foast,” dreggyr Alice lesh tonn offense, “son cha vod mee goaill ny smoo.”

“Ta’d gra nagh vod oo goaill ny sloo ,” dooyrt y Hatter: “t’eh ard-easyl goaill ny smoo na red erbee.”

“Cha row dooinney erbee er n’immeeaght ,” as Alice.

“Cre’n fer ta jannoo obbraghyn persoonagh nish?” ’ ass y Hatter triomfagh.

Cha row Alice dy jarroo fys echey cre v'ee dy ghra rish shoh: as ren ee ymmyd jeh'n tee as jeh'n arran-as-butter, as eisht t'ee er n'irree gys y Dormouse, as t'ee er n'irree reesht. “Cre’n-fa v’ad cummal ec bun y veeal?”

Ghow y Dormouse arragh un minnid ny daa minnid dy smooinaghtyn er, as dooyrt eh, “B’eh treacle-well.”

“Cha nel lheid ayn!” Va Alice toshiaght dy feer glen, agh hie yn Hatter as y Lion March “Sh! sh!” as dooyrt y Dormouse sulkily, “Mannagh vod oo ve civil, t’eh ny share dhyt crommey y story son oo hene.”

“Cha nel, gow er-gerrey!” Dooyrt Alice dy mooar; "Cha jean-ym brishey arragh. Ta mee goaill rish dy vod fer ve ayn."

“Un, dy jarroo!” dooyrt y Dormouse dy ghraih. Agh, ghow eh cooney dy gholl er-gerrey. “As myr shen ny tree sheeraghyn beggey shoh—va’d ry-laghyn dy tharroo, ta fys ayd—”

“Cre’n red ren ad tarroo?” dooyrt Alice, as t’ee er n’yannoo ymmyd jeh’n ghellal.

“Treacle,” dooyrt y Dormouse, fegooish smooinaghtyn er y traa shoh.

“Ta mee erreish cop glan,” as y Hatter y ghra: “lhig dooin ooilley glenney un ard.”

Va'n Dormouse er n'gholl er-gerrey myr v'eh loayrt, as va'n Dormouse er n'gholl er-gerrey da: va'n Lion March er n'gholl stiagh ayns ynnyd y Dormouse, as ghow Alice ynnyd y Lion March raad nagh row eh booiagh. Yn Hatter v'eh yn un red v'er gheddyn buinn jeh'n chaghlaa: as va Alice feer vee-chrauee na rish, son v'eh y March Hare ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi ry-hoi.

Cha row Alice er-y-fa shen er n’yannoo ymmyd jeh’n Dormouse reesht, as shen-y-fa t’ee er n’yannoo ymmyd jeh’n Dormouse: “Agh cha tushtal mee. C’raad v’ad er n’yannoo y treacle?”

“T’ou uss uss coodaghey ushtey ass ushtey-pooar,” dooyrt y Hatter; “son dy beagh mee smooinaghtyn dy row oo able dy tharroo treacle ass poll-treacle—eh, stupid?”

“Agh v’ad ayns y veeal,” dooyrt Alice rish y Dormouse, cha row ee er choyrt tastey da’n obbyr s’jerree shoh.

“S’cosoylagh dy row ad,” dooyrt y Dormouse; “—goaill stiagh.”

Va’n freggyrt shoh er n’yannoo rish Alice bochill, as dy row ee er lesh y Dormouse dy gholl er-gerrey son ymmyd jeh’n traa shen, fegooish y ghra.

“Va’d ry-laghyn dy tharroo,” hie’n Dormouse er-gerrey, glenney as ry-frooish e sooillyn, son v’eh gobbraghey dy mooar; “as v’ad tarroogh ooilley ny reddyn—gagh red ta toshiaght lesh M—”

“Cre’n-fa lesh M?” dooyrt Alice.

"Cre'n fa nagh?" dooyrt y Hare March.

Va Alice sheeyney.

Va'n Dormouse er n'eiyrt e sooillyn ec y traa shoh, as v'eh er n'gholl ersooyl ayns y ghrian; agh, tra v’eh er ny ghleiy liorish y Hatter, hrog eh seose reesht lesh beggan shliaghtey, as hie eh er-gerrey: “—ta shen toshiaght lesh M, myr trappyn-lugh, as y gheay, as cooinaghtyn, as mraane—ta fys ayd dy vel oo gra dy vel ny reddyn “mooar dy mraane”—veagh oo er vaik red erbee myr tarraing jeh?”

“Dy firrinagh, nish ta shiu goaill rish,” dooyrt Alice, feer cheeayllagh, “cha nel mee credjal—”

“Shen-y-fa cha lhisagh oo loayrt,” dooyrt y Hatter.

Va'n rannagh rudey shoh ny s'mooar na v'eh ry-hoi ec Alice: hie ee seose lesh mooar y ghraih, as hie ee ersooyl; t'eh er choayl y Dormouse dy h-oikoil, as cha row fer jeh'n jeh elley er n'yannoo y s'leaie jeh'n aght v'ee er n'gholl, agh v'ee er n'eaishtagh rish un vee ny daa vee, lesh leshtal dy row ad dy gholl er-gerrey: y traa stiagh v'ee er nyn shilley, v'ad shirrey dy chur y Dormouse stiagh 'sy teapot.

“Er dagh cass cha jed mee thie arragh!” dooyrt Alice tra v’ee pickey e raad trooid y chosh. “She’n tea-party smoo stupid t’er n’yannoo rish ayns my vea!”

Dreggys tra dooyrt ee shoh, hug ee tastey da dy row dorrys er-gerrey da un jeh ny craaghyn. “Shen feer curious!” smooinee ee. "Agh ta ooilley ny reddyn curious y laa shoh. Ta mee credjal dy vodym goll stiagh ry-cheilley." As hie ee stiagh.

As ec y traa shen, honnick ee ee hene ayns y hall ard, as gerrey da’n table beg glass. “Agh nish, hig orrym geddyn stiagh ny share y traa shoh,” as ee r’ee hene, as t’ee toshiaght lesh y lhong beg oor, as y dorrys y ghleashaghey stiagh ’sy ghard. As hie ee gys obbyr nibbling y mushroom (va’n mushroom er ny choyrt ayns e phoosey) derrey v’ee mysh un chosh erskyn: as hie ee sheese er y shliawn beg: as eisht —hooar ee ee hene er-jerrey ayns y ghard s’brinne, mestey ny laghyn-flooish glen as ny s’cowrey.
